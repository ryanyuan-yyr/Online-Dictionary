predict
prɪdɪkt priˈdɪkt prɪˈdɪkt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense predicts, present participle predicting, past tense , past participle predicted
1 VERB 预言；预测；预告 If you predict an event, you say that it will happen. 预言；预测；预告

  • The latest opinion polls are predicting a very close contest. [VERB noun]

    最近的民意测验预测竞争将会非常激烈。

  • He predicted that my hair would grow back 'in no time'. [VERB that]

    他预言我的头发“一眨眼”就会长回来。

  • It's hard to predict how a jury will react. [VERB wh]

    很难预测陪审团将会作出何种反应。

  • 'The war will continue another two or three years,' he predicted. [VERB with quote]

    “这场战争还会再持续两三年的时间，”他预测道。

SYN foretell, forecast, divine, foresee
