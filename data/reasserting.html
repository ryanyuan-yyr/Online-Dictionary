reassert
riːəsɜːʳt ˌriːəˈsɜːt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense reasserts, present participle reasserting, past tense , past participle reasserted
1 VERB 重申（地位、权力等） If you reassert your control or authority, you make it clear that you are still in a position of power, or you strengthen the power that you had. 重申（地位、权力等）

  • ...the government's continuing effort to reassert its control in the region. [VERB noun]

    政府不断努力重申其对该地区的控制权

  • The adults had reasserted their old authority. [VERB noun]

    大人们重申他们旧时的权威。

2 VERB （想法、习惯等）再次显现，再次出现 If something such as an idea or habit reasserts itself, it becomes noticeable again. （想法、习惯等）再次显现，再次出现

  • His sense of humour was beginning to reassert itself. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    他的幽默感开始再次显现。
