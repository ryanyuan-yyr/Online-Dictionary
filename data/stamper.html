stamp
stæmp stæmp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense stamps, present participle stamping, past tense , past participle stamped
1 N-COUNT 邮票 A stamp or a postage stamp is a small piece of paper which you lick and stick on an envelope or package before you post it to pay for the cost of the postage. 邮票

  • ...a book of stamps.

    邮票册

  • It's FREEPOST, so there's no need for a stamp.

    这是免费邮寄，不用贴邮票。

  • ...two first class stamps.

    两张一类邮票

2See also:food stamp 
3 N-COUNT 印章;图章;印记;戳记 A stamp is a small block of wood or metal which has a pattern or a group of letters on one side. You press it onto an pad of ink and then onto a piece of paper in order to produce a mark on the paper. The mark that you produce is also called a stamp . 印章;图章;印记;戳记

  • ...a date stamp and an ink pad.

    日期章和印台

  • You may live only where the stamp in your passport says you may.

    你只能在你护照上盖的印记所规定的地点居住。

SYN imprint, mark, brand, cast  
4 VERB 盖(章);打(戳记) If you stamp a mark or word on an object, you press the mark or word onto the object using a stamp or other device. 盖(章);打(戳记)

  • Car manufacturers stamp a vehicle identification number at several places on new cars to help track down stolen vehicles. [VERB noun prep.]

    汽车制造商在新车的好几处地方打上车辆识别号码，以便于追查被偷车辆。

  • In a department store when a gift voucher is exchanged it's stamped with the details of the store to cancel it. [VERB noun prep.]

    在百货公司里,凡是已经兑换的礼券都会在上面盖上将其注销的商店的详细信息。

  • 'Eat before JULY 14' was stamped on the label. [be VERB-ed prep.]

    标签上盖有“7 月 14 日前食用”的戳记。

SYN print, mark, fix, impress  
5 VERB (因生气、双脚发冷等)跺(脚) If you stamp or stamp your foot, you lift your foot and put it down very hard on the ground, for example because you are angry or because your feet are cold. (因生气、双脚发冷等)跺(脚) [Also VERB n]

  • Often he teased me till my temper went and I stamped and screamed, feeling furiously helpless. [VERB]

    他经常和我开玩笑，直到我生气地跺脚尖叫，却又无可奈何。

  • His foot stamped down on the accelerator. [VERB adv./prep.]

    他一脚踩下油门。

  • She stamped her feet on the pavement to keep out the cold. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    她在人行道上跺着双脚取暖。

SYN stomp [informal] , stump, clump, tramp  
Stamp is also a noun.

  • ...hearing the creak of a door and the stamp of cold feet.

    听到门的嘎吱声和跺脚驱寒的声音

6 VERB (因生气)跺着脚走，噔噔地走 If you stamp somewhere, you walk there putting your feet down very hard on the ground because you are angry. (因生气)跺着脚走，噔噔地走

  • 'I'm going before things get any worse!' he shouted as he stamped out of the bedroom. [VERB prep./adv.]

    “我要走了，省得看见事情变得更糟！”他大叫着，噔噔地走出了卧室。

  • Overweight and sweating in the humid weather, she stamped from room to room. [VERB prep./adv.]

    她很胖，在潮湿的天气里浑身大汗，烦躁地在各个房间之间噔噔地走来走去。

7 VERB 用力踩;使劲踏 If you stamp on something, you put your foot down on it very hard. 用力踩;使劲踏

  • He received the original ban last week after stamping on the referee's foot during the supercup final. [VERB + on]

    他在超级杯决赛中踩踏裁判的脚后，于上周收到了书面的禁赛通知。

SYN trample, step, tread, crush  
8 N-SING 特征;痕迹;烙印 If something bears the stamp of a particular quality or person, it clearly has that quality or was done by that person. 特征;痕迹;烙印

  • ...lawns and flowerbeds that bore the stamp of years of confident care.

    显然多年来一直受到精心照料的草地和花床

  • Most of us want to make our home a familiar place and put the stamp of our personality on its walls. [+ of]

    我们大多数人都想把自己的家装饰成一个温馨的地方，并在墙上展示自己的个性。

SYN mark, indication, hallmark, badge  
9 VERB 表明;显示出 A quality, feature, or action that stamps someone or something as a particular thing shows clearly that they are this thing. 表明;显示出

  • I talked to social workers and the police — that had stamped me as a bad woman. [VERB noun as noun]

    我和社会福利工作者及警方都谈过话—因此我便成了坏女人。

  • Chris Boardman stamped himself as the 4,000m favourite by setting the world's fastest outdoor time in Barcelona last night. [V pron-refl as n]

    克里斯·博德曼昨晚在巴塞罗那创下室外世界纪录，证明了自己是 4,000 米赛最有实力的竞争者。

SYN identify, mark, brand, label
