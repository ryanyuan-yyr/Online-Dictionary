indemnify
ɪndemnɪfaɪ ɪnˈdɛmnɪˌfaɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense indemnifies, present participle indemnifying, past tense , past participle indemnified
1 VERB 保证赔偿 To indemnify someone against something bad happening means to promise to protect them, especially financially, if it happens. 保证赔偿 [formal]

  • They agreed to indemnify the taxpayers against any loss. [VERB noun + against]

    他们同意补偿纳税人的任何损失。

  • The printers were indemnified against legal action. [V n against n]

    印刷商获得保证，一旦受到起诉将会得到赔偿。

  • It doesn't have the money to indemnify everybody. [VERB noun]

    它没有钱去补偿所有人。

SYN insure, protect, guarantee, secure
