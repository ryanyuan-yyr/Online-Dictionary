string
strɪŋ strɪŋ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense strings, present participle stringing, past tense , past participle strung
1 N-VAR 细绳;线 String is thin rope made of twisted threads, used for tying things together or tying up parcels. 细绳;线

  • He held out a small bag tied with string.

    他拿出一个系着细绳的小袋子。

  • ...a shiny metallic coin on a string.

    拴在绳子上的闪闪发亮的金属硬币

SYN cord, yarn, twine, wire  
2 N-COUNT 一串 A string of things is a number of them on a piece of string, thread, or wire. 一串

  • She wore a string of pearls around her neck. [+ of]

    她脖子上戴着一串珍珠。

  • ...a string of fairy lights. [+ of]

    一串圣诞树小彩灯

SYN necklace, strand, rope, rosary  
3 N-COUNT [usually singular] 一行;一列;一排 A string of places or objects is a number of them that form a line. 一行;一列;一排

  • The landscape is broken only by a string of villages. [+ of]

    这片风景中只有一排村庄。

  • A string of five rowing boats set out from the opposite bank. [+ of]

    5 只划艇排成一行从对岸出发了。

SYN series, line, row, file  
4 N-COUNT [usually singular] 一系列，一连串(类似事件) A string of similar events is a series of them that happen one after the other. 一系列，一连串(类似事件)

  • The incident was the latest in a string of attacks. [+ of]

    这是一连串袭击事件中最近的一起。

  • Between 1940 and 1943 he had a string of 62 consecutive victories.

    他在 1940 年至 1943 年间连续 62 次获胜。

SYN sequence, run, series, chain  
5 N-COUNT (乐器的)弦 The strings on a musical instrument such as a violin or guitar are the thin pieces of wire or nylon stretched across it that make sounds when the instrument is played. (乐器的)弦

  • He went off to change a guitar string.

    他出去换一根吉他弦。

  • ...a twenty-one-string harp.

    21 弦的竖琴

6 N-PLURAL [oft NOUN n] (管弦乐团的)弦乐器，弦乐器组 The strings are the section of an orchestra which consists of stringed instruments played with a bow. (管弦乐团的)弦乐器，弦乐器组

  • The strings provided a melodic background to the passages played by the soloist.

    弦乐组为独奏乐章配上了动听的背景乐。

  • There was a 20-member string section.

    有一个 20 人组成的弦乐器组。

7 N-COUNT (计算机技术中的)字符串，信息串 In computing, a string is a particular series of letters, numbers, symbols, or spaces, for example a word or phrase that you want to search for in a document. (计算机技术中的)字符串，信息串
8 VERB 悬挂;系;扎 If you string something somewhere, you hang it up between two or more objects. 悬挂;系;扎 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • He had strung a banner across the wall. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他在墙上挂了条横幅。

SYN hang, stretch, suspend, sling  
String up means the same as string .string up 同 string

  • People were stringing up decorations on the fronts of their homes.

    人们正在各家门口挂起装饰物。

9 See also: highly strung  purse strings  second string  strung out 
10 PHRASE [Ns inflect, PHRASE after verb] 另有一手;两手准备 If someone has more than one string to their bow, they have more than one ability or thing they can use if the first one they try is not successful. 另有一手;两手准备

  • I'm never out of work because I have so many strings to my bow.

    我会很多手艺，永远都不会没饭吃。

11 PHRASE 不带附加条件;无任何限制 If something is offered to you with no strings attached or with no strings, it is offered without any special conditions. 不带附加条件;无任何限制

  • Aid should be given to developing countries with no strings attached.

    应该不带任何附加条件地向发展中国家提供援助。

  • ...no-strings grants that last for five years.

    为期5 年的无附加条件的援助赠款

12 PHRASE 拉关系;走后门 If you pull strings, you use your influence with other people in order to get something done, often unfairly. 拉关系;走后门

  • Tony is sure he can pull a few strings and get you in.

    托尼有把握可以托关系让你进去。
