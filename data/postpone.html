postpone
poʊspoʊn pəˈspəʊn poʊstˈpoʊn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense postpones, present participle postponing, past tense , past participle postponed
1 VERB 推迟；使延期；延缓 If you postpone an event, you delay it or arrange for it to take place at a later time than was originally planned. →see usage note at: cancel 推迟；使延期；延缓

  • He decided to postpone the expedition until the following day.

    他决定把探险推迟到第二天。

  • The visit has now been postponed indefinitely.

    参观现在已经被无限期推迟了。
