slog
slɒg slɑg
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense slogs, present participle slogging, past tense , past participle slogged
1 VERB 拼命干;努力苦干 If you slog through something, you work hard and steadily through it. 拼命干;努力苦干 [informal]

  • They secure their degrees by slogging through an intensive 11-month course. [VERB prep.]

    他们苦学了 11 个月的强化课程，拿到了学位。

  • She has slogged her way through ballet classes since the age of six. [V + way through]

    从 6 岁开始，她就一直在努力地学习芭蕾。

  • While slogging at work, have you neglected your marriage? [VERB]

    埋头工作的时候，你是否忽视了自己的婚姻？

SYN work, labour, toil, slave  
Slog away means the same as slog .slog away 同 slog

  • Edward slogged away, always learning.

    爱德华非常努力，总是在学习。

2 N-SING 困难而费力的工作(或任务) If you describe a task as a slog, you mean that it is tiring and requires a lot of effort. 困难而费力的工作(或任务) [informal]

  • I eventually got financial backing, but it was a slog.

    我最终得到了经济支持，但这得来不易。

  • There is little to show for the two years of hard slog.

    两年的埋头苦干却没有获得什么值得炫耀的结果。

SYN work, labour, toil, industry  
3 VERB 长途跋涉;步履艰难地行进 If you slog somewhere, you make a long and tiring journey there. 长途跋涉;步履艰难地行进 [informal]

  • The men had to slog up a steep muddy incline. [VERB prep./adv.]

    人们只得在陡峭而泥泞的斜坡上艰难地行进。

  • Why should Melissa have to slog around the supermarket on her own? [VERB prep./adv.]

    为什么梅利莎会一个人在超市里面费劲地转来转去？

SYN trudge, tramp, plod, trek  
4 N-SING 漫长而艰苦的跋涉 A slog is a long tiring journey. 漫长而艰苦的跋涉 [informal]

  • ...a slog through heather and bracken to the top of a hill.

    穿过石南和欧洲蕨到达山顶的艰难跋涉

SYN trudge, tramp, trek, hike  
5 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 竞相角逐;决一雌雄 If two or more people slog it out, they work very hard to try to be the one who is successful or who has their ideas and wishes accepted. 竞相角逐;决一雌雄 [mainly British]

  • The leading contenders are still slogging it out.

    处于领先位置的竞争者仍在激烈地角逐。
