entourage
ɒntʊrɑːʒ ˈɒntʊˌrɑːʒ ɑ̃turaʒ ˌɑntʊˈrɑʒ ˈɑntʊˌrɑʒ
Word forms: plural entourages
N-COUNT [usually poss NOUN] 随从;随行人员 A famous or important person's entourage is the group of assistants, servants, or other people who travel with them. 随从;随行人员

  • Peter the Great shocked London by his wild behaviour and that of his entourage when he visited England in 1698.

    彼得大帝及其随从1698年踏足英格兰时的野蛮行为震惊了伦敦。

  • He was accompanied by an entourage of a dozen police officers.

    他有12名警官随行。

SYN retinue, company, following, staff
