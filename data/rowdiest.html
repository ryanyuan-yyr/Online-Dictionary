rowdy
raʊdi ˈraʊdi
Word forms: comparative rowdier, superlative rowdiest, plural rowdies
1 ADJ-GRADED 吵闹的;粗野的;惹是生非的 When people are rowdy, they are noisy, rough, and likely to cause trouble. 吵闹的;粗野的;惹是生非的

  • He has complained to the police about rowdy neighbours.

    他向警方投诉邻居们太吵闹。

  • There were rowdy scenes inside parliament during the debate.

    在争论的过程中，国会内部一片混乱。

SYN disorderly, rough, loud, noisy  
rowdiness

  • ...adolescent behaviour like vandalism and rowdiness.

    破坏公物和惹是生非之类的青少年行为

2 N-PLURAL 粗野的人;吵闹的人 If you describe people as rowdies, you mean that they are noisy, rough, and likely to cause trouble. 粗野的人;吵闹的人 [informal]

  • ...a bar in New York where the owner kept a baseball bat to deal with rowdies.

    老板备了一根棒球棒以对付闹事者的一家纽约酒吧

SYN hooligan, tough, rough [informal] , casual
