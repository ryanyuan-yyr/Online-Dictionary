stutter
stʌtəʳ ˈstʌtər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense stutters, present participle stuttering, past tense , past participle stuttered
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 口吃;结巴 If someone has a stutter, they find it difficult to say the first sound of a word, and so they often hesitate or repeat it two or three times. 口吃;结巴

  • He spoke with a pronounced stutter.

    他说话明显结巴。

SYN stammer, falter, speech impediment, speech defect  
2 VERB 口吃;说话结结巴巴 If someone stutters, they have difficulty speaking because they find it hard to say the first sound of a word. 口吃;说话结结巴巴

  • I was trembling so hard, I thought I would stutter when I spoke. [VERB]

    我抖得厉害，感觉说话舌头会打结。

SYN stammer, stumble, falter, hesitate  
stuttering

  • He had to stop talking because if he'd kept on, the stuttering would have started.

    他不得不停止讲话，因为如果他继续讲的话，他的口吃就会发作。

3 VERB 缓慢地颠簸前进;曲折缓慢地进行 If something stutters along, it progresses slowly and unevenly. 缓慢地颠簸前进;曲折缓慢地进行

  • The old truck stuttered along the winding road. [VERB prep./adv.]

    这辆旧卡车在蜿蜒的公路上缓慢地颠簸前行。

  • The political debate stutters on. [VERB prep./adv.]

    政治辩论一波三折地艰难进行着。
