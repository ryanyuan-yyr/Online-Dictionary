quit
kwɪt kwɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense quits, present participle quitting
The form quit is used in the present tense and is the past tense and past participle. quit 的过去式和过去分词与原形相同。
1 VERB 放弃，辞去(工作) If you quit your job, you choose to leave it. 放弃，辞去(工作) [informal]

  • He quit his job as an office boy in Athens. [VERB noun]

    他辞去了在雅典当办公室勤杂工的工作。

  • He figured he would quit before Johnson fired him. [VERB]

    他估计自己会在约翰逊炒他鱿鱼之前先辞职。

SYN resign (from), leave, retire (from), pull out (of)  
2 VERB 停止;退出;放弃 If you quit an activity or quit doing something, you stop doing it. 停止;退出;放弃 [mainly US]

  • A nicotine spray can help smokers quit the habit without putting on weight. [VERB noun]

    尼古丁喷雾剂能够帮助吸烟者在不增加体重的同时戒烟。

  • I was trying to quit smoking at the time. [VERB verb-ing]

    当时我正在努力戒烟。

SYN stop, give up, cease, end  
3 VERB 离开(某地) If you quit a place, you leave it completely and do not go back to it. 离开(某地)

  • ...the idea that humans might one day quit the earth to colonise other planets. [VERB noun]

    人类有朝一日可能会离开地球到其他星球定居繁衍的想法

  • Police were called when he refused to quit the building. [VERB noun]

    他拒绝离开那座大楼，于是人们叫来了警察。

SYN leave, depart from, go out of, abandon  
4 PHRASE 决定结束;就此了结 If you say that you are going to call it quits, you mean that you have decided to stop doing something or being involved in something. 决定结束;就此了结

  • They raised $630,000 through listener donations, and then called it quits.

    他们通过听众捐款的方式筹集了63万美元后宣告结束。

  • You can decide whether there is hope in working for mutual happiness, or if you should call it quits.

    你可以决定是否有希望为双方共同的幸福努力，或者就此一刀两断。
