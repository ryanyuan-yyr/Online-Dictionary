excite
ɪksaɪt ɪkˈsaɪt ɛkˈsaɪt ɪkˈsaɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense excites, present participle exciting, past tense , past participle excited
1 VERB 使激动;使兴奋;使有热情 If something excites you, it makes you feel very happy, eager, or enthusiastic. 使激动;使兴奋;使有热情

  • I only take on work that excites me, even if it means turning down lots of money. [VERB noun]

    我只做那些令我感兴趣的工作，即使这意味着要少赚很多钱。

  • We'd not been excited by anything for about three years. [VERB noun]

    已经有大约3年的时间没有任何事情令我们激动。

  • Where the show really excites is in the display of avant-garde photography. [VERB]

    那场展览真正令人激动的就是先锋派摄影的展示。

SYN thrill, inspire, stir, stimulate  
2 VERB 激起;引起;唤起 If something excites a particular feeling, emotion, or reaction in someone, it causes them to experience it. 激起;引起;唤起

  • By all accounts, the monarchy does not excite strong feelings among the majority of Romanians. [VERB noun]

    据各方面记述，君主制没有在大多数罗马人中激发起强烈的感情。

  • Daniel's early exposure to motor racing did not excite his interest. [VERB noun]

    丹尼尔早期与摩托车赛的接触并没有激发他的兴趣。

  • Reports of the plot of this unusual film tend to excite revulsion. [VERB noun]

    有关这部不同寻常电影的情节的报道常常令人生厌。

3 VERB 激起性欲；使产生性欲 If something or someone excites you, they cause you to feel sexual desire. 激起性欲；使产生性欲 [Also VERB n]

  • Don't try exciting your partner with dirty magazines. [VERB noun with noun]

    不要尝试用黄色杂志激起伴侣的性欲。

excited

  • She makes you feel warm and comfortable, and maybe a little excited.

    她让人感觉温暖舒适，还可能有一点性欲高涨。

exciting

  • ...a sexually exciting thought.

    激起性欲的想法

4 VERB 刺激；使…活动 To excite a physical object such as an atomic particle or an organ in your body means to increase the amount of energy, movement, or activity in it. 刺激；使…活动 [technical]

  • The amount of nicotine in these nicotine substitutes can be enough to excite the heart. [VERB noun]

    这些尼古丁替代品中的烟碱含量足以刺激心脏。

excited
