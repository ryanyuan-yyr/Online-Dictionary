landscape
lændskeɪp ˈlændˌskeɪp ˈlændˌskeɪp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense landscapes, present participle landscaping, past tense , past participle landscaped
1 N-VAR （陆上）风景，自然景色 The landscape is everything you can see when you look across an area of land, including hills, rivers, buildings, trees, and plants. →see usage note at: countryside （陆上）风景，自然景色

  • ...Arizona's desert landscape.

    亚利桑那的沙漠风光

  • We moved to Northamptonshire and a new landscape of hedges and fields.

    我们迁往北安普敦郡，置身于一派树篱和田野的新景致中。

2 N-COUNT 形势；情形；情状 A landscape is all the features that are important in a particular situation. 形势；情形；情状

  • ...a change in the political landscape.

    政治形势的改变

  • ...a landscape of unparalleled ignorance.

    愚不可及的情况

3 N-COUNT 山水风景画；乡村风景画 A landscape is a painting which shows a scene in the countryside. 山水风景画；乡村风景画
4 VERB 对…作景观美化；给…作园林美化 If an area of land is landscaped, it is changed to make it more attractive, for example by adding streams or ponds and planting trees and bushes. 对…作景观美化；给…作园林美化 [Also VERB n]

  • The gravel pits have been landscaped and planted to make them attractive to wildfowl. [be VERB-ed]

    砾石采掘坑已进行了景观美化并种上了草木，希望能吸引野禽的到来。

  • They had landscaped their property with trees, shrubs, and lawns. [VERB noun + with]

    他们用树木、灌木丛和草坪将房产装点了一番。

  • ...a smart suburb of landscaped gardens and wide streets. [VERB-ed]

    园景怡人、街道宽阔的时尚市郊住宅区

    土地所有人坚持以高标准进行美化。
