grate
greɪt greɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense grates, present participle grating, past tense , past participle grated
1 N-COUNT 炉栅；炉格；炉排；炉箅 A grate is a framework of metal bars in a fireplace, which holds the coal or wood. 炉栅；炉格；炉排；炉箅

  • A wood fire burned in the grate.

    木柴在炉栅中燃烧着。

2 VERB 磨碎,擦碎（食物） If you grate food such as cheese or carrots, you rub it over a metal tool called a grater so that the food is cut into very small pieces. 磨碎,擦碎（食物）

  • Grate the cheese into a mixing bowl. [VERB noun]

    将干酪磨碎放进搅拌碗中。

  • ...grated carrot. [VERB-ed]

    磨碎的胡萝卜

SYN shred, mince, pulverize, triturate  
3 VERB 发出吱吱嘎嘎的摩擦声；发出刺耳的摩擦声 When something grates, it rubs against something else making a harsh, unpleasant sound. 发出吱吱嘎嘎的摩擦声；发出刺耳的摩擦声

  • His chair grated as he got to his feet. [VERB]

    他起身时椅子发出吱吱嘎嘎的声音。

  • The gun barrel grated against the floor. [V + against/on]

    枪管拖在地上发出刺耳的声音。

SYN scrape, grind, rub, scratch  
4 VERB 使烦躁；使气恼；使难受 If something such as someone's behaviour grates on you or grates, it makes you feel annoyed. 使烦躁；使气恼；使难受

  • His manner always grated on me. [VERB + on]

    他的态度总是让我感到气恼。

  • What truly grates is the painful banter. [VERB]

    最让人气愤的是那个令人难堪的玩笑。
