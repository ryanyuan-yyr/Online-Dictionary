patrician
pətrɪʃən pəˈtrɪʃən
Word forms: plural patricians
1 N-COUNT 贵族；豪门子弟 A patrician is a person who comes from a family of high social rank. 贵族；豪门子弟 [formal]

  • ...the patrician banker Sir Charles Villiers.

    出身名门的银行家查尔斯·维利尔斯爵士

SYN aristocrat, peer, noble, nobleman  
2 ADJ-GRADED 贵族的；有贵族气派的 If you describe someone as patrician, you mean that they behave in a sophisticated way, and look as though they are from a high social rank. 贵族的；有贵族气派的

  • He was a lean, patrician gent in his early sixties.

    他60岁出头，是位有着贵族气派的清瘦绅士。

  • ...her crisp, patrician voice.

    她清脆的贵族口音

SYN aristocratic, noble, lordly, gentle [archaic]
