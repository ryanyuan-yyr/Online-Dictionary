contrast
kɒntrɑːst kəntrɑːst -træst -træst kənˈtræst ˈkɑnˌtræst
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense contrasts, present participle contrasting, past tense , past participle contrasted
The noun is pronounced /'kɒntrɑːst, -træst/. The verb is pronounced /kən'trɑːst, -'træst/. 名词读作 /'kɒntrɑːst, -træst/，动词读作 /kən'trɑːst, -'træst/。
1 N-VAR 差异;差别;悬殊 A contrast is a great difference between two or more things which is clear when you compare them. 差异;差别;悬殊

  • ...the contrast between town and country. [+ between]

    城乡差别

  • The two visitors provided a startling contrast in appearance.

    这两位访客的外表截然不同。

  • Silk was used with wool for contrast.

    丝绸和毛料用在一起以形成对比。

SYN difference, opposition, comparison, distinction  
2 PHRASE 相比之下;与…相反 You say by contrast or in contrast, or in contrast to something, to show that you are mentioning a very different situation from the one you have just mentioned. 相比之下;与…相反

  • The private sector, by contrast, has plenty of money to spend.

    相比之下，私营部门就有很多钱可供开销。

  • In contrast, the lives of girls in well-to-do families were often very sheltered.

    相反，生活在富裕家庭的女孩子通常都备受呵护。

  • In contrast to similar services in France and Germany, Intercity rolling stock is very rarely idle.

    与法国和德国的类似铁路服务系统不同，“城际列车”很少闲置。

3 PHRASE 与…截然不同;与…对比鲜明 If one thing is in contrast to another, it is very different from it. 与…截然不同;与…对比鲜明 [Also VERB]

  • His public statements have always been in marked contrast to those of his son.

    他和他儿子在公开场合的言论总是截然不同。

  • That is in stark contrast to the situation during the 1970 oil crisis.

    那和1970年石油危机时的情形完全不同。

SYN differ, be contrary, be distinct, be at variance  
4 N-COUNT 截然不同的事物;差异明显的事物 If one thing is a contrast to another, it is very different from it. 截然不同的事物;差异明显的事物 [Also VERB]

  • The boy's room is a complete contrast to the guest room. [+ to]

    这个男孩的房间和客房截然不同。

  • ...a country of great contrasts.

    一个截然不同的国家

SYN differ, be contrary, be distinct, be at variance  
5 VERB 对比;对照 If you contrast one thing with another, you point out or consider the differences between those things. 对比;对照

  • She contrasted the situation then with the present crisis. [VERB noun + with]

    她将当时的情况和目前的危机进行对比。

  • Contrast that approach with what goes on in most organizations. [VERB noun with noun]

    将那个方法和大多数机构现行的做法进行对比。

  • In this section we contrast four possible broad approaches. [VERB noun]

    在这个部分，我们将对4种可行的广义方法进行对比。

SYN differentiate, compare, oppose, distinguish  
6 V-RECIP 截然不同;有明显差异;对比鲜明 If one thing contrasts with another, it is very different from it. 截然不同;有明显差异;对比鲜明 [Also VERB]

  • Johnson's easy charm contrasted sharply with the prickliness of his boss. [VERB + with]

    约翰逊的亲和力和他老板易怒的性格形成了鲜明的对比。

  • Paint the wall in a contrasting colour. [VERB-ing]

    把这面墙刷成对比鲜明的颜色。

SYN differ, be contrary, be distinct, be at variance  
7 N-UNCOUNT (照片、电视图像、画作等的)颜色反差，明暗对比 Contrast is the degree of difference between the darker and lighter parts of a photograph, television picture, or painting. (照片、电视图像、画作等的)颜色反差，明暗对比

  • ...a television with brighter colours, better contrast, and digital sound.

    颜色更为鲜艳、图像对比度更高、具备数字音响效果的电视
