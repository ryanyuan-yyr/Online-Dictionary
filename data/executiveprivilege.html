executive privilege
ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ
N-UNCOUNT 行政特权Executive privilege is the right that a member of the executive branch of government has to withhold information about matters that they consider to be confidential. 行政特权 [US]

  • So far, no documents are being withheld on grounds of executive privilege.

    迄今为止，没有任何文件是因行政特权的理由而不公开的。
