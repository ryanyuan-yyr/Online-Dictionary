babble
bæbəl ˈbæbəl ˈbæbəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense babbles, present participle babbling, past tense , past participle babbled
1 VERB 含糊不清地说；兴奋地说 If someone babbles, they talk in a confused or excited way. 含糊不清地说；兴奋地说

  • Momma babbled on and on about how he was ruining me. [V on/away]

    妈妈喋喋不休地念叨着他正如何毁掉我。

  • They all babbled simultaneously. [VERB]

    他们同时叽里咕噜地说了起来。

  • 'Er, hello, viewers,' he babbled. [VERB with quote]

    “呃，观众们，你们好，”他含混不清地说。

SYN gabble, chatter, gush, spout  
2 N-SING 嘈杂声；含混不清的说话声 You can refer to people's voices as a babble of sound when they are excited and confused, preventing you from understanding what they are saying. 嘈杂声；含混不清的说话声

  • Kemp knocked loudly so as to be heard above the high babble of voices. [+ of]

    肯普大声敲着，好让人们在一片高声喧嚷中听到他的声音。

  • They began to curse and shout in a babble of languages.

    他们开始用不同的语言叽里咕噜地谩骂吵嚷起来。
