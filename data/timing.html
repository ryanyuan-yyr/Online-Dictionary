timing
taɪmɪŋ ˈtaɪmɪŋ
1 N-UNCOUNT 时机掌握;时间选择 Timing is the skill or action of judging the right moment in a situation or activity at which to do something. 时机掌握;时间选择

  • His photo is a wonderful happy moment caught with perfect timing.

    他拍的照片时机掌握完美，记录下了动人的快乐瞬间。

  • If your timing is right, you may be fortunate enough to stumble across a village fiesta.

    如果你时机把握得好，你会幸运地撞上某个乡村节日。

2 N-UNCOUNT 时间安排 Timing is used to refer to the time at which something happens or is planned to happen, or to the length of time that something takes. 时间安排

  • The timing of the minister's visit, however, could somewhat detract from the goodwill it's supposed to generate. [+ of]

    但是，部长访问的时间安排不当，可能会使访问的善意不能得到有效传达。

3 See also: time
