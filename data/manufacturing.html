manufacture
mænjʊfæktʃəʳ ˌmænjuˈfæktʃər ˌmænjəˈfæktʃər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense manufactures, present participle manufacturing, past tense , past participle manufactured
1 VERB （通常指大量）制造，生产 To manufacture something means to make it in a factory, usually in large quantities. （通常指大量）制造，生产 [business]

  • They manufacture the class of plastics known as thermoplastic materials. [VERB noun]

    他们生产这类被称为热塑材料的塑料制品。

  • The first three models are being manufactured at the factory in Ashton-under-Lyne. [VERB noun]

    最初的3种型号在阿什顿安德莱恩的工厂生产。

  • We import foreign manufactured goods. [VERB-ed]

    我们进口国外制成品。

SYN make, build, produce, construct  
Manufacture is also a noun.

  • ...the manufacture of nuclear weapons.

    核武器的制造

  • ...celebrating 90 years of car manufacture.

    庆祝汽车制造90周年

manufacturing

  • ...management headquarters for manufacturing.

    生产管理总部

  • ...the manufacturing of a luxury type automobile.

    豪华汽车的生产

2 N-COUNT [usually plural] 产品；制造品;工业品 Manufactures are goods or products which have been made in a factory. 产品；制造品;工业品 [business]

  • ...a long-term rise in the share of manufactures in nonoil exports.

    非石油出口产品中工业品份额的长期增长

SYN making, production, construction, assembly  
3 VERB 虚构;捏造 If you say that someone manufactures information, you are criticizing them because they invent information that is not true. 虚构;捏造 [disapproval]

  • According to the prosecution, the officers manufactured an elaborate story. [VERB noun]

    根据控方意见，官员们精心编造了事情经过。

  • He said the allegations were manufactured on the flimsiest evidence. [VERB noun]

    他说这些指控都是凭空捏造的。

SYN concoct, make up, invent, devise
