call up
1 PHRASAL VERB (给…)打电话 If you call someone up, you telephone them. (给…)打电话 [mainly US]

  • When I'm in Pittsburgh, I call him up. [VERB noun PREP.]

    我每到匹兹堡,都给他打电话。

  • He called up the museum. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他给博物馆打了电话。

  • Sometimes I'd even call up at 4 a.m. [VERB PREP.]

    有时我甚至早上四点都打电话。

2 PHRASAL VERB (陆、海、空军)征召…入伍 If someone is called up, they are ordered to join the army, navy, or air force. (陆、海、空军)征召…入伍 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Youngsters coming up to university were being called up. [be VERB-ed PREP.]

    快上大学的青年人受到征召。

  • The United States has called up some 150,000 military reservists. [VERB PREP. noun]

    美国已经征召了约15万名预备役军人。

SYN enlist, draft, recruit, muster  
3 PHRASAL VERB 选中,挑选(…参加运动队) If someone is called up, they are chosen to play in a sports team. 选中,挑选(…参加运动队)

  • He is likely to be called up for Thursday's match against Italy. [be VERB-ed PREP.]

    他有可能被选中参加星期四对意大利的比赛。

ˈkɔːlʌp ˈkɔlˌʌp
Word forms: plural call-ups
1 ADJ [ADJ n] (军队)征召的，征集的 If a person gets their call-up papers, they receive an official order to join the armed forces. (军队)征召的，征集的
2 N-COUNT (服兵役的)征召令 A call-up is an occasion on which people are ordered to report for service in the armed forces. (服兵役的)征召令

  • The call-up of National Guard and reserve units begun in late August. [+ of]

    国民警卫队和预备役的征兵工作8月下旬开始。

3 N-COUNT (对运动员的)征集令;征召 If someone receives a call-up to a sports team, such as the national football team, they are chosen to play for that team. (对运动员的)征集令;征召 [British, journalism]
