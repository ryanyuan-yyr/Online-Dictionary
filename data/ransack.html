ransack
rænsæk ˈrænˌsæk
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense ransacks, present participle ransacking, past tense , past participle ransacked
VERB 搜寻；搜遍；洗劫 If people ransack a building, they damage things in it or make it very untidy, often because they are looking for something in a quick and careless way. 搜寻；搜遍；洗劫

  • Demonstrators ransacked and burned the house where he was staying. [VERB noun]

    示威者搜遍了他的住处，并放火烧了那里。

  • He returned from hospital to find thieves had ransacked his home. [VERB noun]

    他从医院回来，发现家里被小偷洗劫一空。

  • ...the wrecked schools and churches, the ransacked embassies and homes. [VERB-ed]

    被毁坏的校舍和教堂，被洗劫的大使馆和家园

SYN plunder, raid, loot, pillage
