fluster
flʌstəʳ ˈflʌstər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense flusters, present participle flustering, past tense , past participle flustered
VERB 使紧张;使慌张 If you fluster someone, you make them feel nervous and confused by rushing them and preventing them from concentrating on what they are doing. 使紧张;使慌张

  • The General refused to be flustered. [be VERB-ed]

    将军没有乱了方寸。

  • She was a very calm person. Nothing could fluster her. [VERB noun]

    她是个非常沉着的人。没什么能让她惊慌的。

SYN upset, bother, disturb, ruffle  
flustered

  • She was so flustered that she forgot her reply.

    她太紧张了，都忘记了该如何作答。
