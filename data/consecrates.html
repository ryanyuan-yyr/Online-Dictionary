consecrate
kɒnsɪkreɪt ˈkɑnsɪˌkreɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense consecrates, present participle consecrating, past tense , past participle consecrated
VERB 为…祝圣;使圣化 When a building, place, or object is consecrated, it is officially declared to be holy. When a person is consecrated, they are officially declared to be a bishop. 为…祝圣;使圣化

  • The church was consecrated in 1234. [be VERB-ed]

    这个教堂于1234年祝的圣。

  • He defied Pope John Paul II by consecrating four bishops without his approval. [VERB noun]

    他违抗教皇约翰·保罗二世的旨意，未经他的批准就给4位主教举行了祝圣仪式。

SYN sanctify, dedicate, ordain, exalt
