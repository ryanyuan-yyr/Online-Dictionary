itch
ɪtʃ ɪtʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense itches, present participle itching, past tense , past participle itched
1 VERB 发痒 When a part of your body itches, you have an unpleasant feeling on your skin that makes you want to scratch. 发痒

  • When someone has hayfever, the eyes and nose will stream and itch. [VERB]

    花粉热临床表现为流泪、流涕，眼睛、鼻子发痒。

  • My skin was hard and scaly and I itched. [VERB]

    我的皮肤又硬又粗糙,而且还很痒。

  • ...dry, itching skin. [VERB-ing]

    干燥发痒的皮肤

SYN prickle, tickle, tingle, crawl  
Itch is also a noun.

  • Scratch my back — I've got an itch.

    帮我挠挠背—有点痒。

itching

  • It may be that the itching is caused by contact with irritant material.

    瘙痒可能是由于接触了刺激性的物质而引起的。

2 VERB [usually cont] 渴望;迫切想要 If you are itching to do something, you are very eager or impatient to do it. 渴望;迫切想要 [informal]

  • I was itching to get involved and to bring my own theories into practice. [VERB to-infinitive]

    我渴望参与其中，把我的理论付诸实践。

  • The general was itching for a fight. [VERB + for]

    将军盼着打一仗。

SYN long, ache, crave, burn  
Itch is also a noun.

  • ...viewers with an insatiable itch to switch channels.

    总想调换频道的电视观众
