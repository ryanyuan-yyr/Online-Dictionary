sub
sʌb sʌb
Word forms: plural subs
1 N-COUNT 替补队员 In team games such as football, a sub is a player who is brought into a match to replace another player. 替补队员 [informal]

  • We had a few injuries and had to use youth team kids as subs.

    我们有几名队员有伤，只好用青年队的小队员做替补。

2 N-COUNT 同 submarine A sub is the same as a submarine . 同 submarine [informal]
3 N-PLURAL (向俱乐部、协会缴纳的)会员费 A fixed amount of money that you pay regularly in order to be a member of a club or society is called your subs . (向俱乐部、协会缴纳的)会员费 [British, old-fashioned]

  • Subs will be raised as from next year.

    从明年开始将提高会费。

sub- sʌb- sʌb səb
1 PREFIX (用于词头)表示“在…之下”，“在…下面” Sub- is used at the beginning of words that have 'under' as part of their meaning. (用于词头)表示“在…之下”，“在…下面”

  • The waters were rising about the rock and would soon submerge it.

    水快涨到跟岩石一样高了，很快就会淹没它。

  • ...a nuclear-powered submarine.

    核潜艇

2 PREFIX (用于名词前构成新名词)表示“次”，“分支”，“部分” Sub- is added to the beginning of nouns in order to form other nouns that refer to things that are part of a larger thing. (用于名词前构成新名词)表示“次”，“分支”，“部分”

  • ...a subcommittee on family values and individual rights.

    一个关注家庭价值观和个人权益的小组委员会

  • ...the subdivision of farms into smallholdings.

    对农田的细分

3 PREFIX (用于形容词前构成新形容词)表示“低于”，“亚于”，“次于” Sub- is added to the beginning of adjectives in order to form other adjectives that describe someone or something as inferior, for example inferior to normal people or to normal things. (用于形容词前构成新形容词)表示“低于”，“亚于”，“次于”

  • The cold has made already substandard living conditions even worse.

    寒冷使得已经较差的生活环境变得更糟糕。

  • ...educationally subnormal children.

    学习能力低下的儿童
