vein
veɪn veɪn
Word forms: plural veins
1 N-COUNT 静脉 Your veins are the thin tubes in your body through which your blood flows towards your heart. 静脉

  • Many veins are found just under the skin.

    许多静脉血管就在皮肤下面。

2See also:varicose vein 
3 N-COUNT [usually singular] [usually adjective NOUN] （行文的）风格;（说话的）语气，情绪 Something that is written or spoken in a particular vein is written or spoken in that style or mood. （行文的）风格;（说话的）语气，情绪

  • It is one of his finest works in a lighter vein.

    这是他行文风格轻松活泼的作品中最出色的作品之一。

  • The girl now replies in similar vein.

    这个女孩现在以相似的口吻回答。

4 N-COUNT [usually singular] 几分，若干（指在某人的行为或工作中常表现出的某种品质） A vein of a particular quality is evidence of that quality which someone often shows in their behaviour or work. 几分，若干（指在某人的行为或工作中常表现出的某种品质）

  • The striker's rich vein of form this season has seen him net 32 goals.

    这位前锋本赛季状态奇佳，进球已达到32个。

  • This Spanish drama has a vein of black humour running through it. [+ of]

    这出西班牙戏剧自始至终都带有几分黑色幽默。

SYN streak, element, thread, suggestion  
5 N-COUNT 矿脉;矿层;岩脉 A vein of a particular metal or mineral is a layer of it lying in rock. 矿脉;矿层;岩脉

  • ...a vein of copper.

    铜矿层

  • ...a rich and deep vein of limestone. [+ of]

    储量丰富的深层石灰石矿脉

6 N-COUNT （叶子的）纹路，纹理;叶脉 The veins on a leaf are the thin lines on it. （叶子的）纹路，纹理;叶脉

  • ...the serrated edges and veins of the feathery leaves. [+ of]

    羽状叶子的锯齿边沿和纹理
