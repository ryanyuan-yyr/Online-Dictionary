fiery
faɪəri ˈfaɪəri ˈfaɪri
Word forms: superlative fieriest
1 ADJ [usually ADJ n] 熊熊燃烧的;含火的 If you describe something as fiery, you mean that it is burning strongly or contains fire. 熊熊燃烧的;含火的 [literary]

  • A helicopter crashed in a fiery explosion in Vallejo.

    一架直升机在瓦列霍爆炸起火并坠毁。

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (颜色)火一般的，如火的 You can use fiery for emphasis when you are referring to bright colours such as red or orange. (颜色)火一般的，如火的 [literary, emphasis]

  • A large terracotta pot planted with Busy Lizzie provides a fiery bright red display.

    一个栽种了凤仙花的大赤陶罐里开出了明艳如火的花朵。

3 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (食物或饮料)辛辣的，刺激的 If you describe food or drink as fiery, you mean that it has a very strong hot or spicy taste. (食物或饮料)辛辣的，刺激的 [written]

  • A fiery combination of chicken, chillies and rice.

    把鸡肉、辣椒和米饭和在一起、口味辛辣的搭配

SYN spicy, hot, pungent, piquant  
4 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (感情、尤指愤怒)激烈的，暴烈的 If you describe someone as fiery, you mean that they express very strong emotions, especially anger, in their behaviour or speech. (感情、尤指愤怒)激烈的，暴烈的 [written]

  • She was a fiery, brilliant and unyielding intellectual and politician.

    她是个充满激情、才华卓越、毫不妥协的知识分子和政治家。

  • She had a fiery temper and liked to get her own way.

    她脾气火爆，喜欢我行我素。
