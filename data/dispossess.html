dispossess
dɪspəzes ˌdɪspəˈzɛs ˌdɪspəˈzɛs
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dispossesses, present participle dispossessing, past tense , past participle dispossessed
VERB 剥夺，夺去(土地、房屋等) If you are dispossessed of something that you own, especially land or buildings, it is taken away from you. 剥夺，夺去(土地、房屋等) [Also V n + of/from]

  • ...people who were dispossessed of their land under apartheid. [be VERB-ed + of]

    在种族隔离政策下被剥夺了土地的人们

  • They settled the land, dispossessing many of its original inhabitants. [VERB noun]

    他们在土地上定居，赶走了许多原来的居民。

  • Droves of dispossessed people emigrated to Canada. [VERB-ed]

    一批批流离失所的人们移民到了加拿大。

SYN strip, deprive  
The dispossessed are people who are dispossessed.

  • ...the plight of the poor and the dispossessed.

    贫穷无依者的困苦处境
