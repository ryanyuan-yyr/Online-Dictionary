dissent
dɪsent dɪˈsɛnt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dissents, present participle dissenting, past tense , past participle dissented
1 N-UNCOUNT (尤指与多数人或权威人士的)意见不一致,意见分歧 Dissent is strong disagreement or dissatisfaction with a decision or opinion, especially one that is supported by most people or by people in authority. (尤指与多数人或权威人士的)意见不一致,意见分歧 [formal]

  • He is the toughest military ruler yet and has responded harshly to any dissent.

    他是迄今最强硬的军事统治者，对任何异议都一律进行打压。

  • Political dissent would no longer be tolerated.

    政见不和将不再被容忍。

  • I made a gesture of dissent.

    我做了个表示不同意的手势。

SYN disagreement, opposition, protest, resistance  
2 VERB 不同意，持异议(尤指与多数人或权威人士支持的观点、决定相左) If you dissent, you express disagreement with a decision or opinion, especially one that is supported by most people or by people in authority. 不同意，持异议(尤指与多数人或权威人士支持的观点、决定相左) [formal]

  • Just one of the 10 members dissented. [VERB]

    只有1/10的成员不同意。

  • No one dissents from the decision to unify. [VERB + from]

    没有人反对统一的决定。

  • There are likely to be many dissenting voices. [VERB-ing]

    可能会有很多不赞成的声音。

SYN disagree, object, disapprove, demur
