sanctify
sæŋktɪfaɪ ˈsæŋktəˌfaɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense sanctifies, present participle sanctifying, past tense , past participle sanctified
1 VERB [usually passive] (牧师、神职人员)认可，批准，使神圣化 If something is sanctified by a priest or other holy person, the priest or holy person officially approves of it, or declares it to be holy. (牧师、神职人员)认可，批准，使神圣化

  • She is trying to make amends for her marriage not being sanctified. [be VERB-ed]

    她正努力弥补自己那段未受牧师认可的婚姻。

2 VERB 批准;认可;使受尊敬;使固定不变 If an organization or event sanctifies something, it makes it approved of and respected, or unable to be changed. 批准;认可;使受尊敬;使固定不变

  • ...a law that sanctifies changes that have already occurred. [VERB noun]

    认可已发生变化的一项法律

SYN approve, back, support, sanction
