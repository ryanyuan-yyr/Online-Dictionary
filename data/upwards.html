upwards
ʌpwəʳdz
(also upward)
1 ADV [ADVERB after verb, n ADVERB] 向上 If someone moves or looks upwards, they move or look up towards a higher place. 向上

  • 'There,' said Jack, pointing upwards.

    “在那里，”杰克向上指着说。

  • They climbed upward along the steep cliffs surrounding the village.

    他们沿着村庄周围的陡峭悬崖往上爬。

  • Hunter nodded again and gazed upwards in fear.

    亨特又点了点头，满眼恐惧地朝上望去。

  • Lie face upwards with a cushion under your head.

    头枕垫子仰卧。

SYN up, skywards, heavenwards  
2 ADV [ADVERB after verb] 上升；上涨 If an amount or rate moves upwards, it increases. 上升；上涨

  • ...with prices soon heading upwards in high street stores.

    商业大街上商店里的价格迅速上涨

  • Unemployment will continue upward for much of this year.

    今年的大部分时间失业率仍将上升。

  • The share price is likely to leap upwards.

    该股票价格可能会猛涨。

3 PREP-PHRASE …以上；多于… A quantity that is upwards of a particular number is more than that number. …以上；多于…

  • ...projects worth upwards of 200 million pounds. [+ of]

    价值2亿多英镑的项目

  • It costs upward of $40,000 a year to keep some prisoners in prison. [+ of]

    关押某些犯人一年的成本超过4万美元。
