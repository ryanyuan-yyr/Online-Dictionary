straighten
streɪtən ˈstreɪtən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense straightens, present participle straightening, past tense , past participle straightened
1 VERB 把…整理好;使端正 If you straighten something, you make it tidy or put it in its proper position. 把…整理好;使端正

  • She sipped her coffee and straightened a picture on the wall. [VERB noun]

    她抿了口咖啡，然后把墙上的一幅画扶正。

  • ...tidying, straightening cushions and organising magazines. [VERB noun]

    收拾、摆放靠垫和整理杂志

2 VERB 挺直，端正(身体) If you are standing in a relaxed or slightly bent position and then you straighten, you make your back or body straight and upright. 挺直，端正(身体)

  • The three men straightened and stood waiting. [VERB]

    3 个男人挺直身体，站着等候。

Straighten up means the same as straighten .straighten up 同 straighten

  • He straightened up and slipped his hands in his pockets.

    他挺直身子，把手插进口袋里。

3 V-ERG (使)变直;(使)变正 If you straighten something, or it straightens, it becomes straight. (使)变直;(使)变正 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Straighten both legs until they are fully extended. [VERB noun]

    挺直双腿直至完全伸展开。

  • The road straightened and we were on a plateau. [VERB]

    路变直了，我们驶上了高原。

Straighten out means the same as straighten .straighten out 同 straighten

  • No one would dream of straightening out the knobbly spire at Empingham Church.

    谁都无法将埃姆平汉教堂疙瘩嶙峋的尖顶弄直。

  • The road twisted its way up the mountain then straightened out for the last two hundred yards.

    路弯弯曲曲地环山而上，在最后的 200 码变得平直。

Phrasal verbs:
straighten out straighten up
