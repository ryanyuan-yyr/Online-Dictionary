blatant
bleɪtənt ˈbleɪtənt
ADJ-GRADED 公然的；露骨的；明目张胆的 You use blatant to describe something bad that is done in an open or very obvious way. 公然的；露骨的；明目张胆的 [emphasis]

  • Outsiders will continue to suffer the most blatant discrimination.

    外来人员会继续受到最露骨的歧视。

  • ...a blatant attempt to spread the blame for the fiasco.

    公然企图推卸这次惨败的责任

  • The elitism was blatant.

    精英统治丝毫不加掩饰。

SYN obvious, open, clear, plain  
blatantly

  • ...a blatantly sexist question.

    赤裸裸的性别歧视问题

  • They said the song blatantly encouraged the killing of policemen.

    他们说这首歌明目张胆地煽动杀害警察。
