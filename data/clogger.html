clog
klɒg klɑg klɔg
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense clogs, present participle clogging, past tense , past participle clogged
1 VERB 阻塞;堵塞 When something clogs a hole or place, it blocks it so that nothing can pass through. 阻塞;堵塞

  • Dirt clogs the pores, causing spots. [VERB noun]

    灰尘堵塞毛孔，从而生出粉刺。

  • The traffic clogged the Thames bridges. [VERB noun]

    往来的车辆把泰晤士河上的桥都堵死了。

SYN obstruct, block, jam, hamper  
clogged

  • ...a clogged drain.

    被堵住的下水道

  • The streets were clogged with people.

    人们把街道围得水泄不通。

2 N-COUNT [usually plural] 厚木底皮鞋;木屐 Clogs are heavy leather or wooden shoes with thick wooden soles. 厚木底皮鞋;木屐
3 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 翘辫子;一命呜呼 If you say that someone has popped their clogs, you mean that they have died. 翘辫子;一命呜呼 [British, informal]
clogged up

  • The drains are badly clogged up.

    下水道严重堵塞。
