articulate
ɑːʳtɪkjʊlət ɑːʳtɪkjʊleɪt ɑrˈtɪkjulɪt ɑrˈtɪkjəlɪt ˈɑrˈtɪkjuˌleɪt ɑrˈtɪkjəˌleɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense articulates, present participle articulating, past tense , past participle articulated
The adjective is pronounced /ɑː'tɪkjʊlət/. The verb is pronounced /ɑː'tɪkjʊleɪt/. 形容词读作 /ɑː'tɪkjʊlət/，动词读作 /ɑː'tɪkjʊleɪt/。
1 ADJ-GRADED 善于表达的;口齿伶俐的 If you describe someone as articulate, you mean that they are able to express their thoughts and ideas easily and well. 善于表达的;口齿伶俐的 [approval]

  • She is an articulate young woman.

    她是一个口才很好的年轻女子。

  • The child was unable to offer an articulate description of what she had witnessed.

    孩子无法对她所目睹的事情作出清楚的描述。

SYN expressive, clear, effective, vocal  
articulacy

  • To start a revolution, you need discipline, incisiveness and articulacy.

    要革命，就要守纪律、看得远、善表达。

2 VERB 清楚地表达（思想或感情） When you articulate your ideas or feelings, you express them clearly in words. 清楚地表达（思想或感情） [formal] [Also VERB wh]

  • The president has been accused of failing to articulate an overall vision in foreign affairs. [VERB noun]

    总统因未能清晰表述对外交事务的整体构想而受到指责。

3 VERB 清楚地说出；吐（字）清楚 If you articulate something, you say it very clearly, so that each word or syllable can be heard. 清楚地说出；吐（字）清楚 [Also VERB]

  • He articulated each syllable carefully. [VERB noun]

    他小心而清晰地发出每个音节。

SYN pronounce, say, talk, speak
