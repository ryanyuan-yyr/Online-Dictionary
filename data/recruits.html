recruit
rɪkruːt rɪˈkrut
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense recruits, present participle recruiting, past tense , past participle recruited
1 VERB 招收；招募；征召 If you recruit people for an organization, you select them and persuade them to join it or work for it. 招收；招募；征召

  • The police are trying to recruit more black and Asian officers. [VERB noun]

    警方正在试图招募更多黑人和亚裔警官。

  • In recruiting students to Computer Systems Engineering, the University looks for evidence of all-round ability. [V n + to/for]

    在招收计算机系统工程专业的学生时，这个大学看重的是学生的综合能力。

  • He helped to recruit volunteers to go to Pakistan to fight. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    他协助招募志愿者到巴基斯坦打仗。

SYN gather, take on, obtain, engage  
recruiter

  • ...a Marine recruiter.

    海军陆战队征兵人员

recruiting

  • A bomb exploded at an army recruiting office.

    一颗炸弹在一个军队征兵办公室爆炸了。

2 N-COUNT 新手；新兵；新成员 A recruit is a person who has recently joined an organization or an army. 新手；新兵；新成员
SYN beginner, trainee, apprentice, novice
