enunciate
ɪnʌnsieɪt iˈnʌnsiˌeɪt ɪˈnʌnsiˌeɪt iˈnʌnʃieɪt ɪˈnʌnʃiˌeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense enunciates, present participle enunciating, past tense , past participle enunciated
1 VERB （清晰地）念（词）；（清晰地）发（音） When you enunciate a word or part of a word, you pronounce it clearly. （清晰地）念（词）；（清晰地）发（音） [formal]

  • His voice was harsh as he enunciated each word carefully. [VERB noun]

    他一字一顿，听上去很生硬。

  • She enunciates very slowly and carefully. [VERB]

    她缓慢、仔细而又清晰地读着。

enunciation

  • ... his grammar always precise, his enunciation always perfect.

    他的语法始终准确，他的发音永远完美

2 VERB 阐明；清楚而准确地阐释 When you enunciate a thought, idea, or plan, you express it very clearly and precisely. 阐明；清楚而准确地阐释 [formal]

  • He was ever ready to enunciate his views to all who would listen. [VERB noun]

    他总是乐意向所有愿意倾听的人阐明他的观点。

SYN state, declare, proclaim, pronounce  
enunciation

  • ...the enunciation of grand moral principles.

    对重要道德原则的明确阐述
