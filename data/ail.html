ail
eɪl eɪl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense ails, present participle ailing, past tense , past participle ailed
1 VERB 使苦恼;困扰;使麻烦 If something ails a group or area of activity, it is a problem or source of trouble for that group or for people involved in that activity. 使苦恼;困扰;使麻烦

  • A full-scale debate is under way on what ails the industry. [VERB noun]

    关于工业为何陷入困境正在展开一场全面的讨论。

SYN trouble, worry, bother, distress  
2 VERB 使患病;使不适 If something ails someone, they are ill. 使患病;使不适 [old-fashioned]

  • 'What ails you?' he asked. [VERB noun]

    “你哪里不舒服？”他问道。
