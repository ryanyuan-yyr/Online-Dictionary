disturb
dɪstɜːʳb dɪˈstɜːb dɪˈstɜrb
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense disturbs, present participle disturbing, past tense , past participle disturbed
1 VERB 打扰;干扰;妨碍 If you disturb someone, you interrupt what they are doing and upset them. 打扰;干扰;妨碍

  • I hope I'm not disturbing you. [VERB noun]

    我希望没有打扰到你。

  • Find a quiet, warm, comfortable room where you won't be disturbed. [VERB noun]

    找一个安静、温暖、舒适且不会被打扰到的房间。

2 VERB 使焦虑;使不安;使烦恼 If something disturbs you, it makes you feel upset or worried. 使焦虑;使不安;使烦恼

  • I dream about him, dreams so vivid that they disturb me for days. [VERB noun]

    我几次梦见了他，梦境如此逼真，我好几天都心神不宁。

  • He had been disturbed by the news of the attack on Hector Coyne. [VERB noun]

    赫克托·科因受袭的消息让他焦虑不安。

SYN upset, concern, worry, trouble  
3 VERB 搅乱;弄乱;搞乱 If something is disturbed, its position or shape is changed. 搅乱;弄乱;搞乱

  • He'd placed his notes in the brown envelope. They hadn't been disturbed. [be VERB-ed]

    他将笔记放到那个棕色的信封里了，并没有人动过。

  • She patted Mona, taking care not to disturb her costume. [VERB noun]

    她轻轻拍了拍莫娜，小心翼翼地，生怕弄乱她的戏服。

4 VERB 干扰，破坏(形势、氛围) If something disturbs a situation or atmosphere, it spoils it or causes trouble. 干扰，破坏(形势、氛围)

  • Neither Baker nor Levy seemed eager to disturb the cordial atmosphere by discussing more sensitive issues. [VERB noun]

    贝克和利维似乎都不愿谈及更为敏感的话题而破坏了友好的氛围。

  • What could possibly disturb such tranquility? [VERB noun]

    什么有可能会破坏这样的宁静呢？

5 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 扰乱治安 If someone is accused of disturbing the peace, they are accused of behaving in a noisy and offensive way in public. 扰乱治安 [law]
