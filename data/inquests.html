inquest
ɪnkwest ˈɪnˌkwɛst
Word forms: plural inquests
1 N-COUNT (调查死因的)讯问 When an inquest is held, a public official hears evidence about someone's death in order to find out the cause. (调查死因的)讯问

  • The inquest into their deaths opened yesterday in Enniskillen. [+ into]

    对他们死因的讯问昨天在恩尼斯基林进行。

SYN inquiry, investigation, probe, inquisition  
2 N-COUNT [usually singular] (失败原因的)调查,查究 You can refer to an investigation by the people involved into the causes of a defeat or failure as an inquest . (失败原因的)调查,查究

  • His plea came last night as party chiefs held an inquest into the election disaster. [+ into]

    昨晚政党领袖就选举惨败原因进行调查时,他提出了申辩。
