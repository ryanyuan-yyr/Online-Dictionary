untoward
ʌntəwɔːʳd -tɔːʳd ʌnˈtoʊərd ʌnˈtɔrd
ADJ [ADJ n] 横生枝节的;意外的 If you say that something untoward happens, you mean that something happens that is unexpected and causes difficulties. 横生枝节的;意外的 [formal]

  • The surveyor's report didn't highlight anything untoward.

    勘测员的报告没有指明有任何麻烦问题。

  • Tampering with a single enzyme can lead to untoward effects elsewhere.

    破坏一种酶可能会导致在别处出现异常。

SYN unfavourable, unfortunate, disastrous, adverse
