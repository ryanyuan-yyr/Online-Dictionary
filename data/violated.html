violate
vaɪəleɪt ˈvaɪəˌleɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense violates, present participle violating, past tense , past participle violated
1 VERB 违反，违背，违犯（协议、法律或承诺） If someone violates an agreement, law, or promise, they break it. 违反，违背，违犯（协议、法律或承诺） [formal]

  • They went to prison because they violated the law. [VERB noun]

    他们触犯了法律，因此坐了牢。

  • They violated the ceasefire agreement. [VERB noun]

    他们违反了停火协议。

SYN break, infringe, disobey, transgress  
violation

  • To deprive the boy of his education is a violation of state law.

    剥夺这个男孩受教育的权利是一种违反国家法律的行为。

  • He was in violation of his contract.

    他违反了自己所签订的合同。

violator

  • ...a government which is a known violator of human rights.

    一个众所周知的践踏人权的政府

2 VERB 侵犯（隐私）;搅扰;打搅 If you violate someone's privacy or peace, you disturb it. 侵犯（隐私）;搅扰;打搅 [formal]

  • These men were violating her family's privacy. [VERB noun]

    这些人侵犯了她的家庭隐私。

SYN invade, infringe on, disturb, upset  
3 VERB 污损;亵渎 If someone violates a special place, for example a grave, they damage it or treat it with disrespect. 污损;亵渎

  • Detectives are still searching for those who violated the graveyard. [VERB noun]

    侦探们仍在搜捕那些亵渎了墓园的家伙。

SYN desecrate, profane, defile, abuse  
violation

  • The violation of the graves is not the first such incident.

    像这种破坏墓地的行为不是第一次出现了。
