regime
reɪʒiːm rəˈʒim reɪˈʒim
Word forms: plural regimes
1 N-COUNT 政权，政体（指非民主且统治手段不被认可的政府或政权体系） If you refer to a government or system of running a country as a regime, you are critical of it because you think it is not democratic and uses unacceptable methods. 政权，政体（指非民主且统治手段不被认可的政府或政权体系） [disapproval]

  • ...the collapse of the Fascist regime at the end of the war.

    在战争末期法西斯政权的倒台

  • Pujol was imprisoned and tortured under the Franco regime.

    普约尔在佛朗哥政权统治时期受到了监禁和折磨。

SYN government, rule, authorities, management  
2 N-COUNT （机构、公司、经济等的）管理制度，组织方法 A regime is the way that something such as an institution, company, or economy is run, especially when it involves tough or severe action. （机构、公司、经济等的）管理制度，组织方法

  • The authorities moved him to the less rigid regime of an open prison.

    当局把他转到一个管制不那么严格的开放式监狱。

  • ...a drastic regime of economic reform and financial discipline.

    雷厉风行的经济改革和财经纪律

SYN plan, course, system, policy  
3 N-COUNT 养生法 A regime is a set of rules about food, exercise, or beauty that some people follow in order to stay healthy or attractive. 养生法

  • He has a new fitness regime to strengthen his back.

    他有一个增强背部力量的新健身计划。
