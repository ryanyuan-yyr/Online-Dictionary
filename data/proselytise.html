proselytize
prɒsɪlɪtaɪz ˈprɑsəlɪˌtaɪz ˈprɑsəˌlaɪtˌaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense proselytizes, present participle proselytizing, past tense , past participle proselytized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 proselytise
VERB 使改变宗教（或政治）信仰 If you proselytize, you try to persuade someone to share your beliefs, especially religious or political beliefs. 使改变宗教（或政治）信仰 [formal]

  • I assured him we didn't come here to proselytize. [VERB]

    我向他保证我们并不是来改变他的信仰的。

  • Christians were arrested for trying to convert people, to proselytise them. [VERB noun]

    基督徒因为试图改变他人信仰而被捕。

SYN convert, win over, spread the gospel to, evangelize to
