inescapable
ɪnɪskeɪpəbəl ˌɪnɛˈskeɪpəbəl ˌɪnɪˈskeɪpəbəl
ADJ 不容忽视的;不可避免的;必然发生的 If you describe a fact, situation, or activity as inescapable, you mean that it is difficult not to notice it or be affected by it. 不容忽视的;不可避免的;必然发生的

  • The economic logic of reform is inescapable.

    改革有其必然的经济学逻辑。

  • A sense of imminent doom was inescapable.

    一种即将来临的毁灭感无可逃避。

SYN unavoidable, inevitable, certain, sure  
inescapably

  • It is inescapably clear that they won't turn round.

    显而易见，他们不会改变主意。
