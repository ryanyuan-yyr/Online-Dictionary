defect
diːfekt dɪfekt ˈdiˌfɛkt diˈfɛkt dɪˈfɛkt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense defects, present participle defecting, past tense , past participle defected
The noun is pronounced /'diːfekt/. The verb is pronounced /dɪ'fekt/. 名词读作 /'diːfekt/。动词读作 /dɪ'fekt/。
1 N-COUNT 缺点;缺陷;毛病;瑕疵 A defect is a fault or imperfection in a person or thing. 缺点;缺陷;毛病;瑕疵

  • He was born with a hearing defect.

    他患有先天性听力缺损。

  • ...a defect in the aircraft caused the crash.

    飞机的一个缺陷导致了这起坠毁事故。

  • A report has pointed out the defects of the present system.

    一份报告指出了当前体制存在的毛病。

SYN deficiency, want, failing, lack  
2 VERB 叛逃;背叛;变节;倒戈 If you defect, you leave your country, political party, or other group, and join an opposing country, party, or group. 叛逃;背叛;变节;倒戈

  • He tried to defect to the West last year. [V + to/from]

    去年他试图叛逃到西方。

  • He defected from the party in the late 1970s. [V to/from n]

    他在20世纪70年代后期叛出了该党。

  • ...a KGB officer who defected in 1963. [VERB]

    1963年叛变的一名克格勃官员

SYN desert, rebel, quit, revolt  
defection

    至少 16 名议会代表的倒戈
