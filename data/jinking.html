jink
dʒɪŋk dʒɪŋk
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense jinks, present participle jinking, past tense , past participle jinked
1 VERB 作急速曲线移动；急转 To jink somewhere means to move there quickly in an irregular way, rather than by moving in a straight line. 作急速曲线移动；急转 [British, informal] [Also VERB]

  • As they reached the start-finish line Prost jinked right and drew abreast. [VERB adv./prep.]

    当他们接近起止线时，普罗斯特猛向右转，两辆车并排驶过了终点。
