annul
ənʌl əˈnʌl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense annuls, present participle annulling, past tense , past participle annulled
VERB [usually passive] 废除，取消（合同等）；宣告（选举等）无效 If an election or a contract is annulled, it is declared invalid, so that legally it is considered never to have existed. 废除，取消（合同等）；宣告（选举等）无效

  • Opposition party leaders are now pressing for the entire election to be annulled. [be VERB-ed]

    反对党领导人现正强烈要求宣告这次选举无效。

  • The marriage was annulled last month. [be VERB-ed]

    上个月这桩婚姻被宣告无效。

SYN invalidate, reverse, cancel, abolish
