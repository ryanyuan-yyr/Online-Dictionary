convert
kənvɜːʳt kɒnvɜːʳt kənˈvɜrt ˈkɑnˌvɜrt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense converts, present participle converting, past tense , past participle converted
The verb is pronounced /kən'vɜːt/. The noun is pronounced /'kɒnvɜːt/. 动词读作 /kən'vɜːt/，名词读作 /'kɒnvɜːt/。
1 V-ERG (使)改变;更改;(使)转变 If one thing is converted or converts into another, it is changed into a different form. (使)改变;更改;(使)转变

  • The signal will be converted into digital code. [be V-ed + into/to]

    信号会被转变为数字代码。

  • ...naturally occurring substances which the body can convert into vitamins. [V n + into/to]

    自然产生的能被身体转化成维生素的物质

  • ...a table that converts into an ironing board. [V + into/to]

    能转换成熨衣板的桌子

SYN change, turn, transform, alter  
2 VERB 改造，改建(房屋等) If someone converts a room or building, they alter it in order to use it for a different purpose. 改造，改建(房屋等)

  • By converting the loft, they were able to have two extra bedrooms. [VERB noun]

    把阁楼改造一下，他们就可以多出两间卧室。

  • ...the entrepreneur who wants to convert County Hall into an hotel. [VERB noun + into]

    想把市政厅改建成旅馆的企业家

  • He is living in a converted barn. [VERB-ed]

    他住在一个改造过的仓库里。

SYN adapt, modify, remodel, reorganize  
3 VERB 改造，改装(车辆或设备) If you convert a vehicle or piece of equipment, you change it so that it can use a different fuel. 改造，改装(车辆或设备) [Also VERB n + into]

  • Save money by converting your car to unleaded. [VERB noun + to]

    将车改装一下，使用无铅汽油，这样可以省钱。

  • The programme to convert every gas burner in Britain took 10 years. [VERB noun]

    完成对英国所有煤气灶的改造花了10年时间。

4 VERB 换算;折算 If you convert a quantity from one system of measurement to another, you calculate what the quantity is in the second system. 换算;折算 [Also VERB n]

  • Converting metric measurements to U.S. equivalents is easy. [VERB noun prep.]

    将公制计量换算成美制计量非常简单。

5 V-ERG (使)改变宗教（或政治）信仰;(使)皈依;(使)归附 If someone converts you, they persuade you to change your religious or political beliefs. You can also say that someone converts to a different religion. (使)改变宗教（或政治）信仰;(使)皈依;(使)归附

  • If you try to convert him, you could find he just walks away. [VERB noun]

    如果你试图改变他的信仰，他就会走开。

  • He was a major influence in converting Godwin to political radicalism. [VERB noun + to]

    戈德温变成政治激进主义者主要是受他的影响。

  • He converted to Catholicism in 1917. [VERB + to]

    他于1917年皈依天主教。

6 N-COUNT 皈依者;归附者;改变信仰者 A convert is someone who has changed their religious or political beliefs. 皈依者;归附者;改变信仰者

  • She, too, was a convert to Roman Catholicism. [+ to]

    她也皈依了天主教。

  • ...a Muslim convert now known as Yusuf Islam.

    现以优素福·伊斯兰这个名字为大家熟知的伊斯兰教皈依者

SYN neophyte [formal] , disciple, proselyte, catechumen  
7 VERB 使…迷上;使…热衷于 If someone converts you to something, they make you very enthusiastic about it. 使…迷上;使…热衷于 [Also VERB n]

  • He quickly converted me to the joys of cross-country skiing. [VERB noun + to]

    他很快就使我迷上了越野滑雪。

8 N-COUNT 刚迷上…的人;最近开始热衷于…的人 If you describe someone as a convert to something, you mean that they have recently become very enthusiastic about it. 刚迷上…的人;最近开始热衷于…的人

  • ...recent converts to vegetarianism. [+ to]

    最近开始热衷于素食主义的人

9 to preach to the converted→see: preach
