herd
hɜːʳd hɜrd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense herds, present participle herding, past tense , past participle herded
1 N-COUNT [oft n NOUN] 兽群;畜群 A herd is a large group of animals of one kind that live together. 兽群;畜群

  • ...large herds of elephant and buffalo. [+ of]

    大群的大象和水牛

  • ...dairy herds.

    奶牛群

SYN flock, crowd, collection, mass  
2 N-SING （缺乏独立思想的）一般民众，芸芸众生 If you say that someone has joined the herd or follows the herd, you are criticizing them because you think that they behave just like everyone else and do not think for themselves. （缺乏独立思想的）一般民众，芸芸众生 [disapproval]

  • They are individuals; they will not follow the herd.

    他们是独立的个体，不愿随大溜。

SYN mob, the masses, rabble, populace  
3 VERB 使集在一起;把…赶在一起 If you herd people somewhere, you make them move there in a group. 使集在一起;把…赶在一起

  • He began to herd the prisoners out. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他开始将犯人集中在一起赶出来。

  • The group was herded into a bus. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    这群人被赶上了一辆公共汽车。

SYN lead, drive, force, direct  
4 VERB 驱赶，牧放（畜群） If you herd animals, you make them move along as a group. 驱赶，牧放（畜群）

  • Stefano used a motor cycle to herd the sheep. [VERB noun]

    斯特凡诺骑一辆摩托车放羊。

  • A boy herded half a dozen camels down towards the water trough. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    一个男孩赶着6头骆驼朝饮水槽走去。

SYN drive, lead, force, guide
