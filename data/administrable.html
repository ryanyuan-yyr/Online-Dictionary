administer
ædmɪnɪstəʳ ædˈmɪnɪstər ədˈmɪnɪstər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense administers, present participle administering, past tense , past participle administered
1 VERB 治理，管理（国家等）；施行，实施（法律、考核等） If someone administers something such as a country, the law, or a test, they take responsibility for organizing and supervising it. 治理，管理（国家等）；施行，实施（法律、考核等） [Also V n to n]

  • The plan calls for the UN to administer the country until elections can be held.

    该方案呼吁联合国来管理该国，直到能进行选举为止。

  • We hope that they're going to administer justice impartially. [VERB noun]

    我们希望他们能主持正义，不偏不倚。

  • Next summer's exams would be straightforward to administer and mark. [VERB noun]

    明年夏天的考试在执行和评分上都将很简单。

SYN manage, run, control, rule  
2 VERB 给予（病人药物） If a doctor or a nurse administers a drug, they give it to a patient. 给予（病人药物） [formal] [Also VERB n + to]

  • Paramedics are trained to administer certain drugs. [VERB noun]

    护理人员们接受了配发某些药物的培训。

3 VERB 给以（一拳或一脚） If someone administers a punch or a kick, they punch or kick someone. 给以（一拳或一脚） [formal]

  • He is shown in the tape of the beating as administering most of the blows. [VERB noun]

    录像显示在斗殴中他打得最凶。

SYN execute, do, give, provide
