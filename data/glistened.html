glisten
glɪsən ˈglɪsən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense glistens, present participle glistening, past tense , past participle glistened
1 VERB （因湿润或油腻而）闪闪发光 If something glistens, it shines, usually because it is wet or oily. （因湿润或油腻而）闪闪发光

  • The calm sea glistened in the sunlight. [VERB]

    平静的海面在阳光下波光粼粼。

  • Darcy's face was white and glistening with sweat. [VERB + with]

    达西脸色苍白，汗珠晶莹。

  • ...tubs of glistening olives. [VERB-ing]

    一盆盆油光发亮的橄榄

SYN gleam, flash, shine, glance  
2 VERB （眼睛）闪闪发光，闪烁光芒 If you say that someone's eyes glisten, you mean their eyes are shining. （眼睛）闪闪发光，闪烁光芒 [written]

  • 'I shall go, leave him.' Laura Stebbing stopped, her eyes glistening. [VERB]

    “我要走，离开他。”劳拉·斯特宾停了下来，眼里闪烁着光芒。

  • His sunken eyes glistened with delight. [VERB with noun]

    他凹陷的眼睛闪现出喜悦的光芒。
