disarm
dɪsɑːʳm dɪsˈɑrm ˈdɪsˈɑrm
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense disarms, present participle disarming, past tense , past participle disarmed
1 VERB 缴…的械;解除…的武装 To disarm a person or group means to take away all their weapons. 缴…的械;解除…的武装

  • We will agree to disarming troops and leaving their weapons at military positions. [VERB noun]

    我们将同意解除军队的武装并把武器留在军事阵地。

2 VERB 裁军;解除武装;裁减军备(尤指核武器) If a country or group disarms, it gives up the use of weapons, especially nuclear weapons. 裁军;解除武装;裁减军备(尤指核武器)

  • There has also been a suggestion that the forces in Lebanon should disarm. [VERB]

    也有人建议黎巴嫩的武装力量应解除武装。

  • We're not ready to disarm ourselves in order to make it easier for them to kill us. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    我们可不准备放下武器，好让他们更轻易地干掉我们。

3 VERB 消除(某人)的怒气(或不满、批评等) If a person or their behaviour disarms you, they cause you to feel less angry, hostile, or critical towards them. 消除(某人)的怒气(或不满、批评等)

  • His unease disarmed her. [VERB noun]

    他的挂念化解了她的怒气。

  • She did her best to disarm her critics. [VERB noun]

    她竭尽所能让批评她的那些人消气。

SYN win over, persuade, convert, charm
