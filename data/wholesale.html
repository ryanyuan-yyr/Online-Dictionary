wholesale
hoʊlseɪl ˈhoʊlˌseɪl
1 N-UNCOUNT [usually NOUN n] 批发;趸售 Wholesale is the activity of buying and selling goods in large quantities and therefore at cheaper prices, usually to shopkeepers who then sell them to the public. 批发;趸售 [business]

  • Warehouse clubs allow members to buy goods at wholesale prices.

    仓储式会员店允许会员以批发价购买商品。

  • I am in the wholesale trade.

    我做批发生意。

2 ADV [ADVERB after verb] 用批发方式;以批发价 If something is sold wholesale, it is sold in large quantities and at cheaper prices, usually to shopkeepers. 用批发方式;以批发价 [business]

  • The fabrics are sold wholesale to retailers, fashion houses, and other manufacturers. [+ to]

    这些纺织品被批发给零售商、时装店和其他制造商。

SYN extensively, comprehensively, across the board, all at once  
3 ADJ [ADJ n] 大规模的;大批的 You use wholesale to describe the destruction, removal, or changing of something when it affects a very large number of things or people. 大规模的;大批的 [emphasis]

  • They are only doing what is necessary to prevent wholesale destruction of vegetation.

    他们只是在采取必要措施避免大规模的植被破坏。

  • It was wholesale slaughter of women and children.

    那是对妇女和儿童的大规模屠杀。

Wholesale is also an adverb.

  • ...a government which kills wholesale and guerrillas who kill selectively.

    实行大规模杀戮的政府和有选择地杀人的游击队
