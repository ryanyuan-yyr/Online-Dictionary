mistrial
mɪstraɪəl -traɪ- ˈmɪsˌtraɪəl
Word forms: plural mistrials
1 N-COUNT 无效审判(指由于没有考虑到所有证据等原因造成审判不公正，所以必须重新审判) A mistrial is a legal trial that is conducted unfairly, for example because not all the evidence is considered, so that there must be a new trial. 无效审判(指由于没有考虑到所有证据等原因造成审判不公正，所以必须重新审判)

  • The past has been scarred by countless mistrials and perversions of justice.

    过去数不清的冤假错案和枉法行为给人们带来了创伤。

2 N-COUNT (由于陪审团意见不一致等而产生的)未决审判，失审 A mistrial is a legal trial which ends without a verdict, for example because the jury cannot agree on one. (由于陪审团意见不一致等而产生的)未决审判，失审 [US]

  • The judge said he would declare a mistrial if the jury did not reach its verdict today.

    法官说，如果陪审团今天不能作出裁决的话，他就宣布失审。
