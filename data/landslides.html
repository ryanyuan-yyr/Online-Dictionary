landslide
lændslaɪd ˈlændˌslaɪd
Word forms: plural landslides
1 N-COUNT （选举中）大获全胜，一面倒的胜利 A landslide is a victory in an election in which a person or political party gets far more votes or seats than their opponents. （选举中）大获全胜，一面倒的胜利

  • He won last month's presidential election by a landslide.

    他以压倒性多数赢得上月的总统选举。

  • The NLD won a landslide victory in the elections five months ago.

    全国民主联盟在5个月前的选举中获得压倒性胜利。

SYN decisive victory, triumph, overwhelming majority, walkover  
2 N-COUNT 塌方；山崩；滑坡 A landslide is a large amount of earth and rocks falling down a cliff or the side of a mountain. 塌方；山崩；滑坡

  • The storm caused landslides and flooding in Savona.

    风暴致使萨沃纳发生塌方和洪灾。

SYN landslip, avalanche, mudslide, rockfall
