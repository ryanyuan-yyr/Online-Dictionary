stalwart
stɔːlwəʳt ˈstɔlwərt
Word forms: plural stalwarts
1 N-COUNT (尤指某个政党的)忠实拥护者，坚定分子 A stalwart is a loyal worker or supporter of an organization, especially a political party. (尤指某个政党的)忠实拥护者，坚定分子

  • His free-trade policies aroused suspicion among Tory stalwarts.

    他的自由贸易政策在保守党的忠实拥护者中间引起了怀疑。

  • Moving to Germany, he became a stalwart of the revered Kurt Edelhagen Orchestra.

    搬到德国后，他成了著名的库尔特·埃德尔哈根管弦乐队的一名忠实听众。

SYN adherent, supporter, fan, follower  
2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 忠实的;坚定的;完全可靠的 A stalwart supporter or worker is loyal, steady, and completely reliable. 忠实的;坚定的;完全可靠的

  • ...a stalwart supporter of the colonial government.

    殖民政府的忠实拥护者

  • The stalwart volunteers marched in this morning ready to go to work.

    忠实的志愿者们今天上午已就位，随时准备开始工作。

SYN loyal, faithful, staunch, strong  
3 ADJ [ADJ n] 强壮的;雄健的 A stalwart man is strong. 强壮的;雄健的 [literary, or old-fashioned]

  • I knew I was never in any danger with my stalwart bodyguard around me.

    我知道有强壮的保镖在我的周围，我永远不会有什么危险。

SYN strong, strapping, robust, athletic
