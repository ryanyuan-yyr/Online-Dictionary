chatter
tʃætəʳ ˈtʃætər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense chatters, present participle chattering, past tense , past participle chattered
1 VERB 唠叨;喋喋不休 If you chatter, you talk quickly and continuously, usually about things which are not important. 唠叨;喋喋不休 [Also VERB]

  • Everyone's chattering away in different languages. [VERB adv./prep.]

    大家操着不同的语言喋喋不休地说着。

  • Erica was friendly and chattered about Andrew's children. [VERB + about]

    埃丽卡很和气，不停地念叨着安德鲁的孩子们。

  • He listened to chattering maids as they passed by. [VERB-ing]

    他留神听着从身边走过的姑娘们嘴里在闲扯着什么。

SYN prattle, chat, rabbit on [British , informal] , babble  
Chatter is also a noun.

  • ...idle chatter.

    闲聊

  • Lila kept up a steady stream of chatter.

    莉拉一直说个没完。

2 VERB (牙齿)打战，咯咯响 If your teeth chatter, they keep knocking together because you are very cold or very nervous. (牙齿)打战，咯咯响

  • She was so cold her teeth chattered. [VERB]

    她冻得牙齿咯咯响。

3 VERB 发出咯咯声(或嗒嗒声) If objects chatter, they make short repeated sounds. 发出咯咯声(或嗒嗒声)

  • The telex chattered all day and night with news bulletins. [VERB]

    电传机没日没夜“嗒嗒嗒”地接收新闻稿。

Chatter is also a noun.

  • ...the mountains where the chatter of the chairlift cable over the pulley wheel is the loudest noise.

    升降椅的缆绳在滑轮上发出的咯吱声盖过其他一切声响的群山

4 VERB (鸟)鸣叫，啁啾;(动物)唧唧叫，吱吱叫 When birds or animals chatter, they make high-pitched noises. (鸟)鸣叫，啁啾;(动物)唧唧叫，吱吱叫 [literary]

  • Birds were chattering somewhere. [VERB]

    鸟儿在某处唧唧喳喳地叫着。

Chatter is also a noun.

  • ...almond trees vibrating with the chatter of crickets.

    伴随蟋蟀的叫声颤动的杏树
