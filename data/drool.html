drool
druːl drul
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense drools, present participle drooling, past tense , past participle drooled
1 VERB 对…过分痴迷;对…垂涎欲滴 To drool over someone or something means to look at them with great pleasure, perhaps in an exaggerated or ridiculous way. 对…过分痴迷;对…垂涎欲滴 [disapproval] [Also VERB]

  • Fashion editors drooled over every item. [VERB + over]

    时尚编辑们兴奋地注视着每一件东西。

  • Advertisers are already drooling at reports that this might bring 20 million dollars. [VERB prep.]

    广告商听闻这或许会带来2,000万美元的收益后已是垂涎欲滴了。

  • ...his wife's constant drooling over a poodle. [VERB-ing]

    他妻子眼睛直勾勾地盯着一只鬈毛狗

SYN gloat over, pet, gush, make much of  
2 VERB 流口水;垂涎 If a person or animal drools, saliva drops slowly from their mouth. 流口水;垂涎

  • My dog Jacques is drooling on my shoulder. [VERB]

    我的狗雅克在我肩膀上流着口水。
