runner
rʌnəʳ ˈrʌnər
Word forms: plural runners
1 N-COUNT 跑步者;赛跑者 A runner is a person who runs, especially for sport or pleasure. 跑步者;赛跑者

  • ...a marathon runner.

    马拉松长跑选手

  • I am a very keen runner and am out training most days.

    我非常喜欢跑步，大多数时候都在户外训练。

SYN athlete, miler, sprinter, harrier  
2 N-COUNT 参加赛马的马匹;赛马 The runners in a horse race are the horses taking part. 参加赛马的马匹;赛马

  • There are 18 runners in the top race of the day.

    在当天最高级别的赛马中有18匹马参加。

3 N-COUNT [n NOUN] 走私者;偷运者 A drug runner or gun runner is someone who illegally takes drugs or guns into a country. 走私者;偷运者
4 N-COUNT 送信人;信使;收账员;听差 Someone who is a runner for a particular person or company is employed to take messages, collect money, or do other small tasks for them. 送信人;信使;收账员;听差

  • ...a bookie's runner.

    赌注经纪人的听差

SYN messenger, courier, errand boy, dispatch bearer  
5 N-COUNT [usually plural] (雪橇的)滑板;(冰鞋的)冰刀;(抽屉的)滑轨 Runners are thin strips of wood or metal underneath something which help it to move smoothly. (雪橇的)滑板;(冰鞋的)冰刀;(抽屉的)滑轨

  • ...the runners of his sled. [+ of]

    他的雪橇的滑板

6 N-COUNT [usually plural] (植物的)长匐茎，纤匐枝 On a plant, runners are long shoots that grow from the main stem and put down roots to form a new plant. (植物的)长匐茎，纤匐枝

  • ...strawberry runners.

    草莓长匐茎

7 N-COUNT 长条地毯;长条饰布 A runner is a long narrow mat that is put on a piece of furniture or on the floor. 长条地毯;长条饰布
8 PHRASE 匆匆离开;迅速逃离;溜掉 If someone does a runner, they leave a place in a hurry, for example in order to escape arrest or to avoid paying for something. 匆匆离开;迅速逃离;溜掉 [British, informal]

  • At this point, the accountant did a runner — with all my bank statements, expenses and receipts.

    这个时候，会计溜了—带走了我所有的银行结算单、资金和收据。

SYN run away, escape, flee, take off
