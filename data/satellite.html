satellite
sætəlaɪt ˈsætəlˌaɪt
Word forms: plural satellites
1 N-COUNT [oft by NOUN] 人造卫星 A satellite is an object which has been sent into space in order to collect information or to be part of a communications system. Satellites move continually round the earth or around another planet. 人造卫星

  • The rocket launched two communications satellites.

    火箭发射了两颗通信卫星。

  • The signals are sent by satellite link.

    信号通过人造卫星链路发出。

  • Worldwide coverage beamed by satellite generates huge audiences accompanied by global advertising revenues.

    通过卫星发射的信号覆盖全世界，从而产生了大量的观众并在全球范围内赚取不菲的广告费。

SYN spacecraft, communications satellite, sputnik, space capsule  
2 ADJ [ADJ n] (电视)使用卫星技术的;卫视的 Satellite television is broadcast using a satellite. (电视)使用卫星技术的;卫视的

  • They have four satellite channels.

    他们有 4 个卫星频道。

SYN dependent, client, subordinate, puppet  
3 N-COUNT 卫星 A satellite is a natural object in space that moves round a planet or star. 卫星

  • ...the satellites of Jupiter.

    木星的卫星

SYN moon, secondary planet  
4 N-COUNT [oft NOUN n] 卫星国;附属国;卫星区;卫星组织 You can refer to a country, area, or organization as a satellite when it is controlled by or depends on a larger and more powerful one. 卫星国;附属国;卫星区;卫星组织

  • Italy became a satellite state of Germany by the end of the 1930s.

    意大利在 20 世纪 30 年代末成了德国的附庸。

  • ...Russia and its former satellites.

    俄国及其曾经的卫星国

SYN colony, dependency, dominion, protectorate
