crossing
krɒsɪŋ krɔːs- ˈkrɔsɪŋ
Word forms: plural crossings
1 N-COUNT 横渡 A crossing is a journey by boat or ship to a place on the other side of a sea, river, or lake. 横渡

  • He made the crossing from Cape Town to Sydney in just over twenty-six days.

    他在短短26天内从开普敦横渡到悉尼。

  • The vessel docked in Swansea after a ten-hour crossing.

    轮船在横渡10个小时后停泊在了斯旺西。

2 N-COUNT (道路的)十字路口;(线的)交叉点 A crossing is a place where two roads, paths, or lines cross. (道路的)十字路口;(线的)交叉点
3 N-COUNT 同 pedestrian crossing A crossing is the same as a pedestrian crossing . 同 pedestrian crossing [British]

  • A car hit her on a crossing.

    一辆汽车在人行横道上撞了她。

in AM, use 美国英语用 crosswalk

5 N-COUNT 同 grade crossing 或 level crossing A crossing is the same as a grade crossing or a level crossing . 同 grade crossing 或 level crossing [British]
