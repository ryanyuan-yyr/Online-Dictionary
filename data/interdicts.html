interdict
ɪntəʳdɪkt ɪntəʳdɪkt ˌɪntərˈdɪkt ˈɪntərˌdɪkt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense interdicts, present participle interdicting, past tense , past participle interdicted
The verb is pronounced /ˌɪntə'dɪkt/. The noun is pronounced /'ɪntədɪkt/. 动词读作/ˌɪntə'dɪkt/。名词读作 /'ɪntədɪkt/。
1 VERB 封锁;阻断 If an armed force interdicts something or someone, they stop them and prevent them from moving. If they interdict a route, they block it or cut it off. 封锁;阻断 [US, formal]

  • Troops could be ferried in to interdict drug shipments. [VERB noun]

    可以把军队渡运过来阻截毒品的装运。

  • ...efforts to isolate the theater of operations by interdicting the bridges. [VERB noun]

    通过封锁桥梁来孤立那一战区的努力

SYN prohibit, bar, ban, prevent  
interdiction

  • ...increased drug interdiction efforts by the military and Coast Guard.

    军方与海岸警卫队对毒品加大了力度的堵截

2 N-COUNT (官方的)禁令,强制令 An interdict is an official order that something must not be done or used. (官方的)禁令,强制令 [formal]

  • The National Trust has placed an interdict on jet-skis in Dorset, Devon and Cornwall. [+ on]

    国民托管组织已颁布禁令，禁止在多塞特、德文与康沃尔地区使用水上摩托艇。

SYN ban, veto, prohibition, taboo
