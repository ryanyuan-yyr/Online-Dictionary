tremendous
trɪmendəs trɪˈmɛndəs trɪˈmɛndəs
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 巨大的;极大的;大量的 You use tremendous to emphasize how strong a feeling or quality is, or how large an amount is. 巨大的;极大的;大量的 [informal, emphasis]

  • I felt a tremendous pressure on my chest.

    我感到胸口堵得难受。

  • There's tremendous tension between the local population and the refugees…

    当地居民与难民之间关系非常紧张。

  • That's a tremendous amount of information.

    那是海量的信息。

SYN huge, great, towering, vast  
tremendously

  • The business is tremendously profitable.

    这笔生意利润巨大。

  • I enjoyed the show tremendously.

    我非常喜欢那场演出。

2 ADJ-GRADED 精彩的;出色的;极好的 You can describe someone or something as tremendous when you think they are very good or very impressive. 精彩的;出色的;极好的 [informal]

  • He was a tremendous person…

    他是个非常了不起的人。

  • I thought it was absolutely tremendous.

    我认为它精彩极了。

SYN excellent, great [informal] , wonderful, brilliant  
tremendously

  • I thought they played tremendously well, didn't you?.

    我认为他们踢得非常棒，你不觉得吗？
