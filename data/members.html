member
membəʳ ˈmɛmbər
Word forms: plural members
1 N-COUNT （团体的）成员，一分子 A member of a group is one of the people, animals, or things belonging to that group. （团体的）成员，一分子

  • He refused to name the members of staff involved. [+ of]

    他拒绝说出所涉及员工的姓名。

  • Their lack of training could put members of the public at risk. [+ of]

    他们缺乏训练，这有可能会使民众面临风险。

  • ...a sunflower or a similar member of the daisy family.

    向日葵或菊科植物中一种类似的植物

  • ...the brightest members of a dense cluster of stars at the Galaxy's centre.

    银河系中央一个密集星团中最耀眼的几颗星

2 N-COUNT 会员;成员 A member of an organization such as a club or a political party is a person who has officially joined the organization. 会员;成员

  • The support of our members is of great importance to the Association.

    会员的支持对于我们协会来说非常重要。

  • Britain is a full member of NATO. [+ of]

    英国是北大西洋公约组织的正式成员国。

SYN representative, associate, supporter, fellow  
3 ADJ [ADJ n] (某国际组织的)会员国，成员国 A member country or member state is one of the countries that has joined an international organization or group. (某国际组织的)会员国，成员国

  • ...the member countries of the European Free Trade Association.

    欧洲自由贸易联盟的成员国

  • ...a co-ordinated approach, with each member state doing what it could.

    各成员国尽其所能的协作方式

4 N-COUNT 议员 A member or Member is a person who has been elected to a parliament or legislature. 议员

  • He was elected to Parliament as the Member for Leeds. [+ for]

    他当选为利兹市议员而进入了议会。

  • ...the Conservative member for Billericay.

    代表比勒里基的保守党议员

  • ...Member of the Knesset, David Magen.

    以色列议会的议员戴维·马根
