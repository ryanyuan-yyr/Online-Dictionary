carry on
1 PHRASAL VERB 继续;接着;坚持 If you carry on doing something, you continue to do it. 继续;接着;坚持

  • The assistant carried on talking. [VERB PREP. verb-ing]

    助理继续说下去。

  • Rachael Carr intends to carry on teaching. [V P -ing]

    雷切尔·卡尔打算继续教书。

  • Her bravery has given him the will to carry on with his life and his work. [VERB PREP. + with]

    她的勇气激发了他继续生活和工作的意愿。

  • His eldest son Joseph carried on his father's traditions. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他的长子约瑟夫继承了乃父的传统。

  • 'Do you mind if I just start with the few formal questions please?' — 'Carry on.' [VERB PREP.]

    “我先问几个例行问题你没有意见吧?”“没关系,问吧。”

SYN continue, last, endure, persist  
2 PHRASAL VERB 举行;进行;开展 If you carry on an activity, you do it or take part in it for a period of time. 举行;进行;开展

  • The consulate will carryon a political dialogue with Indonesia.

    领事馆将和印度尼西亚举行政治对话。

  • He carried on a passionate affair with Mrs Gilbert. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    他和吉尔伯特夫人有一段风流韵事。

SYN engage in, conduct, carry out, undertake  
3 PHRASAL VERB 喋喋不休;高谈阔论 If you say that someone is carrying on, you are irritated with them because they are talking very excitedly and saying a lot of unnecessary things. 喋喋不休;高谈阔论 [informal, disapproval]

  • She was yelling and screaming and carrying on. [VERB PREP.]

    她又喊又叫,嘴里说个不停。

  • He was carrying on about some stupid television series. [V P + about]

    他喋喋不休地说着某个无聊的电视连续剧。

SYN make a fuss, act up [informal] , misbehave, create [slang]  
4 PHRASAL VERB [usually cont] 私通;与…有不正当关系 If you say that someone is carrying on with someone else, you mean that they are having a sexual relationship and you do not approve of this, usually because one or both of them is married. 私通;与…有不正当关系 [informal, disapproval] [Also VERB PREPOSITION]

  • Every week a fresh scandal emerges about ministers carrying on with film actresses and call girls. [VERB PREP. with noun]

    每个星期都传出新的丑闻,说某某部长和女演员或是应召女郎胡搞。
