instrument
ɪnstrəmənt ˈɪnstrəmənt
Word forms: plural instruments
1 N-COUNT 器械;器具 An instrument is a tool or device that is used to do a particular task, especially a scientific task. 器械;器具

  • ...a thin tube-like optical instrument.

    试管状的细长光学仪器

  • ...instruments for cleaning and polishing teeth.

    清洗和抛光牙齿的器械

  • The environment itself will at the same time be measured by about 60 scientific instruments.

    同时环境本身将由大约60种科学器械来测量。

SYN tool, device, implement, mechanism  
2 N-COUNT 乐器 A musical instrument is an object such as a piano, guitar, or flute, which you play in order to produce music. 乐器

  • Learning a musical instrument introduces a child to an understanding of music.

    学一门乐器能引导孩子了解音乐。

3 N-COUNT 仪器;仪表 An instrument is a device that is used for making measurements of something such as speed, height, or sound, for example on a ship or plane or in a car. 仪器;仪表

  • ...crucial instruments on the control panel.

    控制面板上的重要仪表

  • ...navigation instruments.

    导航仪器

4 N-COUNT 工具;手段;被他人利用的人 Something that is an instrument for achieving a particular aim is used by people to achieve that aim. 工具;手段;被他人利用的人

  • The veto has been a traditional instrument of diplomacy for centuries. [+ of]

    几个世纪以来否决权一直是外交上惯用的手段。

SYN agent, means, force, cause
