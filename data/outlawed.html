outlaw
aʊtlɔː ˈaʊtˌlɔ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense outlaws, present participle outlawing, past tense , past participle outlawed
1 VERB 宣布…为非法；使成为非法 When something is outlawed, it is made illegal. 宣布…为非法；使成为非法

  • In 1975 gambling was outlawed. [be VERB-ed]

    1975年赌博被宣布为非法。

  • The German government has outlawed some fascist groups. [VERB noun]

    德国政府已经宣布一些法西斯团体为非法。

  • ...the outlawed political parties. [VERB-ed]

    被宣布为非法的政党

SYN ban, bar, veto, forbid  
2 N-COUNT 逃犯；亡命之徒 An outlaw is a criminal who is hiding from the authorities. 逃犯；亡命之徒 [old-fashioned]

  • Jesse was an outlaw, a bandit, a criminal.

    杰西是个亡命之徒，曾经抢劫犯科。

SYN bandit, criminal, thief, crook [informal]
