officiate
əfɪʃieɪt əˈfɪʃiˌeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense officiates, present participle officiating, past tense , past participle officiated
1 VERB 主持(仪式)；行使(指定的职务) When someone officiates at a ceremony or formal occasion, they are in charge and perform the official part of it. 主持(仪式)；行使(指定的职务)

  • Bishop Silvester officiated at the funeral. [VERB + at]

    西尔韦斯特主教主持了葬礼。

  • A memorial service was held yesterday at Wadhurst Parish Church. The Rev Michael Inch officiated. [VERB]

    昨天，悼念仪式在沃德赫斯特堂区教堂举行，迈克尔·英奇牧师担任主持。

SYN preside, perform, conduct, celebrate  
2 VERB （在体育比赛、竞赛等中）担任裁判 When someone officiates at a sports match or competition, they are in charge and make sure the players do not break the rules. （在体育比赛、竞赛等中）担任裁判 [Also V + in]

  • Mr Ellis was selected to officiate at a cup game between Grimsby and Rotherham. [VERB + at]

    埃利斯先生获选执法格里姆斯比队与罗瑟汉姆队的一场杯赛。

  • Frik Burger will officiate when the Pumas play Scotland. [VERB]

    弗里克·伯格将担任阿根廷队对苏格兰队比赛的裁判。
