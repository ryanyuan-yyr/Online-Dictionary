scurry
skʌri skɜːri ˈskɜri
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense scurries, present participle scurrying, past tense , past participle scurried
1 VERB (尤指受惊吓时)急奔，乱窜 When people or small animals scurry somewhere, they move there quickly and hurriedly, especially because they are frightened. (尤指受惊吓时)急奔，乱窜 [written] [Also V prep/adv]

  • The attack began, sending residents scurrying for cover. [+ for]

    袭击发动后，居民们纷纷奔逃躲避。

  • The rats scurry around, searching for scraps of food in the rubbish.

    老鼠乱窜，在垃圾中寻找残羹剩饭。

SYN hurry, race, dash, fly  
2 VERB 急匆匆;赶忙 If people scurry to do something, they do it as soon as they can. 急匆匆;赶忙 [written]

  • Pictures of starving children have sent many people scurrying to donate money. [VERB to-infinitive]

    看到一张张忍饥挨饿的孩子们的照片，许多人赶忙捐钱。
