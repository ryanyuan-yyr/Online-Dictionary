foreshadow
fɔːʳʃædoʊ fɔrˈʃædoʊ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense foreshadows, present participle foreshadowing, past tense , past participle foreshadowed
VERB 预示;是…的预兆 If something foreshadows an event or situation, it suggests that it will happen. 预示;是…的预兆

  • The disappointing sales figures foreshadow more redundancies. [VERB noun]

    令人失望的销售额预示着会有更多人被裁员。

  • The change proposed last month was foreshadowed in the March Budget. [VERB noun]

    上个月提议的变动在 3 月份的预算中已经有所预兆了。

SYN predict, suggest, promise, indicate
