implication
ɪmplɪkeɪʃən ˌɪmplɪˈkeɪʃən
Word forms: plural implications
1 N-COUNT [usually plural] 可能引发的后果 The implications of something are the things that are likely to happen as a result. 可能引发的后果

  • The Attorney General was aware of the political implications of his decision to prosecute. [+ of]

    司法部长很清楚他决定起诉可能引发的政治后果。

  • The low level of current investment has serious implications for future economic growth. [+ for]

    当前低迷的投资水平会对未来的经济增长产生严重影响。

SYN consequence, result, development, ramification  
2 N-COUNT 暗示;暗指;含意 The implication of a statement, event, or situation is what it implies or suggests is the case. 暗示;暗指;含意

  • The implication was obvious: vote for us or it will be very embarrassing for you.

    弦外之音很明显：投票给我们,否则你会非常难堪。

  • The implication that marital infidelity enhances a leader's credibility is preposterous.

    婚姻不忠会提升领导人可信度的暗示荒谬之极。

If you say that something is the case by implication, you mean that a statement, event, or situation implies that it is the case.含蓄地;暗示地

  • His authority and, by implication, that of his management team is under threat.

    他的权威,同时也意味着他整个管理团队的权威，正面临着威胁。

3 See also: implicate
