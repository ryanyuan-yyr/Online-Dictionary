anarchy
ænəʳki ˈænərki ˈænˌɑrki
1 N-UNCOUNT 无政府（状态）；无法无天；无秩序；混乱 If you describe a situation as anarchy, you mean that nobody seems to be paying any attention to rules or laws. 无政府（状态）；无法无天；无秩序；混乱 [disapproval]

  • Civil war and famine sent the nation plunging into anarchy.

    内战和饥荒使该国陷入了无政府状态。

  • In June, 1960, the Congo was plunged into five years of civil war and political anarchy.

    1960年的6月，刚果陷入了5年的内战和政治上的无政府状态。

SYN lawlessness, revolution, riot, disorder
