dissect
daɪsekt dɪ- dɪˈsɛkt daɪˈsɛkt ˈdaɪˌsɛkt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dissects, present participle dissecting, past tense , past participle dissected
1 VERB 解剖(人体或动物躯体) If someone dissects the body of a dead person or animal, they carefully cut it up in order to examine it scientifically. 解剖(人体或动物躯体)

  • We dissected a frog in biology class. [VERB noun]

    我们在生物课上解剖了一只青蛙。

SYN cut up or apart, dismember, lay open, anatomize  
dissection

  • Researchers need a growing supply of corpses for dissection.

2 VERB 仔细分析;详细评论 If someone dissects something such as a theory, a situation, or a piece of writing, they consider and talk about each detail of it. 仔细分析;详细评论

  • People want to dissect his work and question his motives. [VERB noun]

    人们想仔细分析他的作品，并对他的动机提出质疑。
