acolyte
ækəlaɪt ˈækəˌlaɪt ˈækəˌlaɪt
Word forms: plural acolytes
1 N-COUNT 追随者;侍从;助手 An acolyte is a follower or assistant of an important person. 追随者;侍从;助手 [formal]

  • Richard Brome, an acolyte of Ben Jonson's, wrote 'The Jovial Crew' in 1641.

    本·琼森的仆人理查德·布罗姆在1641年写了《一伙快活人》。

  • To his acolytes, he is known simply as 'the Boss'.

    他被手下人简称为“老板”。

SYN follower, fan, supporter, pupil  
2 N-COUNT 辅祭;教士助手 An acolyte is someone who assists a priest in performing certain religious services. 辅祭;教士助手

  • When the barge reached the shrine, acolytes removed the pall.

    当驳船抵达圣地时，辅祭除去了柩衣。

SYN attendant, assistant, follower, helper
