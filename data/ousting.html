oust
aʊst aʊst
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense ousts, present participle ousting, past tense , past participle ousted
VERB 罢黜；把…撤职；驱逐 If someone is ousted from a position of power, job, or place, they are forced to leave it. 罢黜；把…撤职；驱逐 [journalism]

  • The leaders have been ousted from power by nationalists. [be VERB-ed]

    这些领导人被民族主义者赶下了台。

  • Last week they tried to oust him in a parliamentary vote of no confidence. [VERB noun]

    上周，他们试图通过议会的不信任投票将他罢免。

  • ...the ousted government. [VERB-ed]

    遭推翻的政府

SYN expel, turn out, dismiss, exclude  
ousting
