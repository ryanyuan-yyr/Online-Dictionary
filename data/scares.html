scare
skeəʳ skɛə skɛr
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense scares, present participle scaring, past tense , past participle scared
1 VERB 惊吓;使惊恐;使担忧 If something scares you, it frightens or worries you. 惊吓;使惊恐;使担忧

  • You're scaring me. [VERB noun]

    你吓着我了。

  • What scares me most is that I'm going to end up not being married. [VERB noun]

    最让我担心的是我会一辈子嫁不出去。

  • The prospect of failure scares me rigid. [VERB noun adj.ive]

    一想到失败我就吓得身体发僵。

  • It scared him to realise how close he had come to losing everything. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    一想到他差点儿就失去了一切，他心里直打哆嗦。

If you want to emphasize that something scares you a lot, you can say that it scares the hell out of you or scares the life out of you.(表示非常害怕)吓死了，吓得魂都没了
2 N-SING 惊吓;惊恐 If a sudden unpleasant experience gives you a scare, it frightens you. 惊吓;惊恐

  • Don't you realize what a scare you've given us all?.

    你难道不知道你把我们大家都吓得不轻吗？

  • We got a bit of a scare.

    我们受了点惊吓。

SYN fright, shock, start, turn [informal]  
3 N-COUNT [oft n NOUN] 恐惧;恐慌 A scare is a situation in which many people are afraid or worried because they think something dangerous is happening which will affect them all. 恐惧;恐慌

  • ...the doctor at the centre of an Aids scare.

    身处艾滋病恐慌中心的医生

  • Despite the scare there are no plans to withdraw the drug.

    尽管大家忧心忡忡，可是并无撤回药物的计划。

SYN panic, hysteria  
4 N-COUNT [usually n NOUN] (炸弹或安全)恐慌 A bomb scare or a security scare is a situation in which there is believed to be a bomb in a place. (炸弹或安全)恐慌

  • Despite many recent bomb scares, no one has yet been hurt.

    尽管最近发生多起炸弹恐慌，目前还没有人员受伤。

  • ...a security scare over a suspect package.

    可疑包裹引起的安全恐慌

SYN alert, warning, alarm
