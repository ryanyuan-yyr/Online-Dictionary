cope
koʊp koʊp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , 3rd person singular present tense copes, present participle coping, past tense , past participle coped
1 VERB (成功地)处理，应付，对付 If you cope with a problem or task, you deal with it successfully. (成功地)处理，应付，对付

  • It was amazing how my mother coped with bringing up three children on less than three pounds a week. [VERB + with]

    我妈妈用每周不到3英镑的钱抚养大了3个孩子，简直太不可思议了。

  • The problems were an annoyance, but we managed to cope. [VERB]

    这些问题很讨厌，但是我们都设法解决了。

SYN manage, get by [informal] , struggle through, rise to the occasion  
2 VERB 应付，忍受(不愉快的局面) If you have to cope with an unpleasant situation, you have to accept it or bear it. 应付，忍受(不愉快的局面)

  • Never before has the industry had to cope with war and recession at the same time. [V with n/-ing]

    这个行业以前从未有过必须同时应付战争和萧条的经历。

  • She has had to cope with losing all her previous status and money. [VERB + with]

    她不得不忍受彻底失去以前所拥有的地位和金钱的痛苦。

3 VERB (机器、系统等)能够处理，应付 If a machine or a system can cope with something, it is large enough or complex enough to deal with it satisfactorily. (机器、系统等)能够处理，应付

  • New blades have been designed to cope with the effects of dead insects. [VERB + with]

    新的桨片设计使其可以应付昆虫尸体带来的影响。

  • The country's prisons are filled with drug-takers, and cannot cope with the numbers. [VERB with noun]

    这个国家的监狱里挤满了瘾君子，都容纳不下了。

  • The speed of economic change has been so great that the tax-collecting system has been unable to cope. [VERB]

    经济变化得如此之快，以致税收系统都应付不过来了。

4 N-COUNT (基督教牧师在特殊仪式中所穿的)长袍，法衣 A cope is a long sleeveless piece of clothing worn by some Christian priests on special occasions. (基督教牧师在特殊仪式中所穿的)长袍，法衣
