falsehood
fɔːlshʊd ˈfɔlsˌhʊd
Word forms: plural falsehoods
1 N-UNCOUNT 虚伪性;虚假性;谬误 Falsehood is the quality or fact of being untrue or of being a lie. 虚伪性;虚假性;谬误

  • She called the verdict a victory of truth over falsehood.

    她将这一判决称为真理对谬误的胜利。

SYN untruthfulness, deception, deceit, dishonesty  
2 N-COUNT 谎言 A falsehood is a lie. 谎言 [formal]

  • He accused them of knowingly spreading falsehoods about him.

    他指责他们故意散布有关他的谎言。

SYN lie, story [informal] , fiction, fabrication  
Quotations
The most dangerous of all falsehoods is a slightly distorted truthG.C. Lichtenberg
