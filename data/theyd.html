they'd ðeɪd ðeɪd
1 (尤当had为助动词时，they had的一种口语形式) They'd is a spoken form of 'they had', especially when 'had' is an auxiliary verb. (尤当had为助动词时，they had的一种口语形式)

  • They'd both lived in this road all their lives.

    他们俩在这条路上生活了一辈子。

2 (they would 的一种口语形式) They'd is a spoken form of 'they would'. (they would 的一种口语形式)

  • He agreed that they'd visit her after they stopped at Jan's for coffee.

    他答应他们在简的家里喝完咖啡后就去看她。
