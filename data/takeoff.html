takeoff
teɪkɒf ˈteɪkˌɒf -ɔːf ˈteɪkˌɔf
(also take-off)Word forms: plural takeoffs
1 N-VAR (飞机的)起飞，升空 Takeoff is the beginning of a flight, when an aircraft leaves the ground. (飞机的)起飞，升空

  • The aircraft crashed after takeoff from Heathrow in a reservoir.

    飞机从希思罗机场起飞后坠毁在一个水库里。

  • The commuter plane was waiting for takeoff.

    通勤飞机在等候起飞。

SYN departure, launch, liftoff  
2 N-COUNT [usually singular] (对某人动作的滑稽)模仿 A takeoff of someone is a humorous imitation of the way in which they behave. (对某人动作的滑稽)模仿

  • The programme was worth watching for an inspired takeoff of the Collins sisters. [+ of]

    这个节目值得一看，因为有对柯林斯姐妹的滑稽创意模仿。

SYN parody, imitation, send-up [British , informal] , mocking  
3 N-UNCOUNT (经济、生意等的)开始腾飞，迅速发展 Takeoff is the point in the development of something, such as an economy or a business, when it begins to be successful. (经济、生意等的)开始腾飞，迅速发展

  • The 1950s were the decade of Hong Kong's industrial take-off.

    20世纪50年代是香港工业开始快速发展的10年。
