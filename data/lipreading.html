lip-read
lɪpriːd lɪpred ˈlɪpˌrid
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense lip-reads, present participle lip-reading
The form lip-read is pronounced /'lɪpriːd/ when it is the present tense, and /'lɪpred/ when it is the past tense and past participle. lip-read现在式读作/'lɪpriːd/，过去式和过去分词读作/'lɪpred/。
VERB 唇读；观唇辨意 If someone can lip-read, they are able to understand what someone else is saying by looking at the way the other person's lips move as they speak, without actually hearing any of the words. 唇读；观唇辨意

  • They are not given hearing aids or taught to lip-read. [VERB]

    没有给他们提供助听设备，也没教过他们唇读。
