impugn
ɪmpjuːn ɪmˈpjun
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense impugns, present participle impugning, past tense , past participle impugned
VERB 指责,非难,质疑(他人的动机、诚信等) If you impugn something such as someone's motives or integrity, you imply that they are not entirely honest or honourable. 指责,非难,质疑(他人的动机、诚信等) [formal]

  • The Secretary's letter questions my veracity and impugns my motives. [VERB noun]

    部长的来信怀疑我的诚实,质疑我的动机。

  • All I can hope is that the good name of the Bank will not be impugned in some way. [VERB noun]

    我所希望的,就是该银行的好名声不要在某些方面受到质疑。

SYN challenge, question, attack, dispute
