chorus
kɔːrəs ˈkɔrəs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense choruses, present participle chorusing, past tense , past participle chorused
1 N-COUNT 叠句;副歌 A chorus is a part of a song which is repeated after each verse. 叠句;副歌

  • Caroline sang two verses and the chorus of her song. [+ of]

    卡罗琳唱了她歌曲中的两段和副歌部分。

  • Everyone joined in the chorus.

    唱到副歌时，大家都跟着一起唱起来。

SYN refrain, response, strain, burden  
2 N-COUNT 合唱团;歌咏队 A chorus is a large group of people who sing together. 合唱团;歌咏队

  • The chorus was singing 'The Ode to Joy'.

    合唱团在唱《欢乐颂》。

SYN choir, singers, ensemble, vocalists  
3 N-COUNT 合唱(歌)曲 A chorus is a piece of music written to be sung by a large group of people. 合唱(歌)曲

  • ...the Hallelujah Chorus.

    《哈利路亚合唱曲》

4 N-COUNT 合唱团;歌舞队 A chorus is a group of singers or dancers who perform together in a show, in contrast to the soloists. 合唱团;歌舞队

  • Students played the lesser parts and sang in the chorus.

    学生扮演一些次要角色，并且参与合唱。

5 N-COUNT [usually singular] 齐声，异口同声(表示批评、反对或赞扬) When there is a chorus of criticism, disapproval, or praise, that attitude is expressed by a lot of people at the same time. 齐声，异口同声(表示批评、反对或赞扬)

  • The government is defending its economic policies against a growing chorus of criticism. [+ of]

    面对日渐高涨的齐声指责，政府不断为其经济政策辩护。

6 VERB 齐声说;齐唱 When people chorus something, they say it or sing it together. 齐声说;齐唱 [written]

  • 'Hi,' they chorused. [VERB with quote]

    “嗨，”他们齐声招呼道。

Chorus is also a noun.

  • He was greeted with a rousing chorus of Happy Birthday.

    迎接他的是大家齐唱《祝你生日快乐》，令他非常感动。

  • 'All the best,' called the other typists in chorus.

    “祝万事如意，”其他打字员齐声喊道。
