relative clause
ˈrɛlətɪv klɔːz
Word forms: plural relative clauses
N-COUNT 关系从句 In grammar, a relative clause is a subordinate clause which specifies or gives information about a person or thing. Relative clauses come after a noun or pronoun and, in English, often begin with a relative pronoun such as 'who', 'which', or 'that'. 关系从句
