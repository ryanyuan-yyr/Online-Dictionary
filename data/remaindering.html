remainder
rɪmeɪndəʳ rɪˈmeɪndər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense remainders, present participle remaindering, past tense , past participle remaindered
1 QUANT 剩余部分;剩余物;余下的人(或物) The remainder of a group are the things or people that still remain after the other things or people have gone or have been dealt with. 剩余部分;剩余物;余下的人(或物)

  • He gulped down the remainder of his coffee. [+ of]

    他把剩下的咖啡一口气喝了下去。

  • I spent the remainder of the day feeling terrible.

    我那天余下的时间里心情都很糟糕。

Remainder is also a pronoun.

  • Only 5.9 per cent of the area is now covered in trees. Most of the remainder is farmland.

    这个地区的树木覆盖率只有5.9%。其他多数地方都是农田。

  • Of the 59 committee members, 40 were against legalisation, 13 were in favour and the remainder were undecided.

    在59个委员会委员中，有40人反对这项法案，13人赞成，其余的尚未表明态度。

2 N-SING [the NOUN] (算术中的)余数 In arithmetic, the remainder is the amount that remains when one amount cannot be exactly divided by another. For example, if you divide 22 by 7, the answer is 3 and the remainder is 1. (算术中的)余数
3 VERB [usually passive] (因为滞销而)廉价出售（图书） If a book is remaindered, it is sold at a reduced price because it has not been selling very well and will not be published again. (因为滞销而)廉价出售（图书）

  • It failed to sell and was soon remaindered. [be VERB-ed]

    此书销量不好，很快就被廉价处理了。

4 N-COUNT 廉价销售的书 A remainder is a book that has been remaindered. 廉价销售的书
