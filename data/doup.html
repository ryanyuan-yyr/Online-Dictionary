do up
1 PHRASAL VERB 扣好;系好;捆好;扎好 If you do something up, you fasten it. 扣好;系好;捆好;扎好

  • Mari did up the buttons. [VERB PREP. noun]

    玛丽系好扣子。

  • Keep your scarf on, do your coat up. [VERB noun PREP.]

    围好围巾，扣好衣服。

2 PHRASAL VERB 装修;修缮;重新装饰 If you do up an old building, you decorate and repair it so that it is in a better condition. 装修;修缮;重新装饰 [British] [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • Nicholas has bought a barn in Provence and is spending August doing it up. [VERB noun PREP.]

    尼古拉斯在普罗旺斯买了一座条件简陋的大房子，8月份正在装修。

SYN refurbish, improve, renovate, furbish [formal]  
3 PHRASAL VERB [usually passive] （常指以可笑或夸张的方式）装饰，打扮 If you say that a person or room is done up in a particular way, you mean they are dressed or decorated in that way, often a way that is rather ridiculous or extreme. （常指以可笑或夸张的方式）装饰，打扮

  • ...Beatrice, usually done up like the fairy on the Christmas tree. [be VERB-ed prep./adv.]

    比阿特丽斯通常打扮得像圣诞树上的精灵一样。

  • She's had her blond hair done up exactly like Jackie's. [have n V-ed P]

    她把她的金发盘得和杰姬一模一样。
