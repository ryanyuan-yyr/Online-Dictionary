attendant
ətendənt əˈtɛndənt
Word forms: plural attendants
1 N-COUNT [usually n NOUN] （加油站、停车场、更衣室等的）服务员，侍者 An attendant is someone whose job is to serve or help people in a place such as a petrol station, a car park, or a cloakroom. （加油站、停车场、更衣室等的）服务员，侍者

  • Tony Williams was working as a car-park attendant in Los Angeles.

    托尼·威廉斯在洛杉矶的一个停车场当服务生。

SYN assistant, guide, guard, servant  
2 ADJ [ADJ n] 伴随的；附带的；相关的 You use attendant to describe something that results from a thing already mentioned or that is connected with it. 伴随的；附带的；相关的

  • Mr Branson's victory, and all the attendant publicity, were well deserved.

    布兰森先生的胜利，以及随之而来的关注度，都是理所应当的。

  • ...the risks attendant on the exploration of the unknown. [+ on/upon]

    探索未知所带来的危险

SYN accompanying, related, associated, accessory
