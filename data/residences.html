residence
rezɪdəns ˈrɛzɪdəns ˈrɛzɪˌdɛns
Word forms: plural residences
1 N-COUNT 住处;居所;住宅 A residence is a house where people live. 住处;居所;住宅 [formal]

  • ...hotels and private residences.

    旅馆和私人住宅

  • She travels constantly, moving among her several residences around the world.

    她经常旅行，穿梭于她在世界各地的多处住所。

2 N-UNCOUNT 住处;居住地 Your place of residence is the place where you live. 住处;居住地 [formal]

  • ...differences among women based on age, place of residence and educational levels.

    不同年龄、居住地和教育程度的女性之间的差异

3 N-UNCOUNT 居住（权）；居留（权） Someone's residence in a particular place is the fact that they live there or that they are officially allowed to live there. 居住（权）；居留（权）

  • They had entered the country and had applied for permanent residence.

    他们已经入境该国，并申请了永久居留权。

  • She moved out of 28 Barbary Lane to take up residence in a house in Pacific Heights.

    她从巴巴里街28号搬出来，住进了太平洋高地住宅区的一所房子。

4 See also: hall of residence 
5 PHRASE 住在;居住 If someone is in residence in a particular place, they are living there. 住在;居住

  • Windsor is open to visitors when the Royal Family is not in residence.

    王室未在温莎城堡居住期间，城堡会对游客开放。

6 PHRASE （作家、艺术家等）常驻的 An artist or writer in residence is one who teaches in an institution such as a university or theatre company. （作家、艺术家等）常驻的

  • Wakoski is writer in residence at Michigan State University.

    瓦科斯基是密歇根州立大学的常驻作家。

  • ...former Chicago Symphony composer in residence, John Corliano.

    约翰·科利亚诺，芝加哥交响乐团的前任常驻作曲家
