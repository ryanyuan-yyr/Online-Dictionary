jumped-up
ˈdʒʌmptˈʌp
ADJ [usually ADJ n] 妄自尊大的；自视甚高的 If you describe someone as jumped-up, you disapprove of them because they consider themselves to be more important than they really are. 妄自尊大的；自视甚高的 [British, informal, disapproval]

  • He's nothing better than a jumped-up bank clerk!

    他只不过是个自以为了不起的银行职员罢了！

SYN conceited, arrogant, pompous, stuck-up [informal]
