titbit
tɪtbɪt ˈtɪtˌbɪt
Word forms: plural titbits
in AM, use 美国英语用 tidbit
1 N-COUNT 趣闻;花絮 You can refer to a small piece of information about someone's private affairs as a titbit, especially when it is interesting and shocking. 趣闻;花絮

  • ...titbits of gossip gleaned from the corridors of power. [+ of]

    从权力中心收集来的趣闻轶事

  • Who passed that titbit on to you?

    是谁告诉你那条花边新闻的？

SYN piece, item, scrap, bit  
2 N-COUNT 少量食物 A titbit is a small piece of food. 少量食物

  • She offered Molly tidbits: a chicken drumstick, some cheese.

    她给了莫莉一些吃的：一根鸡腿下段和一些奶酪。

SYN delicacy, goody, dainty, morsel
