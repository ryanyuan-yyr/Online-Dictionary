dispel
dɪspel dɪˈspɛl dɪˈspɛl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dispels, present participle dispelling, past tense , past participle dispelled
VERB 驱散;消除 To dispel an idea or feeling that people have means to stop them having it. 驱散;消除

  • The President is attempting to dispel the notion that he has neglected the economy. [VERB noun]

    有人认为总统忽视了经济，总统正试图消除这种看法。

SYN drive away, dismiss, eliminate, resolve
