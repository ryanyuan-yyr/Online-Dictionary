acquaintance
əkweɪntəns əˈkweɪntəns
Word forms: plural acquaintances
1 N-COUNT [oft with poss] 认识的人；泛泛之交 An acquaintance is someone who you have met and know slightly, but not well. 认识的人；泛泛之交

  • The proprietor was an old acquaintance of his. [+ of]

    业主是他的一位旧相识。

  • I have a large circle of friends and acquaintances engaged in photography.

    我在摄影界交游甚广。

2 N-VAR [oft poss NOUN, on N] 认识;相识 If you have an acquaintance with someone, you have met them and you know them. 认识;相识

  • ...a writer who becomes involved in a real murder mystery through his acquaintance with a police officer. [+ with]

    因为认识一位警官而参与到一件真实谋杀悬案调查的作家

  • On first acquaintance she is cool and slightly distant.

    初次相见时她表现冷淡并有点爱搭不理。

SYN relationship, association, exchange, knowledge  
3 N-UNCOUNT 了解；熟悉 Your acquaintance with a subject is your knowledge or experience of it. 了解；熟悉 [formal]

  • They had little or no acquaintance with philosophy or history. [+ with]

    他们在哲学或是历史方面几乎一无所知。

4 PHRASE 与（某人）初次相识;结识(某人) When you make someone's acquaintance, you meet them for the first time and get to know them a little. 与（某人）初次相识;结识(某人) [formal]

  • I first made his acquaintance in the early 1960s.

    我与他初次相识是在20世纪60年代早期。

  • I am so pleased to make your acquaintance, Mr Tweed.

    很高兴认识您，特威德先生。

5 PHRASE [n PHRASE] 所认识的 A person of your acquaintance is someone who you have met and know. 所认识的 [formal]

  • ...a highly cultivated woman of our acquaintance.

    我们认识的一位很有修养的女性
