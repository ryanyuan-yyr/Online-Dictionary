overall
oʊvərɔːl oʊvərɔːl ˈoʊvərˌɔl ˌ oʊvərˈɔl
Word forms: plural overalls
The adjective and adverb are pronounced /ˌəʊvər'ɔːl/. The noun is pronounced /'əʊvərɔːl/. 形容词和副词读作 /ˌəʊvər'ɔːl/。名词读作 /'əʊvərɔːl/。
1 ADJ [ADJ n] 总的；总体的；全面的 You use overall to indicate that you are talking about a situation in general or about the whole of something. 总的；总体的；全面的

  • ...the overall rise in unemployment.

    失业率总体的上升

  • Cut down your overall amount of physical activity.

    减少体力活动的总量。

  • It is usually the woman who assumes overall care of the baby.

    通常由女性负责照顾孩子的方方面面。

SYN total, full, whole, general  
Overall is also an adverb.

  • Overall, I like Connie. I think she's great.

    总的说来，我喜欢康妮，我认为她人挺不错。

  • Overall I was disappointed.

    总的说来，我很失望。

  • The college has few ways to assess the quality of education overall.

    总体看来，这所大学评估教育质量的方法寥寥无几。

2 N-PLURAL [oft a pair of NOUN] （上下连身的）工作服，防护服 Overalls consist of a single piece of clothing that combines trousers and a jacket. You wear overalls over your clothes in order to protect them while you are working. （上下连身的）工作服，防护服

  • ...workers in blue overalls.

    穿着蓝色连身工作服的工人

3 N-PLURAL [oft a pair of NOUN] （吊带）工装裤 Overalls are trousers that are attached to a piece of cloth which covers your chest and which has straps going over your shoulders. （吊带）工装裤 [US]

  • An elderly man dressed in faded overalls took the witness stand.

    一位穿着褪色吊带工装裤的老人上了证人席。

in BRIT, use 英国英语用 dungarees
4 N-COUNT （工作时穿的）罩衣 An overall is a piece of clothing shaped like a coat that you wear over your clothes in order to protect them while you are working. （工作时穿的）罩衣 [British]
