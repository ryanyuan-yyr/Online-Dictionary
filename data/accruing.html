accrue
əkruː əˈkru
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense accrues, present participle accruing, past tense , past participle accrued
1 V-ERG （使）（钱或利息）逐渐增加 If money or interest accrues or if you accrue it, it gradually increases in amount over a period of time. （使）（钱或利息）逐渐增加 [business]

  • I owed £5,000 — part of this was accrued interest. [VERB-ed]

    我欠款5,000英镑，其中一部分是累计的利息。

  • If you do not pay within 28 days, interest will accrue. [VERB]

    如果28天之内不付款，就会自然生息。

  • Officials say the options will offer investors a longer time in which to accrue profits. [VERB noun]

    官员说这些期权会给投资者提供更长的利润积累期。

2 V-ERG （利润、好处等）累积，积聚，增加 If things such as profits or benefits accrue to someone, they are added to over a period of time. （利润、好处等）累积，积聚，增加 [formal] [Also V n, V to n]

  • ...the expectation that profits will accrue. [VERB]

    希望利润会增加的期待

  • ...a project from which considerable benefit will accrue to the community. [VERB + to]

    使社区持续受益的项目

  • In many cases, the fee structure alone will exceed the tax benefits accrued. [VERB-ed]

    很多情况下，单是费用结构一项就会超过累积的税惠。
