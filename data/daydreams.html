daydream
deɪdriːm ˈdeɪˌdrim
(also day-dream)Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense daydreams, present participle daydreaming, past tense , past participle daydreamed
1 VERB 做白日梦;幻想;空想 If you daydream, you think about pleasant things for a period of time, usually about things that you would like to happen. 做白日梦;幻想;空想

  • Do you work hard for success rather than daydream about it?. [VERB + about]

    你是否付出艰苦努力以取得成功，而不是只在那里做白日梦？

  • He daydreams of being a famous journalist. [VERB + of]

    他幻想着自己成为著名记者。

  • I am inclined to daydream. [VERB]

    我喜欢空想。

SYN fantasize, dream, imagine, envision  
2 N-COUNT 白日梦;幻想;空想 A daydream is a series of pleasant thoughts, usually about things that you would like to happen. 白日梦;幻想;空想

  • He escaped into daydreams of beautiful women.

    他躲进白日梦中幻想美女。

  • The idea for the story came to him in a daydream.

    这个故事的念头是他做白日梦的时候突然想到的。

SYN fantasy, dream, imagining, fancy
