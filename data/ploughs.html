plough
plaʊ plaʊ plaʊ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense ploughs, present participle ploughing, past tense , past participle ploughed
in AM, use 美国英语用 plow
1 N-COUNT 犁 A plough is a large farming tool with sharp blades which is pulled across the soil to turn it over, usually before seeds are planted. 犁
2See also:snowplough 
3 VERB 犁（地）；耕（地） When someone ploughs an area of land, they turn over the soil using a plough. 犁（地）；耕（地）

  • They ploughed nearly 100,000 acres of virgin moorland. [VERB noun]

    他们犁了将近10万英亩未开垦的高沼地。

  • ...a carefully ploughed field. [VERB-ed]

    仔细犁过的田地

SYN turn over, dig, till, ridge  
ploughing

  • In Roman times November was a month of hard work in ploughing and sowing.

    在古罗马时期，11月是辛勤耕耘和播种的月份。

4 PHRASE [verb-link PHRASE, PHRASE after verb] （土地）被开垦成耕地 If an area of land is under the plough, it is used for growing crops. If land is brought or put under the plough, it is ploughed for the first time and is then used for growing crops. （土地）被开垦成耕地

  • There was not one inch of soil that was not under the plough.

    没有一寸土地未被开垦成耕地。

  • ...as we put more and more wilderness under the plough.

    随着我们将越来越多的荒地开垦成耕地

5 to plough a furrow→see: furrow
