duly
djuːli duː- ˈduli ˈdjuli
1 ADV [ADVERB before verb] 正如预期地;正如要求地 If you say that something duly happened or was done, you mean that it was expected to happen or was requested, and it did happen or it was done. 正如预期地;正如要求地

  • It was the beginning of the end and Watson duly went on to win his fourth Open Golf Championship.

    这是一场胜负已定的比赛，沃森不负众望赢得了他第四次高尔夫公开赛的冠军。

  • Westcott appealed to Waite for an apology, which he duly received.

    韦斯科特要求韦特道歉,且如愿以偿。

2 ADV [ADVERB before verb] 适当地;恰当地 If something is duly done, it is done in the correct way. 适当地;恰当地 [formal]

  • ...the duly elected president of the country.

    正式当选的国家总统
