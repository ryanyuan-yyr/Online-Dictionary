robe
roʊb roʊb
Word forms: plural robes
1 N-COUNT 长袍;罩袍 A robe is a loose piece of clothing which covers all of your body and reaches the ground. You can describe someone as wearing a robe or as wearing robes . 长袍;罩袍 [formal]

  • Pope John Paul II knelt in his white robes before the simple altar.

    教皇约翰·保罗二世身着白色长袍在简朴的圣坛前面跪下来。

  • ...a fur-lined robe of green silk.

    毛皮衬里的绿色丝绸长袍

SYN gown, cape, costume, cloak  
2 N-COUNT 浴袍;睡袍 A robe is a piece of clothing, usually made of towelling, which people wear in the house, especially when they have just got up or had a bath. 浴袍;睡袍

  • Ryle put on a robe and went down to the kitchen.

    赖尔穿上睡袍，下楼去了厨房。

SYN dressing gown, wrapper, bathrobe, negligée
