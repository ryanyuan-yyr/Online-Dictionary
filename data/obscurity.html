obscurity
ɒbskjʊərɪti əbˈskjʊrəti ɑbˈskjʊrəti
Word forms: plural obscurities
1 N-UNCOUNT 无名；默默无闻 Obscurity is the state of being known by only a few people. 无名；默默无闻

  • For the lucky few, there's the chance of being plucked from obscurity and thrown into the glamorous world of modelling.

    只有极少数幸运儿才有机会从籍籍无名中脱颖而出，进入光鲜亮丽的模特圈。

  • The latter half of his life was spent in obscurity and loneliness.

    他在默默无闻中孤独地度过了后半生。

SYN insignificance, oblivion, unimportance, non-recognition  
2 N-VAR 晦涩；费解；难懂的事 Obscurity is the quality of being difficult to understand. An obscurity is something that is difficult to understand. 晦涩；费解；难懂的事

  • 'How can that be?' asked Hunt, irritated by the obscurity of Henry's reply. [+ of]

    “怎么会那样呢？”亨特问道，亨利含糊费解的回答让他恼火。

  • Whatever its obscurities, the poem was clear on at least one count.

    这首诗再怎么晦涩，至少有一点是清楚的。

SYN enigma, mystery, puzzle, problem
