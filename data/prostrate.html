prostrate
prɒstreɪt prɒstreɪt prɑːstreɪt ˈprɑsˌtreɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense prostrates, present participle prostrating, past tense , past participle prostrated
The verb is pronounced /prɒ'streɪt, am 'prɑːstreɪt/. The adjective is pronounced /'prɒstreɪt/. 动词读作 /prɒ'streɪt, 美 'prɑːstreɪt/。形容词读作 /'prɒstreɪt/。
1 VERB 使（自己）俯伏；使（自己）拜倒 If you prostrate yourself, you lie down flat on the ground, on your front, usually to show respect for God or a person in authority. 使（自己）俯伏；使（自己）拜倒

  • They prostrated themselves before their king. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    他们向国王跪拜行礼。

2 ADJ [ADJ after verb] 俯卧的；平卧的 If you are lying prostrate, you are lying flat on the ground, on your front. 俯卧的；平卧的

  • Percy was lying prostrate, his arms outstretched and his eyes closed.

    珀西闭着眼四仰八叉地平躺着。

SYN prone, fallen, flat, horizontal  
3 ADJ 衰竭的；沮丧的；一蹶不振的 If someone is prostrate, they are so distressed or affected by a very bad experience that they are unable to do anything at all. 衰竭的；沮丧的；一蹶不振的 [formal]

  • Immediately after my father's death my stepmother was prostrate.

    我父亲死后，我的继母一下就垮了。

  • I was prostrate with grief.

    我当时痛不欲生。

  • ...his country's prostrate economy.

    他的国家一蹶不振的经济

SYN exhausted, overcome, depressed, drained
