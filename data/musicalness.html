musical
mjuːzɪkəl ˈmjuzɪkəl
Word forms: plural musicals
1 ADJ [ADJ n] 音乐的;与音乐有关的 You use musical to indicate that something is connected with playing or studying music. 音乐的;与音乐有关的

  • We have a wealth of musical talent in this region.

    在这一地区有大量的音乐人才。

  • Stan Getz's musical career spanned five decades.

    斯坦·盖兹的音乐生涯跨越50个春秋。

  • London's musical life might become as exciting as Berlin's.

    伦敦的音乐生活可能会变得像柏林的一样令人兴奋。

musically

  • Musically there is a lot to enjoy.

    从音乐角度看， 有很多可以欣赏的东西。

  • ...trying to communicate verbally what he can only communicate musically.

    尝试用语言表达他只能用音乐表达的内容

2 N-COUNT 音乐剧;音乐歌舞片 A musical is a play or film that uses singing and dancing in the story. 音乐剧;音乐歌舞片

  • ...London's smash hit musical Miss Saigon.

    在伦敦轰动一时的音乐剧《西贡小姐》

3 ADJ-GRADED 有音乐天赋的;喜爱音乐的 Someone who is musical has a natural ability and interest in music. 有音乐天赋的;喜爱音乐的

  • I came from a musical family.

    我出身于音乐世家。

musicality

  • ...a people of extraordinary musicality.

    有非凡音乐天赋的民族

4 ADJ-GRADED 好听的;悦耳的 Sounds that are musical are light and pleasant to hear. 好听的;悦耳的

  • He had a soft, almost musical voice.

    他声音柔和，如音乐般动听。

SYN melodious, lyrical, harmonious, melodic  
musically

  • The voice was as musically soft as ever.

    那声音一如既往地轻柔悦耳。
