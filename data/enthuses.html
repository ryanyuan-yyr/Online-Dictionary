enthuse
ɪnθjuːz ɪnˈθjuːz -θuːz ɛnˈθuz ɛnˈθjuz ɪnˈθuz ɪnˈθjuz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense enthuses, present participle enthusing, past tense , past participle enthused
1 VERB 兴奋地说;津津乐道地讲述 If you enthuse about something, you talk about it in a way that shows how excited you are about it. 兴奋地说;津津乐道地讲述 [Also VERB that]

  • Elizabeth David enthuses about the taste, fragrance and character of Provencal cuisine. [V + about/over]

    伊丽莎白·戴维对普罗旺斯菜肴的色香味津津乐道。

  • 'I've found the most wonderful house to buy!' she enthused. [VERB with quote]

    “我已经找到想买的最棒的房子啦！”她兴奋地说。

2 VERB 使兴奋;使有热情 If you are enthused by something, it makes you feel excited and enthusiastic. 使兴奋;使有热情 [Also VERB]

  • I was immediately enthused. [be VERB-ed]

    我立即兴奋起来。

  • Conference participants were clearly enthused by their presence, and the two women responded by listening intently. [be VERB-ed]

    她们的出席令与会者明显兴奋起来，作为回应这两位女士也专心倾听起来。

  • Find a hobby or interest which enthuses you. [VERB noun]

    培养一个自己喜欢的爱好或兴趣。

SYN excite, inspire, stir, stimulate
