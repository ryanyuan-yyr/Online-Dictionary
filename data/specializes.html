specialize
speʃəlaɪz ˈspɛʃəlˌaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense specializes, present participle specializing, past tense , past participle specialized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 specialise
VERB 专门从事;专门经营;专门研究;专攻 If you specialize in a thing, you know a lot about it and concentrate a great deal of your time and energy on it, especially in your work or when you are studying or training. You also use specialize to talk about a restaurant which concentrates on a particular type of food. 专门从事;专门经营;专门研究;专攻

  • ...a University professor who specializes in the history of the Russian empire. [VERB + in]

    专门研究俄罗斯帝国历史的大学教授

  • ...a Portuguese restaurant which specializes in seafood. [VERB + in]

    主打海味的葡萄牙餐馆
