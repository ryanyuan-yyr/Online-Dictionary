quotation
kwoʊteɪʃən kwoʊˈteɪʃən
Word forms: plural quotations
1 N-COUNT 引文;引语;语录 A quotation is a sentence or phrase taken from a book, poem, or play, which is repeated by someone else. 引文;引语;语录

  • He illustrated his argument with quotations from Pasternak. [+ from]

    他引用帕斯捷尔纳克的话来证明自己的论点。

  • The second quotation is from an essay that D H Lawrence wrote in the nineteen-twenties.

    第二段引文出自D. H. 劳伦斯20世纪20年代写的一篇文章。

SYN passage, quote [informal] , excerpt, cutting  
2 N-COUNT 报价;开价 When someone gives you a quotation, they tell you how much they will charge to do a particular piece of work. 报价;开价

  • Get several written quotations and check exactly what's included in the cost.

    要几份书面报价，仔细查对费用中包含的内容。

SYN estimate, price, tender, rate  
3 N-COUNT (股票的)挂牌 A company's quotation on the stock exchange is its registration on the stock exchange, which enables its shares to be officially listed and traded. (股票的)挂牌 [business]

  • ...an American-dominated investment manager with a quotation on the London stock market. [+ on]

    在伦敦股票交易市场挂牌的由美国人主导的投资管理机构
