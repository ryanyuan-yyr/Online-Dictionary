smuggle
smʌgəl ˈsmʌgəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense smuggles, present participle smuggling, past tense , past participle smuggled
VERB 走私;私运;偷运 If someone smuggles things or people into a place or out of it, they take them there illegally or secretly. 走私;私运;偷运

  • My message is 'If you try to smuggle drugs you are stupid'. [VERB noun]

    我的意思是“你要是企图走私毒品，你就是一个笨蛋”。

  • Police have foiled an attempt to smuggle a bomb into Belfast airport. [VERB noun prep.]

    警方挫败了一场企图将炸弹偷偷带入贝尔法斯特机场的阴谋。

  • Had it really been impossible to find someone who could smuggle out a letter?. [VERB noun with adv.]

    真的找不到一个能把信偷偷地带出去的人吗？

  • Everything along the border has its price: drugs, teak, smuggled goods. [VERB-ed]

    边境上所有的东西都有定价，毒品、柚木和走私品无一例外。

SYN sneak, spirit, slip, bring illegally
