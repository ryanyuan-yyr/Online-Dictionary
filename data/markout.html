mark out
1 PHRASAL VERB 划界;画线分出 To mark out an area or shape means to show where it begins and ends. 划界;画线分出 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • When planting seedlings I prefer to mark out the rows in advance. [VERB PREP. noun]

    种树苗的时候，我喜欢事先划出行来。

SYN delineate, outline  
2 PHRASAL VERB 使具有…特色;使…与众不同 If a particular quality or feature marks someone or something out, it makes them obviously different from other people or things. 使具有…特色;使…与众不同 [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • There were several things about that evening that marked it out as very unusual. [V n P + as]

    那晚因几件事情显得非同寻常。

  • It does not appear to possess any of the obvious signs that would mark it out as a restaurant. [V n P as adj/n]

    它似乎没有一处明显的标志让它看上去像一家饭店。

  • Her independence of spirit marked her out from her male fellow officers. [V n P + from]

    她的独立精神使她有别于共事的男性军官。
