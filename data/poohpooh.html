pooh-pooh
puːpuː ˈpuːˈpuː ˈpuˈpu
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense pooh-poohs, present participle pooh-poohing, past tense , past participle pooh-poohed
VERB 轻视；蔑视；贬低 If someone pooh-poohs an idea or suggestion, they say or imply that it is foolish, impractical, or unnecessary. 轻视；蔑视；贬低

  • In the past he has pooh-poohed suggestions that he might succeed Isaacs. [VERB noun]

    过去他对自己会接替艾萨克斯之说嗤之以鼻。

SYN scorn, dismiss, slight, disregard
