allow
əlaʊ əˈlaʊ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense allows, present participle allowing, past tense , past participle allowed
1 VERB 允许；容许；准许 If someone is allowed to do something, it is all right for them to do it and they will not get into trouble. 允许；容许；准许 [Also V n/v-ing]

  • The children are not allowed to watch violent TV programmes. [be VERB-ed to-infinitive]

    儿童不准收看含暴力内容的电视节目。

  • The Government will allow them to advertise on radio and television. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    政府将准许他们在广播和电视上做广告。

  • They will be allowed home. [be VERB-ed adv./prep.]

    他们将获准回家。

  • Smoking will not be allowed. [be VERB-ed]

    吸烟将被禁止。

SYN permit, approve, enable, sanction  
2 VERB 向…提供;准许得到 If you are allowed something, you are given permission to have it or are given it. 向…提供;准许得到

  • Gifts like chocolates or flowers are allowed. [be VERB-ed]

    巧克力、鲜花等礼物在允许之列。

  • He should be allowed the occasional treat. [be VERB-ed noun]

    应该准许他偶尔也轻松一下。

3 VERB 听任;任凭 If you allow something to happen, you do not prevent it. 听任;任凭

  • He won't allow himself to fail. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    他不会任由自己失败的。

  • If the soil is allowed to dry out the tree could die. [be VERB-ed to-infinitive]

    如果听任土壤变干，树木可能会枯死。

4 VERB 使能够；促使 If one thing allows another thing to happen, the first thing creates the opportunity for the second thing to happen. 使能够；促使

  • The compromise will allow him to continue his free market reforms. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    妥协将使他得以继续他的自由市场改革。

  • ...an attempt to allow the Moslem majority a greater share of power. [VERB noun noun]

    使穆斯林多数派拥有更大权力的尝试

  • She said this would allow more effective planning. [VERB noun]

    她说这会为更有效的规划创造可能。

5 VERB 酌留;留出;酌加 If you allow a particular length of time or a particular amount of something for a particular purpose, you include it in your planning. 酌留;留出;酌加

  • Please allow 28 days for delivery. [VERB noun + for]

    请留出28天的送货时间。

  • Allow about 75ml (3fl oz) per six servings. [VERB noun]

    每6份酌加约75毫升(3液盎司)。

SYN give, provide, grant, spare  
6 VERB 承认;同意 If you allow that something is true, you admit or agree that it is true. 承认;同意 [formal]

  • Warren also allows that capitalist development may, in its early stages, result in increased social inequality. [VERB that]

    沃伦也承认资本主义发展初级阶段可能会导致社会不平等现象加剧。

SYN acknowledge, accept, admit, grant  
7 PHRASE 请允许我（用以礼貌地表示为某人提供服务） Some people say 'Allow me' as a polite way of offering to do something for someone. 请允许我（用以礼貌地表示为某人提供服务） [formal, politeness]

  • Allow me to buy you a drink at the bar.

    让我来请你喝一杯。

8 PHRASE 请允许我（用以引出要说的话） Some people use Allow me to... as a way of introducing something that they want to say or do. 请允许我（用以引出要说的话） [formal]

  • Allow me to introduce Dr Amberg.

    请允许我介绍安伯格博士。
