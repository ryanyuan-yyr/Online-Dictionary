cream off
1 PHRASAL VERB 选取(精英);提取(精华) To cream off part of a group of people means to take them away and treat them in a special way, because they are better than the others. 选取(精英);提取(精华) [disapproval]

  • The private schools cream off many of the best pupils. [VERB PREP. noun]

    私立学校招走了很多优秀的学生。

2 PHRASAL VERB 取走(一大笔钱) If a person or organization creams off a large amount of money, they take it and use it for themselves. 取走(一大笔钱) [informal, disapproval]

  • This means smaller banks can cream off big profits during lending booms. [VERB PREP. noun]

    这意味着在贷款业务剧增时小银行能够获得大笔利润。

  • Funds raised through selling these magazines are creamed off to support armed violence. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    通过出售这些杂志筹集到的资金现在被用来支持武装暴乱。
