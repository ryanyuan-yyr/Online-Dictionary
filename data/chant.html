chant
tʃɑːnt tʃænt tʃænt tʃɑnt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense chants, present participle chanting, past tense , past participle chanted
1 N-COUNT 不断重复的话 A chant is a word or group of words that is repeated over and over again. 不断重复的话

  • He was greeted by the chant of 'Judas! Judas!'. [+ of]

    人们看见他就反复不停地喊：“犹大！犹大！”

2 N-COUNT [usually adjective NOUN] (反复吟诵的)圣歌，祷文 A chant is a religious song or prayer that is sung on only a few notes. (反复吟诵的)圣歌，祷文

  • ...a Gregorian chant.

    格列高利圣歌

  • ...a Buddhist chant.

    佛经诵念

3 VERB 反复呼喊;不停地重复 If you chant something or if you chant, you repeat the same words over and over again. 反复呼喊;不停地重复 [Also VERB that]

  • Demonstrators chanted slogans. [VERB noun]

    示威者们反复喊着口号。

  • The crowd chanted 'We are with you.'. [VERB with quote]

    人群一再呼喊：“我们支持你。”

  • Several thousand people chanted and demonstrated outside the building. [VERB]

    好几千人在大楼外喊着口号示威。

chanting

  • A lot of the chanting was in support of the deputy Prime Minister.

    许多口号是支持副总理的。

4 VERB 吟诵(经文);念(经) If you chant or if you chant something, you sing a religious song or prayer. 吟诵(经文);念(经)

  • Muslims chanted and prayed. [VERB]

    穆斯林们诵经、祈祷。

  • Mr Sharma lit incense and chanted Sanskrit mantras. [VERB noun]

    夏尔马先生点上香，念起梵文咒语。

SYN sing, chorus, recite, intone  
chanting

  • The chanting inside the temple stopped.

    寺庙内的诵经声停了。
