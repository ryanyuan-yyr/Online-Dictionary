twinge
twɪndʒ twɪndʒ
Word forms: plural twinges
1 N-COUNT (通常指不快的)一阵强烈情感 A twinge is a sudden sharp feeling or emotion, usually an unpleasant one. (通常指不快的)一阵强烈情感

  • I would have twinges of guilt occasionally…

    我时而会感到阵阵内疚。

  • For a moment, Arnold felt a twinge of sympathy for Mr Wilson. [+ of]

    有一瞬间，阿诺德对威尔逊先生心生同情。

SYN pang, twitch, tweak, throe [rare]  
2 N-COUNT (一阵)剧痛，刺痛 A twinge is a sudden sharp pain. (一阵)剧痛，刺痛

  • He felt a slight twinge in his damaged hamstring. [+ in]

    他受伤的腘绳肌感到一阵轻微的刺痛。

  • …the occasional twinge of indigestion.

    消化不良引起的偶发性剧痛

SYN pain, sharp pain, gripe, stab
