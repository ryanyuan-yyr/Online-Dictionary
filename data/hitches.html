hitch
hɪtʃ hɪtʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense hitches, present participle hitching, past tense , past participle hitched
1 N-COUNT （短暂的）小故障，小障碍 A hitch is a slight problem or difficulty which causes a short delay. （短暂的）小故障，小障碍

  • After some technical hitches the show finally got under way.

    一些小技术故障解决后，演出终于开始了。

  • The five-hour operation went without a hitch.

    5小时的手术进行得非常顺利。

SYN problem, catch [informal] , trouble, check  
2 VERB 搭便车（旅行）；搭顺风车 If you hitch ,hitch a lift, or hitch a ride, you hitchhike. 搭便车（旅行）；搭顺风车 [informal]

  • There was no garage in sight, so I hitched a lift into town. [VERB noun]

    放眼望去看不到修车厂，所以我就搭顺风车进了城。

  • Jean-Phillippe had hitched all over Europe in the 1960s. [VERB]

    让－菲利普在20世纪60年代曾搭便车游遍欧洲。

SYN hitchhike, thumb a lift  
3 VERB 把…钩（或拴、系）在…上 If you hitch something to something else, you hook it or fasten it there. 把…钩（或拴、系）在…上

  • Last night we hitched the horse to the cart and moved here. [V n + onto/to]

    昨晚我们套上马车，拉着东西搬到了这里。

SYN fasten, join, attach, unite  
4 PHRASE 结婚 If you get hitched, you get married. 结婚 [informal]

  • The report shows that fewer couples are getting hitched.

    报告显示，结婚的人数呈下降趋势。

SYN marry, get married, tie the knot [informal]

hitch up
