wheedle
hwiːdəl ˈhwidəl ˈwidəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense wheedles, present participle wheedling, past tense , past participle wheedled
VERB 哄骗;劝诱 If you say that someone wheedles, you mean that they try to persuade someone to do or give them what they want, for example by saying nice things that they do not mean. 哄骗;劝诱 [disapproval] [Also VERB n]

  • Cross decided to beg and wheedle a bit. [VERB]

    克罗斯决定用上点乞求和哄骗。

  • He managed to wheedle his way into the offices.

    他连哄带骗地设法进了办公楼。

  • ...an opportunity to wheedle more money out of Wilson. [V n + out of/from]

    从威尔森先生那里骗取更多钱的机会

  • 'Why don't you try to read on past page 21,' I wheedled and sat down again. [VERB with quote]

    “为什么你不试着读一下前面第21页呢？”，我一边哄劝一边又坐下。

SYN coax, talk, court, draw
