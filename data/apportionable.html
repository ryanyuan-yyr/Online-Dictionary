apportion
əpɔːʳʃən əˈpɔrʃən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense apportions, present participle apportioning, past tense apportioned
VERB 分摊；分派 When you apportion something such as blame, you decide how much of it different people deserve or should be given. 分摊；分派 [formal] [Also VERB n]

  • The experts are even-handed in apportioning blame among EU governments.

    专家们在明确对欧盟各国政府所应追究的责任时十分公正。

  • The allowable deduction is apportioned between the estate and the beneficiaries. [VERB noun prep.]

    减免的税额在地产和受益人之间分摊。

SYN divide, share, deal, distribute  
apportionment

  • ...the apportionment of resources.

    对资源的分配
