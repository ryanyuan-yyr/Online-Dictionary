waltz
wɔːlts wɔːls wɔlts
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense waltzes, present participle waltzing, past tense , past participle waltzed
1 N-COUNT; N-IN-NAMES [oft in names] 华尔兹舞曲;圆舞曲 A waltz is a piece of music with a rhythm of three beats in each bar, which people can dance to. 华尔兹舞曲;圆舞曲

  • ...Tchaikovsky's 'Waltz of the Flowers'.

    柴可夫斯基的《花之圆舞曲》

2 N-COUNT 华尔兹舞 A waltz is a dance in which two people hold each other and move around the floor doing special steps in time to waltz music. 华尔兹舞

  • Arthur Murray taught the foxtrot, the tango and the waltz.

    阿瑟·默里教狐步舞、探戈和华尔兹。

3 V-RECIP 跳华尔兹舞 If you waltz with someone, you dance a waltz with them. 跳华尔兹舞 [Also VERB]

  • 'Waltz with me,' he said, taking her hand. [VERB + with]

    “来和我跳支华尔兹吧，”他边说边牵起她的手。

  • Couples are waltzing round the wooden floor. [V + around]

    人们双双对对在木地板上跳着华尔兹舞。

  • She seized her mother round the waist and waltzed her round the kitchen. [V n prep (non-recip)]

    她搂着母亲的腰，在厨房里跳起了华尔兹舞。

4 VERB (像跳华尔兹舞一样)轻松自如地进行，游刃有余地做 If you say that someone waltzes somewhere, you mean that they do something in a relaxed and confident way. (像跳华尔兹舞一样)轻松自如地进行，游刃有余地做 [informal]

  • She's probably got herself a new man and gone waltzing off with him. [VERB adv./prep.]

    她极有可能有了新男友，和他的感情发展得一日千里。

  • My cousin Henry, he waltzes in a few months later at three times the salary. [VERB adv./prep.]

    我堂弟亨利几个月之后入职时轻而易举地拿到了3倍的薪水。
