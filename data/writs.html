writ
rɪt rɪt
Word forms: plural writs
1 N-COUNT 令状;法院命令;传票 A writ is a legal document that orders a person to do a particular thing. 令状;法院命令;传票 [Also + for]

  • He issued a writ against one of his accusers. [+ against]

    他向其中一个指控他的人发了传票。

SYN summons, document, decree, indictment  
2 PHRASE [verb-link PHRASE, n PHRASE, usu PHR in/on n] [verb-link PHRASE, n PHRASE, usu PHR in/on n] 明显的;显而易见的 If you say that something is writ large, you mean that it is very obvious. 明显的;显而易见的 [literary] [literary]

  • They now have to cope with the legacy of their past incompetence writ large on their balance sheets.

    他们现在不得不处理由于过去工作不力所遗留下的问题,这在资产负债表上一看便知。

3 PHRASE 扩大了的;夸大了的 If you say that one thing is another thing writ large, you mean that the first thing is a larger or more exaggerated version of the second thing. 扩大了的;夸大了的

  • Her life was her personality writ large.

    她的生活是她性格放大了的写照。
