peep
piːp pip
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense peeps, present participle peeping, past tense , past participle peeped
1 VERB 瞥；偷看；窥视 If you peep, or peep at something, you have a quick look at it, often secretly and quietly. 瞥；偷看；窥视

  • Children came to peep at him round the doorway. [VERB + at]

    孩子们围在门口偷看他。

  • Now and then she peeped to see if he was noticing her. [VERB]

    她时不时地偷眼看他是否在注意自己。

SYN peek, look, peer, spy  
Peep is also a noun.

  • 'Fourteen minutes,' Chris said, taking a peep at his watch.

    “14分钟，”克里斯瞥了一眼他的手表说道。

2 VERB （从隐蔽处）隐现，微微露出 If something peeps out from behind or under something, a small part of it is visible or becomes visible. （从隐蔽处）隐现，微微露出

  • Purple and yellow flowers peeped up between rocks. [VERB prep./adv.]

    岩石间隐约可见紫色和黄色的花儿。

  • Here and there a face peeped out from the shop doorway. [VERB prep./adv.]

    商店门口从不同方向露出一张脸。

SYN appear briefly, emerge, pop up, spring up  
3 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 没听到任何话（或动静） If you say that you don't hear a peep from someone, you mean that they do not say anything or make any noise. 没听到任何话（或动静） [informal]

  • You don't hear a peep from her once she's gone to bed.

    她一睡觉，就不会发出一点儿动静。
