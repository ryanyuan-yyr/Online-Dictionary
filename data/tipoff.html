tip off
PHRASAL VERB 给…通风报信;暗中告知 If someone tips you off, they give you information about something that has happened or is going to happen. 给…通风报信;暗中告知

  • Greg tipped police off on his car phone about a suspect drunk driver. [VERB noun PREP.]

    格雷格用他的汽车电话向警方报告了一个有酒后驾驶嫌疑的司机。

  • He was arrested two days later after a friend tipped off the FBI. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他的一个朋友给美国联邦调查局报了信两天以后他就被捕了。

tip-off
ˈtɪpˌɔf
Word forms: plural tip-offs
N-COUNT 密报;密告 A tip-off is a piece of information or a warning that you give to someone, often privately or secretly. 密报;密告

  • The man was arrested at his home after a tip-off to police from a member of the public.

    在一名群众向警方举报以后，那人就在家里被逮捕了。

SYN hint, word, information, warning
