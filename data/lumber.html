lumber
lʌmbəʳ ˈlʌmbər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense lumbers, present participle lumbering, past tense , past participle lumbered
1 N-UNCOUNT 木材；方材 Lumber consists of trees and large pieces of wood that have been roughly cut up. 木材；方材 [mainly US]

  • It was made of soft lumber, spruce by the look of it.

    其制作材料是软木，看起来像是云杉。

  • He was going to have to purchase all his lumber at full retail price.

    他将不得不把所有木材按零售价分文不少地买下来。

SYN junk, refuse, rubbish, discards  
2 VERB （缓慢而笨拙地）挪动，移动 If someone or something lumbers from one place to another, they move there very slowly and clumsily. （缓慢而笨拙地）挪动，移动

  • He turned and lumbered back to his chair. [VERB adv./prep.]

    他转过身来，蹒跚着走回到椅子边坐下。

  • The truck lumbered across the parking lot toward the road. [VERB adv./prep.]

    卡车隆隆地驶过停车场准备上路。

  • He looked straight ahead and overtook a lumbering lorry. [VERB-ing]

    他眼睛直视前方，加速超过了前面一辆慢吞吞行进的大卡车。

SYN plod, shuffle, shamble, trudge
