inhabit
ɪnhæbɪt ɪnˈhæbɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense inhabits, present participle inhabiting, past tense , past participle inhabited
VERB 居住于;栖居在 If a place or region is inhabited by a group of people or a species of animal, those people or animals live there. 居住于;栖居在

  • The valley is inhabited by the Dani tribe. [be VERB-ed]

    山谷里居住着达尼部落。

  • ...the people who inhabit these islands. [VERB noun]

    居住在这些岛上的人们

  • ...the beautifully coloured fish that inhabit the Egyptian reefs. [VERB noun]

    栖息在埃及暗礁间的美丽彩色鱼类

  • ...a land primarily inhabited by nomads. [VERB-ed]

    主要居住着游牧民族的地区

SYN live in, people, occupy, populate
