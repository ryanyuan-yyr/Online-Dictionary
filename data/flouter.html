flout
flaʊt flaʊt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense flouts, present participle flouting, past tense , past participle flouted
VERB 无视，藐视，轻视(法律等) If you flout something such as a law, an order, or an accepted way of behaving, you deliberately do not obey it or follow it. 无视，藐视，轻视(法律等)

  • ...illegal campers who persist in flouting the law. [VERB noun]

    一向对法律熟视无睹的非法露营者

  • Building regulations have been habitually flouted. [VERB noun]

    建筑法规一向都无人遵守。

SYN defy, scorn, spurn, scoff at
