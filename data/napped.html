nap
næp næp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense naps, present participle napping, past tense , past participle napped
1 N-COUNT (尤指在白天的)打盹，小睡 If you have a nap, you have a short sleep, usually during the day. (尤指在白天的)打盹，小睡

  • Use your lunch hour to have a nap in your chair.

    利用午饭时间坐在椅子上打个盹吧。

  • I might take a little nap.

    我可能会打个盹。

SYN sleep, rest, kip [British , slang] , siesta  
2 VERB (尤指在白天)小睡，打盹 If you nap, you sleep for a short period of time, usually during the day. (尤指在白天)小睡，打盹

  • An elderly person may nap during the day and then sleep only five hours a night. [VERB]

    老年人白天会小睡一会儿，然后晚上只睡5个小时。

SYN sleep, rest, nod, drop off [informal]  
3 N-SING (地毯或天鹅绒等表面的)绒毛 The nap of a carpet or of a cloth such as velvet is the top layer of short threads, which usually lie smoothly in one direction. (地毯或天鹅绒等表面的)绒毛
4 PHRASE 被弄得措手不及;猝不及防 If someone is caught napping, something happens when they are not prepared for it, although they should have been. 被弄得措手不及;猝不及防 [informal]

  • The security services were clearly caught napping.

    保安工作明显有漏洞。
