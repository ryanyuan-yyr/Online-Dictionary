screech
skriːtʃ skritʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense screeches, present participle screeching, past tense , past participle screeched
1 VERB (车辆、汽车轮胎)发出刺耳声 If a vehicle screeches somewhere or if its tyres screech, its tyres make an unpleasant high-pitched noise on the road. (车辆、汽车轮胎)发出刺耳声

  • A black Mercedes screeched to a halt beside the helicopter. [VERB prep./adv.]

    一辆黑色的梅赛德斯轿车在直升机旁嘎的一声刹住了。

  • The car wheels screeched as they curved and bounced over the rough broken ground. [VERB]

    车轮在坑坑洼洼的路面上拐弯和颠簸时发出尖锐刺耳的声音。

2 VERB 尖声喊出 When you screech something, you shout it in a loud, unpleasant, high-pitched voice. 尖声喊出 [Also V, V n]

  • 'Get me some water, Jeremy!' I screeched. [VERB with quote]

    “给我来点水，杰里米！”我尖声喊道。

  • ...a player who screeches at you on the field. [VERB + at]

    一名在赛场上向你尖声喊叫的运动员

Screech is also a noun.

  • The figure gave a screech.

    那个身影发出一声尖叫。

3 VERB (鸟、动物等)尖叫，尖鸣，发出刺耳声 When a bird, animal, or thing screeches, it makes a loud, unpleasant, high-pitched noise. (鸟、动物等)尖叫，尖鸣，发出刺耳声

  • A macaw screeched at him from its perch. [VERB + at]

    一只金刚鹦鹉站在栖木上冲他尖叫。

  • ...tropical birds screeching in an aviary. [VERB]

    在鸟舍中尖声鸣叫的热带鸟

  • The air-conditioning screeched all the time. [VERB]

    空调一直在发出刺耳的声音。

  • Bronka is somewhat infamous for his screeching electric guitar work. [VERB-ing]

    布朗卡因为弹出刺耳的电吉他声而有些让人讨厌。

SYN squawk  
Screech is also a noun.

  • He heard the screech of brakes.

    他听到刺耳的刹车声。
