sputter
spʌtəʳ ˈspʌtər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense sputters, present participle sputtering, past tense , past participle sputtered
1 VERB (发动机)发出噼啪声;(火焰)发出毕剥声 If something such as an engine or a flame sputters, it works or burns in an uneven way and makes a series of soft popping sounds. (发动机)发出噼啪声;(火焰)发出毕剥声

  • The truck sputtered and stopped. [VERB]

    卡车噼啪响了几声停下了。

  • Engines sputtered to life again. [VERB prep./adv.]

    发动机噼啪噼啪地重新开动了。

  • The flame sputters out. [VERB prep./adv.]

    火焰毕剥作响。

  • ...the sputtering engine. [VERB-ing]

    噼啪作响的发动机

Sputter is also a noun.

  • All I could hear was the sputter of the fire.

    我只能听到火的毕剥声。

2 VERB (过程、行动或事态)缓慢不稳地进行，慢慢结束 If a process, action, or state of affairs sputters, it progresses slowly and unevenly or starts to end. (过程、行动或事态)缓慢不稳地进行，慢慢结束

  • The economy is already sputtering, with low or no growth. [VERB]

    经济发展速度已经放慢，增长率很低，或者根本没有增长。

  • The battle sputtered to a halt in mid-October. [VERB prep./adv.]

    战斗于 10 月中旬渐渐停了下来。

  • The whole thing sputtered out. [VERB prep./adv.]

    整个事态渐渐平息了下来。

3 VERB (尤指因生气、震惊或激动)结结巴巴地说话，语无伦次地说话 If you sputter, you speak with difficulty and make short sounds, especially because you are angry, shocked, or excited. (尤指因生气、震惊或激动)结结巴巴地说话，语无伦次地说话

  • Stunned, I sputtered, 'What do you mean?'. [VERB with quote]

    我大吃一惊，结结巴巴地问道：“你是什么意思？”

  • Our father's face had reddened with rage and he began to sputter. [VERB]

    我们的父亲气得涨红了脸，说话开始语无伦次。

  • He began to sputter his reply. [VERB noun]

    他开始结结巴巴地回答。
