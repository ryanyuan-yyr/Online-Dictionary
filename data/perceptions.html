perception
pəʳsepʃən pərˈsɛpʃən
Word forms: plural perceptions
1 N-COUNT [usually poss NOUN] 理解；看法；认识 Your perception of something is the way that you think about it or the impression you have of it. 理解；看法；认识

  • He is interested in how our perceptions of death affect the way we live. [+ of]

    他感兴趣的是我们对死亡的看法如何影响我们的生活。

  • ...their perception of foreigners.

    他们对外国人的印象

SYN awareness, understanding, sense, impression  
2 N-UNCOUNT 洞察力；认识能力；悟性 Someone who has perception realizes or notices things that are not obvious. 洞察力；认识能力；悟性

  • It did not require a great deal of perception to realise the interview was over.

    并不需要多少洞察力也能意识到面试已经结束了。

SYN understanding, intelligence, observation, discrimination  
3 N-COUNT （尤指视觉上的）感觉，感知 Perception is the recognition of things using your senses, especially the sense of sight. （尤指视觉上的）感觉，感知
