doggone
dɔgɔn ˈdɒɡɒn ˈdɔgˈgɔn
1 ADJ [ADJ n] 狗日的People sometimes use doggone to emphasize what they are saying, especially when they are annoyed. 狗日的 [US, informal, emphasis]

  • "The doggone business just keeps on deteriorating," says Larry Neihart, president of the Diesel Workers Union.

    “这桩狗日的生意一直在走下坡路，”柴油工会主席拉里·奈哈特说。

2 ADV 他妈地Doggone is also an adverb. 他妈地

  • It was so doggone hot.

    真他妈的热。
