plant
plɑːnt plænt plænt plɑnt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense plants, present participle planting, past tense , past participle planted
1 N-COUNT 植物 A plant is a living thing that grows in the earth and has a stem, leaves, and roots. 植物

  • Water each plant as often as required.

    按要求经常给每一株植物浇水。

  • ...exotic plants.

    异域植物

SYN flower, bush, vegetable, herb  
2See also:bedding plant  pot plant  rubber plant 
3 VERB 播（种）；种植；栽种 When you plant a seed, plant, or young tree, you put it into the ground so that it will grow there. 播（种）；种植；栽种

  • He says he plans to plant fruit trees and vegetables. [VERB noun]

    他说他打算种植果树和蔬菜。

SYN sow, scatter, set out, transplant  
planting

  • Extensive flooding in the country has delayed planting and many crops are still under water.

    这个国家大面积的水灾已延误了播种，而很多农作物还浸在水下。

4 VERB 在…种上植物（或农作物） When someone plants land with a particular type of plant or crop, they put plants, seeds, or young trees into the land to grow them there. 在…种上植物（或农作物）

  • They plan to plant the area with grass and trees. [VERB noun + with]

    他们计划在这一地区种草植树。

  • Recently much of their energy has gone into planting a large vegetable garden. [VERB noun]

    最近他们把很多精力投在种植大菜园上了。

  • ...newly planted fields. [VERB-ed]

    刚栽种完的田地

SYN seed, sow, implant  
5 N-COUNT 工厂；发电厂 A plant is a factory or a place where power is produced. 工厂；发电厂

  • ...Ford's British car assembly plants.

    福特公司在英国的汽车装配厂

  • The plant provides forty per cent of the country's electricity.

    全国总电量的40%是这家发电厂提供的。

SYN factory, works, shop, yard  
6 N-UNCOUNT 成套机械；成套设备；成套装置 Plant is large machinery that is used in industrial processes. 成套机械；成套设备；成套装置

  • ...investment in plant and equipment.

    对机械设备的投资

7 VERB 安放；放置；使固定 If you plant something somewhere, you put it there firmly. 安放；放置；使固定

  • She planted her feet wide and bent her knees slightly. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    她双脚宽分站稳，膝盖微屈。

  • ...with his enormous feet planted heavily apart. [V-ed adv/prep]

    他的大脚重重地分开站好

SYN place, put, set, settle  
8 VERB 藏放；埋设 To plant something such as a bomb means to hide it somewhere so that it explodes or works there. 藏放；埋设

  • So far no one has admitted planting the bomb. [VERB noun]

    到目前为止，还没有人承认是自己放了炸弹。

SYN hide, put, place, conceal  
9 VERB [oft passive] 把（武器、毒品等）栽赃（于某人） If something such as a weapon or drugs is planted on someone, it is put among their possessions or in their house so that they will be wrongly accused of a crime. 把（武器、毒品等）栽赃（于某人）

  • He claimed that the drugs had been planted to incriminate him. [be VERB-ed]

    他声称毒品是有人栽赃陷害他。

10 VERB 派遣；安插 If an organization plants someone somewhere, they send that person there so that they can get information or watch someone secretly. 派遣；安插

  • Journalists informed police who planted an undercover detective to trap Smith. [VERB noun]

    记者通知了警方，后者于是为诱捕史密斯而安插了一名卧底侦探。

11 VERB 给，对着（某人一吻） If you plant a kiss on someone, you give them a kiss. 给，对着（某人一吻）

  • She planted a kiss on each of his leathery cheeks. [VERB noun on noun]

    她在他粗糙的面颊上左右各吻了一下。

12 VERB 灌输（思想） If you plant an idea in someone's mind, they begin to accept the idea without realizing that it has originally come from you and not from them. 灌输（思想）

  • He hoped that he could plant the idea in such a way that Abramov would believe it was his own. [VERB noun]

    他希望能把这个想法注入阿布拉莫夫的头脑，但要让他相信这是他自己的念头。
