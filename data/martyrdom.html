martyrdom
mɑːʳtəʳdəm ˈmɑrtərdəm
1 N-UNCOUNT 受难；(因政治或宗教信仰而)殉道，殉难，殉教 If someone suffers martyrdom, they are killed or made to suffer greatly because of their religious or political beliefs. 受难；(因政治或宗教信仰而)殉道，殉难，殉教

  • ...the martyrdom of Bishop Feliciano. [+ of]

    费利恰诺主教的殉教

  • He suffered martyrdom by stoning.

    他被处以石刑，以身殉难。

2 N-UNCOUNT (为博取同情或赞赏的)夸大痛苦的行为,装可怜 If you describe someone's behaviour as martyrdom, you are critical of them because they are exaggerating their suffering in order to gain sympathy or praise. (为博取同情或赞赏的)夸大痛苦的行为,装可怜 [disapproval]

  • She sat picking at her small plate of rice salad with an air of martyrdom.

    她坐在那里一点点地吃着一小盘米饭色拉，一副可怜兮兮的模样。
