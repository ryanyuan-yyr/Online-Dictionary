downstream
daʊnstriːm ˈdaʊnˌstrim
ADV [ADVERB after verb] [be ADVERB] 顺流而下;向（或在）下游方向 Something that is moving downstream is moving towards the mouth of a river, from a point further up the river. Something that is downstream is further towards the mouth of a river than where you are. 顺流而下;向（或在）下游方向 [Also + of/from]

  • We had drifted downstream.

    我们漂向了下游。

  • ...a district called Lupitu, which in fact was downstream of the dam.

    叫勒普顿的地区，实际上在大坝的下游。

  • Communities downstream have been alerted.

    下游的社区已经接到了预警。

Downstream is also an adjective.

  • Breaking the dam could submerge downstream cities such as Wuhan.

    摧毁大坝可能会淹没下游城市，比如武汉。
