goggle
gɒgəl ˈgɑgəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense goggles, present participle goggling, past tense , past participle goggled
1 VERB （常指吃惊地）瞪大眼睛看 If you goggle at something, you stare at it with your eyes wide open, usually because you are surprised by it. （常指吃惊地）瞪大眼睛看

  • She goggled at me. [VERB + at]

    她瞪大眼睛望着我。

  • He goggled in bewilderment. [VERB]

    他困惑地瞪着眼睛。

SYN stare, gape, gawp [slang] , gawk  
2 N-PLURAL [oft a pair of NOUN] 护目镜；风镜；游泳镜 Goggles are large glasses that fit closely to your face around your eyes to protect them from such things as water, wind, or dust. 护目镜；风镜；游泳镜
