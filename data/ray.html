ray
reɪ reɪ
Word forms: plural rays
1 N-COUNT 光线；光束 Rays of light are narrow beams of light. 光线；光束

  • ...the first rays of light spread over the horizon. [+ of]

    地平线上出现的第一缕曙光

  • It can be seen clearly in a ray of sunlight or under a lamp.

    它在阳光或灯光下清晰可见。

  • The sun's rays can penetrate water up to 10 feet.

    太阳的光线可以穿透到10英尺深的水下。

SYN beam, bar, flash, shaft  
2See also:cosmic rays  gamma rays  X-ray 
3 N-COUNT 一丝，一线（希望、慰藉等） A ray of hope, comfort, or other positive quality is a small amount of it that you welcome because it makes a bad situation seem less bad. 一丝，一线（希望、慰藉等）

  • They could provide a ray of hope amid the general business and economic gloom. [+ of]

    在普遍的商业和经济低潮中，他们能带来一线希望。

  • The one ray of sunlight in this depressing history is her meeting and falling in love with Martin.

    在这段阴郁的时期，遇到并爱上马丁成为她唯一的一丝慰藉。

4 N-COUNT 鳐;魟 A ray is a fairly large sea fish which has a flat body, eyes on the top of its body, and a long tail. 鳐;魟
