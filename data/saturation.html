saturation
sætʃʊreɪʃən ˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən
1 N-UNCOUNT 饱和过程(或状态);饱和 Saturation is the process or state that occurs when a place or thing is filled completely with people or things, so that no more can be added. 饱和过程(或状态);饱和

  • Reforms have led to the saturation of the market with goods. [+ of]

    改革已经使得市场上商品饱和。

  • Road traffic has reached saturation point.

    公路交通已达到饱和点。

2 ADJ [ADJ n] (运动、活动)饱和的，全面的 Saturation is used to describe a campaign or other activity that is carried out very thoroughly, so that nothing is missed. (运动、活动)饱和的，全面的

  • The concept of saturation marketing makes perfect sense.

    饱和营销的概念是完全有道理的。

  • Newspapers, television and radio are all providing saturation coverage.

    报纸、电视和电台全都在铺天盖地地报道。

  • ...saturation bombing.

    饱和轰炸
