tinker
tɪŋkəʳ ˈtɪŋkər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense tinkers, present participle tinkering, past tense , past participle tinkered
1 VERB 小修;小补;摆弄 If you tinker with something, you make some small changes to it, in an attempt to improve it or repair it. 小修;小补;摆弄

  • Instead of the Government admitting its error, it just tinkered with the problem. [VERB + with]

    政府没有承认错误，只是敷衍了事地处理问题。

  • They tinkered with the engine… [VERB with noun]

    他们修了修发动机。

  • It is not enough to tinker at the edges; our objective must be to reconstruct the entire system. [VERB]

    只在外缘做一些小修小补是不够的，我们的目标必须是重建整个系统。

SYN meddle, play, toy, monkey  
tinkering

  • No amount of tinkering is going to improve matters.

    任何小修小补都是于事无补的。

2 N-COUNT (旧时的)补锅匠，修理匠 In former times, a tinker was a person who did not have a fixed home, but travelled from place to place mending metal pots and doing other small repair jobs. (旧时的)补锅匠，修理匠
3 N-COUNT (尤指爱尔兰裔的)流浪者，吉卜赛人 Some people refer to any traveller or gipsy, especially one who is Irish, as a tinker . (尤指爱尔兰裔的)流浪者，吉卜赛人 [British, offensive]
