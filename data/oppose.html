oppose
əpoʊz əˈpoʊz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense opposes, present participle opposing, past tense , past participle opposed
VERB 反对；阻挠 If you oppose someone or oppose their plans or ideas, you disagree with what they want to do and try to prevent them from doing it. 反对；阻挠

  • Mr Taylor was not bitter towards those who had opposed him. [VERB noun]

    泰勒先生对那些曾反对过他的人并未怀恨在心。

  • Many parents oppose bilingual education in schools. [VERB noun]

    很多家长反对学校实行双语教育。

SYN be against, fight (against), check, bar
