vice
vaɪs ˈvaɪsɪ ˈvaɪsi vaɪs vaɪs ˈvaɪsə
Word forms: plural vices
1 N-COUNT 缺点;瑕疵;弱点 A vice is a habit which is regarded as a weakness in someone's character, but not usually as a serious fault. 缺点;瑕疵;弱点

  • His only vice is to get drunk on champagne after concluding a successful piece of business.

    他唯一的缺点就是在成功做成一笔生意后会大喝香槟而醉。

  • Intellectual pretension was never one of his vices.

    他从来不附庸风雅。

2 N-UNCOUNT （尤指与色情业、卖淫行为相关的）犯罪活动,恶行 Vice refers to criminal activities, especially those connected with pornography or prostitution. （尤指与色情业、卖淫行为相关的）犯罪活动,恶行

  • He said those responsible for offences connected with vice, gaming and drugs should be deported on conviction.

    他说，那些被指控与卖淫、赌博和吸毒有关的人一旦罪名成立就应该被驱逐出境。

  • ...allegations of how she worked in a 'seedy vice den'.

    对她在“肮脏淫窝”里如何工作的说法

SYN wickedness, evil, corruption, sin  
3 N-COUNT 老虎钳;台钳 A vice is a tool with a pair of parts that hold an object tightly while you do work on it. 老虎钳;台钳 [British]
in AM, use 美国英语用 vise

PREFIX （用于级别或头衔前）表示“副的”，“次的” Vice- is used before a rank or title to indicate that someone is next in importance to the person who holds the rank or title mentioned. （用于级别或头衔前）表示“副的”，“次的”

  • ...America's vice-president.

    美国副总统

  • Tim Munton becomes the new vice-captain.

    蒂姆·芒顿成为新的副队长。
