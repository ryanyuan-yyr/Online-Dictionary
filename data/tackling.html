tackle
tækəl ˈteɪkəl ˈtækəl ˈteɪkəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense tackles, present participle tackling, past tense , past participle tackled
1 VERB (果断或高效地)应对，处理，解决(难题或任务) If you tackle a difficult problem or task, you deal with it in a very determined or efficient way. (果断或高效地)应对，处理，解决(难题或任务)

  • The first reason to tackle these problems is to save children's lives. [VERB noun]

    果断处理这些问题的首要原因是为了挽救孩子们的生命。

  • Firemen later tackled the blaze. [VERB noun]

    消防队员们后来扑灭了这场大火。

SYN deal with, take on, set about, wade into  
2 VERB (曲棍球、足球等比赛中)抢断，阻截，铲断;(英式、美式橄榄球比赛中)擒抱摔倒 If you tackle someone in a game such as hockey or football, you try to take the ball away from them. If you tackle someone in rugby or American football, you knock them to the ground. (曲棍球、足球等比赛中)抢断，阻截，铲断;(英式、美式橄榄球比赛中)擒抱摔倒

  • Foley tackled the quarterback. [VERB noun]

    福利成功拦截了四分卫。

Tackle is also a noun.

  • ...a tackle by full-back Brian Burrows.

    后卫布赖恩·伯罗斯的拦截

3 VERB (坦率地)与…交谈，与…交涉 If you tackle someone about a particular matter, you speak to them honestly about it, usually in order to get it changed or done. (坦率地)与…交谈，与…交涉

  • I tackled him about how anyone could live amidst so much poverty. [VERB noun + about]

    我坦率地问他，人在如此贫穷的环境中如何还能生存。

SYN confront, speak to, face up to, question  
4 VERB 攻击;与…打架 If you tackle someone, you attack them and fight them. 攻击;与…打架

  • He claims Pasolini overtook and tackled him, pushing him into the dirt. [VERB noun]

    他声称帕索利尼追上去打他，把他推倒在烂泥里。

SYN grab, stop, throw, seize  
5 N-UNCOUNT (体育等活动的)器具，器材;（尤指）渔具 Tackle is the equipment that you need for a sport or activity, especially fishing. (体育等活动的)器具，器材;（尤指）渔具

  • ...fishing tackle.

    渔具

6 N-UNCOUNT （吊拉物体用的）索具，滑轮组 Tackle is the equipment, usually consisting of ropes and pulleys, needed for lifting or pulling something. （吊拉物体用的）索具，滑轮组

  • I finally hoisted him up with a block and tackle.

    我最终用滑轮组把他拉了上去。

SYN rig, rigging, apparatus
