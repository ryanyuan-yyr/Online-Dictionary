combine
kəmbaɪn kɒmbaɪn kəmˈbaɪn ˈkɑmˌbaɪn
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense combines, present participle combining, past tense , past participle combined
The verb is pronounced /kəm'baɪn/. The noun is pronounced /'kɒmbaɪn/. 动词读作 /kəm'baɪn/。名词读作 /'kɒmbaɪn/。
1 V-RECIP-ERG (使)结合;(使)组合;(使)综合 If you combine two or more things or if they combine, they exist together. (使)结合;(使)组合;(使)综合 [Also VERB n]

  • The Church has something to say on how to combine freedom with responsibility. [VERB noun + with]

    教会想就如何把自由与责任结合起来发表一下意见。

  • If improved education is combined with other factors dramatic results can be achieved. [VERB noun with noun]

    如果把改良的教育体系和其他的因素结合起来，会取得非常惊人的效果。

  • Relief workers say it's worse than ever as disease and starvation combine to kill thousands. [VERB to-infinitive]

    救灾人员说疾病加上饥饿夺去了数千人的生命，严重程度前所未有。

  • A stagnant economy combined with a surge in the number of teenagers is likely to have contributed to rising crime levels in the US. [VERB-ed]

    经济停滞不前，加上青少年人数激增，可能是导致美国犯罪率上升的原因之一。

2 V-RECIP-ERG (使)合并;(使)综合 If you combine two or more things or if they combine, they join together to make a single thing. (使)合并;(使)综合

  • David Jacobs was given the job of combining the data from these 19 studies into one giant study. [VERB noun]

    分给戴维·雅格布斯的任务是把这19项研究得出的数据加以综合，使之成为一项大型研究。

  • Combine the flour with 3 tablespoons water to make a paste. [VERB noun + with]

    往面粉里加3大勺水揉成面团。

  • Carbon, hydrogen and oxygen combine chemically to form carbohydrates and fats. [VERB]

    碳、氢、氧化合形成碳水化合物和脂肪。

  • Combined with other compounds, they created a massive dynamite-type bomb. [V-ed + with]

    将其与其他化合物结合，就制造出杀伤力极强的硝酸甘油炸弹。

SYN amalgamate, marry, mix, bond  
3 VERB 兼有;兼备 If someone or something combines two qualities or features, they have both those qualities or features at the same time. 兼有;兼备

  • Their system seems to combine the two ideals of strong government and proportional representation. [VERB noun]

    他们的体制似乎是强有力的政府和比例代表制的完美结合。

  • ...a clever, far-sighted lawyer who combines legal expertise with social concern. [VERB noun + with]

    兼有法律专业知识和社会责任感的精明而又有远见的律师

  • Her tale has a consciously youthful tone and storyline, combined with a sly humour. [VERB-ed]

    她的故事有意采用富于青春气息的笔调和情节，同时还带些调侃。

4 VERB 兼做;同时做(两件事) If someone combines two activities, they do them both at the same time. 兼做;同时做(两件事)

  • It is possible to combine a career with being a mother. [VERB noun + with]

    兼顾职业女性和母亲这两个角色是可能的。

  • He will combine the two jobs over the next three years. [VERB noun]

    今后3年，他将同时兼顾这两项工作。

5 V-RECIP-ERG (使)合并;(使)联合 If two or more groups or organizations combine or if someone combines them, they join to form a single group or organization. (使)合并;(使)联合 [Also VERB n + with]

  • ...an announcement by Steetley and Tarmac of a joint venture that would combine their operations. [VERB noun]

    斯蒂特利公司和泰玛士集团发表的整合其生产经营成立一个合资企业的声明

  • Different states or groups can combine to enlarge their markets. [VERB to-infinitive]

    不同的州或企业集团可以联合起来以扩大市场。

SYN unite, associate, team up, unify  
6 N-COUNT 联盟;联合体 A combine is a group of people or organizations that are working or acting together. 联盟;联合体

  • ...Veba, an energy-and-chemicals combine that is Germany's fourth-biggest company.

    能源化工联合企业费巴是德国的第四大企业
