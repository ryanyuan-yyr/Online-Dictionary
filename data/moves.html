move
muːv muv
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense moves, present participle moving, past tense , past participle moved
1 V-ERG （使）移动;（使）活动 When you move something or when it moves, its position changes and it does not remain still. （使）移动;（使）活动

  • She moved the sheaf of papers into position. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    她把那捆报纸挪到合适的地方。

  • You can move the camera both vertically and horizontally. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    你可以上下左右移动摄像机。

  • A traffic warden asked him to move his car. [VERB noun]

    交通管理员让他把车开走。

  • I could see the branches of the trees moving back and forth. [VERB prep./adv.]

    我能看到树枝来回摇摆。

  • The train began to move. [VERB]

    火车开动了。

SYN transfer, change, carry, transport  
2 VERB 改变姿势;移动位置 When you move, you change your position or go to a different place. 改变姿势;移动位置

  • She waited for him to get up, but he didn't move. [VERB]

    她等他起床，但他没动弹。

  • There was so much furniture you could hardly move without bumping into something. [VERB]

    家具太多，稍挪步就会撞到什么东西上。

  • He moved around the room, putting his possessions together. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他在屋里走来走去，忙着把自己的东西放在一起。

  • She moved away from the window. [VERB prep./adv.]

    她从窗边走开。

SYN go, walk, march, advance  
Move is also a noun.

  • The doctor made a move towards the door.

    医生朝门口走去。

  • Daniel's eyes followed her every move.

    丹尼尔的目光追随着她的一举一动。

3 VERB 采取行动;开始行动 If you move, you act or you begin to do something. 采取行动;开始行动

  • Industrialists must move fast to take advantage of new opportunities in Eastern Europe. [VERB]

    实业家们必须尽快采取行动，抓住东欧的新机遇。

SYN take action, act, do something, take steps  
4 N-COUNT [usually singular] 步骤;措施 A move is an action that you take in order to achieve something. 步骤;措施

  • The one point cut in interest rates was a wise move.

    将利率降低一个点是明智的举措。

  • It may also be a good move to suggest she talks things over.

    建议她把事情谈开了也许是不错的做法。

  • The thirty-five member nations agreed to the move.

    35个成员国同意采取该措施。

  • Her latest disappearing act may be no more than a stunt, or a smart career move.

    她最近玩消失也许只是一个噱头，或是其职业生涯上的一着妙棋。

SYN ploy, action, measure, step  
5 VERB 搬家;迁移 If a person or company moves, they leave the building where they have been living or working, and they go to live or work in a different place, taking their possessions with them. 搬家;迁移

  • My family home is in York-shire and they don't want to move. [VERB]

    我家人住在约克郡，他们不打算搬家。

  • She had often considered moving to London. [VERB + to]

    她过去常想搬到伦敦去住。

  • They move house fairly frequently. [VERB noun]

    他们搬家相当频繁。

  • The London Evening Standard moved offices a few years ago. [VERB noun]

    《伦敦标准晚报》报址几年前迁走了。

SYN relocate, leave, remove, quit  
Move is also a noun.

  • Modigliani announced his move to Montparnasse in 1909.

    莫迪利亚尼1909年宣布他要移居蒙帕尔纳斯。

6 VERB 调动;派遣 If people in authority move someone, they make that person go from one place or job to another one. 调动;派遣

  • His superiors moved him to another parish. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    上级把他派往另一个教区。

  • Ms Clark is still in position and there are no plans to move her. [VERB noun]

    克拉克女士仍然在位，没有要调动她的计划。

  • The family had to be moved because of an attack on their home. [VERB noun]

    这家人在住处遭受袭击后，只好举家迁往别处。

7 VERB 变换职业;改变兴趣 If you move from one job or interest to another, you change to it. 变换职业;改变兴趣

  • He moved from being an extramural tutor to being a lecturer in social history. [VERB + from/to]

    他原先是一名校外辅导老师，后调入学校讲授社会史。

  • In the early days Christina moved jobs to get experience. [VERB noun]

    起初克里斯蒂娜更换不同的工作来积累经验。

SYN change, shift, convert, transform  
Move is also a noun.

  • His move to the chairmanship means he will take a less active role in day-to-day management.

    他调任主席职务，意味着他对日常管理工作的参与将减少。

8 VERB 改变(谈话内容);转到（新的话题） If you move to a new topic in a conversation, you start talking about something different. 改变(谈话内容);转到（新的话题）

  • Let's move to another subject, Dan. [VERB + from/to]

    丹，我们换个话题吧。

9 VERB 改变（…的日期） If you move an event or the date of an event, you change the time at which it happens. 改变（…的日期） [Also VERB n]

  • The club has moved its meeting to Saturday, January 22nd. [VERB noun + to]

    俱乐部把会议日期改到1月22号，星期六。

  • The band have moved forward their Leeds date to October 27. [VERB noun with adv.]

    乐队已把去利兹的日期提前至10月27号。

10 VERB 变得；转而 If you move towards a particular state, activity, or opinion, you start to be in that state, do that activity, or have that opinion. 变得；转而

  • The Labour Party has moved to the right and become like your Democrat Party. [VERB prep./adv.]

    工党已右倾化了，变得像你们民主党。

  • It is already possible to start moving toward the elimination of nuclear weapons. [VERB prep./adv.]

    已有可能朝着消除核武器的目标努力。

  • Since the Convention was drawn up international opinion has begun to move against it. [VERB prep./adv.]

    自从该国际公约起草之日起，国际社会就转而对其持反对意见。

Move is also a noun.

  • His move to the left was not a sudden leap but a natural working out of ideas.

    他转向左派不是一种骤变，而是思想意识的自然发展。

11 VERB [usually cont] 发展;前进;进展 If a situation or process is moving, it is developing or progressing, rather than staying still. 发展;前进;进展

  • Events are moving fast. [VERB]

    事件进展迅速。

  • Someone has got to get things moving. [get n V-ing]

    应该有人牵头把事情做起来。

SYN progress, develop, advance, make progress  
12 VERB [usually passive, with neg] 动摇;改变…的主意 If you say that you will not be moved, you mean that you have come to a decision and nothing will change your mind. 动摇;改变…的主意

  • Everyone thought I was mad to go back, but I wouldn't be moved. [be VERB-ed]

    大家都认为我回去是很不明智的，但我不会动摇。

13 VERB 促使;驱使 If something moves you to do something, it influences you and causes you to do it. 促使;驱使

  • It was punk that first moved him to join a band seriously. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    最初是朋克摇滚乐促使他正式加入了乐队。

  • The president was moved to come up with these suggestions after the hearings. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    听证会促使总统想出了这些建议。

SYN drive, lead, cause, influence  
14 VERB 感动;打动 If something moves you, it has an effect on your emotions and causes you to feel sadness or sympathy for another person. 感动;打动

  • These stories surprised and moved me. [VERB noun]

    这些故事让我吃惊，也让我感动。

  • His prayer moved me to tears. [VERB noun + to]

    他的祈祷把我感动得热泪盈眶。

SYN touch, affect, excite, impress  
moved

  • Those who listened to him were deeply moved.

    听众都被他深深打动了。

15 VERB 活跃，出入，涉足(于某团体、领域或圈子) If you say that someone moves in a particular society, circle, or world, you mean that they know people in a particular social class or group and spend most of their time with them. 活跃，出入，涉足(于某团体、领域或圈子)

  • She moves in high-society circles in London. [VERB + in]

    她时常出入伦敦的上流社会。

  • They moved in a world where hostility to racists was natural. [VERB in noun]

    他们出没在一个对种族主义者的敌视习以为常的世界里。

SYN circulate, mix, associate, go round  
16 VERB (在会议上)提出(动议) At a meeting, if you move a motion, you formally suggest it so that everyone present can vote on it. (在会议上)提出(动议)

  • Labour quickly moved a closure motion to end the debate. [VERB noun]

    工党很快提出终止辩论的动议。

  • I move that the case be dismissed. [VERB that]

    我提议对该案不予受理。

SYN propose, suggest, urge, recommend  
17 N-COUNT 一步(棋);一着(棋) A move is an act of putting a chess piece or other counter in a different position on a board when it is your turn to do so in a game. 一步(棋);一着(棋)

  • With no idea of what to do for my next move, my hand hovered over the board.

    我举着棋子，不知下一步该怎么走。

18 PHRASE 鲁莽之举;不明智之举 If you say that one false move will cause a disaster, you mean that you or someone else must not make any mistakes because the situation is so difficult or dangerous. 鲁莽之举;不明智之举

  • He knew one false move would end in death.

    他明白稍有差池就会被置于死地。

19 PHRASE 快点;抓紧点 If you tell someone to get a move on, you are telling them to hurry. 快点;抓紧点 [informal]
SYN speed up, hurry (up), get going, get moving  
20 PHRASE 离开;出发 If you make a move, you prepare or begin to leave one place and go somewhere else. 离开;出发

  • He glanced at his wristwatch. 'I suppose we'd better make a move.'

    他看一眼手表说：“我想我们最好动身吧。”

  • He made a move to leave.

    他起身离开。

21 PHRASE 采取行动 If you make a move, you take a course of action. 采取行动

  • The week before the deal was supposed to close, fifteen Japanese banks made a move to pull out.

    在预定交易就要结束的前一周，15家日本银行采取行动撤离。

  • Don't wait for others to make the first move: invite friends to visit you.

    别等别人先主动，要先邀请朋友到家来。

22 PHRASE 在行进;在迁移 If you are on the move, you are going from one place to another. 在行进;在迁移

  • Jack never wanted to stay in one place for very long, so they were always on the move.

    杰克从不想在一个地方呆太久，所以他们总在奔波辗转。
