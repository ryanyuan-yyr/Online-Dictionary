muzzle
mʌzəl ˈmʌzəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense muzzles, present participle muzzling, past tense , past participle muzzled
1 N-COUNT (动物的)口鼻部 The muzzle of an animal such as a dog is its nose and mouth. (动物的)口鼻部

  • The mongrel presented his muzzle for scratching.

    那只杂种狗张嘴要伤人。

SYN jaws, mouth, nose, snout  
2 N-COUNT (罩住狗的口鼻的)嘴套，口套 A muzzle is an object that is put over a dog's nose and mouth so that it cannot bite people or make a noise. (罩住狗的口鼻的)嘴套，口套

  • ...dogs like pit bulls which have to wear a muzzle.

    像比特犬这种得戴嘴套的狗

SYN gag, guard, restraint  
3 VERB 给(狗等) 戴上嘴套 If you muzzle a dog or other animal, you put a muzzle over its nose and mouth. 给(狗等) 戴上嘴套

  • He was convicted of failing to muzzle a pit bull. [VERB noun]

    他因没能给比特犬戴嘴套而被判有罪。

4 VERB 使缄默;迫使…保持沉默 If you say that someone is muzzled, you are complaining that they are prevented from expressing their views freely. 使缄默;迫使…保持沉默 [disapproval]

  • He complained of being muzzled by the chairman. [be VERB-ed]

    他抱怨主席不让他发言。

  • She was opposed to new laws to muzzle the press. [VERB noun]

    她反对钳制新闻界言论的新法律。

SYN suppress, silence, curb, restrain  
5 N-COUNT 枪口;炮口 The muzzle of a gun is the end where the bullets come out when it is fired. 枪口;炮口
