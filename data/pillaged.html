pillage
pɪlɪdʒ ˈpɪlɪdʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense pillages, present participle pillaging, past tense , past participle pillaged
VERB 抢劫；劫掠；掠夺 If a group of people pillage a place, they steal property from it using violent methods. 抢劫；劫掠；掠夺

  • Soldiers went on a rampage, pillaging stores and shooting. [VERB noun]

    士兵们横冲直撞，洗劫商店并且开枪射击。

  • ...the boldness to pillage and rape. [VERB]

    抢劫和强奸的胆子

SYN plunder, strip, sack, rob  
Pillage is also a noun.

  • There were no signs of violence or pillage.

    没有暴力或劫掠的迹象。
