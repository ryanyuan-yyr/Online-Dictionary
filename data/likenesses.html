likeness
laɪknəs ˈlaɪknɪs
Word forms: plural likenesses
1 N-SING 相像；相似 If two things or people have a likeness to each other, they are similar to each other. 相像；相似

  • These myths have a startling likeness to one another. [+ to]

    这些神话彼此间有惊人的相似性。

  • There might be a likeness between their features, but their eyes were totally dissimilar. [+ between]

    他们的相貌也许有些相似，但眼睛却完全不同。

SYN resemblance, similarity, correspondence, affinity  
2 N-COUNT [with poss] 肖像；画像；照片；塑像 A likeness of someone is a picture or sculpture of them. 肖像；画像；照片；塑像

  • The museum displays wax likenesses of every US president.

    这家博物馆陈列着历任美国总统的蜡像。

SYN portrait, study, picture, model  
3 N-COUNT [usually singular, usually adjective NOUN] （照片与本人的）相似 If you say that a picture of someone is a good likeness, you mean that it looks just like them. （照片与本人的）相似

  • She says the artist's impression is an excellent likeness of her abductor. [+ of]

    她说这张模拟画像像极了绑架她的人。
