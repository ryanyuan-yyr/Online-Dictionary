beguile
bɪgaɪl biˈgaɪl bɪˈgaɪl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense beguiles, present participle beguiling, past tense , past participle beguiled
1 VERB 迷住；吸引 If something beguiles you, you are charmed and attracted by it. 迷住；吸引

  • His paintings beguiled the Prince of Wales. [VERB noun]

    他的画把威尔士亲王给迷住了。

  • I was beguiled by the romance and exotic atmosphere of the souks in Marrakech. [be VERB-ed]

    我被马拉喀什露天市场的浪漫情调和异域风情深深地吸引了。

SYN charm, please, attract, delight  
2 VERB 哄骗，诱骗（做某事） If someone beguiles you into doing something, they trick you into doing it. 哄骗，诱骗（做某事）

  • He used his newspapers to beguile the readers into buying shares in his company. [VERB noun + into]

    他利用他的报纸诱骗读者买他公司的股票。

SYN fool, trick, take in, cheat
