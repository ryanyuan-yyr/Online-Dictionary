liquidate
lɪkwɪdeɪt ˈlɪkwɪˌdeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense liquidates, present participle liquidating, past tense , past participle liquidated
1 VERB 停业清理，清算，清盘（破产的公司等） To liquidate a company is to close it down and sell all its assets, usually because it is in debt. 停业清理，清算，清盘（破产的公司等） [business]

  • A unanimous vote was taken to liquidate the company. [VERB noun]

    全体投票一致通过停业清理公司。

SYN dissolve, cancel, abolish, terminate  
liquidation

  • The company went into liquidation.

    公司进入了清盘阶段。

  • The number of company liquidations rose 11 per cent.

    停业清理的公司数量上升了11%。

2 VERB 将（资产等）变换成现金；变卖 If a company liquidates its assets, its property such as buildings or machinery is sold in order to get money. 将（资产等）变换成现金；变卖 [business]

  • The company closed down operations and began liquidating its assets in January. [VERB noun]

    公司停止了各项业务，于1月份开始变卖资产。

SYN convert to cash, cash, realize, sell off  
3 VERB 清除；（通常指）杀戮 If someone in a position of power liquidates people who are causing problems, they get rid of them, usually by killing them. 清除；（通常指）杀戮

  • They have not hesitated in the past to liquidate their rivals. [VERB noun]

    过去他们曾毫不犹豫地铲除对手。

SYN kill, murder, remove, destroy
