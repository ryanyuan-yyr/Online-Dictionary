abhor
æbhɔːʳ æbˈhɔr əbhɔr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense abhors, present participle abhorring, past tense , past participle abhorred
VERB （尤指出于道德原因）憎恨，憎恶 If you abhor something, you hate it very much, especially for moral reasons. （尤指出于道德原因）憎恨，憎恶 [formal]

  • He was a man who abhorred violence and was deeply committed to reconciliation. [VERB noun]

    他是一个憎恶采用暴力而坚决主张和解的人。

  • If nature abhors a vacuum, journalists abhor a transition, when there is little news to cover. [VERB noun]

    好比自然界拒绝真空一样，新闻工作者则厌恶没有多少新闻可供报道的过渡时期。

SYN hate, loathe, despise, detest
