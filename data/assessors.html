assessor
əsesəʳ əˈsɛsər
Word forms: plural assessors
1 N-COUNT 估价员;（资产）评估师;估税员 An assessor is a person who is employed to calculate the value of something, or the amount of money that should be paid, for example in tax. 估价员;（资产）评估师;估税员 [business]
2 N-COUNT 专家;（尤指法庭的）陪审推事，顾问 An assessor is a person who is an expert in a subject, especially someone asked to advise a court of law on that subject. 专家;（尤指法庭的）陪审推事，顾问

  • Two judges and six lay assessors sentenced White to life in prison.

    两名法官和六名大众陪审推事判决怀特终身监禁。

3 N-COUNT （考试、面试、体育赛事等的）评分者，评委 An assessor is a person who judges the performance of someone else, for example in an exam, at an interview or at a sporting event. （考试、面试、体育赛事等的）评分者，评委

  • ...external assessors of GCSE exam results.

    普通中等教育证书考试结果的外请评分者

SYN examiner, tester, inspector, investigator
