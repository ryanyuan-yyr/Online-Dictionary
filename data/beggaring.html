beggar
begəʳ ˈbɛgər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense beggars, present participle beggaring, past tense , past participle beggared
1 N-COUNT 乞丐；叫花子 A beggar is someone who lives by asking people for money or food. 乞丐；叫花子
2 VERB 使极度贫穷；使一贫如洗 If something beggars a person, country, or organization, it makes them very poor. 使极度贫穷；使一贫如洗

  • He warned that lifting copyright restrictions could beggar the industry. [VERB noun]

    他警告说解除版权限制会使这个行业一贫如洗。

SYN defy, challenge, defeat, frustrate  
3 PHRASE 无法，难以（相信、描述、形容等） If something beggars belief, it is impossible to believe it. If something beggars description, it is impossible to describe it. 无法，难以（相信、描述、形容等）

  • The statistics beggar belief.

    统计数据让人难以置信。

  • His courage beggars description.

    他的胆量无法用语言来形容。
