irritation
ɪrɪteɪʃən ˌɪrɪˈteɪʃən
Word forms: plural irritations
1 N-UNCOUNT 生气，气恼,恼怒(尤指事情无法阻止或控制时) Irritation is a feeling of annoyance, especially when something is happening that you cannot easily stop or control. 生气，气恼,恼怒(尤指事情无法阻止或控制时)

  • For the first time Leonard felt irritation at her methods.

    伦纳德这还是头一次对她的方法感到恼怒。

  • He tried not to let his irritation show as he blinked in the glare of the television lights.

    电视镁光灯的强光让他直眨眼睛，他努力不让自己的恼怒表现出来。

SYN annoyance, anger, fury, resentment  
2 N-COUNT 恼人事;烦心事 An irritation is something that keeps annoying you. 恼人事;烦心事

  • Don't allow a minor irritation in the workplace to mar your ambitions.

    不要让工作中的一点烦心事打击了你的雄心抱负。

  • He describes the tourists as an irritation.

    他觉得那些游客令人厌烦。

SYN nuisance, annoyance, irritant, pain [informal]  
3 N-VAR 刺激;发炎;轻微疼痛 Irritation in a part of your body is a feeling of slight pain and discomfort there. 刺激;发炎;轻微疼痛

  • These oils may cause irritation to sensitive skins. [+ to]

    这些化妆油会导致敏感皮肤发炎。

  • This is an irritation and inflammation of the edge of the eyelid.

    这是眼睑边缘红肿发炎的症状。
