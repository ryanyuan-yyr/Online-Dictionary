discovery
dɪskʌvəri dɪˈskʌvərɪ dɪˈskʌvəri
Word forms: plural discoveries
1 N-VAR [oft NOUN that] 发现;发觉;察觉 If someone makes a discovery, they become aware of something that they did not know about before. 发现;发觉;察觉

  • I felt I'd made an incredible discovery.

    我觉得我有一个天大的发现。

  • ...the discovery that both his wife and son are HIV positive.

    查出他妻子和儿子艾滋病病毒检测都呈阳性

SYN finding out, news, announcement, revelation  
2 N-VAR (第一个)发现 If someone makes a discovery, they are the first person to find or become aware of a place, substance, or scientific fact that no one knew about before. (第一个)发现

  • In that year, two momentous discoveries were made.

    那一年，有两个重大发现。

  • ...the discovery of the ozone hole over the South Pole.

    南极上空臭氧洞的发现

  • The fascination of discovery has never left him.

    他一直迷恋发现的魅力。

SYN breakthrough, find, finding, development  
3 N-VAR (对不为人知的演员、音乐人等的)发现 If someone makes a discovery, they recognize that an actor, musician, or other performer who is not well-known has talent. (对不为人知的演员、音乐人等的)发现

  • His job is the discovery and promotion of new artists.

    他的任务就是发现并推出艺术新人。

4 N-VAR 找到;（偶然的）发现 When the discovery of people or objects happens, someone finds them, either by accident or as a result of looking for them. 找到;（偶然的）发现

  • ...the discovery and destruction by soldiers of millions of marijuana plants. [+ of]

    士兵发现并销毁了大量大麻作物
