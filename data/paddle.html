paddle
pædəl ˈpædəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense paddles, present participle paddling, past tense , past participle paddled
1 N-COUNT 桨；短桨 A paddle is a short pole with a wide flat part at one end or at both ends. You hold it in your hands and use it as an oar to move a small boat through water. 桨；短桨

  • We might be able to push ourselves across with the paddle.

    我们也许可以用桨把自己推过去。

  • ...a piece of driftwood which he used as a paddle.

    他用作桨使的一块浮木

SYN oar, sweep, scull  
2 VERB 用桨划（船） If you paddle a boat, you move it through water using a paddle. 用桨划（船）

  • ...the skills you will use to paddle the canoe. [VERB noun]

    划独木舟要用到的技术

  • ...paddling around the South Pacific in a kayak. [VERB prep./adv.]

    划着皮划子在南太平洋游弋

SYN row, pull, scull  
3 VERB 玩水；涉水 If you paddle, you walk or stand in shallow water, for example at the edge of the sea, for pleasure. 玩水；涉水

  • Wear sandals when you paddle. [VERB]

    玩水的时候穿上凉鞋。

  • ...a lovely little stream that you can paddle in. [VERB prep.]

    能够在其中戏水的美丽的小溪

Paddle is also a noun.

  • Ruth enjoyed her paddle.

    露丝玩水玩得很开心。
