factor
fæktəʳ ˈfæktər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense factors, present participle factoring, past tense , past participle factored
1 N-COUNT 因素;要素 A factor is one of the things that affects an event, decision, or situation. 因素;要素

  • Physical activity is an important factor in maintaining fitness. [+ in]

    进行体育活动是保持身体健康的一个重要因素。

SYN element, thing, point, part  
2 N-COUNT [usually singular] (增长的)倍数 If an amount increases by a factor of two, for example, or by a factor of eight, then it becomes two times bigger or eight times bigger. (增长的)倍数

  • The cost of butter quadrupled and bread prices increased by a factor of five. [+ of]

    黄油价格涨了 4 倍，面包价格涨了 5 倍。

3 N-SING 系数 You can use factor to refer to a particular level on a scale of measurement. 系数

  • ...suncream with a protection factor of 8.

    防晒系数为 8 的防晒霜

4 N-COUNT 因子;因数 A factor of a whole number is a smaller whole number which can be multiplied with another whole number to produce the first whole number. 因子;因数 [technical]
