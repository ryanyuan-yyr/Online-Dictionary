regenerate
rɪdʒenəreɪt rɪˈdʒɛnərɪt rɪˈdʒɛnəˌreɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense regenerates, present participle regenerating, past tense , past participle regenerated
1 VERB 革新;使复兴;重建 To regenerate something means to develop and improve it to make it more active, successful, or important, especially after a period when it has been getting worse. 革新;使复兴;重建

  • The government will continue to try to regenerate inner city areas. [VERB noun]

    政府将继续努力重建内城地区。

SYN renew, restore, revive, renovate  
regeneration

  • ...the physical and economic regeneration of the area.

    这个地区面貌的改善和经济的复苏

2 V-ERG (使）(器官或肌体组织）再生；(使）再造 If organs or tissues regenerate or if something regenerates them, they heal and grow again after they have been damaged. (使）(器官或肌体组织）再生；(使）再造

  • Nerve cells have limited ability to regenerate if destroyed. [VERB]

    如果受到损伤，神经细胞再造的能力有限。

  • Newts can regenerate their limbs. [VERB noun]

    蝾螈的肢体可以再生。

regeneration
