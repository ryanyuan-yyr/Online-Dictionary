triumph
traɪʌmf ˈtraɪəmf ˈtraɪəmf
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense triumphs, present participle triumphing, past tense , past participle triumphed
1 N-VAR 非凡的成功;杰出的成就 A triumph is a great success or achievement, often one that has been gained with a lot of skill or effort. 非凡的成功;杰出的成就

  • The championships proved to be a personal triumph for the coach, Dave Donovan. [+ for]

    事实证明，在这次锦标赛中教练戴夫·多诺万取得了非凡的个人成就。

  • Cataract operations are a triumph of modern surgery, with a success rate of more than 90 percent. [+ of]

    成功率达到 90% 以上的白内障手术是现代外科学的杰出成果。

  • In the moment of triumph I felt uneasy.

    在成功的那一刻我反而感到了不安。

SYN success, victory, accomplishment, mastery  
2 N-UNCOUNT (成功或获胜时的)极大的满足，狂喜，骄傲 Triumph is a feeling of great satisfaction and pride resulting from a success or victory. (成功或获胜时的)极大的满足，狂喜，骄傲

  • Her sense of triumph was short-lived.

    她只感受了片刻成功的喜悦。

  • He was laughing with triumph.

    他发出胜利的大笑。

SYN joy, pride, happiness, rejoicing  
3 VERB 成功;获胜;得胜 If someone or something triumphs, they gain complete success, control, or victory, often after a long or difficult struggle. 成功;获胜;得胜

  • All her life, Kelly had stuck with difficult tasks and challenges, and triumphed. [VERB]

    凯莉一生经历了各种艰巨的任务与挑战，她都一一成功应对了。

  • The whole world looked to her as a symbol of good triumphing over evil. [VERB + over]

    全世界都把她视为正义战胜邪恶的象征。

SYN succeed, win, overcome, prevail
