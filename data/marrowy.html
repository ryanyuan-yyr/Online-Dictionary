marrow
mæroʊ ˈmæroʊ
Word forms: plural marrows
1 N-VAR 食用葫芦;西葫芦 A marrow is a long, thick, green vegetable with soft white flesh that is eaten cooked. 食用葫芦;西葫芦 [British]
in AM, use 美国英语用 squash
2 N-UNCOUNT 同 bone marrow Marrow is the same as bone marrow . 同 bone marrow

  • The marrow donor is her 14-month-old sister.

    骨髓捐献者是她14个月大的妹妹。

3 N-SING 精华;精髓;核心 The marrow of something is the most important and basic part of it. 精华;精髓;核心

  • We're getting into the marrow of the film. [+ of]

    我们就要进入这部电影最精彩的部分了。

4 PHRASE [usually PHRASE after verb] (寒冷)透骨地;(惊恐)万分地 If you say that you are chilled to the marrow, you are emphasizing that you are extremely cold or extremely frightened. (寒冷)透骨地;(惊恐)万分地 [emphasis]

  • When I got back at about ten a.m. I was frozen to the marrow.

    我早上大约10点钟回来的时候感到寒气透骨。

  • The very thought of it chilled me to the marrow.

    一想到这个我就不寒而栗。
