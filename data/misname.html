misnameˌmɪsˈneɪm mɪsˈneɪm
误称;给…取名不当 If you say that something or someone is misnamed, you mean that they have a name that describes them incorrectly. 误称;给…取名不当

  • ...a high school teacher who was misnamed Mr. Witty.

    被误称为“智多星”的中学老师

  • ...the misnamed Grand Hotel.

    名不副实的“大酒店”

  • The truth is that junk bonds were misnamed, and therefore misunderstood.

    事实上人们对垃圾债券的称谓有误，所以产生了误解。
