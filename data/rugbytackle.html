rugby tackle
ˈrʌɡbɪ ˈtækəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense rugby tackles, present participle rugby tackling, past tense , past participle rugby tackled
1 N-COUNT 擒抱;拦截 A rugby tackle is a way of making someone fall over by throwing your arms around their legs or hips. 擒抱;拦截
2 VERB 擒抱，拦截（某人） To rugby tackle someone means to make them fall over by throwing your arms around their legs or hips. 擒抱，拦截（某人）

  • He rugby tackled her and stole her bag. [VERB noun]

    他将她撂倒，抢走了她的包。

  • He was rugby tackled by a policeman after breaking through police lines. [be VERB-ed]

    他在突破警戒线后被一名警察拦截了。
