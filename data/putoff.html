put off
1 PHRASAL VERB 推迟；拖延 If you put something off, you delay doing it. 推迟；拖延

  • Women who put off having a baby often make the best mothers. [V P v-ing/n]

    晚育的女性经常会成为最优秀的母亲。

  • The Association has put the event off until October. [VERB noun PREP.]

    协会已把这次活动推迟至10月。

SYN postpone, delay, defer, adjourn  
2 PHRASAL VERB 敷衍；搪塞 If you put someone off, you make them wait for something that they want. 敷衍；搪塞

  • The old priest tried to put them off, saying that the hour was late. [VERB noun PREP.]

    老牧师搪塞他们说时间太晚了。

3 PHRASAL VERB 使反感；使对…失去兴趣 If something puts you off something, it makes you dislike it, or decide not to do or have it. 使反感；使对…失去兴趣

  • The high divorce figures don't seem to be putting people off marriage. [V n P n/v-ing]

    离婚的人很多，但这好像并没有打消人们对结婚的热情。

  • His personal habits put them off. [VERB noun PREP.]

    他们很讨厌他的一些个人习惯。

  • The country's worsening reputation does not seem to be putting off the tourists. [VERB PREP. noun]

    该国越来越糟的名声好像并没有减弱游客们去那儿游览的兴趣。

  • We tried to visit the Abbey but were put off by the queues. [be VERB-ed PREP.]

    我们曾想参观威斯敏斯特教堂，但排起的长队使我们打消了这个念头。

SYN discourage, intimidate, deter, daunt  
4 PHRASAL VERB 使分心；搅扰 If someone or something puts you off, they take your attention from what you are trying to do and make it more difficult for you to do it. 使分心；搅扰 [Also VERB PREPOSITION n (not pron)]

  • She asked me to be serious — said it put her off if I laughed. [VERB noun PREP.]

    她要求我严肃一点—说如果我笑的话会使她分心。

  • It put her off revising for her exams. [V n P n/v-ing]

    这影响了她复习备考。
