influence
ɪnfluəns ˈɪnfluəns ɪnˈfluəns
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense influences, present participle influencing, past tense , past participle influenced
1 N-UNCOUNT 势力;权势;影响力;支配力 Influence is the power to make other people agree with your opinions or do what you want. 势力;权势;影响力;支配力

  • As Hugh grew older, she had less influence and couldn't control him.

    休慢慢长大,她的影响力变小了,无法再控制他了。

  • I have rather a large influence over a good many people.

    我对很多人都有相当大的影响力。

  • He denies exerting any political influence over them. [+ over]

    他否认对他们施加了任何政治压力。

  • The government should continue to use its influence for the release of all hostages.

    政府应继续运用其影响力来解救所有人质。

SYN control, power, authority, direction  
2 VERB 影响;左右;支配 If you influence someone, you use your power to make them agree with you or do what you want. 影响;左右;支配

  • He is trying to improperly influence a witness. [VERB noun]

    他在试图误导证人。

  • The angry crowds could influence the government. [VERB noun]

    愤怒的群众可以影响政府。

  • My dad influenced me to do electronics. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    我学电子是受爸爸的影响。

3 N-COUNT 影响;作用 To have an influence on people or situations means to affect what they do or what happens. 影响;作用

  • Van Gogh had a major influence on the development of modern painting. [+ on]

    凡·高对现代绘画的发展有着重大影响。

  • The Shropshire landscape was an influence on Owen too.

    什罗普郡的风景也对欧文产生了影响。

  • Many other medications have an influence on cholesterol levels.

    许多其他的药物治疗对胆固醇水平都有一定的影响。

SYN effect, impact, impression, sway  
4 VERB 影响;对…起作用 If someone or something influences a person or situation, they have an effect on that person's behaviour or that situation. 影响;对…起作用

  • We became the best of friends and he influenced me deeply. [VERB noun]

    我们成了最好的朋友,他深深地影响了我。

  • What you eat may influence your risk of getting cancer. [VERB noun]

    你吃的东西会影响你患癌症的风险。

  • They still influence what's played on the radio. [VERB wh]

    他们对电台播放什么节目仍然会有影响。

SYN affect, have an effect on, have an impact on, control  
5 N-COUNT [usually singular, usually adjective NOUN] 有影响的人(或事物) Someone or something that is a good or bad influence on people has a good or bad effect on them. 有影响的人(或事物)

  • I thought Sue would be a good influence on you. [+ on]

    我认为苏能对你产生良好影响。

  • TV is a bad influence on people.

    电视会对人们产生不良影响。

6 PHRASE 在…影响之下;在…控制之下 If you are under the influence of someone or something, you are being affected or controlled by them. 在…影响之下;在…控制之下

  • I fell under the influence of a history master.

    我当时深受一位历史老师的影响。

  • The very earliest sculptures were made under the influence of Greek art.

    最早的雕塑作品受到了希腊艺术的影响。

  • He was arrested on suspicion of driving under the influence of alcohol.

    他因涉嫌酒后驾驶被捕。

7 PHRASE [verb-link PHRASE] 喝醉;(吸食毒品后)处于迷幻状态 If someone is under the influence, their mind is affected by alcohol or drugs. 喝醉;(吸食毒品后)处于迷幻状态 [informal]

  • Police charged the man with driving under the influence.

    警方指控那名男子酒后驾车。

  • We find members of the opposite sex more attractive when under the influence.

    我们喝醉时感到异性更具吸引力。

SYN drunk, tight [informal] , smashed [slang] , canned [slang]
