gush
gʌʃ gʌʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense gushes, present participle gushing, past tense , past participle gushed
1 V-ERG 喷出；涌出；冒出 When liquid gushes out of something, or when something gushes a liquid, the liquid flows out very quickly and in large quantities. 喷出；涌出；冒出

  • Piping-hot water gushed out. [VERB adv./prep.]

    滚烫的水喷了出来。

  • A supertanker continues to gush oil off the coast of Spain. [VERB noun]

    西班牙海岸附近的一艘超级油轮仍在大量漏油。

SYN flow, run, rush, flood  
2 N-SING 涌流；喷出；冒出 A gush of liquid is a sudden, rapid flow of liquid, or a quantity of it that suddenly flows out. 涌流；喷出；冒出

  • I heard a gush of water. [+ of]

    我听到一股水流涌出。

SYN stream, flow, rush, flood  
3 VERB 过分称赞；吹捧；装腔作势地说 If someone gushes, they express their admiration or pleasure in an exaggerated way. 过分称赞；吹捧；装腔作势地说

  • 'Oh, it was brilliant,' he gushes. [VERB with quote]

    “哦，棒极了。”他夸张地说。

  • He gushed about his love for his wife. [VERB prep.]

    他吹嘘自己如何爱妻子。

gushing

  • He delivered a gushing speech.

    他发表了非常煽情的讲话。

4 N-SING [NOUN of n] （感情的）迸发，爆发 You can use gush in expressions such as a gush of enthusiasm to refer to a sudden intense feeling or an expression of a feeling. （感情的）迸发，爆发

  • She felt a gush of pure affection for her mother.

    对母亲的爱突然涌上她的心头。

  • Their initial gush of sympathy gradually dried up.

    他们最初怀有的深切同情渐渐地消失了。
