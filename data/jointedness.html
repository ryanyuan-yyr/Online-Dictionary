joint
dʒɔɪnt dʒɔɪnt
Word forms: plural joints
1 ADJ [ADJ n] 联合的；共同的；共享的 Joint means shared by or belonging to two or more people. 联合的；共同的；共享的

  • She and Frank had never gotten around to opening a joint account.

    她和弗兰克还没有抽出时间去开联名账户。

  • Jackie and Ben came to a joint decision as to where they would live.

    关于他们将住哪里是杰姬和本共同决定的。

SYN shared, mutual, collective, communal  
jointly

  • The Port Authority is an agency jointly run by New York and New Jersey.

    港务局是由纽约州和新泽西州共同管理的机构。

2 N-COUNT 关节 A joint is a part of your body such as your elbow or knee where two bones meet and are able to move together. 关节

  • Her joints ache if she exercises.

    她一运动关节就疼。

3 N-COUNT 接合处；连接点；接头；接缝 A joint is the place where two things are fastened or fixed together. 接合处；连接点；接头；接缝
4See also:dovetail joint 
5 N-COUNT （适于烧烤的）大块肉 A joint is a fairly large piece of meat which is suitable for roasting. （适于烧烤的）大块肉 [British]

  • He carved the joint of beef. [+ of]

    他切开了那一大块烤牛肉。

in AM, use 美国英语用 roast
6 N-COUNT 廉价娱乐场所；下等娱乐场所 You can refer to a cheap place where people go for some form of entertainment as a joint . 廉价娱乐场所；下等娱乐场所 [informal]

  • They had come to the world's most famous pick-up joint.

    他们来到了全世界最有名的寻花问柳之地。

  • ...a hamburger joint.

    汉堡包店

7 N-COUNT 大麻烟卷 A joint is a cigarette which contains cannabis or marijuana. 大麻烟卷 [informal]
8 PHRASE （因感到无足轻重或不受重视）心烦意乱，感到不快 If something puts someone's nose out of joint, it upsets or offends them because it makes them feel less important or less valued. （因感到无足轻重或不受重视）心烦意乱，感到不快 [informal]

  • Barry had his nose put out of joint by Lucy's aloof sophistication. [+ by]

    露西的冷淡与世故使得巴里十分不快。

  • Her sister-in-law's nose is a little out of joint.

    她嫂子有点儿心烦。

9 PHRASE [usually verb-link PHRASE] 不太正常；不得体；无秩序；紊乱 If something is out of joint, it is not quite right or appropriate, or does not work quite as it should. 不太正常；不得体；无秩序；紊乱

  • There was something out of joint in the situation. Something was strange.

    情况有点儿不太正常。有些事有点儿古怪。

  • The electoral timetable seems to be out of joint with the need for change.

    选举时间表似乎有些混乱，需要改动一下。
