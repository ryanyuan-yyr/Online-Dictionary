grand
grænd grænd
Word forms: comparative grander, superlative grandest, plural grands
The form grand is used as the plural for meaning 8. 义项8的复数形式为grand。
1 ADJ-GRADED 宏伟的；壮丽的；富丽堂皇的 If you describe a building or a piece of scenery as grand, you mean that its size or appearance is very impressive. 宏伟的；壮丽的；富丽堂皇的

  • ...this grand building in the center of town.

    这幢位于市中心的宏伟建筑

  • The scenery of South Island is on a grand scale.

    南岛风光壮观秀丽。

SYN impressive, great, large, magnificent  
2 ADJ-GRADED （计划等）宏伟的，宏大的 Grand plans or actions are intended to achieve important results. （计划等）宏伟的，宏大的

  • Critics will point to other grand schemes that have gone less well, such as Sheffield's World Student Games disaster.

    批评家将会指出其他一些并不成功的大项目，比如谢菲尔德世界大学生运动会这一败笔。

  • The grand design of Europe's monetary union is already agreed.

    欧洲统一货币的宏伟构想已经获得认同。

3 ADJ-GRADED 牛气的；不可一世的 People who are grand think they are important or socially superior. 牛气的；不可一世的 [disapproval]

  • He is grander and even richer than the Prince of Wales.

    他比威尔士亲王还牛气，甚至更有钱。

  • The Duke of Clarence will be there, and many of your grander friends.

    克拉伦斯公爵将会到场，还有你许多牛不可及的朋友亦将出席。

4 ADJ 快乐的；令人非常愉快的 If you describe an activity or experience as grand, you mean that it is very pleasant and enjoyable. 快乐的；令人非常愉快的

  • Few museums could rival the Old Royal Observatory at Greenwich for a grand day out.

    若想找个地儿开心地逛一天，没有哪家博物馆能比得上格林尼治皇家天文台。

  • The dinner was a grand success.

    晚宴办得十分成功。

  • He was having a grand time meeting new sorts of people.

    结识形形色色的人让他很开心。

SYN excellent, great [informal] , fine, wonderful  
5 ADJ-GRADED 了不起的；出色的；极好的 You can describe someone or something as grand when you admire or approve of them very much. 了不起的；出色的；极好的 [informal, spoken, approval]

  • He was a grand bloke.

    他非常了不起。

6 ADJ [ADJ n] 总括的 A grand total is one that is the final amount or the final result of a calculation. 总括的

  • It came to a grand total of £20,329.

    总计20,329英镑。

7 ADJ [ADJ n] （常用于大型建筑物的名称前）大的 Grand is often used in the names of buildings such as hotels, especially when they are very large. （常用于大型建筑物的名称前）大的

  • They stayed at The Grand Hotel, Budapest.

    他们住在布达佩斯大饭店。

8 N-COUNT 一千美元（或英镑） A grand is a thousand dollars or a thousand pounds. 一千美元（或英镑） [informal]

  • They're paying you ten grand now for those adaptations of old plays.

    他们打算付你一万美元作为改编老剧本的报酬。

9 N-COUNT [usually singular] 同grand piano A grand is the same as a grand piano . 同grand piano
10 See also: grandly
