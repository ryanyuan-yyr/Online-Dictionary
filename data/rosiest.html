rosy
roʊzi ˈroʊzi
Word forms: comparative rosier, superlative rosiest
1 ADJ (脸颊)红润的，红扑扑的 If you say that someone has a rosy face, you mean that they have pink cheeks and look very healthy. (脸颊)红润的，红扑扑的

  • Bethan's round, rosy face seemed hardly to have aged at all.

    贝唐红扑扑的圆脸看起来似乎一点也没有变老。

  • She had bright, rosy cheeks.

    她的脸庞红润有光泽。

SYN glowing, fresh, blooming, flushed  
2 ADJ 红粉色的;玫瑰色的 Something that is rosy is reddish-pink in colour. 红粉色的;玫瑰色的

  • ...the rosy brick buildings.

    红粉色砖砌大楼

SYN pink, red, rose-coloured, roseate  
3 ADJ-GRADED 充满希望的;前景美好的;有望成功的 If you say that a situation looks rosy or that the picture looks rosy, you mean that the situation seems likely to be good or successful. 充满希望的;前景美好的;有望成功的

  • Little over a year ago, things looked very rosy for the Social Democrats.

    一年多以前，社会民主党人的形势看起来一片大好。

  • The job prospects for those graduating in engineering are far less rosy now than they used to be.

    现在那些工科毕业生的工作前景大不如从前。

  • Is the picture really so rosy?

    前景真有那么美好吗？

SYN promising, encouraging, bright, reassuring
