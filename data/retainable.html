retain
rɪteɪn rɪˈteɪn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense retains, present participle retaining, past tense , past participle retained
1 VERB 保留;保持;保存 To retain something means to continue to have that thing. 保留;保持;保存 [formal]

  • The interior of the shop still retains a nineteenth-century atmosphere. [VERB noun]

    这家商店的内部装修仍然保留着19世纪的风格。

  • He retains a deep respect for the profession. [VERB noun]

    他对这个职业仍然深怀敬意。

  • Other countries retained their traditional and habitual ways of doing things. [VERB noun]

    其他国家仍然保持着他们做事的传统和习惯。

  • If left covered in a warm place, this rice will retain its heat for a good hour. [VERB noun]

    如果加盖放在暖和的地方，这些米饭可以保温足足一个小时。

SYN maintain, keep, reserve, preserve  
2 VERB 预聘，付定金聘请（律师） If you retain a lawyer, you pay him or her a fee to make sure that he or she will represent you when your case comes before the court. 预聘，付定金聘请（律师） [law]

  • He decided to retain him for the trial. [VERB noun]

    他决定聘请他为出庭律师。
