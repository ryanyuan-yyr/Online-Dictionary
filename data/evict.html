evict
ɪvɪkt iˈvɪkt ɪˈvɪkt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense evicts, present participle evicting, past tense , past participle evicted
VERB （通常因为违法或毁约而）驱逐，赶出 If someone is evicted from the place where they are living, they are forced to leave it, usually because they have broken a law or contract. （通常因为违法或毁约而）驱逐，赶出 [Also V n + from]

  • They were evicted from their apartment after their mother became addicted to drugs. [be VERB-ed + from]

    他们的母亲吸毒上瘾后，他们被从公寓里赶了出来。

  • In the first week, the city police evicted ten families. [VERB noun]

    在第一周内，该市警方就驱逐了10个家庭。

  • If you don't keep up payments you could be evicted. [be VERB-ed]

    如果不继续交费，你会被赶出去。

SYN expel, remove, turn out, put out
