harbour
hɑːʳbəʳ ˈhɑrbər ˈhɑːbə
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense harbours, present participle harbouring, past tense , past participle harboured
in AM, use 美国英语用 harbor
1 N-COUNT; N-IN-NAMES 海港;港口;港湾 A harbour is an area of the sea at the coast which is partly enclosed by land or strong walls, so that boats can be left there safely. 海港;港口;港湾

  • She led us to a room with a balcony overlooking the harbour.

    她把我们领进了一个带阳台的房间，从那里可以俯瞰海港。

  • The ship was permitted to tie up in Boston harbour.

    该船获准在波士顿港口停泊。

SYN port, haven, dock, mooring  
2 VERB 心怀，怀藏，怀有（情感、想法或秘密） If you harbour an emotion, thought, or secret, you have it in your mind over a long period of time. 心怀，怀藏，怀有（情感、想法或秘密）

  • He might have been murdered by a former client or someone harbouring a grudge. [VERB noun]

    他可能已经被以前的客户或某个对他怀恨在心的人谋杀了。

  • Townsend harbours no regrets. [VERB noun]

    汤森心中无怨无悔。

SYN hold, bear, maintain, nurse  
3 VERB 窝藏，庇护（罪犯） If a person or country harbours someone who is wanted by the police, they let them stay in their house or country and offer them protection. 窝藏，庇护（罪犯）

  • Accusations of harbouring suspects were raised against the former Hungarian leadership. [VERB noun]

    匈牙利前领导层被指控窝藏嫌犯。
