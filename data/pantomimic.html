pantomime
pæntəmaɪm ˈpæntəˌmaɪm
Word forms: plural pantomimes
1 N-COUNT 圣诞童话剧 A pantomime is a funny musical play for children. Pantomimes are usually based on fairy stories and are performed at Christmas. 圣诞童话剧 [British]
2 N-UNCOUNT 圣诞童话剧演出 Pantomime is the form of entertainment which involves producing a pantomime. 圣诞童话剧演出 [British]

  • What she does very well is pantomime.

    她擅长圣诞童话剧演出。

  • He is currently starring in pantomime in Weston-super-Mare.

    他目前正在滨海韦斯顿主演圣诞童话剧。

3 N-UNCOUNT 哑剧 Pantomime is acting something out without speaking. 哑剧

  • Chaplin feared that the art of pantomime was under threat.

    卓别林担心哑剧艺术面临威胁。

4 N-SING 闹剧；（行为的）滑稽愚蠢 If you say that a situation or a person's behaviour is a pantomime, you mean that it is silly or exaggerated and that there is something false about it. 闹剧；（行为的）滑稽愚蠢 [mainly British]

  • They were made welcome with the usual pantomime of exaggerated smiles and gestures. [+ of]

    他们受到了一如往常的欢迎，夸张的笑容和姿态如同一场闹剧。

  • The rights of every American to good government have been damaged by the pantomime on Capitol Hill.

    每一位美国人享受良政的权利被国会山的闹剧损害殆尽。
