recharge
riːtʃɑːʳdʒ riːˈtʃɑːdʒ riˈtʃɑrdʒ ˈriˌtʃɑrdʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense recharges, present participle recharging, past tense , past participle recharged
1 VERB 给（电池）充电 If you recharge a battery, you put an electrical charge back into the battery by connecting it to a machine that draws power from another source of electricity such as the mains. 给（电池）充电

  • He is using your mains electricity to recharge his car battery. [VERB noun]

    他在用你家里的电源给他的车载蓄电池充电。

SYN repower, charge up  
2 PHRASE 放松；休息；补充精力 If you recharge your batteries, you take a break from activities which are tiring or difficult in order to relax and feel better when you return to these activities. 放松；休息；补充精力

  • He wanted to recharge his batteries and come back feeling fresh and positive.

    他想去放松休息一下，然后精力充沛、干劲十足地回来。
