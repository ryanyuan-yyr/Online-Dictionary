wash up
1 PHRASAL VERB 洗餐具 If you wash up, you wash the plates, cups, cutlery, and pans which have been used for cooking and eating a meal. 洗餐具 [British] [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • I ran some hot water and washed up. [VERB PREP.]

    我放了一些热水把餐具洗了。

  • I bet you make breakfast and wash up their plates, too. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我肯定是你做的早餐并且清洗了他们的餐具。

SYN do the washing up, wash the dishes, do the dishes  
in AM, use 美国英语用 wash the dishes
2 PHRASAL VERB 洗脸;洗手 If you wash up, you clean part of your body with soap and water, especially your hands and face. 洗脸;洗手 [US]

  • He headed to the bathroom to wash up. [VERB PREP.]

    他走进洗手间去洗漱。

in BRIT, use 英国英语用 wash
3 PHRASAL VERB [usually passive] 退潮留下;(海水或河水)冲刷后留下 If something is washed up on a piece of land, it is carried by a river or sea and left there. 退潮留下;(海水或河水)冲刷后留下

  • Thousands of herring and crab are washed up on the beaches during every storm.

    每次暴风雨过后海滩上都会留下数不尽的鲱鱼和螃蟹。

  • The fossils appear to be an early form of seaweed washed up on a beach. [VERB-ed PREP.]

    这些化石看上去是被海水冲上岸的一种早期的水藻。

SYN strand, ground, beach, wash ashore
