cloak
kloʊk kloʊk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense cloaks, present participle cloaking, past tense , past participle cloaked
1 N-COUNT 斗篷;披风 A cloak is a long, loose, sleeveless piece of clothing which people used to wear over their other clothes when they went out. 斗篷;披风
2 N-SING 覆盖物;遮盖物 A cloak of something such as mist or snow completely covers and hides something. 覆盖物;遮盖物

  • Today most of England will be under a cloak of thick mist. [+ of]

    今天，英格兰大部分地区将被浓雾笼罩。

SYN covering, layer, blanket, shroud  
3 N-SING 掩盖;伪装;幌子 If you refer to something as a cloak, you mean that it is intended to hide the truth about something. 掩盖;伪装;幌子

  • Preparations for the wedding were made under a cloak of secrecy. [+ of]

    婚礼的准备工作是秘密进行的。

4 VERB 笼罩;覆盖;遮掩 To cloak something means to cover it or hide it. 笼罩;覆盖;遮掩 [written]

  • ...the decision to cloak major tourist attractions in unsightly hoardings. [VERB noun + in]

    在主要景点外挂满刺眼的大幅广告牌的决定

  • A fire could have been deliberately started to cloak small coordinated troop movements. [VERB noun]

    可能是有人故意放火掩护小规模的部队协同行动。

  • The beautiful sweeping coastline was cloaked in mist. [V-ed + in]

    美丽的海岸线一望无际，笼罩在迷雾之中。

SYN cover, coat, wrap, blanket
