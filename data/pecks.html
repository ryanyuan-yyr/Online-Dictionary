peck
pek pɛk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense pecks, present participle pecking, past tense , past participle pecked
1 VERB 啄；啄食 If a bird pecks at something or pecks something, it moves its beak forward quickly and bites at it. 啄；啄食 [Also VERB]

  • It was winter and the sparrows were pecking at whatever they could find. [VERB + at]

    时值冬季，麻雀啄食着能够找到的任何东西。

  • Chickens pecked in the dust. [VERB prep./adv.]

    鸡在尘土中啄食。

  • It pecked his leg. [VERB noun]

    它啄了他的腿。

  • They turn on their own kind and peck each other to death. [VERB noun prep.]

    它们开始攻击同类，拼命地互相啄着。

  • These birds peck off all the red flowers. [VERB noun with adv.]

    这些鸟啄掉了所有红色的花。

2 VERB 匆匆轻吻 If you peck someone on the cheek, you give them a quick, light kiss. 匆匆轻吻

  • Elizabeth walked up to him and pecked him on the cheek. [VERB noun + on]

    伊丽莎白走到他面前，在他脸颊上匆匆吻了一下。

  • She pecked his cheek. [VERB noun]

    她轻轻地吻了一下他的脸。

SYN kiss, plant a kiss, give someone a smacker, give someone a peck or kiss  
Peck is also a noun.

  • He gave me a little peck on the cheek.

    他匆匆轻吻了一下我的脸。
