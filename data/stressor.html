stress
stres strɛs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense stresses, present participle stressing, past tense , past participle stressed
1 VERB 强调;着重指出 If you stress a point in a discussion, you put extra emphasis on it because you think it is important. 强调;着重指出

  • The spokesman stressed that the measures did not amount to an overall ban. [VERB that]

    该发言人强调这些措施并不等同于全面禁止。

  • They also stress the need for improved employment opportunities, better transport and health care. [VERB noun]

    他们同时也强调需要增加就业机会，改善交通和医疗保健服务。

  • 'We're not saying we're outside and above all this,' he stresses. [VERB with quote]

    “我们并不是说我们对此不闻不问，”他强调道。

Stress is also a noun.

  • Japanese car makers are laying ever more stress on European sales.

    日本汽车制造商比以往任何时候都更加重视欧洲市场的销售情况。

2 N-VAR [oft under N] 精神压力;心理负担;紧张 If you feel under stress, you feel worried and tense because of difficulties in your life. 精神压力;心理负担;紧张

  • Katy could think clearly when not under stress.

    在没有精神压力的情况下凯蒂思路清晰。

  • Of course, the British will suffer such daily stresses patiently.

    当然，英国人将会耐心地承受这些日常压力。

  • ...a wide range of stress-related problems.

    与精神压力有关的一系列问题

SYN strain, pressure, worry, tension  
3 N-VAR 压力;应力 Stresses are strong physical pressures applied to an object. 压力;应力

  • Earthquakes happen when stresses in rock are suddenly released as the rocks fracture.

    当岩石碎裂时其承受的压力突然释放出来，地震就发生了。

4 VERB 重读;用重音读 If you stress a word or part of a word when you say it, you put emphasis on it so that it sounds slightly louder. 重读;用重音读

  • 'Sit down,' she replied, stressing each word. [VERB noun]

    “坐下，”她一字一顿地说道。

Stress is also a noun.

  • ...the misplaced stress on the first syllable of this last word.

    最后这一个单词第一个音节不应重读
