tame
teɪm teɪm
Word forms: comparative tamer, superlative tamest, 3rd person singular present tense tames, present participle taming, past tense , past participle tamed
1 ADJ-GRADED (动物)驯化的，驯服的 A tame animal or bird is one that is not afraid of humans. (动物)驯化的，驯服的

  • The deer never became tame; they would run away if you approached them.

    那些鹿一直没有被驯服：你一靠近，它们就跑开。

2 ADJ-GRADED 乏味的;没劲的 If you say that something or someone is tame, you are criticizing them for being weak and uninteresting, rather than forceful or shocking. 乏味的;没劲的 [disapproval]

  • Some of today's political demonstrations look rather tame.

    如今有些政治游行显得毫无激情。

  • The report was pretty tame stuff.

    这份报告平淡无奇。

SYN unexciting, boring, dull, bland  
tamely

  • There was no excuse though when Thomas shot tamely wide from eight yards.

    托马斯在 8 码外一脚软弱无力的射门将球打飞，这回的确是没什么借口可言。

3 VERB 驯化，驯服(野生动物或鸟) If someone tames a wild animal or bird, they train it not to be afraid of humans and to do what they say. 驯化，驯服(野生动物或鸟)

  • The Amazons were believed to have been the first to tame horses. [VERB noun]

    据说亚马孙人最先驯化了马。

SYN domesticate, train, break in, gentle  
4 VERB 制服;控制;驾驭 If you tame someone or something that is dangerous, uncontrolled, or likely to cause trouble, you bring them under control. 制服;控制;驾驭

  • Two regiments of cavalry were called out to tame the crowds. [VERB noun]

    调集了两个装甲兵团去威慑、控制人群。

SYN subdue, suppress, master, discipline
