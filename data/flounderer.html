flounder
flaʊndəʳ ˈflaʊndər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense flounders, present participle floundering, past tense , past participle floundered
The plural form for meaning 4 can be either flounder or flounders. 义项 4 的复数形式可以用 flounder 或 flounders。
1 VERB 深陷困境;困难重重 If something is floundering, it has many problems and may soon fail completely. 深陷困境;困难重重

  • What a pity that his career was left to flounder. [VERB]

    多么可惜，他的事业深陷困局。

  • The economy was floundering. [VERB]

    经济深陷困境。

SYN falter, struggle, stall, slow down  
2 VERB 踌躇;不知所措 If you say that someone is floundering, you are criticizing them for not making decisions or for not knowing what to say or do. 踌躇;不知所措 [disapproval]

  • Right now, you've got a president who's floundering, trying to find some way to get his campaign jump-started. [VERB]

    现在，你们的总统正踌躇不前，试图找到某种办法来推动他的竞选活动的开展。

  • I know that you're floundering around, trying to grasp at any straw. [V around]

    我知道你正不知所措，是在病急乱投医。

SYN dither, struggle, blunder, be confused  
3 VERB (在水中或泥里)挣扎 If you flounder in water or mud, you move in an uncontrolled way, trying not to sink. (在水中或泥里)挣扎 [Also VERB]

  • Three men were floundering about in the water. [VERB adv./prep.]

    有 3 个人在水中挣扎。

SYN struggle, toss, thrash, plunge  
4 N-VAR 比目鱼 A flounder is a flat fish that you can eat. 比目鱼
Flounder is this fish eaten as food.(供食用的)比目鱼，偏口

  • Mr. Dambar had loaded his plate with stuffed flounder.

    但拔先生的餐盘里盛满了填馅的比目鱼。
