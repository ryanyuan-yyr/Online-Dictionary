centrepiece
sentəʳpiːs ˈsɛntəˌpiːs
Word forms: plural centrepieces
in AM, use 美国英语用 centerpiece
1 N-COUNT 最好(或最有趣)的部分;精华 The centrepiece of something is the best or most interesting part of it. 最好(或最有趣)的部分;精华

  • The centrepiece of the plan is the idea of regular referendums, initiated by voters. [+ of]

    这个计划的亮点是选民们动议的定期举行公民投票。

SYN focus, highlight, hub, cynosure [literary]  
2 N-COUNT (尤指餐桌的)中心装饰品 A centrepiece is an ornament which you put in the middle of something, especially a dinner table. (尤指餐桌的)中心装饰品
