humanity
hjuːmænɪti hjuˈmænəti juˈmænəti
Word forms: plural humanities
1 N-UNCOUNT 人类;（总称）人 All the people in the world can be referred to as humanity . 人类;（总称）人

  • They face charges of committing crimes against humanity.

    他们被控犯有反人类罪。

  • ...a young lawyer full of illusions and love of humanity.

    满脑子幻想并且博爱的年轻律师

SYN the human race, man, mankind, people  
2 N-UNCOUNT [with poss] 人性;人的属性 A person's humanity is their state of being a human being, rather than an animal or an object. 人性;人的属性 [formal]

  • ...a man who's almost lost his humanity in his bitter hatred of his rivals.

    因痛恨竞争对手而几乎丧失人性的男子

3 N-UNCOUNT 人道;仁慈;博爱 Humanity is the quality of being kind, thoughtful, and sympathetic towards others. 人道;仁慈;博爱

  • Her speech showed great maturity and humanity.

    她的演讲思想高度成熟，并且洋溢着博爱精神。

SYN kindness, charity, compassion, understanding  
4 N-PLURAL （历史、哲学、文学等）人文学科 The humanities are the subjects such as history, philosophy, and literature which are concerned with human ideas and behaviour. （历史、哲学、文学等）人文学科

  • ...students majoring in the humanities.

    主修人文学科的学生
