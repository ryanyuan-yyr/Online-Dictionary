apprehension
æprɪhenʃən ˌæprəˈhɛnʃən
Word forms: plural apprehensions
1 N-VAR 担心；忧虑；恐惧 Apprehension is a feeling of fear that something bad may happen. 担心；忧虑；恐惧 [formal]

  • It reflects real anger and apprehension about the future.

    它反映出对未来真实的愤怒和恐惧。

  • I tensed every muscle in my body in apprehension.

    我吓得全身肌肉紧绷。

SYN anxiety, concern, fear, worry  
2 N-UNCOUNT 逮捕；拘押 The apprehension of someone who is thought to be a criminal is their capture or arrest by the police. 逮捕；拘押 [formal]

  • ...information leading to the apprehension of the alleged killer. [+ of]

    致使凶杀嫌犯被捕的信息

SYN arrest, catching, capture, taking  
3 N-UNCOUNT [oft NOUN of n] 理解;领悟 The apprehension of something is awareness and understanding of it. 理解;领悟 [formal]

  • ...the sudden apprehension of something familiar as something alien.

    突然间领悟到熟悉的事物原来如此陌生

SYN awareness, understanding, knowledge, intelligence
