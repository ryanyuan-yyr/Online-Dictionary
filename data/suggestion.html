suggestion
sədʒestʃən səgdʒ- səgˈdʒɛstʃən səˈdʒɛstʃən
Word forms: plural suggestions
1 N-COUNT 建议;提议 If you make a suggestion, you put forward an idea or plan for someone to think about. 建议;提议 [Also + for]

  • The dietitian was helpful, making suggestions as to how I could improve my diet.

    这位营养学家就如何改善饮食给我提出了些建议，对我很有帮助。

  • Perhaps he'd followed her suggestion of a stroll to the river. [+ of]

    也许他已经听从了她的建议去河边散步了。

  • I have lots of suggestions for the park's future.

    对于公园未来的规划我有很多提议。

SYN recommendation, proposal, proposition, plan  
2 N-COUNT [oft NOUN that] 暗示;示意 A suggestion is something that a person says which implies that something is the case. 暗示;示意

  • We reject any suggestion that the law needs amending.

    对于任何暗示该法律需要修改的观点我们都拒绝接受。

  • There are suggestions that he might be supported by the Socialists.

    有人认为他可能会受到社会党的支持。

3 N-SING [NOUN that] 表明;征兆;根据 If there is no suggestion that something is the case, there is no reason to think that it is the case. 表明;征兆;根据 [Also + of]

  • There is no suggestion whatsoever that the two sides are any closer to agreeing.

    没有任何迹象表明双方将会达成一致。

  • There is absolutely no suggestion of any mainstream political party involvement.

    没有任何依据表明有任何主要政党参与其中。

SYN hint, implication, insinuation, intimation  
4 N-COUNT [usually singular] 些微;些许 If there is a suggestion of something, there is a slight amount or sign of it. 些微;些许

  • ...that fashionably faint suggestion of a tan. [+ of]

    略带一点流行的古铜色

  • ...a firm, well-sprung mattress with not one suggestion of a sag. [+ of]

    结实、没有一点凹陷的高弹力床垫

SYN trace, touch, hint, shadow  
5 N-UNCOUNT 联想;暗示 Suggestion means giving people a particular idea by associating it with other ideas. 联想;暗示

  • The power of suggestion is very strong.

    暗示的力量是无比强大的。
