bleach
bliːtʃ blitʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense bleaches, present participle bleaching, past tense , past participle bleached
1 VERB 漂白；使变白 If you bleach something, you use a chemical to make it white or pale in colour. 漂白；使变白

  • These products don't bleach the hair. [VERB noun]

    这些产品不会使头发变白。

  • Commercial flour is bleached artificially. [VERB noun]

    市面上的面粉是经过人工漂白的。

  • ...bleached pine tables. [VERB-ed]

    漂白了的松木桌子

  • ...a bleaching agent. [VERB-ing]

    漂白剂

SYN lighten, wash out, blanch, peroxide  
2 V-ERG 晒白；（使）褪色；（使）变白 If the sun bleaches something, or something bleaches, its colour gets paler until it is almost white. 晒白；（使）褪色；（使）变白

  • The tree's roots are stripped and hung to season and bleach. [VERB]

    树根剥皮后挂起来风干晒白。

  • The sun will bleach the hairs on your face. [VERB noun]

    太阳会使你脸上的汗毛发白。

  • He has hair which is naturally black but which has been bleached by the sun. [VERB noun]

    他的头发本来是黑色的，不过被太阳晒白了。

  • ...bleached seaweed. [VERB-ed]

    晒得发白的海草

SYN whiten, fade, pale, lighten  
3 N-MASS 漂白剂 Bleach is a chemical that is used to make cloth white, or to clean things thoroughly and kill germs. 漂白剂
