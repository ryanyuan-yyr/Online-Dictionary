excommunicate ekskəmjuːnɪkeɪt ˌɛkskəˈmjunɪˌkeɪt ˌɛkskəˈmjunɪkɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense excommunicates, present participle excommunicating, past tense , past participle excommunicated
VERB （天主教或东正教）将…逐出教会，开除…的教籍 If a Roman Catholic or member of the Orthodox Church is excommunicated, it is publicly and officially stated that the person is no longer allowed to be a member of the Church. This is a punishment for some very great wrong that they have done. （天主教或东正教）将…逐出教会，开除…的教籍

  • Eventually, he was excommunicated along with his mentor. [be VERB-ed]

    最后，他和他的导师一起被开除教籍。

  • In 1766 he excommunicated the village for its 'depraved diversion.' [VERB noun]

    1766年，他以“堕落地享乐”为由将那个村庄逐出教会。

SYN expel, ban, remove, exclude
