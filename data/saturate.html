saturate
sætʃʊreɪt ˈsætʃəˌreɪt ˈsætʃərɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense saturates, present participle saturating, past tense , past participle saturated
1 VERB 使充满;使饱和 If people or things saturate a place or object, they fill it completely so that no more can be added. 使充满;使饱和

  • In the last days before the vote, both sides are saturating the airwaves. [VERB noun]

    选举前最后几天，竞选双方占用了所有的广播电台和电视台。

  • As the market was saturated with goods and the economy became more balanced, inflation went down. [be VERB-ed + with]

    随着市场上商品饱和，经济趋于平衡，通货膨胀就减弱了。

SYN flood, overwhelm, swamp, overrun  
saturated

  • As the domestic market becomes saturated, firms begin to export the product.

    由于国内市场饱和，各公司开始外销。

2 VERB [usually passive] 浸透;渗透;使湿透 If someone or something is saturated, they become extremely wet. 浸透;渗透;使湿透 [Also VERB n]

  • If the filter has been saturated with motor oil, it should be discarded and replaced. [be VERB-ed]

    过滤器如果已被机油浸透，则应丢弃并换上新的。

SYN soak, steep, drench, seep  
saturated

  • His work clothes, having become saturated with oil, had to be cleaned.

    他的工作服已经被油浸透，该洗洗了。
