famously
feɪməsli ˈfeɪməsli
1 ADV [usually ADVERB adjective, oft ADVERB with verb] (通常指由于突出或极端而)著名地，出名地 You use famously to refer to a fact that is well known, usually because it is remarkable or extreme. (通常指由于突出或极端而)著名地，出名地

  • Authors are famously ignorant about the realities of publishing.

    作者们不了解出版界的实际情况，这是人所共知的。

  • As Wren's epitaph famously declares, the cathedral itself is his monument.

    众所周知，雷恩的墓志铭中写道，大教堂本身就是他的纪念碑。

2 ADV [ADVERB after verb] 友好地;融洽地 If you get on or get along famously with someone, you are very friendly with each other and enjoy meeting and being together. 友好地;融洽地 [informal, old-fashioned]

  • I got on famously with Leary from the first time we met.

    我和利里第一次见面就相处甚欢。
