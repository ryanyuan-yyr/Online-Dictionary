total
toʊtəl ˈtoʊtəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense totals, present participle totalling, past tense , past participle totalled
1 N-COUNT 总数;总额;合计 A total is the number that you get when you add several numbers together or when you count how many things there are in a group. 总数;总额;合计

  • The companies have a total of 1,776 employees. [+ of]

    这些公司总计有 1,776 名员工。

SYN sum, mass, entirety, grand total  
2 ADJ [ADJ n] 总的;总计的;全部的 The total number or cost of something is the number or cost that you get when you add together or count all the parts in it. 总的;总计的;全部的

  • They said that the total number of cows dying from BSE would be twenty thousand…

    他们说患疯牛病死亡的牛的总数将达 2 万头。

  • The total cost of the project would be more than $240 million.

    该项目的总成本会超过 2.4 亿美元。

SYN entire, full, whole, complete  
3 PHRASE 总共;总计 If there are a number of things in total, there are that number when you count or add them all together. 总共;总计

  • I was with my husband for eight years in total.

    我和丈夫在一起的时间总共有 8 年。

  • In total, 45 per cent of adults in Britain are exposed to tobacco smoke at home.

    英国总计有 45% 的成人在家里遭受到烟草烟雾的侵害。

4 VERB 总数达;共计 If several numbers or things total a certain figure, that figure is the total of all the numbers or all the things. 总数达;共计

  • The unit's exports will total $85 million this year. [VERB amount]

    该部门今年的出口总额将达 8,500 万美元。

  • They will compete for prizes totalling nearly £3,000. [VERB amount]

    他们将为总额近 3,000 英镑的奖金展开争夺。

SYN amount to, make, come to, reach  
5 VERB 计算…的总和;把…加起来 When you total a set of numbers or objects, you add them all together. 计算…的总和;把…加起来

  • They haven't totalled the exact figures. [VERB noun]

    他们还没有对确切数据进行总计。

SYN add up, work out, sum up, compute  
6 ADJ [usually ADJ n] 完全的;彻底的;绝对的 You can use total to emphasize that something is as great in extent, degree, or amount as it possibly can be. 完全的;彻底的;绝对的 [emphasis]

  • You were a total failure if you hadn't married by the time you were about twenty-three…

    如果到了 23 岁左右还没结婚，你就是个彻底的失败者。

  • There was an almost total lack of management control…

    管理控制几乎是完全缺失的。

  • Why should we trust a total stranger?.

    我们为什么要信任一个素昧平生的人呢？

  • I have total confidence that things will change.

    事情会有所改变的，我对此信心十足。

totally

  • Young people want something totally different from the old ways.

    年轻人想要与以往截然不同的东西。

  • The fire totally destroyed the top floor.

    大火彻底烧毁了顶层。
