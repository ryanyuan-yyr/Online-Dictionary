baby
beɪbi ˈbeɪbi
Word forms: plural babies
1 N-COUNT 婴儿；婴孩 A baby is a very young child, especially one that cannot yet walk or talk. 婴儿；婴孩

  • She used to take care of me when I was a baby.

    我还在襁褓中时，她曾经照顾过我。

  • My wife has just had a baby.

    我妻子刚生了孩子。

  • Claire had to dress her baby sister.

    克莱尔得给年幼的妹妹穿衣服。

SYN child, infant, babe, wean [Scottish]  
2 N-COUNT [usually NOUN n] （动物）幼崽；幼兽；幼鸟 A baby animal is a very young animal. （动物）幼崽；幼兽；幼鸟

  • ...a baby elephant.

    幼象

  • ...baby birds.

    雏鸟

3 ADJ [ADJ n] （蔬菜）幼嫩的 Baby vegetables are vegetables picked when they are very small. （蔬菜）幼嫩的

  • Serve with baby new potatoes.

    配上小个的新土豆。

SYN small, little, minute, tiny  
4 N-VOC; N-COUNT [usually singular] 宝贝儿（昵称，尤用于年轻女性） Some people use baby as an affectionate way of addressing someone, especially a young woman, or referring to them. 宝贝儿（昵称，尤用于年轻女性） [informal]

  • You have to wake up now, baby.

    该起床了，宝贝儿。

  • He was confused, poor baby.

    他都懵了，可怜的家伙。

SYN darling, love, dear, dearest  
5 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 把婴儿和洗澡水一起倒掉；将精华连同糟粕一起丢弃；不分好坏地全盘抛弃 If you throw the baby out with the bath water, you lose the good parts of something as well as the bad parts, because you reject it as a whole instead of just removing what is bad. 把婴儿和洗澡水一起倒掉；将精华连同糟粕一起丢弃；不分好坏地全盘抛弃 [informal]
6 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 替他人擦屁股；替别人干苦差事 If you are left holding the baby, you are put in a situation where you are responsible for something, often in an unfair way because other people fail or refuse to take responsibility for it. 替他人擦屁股；替别人干苦差事 [British, informal]

  • You go off round the world, and leave me to hold the baby.

    你满世界地跑，把苦差事都扔给我。
