résumé
rezjʊmeɪ ˈrɛzjʊˌmeɪ -zʊm- ˈrɛzəˌmeɪ ˈreɪzuˌmeɪ ˈreɪzjuˌmeɪ
(also resumé)Word forms: plural résumés
1 N-COUNT 摘要;A résumé is a short account, either spoken or written, of something that has happened or that someone has said or written. 摘要;

  • I will leave with you a résumé of his most recent speech.

    我将留给你一份他最近一次讲演的摘要.

SYN summary, synopsis, abstract, précis  
2 N-COUNT 简历;Your résumé is a brief account of your personal details, your education, and the jobs you have had. You are often asked to send a résumé when you are applying for a job. 简历; [mainly US]
resume
rɪzjuːm -zuːm rɪˈzum rɪˈzjum
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense resumes, present participle resuming, past tense , past participle resumed
1 V-ERG （使）重新开始;（使）继续进行 If you resume an activity or if it resumes, it begins again. （使）重新开始;（使）继续进行 [formal]

  • After the war he resumed his duties at Emmanuel College. [VERB noun]

    战争过后，他重新开始了在伊曼纽尔学院的工作。

  • The search is expected to resume early today. [VERB]

    预计搜寻工作会于今天早些时候重新开始。

SYN take up again, return to, come back to, assume again  
resumption

  • It is premature to speculate about the resumption of negotiations.

    现在猜测谈判是否会重新开始还为时过早。

2 VERB 恢复(职位);回到（座位） If you resume your seat or position, you return to the seat or position you were in before you moved. 恢复(职位);回到（座位） [formal]

  • 'I changed my mind,' Blanche said, resuming her seat. [VERB noun]

    “我改变主意了，”布兰奇说着回到她的座位上。

3 VERB 继续说;接着说 If someone resumes, they begin speaking again after they have stopped for a short time. 继续说;接着说 [written]

  • 'Hey, Judith,' he resumed, 'tell me all about yourself.' [VERB with quote]

    “嘿，朱迪思，”他接着说，“跟我谈谈你自己吧。”
