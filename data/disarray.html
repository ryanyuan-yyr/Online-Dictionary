disarray
dɪsəreɪ ˌdɪsəˈreɪ
1 N-UNCOUNT [oft in NOUN] 混乱;紊乱 If people or things are in disarray, they are disorganized and confused. 混乱;紊乱

  • The nation is in disarray following rioting led by the military.

    军队发动暴乱之后国家陷入混乱中。

  • 12,000 crossed the border in one day alone, throwing evacuation plans into disarray.

    仅一天中就有1.2万人越过边境，这使得撤退计划全被打乱了。

  • This would add to the disarray in world markets.

    这会给世界市场乱上添乱。

2 N-UNCOUNT [oft in NOUN] 凌乱;不整齐 If things or places are in disarray, they are in a very untidy state. 凌乱;不整齐

  • She was left lying on her side and her clothes were in disarray.

    发现她时她侧躺着，且衣衫不整。

  • He found the room in disarray, with food dumped on the floor and drawers pulled open.

    他发现屋里凌乱不堪，地上丢弃着食物，抽屉大开。

SYN untidiness, state, mess, chaos
