backfire
bækfaɪəʳ ˌbækˈfaɪə -faɪr ˈbækˌfaɪr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense backfires, present participle backfiring, past tense , past participle backfired
1 VERB 事与愿违；适得其反 If a plan or project backfires, it has the opposite result to the one that was intended. 事与愿违；适得其反

  • The President's tactics could backfire. [VERB]

    总统的策略可能会适得其反。

  • It all backfired on me! [V + on/against]

    一切都与我的愿望背道而驰！

SYN fail, founder, flop [informal] , rebound  
2 VERB （车辆或其发动机）逆火，回火 When a motor vehicle or its engine backfires, it produces an explosion in the exhaust pipe. （车辆或其发动机）逆火，回火

  • The car backfired. [VERB]

    车回火了。

SYN misfire
