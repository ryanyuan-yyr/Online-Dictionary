mingle
mɪŋgəl ˈmɪŋgəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense mingles, present participle mingling, past tense , past participle mingled
1 V-RECIP （声音、气味等）混合，混杂;(感情）交织,交错 If things such as sounds, smells, or feelings mingle, they become mixed together but are usually still recognizable. （声音、气味等）混合，混杂;(感情）交织,交错

  • Now the cheers and applause mingled in a single sustained roar. [VERB]

    现在，欢呼声和掌声汇成了一片持续的喧闹声。

  • Foreboding mingled with his excitement. [VERB + with]

    他在兴奋的同时又夹杂着一种不祥的预感。

SYN mix, combine, blend, merge  
2 V-RECIP 应酬;交际 At a party, if you mingle with the other people there, you move around and talk to them. 应酬;交际

  • Go out of your way to mingle with others at the wedding. [VERB + with]

    去跟婚礼上的其他人聊聊。

  • Guests ate and mingled. [VERB]

    客人们边用餐边攀谈。

  • Alison mingled for a while and then went to where Douglas stood with John. [VERB]

    艾利森去应酬了一会儿，然后走到道格拉斯和约翰站着的地方。

SYN associate, circulate, hang out [informal] , consort
