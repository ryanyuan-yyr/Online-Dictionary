croak
kroʊk kroʊk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense croaks, present participle croaking, past tense , past participle croaked
1 VERB (青蛙或鸟)发出低哑叫声，呱呱叫 When a frog or bird croaks, it makes a harsh, low sound. (青蛙或鸟)发出低哑叫声，呱呱叫

  • Thousands of frogs croaked in the reeds by the riverbank. [VERB]

    上千只青蛙在河岸边的芦苇荡里呱呱叫。

Croak is also a noun.

  • ...the guttural croak of the frogs.

    粗嘎的蛙鸣声

2 VERB (人)用低哑的声音说 If someone croaks something, they say it in a low, rough voice. (人)用低哑的声音说

  • Tiller moaned and managed to croak, 'Help me.'. [VERB with quote]

    蒂勒呻吟着，用沙哑的声音勉强喊出了一声“救救我”。

  • She croaked something unintelligible. [VERB noun]

    她声音沙哑地说了些含糊不清的话。

SYN rasp, gasp, grunt, wheeze  
Croak is also a noun.

  • His voice was just a croak.

    他的嗓音沙哑。
