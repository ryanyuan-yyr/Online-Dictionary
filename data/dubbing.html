dub
dʌb dʌb dʌb dʊb
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dubs, present participle dubbing, past tense , past participle dubbed
1 VERB 给…起绰号;把…称作 If someone or something is dubbed a particular thing, they are given that description or name. 给…起绰号;把…称作 [journalism]

  • ...the man whom the Labour opposition dubbed as the 'no change Prime Minister'. [VERB noun + as]

    被工党反对派戏称为“不变首相”的男人

  • At the height of her career, Orson Welles dubbed her 'the most exciting woman in the world'. [VERB noun noun]

    奥森·韦尔斯把处在事业巅峰的她戏称为“世上最令人兴奋的女人”。

SYN name, call, term, style  
2 VERB [usually passive] 为(影片等)配音;译制 If a film or soundtrack in a foreign language is dubbed, a new soundtrack is added with actors giving a translation. 为(影片等)配音;译制 [Also be VERB-ed]

  • It was dubbed into Spanish for Mexican audiences. [be VERB-ed + into]

    它被译制成西班牙语以方便墨西哥观众观看。

  • ...a badly dubbed foreign film. [VERB-ed]

    配音很差劲的外国电影
