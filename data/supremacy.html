supremacy
suːpreməsi səˈprɛməsi sʊˈprɛməsi suˈprɛməsi
1 N-UNCOUNT [usually with poss] 至高无上;最大权力;霸权 If one group of people has supremacy over another group, they have more political or military power than the other group. 至高无上;最大权力;霸权 [Also + over]

  • The conservative old guard had re-established its political supremacy.

    守旧的保守派已经重新掌控了政治大权。

  • The president has been able to assert his ultimate supremacy over the prime minister.

    总统行使了高于总理的最高决策权。

SYN domination, dominance, ascendancy, sovereignty  
2 N-UNCOUNT [usually with poss] 优势 If someone or something has supremacy over another person or thing, they are better. 优势 [Also + over]

  • In the United States Open final, Graf retained overall supremacy.

    在美国网球公开赛决赛中，格拉芙始终保持优势。
