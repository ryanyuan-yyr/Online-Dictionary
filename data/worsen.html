worsen
wɜːʳsən ˈwɜrsən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense worsens, present participle worsening, past tense , past participle worsened
V-ERG （使）变得更坏(或更糟);（使）恶化 If a bad situation worsens or if something worsens it, it becomes more difficult, unpleasant, or unacceptable. （使）变得更坏(或更糟);（使）恶化

  • The security forces had to intervene to prevent the situation worsening. [VERB]

    安全部队不得不介入，以防止局势进一步恶化。

  • These options would actually worsen the economy and add to the deficit. [VERB noun]

    这些选择实际上只会造成经济形势更加恶化，赤字愈见增加。

  • They remain stranded in freezing weather and rapidly worsening conditions. [VERB-ing]

    他们仍然被困着，天气严寒，情况迅速恶化。

SYN aggravate, damage, exacerbate, make worse  
worsening

  • This latest incident is bound to lead to a further worsening of relations between the two countries.

    这一最新事件肯定会导致两国关系进一步恶化。
