rankle
ræŋkəl ˈræŋkəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense rankles, present participle rankling, past tense , past participle rankled
VERB 使人发怒;激起怨恨 If an event or situation rankles, it makes you feel angry or bitter afterwards, because you think it was unfair or wrong. 使人发怒;激起怨恨

  • They paid him only £10 for it and it really rankled. [VERB]

    他们仅为此付给他10英镑，这真让他感到愤怒。

  • Britain's refusal to sell Portugal arms in 1937 still rankled with him. [VERB + with]

    1937年英国拒绝向葡萄牙销售武器的事情仍然令他愤慨。

  • The only thing that rankles me is what she says about Ireland. [VERB noun]

    唯一使我耿耿于怀的就是她那些关于爱尔兰的言论。

SYN annoy, anger, irritate, gall
