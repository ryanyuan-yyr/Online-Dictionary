deliver
dɪlɪvəʳ dɪˈlɪvər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense delivers, present participle delivering, past tense , past participle delivered
1 VERB 递送;投递;运送 If you deliver something somewhere, you take it there. 递送;投递;运送 [Also VERB]

  • The Canadians plan to deliver more food to southern Somalia. [VERB noun + to]

    加拿大计划向索马里南部地区运送更多的食品。

  • The spy returned to deliver a second batch of classified documents. [VERB noun]

    那名间谍返回来递送第二批机密文件。

  • We were told the pizza would be delivered in 20 minutes. [VERB noun]

    我们被告知比萨饼 20 分钟后送到。

SYN bring, take, carry, bear  
2 VERB 履行;兑现;实现 If you deliver something that you have promised to do, make, or produce, you do, make, or produce it. 履行;兑现;实现

  • They have yet to show that they can really deliver working technologies. [VERB noun]

    他们尚未证明自己真的能够开发出实用技术。

  • ... proving they could deliver the vote in their areas. [VERB noun]

    证明他们能够得到自己选区的选票

  • We don't promise what we can't deliver. [VERB]

    我们承诺的就一定会兑现。

SYN fulfil, provide, give, produce  
3 VERB 托付;交托 If you deliver a person or thing into someone's care, you give them responsibility for that person or thing. 托付;交托 [formal]

  • Mrs Montgomery was delivered into Mr Hinchcliffe's care. [be V-ed + into/to]

    蒙哥马利夫人托给了欣奇利夫先生照顾。

  • David delivered Holly gratefully into the woman's outstretched arms. [V n + into/to]

    戴维非常感激地把霍利送进那个女人张开的双臂中。

  • He was led in in handcuffs and delivered over to me. [be V-ed over]

    他戴着手铐被带了过来，交到了我手里。

SYN hand over, present, commit, give up  
4 VERB 作(讲座);发表(讲话) If you deliver a lecture or speech, you give it in public. 作(讲座);发表(讲话) [formal]

  • The president will deliver a speech about schools. [VERB noun]

    总统将就学校问题发表讲话。

  • It is shocking that only one woman has delivered the lecture in 44 years. [VERB noun]

    在 44 年间仅有一名女性作过讲座，这真令人震惊。

SYN give, read, present, announce  
5 VERB 接生(婴儿) When someone delivers a baby, they help the woman who is giving birth to the baby. 接生(婴儿)

  • Her husband had to deliver the baby himself. [VERB noun]

    她丈夫不得不自己接生孩子。

6 VERB 给予(打击) If someone delivers a blow to someone else, they hit them. 给予(打击) [written] [Also VERB n]

  • Those blows to the head could have been delivered by a woman. [be VERB-ed]

    头部所受的那些重击有可能出自一个女人之手。

7 VERB 解救;拯救;使解脱 If someone delivers you from something, they rescue or save you from it. 解救;拯救;使解脱 [old-fashioned]

  • I have given thanks to God for delivering me from that pain. [VERB noun from noun]

    我感谢上帝把我从那种痛苦中拯救出来。

SYN release, free, save, rescue
