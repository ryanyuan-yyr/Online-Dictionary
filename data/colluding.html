collude
kəluːd kəˈlud
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense colludes, present participle colluding, past tense , past participle colluded
V-RECIP 共谋;勾结;串通 If one person colludes with another, they co-operate with them secretly or illegally. 共谋;勾结;串通 [disapproval] [Also pl-n V to-inf]

  • Several local officials are in jail on charges of colluding with the Mafia. [VERB + with]

    几位当地官员因被控勾结黑手党而入狱。

  • My mother colluded in the myth of him as the swanky businessman. [VERB + in]

    我母亲和别人串通，把他杜撰成一个阔气的商人。

  • The store's 'no refunds' policy makes it harder for dishonest cashiers and customers to collude. [VERB]

    商店“概不退货”的规定使得一些不老实的收银员和顾客更难以串通。

SYN conspire, scheme, plot, intrigue
