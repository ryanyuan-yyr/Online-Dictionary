extension
ɪkstenʃən ɪkˈstɛnʃən ɛkˈstɛnʃən
Word forms: plural extensions
1 N-COUNT （房屋的）扩建部分 An extension is a new room or building which is added to an existing building or group of buildings. （房屋的）扩建部分
2 N-COUNT （公路、铁路等的）延长线 An extension is a new section of a road or rail line that is added to an existing road or line. （公路、铁路等的）延长线

  • ...the Jubilee Line extension.

    银禧延长线

3 N-COUNT 展期；延长期 An extension is an extra period of time for which something lasts or is valid, usually as a result of official permission. 展期；延长期

  • He first entered Britain on a six-month visa, and was given a further extension of six months. [+ of]

    他最初持6个月的签证进入英国，后来又获得6个月的续签。

  • Ian Lentern has been granted a three-year extension.

    伊恩·伦特恩已经被批准延期3年。

SYN lengthening, extra time, continuation, postponement  
4 N-COUNT 扩大;扩展;延伸 Something that is an extension of something else is a development of it that includes or affects more people, things, or activities. 扩大;扩展;延伸

  • Many Filipinos see the bases as an extension of American colonial rule. [+ of]

    很多菲律宾人视这些基地为美国殖民统治的延续。

  • That's the logical extension of my approach.

    那是对我的方法的合乎逻辑的扩展。

5 N-COUNT 电话分机（亦用书面缩略ext.） An extension is a telephone line that is connected to the switchboard of a company or institution, and that has its own number. The written abbreviation ext. is also used. 电话分机（亦用书面缩略ext.）

  • She can get me on extension 308.

    她打308分机就能联系到我。

  • For further information, please contact 414 3925, extension 2253.

    详情请致电414 3925,转分机2253。

6 N-COUNT （设备的）延伸部分 An extension is a part which is connected to a piece of equipment in order to make it reach something further away. （设备的）延伸部分

  • ...a 30-foot extension cord... 30

    英尺的延长线

  • Some of the best extensions are made from sections of rod tube or drainpipe.

    最好的延长管中有些是用杆管或排水管制成的。
