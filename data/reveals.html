reveal
rɪviːl rɪˈvil
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense reveals, present participle revealing, past tense , past participle revealed
1 VERB 显示;表明;透露 To reveal something means to make people aware of it. 显示;表明;透露 [Also be V-ed as n]

  • She has refused to reveal the whereabouts of her daughter. [VERB noun]

    她不肯透露她女儿的行踪。

  • A survey of the British diet has revealed that a growing number of people are overweight. [VERB that]

    对英国人饮食进行的一项调查显示有越来越多的人超重。

  • After the fire, it was revealed that North Carolina officials had never inspected the factory. [it be VERB-ed that]

    大火过后人们发现北卡罗来纳州的官员从没对这家工厂进行过检查。

  • No test will reveal how much of the drug was taken. [VERB wh]

    没有哪项测试可以测出用药量。

SYN make known, disclose, give away, make public  
2 VERB 揭露;展示;使显露 If you reveal something that has been out of sight, you uncover it so that people can see it. 揭露;展示;使显露

  • A grey carpet was removed to reveal the original pine floor. [VERB noun]

    一块灰色的地毯被揭开，露出了原来的松木地板。

SYN show, display, bare, exhibit
