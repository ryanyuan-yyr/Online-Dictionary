plagiarize
pleɪdʒəraɪz ˈpleɪdʒəˌraɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense plagiarizes, present participle plagiarizing, past tense , past participle plagiarized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 plagiarise
VERB 剽窃；抄袭 If someone plagiarizes another person's idea or work, they use it or copy it and pretend that they thought of it or created it. 剽窃；抄袭 [Also VERB]

  • Moderates are plagiarizing his ideas in hopes of wooing voters. [VERB noun]

    温和派为讨好选民在盗用他的观点。

  • The poem employs as its first lines a verse plagiarized from a billboard. [V-ed + from]

    这首诗开头的几行抄袭了一个广告牌上的一节诗。

SYN copy, steal, appropriate, borrow
