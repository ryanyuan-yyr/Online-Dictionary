eyeball
aɪbɔːl ˈaɪˌbɔl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense eyeballs, present participle eyeballing, past tense , past participle eyeballed
1 N-COUNT 眼球；眼珠 Your eyeballs are your whole eyes, rather than just the part which can be seen between your eyelids. 眼球；眼珠
2 VERB 凝视;盯着看 If you eyeball someone or something, you stare at them. 凝视;盯着看 [informal]

  • The guard eyeballed him pretty hard despite his pass. [VERB noun]

    虽然他有通行证，卫兵还是直勾勾地盯着他。

3 PHRASE （与…）面对面的，剑拔弩张的 If you are eyeball to eyeball with someone, you are in their presence and involved in a meeting, dispute, or contest with them. You can also talk about having an eyeball to eyeball meeting or confrontation. （与…）面对面的，剑拔弩张的 [informal]

  • ...the young thug who stands eyeball-to-eyeball with his victim.

    与受害人对峙的那个小混混儿

  • ...proposals that the two armies end their eyeball to eyeball confrontation and withdraw.

    两军结束剑拔弩张的对峙、各自撤军的提议

4 PHRASE 深陷于…中 You use up to the eyeballs to emphasize that someone is in an undesirable state to a very great degree. 深陷于…中 [informal, emphasis]

  • ...driving around Los Angeles drugged up to the eyeballs.

    吸食大量毒品后在洛杉矶开车闲逛

  • He is out of a job and up to his eyeballs in debt.

    他失业了，而且债台高筑。
