blessed
blest blesɪd blest blesɪd blɛst blɛst ˈblɛsɪd
Pronounced /'blest/ for meaning 1, and /'blesɪd/ for meanings 2 and 3. 义项1读作/'blest/, 义项2读作/'blesɪd/
1 ADJ-GRADED [verb-link ADJ with n] 拥有…的；享有…的 If someone is blessed with a particular good quality or skill, they have that good quality or skill. 拥有…的；享有…的

  • Both are blessed with uncommon ability to fix things.

    两人在修理东西上都有一手绝活。

  • ...the son of a doctor and well blessed with money.

    非常富有的医生家的公子

SYN endowed, supplied, granted, favoured  
2 ADJ [ADJ n] 令人快乐的；可喜的；美好的 You use blessed to describe something that you think is wonderful, and that you are grateful for or relieved about. 令人快乐的；可喜的；美好的 [approval]

  • The birth of a live healthy baby is a truly blessed event.

    一个健康活泼的婴儿的出生真是一件可喜的事情。

  • Rainy weather brings blessed relief to hay fever victims.

    雨天会给枯草热患者带来欣喜，减轻他们的痛苦。

SYN happy, contented, glad [archaic] , merry  
blessedly

  • Most British election campaigns are blessedly brief.

    英国的大多数竞选活动持续时间都非常短。

3 ADJ [ADJ n] （强调生气）该死的，倒霉的，讨厌的 Some people use blessed to emphasize that they are annoyed about something. （强调生气）该死的，倒霉的，讨厌的 [British, old-fashioned, emphasis]

  • No-one knows a blessed thing.

    竟然没有一个人了解点儿情况。

SYN damned [slang] , bloody [slang] , freaking [slang , mainly US] , confounded
