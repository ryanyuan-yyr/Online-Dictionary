secure
sɪkjʊəʳ sɪˈkjʊr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense secures, present participle securing, past tense , past participle secured
1 VERB (经过努力)获得，争取到 If you secure something that you want or need, you obtain it, often after a lot of effort. (经过努力)获得，争取到 [formal] [Also VERB n + for]

  • Federal leaders continued their efforts to secure a ceasefire. [VERB noun]

    联邦领导人继续争取促成停火协议。

  • Graham's achievements helped secure him the job. [VERB noun noun]

    出色的成绩帮助格雷厄姆拿下了这份工作。

SYN obtain, get, acquire, land [informal]  
2 VERB 使…免遭攻击(或伤害);保卫;保护 If you secure a place, you make it safe from harm or attack. 使…免遭攻击(或伤害);保卫;保护 [formal]

  • Staff withdrew from the main part of the prison but secured the perimeter. [VERB noun]

    工作人员从监狱的主要区域撤出，但外围仍派人守卫。

  • The shed was secured by a hasp and staple fastener. [VERB noun]

    棚屋由一个搭钩和U型扣件固定着。

3 ADJ-GRADED 锁牢的;严密防守的 A secure place is tightly locked or well protected, so that people cannot enter it or leave it. 锁牢的;严密防守的

  • We shall make sure our home is as secure as possible from now on.

    我们应该确保家里从今往后尽量锁紧门窗。

SYN safe, protected, shielded, sheltered  
securely

  • He locked the heavy door securely and kept the key in his pocket.

    他把笨重的大门锁紧，将钥匙放在口袋里。

  • ...territory once securely under the control of the rebels.

    一度被叛军牢牢控制的领土

4 VERB 使固定;缚牢;系紧 If you secure an object, you fasten it firmly to another object. 使固定;缚牢;系紧

  • He helped her close the cases up, and then he secured the canvas straps as tight as they would go. [VERB noun]

    他帮她关上箱子，然后将帆布带子尽量系紧。

  • The frames are secured by horizontal rails to the back wall. [VERB noun]

    框架用横杆牢牢固定在后墙上。

SYN attach, stick, fix, bind  
5 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 固定不动的;绑紧的;系牢的 If an object is secure, it is fixed firmly in position. 固定不动的;绑紧的;系牢的

  • Check joints are secure and the wood is sound.

    检查一下连接处是否稳固及木料是否结实。

  • Shelves are only as secure as their fixings.

    架子的牢固程度取决于固件。

SYN fast, firm, fixed, tight  
securely

  • Ensure that the frame is securely fixed to the ground with bolts.

    确保用螺栓把框架牢牢地固定在地面上。

  • Builders must fasten down roofs of newly-built homes more securely.

    建造工人必须把新建房屋的屋顶固定得更牢靠些。

6 ADJ-GRADED 稳固的;稳定的;可靠的 If you describe something such as a job as secure, it is certain not to change or end. 稳固的;稳定的;可靠的

  • ...trade union demands for secure wages and employment.

    工会要求稳定的工资和工作。

  • Senior citizens long for a more predictable and secure future.

    老年人渴望有一个更稳定、更可预见的未来。

  • ...the failure of financial institutions once thought to be secure.

    一度被认为安全可靠的一些金融机构的倒闭

SYN reliable, definite, solid, absolute  
7 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (基础)坚固的，坚实的，可靠的 A secure base or foundation is strong and reliable. (基础)坚固的，坚实的，可靠的

  • He was determined to give his family a secure and solid base.

    他决定给家人一个牢固坚实的生活基础。

SYN protected, safe, guarded, sheltered  
8 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 安心的;心里踏实的;无忧无虑的 If you feel secure, you feel safe and happy and are not worried about life. 安心的;心里踏实的;无忧无虑的

  • She felt secure and protected when she was with him.

    和他在一起的时候，她感到一种被人保护的安全感。

  • The government must feel secure before it will be willing to make the concessions needed for peace.

    政府只有有足够的把够了才会愿意为和平作出让步。

SYN confident, relaxed, sure, easy  
9 VERB [usually passive] 为(借款等)作保 If a loan is secured, the person who lends the money may take property such as a house from the person who borrows the money if they fail to repay it. 为(借款等)作保 [business]

  • The loan is secured against your home. [be VERB-ed adv./prep.]

    这笔贷款以你的房子作为抵押。

  • His main task is to raise enough finance to repay secured loans. [VERB-ed]

    他的主要任务是筹集足够的资金以偿还抵押贷款。

SYN guarantee, protect, insure, ensure
