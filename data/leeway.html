leeway
liːweɪ ˈliˌweɪ
1 N-UNCOUNT 自由；自由空间；回旋余地 Leeway is the freedom that someone has to take the action they want to or to change their plans. 自由；自由空间；回旋余地

  • Rarely do schoolteachers have leeway to teach classes the way they want. [+ to-infinitive]

    鲜有学校老师可随心所欲地教书的情况。

  • The President said that he wanted to give states more leeway to pursue their own health-care reforms.

    总统说他希望给各州更多的医疗改革空间。

SYN room, play, space, margin  
2 N-UNCOUNT （需要弥补的）失去时间，落后差距 If you have leeway to make up, you have to work hard because you do not have much time to reach a particular goal. （需要弥补的）失去时间，落后差距 [mainly British]

  • He just could not make up the leeway from the earlier stages in which Ryan scored well.

    瑞安在前几轮得分很高，他现在再想赶也赶不上了。
