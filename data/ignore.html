ignore
ɪgnɔːʳ ɪgˈnɔr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense ignores, present participle ignoring, past tense , past participle ignored
1 VERB 不顾;不理;忽视 If you ignore someone or something, you pay no attention to them. 不顾;不理;忽视

  • She said her husband ignored her. [VERB noun]

    她说丈夫不关心她。

  • The government had ignored his views on the subject. [VERB noun]

    政府没有理睬他对这一问题的看法。

  • She ignored legal advice to drop the case. [VERB noun]

    她没有理睬律师关于放弃诉讼的建议。

  • For two decades her theatrical talents were ignored by the film industry. [VERB noun]

    她的舞台表演天赋在电影行业中被埋没了 20 年。

SYN pay no attention to, neglect, disregard, slight  
2 VERB 忽略;忽视 If you say that an argument or theory ignores an important aspect of a situation, you are criticizing it because it fails to consider that aspect or to take it into account. 忽略;忽视

  • Such arguments ignore the question of where ultimate responsibility lay. [VERB noun]

    这些争论忽略了最终责任由谁来负的问题。
