thrill
θrɪl θrɪl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense thrills, present participle thrilling, past tense , past participle thrilled
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] (突然而强烈的)激动，狂喜，恐惧 If something gives you a thrill, it gives you a sudden feeling of great excitement, pleasure, or fear. (突然而强烈的)激动，狂喜，恐惧

  • I can remember the thrill of not knowing what I would get on Christmas morning. [+ of]

    我还记得自己不知道圣诞节早晨会得到什么礼物时内心有多么激动。

  • It's a great thrill for a cricket-lover like me to play at the home of cricket. [+ for]

    对我这样的板球爱好者来说，能够在板球的故乡比赛是一件令人无比兴奋的事。

  • ...the realization that new adventures, new thrills, and new worlds lie ahead.

    认识到前面有新的冒险，新的惊喜和新的世界

SYN pleasure, charge [slang] , kick [informal] , glow  
2 V-ERG [usually singular] (使)狂喜;(使)激动;(使)极度兴奋 If something thrills you, or if you thrill at it, it gives you a feeling of great pleasure and excitement. (使)狂喜;(使)激动;(使)极度兴奋

  • The electric atmosphere both terrified and thrilled him. [VERB noun]

    极度紧张的气氛让他既害怕又兴奋。

  • The children will thrill at all their favourite characters. [V + at/to]

    孩子们看到所有那些他们最喜爱的人物形象会兴奋不已。

SYN pleasure, charge [slang] , kick [informal] , glow  
3 See also: thrilled  thrilling 
4 PHRASE 惊险刺激 If you refer to thrills and spills, you are referring to an experience which is exciting and full of surprises. 惊险刺激

  • Its prime audience lies in the 17 to 24 age group, and they want instant thrills and spills.

    它的主要观众的年龄集中在 17 岁到 24 岁之间，而这些人喜欢极速的惊险刺激。
