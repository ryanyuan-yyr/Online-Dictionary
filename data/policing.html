police
pəliːs pəˈlis
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense polices, present participle policing, past tense , past participle policed
1 N-SING-COLL [with singular or plural verb] 警察部门；警察机关；警方 The police are the official organization that is responsible for making sure that people obey the law. 警察部门；警察机关；警方

  • The police are also looking for a second car.

    警方也在寻找另一辆车。

  • Police say they have arrested twenty people following the disturbances.

    警方说骚乱发生后他们已逮捕了20人。

  • I noticed a police car shadowing us.

    我注意到一辆警车尾随着我们。

SYN the law [informal] , police force, constabulary, the fuzz [slang]  
2 N-PLURAL 警察 Police are men and women who are members of the official organization that is responsible for making sure that people obey the law. 警察

  • More than one hundred police have ringed the area.

    这一地区已被上百名警察包围。

3 VERB 维持…的治安；守卫；保卫 If the police or military forces police an area or event, they make sure that law and order is preserved in that area or at that event. 维持…的治安；守卫；保卫

  • ...the tiny UN observer force whose job it is to police the border.

    承担守卫边界职责的一小支联合国观察员部队

  • The march was heavily policed. [VERB-ed]

    出动了大批警察维持游行示威的秩序。

SYN control, patrol, guard, watch  
4See also:secret police 
policing
See also: community policing 

  • ...the policing of public places.

    公共场所治安的维持

5 VERB 管理；监督 If a person or group in authority polices a law or an area of public life, they make sure that what is done is fair and legal. 管理；监督

  • ...IMRO, the self-regulatory body that polices the investment management business. [VERB noun]

    投资管理监管组织，监督投资管理业务的自律机构

SYN monitor, check, observe, oversee  
policing

  • Policing of business courses varies widely.

    对商业行为的监管差别很大。
