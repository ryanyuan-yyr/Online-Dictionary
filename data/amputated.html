amputate
æmpjʊteɪt ˈæmpjuˌteɪt ˈæmpjəˌteɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense amputates, present participle amputating, past tense , past participle amputated
VERB 切断；截(肢) To amputate someone's arm or leg means to cut all or part of it off in an operation because it is diseased or badly damaged. 切断；截(肢) [Also VERB]

  • To save his life, doctors amputated his legs. [VERB noun]

    为了挽救他的生命，医生们截掉了他的双腿。

  • He had to have one leg amputated above the knee. [have noun VERB-ed]

    他的一条腿不得不从膝部以上截断。

SYN cut off, remove, separate, sever  
amputation

  • He lived only hours after the amputation.

    截肢后，他只活了几个小时。
