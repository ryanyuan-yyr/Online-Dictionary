feed
fiːd fid
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense feeds, present participle feeding, past tense , past participle fed
1 VERB 养;喂;饲养 If you feed a person or animal, you give them food to eat and sometimes actually put it in their mouths. 养;喂;饲养 [Also VERB pron-reflexive] [mainly British]

  • We brought along pieces of old bread and fed the birds. [VERB noun]

    我们拿了几片陈面包来喂鸟。

  • She fed him a cookie. [VERB noun noun]

    她给他吃了一块饼干。

  • In that part of the world you can feed cattle on almost any green vegetable or fruit. [V n + on/with]

    在那个地方，几乎任何绿色蔬菜或水果都可以给牛吃。

  • He spooned the ice cream into a cup and fed it to her. [VERB noun + to]

    他用勺子把冰激凌舀进杯子，喂给她吃。

Feed is also a noun.

  • She's had a good feed.

    她大吃了一顿。

feeding

  • The feeding of dairy cows has undergone a revolution.

    乳牛的喂养方法发生了巨大的改变。

2 VERB 供养，养活(家庭或群体) To feed a family or a community means to supply food for them. 供养，养活(家庭或群体)

  • Feeding a hungry family can be expensive. [VERB noun]

    养活饥肠辘辘的一家人花费很大。

  • ...a food reserve large enough to feed the Sudanese population for many months. [VERB noun]

    足够苏丹人吃好多个月的食物储备

SYN cater for, provide for, nourish, provide with food  
3 VERB (动物)吃食，进食 When an animal feeds, it eats or drinks something. (动物)吃食，进食

  • After a few days the caterpillars stopped feeding. [VERB]

    几天后毛虫停止进食。

  • Slugs feed on decaying plant and animal material. [V + on/off]

    蛞蝓以腐烂的动植物为食。

4 V-ERG 吃奶;给…喂奶 When a baby feeds, or when you feed it, it drinks breast milk or milk from a bottle. 吃奶;给…喂奶

  • When a baby is thirsty, it feeds more often. [VERB]

    婴儿渴的时候，吃奶就会更频繁。

  • I knew absolutely nothing about handling or feeding a baby. [VERB noun]

    我对如何抱婴儿、如何给婴儿喂食一无所知。

SYN eat, drink milk, take nourishment  
5 N-MASS [usually n NOUN] 饲料 Animal feed is food given to animals, especially farm animals. 饲料

  • The grain just rotted and all they could use it for was animal feed.

    谷物完全烂掉了，他们能将其派上的唯一用场就是作动物饲料。

  • ...poultry feed.

    家禽饲料

6 VERB 源源不断地供给;输送 To feed something to a place, means to supply it to that place in a steady flow. 源源不断地供给;输送

  • ...blood vessels that feed blood to the brain. [VERB noun prep.]

    将血液输送到大脑的血管

  • ...gas fed through pipelines. [VERB noun prep.]

    通过管道输送的燃气

SYN supply, take, send, carry  
7 VERB 将…放到，将…投入(容器或装置中) If you feed something into a container or piece of equipment, you put it into it. 将…放到，将…投入(容器或装置中)

  • He took the compact disc from her, then fed it into the player. [VERB noun prep.]

    他从她手里接过光盘，放入播放机中。

  • She was feeding documents into a paper shredder. [VERB noun prep.]

    她将文件放入碎纸机。

8 VERB 向…泄露(秘密);向…灌输,向…通报(虚假信息) If someone feeds you false or secret information, they deliberately tell it to you. 向…泄露(秘密);向…灌输,向…通报(虚假信息) [Also VERB n + to]

  • He was surrounded by people who fed him ghastly lies. [VERB noun noun]

    他周围的人都在给他灌输可怕的谎言。

  • At least one British officer was feeding him with classified information. [VERB noun + with]

    至少有一个英国官员在向他提供机密情报。

9 VERB 加深，加强(厌恶或渴望) If you feed someone's dislike or desire for something, you make it stronger. 加深，加强(厌恶或渴望)

  • The divorce was painfully public, feeding her dislike of the press. [VERB noun]

    离婚的事张扬出去，她十分痛苦，更加厌恶媒体了。

10 VERB 给…施肥 If you feed a plant, you add substances to it to make it grow well. 给…施肥

  • Feed plants to encourage steady growth. [VERB noun]

    给植物施肥促使其不断生长。

11 VERB (因…而)壮大;(从…中)得到滋养 If one thing feeds on another, it becomes stronger as a result of the other thing's existence. (因…而)壮大;(从…中)得到滋养

  • The drinking and the guilt fed on each other. [VERB + on]

    酗酒和犯罪彼此助长。

12 VERB 将(信息)输入(计算机) To feed information into a computer means to gradually put it into it. 将(信息)输入(计算机)

  • An automatic weather station feeds information on wind direction to the computer. [V n + into/to]

    自动化气象站将风向信息输入计算机。
