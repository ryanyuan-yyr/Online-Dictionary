fulsome
fʊlsəm ˈfʊlsəm
ADJ-GRADED (赞扬、道歉、感激等)虚伪的，夸张的，过度的 If you describe expressions of praise, apology, or gratitude as fulsome, you disapprove of them because they are exaggerated and elaborate, so that they sound insincere. (赞扬、道歉、感激等)虚伪的，夸张的，过度的 [disapproval]

  • Newspapers have been fulsome in their praise of the former president.

    报纸上对前总统都是些溢美之词。

SYN extravagant, excessive, over the top, sickening  
fulsomely

  • She chatted to them about the show and praised them fulsomely.

    她和他们聊起了演出，把他们捧上了天。
