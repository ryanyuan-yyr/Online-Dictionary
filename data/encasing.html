encase
ɪnkeɪs ɛnˈkeɪs ɪnˈkeɪs
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense encases, present participle encasing, past tense , past participle encased
1 VERB 把…包住;把…封起来 If a person or an object is encased in something, they are completely covered or surrounded by it. 把…包住;把…封起来

  • When nuclear fuel is manufactured it is encased in metal cans. [be VERB-ed + in]

    核燃料生产出来后被装在金属罐中。

  • These weapons also had a heavy brass guard which encased almost the whole hand. [VERB noun]

    这些武器还有一个沉重的黄铜护手，几乎把整个手都包住了。

  • It will actually be cheaper to leave the machine encased in concrete to one side of the tunnel, than to take it apart and bring it back to Britain. [VERB-ed]

    事实上，将机器用混凝土封存在隧道一侧比将其拆开运回英国更加便宜。

  • The original plan was to encase a small amount of a radioactive substance in a protective steel container. [V n + in/with]

    原先计划是要将少量的放射性物质封存在一个钢质防护罩中。
