deprive
dɪpraɪv diˈpraɪv dɪˈpraɪv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense deprives, present participle depriving, past tense , past participle deprived
VERB 剥夺;使丧失;使不能享有 If you deprive someone of something that they want or need, you take it away from them, or you prevent them from having it. 剥夺;使丧失;使不能享有

  • The disintegration of the Soviet Union deprived western intelligence agencies of their main enemies. [V n of n]

    苏联解体使西方情报机构丧失了主敌。

  • They've been deprived of the fuel necessary to heat their homes. [VERB noun + of]

    他们没法得到家里取暖所必需的燃料。

SYN dispossess, rob, strip, divest
