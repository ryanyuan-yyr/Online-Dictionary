jabber
dʒæbəʳ ˈdʒæbər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense jabbers, present participle jabbering, past tense , past participle jabbered
VERB 急促而含混不清地说；激动而口齿不清地说 If you say that someone is jabbering, you mean that they are talking very quickly and excitedly, and you cannot understand them. 急促而含混不清地说；激动而口齿不清地说 [disapproval]

  • The girl jabbered incomprehensibly. [VERB]

    那个女孩儿叽里咕噜地说着，谁也没听懂。

  • After a minute or two I left them there jabbering away. [VERB PREP.]

    没几分钟，我就走开了，留下他们在那儿叽里咕噜地说个不停。

  • ...a roomful of jabbering tourists. [VERB-ing]

    一屋子叽里呱啦说个不停的游客

SYN chatter, rabbit (on) [British , informal] , ramble, mumble
