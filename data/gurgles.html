gurgle
gɜːʳgəl ˈɡɜːɡəl ˈgɜrgəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense gurgles, present participle gurgling, past tense , past participle gurgled
1 VERB 潺潺地流；汩汩地流 If water is gurgling, it is making the sound that it makes when it flows quickly and unevenly through a narrow space. 潺潺地流；汩汩地流

  • ...a narrow stone-edged channel along which water gurgles unseen. [VERB adv./prep.]

    暗流潺潺的石砌狭窄水道

SYN ripple, lap, bubble, splash  
Gurgle is also a noun.

  • We could hear the swish and gurgle of water against the hull.

    我们能听到水拍打船体时的哗哗声。

2 VERB （尤指婴儿）发咯咯声 If someone, especially a baby, is gurgling, they are making a sound in their throat similar to the gurgling of water. （尤指婴儿）发咯咯声

  • Henry gurgles happily in his baby chair. [VERB]

    亨利在婴儿椅里开心地咯咯笑。

SYN burble, crow, chuckle, babble  
Gurgle is also a noun.

  • There was a gurgle of laughter on the other end of the line.

    从队伍的另一头传来咯咯的笑声。
