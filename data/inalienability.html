inalienable
ɪneɪljənəbəl ɪnˈeɪljənəbəl
ADJ [usually ADJ n] 不可剥夺的;不能让与的 If you say that someone has an inalienable right to something, you are emphasizing that they have a right to it which cannot be changed or taken away. 不可剥夺的;不能让与的 [formal, emphasis]

  • He said the republic now had an inalienable right to self-determination.

    他说共和国现在拥有不可剥夺的自决权。

  • ...respect for the inalienable rights of peoples and nations.

    对各民族、国家不可剥夺权利的尊重

SYN sacrosanct, absolute, unassailable, inherent
