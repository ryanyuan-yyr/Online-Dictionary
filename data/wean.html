wean
wiːn weɪn win
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense weans, present participle weaning, past tense , past participle weaned
1 VERB 给(婴儿或幼崽)断奶 When a baby or baby animal is weaned, its mother stops feeding it milk and starts giving it other food, especially solid food. 给(婴儿或幼崽)断奶

  • The baby would be weaned and she would bring it home. [be VERB-ed]

    婴儿断奶后，她会把婴儿带回家。

  • When would be the best time to start weaning my baby?. [VERB noun]

    什么时候开始给我的孩子断奶最好？

  • Phil took the labrador home and is weaning him off milk on to meat. [V n + off]

    菲尔把拉布拉多犬带回了家，正给它断奶，改喂肉食。

  • Once weaned, the piglets may be put in pens. [VERB-ed]

    一旦断了奶，小猪就可以放进猪圈了。

weaning

  • Weaning should be a gradual process.

    断奶要一步步来。

2 VERB 使放弃，使戒掉，使不再依赖(尤指恶习) If you wean someone off a habit or something they like, you gradually make them stop doing it or liking it, especially when you think is bad for them. 使放弃，使戒掉，使不再依赖(尤指恶习)

  • He was mellowed by a wife who weaned him off violence and drink. [V n off/from n]

    他妻子帮他戒除了动粗和嗜酒的恶习，使他变得成熟了。

  • You are given pills with small quantities of nicotine to wean you from cigarettes. [VERB noun + from]

    会给你一些含少量尼古丁的药片帮你戒除烟瘾。

  • Children should be weaned off television. [V n off/from n]

    孩子应该少看电视。

  • It's two years since I've seen Iain. I'm still trying to wean myself off him but it's hard. [V pron-refl off/from n]

    我已有两年没见过伊恩了。我试图让自己不再去想他，可很难做到。
