vogue
voʊg voʊg
1 N-SING 时尚;时髦;流行;风尚 If there is a vogue for something, it is very popular and fashionable. 时尚;时髦;流行;风尚

  • Despite the vogue for so-called health teas, there is no evidence that they are any healthier. [+ for]

    尽管现在流行所谓的健康茶饮，但没有证据证明这些茶更有益健康。

  • In Britain there's been something of a vogue for these books.

    在英国，这些书还算流行。

SYN fashion, trend, craze, style  
2 PHRASE 正在流行/变得流行 If something is in vogue, is very popular and fashionable. If it comes into vogue, it becomes very popular and fashionable. 正在流行/变得流行

  • Pale colours are much more in vogue than autumnal bronzes and coppers.

    浅色比黄褐色和红棕色这些秋季色彩更流行。

  • ...the hippie-ethnic look which came into vogue in the late 60s.

    60年代末流行的嬉皮士形象

SYN popular, big, fashionable, all the rage
