traffic
træfɪk ˈtræfɪk
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense traffics, present participle trafficking, past tense , past participle trafficked
1 N-UNCOUNT [oft the NOUN] 车流;交通 Traffic refers to all the vehicles that are moving along the roads in a particular area. 车流;交通

  • There was heavy traffic on the roads.

    道路上车流拥挤。

  • Traffic was unusually light for that time of day.

    对于一天中的那个时间段而言，来往车辆少得有些不寻常。

  • ...the problems of city life, such as traffic congestion.

    交通拥堵等城市生活问题

SYN transport, movement, vehicles, transportation  
2See also:traffic jam 
3 N-UNCOUNT [usually n NOUN] (海上、铁路或空中的)交通，通行;(客、货)运输量 Traffic refers to the movement of ships, trains, or aircraft between one place and another. Traffic also refers to the people and goods that are being transported. (海上、铁路或空中的)交通，通行;(客、货)运输量

  • Air traffic had returned to normal.

    空中交通已恢复正常。

  • The railways will carry a far higher proportion of freight traffic.

    铁路将承担比例高得多的货运量。

  • The ferries can cope with the traffic of both goods and passengers.

    货运与客运渡船都可应付。

SYN transportation, shipping, transport, freight  
4See also:air traffic control 
5 N-UNCOUNT (毒品、赃物等的)非法买卖，非法交易 Traffic in something such as drugs or stolen goods is an illegal trade in them. (毒品、赃物等的)非法买卖，非法交易

  • Traffic in illicit drugs was now worth some 500 thousand million dollars a year. [+ in]

    如今每年非法毒品的交易值约在 5,000 亿美元。

6 VERB 非法买卖(毒品、赃物等) Someone who traffics in something such as drugs or stolen goods buys and sells them even though it is illegal to do so. 非法买卖(毒品、赃物等)

  • The president said illegal drugs are hurting the entire world and anyone who traffics in them should be brought to justice. [VERB + in]

    总统称毒品正在危害整个世界，任何非法贩卖毒品的人都应依法受到惩处。

SYN trade, market, deal, exchange  
trafficking

    他因贩毒被判刑 10 年。

  • …the trafficking of illegal weapons.

    非法武器交易
