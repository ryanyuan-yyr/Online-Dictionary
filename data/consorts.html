consort
kənsɔːʳt kɒnsɔːʳt ˈkɑnˌsɔrt kənˈsɔrt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense consorts, present participle consorting, past tense , past participle consorted
The verb is pronounced /kən'sɔːt/. The noun is pronounced /'kɒnsɔːt/. 动词读作 /kən'sɔːt/,名词读作 /'kɒnsɔːt/。
1 VERB 来往;厮混;鬼混 If you say that someone consorts with a particular person or group, you mean that they spend a lot of time with them, and usually that you do not think this is a good thing. 来往;厮混;鬼混 [formal, disapproval]

  • He regularly consorted with known drug-dealers. [VERB + with]

    他经常与几个臭名昭著的毒贩鬼混。

SYN associate with, mix with, mingle with, hang with [informal , mainly US]  
2 N-COUNT; N-TITLE [oft n NOUN] 在位君主的配偶 The ruling monarch's wife or husband is called their consort . 在位君主的配偶

  • At teatime, Victoria sang duets with her Consort, Prince Albert.

    喝下午茶的时候，维多利亚与其夫君艾伯特亲王表演了二重唱。

  • She was surely the most distinguished queen consort we have had.

    毫无疑问她是我们拥有的最为出众的王后。

3 N-COUNT 一群(乐师);一组(乐器) A consort of musicians or instruments is a group of them 一群(乐师);一组(乐器) [formal]

  • The Sinfonietta shares the stage with a consort of viols.

    这支小交响乐队与一群六弦提琴师同台献艺。
