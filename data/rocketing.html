rocket
rɒkɪt ˈrɑkɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense rockets, present participle rocketing, past tense , past participle rocketed
1 N-COUNT 火箭 A rocket is a space vehicle that is shaped like a long tube. 火箭
2 N-COUNT [oft NOUN n] 火箭弹;火箭推进式导弹 A rocket is a missile containing explosive that is powered by gas. 火箭弹;火箭推进式导弹

  • There has been a renewed rocket attack on the capital.

    首都遭受了新一轮的火箭袭击。

SYN missile, projectile  
3 N-COUNT 烟花;焰火 A rocket is a firework that quickly goes high into the air and then explodes. 烟花;焰火
4 VERB (价格)猛涨，飙升;（社会问题）急剧增多 If things such as prices or social problems rocket, they increase very quickly and suddenly. (价格)猛涨，飙升;（社会问题）急剧增多 [journalism]

  • Fresh food is so scarce that prices have rocketed. [VERB]

    新鲜食物非常匮乏，导致价格猛涨。

  • The nation has experienced four years of rocketing crime. [VERB-ing]

    4年来，这个国家的犯罪率急剧上升。

SYN escalate, rise, soar, spiral  
5 VERB 疾驰;飞奔 If something such as a vehicle rockets somewhere, it moves there very quickly. 疾驰;飞奔

  • A train rocketed by, shaking the walls of the row houses. [VERB prep./adv.]

    一辆火车疾驰而过，排屋的墙都摇晃起来。

  • Dublin has rocketed up the charts to become one of Europe's most popular tourist destinations for city breaks. [VERB prep./adv.]

    都柏林的排名飞速攀升，成为了欧洲最受欢迎的旅游度假城市之一。
