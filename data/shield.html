shield
ʃiːld ʃild
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense shields, present participle shielding, past tense , past participle shielded
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 屏障;保护物;保护人 Something or someone which is a shield against a particular danger or risk provides protection from it. 屏障;保护物;保护人

  • He used his left hand as a shield against the reflecting sunlight.

    他用左手挡住反射过来的阳光。

  • ...asbestos heat shields.

    石棉隔热物

2 VERB 保护;掩护;庇护 If something or someone shields you from a danger or risk, they protect you from it. 保护;掩护;庇护

  • He shielded his head from the sun with an old sack. [VERB noun + from]

    他用一只旧口袋挡住头顶的阳光。

  • The company does not bet its own money on equities, and so is shielded from market risk. [VERB noun from noun]

    该公司没有把自己的钱押在普通股上，这样就规避了市场风险。

SYN protect, cover, screen, guard  
3 VERB (为阻挡阳光直射而)挡住，遮住(眼睛) If you shield your eyes, you put your hand above your eyes to protect them from direct sunlight. (为阻挡阳光直射而)挡住，遮住(眼睛)

  • He squinted and shielded his eyes. [VERB noun]

    他眯着眼，用手挡住眼睛。

4 N-COUNT 盾;盾牌 A shield is a large piece of metal or leather which soldiers used to carry to protect their bodies while they were fighting. 盾;盾牌
5 N-COUNT (体育运动的)盾形奖牌，盾形徽章 A shield is a sports prize or badge that is shaped like a shield. (体育运动的)盾形奖牌，盾形徽章
