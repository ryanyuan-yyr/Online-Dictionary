retrieve
rɪtriːv rɪˈtriv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense retrieves, present participle retrieving, past tense , past participle retrieved
1 VERB 取回;找回 If you retrieve something, you get it back from the place where you left it. 取回;找回

  • He reached over and retrieved his jacket from the back seat. [VERB noun]

    他伸手从后座上取回了自己的夹克。

  • The men were trying to retrieve weapons left when the army abandoned the island. [VERB noun]

    士兵们正试图找回军队从该岛撤退时留下的武器。

SYN get back, regain, repossess, fetch back  
2 VERB 使重新回到，挽回(某种局面) If you manage to retrieve a situation, you succeed in bringing it back into a more acceptable state. 使重新回到，挽回(某种局面)

  • He is the one man who could retrieve that situation. [VERB noun]

    他是唯一能挽回局面的人。

SYN redeem, save, rescue, repair  
3 VERB （计算机、大脑）检索，搜索（信息） To retrieve information from a computer or from your memory means to get it back. （计算机、大脑）检索，搜索（信息）

  • Computers can instantly retrieve millions of information bits. [VERB noun]

    计算机可以立即检索到数百万条信息。

  • As the child gets older, so his or her strategies for storing and retrieving information improve. [VERB noun]

    随着孩子们长大，他们储存和检索信息的能力也会提高。
