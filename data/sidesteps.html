sidestep
saɪdstep ˈsaɪdˌstɛp
(also side-step)Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense sidesteps, present participle sidestepping, past tense , past participle sidestepped
1 VERB 回避，规避(问题等) If you sidestep a problem, you avoid discussing it or dealing with it. 回避，规避(问题等) [Also VERB]

  • Rarely, if ever, does he sidestep a question. [VERB noun]

    他极少逃避问题。

  • He was trying to sidestep responsibility. [VERB noun]

    他正试图回避责任。

SYN avoid, dodge, evade, duck [informal]  
2 VERB 横跨一步躲过;侧移一步闪开 If you sidestep, you step sideways in order to avoid something or someone that is coming towards you or going to hit you. 横跨一步躲过;侧移一步闪开

  • As I sidestepped, the bottle hit me on the left hip. [VERB]

    我侧一步要躲闪的时候，瓶子打中了我的左髋部。

  • He made a grab for her but she sidestepped him. [VERB noun]

    他想要抓住她，但她侧步闪开了。
