tootle
tuːtəl ˈtuːtəl ˈtutəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense tootles, present participle tootling, past tense , past participle tootled
1 VERB 悠闲自得地去；不慌不忙地开车去 If you tootle somewhere, you travel or go there without rushing or without any particular aim. 悠闲自得地去；不慌不忙地开车去 [mainly British, informal]

  • I'm sure Ted is tootling down the motorway at this very moment. [VERB prep./adv.]

    我确信泰德此刻正悠闲自得地开车行驶在高速路上。

2 VERB (轻声随意地)演奏 If you tootle a tune on an instrument, you play it quietly, without concentrating or taking it seriously. (轻声随意地)演奏 [informal] [Also VERB]

  • McCann tootled a tune on the piano. [VERB noun]

    麦卡恩在钢琴上随手轻弹一曲。
