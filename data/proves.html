prove
pruːv pruv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense proves, present participle proving, past tense proved, past participle proved, past participle proven
1 V-LINK 证明是；原来是；结果是 If something proves to be true or to have a particular quality, it becomes clear after a period of time that it is true or has that quality. 证明是；原来是；结果是

  • We have been accused of exaggerating before, but unfortunately all our reports proved to be true. [VERB to-infinitive]

    以前一直有人指责我们夸大事实，但遗憾的是，我们所有的报道都证明是真实的。

  • In the past this process of transition has often proven difficult. [VERB adj.ive]

    过去这一过渡过程常常很艰难。

  • ...an experiment which was to prove a source of inspiration for many years to come. [VERB noun]

    在之后多年证明一直都是灵感源泉的一项实验

SYN turn out, come out, end up, be found to be  
2 VERB 证明；证实 If you prove that something is true, you show by means of argument or evidence that it is definitely true. 证明；证实

  • You brought this charge. You prove it!. [VERB noun]

    你提出了这项控告。你拿出证据来！

  • The results prove that regulation of the salmon farming industry is inadequate. [VERB that]

    结果证明对鲑鱼饲养行业的管理不够完善。

  • ...trying to prove how groups of animals have evolved. [VERB wh]

    试图证明动物群体是如何进化的

  • That made me hopping mad and determined to prove him wrong. [VERB noun adj.ive]

    那使我极为恼火，下决心要证明他是错的。

  • History will prove him to have been right all along. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    历史将证明他一直是对的。

  • ...a proven cause of cancer. [VERB-ed]

    已确证的癌症病因

3 VERB 证明；显示 If you prove yourself to have a certain good quality, you show by your actions that you have it. 证明；显示

  • Margaret proved herself to be a good mother. [VERB pronoun-reflexive to-infinitive]

    玛格丽特证明了自己是个好妈妈。

  • As a composer he proved himself adept at large dramatic forms. [V pron-refl adj]

    身为作曲家的他证明了自己在创作大型戏剧方面驾轻就熟。

  • A man needs time to prove himself. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    人需要时间来证明自己。

  • Few would argue that this team has experience and proven ability. [VERB-ed]

    这个队伍的丰富经验和表现出来的实力是众所公认的。

4 PHRASE 证明自己的能力（或知识） If you prove a point, you show other people that you know something or can do something, although your action may have no other purpose. 证明自己的能力（或知识）

  • They made a 3,000 mile detour simply to prove a point.

    他们绕行3,000英里只是为了证明自己能够做到。
