riddle
rɪdəl ˈrɪdəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense riddles, present participle riddling, past tense , past participle riddled
1 N-COUNT 谜;谜语 A riddle is a puzzle or joke in which you ask a question that seems to be nonsense but which has a clever or amusing answer. 谜;谜语
2 N-COUNT 谜团;费解的事 You can describe something as a riddle if people have been trying to understand or explain it but have not been able to. 谜团;费解的事

  • Scientists claimed yesterday to have solved the riddle of the birth of the Universe. [+ of]

    科学家们昨天声称已经解开了宇宙形成之谜。

3 VERB （用子弹）把…打得到处是洞，使满是弹孔 If someone riddles something with bullets or bullet holes, they fire a lot of bullets into it. （用子弹）把…打得到处是洞，使满是弹孔

  • Unknown attackers riddled two homes with gunfire. [VERB noun + with]

    来路不明的袭击者用枪炮将两处住宅打得千疮百孔。

  • The darkness saved me from being riddled with bullets. [VERB noun with noun]

    身处黑暗中让我不至于被子弹打得浑身是洞。

SYN pierce, pepper, puncture, perforate
