collar
kɒləʳ ˈkɑlər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense collars, present participle collaring, past tense , past participle collared
1 N-COUNT 衣领;领子 The collar of a shirt or coat is the part which fits round the neck and is usually folded over. 衣领;领子

  • His tie was pulled loose and his collar hung open.

    他的领带扯松了，领口也敞着。

  • ...a coat with a huge fake fur collar.

    配有一个大大的假毛皮领的大衣

2See also:blue-collar  dog-collar  white-collar 
3 N-COUNT (狗或猫的)颈圈，项圈 A collar is a band of leather or plastic which is put round the neck of a dog or cat. (狗或猫的)颈圈，项圈
4 VERB 抓住;逮住;揪住 If you collar someone who has done something wrong or who is running away, you catch them and hold them so that they cannot escape. 抓住;逮住;揪住 [informal]

  • As Kerr fled towards the exit, Boycott collared him at the ticket barrier. [VERB noun]

    克尔往出口方向逃窜，博伊科特在验票口逮住了他。

SYN seize, catch, arrest, appropriate  
5 VERB 拦住（某人）与其谈话 If you collar someone, you stop them and make them listen to you. 拦住（某人）与其谈话 [informal]

  • Beattie managed to collar Atkins in a hallway. [VERB noun]

    贝蒂设法在走廊里拦住了阿特金斯，和他谈了几句话。

  • Bernard was once collared by an aggressive stranger in Soho. [VERB noun]

    有一次，伯纳德在索霍区被一个气势汹汹的陌生人拦住，硬要与他说话。

6 PHRASE [VERB inflects, oft PHR about n] 生气;愤怒;激动 If someone gets hot under the collar about something, they get very annoyed, angry, or excited about it. 生气;愤怒;激动 [informal]

  • Some of you were getting very hot under the collar about Royals.

    你们当中有些人对皇室成员非常恼火。
