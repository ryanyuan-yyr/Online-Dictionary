labyrinthine
læbɪrɪnθaɪn ˌlæbəˈrɪnθɪən ˌlæbəˈrɪnθɪk ˌlæbəˈrɪnθɪn ˌlæbəˈrɪnˌθin ˌlæbəˈrɪnˌθaɪn
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 迷宫般的；迷阵似的 If you describe a place as labyrinthine, you mean that it is like a labyrinth. 迷宫般的；迷阵似的 [formal]

  • The streets of the Old City are narrow and labyrinthine.

    老城区的街道狭促曲折，好似迷宫一般。

SYN mazelike, winding, tangled, intricate  
2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 错综复杂的；盘根错节的；令人费解的 If you describe a situation, process, or field of knowledge as labyrinthine, you mean that it is very complicated and difficult to understand. 错综复杂的；盘根错节的；令人费解的 [formal]

  • ...his failure to understand the labyrinthine complexities of the situation.

    他对这一错综复杂的情况摸不着头脑

SYN complex, puzzling, perplexing, involved
