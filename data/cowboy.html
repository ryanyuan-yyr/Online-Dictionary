cowboy
kaʊbɔɪ ˈkaʊˌbɔɪ
Word forms: plural cowboys
1 N-COUNT (西部电影中的)牛仔，骑马牧人 A cowboy is a male character in a western. (西部电影中的)牛仔，骑马牧人

  • Boys used to play at cowboys and Indians.

    男孩们过去常玩西部牛仔与印第安人的游戏。

  • ...cowboy films.

    西部牛仔影片

2 N-COUNT (尤指旧时北美的)牧牛工，牛仔 A cowboy is a man employed to look after cattle in North America, especially in former times. (尤指旧时北美的)牧牛工，牛仔
3 N-COUNT [oft NOUN n] 奸商;粗心大意的商人;无经验的商人 You can refer to someone who runs a business as a cowboy if they run it dishonestly or are not experienced, skilful, or careful in their work. 奸商;粗心大意的商人;无经验的商人 [British, disapproval]

  • We don't want to look like a bunch of cowboys.

    我们不想看上去像一帮奸商。

  • Fortunately, such cowboy firms are becoming rarer.

    幸运的是，这样的欺诈公司越来越少了。
