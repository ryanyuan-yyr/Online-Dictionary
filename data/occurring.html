occur
əkɜːʳ əˈkɜr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense occurs, present participle occurring, past tense , past participle occurred
1 VERB 发生 When something occurs, it happens. 发生

  • If headaches only occur at night, lack of fresh air and oxygen is often the cause. [VERB]

    如果只是在晚上头疼，那常常是由于缺乏新鲜空气和氧气的缘故。

  • The crash occurred when the crew shut down the wrong engine. [VERB]

    机务人员关错了引擎，随即飞机坠毁。

  • In March 1770, there occurred what became known as the Boston Massacre.

    1770年3月在那里发生了后来闻名的波士顿惨案。

2 VERB 存在；出现；有 When something occurs in a particular place, it exists or is present there. 存在；出现；有

  • The cattle disease occurs more or less anywhere in Africa where the fly occurs. [VERB adv./prep.]

    在非洲凡是有这种蝇虫的地方，几乎都会出现这一牛病。

  • These snails do not occur on low-lying coral islands or atolls. [VERB adv./prep.]

    在地势低平的珊瑚岛或环礁上没有这种螺。

3 VERB [no passive, no cont] （想法或主意）被想到，被想起，浮现于脑海中 If a thought or idea occurs to you, you suddenly think of it or realize it. （想法或主意）被想到，被想起，浮现于脑海中

  • It did not occur to me to check my insurance policy. [V to n to-inf]

    我没有想到要核实我的保险单条款。

  • It occurred to me that I could have the book sent to me. [it V to n that]

    我想到可以让人把书寄给我。

  • The same idea had occurred to Elizabeth. [VERB + to]

    伊丽莎白也想到了这个主意。
