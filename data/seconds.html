PART OF A MINUTE 秒

N-COUNT 秒(a second 或 seconds 往往用以表示很短的时间) A second is one of the sixty parts that a minute is divided into. People often say 'a second' or 'seconds' when they simply mean a very short time. 秒(a second 或 seconds 往往用以表示很短的时间)

  • For a few seconds nobody said anything.

    有几秒钟大家都沉默无语。

  • It only takes forty seconds.

    只需40秒。

  • Her orbital speed must be a few hundred meters per second.

    其轨道速度一定是在每秒几百米。

  • Within seconds the other soldiers began firing too.

    几秒钟内，其他的士兵也开始射击。

  • Seconds later, firemen reached his door.

    片刻之后，消防队员们赶到他家门口。

SYN moment, minute, instant, flash  

COMING AFTER SOMETHING ELSE 第二

1 ORD 第二的;第二个的 The second item in a series is the one that you count as number two. 第二的;第二个的

  • ...the second day of his visit to Delhi.

    他到访德里的第二天

  • ...their second child.

    他们的第二个孩子

  • My son just got married for the second time.

    我的儿子刚刚再婚。

  • ...the Second World War.

    第二次世界大战

  • She was the second of nine children.

    她在9个孩子中排行老二。

  • ...King Charles the Second.

    查理二世

  • Britain came second in the Prix St Georges Derby.

    英国在圣乔治赛马大奖赛中荣获亚军。

2 ORD (用于形容词最高级前)第二…的 Second is used before superlative adjectives to indicate that there is only one thing better or larger than the thing you are referring to. (用于形容词最高级前)第二…的

  • The party is still the second strongest in Italy.

    该党仍是意大利的第二大党。

  • ...the second-largest city in the United States.

    美国第二大城市

3 ADV 第二;其次 You say second when you want to make a second point or give a second reason for something. 第二;其次

  • The soil is depleted first by having crops grown in it and second by natural weathering and bacterial action.

    土壤首先是被种植其中的作物消耗掉，其次受自然风化和细菌活动的侵蚀。

4 N-COUNT 二级甲等/乙等优秀学位 In Britain, an upper second is a good honours degree and a lower second is an average honours degree. 二级甲等/乙等优秀学位

  • I then went up to Lancaster University and got an upper second.

    后来，我考上了兰开斯特大学，获得了二级甲等学位。

5 N-PLURAL 添菜 If you have seconds, you have a second helping of food. 添菜 [informal]

  • There's seconds if you want them.

    如果需要的话可以添菜。

6 N-COUNT [usually plural] 二级品;二等货;次货;等外品 Seconds are goods that are sold cheaply in shops because they have slight faults. 二级品;二等货;次货;等外品

  • It's a new shop selling discounted lines and seconds.

    这是家新开的商店，出售打折商品和等外品。

7 N-COUNT [usually plural] (拳击或国际象棋锦标赛的)副手，助手 The seconds of someone who is taking part in a boxing match or chess tournament are the people who assist and encourage them. (拳击或国际象棋锦标赛的)副手，助手

  • He shouted to his seconds, 'I did it! I did it!'

    他对助手们喊道：“我赢了！我赢了!”

SYN supporter, assistant, aide, partner  
8 VERB 附议，赞同(提案) If you second a proposal in a meeting or debate, you formally express your agreement with it so that it can then be discussed or voted on. 附议，赞同(提案)

  • ...Bryan Sutton, who seconded the motion against fox hunting. [VERB noun]

    赞同反对猎狐动议的布赖恩·萨顿

  • Your application must be proposed and seconded by current members. [VERB noun]

    你的申请必须由现任成员推荐并得到他们的支持。

SYN support, back, endorse, forward  
seconder

  • Candidates need a proposer and seconder whose names are kept secret.

    候选人需要一名匿名的推荐人兼支持者。

9 VERB 赞成;持相同观点 If you second what someone has said, you say that you agree with them or say the same thing yourself. 赞成;持相同观点

  • The Prime Minister seconded the call for discipline in a speech last week. [VERB noun]

    上周首相在一次演讲中对严肃纪律的号召表示支持。

10 PHRASE 听说地;非亲身经历地 If you experience something at second hand, you are told about it by other people rather than experiencing it yourself. 听说地;非亲身经历地

  • Most of them had only heard of the massacre at second hand.

    他们中的大多数人只是听说了那次大屠杀。

11See also:second-hand 
12 PHRASE 不比任何事物差;首屈一指 If you say that something is second to none, you are emphasizing that it is very good indeed or the best that there is. 不比任何事物差;首屈一指 [emphasis]

  • Our scientific research is second to none.

    我们的科学研究是顶尖的。

SYN the best, the highest, perfect, outstanding  
13 PHRASE 只比…差;仅次于 If you say that something is second only to something else, you mean that only that thing is better or greater than it. 只比…差;仅次于

  • As a major health risk hepatitis is second only to tobacco.

    肝炎是仅次于吸烟的主要健康隐患。

SENDING SOMEONE TO DO A JOB 调派

VERB [usually passive] 临时调任;临时调派 If you are seconded somewhere, you are sent there temporarily by your employer in order to do special duties. 临时调任;临时调派 [British]

  • In 1937 he was seconded to the Royal Canadian Air Force in Ottawa as air armament adviser. [be VERB-ed + to]

    1937 年他被临时调往渥太华的加拿大皇家空军担任军备顾问。

  • Several hundred soldiers have been seconded to help farmers. [be VERB-ed to-infinitive]

    数百名士兵被临时派去帮助农民。
