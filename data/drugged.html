drug
drʌg drʌɡ drʌg
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense drugs, present participle drugging, past tense , past participle drugged
1 N-COUNT 药;药物;药品 A drug is a chemical which is given to people in order to treat or prevent an illness or disease. 药;药物;药品

  • The drug will be useful to hundreds of thousands of infected people.

    这种药将惠及成千上万的感染者。

  • ...the drug companies.

    制药公司

2 N-COUNT 毒品 Drugs are substances that some people take because of their pleasant effects, but which are usually illegal. 毒品

  • His mother was on drugs, on cocaine.

    他母亲吸毒，吸可卡因。

  • She was sure Leo was taking drugs.

    她确定利奥在吸毒。

  • ...the problem of drug abuse.

    吸毒问题

SYN dope [slang] , narcotic [slang] , stimulant, illegal drug  
3 VERB 将…麻醉;给…服麻醉剂 If you drug a person or animal, you give them a chemical substance in order to make them sleepy or unconscious. 将…麻醉;给…服麻醉剂

  • They drugged the guard dog with doped meatballs. [VERB noun]

    他们用掺了麻醉药的肉丸子麻醉了看门狗。

  • She was drugged and robbed. [VERB noun]

    她被人下了药，然后遭到抢劫。

  • He grew tired, and drifted off into a drugged sleep. [VERB-ed]

    他累了,在药物的作用下迷迷糊糊地睡着了。

SYN knock out, dope [slang] , give drugs to, numb  
4 VERB 往（食物或饮料中）投放麻醉药 If food or drink is drugged, a chemical substance is added to it in order to make someone sleepy or unconscious when they eat or drink it. 往（食物或饮料中）投放麻醉药

  • I wonder now if that drink had been drugged. [be VERB-ed]

    我现在想那饮料是否被人动过手脚。

  • Anyone could have drugged that wine. [VERB noun]

    指不定谁在那酒里下了药。

  • A tourist was robbed after being given a drugged orange. [VERB-ed]

    一名旅游者在喝了一杯被动了手脚的橙汁饮料后遭到了抢劫。
