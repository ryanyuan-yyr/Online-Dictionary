pronounce
prənaʊns prəˈnaʊns proʊˈnaʊns
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense pronounces, present participle pronouncing, past tense , past participle pronounced
1 VERB 发…的音；读 To pronounce a word means to say it using particular sounds. 发…的音；读

  • Have I pronounced your name correctly?. [VERB noun]

    你的名字我念得对吗？

  • He pronounced it Per-sha, the way the English do. [VERB noun noun]

    他像英格兰人那样将它读成Per-sha。

SYN say, speak, voice, stress  
2 VERB 宣称；宣告；宣布；断言 If you pronounce something to be true, you state that it is the case. 宣称；宣告；宣布；断言 [formal]

  • A specialist has now pronounced him fully fit. [VERB noun adj.ive]

    一位专家已宣布他的身体完全恢复了。

  • I now pronounce you man and wife. [VERB noun noun]

    我现在宣布你们结为夫妻。

SYN declare, announce, judge, deliver  
3 VERB 宣称；宣布 If someone pronounces a verdict or opinion on something, they give their verdict or opinion. 宣称；宣布 [formal] [Also VERB that]

  • The authorities took time to pronounce their verdicts. [VERB noun]

    当局斟酌权衡再三才宣布他们的决定。

  • 'As for me,' he pronounced, 'I can recognize a good deal when I see one'. [VERB with quote]

    “至于我，”他宣称，“买卖好坏我一看便知。”

  • He walked around the garden and pronounced himself satisfied. [V pron-refl adj]

    他绕着花园走了走，说自己很满意。

  • Men feel perfectly free to pronounce on the way women should look. [VERB on noun]

    男人随心所欲地对女性美的标准发表看法。
