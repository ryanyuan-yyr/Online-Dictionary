presumption
prɪzʌmpʃən priˈzʌmpʃən prɪˈzʌmpʃən
Word forms: plural presumptions
1 N-COUNT 假定；假设 A presumption is something that is accepted as true but is not certain to be true. 假定；假设

  • ...the presumption that a defendant is innocent until proved guilty.

    被告在被证实有罪之前都被视为无罪的假定

  • I'm having to make a lot of presumptions since I don't really know anything about the case.

    事实上我对这个案件一无所知，很多观点都只能是假设。

SYN assumption, opinion, belief, guess  
2 N-UNCOUNT 擅自；放肆；冒昧 If you describe someone's behaviour as presumption, you disapprove of it because they are doing something that they have no right to do. 擅自；放肆；冒昧 [formal, disapproval]

  • They were angered by his presumption.

    他们对他的放肆感到非常愤怒。
