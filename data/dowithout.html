do without
1 PHRASAL VERB 没有…也行，没有…而设法对付过去 If you do without something you need, want, or usually have, you are able to survive, continue, or succeed although you do not have it. 没有…也行，没有…而设法对付过去

  • We can't do without the help of your organisation. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我们不能没有你们机构的帮助。

  • We've had a bit more money and that, and the baby doesn't do without. [VERB PREP.]

    我们还有一点钱什么的，婴儿没有这些不行。

2 PHRASAL VERB 情愿没有;用不着 If you say that you could do without something, you mean that you would prefer not to have it or it is of no benefit to you. 情愿没有;用不着 [informal]

  • He could do without her rhetorical questions at five o'clock in the morning. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他真不愿早上 5 点就听到她一遍遍地反问。

  • ...those who love France but can do without the natives. [VERB PREP. noun]

    喜欢法国却看不上法国人的人们

  • Like all teenagers there's one thing she'd rather do without — spots. [VERB PREP. noun]

    和所有十几岁的孩子一样，有一样东西她宁可没有—青春痘。
