react
riækt rɪˈækt riˈækt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense reacts, present participle reacting, past tense , past participle reacted
1 VERB （作出）反应；回应 When you react to something that has happened to you, you behave in a particular way because of it. （作出）反应；回应 [Also VERB]

  • They reacted violently to the news. [VERB + to]

    他们对这条新闻反应强烈。

  • It's natural to react with disbelief if your child is accused of bullying. [VERB adv./prep.]

    如果你的孩子被指责欺负了别的孩子，你不相信是很正常的。

  • 'How did he react?' — 'Very calmly.' [VERB adv./prep.]

    “他有什么反应？”—“非常平静。”

SYN respond, act, take, proceed  
2 VERB 反抗;不服;反对 If you react against someone's way of behaving, you deliberately behave in a different way because you do not like the way they behave. 反抗;不服;反对

  • She reacted against the mindlessness and luxury of their lives. [V against n]

    她对他们生活的盲目和奢侈很反对。

  • My father never saved and perhaps I reacted against that. [VERB + against]

    我父亲从来不存钱，我可能正好相反。

SYN rebel against, oppose, revolt against, rise up against  
3 VERB 产生不良反应;过敏 If you react to a substance such as a drug, or to something you have touched, you are affected unpleasantly or made ill by it. 产生不良反应;过敏 [Also VERB]

  • Someone allergic to milk is likely to react to cheese. [VERB + to]

    对牛奶过敏的人可能对奶酪也有不良反应。

  • He reacted very badly to the radiation therapy. [VERB to noun]

    他接受放射治疗，副作用很大。

SYN respond  
4 V-RECIP 起（化学）变化;发生（化学）反应 When one chemical substance reacts with another, or when two chemical substances react, they combine chemically to form another substance. 起（化学）变化;发生（化学）反应

  • Calcium reacts with water. [VERB + with]

    钙和水会发生化学反应

  • Under normal circumstances, these two gases react readily to produce carbon dioxide and water. [VERB]

    在正常情况下，这两种气体容易发生反应产生二氧化碳和水。
