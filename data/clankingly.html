clank
klæŋk klæŋk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense clanks, present participle clanking, past tense , past participle clanked
VERB (巨大金属物)发出咣当声，发出当啷声 When large metal objects clank, they make a noise because they are hitting together or hitting against something hard. (巨大金属物)发出咣当声，发出当啷声

  • A pan rattled and clanked. [VERB]

    平底锅叮叮咣咣地响。

  • 'Here we are now,' Beth said, as the train clanked into a tiny station. [VERB prep.]

    火车咣当咣当进了小站，“我们到站了，”贝丝说。

  • ...the clanking noise of the ferry. [VERB-ing]

    渡船当啷当啷的声音

Clank is also a noun.

  • ...the clank and rattle of human activity.

    人们活动时叮叮咣咣的声音
