backtrack
bæktræk ˈbækˌtræk
(also back-track)Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense backtracks, present participle backtracking, past tense , past participle backtracked
1 VERB 改变（说法、决定等）；出尔反尔 If you backtrack on a statement or decision you have made, you do or say something that shows that you no longer agree with it or support it. 改变（说法、决定等）；出尔反尔 [Also V + from]

  • The committee backtracked by scrapping the controversial bonus system. [VERB]

    委员会改变主意，放弃了有争议的奖金制度。

  • The finance minister backtracked on his decision. [VERB + on]

    财政部长收回了他的决定。

SYN retract, withdraw, retreat, draw back  
backtracking

  • Some backtracking is probably inevitable.

    有时改变决定可能是不可避免的。

  • He promised there would be no backtracking on policies.

    他承诺政策不会出尔反尔。

2 VERB 原路返回 If you backtrack, you go back along a path or route you have just used. 原路返回

  • Leonard jumped in his car and started backtracking. [VERB]

    伦纳德跳进车里，开始原路折返。

  • We had to backtrack to the corner and cross the street. [VERB prep.]

    我们不得不原路返回到拐角处过街。

SYN retrace your steps, go back, reverse, retreat  
3 VERB 倒回（描述或解释内容） If you backtrack in an account or explanation, you talk about things which happened before the ones you were previously talking about. 倒回（描述或解释内容）

  • Can we just backtrack a little bit and look at your primary and secondary education? [VERB]

    我们能不能回过头来谈谈你的小学和中学教育？
