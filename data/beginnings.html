beginning
bɪgɪnɪŋ bɪˈgɪnɪŋ
Word forms: plural beginnings
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 开始；开端；开头；起点 The beginning of an event or process is the first part of it. 开始；开端；开头；起点

  • This was also the beginning of her recording career.

    这也是她唱片生涯的开始。

  • Think of this as a new beginning.

    把这当作一个新起点。

SYN start, opening, break [informal] , chance  
2 N-PLURAL 先兆；开始部分 The beginnings of something are the signs or events which form the first part of it. 先兆；开始部分

  • I had the beginnings of a headache.

    我有了头痛的先兆。

  • The discussions were the beginnings of a dialogue with Moscow.

    讨论拉开了同莫斯科对话的序幕。

3 N-SING 初期；早期 The beginning of a period of time is the time at which it starts. 初期；早期

  • The wedding will be at the beginning of March.

    婚礼定于三月初举行。

4 N-COUNT [usually singular] 开头；开篇 The beginning of a piece of written material is the first words or sentences of it. 开头；开篇

  • ...the question which was raised at the beginning of this chapter. [+ of]

    在本章开头所提出的问题

SYN outset, start, opening, birth  
5 N-PLURAL 出身；背景；起源 If you talk about the beginnings of a person, company, or group, you are referring to their backgrounds or origins. 出身；背景；起源

  • His views come from his own humble beginnings.

    他的观点源于他卑微的出身。

SYN origins, family, beginnings, stock  
6 ADJ [ADJ n] 刚入门的；初级的 You use beginning to describe someone who is in the early stages of learning to do something. 刚入门的；初级的

  • The people that she had in her classroom were beginning learners.

    她带的这班学生都是初学者。
