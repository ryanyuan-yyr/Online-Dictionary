graze
greɪz greɪz
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense grazes, present participle grazing, past tense , past participle grazed
1 V-ERG （动物）吃青草；放牛；放羊；放牧 When animals graze or are grazed, they eat the grass or other plants that are growing in a particular place. You can also say that a field is grazed by animals. （动物）吃青草；放牛；放羊；放牧

  • Five cows graze serenely around a massive oak. [VERB]

    5头奶牛在一棵大橡树旁悠闲自在地啃着青草。

  • The hills have been grazed by sheep because they were too steep to be ploughed. [be VERB-ed]

    这几座山过于陡峭，不适于耕种，所以就用来牧羊了。

  • Several horses grazed the meadowland. [VERB noun]

    几匹马在牧场吃草。

  • ...a large herd of grazing animals. [VERB-ing]

    一大群正在吃草的动物

2 VERB 擦伤（身体部位） If you graze a part of your body, you injure your skin by scraping against something. 擦伤（身体部位）

  • I had grazed my knees a little. [VERB noun]

    我的膝盖擦破了一点皮。

SYN scratch, skin, bark, scrape  
grazed

  • ...grazed arms and legs.

    擦伤了的四肢

3 N-COUNT 擦伤 A graze is a small wound caused by scraping against something. 擦伤
SYN scratch, scrape, abrasion  
4 VERB 轻擦；轻触；蹭 If something grazes another thing, it touches that thing lightly as it passes by. 轻擦；轻触；蹭

  • A bullet had grazed his arm. [VERB noun]

    一颗子弹擦过他的手臂。

  • Wright managed a shot but it grazed the near post and rolled harmlessly across the goal. [VERB noun]

    赖特勉强起脚射门，但是球擦着近门柱有惊无险地偏出了球门。

SYN touch, brush, rub, scrape
