incarcerate
ɪnkɑːʳsəreɪt ɪnˈkɑrsərˌeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense incarcerates, present participle incarcerating, past tense , past participle incarcerated
VERB 监禁;禁闭 If people are incarcerated, they are kept in a prison or other place. 监禁;禁闭 [formal]

  • They were incarcerated for the duration of the war. [be VERB-ed]

    战争期间,他们被关在狱中。

  • It can cost $40,000 to $50,000 to incarcerate a prisoner for a year. [VERB noun]

    监禁一名囚犯一年要花费 4 万到 5 万美元。

SYN imprison, confine, detain, lock up
