errand
erənd ˈɛrənd
Word forms: plural errands
1 N-COUNT （短程的）差事;跑腿 An errand is a short trip that you make in order to do a job for someone, for example when you go to a shop to buy something for them. （短程的）差事;跑腿

  • She went off on some errand.

    她办事去了。

  • She had a more urgent errand.

    她有一个更加紧急的差事要办。

SYN job, charge, commission, message [Scottish]  
2 PHRASE 为…跑腿；为…办事 If you run an errand for someone, you do or get something for them, usually by making a short trip somewhere. 为…跑腿；为…办事

  • She was forever running errands for her housebound grandmother.

    她总是在为她不方便外出的祖母跑前跑后。

  • Frank drifted into running dodgy errands for a seedy local villain.

    弗兰克开始为当地一个下流的恶人跑腿做坏事。
