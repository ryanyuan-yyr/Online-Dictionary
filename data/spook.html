spook
spuːk spuk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense spooks, present participle spooking, past tense , past participle spooked
1 N-COUNT 鬼魂;幽灵 A spook is a ghost. 鬼魂;幽灵 [informal]
2 N-COUNT 间谍 A spook is a spy. 间谍 [US, informal]

  • ...as a US intelligence spook said yesterday.

    据昨天一位美国情报员所说…

SYN spy, secret agent, double agent, secret service agent  
3 VERB 使受到惊吓;使神经紧张 If people are spooked, something has scared them or made them nervous. 使受到惊吓;使神经紧张 [mainly US]

  • But was it the wind that spooked her?. [VERB noun]

    但会是风吓到她了吗？

  • Investors were spooked by slowing economies. [be VERB-ed]

    经济增长速度的放慢让投资者神经紧张。

SYN frighten, alarm, scare, terrify  
spooked

  • He was so spooked that he, too, began to believe that he heard strange clicks and noises on their telephones.

    他太害怕了，以至于也开始相信自己听到了他们的电话发出奇怪的咔哒声响。
