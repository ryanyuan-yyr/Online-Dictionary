Sun. is a written abbreviation for (书面缩略=) Sunday .

  • The Palace is open Mon-Sun.

    该宫殿从周一至周日均对外开放。

sun
sʌn sʌn
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , plural suns, present participle sunning, past tense , past participle sunned
1 N-SING 太阳;日 The sun is the ball of fire in the sky that the Earth goes round, and that gives us heat and light. 太阳;日

  • The sun was now high in the southern sky.

    太阳正高挂在南边的天空上。

  • The sun came out, briefly.

    太阳短暂地露了露脸。

  • ...the sun's rays.

    太阳的光线

  • The sun was shining.

    阳光照耀。

SYN Sol [Roman mythology] , Helios [Greek mythology] , Phoebus [Greek mythology] , daystar [poetic]  
2 N-UNCOUNT 太阳的光和热;日光;阳光 You refer to the light and heat that reach us from the sun as the sun . 太阳的光和热;日光;阳光

  • Dena took them into the courtyard to sit in the sun.

    德娜带他们到院子里晒太阳。

  • They were trying to soak up some sun.

    他们想晒晒太阳。

SYN sunshine, sunlight, daylight, light  
3 VERB [usually cont] 晒太阳 If you are sunning yourself, you are sitting or lying in a place where the sun is shining on you. 晒太阳

  • She was last seen sunning herself in a riverside park. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    最后一次有人看见她时，她正在河边公园晒太阳。

4 N-COUNT 恒星 A sun is any star which has planets going around it. 恒星
5 PHRASE 天下万物;世上一切;全世界 Everything under the sun means a very great number of things. Anything under the sun means anything at all. 天下万物;世上一切;全世界

  • We sat there for hours talking about everything under the sun.

    我们在那儿坐了几个小时，谈天说地。

  • The fashion-conscious will go for anything under the sun!

    流行意识将无所不在。
