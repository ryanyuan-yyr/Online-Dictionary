lead off
liːd
1 PHRASAL VERB 起始于（某处） If a door, room, or path leads off a place or leads off from a place, you can go directly from that place through that door, into that room, or along that path. 起始于（某处）

  • There were two doors leading off the central room. [VERB PREP. noun]

    中厅开有两扇门。

  • The treatment rooms lead off from the swimming pool. [VERB PREP. + from]

    从游泳池可以通到治疗室。

  • A corridor led off to the left. [VERB PREP. prep.]

    一条走廊通往左边。

2 PHRASAL VERB 开始；启动；带头 If someone leads off in an activity, meeting, or conversation, they start it. 开始；启动；带头

  • Whenever there was a dance he and I led off. [VERB PREP.]

    任何一支舞都是我和他领跳。

  • Boren surprisingly led off the most intensive line of questioning today. [VERB PREP. noun]

    博伦今天出人意料地一上来就开始连珠炮似地盘问起来。

SYN begin, start, open, set out  
3 PHRASAL VERB （在棒球比赛或其一局中）率先击球 In baseball, the player who leads off is the first player to bat for their team in a game or inning. （在棒球比赛或其一局中）率先击球

  • Terry Puhl leads off for the Houston Astros. [V P for n]

    特里·皮尔为休斯敦太空人队率先击球。
