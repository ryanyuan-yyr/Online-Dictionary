skeleton
skelɪtən ˈskɛlətən
Word forms: plural skeletons
1 N-COUNT 骨骼;骸骨 Your skeleton is the framework of bones in your body. 骨骼;骸骨

  • ...a human skeleton.

    人体骨骼

SYN bones, bare bones  
2 ADJ [ADJ n] (员工)最基本的，基干的 A skeleton staff is the smallest number of staff necessary in order to run an organization or service. (员工)最基本的，基干的

  • Only a skeleton staff remains to show anyone interested around the site.

    只有一小部分必需的工作人员留在那里，带有兴趣的人参观遗址。

3 N-COUNT 轮廓;框架;构架;梗概 The skeleton of something such as a building or a plan is its basic framework. 轮廓;框架;构架;梗概

  • Only skeletons of buildings remained. [+ of]

    只剩下了建筑物的框架。

  • ...a skeleton of policy guidelines.

    政策纲领的概要

SYN plan, structure, frame, draft  
4 PHRASE 家丑;不可告人的丑事 If you say that someone has a skeleton in the closet, or in British English a skeleton in the cupboard, you mean that they are keeping secret a bad or embarrassing fact about themselves. 家丑;不可告人的丑事

  • Mr Worthing is this election's Mr Nice Guy, without any skeletons in his cupboard.

    沃辛先生是此次选举中的正派先生，本身没有任何见不得人的丑事。
