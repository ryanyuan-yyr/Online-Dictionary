reckon
rekən ˈrɛkən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense reckons, present participle reckoning, past tense , past participle reckoned
1 VERB 料想;估计;认为 If you reckon that something is true, you think that it is true. 料想;估计;认为 [informal]

  • Toni reckoned that it must be about three o'clock. [VERB that]

    托妮估计当时肯定是3点钟左右。

  • He reckoned he was still fond of her. [VERB that]

    他认为自己还喜欢着她。

SYN think, believe, suppose, imagine  
2 VERB [usually passive] 认为…；把…当作 If you say that something is reckoned to be true, you mean that people think that it is true. 认为…；把…当作 [informal]

  • The sale has been held up because the price is reckoned to be too high. [be VERB-ed to-infinitive]

    销售陷入停滞，因为大家认为价格太高了。

SYN consider, hold, rate, account  
3 VERB 预计；料想；期望 If you say that someone reckons to do something, you mean that they expect to do it. 预计；料想；期望 [informal]

  • The merged banks reckon to raise 4 billion dollars of new equity next year. [VERB to-infinitive]

    几家银行合并后明年有望增发40亿美元的新股。

  • Police officers on the case are reckoning to charge someone very shortly. [VERB to-infinitive]

    调查这个案子的警官正在考虑尽快起诉嫌疑人。

SYN expect, hope, anticipate  
4 VERB [usually passive] 估算;估计 If something is reckoned to be a particular figure, it is calculated to be roughly that amount. 估算;估计

  • The star's surface temperature is reckoned to be minus 75 degrees Celsius. [be VERB-ed to-infinitive]

    这颗恒星的表面温度估计在零下75摄氏度左右。

  • There was a proportion of research, which I reckoned at not more than 30 percent, that was basic research. [VERB noun + at]

    我估计基础研究的比例最多只占30％。

Phrasal verbs:
reckon on reckon with reckon without
