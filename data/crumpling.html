crumple
krʌmpəl ˈkrʌmpəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense crumples, present participle crumpling, past tense , past participle crumpled
1 V-ERG 压皱;(使)变皱;(使)起皱 If you crumple something such as paper or cloth, or if it crumples, it is squashed and becomes full of untidy creases and folds. 压皱;(使)变皱;(使)起皱

  • She crumpled the paper in her hand. [VERB noun]

    她把那张纸在手里揉成一团。

  • The front and rear of the car will crumple during a collision. [VERB]

    碰撞时，汽车的前部和后部会被撞瘪。

SYN crush, squash, screw up, scrumple  
Crumple up means the same as crumple .crumple up 同 crumple

  • She crumpled up her coffee cup.

    她把咖啡杯捏扁了。

  • Nancy looked at the note angrily, then crumpled it up and threw it in a nearby wastepaper basket.

    南希愤怒地看完那张便条，把它狠狠地揉成一团扔进了旁边的废纸篓里。

crumpled

  • His uniform was crumpled, untidy, splashed with mud.

    他的制服皱皱巴巴、邋邋遢遢，还溅满了泥污。

2 VERB (因受到惊吓等)瘫倒 If someone crumples, they collapse, for example when they have received a shock. (因受到惊吓等)瘫倒 [written]

  • His body crumpled. [VERB]

    他身子一软，倒在地上。

  • He immediately crumpled to the floor. [VERB prep.]

    他一下子瘫倒在地。

  • Chance McAllister lay crumpled on the floor. [VERB-ed]

    钱斯·麦卡利斯特瘫倒在地。

SYN collapse, sink, go down, fall  
3 VERB (指脸突然)沮丧地皱起，哭丧着脸 If someone's face crumples, they suddenly look very disappointed or as if they want to cry. (指脸突然)沮丧地皱起，哭丧着脸 [written]

  • She faltered, and then her face crumpled once more. [VERB]

    她的声音颤抖起来，随后又露出一脸苦相。

SYN screw up, pucker
