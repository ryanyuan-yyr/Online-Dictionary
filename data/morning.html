morning
mɔːʳnɪŋ ˈmɔrnɪŋ
Word forms: plural mornings
1 N-VAR 早晨;上午(从人们通常醒来到中午或午餐前的一段时间) The morning is the part of each day between the time that people usually wake up and 12 o'clock noon or lunchtime. 早晨;上午(从人们通常醒来到中午或午餐前的一段时间)

  • During the morning your guide will take you around the city.

    导游上午会带你们游览全城。

  • On Sunday morning Bill was woken by the telephone.

    星期天早晨，比尔被电话铃声吵醒了。

  • He read about it in his morning paper.

    他在晨报上读到这条消息。

SYN before noon, forenoon, morn [poetic] , a.m.  
2 N-SING 凌晨，早晨，上午(从子夜到中午的这段时间) If you refer to a particular time in the morning, you mean a time between 12 o'clock midnight and 12 o'clock noon. 凌晨，早晨，上午(从子夜到中午的这段时间)

  • I often stayed up until two or three in the morning.

    我经常凌晨两三点才睡。

  • The attack happened in the early hours of the morning.

    在凌晨发起了进攻。

3 PHRASE 第二天上午;次日上午 If you say that something will happen in the morning, you mean that it will happen during the morning of the following day. 第二天上午;次日上午

  • I'll fly it to London in the morning.

    我明天上午会把它用航空快件寄到伦敦。

  • Melanie promised that she would call them in the morning.

    梅拉妮许诺第二天上午给他们打电话。

4 PHRASE 日日夜夜;夜以继日；一直 If you say that something happens morning, noon and night, you mean that it happens all the time. 日日夜夜;夜以继日；一直

  • You get fit by playing the game, day in, day out, morning, noon and night.

    从早到晚都坚持做这项运动，日复一日，你就能强壮起来。
