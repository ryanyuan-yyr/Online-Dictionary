omit
oʊmɪt oʊˈmɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense omits, present participle omitting, past tense , past participle omitted
1 VERB 省略；删节；排除；遗漏 If you omit something, you do not include it in an activity or piece of work, deliberately or accidentally. 省略；删节；排除；遗漏

  • Omit the salt in this recipe. [VERB noun]

    省略菜谱中放盐这一步。

  • Our apologies to David Pannick for omitting his name from last week's article. [VERB noun + from]

    我们在上周的文章里漏掉了戴维·潘尼克的名字，特此向他致歉。

SYN leave out, miss (out), drop, exclude  
2 VERB 未（做）；没有（做） If you omit to do something, you do not do it. 未（做）；没有（做） [formal]

  • His new girlfriend had omitted to tell him she was married. [VERB to-infinitive]

    他的新女友未把自己已婚的事实告诉他。

SYN forget, fail, overlook, neglect
