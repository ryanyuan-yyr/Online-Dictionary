prissy
prɪsi ˈprɪsi
Word forms: comparative prissier, superlative prissiest
ADJ-GRADED 大惊小怪的；一本正经的；谨小慎微的 If you say that someone is prissy, you are critical of them because they are very easily shocked by anything rude or bad. 大惊小怪的；一本正经的；谨小慎微的 [informal, disapproval]

  • I grew to dislike the people from my background — they were rather uptight and prissy.

    我变得讨厌和我有着同样出身背景的人—他们太保守、太一本正经了。

SYN prim, precious, fussy, fastidious
