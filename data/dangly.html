dangle
dæŋgəl ˈdæŋgəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dangles, present participle dangling, past tense , past participle dangled
1 V-ERG 悬挂;悬摆 If something dangles from somewhere or if you dangle it somewhere, it hangs or swings loosely. 悬挂;悬摆 [Also V, V n]

  • A gold bracelet dangled from his left wrist. [VERB prep./adv.]

    一只金手镯在他的左手腕上晃来晃去。

  • Crystal chandeliers dangled from every ceiling. [VERB prep./adv.]

    每个房间的天花板上都悬有枝形水晶吊灯。

  • He and I were sitting out on his jetty dangling our legs in the water. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    我和他坐在他的码头上，双腿在水中荡悠。

SYN hang, swing, trail, sway  
2 VERB 炫示（诱惑物） If you say that someone is dangling something attractive before you, you mean they are offering it to you in order to try to influence you in some way. 炫示（诱惑物）

  • They've dangled rich rewards before me. [V n + before/in front of]

    他们以丰厚的酬劳诱惑我。

SYN offer, flourish, brandish, flaunt
