transgress
trænzgres trænzˈɡrɛs trænsˈgrɛs trænzˈgrɛs
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense transgresses, present participle transgressing, past tense , past participle transgressed
VERB 违犯道德(或行为)准则 If someone transgresses, they break a moral law or a rule of behaviour. 违犯道德(或行为)准则

  • If a politician transgresses, that is not the fault of the media. [VERB]

    如果政客行为失检，那可不是媒体的责任。

  • ...a monk who had transgressed against the law of celibacy. [VERB + against]

    触犯禁欲戒律的僧侣

  • It seemed to me that he had transgressed the boundaries of good taste. [VERB noun]

    在我看来他有伤大雅。

SYN go beyond, exceed, infringe, overstep  
transgression

    有关这位候选人所谓的过往越轨行为的流言蜚语已经开始涌现。
