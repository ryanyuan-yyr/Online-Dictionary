seedy
siːdi ˈsidi
Word forms: comparative seedier, superlative seediest
ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 邋遢的;脏乱的;声名狼藉的 If you describe a person or place as seedy, you disapprove of them because they look dirty and untidy, or they have a bad reputation. 邋遢的;脏乱的;声名狼藉的 [disapproval]

  • Frank ran dodgy errands for a seedy local villain.

    弗兰克为当地一个臭名远扬的恶棍跑腿，干些骗人勾当。

  • We were staying in a seedy hotel close to the red light district.

    我们当时住在紧挨着红灯区的一家脏乱不堪的旅馆里。

  • They suck you in to their seedy world.

    他们把你卷入他们肮脏糜烂的圈子里。

SYN shabby, rundown, scruffy, old  
seediness

  • ...the atmosphere of seediness and decay about the city.

    这个城市给人的一种脏乱和衰败的感觉
