denunciation
dɪnʌnsieɪʃən diˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən dɪˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən
Word forms: plural denunciations
1 N-VAR 谴责;痛斥 Denunciation of someone or something is severe public criticism of them. 谴责;痛斥

  • On September 24, he wrote a stinging denunciation of his critics. [+ of]

    9月24号，他写了一篇痛斥那些批评者的措词强烈的文章。

  • He has been scathing in his denunciation of corrupt and incompetent politicians.

    他抨击起那些腐败无能的政客来一直都很尖刻犀利。

SYN condemnation, criticism, accusation, censure  
2 N-VAR 告发;检举 Denunciation is the act of reporting someone who has broken a rule or law to the authorities. 告发;检举

  • ...the denunciation of Jews to the Nazis during the Second World War. [+ of]

    第二次世界大战期间向纳粹分子告发犹太人
