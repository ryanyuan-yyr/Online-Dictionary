fringe
frɪndʒ frɪndʒ
Word forms: plural fringes
1 N-COUNT 刘海儿;额前短垂发 A fringe is hair which is cut so that it hangs over your forehead. 刘海儿;额前短垂发 [British]
in AM, use 美国英语用 bangs
2 N-COUNT 穗;流苏;缘饰 A fringe is a decoration attached to clothes, or other objects such as curtains, consisting of a row of hanging strips or threads. 穗;流苏;缘饰

  • The jacket had leather fringes.

    这件夹克有皮穗。

SYN border, edging, edge, binding  
3 N-COUNT [usu on the N of n] (某地的)周边，边缘 To be on the fringe or the fringes of a place means to be on the outside edge of it, or to be in one of the parts that are farthest from its centre. (某地的)周边，边缘

  • ...black townships located on the fringes of the city. [+ of]

    城市周边的黑人居住区

  • They lived together in a mixed household on the fringe of a campus.

    他们一起住在校园边上一处男女合租的房子里。

SYN edge, limits, border, margin  
4 N-COUNT [usually plural] (某活动或机构的)次要部分，极端部分，外围 The fringe or the fringes of an activity or organization are its less important, least typical, or most extreme parts, rather than its main and central part. (某活动或机构的)次要部分，极端部分，外围

  • The party remained on the fringe of the political scene until last year. [+ of]

    直到去年为止这个政党一直处在政治舞台的外围。

5 ADJ [ADJ n] 次要的;非主流的;外围的 Fringe groups or events are less important or popular than other related groups or events. 次要的;非主流的;外围的

  • The monarchists are a small fringe group who quarrel fiercely among themselves.

    君主主义者是一个内部争吵激烈的非主流的小团体。

  • ...the numerous fringe meetings held during the party conference.

    在全党大会期间召开的众多次要会议
