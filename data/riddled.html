riddled
rɪdəld ˈrɪdəld
1 ADJ-GRADED 布满（弹孔）的;遍布（枪眼）的 If something is riddled with bullets or bullet holes, it is full of bullet holes. 布满（弹孔）的;遍布（枪眼）的

  • The bodies of four people were found riddled with bullets. [+ with]

    4具尸体被发现时布满了枪眼。

2 ADJ-GRADED [verb-link ADJ with n] 充满了…的;满是…的;到处是…的 If something is riddled with undesirable qualities or features, it is full of them. 充满了…的;满是…的;到处是…的

  • They were the principal shareholders in a bank riddled with corruption. [+ with]

    他们是一家腐败成风的银行的主要股东。

  • The report was riddled with errors.

    这份报告错误百出。

-riddled -rɪdəld
COMB in ADJ [usually ADJ n] (与名词连用构成形容词)表示“充满…的”,“充斥着…的” -riddled combines with nouns to form adjectives that describe something as being full of a particular undesirable thing or quality. (与名词连用构成形容词)表示“充满…的”,“充斥着…的”

  • She pushed the bullet-riddled door open.

    她推开了布满弹孔的门。

  • It is a dangerous, crime-riddled, filthy city.

    这是一个危机四伏、犯罪横行的肮脏城市。
