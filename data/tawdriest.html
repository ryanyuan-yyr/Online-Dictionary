tawdry
tɔːdri ˈtɔdri
Word forms: comparative tawdrier, superlative tawdriest
1 ADJ-GRADED 廉价的;俗气的 If you describe something such as clothes or decorations as tawdry, you mean that they are cheap and show a lack of taste. 廉价的;俗气的

  • ...tawdry jewellery.

    廉价珠宝

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (故事、事件)令人讨厌的，卑鄙的，不道德的 If you describe something such as a story or an event as tawdry, you mean that it is unpleasant or immoral. (故事、事件)令人讨厌的，卑鄙的，不道德的

  • ...the yawning gulf between her fantasies and the tawdry reality.

    她的幻想和冷酷的现实之间的巨大差距

  • ...the tawdry business of day-to-day bartering and bargaining.

    日复一日做交易讨价还价，让人不胜其烦
