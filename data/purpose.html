purpose
pɜːʳpəs ˈpɜrpəs
Word forms: plural purposes
1 N-COUNT 意义；用途 The purpose of something is the reason for which it is made or done. 意义；用途

  • The purpose of the occasion was to raise money for medical supplies. [+ of]

    那次活动是为筹集资金购买医药物资。

  • Various insurance schemes already exist for this purpose.

    已经有各种保险计划可以实现这一目的。

  • ...the use of nuclear energy for military purposes.

    核能在军事上的运用

  • He was asked about casualties, but said it would serve no purpose to count bodies.

    有人问他伤亡情况，但他说清点尸体是没有意义的。

  • Most of them are destroyed because they've served their purpose.

    它们中大多数在完成使命后被销毁。

SYN reason, point, idea, goal  
2 N-COUNT [with poss] 目标；目的 Your purpose is the thing that you want to achieve. 目标；目的

  • They might well be prepared to do you harm in order to achieve their purpose.

    为达成自己的目的，他们说不定已经做好了伤害你的准备。

  • His purpose was to make a profit by improving the company's performance.

    他的目标是通过提高公司的业绩来实现盈利。

SYN aim, end, plan, hope  
3 N-UNCOUNT 意志；毅力；决心 Purpose is the feeling of having a definite aim and of being determined to achieve it. 意志；毅力；决心

  • The teachers are enthusiastic and have a sense of purpose.

    教师们很热情并且很有决心。

SYN determination, commitment, resolve, will  
4 See also: cross-purposes 
5 PHRASE 实际上 You use for all practical purposes or to all intents and purposes to suggest that a situation is not exactly as you describe it, but the effect is the same as if it were. 实际上

  • For all practical purposes the treaty has already ceased to exist.

    实际上协议已经不复存在。

  • To all intents and purposes the case was closed.

    实际上该案已经了结了。

6 PHRASE 特意地；故意地；有意地 If you do something on purpose, you do it intentionally. 特意地；故意地；有意地

  • Was it an accident or did David do it on purpose?

    是意外还是戴维故意所为？

SYN deliberately, purposely, consciously, intentionally
