de facto deɪfæktoʊ deɪ ˈfæktəʊ diˈfæktoʊ dæˈfæktoʊ dəˈfæktoʊ
ADJ [ADJ n] 事实上的;实际上的 De facto is used to indicate that something is a particular thing, even though it was not planned or intended to be that thing. 事实上的;实际上的 [formal]

  • This might be interpreted as a de facto recognition of the republic's independence.

    可以认为这实际上承认了该共和国的独立。

SYN actual, real, effective, existing  
De facto is also an adverb.

  • They will be de facto in a state of war.

    他们将在事实上处于交战状态。
