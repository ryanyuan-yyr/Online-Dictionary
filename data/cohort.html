cohort
koʊhɔːʳt ˈkoʊˌhɔrt
Word forms: plural cohorts
1 N-COUNT [usually poss NOUN] 朋友；支持者；助手 A person's cohorts are their friends, supporters, or associates. 朋友；支持者；助手 [disapproval]

  • Drake and his cohorts were not pleased with my appointment.

    德雷克那一帮人对我的任命很不满。

SYN supporter, partner, associate, mate  
2 N-COUNT [usually with supplement] (尤指为了进行统计而被看作一个整体的)一群人 A cohort of people is a group who have something in common. Cohort is used especially when a group is being looked at as a whole for statistical purposes. (尤指为了进行统计而被看作一个整体的)一群人

  • Tests were carried out by teachers on the entire cohort of eight to nine year-olds in their third year at primary school.

    测试是由老师在小学三年级所有八岁到九岁的学生中进行的。

  • She speaks for a whole cohort of young Japanese writers.

    她是所有日本年轻作家的代言人。

SYN group, set, band, contingent
