blossom
blɒsəm ˈblɑsəm
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense blossoms, present participle blossoming, past tense , past participle blossomed
1 N-VAR （树木的）花， 花簇 Blossom is the flowers that appear on a tree before the fruit. （树木的）花， 花簇

  • The cherry blossom came out early in Washington this year.

    今年华盛顿的樱花开得很早。

  • ...the blossoms of plants, shrubs and trees.

    草木的花

SYN flower, bloom, bud, efflorescence  
2 VERB 兴盛；变得有魅力；变得成功 If someone or something blossoms, they develop good, attractive, or successful qualities. 兴盛；变得有魅力；变得成功

  • Why do some people take longer than others to blossom?. [VERB]

    为什么有的人比他人大器晚成？

  • What began as a local festival has blossomed into an international event. [VERB + into]

    最初的地方节日已经发展成了国际性盛会。

  • ...the blossoming relationship between Israel and Eastern Europe. [VERB-ing]

    以色列和东欧国家之间日益良好的关系

SYN develop, bloom, grow, mature  
blossoming

  • ...the blossoming of British art, pop and fashion.

    英国艺术、流行乐和时装业的兴盛

3 VERB （树木）开花 When a tree blossoms, it produces blossom. （树木）开花

  • Rain begins to fall and peach trees blossom. [VERB]

    开始下雨了，桃树也开花了。

SYN flower, bloom, bud
