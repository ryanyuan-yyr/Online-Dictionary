chide
tʃaɪd tʃaɪd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense chides, present participle chiding, past tense , past participle chided
VERB 斥责;呵斥;指责 If you chide someone, you speak to them angrily because they have done something wicked or foolish. 斥责;呵斥;指责 [old-fashioned]

  • Cross chided himself for worrying. [V n + for/about]

    克罗斯怨怪自己瞎操心。

  • He gently chided the two women. [VERB noun]

    他温和地责备了那两名女子。

SYN scold, blame, lecture, carpet [informal]
