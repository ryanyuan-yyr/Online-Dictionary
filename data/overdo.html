overdo
oʊvəʳduː ˌoʊvərˈdu
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense overdoes, present participle overdoing, past tense overdid, past participle overdone
1 VERB 把…做得过分；把…做得过火；对…过于夸张 If someone overdoes something, they behave in an exaggerated or extreme way. 把…做得过分；把…做得过火；对…过于夸张

  • ...a recognition by the US central bank that it may have overdone its tightening when it pushed rates up to 6 per cent. [VERB noun]

    美国中央银行承认将利率上调至6%可能是过度紧缩了银根

  • He wants to give up working and stay home to look after the children. She feels, however, that this is overdoing it a bit. [VERB it]

    他想辞去工作，在家照看孩子。而她却觉得这样做有些过头。

2 VERB 超出体力负荷地做 If you overdo an activity, you try to do more than you can physically manage. 超出体力负荷地做

  • It is important never to overdo new exercises. [VERB noun]

    重要的是,不要过度练习新学的动作。

  • The taxi drivers' association is urging its members, who can work as many hours as they want, not to overdo it. [VERB it]

    出租车司机协会劝告那些工作时长不受限制的会员不要疲劳驾驶。
