immediately
ɪmiːdiətli ɪˈmidiɪtli
1 ADV [ADVERB with verb] 立即;马上 If something happens immediately, it happens without any delay. 立即;马上

  • He immediately flung himself to the floor.

    他立即飞身扑到地上。

  • Ingrid answered Peter's letter immediately.

    英格丽德立即给彼得回了信。

2 ADV [ADVERB adjective] 即刻(可见或明了的) If something is immediately obvious, it can be seen or understood without any delay. 即刻(可见或明了的)

  • The cause of the accident was not immediately apparent.

    事故的起因并非一目了然。

3 ADV [ADVERB adjective/-ed] 直接地 Immediately is used to indicate that someone or something is closely and directly involved in a situation. 直接地

  • The man immediately responsible for this misery is the province's governor.

    对这种不幸境况负有直接责任的人是该省的省长。

  • We had the people immediately concerned in the plot.

    我们让故事情节与人们息息相关。

SYN directly, closely, at first hand  
4 ADV 紧接地;紧靠地;贴近地 Immediately is used to emphasize that something comes next, or is next to something else. 紧接地;紧靠地;贴近地

  • They wish to begin immediately after dinner.

    他们希望饭后马上就开始。

  • She always sits immediately behind the driver.

    她总是坐在司机驾驶位置的正后方。

  • Immediately to our right the old German summer resort was awakening.

    紧靠我们右面,古老的德国避暑胜地正在苏醒。

SYN exactly, just, right, closely  
5 CONJ-SUBORD 一…(就…) If one thing happens immediately something else happens, it happens after that event, without any delay. 一…(就…) [mainly British]

  • Immediately I've done it I feel completely disgusted with myself.

    我一做完那件事就对自己感到十分厌恶。
