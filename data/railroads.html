railroad
reɪlroʊd ˈreɪlˌroʊd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense railroads, present participle railroading, past tense , past participle railroaded
1 N-COUNT 铁路；铁道 A railroad is a route between two places along which trains travel on steel rails. 铁路；铁道 [US]

  • ...railroad tracks that led to nowhere.

    向远方延伸的铁轨

  • The railroad finally reached Santa Barbara in 1877.

    铁路终于在1877年修到了圣巴巴拉。

in BRIT, use 英国英语用 railway
2 N-COUNT 铁路公司；铁路部门 A railroad is a company or organization that operates railway routes. 铁路公司；铁路部门 [US]

  • ...The Chicago and Northwestern Railroad.

    芝加哥和西北铁路公司

in BRIT, use 英国英语用 railway
3 VERB 强迫，迫使（某人仓促行事） If you railroad someone into doing something, you make them do it although they do not really want to, by hurrying them and putting pressure on them. 强迫，迫使（某人仓促行事）

  • He more or less railroaded the rest of Europe into recognising the new 'independent' states. [VERB noun + into]

    他几乎是迫使着其他欧洲国家承认了那些新兴的“独立”国家。

  • He railroaded the reforms through. [V n through]

    他强制性地推行了改革。
