subscribe
səbskraɪb səbˈskraɪb
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense subscribes, present participle subscribing, past tense , past participle subscribed
1 VERB 同意;赞成 If you subscribe to an opinion or belief, you are one of a number of people who have this opinion or belief. 同意;赞成

  • I've personally never subscribed to the view that either sex is superior to the other. [VERB + to]

    我个人从来都不同意性别有优劣之分的观点。

2 VERB 订阅，订购(杂志或报纸) If you subscribe to a magazine or a newspaper, you pay to receive copies of it regularly. 订阅，订购(杂志或报纸)

  • My main reason for subscribing to New Scientist is to keep abreast of advances in science. [VERB + to]

    我订阅《新科学家》主要是为了了解科学的最新进展。

3 VERB 申请;注册;报名 If you subscribe to an online newsgroup or service, you send a message saying that you wish to receive it or belong to it. 申请;注册;报名 [computing] [Also VERB]

  • Usenet is a collection of discussion groups, known as newsgroups, to which anybody can subscribe. [VERB + to]

    全球新闻网络是由多个被称为新闻组的讨论组组成，任何人都可以注册加入。

4 VERB 定期资助;定期捐款;定期捐助 If you subscribe to a charity or a campaign, you send money to it regularly. 定期资助;定期捐款;定期捐助

  • I subscribe to a few favourite charities. [VERB to noun]

    我定期向几个中意的慈善机构捐款。

5 VERB 认购(股份) If you subscribe for shares in a company, you apply to buy shares in that company. 认购(股份) [business] [Also VERB n]

  • Employees subscribed for far more shares than were available. [VERB + for]

    员工申请认购的股份远远超出实际可认购的额度。
