chink
tʃɪŋk tʃɪŋk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , plural chinks, present participle chinking, past tense , past participle chinked
1 N-COUNT 裂缝;裂痕;裂纹 A chink in a surface is a very narrow crack or opening in it. 裂缝;裂痕;裂纹

  • ...a chink in the wall. [+ in]

    墙上的裂缝

  • He peered through a chink in the curtains. [+ in]

    他透过帘子之间的缝隙偷看。

SYN opening, crack, gap, rift  
2 N-COUNT (指从缝隙间透入的光)一束，一缕，一线 A chink of light is a small patch of light that shines through a small opening in something. (指从缝隙间透入的光)一束，一缕，一线

  • I noticed a chink of light at the end of the corridor. [+ of]

    我注意到走廊尽头的一缕光线。

3 V-ERG (使)发出叮当声 When objects chink, or you chink them, they touch each other, making a light ringing sound. (使)发出叮当声

  • ...cutlery chinking in the silence. [VERB]

    寂静中叮当作响的餐具

  • They poured out the rest of the wine and she chinked his mug. [VERB noun]

    他们把剩下的酒全倒出来，她和他碰了一下杯。

4 PHRASE [chink inflects] (性格或想法的)弱点，缺陷 If you say that someone has a chink in their armour, you mean that they have a small weakness in their character or in their ideas which makes it easy to harm them. (性格或想法的)弱点，缺陷
