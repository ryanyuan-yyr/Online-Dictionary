proclaim
proʊkleɪm proʊˈkleɪm prəˈkleɪm
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense proclaims, present participle proclaiming, past tense , past participle proclaimed
1 VERB 宣布；声明；公布 If people proclaim something, they formally make it known to the public. 宣布；声明；公布 [Also V n n, V n as n]

  • The Boers rebelled against British rule, proclaiming their independence on 30 December 1880. [VERB noun]

    布尔人反抗英国统治，并于1880年12月30日宣布独立。

  • Britain proudly proclaims that it is a nation of animal lovers. [VERB that]

    英国骄傲地声称自己是一个热爱动物的国家。

  • He still proclaims himself a believer in the Revolution. [V pron-refl n]

    他仍然声称自己拥护革命。

2 VERB 明确表示；清楚地表明 If you proclaim something, you state it in an emphatic way. 明确表示；清楚地表明

  • 'I think we have been heard today,' he proclaimed. [VERB with quote]

    “我想今天我们说的大家都听见了，”他明确地说。

  • He confidently proclaims that he is offering the best value in the market. [VERB that]

    他信心百倍地声称他的产品是市场上最物有所值的。
