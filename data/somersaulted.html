somersault
sʌməʳsɔːlt ˈsʌmərˌsɔlt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense somersaults, present participle somersaulting, past tense , past participle somersaulted
1 N-COUNT 筋斗；跟头 If someone or something does a somersault, they turn over completely in the air. 筋斗；跟头
2 N-COUNT (态度、政策等的) 180 度转变，颠倒 If you say that someone does a somersault, you mean that they change suddenly from having a particular opinion or policy about something to having a completely different opinion or policy. (态度、政策等的) 180 度转变，颠倒

  • What worries some of us is that the Prime Minister may feel simply unable to do a somersault.

    使我们中间的一些人担心的是，首相也许觉得根本就无法做到180度的态度大转变。

  • In the last 30 years, both sexes have performed a somersault in terms of expectations, roles and relationships.

    最近 30 年里，男性和女性在个人期望、社会角色和人际关系方面都发生了 180 度的大转变。

3 VERB (一次或多次)翻筋斗 If someone or something somersaults, they perform one or more somersaults. (一次或多次)翻筋斗

  • His boat hit a wave and somersaulted at speed. [VERB]

    他的船撞上了大浪，飞速地翻转着。

  • I hit him back and he somersaulted down the stairs. [VERB prep.]

    我还手打了他，他顺着楼梯滚了下去。
