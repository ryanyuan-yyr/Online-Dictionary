pack in
1 PHRASAL VERB 停止做 If you pack something in, you stop doing it. 停止做 [mainly British, informal]

  • I'd just packed in a job the day before. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我前天刚刚辞了工作。

  • Pack it in. Stop being spiteful. [VERB noun PREP.]

    别闹了，不要恶意伤人。

SYN resign from, leave, give up, quit [informal]  
2 PHRASAL VERB 堆满；挤满；塞满 If someone packs in things or people, they fit a lot of them into a limited space or time. 堆满；挤满；塞满 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Prisons are having to pack in as many inmates as possible. [VERB PREP. noun]

    监狱只好拼命往里面塞犯人。

  • It's kind of a referendum, though a lot of issues are packed in. [be VERB-ed PREP.]

    这算是全民公决，虽然牵涉到很多问题。

SYN cram, squeeze in, fill to capacity  
3 PHRASE （戏剧、电影、活动等）吸引大批观众 If a play, film or event packs them in, lots of people go to see it. （戏剧、电影、活动等）吸引大批观众

  • 'Blow your head!' is still packing them in at Camden's Jazz Café every Friday night.

    每个星期五晚上，“爆头”乐队在卡姆登爵士咖啡馆仍然人气十足。
