picnic
pɪknɪk ˈpɪknɪk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense picnics, present participle picnicking, past tense , past participle picnicked
1 N-COUNT 野餐；户外用餐 When people have a picnic, they eat a meal out of doors, usually in a field or a forest, or at the beach. 野餐；户外用餐

  • We're going on a picnic tomorrow.

    我们明天去野餐。

  • We'll take a picnic lunch.

    我们要到户外用午餐。

SYN excursion, fête champêtre, barbecue, barbie [informal]  
2 VERB 野餐 When people picnic somewhere, they have a picnic. 野餐

  • Afterwards, we picnicked on the riverbank. [VERB]

    之后，我们在河边野餐。

  • ...such a perfect day for picnicking. [VERB-ing]

    适合野餐的好天气

picnicker

  • ...fires started by careless picnickers.

    粗心大意的野餐者引发的火灾

3 PHRASE 不是件轻松（或愉快）的事 If you say that an experience, task, or activity is no picnic, you mean that it is quite difficult or unpleasant. 不是件轻松（或愉快）的事 [informal]

  • Emigrating is no picnic.

    移民不是件轻松的事。
