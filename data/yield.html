yield
jɪəld jild
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense yields, present participle yielding, past tense , past participle yielded
1 VERB 屈服;屈从;让步 If you yield to someone or something, you stop resisting them. 屈服;屈从;让步 [formal]

  • Will she yield to growing pressure for her to retire?. [VERB + to]

    面对要求其隐退的压力越来越大，她会屈服吗？

  • I yielded to an impulse. [VERB to noun]

    我没有忍住冲动。

  • If the government does not yield, it should face sufficient military force to ensure its certain and swift defeat. [VERB]

    如果政府不让步，它就会面临重兵围困，必将迅速落败。

2 VERB . 放弃;缴出;让出 If you yield something that you have control of or responsibility for, you allow someone else to have control or responsibility for it . 放弃;缴出;让出 [formal]

  • He may yield control. [VERB noun]

    他可能会放弃控制权。

  • The President is now under pressure to yield power to the republics. [VERB noun to noun]

    总统现在面临着让权给共和党人的压力。

SYN relinquish [formal] , resign, hand over, surrender  
Yield up means the same as yield .yield up同yield

  • Giulio Andreotti yielded up the prime ministership last summer.

    朱利奥·安德烈奥蒂去年夏天辞去了总理之职。

3 VERB 被…替代;为…所取代 If one thing yields to another thing, it is replaced by this other thing. 被…替代;为…所取代 [formal]

  • Boston's traditional drab brick was slow to yield to the modern glass palaces of so many American urban areas. [VERB to noun]

    波士顿传统而单调的砖石建筑并没有迅速被许多美国城市地区流行的现代玻璃大厦所取代。

4 VERB （行人或车辆）让路，让行 If a moving person or a vehicle yields, they slow down or stop in order to allow other people or vehicles to pass in front of them. （行人或车辆）让路，让行 [US]

  • When entering a trail or starting a descent, yield to other skiers. [VERB + to]

    进入滑雪道或开始下滑时，要给其他滑雪者让路。

  • ...examples of common signs like No Smoking or Yield. [VERB]

    “请勿吸烟”或“让行”之类的常见标志的例子

in BRIT, usually use 英国英语通常用 give way
5 VERB （受压）折断，活动，移位 If something yields, it breaks or moves position because force or pressure has been put on it. （受压）折断，活动，移位

  • The door yielded easily when he pushed it. [VERB]

    他一推，门就开了。

6 VERB 出产（作物）;产（肉） If an area of land yields a particular amount of a crop, this is the amount that is produced. You can also say that a number of animals yield a particular amount of meat. 出产（作物）;产（肉）

  • Last year 400,000 acres of land yielded a crop worth $1.75 billion. [VERB noun]

    去年40万英亩的土地产值达17.5亿美元。

SYN produce, give, provide, pay  
Yield up means the same as yield .yield up同yield

  • The shallow sea bed yields up an abundance of food.

    浅海床提供了丰富的食物。

7 N-COUNT （农作物或牲畜的）产量，产出 A yield is the amount of food produced on an area of land or by a number of animals. （农作物或牲畜的）产量，产出

  • ...improving the yield of the crop.

    提高农作物产量

  • Polluted water lessens crop yields.

    受污染的水源会降低农作物产量。

SYN produce, crop, harvest, output  
8 VERB （税收或投资）产生，带来（收益或效益） If a tax or investment yields an amount of money or profit, this money or profit is obtained from it. （税收或投资）产生，带来（收益或效益） [business]

  • It yielded a profit of at least $36 million. [VERB noun]

    它带来了至少3,600万美元的收益。

9 N-COUNT 投资收益;利润 A yield is the amount of money or profit produced by an investment. 投资收益;利润 [business]

  • ...a yield of 4%.

    4%的收益

  • The high yields available on the dividend shares made them attractive to private investors. [+ on]

    红利股票的高收益率对私人投资者极具吸引力。

  • ...the yield on a bank's investments. [+ on]

    一家银行投资带来的收益

SYN profit, return, income, revenue  
10 VERB 产生（结果）；得出（信息） If something yields a result or piece of information, it produces it. 产生（结果）；得出（信息）

  • This research has been in progress since 1961 and has yielded a great number of positive results.

    这项研究自1961年起一直在开展，已取得了不少积极成果。

  • His trip to Melbourne had yielded a lot of information. [VERB noun]

    他的墨尔本之行让他大开眼界。
