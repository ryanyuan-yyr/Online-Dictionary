gorge
gɔːʳdʒ gɔrdʒ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense gorges, present participle gorging, past tense , past participle gorged
1 N-COUNT 峡；峡谷 A gorge is a deep, narrow valley with very steep sides, usually where a river passes through mountains or an area of hard rock. 峡；峡谷
SYN ravine, canyon, pass, clough [dialect]  
2 VERB 贪婪地吃；狼吞虎咽 If you gorge on something or gorge yourself on it, you eat lots of it in a very greedy way. 贪婪地吃；狼吞虎咽 [Also VERB]

  • I could spend each day gorging on chocolate. [VERB + on]

    我可以每天只吃巧克力就够了。

  • ...teenagers gorging themselves on ice-cream sundaes. [V pron-refl + on]

    大口吃着圣代冰激凌的青少年

SYN overeat, bolt, devour, gobble
