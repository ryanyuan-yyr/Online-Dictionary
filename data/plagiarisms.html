plagiarism
pleɪdʒərɪzəm ˈpleɪdʒəˌrɪzəm ˈpleɪdʒəˌrɪzəm
Word forms: plural plagiarisms
1 N-UNCOUNT 剽窃；抄袭 Plagiarism is the practice of using or copying someone else's idea or work and pretending that you thought of it or created it. 剽窃；抄袭

  • Now he's in real trouble. He's accused of plagiarism.

    现在他是真遇到麻烦了。他被指控剽窃。

SYN copying, borrowing, theft, appropriation  
plagiarist

  • Colleagues call Oates an unlikely plagiarist.

    同事称难以相信奥茨是剽窃者。

2 N-COUNT 剽窃物；抄袭物 A plagiarism is an idea or a piece of writing or music that has been secretly copied from someone else's work. 剽窃物；抄袭物

  • Most famous political quotes are plagiarisms and this was no exception.

    大部分著名的政治语录都是盗用前人的，这个也不例外。

Quotations
If you steal from one author, it's plagiarism; if you steal from many, it's researchWilson Mizner
