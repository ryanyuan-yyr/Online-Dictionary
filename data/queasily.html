queasy
kwiːzi ˈkwizi
Word forms: comparative queasier, superlative queasiest
1 ADJ-GRADED 恶心的;想呕吐的 If you feel queasy or if you have a queasy stomach, you feel rather ill, as if you are going to be sick. 恶心的;想呕吐的 [informal]

  • He was very prone to seasickness and already felt queasy.

    他快晕船了，已经感到恶心了。

SYN sick, ill, nauseous, squeamish  
queasiness

  • The food did nothing to stifle her queasiness.

    食物并没有压住她想呕吐的感觉。

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 忧虑的；不安的;担心的 If you feel queasy about something, you are a little worried about it. 忧虑的；不安的;担心的 [informal]

  • Some people feel queasy about how their names and addresses have been obtained.

    一些人为自己的姓名和地址是如何被泄露出去的感到不安。

SYN uneasy, concerned, worried, troubled  
queasiness

  • Despite their queasiness, if war comes, most MPs will back our lads.

    虽然感到不安，但是如果战争来临，大多数议员还是会支持我们的小伙子的。
