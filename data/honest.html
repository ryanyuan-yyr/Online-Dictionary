honest
ɒnɪst ˈɑnɪst
1 ADJ-GRADED 诚实的;正直的 If you describe someone as honest, you mean that they always tell the truth, and do not try to deceive people or break the law. 诚实的;正直的

  • My dad was the most honest man I ever met.

    爸爸是我所见过的最正直的人。

  • I know she's honest and reliable.

    我知道她诚实可靠。

SYN trustworthy, decent, upright, reliable  
honestly

  • She fought honestly for a just cause and for freedom.

    她为了正义的事业和自由而光明正大地斗争。

2 ADJ-GRADED 坦诚的;直率的;不隐瞒真相的 If you are honest in a particular situation, you tell the complete truth or give your sincere opinion, even if this is not very pleasant. 坦诚的;直率的;不隐瞒真相的

  • I was honest about what I was doing. [+ about]

    我对自己所做的事情并无隐瞒。

  • He had been honest with her and she had tricked him!. [+ with]

    他对她坦诚相见，而她却骗了他！

  • What do you think of the school, in your honest opinion?

    坦率地说，你认为这个学校怎么样？

SYN open, direct, frank, plain  
honestly

  • It came as a shock to hear an old friend speak so honestly about Ted.

    听到一位老朋友如此坦率地谈起特德真让人大吃一惊。

3 ADV 真的;确实 You say 'honest' before or after a statement to emphasize that you are telling the truth and that you want people to believe you. 真的;确实 [informal, emphasis]

  • I'm not sure, honest.

    我不能肯定，真的。

4 PHRASE （表示强调）真的，实在，千真万确 Some people say 'honest to God' to emphasize their feelings or to emphasize that something is really true. （表示强调）真的，实在，千真万确 [informal, emphasis]

  • I wish we weren't doing this, Lillian, honest to God, I really do.

    我希望我们没有在干这事，莉莲，真的，我真这么想。

  • You wanna know the honest-to-God truth?

    你想知道真正的事实吗？

5 PHRASE 老实说;说实在的 You can say 'to be honest' before or after a statement to indicate that you are telling the truth about your own opinions or feelings, especially if you think these will disappoint the person you are talking to. 老实说;说实在的 [feelings]

  • To be honest the house is not quite our style.

    说实话，这所房子不太适合我们。

  • I'd rather get it out the way, to be honest.

    老实说，我情愿把它处理掉。
