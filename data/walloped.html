wallop
wɒləp ˈwɑləp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense wallops, present participle walloping, past tense , past participle walloped
VERB 重击;狠揍 If you wallop someone or something, you hit them very hard, often causing a dull sound. 重击;狠揍 [informal]

  • Once, she walloped me over the head with a frying pan. [VERB noun prep.]

    有一次，她用煎锅狠狠地砸了我的头。

  • ...a big-serving tennis player who wallops the ball so hard that it often finishes out of court. [VERB noun]

    常常因用力过度而将球击出场外的大力发球的网球运动员

SYN hit, beat, strike, knock  
Wallop is also a noun.

  • With one brutal wallop, Clarke flattened him.

    克拉克毫不留情地一拳把他打翻在地。
