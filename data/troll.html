troll
trɒl troʊl trəʊl troʊl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense trolls, present participle trolling, past tense , past participle trolled
1 N-COUNT (斯堪的纳维亚神话中居于洞穴或山上、专门偷小孩的)丑陋怪物，山精，巨怪 In Scandinavian mythology, trolls are creatures who look like ugly people. They live in caves or on mountains and steal children. (斯堪的纳维亚神话中居于洞穴或山上、专门偷小孩的)丑陋怪物，山精，巨怪
2 VERB 闲逛;游荡 If you troll somewhere, you go there in a casual and unhurried way. 闲逛;游荡 [mainly British, informal]

  • I trolled along to see Michael Frayn's play, 'Noises Off'. [VERB prep./adv.]

    我慢悠悠地走着去看迈克尔·弗莱恩的戏剧《糊涂戏班》。

  • He rowed her ashore and then continued trolling around the lake. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他拢岸让她下船，然后继续悠闲地在湖上划着。

3 VERB 随便浏览;随便翻翻 If you troll through papers or files, you look through them in a fairly casual way. 随便浏览;随便翻翻 [mainly British, informal]

  • Trolling through the files revealed a photograph of me drinking coffee in the office. [VERB + through]

    我随便浏览文件时发现了一张我在办公室喝咖啡的照片。
