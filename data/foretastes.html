foretaste
fɔːʳteɪst ˈfɔrˌteɪst fɔrˈteɪst
Word forms: plural foretastes
N-COUNT [usu a N of n] 预示;预先的体验;征兆 If you describe an event as a foretaste of a future situation, you mean that it suggests to you what that future situation will be like. 预示;预先的体验;征兆

  • It was a foretaste of things to come. [+ of]

    事情初露端倪。

  • This is but a foretaste of what the emerging technologies will enable us to do.

    新科技将会给我们带来什么，这仅仅是一次初体验。
