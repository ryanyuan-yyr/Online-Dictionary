blind
blaɪnd blaɪnd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense blinds, present participle blinding, past tense , past participle blinded
1 ADJ 盲的；瞎的；失明的 Someone who is blind is unable to see because their eyes are damaged. 盲的；瞎的；失明的

  • I started helping him run the business when he went blind.

    他失明以后，我就开始帮他打理生意。

  • How would you explain colour to a blind person?

    你如何向盲人解释颜色？

SYN sightless, unsighted, unseeing, eyeless  
The blind are people who are blind.盲人；瞎子；失明的人

  • He was a teacher of the blind.

    他曾教过盲人。

blindness

  • Early diagnosis and treatment can usually prevent blindness.

    早期的诊断和治疗通常可以预防失明。

2 VERB 使…看不见；使…失明 If something blinds you, it makes you unable to see, either for a short time or permanently. 使…看不见；使…失明

  • The sun hit the windscreen, momentarily blinding him. [VERB noun]

    太阳照在挡风玻璃上，晃得他一时看不见东西。

SYN stop someone seeing, block someone's vision, obscure someone's vision  
3 ADJ [verb-link ADJ] 暂时看不见的；双眼模糊的；目眩的 If you are blind with something such as tears or a bright light, you are unable to see for a short time because of the tears or light. 暂时看不见的；双眼模糊的；目眩的

  • Her mother groped for the back of the chair, her eyes blind with tears. [+ with]

    她妈妈泪眼蒙眬地伸手去摸椅背。

blindly

  • Lettie groped blindly for the glass.

    莱蒂摸索着找杯子。

4 ADJ-GRADED 看不到的；视而不见的；没有意识到的 If you say that someone is blind to a fact or a situation, you mean that they ignore it or are unaware of it, although you think that they should take notice of it or be aware of it. 看不到的；视而不见的；没有意识到的 [disapproval]

  • David's good looks and impeccable manners had always made her blind to his faults.

    戴维英俊的长相和完美的举止总是让她对他的缺点视而不见。

  • All the time I was blind to your suffering.

    一直以来，我都没有意识到你遭受的痛苦。

SYN unaware of, unconscious of, deaf to, ignorant of  
blindness

  • ...blindness in government policy to the very existence of the unemployed.

    政府政策对失业者基本生存问题的漠视

5 VERB 使无法意识到；使无视；使无法认清 If something blinds you to the real situation, it prevents you from realizing that it exists or from understanding it properly. 使无法意识到；使无视；使无法认清

  • He never allowed his love of Australia to blind him to his countrymen's faults. [VERB noun + to]

    他从来不会因为对澳大利亚的热爱而看不到自己同胞的缺点。

6 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] （信仰或行动）盲目的，不理智的 You can describe someone's beliefs or actions as blind when you think that they seem to take no notice of important facts or behave in an unreasonable way. （信仰或行动）盲目的，不理智的 [disapproval]

  • ...her blind faith in the wisdom of the Church.

    她对教会智慧的盲目信仰

  • Lesley yelled at him with blind, hating rage.

    莱斯莉心生愤恨，盛怒之下对他大嚷大叫。

SYN unquestioning, prejudiced, wholesale, implicit  
7 ADJ [ADJ n] （拐角）不在视线中的，看不见的 A blind corner is one that you cannot see round because something is blocking your view. （拐角）不在视线中的，看不见的

  • He tried to overtake three cars on a blind corner and crashed head-on into a lorry.

    他试图在一个死角处超过前面的三辆车，结果一头撞在了一辆卡车上。

8 ADJ [ADJ n] 无窗的；无门的；没有开口的；封死的 A blind wall or building is one which has no windows or doors. 无窗的；无门的；没有开口的；封死的

  • I remembered a huddle of stone buildings with blind walls.

    我记得一片没有窗户的石头建筑。

9 N-COUNT 百叶窗；卷帘 A blind is a roll of cloth or paper which you can pull down over a window as a covering. 百叶窗；卷帘
10See also:Venetian blind 
11 See also: blinding  blindly  colour-blind 
12 PHRASE 对…视而不见；对…假装看不见 If you say that someone is turning a blind eye to something bad or illegal that is happening, you mean that you think they are pretending not to notice that it is happening so that they will not have to do anything about it. 对…视而不见；对…假装看不见 [disapproval]

  • Teachers are turning a blind eye to pupils smoking at school, a report reveals today.

    今日一篇报道称，老师们对学生在学校抽烟睁一只眼闭一只眼。

  • I can't turn a blind eye when someone is being robbed.

    当有人遭到抢劫时，我不能视而不见。
