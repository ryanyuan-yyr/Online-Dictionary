costume
kɒstjuːm -tuːm ˈkɑsˌtum ˈkɑsˌtjum
Word forms: plural costumes
1 N-VAR (演员或表演者的)戏服，表演服装 An actor's or performer's costume is the set of clothes they wear while they are performing. (演员或表演者的)戏服，表演服装

  • Even from a distance the effect of his fox costume was stunning.

    即使从远处看,他的狐狸戏服也很抢眼。

  • The performers, in costume and make-up, were walking up and down backstage.

    上了妆穿着戏服的演员在后台走来走去。

  • In all, she has eight costume changes.

    她总共换了8次演出服。

SYN outfit, dress, clothing, get-up [informal]  
2 N-UNCOUNT (某历史时期或某国的)服装，装束 The clothes worn by people at a particular time in history, or in a particular country, are referred to as a particular type of costume . (某历史时期或某国的)服装，装束

  • ...men and women in eighteenth-century costume.

    身穿18世纪服装的男男女女

  • In the colourful markets at Chincero and Pisac, women still wear their traditional costume.

    在金测罗和皮萨克五颜六色的集市上，女人们还是身着传统装束。

3 ADJ [ADJ n] (戏剧)古装的 A costume play or drama is one which is set in the past and in which the actors wear the type of clothes that were worn in that period. (戏剧)古装的

  • ...a lavish costume drama set in Ireland and the US in the 1890s.

    一部耗资巨大、以19世纪90年代的爱尔兰和美国为背景的古装戏
