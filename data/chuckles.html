chuckle
tʃʌkəl ˈtʃʌkəl ˈtʃʌkəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense chuckles, present participle chuckling, past tense , past participle chuckled
VERB 低声轻笑;偷笑 When you chuckle, you laugh quietly. 低声轻笑;偷笑 [Also VERB with quote]

  • The banker chuckled and said, 'Of course not.'. [VERB]

    银行家暗自发笑并说道：“当然不。”

  • He chuckled at her forthrightness. [V + at/over]

    她的直率让他窃笑。

SYN laugh, giggle, snigger, chortle  
Chuckle is also a noun.

  • He gave a little chuckle.

    他轻声一笑。
