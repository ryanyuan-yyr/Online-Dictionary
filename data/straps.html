strap
stræp stræp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense straps, present participle strapping, past tense , past participle strapped
1 N-COUNT 带子;皮带;布带 A strap is a narrow piece of leather, cloth, or other material. Straps are used to carry things, fasten things together, or to hold a piece of clothing in place. 带子;皮带;布带

  • Nancy gripped the strap of her beach bag. [+ of]

    南希抓住了自己沙滩包的带子。

  • Brian pulled the straps through the buckles of his suitcase.

    布赖恩将带子穿过自己手提箱的带扣。

  • She pulled the strap of her nightgown onto her shoulder. [+ of]

    她将自己睡袍的吊带拉到肩膀上。

  • I undid my watch strap.

    我解开了表带。

SYN tie, thong, leash, belt  
2 VERB 用带子系(或绑、捆、扎) If you strap something somewhere, you fasten it there with a strap. 用带子系(或绑、捆、扎)

  • Strapping the skis on the roof, we boarded the hovercraft in Dover. [VERB noun prep.]

    用带子把滑水板绑在船顶后，我们就在多佛登上了气垫船。

  • She strapped the gun belt around her middle. [VERB noun prep.]

    她将枪带系在腰上。

  • Through the basement window I saw him strap on his pink cycling helmet. [V n with on/in/down]

    透过地下室的窗户，我看到他正在把他那粉色的自行车头盔系在头上。

  • The carer will have to do all the work: placing the patient on the seat, strapping him in, taking him off again at the top. [V n with on/in/down]

    护工将要全程照顾：将病人抱到座位上，替他系好安全带，到顶层后再将他抱下来。

SYN fasten, tie, secure, bind
