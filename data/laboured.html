laboured
leɪbəʳd
1 ADJ-GRADED （呼吸）缓慢的，困难的，费力的 If someone's breathing is laboured, it is slow and seems to take a lot of effort. （呼吸）缓慢的，困难的，费力的

  • From his slow walk and laboured breathing, Ginny realized he was far from well.

    看他步履缓慢、呼吸困难的样子，金尼意识到他的身体还远未恢复。

SYN difficult, forced, strained, heavy  
2 ADJ-GRADED （文章或言谈）刻意的，矫揉造作的 If something such as someone's writing or speech is laboured, they have put too much effort into it so it seems awkward and unnatural. （文章或言谈）刻意的，矫揉造作的

  • The prose of his official communications was so laboured, pompous and verbose.

    他笔下的公文矫揉浮华，长篇大论。

  • Daniel's few encounters with Gold had been characterized by a laboured politeness.

    丹尼尔与戈尔德的几次碰面都只是虚礼客套一番。

SYN contrived, studied, affected, awkward
