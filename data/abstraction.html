abstraction
æbstrækʃən æbˈstrækʃən
Word forms: plural abstractions
1 N-VAR 抽象概念；抽象名称 An abstraction is a general idea rather than one relating to a particular object, person, or situation. 抽象概念；抽象名称 [formal]

  • Is it worth fighting a big war, in the name of an abstraction like sovereignty?

    在主权之类抽象概念的名义下打一场大战是否值得呢？

SYN concept, thought, idea, view  
2 N-UNCOUNT 出神；心不在焉 Abstraction is the state of being very deep in thought. 出神；心不在焉 [written]

  • Andrew noticed her abstraction and asked, 'What's bothering you?'

    安德鲁注意到她在愣神，便问道：“你怎么啦？”

SYN absent-mindedness, musing, preoccupation, daydreaming  
3 N-UNCOUNT （艺术的）抽象主义 Abstraction is the use of shapes and patterns in art. （艺术的）抽象主义 [technical]
