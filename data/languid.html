languid
læŋgwɪd ˈlæŋgwɪd
ADJ-GRADED 无精打采的；懒洋洋的；倦怠的 If you describe someone as languid, you mean that they show little energy or interest and are very slow and casual in their movements. 无精打采的；懒洋洋的；倦怠的 [literary]

  • To his delight a familiar, tall, languid figure lowered itself down the steps of a club.

    看到一个熟悉的高大身影沿着俱乐部的台阶无精打采地走下来，他高兴极了。

  • Time spent at Jumby Bay can be as energetic or as languid as you wish.

    来琼比湾消磨时光，动静皆宜，尽可随心所欲。

SYN inactive, lazy, indifferent, lethargic  
languidly

  • We sat about languidly after dinner.

    晚饭后我们懒洋洋地闲坐着。

  • A tanned blonde in a bikini swims languidly in the clear swimming pool.

    一位身穿比基尼泳装、皮肤晒成古铜色的金发女郎在清澈的泳池里慵懒地游着泳。
