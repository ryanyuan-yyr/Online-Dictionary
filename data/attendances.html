attendance
ətendəns əˈtɛndəns
Word forms: plural attendances
1 N-UNCOUNT 出席;参加;出勤 Someone's attendance at an event or an institution is the fact that they are present at the event or go regularly to the institution. 出席;参加;出勤

  • Her attendance at school was sporadic. [+ at]

    她三天两头旷课。

SYN presence, being there, attending, appearance  
2 N-VAR 出席的人数 The attendance at an event is the number of people who are present at it. 出席的人数

  • Average weekly cinema attendance in February was 2.41 million.

    2月份，平均每周的观影人数为241万。

  • This year attendances were 28% lower than forecast.

    今年的出席人数比预期少28%。

  • Some estimates put the attendance at 60,000.

    一些预测认为将有6万人参加。

SYN turnout, audience, gate, congregation  
3 PHRASE 到（某地）；出席（某活动） If someone is in attendance at a place or an event, they are there. 到（某地）；出席（某活动）

  • Councillor Paul Tisley will be in attendance at the start of the meeting to welcome us.

    议员保罗·提斯里将在会议开始时出席，以欢迎我们。

  • Police with riot gear and several fire engines are in attendance.

    防暴警察和数辆消防车都到了。

4 PHRASE 陪同；陪伴 If someone is in attendance on an important person, they are accompanying that person as a servant or assistant. 陪同；陪伴

  • He lived in considerable style, travelling widely, usually with a cook, valet, butler and chauffeur in attendance.

    他活得相当潇洒，通常带着厨师、用人、管家和司机到处游玩。
