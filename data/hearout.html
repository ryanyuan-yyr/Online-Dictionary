hear out
PHRASAL VERB 听…把话说完 If you hear someone out, you listen to them without interrupting them until they have finished saying everything that they want to say. 听…把话说完

  • MPs heard him out in silence. [VERB noun PREP.]

    下院议员静静地听他把话讲完。

  • Perhaps, when you've heard me out, you'll appreciate the reason for secrecy. [VERB noun PREP.]

    也许当你听我把话讲完后，你就会明白为什么要保密了。

  • He shows keen interest in his friends, hearing out their problems and offering counsel. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他对自己的朋友表现出极大的关注，聆听他们的难题并提出忠告。
