choosy
tʃuːzi ˈtʃuzi
ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 挑剔的;过分讲究的 Someone who is choosy is difficult to please because they will only accept something if it is exactly what they want or if it is of very high quality. 挑剔的;过分讲究的 [mainly informal]

  • Skiers should be particularly choosy about the insurance policy they buy. [+ about]

    滑雪者在选择购买保险的问题上应该尤其慎重。

SYN fussy, particular, exacting, discriminating
