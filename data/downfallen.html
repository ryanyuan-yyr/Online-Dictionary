downfall
daʊnfɔːl ˈdaʊnˌfɔl
Word forms: plural downfalls
1 N-COUNT [usually with poss] 衰落;衰败;垮台 The downfall of a successful or powerful person or institution is their loss of success or power. 衰落;衰败;垮台

  • His lack of experience had led to his downfall.

    经验不足导致了他的下台。

  • ...people wishing to see the downfall of the government.

    希望看到政府倒台的人们

SYN ruin, fall, destruction, collapse  
2 N-COUNT [usually with poss] 失败(或垮台)的原因 The thing that was a person's downfall caused them to fail or lose power. 失败(或垮台)的原因

  • His honesty had been his downfall.

    他败在诚实。

  • Alan's downfall was women.

    艾伦栽在了“女人”这两个字上。
