flaming
fleɪmɪŋ ˈfleɪmɪŋ
1 ADJ [usually ADJ n] 燃烧的;冒火焰的 Flaming is used to describe something that is burning and producing a lot of flames. 燃烧的;冒火焰的

  • The plane, which was full of fuel, scattered flaming fragments over a large area.

    燃烧的碎片从装满燃油的飞机上散落到大片区域里。

2 ADJ [ADJ n] 鲜红的;火红的;橙黄的 Something that is flaming red or orange is bright red or orange in colour. 鲜红的;火红的;橙黄的

  • He has flaming red hair.

    他有一头火红的头发。

3 ADJ [ADJ n] (争吵、脾气)激烈的，火暴的 A flaming row or a flaming temper, for example, is a very angry row or a very bad temper. (争吵、脾气)激烈的，火暴的 [emphasis]

  • She has had a flaming row with her lover.

    她刚和恋人发生了激烈的争吵。

SYN intense, angry, raging, impassioned  
4 ADJ [ADJ n] (尤用于恼怒时表示强调)该死的，讨厌的，可恶的 Some people use flaming to emphasize what they are saying, especially when they are annoyed. (尤用于恼怒时表示强调)该死的，讨厌的，可恶的 [mainly British, informal, rude, emphasis]

  • Farmers don't need much prompting to tell you that 'flaming dog owners' are the scourge of their lives.

    不用怎么提示农场主们就会告诉你，“该死的养狗人”是他们生活的灾星。

Flaming is also an adverb.

  • Oh God, you get so flaming worked up about everything.

    哦，天哪，你总是这么激动，真该死。
