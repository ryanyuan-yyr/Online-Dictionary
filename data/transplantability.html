transplant
trænsplɑːnt trænsplɑːnt -plænt -plænt trænsˈplænt ˈtrænsˌplænt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense transplants, present participle transplanting, past tense , past participle transplanted
The noun is pronounced /'trænsplɑːnt, -plænt/. The verb is pronounced /træns'plɑːnt, -'plænt/. 名词读作 /'trænsplɑːnt, -plænt/。动词读作 /træns'plɑːnt, -'plænt/。
1 N-VAR (器官)移植 A transplant is a medical operation in which a part of a person's body is replaced because it is diseased. (器官)移植

  • He was recovering from a heart transplant operation.

    他做了心脏移植手术，正在康复。

  • ...the controversy over the sale of human organs for transplant.

    出售移植用人体器官引起的争议

2 VERB 移植(器官) If doctors transplant an organ such as a heart or a kidney, they use it to replace a patient's diseased organ. 移植(器官)

  • The operation to transplant a kidney is now fairly routine. [VERB noun]

    肾脏移植手术如今已相当常见。

  • ...transplanted organs such as hearts and kidneys. [VERB-ed]

    类似心脏、肾脏这样的移植器官

SYN implant, transfer, graft  
transplantation

  • ...a shortage of kidneys for transplantation.

    移植用肾脏的短缺

  • Bone marrow transplantation began 20 years ago.

    骨髓移植开始于 20 年前。

3 VERB 使迁移;使移民;移种 To transplant someone or something means to move them to a different place. 使迁移;使移民;移种

  • Marriage had transplanted Rebecca from London to Manchester. [V n + from/to/into]

    因为结婚，丽贝卡从伦敦移居到了曼彻斯特。

  • In the 19th century, the Santa Claus tradition seems to have been transplanted back to Europe… [V n from/to/into n]

    19 世纪，圣诞老人的习俗似乎又传回了欧洲。

  • Farmers will be able to seed it directly, rather than having to transplant seedlings. [VERB noun]

    农民将能够直接播种，而不用移种秧苗。

SYN transfer, take, bring, carry
