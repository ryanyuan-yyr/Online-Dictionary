personify
pəʳsɒnɪfaɪ pərˈsɑnəˌfaɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense personifies, present participle personifying, past tense , past participle personified
VERB 是…的典范；集中体现；象征 If you say that someone personifies a particular thing or quality, you mean that they seem to be a perfect example of that thing, or to have that quality to a very large degree. 是…的典范；集中体现；象征

  • She seemed to personify goodness and nobility. [VERB noun]

    她宛如善良和高贵的化身。

  • ...the world of inter-war decency as personified by Stanley Baldwin. [VERB-ed]

    在斯坦利·鲍德温身上所体现的两次大战间体面正派的人生

  • On other occasions she can be charm personified. [VERB-ed]

    在其他场合，她是魅力的化身。

SYN embody, represent, express, mirror
