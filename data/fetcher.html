fetch
fetʃ fɛtʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense fetches, present participle fetching, past tense , past participle fetched
1 VERB 去拿;去取;去接 If you fetch something or someone, you go and get them from the place where they are. 去拿;去取;去接

  • Sylvia fetched a towel from the bathroom. [VERB noun]

    西尔维娅去卫生间拿了一条毛巾。

  • Fetch me a glass of water. [VERB noun noun]

    去给我拿杯水来。

  • The caddie ran over to fetch something for him. [VERB noun + for]

    球童跑过去给他拿东西。

SYN bring, pick up, collect, go and get  
2 VERB 卖得;售得 If something fetches a particular sum of money, it is sold for that amount. 卖得;售得

  • The painting is expected to fetch between two and three million pounds. [VERB noun]

    预计这幅画将售得两三百万英镑。

SYN sell for, make, raise, earn  
3 See also: far-fetched  fetching 
4 PHRASE [Vs inflect] 做零活;干杂务;当听差 If you fetch and carry, you perform simple, often boring tasks for someone, such as collecting and carrying things for them. 做零活;干杂务;当听差

  • I helped out in the tents fetching and carrying.

    我在帐篷里帮忙打杂。

fetch up
