chord
kɔːʳd kɔrd
Word forms: plural chords
1 N-COUNT 和弦;和音 A chord is a number of musical notes played or sung at the same time with a pleasing effect. 和弦;和音

  • ...the opening chords of 'Stairway to Heaven'. [+ of]

    《通往天堂的阶梯》的开篇和弦

2See also:vocal cords 
3 PHRASE 激起共鸣;触动心弦 If something strikes a chord with you, it makes you feel sympathy or enthusiasm. 激起共鸣;触动心弦

  • Mr Jenkins' arguments for stability struck a chord with Europe's two most powerful politicians.

    詹金斯先生关于稳定的论点引起了欧洲两位最有影响的政治家的共鸣。

  • Burke's sentiments undoubtedly struck a responsive chord in Parliament.

    伯克的观点无疑引起了议会的积极回应。
