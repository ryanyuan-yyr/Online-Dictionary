dull
dʌl dʌl
Word forms: comparative duller, superlative dullest, 3rd person singular present tense dulls, present participle dulling, past tense , past participle dulled
1 ADJ-GRADED 枯燥无味的;沉闷的;无聊的 If you describe someone or something as dull, you mean they are not interesting or exciting. 枯燥无味的;沉闷的;无聊的 [disapproval]

  • They are both nice people but can be rather dull.

    他俩都是好人但非常沉闷。

  • I felt she found me boring and dull.

    我觉得她认为我既无趣又沉闷。

  • The documentary lasts for more than two-and-a-half hours, and there is scarcely a dull minute.

    该纪录片片长超过两个半小时,却鲜有沉闷之处。

SYN boring, tedious, dreary, flat  
dullness

  • They enjoy anything that breaks the dullness of their routine life.

    只要能打破他们日常刻板、沉闷的生活，任何事情他们都喜欢。

2 ADJ-GRADED 没精打采的;无生气的 Someone or something that is dull is not very lively or energetic. 没精打采的;无生气的

  • The body's natural rhythms mean we all feel dull and sleepy between 1 and 3pm.

    人体生物钟会让我们在下午 1 点到 3 点之间感觉没精打采、昏昏欲睡。

SYN lifeless, dead, heavy, slow  
dully

  • His eyes looked dully ahead.

    他目光呆滞地看着前方。

dullness

  • Did you notice any unusual depression or dullness of mind?

    你有没有觉察到抑郁、反应迟钝等反常现象？

3 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (颜色或光)不明亮的,晦暗的 A dull colour or light is not bright. (颜色或光)不明亮的,晦暗的

  • The stamp was a dull blue colour.

    邮票是晦暗的深蓝色。

SYN drab, faded, muted, subdued  
dully

  • The street lamps gleamed dully through the night's mist.

    街灯透过夜晚的薄雾隐约泛出亮光。

4 ADJ-GRADED 阴沉的;昏暗的;多云的 You say the weather is dull when it is very cloudy. 阴沉的;昏暗的;多云的

  • It's always dull and raining.

    天气总是阴雨连绵。

SYN cloudy, dim, gloomy, dismal  
5 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] （声音）不清晰的，隐约的，低沉的 Dull sounds are not very clear or loud. （声音）不清晰的，隐约的，低沉的

  • The long whining whistle of a shell was followed by the dull boom of the explosion.

    在炮弹长长的哀鸣声呼啸而过之后是隆隆而低沉的爆炸声。

  • The coffin closed with a dull thud.

    棺材合上时发出一声闷响。

SYN muted, faint, suppressed, subdued  
dully

  • He heard his heart thump dully but more quickly.

    他听见低沉的心跳声，跳动的速度却更快了。

6 ADJ-GRADED [ADJ n] (感觉)不明显的，不强烈的，隐约的 Dull feelings are weak and not intense. (感觉)不明显的，不强烈的，隐约的

  • The pain, usually a dull ache, gets worse with exercise.

    通常不明显的疼痛在运动后会加剧。

  • I realized with a kind of dull shock that I didn't recognize a single name.

    我暗自一惊，发现一个名字都不认识。

dully

  • His arm throbbed dully.

    他的胳膊隐约在抽动。

7 ADJ-GRADED 钝的;不锋利的 If a knife or blade is dull, it is not sharp. 钝的;不锋利的 [old-fashioned]
8 V-ERG (使)不强烈;(使)变得无光泽;(使)无生气 If something dulls or if it is dulled, it becomes less intense, bright, or lively. (使)不强烈;(使)变得无光泽;(使)无生气

  • Her eyes dulled and she gazed blankly. [VERB]

    她眼神暗淡，呆呆地凝视着。

  • He can dull your senses with facts and figures. [VERB noun]

    他会用事实和数据蒙蔽你的理智。

  • Share prices and trading have been dulled by worries over the war. [VERB noun]

    股票价格和交易因人们对战争的忧虑而走低。

SYN cloud over, darken, grow dim, become cloudy
