mince
mɪns mɪns
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense minces, present participle mincing, past tense , past participle minced
1 N-UNCOUNT 肉末;切碎的肉 Mince is meat which has been cut into very small pieces using a machine. 肉末;切碎的肉 [mainly British]

  • Brown the mince in a frying pan.

    在煎锅里把肉末煎至棕色。

in AM, use 美国英语用 ground beef, hamburger meat
2 VERB (用机器)切碎，绞碎，剁碎(肉等) If you mince food such as meat, you put it into a machine which cuts it into very small pieces. (用机器)切碎，绞碎，剁碎(肉等) [mainly British]

  • Perhaps I'll buy lean meat and mince it myself. [VERB noun]

    也许我会买点瘦肉自己绞。

  • ...minced beef. [VERB-ed]

    绞碎的牛肉

SYN cut, grind, crumble, dice  
in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 grind
3 VERB (尤指同性恋男子)迈着小碎步快走 If you say that someone, especially a homosexual man, minces somewhere, you mean that they walk there with quick small steps. (尤指同性恋男子)迈着小碎步快走 [disapproval]

  • They minced in, in beach costumes and make-up. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他们穿着沙滩服，化了妆，一扭一摆地进来了。

4 PHRASE 说话转弯抹角；言语婉转 If you say that someone does not mince their words, you mean that they speak in a forceful and direct way, especially when saying something unpleasant to someone. 说话转弯抹角；言语婉转

  • The doctors didn't mince their words, and predicted the worst.

    医生直言相告，并做了最坏的预测。

  • Never one to mince words, Carlie told her daughter that her looks were fading.

    卡莉一向心直口快，告诉女儿说她的容颜正在衰老。
