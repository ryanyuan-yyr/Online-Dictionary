produce
prədjuːs prɒdjuːs -duːs -duːs prəˈdus prəˈdjus proʊˈdus proʊˈdjus ˈproʊˌdus ˈproʊˌdjus ˈprɑˌdus ˈprɑˌus
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense produces, present participle producing, past tense , past participle produced
The verb is pronounced /prə'djuːs, am -'duːs/. The noun is pronounced /'prɒdjuːs, am -duːs/ and is hyphenated prod|uce. 动词读作 /prə'djuːs, 美 -'duːs/。名词读作 /'prɒdjuːs, 美 -duːs/，分音节形式为 prod|uce。
1 VERB 产生；引起；造成 To produce something means to cause it to happen. 产生；引起；造成

  • The drug is known to produce side-effects in women. [VERB noun]

    据悉，这种药会对女性产生副作用。

  • Talks aimed at producing a new world trade treaty have been under way for six years. [VERB noun]

    旨在制定出一个新的世界贸易条约的谈判已经进行了6年。

SYN cause, lead to, result in, effect  
2 VERB 生产；出产 If you produce something, you make or create it. 生产；出产

  • The company produced circuitry for communications systems. [VERB noun]

    该公司为通信系统生产电路。

  • I'm quite pleased that we do have the capacity to produce that much food.

    我很高兴我们确实有能力生产这么多粮食。

SYN make, build, create, develop  
3 VERB 生长；长出；产生 When things or people produce something, it comes from them or slowly forms from them, especially as the result of a biological or chemical process. 生长；长出；产生

  • These plants are then pollinated and allowed to mature and produce seed. [VERB noun]

    然后，这些植物就被授粉，从而成熟结籽。

  • ...gases produced by burning coal and oil. [VERB-ed]

    煤和油燃烧时产生的气体

4 VERB 提出；提供 If you produce evidence or an argument, you show it or explain it to people in order to make them agree with you. 提出；提供

  • They challenged him to produce evidence to support his allegations. [VERB noun]

    他们要他拿出证据证明自己的主张。

  • Scientists have produced powerful arguments against his ideas. [VERB noun]

    科学家们提出了有力论据反驳他的观点。

SYN show, provide, present, advance  
5 VERB 出示；拿出；亮出 If you produce an object from somewhere, you show it or bring it out so that it can be seen. 出示；拿出；亮出

  • To hire a car you must produce a passport and a current driving licence. [VERB noun]

    租车必须出示护照和有效驾驶执照。

  • She produced the knife during arguments with her friends. [VERB noun]

    在和朋友争吵的时候，她掏出了刀。

6 VERB 上演（戏剧）；制作（电影、电视节目）；灌制（唱片） If someone produces something such as a film, a magazine, or a CD, they organize it and decide how it should be done. 上演（戏剧）；制作（电影、电视节目）；灌制（唱片）

  • He has produced his own sports magazine called Yes Sport. [VERB noun]

    他推出了自己的体育杂志，叫做《是的，体育》。

  • He produced 'A Chorus Line', Broadway's longest running show. [VERB noun]

    他制作推出了《歌舞线上》这一百老汇经久不衰的剧目。

SYN present, stage, direct, put on  
7 N-UNCOUNT 农产品 Produce is food or other things that are grown in large quantities to be sold. 农产品

  • We manage to get most of our produce in Britain.

    我们设法在英国搞到我们所需的大部分农产品。

  • Winter produce will cost more for the next few weeks.

    在未来几周，冬季农产品会更贵。

SYN fruit and vegetables, goods, food, products
