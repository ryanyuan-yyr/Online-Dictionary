blunder
blʌndəʳ ˈblʌndər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense blunders, present participle blundering, past tense , past participle blundered
1 N-COUNT 愚蠢的错误；疏忽；失误 A blunder is a stupid or careless mistake. 愚蠢的错误；疏忽；失误

  • I think he made a tactical blunder by announcing it so far ahead of time.

    我认为他在时机远未成熟时就把这事说了出去是一种失策。

SYN mistake, slip, fault, error  
2 VERB 犯愚蠢的错误；失误 If you blunder, you make a stupid or careless mistake. 犯愚蠢的错误；失误

  • No doubt I had blundered again. [VERB]

    无疑，我又犯了一个愚蠢的错误。

  • You're a blundering fool. [VERB-ing]

    你是一个大笨蛋。

SYN make a mistake, blow it [slang] , err, slip up [informal]  
3 VERB 不慎陷入，误打误撞地进入（危险或困难的境地） If you blunder into a dangerous or difficult situation, you get involved in it by mistake. 不慎陷入，误打误撞地进入（危险或困难的境地）

  • People wanted to know how they had blundered into war, and how to avoid it in future. [VERB + into]

    人们想知道他们是怎样稀里糊涂地陷入战争的，以及日后该如何避免重蹈覆辙。

SYN stumble, confuse, flounder, bumble  
4 VERB 跌跌撞撞；慌乱地走 If you blunder somewhere, you move there in a clumsy and careless way. 跌跌撞撞；慌乱地走

  • He had blundered into the table, upsetting the flowers. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他不小心撞到了桌子，把花打翻了。

SYN stumble, fall, reel, stagger
