vitality
vaɪtælɪti vaɪˈtæləti
1 N-UNCOUNT 活力;生命力;热情 If you say that someone or something has vitality, you mean that they have great energy and liveliness. 活力;生命力;热情

  • Without continued learning, graduates will lose their intellectual vitality.

    如果不继续学习，毕业生就会失去思想上的活力。

  • Mr Li said China's reforms had brought vitality to its economy.

    李先生说中国的改革给其经济注入了活力。

SYN energy, vivacity, sparkle, go [informal]
