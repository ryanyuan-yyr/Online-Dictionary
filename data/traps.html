trap
træp træp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense traps, present participle trapping, past tense , past participle trapped
1 N-COUNT (捕鸟兽的)陷阱，罗网，夹子，捕捉器 A trap is a device which is placed somewhere or a hole which is dug somewhere in order to catch animals or birds. (捕鸟兽的)陷阱，罗网，夹子，捕捉器
2 VERB 设陷阱捕捉 If a person traps animals or birds, he or she catches them using traps. 设陷阱捕捉

  • The locals were encouraged to trap and kill the birds. [VERB noun]

    当地居民过去被鼓励设捕捉器捕杀这种鸟。

SYN catch, snare, ensnare, entrap  
3 N-COUNT 圈套;诡计;骗局 A trap is a trick that is intended to catch or deceive someone. 圈套;诡计;骗局

  • He failed to keep a rendezvous after sensing a police trap…

    他察觉到警方设了圈套以后就没有如约会面。

  • He was trying to decide whether the question was some sort of a trap.

    他试图判断那个问题是不是个套。

SYN trick, set-up [informal] , deception, ploy  
4 VERB 诱骗;使上当;使中计 If you trap someone into doing or saying something, you trick them so that they do or say it, although they did not want to. 诱骗;使上当;使中计

  • Were you just trying to trap her into making some admission?. [VERB noun + into]

    你刚才是想诱使她供认吗？

  • She had trapped him so neatly that he wanted to slap her. [VERB noun]

    她骗他骗得如此干净利落，他恨不得给她一记耳光。

SYN trick, fool, cheat, lure  
5 VERB 诱捕；抓捕 To trap someone, especially a criminal, means to capture them. 诱捕；抓捕 [journalism]

  • The police knew that to trap the killer they had to play him at his own game. [VERB noun]

    警方知道要捉到凶手必须将计就计。

  • The couple set up a 24-hour security camera to trap the vandal scratching their car. [VERB noun]

    夫妇俩装了个 24 小时监控摄像头，想要抓住故意划坏他们车子的家伙。

SYN capture, catch, arrest, seize  
6 N-COUNT [usually singular] 困境;窘境 A trap is an unpleasant situation that you cannot easily escape from. 困境;窘境

  • The Government has found it's caught in a trap of its own making.

    政府发现自己作茧自缚。

SYN problem, snare, prison, cage  
7 VERB 使困住;使受限制 If you are trapped somewhere, something falls onto you or blocks your way and prevents you from moving or escaping. 使困住;使受限制

  • The train was trapped underground by a fire. [be VERB-ed]

    列车被大火困在地下。

  • The light aircraft then cartwheeled, trapping both men. [VERB noun]

    这架轻型飞机接着一个侧翻，把两个人都困住了。

  • Until he saw the trapped wagons and animals, he did not realize the full extent of the catastrophe. [VERB-ed]

    直到他看见受困的马车和动物，才意识到灾难的严重性。

SYN imprison, confine, cut off, close in  
8 VERB 采集;储存;使存留 When something traps gas, water, or energy, it prevents it from escaping. 采集;储存;使存留

  • Wool traps your body heat, keeping the chill at bay. [VERB noun]

    毛织品能够保暖御寒。

  • The volume of gas trapped on these surfaces can be considerable. [VERB-ed]

    存留在这些表面的气体体积可能相当大。

9 N-COUNT 双轮轻便马车 A trap is a light carriage with two wheels pulled by horses in which people used to travel. 双轮轻便马车
10 See also: trapped  booby-trap  death trap  poverty trap 
11 PHRASE 落入陷阱；陷入误区 If someone falls into the trap of doing something, they think or behave in a way which is not wise or sensible. 落入陷阱；陷入误区

  • Many people fall into the trap of believing that home decorating must always be done on a large scale.

    很多人错误地认为家居装修总是要大动一番干戈。

  • It's a trap too many people fall into.

    太多人都掉进了这个陷阱中。

12 PHRASE 住嘴;闭嘴 If someone tells you to shut your trap or keep your trap shut, they are telling you rudely that you should be quiet and not say anything. 住嘴;闭嘴 [informal, rude]
