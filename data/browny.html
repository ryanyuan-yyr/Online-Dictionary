brown
braʊn braʊn
Word forms: comparative browner, superlative brownest, plural , 3rd person singular present tense browns, present participle browning, past tense , past participle browned
1 COLOUR 褐色；棕色 Something that is brown is the colour of earth or of wood. 褐色；棕色

  • ...her deep brown eyes.

    她深褐色的眼睛

  • The stairs are decorated in golds and earthy browns.

    楼梯油漆成金色和土褐色。

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 皮肤晒成棕色的；皮肤晒黑的 You can describe a white-skinned person as brown when they have been sitting in the sun until their skin has become darker than usual. 皮肤晒成棕色的；皮肤晒黑的

  • I don't want to be really really brown, just have a nice light golden colour.

    我不想晒得黝黑，只想晒成一种漂亮的淡金色。

3 VERB 晒黑 If someone browns in the sun they become brown in colour. 晒黑

  • Her skin was of the fortunate kind that could brown in the sun without burning. [VERB]

    她的皮肤属于那种很幸运的类型，在阳光下无需长时间晒着就能变黑。

  • There were many gorgeous females busy browning themselves. [VERB noun]

    有许多漂亮的女性忙着晒黑自己。

4 ADJ [usually ADJ n] 棕种人的；棕色皮肤的 A brown person is someone who belongs to a race of people who have brown-coloured skins. 棕种人的；棕色皮肤的

  • ...a slim brown man with a speckled turban.

    一个戴着花头巾、身材瘦削、棕色皮肤的男子

5 ADJ-UNGRADED [usually ADJ n] （谷物）未去壳的，糙的 Brown is used to describe grains that have not had their outer layers removed, and foods made from these grains. （谷物）未去壳的，糙的

  • ...brown bread.

    黑面包

  • ...spicy tomato sauce served over a bed of brown rice.

    浇在一层糙米上的辣味番茄酱

6 V-ERG （用旺火把)(食物)烤得焦黄；（把…）炸成焦黄色 When food browns or when you brown food, you cook it, usually for a short time on a high flame. （用旺火把)(食物)烤得焦黄；（把…）炸成焦黄色

  • Cook for ten minutes until the sugar browns. [VERB]

    熬煮10分钟，直到糖变成褐色。

  • He browned the chicken in a frying pan. [VERB noun]

    他用煎锅把鸡肉煎成焦黄色。

SYN fry, cook, grill, sear
