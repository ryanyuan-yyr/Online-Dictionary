intertwine
ɪntəʳtwaɪn ˌɪntərˈtwaɪn ˈɪntərˌtwaɪn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense intertwines, present participle intertwining, past tense , past participle intertwined
1 V-RECIP-ERG (使)密切关联;(使)密切联系 If two or more things are intertwined or intertwine, they are closely connected with each other in many ways. (使)密切关联;(使)密切联系 [Also VERB n]

  • Their destinies are intertwined. [be VERB-ed]

    他们的命运交织在一起。

  • Three major narratives intertwine within Foucault's text, 'Madness and Civilisation'. [VERB]

    3个主要故事在福柯的《疯癫与文明》一书当中密切相联。

  • He intertwines personal reminiscences with the story of British television. [VERB noun + with]

    他把个人回忆录与英国电视的故事紧密结合在一起。

  • Her fate intertwined with his. [VERB + with]

    她与他的命运纠缠在一起。

2 V-RECIP 缠结;缠绕在一起 If two things intertwine, they are twisted together or go over and under each other. 缠结;缠绕在一起 [Also V + with]

  • Trees, undergrowth and creepers intertwined, blocking our way. [VERB]

    树木、灌木丛与藤蔓植物盘根错节,挡住了我们的去路。

  • The towels were embroidered with their intertwined initials. [VERB-ed]

    毛巾上绣着他们姓名首字母的连写组合。

SYN interweave, entwine, interlace, cross
