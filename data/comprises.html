comprise
kəmpraɪz kəmˈpraɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense comprises, present participle comprising, past tense , past participle comprised
1 VERB 包括;由…组成 If you say that something comprises or is comprised of a number of things or people, you mean it has them as its parts or members. 包括;由…组成 [formal]

  • The special cabinet committee comprises Mr Brown, Mr Mandelson, and Mr Straw. [VERB noun]

    特别内阁委员会包括布朗先生、曼德尔森先生和斯特劳先生。

  • The exhibition comprises 50 oils and watercolours. [VERB noun]

    展览陈列了50幅油画和水彩画。

  • The task force is comprised of congressional leaders, cabinet heads and administration officials. [be VERB-ed + of]

    工作小组由国会领导人、内阁首脑和行政官员组成。

  • A crowd comprised of the wives and children of scientists staged a demonstration. [VERB-ed]

    由科学家们的妻儿们组成的队伍举行了示威游行。

SYN be composed of, include, contain, consist of  
2 VERB 构成;组成 The things or people that comprise something are the parts or members that form it. 构成;组成 [formal]

  • ...the multitude of ideas, ambitions and regrets that comprises the culture of Russia today. [VERB noun]

    构成现今俄罗斯文化的众多观点、抱负和遗憾

  • Women comprise 44% of hospital medical staff. [VERB noun]

    女性占医院医护人员的44%。

SYN make up, form, constitute, compose
