minimize
mɪnɪmaɪz ˈmɪnɪˌmaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense minimizes, present participle minimizing, past tense , past participle minimized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 minimise
1 VERB 使减至最低程度;使保持最低程度 If you minimize a risk, problem, or unpleasant situation, you reduce it to the lowest possible level, or prevent it increasing beyond that level. 使减至最低程度;使保持最低程度

  • Concerned people want to minimize the risk of developing cancer. [VERB noun]

    相关人员希望尽可能降低罹癌风险。

  • Many of these problems can be minimised by sensible planning. [be VERB-ed]

    这些问题很多可以通过合理安排尽可能避免。

SYN reduce, decrease, shrink, diminish  
2 VERB 对…轻描淡写；使显得不重要 If you minimize something, you make it seem smaller or less significant than it really is. 对…轻描淡写；使显得不重要

  • Some have minimized the importance of ideological factors. [VERB noun]

    有些人淡化了意识形态因素的重要性。

  • At his trial, he tried to minimize his behavior. [VERB noun]

    接受审判的时候，他企图对自己的行为轻描淡写。

SYN play down, discount, underestimate, belittle  
3 VERB 把(计算机屏幕上的窗口)最小化 If you minimize a window on a computer screen, you make it very small, because you do not want to use it. 把(计算机屏幕上的窗口)最小化

  • Click the square icon again to minimize the window. [VERB noun]

    再次点击正方形图标，把窗口最小化。
