faction
fækʃən ˈfækʃən
Word forms: plural factions
1 N-COUNT (大团体中的)派系，派别，小集团 A faction is an organized group of people within a larger group, which opposes some of the ideas of the larger group and fights for its own ideas. (大团体中的)派系，派别，小集团

  • A peace agreement will be signed by the leaders of the country's warring factions.

    该国各敌对派系领导人将签署和平协议。

SYN group, set, party, division  
2 N-UNCOUNT 派系之争;内讧 Faction is also used to describe argument and disagreement within a group of people. 派系之争;内讧

  • Faction and self-interest appear to be the norm.

    派系之争和自私自利看来非常普遍。

SYN dissension, division, conflict, rebellion
