reprimand
reprɪmɑːnd -mænd ˈrɛprəˌmænd ˈrɛprəˌmɑnd ˌ rɛprəˈmænd ˌrɛprəˈmɑnd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense reprimands, present participle reprimanding, past tense , past participle reprimanded
VERB 训斥;斥责;谴责 If someone is reprimanded, they are spoken to angrily or seriously for doing something wrong, usually by a person in authority. 训斥;斥责;谴责 [formal]

  • He was reprimanded by a teacher for talking in the corridor. [be VERB-ed + for]

    他因为在走廊里讲话受到了一位老师的斥责。

  • Her attempts to reprimand him were quickly shouted down. [VERB noun]

    她本想斥责他，但很快就被大声喝止了。

SYN blame, censure, rebuke, reproach  
Reprimand is also a noun.

  • He has been fined five thousand pounds and given a severe reprimand.

    他被罚款5,000英镑，并受到了严厉斥责。
