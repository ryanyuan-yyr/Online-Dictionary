outgrow
aʊtgroʊ ˌaʊtˈgroʊ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense outgrows, present participle outgrowing, past tense outgrew, past participle outgrown
1 VERB （小孩）长得穿不下（衣服） If a child outgrows a piece of clothing, they grow bigger, so that it no longer fits them. （小孩）长得穿不下（衣服）

  • She outgrew her clothes so rapidly that Patsy was always having to buy new ones. [VERB noun]

    她长得太快，衣服很快便穿不下了，帕齐只好经常给她买新衣服。

  • ...outgrown baby clothes. [VERB-ed]

    长大了穿不下的宝宝装

2 VERB 因长大而放弃（以前的行为或想法） If you outgrow a particular way of behaving or thinking, you change and become more mature, so that you no longer behave or think in that way. 因长大而放弃（以前的行为或想法）

  • The girl may or may not outgrow her interest in fashion. [VERB noun]

    这个女孩对时尚的兴趣可能会随着年龄的增长而消失，也可能不会。
