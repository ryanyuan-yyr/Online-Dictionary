tidy
taɪdi ˈtaɪdi
Word forms: comparative tidier, superlative tidiest, 3rd person singular present tense tidies, present participle tidying, past tense , past participle tidied
1 ADJ-GRADED 整洁的;整齐的 Something that is tidy is neat and is arranged in an organized way. 整洁的;整齐的

  • Having a tidy desk can seem impossible if you have a busy, demanding job.

    如果工作忙、难度大，要想保持桌面整洁似乎不太可能。

  • I'll do your garden, I'll keep that tidy for you.

    我会帮你弄弄花园，帮你保持它的整洁。

SYN neat, orderly, ordered, clean  
tidily

  • ...books and magazines stacked tidily on shelves.

    整整齐齐地摞在架子上的书和杂志

  • ...some tidily arranged papers.

    一些排列整齐的文件

tidiness

  • Employees are expected to maintain a high standard of tidiness in their dress and appearance.

    员工们要保持着装和仪表高度整洁。

2 ADJ-GRADED 整洁的;有条理的 Someone who is tidy likes everything to be neat and arranged in an organized way. 整洁的;有条理的

  • She's obsessively tidy, always hoovering and polishing.

    她有洁癖，整天不是用吸尘器吸尘，就是擦来擦去。

tidiness

  • I'm very impressed by your tidiness and order.

    你的整洁、有条理给我留下了很深的印象。

3 VERB 收拾，整理(房间、橱柜等) When you tidy a place such as a room or cupboard, you make it neat by putting things in their proper places. 收拾，整理(房间、橱柜等)

  • She made her bed, and tidied her room. [VERB noun]

    她整理了床铺，收拾了自己的房间。

SYN neaten, straighten, put in order, order  
4 ADJ-GRADED [ADJ n] 一大笔(钱) A tidy amount of money is a large amount. 一大笔(钱) [informal]

  • The opportunities are there to make a tidy profit.

    赚大钱的机会就在那儿摆着。

SYN considerable, large, substantial, good
