confound
kənfaʊnd kənˈfaʊnd ˈkɑnfaʊnd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense confounds, present participle confounding, past tense , past participle confounded
VERB 使困惑;使惊讶 If someone or something confounds you, they make you feel surprised or confused, often by showing you that your opinions or expectations of them were wrong. 使困惑;使惊讶

  • He momentarily confounded his critics by his cool handling of the hostage crisis. [VERB noun]

    他对人质危机的冷静处理一时让批评者们惊讶不已。

  • The choice of Governor may confound us all. [VERB noun]

    州长的选择可能会让我们所有人感到困惑。
