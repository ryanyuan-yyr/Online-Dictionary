eradicate
ɪrædɪkeɪt iˈrædɪˌkeɪt ɪˈrædəˌkeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense eradicates, present participle eradicating, past tense , past participle eradicated
VERB 消灭;灭绝;根除 To eradicate something means to get rid of it completely. 消灭;灭绝;根除 [formal]

  • They are already battling to eradicate illnesses such as malaria and tetanus. [VERB noun]

    他们已经在努力消灭疟疾、破伤风等疾病。

  • If tedious tasks could be eradicated, the world would be a much better place. [be VERB-ed]

    如果可以消灭那些单调乏味的工作，世界将会变得更加美好。

SYN wipe out, eliminate, remove, destroy
