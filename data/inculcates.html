inculcate
ɪnkʌlkeɪt ɪnkʌl- ɪnˈkʌlˌkeɪt ˈɪnkʌlˌkeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense inculcates, present participle inculcating, past tense , past participle inculcated
VERB 反复灌输 If you inculcate an idea or opinion in someone's mind, you teach it to them by repeating it until it is fixed in their mind. 反复灌输 [formal]

  • We have tried to inculcate a feeling of citizenship in youngsters. [VERB noun + in]

    我们试图向年轻人灌输公民意识。

  • The aim is to inculcate business people with an appreciation of different cultures. [VERB noun + with]

    其目的是教导商界管理人员逐渐学会欣赏不同的文化。

  • Great care was taken to inculcate the values of nationhood and family. [VERB noun]

    花费了很多心力反复灌输国家与家庭观念。
