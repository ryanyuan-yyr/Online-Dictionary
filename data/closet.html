closet
klɒzɪt ˈklɑzɪt
Word forms: plural closets
1 N-COUNT 壁橱 A closet is a piece of furniture with doors at the front and shelves inside, which is used for storing things. 壁橱 [US]
in BRIT, use 英国英语用 cupboard
2 N-COUNT (尤指不带窗户的)小储藏间 A closet is a very small room for storing things, especially one without windows. (尤指不带窗户的)小储藏间 [US, also British, old-fashioned]
3 ADJ [ADJ n] (信仰、习惯、情感)隐蔽的，不公开的 Closet is used to describe a person who has beliefs, habits, or feelings which they keep secret, often because they are embarrassed about them. Closet is also used of their beliefs, habits, or feelings. (信仰、习惯、情感)隐蔽的，不公开的

  • He is a closet Fascist.

    他是个秘密的法西斯分子。

  • ...closet misogyny.

    内心对女性的仇视

SYN secret, private, hidden, unknown  
4 PHRASE [VERB inflects] [VERB inflects] 公开同性恋身份；出柜 If someone comes out of the closet, they tell people that they are homosexual after having kept this a secret. 公开同性恋身份；出柜
5 PHRASE 公开（或谈论）以前保密的事;(问题)公开 If someone comes out of the closet, they reveal or discuss something which they have previously kept secret. You can also say that an issue comes out of the closet when it starts to be publicly discussed. 公开（或谈论）以前保密的事;(问题)公开
6 See also: closeted  a skeleton in the closet→see: skeleton
