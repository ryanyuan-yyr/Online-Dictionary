scare up
PHRASAL VERB 费力张罗;勉强凑合 If you scare up something, you provide, produce, or obtain it, often when it is difficult to do so or when you do not have many resources. 费力张罗;勉强凑合 [mainly US, informal]

  • An all-star game might scare up a little interest. [VERB PREP. noun]

    一场全明星比赛可能勉强会激起一点点兴趣。

  • Why don't you see if you can scare up a cup of coffee? [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    你看看能不能好歹沏一杯咖啡？
