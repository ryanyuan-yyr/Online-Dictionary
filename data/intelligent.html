intelligent
ɪntelɪdʒənt ɪnˈtɛlədʒənt
1 ADJ-GRADED 聪颖的;有才智的;有灵性的 A person or animal that is intelligent has the ability to think, understand, and learn things quickly and well. 聪颖的;有才智的;有灵性的

  • Susan's a very bright and intelligent woman who knows her own mind.

    苏珊是个非常聪明、有主见的女子。

  • ...lively and intelligent conversation.

    生动有趣、聪明睿智的谈话

  • He ventured the opinion that 'whales are as intelligent as human beings'.

    他大胆提出了“鲸与人类一样聪明”的观点。

SYN clever, bright [informal] , smart, knowing  
intelligently

  • They are incapable of thinking intelligently about politics.

    他们对政治没有什么高见。

2 ADJ 能思维的;智能的 Something that is intelligent has the ability to think and understand instead of doing things automatically or by instinct. 能思维的;智能的

  • An intelligent computer will be an indispensable diagnostic tool for doctors.

    智能计算机将成为医生不可或缺的诊断工具。

  • ...the biggest-ever search for intelligent life elsewhere in the universe.

    有史以来规模最大的寻找外星智能生命的行动

SYN rational, cognitive, capable of thought, thinking
