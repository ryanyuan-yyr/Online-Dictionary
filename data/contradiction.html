contradiction
kɒntrədɪkʃən ˌkɑntrəˈdɪkʃən
Word forms: plural contradictions
1 N-COUNT 矛盾;不一致 If you describe an aspect of a situation as a contradiction, you mean that it is completely different from other aspects, and so makes the situation confused or difficult to understand. 矛盾;不一致

  • ...the contradictions between her private life and the public persona.

    她的私生活与公众形象之间的巨大反差

  • The performance seemed to me unpardonable, a contradiction of all that the Olympics is supposed to be. [+ of]

    在我看来，这次的表现是不可宽恕的，和奥运精神完全相悖。

  • The militants see no contradiction in using violence to bring about a religious state.

    好战者觉得用暴力建立一个宗教国家这种做法并无矛盾之处。

SYN conflict, inconsistency, contravention, incongruity  
2 PHRASE [contradiction inflects] 自相矛盾的说法 If you say that something is a contradiction in terms, you mean that it is described as having a quality that it cannot have. 自相矛盾的说法

  • A public service run for profit — a contradiction in terms if there ever was one.

    以营利为目的的公共服务机构—这是典型的用词上的自相矛盾（如果真有这种说法的话）。
