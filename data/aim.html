aim
eɪm eɪm
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense aims, present participle aiming, past tense , past participle aimed
1 VERB 致力于;旨在;以…为目标 If you aim for something or aim to do something, you plan or hope to achieve it. 致力于;旨在;以…为目标 [US, informal]

  • He is aiming for the 100 metres world record. [V + for/at]

    他志在打破100米世界纪录。

  • Businesses will have to aim at long-term growth. [V for/at n/-ing]

    企业必须以长期增长为目标。

  • ...an appeal which aims to raise funds for children with special needs. [VERB to-infinitive]

    旨在为有特殊需要的孩子筹集资金而发出的呼吁

SYN try for, want, seek, work for  
2 N-COUNT [oft with poss] 目的;目标 The aim of something that you do is the purpose for which you do it or the result that it is intended to achieve. 目的;目标

  • The aim of the festival is to increase awareness of Hindu culture and traditions. [+ of]

    该节庆日的目的在于增强人们对印度文化和传统的认识。

  • ...a research programme that has largely failed to achieve its principal aims.

    在较大程度上未能实现其主要目标的研究项目

SYN intention, end, point, plan  
3 V-PASSIVE 旨在;目的是 If an action or plan is aimed at achieving something, it is intended or planned to achieve it. 旨在;目的是

  • The new measures are aimed at tightening existing sanctions. [be V-ed at n/v-ing]

    新措施旨在加强现行的制裁。

  • ...talks aimed at ending the war. [VERB-ed]

    旨在结束战争的谈判

4 VERB 决定;想要 If you aim to do something, you decide or want to do it. 决定;想要 [US, informal]

  • Are you aiming to visit the gardens?. [VERB to-infinitive]

    你想去逛公园吗？

  • I didn't aim to get caught. [VERB to-infinitive]

    我并不想被抓住。

SYN mean, attempt, propose, intend  
5 VERB [usually passive] （行为、言论）针对，指向 If your actions or remarks are aimed at a particular person or group, you intend that the person or group should notice them and be influenced by them. （行为、言论）针对，指向

  • His message was aimed at the undecided middle ground of Israeli politics. [be V-ed + at]

    他所传达的信息针对的是以色列政局中尚未表态的中间派。

  • Advertising aimed at children should be curbed. [VERB-ed]

    针对儿童的广告应受到限制。

6 VERB 瞄准；对准 If you aim a weapon or object at something or someone, you point it towards them before firing or throwing it. 瞄准；对准 [Also VERB]

  • He was aiming the rifle at Wade. [VERB noun + at]

    他将步枪瞄准了韦德。

  • ...a missile aimed at the arms factory. [VERB-ed]

    瞄准军工厂的导弹

  • I didn't know I was supposed to aim at the same spot all the time. [VERB + at]

    我不知道自己应该一直瞄准同一目标。

SYN point, level, train, direct  
7 N-SING [oft poss NOUN] 瞄准，对准（指技巧或动作） Your aim is your skill or action in pointing a weapon or other object at its target. 瞄准，对准（指技巧或动作）

  • He stood with the gun in his right hand and his left hand steadying his aim.

    他站立着，右手举枪，左手稳住枪对准目标。

SYN aiming, directing, sight  
8 VERB 踢向;挥（拳）向 If you aim a kick or punch at someone, you try to kick or punch them. 踢向;挥（拳）向 [Also VERB n]

  • They aimed kicks at his shins. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他们踢向他的小腿。

9 PHRASE 瞄准;对准 When you take aim, you point a weapon or object at someone or something, before firing or throwing it. 瞄准;对准

  • She had spotted a man with a shotgun taking aim.

    她发现一个正持猎枪瞄准的男子。

10 PHRASE 激烈地批评 If you take aim at someone or something, you criticize them strongly. 激烈地批评 [US]

  • Republican strategists are taking particular aim at Democratic senators.

    共和党的战略家正尖锐地攻击民主党的参议员。
