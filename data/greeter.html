greet
griːt grit
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense greets, present participle greeting, past tense , past participle greeted
1 VERB 同…打招呼;向…问好；欢迎；迎接 When you greet someone, you say 'Hello' or shake hands with them. 同…打招呼;向…问好；欢迎；迎接

  • She liked to be home to greet Steve when he came in from school. [VERB noun]

    她喜欢呆在家里迎接放学回家的史蒂夫。

2 VERB [usually passive] 对…作出反应；回应 If something is greeted in a particular way, people react to it in that way. 对…作出反应；回应

  • The European Court's decision has been greeted with dismay by fishermen. [be V-ed + with/by]

    渔民对欧洲法庭的裁决感到失望。

  • It is unlikely that this suggestion will be greeted enthusiastically in the Baltic States. [be V-ed adv]

    该提议不大可能在波罗的海诸国得到热烈响应。

SYN receive, take, respond to, react to  
3 VERB 呈现在…面前；映入…眼帘；最先被…感觉到 If you are greeted by something, it is the first thing you notice in a particular place. 呈现在…面前；映入…眼帘；最先被…感觉到 [written]

  • I was greeted by a shocking sight. [be VERB-ed + by]

    一幅令人震惊的画面映入我的眼帘。

  • The savoury smell greeted them as they went through the door. [VERB noun]

    他们进门时一阵香味扑鼻而来。
