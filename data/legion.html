legion
liːdʒən ˈlidʒən
Word forms: plural legions
1 N-COUNT [oft in names] 军团；兵团 A legion is a large group of soldiers who form one section of an army. 军团；兵团

  • ...the Sudan-based troops of the Libyan Islamic Legion.

    利比亚伊斯兰军团驻扎在苏丹的部队

  • The last of the Roman legions left Britain in AD 410.

    罗马军团最后一支部队于公元410年离开英国。

SYN army, company, force, division  
2 N-COUNT 众多；大批；大量 A legion of people or things is a great number of them. 众多；大批；大量 [written]

  • His delightful sense of humour won him a legion of friends. [+ of]

    他的幽默风趣为他赢得了众多的朋友。

  • ...a legion of stories about noisy neighbours.

    有关吵闹的邻居的众多故事

SYN multitude, host, mass, drove  
3 ADJ [verb-link ADJ] 比比皆是的；不胜枚举的；多如牛毛的 If you say that things of a particular kind are legion, you mean that there are a great number of them. 比比皆是的；不胜枚举的；多如牛毛的 [formal]

  • Ellie's problems are legion.

    埃莉的问题多如牛毛。

  • The number of women who become pregnant after adopting children is legion.

    领养小孩后怀孕的妇女比比皆是。

SYN very many, numerous, countless, myriad
