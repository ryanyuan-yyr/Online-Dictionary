cold call
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense cold calls, present participle cold calling, past tense , past participle cold called
1 N-COUNT （冷不防打来的）推销电话;上门推销；冷访 If someone makes a cold call, they telephone or visit someone they have never contacted, without making an appointment, in order to try and sell something. （冷不防打来的）推销电话;上门推销；冷访

  • She had worked as a call centre operator making cold calls for time-share holidays.

    她曾经在话务中心当过话务员，负责打电话推销分时度假。

2 VERB (未事先联系或约定而)进行电话推销，上门推销 To cold call means to make a cold call. (未事先联系或约定而)进行电话推销，上门推销 [Also VERB n]

  • You should refuse to meet anyone who cold calls with an offer of financial advice. [VERB]

    你应该拒绝和那些贸然打电话提供理财建议的陌生人见面。
