pot
pɒt pɑt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense pots, present participle potting, past tense , past participle potted
1 N-COUNT 锅 A pot is a deep round container used for cooking stews, soups, and other food. 锅

  • ...metal cooking pots. [+ of]

    金属炒锅

SYN container, bowl, pan, vessel  
A pot of stew, soup, or other food is an amount of it contained in a pot.一锅的量

  • He was stirring a pot of soup.

    他在搅拌一锅汤。

2 N-COUNT 茶壶；咖啡壶 You can use pot to refer to a teapot or coffee pot. 茶壶；咖啡壶

  • There's tea in the pot.

    壶里有茶。

A pot of tea or coffee is an amount of it contained in a pot.一壶的量

  • He spilt a pot of coffee.

    他把一壶咖啡弄洒了。

3 N-COUNT [oft n NOUN] （装果酱、油漆等的）罐，桶 A pot is a cylindrical container for jam, paint, or some other thick liquid. （装果酱、油漆等的）罐，桶

  • Hundreds of jam pots lined her scrubbed shelves.

    她那擦得干干净净的架子上排列着几百个果酱罐。

A pot of jam, paint, or some other thick liquid is an amount of it contained in a pot.一罐之量

  • ...a pot of red paint.

    一罐红色颜料

4 N-COUNT 同 flowerpot A pot is the same as a flowerpot . 同 flowerpot
5 VERB 把…栽入盆中 If you pot a young plant, or part of a plant, you put it into a container filled with soil, so it can grow there. 把…栽入盆中

  • Pot the cuttings individually. [VERB noun]

    把插条分别栽入盆中。

  • ...potted plants. [VERB-ed]

    盆栽植物

6 N-UNCOUNT 大麻 Pot is sometimes used to refer to the drugs cannabis and marijuana. 大麻 [informal]
7 QUANT [QUANT of pl-n/n-uncount] 一大笔；很多；大量 If you have pots of money, you have a lot of it. 一大笔；很多；大量 [informal]

  • He must have pots of money.

    他一定有很多钱。

8 N-SING [the NOUN] （一盘纸牌游戏中各玩家所下的）赌注总额，全部赌注 In a card game, the pot is the money from all the players which the winner of the game will take as a prize. （一盘纸牌游戏中各玩家所下的）赌注总额，全部赌注
9 N-SING [the NOUN] 公共基金 You can refer to a fund consisting of money from several people as the pot . 公共基金 [mainly US]

  • I've taken some money from the pot for wrapping paper.

    我已从公共基金里拿了一些钱买包装纸。

10 N-COUNT 大肚子；罗汉肚 Someone who has a pot has a round, fat stomach which sticks out, either because they eat or drink too much, or because they have had very little to eat for some time. 大肚子；罗汉肚 [mainly US]
11 N-COUNT （幼儿用的）便壶 A pot is a deep bowl which a small child uses instead of a toilet. （幼儿用的）便壶
12 VERB （斯诺克及台球中）击（球）入袋 In the games of snooker and billiards, if you pot a ball, you succeed in hitting it into one of the pockets. （斯诺克及台球中）击（球）入袋

  • He did not pot a ball for the next two frames. [VERB noun]

    他在接下来的两局中一杆未进。

13 See also: potted  chamber pot  chimney pot  coffee pot  lobster pot  melting pot  plant pot 
14 PHRASE [VERB inflects] （因无人照料或经营）垮掉，没落，衰落；荒废 If something goes to pot, it loses all its good qualities because nobody looks after it or works at it. （因无人照料或经营）垮掉，没落，衰落；荒废 [informal]

  • The neighbourhood really is going to pot.

    这个街区确实在走向败落。

SYN decline, slump, deteriorate, worsen  
15 PHRASE 碰运气；撞大运 If you take pot luck, you decide to do something even though you do not know what you will get as a result. pot of gold→see: gold  碰运气；撞大运

  • If you haven't made an appointment, take pot luck and knock on the door.

    如果你没有预约，就敲门试试运气吧。

  • He scorns the 'pot-luck' approach.

    他对这种“碰运气”的方法不屑一顾。
