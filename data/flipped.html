flip
flɪp flɪp
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense flips, present participle flipping, past tense , past participle flipped
1 VERB 迅速打开(或关掉)(装置);按(开关) If you flip a device on or off, or if you flip a switch, you turn it on or off by pressing the switch quickly. 迅速打开(或关掉)(装置);按(开关)

  • He didn't flip on the headlights until he was two blocks away. [V n with on/off]

    他开出两个街区后才打开车头灯。

  • Then he walked out, flipping the lights off. [V n with on/off]

    然后他走了出来，把灯关掉了。

  • He flipped the timer switch. [VERB noun]

    他按了一下定时开关。

SYN flick, switch, snap, click  
2 VERB 快速翻阅;浏览 If you flip through the pages of a book, for example, you quickly turn over the pages in order to find a particular one or to get an idea of the contents. 快速翻阅;浏览

  • He was flipping through a magazine in the living room. [VERB + through]

    他在起居室里翻看着杂志。

  • He flipped the pages of the diary and began reading the last entry. [VERB noun]

    他快速翻了一下那本日志，然后开始读最后一篇。

SYN thumb, leaf, glance, thumb  
3 V-ERG (使)移动;(使)转动;(使)翻动 If something flips over, or if you flip it over or into a different position, it moves or is moved into a different position. (使)移动;(使)转动;(使)翻动

  • The plane then flipped over and burst into flames. [VERB adv./prep.]

    接着飞机突然翻转并燃起了大火。

  • He flipped it neatly on to the plate. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他干净利索地把它盛到了盘子里。

SYN spin, turn, overturn, turn over  
4 VERB (用拇指)弹，抛，掷 If you flip something, especially a coin, you use your thumb to make it turn over and over, as it goes through the air. (用拇指)弹，抛，掷

  • I pulled a coin from my pocket and flipped it. [VERB noun]

    我从口袋里掏出一个硬币，把它弹向空中。

  • I flipped a butt out of the window and drove on. [VERB noun prep.]

    我把烟蒂弹出车窗外继续开车。

SYN toss, throw, cast, pitch  
Flip is also a noun.

  • ...having gambled all on the flip of a coin.

    把所有赌注都押在了一次抛硬币上

5 VERB 突然心烦意乱;勃然大怒 If someone flips, they suddenly become extremely upset or angry because of something that has happened. 突然心烦意乱;勃然大怒 [informal]

  • He got so provoked that he flipped. [VERB]

    他被激怒了，发起火来。

6 ADJ-GRADED 轻率的;随便的;不严肃的 If you say that someone is being flip, you disapprove of them because you think that what they are saying shows they are not being serious enough about something. 轻率的;随便的;不严肃的 [disapproval]

  • ...a flip answer.

    草率的回答

  • The tone of the book is sometimes too flip.

    这本书的笔调有些时候太不严肃了。
