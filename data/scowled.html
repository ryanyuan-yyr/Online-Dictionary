scowl
skaʊl skaʊl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense scowls, present participle scowling, past tense , past participle scowled
VERB 作怒容;沉下脸;绷着脸 When someone scowls, an angry or hostile expression appears on their face. 作怒容;沉下脸;绷着脸

  • He scowled, and slammed the door behind him. [VERB]

    他怒气冲冲，摔门而去。

  • She scowled at the two men as they entered the room. [VERB + at]

    两个男人进屋时，她怒目而视。

SYN glower, frown, look daggers, grimace  
Scowl is also a noun.

  • Chris met the remark with a scowl.

    克里斯听到这句话，脸沉了下来。
