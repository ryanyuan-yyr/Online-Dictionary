objection
əbdʒekʃən əbˈdʒɛkʃən ɑbˈdʒɛkʃən
Word forms: plural objections
1 N-VAR 不喜欢;反对;异议 If you make or raise an objection to something, you say that you do not like it or agree with it. 不喜欢;反对;异议

  • Some managers have recently raised objection to the PFA handling these negotiations.

    一些主教练最近就英格兰职业足球运动员协会负责这些谈判提出了异议。

  • Despite objections by the White House, the Senate voted today to cut off aid. [+ by]

    参议院不顾白宫的反对于今天投票决定中止援助。

2 N-UNCOUNT 恼火；烦心 If you say that you have no objection to something, you mean that you are not annoyed or bothered by it. 恼火；烦心

  • I have no objection to banks making money.

    我对银行营利并不抵触。

  • I no longer have any objection to your going to see her.

    我不会再因为你去见她而不高兴。
