comic
kɒmɪk ˈkɑmɪk
Word forms: plural comics
1 ADJ-GRADED 滑稽的;好笑的 If you describe something as comic, you mean that it makes you laugh, and is often intended to make you laugh. 滑稽的;好笑的

  • The novel is comic and tragic.

    这部小说令人笑中带泪。

  • Most of these trips had exciting or comic moments.

    大部分旅行途中都有激动人心或逗人开怀的时刻。

SYN funny, amusing, witty, humorous  
2 ADJ [ADJ n] 喜剧的 Comic is used to describe comedy as a form of entertainment, and the actors and entertainers who perform it. 喜剧的

  • Grodin is a fine comic actor.

    格罗丁是一位优秀的喜剧演员。

  • ...a comic opera.

    喜歌剧

3 N-COUNT 喜剧演员 A comic is an entertainer who tells jokes in order to make people laugh. 喜剧演员
4 N-COUNT 连环画杂志;漫画书 A comic is a magazine that contains stories told in pictures. 连环画杂志;漫画书 [mainly British]

  • Joe loved to read 'Superman' comics.

    乔喜欢看《超人》的漫画书。

in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 comic book
5 N-PLURAL [usually the NOUN] (报纸上的)连环漫画栏 The comics is the part of a newspaper that contains the comic strips. (报纸上的)连环漫画栏 [US]
