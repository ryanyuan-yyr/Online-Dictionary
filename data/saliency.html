salient
seɪliənt ˈseɪljənt ˈseɪliənt
Word forms: plural salients
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 最重要的;显著的;突出的 The salient points or facts of a situation are the most important ones. 最重要的;显著的;突出的 [formal]

  • He read the salient facts quickly.

    他快速浏览了重要的事实。

  • Chronic fatigue is also one of the salient features of depression.

    慢性疲劳也是抑郁症的显著特点之一。

SYN prominent, outstanding, important, marked  
salience

  • ...the salience of social reforms.

    社会改革的重要性

2 N-COUNT [usually singular] 突出阵线，突出部(战线向敌人阵地推进的狭小范围) A salient is a narrow area where an army has pushed its front line forward into enemy territory. 突出阵线，突出部(战线向敌人阵地推进的狭小范围) [military]

  • The soldiers had to remain in a deathtrap salient for most of the rest of the war.

    随后的战争中，士兵们大部分时间只能呆在最危险的突出阵地里。
