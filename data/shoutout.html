shout out
PHRASAL VERB 大声说出;大声喊出 If you shout something out, you say it very loudly so that people can hear you clearly. 大声说出;大声喊出 [Also VERB PREPOSITION]

  • They shouted out the names of those detained. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他们大声喊出了被羁押人的名字。

  • I shouted out 'I'm OK'. [VERB PREP. with quote]

    我大声喊道“我没事”。

  • I wanted to shout it out, let her know what I had overheard. [VERB noun PREP.]

    我想大声说出来，让她知道我无意中所听到的一切。

shout-out
(also shoutout)Word forms: plural shout-outs
N-COUNT 公开致谢; 当众问候 A shout-out is a short, public expression of greeting, thanks, or praise, especially on a broadcast or a recording. 公开致谢; 当众问候 [informal]

  • Each show even included a timely shout-out to the late Johnny Cash.

    每场演出都包括了对晚年约翰尼·卡什的公开致敬。
