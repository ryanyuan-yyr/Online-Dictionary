stormy
stɔːʳmi ˈstɔrmi
Word forms: comparative stormier, superlative stormiest
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 有暴风雨的 If there is stormy weather, there are strong winds and heavy rain. 有暴风雨的

  • It had been a night of stormy weather, with torrential rain and high winds.

    这是一个暴风雨之夜，大雨倾盆，强风呼啸。

  • ...the long stormy winter of 1942.

    1942 年风雪交加的漫长冬天

SYN wild, rough, tempestuous, raging  
2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (大海)波涛汹涌的，波浪滔天的 Stormy seas have very large strong waves because there are strong winds. (大海)波涛汹涌的，波浪滔天的

  • They make the treacherous journey across stormy seas.

    他们穿越了波浪滔天的大海，完成了这次危险之旅。

  • ...the stormy waters that surround the British Isles.

    不列颠群岛周围波浪滔天的水域

SYN rough, wild, turbulent, tempestuous  
3 ADJ-GRADED 争吵激烈的;群情激愤的 If you describe a situation as stormy, you mean it involves a lot of angry argument or criticism. 争吵激烈的;群情激愤的

  • The letter was read at a stormy meeting.

    这封信是在一次争吵激烈的会议上念的。

  • Their working relationship was stormy at times.

    他们在工作中关系有时也闹得很僵。

SYN angry, heated, fierce, passionate
