enfranchise
ɪnfræntʃaɪz ɛnˈfrænˌtʃaɪz ɪnˈfrænˌtʃaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense enfranchises, present participle enfranchising, past tense , past participle enfranchised
VERB 给予…选举权 To enfranchise someone means to give them the right to vote in elections. 给予…选举权 [formal]

  • The company voted to enfranchise its 120 women members. [VERB noun]

    公司投票决定给予其120名女职员选举权。

  • If the city's foreign residents are enfranchised, they won't be able to vote until 1996. [VERB noun]

    如果该市的外国居民获得选举权，他们要到1996年才能投票。

SYN give the vote to, give voting rights to, grant suffrage to, grant the franchise to
