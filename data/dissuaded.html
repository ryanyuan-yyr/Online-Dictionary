dissuade
dɪsweɪd dɪˈsweɪd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dissuades, present participle dissuading, past tense , past participle dissuaded
1 VERB 劝阻;劝(某人)不要做(或相信) If you dissuade someone from doing or believing something, you persuade them not to do or believe it. 劝阻;劝(某人)不要做(或相信) [formal]

  • Doctors had tried to dissuade patients from smoking. [VERB noun + from]

    医生们劝病人不要吸烟。

  • She steadfastly maintained that her grandsons were innocent, and nothing could dissuade her from that belief. [V n from -ing/n]

    她坚持认为她孙子是无辜的，什么都不能改变她这一看法。

  • He considered emigrating, but his family managed to dissuade him. [VERB noun]

    他考虑过移民，但他家人劝阻了他。
