ossify
ɒsɪfaɪ ˈɑsəˌfaɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense ossifies, present participle ossifying, past tense , past participle ossified
V-ERG （使）僵化；（使）一成不变；（使）墨守成规 If an idea, system, or organization ossifies or if something ossifies it, it becomes fixed and difficult to change. （使）僵化；（使）一成不变；（使）墨守成规 [formal, disapproval] [Also V into n]

  • It reckons that rationing would ossify the farm industry. [VERB noun]

    人们认为实行配给制会使农业失去活力。

  • British society tended to ossify and close ranks as the 1930s drew to their close. [VERB]

    英国社会在20世纪30年代末期趋于保守和自闭。

  • ...an ossified institution. [VERB-ed]

    僵化的机构

SYN harden, freeze, stiffen, petrify
