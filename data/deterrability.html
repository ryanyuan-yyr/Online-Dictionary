deter
dɪtɜːʳ diˈtɜr dɪˈtɜr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense deters, present participle deterring, past tense , past participle deterred
VERB 威慑住;吓住;阻止;制止 To deter someone from doing something means to make them not want to do it or continue doing it. 威慑住;吓住;阻止;制止

  • Supporters of the death penalty argue that it would deter criminals from carrying guns. [VERB noun + from]

    死刑的支持者认为它能阻吓罪犯携带枪支。

  • Arrests and jail sentences have done nothing to deter the protesters. [VERB noun]

    逮捕和监禁都没能吓倒抗议者。

  • Jeremy was not deterred by this criticism. [VERB noun]

    杰里米没有因这一批评而却步。

SYN discourage, inhibit, put off, frighten
