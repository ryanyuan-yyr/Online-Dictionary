swamp
swɒmp swɑmp swɔmp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense swamps, present participle swamping, past tense , past participle swamped
1 N-VAR 沼泽(地) A swamp is an area of very wet land with wild plants growing in it. 沼泽(地)
SYN bog, marsh, quagmire, moss [Scottish , Northern England , dialect]  
2 VERB 淹没;浸没 If something swamps a place or object, it fills it with water. 淹没;浸没

  • A rogue wave swamped the boat. [VERB noun]

    凶猛的海浪淹没了船只。

  • The Ventura river burst its banks, swamping a mobile home park. [VERB noun]

    本图拉河的河水冲破堤岸，淹没了活动房区。

SYN flood, engulf, submerge, inundate  
3 VERB [usually passive] 使不堪承受;使疲于应对;使应接不暇 If you are swamped by things or people, you have more of them than you can deal with. 使不堪承受;使疲于应对;使应接不暇

  • He is swamped with work. [be VERB-ed]

    他工作忙得不可开交。

  • The railway station was swamped with thousands of families trying to flee the city. [be VERB-ed]

    火车站挤满了成千上万想要逃离这座城市的家庭。
