blacken
blækən ˈblækən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense blackens, present participle blackening, past tense , past participle blackened
1 V-ERG （使）变黑；弄黑；涂黑 To blacken something means to make it black or very dark in colour. Something that blackens becomes black or very dark in colour. （使）变黑；弄黑；涂黑

  • The married women of Shitamachi maintained the custom of blackening their teeth. [VERB noun]

    下町的已婚女性还保留着把牙齿涂黑的习俗。

  • You need to grill the tomatoes until the skins blacken. [VERB]

    你得把番茄烤到皮变黑为止。

2 VERB 诬蔑；玷污；给…抹黑 If someone blackens your character, they make other people believe that you are a bad person. 诬蔑；玷污；给…抹黑

  • He accused him of knowingly spreading falsehoods in an effort to blacken my character. [VERB noun]

    他指控他蓄意散布虚假信息以玷污我的人格。

  • They're trying to blacken our name. [VERB noun]

    他们企图败坏我们的名声。

SYN discredit, stain, disgrace, smear
