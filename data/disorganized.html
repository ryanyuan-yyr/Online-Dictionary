disorganized
dɪsɔːʳgənaɪzd dɪsˈɔːɡəˌnaɪzd
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 disorganised
1 ADJ-GRADED 缺乏组织的;无系统的;杂乱无章的 Something that is disorganized is in a confused state or is badly planned or managed. 缺乏组织的;无系统的;杂乱无章的

  • A report by the state prosecutor described the police action as confused and disorganised.

    州检察官的报告称警方的这次行动混乱无序、毫无章法。

  • He has helped to transform the party from a disorganised, demoralised rabble into a force which must again be taken seriously.

    他帮助把该政党从一群组织涣散、意志消沉的乌合之众打造成了一只不容小觑的力量。

2 ADJ-GRADED (指人做事)无条理的，计划不周的 Someone who is disorganized is very bad at organizing things in their life. (指人做事)无条理的，计划不周的

  • My boss is completely disorganised.

    我的老板做事毫无条理可言。
