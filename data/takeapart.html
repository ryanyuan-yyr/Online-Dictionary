take apart
1 PHRASAL VERB 拆开;拆散;拆解 If you take something apart, you separate it into the different parts that it is made of. 拆开;拆散;拆解 [Also VERB PREPOSITION n (not pron)]

  • When the clock stopped, he took it apart, found what was wrong, and put the whole thing together again. [VERB noun PREP.]

    钟停以后，他把它拆开，查出毛病所在，又把它重新组装了起来。

SYN dismantle, break up, pull apart, disassemble  
2 PHRASAL VERB 仔细分析，剖析（以指出不足或缺点) If you take apart something such as an argument or an idea, you show what its weaknesses are, usually by analyzing it carefully. 仔细分析，剖析（以指出不足或缺点)

  • They will take that problem apart and analyze it in great detail. [VERB noun PREP.]

    他们会认真分析那个问题，指出其症结所在。

  • He proceeds to take apart every preconception anyone might have ever had about him. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他继续逐一剖析人们对他可能怀有的成见。

SYN dissect, study, analyse, scrutinize
