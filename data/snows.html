snow
snoʊ snoʊ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense snows, present participle snowing, past tense , past participle snowed
1 N-UNCOUNT 雪 Snow consists of a lot of soft white bits of frozen water that fall from the sky in cold weather. 雪

  • In Mid-Wales six inches of snow blocked roads.

    在威尔士的中部地区，6英寸厚的大雪阻塞了道路。

  • They tramped through the falling snow.

    他们在纷飞的大雪里艰难前行。

SYN snowflakes, blizzard, snowfall, snowstorm  
2 N-PLURAL 降雪;雪季 You can refer to a great deal of snow in an area as the snows . 降雪;雪季

  • ...the first snows of winter.

    冬天里的第一场雪

  • As the snows melt, the flood waters rise.

    雪融化的时候，洪水会上涨。

3 VERB 下雪 When it snows, snow falls from the sky. 下雪

  • It had been snowing all night. [VERB]

    整晚都在下雪。

4 VERB 用花言巧语鼓动(或迷惑) If someone snows you, they persuade you to do something or convince you of something by flattering or deceiving you. 用花言巧语鼓动(或迷惑) [US, informal]

  • I'd been a fool letting him snow me with his big ideas. [VERB noun]

    我真蠢，居然被他的胡乱吹嘘给蒙蔽了。

5 See also: snowed in  snowed under
