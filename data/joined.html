join
dʒɔɪn dʒɔɪn
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense joins, present participle joining, past tense , past participle joined
1 VERB （人或交通工具）加入…之中，与…一道去，与…会合 If one person or vehicle joins another, they move or go to the same place, for example so that both of them can do something together. （人或交通工具）加入…之中，与…一道去，与…会合

  • His wife and children moved to join him in their new home. [VERB noun]

    他的妻子和孩子们搬进了他们的新家，和他一起生活。

  • The two policemen were joined by another policeman also carrying a pistol. [VERB noun]

    另一名持枪警察和那两名警察会合了。

2 VERB 加入（组织或机构）；成为…的一员；参加 If you join an organization, you become a member of it or start work as an employee of it. 加入（组织或机构）；成为…的一员；参加

  • He joined the Army five years ago. [VERB noun]

    他5年前参军了。

  • She joined a dance company which took her around the world. [VERB noun]

    她加入了一个舞蹈团，到世界各地去演出。

SYN enrol in, enter, sign up for, become a member of  
3 VERB 参与（活动） If you join an activity that other people are doing, you take part in it or become involved with it. 参与（活动）

  • Telephone operators joined the strike and four million engineering workers are also planning action. [VERB noun]

    电话接线员加入了罢工的行列，400万工程技术工人也在计划采取行动。

  • The pastor requested the women present to join him in prayer. [VERB noun + in]

    牧师请在场的妇女们和他一起祈祷。

  • Private contractors joined in condemning the Government's stance. [VERB + in]

    私人承包商们也一道谴责政府的立场。

4 VERB 加入（队伍）；排（队） If you join a queue, you stand at the end of it so that you are part of it. 加入（队伍）；排（队）

  • Make sure you join the queue inside the bank. [VERB noun]

    在银行里一定要排队。

5 VERB 连接；接合 To join two things means to fix or fasten them together. 连接；接合

  • The opened link is used to join the two ends of the chain. [VERB noun]

    开口的链环是用来将链子的两头连接起来的。

  • ...the conjunctiva, the skin which joins the eye to the lid. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    将眼睛和眼睑连在一起的薄膜—结膜

  • ...two springs that are joined together by a string. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    由一根细绳连在一起的两根弹簧

SYN connect, unite, couple, link  
6 VERB （线、小路等）连接，贯通 If something such as a line or path joins two things, it connects them. （线、小路等）连接，贯通

  • It has a dormer roof joining both gable ends. [VERB noun]

    带天窗的斜屋顶连接着两边的山墙。

  • The car parks are joined by a footpath. [V pl-n]

    停车场中间有一条人行道。

  • ...a global highway of cables joining all the continents together. [VERB-ing]

    将各大洲连接在一起的全球光缆通道

7 V-RECIP （道路或河流）会合，汇合，交汇 If two roads or rivers join, they meet or come together at a particular point. （道路或河流）会合，汇合，交汇

  • Do you know the highway to Tulsa? The airport road joins it. [VERB noun]

    你知道通向塔尔萨的公路吗？去机场的路和它交会。

  • ...Allahabad, where the Ganges and the Yamuna rivers join. [VERB]

    安拉阿巴德，恒河和亚穆纳河的交汇之处

SYN meet, touch, border, extend  
8 N-COUNT 连接处；接合点；接头；接缝 A join is a place where two things are fastened or fixed together. 连接处；接合点；接头；接缝
