repress
rɪpres rɪˈprɛs
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense represses, present participle repressing, past tense , past participle repressed
1 VERB 压制，压抑，克制(情感) If you repress a feeling, you make a deliberate effort not to show or have this feeling. 压制，压抑，克制(情感)

  • People who repress their emotions risk having nightmares. [VERB noun]

    压抑情感的人容易做噩梦。

  • It is anger that is repressed that leads to violence and loss of control. [be VERB-ed]

    是被压制的愤怒导致暴力和失控。

  • ...repressed aggression. [VERB-ed]

    被压抑的敌对情绪

SYN control, suppress, hold back, bottle up  
2 VERB 忍住;控制住 If you repress a smile, sigh, or moan, you try hard not to smile, sigh, or moan. 忍住;控制住

  • He repressed a smile. [VERB noun]

    他忍住笑。

  • I couldn't repress a sigh of admiration. [VERB noun]

    我忍不住发出一声赞叹。

SYN hold back, suppress, stifle, smother  
3 VERB 压制，限制(自由) If a section of society is repressed, their freedom is restricted by the people who have authority over them. 压制，限制(自由) [disapproval]

  • Turkish Kurds have been repressed for decades by the Ankara government. [be VERB-ed]

    土耳其的库尔德人已经被安卡拉政府压制了几十年。

  • ...a UN resolution banning him from repressing his people.

    禁止他压制民众的联合国决议

SYN subdue, abuse, crush, oppress
