rehash
riːhæʃ riːhæʃ ˈriˌhæʃ ˈriˈhæʃ riˈhæʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense rehashes, present participle rehashing, past tense , past participle rehashed
The noun is pronounced /'riːhæʃ/. The verb is pronounced /riː'hæʃ/. 名词读作/'riːhæʃ/。动词读作/riː'hæʃ/。
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 重复;雷同;翻版;老调重弹 If you describe something as a rehash, you are criticizing it because it repeats old ideas, facts, or themes, though some things have been changed to make it appear new. 重复;雷同;翻版;老调重弹 [disapproval]

  • The Observer found the play 'a feeble rehash of familiar Miller themes'. [+ of]

    《观察家报》认为这部剧“是大家熟悉的米勒式主题的拙劣翻版”。

  • Most of the 'new' models promised by American car makers look set to be little more than rehashes of existing products.

    这些美国汽车制造商承诺推出的“新款”多半都像是现有车型的翻版。

SYN reworking, rewrite, new version, rearrangement  
2 VERB 对…改头换面;只作轻微改动地重复 If you say that someone rehashes old ideas, facts, or accusations, you disapprove of the fact that they present them in a slightly different way so that they seem new or original. 对…改头换面;只作轻微改动地重复 [disapproval]

  • They've taken some of the best bits out of the best things and rehashed them. [VERB noun]

    他们从最好的东西中提取一些精华的部分，然后新瓶装旧酒。

  • They are just bringing up all their old rehashed claims with no prospect or vision for the future. [VERB-ed]

    他们只不过是在老调重弹，提出的声明中没有对未来的展望或设想。

SYN rework, rewrite, rearrange, change
