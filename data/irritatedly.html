irritate
ɪrɪteɪt ˈɪrɪˌteɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense irritates, present participle irritating, past tense , past participle irritated
1 VERB 激怒;使恼怒;使烦躁 If something irritates you, it keeps annoying you. 激怒;使恼怒;使烦躁

  • Their attitude irritates me.

    他们的态度激怒了我。

  • Perhaps they were irritated by the sound of crying.

    也许他们被哭声惹烦了。

irritated
→see usage note at: angry

  • Not surprisingly, her teacher is getting irritated with her.

    毫不奇怪,她的老师有点被她惹恼了。

2 VERB 刺激(身体部位);使发痒;使疼痛 If something irritates a part of your body, it causes it to itch or become sore. 刺激(身体部位);使发痒;使疼痛

  • Wear rubber gloves while chopping chillies as they can irritate the skin. [VERB noun]

    切辣椒时要戴橡胶手套,因为辣椒会刺激皮肤。
