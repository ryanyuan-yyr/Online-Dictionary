guillotine
gɪlətiːn ˈgɪləˌtin ˈgiətin ˌ gɪləˈtin ˌ giəˈtin
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense guillotines, present participle guillotining, past tense , past participle guillotined
1 N-COUNT [oft by NOUN] 断头台（尤见于旧时法国） A guillotine is a device used to execute people, especially in France in the past. A sharp blade was raised up on a frame and dropped onto the person's neck. 断头台（尤见于旧时法国）

  • One after the other Danton, Robespierre and the rest went to the guillotine.

    丹东、罗伯斯比尔以及其他人被逐一送上了断头台。

2 VERB [usually passive] 把…送上断头台 If someone is guillotined, they are killed with a guillotine. 把…送上断头台

  • After Marie Antoinette was guillotined, her lips moved in an attempt to speak. [be VERB-ed]

    玛丽·安托瓦内特被送上断头台处决后，她的嘴唇动了动，想开口说话。

3 N-COUNT 切纸机 A guillotine is a device used for cutting paper. 切纸机
