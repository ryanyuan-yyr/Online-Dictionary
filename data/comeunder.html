come under
1 PHRASAL VERB [no passive] 受到(袭击);遭受(压力) If you come under attack or pressure, for example, people attack you or put pressure on you. 受到(袭击);遭受(压力)

  • The police came under attack from angry crowds. [VERB PREP. noun]

    警察遭到愤怒的人群的袭击。

  • In parliament last week the Finance Minister came under heavy pressure to resign. [VERB PREP. noun]

    财政部长上星期在议会背负着巨大的辞职压力。

  • His relationship with the KGB came under scrutiny. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他与克格勃的关系受到了审查。

2 PHRASAL VERB [no passive] 受…管辖;在…控制之下 If something comes under a particular authority, it is managed or controlled by that authority. 受…管辖;在…控制之下

  • They were neglected before because they did not come under the Ministry of Defence. [VERB PREP. noun]

    以前他们被人忽视是因为不受国防部管辖。

3 PHRASAL VERB [no passive] 属于…范畴;列在…之下 If something comes under a particular heading, it is in the category mentioned. 属于…范畴;列在…之下

  • There was more news about Britain, but it came under the heading of human interest. [VERB PREP. noun]

    还有更多关于英国的新闻，不过被列在了“人情”的标题下。
