opening
oʊpənɪŋ ˈəʊpənɪŋ ˈoʊpəˌnɪŋ ˈoʊpˌnɪŋ
Word forms: plural openings
1 ADJ [ADJ n] 开始的;开端的;开场的 The opening event, item, day, or week in a series is the first one. 开始的;开端的;开场的

  • They returned to take part in the season's opening game.

    他们回来参加本赛季的开幕赛。

  • ...the opening day of the fifth General Synod.

    第5届总议会的开幕日

SYN first, early, earliest, beginning  
2 N-COUNT 开始；开端；开场 The opening of something such as a book, play, or concert is the first part of it. 开始；开端；开场

  • The opening of the scene depicts Akhnaten and his family in a moment of intimacy. [+ of]

    这幕戏的开场描绘了埃及法老阿肯那顿和他的家人其乐融融的情景。

3 N-COUNT 孔；洞；缺口 An opening is a hole or empty space through which things or people can pass. 孔；洞；缺口

  • He squeezed through a narrow opening in the fence. [+ in]

    他从围栏上的狭窄缺口里挤了过去。

SYN hole, break, space, tear  
4 N-COUNT （林中）空地 An opening in a forest is a small area where there are no trees or bushes. （林中）空地 [mainly US]

  • I glanced down at the beach as we passed an opening in the trees. [+ by]

    我们经过一块林中空地时，我往下扫了一眼海滩。

in BRIT, usually use 英国英语通常用 clearing
5 N-COUNT 有利机会；机遇 An opening is a good opportunity to do something, for example to show people how good you are. 有利机会；机遇

  • Her capabilities were always there; all she needed was an opening to show them.

    她一直都很有能力；她需要的只是一次展示才华的机会。

SYN opportunity, chance, break [informal] , time  
6 N-COUNT （职位的）空缺 An opening is a job that is available. （职位的）空缺

  • We don't have any openings now, but we'll call you if something comes up.

    我们现在没有职位空缺，一旦有的话我们会给你打电话。

SYN job, position, post, situation
