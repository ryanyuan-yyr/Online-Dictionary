sever
sevəʳ ˈsɛvər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense severs, present participle severing, past tense , past participle severed
1 VERB 切开;割断;切下;割下 To sever something means to cut completely through it or to cut it completely off. 切开;割断;切下;割下 [formal]

  • Richardson severed his right foot in a motorbike accident. [VERB noun]

    理查森在一次摩托车事故中失去了他的右脚。

  • ...oil still gushing from a severed fuel line. [VERB-ed]

    石油仍然从被切断的燃料运输管中喷涌而出

SYN cut, separate, split, part  
2 VERB (突然)断绝，中断(关系) If you sever a relationship or connection that you have with someone, you end it suddenly and completely. (突然)断绝，中断(关系) [formal]

  • She severed her ties with England. [VERB noun]

    她断绝了和英国的往来。

  • He was able to sever all emotional bonds to his family. [VERB noun]

    他切断了和家人的所有感情纽带。

SYN discontinue, terminate, break off, abandon
