rejoice
rɪdʒɔɪs rɪˈdʒɔɪs
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense rejoices, present participle rejoicing, past tense , past participle rejoiced
1 VERB 感到欣喜;高兴;喜悦 If you rejoice, you are very pleased about something and you show it in your behaviour. 感到欣喜;高兴;喜悦 [Also VERB]

  • Garbo plays the Queen, rejoicing in the love she has found with Antonio. [V + in/at]

    嘉宝饰演王后，陶醉于和安东尼奥的热恋中。

  • A Foreign Ministry statement said that the French government rejoiced at the happy outcome to events. [V in/at n]

    外交部的声明称法国政府很高兴看到事件得到圆满解决。

  • Party activists in New Hampshire rejoiced that the presidential campaign had finally started. [VERB that]

    新罕布什尔州党派激进分子对于总统选举终于启动感到欣喜若狂。

SYN be glad, celebrate, delight, be happy  
rejoicing

  • There was general rejoicing at the news.

    听到这一消息大家普遍感到高兴。

2 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 有个滑稽的名字叫… If you say that a person or thing rejoices in the name of something, you mean that they have a silly or amusing name. 有个滑稽的名字叫…

  • ...their tortoise, who rejoiced in the name of Carruthers.

    他们养的龟有个搞笑的名字—卡拉瑟斯
