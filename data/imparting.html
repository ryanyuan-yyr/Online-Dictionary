impart
ɪmpɑːʳt ɪmˈpɑrt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense imparts, present participle imparting, past tense , past participle imparted
1 VERB 传授;告知 If you impart information to people, you tell it to them. 传授;告知 [formal]

  • The ability to impart knowledge and command respect is the essential qualification for teachers. [VERB noun]

    具备传授知识并赢得尊敬的能力是对教师的基本要求。

  • I am about to impart knowledge to you that you will never forget. [VERB noun + to]

    我会向你传授让你终生牢记的知识。

SYN communicate, pass on, convey, tell  
2 VERB 给予(特定品质) To impart a particular quality to something means to give it that quality. 给予(特定品质) [formal]

  • She managed to impart great elegance to the unpretentious dress she was wearing. [VERB noun + to]

    她使自己朴素的衣着看起来也十分优雅。

  • His production of Harold Pinter's play fails to impart a sense of excitement or danger. [VERB noun]

    他所执导的哈罗德·品特的剧作没能表现出紧张、危险感。

SYN give, accord, lend, bestow
