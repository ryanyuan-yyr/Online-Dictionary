satire
sætaɪəʳ ˈsæˌtaɪr
Word forms: plural satires
1 N-UNCOUNT 讽刺;讥讽 Satire is the use of humour or exaggeration in order to show how foolish or wicked some people's behaviour or ideas are. 讽刺;讥讽

  • The commercial side of the Christmas season is an easy target for satire.

    圣诞季商业化的一面很容易招来讥讽。

2 N-COUNT 讽刺作品 A satire is a play, film, or novel in which humour or exaggeration is used to criticize something. 讽刺作品

  • ...a sharp satire on the American political process. [+ on]

    一部尖刻讽刺美国政治进程的作品

SYN parody, mockery, caricature, send-up [British , informal]
