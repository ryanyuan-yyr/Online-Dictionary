bray
breɪ breɪ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , 3rd person singular present tense brays, present participle braying, past tense , past participle brayed
1 VERB （驴）嘶叫 When a donkey brays, it makes a loud harsh sound. （驴）嘶叫

  • The donkey brayed and tried to bolt. [VERB]

    这头驴嘶叫着试图脱缰而逃。

SYN neigh, bellow, screech, whinny  
2 VERB 发出驴叫似的声音；刺耳地高声说话 If someone brays, they make a loud harsh sound or talk in a loud harsh way. 发出驴叫似的声音；刺耳地高声说话 [written]

  • Neil brayed with angry laughter. [VERB prep.]

    尼尔愤怒地尖声大笑。

  • Her voice was shockingly loud. 'Put the chair down,' she brayed. [VERB with quote]

    她的声音大得吓人。“放下椅子，”她粗声说道。

  • William had a loud, braying voice. [VERB-ing]

    威廉有一副大嗓门，说起话来粗声粗气。

SYN roar, trumpet, bellow, hoot  
Bray is also a noun.

  • She cut him off with a wild bray of laughter.

    她用刺耳的狂笑打断了他的讲话。
