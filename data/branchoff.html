branch off
PHRASAL VERB 分叉；分岔；（在某处）改变方向 A road or path that branches off from another one starts from it and goes in a slightly different direction. If you branch off somewhere, you change the direction in which you are going. 分叉；分岔；（在某处）改变方向 [Also VERB PREPOSITION]

  • After a few miles, a small road branched off to the right. [VERB PREP. prep./adv.]

    过了几英里，右侧出现一条小岔路。

  • She branched off down the earth track. [VERB PREP. prep./adv.

    她改变路线，沿着土路走了下去。

SYN turn off, deviate, change direction, leave the road
