escalate
eskəleɪt ˈɛskəˌleɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense escalates, present participle escalating, past tense , past participle escalated
1 V-ERG （使）扩大;（使）恶化;（使）升级 If a bad situation escalates or if someone or something escalates it, it becomes greater in size, seriousness, or intensity. （使）扩大;（使）恶化;（使）升级 [journalism]

  • Both unions and management fear the dispute could escalate. [VERB]

    工会和管理层都担心争端会恶化。

  • The protests escalated into five days of rioting. [VERB + into]

    抗议升级为为期5天的骚乱。

  • Defeat could cause one side or other to escalate the conflict. [VERB noun]

    失败可能会导致其中一方将冲突升级。

SYN increase, develop, extend, intensify  
escalation

  • The threat of nuclear escalation remains.

    核升级的威胁仍旧存在。

  • ...a sudden escalation of violence.

    暴力事件的突然恶化
