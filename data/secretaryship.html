secretary
sekrətri -teri ˈsɛkrəˌtɛri
Word forms: plural secretaries
1 N-COUNT 秘书 A secretary is a person who is employed to do office work, such as typing letters, answering phone calls, and arranging meetings. 秘书
2 N-COUNT (工会、政党、俱乐部等的)干事,书记员 The secretary of an organization such as a trade union, a political party, or a club is its official manager. (工会、政党、俱乐部等的)干事,书记员 [British]

  • My grandfather was secretary of the Scottish Miners' Union. [+ of]

    我的祖父是苏格兰矿工工会干事。

3 N-COUNT (公司的)秘书，文书 The secretary of a company is the person who has the legal duty of keeping the company's records. (公司的)秘书，文书
4 N-COUNT; N-TITLE 部长;大臣 Secretary is used in the titles of ministers and officials who are in charge of main government departments. 部长;大臣

  • ...the British Foreign Secretary.

    英国外交大臣

  • ...Defense Secretary Caspar Weinberger.

    国防部长卡斯珀·温伯格
