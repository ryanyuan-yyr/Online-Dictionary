go around
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 go round
1 PHRASAL VERB 造访；拜访 If you go around to someone's house, you go to visit them at their house. 造访；拜访

  • I asked them to go around to the house to see if they were there. [VERB PREP. + to]

    我叫他们去那所房子看看他们是否在那里。

  • Mike went round to see them. [VERB PREP. to-infinitive]

    迈克去拜访了他们。

2 PHRASAL VERB 经常做；习惯打扮成 If you go around in a particular way, you behave or dress in that way, often as part of your normal life. 经常做；习惯打扮成 [Also V P adj]

  • I had got in the habit of going around with bare feet. [VERB PREP. prep.]

    我已经习惯光脚。

  • If they went around complaining publicly, they might not find it so easy to get another job. [VERB PREP. verb-ing]

    如果他们总是当众发牢骚，再找别的工作可就没那么容易了。

3 PHRASAL VERB （消息或笑话）流传，传播 If a piece of news or a joke is going around, it is being told by many people in the same period of time. （消息或笑话）流传，传播

  • There's a nasty sort of rumour going around about it. [VERB PREP.]

    关于这件事正有一则恶意的谣言在流传。

4 PHRASAL VERB 都能分到；够每人一份 If there is enough of something to go around, there is enough of it to be shared among a group of people, or to do all the things for which it is needed. 都能分到；够每人一份

  • Eventually we will not have enough water to go around. [VERB PREP.]

    最终，我们将没有足够的水供大家喝。
