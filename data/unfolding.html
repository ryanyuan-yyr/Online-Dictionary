unfold
ʌnfoʊld ʌnˈfoʊld
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense unfolds, present participle unfolding, past tense , past participle unfolded
1 VERB 展现；呈现；逐渐明朗 If a situation unfolds, it develops and becomes known or understood. 展现；呈现；逐渐明朗

  • The outcome depends on conditions as well as how events unfold. [VERB]

    结果取决于条件，还取决于事件的进展。

  • The facts started to unfold before them. [VERB]

    真相开始在他们面前显露出来。

SYN develop, happen, progress, grow  
2 V-ERG （故事）展开；讲述 If a story unfolds or if someone unfolds it, it is told to someone else. （故事）展开；讲述

  • Don's story unfolded as the cruise got under way. [VERB]

    随着航行的开始，唐的故事也随之展开。

  • Mr Wills unfolds his story with evident enjoyment. [VERB noun]

    威尔斯先生喜形于色地讲述自己的经历。

SYN reveal, tell, present, show  
3 V-ERG 展开；打开；伸展 If someone unfolds something which has been folded or if it unfolds, it is opened out and becomes flat. 展开；打开；伸展

  • He quickly unfolded the blankets and spread them on the mattress. [VERB noun]

    他迅速展开毯子，铺在床垫上。

  • When the bird lifts off into flight, its wings unfold to an impressive six-foot span. [VERB]

    这种鸟起飞时，翼展可达6英尺，很惊人。

SYN open, spread out, undo, expand
