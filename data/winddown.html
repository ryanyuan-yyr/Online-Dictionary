wind down
waɪnd
1 PHRASAL VERB 摇下(车窗等) When you wind down something such as the window of a car, you make it move downwards by turning a handle. 摇下(车窗等)

  • Glass motioned to him to wind down the window. [VERB PREP. noun]

    格拉斯示意他把车窗摇下。

  • If a stranger stops you, just wind the window down a fraction. [VERB noun PREP.]

    如果有陌生人拦你的车，把车窗摇下一点点就行。

2 PHRASAL VERB 放松;松弛 If you wind down, you relax after doing something that has made you feel tired or tense. 放松;松弛 [informal]

  • I regularly have a drink to wind down. [VERB PREP.]

    我经常喝点酒放松一下。

SYN calm down, unwind, take it easy, unbutton [informal]  
3 PHRASAL VERB-ERG 逐渐减小(工作规模);使逐步停止;使逐渐关闭 If someone winds down a business or activity, they gradually reduce the amount of work that is done or the number of people that are involved, usually before closing or stopping it completely. 逐渐减小(工作规模);使逐步停止;使逐渐关闭 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Foreign aid workers have already begun winding down their operation. [VERB PREP. noun]

    外国救援人员已开始逐步缩减救援活动。

  • In 1991 the Ada plant began to wind down. [VERB PREP.]

    1991年埃达厂开始逐渐关闭。

SYN reduce, relax, lessen, slacken
