merchant
mɜːʳtʃənt ˈmɜrtʃənt
Word forms: plural merchants
1 N-COUNT (尤指外贸)商人，批发商 A merchant is a person who buys or sells goods in large quantities, especially one who imports and exports them. (尤指外贸)商人，批发商

  • Any knowledgeable wine merchant would be able to advise you.

    任何一个懂行的葡萄酒商人都可以给你提出建议。

SYN tradesman, dealer, trader, broker  
2 N-COUNT 店主;商人 A merchant is a person who owns or runs a shop, store, or other business. 店主;商人 [US]

  • The family was forced to live on credit from local merchants.

    这家人不得不靠向当地商人赊账生活。

in BRIT, usually use 英国英语通常用 retailer, shopkeeper
3 ADJ [ADJ n] 海上货运的;商船的 Merchant seamen or ships are involved in carrying goods for trade. 海上货运的;商船的

  • There's been a big reduction in the size of the British merchant fleet in recent years.

    近些年来英国商船队伍的规模大为缩减。
