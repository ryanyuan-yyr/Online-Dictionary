offload ɒfloʊd ɔːf- ˈɔfˈloʊd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense offloads, present participle offloading, past tense , past participle offloaded
1 VERB 出手，转手，清理（不需要之物） If you offload something that you do not want, you get rid of it by giving it or selling it to someone else. 出手，转手，清理（不需要之物） [mainly British]

  • Prices have been cut by developers anxious to offload unsold apartments. [VERB noun]

    急于脱手未售出公寓的开发商降低了房价。

  • Already in financial difficulties, Turner offloaded the painting on to the Getty Museum. [V n + onto]

    特纳已经陷入财政困难，于是将这幅画卖给了盖蒂美术馆。

in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 unload
2 VERB 卸（货） When goods are offloaded, they are removed from a container or vehicle and put somewhere else. 卸（货） [mainly British] [Also VERB n]

  • The supplies need to be offloaded and put on helicopters. [be VERB-ed]

    这些补给要卸下来装上直升机。

  • The cargo was due to be offloaded in Singapore three days later. [be VERB-ed]

    货物定于3天后在新加坡卸载。

in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 unload
