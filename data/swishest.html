swish
swɪʃ swɪʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense swishes, present participle swishing, past tense , past participle swished, comparative swisher, superlative swishest
1 V-ERG (使)刷地(或嗖地、呼地)快速移动 If something swishes or if you swish it, it moves quickly through the air, making a soft sound. (使)刷地(或嗖地、呼地)快速移动

  • A car swished by heading for the coast. [VERB adv./prep.]

    一辆车嗖地驶过朝海岸开去。

  • He swished his cape around his shoulders. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他刷地一下披上斗篷。

  • He heard a swishing sound. [VERB-ing]

    他听到嗖的一声。

Swish is also a noun.

  • She turned with a swish of her skirt.

    她转身时裙摆也跟着旋转飘起。

2 ADJ-GRADED 好看的;时髦的;高档的 If you describe something as swish, you mean that it is smart and fashionable. 好看的;时髦的;高档的 [British, informal, old-fashioned]

  • ...a swish cocktail bar.

    时髦的鸡尾酒酒吧

SYN smart, grand, posh [informal , mainly British] , exclusive
