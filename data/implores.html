implore
ɪmplɔːʳ ɪmˈplɔr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense implores, present participle imploring, past tense , past participle implored
VERB 恳求;乞求;哀求 If you implore someone to do something, you ask them to do it in a forceful, emotional way. 恳求;乞求;哀求 [Also V with quote, V n]

  • Opposition leaders this week implored the president to break the deadlock. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    反对党领袖本周恳求总统打破僵局。

  • 'Tell me what to do!' she implored him. [VERB noun with quote]

    她哀求他说：“求你告诉我该怎么办！”

SYN beg, beseech, entreat, conjure [formal]
