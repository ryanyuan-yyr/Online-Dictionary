flaw
flɔː flɔ
Word forms: plural flaws
1 N-COUNT 错误;谬误 A flaw in something such as a theory or argument is a mistake in it, which causes it to be less effective or valid. 错误;谬误

  • There were, however, a number of crucial flaws in his monetary theory.

    但是，他的货币理论中存在若干关键性错误。

  • Almost all of these studies have serious flaws.

    几乎所有这些研究中都存在严重谬误。

SYN error, mistake, fault, blunder  
2 N-COUNT （性格中的）缺点，弱点 A flaw in someone's character is an undesirable quality that they have. （性格中的）缺点，弱点

  • The only flaw in his character seems to be a short temper. [+ in]

    他性格中的唯一缺点好像就是脾气急。

SYN weakness, failing, defect, weak spot  
3 N-COUNT 瑕疵;缺陷 A flaw in something such as a pattern or material is a fault in it that should not be there. 瑕疵;缺陷
