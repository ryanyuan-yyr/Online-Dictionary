puzzle
pʌzəl ˈpʌzəl ˈpʌzəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense puzzles, present participle puzzling, past tense , past participle puzzled
1 VERB 使迷惑；使困惑 If something puzzles you, you do not understand it and feel confused. 使迷惑；使困惑

  • My sister puzzles me and causes me anxiety. [VERB noun]

    我妹妹总让我捉摸不透，弄得我焦虑不安。

puzzling

  • His letter poses a number of puzzling questions.

    他的信中提出了许多令人困惑的问题。

2 VERB 对…苦苦思索；因…大伤脑筋 If you puzzle over something, you try hard to think of the answer to it or the explanation for it. 对…苦苦思索；因…大伤脑筋

  • In rehearsing Shakespeare, I puzzle over the complexities of his verse and prose. [VERB + about]

    在排演莎士比亚的戏剧时，其中诗句和散文的复杂让我大伤脑筋。

3 N-COUNT 难题；智力问题（或游戏、玩具）；谜 A puzzle is a question, game, or toy which you have to think about carefully in order to answer it correctly or put it together properly. 难题；智力问题（或游戏、玩具）；谜

  • ...a word puzzle.

    字谜

SYN problem, riddle, maze, labyrinth  
4See also:crossword 
5 N-SING 令人费解的人；令人困惑的事物 You can describe a person or thing that is hard to understand as a puzzle . 令人费解的人；令人困惑的事物

  • Data from Voyager II has presented astronomers with a puzzle about why our outermost planet exists.

    “旅行者二号”发回的数据给天文学家们提出了一个难题：离我们最远的那颗行星为什么会存在。

  • She was a puzzle.

    她是个谜。
