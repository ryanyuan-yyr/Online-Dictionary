pipe
paɪp paɪp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense pipes, present participle piping, past tense , past participle piped
1 N-COUNT （通常指金属或塑料制的）管子，管道 A pipe is a long, round, hollow object, usually made of metal or plastic, through which a liquid or gas can flow. （通常指金属或塑料制的）管子，管道

  • They had accidentally damaged a gas pipe while drilling.

    打钻时，他们不小心钻破了一根燃气管道。

  • The plant makes plastic covered steel pipes for the oil and gas industries.

    这家工厂为石油和燃气企业生产包塑钢管。

2 N-COUNT 烟斗；烟筒 A pipe is an object which is used for smoking tobacco. You put the tobacco into the cup-shaped part at the end of the pipe, light it, and breathe in the smoke through a narrow tube. 烟斗；烟筒
3 N-COUNT 管乐器 A pipe is a simple musical instrument in the shape of a tube with holes in it. You play a pipe by blowing into it while covering and uncovering the holes with your fingers. 管乐器
4 N-PLURAL pipes 同 bagpipes Pipes are the same as bagpipes . pipes 同 bagpipes
5 N-COUNT （管风琴的）音管， 风管 An organ pipe is one of the long hollow tubes in which air vibrates and produces a musical note. （管风琴的）音管， 风管
6 VERB 用管子输送，用管道运输（液体或气体） If liquid or gas is piped somewhere, it is transferred from one place to another through a pipe. 用管子输送，用管道运输（液体或气体）

  • The heated gas is piped through a coil surrounded by water. [be VERB-ed prep.]

    受热气体通过水中的盘管输送。

  • The villagers piped in drinking water from the reservoir. [VERB noun with adv.]

    村民用管道从水库引来饮用水。

  • Most of the houses in the capital don't have piped water. [VERB-ed]

    首府的大部分住房没有自来水。

SYN convey, channel, supply, conduct  
7 VERB （尤指儿童）用尖嗓子说 If someone, especially a child, pipes something, they say it in a high-pitched voice. （尤指儿童）用尖嗓子说 [written]

  • 'But I want to help,' Bessie piped. [VERB with quote]

    贝茜尖声说：“但我想去帮忙。”

8 N-COUNT （一些计算机系统中用来在程序间传送信息的）管道 In some computer systems, a pipe is a method of passing information from one program to another. （一些计算机系统中用来在程序间传送信息的）管道
9 See also: piping  piping hot
