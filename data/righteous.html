righteous
raɪtʃəs ˈraɪtʃəs
ADJ-GRADED 正直的;正义的;正派的;（有时指）假正经的，道貌岸然的 If you think that someone behaves or lives in a way that is morally good, you can say that they are righteous. People sometimes use righteous to express their disapproval when they think someone is only behaving in this way so that others will admire or support them. 正直的;正义的;正派的;（有时指）假正经的，道貌岸然的 [formal]

  • Aren't you afraid of being seen as a righteous crusader?.

    你不怕被人看作是一个装腔作势的改革者？

  • He was full of righteous indignation.

    他义愤填膺。

  • ...petty dictators and righteous politicians.

    心胸狭窄的独裁者和正直的政治家

SYN justified, just, valid, legitimate  
righteousness

  • Both sides in the dispute have been adopting a tone of moral righteousness.

    纠纷双方说起话来都是冠冕堂皇。

righteously

  • They righteously maintain that they do not practise rationing.

    他们一本正经地坚称不实行定量配给制度。
