venture
ventʃəʳ ˈvɛntʃər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense ventures, present participle venturing, past tense , past participle ventured
1 N-COUNT 风险项目（或活动);冒险事业 A venture is a project or activity which is new, exciting, and difficult because it involves the risk of failure. 风险项目（或活动);冒险事业

  • ...his latest writing venture.

    他最新的写作尝试

  • ...a Russian-American joint venture.

    一家俄美合资企业

SYN undertaking, project, enterprise, chance  
2 VERB 冒险去（某处） If you venture somewhere, you go somewhere that might be dangerous. 冒险去（某处） [literary]

  • People are afraid to venture out for fear of sniper attacks. [VERB adv./prep.]

    由于害怕狙击手，人们不敢冒险外出。

  • Few Europeans who had ventured beyond the Himalayas had returned to tell the tale. [VERB adv./prep.]

    少数几个冒险翻越了喜马拉雅山的欧洲人回来后讲述了这个故事。

SYN go, travel, journey, set out  
3 VERB 斗胆提出（问题）;冒险发表（陈述） If you venture a question or statement, you say it in an uncertain way because you are afraid it might be stupid or wrong. 斗胆提出（问题）;冒险发表（陈述） [written]

  • 'So you're Leo's girlfriend?' he ventured. [VERB with quote]

    “那么你就是列奥的女朋友了？”他大胆问道。

  • He ventured that plants draw part of their nourishment from the air. [VERB that]

    他大胆提出植物从空气中吸收部分养分的观点。

  • Stephen ventured a few more sentences in halting Welsh. [VERB noun]

    在阻止韦尔什时，斯蒂芬壮着胆子多说了几句。

4 VERB 勇于做;冒险做 If you venture to do something that requires courage or is risky, you do it. 勇于做;冒险做

  • 'Don't ask,' he said, whenever Ginny ventured to raise the subject. [VERB to-infinitive]

    每当金尼鼓起勇气提起这个话题时，他就说“不要问”。

5 VERB 冒险尝试 If you venture into an activity, you do something that involves the risk of failure because it is new and different. 冒险尝试

  • He enjoyed little success when he ventured into business. [VERB + into]

    他冒险涉足商界后几乎没有取得什么成功。
