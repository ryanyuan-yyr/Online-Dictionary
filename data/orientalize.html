oriental
ɔːrientəl ˌɔriˈɛntəl ˌ oʊriˈɛntəl
Word forms: plural orientals
1 ADJ [usually ADJ n] 东方的，东亚的（尤指中国和日本的） Oriental means coming from or associated with eastern Asia, especially China and Japan. 东方的，东亚的（尤指中国和日本的）

  • There were Oriental carpets on the floors.

    地上铺的是来自东方的地毯。

  • ...oriental food.

    东方食物

2 N-COUNT 东方人（尤指中国人或日本人）（可能具冒犯意味） Some people refer to people from eastern Asia, especially China or Japan as Orientals. This use could cause offence. 东方人（尤指中国人或日本人）（可能具冒犯意味）

  • The Orientals use no butter. They prefer the very healthful peanut oil.

    东方人不吃黄油，他们更喜欢对健康非常有益处的花生油。

+−Usage
UsageOriental
Oriental should not be used to refer to people, because it may cause offence. Instead, use either Asian or the person's nationality. Use Oriental only to describe things: I bought an Oriental vase.
