sordid
sɔːʳdɪd ˈsɔrdɪd
1 ADJ-GRADED (行为)卑鄙的，可耻的 If you describe someone's behaviour as sordid, you mean that it is immoral or dishonest. (行为)卑鄙的，可耻的 [disapproval]

  • He sat with his head buried in his hands as his sordid double life was revealed.

    自己卑鄙的双重生活被揭穿后，他双手抱头坐在那里。

  • I don't want to hear the sordid details of your relationship with Sandra.

    我不想听你和桑德拉之间的那些龌龊细节。

  • She listened to Kate's explanation of the sordid affair.

    她听凯特对这件丑事作何解释。

SYN base, degraded, shameful, low  
2 ADJ-GRADED (地方)肮脏的，令人不快的，令人沮丧的 If you describe a place as sordid, you mean that it is dirty, unpleasant, or depressing. (地方)肮脏的，令人不快的，令人沮丧的 [disapproval]

  • ...the attic windows of their sordid little rooms.

    他们那些肮脏的小房间的阁楼窗户

SYN dirty, seedy, sleazy, squalid
