fur
fɜːʳ fɜr
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , plural furs, present participle furring, past tense , past participle furred
1 N-MASS (哺乳动物的)软毛 Fur is the thick and usually soft hair that grows on the bodies of many mammals. (哺乳动物的)软毛

  • This creature's fur is short, dense and silky.

    这种动物的毛又短又密、丝般柔滑。

SYN coat, hair, skin, hide  
2 N-VAR [oft NOUN n] (用于制衣等的)毛皮，皮草 Fur is the fur-covered skin of an animal that is used to make clothing or small carpets. (用于制衣等的)毛皮，皮草

  • She had on a black coat with a fur collar.

    她穿着一件毛皮领子的黑色外套。

  • Women are also wearing fur again.

    女性还重新穿起了皮草。

  • ...the trading of furs from Canada.

    加拿大皮草生意

3 N-COUNT 毛皮大衣;毛皮围脖 A fur is a coat made from real or artificial fur, or a piece of fur worn round your neck. 毛皮大衣;毛皮围脖

  • There were women in furs and men in comfortable overcoats.

    女人们穿着毛皮大衣，男人们穿着舒适的外套。

4 N-MASS 仿毛皮 Fur is an artificial fabric that looks like fur and is used, for example, to make clothing, soft toys, and seat covers. 仿毛皮
5 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 引起激烈争吵(或争论) If an event sets the fur flying, it causes a great argument. 引起激烈争吵(或争论)

  • Last week's meeting of the 1922 Committee set the fur flying again on the Tory backbenches.

    1922 年委员会上个星期的会议又一次引发了保守党后座议员激烈的争吵。

  • The fur will really fly over this.

    这个问题将会掀起轩然大波。

6 PHRASAL VERB-ERG (使)(血管)堵塞 If your veins or arteries fur up or are furred up, they become blocked, so that your blood cannot flow properly. (使)(血管)堵塞

  • Three of my veins had furred up and I needed a triple bypass.

    我有三条静脉出现了堵塞，需要做个三支搭桥手术。

  • Oxidized cholesterol may be responsible for furring up the arteries.

    氧化胆固醇可能是造成动脉堵塞的元凶。
