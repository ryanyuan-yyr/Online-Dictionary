procrastinate
proʊkræstɪneɪt proʊˈkræstəˌneɪt prəˈkræstəˌneɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense procrastinates, present participle procrastinating, past tense , past participle procrastinated
VERB 拖延；耽搁；延迟 If you procrastinate, you keep leaving things you should do until later, often because you do not want to do them. 拖延；耽搁；延迟 [formal]

  • Most often we procrastinate when faced with something we do not want to do. [VERB]

    面对不想做的事情，我们经常拖延。

SYN delay, stall, postpone, prolong  
procrastination

  • He hates delay and procrastination in all its forms.

    他讨厌任何形式的拖延和耽搁。
