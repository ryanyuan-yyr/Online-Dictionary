mega
megə ˈmɛgə
1 ADV [usually ADVERB adjective/adverb] （置于形容词和副词前，表示强调） Young people sometimes use mega in front of adjectives or adverbs in order to emphasize them. （置于形容词和副词前，表示强调） [informal, emphasis]

  • He has become mega rich.

    他成了巨富。

  • Roy was tremendously ugly, mega ugly.

    罗伊巨丑特丑。

2 ADJ [ADJ n] (用于名词前)特别好的，异常巨大的，非常重要的 Young people sometimes use mega in front of nouns in order to emphasize that the thing they are talking about is very good, very large, or very impressive. (用于名词前)特别好的，异常巨大的，非常重要的 [informal, emphasis]

  • ...her newly acquired mega salary.

    她刚刚到手的巨额薪水

  • ...the mega superstar Madonna.

    超级巨星麦当娜

mega- megə- ˈmɛgə
1 PREFIX (加在计量单位之前)表示“兆”，“百万” Mega- is added to nouns that refer to units of measurement in order to form other nouns referring to units that are a million times bigger. (加在计量单位之前)表示“兆”，“百万”

  • ...a 100 megaton explosion.

    100兆吨级的爆炸

  • ...a two thousand megawatt surge in electricity.

    2,000兆瓦的电流

2 PREFIX (与名词、形容词构成合成词，强调大小、性质或重要性) Mega- combines with nouns and adjectives in order to emphasize the size, quality, or importance of something. (与名词、形容词构成合成词，强调大小、性质或重要性) [informal, emphasis]

  • Now he can begin to earn the sort of mega-bucks he has always dreamed about.

    现在他可以开始挣他一直梦寐以求的大钱了。

  • ...a Hollywood mega-star.

    好莱坞的超级大腕
