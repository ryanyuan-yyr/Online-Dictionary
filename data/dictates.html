dictate
dɪkteɪt dɪkteɪt dɪkteɪt ˈdɪkˌteɪt dɪkˈteɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense dictates, present participle dictating, past tense , past participle dictated
The verb is pronounced /dɪk'teɪt, am 'dɪkteɪt/. The noun is pronounced /'dɪkteɪt/. 动词读作/dɪk'teɪt, 美 'dɪkteɪt/。名词读作/'dɪkteɪt/。
1 VERB 口授;口述;使听写 If you dictate something, you say or read it aloud for someone else to write down. 口授;口述;使听写 [Also VERB]

  • Sheldon writes every day of the week, dictating his novels in the morning. [VERB noun]

    谢尔登一周 7 天都要写作，每天早上口述小说让别人记录。

  • Everything he dictated was signed and sent out the same day. [VERB noun]

    他口授的所有内容都签上名并在同一天寄出了。

2 VERB 命令;发号施令;强行规定 If someone dictates to someone else, they tell them what they should do or can do. 命令;发号施令;强行规定

  • He had warned the West against trying to dictate to the Soviet Union. [VERB to noun]

    他警告西方国家不要试图对苏联发号施令。

  • What right has one country to dictate the environmental standards of another?. [VERB noun]

    一个国家有什么权力强行给另一个国家制定环境标准？

  • He cannot be allowed to dictate what can and cannot be inspected. [VERB wh]

    什么能检查什么不能检查不能由他说了算。

  • What gives them the right to dictate to us what we should eat?. [VERB + to]

    他们有什么权利来规定我们应该吃什么？

  • The officers were more or less able to dictate terms to successive governments. [VERB noun + to]

    这些军官多多少少都能和继任的各届政府谈些条件。

  • The rules of court dictate that a defendant is entitled to all evidence which may help his case. [VERB that]

    法庭规定被告可以出示任何对其辩护有帮助的证据。

3 VERB 决定;支配;影响 If one thing dictates another, the first thing causes or influences the second thing. 决定;支配;影响

  • The film's budget dictated a tough schedule. [VERB noun]

    影片的预算决定了拍摄进度会很紧张。

  • The way in which they dress is dictated by very rigid fashion rules. [VERB noun]

    他们的着装受到时尚规则的严格限制。

  • Of course, a number of factors will dictate how long an apple tree can survive. [VERB wh]

    当然，影响一棵苹果树寿命的因素有很多。

  • Circumstances dictated that they played a defensive rather than attacking game. [VERB that]

    比赛形势决定了他们要打防守战而不是进攻战。

SYN determine, demand, command, establish  
4 VERB （理智或常识）认为，告诉 You say that reason or common sense dictates that a particular thing is the case when you believe strongly that it is the case and that reason or common sense will cause other people to agree. （理智或常识）认为，告诉

  • Commonsense now dictates that it would be wise to sell a few shares. [VERB that]

    现在，常识告诉我们卖掉一些股票是明智的。

5 N-COUNT 命令;指令 A dictate is an order which you have to obey. 命令;指令

  • Their job is to ensure that the dictates of the Party are followed. [+ of]

    他们的工作就是确保党的指示得到执行。

6 N-COUNT [usually plural] 原则;规则;准则 Dictates are principles or rules which you consider to be extremely important. 原则;规则;准则

  • We have followed the dictates of our consciences and have done our duty. [+ of]

    我们遵从了自己的良知，尽到了自己的义务。

SYN principle, law, rule, standard
