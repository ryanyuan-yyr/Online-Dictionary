fathom
fæðəm ˈfæðəm
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense fathoms, present participle fathoming, past tense , past participle fathomed
1 N-COUNT 英寻(度量单位，等于 1.8 米或 6 英尺，用于指水深) A fathom is a measurement of 1.8 metres or 6 feet, used when referring to the depth of water. 英寻(度量单位，等于 1.8 米或 6 英尺，用于指水深)

  • We sailed into the bay and dropped anchor in five fathoms of water.

    我们驶进海湾，将锚抛进 5 英寻深的水里。

2 VERB [no cont] 透彻理解;深入了解;弄清…的真相 If you cannot fathom something, you are unable to understand it, although you think carefully about it. 透彻理解;深入了解;弄清…的真相 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • I really couldn't fathom what Steiner was talking about. [VERB wh]

    我真搞不懂斯坦纳在说些什么。

  • Jeremy's passive attitude was hard to fathom. [VERB noun]

    杰里米的消极态度令人费解。

SYN understand, grasp, comprehend, interpret  
Fathom out means the same as fathom .fathom out 同 fathom

  • We're trying to fathom out what's going on.

    我们在尽力搞清楚发生了什么事情。

  • I'm having difficulty using my video editing equipment and can't fathom out the various connections.

    我不会使用视频编辑设备，而且搞不清各种各样的连接。
