naff
næf næf
Word forms: comparative naffer, superlative naffest
1 ADJ-GRADED 不时髦的;没有品位的;不老练的 If you say that something is naff, you mean it is very unfashionable or unsophisticated. 不时髦的;没有品位的;不老练的 [British, informal]

  • The music's really naff.

    这音乐真是太土了。

  • ...naff 'his and hers' matching outfits.

    土得掉渣的情侣装

SYN bad, poor, inferior, worthless  
2 CONVENTION 走开;滚开 If someone tells you to naff off, they are rudely telling you to go away. 走开;滚开 [British, informal, disapproval]

  • She didn't hesitate to tell intrusive photographers to 'naff off'.

    她毫不客气地叫那些烦人的摄影者“滚开”。
