invidious
ɪnvɪdiəs ɪnˈvɪdiəs
1 ADJ-GRADED (任务、工作)招致不满的，引起反感的 If you describe a task or job as invidious, you mean that it is unpleasant because it is likely to make you unpopular. (任务、工作)招致不满的，引起反感的

  • The local authority could find itself in the invidious position of having to refuse.

    当局可能会发现自己处于必须要拒绝但又会因此而不得人心的境地。

SYN undesirable, unpleasant, hateful, thankless  
2 ADJ (比较、选择)不公平的，难以决定的 An invidious comparison or choice between two things is an unfair one because the two things are very different or are equally good or bad. (比较、选择)不公平的，难以决定的

  • Police officers fear invidious comparisons.

    警官担心会进行不公正的比较。

  • It is invidious to make a selection.

    不相上下，很难选择。

SYN unfair, unjust, unjustified, inequitable
