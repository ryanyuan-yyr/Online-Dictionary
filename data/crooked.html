crooked
krʊkɪd krʊkt ˈkrʊkɪd
1 ADJ-GRADED 不直的;弯曲的;扭曲的 If you describe something as crooked, especially something that is usually straight, you mean that it is bent or twisted. 不直的;弯曲的;扭曲的

  • ...the crooked line of his broken nose.

    他骨折鼻子的歪曲鼻骨线条

  • ...a crooked little tree.

    长得歪歪曲曲的小树

SYN bent, twisted, bowed, curved  
2 ADJ-GRADED (笑)不自然的，歪向一边的 A crooked smile is uneven and bigger on one side than the other. (笑)不自然的，歪向一边的

  • Polly gave her a crooked grin.

    波莉向她不自然地咧嘴一笑。

SYN at an angle, angled, tilted, to one side  
crookedly

  • Nick was smiling crookedly at her.

    尼克朝她歪着嘴笑。

3 ADJ 不诚实的;欺诈的 If you describe a person or an activity as crooked, you mean that they are dishonest or criminal. 不诚实的;欺诈的 [informal]

  • ...a crooked cop.

    贪赃枉法的警察

  • She might expose his crooked business deals to her tax inspector brother.

    她可能会向当税务稽查员的哥哥揭发他那些不正当的交易。

SYN dishonest, criminal, illegal, corrupt
