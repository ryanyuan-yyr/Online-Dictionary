pendulum
pendʒʊləm ˈpɛndjuləm ˈpɛndjələm ˈpɛndələm ˈpɛndʒuləm ˈpɛndʒəɛm
Word forms: plural pendulums
1 N-COUNT 钟摆 The pendulum of a clock is a rod with a weight at the end which swings from side to side in order to make the clock work. 钟摆
2 N-SING （局势意见等的）摇摆不定，变化不定 You can use the idea of a pendulum and the way it swings regularly as a way of talking about regular changes in a situation or in people's opinions. （局势意见等的）摇摆不定，变化不定

  • The pendulum has swung back and the American car companies have made dramatic advances in safety.

    三十年河东，三十年河西，美国汽车公司在安全方面已经取得了巨大进步。

  • The political pendulum has swung in favour of the liberals.

    政治形势转而对自由主义者有利。
