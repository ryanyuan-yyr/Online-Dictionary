serenade
serɪneɪd ˌsɛrɪˈneɪd ˌsɛrəˈneɪd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense serenades, present participle serenading, past tense , past participle serenaded
1 VERB (在所爱慕女子的窗下)为(其)唱(或演奏)小夜曲 If one person serenades another, they sing or play a piece of music for them. Traditionally men did this outside the window of the woman they loved. (在所爱慕女子的窗下)为(其)唱(或演奏)小夜曲

  • In the interval a blond boy dressed in white serenaded the company on the flute. [VERB noun]

    幕间休息时,一位身着白衣的金发男孩用长笛为来宾演奏了一支小夜曲。

  • It happened to be my birthday, and after breakfast I was serenaded by the crew. [VERB noun]

    那天恰逢我的生日，吃完早餐后全体工作人员为我唱了一首小夜曲。

Serenade is also a noun.

  • Placido Domingo sang his serenade of love.

    普拉西多·多明戈演唱他的爱情小夜曲。

2 N-COUNT [oft in names] (古典音乐中为小型管弦乐队谱写的)组曲，小夜曲 In classical music, a serenade is a piece in several parts written for a small orchestra. (古典音乐中为小型管弦乐队谱写的)组曲，小夜曲

  • ...Vaughan Williams's Serenade to Music.

    沃恩·威廉斯的《音乐小夜曲》

SYN song, air, ballad, lay
