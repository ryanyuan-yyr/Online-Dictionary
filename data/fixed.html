fixed
fɪkst fɪkst
1 ADJ [usually ADJ n] 固定的;不变的 You use fixed to describe something which stays the same and does not or cannot vary. 固定的;不变的

  • They issue a fixed number of shares that trade publicly.

    他们发行一定数量的可公开交易的股票。

  • ...a world without fixed laws.

    没有固定法规的世界

  • Tickets will be printed with fixed entry times.

    票面上将印有固定的入场时间。

  • Many restaurants offer fixed-price menus.

    很多餐馆都提供价格固定的菜单。

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (观念或看法)顽固的，固执的，一成不变的 If you say that someone has fixed ideas or opinions, you mean that they do not often change their ideas and opinions, although perhaps they should. (观念或看法)顽固的，固执的，一成不变的

  • ...people who have fixed ideas about things.

    对事物看法一成不变的人

SYN inflexible, set, steady, resolute  
3 ADJ (笑容)僵硬的，呆板的,皮笑肉不笑的 If someone has a fixed smile on their face, they are smiling even though they do not feel happy or pleased. (笑容)僵硬的，呆板的,皮笑肉不笑的

  • I had to go through the rest of the evening with a fixed smile on my face.

    晚会剩下的时间里，我只好脸上挂着一副僵硬的笑容。

SYN false, fake, feigned, insincere  
4 PHRASE 无固定住所的;居无定所的 Someone who is of no fixed address, or in British English no fixed abode, does not have a permanent place to live. 无固定住所的;居无定所的

  • They are not able to get a job interview because they have no fixed address.

    因为居无定所，他们无法得到工作面试的机会。

  • He's of no fixed abode and we found him on the streets.

    他居无定所，我们发现他流落街头。
