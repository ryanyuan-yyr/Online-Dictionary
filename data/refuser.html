refuse
rɪfjuːz refjuːs ˈrɛfjus rɪˈfjuz ˈrɛfjuz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense refuses, present participle refusing, past tense , past participle refused
The verb is pronounced /rɪ'fjuːz/. The noun is pronounced /'refjuːs/ and is hyphenated ref|use. 动词读作/rɪ'fjuːz/。名词读作/'refjuːs/，分音节点位置为ref|use。
1 VERB 拒绝;回绝 If you refuse to do something, you deliberately do not do it, or you say firmly that you will not do it. 拒绝;回绝

  • He refused to comment after the trial. [VERB to-infinitive]

    庭审之后他拒绝作出评论。

  • He expects me to stay on here and I can hardly refuse. [VERB]

    他希望我继续留在这里，我很难拒绝。

2 VERB 拒绝给予;不肯给予 If someone refuses you something, they do not give it to you or do not allow you to have it. 拒绝给予;不肯给予

  • The United States has refused him a visa. [VERB noun noun]

    美国拒绝给他发放签证。

  • She was refused access to her children. [VERB noun noun]

    她被禁止探视她的孩子。

  • The town council had refused permission for the march. [VERB noun]

    市政委员会未批准这一游行示威。

SYN deny, decline, withhold, not grant  
3 VERB 拒绝（接受） If you refuse something that is offered to you, you do not accept it. 拒绝（接受）

  • He offered me a second drink which I refused. [VERB noun]

    他请我再喝一杯，我没有接受。

  • The patient has the right to refuse treatment. [VERB noun]

    病人有权利拒绝接受治疗。

4 N-UNCOUNT 废物，垃圾（主要用于官方语言） Refuse consists of the rubbish and all the things that are not wanted in a house, shop, or factory, and that are regularly thrown away; used mainly in official language. 废物，垃圾（主要用于官方语言）

  • The District Council made a weekly collection of refuse.

    区政务委员会每周收取一次垃圾。

SYN rubbish, waste, sweepings, junk [informal]  
Usage Note :

  • Do not confuse refuse and deny. If you refuse to do something, you deliberately do not do it, or you say firmly that you will not do it. ...people who refuse to change their opinions... He refused to condemn them. You can refuse something that someone offers you. The patient has the right to refuse treatment. If someone does not allow you to have something you ask for, or to do something you have asked to do, you can say that they refuse you. He can run to Dad for money if I refuse him. If you deny something, you say that it is not true. The allegation was denied by government spokesmen. If someone denies you something, they do not allow you to have it. I never denied her anything.

    不要混淆 refuse 和 deny。refuse 指有意或坚决地拒绝。如，people who refuse to change their opinions（拒绝改变观点的人），He refused to condemn them（他拒绝谴责他们）。refuse 也可以表示拒绝接受别人给你的东西：The patient has the right to refuse treatment（病人有权利拒绝接受治疗）。refuse 后面可以直接接人，表示拒绝某人的要求或拒绝某人做某事。如，He can run to Dad for money if I refuse him（如果我不给他的话，他可能跑去找爸爸要钱）。deny 指否认某事属实，如，The allegation was denied by government spokesmen（政府的发言人否认了该项指控）。deny 也指不给予，如，I never denied her anything（我对她从来都是有求必应）。
