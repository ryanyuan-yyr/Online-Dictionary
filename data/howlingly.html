howl
haʊl haʊl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense howls, present participle howling, past tense , past participle howled
1 VERB （狼、狗等）长嚎，嗥叫 If an animal such as a wolf or a dog howls, it makes a long, loud, crying sound. （狼、狗等）长嚎，嗥叫

  • Somewhere in the streets beyond a dog suddenly howled, baying at the moon. [VERB]

    远处的街上，有只狗突然对月长嚎起来。

SYN bay, cry, bark, yelp  
Howl is also a noun.

  • The dog let out a savage howl and, wheeling round, flew at him.

    那只狗发出一声凶猛的狂吠，突然转身向他扑去。

2 VERB （人因疼痛、愤怒、愁苦而）吼叫，嚎哭，哀号 If a person howls, they make a long, loud cry expressing pain, anger, or unhappiness. （人因疼痛、愤怒、愁苦而）吼叫，嚎哭，哀号

  • He howled like a wounded animal as blood spurted from the gash. [VERB]

    当血从伤口中喷出时，他像一只受伤的动物一样嚎叫起来。

  • The baby was howling for her 3am feed. [VERB for noun]

    宝宝哀号着要吃凌晨3点的那顿奶。

SYN cry, shout, bell, scream  
Howl is also a noun.

  • With a howl of rage, he grabbed the neck of a broken bottle and advanced.

    他一声怒吼，抓起一个破瓶子的瓶颈冲上前来。

3 VERB （风）呼啸，怒号 When the wind howls, it blows hard and makes a loud noise. （风）呼啸，怒号

  • The wind howled all night, but I slept a little. [VERB]

    风整夜怒号着，但我还是睡了一会儿。

  • It sank in a howling gale. [VERB-ing]

    它在呼啸的狂风中沉没了。

4 VERB （人）高声嚷，大声呼叫 If you howl something, you say it in a very loud voice. （人）高声嚷，大声呼叫 [informal]

  • 'Get away, get away, get away' he howled. [VERB with quote]

    “滚开，滚开，都滚开”他高声嚷道。

  • The crowd howled its approval. [VERB noun]

    人群高声欢呼，以示赞同。

5 VERB 放声（大笑） If you howl with laughter, you laugh very loudly. 放声（大笑）

  • Joe, Pink, and Booker howled with delight. [VERB + with]

    乔、平克和布克高兴得放声大笑。

  • The crowd howled, delirious. [VERB]

    人群笑声震天，欣喜若狂。

Howl is also a noun.

  • His stories caused howls of laughter.

    他讲的故事引起了阵阵大笑。

Phrasal verbs:
howl down
