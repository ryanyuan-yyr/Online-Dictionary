meditation
medɪteɪʃən ˌmɛdəˈteɪʃən
Word forms: plural meditations
1 N-UNCOUNT （宗教中）默想，打坐，冥想 Meditation is the act of remaining in a silent and calm state for a period of time, as part of a religious training, or so that you are more able to deal with the problems of everyday life. （宗教中）默想，打坐，冥想

  • Many busy executives have begun to practice yoga and meditation.

    许多忙碌的主管开始练习瑜伽和冥想。

  • Having chosen a meditation it is important that you stick to that meditation for, at the very least, a fortnight.

    选择了一种冥想方法后，至少要坚持练习两周时间，这一点非常重要。

2See also:transcendental meditation 
3 N-UNCOUNT 深思熟虑;苦思冥想 Meditation is the act of thinking about something very carefully and deeply for a long time. 深思熟虑;苦思冥想

  • ...the man, lost in meditation, walking with slow steps along the shore.

    这名陷入沉思中的男子，沿着岸边慢慢地走着

  • In his lonely meditations Antony had been forced to the conclusion that there had been rumours.

    独自静心想来，安东尼断定曾有流言蜚语。

SYN reflection, thought, concentration, study  
4 N-COUNT [usu N on/upon n] （关于某主题的）感想,沉思录 A meditation on a particular subject is something such as a piece of writing or a speech which expresses deep thoughts about that subject. （关于某主题的）感想,沉思录

  • In fact, the entire novel is a long meditation on childbearing and mortality.

    实际上，整部小说就是一部关于生儿育女与道德伦理的长篇沉思录。

  • The title track is a pointed meditation on a continent gone wrong.

    主打歌是对一个误入歧途的大陆的深刻沉思。
