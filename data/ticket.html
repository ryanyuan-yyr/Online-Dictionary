ticket
tɪkɪt ˈtɪkɪt
Word forms: plural tickets
1 N-COUNT [oft by NOUN] (剧院、运动场、表演等的)入场券，门票，票 A ticket is a small, official piece of paper or card which shows that you have paid to enter a place such as a theatre or a sports ground, or shows that you have paid for a journey. (剧院、运动场、表演等的)入场券，门票，票

  • I queued for two hours to get a ticket to see the football game.

    我排了两个小时的队，就是为了买张足球赛的票。

  • I love opera and last year I got tickets for Covent Garden. [+ for]

    我喜爱歌剧，去年我搞到几张科芬园皇家歌剧院的票。

  • Entrance is free, but by ticket only.

    免费入场，但是要凭票进去。

  • He became a ticket collector at Waterloo Station.

    他在滑铁卢车站当上了一名检票员。

SYN voucher, pass, coupon, card  
2 N-COUNT (交通)罚款通知单，违章通知单 A ticket is an official piece of paper which orders you to pay a fine or to appear in court because you have committed a driving or parking offence. (交通)罚款通知单，违章通知单

  • I want to know at what point I break the speed limit and get a ticket.

    我想知道我是在什么地方超速而受到处罚的。

3 N-COUNT [usually n NOUN] 兑奖券;彩票 A ticket for a game of chance such as a raffle or a lottery is a piece of paper with a number on it. If the number on your ticket matches the number chosen, you win a prize. 兑奖券;彩票

  • She bought a lottery ticket and won more than $33 million.

    她买了张彩票，中了3,300多万美元。

4 N-SING (竞选时所代表的)政党;(竞选时所支持的)纲领，政见 The particular ticket on which a person fights an election is the party they represent or the policies they support. (竞选时所代表的)政党;(竞选时所支持的)纲领，政见 [British]

  • He first ran for president on a far-left ticket.

    他第一次代表极左政党参加总统竞选。

  • She would want to fight the election on a ticket of parliamentary democracy.

    她想以实施议会民主制为纲领去参加竞选。

  • It's a ticket that was designed to appeal to suburban and small town voters.

    这一竞选政策意在吸引郊区和小城镇选民。

5 N-COUNT [usually ADJ n] (政党、团体的)候选人名单 A ticket is the list of candidates who are representing a particular political party or group in an election. (政党、团体的)候选人名单 [US]

  • He plans to remain on the Republican ticket for the November election.

    他计划继续代表共和党参加 11 月份的选举。

6 PHRASE 正是所需要的东西 If you say that something is just the ticket, you mean that it is exactly what is needed. 正是所需要的东西 [informal]

  • Young kids need all the energy and protein they can get and whole milk is just the ticket.

    儿童需要摄入足量能量和蛋白质，而全脂牛奶正好能满足这一需求。
