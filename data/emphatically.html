emphatically
ɪmfætɪkli
1 ADV-GRADED [ADVERB with verb] 强调地;加强语气地;断然地 If you say something emphatically, you say it in a forceful way which shows that you feel very strongly about what you are saying. 强调地;加强语气地;断然地

  • 'No fast food', she said emphatically.

    “不能吃快餐，”她坚决地说。

  • Mr Davies has emphatically denied the charges.

    戴维斯先生断然否认了那些控诉。

SYN forcefully, vigorously, vehemently, earnestly  
2 ADV-GRADED 肯定地;显然地 You use emphatically to emphasize the statement you are making. 肯定地;显然地 [emphasis]

  • Making people feel foolish is emphatically not my strategy.

    让人出丑显然不是我的策略。

  • Politics is most emphatically back on the agenda.

    政治问题显然重新提上了日程。
