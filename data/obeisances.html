obeisance
oʊbeɪsəns oʊˈbeɪsəns oʊˈbisəns
Word forms: plural obeisances
1 N-UNCOUNT 敬意；尊敬；敬重 Obeisance to someone or something is great respect shown for them. 敬意；尊敬；敬重 [formal]

  • While he was still young and strong all paid obeisance to him. [+ to]

    他年轻力壮时所有人都对他毕恭毕敬。

2 N-VAR 敬礼；（尤指）鞠躬 An obeisance is a physical gesture, especially a bow, that you make in order to show your respect for someone or something. 敬礼；（尤指）鞠躬 [formal]

  • One by one they came forward, mumbled grudging words of welcome, made awkward obeisances.

    他们挨个儿走上前去，勉强地咕哝出几句欢迎词，生硬地鞠躬致礼。
