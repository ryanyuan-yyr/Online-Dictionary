theorize
θiːəraɪz ˈθiəˌraɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense theorizes, present participle theorizing, past tense , past participle theorized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 theorise
VERB 从理论上说明;建立理论 If you theorize that something is true or theorize about it, you develop an abstract idea or set of ideas about something in order to explain it. 从理论上说明;建立理论 [Also VERB]

  • Police are theorizing that the killers may be posing as hitchhikers. [VERB that]

    警方推测那些杀手可能会假装成搭便车的人。

  • By studying the way people behave, we can theorize about what is going on in their mind. [VERB + about]

    通过研究人们的行为方式，我们可以从中推断他们内心的想法。

SYN speculate, conjecture, hypothesize, project  
theorizing

  • This was no time for theorizing.

    现在不是谈理论的时候。
