disown
dɪsoʊn dɪsˈoʊn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense disowns, present participle disowning, past tense , past participle disowned
VERB 与…断绝关系;否认对…的责任;否认…是自己的 If you disown someone or something, you say or show that you no longer want to have any connection with them or any responsibility for them. 与…断绝关系;否认对…的责任;否认…是自己的

  • The man who murdered the girl is no son of mine. I disown him. [VERB noun]

    杀害那女孩的人不配做我的儿子。我要与他脱离关系。

  • Those comments were later disowned by an official army spokesman. [VERB noun]

    这些话随后遭到了军方一位官方发言人的否认。

SYN deny, reject, abandon, renounce
