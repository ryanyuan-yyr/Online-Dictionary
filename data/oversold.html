oversell
oʊvəʳsel ˌoʊvərˈsɛl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense oversells, present participle overselling, past tense , past participle oversold
VERB 过分吹嘘；过分宣扬 If you say that something or someone is oversold, you mean that people say they are better or more useful than they really are. 过分吹嘘；过分宣扬

  • The couple idea is certainly oversold. There's so much pressure to become a couple that people feel failure if they don't conform. [be VERB-ed]

    结婚的观念无疑被过分渲染了。人们面临来自各方的结婚压力，不结婚对他们来说就是一种失败。

  • I think the reformers have at times oversold the reforms. [VERB noun]

    我认为改革者有时过分吹嘘了改革的好处。
