lie around
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 lie about
1 PHRASAL VERB 到处乱扔乱放 If things are left lying around or lying about, they are not tidied away but left casually somewhere where they can be seen. 到处乱扔乱放

  • People should be careful about their possessions and not leave them lying around. [VERB PREP.]

    人们应该小心自己的财物，不要随便乱放。

  • My dad had a couple of Bob Dylan and Beatles songbooks lying around the house. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我爸爸有几本鲍勃·迪伦和披头士乐队的歌集不知道扔家里什么地方了。

2 PHRASAL VERB 闲着；不干事 If you lie around or lie about, you spend your time relaxing and being lazy. 闲着；不干事 [informal]

  • I'll just lie around in the sun. [VERB PREP.]

    我会什么都不干，就只悠闲地晒晒太阳。

  • On Sunday Cohen lay around the house all day. [VERB PREP. noun]

    科恩星期天一整天都在家无所事事。
