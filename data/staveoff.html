stave off
PHRASAL VERB 推迟;暂时避开 If you stave off something bad, or if you stave it off, you succeed in stopping it happening for a while. 推迟;暂时避开

  • In a desperate attempt to stave off defeat, he reluctantly promised wholesale reform of the constitution. [VERB PREP. noun]

    为了竭力避免败局，他勉强答应要对宪法进行大规模修改。

  • But the reality of discovery was a different matter, and he did all he could to stave it off. [VERB noun PREP.]

    但所发现的实际情况完全是另一回事，所以他只好尽自己所能暂缓其发生。
