seem
siːm sim
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense seems, present participle seeming, past tense , past participle seemed
1 V-LINK [no cont] 好像;似乎;看来 You use seem to say that someone or something gives the impression of having a particular quality, or of happening in the way you describe. 好像;似乎;看来

  • We heard a series of explosions. They seemed quite close by. [VERB adj.ive]

    我们听到一连串的爆炸声，似乎就在附近。

  • Everyone seems busy except us. [VERB adj.ive]

    除了我们，大家好像都很忙碌。

  • To everyone who knew them, they seemed an ideal couple. [VERB noun]

    在每个认识他们的人看来，他们似乎是天作之合。

  • £50 seems a lot to pay. [VERB noun]

    要支付 50 英镑，似乎太贵了。

  • The calming effect seemed to last for about ten minutes. [VERB to-infinitive]

    镇静效果好像持续了大约 10 分钟。

  • The government seems not to be troubled by its inconsistent policies on minority rights. [VERB to-infinitive]

    政府好像并不觉得其在少数民族权利问题上政策的前后不一有什么不妥。

  • It was a record that seemed beyond reach. [VERB prep.]

    这似乎是一个不可企及的纪录。

  • The proposal seems designed to break opposition to the government's economic programme. [VERB-ed]

    这项建议似乎旨在为政府经济计划的实施扫清障碍。

  • It seems that the attack this morning was very carefully planned to cause few casualties. [VERB that]

    今天早上的攻击似乎是经过精心策划、想要尽量减少伤亡的。

  • It seems clear that he has no reasonable alternative. [V adj that]

    他没有其他合适的选择，这一点似乎很清楚。

  • It seemed as if she'd been gone forever. [V as if]

    好像她已经永远地离开了。

  • There seems to be a lot of support in Congress for this move. [VERB to-infinitive]

    国会里好像有很多人支持这一举措。

  • There seems no possibility that such action can be averted. [VERB noun]

    此类行为似乎无法避免。

  • This phenomenon is not as outrageous as it seems. [VERB]

    这种现象并不像它看上去那样骇人。

SYN appear, give the impression of being, look, sound  
2 V-LINK [no cont] (在描述自己的感觉、想法、经历时用于缓和语气)好像，似乎 You use seem when you are describing your own feelings or thoughts, or describing something that has happened to you, in order to make your statement less forceful. (在描述自己的感觉、想法、经历时用于缓和语气)好像，似乎 [vagueness]

  • I seem to have lost all my self-confidence. [VERB to-infinitive]

    我好像已经自信全无。

  • I seem to remember giving you very precise instructions. [VERB to-infinitive]

    我好像记得给过你非常明确的指示。

  • I seemed to have contracted the stomach problem. [VERB to-infinitive]

    我好像得了胃病。

  • Excuse me I seem to be a little bit lost. [VERB to-infinitive]

    对不起，我好像有点听不明白了。

3 PHRASE 似乎不能;好像无法 If you say that you cannot seem or could not seem to do something, you mean that you have tried to do it and were unable to. 似乎不能;好像无法

  • No matter how hard I try I cannot seem to catch up on all the bills.

    无论怎么努力，我好像就是无法付清所有的账单。

  • Kim's mother couldn't seem to stop crying.

    金的母亲似乎要哭个没完。
