huff
hʌf hʌf
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense huffs, present participle huffing, past tense , past participle huffed
1 VERB 生气;气恼;恼怒地说 If you huff, you indicate that you are annoyed or offended about something, usually by the way that you say something. 生气;气恼;恼怒地说

  • 'This', huffed Mr Buthelezi, 'was discrimination.' [VERB with quote]

    “这个，”布特莱齐先生气愤地说，“是歧视。”

SYN puff, blow, exhale  
2 PHRASE 生气;气恼;怒气冲冲 If someone is in a huff, they are behaving in a bad-tempered way because they are annoyed and offended. 生气;气恼;怒气冲冲 [informal]

  • After the row in a pub he drove off in a huff.

    在酒吧里吵了一架后，他气鼓鼓地开车走了。

SYN offended, hurt, angered, provoked  
3 PHRASE [Vs inflect] 愤愤不满;生闷气 If someone huffs and puffs, they express their annoyance or dissatisfaction with a decision or situation loudly but do not do anything to change it. 愤愤不满;生闷气

  • The British government huffed and puffed at the commission's decision.

    英国政府对委员会的决定愤愤不满。
