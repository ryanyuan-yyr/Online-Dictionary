bequeath
bɪkwiːð biˈkwið biˈkwiθ bɪˈkwið bɪˈkwiθ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense bequeaths, present participle bequeathing, past tense , past participle bequeathed
1 VERB 遗赠 If you bequeath your money or property to someone, you legally state that they should have it when you die. 遗赠 [formal]

  • Fields's will bequeathed his wife Hattie and son Claude the sum of twenty thousand dollars. [VERB noun noun]

    菲尔茨将遗赠给他妻子哈蒂和儿子克劳德共计两万美元。

  • He bequeathed all his silver to his children. [VERB noun + to]

    他把他所有的钱财都留给了孩子。

SYN leave, will, give, grant  
2 VERB 留下；把…传下去 If you bequeath an idea or system, you leave it for other people to use or develop. 留下；把…传下去 [formal] [Also VERB n]

  • He bequeaths his successor an economy that is doing quite well. [VERB noun noun]

    他给继任者留下了发展势头非常不错的经济。

  • It is true that colonialism did not bequeath much to Africa. [VERB noun + to]

    殖民主义给非洲确实没有留下多少东西。

SYN give, offer, accord, grant
