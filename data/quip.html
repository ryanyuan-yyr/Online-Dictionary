quip
kwɪp kwɪp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense quips, present participle quipping, past tense , past participle quipped
1 N-COUNT 俏皮话;谐语;妙语 A quip is a remark that is intended to be amusing or clever. 俏皮话;谐语;妙语 [written]

  • The commentators make endless quips about the female players' appearance. [+ about]

    评论员们没完没了地拿女选手的容貌打趣。

SYN joke, sally, jest, riposte  
2 VERB 说俏皮话;口出妙语 To quip means to say something that is intended to be amusing or clever. 说俏皮话;口出妙语 [written]

  • 'He'll have to go on a diet,' Ballard quipped. [VERB with quote]

    “他得减肥啦，”巴拉德俏皮地说。

  • The chairman quipped that he would rather sell his airline than his computer systems. [VERB that]

    主席风趣地说，他宁可卖掉他的航空公司，也不愿出售他的计算机系统。

SYN joke, jest, wisecrack [informal] , pun
