lay down
1 PHRASAL VERB （通常指用完后）放下， 搁下 If you lay something down, you put it down, usually because you have finished using it. （通常指用完后）放下， 搁下

  • Daniel finished the article and laid the newspaper down on his desk. [VERB noun PREP.]

    丹尼尔看完文章后把报纸放在他的书桌上。

  • She laid down her knife and fork and pushed her plate away. [VERB PREP. noun]

    她放下刀叉，推开盘子。

2 PHRASAL VERB 规定；制定；颁布 If rules or people in authority lay down what people should do or must do, they officially state what they should or must do. 规定；制定；颁布

  • The Companies Act lays down a set of minimum requirements. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    《公司法》规定了一系列最低标准。

  • Taxis must conform to the rigorous standards laid down by the police. [VERB PREP. noun]

    出租车必须遵守警方的严格规定。

SYN stipulate, state, establish, prescribe  
3 PHRASAL VERB 放下（武器） If someone lays down their weapons, they stop fighting a battle or war and make peace. 放下（武器）

  • The drug-traffickers have offered to lay down their arms. [VERB PREP. noun]

    毒贩们已同意放下武器。

SYN sacrifice, give up, yield, surrender
