barrack
bærək ˈbærək ˈbɛrək
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense barracks, present participle barracking, past tense , past participle barracked
1 N-COUNT [oft in names] 兵营；营房 A barracks is a building or group of buildings where soldiers or other members of the armed forces live and work. 'Barracks' is the singular and plural form. 兵营；营房

  • ...an army barracks in the north of the city.

    城北部的军营

  • ...the parade grounds of Wellington Barracks.

    威灵顿兵营的阅兵场

2 VERB 向…起哄；给…喝倒彩 If people in an audience barrack public speakers or performers, they interrupt them, for example by making rude remarks. 向…起哄；给…喝倒彩 [British]

  • Fans gained more enjoyment barracking him than cheering on the team. [VERB noun]

    球迷一个劲儿地嘘他，兴头远胜于为球队加油。

  • President Alan Garcia was barracked by the right wing opposition. [VERB noun]

    阿兰·加西亚总统遭右翼反对派喝倒彩。

SYN heckle, abuse, mock, bait  
barracking

  • He was affected badly by the barracking that he got from the crowd.

    人群中的一片嘘声令他大受影响。
