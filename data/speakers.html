speaker
spiːkəʳ ˈspikər
Word forms: plural speakers
1 N-COUNT 发言人;演讲者;演说家 A speaker at a meeting, conference, or other gathering is a person who is making a speech or giving a talk. 发言人;演讲者;演说家

  • Among the speakers at the gathering was Treasury Secretary Nicholas Brady.

    与会的发言人中有财政部长尼古拉斯·布莱迪。

  • Bruce Wyatt will be the guest speaker at next month's meeting.

    布鲁斯·怀亚特将成为下月会议的特邀发言人。

  • He was not a good speaker.

    他不擅长演讲。

SYN orator, public speaker, lecturer, spokesperson  
2 N-COUNT [n NOUN] 讲某种语言的人 A speaker of a particular language is a person who speaks it, especially one who speaks it as their first language. 讲某种语言的人

  • ...in the Ukraine, where a fifth of the population are Russian speakers.

    在乌克兰，有五分之一人口说俄语

  • The Department has a growing section which teaches English to speakers of other languages. [+ of]

    这个系有一个日益壮大的教研组负责向操其他语言的人教授英语。

3See also:native speaker 
4 N-PROPER; N-VOC (议会或立法机构的)议长 In the parliament or legislature of many countries, the Speaker is the person who is in charge of meetings. (议会或立法机构的)议长

  • For twenty minutes, the Speaker tried to keep order.

    20 分钟的时间里，议长一直在竭力维持秩序。

  • ...the Speaker of the Polish Parliament.

    波兰议会的议长

  • Mr. Speaker, our message to the president is simple.

    议长先生，我们向总统表达的意思非常简单。

5 N-COUNT 说话人 A speaker is a person who is speaking. 说话人

  • From a simple gesture or the speaker's tone of voice, the Japanese listener gleans the whole meaning.

    仅从一个简单的手势或者说话人的语气里，日本人就能够领悟到所有的意思。

6 N-COUNT 扬声器;喇叭 A speaker is a piece of electrical equipment, for example part of a radio or set of equipment for playing CDs or tapes, through which sound comes out. 扬声器;喇叭

  • For a good stereo effect, the speakers should not be too wide apart.

    为了达到理想的立体声效果，扬声器之间的距离不能太远。
