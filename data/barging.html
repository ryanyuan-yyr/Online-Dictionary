barge
bɑːʳdʒ bɑrdʒ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense barges, present participle barging, past tense , past participle barged
1 N-COUNT [oft by NOUN] （尤指运河上载货用的）大型平底船，驳船 A barge is a long, narrow boat with a flat bottom. Barges are used for carrying heavy loads, especially on canals. （尤指运河上载货用的）大型平底船，驳船

  • Carrying goods by train costs nearly three times more than carrying them by barge.

    通过铁路运货的成本比驳船运货成本高出近3倍。

SYN canal boat, lighter, narrow boat, scow  
2 VERB 硬闯；闯入 If you barge into a place or barge through it, you rush or push into it in a rough and rude way. 硬闯；闯入 [informal] [Also V + through]

  • Students tried to barge into the secretariat buildings. [VERB + into]

    学生们试图闯进秘书处的办公大楼。

  • The FBI barged through the gates, demanding to search the plant for evidence. [V into/through n]

    联邦调查局的探员破门而入，要求在工厂内搜寻证据。

SYN force, break, push, blast  
3 VERB 鲁莽地撞；大力冲撞 If you barge into someone or barge past them, you bump against them roughly and rudely. 鲁莽地撞；大力冲撞 [informal] [Also V + into]

  • He would barge into them and kick them in the shins. [V into/past n]

    他会向他们冲过去，踹他们的小腿肚子。

  • He barged past her and sprang at Gillian, knocking her to the floor. [V + past]

    他从她身边冲过去扑向吉利恩时，将其撞倒在地。
