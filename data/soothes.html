soothe
suːð suð
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense soothes, present participle soothing, past tense , past participle soothed
1 VERB 安抚;抚慰;使平静 If you soothe someone who is angry or upset, you make them feel calmer. 安抚;抚慰;使平静

  • He would take her in his arms and soothe her. [VERB noun]

    他会把她揽在怀中抚慰她。

  • It did not take long for the central bank to soothe investors' fears. [VERB noun]

    中央银行很快便消除了投资者的担忧。

SYN calm, still, quiet, hush  
soothing

  • Put on some nice soothing music.

    播放一些柔和舒缓的音乐。

  • His casual, relaxed manner was very soothing.

    他随意而放松的举动让人很快便平静下来。

soothingly

  • 'Now don't you worry,' she said soothingly.

    “现在你就别担心了，”她的语气里充满了抚慰。

2 VERB 减轻，缓和(身体的痛处或不适) Something that soothes a part of your body where there is pain or discomfort makes the pain or discomfort less severe. 减轻，缓和(身体的痛处或不适)

  • ...body lotion to soothe dry skin. [VERB noun]

    滋润干燥皮肤的润肤液

soothing

  • Cold tea is very soothing for burns.

    凉的茶水非常有助于减轻烧伤灼痛。
