debilitate
dɪbɪlɪteɪt diˈbɪləˌteɪt dɪˈbɪləˌteɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense debilitates, present participle debilitating, past tense , past participle debilitated
1 VERB [usually passive] 使虚弱;使衰弱 If you are debilitated by something such as an illness, it causes your body or mind to become gradually weaker. 使虚弱;使衰弱 [formal]

  • Stewart took over yesterday when Russell was debilitated by a stomach virus. [be VERB-ed + by]

    昨天拉塞尔因肠胃病毒感染身体不适，由斯图尔特暂时接手。

debilitating

  • ...a debilitating illness.

    导致身体虚弱的疾病

debilitated

  • Occasionally a patient is so debilitated that he must be fed intravenously.

    偶尔会有病人过于虚弱，必须通过静脉注射进食。

2 VERB 削弱（组织、社团或政府）的力量 To debilitate an organization, society, or government means to gradually make it weaker. 削弱（组织、社团或政府）的力量 [formal]

  • ...their efforts to debilitate the political will of the Western alliance. [VERB noun]

    他们试图削弱西方联盟的政治决心的努力

debilitating

  • ...years of debilitating economic crisis.

    多年来拖累经济的经济危机

debilitated

  • ...the debilitated ruling party.

    力量遭到削弱的执政党
