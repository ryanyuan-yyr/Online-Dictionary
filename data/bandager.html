bandage
bændɪdʒ ˈbændɪdʒ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense bandages, present participle bandaging, past tense , past participle bandaged
1 N-COUNT 绷带 A bandage is a long strip of cloth which is wrapped around a wounded part of someone's body to protect or support it. 绷带

  • We put some ointment and a bandage on his knee.

    我们在他的膝盖上涂了些药膏并打上了绷带。

  • His chest was swathed in bandages.

    他的胸部缠着绷带。

SYN dressing, plaster, sling, compress  
2 VERB 用绷带包扎 If you bandage a wound or part of someone's body, you tie a bandage around it. 用绷带包扎 [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • Apply a dressing to the wound and bandage it. [VERB noun]

    给伤口盖上敷料并用绷带包扎起来。

  • ...a bandaged hand. [VERB-ed]

    缠着绷带的手

SYN dress, cover, bind, wrap  
Bandage up means the same as bandage .bandage up同bandage

  • I bandaged the leg up and gave her aspirin for the pain.

    我把她的腿用绷带包扎起来，并给了她阿司匹林止痛。
