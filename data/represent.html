represent
reprɪzent ˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense represents, present participle representing, past tense , past participle represented
1 VERB 代理，代表（个人或团体） If someone such as a lawyer or a politician represents a person or group of people, they act on behalf of that person or group. 代理，代表（个人或团体）

  • ...the politicians we elect to represent us. [VERB noun]

    我们选举出来代表我们的政界人士

  • The offer has yet to be accepted by the lawyers representing the victims. [VERB noun]

    受害者的代理律师还没有接受这个提议。

2 VERB 代表（个人或团体）出席 If you represent a person or group at an official event, you go there on their behalf. 代表（个人或团体）出席

  • The general secretary may represent the president at official ceremonies. [VERB noun]

    总书记可以在一些官方仪式上代表主席。

SYN stand for, substitute for, play the part of, assume the role of  
3 VERB (在竞赛或体育赛事中)代表（国家或地区） If you represent your country or town in a competition or sports event, you take part in it on behalf of the country or town where you live. (在竞赛或体育赛事中)代表（国家或地区）

  • My only aim is to represent Britain at the Olympics. [VERB noun]

    我唯一的目标是代表英国参加奥运会。

SYN play for, appear for, be a member of the team  
4 V-PASSIVE 使有代表性 If a group of people or things is well represented in a particular activity or in a particular place, a lot of them can be found there. 使有代表性

  • Women are already well represented in the area of TV drama. [be adv V-ed]

    女性在电视剧领域占据了一席之地。

  • In New Mexico all kinds of cuisines are represented. [be VERB-ed]

    在新墨西哥州， 有各种各样的烹饪方法。

5 V-LINK 相当于，代表着（变化、成就、胜利等） If you say that something represents a change, achievement, or victory, you mean that it is a change, achievement, or victory. 相当于，代表着（变化、成就、胜利等） [formal, or written]

  • The pieces on view are not grouped around any one theme, but rather represent superb examples from various periods. [VERB noun]

    展出的作品并非围绕着一个主题，而是不同时期的经典之作。

  • These developments represented a major change in the established order. [VERB noun]

    这些发展代表了现有秩序的重大转变。

6 VERB [no cont] (标志或符号)意味着，代表着，标志着 If a sign or symbol represents something, it is accepted as meaning that thing. (标志或符号)意味着，代表着，标志着

  • ...a black dot in the middle of the circle is supposed to represent the source of the radiation. [VERB noun]

    圆圈中心的黑点应该是表示放射源。

7 VERB [no cont, no passive] 象征;体现;反映 To represent an idea or quality means to be a symbol or an expression of that idea or quality. 象征;体现;反映

  • We believe you represent everything British racing needs. [VERB noun]

    我们相信你代表了英国赛马所需要的一切。

SYN exemplify, embody, symbolize, typify  
8 VERB 描绘;描写 If you represent a person or thing as a particular thing, you describe them as being that thing. 描绘;描写

  • The popular press tends to represent him as an environmental guru. [VERB noun + as]

    大众媒体往往把他描绘成环保领袖。
