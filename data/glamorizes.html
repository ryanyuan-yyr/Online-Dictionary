glamorize
glæməraɪz ˈglæmərˌaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense glamorizes, present participle glamorizing, past tense , past participle glamorized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 glamorise
VERB 使有魅力；美化 If someone glamorizes something, they make it look or seem more attractive than it really is, especially in a film, book, or programme. 使有魅力；美化 [disapproval]

  • Filmmakers have often been accused of glamorizing organized crime. [VERB noun]

    电影制片人常常被指责美化了集团犯罪。

  • ...a glamorised view of the past. [VERB-ed]

    被美化的历史
