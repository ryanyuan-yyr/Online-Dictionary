reel
riːl rɪəl ril
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense reels, present participle reeling, past tense , past participle reeled
1 N-COUNT 卷轴;卷筒 A reel is a cylindrical object around which you wrap something such as cinema film, magnetic tape, fishing line, or cotton thread. 卷轴;卷筒 [mainly British]

  • ...a 30m reel of cable. [+ of]

    一卷30米长的电缆

in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 spool
2 N-COUNT [usually with supplement] 电影胶片 You can talk about a reel as a way of referring to all the scenes in a film which fit onto one reel of film. 电影胶片

  • I shall not reveal the movie's final reel.

    我不会透露该片的结局。

3 VERB 蹒跚;摇摆;踉跄 If someone reels, they move about in an unsteady way as if they are going to fall. 蹒跚;摇摆;踉跄

  • He was reeling a little. He must be very drunk. [VERB]

    他走起来有点儿摇晃，一定是喝多了。

  • He lost his balance and reeled back. [VERB adv./prep.]

    他身体失去了平衡，向后一个趔趄。

  • I stood up and almost fell, reeling against the deck rail. [VERB adv./prep.]

    我站了起来，几乎跌倒，身体摇晃着靠在甲板的围栏上。

SYN stagger, rock, roll, pitch  
4 VERB [usually cont] 感到震惊;感到心烦 If you are reeling from a shock, you are feeling extremely surprised or upset because of it. 感到震惊;感到心烦

  • I'm still reeling from the shock of hearing of it. [VERB + from]

    我听了此事后现在仍然震惊不已。

  • It left us reeling with disbelief. [VERB prep.]

    它让我们感到心烦意乱，难以置信。

SYN be shaken, be shocked, be stunned, be staggered  
5 VERB 混乱;眩晕 If you say that your brain or your mind is reeling, you mean that you are very confused because you have too many things to think about. 混乱;眩晕

  • His mind reeled at the question. [VERB + at]

    这个问题让他大脑一片混乱。

6 N-COUNT 里尔舞（一种快节奏的苏格兰舞，或快节奏的乡村舞） A reel is a type of fast Scottish dance, or fast country dance. 里尔舞（一种快节奏的苏格兰舞，或快节奏的乡村舞）
