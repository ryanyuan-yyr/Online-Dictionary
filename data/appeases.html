appease
əpiːz əˈpiz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense appeases, present participle appeasing, past tense appeased
VERB 平息;安抚;抚慰 If you try to appease someone, you try to stop them from being angry by giving them what they want. 平息;安抚;抚慰 [disapproval]

  • Gandhi was accused by some of trying to appease both factions of the electorate. [VERB noun]

    一些人指责甘地试图安抚两派选民。

  • The offer has not appeased separatists. [VERB noun]

    该提议未能平抚分裂分子。

SYN pacify, satisfy, calm, soothe
