invigorate
ɪnvɪgəreɪt ɪnˈvɪgərˌeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense invigorates, present participle invigorating, past tense , past participle invigorated
1 VERB 使精神振奋;使精力充沛;使活跃 If something invigorates you, it makes you feel more energetic. 使精神振奋;使精力充沛;使活跃

  • Take a deep breath in to invigorate you. [VERB noun]

    深吸一口气来提提神。

SYN refresh, stimulate, brace, strengthen  
invigorated

  • She seemed invigorated, full of life and energy.

    她似乎精神焕发,充满生机与活力。

2 VERB 激励;刺激;振兴 To invigorate a situation or a process means to make it more efficient or more effective. 激励;刺激;振兴

  • ...the promise that they would invigorate the economy. [VERB noun]

    他们许下的振兴经济的承诺

  • The tactic could well help invigorate a struggling campaign. [VERB noun]

    该策略会非常有助于促进处境艰难的竞选活动。
