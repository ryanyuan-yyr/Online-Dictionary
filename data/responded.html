respond
rɪspɒnd rɪˈspɒnd rɪˈspɑnd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense responds, present participle responding, past tense , past participle responded
1 VERB 回复;回答;回应 When you respond to something that is done or said, you react to it by doing or saying something yourself. 回复;回答;回应

  • They are likely to respond positively to the President's request for aid. [VERB + to]

    他们可能会对总统的援助要求作出积极回应。

  • The army responded with gunfire and tear gas. [VERB + with]

    军队用炮火和催泪瓦斯予以反击。

  • 'Are you well enough to carry on?' — 'Of course,' she responded scornfully. [VERB with quote]

    “你的身体状况可以继续工作吗？”—“当然，”她不屑地回答道。

  • The Belgian Minister of Foreign Affairs responded that the protection of refugees was a matter for an international organization. [VERB that]

    比利时外交大臣回应说，保护难民是国际组织的事情。

SYN answer, return, reply, come back  
2 VERB 应对;作出回应;作出反应 When you respond to a need, crisis, or challenge, you take the necessary or appropriate action. 应对;作出回应;作出反应

  • This modest group size allows our teachers to respond to the needs of each student. [VERB + to]

    这样的小组规模适中，可以使老师照顾到每一个学生的需求。

3 VERB 有良好反应;有积极效果 If a patient or their injury or illness is responding to treatment, the treatment is working and they are getting better. 有良好反应;有积极效果

  • I'm pleased to say that he is now doing well and responding to treatment. [VERB + to]

    我很高兴地说，他现在恢复得不错，治疗取得了积极的效果。
