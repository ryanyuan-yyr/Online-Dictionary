chronicle
krɒnɪkəl ˈkrɑnɪkəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense chronicles, present participle chronicling, past tense , past participle chronicled
1 VERB 按时间顺序记述(或播放) To chronicle a series of events means to write about them or show them in broadcasts in the order in which they happened. 按时间顺序记述(或播放) [Also VERB wh]

  • The series chronicles the everyday adventures of two eternal bachelors. [VERB noun]

    这个系列剧按照时间顺序记述了两个老光棍每天的奇遇。

SYN record, tell, report, enter  
chronicler

  • ...the chronicler of the English civil war.

    记述英国内战的编年史家

2 N-COUNT 编年(史);大事年表 A chronicle is an account or record of a series of events. 编年(史);大事年表

  • ...this vast chronicle of Napoleonic times. [+ of]

    这套大部头的拿破仑时代编年史

SYN record, story, history, account  
3 N-IN-NAMES (有时用于报纸名称中) Chronicle is sometimes used as part of the name of a newspaper. (有时用于报纸名称中)

  • ...the San Francisco Chronicle.

    《旧金山纪事报》
