point out
1 PHRASAL VERB 指点；指明 If you point out an object or place, you make people look at it or show them where it is. 指点；指明

  • They kept standing up to take pictures and point things out to each other. [VERB noun PREP.]

    他们不停地站起来拍照，还互相指点景物给对方看。

  • They'd already driven along the wharf so that she could point out her father's boat. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他们已经开车沿码头走了一遍，好让她指出哪艘是她父亲的船。

2 PHRASAL VERB 指出，说出（事实或错误） If you point out a fact or mistake, you tell someone about it or draw their attention to it. 指出，说出（事实或错误） [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Critics point out that the prince, on his income, should be paying tax. [VERB PREP. that]

    批评家指出王子的收入应该纳税。

  • I should point out that these estimates cover just the hospital expenditures. [VERB PREP. that]

    我应该指出，这些估算仅包括医院的费用。

  • We all too easily point out our mothers' failings. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我们都太过轻易地指出母亲的缺点。
