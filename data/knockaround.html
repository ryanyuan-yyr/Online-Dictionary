knock around
in AM, use 美国英语用 knock about
1 PHRASAL VERB 接连打（或踢） If someone knocks you around or knocks you about, they hit or kick you several times. 接连打（或踢） [mainly British, informal]

  • He lied to me constantly and started knocking me around. [VERB noun PREP.]

    他经常对我撒谎而且开始打我。

2 PHRASAL VERB 闲逛；漫游；漂泊；游荡 If someone knocks around or knocks about somewhere, they spend time there, experiencing different situations or just passing time. 闲逛；漫游；漂泊；游荡

  • ...reporters who knock around in troubled parts of the world. [VERB PREP. prep./adv.]

    穿梭于世界动乱地区的记者们

  • They knock around on weekends in grubby sweaters and pants. [VERB PREP.]

    他们周末穿着邋遢的毛衫和裤子到处闲逛。

  • I know nothing about him except that he knocked about South Africa for a while. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我除了知道他在南非流浪过一段时间外对他一无所知。

3 PHRASAL VERB [only cont] 在场;存在 If someone or something is knocking around or knocking about, they are present in a particular place. 在场;存在 [mainly British]

  • There were a couple of decent kits knocking around, but this wasn't one of them!. [VERB PREP.]

    这里有几套像样的装备，但这套不是！

  • His paintings look as if they have been knocking around for centuries. [VERB PREP.]

    他的画看上去就像是已经存在几百年了。

4 PHR-V-RECIP 常与某人交往（或作伴） If you knock around or knock about with someone, you spend your spare time with them, either because you are one of their friends or because you are their boyfriend or girlfriend. 常与某人交往（或作伴） [mainly British]

  • I used to knock about with all the lads from round where Mum lives. [VERB PREP. + with]

    我过去常同妈妈住所周围的伙伴们一起玩儿。

  • They were knocking around together for about a year. [V P together]

    他们交往大概有一年了。
