target
tɑːʳgɪt ˈtɑrgɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense targets, present participle targeting, present participle targetting, past tense , past participle targeted, past tense , past participle targetted
1 N-COUNT 靶子;目标;对象 A target is something at which someone is aiming a weapon or other object. 靶子;目标;对象

  • The village lies beside a main road, making it an easy target for bandits.

    村子坐落在大路边上，很容易成为土匪袭击的对象。

  • The missiles missed their target.

    导弹没有击中目标。

  • He missed the target only once yesterday.

    昨天他只有一次脱靶。

  • We threw knives at targets.

    我们往靶子上掷飞刀。

SYN mark, goal, bull's-eye  
2 N-COUNT 目标;目的 A target is a result that you are trying to achieve. 目标;目的

  • He's won back his place too late to achieve his target of 20 goals this season. [+ of]

    他赢回自己的位置时为时已晚，无法实现本赛季进 20 粒球的目标。

  • ...school leavers who failed to reach their target grades.

    未能达到规定分数的肄业生

SYN goal, aim, objective, end  
3 VERB 攻击;批评;以…为攻击目标 To target a particular person or thing means to decide to attack or criticize them. 攻击;批评;以…为攻击目标 [Also + for]

  • Republicans targeted Unsoeld as vulnerable in her bid for reelection this year. [VERB noun]

    共和党人将安索尔德定为攻击目标，认为她在争取今年连任的竞选中不堪一击。

  • He targets the economy as the root cause of the deteriorating law and order situation. [VERB noun]

    他抨击经济是治安状况不断恶化的根源。

Target is also a noun.

  • In the past they have been the target of racist abuse.

    过去，他们一直是种族虐待的对象。

  • The professor has been a frequent target for animal rights extremists.

    那位教授屡屡受到极端动物保护主义者的抨击。

4 VERB 瞄准;面向;尽力吸引 If you target a particular group of people, you try to appeal to those people or affect them. 瞄准;面向;尽力吸引

  • The campaign will target American insurance companies. [VERB noun]

    这场活动将以美国各保险公司为目标。

  • The company has targeted adults as its primary customers. [VERB noun]

    这家公司将成年人作为其主要顾客。

SYN choose, select, single out, earmark  
Target is also a noun.

  • Yuppies are a prime target group for marketing strategies.

    雅皮士是市场营销策略面向的主要目标群体。

5 PHRASE 进展顺利;有可能实现目标 If someone or something is on target, they are making good progress and are likely to achieve the result that is wanted. 进展顺利;有可能实现目标

  • We were still right on target for our deadline. [+ for]

    我们仍然有可能赶在最后期限前达成目标。

SYN on schedule, on time, on course, on track
