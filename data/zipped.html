zip
zɪp zɪp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense zips, present participle zipping, past tense , past participle zipped
1 N-COUNT 拉锁;拉链 A zip or zip fastener is a device used to open and close parts of clothes and bags. It consists of two rows of metal or plastic teeth which separate or fasten together as you pull a small tag along them. 拉锁;拉链 [mainly British]

  • He pulled the zip of his leather jacket down slightly. [+ of]

    他稍稍往下拉了拉皮夹克的拉链。

in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 zipper
2 VERB 拉上…的拉链 When you zip something, you fasten it using a zip. 拉上…的拉链

  • She zipped her jeans. [VERB noun]

    她拉上了牛仔裤的拉链。

  • I slowly zipped and locked the heavy black nylon bags. [VERB noun]

    我慢慢地把那些沉重的黑色尼龙袋的拉链拉上并锁好。

3 VERB 压缩（文档） To zip a computer file means to compress it so that it needs less space for storage on disk and can be transmitted more quickly. 压缩（文档） [computing] [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]
Zip up means the same as zip .zip up 同 zip

  • These files have been zipped up to take up less disk space.

    这些文档已经进行了压缩，以节省磁盘空间。

4 VERB 快速移动；飞速行动 If you say that something or someone zips somewhere, you mean that they move there very quickly. 快速移动；飞速行动 [informal]

  • My craft zipped across the bay. [VERB prep./adv.]

    我的小艇飞速驶过海湾。

  • Max zips back and forth across the living room. [VERB prep./adv.]

    马克斯在客厅里快速地来回走动。

SYN speed, shoot, fly, tear  
5 N-UNCOUNT 精力;活力;热情 If you say that someone or something has zip, you mean that they show a lot of energy and enthusiasm. 精力;活力;热情 [informal]

  • Share options will play a bigger role in future, putting zip into the performance of middle managers.

    认股权在未来会发挥更大的作用，将激发中层管理者的工作热情，使其有更出色的表现。

  • Tommy Tune gives the choreography his usual class and zip.

    汤米·图恩在编排的舞蹈中注入其一贯的优雅和活力。

SYN energy, go [informal] , life, drive
