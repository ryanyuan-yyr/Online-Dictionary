obtrude
ɒbtruːd əbˈtrud ɑbˈtrud
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense obtrudes, present participle obtruding, past tense , past participle obtruded
1 V-ERG （使）强行闯入；（使）搅扰；（使）打扰 When something obtrudes or when you obtrude it, it becomes noticeable in an undesirable way. （使）强行闯入；（使）搅扰；（使）打扰 [literary]

  • A 40 watt bulb would be quite sufficient and would not obtrude. [VERB]

    40瓦的灯泡完全够亮了，并且不会晃眼。

  • Gertrude now clearly felt that she had obtruded her sorrow. [VERB noun]

    格特鲁德此时明显察觉自己的悲伤情绪已影响到他人。

  • He didn't want to obtrude on her privacy. [VERB + on]

    他不想侵扰她的隐私。
