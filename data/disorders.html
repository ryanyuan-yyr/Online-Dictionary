disorder
dɪsɔːʳdəʳ dɪsˈɔrdər
Word forms: plural disorders
1 N-VAR (身心机能的)失调，不适，紊乱，疾病 A disorder is a problem or illness which affects someone's mind or body. (身心机能的)失调，不适，紊乱，疾病

  • ...a rare nerve disorder that can cause paralysis of the arms.

    一种可致手臂瘫痪的罕见的神经疾病

  • ...a psychiatrist who specialises in eating disorders.

    一位长于治疗饮食失调的精神病专家

  • He appeared to be suffering from a severe mental disorder and had served a term in prison.

    他看上去患有严重的精神疾病，而且坐过牢。

SYN illness, disease, complaint, condition  
2 N-UNCOUNT [oft in NOUN] 杂乱;混乱;凌乱 Disorder is a state of being untidy, badly prepared, or badly organized. 杂乱;混乱;凌乱

  • The emergency room was in disorder.

    急诊室里一片混乱。

  • Inside all was disorder: drawers fallen out, shoes and boots scattered.

    屋内凌乱不堪：抽屉都掉落出来，鞋靴东一只西一只满地都是。

SYN untidiness, mess, confusion, chaos  
3 N-VAR 骚乱;动乱 Disorder is violence or rioting in public. 骚乱;动乱

  • He called on the authorities to stop public disorder.

    他呼吁当局制止公众骚乱。

  • There are other forms of civil disorder — most notably, football hooliganism.

    还有其他形式的民众骚乱，最出名的是足球流氓行为。

SYN disturbance, fight, riot, turmoil
