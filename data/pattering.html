patter
pætəʳ ˈpætər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense patters, present participle pattering, past tense , past participle pattered
1 VERB 嗒嗒作响；嗒嗒地拍 If something patters on a surface, it hits it quickly several times, making quiet, tapping sounds. 嗒嗒作响；嗒嗒地拍

  • Rain pattered gently outside, dripping on to the roof from the pines. [VERB adv./prep.]

    外面，松树上的雨水滴落在屋顶上，嗒嗒作响。

SYN chatter, spout [informal] , babble, rattle off  
2 N-SING 急促的轻拍声；嗒嗒声 A patter is a series of quick, quiet, tapping sounds. 急促的轻拍声；嗒嗒声

  • ...the patter of the driving rain on the roof. [+ of]

    暴雨打在屋顶上发出的啪嗒声

SYN tapping, pattering, pitter-patter, pitapat  
3 N-SING [usually poss NOUN] （通常以娱乐、推销或劝说为目的的）顺口溜，伶俐话，连珠炮似的叫卖语 Someone's patter is a series of things that they say quickly and easily, usually in order to entertain people or to persuade them to buy or do something. （通常以娱乐、推销或劝说为目的的）顺口溜，伶俐话，连珠炮似的叫卖语

  • There's no doubt women found him charming. It must have been his patter because he's certainly not at all good-looking.

    女人们无疑会觉得他很迷人。这肯定是因为他的顺口溜，因为他长得一点儿都不好看。

  • Fran began her automatic patter about how Jon had been unavoidably detained.

    弗兰开始不由自主地唠叨起乔恩是怎样无可避免地被扣留的。
