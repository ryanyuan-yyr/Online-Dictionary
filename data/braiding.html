braid
breɪd bred breɪd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense braids, present participle braiding, past tense , past participle braided
1 N-UNCOUNT （用来装饰衣服、窗帘等的）布带，穗带 Braid is a narrow piece of decorated cloth or twisted threads, which is used to decorate clothes or curtains. （用来装饰衣服、窗帘等的）布带，穗带

  • ...a plum-coloured uniform with lots of gold braid.

    饰有很多金色穗带的暗紫色制服

SYN binding, cording, cord, ribbon  
2 VERB 把（头发）编成辫子；把（丝线）编成辫状 If you braid hair or a group of threads, you twist three or more lengths of the hair or threads over and under each other to make one thick length. 把（头发）编成辫子；把（丝线）编成辫状 [US]

  • She had almost finished braiding Louisa's hair. [VERB noun]

    她马上就要为路易莎编好辫子了。

  • He pictured her with long black braided hair. [VERB-ed]

    他想象她梳着又黑又长的辫子。

SYN interweave, weave, lace, intertwine  
in BRIT, use 英国英语用 plait
3 N-COUNT 辫子 A braid is a length of hair which has been divided into three or more lengths and then braided. 辫子 [US]
in BRIT, use 英国英语用 plait
