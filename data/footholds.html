foothold
fʊthoʊld ˈfʊtˌhoʊld
Word forms: plural footholds
1 N-COUNT [oft adjective NOUN] 立足点;稳固地位 A foothold is a strong or favourable position from which further advances or progress may be made. 立足点;稳固地位

  • If British business is to have a successful future, companies must establish a firm foothold in Europe. [+ in]

    如果英国公司想在未来成功发展，就必须在欧洲建立稳固的基础。

SYN basis, standing, base, position  
2 N-COUNT [oft adjective NOUN] (攀登时的)立足点,支点 A foothold is a place such as a small hole or area of rock where you can safely put your foot when climbing. (攀登时的)立足点,支点

  • He lowered his legs until he felt he had a solid foothold on the rockface beneath him. [+ on]

    他把腿向下伸，直到他感觉稳稳地踩住下面的石头。

SYN toehold, hold, support, footing
