chasten
tʃeɪsən ˈtʃeɪsən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense chastens, present participle chastening, past tense , past participle chastened
VERB [usually passive] 使后悔;使愧疚 If you are chastened by something, it makes you regret that you have behaved badly or stupidly. 使后悔;使愧疚 [formal] [Also be V-ed + into]

  • He has clearly not been chastened by his thirteen days in detention. [be VERB-ed + by]

    显然，13天拘留并没能让他悔过自新。

  • A chastened Agassi flew home for a period of deep contemplation. [VERB-ed]

    追悔莫及的阿加西坐飞机回家面壁思过去了。

SYN subdue, discipline, cow, curb  
chastened

  • The President now seems a more chastened and less confident politician than when he set out a week ago.

    总统现在看起来是一个比一星期前刚就任时多了些历练、少了些自信的政治家。
