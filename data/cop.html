cop
kɒp kɑp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , plural cops, present participle copping, past tense , past participle copped
1 N-COUNT 警察 A cop is a policeman or policewoman. 警察 [informal]

  • Frank didn't like having the cops know where to find him.

    弗兰克不想让警察知道去哪里可以找到他。

2 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 受责骂;受罚 If you cop it, someone punishes you or speaks to you angrily because you have done something wrong. 受责骂;受罚 [British, informal]

  • Motel owners and restaurant managers copped it for neglecting their clients.

    汽车旅馆老板和饭店经理因为冷落顾客而受到责骂。

3 PHRASE 没有什么价值;不怎么样 If you say that something is not much cop, you mean that it is not very good, and is disappointing. 没有什么价值;不怎么样 [British, informal]

  • The Jane's 'Triple X Album' came out in 1986, and wasn't much cop actually.

    简在1986年推出了《XXX》专辑，但事实上并不怎么样。
