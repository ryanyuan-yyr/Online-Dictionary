gore
gɔːʳ gɔr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense gores, present participle goring, past tense , past participle gored
1 VERB [usually passive] （动物用角或长牙）顶伤，戳伤 If someone is gored by an animal, they are badly wounded by its horns or tusks. （动物用角或长牙）顶伤，戳伤

  • Carruthers had been gored by a rhinoceros. [be VERB-ed]

    卡拉瑟斯被犀牛顶伤了。

  • He was gored to death in front of his family. [be VERB-ed + to]

    他在家人眼前被野兽活生生地戳死了。

SYN pierce, wound, stab, spit  
2 N-UNCOUNT 凝固的血；血块 Gore is blood from a wound that has become thick. 凝固的血；血块

  • There were pools of blood and gore on the pavement.

    人行道上有一摊摊血和血块。

SYN blood, slaughter, bloodshed, carnage
