rinse
rɪns rɪns
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense rinses, present participle rinsing, past tense , past participle rinsed
1 VERB 清洗;冲洗 When you rinse something, you wash it in clean water in order to remove dirt or soap from it. 清洗;冲洗

  • After shampooing, always rinse the hair several times in clear water. [VERB noun]

    每次用完洗发剂后都要用清水将头发冲洗几次。

  • It's important to rinse the rice to remove the starch. [VERB noun]

    一定要淘米以洗掉淀粉。

SYN wash, clean, wet, dip  
Rinse is also a noun.

  • A quick rinse isn't sufficient. Use plenty of running water to wash away all traces of shampoo.

    光快速冲洗一下是不够的。得用大量流水把残余的洗发水都洗干净。

2 VERB 漱（口） If you rinse your mouth, you wash it by filling your mouth with water or with a liquid that kills germs, then spitting it out. 漱（口）

  • Use a toothbrush on your tongue as well, and rinse your mouth frequently. [VERB noun]

    舌头也要用牙刷清洁，并且经常漱口。

Rinse out means the same as rinse .rinse out 同 rinse

  • After her meal she invariably rinsed out her mouth.

    饭后她总是要漱口。

  • You should rinse your mouth out after eating.

    吃东西后应该漱口。

Rinse is also a noun.

  • ...mouth rinses with fluoride.

    含氟的漱口水

3 N-COUNT 染发剂 A hair rinse is a dye whose colour gradually disappears after you have washed your hair a number of times, rather than being permanent. 染发剂
