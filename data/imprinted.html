imprint
ɪmprɪnt ɪmprɪnt ɪmˈprɪnt ˈɪmˌprɪnt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense imprints, present participle imprinting, past tense , past participle imprinted
The noun is pronounced /'ɪmprɪnt/. The verb is pronounced /ɪm'prɪnt/. 名词读作 /'ɪmprɪnt/,动词读作 /ɪm'prɪnt/。
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 印记;深远影响 If something leaves an imprint on a place or on your mind, it has a strong and lasting effect on it. 印记;深远影响 [Also + on]

  • The city bears the imprint of Japanese investment. [+ of]

    该市带有日本投资的深刻印记。

  • Both King and Gandhi were tremendously brave men whose unique form of courage left a lasting imprint on their nations' histories.

    金和甘地都是勇敢无畏的人,他们独一无二的勇气在他们各自国家的历史上留下了深远影响。

2 VERB 铭刻;使牢记 When something is imprinted on your memory, it is firmly fixed in your memory so that you will not forget it. 铭刻;使牢记

  • The skyline of domes and minarets was imprinted on my memory. [be V-ed + on/in]

    天空中穹顶与尖塔的轮廓深深刻在了我的记忆中。

  • He repeated the names, as if to imprint them in his mind. [V n + on/in]

    他一遍遍重复着那些名字,仿佛要把它们铭刻在心里。

  • He could not dislodge the images imprinted on his brain. [VERB-ed]

    他难以把刻在脑海中的形象抹去。

SYN fix, establish, place, set  
3 N-COUNT 印痕;痕迹 An imprint is a mark or outline made by the pressure of one object on another. 印痕;痕迹

  • The ground still bore the imprints of their feet. [+ of]

    地面上仍然留有他们的脚印。

4 VERB [usually passive] 印;压印 If a surface is imprinted with a mark or design, that mark or design is printed on the surface or pressed into it. 印;压印 [Also V n + on]

  • Stationery can be imprinted with your message or logo. [be VERB-ed + with]

    可以把你的信息或标志印在信纸上。

  • They also left a card, imprinted with the name Sean Lynch. [VERB-ed]

    他们还留下了一张名片,上面印着肖恩·林奇的名字。

  • ...a racket with the club's badge imprinted on the strings. [VERB-ed]

    网线上印有俱乐部徽章的网球拍

SYN engrave, print, stamp, impress
