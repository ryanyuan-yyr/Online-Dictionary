excise
eksaɪz eksaɪz ɪksaɪz ɪkˈsaɪz ɛkˈsaɪz ˈɛkˌsaɪz ɪkˈsaɪz ˈɛkˌsaɪs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , plural excises, present participle excising, past tense , past participle excised
The noun is pronounced /'eksaɪz/. The verb is pronounced /ɪk'saɪz/. 名词读作 /'eksaɪz/。动词读作 /ɪk'saɪz/。
1 N-VAR [usually NOUN n] 国内货物税;消费税 Excise is a tax that the government of a country puts on particular goods, such as cigarettes and alcoholic drinks, which are produced for sale in its own country. 国内货物税;消费税

  • ...this year's rise in excise duties.

    今年消费税的提高

  • New car buyers and smokers will be hit by increases in taxes and excise.

    税率和消费税的提高将使新车买主和烟民受到打击。

SYN tax, duty, customs, toll  
2 VERB 切除;删除;消除 If someone excises something, they remove it deliberately and completely. 切除;删除;消除 [formal]

  • ...a personal crusade to excise racist and sexist references in newspapers. [VERB noun]

    消除报纸中涉及种族和性别歧视内容的个人努力

  • ...the question of permanently excising madness from the world. [VERB noun + from]

    关于永久清除世界上疯狂举动的问题

SYN delete, cut, remove, erase  
excision

  • The authors demanded excision of foreign words.

    作者们要求删除外国文字。
