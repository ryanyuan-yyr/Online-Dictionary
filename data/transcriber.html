transcribe
trænskraɪb trænˈskraɪb
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense transcribes, present participle transcribing, past tense , past participle transcribed
1 VERB 笔录;转写 If you transcribe a speech or text, you write it out in a different form from the one in which it exists, for example by writing it out in full from notes or from a tape recording. 笔录;转写

  • She is transcribing, from his dictation, the diaries of Simon Forman. [VERB noun]

    她正根据他的口述记录西蒙·福曼的日记。

  • Every telephone conversation will be recorded and transcribed. [VERB noun]

    所有电话交谈都将被录音并作全文转写。

SYN write out, reproduce, take down, copy out  
2 VERB (为适合其他乐器演奏而)改编(乐曲) If you transcribe a piece of music for an instrument which is not the one for which it was originally written, you rewrite it so that it can be played on that instrument. (为适合其他乐器演奏而)改编(乐曲) [Also VERB n]

  • He gave up trying to write for the guitar and decided to transcribe the work for piano. [VERB noun + for]

    他放弃了为吉他谱曲，决定把作品改编成钢琴曲。

SYN translate, interpret, render, transliterate
