soil
sɔɪl sɔɪl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense soils, present participle soiling, past tense , past participle soiled
1 N-MASS 土壤;土地 Soil is the substance on the surface of the earth in which plants grow. 土壤;土地

  • We have the most fertile soil in Europe.

    我们拥有欧洲最肥沃的土地。

  • ...regions with sandy soils.

    沙土地区

SYN earth, ground, clay, dust  
2 N-UNCOUNT 国土;领土 You can use soil in expressions like British soil to refer to a country's territory. 国土;领土

  • The issue of foreign troops on Turkish soil is a sensitive one.

    在土耳其领土上驻扎外国军队这个问题非常敏感。

SYN territory, country, land, region  
3 VERB 弄脏;玷污 If you soil something, you make it dirty. 弄脏;玷污 [formal]

  • Young people don't want to do things that soil their hands. [VERB noun]

    年轻人不愿做那些容易弄脏手的事情。

  • He raised his eyes slightly as though her words might somehow soil him. [VERB noun]

    他略略地抬了抬眼皮，仿佛她的话会不小心玷污了他。

SYN dirty, foul, stain, smear

    弄脏了的白围裙
