joke
dʒoʊk dʒoʊk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense jokes, present participle joking, past tense , past participle joked
1 N-COUNT 玩笑；笑话 A joke is something that is said or done to make you laugh, for example a funny story. 玩笑；笑话

  • He debated whether to make a joke about shooting rabbits, but decided against it. [+ about]

    他心里琢磨是否该开个有关打兔子的玩笑，但决定还是算了。

  • No one told worse jokes than Claus.

    克劳斯讲的笑话糟糕透了。

SYN jest, gag [informal] , wisecrack [informal] , witticism  
2 VERB 讲笑话；开玩笑 If you joke, you tell funny stories or say amusing things. 讲笑话；开玩笑

  • She would joke about her appearance. [VERB + about]

    她会拿自己的长相开玩笑。

  • Lorna was laughing and joking with Trevor. [VERB + with]

    洛娜边笑边和特雷弗打趣。

  • The project was taking so long that Stephen joked that it would never be finished. [VERB that]

    这个项目花了很长的时间，斯蒂芬甚至打趣说它永远都没有结束的那一天。

  • 'Well, a beautiful spring Thursday would probably be a nice day to be buried on,' Nancy joked. [VERB with quote]

    “嗯，一个美丽的春天的周四可能是个下葬的好日子。”南希开玩笑说。

3 N-COUNT 恶作剧；捉弄人的玩笑 A joke is something untrue that you tell another person in order to amuse yourself. 恶作剧；捉弄人的玩笑

  • It was probably just a joke to them, but it wasn't funny to me.

    对他们而言，那可能仅仅是个恶作剧，但对我来说，它一点也不好笑。

SYN laugh [informal] , jest, fun, josh [slang , mainly US , Canadian]  
4 VERB 开玩笑；搞恶作剧 If you joke, you tell someone something that is not true in order to amuse yourself. 开玩笑；搞恶作剧

  • Don't get defensive, Charlie. I was only joking. [VERB]

    别这么大戒心，查理。我只不过是开开玩笑。

  • 'I wish you made as much fuss of me,' Vera joked. [VERB with quote]

    “我倒真希望你也能这么好好地关心我一下，”薇拉打趣道。

SYN jest, kid [informal] , fool, mock  
5 N-SING 荒唐可笑的事（或人）；笑料；笑柄 If you say that something or someone is a joke, you think they are ridiculous and do not deserve respect. 荒唐可笑的事（或人）；笑料；笑柄 [informal, disapproval]

  • It's ridiculous, it's pathetic, it's a joke.

    这真荒谬，简直是可悲、可笑。

  • The police investigation was a joke. A total cover-up.

    警方的调查简直荒唐可笑，完全是在掩盖真相。

SYN farce, nonsense, parody, sham  
6 PHRASE （恼人或令人担心的事）不可理喻，使人无法接受 If you say that an annoying or worrying situation is beyond a joke, you are emphasizing that it is worse than you think is fair or reasonable. （恼人或令人担心的事）不可理喻，使人无法接受 [British, emphasis]

  • Giving an arsonist a lighter is beyond a joke.

    把打火机交给纵火犯简直是不可理喻。

  • I'm not afraid of a fair fight but this is beginning to get beyond a joke.

    “我不怕公平地打上一架，但是这玩笑有点儿开过头了。”

7 PHRASE 拿（严肃或悲惨的事情）开玩笑；对…一笑置之 If you make a joke of something, you laugh at it even though it is in fact rather serious or sad. 拿（严肃或悲惨的事情）开玩笑；对…一笑置之

  • I wish I had your courage, Michael, to make a joke of it like that.

    我真希望能有你那样的勇气，迈克尔，能把事情那么一笑置之。

8 PHRASE 不是轻而易举的事；不是闹着玩的 If you describe a situation as no joke, you are emphasizing that it is very difficult or unpleasant. 不是轻而易举的事；不是闹着玩的 [informal, emphasis]

  • Two hours on a bus is no joke, is it?

    在公交车上坐上两个小时可不轻松，对吧？

9 PHRASE 对…开玩笑；戏弄 If you say that the joke is on a particular person, you mean that they have been made to look very foolish by something. 对…开玩笑；戏弄

  • 'For once,' he said, 'the joke's on me. And it's not very funny.'

    “他说：“这一次玩笑反而开到自己身上来了。这一点儿也不好玩。”

10 PHRASE 开不起玩笑；经不起玩笑话 If you say that someone cannot take a joke, you are criticizing them for getting upset or angry at something you think is funny. 开不起玩笑；经不起玩笑话 [disapproval]

  • 'What's the matter with you, Simon?' Curly said. 'Can't you take a joke?'

    “西蒙，你怎么回事？”柯利说。“你难道开不起玩笑么？”

11 CONVENTION （用于表示吃惊或认为不可理喻）你一定是在开玩笑吧 You say you're joking or you must be joking to someone when they have just told you something that is so surprising or unreasonable that you find it difficult to believe. （用于表示吃惊或认为不可理喻）你一定是在开玩笑吧 [spoken, feelings]

  • You're joking. Are you serious?.

    你准是在开玩笑。你是认真的吗？

  • One hundred and forty quid for a pair of headphones, you've got to be joking!

    一副耳机140英镑，你不是在开玩笑吧！
