amend
əmend əˈmɛnd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense amends, present participle amending, past tense , past participle amended
1 VERB 修改；修订 If you amend something that has been written such as a law, or something that is said, you change it in order to improve it or make it more accurate. 修改；修订

  • The president agreed to amend the constitution and allow multi-party elections. [VERB noun]

    总统同意修订宪法，允许多党选举。

  • 'You must admit that the man has got charm,' said Nicolson. 'Glamour,' amended Wells. [VERB with quote]

    “你必须承认这个男人有魅力，”尼科尔森说。“是诱惑力，”韦尔斯纠正说。

  • ...the amended version of the Act. [VERB-ed]

    该法令的修正案

SYN change, improve, reform, fix  
2 PHRASE 赔罪；赔偿；补偿 If you make amends when you have harmed someone, you show that you are sorry by doing something to please them. 赔罪；赔偿；补偿

  • He wanted to make amends for causing their marriage to fail.

    他导致了他们的婚姻破裂，因此想作出补偿。
