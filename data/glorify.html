glorify
glɔːrɪfaɪ ˈglɔrəˌfaɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense glorifies, present participle glorifying, past tense , past participle glorified
1 VERB 赞美；颂扬；吹捧；美化 To glorify something means to praise it or make it seem good or special, usually when it is not. 赞美；颂扬；吹捧；美化

  • This magazine in no way glorifies gangs. [VERB noun]

    这本杂志绝对没有美化混混们。

  • ...the banning of songs glorifying war and racism. [VERB noun]

    对吹捧战争和种族歧视的歌曲的禁播令

SYN praise, celebrate, magnify, laud [literary]  
glorification

  • ...the glorification of violence.

    对暴力行径的渲染美化

  • ...a glorification of the past.

    对往昔的颂扬

2 VERB 颂扬，赞美（上帝） If you glorify God, you praise Him. 颂扬，赞美（上帝）

  • We are all committed to serving the Lord and glorifying His name in the best way we know. [VERB noun]

    我们全心全意敬奉上帝，竭尽所能颂扬他的美名。

SYN worship, honour, bless, adore
