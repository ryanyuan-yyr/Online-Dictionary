fraud
frɔːd frɔd
Word forms: plural frauds
1 N-VAR 诈骗;欺诈 Fraud is the crime of gaining money or financial benefits by a trick or by lying. 诈骗;欺诈

  • He was jailed for two years for fraud and deception.

    他因为诈骗和欺诈入狱服刑两年。

  • Tax frauds are dealt with by the Inland Revenue.

    税务欺诈由国内税务局负责处理。

SYN deception, deceit, treachery, swindling  
2 N-COUNT 骗子;诈骗者;骗人的东西 A fraud is something or someone that deceives people in a way that is illegal or dishonest. 骗子;诈骗者;骗人的东西

  • Unfortunately the portraits were frauds.

    不幸的是这些肖像都是骗人的。

  • He believes many 'psychics' are frauds who rely on perception and subtle deception.

    他认为许多“通灵者”都是骗子，他们都是靠直觉和巧妙的骗术蒙人。

SYN impostor, cheat, fake, bluffer  
3 N-COUNT 骗子;假货;赝品 If you call someone or something a fraud, you are criticizing them because you think that they are not genuine, or are less good than they claim or appear to be. 骗子;假货;赝品 [disapproval]

  • ...all those fashion frauds who think they are being original by raiding the tired old styles of the '60s.

    所有那些抄袭 60 年代的过时风格却自认为具有原创性的时尚造假者
