excessive
ɪksesɪv ɪkˈsɛsɪv ɛkˈsɛsɪv ɪkˈsɛsɪv
ADJ-GRADED 过分的;过多的 If you describe the amount or level of something as excessive, you disapprove of it because it is more or higher than is necessary or reasonable. 过分的;过多的 [disapproval]

  • ...the alleged use of excessive force by police.

    警察涉嫌滥用武力

  • The government says that local authority spending is excessive.

    政府称地方当局开支过大。

SYN immoderate, too much, enormous, extreme  
excessively

  • Managers are also accused of paying themselves excessively high salaries.

    经理们还被指责给自己支付过高的薪水。

  • Mum had started taking pills and drinking excessively.

    妈妈开始服药酗酒。
