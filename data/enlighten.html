enlighten
ɪnlaɪtən ɛnˈlaɪtən ɪnˈlaɪtən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense enlightens, present participle enlightening, past tense , past participle enlightened
1 VERB [no cont] 启发;开导;教化 To enlighten someone means to give them more knowledge and greater understanding about something. 启发;开导;教化 [formal]

  • A few dedicated doctors have fought for years to enlighten the profession. [VERB noun]

    一些有献身精神的医生多年来一直努力指导业内同仁。

  • If you know what is wrong with her, please enlighten me. [VERB noun]

    如果你知道她出什么问题了，请告诉我。

SYN inform, tell, teach, advise  
enlightening

  • ...an enlightening talk on the work done at the animal park.

    介绍动物园工作情况的启发性讲话

  • It can be an enormously enlightening and exciting experience.

    它会是一次非常有启发性、令人兴奋的经历。
