detect
dɪtekt diˈtɛkt dɪˈtɛkt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense detects, present participle detecting, past tense , past participle detected
1 VERB 发现;查出;测出 To detect something means to find it or discover that it is present somewhere by using equipment or making an investigation. 发现;查出;测出

  • ...a sensitive piece of equipment used to detect radiation. [VERB noun]

    一件用于探测辐射的灵敏设备

  • Most skin cancers can be cured if detected and treated early. [VERB noun]

    如果早发现早治疗，大多数皮肤癌是可以治愈的。

  • ...a device which can detect who is more at risk of a heart attack. [VERB wh]

    可以查出心脏病发作概率的仪器

SYN discover, find, reveal, catch  
2 VERB 觉察;发觉 If you detect something, you notice it or sense it, even though it is not very obvious. 觉察;发觉

  • Arnold could detect a certain sadness in the old man's face. [VERB noun]

    阿诺德能觉察到老人脸上的一丝悲伤。

SYN notice, see, spot, catch
