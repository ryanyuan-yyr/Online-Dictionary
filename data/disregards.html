disregard
dɪsrɪgɑːʳd ˌdɪsrɪˈɡɑːd ˌdɪsrɪˈgɑrd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense disregards, present participle disregarding, past tense , past participle disregarded
VERB 不理会;不顾;漠视 If you disregard something, you ignore it or do not take account of it. 不理会;不顾;漠视

  • He disregarded the advice of his executives. [VERB noun]

    他对主管层的建议置若罔闻。

  • Critics say he allowed the police and security forces to disregard human rights. [VERB noun]

    批评人士说他听任警察和安全部队漠视人权。

SYN ignore, discount, take no notice of, overlook  
Disregard is also a noun.

  • Whoever planted the bomb showed a total disregard for the safety of the public.

    无论是谁放置的炸弹都是对公众安全的极端漠视。
