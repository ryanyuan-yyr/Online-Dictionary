weary
wɪəri ˈwɪri
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense wearies, present participle wearying, past tense , past participle wearied, comparative wearier, superlative weariest
1 ADJ-GRADED 疲倦的;疲劳的 If you are weary, you are very tired. 疲倦的;疲劳的

  • Rachel looked pale and weary.

    雷切尔看起来脸色苍白，疲惫不堪。

  • ...a weary traveller.

    疲惫不堪的旅行者

  • He managed a weary smile.

    他勉强挤出了一丝疲倦的笑容。

wearily

  • I sighed wearily.

    我疲倦地叹了口气。

  • He trudged wearily down Arthur Street.

    他沿着亚瑟大街精疲力竭地跋涉。

weariness

  • Despite his weariness, Brand mustered a wan smile.

    虽然精疲力竭，布兰德还是无力地笑了笑。

  • He showed absolutely no signs of weariness.

    他毫无倦意。

2 ADJ-GRADED 不再感兴趣的;感到厌烦的 If you are weary of something, you have become tired of it and have lost your enthusiasm for it. 不再感兴趣的;感到厌烦的

  • They're getting awfully weary of this silly war. [+ of]

    他们越来越厌烦这场无聊的战争。

  • She was weary of being alone.

    她厌倦了独处。

wearily

  • 'I'm not Mrs Reynolds,' she said wearily.

    “我不是雷诺兹夫人，”她不耐烦地说道。

  • Measures like these sound wearily familiar.

    这些措施听得都让人耳朵生茧了。

3 VERB （使）厌倦；（使）失去热情 If you weary of something or it wearies you, you become tired of it and lose your enthusiasm for it. （使）厌倦；（使）失去热情 [formal]

  • The public had wearied of his repeated warnings of a revolution that never seemed to start. [VERB + of]

    他一再预警的革命迟迟没有开始，公众已对此感到厌倦。

  • He had wearied of teaching in state universities. [V of n/-ing]

    他已经厌烦了在州立大学教书。

  • The political hysteria soon wearied him and he dropped the newspaper to the floor. [VERB noun]

    政界的歇斯底里让他厌烦，他把报纸扔到了地板上。

SYN grow tired, tire, sicken, have had enough
