dusty
dʌsti ˈdʌsti
Word forms: comparative dustier, superlative dustiest
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (通常指因为少雨而)布满灰尘的,满是尘土的 If places, roads, or other things outside are dusty, they are covered with tiny bits of earth or sand, usually because it has not rained for a long time. (通常指因为少雨而)布满灰尘的,满是尘土的

  • They started strolling down the dusty road in the moonlight.

    在月光下他们开始沿着满是尘土的路漫步。

  • ...a dusty old car.

    落满灰尘的老爷车

2 ADJ-GRADED (房子、物件等)覆有灰尘的,尘封的 If a room, house, or object is dusty, it is covered with very small pieces of dirt. (房子、物件等)覆有灰尘的,尘封的

  • ...a dusty attic.

    满是灰尘的阁楼

  • The books looked faded, dusty and unused.

    那些书看上去褪了色,尘封已久。

SYN dirty, grubby, unclean, unswept
