pillory
pɪləri ˈpɪləri
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense pillories, present participle pillorying, past tense , past participle pilloried
1 VERB [usually passive] 使受公众（尤指记者）批评；使受羞辱 If someone is pilloried, a lot of people, especially journalists, criticize them and make them look stupid. 使受公众（尤指记者）批评；使受羞辱

  • A man has been forced to resign as a result of being pilloried by some of the press. [be VERB-ed]

    一人因为受到一些媒体的抨击已被迫辞职。

SYN ridicule, denounce, stigmatize, brand  
2 N-COUNT 颈手枷（欧洲旧时用以将罪犯示众的刑具） A pillory is a wooden frame with holes for the head and hands. In Europe in former times criminals were sometimes locked in a pillory as a form of punishment. 颈手枷（欧洲旧时用以将罪犯示众的刑具）
