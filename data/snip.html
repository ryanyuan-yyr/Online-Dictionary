snip
snɪp snɪp
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense snips, present participle snipping, past tense , past participle snipped
1 VERB 剪;剪下;剪断 If you snip something, or if you snip at or through something, you cut it quickly using sharp scissors. 剪;剪下;剪断

  • He has now begun to snip away at the piece of paper. [VERB adv./prep.]

    现在他已经开始剪这张纸。

  • He snipped a length of new bandage and placed it around Peter's chest. [VERB noun]

    他剪下一段新绷带，缠在了彼得的胸口。

SYN cut, nick, clip, crop  
2 N-SING 货真价实之物;物美价廉的物品 If you say that something is a snip, you mean that it is very good value. 货真价实之物;物美价廉的物品 [British, informal]

  • The beautifully made briefcase is a snip at £74.25.

    这个做工精美的公文包售价才 74.25 英镑，可谓物美价廉。

SYN bargain, steal [informal] , good buy, giveaway
