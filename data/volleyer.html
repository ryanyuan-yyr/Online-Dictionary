volley
vɒli ˈvɑli
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense volleys, present participle volleying, past tense , past participle volleyed
1 VERB 截踢,截击(空中球) In sport, if someone volleys the ball, they hit it before it touches the ground. 截踢,截击(空中球)

  • He volleyed the ball spectacularly into the far corner of the net. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他一脚漂亮的凌空抽射，把球踢进了球门的远角。

  • McNeil volleyed more effectively in the second set. [VERB]

    在第二局中，麦克尼尔对球的截击更加成功。

Volley is also a noun.

  • She hit most of the winning volleys.

    她的截击球大多都成功得分了。

2 N-COUNT （子弹的）群射，齐发 A volley of gunfire is a lot of bullets that travel through the air at the same time. （子弹的）群射，齐发

  • It's still not known how many died in the volleys of gunfire. [+ of]

    现在还不知道有多少人在枪林弹雨中身亡。

  • Three mounted officers rode into the field after the volley.

    枪炮一起开火后，3名军官骑着马冲进战场。

SYN barrage, blast, burst, explosion
