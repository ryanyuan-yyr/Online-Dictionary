seat
siːt sit
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense seats, present participle seating, past tense , past participle seated
1 N-COUNT 座椅;椅子;座位 A seat is an object that you can sit on, for example a chair. 座椅;椅子;座位

  • Stephen returned to his seat.

    斯蒂芬回到自己的座位上。

  • Ann could remember sitting in the back seat of their car.

    安记得坐在他们车里的后排座上了。

SYN chair, place, bench, stall  
2 N-COUNT (椅子等的)座部,椅面 The seat of a chair is the part that you sit on. (椅子等的)座部,椅面

  • The stool had a torn, red plastic seat.

    那个凳子的红色塑料座面已经破旧不堪。

3 VERB 坐下;就座 If you seat yourself somewhere, you sit down. 坐下;就座 [written]

  • He waved towards a chair, and seated himself at the desk. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    他挥手指了指一把椅子，自己在书桌旁坐了下来。

  • ...a portrait of one of his favourite models seated on an elegant sofa. [VERB-ed]

    一幅他最喜欢的模特之一的画像，画中人坐在雅致的沙发上

SYN sit, place, settle, set  
4 VERB (房间、车辆)可坐…人，容纳…人 A building or vehicle that seats a particular number of people has enough seats for that number. (房间、车辆)可坐…人，容纳…人

  • The Theatre seats 570. [VERB amount]

    那个剧院可容纳 570 人。

SYN hold, take, accommodate, sit  
5 N-SING (裤子的)臀部 The seat of a piece of clothing is the part that covers your bottom. (裤子的)臀部

  • Then he got up and brushed off the seat of his jeans. [+ of]

    然后他站起来，拍了拍牛仔裤的臀部。

6 N-COUNT (国会、议会、参议院中的)席位 When someone is elected to a parliament, congress, or senate, you can say that they, or their party, have won a seat . (国会、议会、参议院中的)席位

  • Independent candidates won the majority of seats on the local council.

    无党派候选人在地方议会选举中赢得了多数席位。

  • ...a Maryland Republican who lost his seat.

    一名失掉席位的马里兰州共和党党员

7 N-COUNT (公司董事会、委员会中的)席位，职位 If someone has a seat on the board of a company or on a committee, they are a member of it. (公司董事会、委员会中的)席位，职位

  • He has been unsuccessful in his attempt to win a seat on the board of the company. [+ on]

    他始终未能如愿以偿地在公司董事会中谋得一席之地。

SYN bottom [informal] , behind [informal] , rear, bum [British , slang]  
8 N-COUNT (组织、活动的)中心地，所在地;(豪门的)宅邸 The seat of an organization, a wealthy family, or an activity is its base. (组织、活动的)中心地，所在地;(豪门的)宅邸

  • Gunfire broke out early this morning around the seat of government in Lagos. [+ of]

    今天凌晨在拉各斯的政府大楼周围发生了枪战。

  • ...Weston Park, family seat of the Earl of Bradford.

    韦斯顿公园，布拉德福德伯爵的祖宅

SYN centre, place, site, heart  
9 See also: deep-seated  hot seat 
10 PHRASE 让权于他人;退居次要位置 If you take a back seat, you allow other people to have all the power and to make all the decisions. 让权于他人;退居次要位置

  • You need to take a back seat and think about both past and future.

    你得退居二线，把过去和将来都捋清楚。

  • This is a country where women usually take a back seat.

    在这个国家通常是男尊女卑。

11 PHRASE 坐下;入座 If you take a seat, you sit down. 坐下;入座 [formal]

  • 'Take a seat,' he said in a bored tone.

    “请坐，”他不耐烦地说道。

  • Rachel smiled at him as they took their seats on opposite sides of the table.

    他们分坐在桌子两边，雷切尔对他微微笑了一下。
