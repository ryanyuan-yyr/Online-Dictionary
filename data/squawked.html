squawk
skwɔːk skwɔk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense squawks, present participle squawking, past tense , past participle squawked
1 VERB (鸟)发出响亮而粗厉的叫声 When a bird squawks, it makes a loud harsh noise. (鸟)发出响亮而粗厉的叫声

  • I threw pebbles at the hens, and that made them jump and squawk. [VERB]

    我朝母鸡扔卵石，吓得它们乱跳乱叫。

SYN cry, crow, screech, hoot  
Squawk is also a noun.

  • A mallard suddenly took wing, rising steeply into the air with an angry squawk.

    一只绿头鸭突然飞起，愤怒地尖鸣一声直冲天空。

2 VERB (人)大声抱怨，粗声抗议 If a person squawks, they complain loudly, often in a high-pitched, harsh tone. (人)大声抱怨，粗声抗议 [informal] [Also VERB]

  • Mr Arbor squawked that the deal was a double-cross. [VERB that]

    阿伯先生大声抱怨说这笔交易是个骗局。

  • 'Wait for me!' Melanie squawked. 'I'm not staying here alone.' [VERB with quote]

    “等等我！”梅拉尼粗声抗议道，“我不想一个人呆在这里。”

SYN complain, protest, squeal [informal , mainly British] , kick up a fuss [informal]  
Squawk is also a noun.

  • She gave a loud squawk when the water was poured on her.

    水泼到她身上时，她大叫了一声。
