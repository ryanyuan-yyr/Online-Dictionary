heat up
1 PHRASAL VERB （尤指将已经冷掉的熟食）加热 When you heat something up, especially food which has already been cooked and allowed to go cold, you make it hot. （尤指将已经冷掉的熟食）加热 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Freda heated up a pie for me. [VERB PREP. noun]

    弗蕾达为我热了一块馅饼。

2 PHRASAL VERB 激化;加剧;变得激烈（或热烈） When a situation heats up, things start to happen much more quickly and with increased interest and excitement among the people involved. 激化;加剧;变得激烈（或热烈）

  • Then in the last couple of years, the movement for democracy began to heat up. [VERB PREP.]

    然后在过去几年里，民主运动开始升温。

3 PHRASAL VERB 变热 When something heats up, it gradually becomes hotter. 变热

  • In the summer her mobile home heats up like an oven. [VERB PREP.]

    夏天一到，她的活动住房就变得像烤炉一样热。

SYN warm up, get hotter, become hot, rise in temperature
