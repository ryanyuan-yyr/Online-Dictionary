guzzle
gʌzəl ˈgʌzəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense guzzles, present participle guzzling, past tense , past participle guzzled
1 VERB 狂饮；暴食；狼吞虎咽 If you guzzle something, you drink it or eat it quickly and greedily. 狂饮；暴食；狼吞虎咽 [informal] [Also VERB]

  • Melissa had guzzled gin and tonics like they were lemonade. [VERB noun]

    梅利莎像喝柠檬汽水一样大口地喝着加奎宁水的杜松子酒。

2 VERB 大量消耗（汽油）；浪费 If you say that a vehicle guzzles fuel, you mean that it uses a lot of it in a way that is wasteful and unnecessary. 大量消耗（汽油）；浪费

  • The plane was deafeningly noisy, guzzled fuel, and left a trail of smoke. [VERB noun]

    这架飞机发出震耳欲聋的噪音，耗油量大，而且留下一路烟迹。

-guzzling
See also: gas guzzler 

  • The boom of the 1980s led to a taste for petrol-guzzling cars.

    20世纪80年代的经济繁荣使人们喜欢上高油耗的汽车。

  • ...big energy-guzzling houses.

    高能耗的大房子
