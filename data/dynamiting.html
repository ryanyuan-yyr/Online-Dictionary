dynamite
daɪnəmaɪt ˈdaɪnəˌmaɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dynamites, present participle dynamiting, past tense , past participle dynamited
1 N-UNCOUNT 甘油炸药 Dynamite is a type of explosive that contains nitroglycerin. 甘油炸药

  • Fifty yards of track was blown up with dynamite.

    50 码铁轨被炸毁。

2 VERB 炸毁 If someone dynamites something, they blow it up by using dynamite. 炸毁

  • The rebels dynamited power lines. [VERB noun]

    叛乱分子炸毁了输电线。

  • The slum is to be dynamited. [VERB noun]

    贫民窟要被炸掉了。

3 N-UNCOUNT 爆炸性消息;引起轰动的事物 If you describe a piece of information as dynamite, you think that people will react strongly to it. 爆炸性消息;引起轰动的事物 [informal]

  • Her diaries are political dynamite.

    她的日记是政治“炸药”。

4 N-UNCOUNT 令人振奋的人（或事物）；令人激动的人（或事物） If you describe someone or something as dynamite, you think that they are exciting. 令人振奋的人（或事物）；令人激动的人（或事物） [informal, approval]

  • Carmen was pure dynamite.

    卡门充满激情。

  • The first kiss is dynamite.

    初吻令人心潮澎湃。
