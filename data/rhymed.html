rhyme
raɪm raɪm
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense rhymes, present participle rhyming, past tense , past participle rhymed
1 V-RECIP-ERG 押韵;成韵 If one word rhymes with another or if two words rhyme, they have a very similar sound. Words that rhyme with each other are often used in poems. 押韵;成韵 [Also V n (non-recip)]

  • June always rhymes with moon in old love songs. [VERB + with]

    在老情歌中June总是和moon成韵。

  • ...the sort of people who give their children names that rhyme: Donnie, Ronnie, Connie. [VERB]

    给孩子取Donnie, Ronnie, Connie之类押韵的名字的父母

  • ...a singer rhyming 'eyes' with 'realise'. [VERB noun + with]

    一个歌词用eyes和realise押韵的歌手

  • ...rhymed couplets. [VERB-ed]

    押韵的对联

SYN sound like, chime, harmonize  
2 VERB （诗或歌）押韵 If a poem or song rhymes, the lines end with words that have very similar sounds. （诗或歌）押韵

  • In his efforts to make it rhyme he seems to have chosen the first word that comes into his head. [VERB]

    为了押韵，他似乎选择了脑海里出现的第一个词。

  • ...rhyming couplets. [VERB-ing]

    押韵的对句

3 N-COUNT 押韵词;同韵词;押韵的诗行 A rhyme is a word which rhymes with another word, or a set of lines which rhyme. 押韵词;同韵词;押韵的诗行

  • The one rhyme for passion is fashion. [+ for]

    和passion押韵的词是fashion。

  • The lyrics are banal and the rhymes clumsy.

    歌词平淡老套，押韵生硬蹩脚。

4 N-COUNT 押韵短诗 A rhyme is a short poem which has rhyming words at the ends of its lines. 押韵短诗

  • He was teaching Helen a little rhyme.

    他当时正教海伦一首押韵小诗。

5See also:nursery rhyme 
6 N-UNCOUNT 押韵;用韵 Rhyme is the use of rhyming words as a technique in poetry. If something is written in rhyme, it is written as a poem in which the lines rhyme. 押韵;用韵

  • Porter stayed within the rules of rhyme.

    波特遵循押韵的规则。

  • The plays are in rhyme.

    这些剧本以韵文写成。

7 PHRASE 莫名其妙;无缘无故;毫无道理 If something happens or is done without rhyme or reason, there seems to be no logical reason for it to happen or be done. 莫名其妙;无缘无故;毫无道理

  • He picked people on a whim, without rhyme or reason.

    他选人时总是随心所欲，毫无根据。

SYN sense, meaning, plan, planning
