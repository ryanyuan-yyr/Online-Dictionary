transform
trænsfɔːʳm trænsˈfɔrm ˈtrænsˌfɔrm
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense transforms, present participle transforming, past tense , past participle transformed
1 VERB 转换;改变;改造 To transform something into something else means to change or convert it into that thing. 转换;改变;改造 [Also VERB n]

  • Your metabolic rate is the speed at which your body transforms food into energy. [VERB noun + into]

    新陈代谢率是身体把食物转换为能量的速度。

  • Delegates also discussed transforming them from a guerrilla force into a regular army. [V n + from/into]

    代表们还讨论了把他们从游击队武装改造为正规军的问题。

SYN change, convert, alter, translate  
transformation

  • Norah made plans for the transformation of an attic room into a study.

    诺拉计划把一个阁楼间改建成书房。

  • Chemical transformations occur.

    发生了化学转换。

2 VERB 改善;使改观;使改头换面 To transform something or someone means to change them completely and suddenly so that they are much better or more attractive. 改善;使改观;使改头换面 [Also VERB n + into]

  • The Minister said the Urban Development Corporation was now transforming the area. [VERB noun]

    部长声称城市开发公司如今正使该地区改头换面。

  • A cheap table can be transformed by an interesting cover… [VERB noun]

    一块趣味盎然的桌布就能使一张廉价桌子面目一新。

  • He said she had transformed him from a hard-drinking womaniser into a devoted husband and father. [V n + from/into]

    他说她已经把他由一个酗酒玩女人的浪荡子彻底改造成了一位忠诚的丈夫、一位慈爱的父亲。

SYN make over, overhaul, revamp, remake  
transformation

  • In the last five years he's undergone a personal transformation.

    过去 5 年里他整个人发生了全新的转变。

  • …one of the most astonishing economic transformations seen since the second world war.

    二战以来最惊人的经济改革之一
