stump
stʌmp stʌmp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense stumps, present participle stumping, past tense , past participle stumped
1 N-COUNT 残余部分;残段;残根 A stump is a small part of something that remains when the rest of it has been removed or broken off. 残余部分;残段;残根

  • If you have a tree stump, check it for fungus.

    要是有树桩的话，检查一下上面有没有长菌类。

  • The tramp produced a stump of candle from his deep pockets. [+ of]

    流浪汉从深口袋里掏出了一截蜡烛。

SYN tail end, end, remnant, remainder  
2 N-COUNT (板球三柱门的)柱 In cricket, the stumps are the three wooden sticks that are placed upright in the ground to form the wicket. (板球三柱门的)柱
3 VERB 把…难住;难倒;使…一筹莫展 If you are stumped by a question or problem, you cannot think of any solution or answer to it. 把…难住;难倒;使…一筹莫展

  • John is stumped by an unexpected question. [be VERB-ed]

    一个意想不到的问题把约翰难住了。

  • Well, maybe I stumped you on that one. [VERB noun]

    好吧，也许我在那件事上使你为难了。

SYN baffle, confuse, puzzle, snooker  
4 VERB 迈着沉重的步子走;跺着脚走 If you stump somewhere, you walk there with heavy steps. 迈着沉重的步子走;跺着脚走 [literary]

  • The Marshal stepped over the vacuum-cleaner and stumped out of the room. [VERB prep./adv.]

    元帅迈过真空吸尘器，脚步沉重地走出了房间。

SYN stamp, clump, stomp [informal] , trudge  
5 VERB (在选举前)作巡回演说，游说 If politicians stump the country or stump for a candidate, they travel around making campaign speeches before an election. (在选举前)作巡回演说，游说 [mainly US] [Also VERB]

  • When candidates went stumping around the country, people traveled for miles on foot, by horse, by carriage to hear them speak. [VERB noun]

    当竞选人在全国作巡回演说时，人们会步行、骑马或坐车数英里赶去听他们的演讲。

  • He was in Georgia stumping for Senator Wyche Fowler, a Democrat. [VERB + for]

    他正在佐治亚州为民主党参议员威奇·福勒作巡回演说。

6 PHRASE (选举前)作巡回演说，在游说 If politicians are on the stump, they are campaigning for an election. (选举前)作巡回演说，在游说 [mainly US]

  • The presidential candidates are on the stump today.

    总统候选人今天在作巡回演说。
