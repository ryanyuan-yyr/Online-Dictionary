attend
ətend əˈtɛnd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense attends, present participle attending, past tense , past participle attended
1 VERB 出席；参加 If you attend a meeting or other event, you are present at it. 出席；参加

  • Thousands of people attended the funeral. [VERB noun]

    数千人参加了葬礼。

  • The meeting will be attended by finance ministers from many countries. [be VERB-ed + by]

    很多国家的财政部长将出席会议。

  • We want the maximum number of people to attend to help us cover our costs. [VERB]

    我们希望有尽量多的人参加，以帮我们平衡支出。

SYN be present, go to, visit, be at  
2 VERB 去（学校、教堂等）；上（学） If you attend an institution such as a school, college, or church, you go there regularly. 去（学校、教堂等）；上（学） [Also VERB]

  • They attended college together at the University of Pennsylvania. [VERB noun]

    他们一起就读于宾夕法尼亚大学。

3 VERB 处理，料理（事情）；照顾，护理（受伤的人） If you attend to something, you deal with it. If you attend to someone who is hurt or injured, you care for them. 处理，料理（事情）；照顾，护理（受伤的人）

  • The staff will helpfully attend to your needs. [VERB + to]

    工作人员会帮忙满足您的需求。

  • There are more pressing matters to be attended to today. [VERB to noun]

    今天有更紧急的事情要处理。

  • The main thing is to attend to the injured. [VERB + to]

    首要任务是照顾伤者。
