sin
sɪn saɪn siːn sɪn sin
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense sins, present participle sinning, past tense , past participle sinned
1 N-VAR 罪;罪过;罪孽 Sin or a sin is an action or type of behaviour which is believed to break the laws of God. 罪;罪过;罪孽

  • The Vatican's teaching on abortion is clear: it is a sin.

    罗马教廷对堕胎的教义非常清楚：这是罪过。

  • Was it the sin of pride to have believed too much in themselves?

    他们过分自信，这是否犯了骄纵之罪？

SYN crime, offence, misdemeanour, error  
2 See also: cardinal sin  mortal sin 
3 VERB 犯戒律;犯过失 If you sin, you do something that is believed to break the laws of God. 犯戒律;犯过失

  • The Spanish Inquisition charged him with sinning against God and man. [VERB + against]

    西班牙宗教法庭指控他亵渎上帝和人类。

  • You have sinned and must repent your ways. [VERB]

    你犯了罪，必须为你的所作所为作出忏悔。

SYN transgress, offend, lapse, err  
sinner

  • I am a sinner and I need to repent of my sins.

    我是个罪人，必须忏悔我的罪过。

4 N-COUNT 罪恶;恶行;不道德的行为 A sin is any action or behaviour that people disapprove of or consider morally wrong. 罪恶;恶行;不道德的行为

  • ...the sin of arrogant hard-heartedness.

    傲慢无情的恶行

  • The ultimate sin was not infidelity, but public mention which led to scandal.

    最大的罪过不是通奸而是公众议论，是它导致了丑闻的发生。

5 PHRASE 同居 If you say that a man and a woman are living in sin, you mean that they are living together as a couple although they are not married. 同居 [old-fashioned]

  • She was living in sin with her boyfriend.

    她和男友同居。

6 a multitude of sins→see: multitude
