deplore
dɪplɔːʳ diˈplɔr dɪˈplɔr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense deplores, present participle deploring, past tense , past participle deplored
VERB 谴责;强烈反对 If you say that you deplore something, you think it is very wrong or immoral. 谴责;强烈反对 [formal]

  • He's a judo black belt but he says he deplores violence. [VERB noun]

    他是一名柔道黑带选手，但他说自己强烈反对暴力。

  • He deplored the fact that the Foreign Secretary was driven into resignation. [VERB noun]

    他对外交大臣被迫辞职一事表示强烈谴责。

  • I deplore what has happened. [VERB noun]

    我为所发生的事深感愤慨。

SYN disapprove of, condemn, object to, denounce
