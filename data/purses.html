purse
pɜːʳs pɜrs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense purses, present participle pursing, past tense , past participle pursed
1 N-COUNT （尤指女式）钱包 A purse is a very small bag that people, especially women, keep their money in. （尤指女式）钱包 [British]
in AM, use 美国英语用 change purse
2 N-COUNT 女用小包；坤包 A purse is a small bag that women carry. 女用小包；坤包 [US]

  • She looked at me and then reached in her purse for cigarettes.

    她看看我，接着伸手去包里拿香烟。

SYN handbag, bag, shoulder bag, pocket book  
in BRIT, use 英国英语用 bag, handbag
3 N-SING 资金；财力；财源 Purse is used to refer to the total amount of money that a country, family, or group has. 资金；财力；财源

  • The money could simply go into the public purse, helping to lower taxes.

    这笔钱可以直接划入公共资金，帮助降低税收。

SYN funds, means, money, resources  
4 VERB 缩拢，皱起，撅起（嘴唇） If you purse your lips, you move them into a small, rounded shape, usually because you disapprove of something or when you are thinking. 缩拢，皱起，撅起（嘴唇）

  • She pursed her lips in disapproval. [VERB noun]

    她撅起嘴唇，表示不赞成。

SYN pucker, close, contract, tighten
