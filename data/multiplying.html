multiply
mʌltɪplaɪ ˈmʌltəpli ˈmʌltəˌplaɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense multiplies, present participle multiplying, past tense , past participle multiplied
1 V-ERG (使)成倍地增加;(使)大量地增加 When something multiplies or when you multiply it, it increases greatly in number or amount. (使)成倍地增加;(使)大量地增加

  • Such disputes multiplied in the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries. [VERB]

    这类纠纷在18和19世纪大量增多。

  • Her husband multiplied his demands on her time. [VERB noun]

    她丈夫需要她的时间大大增加。

SYN increase, extend, expand, spread  
2 VERB 繁殖;滋生 When animals and insects multiply, they increase in number by giving birth to large numbers of young. 繁殖;滋生

  • These creatures can multiply quickly. [VERB]

    这些动物能迅速繁殖。

SYN reproduce, breed, propagate  
3 VERB 乘;使相乘 If you multiply one number by another, you add the first number to itself as many times as is indicated by the second number. For example 2 multiplied by 3 is equal to 6. 乘;使相乘

  • What do you get if you multiply six by nine? [V n + by]

    9乘以6得多少？

  • ...the remarkable ability to multiply huge numbers correctly without pen or paper. [VERB noun]

    不借助纸笔而能正确计算大数字乘积的非凡能力
