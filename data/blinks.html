blink
blɪŋk blɪŋk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense blinks, present participle blinking, past tense , past participle blinked
1 VERB 眨（眼） When you blink or when you blink your eyes, you shut your eyes and very quickly open them again. 眨（眼）

  • Kathryn blinked and forced a smile. [VERB]

    凯瑟琳眨了眨眼睛，挤出一丝微笑。

  • Suddenly, Momma's eyes blinked. [VERB]

    突然，妈妈眼睛一闪。

  • She was blinking her eyes rapidly. [VERB noun]

    她飞快地眨着眼睛。

  • He blinked at her. [VERB + at]

    他朝她眨了眨眼。

SYN flutter, wink, bat  
Blink is also a noun.

  • He kept giving quick blinks.

    他眼睛飞快地眨个不停。

2 VERB （灯光）闪烁，闪动，忽明忽灭 When a light blinks, it flashes on and off. （灯光）闪烁，闪动，忽明忽灭 [Also V out/off]

  • Green and yellow lights blinked on the surface of the harbour. [VERB]

    海港的水面上闪烁着绿色和黄色的灯光。

  • A warning light blinked on. [V on]

    警报灯闪亮了。

  • ...the blinking lights at the top of the Tutweiler Hotel. [VERB-ing]

    塔特怀勒酒店楼顶闪烁的灯光

SYN flash, flicker, sparkle, wink  
3 PHRASE [oft in PHR] 一眨眼的工夫；转眼之间；瞬间 If you say that something happens in the blink of an eye, you mean that it happens very quickly. 一眨眼的工夫；转眼之间；瞬间

  • It was all over in the blink of an eye.

    转眼之间，一切都结束了。

4 PHRASE （机器）不能正常工作，出故障，出毛病 If a machine goes on the blink, it stops working properly. （机器）不能正常工作，出故障，出毛病 [informal]

  • ...an old TV that's on the blink.

    坏了的旧电视机

SYN not working (properly), faulty, defective, playing up
