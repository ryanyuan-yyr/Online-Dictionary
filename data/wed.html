we'd
wɪd wiːd wɪd wid
1 (尤当 had 为助动词时，we had 的常用口语形式) We'd is the usual spoken form of 'we had', especially when 'had' is an auxiliary verb. (尤当 had 为助动词时，we had 的常用口语形式)

  • Come on, George, we'd better get back now.

    快点，乔治，我们最好现在就回去。

  • At the time we'd really nothing to tell the police.

    当时我们真的没有什么能跟警方说的。

2 (we would 的常用口语形式) We'd is the usual spoken form of 'we would'. (we would 的常用口语形式)

  • If we smoked, we'd light a cigarette and let her try it out.

    如果我们吸烟的话，会点一支让她也试试的。

  • I don't know how we'd have managed without her!

    我真不知道没有她我们该怎么办！

Wed.
(also Weds.)
Wed .is a written abbreviation for (书面缩略=) Wednesday .

  • Our big task for tomorrow (Wed.) is to get them exit visas.

    明天(星期三)我们最重要的任务就是拿到他们的出境签证。

wed
wed wɛd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense weds, past tense wedded
The form wed is used in the present tense and is the past tense. The past participle can be either wed or wedded. wed 的过去式与原形相同，过去分词为 wed 或 wedded。
See also: [no cont] newlywed  wedded  (与…)结婚;（使）完婚 If one person weds another or if two people wed or are wed, they get married. (与…)结婚;（使）完婚 [journalism, old-fashioned]

  • In 1952 she wed film director Roger Vadim. [VERB noun]

    1952年她嫁给了电影导演罗杰·瓦丹 。

  • The couple wed late at night in front of just nine guests. [VERB]

    深夜这对情侣在仅有的9位宾客面前举行了婚礼。

  • They were wed at Amiens last August. [be VERB-ed]

    他们去年8月在亚眠结婚。

  • His cousin was about to wed. [V (non-recip)]

    他表弟要结婚了。

SYN get married, marry, be united, tie the knot [informal]
