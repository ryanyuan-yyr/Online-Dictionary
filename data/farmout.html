farm out
PHRASAL VERB 外包出;分包 If you say that someone farms out work, especially work that you would normally expect them to do themselves, you mean that they give it to other people to do. 外包出;分包 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • The move is consistent with a trend for corporate legal staffs to do more work in-house, instead of farming it out to law firms. [V n P to n]

    这一举措符合让公司的法律专员在单位内部做更多的工作，而不是将工作分包给律师事务所的趋势。

  • We can take advantage of new technology more quickly by farming out computer operations. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    把计算机业务分包出去，我们就可以更快地利用新技术。

  • They've tended to farm out work to consultants. [V P n (not pron) to n]

    他们经常把业务分包给顾问。

SYN contract out, hire out, subcontract, outsource
