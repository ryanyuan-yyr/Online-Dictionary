go up
1 PHRASAL VERB （价格、数量或水平）上涨，上升 If a price, amount, or level goes up, it becomes higher or greater than it was. （价格、数量或水平）上涨，上升

  • Interest rates went up. [VERB PREP.]

    利率上调了。

  • The cost has gone up to $1.95 a minute. [V P + to/from/by]

    该项费用已上涨到每分钟1.95美元。

  • Prices have gone up 61 percent since deregulation. [V P amount]

    价格自解除管制以来已经上涨了61%。

2 PHRASAL VERB （建筑物等）被兴建，被建起 When a building, wall, or other structure goes up, it is built or fixed in place. （建筑物等）被兴建，被建起

  • He noticed a new building going up near Whitaker Park. [VERB PREP.]

    他注意到惠特克公园附近正在兴建一幢新楼。

3 PHRASAL VERB 爆炸；突然燃烧起来 If something goes up, it explodes or starts to burn, usually suddenly and with great intensity. 爆炸；突然燃烧起来

  • I was going to get out of the building in case it went up. [VERB PREP.]

    我怕大楼起火，正打算要跑出去。

  • The hotel went up in flames. [V P + in]

    这座宾馆燃起熊熊大火。

4 PHRASAL VERB （喊声或欢呼声）响起，大作 If a shout or cheer goes up, it is made by a lot of people together. （喊声或欢呼声）响起，大作

  • A cheer went up from the other passengers. [VERB PREP.]

    其他乘客发出一阵欢呼声。

5 PHRASAL VERB （运动队）晋级 In sport, if a team goes up, they move to a higher division in a league. （运动队）晋级 [British]

  • I fancy Leicester to go up. [VERB PREP.]

    我认为莱斯特队会晋级。
