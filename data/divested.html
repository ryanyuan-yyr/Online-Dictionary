divest
daɪvest dɪ- dəˈvɛst daɪˈvɛst
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense divests, present participle divesting, past tense , past participle divested
1 VERB 使摆脱;使丢弃;使放弃 If you divest yourself of something that you own or are responsible for, you get rid of it or stop being responsible for it. 使摆脱;使丢弃;使放弃 [formal]

  • The company divested itself of its oil interests. [V pron-refl + of]

    公司放弃了自己的石油股份。

2 VERB 使失去;剥夺 If something or someone is divested of a particular quality, they lose that quality or it is taken away from them. 使失去;剥夺 [formal]

  • ...in the 1960s, when sexual love had been divested of sin. [be VERB-ed + of]

    在20世纪60年代，性爱不再被看作是罪恶时

  • They have divested rituals of their original meaning. [VERB noun + of]

    他们使仪式失去了原来的意义。

  • Divested of the hype surrounding its launch, the show can now emerge as a full-fledged classic. [VERB-ed]

    没有了上映时的炒作，这场演出现在可以作为一部真正意义上的经典之作亮相了。

3 VERB 使脱去;使放下 If you divest someone of something that they are wearing or carrying, you take it off them or away from them. 使脱去;使放下 [old-fashioned] [Also VERB n]

  • ...the formalities of divesting her of her coat. [V n of n]

    为她脱去外衣的礼节

  • ...detectors installed at the entrances to make youngsters divest themselves of guns and knives. [V pron-refl of n]

    安装在入口处让年轻人放下身上带的枪和刀的探测器

SYN strip, remove, take off, undress
