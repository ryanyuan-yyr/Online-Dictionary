drag out
1 PHRASAL VERB 不必要地拖延;使持续过久 If you drag something out, you make it last for longer than is necessary. 不必要地拖延;使持续过久

  • The company was willing to drag out the proceedings for years. [VERB PREP. noun]

    公司愿意将诉讼拖上几年。

  • Let's get it over with as soon as possible, rather than drag it out. [VERB noun PREP.]

    我们尽快把它完成吧，不要再拖了。

SYN prolong, extend, keep going, stretch out  
2 PHRASAL VERB 套…的话;说服…说出 If you drag something out of a person, you persuade them to tell you something that they do not want to tell you. 套…的话;说服…说出

  • The families soon discovered that every piece of information had to be dragged out of the authorities. [VERB noun PREP. + of]

    这些家庭很快发现每条消息都得从有关当局口中套出来。
