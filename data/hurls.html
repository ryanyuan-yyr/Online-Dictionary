hurl
hɜːʳl hɜrl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense hurls, present participle hurling, past tense , past participle hurled
1 VERB 猛投;用力掷 If you hurl something, you throw it violently and with a lot of force. 猛投;用力掷

  • Groups of angry youths hurled stones at police. [VERB noun prep.]

    成群结伙愤怒的年轻人朝警察投掷石块。

  • One prisoner set fire to rags and hurled them into the courtyard. [VERB noun prep.]

    一名囚犯将破布点着，扔进院子。

  • Simon caught the grenade and hurled it back. [VERB noun with adv.]

    西蒙接住手榴弹，又把它大力掷了回去。

  • Gangs rioted last night, breaking storefront windows and hurling rocks and bottles. [VERB noun]

    几个帮派昨晚聚众闹事，他们打碎商店临街的玻璃窗，并投掷石块和瓶子。

SYN throw, fling, chuck [informal] , send  
2 VERB 气势汹汹地吐（恶言） If you hurl abuse or insults at someone, you shout insults at them aggressively. 气势汹汹地吐（恶言） [Also VERB n]

  • How would you handle being locked in the back of a cab while the driver hurled abuse at you? [VERB noun + at]

    如果你被锁在出租车后座上而且还遭到司机的无礼谩骂，你会怎么办？
