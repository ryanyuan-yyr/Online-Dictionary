overshoot
oʊvəʳʃuːt oʊvəʳʃuːt ˌəʊvəˈʃuːt ˌoʊvərˈʃut ˈoʊvərˌʃut
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense overshoots, present participle overshooting, past tense , past participle overshot
The verb is pronounced /ˌəʊvə'ʃuːt/. The noun is pronounced /'əʊvəʃuːt/. 动词读作/ˌəʊvə'ʃuːt/。名词读作 /'əʊvəʃuːt/。
1 VERB 超过，越过（预定地点） If you overshoot a place that you want to get to, you go past it by mistake. 超过，越过（预定地点） [Also VERB]

  • The plane apparently overshot the runway after landing. [VERB noun]

    飞机看样子在降落后滑出了跑道。

  • They had already overshot the corner once. [VERB noun]

    他们刚才已经错过那个街角一次了。

2 VERB 超出，突破（预算） If a government or organization overshoots its budget, it spends more than it had planned to. 超出，突破（预算）

  • The government usually overshot its original spending target. [VERB noun]

    政府通常会超出其最初的开支目标。

Overshoot is also a noun.

  • ...the 100 million pounds overshoot in the cost of building the hospital.

    修建医院费用中超支的1亿英镑
