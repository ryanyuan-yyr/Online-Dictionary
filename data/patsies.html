patsy
pætsi ˈpætsɪ ˈpætsi
Word forms: plural patsies
N-COUNT 糊涂蛋；容易上当的人 If you describe someone as a patsy, you mean that they are rather stupid and are easily tricked by other people, or can be made to take the blame for other people's actions. 糊涂蛋；容易上当的人 [US, informal, disapproval]

  • Davis was nobody's patsy.

    戴维斯不会上任何人的当。

  • He has long felt that Ray was set up, that he was a patsy.

    他一直觉得雷是被人陷害的，他是个糊涂蛋。
