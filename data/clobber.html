clobber
klɒbəʳ ˈklɑbər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense clobbers, present participle clobbering, past tense , past participle clobbered
1 N-UNCOUNT 财物;(尤指)衣服 You can refer to someone's possessions, especially their clothes, as their clobber . 财物;(尤指)衣服 [British, informal]
SYN belongings, things, effects, property  
2 VERB 打;揍 If you clobber someone, you hit them. 打;揍 [informal]

  • Hillary clobbered him with a vase. [VERB noun]

    希拉里用花瓶打了他。

SYN batter, beat, assault, smash  
3 VERB 使受到严重影响;使遭受沉重打击 If a person or company is clobbered by something, they are very badly affected by it. 使受到严重影响;使遭受沉重打击 [informal]

  • The construction industry was clobbered by recession. [be VERB-ed]

    建筑业受到经济衰退的严重影响。

  • Sticky weather in May and June clobbered sales of Thorntons' chocolates. [VERB noun]

    五六月湿热的天气严重影响了桑顿巧克力的销售。
