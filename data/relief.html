relief
rɪliːf rɪˈlif
Word forms: plural reliefs
1 N-UNCOUNT [oft a NOUN] 宽慰;安心;欣慰;解脱 If you feel a sense of relief, you feel happy because something unpleasant has not happened or is no longer happening. 宽慰;安心;欣慰;解脱

  • I breathed a sigh of relief.

    我如释重负地松了口气。

  • The news will come as a great relief to the French authorities. [+ to]

    这个消息会让法国当局大大松一口气。

  • To his relief a loud knock on the door spared him from giving an explanation.

    一声重重的敲门声让他松了一口气，他可以不用解释了。

  • It's a relief to get out of the office once in a while.

    偶尔走出办公室去透透气也是一种调剂。

SYN ease, release, comfort, cure  
2 N-UNCOUNT (疼痛或苦痛的)解除，免除，减轻，缓解 If something provides relief from pain or distress, it stops the pain or distress. (疼痛或苦痛的)解除，免除，减轻，缓解

  • This brought considerable relief from the pain.

    这相当大地缓解了疼痛。

  • ...a self-help programme which can give lasting relief from the torment of hay fever. [+ from]

    可以持续缓解枯草热病痛的自助项目

SYN rest, respite, let-up [informal] , relaxation  
3 N-UNCOUNT [oft NOUN n, n NOUN] 救济;救援物资 Relief is money, food, or clothing that is provided for people who are very poor, or who have been affected by war or a natural disaster. 救济;救援物资

  • Relief agencies are stepping up efforts to provide food, shelter and agricultural equipment.

    救济机构正加大力度提供食物、住所和农用器械。

  • ...famine relief.

    饥荒救济

4 N-COUNT [usually NOUN n] 代班人;换班工人 A relief worker is someone who does your work when you go home, or who is employed to do it instead of you when you are sick. 代班人;换班工人

  • No relief drivers were available.

    找不到代班司机。

SYN replacement, cover, supply, reserve  
5 N-COUNT 浮雕 A relief is a sculpture that is carved out of a flat vertical surface. 浮雕 [technical]
6 See also: bas-relief  tax relief
