relax
rɪlæks rɪˈlæks
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense relaxes, present participle relaxing, past tense , past participle relaxed
1 V-ERG (使)放松;(使)松弛 If you relax or if something relaxes you, you feel more calm and less worried or tense. (使)放松;(使)松弛

  • I ought to relax and stop worrying about it. [VERB]

    我应该放松一下，不要再担心它了。

  • For the first time since his arrival he relaxed slightly. [VERB]

    自从他来了之后这是他第一次稍微放松一点儿。

  • Do something that you know relaxes you. [VERB noun]

    做点儿让你自己放松的事情。

SYN calm down, calm, soothe, unwind  
2 V-ERG (使）（身体)放松;（使）松弛 When a part of your body relaxes, or when you relax it, it becomes less stiff or firm. (使）（身体)放松;（使）松弛

  • Massage is used to relax muscles, relieve stress and improve the circulation. [VERB noun]

    按摩可以使肌肉放松，缓解压力和促进血液循环。

  • His face relaxes into a contented smile. [VERB]

    他面部肌肉放松下来，露出了满意的微笑。

SYN make less tense, soften, loosen up, unbend  
3 VERB 松手;放开;放松 If you relax your grip or hold on something, you hold it less tightly than before. 松手;放开;放松

  • He gradually relaxed his grip on the arms of the chair. [VERB noun]

    他紧握椅子扶手的手逐渐松开了。

SYN lessen, reduce, ease, relieve  
4 V-ERG 放宽，放松，削弱(规定或控制) If you relax a rule or your control over something, or if it relaxes, it becomes less firm or strong. 放宽，放松，削弱(规定或控制)

  • Rules governing student conduct relaxed somewhat in recent years. [VERB]

    近年来约束学生行为的规定有所放宽。

  • How much can the President relax his grip over the nation?. [VERB noun]

    总统可以对国家的控制放松到什么样的程度？

  • Some analysts believe that the government soon will begin relaxing economic controls. [VERB noun]

    一些分析人士认为政府不久将开始放松经济管制。

SYN moderate, ease, relieve, weaken
