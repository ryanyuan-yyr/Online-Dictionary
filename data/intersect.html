intersect
ɪntəʳsekt ˌɪntərˈsɛkt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense intersects, present participle intersecting, past tense , past participle intersected
1 V-RECIP (线、道路)(与…)相交，(与…)交叉 If two or more lines or roads intersect, they meet or cross each other. You can also say that one line or road intersects another. (线、道路)(与…)相交，(与…)交叉

  • The orbit of this comet intersects the orbit of the Earth. [VERB noun]

    这颗彗星的轨道与地球的轨道交叉。

  • The circles will intersect in two places. [VERB]

    这两个圆将在两处交叉。

  • The centre of the city is full of tiny intersecting alleyways.

    市中心到处是纵横交错的小巷道。

2 V-RECIP (与…)交叉;(与…)相关联 If one thing intersects with another or if two things intersect, the two things have a connection at a particular point. (与…)交叉;(与…)相关联

  • ...the ways in which historical events intersect with individual lives. [VERB + with]

    历史事件与个人生活发生关联的方式

  • Their histories intersect. [VERB]

    他们的历史相互交叉。

3 VERB [usually passive] 横穿;贯穿;横断 If a place, area, or surface is intersected by things such as roads or lines, they cross it. 横穿;贯穿;横断

  • The city is intersected by three main waterways. [be VERB-ed]

    该城市由 3 条主要水路贯穿。

SYN cross, meet, cut, divide
