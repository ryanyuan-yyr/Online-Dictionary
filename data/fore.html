fore
fɔːʳ fɔr foʊr
1 PHRASE 崭露头角;引人关注 If someone or something comes to the fore in a particular situation or group, they become important or popular. 崭露头角;引人关注

  • A number of low-budget independent films brought new directors and actors to the fore.

    许多低成本独立制作的电影使新导演和新演员崭露头角。

2 ADJ [ADJ n] (动物、船只或飞行器)前部的，前端的 Fore is used to refer to parts at the front of an animal, ship, or aircraft. (动物、船只或飞行器)前部的，前端的

  • There had been no direct damage in the fore part of the ship.

    船的前部没有受到直接破坏。

  • ...a swelling appeared below his near fore knee.

    左前腿膝关节下出现的一个肿块

3 ADV [n ADVERB, ADVERB after verb] 在船头;在飞行器前部 If something is fore in a boat or plane, it is at the front of it. 在船头;在飞行器前部

  • Our yacht was well equipped with two double cabins fore and aft.

    我们的游艇设施完备，首尾有两个双人舱。

  • He loosened his very strong hold on the ship's rail and glanced fore and aft.

    他松开紧紧抓住护栏的手，往船的两头扫了一眼。
