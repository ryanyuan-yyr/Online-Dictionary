plough into
1 PHRASAL VERB 猛力冲入；猛撞 If something, for example a car, ploughs into something else, it goes out of control and crashes violently into it. 猛力冲入；猛撞

  • A young girl and her little brother were seriously hurt when a car ploughed into them on a crossing. [VERB PREP. noun]

    一个小姑娘和她的弟弟在十字路口被车撞成了重伤。

2 PHRASAL VERB 把（大笔资金）投入 If you say that money is ploughed into something such as a business or a service, you are emphasizing that the amount of money which is invested in it or spent on it in order to improve it is very large. 把（大笔资金）投入 [business, emphasis]

  • Huge sums of private capital will be ploughed into the ailing industries of the east. [be V-ed P n/v-ing]

    巨额私人资本将投入东部处境困难的一些行业。

  • He claimed he ploughed all his money into his antique business. [V n P n/v-ing]

    他声称把所有的钱都投进了自己的古董生意。
