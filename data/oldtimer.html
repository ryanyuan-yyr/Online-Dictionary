old-timer ˈoʊldˈtaɪmər ˌ taɪmmər
Word forms: plural old-timers
1 N-COUNT 老居民；老前辈；老手 If you refer to someone as an old-timer, you mean that he or she has been living in a particular place or doing a particular job for a long time. 老居民；老前辈；老手 [informal]

  • The old-timers and established families clutched the reins of power.

    久居此地的居民和根基稳固的家族牢牢掌控着权力。

2 N-COUNT 老人 An old man is sometimes referred to as an old-timer . 老人 [US, informal]

  • The old-timers used to recall how hot 1886 was.

    老人们常常回想起1886年的酷热。
