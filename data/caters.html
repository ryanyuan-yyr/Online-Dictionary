cater
keɪtəʳ ˈkeɪtər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense caters, present participle catering, past tense , past participle catered
1 VERB 为…提供服务;满足…的需要;接待 In British English, to cater for a group of people means to provide all the things that they need or want. In American English, you say you cater to a person or group of people. 为…提供服务;满足…的需要;接待

  • Minorca is the sort of place that caters for families. [VERB + for]

    梅诺卡岛是那种适合家庭旅游的地方。

  • We cater to an exclusive clientele. [VERB + to]

    我们只接待特定的高端客户。

2 VERB 将…考虑进去;考虑到 In British English, to cater for something means to take it into account. In American English, you say you cater to something. 将…考虑进去;考虑到

  • We have to cater for demand. [VERB for noun]

    我们得考虑需求。

  • ...shops that cater for the needs of men. [VERB + for]

    男性用品商店

  • Exercise classes cater to all levels of fitness. [VERB + to]

    各种健身班会照顾到不同身体状况的人。

3 VERB 为…提供饮食;承办…的酒席 If a person or company caters for an occasion such as a wedding or a party, they provide food and drink for all the people there. 为…提供饮食;承办…的酒席

  • Nunsmere Hall can cater for receptions of up to 300 people. [VERB + for]

    纳恩斯米尔酒店最多可以同时接待300人。

  • The chef is pleased to cater for vegetarian diets. [VERB for noun]

    厨师非常乐意为客人做素食。

  • Does he cater parties too? [VERB noun]

    他也承办派对的饮食吗？
