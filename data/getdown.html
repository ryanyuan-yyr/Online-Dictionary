get down
1 PHRASAL VERB 使沮丧；使忧郁 If something gets you down, it makes you unhappy. 使沮丧；使忧郁

  • At times when my work gets me down, I like to fantasize about being a farmer. [VERB noun PREP.]

    有时当工作让我沮丧时，我喜欢幻想自己是一个农场主。

SYN depress, discourage, bring down, daunt  
2 PHRASAL VERB 俯身；跪下；趴下 If you get down, you lower your body until you are sitting, kneeling, or lying on the ground. 俯身；跪下；趴下

  • She got down on her hands and knees on the floor. [VERB PREP. + on]

    她双手撑地跪在地上。

  • 'Get down!' she yelled. 'Somebody's shooting!' [VERB PREP.]

    “趴下！”她大声喊道，“有人开枪！”

3 PHRASAL VERB 写下；记下 If you get something down, especially something that someone has just said, you write it down. 写下；记下 [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • The idea has been going around in my head for quite a while and now I am getting it down on paper. [VERB noun PREP.]

    这个想法在我脑海里萦绕许久了，我现在就将它写在纸上。

4 PHRASAL VERB （费力地）吞下；勉强咽下 If you get food or medicine down, you swallow it, especially with difficulty. （费力地）吞下；勉强咽下 [informal] [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • I bit into a hefty slab of bread and cheese. When I had got it down I started talking. [VERB noun PREP.]

    我咬了一大块面包和奶酪，强咽下去之后开始说话。
