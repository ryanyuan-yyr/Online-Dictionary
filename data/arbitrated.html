arbitrate
ɑːʳbɪtreɪt ˈɑrbəˌtreɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense arbitrates, present participle arbitrating, past tense arbitrated
VERB 仲裁；公断 When someone in authority arbitrates between two people or groups who are in dispute, they consider all the facts and make an official decision about who is right. 仲裁；公断

  • He arbitrates between investors and members of the association. [V + between]

    他在投资者和该协会成员之间作出仲裁。

  • The tribunal had been set up to arbitrate in the dispute. [VERB]

    曾设立过特别法庭对此纷争作出仲裁。

SYN decide, judge, determine, settle  
arbitrator

  • He served as an arbitrator in a series of commercial disputes in India.

    他担任过印度一系列商业纠纷的仲裁者。
