short-change
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense short-changes, present participle short-changing, past tense , past participle short-changed
1 VERB 少找给…钱 If someone short-changes you, they do not give you enough change after you have bought something from them. 少找给…钱

  • The cashier made a mistake and short-changed him. [VERB noun]

    收银员弄错了，少找了他钱。

2 VERB [usually passive] 欺骗;亏待;克扣 If you are short-changed, you are treated unfairly or dishonestly, often because you are given less of something than you deserve. 欺骗;亏待;克扣

  • Women are in fact still being short-changed in the press. [be VERB-ed]

    实际上女性在新闻界仍然没有受到公平对待。
