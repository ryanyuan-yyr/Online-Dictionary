shuttle
ʃʌtəl ˈʃʌtəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense shuttles, present participle shuttling, past tense , past participle shuttled
1 N-COUNT 同space shuttle A shuttle is the same as a space shuttle . 同space shuttle
2 N-COUNT [oft NOUN n] 往来于两地之间的航班(或班车、火车) A shuttle is a plane, bus, or train which makes frequent journeys between two places. 往来于两地之间的航班(或班车、火车)

  • ...the BA shuttle to Glasgow.

    飞往格拉斯哥的英国航空公司的航班

  • ...shuttle flights between London and Manchester.

    往返于伦敦和曼彻斯特的航班

3 V-ERG (使)频繁往来于(两地之间) If someone or something shuttles or is shuttled from one place to another place, they frequently go from one place to the other. (使)频繁往来于(两地之间) [Also VERB n preposition/adverb]

  • He and colleagues have shuttled back and forth between the three capitals. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他和同事们在这3个首都之间往来穿梭。

  • Machine parts were also being shuttled across the border without authorisation. [be VERB-ed prep./adv.]

    机器零部件也在未经批准的情况下出入边境。

SYN go back and forth, commute, go to and fro, alternate  
4 N-COUNT (织布用的)梭 A shuttle is a piece of equipment used in weaving. It takes a thread backwards and forwards over the other threads in order to make a piece of cloth. (织布用的)梭
