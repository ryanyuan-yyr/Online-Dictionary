slice
slaɪs slaɪs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense slices, present participle slicing, past tense , past participle sliced
1 N-COUNT (指食物切下的)片，薄片 A slice of bread, meat, fruit, or other food is a thin piece that has been cut from a larger piece. (指食物切下的)片，薄片

  • Try to eat at least four slices of bread a day. [+ of]

    每天尽量至少吃 4 片面包。

  • ...water flavored with a slice of lemon.

    加了一片柠檬调味的水

SYN piece, segment, portion, wedge  
2 VERB 将…切成薄片 If you slice bread, meat, fruit, or other food, you cut it into thin pieces. 将…切成薄片

  • Helen sliced the cake. [VERB noun]

    海伦把蛋糕切成片。

  • Slice the steak into long thin slices. [VERB noun + into]

    将牛排切成长长的薄片。

SYN cut, divide, carve, segment  
Slice up means the same as slice.slice up 同 slice

  • I sliced up an onion.

    我把一个洋葱切了片。

  • He began slicing the pie up.

    他开始把馅饼切片。

3 N-COUNT 部分;份 You can use slice to refer to a part of a situation or activity. 部分;份

  • Fiction takes up a large slice of the publishing market. [+ of]

    小说在出版市场上占了很大的份额。

  • ...a car that represents a slice of motoring history.

    一辆代表了一段汽车发展史的轿车

SYN part, share, piece, proportion  
4 VERB (网球、高尔夫球等中)曲打，斜击，使(球)侧旋 In tennis, golf, and other sports, if you slice a ball, you hit its edge rather than its centre, so that it travels at an angle. (网球、高尔夫球等中)曲打，斜击，使(球)侧旋 [Also VERB n]

  • The captain swung his left foot, but sliced the ball wide. [V n adv/adj]

    队长飞起左脚一踢，球却侧着旋出去老远。

Slice is also a noun.

  • ...a ball that would reduce hooks and slices.

    一只能够减少左曲球和侧旋球的球

5 VERB 切开;划破 If something slices through a substance, it moves through it quickly, like a knife. 切开;划破 [literary] [Also VERB n]

  • The ship sliced through the water. [VERB through noun]

    轮船在水中破浪前行。
