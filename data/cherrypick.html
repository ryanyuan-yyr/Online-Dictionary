cherry-pick
tʃeripɪk ˈtʃɛriˌpɪk
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense cherry-picks, present participle cherry-picking, past tense , past participle cherry-picked
VERB 精挑细选;择优而取 If someone cherry-picks people or things, they choose the best ones from a group of them, often in a way that other people consider unfair. 精挑细选;择优而取

  • The club is in debt while others are queuing to cherry-pick their best players. [VERB noun]

    这家俱乐部债台高筑，其他俱乐部则排着队等候挑选它最优秀的球员。
