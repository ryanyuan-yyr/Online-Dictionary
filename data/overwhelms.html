overwhelm
oʊvəʳhwelm ˌoʊvərˈhwɛlm ˌoʊvərˈwɛlm
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense overwhelms, present participle overwhelming, past tense , past participle overwhelmed
1 VERB （情感、事件等）使不知所措，使难以承受 If you are overwhelmed by a feeling or event, it affects you very strongly, and you do not know how to deal with it. （情感、事件等）使不知所措，使难以承受

  • He was overwhelmed by a longing for times past. [be VERB-ed]

    他一心渴望回到从前。

  • The need to talk to someone, anyone, overwhelmed her. [VERB noun]

    她迫不及待地要找个人说说话，随便是谁都行。

SYN overcome, overpower, devastate [informal] , stagger  
overwhelmed

  • Sightseers may be a little overwhelmed by the crowds and noise.

    拥挤的人群和喧闹的噪音可能会让游客有些茫然不知所措。

2 VERB 征服；制服；击败 If a group of people overwhelm a place or another group, they gain complete control or victory over them. 征服；制服；击败

  • It was clear that one massive Allied offensive would overwhelm the weakened enemy. [VERB noun]

    毫无疑问，盟军一次大规模的进攻就会击溃已是强弩之末的敌军。

SYN destroy, beat, defeat, overcome
