pursuit
pəʳsjuːt -suːt pərˈsut pərˈsjut
Word forms: plural pursuits
1 N-UNCOUNT 追求；寻求 Your pursuit of something is your attempts at achieving it. If you do something in pursuit of a particular result, you do it in order to achieve that result. 追求；寻求

  • ...a young man whose relentless pursuit of excellence is conducted with single-minded determination. [+ of]

    一心一意、坚定不移地追求完美的年轻人

  • ...individuals who impoverish their families in pursuit of some dream.

    为了追求某个梦想而使家庭陷于贫困的人

2 N-UNCOUNT 实行；贯彻；实施 The pursuit of an activity, interest, or plan consists of all the things that you do when you are carrying it out. 实行；贯彻；实施

  • The vigorous pursuit of policies is no guarantee of success. [+ of]

    坚决贯彻方针政策并不能保证成功。

3 N-UNCOUNT [usu in N of n] 追赶；追逐；追捕 Someone who is in pursuit of a person, vehicle, or animal is chasing them. 追赶；追逐；追捕

  • ...a police officer who drove a patrol car at more than 120mph in pursuit of a motor cycle.

    以超过每小时120英里的时速驾驶巡逻车追赶一辆摩托车的警官

4 N-SING [the NOUN, oft NOUN n] （自行车赛和滑冰竞赛中的）追逐赛 In cycling and skating, the pursuit is a race in which two competitors or teams start on opposite sides of a circular track and try to catch up with each other. （自行车赛和滑冰竞赛中的）追逐赛

  • Moreau took gold in the five-kilometre individual pursuit competition.

    莫罗在个人五公里追逐赛中获得金牌。

5 N-COUNT [usually plural] 娱乐；爱好 Your pursuits are your activities, usually activities that you enjoy when you are not working. 娱乐；爱好

  • They both love outdoor pursuits.

    他们俩都喜欢户外活动。

  • His favourite childhood pursuits were sailing, swimming and cycling.

    儿时他最大的爱好是帆船运动、游泳和骑自行车。

SYN occupation, activity, interest, line  
6 PHRASE 穷追不舍 If you are in hot pursuit of someone, you are chasing after them with great determination. 穷追不舍

  • I rushed through with Sue in hot pursuit.

    我冲了过去，苏在后面穷追不舍。
