frog-march ˈfrɔgmɑrtʃ
(also frogmarch)Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense frog-marches, present participle frog-marching, past tense , past participle frog-marched
VERB 将…反拧双臂强行押走;挟持而行 If you are frog-marched somewhere, someone takes you there by force, holding you by the arms or another part of your body so that you have to walk along with them. 将…反拧双臂强行押走;挟持而行

  • He was frog-marched through the kitchen and out into the yard. [be VERB-ed prep./adv.]

    他被扭住双臂强推着走过厨房，来到院子里。

  • They arrested the men and frog-marched them to the local police station. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他们逮捕了这些人，并把他们押送到当地警察局。
