ashore
əʃɔːʳ əˈʃɔr
ADV [ADVERB after verb, be ADVERB] 上岸；到陆地上 Someone or something that comes ashore comes from the sea onto the shore. 上岸；到陆地上

  • Oil has come ashore on a ten mile stretch to the east of Plymouth.

    绵延10英里的浮油已经到达了普利茅斯东岸。

  • Once ashore, the vessel was thoroughly inspected.

    船只刚一靠岸就接受了彻底检查。

SYN on land, on the beach, on the shore, aground
