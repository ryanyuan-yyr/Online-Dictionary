duel
djuːəl ˈdjuːəl duː- ˈduəl ˈdjuəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense duels, present participle duelling, past tense , past participle duelled
in AM, use 美国英语用 dueling, dueled
1 N-COUNT 决斗 A duel is a formal fight between two people in which they use guns or swords in order to settle a quarrel. 决斗

  • He had killed a man in a duel.

    他在一场决斗中杀了一名男子。

SYN single combat, fight, battle, confrontation  
2 N-COUNT (双方的)争斗,冲突 You can refer to a conflict between two people or groups as a duel . (双方的)争斗,冲突

  • The area has been the scene of sporadic artillery duels over the last six weeks.

    在过去的 6 周中,该地区曾经发生过零星的炮击。

3 V-RECIP 进行决斗;卷入决斗 To duel means to fight a duel or be involved in a conflict. 进行决斗;卷入决斗

  • We duelled for two years and Peterson made the most of it, playing us off against each other. [VERB]

    我们争斗了两年，彼得森充分利用这点挑拨我俩对阵。

  • ...two silver French duelling pistols. [VERB-ing]

    两把银制法国决斗手枪

SYN fight, struggle, clash, compete
