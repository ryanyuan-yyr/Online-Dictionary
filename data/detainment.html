detain
dɪteɪn diˈteɪn dɪˈteɪn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense detains, present participle detaining, past tense , past participle detained
1 VERB 拘留;扣押 When people such as the police detain someone, they keep them in a place under their control. 拘留;扣押 [formal]

  • The act allows police to detain a suspect for up to 48 hours. [VERB noun]

    该法令允许警方将嫌疑犯扣押最多48小时。

  • He was arrested and detained for questioning. [VERB noun]

    他遭到逮捕、拘留以接受问讯。

SYN hold, arrest, confine, restrain  
2 VERB 阻留;耽搁 To detain someone means to delay them, for example by talking to them. 阻留;耽搁 [formal]

  • Thank you. We won't detain you any further. [VERB noun]

    非常感谢。我们就不再耽搁您的时间了。

SYN delay, keep, stop, hold up
