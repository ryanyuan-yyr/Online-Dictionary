clump
klʌmp klʌmp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense clumps, present participle clumping, past tense , past participle clumped
1 N-COUNT (树木等的)丛，簇，群 A clump of things such as trees or plants is a small group of them growing together. (树木等的)丛，簇，群

  • ...a clump of trees bordering a side road. [+ of]

    旁路边的树丛

  • ...a sweetly scented perennial that grows in clumps.

    一簇簇芳香宜人的多年生植物

SYN cluster, group, mass, bunch  
2 N-COUNT (电线、头发等的)团，堆 A clump of things such as wires or hair is a group of them collected together in one place. (电线、头发等的)团，堆 [Also + of]

  • I was combing my hair and it was just falling out in clumps.

    我梳头的时候，头发一团一团地往下掉。

3 VERB 拖着沉重的步子走 If someone clumps somewhere, they walk there with heavy, clumsy steps. 拖着沉重的步子走

  • They went clumping up the stairs. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他们拖着沉重的脚步走上楼。

  • Men in big construction boots were clumping in and out with plans in their hands. [VERB prep./adv.]

    一群男人穿着大建筑靴，手中拿着图纸，噔噔噔地走进走出。

SYN stomp, stamp, stump, thump  
4 VERB 聚集成群;凝集成块 If things clump together, they gather together and form small groups or lumps. 聚集成群;凝集成块

  • Brown rice takes longer to cook but it doesn't clump together as easily as white rice. [V + together]

    糙米不容易熟，但不会像精米那样容易粘在一起。
