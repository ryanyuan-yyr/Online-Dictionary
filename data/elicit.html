elicit
ɪlɪsɪt iˈlɪsɪt ɪˈlɪsɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense elicits, present participle eliciting, past tense , past participle elicited
1 VERB 引起;引出 If you elicit a response or a reaction, you do or say something which makes other people respond or react. 引起;引出

  • Mr Norris said he was hopeful that his request would elicit a positive response. [VERB noun]

    诺里斯先生说希望他的请求能够引起积极反响。

  • The crime elicited an outcry against illicit drugs. [VERB noun]

    那起罪行引发了打击违禁药品的呼声。

SYN bring about, cause, derive, bring out  
2 VERB 探出;套出 If you elicit a piece of information, you get it by asking the right questions. 探出;套出 [formal]

  • Phone calls elicited no further information. [VERB noun]

    几个电话都没有打听到进一步的消息。
