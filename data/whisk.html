whisk
hwɪsk hwɪsk wɪsk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense whisks, present participle whisking, past tense , past participle whisked
1 VERB 迅速送走;匆匆带走 If you whisk someone or something somewhere, you take them or move them there quickly. 迅速送走;匆匆带走

  • He whisked her across the dance floor. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他带着她飞快地穿过舞池。

  • I was whisked away in a police car. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    我被一辆警车匆匆带走了。

SYN rush, sweep, hurry  
2 VERB 搅拌，搅打(鸡蛋、奶油等) If you whisk something such as eggs or cream, you stir it very fast, often with an electric device, so that it becomes full of small bubbles. 搅拌，搅打(鸡蛋、奶油等)

  • Just before serving, whisk the cream. [VERB noun]

    在上桌之前，要搅打一下奶油。

  • Gently fold in the whisked egg whites. [VERB-ed]

    轻轻地调入搅拌好的蛋清。

  • In a separate bowl, whisk together the remaining sugar and the yolks. [VERB noun with together]

    用另外一只碗把剩下的糖和蛋黄搅打均匀。

SYN beat, mix vigorously, stir vigorously, whip  
3 N-COUNT 搅拌器;打蛋器 A whisk is a kitchen tool used for whisking eggs or cream. 搅拌器;打蛋器

  • Using a whisk, mix the yolks and sugar to a smooth paste.

    用打蛋器将蛋黄和糖打成均匀的糊状。

  • ...an electric whisk.

    电动搅拌机
