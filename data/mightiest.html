mighty
maɪti ˈmaɪti
Word forms: comparative mightier, superlative mightiest
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 强大的;强有力的;巨大的 Mighty is used to describe something that is very large or powerful. 强大的;强有力的;巨大的 [literary]

  • There was a flash and a mighty bang.

    一道闪光和一声巨响接踵而至。

  • ...a land marked with vast lakes and mighty rivers.

    一片拥有大片湖泊和宽广河流的土地

SYN great, large, huge, grand  
2 ADV-GRADED [ADVERB adjective/adverb] (置于形容词和副词之前表示强调)很，非常，十分 Mighty is used in front of adjectives and adverbs to emphasize the quality that they are describing. (置于形容词和副词之前表示强调)很，非常，十分 [mainly US, informal, emphasis]

  • It's something you'll be mighty proud of.

    这是你们将会非常引以为豪的事情。

  • Being paper money, it rotted away mighty fast.

    由于是纸币，所以烂得很快。

3 See also: high and mighty
