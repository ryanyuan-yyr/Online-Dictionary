revenge
rɪvendʒ rɪˈvɛndʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense revenges, present participle revenging, past tense , past participle revenged
1 N-UNCOUNT 报仇;报复 Revenge involves hurting or punishing someone who has hurt or harmed you. 报仇;报复 [Also + for]

  • The attackers were said to be taking revenge on the 14-year-old, claiming he was a school bully. [+ on/against]

    据说，这些攻击者是在报复这个14岁的孩子，说他是学校的小霸王。

  • The killings were said to have been in revenge for the murder of her lover.

    据说，她杀人是为了替她被害的情人报仇。

SYN retaliation, satisfaction, vengeance, reprisal  
2 VERB 向…报仇;报复 If you revenge yourself on someone who has hurt you, you hurt them in return. 向…报仇;报复 [written]

  • The Sunday Mercury accused her of trying to revenge herself on her former lover. [V pron-refl + on]

    《星期日信使报》指责她企图报复她以前的情人。

  • ...the relatives of murdered villagers wanting to revenge the dead. [VERB noun]

    想要为死者报仇的被害村民的亲属

SYN avenge, repay, vindicate, pay (someone) back
