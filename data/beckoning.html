beckon
bekən ˈbɛkən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense beckons, present participle beckoning, past tense , past participle beckoned
1 VERB （招手）示意，召唤 If you beckon to someone, you signal to them to come to you. （招手）示意，召唤 [Also VERB]

  • He beckoned to the waiter. [VERB + to]

    他招呼服务员过来。

  • I beckoned her over. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    我示意她过来。

  • Hughes beckoned him to sit down on a sofa. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    休斯示意他坐在沙发上。

SYN gesture, sign, wave, indicate  
2 VERB 吸引；引诱；诱惑 If something beckons, it is so attractive to someone that they feel they must become involved in it. 吸引；引诱；诱惑 [Also V + to]

  • All the attractions of the peninsula beckon. [VERB]

    半岛上的各处景点无不令人心驰神往。

  • The bright lights of Hollywood beckon many. [VERB noun]

    好莱坞的霓虹灯吸引着很多人。

SYN lure, call, draw, pull [informal]  
3 VERB 很可能发生在…身上 If something beckons for someone, it is very likely to happen to them. 很可能发生在…身上

  • The big time beckons for him. [VERB + for]

    辉煌时刻在向他召唤。

  • Old age beckons. [VERB]

    岁月催人老。

SYN draw near, near, move towards someone, come close
