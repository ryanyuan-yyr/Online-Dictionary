rogue
roʊg roʊg
Word forms: plural rogues
1 N-COUNT 无赖;流氓;恶棍 A rogue is a man who behaves in a dishonest or criminal way. 无赖;流氓;恶棍

  • Mr Ward wasn't a rogue at all.

    沃德先生根本不是恶棍。

SYN scoundrel [old-fashioned] , crook [informal] , villain, fraudster  
2 N-COUNT [oft adjective NOUN] 捣蛋鬼;小淘气;小坏蛋 If a man behaves in a way that you do not approve of but you still like him, you can refer to him as a rogue . 捣蛋鬼;小淘气;小坏蛋 [feelings]

  • ...Falstaff, the loveable rogue.

    福斯塔夫，那个可爱的捣蛋鬼

3 ADJ [ADJ n] (人或事物)不寻常的，破坏性的 A rogue element is someone or something that behaves differently from others of its kind, often causing damage. (人或事物)不寻常的，破坏性的

  • Computer systems throughout the country are being affected by a series of mysterious rogue programs, known as viruses.

    全国的计算机系统都感染了一系列诡异的流氓程序，即病毒。

  • The rogue male is not a twentieth-century phenomenon.

    男性不安分守己并非20世纪特有的现象。
