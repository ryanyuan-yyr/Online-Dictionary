ford
fɔːʳd fɔrd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense fords, present participle fording, past tense , past participle forded
1 N-COUNT 浅滩;河流浅水处 A ford is a shallow place in a river or stream where it is possible to cross safely without using a boat. 浅滩;河流浅水处
SYN crossing, causeway, crossing place  
2 VERB 涉渡;涉过 If you ford a river or stream, you cross it without using a boat, usually at a shallow point. 涉渡;涉过

  • They were guarding the bridge, so we forded the river. [VERB noun]

    他们驻守在那座桥上，所以我们只能涉水过河。

SYN cross, pass over, traverse, go across
