snipe
snaɪp snaɪp
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense snipes, present participle sniping, past tense , past participle sniped, plural snipe
1 VERB 批评;攻击;中伤 If someone snipes at you, they criticize you. 批评;攻击;中伤

  • The Spanish media were still sniping at the British press yesterday. [VERB + at]

    西班牙媒体昨天仍然在攻击英国媒体。

sniping

  • This leaves him vulnerable to sniping from within his own party.

    这使他容易受到来自党内的攻击。

2 VERB 狙击;放冷枪 To snipe at someone means to shoot at them from a hidden position. 狙击;放冷枪

  • Gunmen have repeatedly sniped at US Army positions.

    持枪匪徒一再朝美军阵地放冷枪。

  • A member of the security forces was killed in a sniping incident. [VERB-ing]

    有一名安全部队成员在一场狙击战中被打死。

SYN shoot at, open fire on, hit at, blast at [slang]  
3 N-COUNT 鹬;沙锥 A snipe is a type of bird with a very long beak which lives in wet areas of land. 鹬;沙锥
