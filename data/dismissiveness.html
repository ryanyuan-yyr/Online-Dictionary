dismissive
dɪsmɪsɪv dɪsˈmɪsɪv
ADJ-GRADED 拒绝考虑…的;对…轻视的;对…鄙视的 If you are dismissive of someone or something, you say or show that you think they are not important or have no value. 拒绝考虑…的;对…轻视的;对…鄙视的

  • Mr Jones was dismissive of the report, saying it was riddled with inaccuracies. [+ of]

    琼斯先生对报告不屑一顾，称其中充斥着谬误。

  • ...the dismissive attitude scientists often take in regard to questions such as telepathy or homeopathic medicine.

    涉及心电感应、顺势疗法等问题时科学家们常抱有的鄙视态度

SYN contemptuous, scornful, disdainful, insulting  
dismissively

  • 'Forget it,' he replied dismissively.

    “算了，”他不屑地答道。
