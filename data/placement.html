placement
pleɪsmənt ˈpleɪsmənt
Word forms: plural placements
1 N-UNCOUNT 放置；布置；部署 The placement of something or someone is the act of putting them in a particular place or position. 放置；布置；部署

  • The treatment involves the placement of twenty-two electrodes in the inner ear. [+ of]

    这一治疗需要在内耳放置22个电极。

SYN positioning, stationing, arrangement, location  
2 N-COUNT （培训中的）实习安排 If someone who is training gets a placement, they get a job for a period of time which is intended to give them experience in the work they are training for. （培训中的）实习安排

  • He had a six-month work placement with the Japanese government. [+ with]

    他被安排在日本政府部门实习了6个月。

SYN appointment, employment, engagement, assignment  
3 N-UNCOUNT （人员的）安插，安置 The placement of someone in a job, home, or school is the act or process of finding them a job, home, or school. （人员的）安插，安置

  • The children were waiting for placement in a foster care home. [+ in]

    这些孩子在抚育院等待安置。

  • A job placement program exists to help those who are unemployed.

    就业安置计划是为了帮助那些失业者。

4 N-COUNT 收容所；安置所 You can refer to a home that is found for someone who is unable to look after themselves, for example a child, as a placement . 收容所；安置所

  • This home seemed like a good placement for Sarah.

    这家养育院对萨拉来说似乎是一个合适的收容之所。
