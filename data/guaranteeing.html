guarantee
gærəntiː ˌgærənˈti ˈgærənˌti
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense guarantees, present participle guaranteeing, past tense , past participle guaranteed
1 VERB 确保；使必然发生 If one thing guarantees another, the first is certain to cause the second thing to happen. 确保；使必然发生 [Also VERB n n]

  • Surplus resources alone do not guarantee growth. [VERB noun]

    仅有富余的资源并不能确保发展。

  • ...a man whose fame guarantees that his calls will nearly always be returned. [VERB that]

    其名望能确保他的呼吁几乎总能得到回应的人

SYN ensure, secure, assure, warrant  
2 N-COUNT [NOUN that] 保证 Something that is a guarantee of something else makes it certain that it will happen or that it is true. 保证

  • A famous old name on a firm is not necessarily a guarantee of quality. [+ of]

    公司悠久的品牌并不能确保商品的质量。

  • There is still no guarantee that a formula could be found.

    还没有把握是否能找到一个配方。

3 VERB 保证…必定发生；承诺做；答应提供 If you guarantee something, you promise that it will definitely happen, or that you will do or provide it for someone. 保证…必定发生；承诺做；答应提供

  • Most states guarantee the right to free and adequate education. [VERB noun]

    大多数州都保障人们免费享受充分教育的权利。

  • All students are guaranteed campus accommodation for their first year. [be VERB-ed noun]

    保证安排所有的学生第一年在校内住宿。

  • We guarantee that you will find a community with which to socialise. [VERB that]

    我们保证你们会找到自己的交际圈。

  • We guarantee to refund your money if you are not delighted with your purchase. [VERB to-infinitive]

    若您对所购商品不满意，我们保证退款。

  • ...a guaranteed income of £1.6 million. [VERB-ed]

    可确保的160万英镑的收入

SYN promise, pledge, undertake, swear  
Guarantee is also a noun.

  • The Editor can give no guarantee that they will fulfil their obligations.

    《编辑家》无法保证他们会履行职责。

  • California's state Constitution includes a guarantee of privacy.

    加利福尼亚的州宪法涵盖对隐私权的保障。

4 N-COUNT [oft under N] 保修单 A guarantee is a written promise by a company to replace or repair a product free of charge if it has any faults within a particular time. 保修单

  • Whatever a guarantee says, when something goes wrong, you can still claim your rights from the shop.

    不论保修单如何规定，若商品出了问题，消费者仍然可以去商店维护自身权益。

  • It was still under guarantee.

    它还在保修期内。

5 VERB 为…提供质量保证；担保 If a company guarantees its product or work, they provide a guarantee for it. 为…提供质量保证；担保

  • Some builders guarantee their work. [VERB noun]

    一些建筑公司对其承建工程提供质量保证。

  • All Dreamland's electric blankets are guaranteed for three years. [VERB noun]

    所有的梦乡牌电热毯质保3年。

  • ... parts of guaranteed quality. [VERB-ed]

    有质量保证的零件

6 N-COUNT 担保金；担保物；抵押品 A guarantee is money or something valuable which you give to someone to show that you will do what you have promised. 担保金；担保物；抵押品

  • Males between 18 and 20 had to leave a deposit as a guarantee of returning to do their military service. [+ of]

    18至20岁的男性必须缴纳一笔保证金，作为回来服兵役的担保。

SYN security, earnest [old-fashioned] , collateral, surety
