audience
ɔːdiəns ˈɔdiəns
Word forms: plural audiences
1 N-COUNT-COLL [with singular or plural verb] 观众；听众 The audience at a play, concert, film, or public meeting is the group of people watching or listening to it. 观众；听众

  • The entire audience broke into loud applause.

    全场观众爆发出热烈的掌声。

  • He was speaking to an audience of students at the Institute for International Affairs.

    他在国际事务学院给学生们作演讲。

SYN spectators, company, house, crowd  
2 N-COUNT-COLL [with singular or plural verb] （电视或广播节目的）观众，听众 The audience for a television or radio programme consists of all the people who watch or listen to it. （电视或广播节目的）观众，听众

  • The concert will be relayed to a worldwide television audience estimated at one thousand million.

    这场音乐会将向全球约10亿电视观众转播。

3See also:studio audience 
4 N-COUNT-COLL [with singular or plural verb, usually singular] 读者 The audience of a writer or artist is the people who read their books or look at their work. 读者

  • Say's writings reached a wide audience during his lifetime.

    在塞伊有生之年，他的作品拥有大量的读者。

  • She began to find a receptive audience for her work.

    她开始为作品寻找知音。

SYN public, market, following, fans  
5 N-COUNT [usually singular] （与要人的）接见，会见，拜见 If you have an audience with someone important, you have a formal meeting with them. （与要人的）接见，会见，拜见

  • The Prime Minister will seek an audience with the Queen later this morning. [+ with]

    今天上午晚些时候首相将会觐见女王陛下。

SYN interview, meeting, hearing, exchange
