adjudge
ədʒʌdʒ əˈdʒʌdʒ əˈdʒʌdʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense adjudges, present participle adjudging, past tense , past participle adjudged
VERB [usually passive] 判决;裁决 If someone is adjudged to be something, they are judged or considered to be that thing. 判决;裁决 [formal]

  • He was adjudged to be guilty. [be VERB-ed to-infinitive]

    他被判有罪。

  • He was adjudged the winner by 54 votes to 3. [be VERB-ed noun]

    他被裁定以54票对3票获胜。

SYN judge, determine, declare, decide
