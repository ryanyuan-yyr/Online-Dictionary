dehumanize
diːhjuːmənaɪz diˈhjuməˌnaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dehumanizes, present participle dehumanizing, past tense , past participle dehumanized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 dehumanise
VERB 使失去人性;使无人性 If you say that something dehumanizes people, you mean it takes away from them good human qualities such as kindness, generosity, and independence. 使失去人性;使无人性

  • The years of civil war have dehumanized all of us. [VERB noun]

    连年内战剥夺了我们所有人的人性。

dehumanizing

  • ...the brutal, dehumanising experience of slavery.

    野蛮的、剥夺人性的奴隶制生活

dehumanization

  • She was horrified at the dehumanisation involved in much of the imagery.

    这些意象在很大程度上表现出的人性泯灭让她深感惊骇。
