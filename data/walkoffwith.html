walk off with
1 PHRASAL VERB 不经允许就拿走;偷走 If someone walks off with something that does not belong to them, they take it without permission. 不经允许就拿走;偷走 [informal]

  • I'll bet you walked off with my coat, too. [VERB PREP. PREP. noun]

    我打赌你还顺手拿走了我的大衣。

2 PHRASAL VERB 轻松赢得;轻易获得 If you walk off with something such as a prize, you win it or get it very easily. 轻松赢得;轻易获得 [journalism]

  • The delighted pensioner walked off with a £2,000 prize. [VERB PREP. PREP. noun]

    那个退休老人轻松赢得了2,000英镑的奖金，高兴得不得了。
