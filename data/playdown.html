play down
PHRASAL VERB 降低…的重要性；贬低；淡化 If you play down something, you try to make people believe that it is not particularly important. 降低…的重要性；贬低；淡化

  • Western diplomats have played down the significance of the reports. [VERB PREP. noun]

    西方外交官刻意贬低这些报告的重要性。

  • He plays down rumours that he aims to become a Labour MP. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    他对那些说他想要成为工党下院议员的传言轻描淡写。

  • Both London and Dublin are playing the matter down. [VERB noun PREP.]

    伦敦和都柏林方面对此事都在有意淡化。

SYN minimize, make light of, gloss over, talk down
