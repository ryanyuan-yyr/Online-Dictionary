fickle
fɪkəl ˈfɪkəl
1 ADJ-GRADED 变化无常的;善变的 If you describe someone as fickle, you disapprove of them because they keep changing their mind about what they like or want. 变化无常的;善变的 [disapproval]

  • The group has been notoriously fickle in the past.

    这伙人过去一直是出了名的善变。

fickleness

  • ...the fickleness of businessmen and politicians.

    商人和政客的反复无常

2 ADJ-GRADED 无常的;靠不住的 If you say that something is fickle, you mean that it often changes and is unreliable. 无常的;靠不住的

  • Orta's weather can be fickle.

    奥尔塔的天气变幻莫测。

Quotations
The fickleness of the women I love is matched only by the infernal constancy of the women who love meGeorge Bernard Shaw The Philanderer
