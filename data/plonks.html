plonk
plɒŋk plɑŋk
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense plonks, present participle plonking, past tense , past participle plonked
1 VERB 重重放下；随意放下 If you plonk something somewhere, you put it or drop it there heavily and carelessly. 重重放下；随意放下 [British, informal]

  • She plonked the beer on the counter. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    她砰的一声把啤酒搁在柜台上。

in AM, use 美国英语用 plunk
2 VERB 使重重坐下；使随意坐下 If you plonk yourself somewhere, you sit down carelessly without paying attention to the people around you. 使重重坐下；使随意坐下 [British, informal]

  • Steve plonked himself down on a seat and stayed motionless as the bus moved away. [V pron-refl adv/prep]

    史蒂夫一屁股坐在位子上，公共汽车开动起来时他一动都不动。

in AM, use 美国英语用 plunk
3 N-MASS 廉价酒；劣质酒 Plonk is cheap or poor quality wine. 廉价酒；劣质酒 [mainly British, informal]
4 N-SING; SOUND 砰的一声；扑通声 A plonk is a heavy, hollow sound. 砰的一声；扑通声 [mainly British]

  • ...the dry plonk of tennis balls. [+ of]

    网球发出的沉闷的砰砰声

  • Then plonk, down went the fork.

    随后啪的一声，叉子掉了。
