do over
1 PHRASAL VERB 重做;重复 If you do a task over, you perform it again from the beginning. 重做;重复 [US]

  • If she had the chance to do it over, she would have hired a press secretary. [VERB noun PREP.]

    如果有机会重头来过，她会雇一个新闻秘书。

2 PHRASAL VERB 抢劫;搜掠 If someone does a place over, they steal things or search the place and leave it very untidy. 抢劫;搜掠 [British, informal] [Also VERB PREPOSITION n (not pron)]

  • The door was open. They had done the place over. [VERB noun PREP.]

    门开着。他们早将此处洗劫一空。

3 PHRASAL VERB 痛打;猛击 To do someone over means to hurt them badly, for example by hitting or kicking them. 痛打;猛击 [British, informal] [Also VERB PREPOSITION n (not pron)]

  • We could get someone to do him over, couldn't we? [VERB noun PREP.]

    我们可以找个人把他痛打一顿，行吗？
