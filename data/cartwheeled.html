cartwheel
kɑːʳthwiːl ˈkɑrtˌhwil ˈkɑrtˌwil
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , plural cartwheels, present participle cartwheeling, past tense , past participle cartwheeled
1 N-COUNT 侧手翻;侧身筋斗 If you do a cartwheel, you do a fast, circular movement with your body. You fall sideways, put your hands on the ground, swing your legs over, and return to a standing position. 侧手翻;侧身筋斗

  • Their four children turn cartwheels in the grass as we talk.

    我们说话的时候，他们家的4个孩子在草地上玩侧手翻。

2 VERB (人、车等)连续翻滚 If a person or something such as a vehicle cartwheels down or across something in an uncontrollable way, they turn over and over. (人、车等)连续翻滚 [Also VERB]

  • Suddenly I was cartwheeling down the slope, all orientation gone. [VERB prep./adv.]

    突然，我骨碌碌地滚下坡，完全晕头转向了。

  • The two cars cartwheeled horrifyingly into the sand trap at the first corner. [VERB prep./adv.]

    那两辆车在第一个拐弯处翻滚着跌入了沙坑，场面十分骇人。
