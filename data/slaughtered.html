slaughter
slɔːtəʳ ˈslɔtər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense slaughters, present participle slaughtering, past tense , past participle slaughtered
1 VERB [usually passive] 杀戮;屠杀;残杀;滥杀 If large numbers of people or animals are slaughtered, they are killed in a way that is cruel or unnecessary. 杀戮;屠杀;残杀;滥杀

  • Thirty four people were slaughtered while queuing up to cast their votes. [be VERB-ed]

    有 34 人在排队投票时遭到残杀。

  • Whales and dolphins are still being slaughtered for commercial gain. [be VERB-ed]

    仍然有人为了商业利益大肆捕杀鲸鱼和海豚。

SYN butcher, kill, slay [archaic , literary] , destroy  
Slaughter is also a noun.

  • ...a war where the slaughter of civilians was commonplace.

    在战争中杀戮平民的事情再平常不过

  • The annual slaughter of wildlife in Italy is horrific.

    在意大利，每年大量捕杀野生动物的现象令人震惊。

2 VERB 屠宰，宰杀(动物) To slaughter animals such as cows and sheep means to kill them for their meat. →see usage note at: kill 屠宰，宰杀(动物)

  • Lack of chicken feed means that chicken farms are having to slaughter their stock.

    鸡饲料的缺乏意味着养鸡场将不得不杀掉饲养的鸡。

Slaughter is also a noun.

  • More than 491,000 sheep were exported to the Continent for slaughter last year.

    去年有 491,000 多只羊被出口到欧洲大陆供宰杀。
