transition
trænzɪʃən trænˈzɪʃən trænˈsɪʃən
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense transitions, present participle transitioning, past tense , past participle transitioned
1 N-VAR 转变;过渡;变革;变迁 Transition is the process in which something changes from one state to another. 转变;过渡;变革;变迁

  • The transition to a multi-party democracy is proving to be difficult. [+ to]

    事实证明，向多党民主制的转型非常艰难。

  • ...a period of transition.

    过渡时期

SYN change, passing, development, shift  
2 VERB (从某一状态或活动)转变，过渡(到另一状态或活动) If someone transitions from one state or activity to another, they move gradually from one to the other. (从某一状态或活动)转变，过渡(到另一状态或活动) [Also VERB n]

  • Most of the discussion was on what needed to be done now as we transitioned from the security issues to the challenging economic issues. [VERB + from/to]

    随着我们由安全议题转向具有挑战性的经济议题，讨论最多的就是目前需要做些什么。

  • There was a significant decline in the size of the business as the company transitioned to an intellectual property company. [VERB + to]

    随着公司向知识产权公司转型，其业务规模显著下降。
