detract
dɪtrækt diˈtrækt dɪˈtrækt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense detracts, present participle detracting, past tense , past participle detracted
VERB 减损;诋毁;贬低 If one thing detracts from another, it makes it seem less good or impressive. 减损;诋毁;贬低 [Also V n + from]

  • The publicity could detract from our election campaign. [VERB + from]

    这些宣传报道可能会有损我们的竞选活动。
