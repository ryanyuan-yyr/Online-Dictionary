purchase
pɜːʳtʃɪs ˈpɜrtʃəs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense purchases, present participle purchasing, past tense , past participle purchased
1 VERB 买；购买；采购 When you purchase something, you buy it. 买；购买；采购 [formal]

  • He purchased a ticket and went up on the top deck. [VERB noun]

    他买了一张票，然后上到顶层甲板。

  • Most of those shares were purchased from brokers. [VERB noun]

    大部分的股份是从经纪人手中购得的。

purchaser

  • The broker will get 5% if he finds a purchaser.

    找到一个买家，经纪人就能提成5%。

  • The group is the second largest purchaser of fresh fruit in the US.

    该集团是美国新鲜水果的第二大买主。

2 N-UNCOUNT 购买行为 The purchase of something is the act of buying it. 购买行为 [formal]

  • Some of the receipts had been for the purchase of cars. [+ of]

    其中一些收据是购买汽车时开的。

3See also:hire purchase 
4 N-COUNT 所购之物；购置物 A purchase is something that you buy. 所购之物；购置物 [formal]

  • She opened the tie box and looked at her purchase. It was silk, with maroon stripes.

    她打开领带盒，看着她买回的东西：一条带有褐红色条纹的丝制领带。

SYN acquisition, buy, investment, property  
5 N-UNCOUNT [oft a NOUN] 紧握；牢牢抓住 If you get a purchase on something, you manage to get a firm grip on it. 紧握；牢牢抓住 [formal]

  • I got a purchase on the rope and pulled.

    我紧抓住绳子，用力去拉。

  • I couldn't get any purchase with the screwdriver on the damn screws.

    我用螺丝刀拧那些该死的螺丝钉时一点也使不上劲儿。

SYN grip, hold, support, footing
