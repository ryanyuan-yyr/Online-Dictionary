outfit
aʊtfɪt ˈaʊtˌfɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense outfits, present participle outfitting, past tense , past participle outfitted
1 N-COUNT 全套服装 An outfit is a set of clothes. 全套服装

  • She was wearing an outfit she'd bought the previous day.

    她穿着前一天买的那套衣服。

  • I spent lots of money on smart new outfits for work.

    我花大笔钱购置了上班时穿的漂亮新套装。

SYN costume, dress, clothes, clothing  
2 N-COUNT 机构;组织 You can refer to an organization as an outfit . 机构;组织

  • He works for a private security outfit.

    他在一家私人保安公司工作。

  • We are a professional outfit and we do require payment for our services.

    我们是一家专业机构，对所提供服务确实要收取费用。

SYN group, company, team, set  
3 VERB 装备；配备；供给 To outfit someone or something means to provide them with equipment for a particular purpose. 装备；配备；供给 [mainly US] [Also VERB n + as]

  • They outfitted him with artificial legs. [VERB noun + with]

    他们为他安了假腿。

  • I outfitted an attic bedroom as a studio. [V n with/as n]

    我把阁楼卧室改装成了工作室。
