detox
diːtɒks ˈdiːˌtɒks diˈtɑks ˈdiˌtɑks
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense detoxes, present participle detoxing, past tense , past participle detoxed
1 N-UNCOUNT [oft NOUN n] 脱瘾治疗;戒毒;戒酒 Detox is treatment given to people who are addicted to drugs or alcohol in order to stop them from being addicted. 脱瘾治疗;戒毒;戒酒

  • A patient going through acute detox will have an assigned nurse nearby.

    接受急性脱瘾治疗的病人身边会有一名指派的护士看护。

  • ...a detox therapist.

    脱瘾治疗专家

2 N-COUNT [oft NOUN n] (身体的)解毒，排毒 A detox is a treatment that is intended to remove poisonous or harmful substances from your body. (身体的)解毒，排毒

  • Overhaul your body with a cleansing detox.

    通过清洁排毒法对身体进行“大扫除”。

  • Give yourself a healthy glow on our detox diet.

    我们的解毒食谱将会让您容光焕发。

3 VERB (使)脱瘾;(使)戒毒;(使)戒酒 If someone who is addicted to drugs or alcohol detoxes, or if another person detoxes them, they undergo treatment which stops them from being addicted. (使)脱瘾;(使)戒毒;(使)戒酒

  • ...mums trying to detox their kids. [VERB noun]

    正努力帮助她们的孩子脱瘾的妈妈们

  • ...drugs binges and failed attempts to detox. [VERB]

    常常肆意吸毒并屡次戒毒失败

4 VERB （使）(身体)解毒;（使）排毒 If you detox, or if something detoxes your body, you do something to remove poisonous or harmful substances from your body. （使）(身体)解毒;（使）排毒

  • It might be an idea to detox after the indulgences of Christmas. [VERB]

    圣诞节一番放纵之后排排毒或许是个不错的主意。

  • Honey can help to detox the body. [VERB noun]

    蜂蜜能帮助身体解毒。
