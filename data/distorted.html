distort
dɪstɔːʳt dɪˈstɔːt dɪˈstɔrt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense distorts, present participle distorting, past tense , past participle distorted
1 VERB 歪曲;扭曲;曲解 If you distort a statement, fact, or idea, you report or represent it in an untrue way. 歪曲;扭曲;曲解

  • The media distorts reality; categorises people as all good or all bad. [VERB noun]

    媒体会歪曲事实，将人说得不是完美无缺就是一无是处。

  • The minister has said his remarks at the weekend have been distorted. [VERB noun]

    部长说他周末的讲话被曲解了。

distorted

  • These figures give a distorted view of the significance for the local economy.

    这些数据会使人误解其对地方经济的重要性。

2 V-ERG (使）变形;(使)失真 If something you can see or hear is distorted or distorts, its appearance or sound is changed so that it seems unclear. (使）变形;(使)失真

  • A painter may exaggerate or distort shapes and forms. [VERB noun]

    画家可能会对线条和形状进行夸大或扭曲。

  • His size was persistently distorted by the cartoonists. [VERB noun]

    漫画家们总是把他画得变形了。

  • This caused the sound to distort. [VERB]

    这导致了声音的失真。

distorted

  • Sound was becoming more and more distorted through the use of hearing aids.

    使用助听器会使声音变得越来越失真。
