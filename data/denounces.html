denounce
dɪnaʊns diˈnaʊns dɪˈnaʊns
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense denounces, present participle denouncing, past tense , past participle denounced
1 VERB 谴责;痛斥 If you denounce a person or an action, you criticize them severely and publicly because you feel strongly that they are wrong or evil. 谴责;痛斥

  • The letter called for trade union freedom and civil rights, but did not openly denounce the regime. [VERB noun]

    这封信呼吁保障工会自由和公民权利，但没有公开谴责该政权。

  • German leaders denounced the attacks and pleaded for tolerance. [VERB noun]

    德国领导人谴责袭击活动，恳请各方克制。

  • Some 25,000 demonstrators denounced him as a traitor. [VERB noun + as]

    大约 2.5万名示威者痛斥他是叛徒。

2 VERB 告发;检举 If you denounce someone who has broken a rule or law, you report them to the authorities. 告发;检举 [Also VERB n + to]

  • ...informers who might denounce you at any moment. [VERB noun]

    随时都可能告发你们的那些告密者

SYN report, expose, betray, accuse
