cherish
tʃerɪʃ ˈtʃɛrɪʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense cherishes, present participle cherishing, past tense , past participle cherished
1 VERB 怀有，抱有(希望);怀念(过去) If you cherish something such as a hope or a pleasant memory, you keep it in your mind for a long period of time. 怀有，抱有(希望);怀念(过去)

  • The president will cherish the memory of this visit to Ohio. [VERB noun]

    总统将铭记这次俄亥俄之行。

  • It was a wonderful occasion which we will cherish for many years to come. [VERB noun]

    那是一个美好的时刻，我们将多年不忘。

SYN cling to, prize, treasure, hold dear  
cherished

  • ...the cherished dream of a world without wars.

    梦想着一个没有战争的世界

2 VERB 珍爱;珍惜;疼爱 If you cherish someone or something, you take good care of them because you love them. 珍爱;珍惜;疼爱

  • He genuinely loved and cherished her. [VERB noun]

    他由衷地爱恋并珍惜她。

  • The previous owners had cherished the house. [VERB noun]

    房子先前的主人非常爱惜这房子。

SYN care for, love, support, comfort  
cherished

  • He described the picture as his most cherished possession.

    他说那幅画是他最珍爱的财产。

3 VERB 珍视，维护，捍卫(权利、特权或原则) If you cherish a right, a privilege, or a principle, you regard it as important and try hard to keep it. 珍视，维护，捍卫(权利、特权或原则)

  • These people cherish their independence and sovereignty. [VERB noun]

    这些人民珍视他们的独立和主权。

cherished

  • Freud called into question some deeply cherished beliefs.

    弗洛伊德对人们先前深信不疑的一些观念提出了质疑。
