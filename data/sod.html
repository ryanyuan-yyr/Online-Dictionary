sod
sɒd sɑd
Word forms: plural sods
1 N-COUNT 该死的东西;讨厌的家伙 If someone calls another person or something such as a job a sod, they are expressing anger or annoyance towards that person or thing. 该死的东西;讨厌的家伙 [British, informal, rude, disapproval]
2 EXCLAM (表示不在乎)去它的，爱谁谁 If someone uses an expression such as sod it ,sod you, or sod that, they are expressing anger or showing that they do not care about something. (表示不在乎)去它的，爱谁谁 [British, informal, rude, feelings]
3 N-SING 草地表层;草皮 The sod is the surface of the earth, with the grass and roots that are growing in it. 草地表层;草皮 [literary]
SYN rogue, scoundrel, devil, villain  
4 PHRASE 屁都没有 Sod all means 'nothing at all'. 屁都没有 [British, informal, rude, emphasis]
5 PHRASE 墨菲法则(一种认为凡有可能出错的事终将出错的理论) Sod's Law or sod's law is the idea that if something can go wrong, it will go wrong. 墨菲法则(一种认为凡有可能出错的事终将出错的理论) [British, informal]

  • This was sod's law: when the spare tyre is in use, you will have a puncture.

    这真是墨菲法则：一用备用轮胎，保准扎孔。
