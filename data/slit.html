slit
slɪt slɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense slits, present participle slitting
The form slit is used in the present tense and is the past tense and past participle. slit 的过去式和过去分词与原形相同。
1 VERB 切开;剖开;割开 If you slit something, you make a long narrow cut in it. 切开;剖开;割开

  • They say somebody slit her throat. [VERB noun]

    他们说有人割断了她的喉咙。

  • He began to slit open each envelope. [V n with open]

    他开始裁开每个信封。

  • She was wearing a white dress slit to the thigh. [V-ed + to/from]

    她身穿一条开叉开到了大腿的白色连衣裙。

SYN cut (open), rip, slash, knife  
2 N-COUNT 狭长的切口;裂口 A slit is a long narrow cut. 狭长的切口;裂口

  • Make a slit in the stem about half an inch long. [+ in]

    在树干上切一道大约半英寸长的口子。

SYN cut, gash, incision, tear  
3 N-COUNT 狭缝 A slit is a long narrow opening in something. 狭缝

  • She watched them through a slit in the curtains. [+ in]

    她透过窗帘上的一道窄缝注视着他们。

SYN opening, split, crack, aperture
