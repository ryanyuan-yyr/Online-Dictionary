gnaw
nɔː nɔ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense gnaws, present participle gnawing, past tense , past participle gnawed
1 VERB 咬；啃；啮 If people or animals gnaw something or gnaw at it, they bite it repeatedly. 咬；啃；啮

  • Woodlice attack living plants and gnaw at the stems. [V + at/on]

    木虱会侵害活体植物并啃咬它们的茎。

  • Melanie gnawed a long, painted fingernail. [VERB noun]

    梅拉妮咬着涂过指甲油的长指甲。

SYN bite, chew, nibble, munch  
2 VERB 折磨；使不安 If a feeling or thought gnaws at you, it causes you to keep worrying. 折磨；使不安 [written]

  • Doubts were already gnawing away at the back of his mind. [VERB + at]

    心底的疑团已经开始困扰他。

  • Mary Ann's exhilaration gave way to gnawing fear. [VERB-ing]

    玛丽·安从欣喜若狂变得忧惧不安。

SYN distress, worry, trouble, harry
