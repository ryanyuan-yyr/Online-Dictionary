trade in
1 PHRASAL VERB 以(旧物)折价贴换同类新物 If you trade in an old car or appliance, you give it to the person you are buying a new one from so that you pay less. 以(旧物)折价贴换同类新物

  • He had a Rolls-Royce, and he traded it in for two matching silver Range Rovers. [VERB noun PREP.]

    他有辆劳斯莱斯，他把它折价贴换了两辆外形相配的银色路虎车。

  • Richard refused to trade in his old Canon cameras. [VERB PREP. noun]

    理查德拒绝用他的旧佳能相机以旧换新。

N-COUNT [oft NOUN n] 折价贴换交易；以旧换新交易 A trade-in is an arrangement in which someone buys something such as a new car or washing machine at a reduced price by giving their old one, as well as money, in payment. 折价贴换交易；以旧换新交易

  • ...the trade-in value of the car.

    这辆车的折价贴换交易价值
