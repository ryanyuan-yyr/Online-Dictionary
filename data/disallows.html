disallow
dɪsəlaʊ ˌdɪsəˈlaʊ ˌdɪsəˈlaʊ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense disallows, present participle disallowing, past tense , past participle disallowed
VERB 不允许;不接受;驳回 If something is disallowed, it is not allowed or accepted officially, because it has not been done correctly. 不允许;不接受;驳回

  • England scored again, but the whistle had gone and the goal was disallowed. [be VERB-ed]

    英格兰又进一球，但哨声已响，进球无效。

  • The Internal Revenue Service sought to disallow the payments. [VERB noun]

    国内收入署试图驳回这些缴纳款。

  • It was a shock to hear him rule that my testimony would be disallowed. [VERB noun]

    听到他裁决我的证词将不被采纳时，我很震惊。

SYN reject, refuse, ban, dismiss
