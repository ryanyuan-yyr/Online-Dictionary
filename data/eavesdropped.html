eavesdrop
iːvzdrɒp ˈivzˌdrɑp
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense eavesdrops, present participle eavesdropping, past tense , past participle eavesdropped
VERB 偷听;窃听 If you eavesdrop on someone, you listen secretly to what they are saying. 偷听;窃听

  • The government illegally eavesdropped on his telephone conversations. [VERB + on]

    政府非法窃听了他的电话。

  • The housemaid eavesdropped from behind the kitchen door. [VERB]

    女佣在厨房门后偷听。

SYN listen in, spy, overhear, bug [informal]  
eavesdropping

  • ...foreign electronic eavesdropping on army communications.

    外国对陆军通讯的电子窃听

eavesdropper

  • Modern technology enables eavesdroppers to pick up conversations through windows or walls.

    现代技术使窃听者能够隔着窗户或墙偷听谈话。
