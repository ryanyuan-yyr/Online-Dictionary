indignity
ɪndɪgnɪti ɪnˈdɪgnəti
Word forms: plural indignities
N-VAR 侮辱;轻蔑 If you talk about the indignity of doing something, you mean that it makes you feel embarrassed or unimportant. 侮辱;轻蔑 [formal]

  • Later, he suffered the indignity of having to flee angry protesters. [+ of]

    后来,他不得不极不光彩地避开愤怒的抗议者溜走了。

  • What sort of indignities would he be forced to endure?

    他会被迫忍受什么样的侮辱呢？

SYN humiliation, abuse, outrage, injury
