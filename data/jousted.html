joust
dʒaʊst dʒaʊst dʒust dʒʌst
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense jousts, present participle jousting, past tense , past participle jousted
1 V-RECIP 竞争；角逐 When two or more people or organizations joust, they compete to see who is better. 竞争；角逐 [literary]

  • ...lawyers joust in the courtroom. [VERB]

    法庭上展开激辩的律师们

  • The oil company jousts with Esso for lead position in UK sales.

    这家石油公司和埃索公司角逐英国市场销量的榜首位置。

SYN compete, fight, contend, vie  
Joust is also a noun.

  • There were notable jousts with the Secretary of Commerce.

    和商业部长之间明显存在竞争。

2 V-RECIP （中世纪的骑士）进行马上长矛比武，骑马比武 In medieval times, when two knights on horseback jousted, they fought against each other using long spears called lances. （中世纪的骑士）进行马上长矛比武，骑马比武 [Also + V with]

  • Knights joust and frolic. [VERB]

    骑士们骑马比武，嬉戏作乐。

jousting

  • ...medieval jousting tournaments.

    中世纪的骑士比武大赛
