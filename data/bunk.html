bunk
bʌŋk bʌŋk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , plural bunks, present participle bunking, past tense , past participle bunked
1 N-COUNT （尤指船上或车上的）铺位，卧铺 A bunk is a bed that is fixed to a wall, especially in a ship or caravan. （尤指船上或车上的）铺位，卧铺

  • He left his bunk and went up on deck again.

    他离开自己的铺位再次走到甲板上。

SYN berth, bed, cot [nautical] , hammock  
2 N-UNCOUNT 胡说；谎言 If you describe something as bunk, you think that it is foolish or untrue. 胡说；谎言 [informal, disapproval]

  • ...Henry Ford's opinion that 'history is bunk'.

    亨利·福特认为“历史都是谎言”的观点

SYN nonsense, rubbish, rot, crap [slang]  
3 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 不辞而别；溜走 If you do a bunk, you suddenly leave a place without telling anyone. 不辞而别；溜走 [British, informal]
SYN run away, flee, bolt, clear out [informal]  
Phrasal verbs:
bunk off
