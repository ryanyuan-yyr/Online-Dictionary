swot
swɒt swɒt swɑt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , 3rd person singular present tense swots, present participle swotting, past tense , past participle swotted
1 VERB (尤指为准备考试)刻苦学习，用功 If you swot, you study very hard, especially when you are preparing for an examination. (尤指为准备考试)刻苦学习，用功 [British, informal] [Also VERB]

  • They swotted for their A levels. [VERB + for]

    他们为准备高级程度考试而刻苦学习。

SYN study, revise, cram [informal] , work  
Swot up means the same as swot .swot up 同 swot

  • ...several hours spent swotting up on how to be a pop star.

    努力学习如何成为一名流行歌手的几个小时

  • The orchestra's been swotting up their Holst and Stravinsky.

    该管弦乐队一直在刻苦排练演奏霍尔斯特和斯特拉文斯基的作品。

2 N-COUNT 只知一味用功学习的人;书呆子 If you call someone a swot, you disapprove of the fact that they study extremely hard and are not interested in other things. 只知一味用功学习的人;书呆子 [British, informal, disapproval]
SYN academic, intellectual, bookworm, egghead [informal]
