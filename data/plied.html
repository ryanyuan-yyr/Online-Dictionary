ply
plaɪ plaɪ plaɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense plies, present participle plying, past tense , past participle plied
1 VERB 不断供给 If you ply someone with food or drink, you keep giving them more of it. 不断供给

  • Elsie, who had been told that Maria wasn't well, plied her with food. [VERB noun + with]

    埃尔茜得知玛丽亚身体不适，就不停地给她弄吃的。

  • The poor priest was plied with drink at a dinner party. [VERB noun with noun]

    宴会上不断有人给可怜的牧师灌酒。

2 VERB 不断问；追问 If you ply someone with questions, you keep asking them questions. 不断问；追问

  • Giovanni plied him with questions with the intention of prolonging his stay. [VERB noun + with]

    乔瓦尼一个劲儿地问他问题，想要拖住他。

SYN bombard, press, harass, besiege  
3 VERB 从事，经营（尤指街头销售或服务） If you ply a trade, you do a particular kind of work regularly as your job, especially a kind of work that involves trying to sell goods or services to people outdoors. 从事，经营（尤指街头销售或服务）

  • ...the market traders noisily plying their wares. [VERB noun]

    大声吆喝货物的集市商贩

  • It's illegal for unmarked minicabs to ply for hire.

    无标志的电话预订出租车沿街拉客是非法的。

4 VERB （船、飞机或车辆）定期往返于 If a ship, aircraft, or vehicle plies a route, it makes regular journeys along that route. （船、飞机或车辆）定期往返于

  • Eighteen boats plied the 1,000 miles of river along a trading route. [VERB noun]

    18艘船沿着贸易航线往返于1,000英里的河面上。

  • The brightly-coloured boats ply between the islands. [VERB prep.]

    色彩明艳的船只往来于岛屿之间。

SYN travel, go, ferry, shuttle  
-ply -plaɪ
COMB in ADJ [ADJ n] （用于数字后）表示“股” You use -ply after a number to indicate how many pieces are twisted together to make a type of wool, thread, or rope. （用于数字后）表示“股”

  • You need 3 balls of any 4-ply knitting wool.

    需要随便3团4股羊毛线。
