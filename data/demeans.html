demean
dɪmiːn diˈmin dɪˈmin
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense demeans, present participle demeaning, past tense , past participle demeaned
1 VERB 降低(自己)的身份;有辱（自己）的人格 If you demean yourself, you do something which makes people have less respect for you. 降低(自己)的身份;有辱（自己）的人格

  • I wasn't going to demean myself by acting like a suspicious wife. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    我不会表现得像个疑神疑鬼的妻子一样自贬身价。

2 VERB 贬低;贬损 To demean someone or something means to make people have less respect for them. 贬低;贬损

  • Some groups say that pornography demeans women. [VERB noun]

    一些团体认为色情作品贬低了女性。
