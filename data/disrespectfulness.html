disrespect
dɪsrɪspekt ˌdɪsrɪˈspɛkt ˌdɪsrɪˈspɛkt ˈdɪsrɪˌspɛkt
1 N-UNCOUNT 不尊敬;无礼;轻蔑 If someone shows disrespect, they speak or behave in a way that shows lack of respect for a person, law, or custom. 不尊敬;无礼;轻蔑

  • ...young people with complete disrespect for authority. [+ for]

    根本不把权威放在眼里的年轻人

SYN contempt, cheek [informal] , disregard, rudeness  
2 PHRASE (在表示批评某人或某物时)绝无不尊敬之意 You can say 'no disrespect to someone or something' when you are just about to criticize them, in order to indicate that you are not hostile towards them or admire them for other things. (在表示批评某人或某物时)绝无不尊敬之意

  • No disrespect to John Beck, but the club has been happier since he left.

    我对约翰·贝克绝无不敬之意，但自他离开后俱乐部气氛的确更活跃了。
