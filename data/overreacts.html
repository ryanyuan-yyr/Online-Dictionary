overreact
oʊvəʳriækt ˌoʊvərriˈækt
(also over-react)Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense overreacts, present participle overreacting, past tense , past participle overreacted
VERB 反应过火；反应过激 If you say that someone overreacts to something, you mean that they have and show more of an emotion than is necessary or appropriate. 反应过火；反应过激

  • Is the council right to be concerned, or is it overreacting?. [VERB]

    市政委员会表示关切是合情合理呢，还是反应过激？

  • The market appeared to overreact, but this is not the case. [VERB]

    市场反应看似过激，但实际并非如此。

  • I overreact to anything sad. [VERB + to]

    我一碰上悲伤的事物就难以控制情绪。

SYN get things out of proportion, go over the top [British , informal] , blow things out of all proportion, make a mountain out of a molehill
