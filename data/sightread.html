sight-read saɪt riːd saɪt red
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense sight-reads, present participle sight-reading
The form sight-read is used in the present tense, where it is pronounced /'saɪt riːd/, and is the past tense and past participle, pronounced /'saɪt red/. sight-read 用作现在式时，读作 /'saɪt riːd/,用作过去式和过去分词时，读作 /'saɪt red/。
VERB 视唱，视奏(事先没有练习，直接看着乐谱演奏或演唱) Someone who can sight-read can play or sing music from a printed sheet the first time they see it, without practising it beforehand. 视唱，视奏(事先没有练习，直接看着乐谱演奏或演唱) [Also VERB n]

  • Symphony musicians cannot necessarily sight-read. [VERB]

    交响乐乐师未必就能视奏。
