maniac
meɪniæk ˈmeɪniˌæk
Word forms: plural maniacs
1 N-COUNT 疯子;狂人 A maniac is a mad person who is violent and dangerous. 疯子;狂人

  • The cabin looked as if a maniac had been let loose there.

    小屋看上去像是被疯子在那撒过野一样。

  • ...a drug-crazed maniac.

    瘾君子

SYN madman or madwoman, psycho [slang] , lunatic, loony [slang]  
2 ADJ [ADJ n] 极其愚蠢的;疯狂的 If you describe someone's behaviour as maniac, you are emphasizing that it is extremely foolish and uncontrolled. 极其愚蠢的;疯狂的 [emphasis]

  • A maniac driver sped 35 miles along the wrong side of a motorway at 110 mph.

    一个疯狂的司机以每小时110英里的速度在高速公路上逆行飙车35英里。

  • ...a maniac cyclist.

    疯狂骑自行车的人

3 N-COUNT (宗教或体育的)狂热分子，过激分子 If you call someone, for example, a religious maniac or a sports maniac, you are critical of them because they have such a strong interest in religion or sport. (宗教或体育的)狂热分子，过激分子 [disapproval]

  • My mum is turning into a religious maniac.

    我妈妈正在变为一个宗教狂热分子。

  • ...football maniacs.

    足球迷
