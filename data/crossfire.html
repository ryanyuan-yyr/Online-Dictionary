crossfire
krɒsfaɪəʳ krɔːs- krɔsˌfaɪr krɑsˌfaɪr
(also cross-fire)
1 N-UNCOUNT 交叉火力 Crossfire is gunfire, for example in a battle, that comes from two or more different directions and passes through the same area. 交叉火力
2 PHRASE 被卷进争端 If you are caught in the crossfire, you become involved in an unpleasant situation in which people are arguing with each other, although you do not want to be involved or say which person you agree with. 被卷进争端

  • They say they are caught in the crossfire between the education establishment and the government. [+ between]

    他们称自己被卷进了教育机构与政府之间的争端。
