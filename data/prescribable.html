prescribe
prɪskraɪb priˈskraɪb prɪˈskraɪb
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense prescribes, present participle prescribing, past tense , past participle prescribed
1 VERB 开（药、处方） If a doctor prescribes medicine or treatment for you, he or she tells you what medicine or treatment to have. 开（药、处方）

  • Our doctor diagnosed a throat infection and prescribed antibiotic and junior aspirin. [VERB noun]

    我们的医生诊断是喉部感染，并且开了一些抗生素和小剂量阿司匹林。

  • She took twice the prescribed dose of sleeping tablets. [VERB-ed]

    她服用了双倍医嘱剂量的安眠药片。

  • The law allows doctors to prescribe contraception to the under 16s. [VERB noun + to]

    法律允许医生给16岁以下未成年人开避孕药。

2 VERB （人、法律、规则）规定，指定 If a person or set of laws or rules prescribes an action or duty, they state that it must be carried out. （人、法律、规则）规定，指定 [formal]

  • ...article II of the constitution, which prescribes the method of electing a president. [VERB noun]

    规定了总统选举方法的宪法第二条

  • Alliott told Singleton he was passing the sentence prescribed by law. [VERB-ed]

    阿利奥特告诉辛格尔顿他是依法判决。

SYN ordain [formal] , set, order, establish
