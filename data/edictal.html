edict
iːdɪkt ˈiˌdɪkt
Word forms: plural edicts
N-COUNT [oft NOUN that] 法令;敕令 An edict is a command or instruction given by someone in authority. 法令;敕令 [formal]

  • In 1741 Catherine the Great issued an edict of toleration for Buddhism.

    1741年，叶卡捷琳娜大帝颁布法令，允许佛教的存在。

  • He issued an edict that none of his writings be destroyed.

    他下令不得毁坏他的任何作品。

SYN decree, law, act, order
