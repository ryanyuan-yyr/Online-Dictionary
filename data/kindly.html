kindly
kaɪndli ˈkaɪndli
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 仁慈的；体贴的；有同情心的 A kindly person is kind, caring, and sympathetic. 仁慈的；体贴的；有同情心的

  • He was a stern critic but an extremely kindly man.

    他是个苛刻的批评家，但却是个非常善良的人。

SYN benevolent, kind, caring, nice  
kindliness

  • His kindliness and warmth made him particularly effective with staff welfare.

    他的友好和热心使他将员工福利工作做得相当出色。

2 ADV-GRADED [ADVERB before verb] 体贴地；友好地 If someone kindly does something for you, they act in a thoughtful and helpful way. 体贴地；友好地

  • He kindly carried our picnic in a rucksack.

    他很体贴地背着装有我们野餐食物的背包。

  • He had very kindly asked me to the cocktail party that evening.

    他很客气地邀请我参加那晚的鸡尾酒会。

SYN benevolently, politely, generously, thoughtfully  
3 ADV [ADVERB before verb] （表示因有权让人做某事或对其恼怒）劳驾，请 If someone asks you to kindly do something, they are asking you in a way which shows that they have authority over you, or that they are angry with you. （表示因有权让人做某事或对其恼怒）劳驾，请 [formal]

  • Will you kindly obey the instructions I am about to give?

    劳驾你服从我要给出的指令好不好？

4 See also: kind 
5 PHRASE 支持；赞同 If you look kindly on or look kindly upon someone or something, you support them or approve of what they are doing. 支持；赞同

  • Recent historical work looks kindly on the regime.

    近来的历史论著对该政权持赞同态度。

6 PHRASE 不喜欢 If someone does not take kindly to something, they do not like it. 不喜欢

  • She did not take kindly to being offered advice.

    她不喜欢别人给她提建议。

  • It is hard to imagine her taking kindly to too much interference.

    很难想象她会喜欢被人过多地干涉。
