ensnare
ɪnsneəʳ ɪnˈsnɛə ɛnˈsnɛr ɪnˈsnɛr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense ensnares, present participle ensnaring, past tense , past participle ensnared
1 VERB 使陷入圈套；使入陷阱 If you ensnare someone, you gain power over them, especially by using dishonest or deceitful methods. 使陷入圈套；使入陷阱

  • Feminism is simply another device to ensnare women. [VERB noun]

    女权主义只是使女性陷入圈套的另一种手段。

  • We find ourselves ensnared in employment acts which do not help resolve industrial disputes. [V-ed + in]

    我们发现自己陷入了雇佣法案的圈套之中，因为它们并不能帮助解决劳资争端。

2 VERB （用陷阱）诱捕，捕获 If an animal is ensnared, it is caught in a trap. （用陷阱）诱捕，捕获

  • The spider must wait for prey to be ensnared on its web. [be V-ed + on/in]

    蜘蛛必须等待猎物被它的蛛网困住。

SYN trap, catch, capture, seize
