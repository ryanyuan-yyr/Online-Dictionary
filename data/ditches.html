ditch
dɪtʃ dɪtʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense ditches, present participle ditching, past tense , past participle ditched
1 N-COUNT (道路、田地边上的)沟，渠 A ditch is a long narrow channel cut into the ground at the side of a road or field. (道路、田地边上的)沟，渠
SYN channel, drain, trench, gutter  
2 VERB 摆脱;抛弃;丢弃 If you ditch something that you have or are responsible for, you abandon it or get rid of it, because you no longer want it. 摆脱;抛弃;丢弃 [informal]

  • I decided to ditch the sofa bed. [VERB noun]

    我决定把沙发床扔了。

  • Unpopular policies such as unilateral disarmament were ditched. [VERB noun]

    诸如单边裁军之类不得人心的政策已被废除了。

SYN get rid of, dump [informal] , scrap, bin [informal]  
3 VERB 抛弃;甩掉;与…断交 If someone ditches someone, they end a relationship with that person. 抛弃;甩掉;与…断交 [informal]

  • I can't bring myself to ditch him and start again. [VERB noun]

    我不忍心蹬掉他再觅新欢。

SYN leave, drop, abandon, desert  
4 V-ERG (使）(飞机)紧急迫降 If a pilot ditches an aircraft or if it ditches, the pilot makes an emergency landing. (使）(飞机)紧急迫降

  • One American pilot was forced to ditch his jet in the Gulf. [VERB noun]

    一位美国飞行员被迫将喷气式飞机紧急迫降在波斯湾上。

  • A survivor was knocked unconscious when the helicopter ditched. [VERB]

    在直升机紧急迫降时，一名幸存者被撞昏过去。

5 See also: last-ditch
