network
netwɜːʳk ˈnɛtˌwɜrk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense networks, present participle networking, past tense , past participle networked
1 N-COUNT 网状物;网状系统 A network of lines, roads, veins, or other long thin things is a large number of them which cross each other or meet at many points. 网状物;网状系统

  • ...Strasbourg, with its rambling network of medieval streets. [+ of]

    中世纪街道纵横交织的斯特拉斯堡

  • The uterus is supplied with a rich network of blood vessels and nerves. [+ of]

    密布的血管和神经网向子宫输送养料。

SYN maze, warren, labyrinth  
2 N-COUNT (人或机构组成的)网络 A network of people or institutions is a large number of them that have a connection with each other and work together as a system. (人或机构组成的)网络

  • Distribution of the food is going ahead using a network of local church people and other volunteers. [+ of]

    食品正通过当地教会人士和其他志愿者形成的网络分发下去。

  • He is keen to point out the benefits which the family network can provide.

    他很乐意指出家庭网络可以带来的好处。

SYN system, structure, complex, organization  
3See also:old-boy network 
4 N-COUNT [oft n NOUN] 网络;联动系统 A particular network is a system of things which are connected and which operate together. For example, a computer network consists of a number of computers that are part of the same system. 网络;联动系统

  • ...a computer network with 154 terminals. [+ with]

    有154个终端的计算机网络

  • Huge sections of the rail network are out of action.

    铁路网的很多区段停运。

5See also:neural network 
6 N-COUNT 广播网;电视网 A radio or television network is a company or group of companies that broadcasts radio or television programmes throughout an area. 广播网;电视网

  • An American network says it has obtained the recordings.

    美国一家广播网声称已经得到了那些录音材料。

  • ...Fuji Television Network, a highly successful commercial station.

    富士电视网,一家非常成功的商业电视台

7 VERB [usually passive] 对(电视或广播节目)进行联播 When a television or radio programme is networked, it is broadcast at the same time by several different television companies. 对(电视或广播节目)进行联播

  • Lumsdon would like to see his programme sold and networked. [be VERB-ed]

    拉姆斯敦希望看到自己的节目卖出，并进行联播。

  • He had once had his own networked chat show. [VERB-ed]

    他曾经拥有自己的电视联播访谈节目。

8 VERB 建立工作关系网;交际 If you network, you try to meet new people who might be useful to you in your job. 建立工作关系网;交际 [business] [Also VERB]

  • In business, it is important to network with as many people as possible on a face to face basis. [VERB + with]

    在生意场上，通过与尽可能多的人面对面地打交道来建立工作关系很重要。
