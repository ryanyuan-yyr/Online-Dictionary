do down
PHRASAL VERB 说…的坏话;贬低 If someone does you down, they try to make other people think that you are unpleasant or unsuccessful by criticizing you. 说…的坏话;贬低 [British, informal] [Also VERB PREPOSITION n (not pron)]

  • Glover thinks that Smith did him down, perhaps out of envy. [VERB noun PREP.]

    格洛弗认为史密斯说他坏话，也许是出于嫉妒。
