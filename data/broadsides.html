broadside
brɔːdsaɪd ˈbrɔdˌsaɪd
Word forms: plural broadsides
1 N-COUNT （书面或口头的）猛烈抨击 A broadside is a strong written or spoken attack on a person or institution. （书面或口头的）猛烈抨击

  • The Social Democratic leader launched a broadside against both monetary and political union. [+ against]

    这位社会民主党领袖对金钱和政治联盟进行了猛烈抨击。

SYN attack, criticism, censure, swipe  
2 ADV [ADVERB after verb, be ADVERB, oft ADV on] （船只）以舷侧对着 If a ship is broadside to something, it has its longest side facing in the direction of that thing. （船只）以舷侧对着 [technical]

  • The ship was moored broadside to the pier. [+ to]

    这艘船横泊在码头旁。
