enact
ɪnækt ɪnˈækt ɛnˈækt ɪnˈækt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense enacts, present participle enacting, past tense , past participle enacted
1 VERB 将…制定成法律；通过（法案） When a government or authority enacts a proposal, they make it into a law. 将…制定成法律；通过（法案） [technical]

  • The authorities have failed so far to enact a law allowing unrestricted emigration. [VERB noun]

    到目前为止，当局未能通过允许自由移民的法律。

  • The bill would be submitted for public discussion before being enacted as law. [VERB noun]

    该法案在获得通过成为法律前，将提请公众讨论。

SYN establish, order, pass, command  
2 VERB 演出;演绎;表演 If people enact a story or play, they perform it by acting. 演出;演绎;表演

  • She often enacted the stories told to her by her father. [VERB noun]

    她经常把父亲讲给她的那些故事表演出来。

SYN perform, play, act, present  
3 VERB [usually passive] 使出现，使发生（尤指以前发生过的事情） If a particular event or situation is enacted, it happens; used especially to talk about something that has happened before. 使出现，使发生（尤指以前发生过的事情） [journalism]

  • It was a scene which was enacted month after month for eight years. [be VERB-ed]

    8年里，那一情况每个月都会发生。
