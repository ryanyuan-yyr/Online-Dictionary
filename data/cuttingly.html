cutting
kʌtɪŋ ˈkʌtɪŋ
Word forms: plural cuttings
1 N-COUNT 剪报;杂志剪辑资料 A cutting is a piece of writing which has been cut from a newspaper or magazine. 剪报;杂志剪辑资料 [British]

  • ...a stack of old photographs and newspaper cuttings.

    一堆旧照片和剪报

  • Here are the press cuttings and reviews.

    这是些新闻剪辑和评论。

SYN clipping, extract, excerpt, piece  
in AM, use 美国英语用 clipping
2 N-COUNT (从植物上剪下的)插枝，插条 A cutting from a plant is a part of the plant that you have cut off so that you can grow a new plant from it. (从植物上剪下的)插枝，插条

  • Take cuttings from it in July or August. [+ from]

    7月或8月时从它上边剪一些插枝。

3 N-COUNT (在山中开凿的供铁路或道路通过的)狭窄通道，路堑 A cutting is a narrow valley cut through a hill so that a railway line or road can pass through. (在山中开凿的供铁路或道路通过的)狭窄通道，路堑 [British]
in AM, use 美国英语用 cut
4 ADJ-GRADED 尖刻的;挖苦人的 A cutting remark is unkind and likely to hurt someone's feelings. 尖刻的;挖苦人的

  • People make cutting remarks to help themselves feel superior or powerful.

    人们说话尖酸刻薄是为了使自己感觉高高在上或是有权有势。

SYN hurtful, wounding, severe, acid
