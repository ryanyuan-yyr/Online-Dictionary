hidebound
haɪdbaʊnd ˈhaɪdˌbaʊnd
ADJ-GRADED 顽固守旧的;死板保守的 If you describe someone or something as hidebound, you are criticizing them for having old-fashioned ideas or ways of doing things and being unwilling or unlikely to change. 顽固守旧的;死板保守的 [disapproval]

  • The men are hidebound and reactionary.

    那些人顽固且反动。

  • The economy was hidebound by public spending and private monopolies. [+ by]

    经济由于公共开支和私有垄断而变得僵化。

SYN conventional, set, rigid, narrow
