characterize
kærɪktəraɪz ˈkærəktərˌaɪz ˈkærɪktərˌaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense characterizes, present participle characterizing, past tense , past participle characterized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 characterise
1 VERB 使具有特点 If something is characterized by a particular feature or quality, that feature or quality is an obvious part of it. 使具有特点 [formal]

  • This election campaign has been characterized by violence. [be VERB-ed + by]

    暴力行为已成为这次竞选活动的一大特点。

  • A bold use of colour characterizes the bedroom. [VERB noun]

    这间卧室的特点是用色大胆。

SYN distinguish, mark, identify, brand  
2 VERB 描绘;描述 If you characterize someone or something as a particular thing, you describe them as that thing. 描绘;描述 [formal]

  • Both companies have characterized the relationship as friendly. [VERB noun + as]

    两家公司都认为彼此之间关系友好。

  • This play is characterized as a comedy. [V n as adj/n]

    这出戏被认为是喜剧。
