complain
kəmpleɪn kəmˈpleɪn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense complains, present participle complaining, past tense , past participle complained
1 VERB 抱怨;发牢骚 If you complain about a situation, you say that you are not satisfied with it. 抱怨;发牢骚

  • Miners have complained bitterly that the government did not fulfill their promises. [VERB that]

    矿工们愤愤不平地抱怨说政府没有履行诺言。

  • The American couple complained about the high cost of visiting Europe. [V + about/of]

    这对美国夫妇抱怨到欧洲旅行的花销太高。

  • For my own part, I have nothing to complain of. [V about/of n]

    我这方面没有什么好抱怨的。

  • They are liable to face more mistreatment if they complain to the police. [VERB + to]

    如果他们向警方投诉，可能会受到更多虐待。

  • People should complain when they consider an advert offensive. [VERB]

    民众如果认为某个广告具有冒犯性就应该投诉。

  • 'I do everything you ask of me,' he complained. [VERB with quote]

    “你要我做的事我都做了。”他抱怨道。

2 VERB 诉说有…病痛;主诉 If you complain of pain or illness, you say that you are feeling pain or feeling ill. 诉说有…病痛;主诉

  • He complained of a headache. [VERB + of]

    他说头痛。
