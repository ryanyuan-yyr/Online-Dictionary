scalp
skælp skælp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense scalps, present participle scalping, past tense , past participle scalped
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 头皮 Your scalp is the skin under the hair on your head. 头皮

  • He smoothed his hair back over his scalp.

    他把头发向后理顺。

  • Massage the shampoo into the scalp.

    通过按摩让洗发香波渗入头皮。

2 VERB 剥下…的头皮 To scalp someone means to remove the skin and hair from the top of their head. 剥下…的头皮

  • He pretended to scalp me with his sword. [VERB noun]

    他假装要用剑剥下我的头皮。

3 N-COUNT 带发头皮 A scalp is the piece of skin and hair that is removed when someone is scalped. 带发头皮
4 N-COUNT [with poss] 战利品;对…的惩罚 If you say that you want someone's scalp, you mean that you want to defeat or punish them. 战利品;对…的惩罚 [informal]

  • Stock speculators wanted his scalp.

    股票投机商想整他一顿。

  • He told friends I was after his scalp.

    他告诉朋友们我想打败他。

5 VERB 倒卖，炒卖(票) If someone scalps tickets, they sell them outside a sports ground or theatre, usually for more than their original value. 倒卖，炒卖(票) [US]

  • He was trying to pick up some cash scalping tickets. [VERB noun]

    他那时正想通过倒票赚点儿钱。
