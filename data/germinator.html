germinate
dʒɜːʳmɪneɪt ˈdʒɜrməˌneɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense germinates, present participle germinating, past tense , past participle germinated
1 V-ERG （使）发芽；（使）开始生长；（使）萌芽 If a seed germinates or if it is germinated, it starts to grow. （使）发芽；（使）开始生长；（使）萌芽

  • Some seed varieties germinate fast, so check every day or so. [VERB]

    有一些种子发芽快，所以差不多每天得察看一下。

  • First, the researchers germinated the seeds. [VERB noun]

    研究人员首先让种子发芽。

germination

  • The poor germination of your seed could be because the soil was too cold.

    你的种子发芽率很低，可能是土壤温度太低的缘故。

2 VERB （想法、计划或感觉）形成，产生 If an idea, plan, or feeling germinates, it comes into existence and begins to develop. （想法、计划或感觉）形成，产生 [Also V + into]

  • He wrote to Eliot about a 'big book' that was germinating in his mind. [VERB]

    他写信告诉埃利奥特他脑海中正在酝酿的一本“巨著”。

SYN develop, grow, progress, generate
