cracking
krækɪŋ ˈkrækɪŋ
1 ADJ [usually ADJ n] 优秀的;极好的;顶呱呱的 You use cracking to describe something you think is very good or exciting. 优秀的;极好的;顶呱呱的 [British, informal]

  • It's a cracking novel.

    这是一部很优秀的小说。

  • The way Liverpool play, and the way we play, I think it will be a cracking game.

    利物浦队的打法再加上我们的打法，我想那会是一场很精彩的比赛。

2 PHRASE 马上开始;加紧做 If you tell someone to get cracking, you are telling them to start doing something immediately. 马上开始;加紧做 [British, informal]

  • Mark, you'd better get cracking, the sooner the better.

    马克，你最好尽快开始，越快越好。

  • Vouchers must be redeemed before September 14th so you'd better get cracking to cash in.

    代金券必须于9月14日之前兑现，所以你最好快点。

3 PHRASE [PHRASE after verb] 迅速地;飞速地 If you say that someone or something is moving at a cracking pace, you mean that they are moving very quickly. 迅速地;飞速地 [British, informal, emphasis]

  • She set off at a cracking pace to Mr Ramzan's Superstore.

    她飞速赶往拉姆赞先生的大型商场。

  • The film belts along at a cracking pace.

    影片节奏紧凑。
