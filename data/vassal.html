vassal
væsəl ˈvæsəl
Word forms: plural vassals
1 N-COUNT （封建社会的）封臣 In feudal society, a vassal was a man who gave military service to a lord, in return for which he was protected by the lord and received land to live on. （封建社会的）封臣
2 N-COUNT [usually singular] 附属国 If you say that one country is a vassal of another, you mean that it is controlled by it. 附属国 [written, disapproval]

  • Opponents of the treaty argue that monetary union will turn France into a vassal of Germany. [+ of]

    协定的反对者认为货币联盟会使法国沦为德国的附庸国。
