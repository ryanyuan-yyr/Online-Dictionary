sloppy
slɒpi ˈslɑpi
Word forms: comparative sloppier, superlative sloppiest
1 ADJ-GRADED 马虎的;敷衍的;草率的 If you describe someone's work or activities as sloppy, you mean they have been done in a careless and lazy way. 马虎的;敷衍的;草率的 [disapproval]

  • He has little patience for sloppy work from colleagues.

    他对同事们草草敷衍的工作鲜有耐心。

  • His language is disjointed and sloppy.

    他的语言支离破碎，混乱不堪。

sloppily

  • They lost because they played sloppily.

    他们打得松松垮垮，因而输了。

sloppiness

  • Miss Furniss could not abide sloppiness.

    弗尼斯小姐受不了马马虎虎的作风。

2 ADJ-GRADED 伤感浪漫的;无病呻吟的 If you describe someone or something as sloppy, you mean that they are sentimental and romantic. 伤感浪漫的;无病呻吟的

  • It's ideal for people who like a sloppy movie.

    对于喜欢伤感电影的人来说，这部影片再合适不过了。

  • ...some sloppy love-story.

    一场风花雪月的爱情故事

SYN sentimental, mushy [informal] , soppy [British , informal] , slushy [informal]
