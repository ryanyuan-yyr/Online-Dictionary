clasp
klɑːsp klæsp klæsp klɑsp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense clasps, present participle clasping, past tense , past participle clasped
1 VERB 紧紧抓住;攥紧；抱紧 If you clasp someone or something, you hold them tightly in your hands or arms. 紧紧抓住;攥紧；抱紧

  • She clasped the children to her. [VERB noun]

    她把孩子们紧紧地搂进怀里。

  • He paced the corridor, hands clasped behind his back. [VERB-ed]

    他双手紧扣，背在身后，在走廊里踱来踱去。

SYN grasp, hold, press, grip  
Clasp is also a noun.

  • With one last clasp of his hand, she left him and went to her usual chair.

    她最后一次紧握了一下他的手，然后离开他，回到了自己通常坐的椅子上。

2 N-COUNT 扣环;搭扣 A clasp is a small device that fastens something. 扣环;搭扣

  • ...the clasp of her handbag. [+ of]

    她手提包的搭扣
