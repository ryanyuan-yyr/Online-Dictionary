excerpt
eksɜːʳpt eksɜːʳpt eksɜːʳpt ɛkˈsɜrpt ɪksɜrpt ˈɛkˌsɜrpt
Word forms: plural excerpts, past tense , past participle excerpted
The noun is pronounced /'eksɜːpt/. The verb is pronounced /ek'sɜːpt/. 名词读作 /'eksɜːpt/，动词读作 /ek'sɜːpt/。
1 N-COUNT 摘录;节选;选段 An excerpt is a short piece of writing or music which is taken from a larger piece. 摘录;节选;选段

  • ...an excerpt from Tchaikovsky's Nutcracker. [+ from]

    柴可夫斯基的《胡桃夹子》选段

SYN extract, part, piece, section  
2 V-PASSIVE 摘录;节选 If a long piece of writing or music is excerpted, short pieces from it are printed or played on their own. 摘录;节选

  • The readings you heard earlier were excerpted from a new oral history of Pearl Harbor. [be V-ed from n]

    你之前听到的朗读片段节选自一部新的珍珠港口述史。

SYN extract, take, select, quote
