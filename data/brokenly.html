broken
broʊkən ˈbroʊkən
1 Broken is the past participle of break .
2 ADJ [ADJ n] （线条）不连贯的，断续的，中断的 A broken line is not continuous but has gaps or spaces in it. （线条）不连贯的，断续的，中断的

  • A broken blue line means the course of a waterless valley.

    蓝色虚线代表无水流过的山谷。

3 ADJ [ADJ n] （婚姻）破裂的 You can use broken to describe a marriage that has ended in divorce, or a home in which the parents of the family are divorced, when you think this is a sad or bad thing. （婚姻）破裂的 [disapproval]

  • She spoke for the first time about the traumas of a broken marriage.

    她首度谈起婚姻破裂带来的精神创伤。

  • Children from broken homes are more likely to leave home before the age of 18.

    破裂家庭里的孩子在18岁之前离家的可能性更大。

SYN failed, ruined, wrecked, unsuccessful  
4 ADJ [ADJ n] （语言）蹩脚的，不流利的 If someone talks in broken English, for example, or in broken French, they speak slowly and make a lot of mistakes because they do not know the language very well. （语言）蹩脚的，不流利的

  • Eric could only respond in broken English.

    埃里克只能用蹩脚的英语回答。

SYN imperfect, halting, hesitating, stammering
