fountain
faʊntɪn ˈfaʊntən
Word forms: plural fountains
1 N-COUNT 喷泉;喷水池 A fountain is an ornamental feature in a pool or lake which consists of a long narrow stream of water that is forced up into the air by a pump. 喷泉;喷水池
2 N-COUNT 喷射;喷涌 A fountain of a liquid is an amount of it which is sent up into the air and falls back. 喷射;喷涌 [literary]

  • The volcano spewed a fountain of molten rock 650 feet in the air. [+ of]

    这座火山喷射出的岩浆高达650英尺。

SYN jet, stream, spray, gush  
3 N-COUNT [NOUN of n] 源泉;根源;来源 If you describe a person or thing as a fountain of something, you mean they are an important source of it and supply a lot of it. 源泉;根源;来源 [literary]

  • You are a fountain of ideas.

    你就是思想的源泉。

SYN source, fount, wellspring, wellhead
