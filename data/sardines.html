sardine
sɑːʳdiːn ˈsɑːdiːn ˈsɑrdɪn sɑrˈdin ˈsɑrˌdaɪn
Word forms: plural sardines
1 N-COUNT 沙丁鱼 Sardines are a kind of small sea fish, often eaten as food. 沙丁鱼

  • They opened a tin of sardines.

    他们打开了一罐沙丁鱼。

2 PHRASE 像沙丁鱼般紧紧挤在一起;拥挤不堪 If you say that a crowd of people are packed like sardines, you are emphasizing that they are sitting or standing so close together that they cannot move easily. 像沙丁鱼般紧紧挤在一起;拥挤不堪 [emphasis]

  • The refugees were packed like sardines.

    难民们像罐头里的沙丁鱼般紧紧挤在一起。
