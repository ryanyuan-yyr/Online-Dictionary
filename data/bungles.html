bungle
bʌŋgəl ˈbʌŋɡəl ˈbʌŋgəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense bungles, present participle bungling, past tense , past participle bungled
VERB （因出错或手脚笨拙）把…搞砸 If you bungle something, you fail to do it properly, because you make mistakes or are clumsy. （因出错或手脚笨拙）把…搞砸

  • Two prisoners bungled an escape bid after running either side of a lamp-post while handcuffed. [VERB noun]

    两名被手铐铐在一起的囚犯试图逃跑时分别往电灯杆两边跑，结果没逃掉。

  • ...the FBI's bungled attempt to end the 51 day siege. [VERB-ed]

    联邦调查局为结束持续51天的围困进行的糟糕尝试

SYN mess up, blow [slang] , ruin, spoil  
Bungle is also a noun.

  • ...an appalling administrative bungle.

    重大管理失误

bungling

  • ...a bungling burglar.

    笨手笨脚的窃贼
