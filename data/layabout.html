layabout
leɪəbaʊt ˈleɪəˌbaʊt
Word forms: plural layabouts
N-COUNT 游手好闲之徒；好逸恶劳之辈 If you say that someone is a layabout, you disapprove of them because they do not work and you think they are lazy. 游手好闲之徒；好逸恶劳之辈 [mainly British, disapproval]

  • The plaintiff's sole witness, a gambler and layabout, was easily discredited.

    原告唯一的证人嗜赌成性，游手好闲，其证词一下子就被推翻了。

SYN idler, lounger, piker [Australian , New Zealand , slang] , shirker
