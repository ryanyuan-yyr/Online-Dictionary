cadge
kædʒ kædʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense cadges, present participle cadging, past tense , past participle cadged
VERB 乞得;索取;讨到 If someone cadges food, money, or help from you, they ask you for it and succeed in getting it. 乞得;索取;讨到 [mainly British, informal]

  • Can I cadge a cigarette?. [VERB noun]

    能不能给我支烟?

  • He could cadge a ride from somebody. [V n + from/off]

    他可以请求别人搭他一程。

SYN scrounge [informal] , beg, bum [informal] , blag [slang]
