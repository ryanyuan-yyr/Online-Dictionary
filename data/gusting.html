gust
gʌst gʌst
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense gusts, present participle gusting, past tense , past participle gusted
1 N-COUNT 一阵强风；一阵狂风 A gust is a short, strong, sudden rush of wind. 一阵强风；一阵狂风

  • A gust of wind drove down the valley. [+ of]

    一阵狂风刮过山谷。

  • A hurricane-force gust blew off part of a church tower.

    一阵飓风级强风掀掉了教堂塔楼的一角。

SYN blast, blow, rush, breeze  
2 VERB 猛刮；劲吹 When the wind gusts, it blows with short, strong, sudden rushes. 猛刮；劲吹

  • The wind gusted again. [VERB]

    风再次猛烈地刮了起来。

  • The wind gusted up to 164 miles an hour. [VERB prep./adv.]

    狂风大作，时速达164英里。

SYN blow, blast, puff, squall  
3 N-COUNT （感情的）迸发，爆发 If you feel a gust of emotion, you feel the emotion suddenly and intensely. （感情的）迸发，爆发

  • ...a small gust of pleasure. [+ of]

    短暂的一阵欢喜
