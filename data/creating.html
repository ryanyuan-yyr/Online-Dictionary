create
krieɪt kriˈeɪt ˈkriˌeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense creates, present participle creating, past tense , past participle created
1 VERB 创造;使发生;创建 To create something means to cause it to happen or exist. 创造;使发生;创建

  • We set business free to create more jobs in Britain. [VERB noun]

    我们放开对企业的限制以便在英国创造更多的就业机会。

  • She could create a fight out of anything. [VERB noun]

    她能无故挑起事端。

  • The lights create such a glare it's next to impossible to see anything behind them. [VERB noun]

    那些灯发出的光太强了，几乎看不清灯后面的任何东西。

  • Criticizing will only destroy a relationship and create feelings of failure. [VERB noun]

    批评责备只能破坏关系，让人产生失败感。

SYN cause, lead to, occasion [formal] , beget [old-fashioned]  
creation

    这些企业有助于为当地创造更多就业机会。

  • The creation of large parks and forests is of lower priority than some twenty years ago.

2 VERB 创造;发明;设计 When someone creates a new product or process, they invent it or design it. 创造;发明;设计

  • It is really great for a radio producer to create a show like this. [VERB noun]

    电台监制能够制作出这样的节目真是不简单。

  • He's creating a whole new language of painting. [VERB noun]

    他正在创造一种全新的绘画语言。

SYN make, form, produce, develop
