spearhead
spɪəʳhed ˈspɪrˌhɛd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense spearheads, present participle spearheading, past tense , past participle spearheaded
1 VERB 领导;倡导;当…的先锋 If someone spearheads a campaign or an attack, they lead it. 领导;倡导;当…的先锋 [journalism]

  • ...Esther Rantzen, who is spearheading a national campaign against bullying. [VERB noun]

    埃丝特·兰森，一场全国性的反抗暴力运动的领导者

  • Helicopters can to some extent take the place of tanks by spearheading the airborne attack. [VERB noun]

    直升机可以担任空中进攻的前锋，从而在某种程度上取代坦克的地位。

SYN lead, head, pioneer, launch  
2 N-COUNT [usually singular, usually with supplement] 前锋;先头部队 The spearhead of a campaign is the person or group that leads it. 前锋;先头部队 [journalism]

  • The marines went ashore as a spearhead this morning to capture key targets.

    今天早晨，海军陆战队作为先头部队率先登陆以占领主要目标。

SYN leader, front runner, driving force, front line
