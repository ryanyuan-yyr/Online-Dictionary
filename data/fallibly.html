fallible
fælɪbəl ˈfæləbəl
ADJ-GRADED 可能出错的;会犯错的 If you say that someone or something is fallible, you mean that they are not perfect and are likely to make mistakes or to fail in what they are doing. 可能出错的;会犯错的 [formal]

  • They are only human and all too fallible.

    他们只不过是普通人，太容易犯错误了。

  • The system has proved fallible time after time.

    时间一次又一次证明,这个系统是会出错的。

  • Human reason is a fallible guide.

    人的理智会给人错误的指引。

SYN imperfect, weak, uncertain, ignorant  
fallibility

  • Errors may have been made due to human fallibility.

    由于人难免犯错，错误可能已由此而生。

  • The fallibility of science is one of the great betrayals of our times.

    科学的不可靠是我们的时代最大的一种背叛。
