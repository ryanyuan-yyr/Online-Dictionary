flank
flæŋk flæŋk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense flanks, present participle flanking, past tense , past participle flanked
1 N-COUNT 胁;胁腹 An animal's flank is its side, between the ribs and the hip. 胁;胁腹

  • He put his hand on the dog's flank.

    他把手放在狗的胁腹上。

SYN side, quarter, hip, thigh  
2 N-COUNT (军队的)侧翼，翼侧 A flank of an army or navy force is one side of it when it is organized for battle. (军队的)侧翼，翼侧

  • The assault element, led by Captain Ramirez, opened up from their right flank.

    拉米雷斯上尉率领的突击队从他们的右翼开火。

SYN wing, side, sector, aspect  
3 N-COUNT (大型物体的)侧面，一侧，一边 The side of anything large can be referred to as its flank . (大型物体的)侧面，一侧，一边

  • They continued along the flank of the mountain. [+ of]

    他们沿着山的一侧继续前行。

4 VERB 两侧有;(有时指)一侧有 If something is flanked by things, it has them on both sides of it, or sometimes on one side of it. 两侧有;(有时指)一侧有

  • The altar was flanked by two Christmas trees. [be VERB-ed + by]

    圣坛两侧是两棵圣诞树。

  • Bookcases flank the bed. [VERB noun]

    床的两侧有书柜。

  • She crossed the room and sat in the armchair flanking the window seat. [VERB noun]

    她穿过房间，坐在座位靠窗一侧的扶手椅上。

  • He walks briskly, flanked by heavily armed guards. [VERB-ed]

    他步伐轻快，两旁是全副武装的侍卫。

SYN border, line, wall, screen
