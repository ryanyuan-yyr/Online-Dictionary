unequal
ʌniːkwəl ʌnˈiːkwəl ʌnˈikwəl
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 不平等的；不公平的 An unequal system or situation is unfair because it gives more power or privileges to one person or group of people than to others. 不平等的；不公平的

  • This country still had a deeply oppressive, unequal and divisive political system.

    这个国家的政治体制依然残暴异常，缺少平等，四分五裂。

  • ...the unequal power relationships between men and women.

    男女之间不平等的权力关系

  • ...unequal pay.

    不公平的薪水

SYN disproportionate, uneven, unbalanced, unfair  
unequally

  • ...unequally distributed assets.

    分配不均的资产

  • The victims were treated unequally.

    受害者遭受了不公平的待遇。

2 ADJ 不能胜任的；力不能及的 If someone is unequal to a task they have to do, they do not have the abilities needed to do it well. 不能胜任的；力不能及的 [formal]

  • Her critics say she has proved unequal to the task.

    批评她的人说事实证明她并不具备完成这项工作的能力。

  • He felt unequal to the job and wished there were someone he could go to for advice. [+ to]

    他感觉难以胜任这项工作，希望有人能给点建议。

SYN not up to, not qualified for, inadequate for, insufficient for  
3 ADJ （大小、力量或数量上）不均等的，不对等的 Unequal means being different in size, strength, or amount. （大小、力量或数量上）不均等的，不对等的

  • The Egyptians probably measured their day in twenty-four hours of unequal length.

    埃及人很有可能用不同长度的24小时来度量日子。
