gloomy
gluːmi ˈglumi
Word forms: comparative gloomier, superlative gloomiest
1 ADJ-GRADED 昏暗的；幽暗的 If a place is gloomy, it is almost dark so that you cannot see very well. 昏暗的；幽暗的

  • Inside it's gloomy after all that sunshine.

    尽管阳光明媚，里面依然暗淡无光。

  • ...this huge gloomy church.

    这座昏暗的大教堂

SYN dark, dull, dim, dismal  
2 ADJ-GRADED 忧伤的；沮丧的；无望的 If people are gloomy, they are unhappy and have no hope. 忧伤的；沮丧的；无望的

  • Miller is gloomy about the fate of the serious playwright in America.

    米勒对这位严肃剧作家在美国的命运颇为忧虑。

SYN miserable, down, sad, dismal  
gloomily

  • He tells me gloomily that he has been called up for army service.

    他伤感地告诉我他已经被征召入伍了。

3 ADJ-GRADED 前景黯淡的；悲观的 If a situation is gloomy, it does not give you much hope of success or happiness. 前景黯淡的；悲观的

  • ...a gloomy picture of an economy sliding into recession.

    经济陷入萧条的惨淡景象

  • Officials say the outlook for next year is gloomy.

    官员们表示来年前景黯淡。

SYN depressing, bad, dismal, dreary
