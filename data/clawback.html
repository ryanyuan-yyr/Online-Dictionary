claw back
1 PHRASAL VERB 捞回，夺回(钱财或权势) If someone claws back some of the money or power they had lost, they get some of it back again. 捞回，夺回(钱财或权势) [British] [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • They will eventually be able to claw back all or most of the debt. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他们最终能捞回全部或大部分的债务。

  • In the meantime his generals will want to claw back some of their old influence. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    与此同时，他的将军们还想东山再起。

2 PHRASAL VERB (政府)收回(已支付给民众的款项) If a government claws back money, it finds a way of taking money back from people that it gave money to in another way. (政府)收回(已支付给民众的款项) [British] [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • The Chancellor will try to claw back £3.5 billion in next year's Budget. [VERB PREP. noun]

    财政大臣在明年的预算中将争取收回35亿英镑。
