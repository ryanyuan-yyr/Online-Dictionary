uproot
ʌpruːt ʌpˈrut
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense uproots, present participle uprooting, past tense , past participle uprooted
1 VERB 使离开久住之处；使迁移他处居住 If you uproot yourself or if you are uprooted, you leave, or are made to leave, a place where you have lived for a long time. 使离开久住之处；使迁移他处居住

  • ...the trauma of uprooting themselves from their homes. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    背井离乡给他们造成的心灵创伤

  • He had no wish to uproot Dena from her present home. [VERB noun]

    他没想过让德娜离开现在的家去别处居住。

  • ...refugees who were uprooted during Ethiopia's civil war. [be VERB-ed]

    埃塞俄比亚内战期间离乡背井的难民

SYN displace, remove, exile, disorient  
2 VERB 把…连根拔起；根除 If someone uproots a tree or plant, or if the wind uproots it, it is pulled out of the ground. 把…连根拔起；根除

  • They had been forced to uproot their vines and plant wheat. [VERB noun]

    他们已被迫将葡萄藤连根拔除，改种小麦。

  • ...fallen trees which have been uprooted by the storm. [VERB noun]

    被暴风雨连根拔起、倒伏在地上的树木

  • ...uprooted trees. [VERB-ed]

    被连根拔起的树

SYN pull up, dig up, root out, weed out
