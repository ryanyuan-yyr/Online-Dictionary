enamoured
ɪnæməʳd ɪnˈæməd
in AM, use 美国英语用 enamored
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] （对某物）喜爱的，迷恋的，仰慕的 If you are enamoured of something, you like or admire it a lot. If you are not enamoured of something, you dislike or disapprove of it. （对某物）喜爱的，迷恋的，仰慕的 [literary]

  • I became totally enamored of the wildflowers there. [+ of/with]

    我特别喜欢那里的野花。

  • The religious conservatives are not enamoured of the West and its values. [+ of/with]

    宗教保守派人士不喜欢西方国家及其价值观。

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ, usu ADJ of/with n] (对某人)迷恋的，倾心的 If you are enamoured of a person, you are in love with them. (对某人)迷恋的，倾心的 [literary]

  • I remember being very young and being totally enamored of him.

    我记得当时很年轻，非常迷恋他。
