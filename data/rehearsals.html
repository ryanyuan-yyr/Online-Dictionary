rehearsal
rɪhɜːʳsəl rɪˈhɜrsəl
Word forms: plural rehearsals
1 N-VAR 排练;彩排 A rehearsal of a play, dance, or piece of music is a practice of it in preparation for a performance. 排练;彩排 [Also + of]

  • The band was scheduled to begin rehearsals for a concert tour. [+ for]

    这支乐队准备开始为巡演排练。

  • When 'Can-Can' was in rehearsal, Porter felt that the title song needed a musical introduction.

    在排练《康康舞》的时候，波特感觉主题曲需要一个前奏乐曲。

SYN practice, rehearsing, practice session, run-through  
2See also:dress rehearsal 
3 N-COUNT 预演;试演;演习;演练 You can describe an event or object which is a preparation for a more important event or object as a rehearsal for it. 预演;试演;演习;演练

  • The sketch should be a kind of rehearsal for the eventual painting.

    草图应该是最终绘画的某种演练。

  • Daydreams may seem to be rehearsals for real-life situations.

    白日梦可能像是真实情景的预演。
