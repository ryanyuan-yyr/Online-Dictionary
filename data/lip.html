lip
lɪp lɪp
Word forms: plural lips
1 N-COUNT [usually plural, oft poss NOUN] 嘴唇；口；嘴 Your lips are the two outer parts of the edge of your mouth. 嘴唇；口；嘴

  • Wade stuck the cigarette between his lips.

    韦德把香烟叼在嘴里。

2 N-COUNT 唇状物；边缘；（容器等的）嘴 The lip of something such as a container or a high area of land is its edge. 唇状物；边缘；（容器等的）嘴

  • ...the lip of the jug. [+ of]

    壶嘴

  • ...the lip of Mount Etna's smouldering crater. [+ of]

    埃特纳火山冒着烟的火山口

SYN edge, rim, brim, margin  
3 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 咬嘴唇（以按下怒气或抑制痛苦等） If you bite your lip, you try very hard not to show the anger or distress that you are feeling. 咬嘴唇（以按下怒气或抑制痛苦等）

  • She bit her lip as she recalled the words he'd thrown at her.

    回想起他甩给她的那些话，她咬了咬唇。

4 PHRASE [verb-link PHRASE] 大家都说的；大家都在谈论的 If you say that something is on everyone's lips, you mean that a lot of people seem to be interested in it and are talking about it. 大家都说的；大家都在谈论的

  • A reporter asks the president the question on everyone's lips: 'Do you anticipate being re-appointed?'.

    一名记者向总裁提了大家都想问的问题：“您觉得自己会再获任命吗？”

  • He is the guy whose name is on everyone's lips at the moment.

    他就是那个现在大家都在谈论的人。

5 PHRASE （在想到美好的事物或尝到好东西时）舔嘴唇 If you lick your lips, you move your tongue across your lips as you think about or taste something pleasant. （在想到美好的事物或尝到好东西时）舔嘴唇

  • They licked their lips in anticipation.

    他们舔舔嘴唇，满怀期待。

  • We swallowed the chocolates in one gulp, licking our lips.

    我们一口吞下了巧克力，满意地咂着嘴。

6 PHRASE 把嘴封住；绝口不提 If you tell someone that your lips are sealed, you are promising them that you will keep a secret that they have told you. 把嘴封住；绝口不提

  • As for anything told to me in confidence, well, my lips are sealed.

    至于私底下告诉我的任何事情，我会守口如瓶的。

7 PHRASE [usually verb PHRASE, verb-link PHRASE] 坚定沉着；感情不外露 If you say that someone is keeping a stiff upper lip, you mean that they are not showing any emotion even though it is difficult for them not to. 坚定沉着；感情不外露
