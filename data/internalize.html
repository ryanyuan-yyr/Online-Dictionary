internalize
ɪntɜːʳnəlaɪz ɪnˈtɜrnəlˌaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense internalizes, present participle internalizing, past tense , past participle internalized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 internalise
VERB 使(信仰、价值观等)内在化;吸收 If you internalize something such as a belief or a set of values, you make it become part of your attitude or way of thinking. 使(信仰、价值观等)内在化;吸收 [formal]

  • Over time she internalized her parents' attitudes. [VERB noun]

    随着时间的流逝她认同了父母的看法并将之融为自我意识的一部分。

  • ...a social order which depends on internalized feelings of what is right and wrong. [VERB-ed]

    依赖内化的是非观来维持的社会秩序

internalization

  • ...my internalisation of hatred, disgust and fear.

    仇恨、厌恶和恐惧慢慢在我内心沉淀
