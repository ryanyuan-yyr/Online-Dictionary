throng
θrɒŋ θrɔːŋ θrɔŋ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense throngs, present participle thronging, past tense , past participle thronged
1 N-COUNT 一大群人;人群 A throng is a large crowd of people. 一大群人;人群 [literary]

  • An official pushed through the throng.

    一名官员费力地挤过人群。

SYN crowd, mob, horde, press  
2 VERB (人群)涌向，蜂拥 When people throng somewhere, they go there in great numbers. (人群)涌向，蜂拥 [literary]

  • The crowds thronged into the mall. [V + to/into/around]

    人群涌进大厅。

  • ...the multitudes that throng around the Pope. [V to/into/around n]

    拥在教皇周围的教众甚多

SYN rush, pour, stream, press  
3 VERB 挤满;群集 If people throng a place, they are present there in great numbers. 挤满;群集

  • They throng the beaches between late June and early August. [VERB noun]

    6 月底到 8 月初，他们群聚在海滩上。

SYN pack, fill, crowd, press  
thronged

  • The streets are thronged with people.

    街上挤满了人。
