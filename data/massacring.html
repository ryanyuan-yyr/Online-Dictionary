massacre
mæsəkəʳ ˈmæsəkər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense massacres, present participle massacring, past tense , past participle massacred
1 N-VAR 大屠杀;大残杀 A massacre is the killing of a large number of people at the same time in a violent and cruel way. →see usage note at: kill 大屠杀;大残杀

  • Maria lost her 62-year-old mother in the massacre.

    在大屠杀中玛丽亚失去了62岁的母亲。

  • ...reports of massacre, torture and starvation.

    关于屠杀、拷打与挨饿的报道

2 VERB 大规模屠杀;大规模残杀 If people are massacred, a large number of them are attacked and killed in a violent and cruel way. 大规模屠杀;大规模残杀

  • 300 civilians are believed to have been massacred by the rebels. [be VERB-ed]

    据信有300位平民被叛乱分子残杀。

  • Troops indiscriminately massacred the defenceless population. [VERB noun]

    军队肆无忌惮地屠杀手无寸铁的民众。

SYN slaughter, kill, murder, butcher
