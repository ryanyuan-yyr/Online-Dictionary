damn
dæm dæm
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense damns, present participle damning, past tense , past participle damned
1 EXCLAM (表示愤怒或不耐烦)该死，可恶，他妈的 Damn ,damn it, and dammit are used by some people to express anger or impatience. (表示愤怒或不耐烦)该死，可恶，他妈的 [informal, rude, feelings]

  • Don't be flippant, damn it! This is serious.

    别不当回事，该死！这可不是闹着玩儿的事。

2 ADJ [ADJ n] (用于加强语气)该死的，可恶的，十足的，完全的 Damn is used by some people to emphasize what they are saying. (用于加强语气)该死的，可恶的，十足的，完全的 [informal, rude, emphasis]

  • There's not a damn thing you can do about it now.

    现在你对此做什么都完全无济于事。

Damn is also an adverb.

  • As it turned out, I was damn right.

    结果证明我百分百正确。

  • Let's have a damn good party.

    我们来搞一个超棒的聚会吧。

3 VERB 谴责，指责(政策、行动等) If you say that a person or a news report damns something such as a policy or action, you mean that they are very critical of it. 谴责，指责(政策、行动等)

  • ...a sensational book in which she damns the ultra-right party. [VERB noun]

    她在其中大力鞭挞该极右党派的耸人听闻的书

  • ...a report damning the chocolate advertising people for targeting women in their campaigns. [VERB noun]

    谴责巧克力广告企划人员在宣传中以女性为目标人群的一份报告

4 See also: damned  damning 
5 PHRASE 毫不在乎;完全不关心 If you say that someone does not give a damn about something, you are emphasizing that they do not care about it at all. 毫不在乎;完全不关心 [informal, rude, emphasis]
6 PHRASE [PHRASE before verb, PHR n/adj] 几乎;差一点 People use damn near to emphasize that what they are saying is not actually true, but is very close to being true. 几乎;差一点 [informal, rude, emphasis]

  • I damn near went crazy.

    我当时都快要疯了。

  • That's a question damn near every woman who ever lived has had to answer for herself.

    那几乎是每个有过生活经历的女人都必须要自己回答的问题。

7 PHRASE 几乎;差不多 Some people say as near as damn it or as near as dammit to emphasize that what they have said is almost completely accurate, but not quite. 几乎;差不多 [British, informal, rude, emphasis]

  • It's as near as damn it the same thing.

    这几乎没什么分别。

  • The stadium will be as near as dammit empty.

    体育场里将几乎空无一人。
