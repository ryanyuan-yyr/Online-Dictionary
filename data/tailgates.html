tailgate
teɪlgeɪt ˈteɪlˌgeɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense tailgates, present participle tailgating, past tense , past participle tailgated
1 N-COUNT (卡车、小轿车后面的)后背门，尾门，车斗门 A tailgate is a door at the back of a truck or car, that is hinged at the bottom so that it opens downwards. (卡车、小轿车后面的)后背门，尾门，车斗门

  • He let down the tailgate so the dog could jump out.

    他放下后背门，好让狗跳出来。

2 VERB 紧随…行驶 If you tailgate someone, you drive very closely behind them. 紧随…行驶

  • Perhaps the fact that the car was tailgating him made him accelerate. [VERB noun]

    或许是因为那辆汽车跟得太紧，他才加速的。

  • Police pulled him over doing 120km/h, making rapid changes and tailgating. [VERB]

    警察令他把车靠边停下，因为他的时速高达 120 公里还一直快速变道、紧贴行驶。
