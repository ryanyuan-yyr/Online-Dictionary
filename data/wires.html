wire
waɪəʳ waɪə waɪr
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense wires, present participle wiring, past tense , past participle wired
1 N-VAR 金属丝;金属线 A wire is a long thin piece of metal that is used to fasten things or to carry electric current. 金属丝;金属线

  • ...fine copper wire.

    细铜丝

  • ...gadgets which detect electrical wires, pipes and timbers in walls.

    探测墙内电线、管道和木材的小装置

2 N-COUNT 电线;导线;电缆 A wire is a cable which carries power or signals from one place to another. 电线;导线;电缆

  • I ripped out the telephone wire that ran through to his office.

    我把通到他办公室的电话线拔掉了。

  • ...the voltage of the overhead wires.

    头顶上方电线的电压

SYN cable, lead, flex  
3 VERB 在…里装电线;给…布线 If you wire something such as a building or piece of equipment, you put wires inside it so that electricity or signals can pass into or through it. 在…里装电线;给…布线 [Also V n P to/into n]

  • ...learning to wire and plumb the house herself. [VERB noun]

    她学着自己给房子布线走管

  • Lamps should be safely wired. [VERB noun]

    电灯布线要安全稳妥。

  • Each of the homes has a security system and is wired for cable television. [be VERB-ed + for]

    各户都有安防系统，还装了有线电视。

  • ...a badly wired appliance. [VERB-ed]

    电路设计不合理的电器

Wire up means the same as wire .wire up 同 wire

  • He was helping wire up the Channel Tunnel last season.

    他上一季在协助英吉利海峡隧道的电缆铺设工作。

  • Wire the thermometers up to trigger off an alarm bell if the temperature drops.

    给温度计接上线，如果温度下降，报警铃就会响。

  • Security experts wired up dozens of expensive plants to the main alarm system at his mansion.

    安防专家在数十株名贵苗木上布线，使与其别墅的主控警报系统连接。

4 N-COUNT 同 telegram A wire is the same as a telegram . 同 telegram [mainly US]
5 VERB 给…发电报 If you wire a person, you send them a telegram. 给…发电报 [mainly US] [Also VERB]

  • He wired the chairman immediately. [VERB noun]

    他马上给主席发了电报。

  • They wired back a long list of books. [V n with back]

    他们用电报发回了一长串书单。

  • If I get another tummy ache, I will wire you to come. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    如果我再肚子疼，我会发电报让你来。

6 VERB 电汇(款) If you wire an amount of money to a person or place, you tell a bank to send it to the person or place using a telegram message. 电汇(款) [mainly US] [Also VERB n]

  • I'm wiring you some money. [VERB noun noun]

    我要给你汇些钱。

  • They arranged to wire the money from the United States. [VERB noun prep.]

    他们安排从美国汇款。

  • Investigators say nearly $100,000 was wired into the suspect's bank accounts. [VERB noun prep.]

    调查人员说有近10万美元汇入了嫌疑人的银行账户。

SYN send, cable, telegraph  
7 PHRASE 到最后一刻 If something goes to the wire, it continues until the last possible moment. 到最后一刻 [mainly journalism]

  • Negotiators again worked right down to the wire to reach an agreement.

    谈判人员又是直到最后时刻才达成了协议。

8 See also: barbed wire  high wire  hot-wire  live wire
