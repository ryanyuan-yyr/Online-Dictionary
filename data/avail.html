avail
əveɪl əˈveɪl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense avails, present participle availing, past tense , past participle availed
1 PHRASE 毫无用处;没效果 If you do something to no avail or to little avail, what you do fails to achieve what you want. 毫无用处;没效果 [written]

  • His efforts were to no avail.

    他徒劳无功。

  • I apologized repeatedly, but to little avail.

    我一再道歉，但丝毫没有奏效。

2 VERB 接受（提议）；利用（机会） If you avail yourself of an offer or an opportunity, you accept the offer or make use of the opportunity. 接受（提议）；利用（机会） [formal]

  • Guests should feel at liberty to avail themselves of your facilities. [V pron-refl + of]

    宾客们应该可以随意使用你们的各种设施。

SYN benefit, work, help, serve
