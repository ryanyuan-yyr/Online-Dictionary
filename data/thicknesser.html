thick
θɪk θɪk
Word forms: comparative thicker, superlative thickest
1 ADJ-GRADED 厚的;粗的 Something that is thick has a large distance between its two opposite sides. 厚的;粗的

  • For breakfast I had a thick slice of bread and syrup.

    早餐我吃了一片厚厚的涂了糖浆的面包。

  • He wore glasses with thick rims.

    他戴了一副厚框眼镜。

  • This material is very thick and this needle is not strong enough to go through it.

    这种料子很厚，这根针都扎不透。

thickly

  • An old man sat in a great carved armchair of black wood, thickly padded with soft cushions.

    一个老人坐在一把精雕的黑檀木扶手椅上，椅子上铺了厚厚的软垫。

  • Slice the meat thickly.

    把肉切成厚片。

2 ADJ [n ADJ] [as ADJ as] 宽的;深的 You can use thick to talk or ask about how wide or deep something is. 宽的;深的

  • The folder was two inches thick.

    那个文件夹有两英寸厚。

  • How thick are these walls?

    这些墙有多厚？

  • ...a finger as thick as a sausage.

    和香肠一样粗的手指

SYN wide, across, deep, broad  
Thick is also a combining form.(亦可用于构词)

  • His life was saved by a quarter-inch-thick bullet-proof steel screen.

    多亏一块 1/4 英寸厚的防弹钢板，他才捡了条命。

thickness

  • The size of the fish will determine the thickness of the steaks.

    鱼的大小决定鱼排的厚度。

  • The egg had a shell thickness of 0.14mm and the newly-hatched chick weighed nine grams.

    那只蛋的壳厚 0.14 毫米，新孵出的雏鸟重 9 克。

  • ...a layer of gases about 200 miles in thickness.

    厚约 200 英里的大气层

3 ADJ-GRADED 浓的;密的;密集的;茂密的 If something that consists of several things is thick, it has a large number of them very close together. 浓的;密的;密集的;茂密的

  • She inherited our father's thick, wavy hair.

    她继承了我们父亲那头浓密的卷发。

  • They walked through thick forest.

    他们徒步穿越了茂密的森林。

SYN luxuriant, heavy, dense, abundant  
thickly

  • I rounded a bend where the trees and brush grew thickly.

    我绕过一个林木茂盛的弯。

  • The interior flatlands and valleys are thickly planted with coconuts.

    内陆的平地和山谷中种着茂密的椰子树。

4 ADJ-GRADED [verb-link ADJ with n] 充满的;密布的 If something is thick with another thing, the first thing is full of or covered with the second. 充满的;密布的

  • The air is thick with acrid smoke from the fires. [+ with]

    空气中弥漫着火灾产生的刺鼻浓烟。

  • She ate scones thick with butter.

    她吃了一些涂满了黄油的烤饼。

SYN crowded, full, packed, covered  
5 ADJ-GRADED (衣服)厚的，厚重的，暖和的 Thick clothes are made from heavy cloth, so that they will keep you warm in cold weather. (衣服)厚的，厚重的，暖和的

  • In the winter she wears thick socks, Wellington boots and gloves.

    冬天，她穿着厚袜子和橡胶雨靴，戴着手套。

  • She wore a thick tartan skirt and a red cashmere sweater.

    她穿了一条厚厚的格子呢裙和一件红色羊绒衫。

SYN heavy, heavyweight, dense, chunky  
6 ADJ-GRADED (烟、雾、云)浓的，混浊的，能见度低的 Thick smoke, fog, or cloud is difficult to see through. (烟、雾、云)浓的，混浊的，能见度低的

  • The smoke was bluish-black and thick.

    那烟黑中带蓝，而且很浓。

  • It wasn't very thick fog.

    雾不算很浓。

SYN opaque, heavy, dense, impenetrable  
7 ADJ-GRADED (液体)浓的，稠的 Thick liquids are fairly stiff and solid and do not flow easily. (液体)浓的，稠的

  • They had to battle through thick mud to reach construction workers.

    他们只好挣扎着穿过厚厚的泥巴去找建筑工人。

  • The sauce is thick and rich so don't bother trying to diet.

    这个酱汁又浓又香，别费劲节食了。

SYN viscous, concentrated, stiff, condensed  
8 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] (声音)不清楚的，沙哑的，低沉的 If someone's voice is thick, they are not speaking clearly, for example because they are ill, upset, or drunk. (声音)不清楚的，沙哑的，低沉的

  • When he spoke his voice was thick with bitterness. [+ with]

    他说话时，声音低沉，充满痛苦。

SYN husky, rough, hoarse, distorted  
thickly

  • 'It's all my fault,' he mumbled thickly.

    “全都怪我，”他咕哝道。

9 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (口音)重的，浓的 A thick accent is very obvious and easy to identify. (口音)重的，浓的

  • He answered our questions in English but with a thick accent.

    他用英语回答了我们的问题，不过口音很重。

  • 'What do you want?' a teenage girl demanded in a thick German accent.

    “你想要什么？”一个十几岁少女操着一口浓重的德国口音问道。

SYN strong, marked, broad, decided  
10 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 笨的;蠢的 If you describe someone as thick, you think they are stupid. 笨的;蠢的 [British, informal, disapproval]

  • How could she have been so thick?

    她怎么能蠢到那个地步？

SYN stupid, slow, dull, dense  
11 PHRASE 大量而迅速地;接二连三地 If things happen thick and fast, they happen very quickly and in large numbers. 大量而迅速地;接二连三地

  • The rumours have been coming thick and fast.

    谣言接二连三地传来。

  • Distress calls were pouring in thick and fast from all over the area.

    求救电话从该地区各地蜂拥而至。

12 PHRASE 积极从事;深度参与 If you are in the thick of an activity or situation, you are very involved in it. 积极从事;深度参与

  • I enjoy being in the thick of things.

    我喜欢全身心投入到事情当中。

  • Peterson suddenly found himself in the thick of desperate fighting.

    彼得森突然发现自己身陷一场殊死搏斗中。

13 PHRASE 不顾艰难险阻;历尽千辛万苦 If you do something through thick and thin, you do it although the conditions or circumstances are very bad. 不顾艰难险阻;历尽千辛万苦

  • She'd stuck by Bob through thick and thin.

    她对鲍勃不离不弃，甘苦与共。

  • I will go on loving James through thick and thin no matter what happens.

    不管发生什么，不管经历多少艰辛，我都会一直爱着詹姆斯。
