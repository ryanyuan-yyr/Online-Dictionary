expel
ɪkspel ɪkˈspɛl ɛkˈspɛl ɪkˈspɛl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense expels, present participle expelling, past tense , past participle expelled
1 VERB [usually passive] 开除;将…除名 If someone is expelled from a school or organization, they are officially told to leave because they have behaved badly. 开除;将…除名

  • More than five-thousand secondary school students have been expelled for cheating. [be VERB-ed]

    5,000多名中学生因为作弊被开除。

  • ...a boy expelled from school for making death threats to his teacher. [VERB-ed]

    因为威胁要杀死老师而被学校开除的男孩

SYN throw out, exclude, ban, bar  
2 VERB 逐出;驱逐 If people are expelled from a place, they are made to leave it, often by force. 逐出;驱逐

  • An American academic was expelled from the country yesterday. [be VERB-ed + from]

    昨天一位美国学者被逐出该国。

  • They were told at first that they should simply expel the refugees. [VERB noun]

    一开始有人告诉他们应该直接将那些难民驱逐出境。

SYN banish, exile, oust, deport  
3 VERB 排出;喷出 To expel something means to force it out from a container or from your body. 排出;喷出

  • Daily brushing of the skin helps the skin expel toxins. [VERB noun]

    每天擦洗皮肤有助于皮肤排毒。

  • As the lungs exhale this waste, gas is expelled into the atmosphere. [be VERB-ed]

    肺呼出这些废气被排到空气中。

SYN drive out, discharge, throw out, force out
