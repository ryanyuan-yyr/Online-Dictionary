applaud
əplɔːd əˈplɔd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense applauds, present participle applauding, past tense , past participle applauded
1 VERB 鼓掌（以示赞赏） When a group of people applaud, they clap their hands in order to show approval, for example when they have enjoyed a play or concert. 鼓掌（以示赞赏）

  • The audience laughed and applauded. [VERB]

    观众边笑边鼓掌。

  • Every person stood to applaud his unforgettable act of courage. [VERB noun]

    所有人起立为他不可磨灭的英勇之举鼓掌。

SYN clap, encourage, praise, cheer  
2 VERB 称赞；赞许 When an attitude or action is ap-plauded, people praise it. 称赞；赞许

  • He should be applauded for his courage. [be VERB-ed + for]

    他的勇气值得称道。

  • This last move can only be applauded. [be VERB-ed]

    最后这招令人拍手叫绝。

  • She applauds the fact that they are promoting new ideas. [VERB noun]

    他们在宣传新思想，对此她表示赞许。

SYN praise, celebrate, approve, acclaim
