deputize
depjʊtaɪz ˈdɛpjuˌtaɪz ˈdɛpjəˌtaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense deputizes, present participle deputizing, past tense , past participle deputized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 deputise
VERB 担任代表;充当代理人 If you deputize for someone, you do something on their behalf, for example attend a meeting. 担任代表;充当代理人

  • I sometimes had to deputise for him in the kitchen. [VERB + for]

    有时候我不得不代替他下厨。

  • Herr Schulmann cannot be here to welcome you and has asked me to deputize. [VERB]

    赫尔·舒尔曼无法亲自来迎接您，所以特请我作为代表。

SYN stand in for, act for, take the place of, understudy
