unnerve
ʌnnɜːʳv ʌnˈnɜːv ʌnˈnɜrv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense unnerves, present participle unnerving, past tense , past participle unnerved
VERB 使担忧；使烦恼 If you say that something unnerves you, you mean that it worries or troubles you. 使担忧；使烦恼

  • The news about Dermot had unnerved me. [VERB noun]

    关于德莫特的消息让我很担心。

  • Tony was unnerved by the uncanny familiarity of her face. [VERB noun]

    托尼对她的样子异常熟悉，这很让他烦恼。

SYN shake, upset, disconcert, disturb
