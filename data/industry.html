industry
ɪndəstri ˈɪndəstri
Word forms: plural industries
1 N-UNCOUNT 工业 Industry is the work and processes involved in collecting raw materials, and making them into products in factories. 工业

  • British industry suffers through insufficient investment in research.

    研究投入不足让英国工业深受其害。

  • ...in countries where industry is developing rapidly.

    在工业迅速发展的国家

SYN business, production, manufacturing, trade  
2 N-COUNT [oft n NOUN] 行业 A particular industry consists of all the people and activities involved in making a particular product or providing a particular service. 行业

  • ...the motor vehicle and textile industries.

    汽车业与纺织业

  • ...the Scottish tourist industry.

    苏格兰旅游业

3 N-COUNT [usually singular] 产业;声势浩大的活动 If you refer to a social or political activity as an industry, you are criticizing it because you think it involves a lot of people in unnecessary or useless work. 产业;声势浩大的活动 [disapproval]

  • Some Afro-Caribbeans are rejecting the whole race relations industry.

    一些加勒比黑人在抵制整个种族关系运动。

  • The multibillion-dollar fitness industry rakes in fat profits from our hunger to look good.

    数十亿美元的健身产业利用我们的爱美之心赚取巨额利润。

4 N-UNCOUNT 勤劳;勤奋 Industry is the fact of working very hard. 勤劳;勤奋 [formal]

  • No one doubted his ability, his industry or his integrity.

    没人怀疑他的能力、勤奋和正直。

SYN diligence, effort, labour, hard work
