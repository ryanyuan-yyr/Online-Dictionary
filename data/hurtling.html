hurtle
hɜːʳtəl ˈhɜrtəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense hurtles, present participle hurtling, past tense , past participle hurtled
VERB 猛冲;飞驰 If someone or something hurtles somewhere, they move there very quickly, often in a rough or violent way. 猛冲;飞驰

  • A pretty young girl came hurtling down the stairs. [VERB prep.]

    一个漂亮的小女孩从楼梯上冲下来。

SYN rush, charge, race, shoot
