move on
1 PHRASAL VERB 离去;继续前进 When you move on somewhere, you leave the place where you have been staying or waiting and go there. 离去;继续前进

  • Mr Brooke moved on from Paris to Belgrade. [VERB PREP. prep./adv.]

    布鲁克先生离开巴黎，继续前往贝尔格莱德。

  • What's wrong with his wanting to sell his land and move on? [VERB PREP.]

    他想卖掉土地离开这里，这有什么错呢？

2 PHRASAL VERB 命令…(或不要停留走开) If someone such as a policeman moves you on, they order you to stop standing in a particular place and to go somewhere else. 命令…(或不要停留走开) [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • Eventually the police were called to move them on. [VERB noun PREP.]

    终于有人叫来警察驱散他们。

3 PHRASAL VERB (结束某活动后) 改做别事 If you move on, you finish or stop one activity and start doing something different. (结束某活动后) 改做别事

  • She ran this shop for ten years before deciding to move on to fresh challenges. [VERB PREP. + to]

    在决定涉足新的领域时，她经营这家商店已有10年。

  • His mother, Julia, soon moved on to a new relationship. [V P to n]

    他妈妈朱莉娅很快又开始了一段新恋情。

  • Now, can we move on and discuss the vital business of the day. [VERB PREP.]

    现在，我们接着往下讨论今天的重要事宜。
