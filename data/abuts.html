abut
əbʌt əˈbʌt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense abuts, present participle abutting, past tense , past participle abutted
VERB 邻接；毗连；紧靠 When land or a building abuts something or abuts on something, it is next to it. 邻接；毗连；紧靠 [formal]

  • One edge of the garden abutted on an old entrance to the mine. [VERB on noun]

    花园的一侧紧靠着矿井的旧入口。

  • He was born in 1768 in the house abutting our hotel. [VERB noun]

    他于1768年出生于我们旅馆旁边的一幢房子里。

SYN adjoin, join, touch, border
