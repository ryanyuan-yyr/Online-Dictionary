visit
vɪzɪt ˈvɪzɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense visits, present participle visiting, past tense , past participle visited
1 VERB 看望;拜访 If you visit someone, you go to see them and spend time with them. 看望;拜访

  • He wanted to visit his brother in Worcester. [VERB noun]

    他想去看望住在伍斯特的哥哥。

  • He was visited by an old friend from Iraq. [VERB noun]

    一位来自伊拉克的老朋友来看望他。

  • Bill would visit on weekends. [VERB]

    周末比尔会来。

Visit is also a noun.

  • Helen had recently paid him a visit.

    海伦最近去探望了他。

2 VERB 访问;参观;游览 If you visit a place, you go there for a short time. 访问;参观;游览

  • He'll be visiting four cities including Cagliari in Sardinia. [VERB noun]

    他将要访问4个城市，包括萨迪尼亚的卡利亚里。

  • Caroline visited all the big stores. [VERB noun]

    卡罗琳光顾了所有的大型商店。

  • ...a visiting truck driver. [VERB-ing]

    一位路过的卡车司机

SYN stay in, see, tour, explore  
Visit is also a noun.

  • ...the Pope's visit to Canada.

    教皇对加拿大的访问

  • I paid a visit to my local print shop.

    我去了我们当地的照片冲洗店。

3 VERB 访问，浏览（网站） If you visit a website, you look at it. 访问，浏览（网站） [computing]

  • For details visit our website at www.cobuild.collins.co.uk. [VERB noun]

    欲知详情，请访问我们的网站www.cobuild.collins.co.uk。

4 VERB 上门咨询（专业人士）;（专业人士）上门服务 If you visit a professional person such as a doctor or lawyer, you go and see them in order to get professional advice. If they visit you, they come to see you in order to give you professional advice. 上门咨询（专业人士）;（专业人士）上门服务 [mainly British]

  • If necessary the patient can then visit his doctor for further advice. [VERB noun]

    如有必要，病人可以上门咨询自己的医生以得到更多的建议。

  • A doctor will visit you in your apartment. [VERB noun]

    会有医生到您的住所出诊。

Visit is also a noun.

  • You may have regular home visits from a neonatal nurse.

    可以雇请育婴护士定期到家里提供服务。

5 V-PASSIVE （不快之事）降临在…身上 If something very unpleasant is visited upon you, it happens to you. （不快之事）降临在…身上 [formal]

  • Violence is visited upon us every day. [be V-ed upon/on n]

    暴力事件每天都会降临在我们身上。

  • Death and suffering had been visited on thousands of innocents. [be V-ed upon/on n]

    死亡和苦难降临在了数千名无辜的人身上。
