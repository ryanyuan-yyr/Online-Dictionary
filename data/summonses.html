summons
sʌmənz ˈsʌmənz
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense summonses, present participle summonsing, past tense , past participle summonsed
1 N-COUNT 召唤;召见令 A summons is an order to come and see someone. 召唤;召见令

  • I received a summons to the Palace from Sir Robert Fellowes, the Queen's private secretary.

    我被女王的私人秘书罗伯特·费洛斯爵士召往白金汉宫。

2 N-COUNT (法院的)传讯，传票，传唤 A summons is an official order to appear in court. (法院的)传讯，传票，传唤

  • She had received a summons to appear in court.

    她已经接到法院传唤其出庭的传票。

SYN court order, warrant, writ, subpoena  
3 VERB [usually passive] 传讯;传唤;发传票给 If someone is summonsed, they are officially ordered to appear in court. 传讯;传唤;发传票给

  • The men were summonsed and last week 30 appeared before Hove magistrates. [be VERB-ed]

    这些人被传唤，上周有30人到霍夫地方法院接受讯问。

  • She has been summonsed to appear at St Albans magistrates' court. [be VERB-ed to-infinitive]

    她已经收到传票，要求其到圣奥尔本斯地方法院出庭。

SYN serve with a writ, summon, subpoena, cite
