belabour
bɪleɪbəʳ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense belabours, present participle belabouring, past tense , past participle belaboured
in AM, use 美国英语用 belabor
1 VERB 痛打；殴打；重击 If you belabour someone or something, you hit them hard and repeatedly. 痛打；殴打；重击 [old-fashioned]

  • Men began to belabour his shoulders, his head, his arms with sticks. [VERB noun]

    暴徒开始用棍棒猛打他的肩、头和胳膊。

SYN beat, hit, strike, knock  
2 VERB 唠叨；絮絮叨叨地谈 If you say that someone belabours the point, you mean that they keep on talking about it, perhaps in an annoying or boring way. 唠叨；絮絮叨叨地谈

  • I won't belabour the point, for this is a familiar story. [VERB noun]

    因为这点已经是众所周知了，我就不再啰唆了。
