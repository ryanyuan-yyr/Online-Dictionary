snake
sneɪk sneɪk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense snakes, present participle snaking, past tense , past participle snaked
1 N-COUNT 蛇 A snake is a long, thin reptile without legs. 蛇
SYN serpent  
2 VERB 蜿蜒而行;曲折而行 Something that snakes in a particular direction goes in that direction in a line with a lot of bends. 蜿蜒而行;曲折而行 [literary]

  • The road snaked through forested mountains. [VERB prep./adv.]

    道路在林间蜿蜒延伸。

  • The three-mile procession snaked its way through the richest streets of the capital. [VERB way prep./adv.]

    3 英里长的游行队伍弯弯曲曲地穿过了首都最繁华的街道。

SYN wind, twist, curve, turn
