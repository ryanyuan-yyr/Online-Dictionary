vacillate
væsɪleɪt ˈvæsəˌleɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense vacillates, present participle vacillating, past tense , past participle vacillated
VERB 犹豫不定;踌躇;拿不定主意 If you vacillate between two alternatives or choices, you keep changing your mind. 犹豫不定;踌躇;拿不定主意 [formal]

  • She vacillates between men twice her age and men younger than she. [V + between]

    她犹豫不决，不知道是该选择年龄比她大一倍的男人还是比她小的男人。

  • We cannot vacillate on the question of the party's leadership. [+ on

    在党的领导权问题上我们不能动摇。

SYN waver, hesitate, dither [mainly British] , haver  
vacillation

  • He accused President Carter of vacillation and retreat.

    他指责卡特总统的动摇和退缩。

  • ...Stalin's miscalculations and vacillations.

    斯大林的判断失误和摇摆不定
