trousers
traʊzəʳz ˈtraʊzəz ˈtraʊzərz
The form trouser is used as a modifier. trouser 用作修饰词。
1 N-PLURAL [oft a pair of NOUN] 裤子 Trousers are a piece of clothing that you wear over your body from the waist downwards, and that cover each leg separately. 裤子 [mainly British]

  • He was smartly dressed in a shirt, dark trousers and boots.

    他穿着衬衫和黑裤子，脚蹬皮靴，看上去很帅气。

  • Alexander rolled up his trouser legs.

    亚历山大把裤腿卷起来。
