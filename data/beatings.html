beating
biːtɪŋ ˈbitɪŋ
Word forms: plural beatings
1 N-COUNT （尤指用棍子等进行的）殴打，痛打 If someone is given a beating, they are hit hard many times, especially with something such as a stick. （尤指用棍子等进行的）殴打，痛打

  • ...the savage beating of a black motorist by white police officers.

    白人警察对一名黑人驾车者的野蛮殴打。

  • The team secured pictures of prisoners showing signs of severe beatings.

    这个小组搞到了一些显示囚犯有被毒打痕迹的照片。

SYN thrashing, hiding [informal] , belting [informal] , whipping [slang]  
2 N-SING （竞争或选举中的）大败，惨败 If something such as a business, a political party, or a team takes a beating, it is defeated by a large amount in a competition or election. （竞争或选举中的）大败，惨败

  • Our firm has taken a terrible beating in recent years.

    我们公司近几年败得一塌糊涂。

3 PHRASE 难以被超越 If you say that something will take some beating, you mean that it is very good and it is unlikely that anything better will be done or made. 难以被超越 [informal]

  • For sheer scale and grandeur, Leeds Castle in Kent takes some beating.

    单从规模和气势来说，肯特的利兹城堡独领风骚。
