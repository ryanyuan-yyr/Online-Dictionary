dagger
dægəʳ ˈdægər
Word forms: plural daggers
1 N-COUNT 匕首;短剑 A dagger is a weapon like a knife with two sharp edges. 匕首;短剑
SYN knife, blade, bayonet, dirk  
2 PHRASE 剑拔弩张;势不两立 If you say that two people are at daggers drawn, you mean they are having an argument and are still very angry with each other. 剑拔弩张;势不两立 [British]

  • She and her mother were at daggers drawn.

    她和她母亲之间的关系异常紧张。

SYN on bad terms, at odds, at war, at loggerheads  
Quotations
Is this a dagger which I see before me
The handle toward my hand?William Shakespeare Macbeth
