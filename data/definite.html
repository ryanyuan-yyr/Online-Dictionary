definite
defɪnɪt ˈdɛfənɪt
1 ADJ-GRADED 明确的;确定的 If something such as a decision or an arrangement is definite, it is firm and clear, and unlikely to be changed. 明确的;确定的

  • It's too soon to give a definite answer.

    要给出一个明确的答复，现在还为时过早。

  • Her Royal Highness has definite views about most things.

    女王陛下对大多数事情都有明确的观点。

  • She made no definite plans for her future.

    她对未来没有确定的计划。

SYN specific, exact, precise, clear  
2 ADJ [usually ADJ n] (证据或信息)确凿的，确切的 Definite evidence or information is true, rather than being someone's opinion or guess. (证据或信息)确凿的，确切的

  • We didn't have any definite proof.

    我们没有任何确实的证据。

  • If you have any definite news of my husband, please let me know.

    如果有我丈夫的确切消息，请一定告诉我。

  • The police had nothing definite against her.

    警方没有掌握对她不利的确凿证据。

SYN clear, explicit, black-and-white, clear-cut  
3 ADJ-GRADED [ADJ n] 清楚的;明显的;无疑的 You use definite to emphasize the strength of your opinion or belief. 清楚的;明显的;无疑的 [emphasis]

  • There has already been a definite improvement.

    已经有了显著的改进。

  • That's a very definite possibility.

    那是很有可能的。

SYN noticeable, marked, clear, decided  
4 ADJ-GRADED 肯定的;有把握的 Someone who is definite behaves or talks in a firm, confident way. 肯定的;有把握的

  • Mary is very definite about this.

    玛丽对此非常肯定。

5 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 清晰的;分明的 A definite shape or colour is clear and noticeable. 清晰的;分明的

  • Studying his face in the bathroom mirror he wished he had more definite features.

    看着浴室镜子中的脸，他真希望自己的五官能够更分明一些。
