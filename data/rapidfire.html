rapid-fire
ˈræpɪdˌfaɪə ˈræpɪdˈfaɪr
1 ADJ [ADJ n] （枪）速射的 A rapid-fire gun is one that shoots a lot of bullets very quickly, one after the other. （枪）速射的

  • In the back of the truck was a 12.7 millimeter rapid-fire machine gun.

    在卡车后面有一只12.7毫米口径的速射机关枪。

2 ADJ [ADJ n] （说话或演讲）语速快的，连珠炮似的 A rapid-fire conversation or speech is one in which people talk or reply very quickly. （说话或演讲）语速快的，连珠炮似的

  • Yul listened to their sophisticated, rapid-fire conversation.

    尤尔听了他们老练的、连珠炮似的一番谈话。

  • ...arguing a point in rapid-fire Spanish.

    用连珠炮似的西班牙语争论一件事情

3 ADJ [ADJ n] （经济活动或发展）迅速发生的 A rapid-fire economic activity or development is one that takes place very quickly. （经济活动或发展）迅速发生的 [mainly US, journalism]

  • ...the rapid-fire buying and selling of stocks.

    原料的迅速购进与卖出

  • The company made a rapid-fire series of settlements with 25 States.

    公司同25个州进行了一连串迅速的结算。

  • ...Japan's rapid-fire industrialization.

    日本的迅速工业化
