break into
1 PHRASAL VERB 强行进入；闯入 If someone breaks into a building, they get into it by force. 强行进入；闯入

  • There was no one nearby who might see him trying to break into the house. [VERB PREP. noun]

    附近没有人可能会看见他试图闯入房子里。

  • In this country a house is broken into every 24 seconds. [VERB PREP. noun]

    在这个国家每24秒就会有一所房子被人破门而入。

SYN burgle  
2 PHRASAL VERB 突然开始做（某事） If someone breaks into something they suddenly start doing it. For example if someone breaks into a run they suddenly start running, and if they break into song they suddenly start singing. 突然开始做（某事）

  • The moment she was out of sight she broke into a run. [VERB PREP. noun]

    她一走出众人的视线就开始飞奔起来。

  • Then, breaking into a smile, he said, 'I brought you something.' [VERB PREP. noun]

    然后，他忽然露出笑容，说道：“我给你带了一样东西。”

SYN begin, start, burst into, give way to  
3 PHRASAL VERB 成功打入，顺利进入（某行业，尤指很难取得成功的领域） If you break into a profession or area of business, especially one that is difficult to succeed in, you manage to have some success in it. 成功打入，顺利进入（某行业，尤指很难取得成功的领域）

  • She finally broke into films after an acclaimed stage career. [VERB PREP. noun]

    她在取得舞台生涯的成功后，最终进入电影界发展。
