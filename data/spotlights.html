spotlight
spɒtlaɪt ˈspɑtˌlaɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense spotlights, present participle spotlighting, past tense , past participle spotlighted
1 N-COUNT 聚光灯 A spotlight is a powerful light, for example in a theatre, which can be directed so that it lights up a small area. 聚光灯
2 VERB 使受关注;使突出醒目 If something spotlights a particular problem or situation, it makes people notice it and think about it. 使受关注;使突出醒目

  • The budget crisis also spotlighted a weakening American economy. [VERB noun]

    预算危机也突显了正在衰退的美国经济。

  • ...a new book spotlighting female entrepreneurs. [VERB noun]

    一本关注女企业家的新书

SYN highlight, feature, draw attention to, focus attention on  
3 PHRASE 成为公众关注的焦点;受到瞩目 Someone or something that is in the spotlight is getting a great deal of public attention. 成为公众关注的焦点;受到瞩目

  • Webb is back in the spotlight.

    韦布又重新成为焦点。

4 PHRASE [PHRASE after verb] 备受(媒体和公众的)关注 If someone or something comes under the spotlight, they are thoroughly examined, especially by journalists and the public. 备受(媒体和公众的)关注

  • The economy will come under the spotlight today at the conference of the Trades Union Congress.

    经济将会成为今天工会代表大会会议上的焦点。
