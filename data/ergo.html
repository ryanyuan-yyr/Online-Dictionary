ergo
ɜːʳgoʊ ˈɛrgoʊ
ADV 因此，所以（有时替代therefore） Ergo is sometimes used instead of 'therefore' to introduce a clause in which you mention something that is the consequence or logical result of what you have just said. 因此，所以（有时替代therefore） [formal, or literary]

  • Neither side would have an incentive to start a war. Ergo, peace would reign.

    双方都不会有开战的动机，所以将会是和平时期。
