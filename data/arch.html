arch
ɑːʳtʃ ɑrtʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense arches, present participle arching, past tense , past participle arched
1 N-COUNT 拱;拱门;拱形结构 An arch is a structure that is curved at the top and is supported on either side by a pillar, post, or wall. 拱;拱门;拱形结构
2 N-COUNT 弧形;弧形运动 An arch is a curved line or movement. 弧形;弧形运动
3 N-COUNT 足弓;足背 The arch of your foot is the curved section at the bottom in the middle. 足弓;足背
4 V-ERG 弓着,佝着（背）等 If you arch a part of your body such as your back or if it arches, you bend it so that it forms a curve. 弓着,佝着（背）等 [Also VERB]

  • Don't arch your back, keep your spine straight. [VERB noun]

    别佝着背，把腰挺直。

5 V-ERG （表示惊奇或不赞同）扬起（眉毛） If you arch your eyebrows or if they arch, you move them upwards as a way of showing surprise or disapproval. （表示惊奇或不赞同）扬起（眉毛） [literary] [Also VERB]

  • 'Oh really?' he said, arching an eyebrow. [VERB noun]

    “哦，真的吗？”他扬起一边的眉毛说。

6 VERB 拱起；成弓形；呈弧形运动 If something arches in a particular direction, it makes a curved line or movement. 拱起；成弓形；呈弧形运动

  • I gazed up at the domed ceiling arching overhead. [VERB adv./prep.]

    我抬头凝望着上方弧形的穹顶。

SYN curve, bridge, bend, bow  
7 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 神秘的；淘气的；调皮的 An arch look is mysterious and mischievous. 神秘的；淘气的；调皮的

  • Both of them looked at him with that curious, slightly amused and even arch expression.

    他们俩都看着他，带着一副好奇的、有点觉得好笑甚至是淘气的表情。

archly

  • She looked at me rather archly.

    她有些调皮地看着我。

8 ADJ-GRADED 傲慢自大的 If you say that someone is arch, you are criticizing them for talking or behaving as if they are better or more important than other people. 傲慢自大的 [disapproval]

  • Their attempts to be casual have so far just looked arch or patronising.

    他们本想做出漫不经心的样子，结果反而显得傲慢自大或自视甚高。

archly

  • 'You can't fool me,' Shirley said archly.

    “你骗不了我，”雪莉傲慢地说。

9 See also: arched 
arch- ɑːʳtʃ- ɑrtʃ ɑrk
COMB in N-COUNT （与指人的名词连用，构成复合名词）表示“主要的”，“最重要的” Arch- combines with nouns referring to people to form new nouns that refer to people who are extreme examples of something. For example, your arch-rival is the rival you most want to beat. （与指人的名词连用，构成复合名词）表示“主要的”，“最重要的”

  • Neither he nor his arch-rival, Giuseppe De Rita, won.

    他和他的死对头朱塞佩·德里察双双落败。

  • ...his arch-enemy.

    他的死敌
