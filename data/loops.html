loop
luːp lup
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense loops, present participle looping, past tense , past participle looped
1 N-COUNT （绳等形成的）圈，环 A loop is a curved or circular shape in something long, for example in a piece of string. （绳等形成的）圈，环

  • Mrs. Morrell reached for a loop of garden hose.

    莫雷尔太太伸手去拿绕成圈的花园浇水用的软管。

SYN curve, ring, circle, bend  
2 VERB 把（绳等）打成圈（或环） If you loop something such as a piece of rope around an object, you tie a length of it in a loop around the object, for example in order to fasten it to the object. 把（绳等）打成圈（或环）

  • He looped the rope over the wood. [VERB noun prep.]

    他把绳子绕到木头上。

  • He wore the watch and chain looped round his neck like a medallion. [VERB-ed]

    他将链表绕挂在脖子上，就像戴了一枚大奖章。

SYN twist, turn, join, roll  
3 VERB 打环；成圈 If something loops somewhere, it goes there in a circular direction that makes the shape of a loop. 打环；成圈

  • The enemy was looping around the south side. [VERB prep./adv.]

    敌人正从南侧包抄过来。

  • The helicopter took off and headed north. Then it looped west, heading for the hills. [VERB prep./adv.]

    直升机起飞后向北飞行，接着又兜向西边，朝山那边飞去。

SYN turn, roll, circle, bend  
4 PHRASE 属于/不属于圈内人士 If someone is in the loop, they are part of a group of people who make decisions about important things, or they know about these decisions. If they are out of the loop, they do not make or know about important decisions. 属于/不属于圈内人士 [mainly US, informal]

  • I think that the vice president was in the loop.

    我认为副总统是局内人。

  • These activists don't want to feel out of the loop.

    这些活跃分子不想做局外人。
