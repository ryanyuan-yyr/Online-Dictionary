propitiate
prəpɪʃieɪt proʊˈpɪʃiˌeɪt prəˈpɪʃiˌeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense propitiates, present participle propitiating, past tense , past participle propitiated
VERB 劝解；抚慰；使息怒 If you propitiate someone, you stop them being angry or impatient by doing something to please them. 劝解；抚慰；使息怒 [formal]

  • I've never gone out of my way to propitiate people. [VERB noun]

    我从未刻意取悦别人。

  • These ancient ceremonies propitiate the spirits of the waters. [VERB noun]

    这些古老的仪式让水中的神灵平息了怒气。

SYN appease, satisfy, reconcile, placate
