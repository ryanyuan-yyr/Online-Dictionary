so-so
ˈsoʊˌsoʊ
ADJ 一般;过得去;不好不坏 If you say that something is so-so, you mean that it is average in quality, rather than being very good or very bad. 一般;过得去;不好不坏 [informal]

  • Their lunch was only so-so.

    他们的午餐只能算是凑合。

  • Sommers performed a flute solo a few weeks ago that got so-so reviews.

    萨默斯几周前表演了一场长笛独奏，反映平平。

SYN average, middling, fair, ordinary  
So-so is also an adverb.

  • 'How's it going?' — 'So-so.'

    “进展如何？”“马马虎虎。”
