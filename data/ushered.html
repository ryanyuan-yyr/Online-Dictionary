usher
ʌʃəʳ ˈʌʃər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense ushers, present participle ushering, past tense , past participle ushered
1 VERB 引;领;陪同 If you usher someone somewhere, you show them where they should go, often by going with them. 引;领;陪同 [formal]

  • I ushered him into the office. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    我领他去办公室。

  • They were quickly ushered away. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他们被迅速领开。

SYN escort, lead, direct, guide  
2 N-COUNT （婚礼、音乐会等的）引座员，招待员，迎宾员 An usher is a person who shows people where to sit, for example at a wedding or at a concert. （婚礼、音乐会等的）引座员，招待员，迎宾员

  • He did part-time work as an usher in a theatre.

    他在一家戏院兼职当引座员。

SYN attendant, guide, doorman, usherette  
3 N-COUNT （英国）法庭庭警 An usher is a person who organizes people who are attending a law court in Britain. （英国）法庭庭警
Phrasal verbs:
usher in
