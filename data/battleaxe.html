battle-axe
Word forms: plural battle-axes
The spellings battleaxe, and in American English battle-ax are also used. 亦拼作battleaxe和battle-ax（美国英语）。
1 N-COUNT 悍妇；母老虎 If you call a middle-aged or older woman a battle-axe, you mean she is very difficult and unpleasant because of her fierce and determined attitude. 悍妇；母老虎 [informal, disapproval]

  • Grandma is something else — a battle-axe from the old country who hasn't smiled in decades.

    奶奶不太像奶奶—倒像是来自故乡的母老虎，她已经几十年没有露出过笑脸了。

SYN harridan, witch, fury, nag  
2 N-COUNT 战斧 A battle-axe is a large axe that was used as a weapon. 战斧
