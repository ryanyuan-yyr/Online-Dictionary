graveyard
greɪvjɑːʳd ˈgreɪvˌjɑrd
Word forms: plural graveyards
1 N-COUNT （有时指教堂边的）墓地，坟场 A graveyard is an area of land, sometimes near a church, where dead people are buried. （有时指教堂边的）墓地，坟场

  • They made their way to a graveyard to pay their traditional respects to the dead.

    他们按惯例前往墓地悼念死者。

SYN cemetery, churchyard, burial ground, charnel house  
2 N-COUNT [usually singular] 废物堆积处；垃圾场 If you call a place a graveyard of particular things, you mean that there are many broken or unwanted things of that kind there. 废物堆积处；垃圾场 [disapproval]

  • This had once been the greatest port in the world, now it was a graveyard of rusting cranes. [+ of]

    这里一度是全球最大的港口，现在堆满了锈迹斑斑的吊车。

3 N-COUNT [usu N for/of n] 令人屡遭失败的事;失意之地 If you call an event or place the graveyard for particular people or their hopes, you mean that those people have often failed in such events or in that place. 令人屡遭失败的事;失意之地 [written]

  • Europe has been the graveyard for American golfers recently.

    欧洲最近成了美国高尔夫运动员的滑铁卢。
