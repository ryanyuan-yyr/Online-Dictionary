glide
glaɪd glaɪd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense glides, present participle gliding, past tense , past participle glided
1 VERB 滑动；滑行；悄悄地走 If you glide somewhere, you move silently and in a smooth and effortless way. 滑动；滑行；悄悄地走

  • Waiters glide between tightly packed tables bearing trays of pasta. [VERB prep./adv.]

    侍者们端着一盘盘意大利面在拥挤的餐桌之间自如穿行。

SYN slip, sail, slide, ghost  
2 VERB 滑翔 When birds or aeroplanes glide, they float on air currents. 滑翔 [Also VERB]

  • Our only companion is the wandering albatross, which glides effortlessly and gracefully behind the yacht. [VERB prep./adv.]

    陪伴我们的仅有那只盘旋的信天翁，它在游艇后面优雅轻盈地翱翔。

SYN float, fly, soar, skim
