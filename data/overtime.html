overtime
oʊvəʳtaɪm ˈoʊvərˌtaɪm ˌ oʊvərˈtaɪm
1 N-UNCOUNT 加班；加班时间 Overtime is time that you spend doing your job in addition to your normal working hours. 加班；加班时间

  • He would work overtime, without pay, to finish a job.

    他会为了完成工作无偿加班。

  • Union leaders had urged miners to vote in favour of an overtime ban.

    工会领导人呼吁矿工投票支持加班禁令。

2 PHRASE 投入全力；热情高涨 If you say that someone is working overtime to do something, you mean that they are using a lot of energy, effort, or enthusiasm trying to do it. 投入全力；热情高涨 [informal]

  • We had to battle very hard and our defence worked overtime to keep us in the game.

    我们不得不奋力搏杀；为保证我们不被淘汰出局，防守队员拼尽了全力。

  • She works overtime with her vacuum cleaner to keep grit out of the kitchen.

    她动用真空吸尘器力争不让厨房出现沙砾。

3 N-UNCOUNT （体育比赛中为决胜负而延长的）加时赛 Overtime is an additional period of time that is added to the end of a sports match in which the two teams are level, as a way of allowing one of the teams to win. （体育比赛中为决胜负而延长的）加时赛 [US]

  • Denver had won the championship by defeating the Cleveland Browns 23-20 in overtime.

    丹佛队在加时赛中以23比20击败克利夫兰布朗队夺得冠军。
