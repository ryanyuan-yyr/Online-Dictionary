sunbathe
sʌnbeɪθ ˈsʌnˌbeɪð ˈsʌnˌbeɪð
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense sunbathes, present participle sunbathing, past tense , past participle sunbathed
VERB 晒太阳;沐日光浴 When people sunbathe, they sit or lie in a place where the sun shines on them, so that their skin becomes browner. 晒太阳;沐日光浴

  • Franklin swam and sunbathed at the pool every morning. [VERB]

    每天上午富兰克林都在游泳池中游泳，在池边晒太阳。

SYN sun yourself, tan, bask, get a tan
