pushover
pʊʃoʊvəʳ ˈpʊʃˌoʊvər
Word forms: plural pushovers
1 N-COUNT 易被劝服的人 You say that someone is a pushover when you find it easy to persuade them to do what you want. 易被劝服的人 [informal]

  • He is a tough negotiator. We did not expect to find him a pushover and he has not been one.

    他是个难对付的谈判者。我们没指望说服他，而且也没说服他。

SYN sucker [slang] , mug [British , slang] , stooge [slang] , soft touch [slang]  
2 N-COUNT [usually singular] 容易做的工作；易如反掌的事；唾手可得的东西 You say that something is a pushover when it is easy to do or easy to get. 容易做的工作；易如反掌的事；唾手可得的东西 [informal]

  • You might think Hungarian a pushover to learn. It is not.

    你可能认为匈牙利语很容易学。其实不然。

SYN piece of cake [British , informal] , breeze [US , Canadian , informal] , picnic [informal] , child's play [informal]
