giggle
gɪgəl ˈɡɪɡəl ˈgɪgəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense giggles, present participle giggling, past tense , past participle giggled
1 VERB 咯咯地笑；傻笑 If someone giggles, they laugh in a childlike way, because they are amused, nervous, or embarrassed. 咯咯地笑；傻笑

  • Both girls began to giggle. [VERB]

    两个女孩都咯咯地笑了起来。

  • 'I beg your pardon?' she giggled. [VERB with quote]

    “您可以重复一遍吗？”她咯咯地笑着说。

  • ...a giggling little girl. [VERB-ing]

    咯咯笑的小女孩

SYN laugh, chuckle, snigger, chortle  
Giggle is also a noun.

  • She gave a little giggle.

    她咯咯地笑了一声。

2 N-PLURAL 傻笑 If you say that someone has the giggles, you mean they cannot stop giggling. 傻笑

  • I was so nervous I got the giggles.

    我紧张得傻笑起来。

  • She had a fit of the giggles.

    她咯咯地笑个不停。

3 N-SING 趣事；玩笑 If you say that something is a giggle, you mean it is fun or is amusing. 趣事；玩笑 [mainly British, informal]

  • I might buy one for a friend's birthday as a giggle.

    我可能会买一件送给朋友当生日礼物，逗他开心。
