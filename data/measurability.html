measurable
meʒərəbəl ˈmɛʒərəbəl ˈmɛʒrəbəl
1 ADJ [usually ADJ n] 可觉察到的;明显的;显著的 If you describe something as measurable, you mean that it is large enough to be noticed or to be significant. 可觉察到的;明显的;显著的 [formal]

  • Both leaders seemed to expect measurable progress.

    两位领导人似乎都期望取得显著的进展。

SYN perceptible, material, significant, distinct  
measurably

  • The old man's voice was measurably weaker than the last time they'd talked.

    那位老人的声音明显比他们上次谈话时微弱。

  • After this, the pace of events quickened measurably.

    从此以后，事情进展的速度明显加快了。

2 ADJ-GRADED 可测量的；可计量的 Something that is measurable can be measured. 可测量的；可计量的

  • Economists emphasize measurable quantities — the number of jobs, the per capita income.

    经济学家看重的是可测定的量—岗位数量、人均收入等。

  • So far the effect is barely measurable.

    迄今为止，效果微乎其微。

SYN quantifiable, material, quantitative, assessable
