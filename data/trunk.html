trunk
trʌŋk trʌŋk
Word forms: plural trunks
1 N-COUNT [n NOUN] 树干 The trunk of a tree is the large main stem from which the branches grow. 树干

  • ...the gnarled trunk of a birch tree. [+ of]

    白桦树粗糙多瘤的树干

  • …toadstools growing on fallen tree trunks.

    在倒下的树干上长出的伞菌

SYN stem, stock, stalk, bole  
2 N-COUNT 旅行箱;衣箱 A trunk is a large, strong case or box used for storing things or for taking on a journey. 旅行箱;衣箱
3 N-COUNT [usually with poss] 象鼻 An elephant's trunk is its very long nose that it uses to lift food and water to its mouth. 象鼻
4 N-COUNT (汽车的)行李箱 The trunk of a car is a covered space at the back or front in which you put luggage or other things. (汽车的)行李箱 [US]
in BRIT, use 英国英语用 boot
5 N-PLURAL (男式)游泳裤 Trunks are shorts that a man wears when he goes swimming. (男式)游泳裤

  • I wear these trunks because they have a streamline effect in the water.

    我穿这条游泳裤因为它在水中有流线效果。

6 N-COUNT [usually singular] (人体从颈部到腰部的)躯干 Your trunk is the central part of your body, from your neck to your waist. (人体从颈部到腰部的)躯干 [formal]
