slander
slɑːndəʳ slæn- ˈslændər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense slanders, present participle slandering, past tense , past participle slandered
1 N-VAR (口头的)诋毁，诽谤 Slander is an untrue spoken statement about someone which is intended to damage their reputation. (口头的)诋毁，诽谤

  • Dr. Bach is now suing the company for slander.

    巴赫博士目前正以诽谤罪起诉该公司。

  • Korea has been a target of threats and slanders from the major western powers.

    朝鲜一直是西方主要大国威胁和诋毁的目标。

2 VERB (口头)诽谤，中伤，诋毁 To slander someone means to say untrue things about them in order to damage their reputation. (口头)诽谤，中伤，诋毁

  • He has been questioned on suspicion of slandering the Prime Minister. [VERB noun]

    他由于涉嫌诽谤首相而受到了盘问。

SYN defame, smear, libel, slur
