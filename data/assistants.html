assistant
əsɪstənt əˈsɪstənt
Word forms: plural assistants
1 ADJ [ADJ n] （用于头衔或职务前，表示级别略低）助理的，副的 Assistant is used in front of titles or jobs to indicate a slightly lower rank. For example, an assistant director is one rank lower than a director in an organization. （用于头衔或职务前，表示级别略低）助理的，副的

  • ...the Assistant Secretary of Defense.

    助理国防部长

  • ...a young assistant professor at Harvard.

    年轻的哈佛助理教授

2 N-COUNT 助手;助理;副手 Someone's assistant is a person who helps them in their work. 助手;助理;副手

  • Kalan called his assistant, Hashim, to take over while he went out.

    考兰叫助理哈希姆在自己外出时代理其职。

  • The salesman had been accompanied to the meeting by an assistant.

    营销人员在助手的陪同下参加了会议。

SYN helper, partner, ally, colleague  
3 N-COUNT 店员；售货员 An assistant is a person who works in a shop selling things to customers. 店员；售货员

  • The assistant took the book and checked the price on the back cover.

    店员拿起书，核对了一下封底的定价。

  • She got a job as a sales assistant selling handbags.

    她得到了一份手提包售货员的工作。

SYN salesperson, shop assistant, checkout person, retail assistant
