agree
əgriː əˈgri
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense agrees, present participle agreeing, past tense , past participle agreed
1 V-RECIP 同意;赞成;意见一致 If people agree with each other about something, they have the same opinion about it or say that they have the same opinion. 同意;赞成;意见一致

  • If we agreed all the time it would be a bit boring, wouldn't it?. [VERB]

    如果我们总是意见一致，就会有点无趣，对吗？

  • Both have agreed on the need for the money. [VERB + on]

    两个人一致表示需要那笔钱。

  • So we both agree there's a problem?. [VERB]

    那么我们两个人都认为存在问题，对吧？

  • I see your point but I'm not sure I agree with you. [VERB + with]

    我明白你的观点，但恐怕我不能苟同。

  • I agree with you that the open system is by far the best. [VERB + with]

    我同意你的观点，开放的系统显然是最佳的。

  • 'It's appalling.' — 'It is. I agree.'. [VERB]

    “真是骇人听闻。”—“嗯，我同意。”

  • I agree that the demise of London zoo would be terrible. [VERB that]

    我也认为如果伦敦动物园不复存在的话那真是太糟糕了。

  • I agree with every word you've just said. [VERB + with]

    我同意你刚说的每句话。

  • 'Frankly I found it rather frightening.' — 'A little startling,' Mark agreed. [VERB with quote]

    “坦白说我觉得这相当可怕。”—“是有点吓人，”马克表示同意。

SYN concur, engage, be as one, sympathize  
2 VERB 同意;应允;答应 If you agree to do something, you say that you will do it. If you agree to a proposal, you accept it. 同意;应允;答应 [Also VERB]

  • He agreed to pay me for the drawings. [VERB to-infinitive]

    他答应向我支付这些画的钱。

  • Donna agreed to both requests. [VERB + to]

    两个请求唐纳都同意了。

  • All 100 senators agree to a postponement. [VERB to noun]

    100位议员全都同意延期。

3 V-RECIP 商定；议定；就…达成协议 If people agree on something, or in British English if they agree something, they all decide to accept or do something. 商定；议定；就…达成协议

  • The warring sides have agreed on an unconditional ceasefire. [VERB + on/upon]

    交战各方已经同意无条件休战。

  • We never agreed a date. [VERB noun]

    我们从未商定好日期。

  • The court had given the unions until September to agree terms with a buyer. [VERB noun + with]

    法庭已指定工会在9月之前与一个买家谈成条件。

4 PHRASE 搁置争议;同意各自保留不同意见 If two people who are arguing about something agree to disagree or agree to differ, they decide to stop arguing because neither of them is going to change their opinion. 搁置争议;同意各自保留不同意见

  • You and I are going to have to agree to disagree then.

    那你我只能各自保留不同意见了。

5 VERB 赞成；赞同 If you agree with an action or suggestion, you approve of it. 赞成；赞同

  • I don't agree with what they're doing. [VERB + with]

    我不赞同他们正在做的事情。

  • In his heart he knew they'd agree with his stand. [VERB with noun]

    他内心明白他们会赞成他的立场。

6 V-RECIP 相符;吻合;与…一致 If one account of an event or one set of figures agrees with another, the two accounts or sets of figures are the same or are consistent with each other. 相符;吻合;与…一致 [Also VERB]

  • His second statement agrees with facts as stated by the other witnesses. [VERB + with]

    他的第二份供述与其他目击证人所陈述的事实相符。

SYN correspond, match, accord, answer  
7 VERB [with neg] （食物）不对胃口，使不舒服 If some food that you eat does not agree with you, it makes you feel ill. （食物）不对胃口，使不舒服

  • I don't think the food here agrees with me. [VERB + with]

    我觉得这里的食物不对我的胃口。

8 VERB （地方、经历）适合，适宜，对…有益 If a place or experience agrees with you, it makes you feel healthy and happy. （地方、经历）适合，适宜，对…有益

  • You look great, Brian. The Bahamas certainly agree with you. [VERB with noun]

    你看上去气色好极了，布赖恩。巴哈马显然很适合你。

9 V-RECIP （在数、性等方面）呼应，一致 In grammar, if a word agrees with a noun or pronoun, it has a form that is appropriate to the number or gender of the noun or pronoun. For example, in 'He hates it', the singular verb agrees with the singular pronoun 'he'. （在数、性等方面）呼应，一致
