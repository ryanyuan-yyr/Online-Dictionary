formerly
fɔːʳməʳli ˈfɔrmərli
1 ADV [ADVERB before verb] 从前;原来;以前 If something happened or was true formerly, it happened or was true in the past. 从前;原来;以前

  • He had formerly been in the Navy.

    他从前在海军服役。

  • ...east Germany's formerly state-controlled companies.

    东德原来的国有公司

SYN previously, earlier, in the past, at one time  
+−Usage
Usageformerly and formally
Formerly and formally sound very similar but have very different meanings. Formerly is used to talk about something that used to be true but isn't true now; formally means 'in a formal manner': Jacques was formerly the president of our club, but he formally resigned last week by sending a letter to the club secretary.
