misinform
mɪsɪnfɔːʳm ˌmɪsɪnˈfɔrm
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense misinforms, present participle misinforming, past tense , past participle misinformed
VERB 误传;提供错误消息 If you are misinformed, you are told something that is wrong or inaccurate. 误传;提供错误消息

  • He has been misinformed by members of his own party. [be VERB-ed]

    他从自己党内成员那里得到了错误的消息。

  • We were clearly misinformed. [be VERB-ed]

    显然我们得到的消息是错误的。

  • The president defended the news blackout, accusing the media of misinforming the people. [VERB noun]

    总统为新闻封锁进行了辩解，指责新闻媒体误导民众。

SYN mislead, deceive, misdirect, misguide
