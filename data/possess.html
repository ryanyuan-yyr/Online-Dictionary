possess
pəzes pəˈzɛs
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense possesses, present participle possessing, past tense , past participle possessed
1 VERB [no passive] 占有；拥有 If you possess something, you have it or own it. 占有；拥有

  • He was then arrested and charged with possessing an offensive weapon. [VERB noun]

    他随后被逮捕，并被控拥有攻击性武器。

  • He is said to possess a fortune of more than two-and-a-half-thousand million dollars. [VERB noun]

    据说他拥有超出25亿美元的财富。

SYN own, have, hold, be in possession of  
2 VERB [no cont] 具备，具有（特定品质、才能等） If someone or something possesses a particular quality, ability, or feature, they have it. 具备，具有（特定品质、才能等） [formal]

  • ...individuals who are deemed to possess the qualities of sense, loyalty and discretion. [VERB noun]

    被认为具备了理性、忠诚和判断力的人

  • This figure has long been held to possess miraculous power. [VERB noun]

    这个数字长时间以来都被认为拥有神奇的力量。

SYN be endowed with, have, enjoy, benefit from  
3 VERB （情感或信仰）影响，控制 If a feeling or belief possesses you, it strongly influences your thinking or behaviour. （情感或信仰）影响，控制 [literary]

  • Absolute terror possessed her. [VERB noun]

    她恐惧到了极点。

  • Tsvetayeva was possessed by a frenzied urge to get out of Moscow. [VERB noun]

    茨维塔耶娃内心有一种想要离开莫斯科的狂热冲动。

SYN control, influence, dominate, consume  
4 See also: possessed 
5 PHRASE （表示惊讶）是什么驱使…（干…），是什么促使…（做…） If you ask what possessed someone to do something, you are emphasizing your great surprise that they have done something which you consider foolish or dangerous. （表示惊讶）是什么驱使…（干…），是什么促使…（做…） [feelings]

  • What on earth had possessed her to agree to marry him?

    究竟是什么让她同意嫁给他的？
