colony
kɒləni ˈkɑləni
Word forms: plural colonies
1 N-COUNT 殖民地 A colony is a country which is controlled by a more powerful country. 殖民地

  • In France's former North African colonies, anti-French feeling is growing.

    在北非的一些前法属殖民地国家，反法情绪日益增长。

  • Puerto Rico, though it calls itself a Commonwealth, is really a self-governing American colony.

    波多黎各尽管自称共和国，但实际上是有自治权的美国殖民地。

2 N-COUNT (某一类人的)聚居地，聚居区 You can refer to a place where a particular group of people lives as a particular kind of colony . (某一类人的)聚居地，聚居区

  • In 1932, he established a school and artists' colony in Stone City, Iowa.

    1932年，他在艾奥瓦州的石头城创建了一所学校，那儿成了艺术家的聚居地。

  • ...a penal colony.

    罪犯流放地

  • ...industrial colonies.

    工业聚集区

3 N-PLURAL [the NOUN] (统称)英属殖民地 The colonies means all the countries that used to be British colonies. (统称)英属殖民地 [British, old-fashioned]

  • Many of our troops and officers were scattered around the world in the service of His Majesty in the colonies.

    我们的很多部队和军官分驻世界各地的英属殖民地，为英王效力。

4 N-PLURAL (美国独立前的)北美十三州 The colonies means the 13 British colonies in North America which formed the original United States. (美国独立前的)北美十三州 [US]

  • On the eve of the Revolution, the colonies produced thirty thousand tons of crude iron a year.

    独立战争前夕，北美十三州每年生产3万吨生铁。

5 N-COUNT (鸟、昆虫或动物的)群体，集群，群落 A colony of birds, insects, or animals is a group of them that live together. (鸟、昆虫或动物的)群体，集群，群落

  • The Shetlands are famed for their colonies of sea birds. [+ of]

    设得兰群岛以其众多的海鸟而闻名。

  • The caterpillars feed in large colonies.

    毛毛虫总是成群进食。
