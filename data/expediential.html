expediency
ɪkspiːdiənsi ɪkˈspiːdɪənsɪ ɛkˈspidiənsi ɪkˈspidiənsi
N-UNCOUNT 不得已而做的事;权宜之计 Expediency means doing what is convenient rather than what is morally right. 不得已而做的事;权宜之计 [formal]

  • It seems political expediency, rather than absolute economic need, will determine who gains from the conflict.

    看来是政治上的权宜之计，而不是绝对的经济需求，将决定谁会从这场冲突中获益。

  • This was a matter less of morals than of expediency.

    此举只是权宜之计，不关乎道德问题。
