luxury
lʌkʃəri ˈlʌkʃəri ˈlʌkʃʊri ˈlʌgʒəri ˈlʌgʒʊri
Word forms: plural luxuries
1 N-UNCOUNT 奢侈；奢华；奢侈的享受 Luxury is very great comfort, especially among beautiful and expensive surroundings. 奢侈；奢华；奢侈的享受

  • By all accounts he leads a life of considerable luxury.

    据说他的生活颇为奢侈。

  • She was brought up in an atmosphere of luxury and wealth.

    她在锦衣玉食的环境中长大。

SYN opulence, splendour, richness, extravagance  
2 N-COUNT [ADJ n] 奢侈品 A luxury is something expensive which is not necessary but which gives you pleasure. 奢侈品

  • A week by the sea is a luxury they can no longer afford.

    到海边度假一周成了他们再也消费不起的一种奢侈。

  • Telephones are still a luxury in some parts of Spain, Portugal, and Greece.

    在西班牙、葡萄牙和希腊的一些地方，电话仍然是奢侈品。

3 ADJ [ADJ n] 奢侈的；奢华的 A luxury item is something expensive which is not necessary but which gives you pleasure. 奢侈的；奢华的

  • He could not afford luxury food on his pay.

    靠自己的工资他买不起奢侈的食品。

  • He rode on the president's luxury train through his own state.

    他乘坐总统的豪华列车经过自己所在的州。

4 N-SING 不常有的乐趣；难得的享受 A luxury is a pleasure which you do not often have the opportunity to enjoy. 不常有的乐趣；难得的享受

  • Hot baths are my favourite luxury.

    泡个热水澡是我的最大乐事。

  • We were going to have the luxury of a free weekend, to rest and do whatever we pleased. [+ of]

    我们将有一个难得空闲的周末，可以休息，还可以高兴干什么就干什么。

Quotations
Give us the luxuries of life, and we will dispense with its necessitiesJohn Lothrop Motley
