sensitize
sensɪtaɪz ˈsɛnsəˌtaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense sensitizes, present participle sensitizing, past tense , past participle sensitized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 sensitise
1 VERB 使敏感;使意识到 If you sensitize people to a particular problem or situation, you make them aware of it. 使敏感;使意识到 [formal] [Also VERB n]

  • It seems important to sensitize people to the fact that depression is more than the blues. [VERB noun + to]

    让人们意识到抑郁症不仅仅是心情忧郁，这点似乎很重要。

  • How many judges in our male-dominated courts are sensitized to women's issues? [be VERB-ed]

    在男性占主导地位的法庭上，有多少法官对妇女问题是比较敏感的呢？

2 VERB [usually passive] 使对…有反应;使敏化;使过敏 If a substance is sensitized to something such as light or touch, it is made sensitive to it. 使对…有反应;使敏化;使过敏

  • Skin is easily irritated, chapped, chafed, and sensitized. [be VERB-ed]

    皮肤很容易受到刺激、发生皴裂、擦得红肿和出现过敏。

  • ...sensitised nerve endings. [VERB-ed]

    变得敏感的神经末梢
