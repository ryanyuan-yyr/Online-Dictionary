tailspin
teɪlspɪn ˈteɪlˌspɪn ˈteɪlˌspɪn
1 N-SING [a N] 衰落; 困境If something such as an industry or an economy goes into a tailspin, it begins to perform very badly or to fail. 衰落; 困境

  • The disruption of European trade during the war had thrown the economy into a tailspin.

    战争期间欧洲贸易的中断使经济陷入一片困境。

  • The stock market is in a tailspin.

    股票市场一蹶不振。

2 N-SING [a N] 旋冲下坠If an aircraft goes into a tailspin, it falls very rapidly toward the ground in a spiral movement. 旋冲下坠

  • The aircraft went into a tailspin before crashing.

    这架飞机向下旋冲后坠毁。
