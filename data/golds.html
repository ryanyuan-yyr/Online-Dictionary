gold
goʊld goʊld
Word forms: plural golds
1 N-UNCOUNT 黄金 Gold is a valuable, yellow-coloured metal that is used for making jewellery and ornaments, and as an international currency. 黄金

  • ...a sapphire set in gold.

    镶嵌在黄金上的蓝宝石

  • The price of gold was going up.

    黄金的价格在上涨。

  • ...gold coins.

    金币

2 N-UNCOUNT 金饰物；黄金制品 Gold is jewellery and other things that are made of gold. 金饰物；黄金制品

  • We handed over all our gold and money.

    我们将所有的金首饰和钱都交了出来。

3 COLOUR 金色；金黄色 Something that is gold is a bright yellow colour, and is often shiny. 金色；金黄色

  • I'd been wearing Michel's black and gold shirt.

    我一直都穿着米歇尔那件黑色和金色相间的衬衫。

4 N-VAR 同gold medal A gold is the same as a gold medal . 同gold medal [informal]

  • His ambition was to win gold at the Atlanta Games in 1996.

    他的志向是在1996年亚特兰大奥运会上夺取金牌。

  • This Saturday the British star is going for gold in the Winter Olympics.

    本周六，这位英国明星将向冬奥会的金牌发起冲击。

5 PHRASE （小孩）规规矩矩的,乖巧的 If you say that a child is being as good as gold, you are emphasizing that they are behaving very well and are not causing you any problems. （小孩）规规矩矩的,乖巧的 [emphasis]

  • The boys were as good as gold on our walk.

    我们散步的时候，这帮小男孩表现得很乖。

6 PHRASE 一颗金子般的心；赤子之心 If you say that someone has a heart of gold, you are emphasizing that they are very good and kind to other people. 一颗金子般的心；赤子之心 [emphasis]

  • They are all good boys with hearts of gold. They would never steal.

    他们都是有着金子般心灵的好孩子，绝不会干偷摸扒窃之事。

7 PHRASE 期望得到的一大笔钱，未来的一桶金（英国英语中亦作a crock of gold） Money that someone hopes they will get in the future is sometimes referred to as a pot of gold. Also a crock of gold, mainly in British English. 期望得到的一大笔钱，未来的一桶金（英国英语中亦作a crock of gold）

  • I would rather be honest with people than tell them that there is going to be some pot of gold at the end of the rainbow.

    我宁肯实话实说也不愿意给予他们不切实际的期望。
