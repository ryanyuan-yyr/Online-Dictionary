column
kɒləm ˈkɑləm
Word forms: plural columns
1 N-COUNT 柱;圆形石柱;纪念柱 A column is a tall, often decorated cylinder of stone which is built to honour someone or forms part of a building. 柱;圆形石柱;纪念柱

  • ...a London landmark, Nelson's Column in Trafalgar Square.

    伦敦的标志性建筑，特拉法尔加广场上的纳尔逊将军纪念柱

2 N-COUNT 柱状物 A column is something that has a tall narrow shape. 柱状物

  • The explosion sent a column of smoke thousands of feet into the air. [+ of]

    爆炸在空中形成了几千英尺高的烟柱。

3 N-COUNT (人或动物排成的)队，纵行 A column is a group of people or animals which moves in a long line. (人或动物排成的)队，纵行

  • There were reports of columns of military vehicles appearing on the streets. [+ of]

    有报道说几队军用车辆出现在街头。

SYN line, train, row, file  
4 N-COUNT (字典、报纸或图表的)栏 On a printed page such as a page of a dictionary, newspaper, or printed chart, a column is one of two or more vertical sections which are read downwards. (字典、报纸或图表的)栏

  • We had stupidly been looking at the wrong column of figures. [+ of]

    我们稀里糊涂，看串了数字栏。

  • In The Dictionary of Quotations, there are no fewer than one and a half columns devoted to 'kiss'.

    在《引语词典》中，“亲吻”这一条目不下一栏半。

5 N-COUNT (报纸或杂志的)专栏 In a newspaper or magazine, a column is a section that is always written by the same person or is always about the same topic. (报纸或杂志的)专栏

  • His name features frequently in the social columns of the tabloid newspapers.

    他的名字频繁地出现在各种小报社会专栏的显著位置上。

  • She also writes a regular column for the Times Educational Supplement. [+ for]

    她也定期为《泰晤士报高等教育增刊》写专栏文章。

SYN article, story, feature, piece
