eulogize
juːlədʒaɪz ˈjuləˌdʒaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense eulogizes, present participle eulogizing, past tense , past participle eulogized
in AM, also use 美国英语亦用 eulogise
1 VERB 称赞;颂扬 If you eulogize someone or something, you praise them very highly. 称赞;颂扬 [formal]

  • Barry Davies eulogized Keegan's part in the operation. [VERB noun]

    巴里·戴维斯盛赞基根在行动中发挥的作用。

  • Taylor eulogised about Steven's versatility. [VERB prep.]

    泰勒称赞史蒂文多才多艺。

SYN praise, acclaim, applaud, compliment  
2 VERB （通常指在葬礼上）念悼词称赞，为…念悼文 If you eulogize someone who has died, you make a speech praising them, usually at their funeral. （通常指在葬礼上）念悼词称赞，为…念悼文 [US]

  • Leaders from around the world eulogized the Egyptian president. [VERB noun]

    全世界的领导人都致悼词称颂那位埃及总统。
