rehabilitate
riːhəbɪlɪteɪt ˌrihəˈbɪləˌteɪt ˌ riəˈbɪləˌteɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense rehabilitates, present participle rehabilitating, past tense , past participle rehabilitated
1 VERB 使康复;使戒毒(或戒酒);改造(罪犯) To rehabilitate someone who has been ill or in prison means to help them to live a normal life again. To rehabilitate someone who has a drug or alcohol problem means to help them stop using drugs or alcohol. 使康复;使戒毒(或戒酒);改造(罪犯)

  • Considerable efforts have been made to rehabilitate patients who have suffered in this way. [VERB noun]

    为帮助遭受这种病痛折磨的病人康复付出了巨大努力。

SYN reintegrate, retrain, restore to health, readapt  
rehabilitation

  • ...the rehabilitation of young offenders.

    少年犯的改造

  • ...an alcohol and drug rehabilitation centre.

    戒酒和戒毒康复中心

2 VERB 使恢复名誉;给…平反昭雪 If someone is rehabilitated, they begin to be considered acceptable again after a period during which they have been rejected or severely criticized. 使恢复名誉;给…平反昭雪 [formal]

  • Ten years later, Dreyfus was rehabilitated. [be VERB-ed]

    10年之后，德雷福斯的冤情得以昭雪。

  • His candidacy has divided the party; while most have scorned him, others have sought to rehabilitate him. [VERB noun]

    他的候选资格使该党出现分化;其中大多数人都嘲弄他，而另外一些人则想办法为他恢复名誉。

SYN reinstate, restore, re-establish, reinstall  
rehabilitation

  • The party has taken an important step towards the rehabilitation of the disgraced MP.

    该党朝着为这位蒙羞的议员恢复名誉迈出了重要的一步。

3 VERB 复原，修葺，整修，修复(建筑物或某区域) To rehabilitate a building or an area means to improve its condition so that it can be used again. 复原，修葺，整修，修复(建筑物或某区域)

  • ...a program for rehabilitating low-income housing. [VERB noun]

    对低收入者的住房进行整修的项目

SYN restore, develop, convert, renew  
rehabilitation

  • Money will be directed primarily towards the maintenance and rehabilitation of schools, clinics and machinery.

    资金将主要用于学校、诊所和机器设备的维护和修缮。
