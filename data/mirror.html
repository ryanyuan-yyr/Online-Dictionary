mirror
mɪrəʳ ˈmɪrər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense mirrors, present participle mirroring, past tense , past participle mirrored
1 N-COUNT 镜子 A mirror is a flat piece of glass which reflects light, so that when you look at it you can see yourself reflected in it. 镜子

  • He absent-mindedly looked at himself in the mirror.

    他心不在焉地看着镜中的自己。

  • He checked his mirror and saw that a dark coloured van was immediately behind him.

    他看了一下后视镜，发现一辆深色货车紧随其后。

SYN looking-glass, glass [British] , reflector, speculum  
mirrored

  • ...a mirrored ceiling.

    装了镜子的天花板

2 VERB 反映;再现;与…相似 If something mirrors something else, it has similar features to it, and therefore seems like a copy or representation of it. 反映;再现;与…相似

  • The book inevitably mirrors my own interests and experiences. [VERB noun]

    这本书不可避免地反映出我个人的喜好与经历。

  • His own shock was mirrored on her face. [VERB noun]

    他和她面面相觑。

SYN reflect, show, follow, match  
3 VERB (水)照出，映出 If you see something reflected in water, you can say that the water mirrors it. (水)照出，映出 [literary]

  • ...the sudden glitter where a newly-flooded field mirrors the sky. [VERB noun]

    刚刚被洪水淹没的田野映照出了天空，那里突然泛起亮光

  • The ship would lie there mirrored in a perfectly unmoving glossy sea. [VERB-ed]

    船会停靠在那里，映衬在一片波澜不兴的湛蓝海水之中。
