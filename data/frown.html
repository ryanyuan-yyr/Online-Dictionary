frown
fraʊn fraʊn
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense frowns, present participle frowning, past tense , past participle frowned
1 VERB (因恼怒、担心、困惑、全神贯注等而)皱眉，蹙额 When someone frowns, their eyebrows become drawn together, because they are annoyed, worried, or puzzled, or because they are concentrating. (因恼怒、担心、困惑、全神贯注等而)皱眉，蹙额

  • Nancy shook her head, frowning. [VERB]

    南希摇了摇头，皱着眉头。

  • He frowned at her anxiously. [VERB + at]

    他不安地朝她皱了皱眉。

  • ...a frowning man. [VERB-ing]

    皱着眉头的男子

SYN scowl, glare, glower, make a face  
Frown is also a noun.

  • There was a deep frown on the boy's face.

    男孩眉头紧锁。

2 PHRASAL VERB 反对;不赞成;不同意 If something is frowned upon or is frowned on people disapprove of it. 反对;不赞成;不同意

  • This practice is frowned upon as being wasteful.

    这种行为被认为是浪费而不获认可。

  • Many teachers frown on such practices.

    许多教师不赞同这样的做法。
