worry
wʌri wɜːri ˈwɜri
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense worries, present participle worrying, past tense , past participle worried
1 VERB 担心;忧虑；发愁 If you worry, you keep thinking about problems that you have or about unpleasant things that might happen. 担心;忧虑；发愁

  • Don't worry, your luggage will come on afterwards by taxi. [VERB]

    别担心，你的行李随后会由出租车送过来。

  • I worry about her constantly. [VERB + about]

    我无时无刻不为她担心。

  • I work in a school so I don't have to worry about finding someone to look after my little boy. [V about n/-ing]

    我在学校工作，所以不用为找人照顾我年幼的儿子而操心。

  • They worry that extremists might gain control. [VERB that]

    他们担心极端分子可能会取得控制权。

SYN be anxious, be concerned, be worried, obsess  
2 VERB 使担心;使忧虑;使发愁 If someone or something worries you, they make you anxious because you keep thinking about problems or unpleasant things that might be connected with them. 使担心;使忧虑;使发愁

  • I'm still in the early days of my recovery and that worries me. [VERB noun]

    我还处于恢复的早期阶段，这让我很担心。

  • 'Why didn't you tell us?' — 'I didn't want to worry you.'. [VERB noun]

    “你为什么不告诉我们？”“我不想让你们担心。”

  • The English, worried by the growing power of Prince Henry, sent a raiding party to Scotland to kill him. [VERB-ed]

    英格兰人对亨利亲王权势日增感到不安，便派突袭队到苏格兰除掉他。

  • Does it worry you that the Americans are discussing this? [it V n that/to-inf]

    美国人在讨论这事，你不担心吗？

SYN trouble, upset, harry, bother  
3 VERB [oft with neg] 让…厌烦；使不安宁;困扰 If someone or something does not worry you, you do not dislike them or you are not annoyed by them. 让…厌烦；使不安宁;困扰 [spoken]

  • The cold doesn't worry me. [VERB noun]

    冷我不怕。

  • It wouldn't worry me if he came to my house, but I don't know if I would go out of my way to ask him. [it V n if]

    我倒不介意他来我家，但我不知道自己是否会特意邀请他。

4 N-UNCOUNT 担心;忧虑;发愁 Worry is the state or feeling of anxiety and unhappiness caused by the problems that you have or by thinking about unpleasant things that might happen. 担心;忧虑;发愁

  • The admission shows the depth of worry among the Tories over the state of the economy.

    这一认可表明了保守党党内对经济状况的忧虑之深。

  • His last years were overshadowed by financial worry.

    他生命的最后几年穷困潦倒，十分惨淡。

SYN anxiety, concern, care, fear  
5 N-COUNT 使人担心(或发愁)的问题；烦心事 A worry is a problem that you keep thinking about and that makes you unhappy. 使人担心(或发愁)的问题；烦心事

  • My main worry was that Madeleine Johnson would still be there.

    我最担心的是马德琳·约翰逊仍会在那儿。

  • The worry is that the use of force could make life impossible for the UN peacekeepers.

    让人担心的是，使用武力可能会使联合国维和人员性命难保。

  • His wife Cheryl said she had no worries about his health. [+ about]

    他的妻子谢里尔说她并不担心他的健康。

6 CONVENTION （表示自己并不烦恼或生气）别担心；没关系 You say not to worry to someone to indicate that you are not upset or angry when something has gone wrong. （表示自己并不烦恼或生气）别担心；没关系 [informal]

  • 'Not to worry, Baby,' he said, and kissed her tenderly.

    “没关系，宝贝儿。”他说道，然后温柔地吻了她一下。
