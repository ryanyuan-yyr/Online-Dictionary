glorified
glɔːrɪfaɪd ˈɡlɔːrɪˌfaɪd
ADJ [ADJ n] 吹嘘的；美化的 You use glorified to indicate that something is less important or impressive than its name suggests. 吹嘘的；美化的

  • Sometimes they tell me I'm just a glorified waitress.

    他们有时候对我说我只是个女招待，只不过称呼好听一些罢了。

  • Some places were no more than glorified pubs.

    一些地方名字叫得好听，其实不过是酒馆而已。
