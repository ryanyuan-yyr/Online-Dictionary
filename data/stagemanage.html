stage-manage
ˈsteɪdʒˌmænɪdʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense stage-manages, present participle stage-managing, past tense , past participle stage-managed
VERB 精心安排并操纵 If someone stage-manages an event, they carefully organize and control it, rather than letting it happen in a natural way. 精心安排并操纵 [disapproval]

  • Some radicals may oppose him in protest at the attempt of his supporters to stage-manage the congress. [VERB noun]

    有些激进分子可能会反对他，以作为对他的支持者企图操纵国会的抗议。

  • Today's council sessions have been carefully stage-managed to avoid embarrassing disclosures or signs of internal dissent. [VERB noun]

    今天的理事会会议已经过精心安排，以避免爆出令人难堪的讯息或显示出内部不和的迹象。

  • ...a stage-managed demonstration. [VERB-ed]

    受到操纵的示威活动
