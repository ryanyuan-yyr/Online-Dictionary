enable
ɪneɪbəl ɛnˈeɪbəl ɪnˈeɪbəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense enables, present participle enabling, past tense , past participle enabled
1 VERB 使能够;使有机会 If someone or something enables you to do a particular thing, they give you the opportunity to do it. 使能够;使有机会

  • The new test should enable doctors to detect the disease early. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    新的检测手段应该能够使医生们尽早查出这种疾病。

  • ...a new charter for training to enable young people to make the most of their potential. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    能够使年轻人充分发挥潜能的新培训规章

SYN allow, permit, facilitate, empower  
enabling

  • Researchers describe it as an enabling technology.

    研究者们将其描述成一种实现性技术。

2 VERB 使成为可能；使可行 To enable something to happen means to make it possible for it to happen. 使成为可能；使可行

  • The hot sun enables the grapes to reach optimum ripeness. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    炙热的阳光使葡萄能够达到最佳成熟状态。

  • A series of holes in the side panels enables the position of the shelves to be adjusted. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    通过侧板上的一系列小孔可以对隔板位置进行调整。

  • The working class is still too small to enable a successful socialist revolution. [VERB noun]

    工人阶级队伍还是非常弱小，不能成功实现社会主义革命。

3 VERB 准许;许可;授权 To enable someone to do something means to give them permission or the right to do it. 准许;许可;授权

  • The republic's legislation enables young people to do a form of alternative service. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    共和国的法律允许年轻人服某种形式的替代役。

enabling

  • Some protection for victims must be written into the enabling legislation.

    对受害者的一些保护必须写进权力授予法。
