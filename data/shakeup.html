shake up
1 PHRASAL VERB 彻底调整，重组，改革(组织、机构、行业等) If someone shakes up something such as an organization, an institution, or a profession, they make major changes to it. 彻底调整，重组，改革(组织、机构、行业等)

  • The government wanted to reform the institutions, to shake up the country. [VERB PREP. noun]

    政府想要实施机构改革，整顿国家。

  • Shareholders are preparing to shake things up in the boardrooms of America. [VERB noun PREP.]

    股东们正准备在美国的公司董事会议上作出重大调整。

SYN restructure, reorganize, revolutionize, reform  
2See also:shake-up 
3 PHRASAL VERB 使震动;使激动;使心烦意乱 If you are shaken up or shook up by an unpleasant experience, it makes you feel shocked and upset, and unable to think calmly or clearly. 使震动;使激动;使心烦意乱 [Also VERB PREPOSITION n (not pron)]

  • The jockey was shaken up when he was thrown twice from his horse yesterday. [be VERB-ed PREP.]

    昨天赛马骑师两次从马背上被摔下来,受惊了。

  • He was in the car when those people died. That really shook him up. [VERB noun PREP.]

    那些人死的时候他正在车里，当时他真是吓坏了。

  • He said that the accident had left her a bit shook up, but she was going to be just fine. [VERB-ed PREP.]

    他说那场事故让她受了点惊吓，不过她会好起来的。

SYN upset, shock, frighten, disturb
