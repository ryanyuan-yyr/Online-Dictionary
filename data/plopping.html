plop
plɒp plɑp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense plops, present participle plopping, past tense , past participle plopped
1 N-COUNT; SOUND （某物落入水中而未溅泼的）扑通声，啪嗒声 A plop is a soft, gentle sound, like the sound made by something dropping into water without disturbing the surface much. （某物落入水中而未溅泼的）扑通声，啪嗒声

  • Another drop of water fell with a soft plop.

    又一滴水嘀嗒一声落下。

2 VERB 扑通落下；啪嗒落下 If something plops somewhere, it drops there with a soft, gentle sound. 扑通落下；啪嗒落下

  • The ice cream plopped to the ground. [VERB prep.]

    冰激凌啪的一声掉地上了。

3 VERB 轻轻地放下；轻巧地放下 If you plop something somewhere, you drop it there gently and without making a loud noise. 轻轻地放下；轻巧地放下 [informal]

  • Just plop the noodles over the center of the sauce. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    把面条轻放在调味汁中间就行了。

  • She picked up the coffee pot, then plopped it down and went into the living room. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    她拿起咖啡壶，又轻手放下去了客厅。
