appreciation
əpriːʃieɪʃən əˌpriʃiˈeɪʃən
Word forms: plural appreciations
1 N-SING 欣赏；鉴赏;赏识 Appreciation of something is the recognition and enjoyment of its good qualities. 欣赏；鉴赏;赏识

  • ...an investigation into children's understanding and appreciation of art. [+ of]

    就孩子对艺术的理解和欣赏所作的调查

  • Brian whistled in appreciation.

    布赖恩吹口哨以示赞赏。

SYN admiration, liking, respect, assessment  
2 N-SING [oft with poss] 感谢；感激 Your appreciation for something that someone does for you is your gratitude for it. 感谢；感激

  • He expressed his appreciation for what he called Saudi Arabia's moderate and realistic oil policies. [+ for]

    他表示很感激沙特阿拉伯推行的被他称作稳健而务实的石油政策。

  • ...the gifts presented to them in appreciation of their work. [+ of]

    为感谢他们所做的工作而赠与他们的礼物

SYN gratitude, thanks, recognition, obligation  
3 N-SING 领会；了解；认识 An appreciation of a situation or problem is an understanding of what it involves. 领会；了解；认识

  • They have a stronger appreciation of the importance of economic incentives. [+ of]

    他们对经济激励的重要性有了更深的理解。

SYN awareness, understanding, regard, knowledge  
4 N-UNCOUNT 升值；增值 Appreciation in the value of something is an increase in its value over a period of time. 升值；增值

  • You have to take capital appreciation of the property into account. [+ of]

    你必须将该处房产的资本增值考虑在内。

SYN increase, rise, gain, growth  
5 N-COUNT [usually NOUN of n] （对艺术家、表演者或其作品的）评论，评价 An appreciation of an artist or performer or of their work is a speech or piece of writing in which they are discussed and assessed. （对艺术家、表演者或其作品的）评论，评价

  • I had written an appreciation of Hernandez for a magazine.

    我为一家杂志社写过一篇评论埃尔南德斯的文章。

SYN review, report, notice, analysis
