council
kaʊnsəl ˈkaʊnsəl ˈkaʊnsəl
Word forms: plural councils
1 N-COUNT-COLL; N-IN-NAMES 市政委员会;地方议会 A council is a group of people who are elected to govern a local area such as a city or, in Britain, a county. 市政委员会;地方议会

  • ...Cheshire County Council.

    柴郡政务委员会

  • The city council has voted almost unanimously in favour.

    市政委员会几乎一致投票表示赞同。

  • ...David Ward, one of just two Liberal Democrats on the council.

    戴维·沃德，市政委员会中仅有的两名自由民主党人之一

  • ...reports of local council meetings.

    对当地政务委员会会议的报道

SYN committee, governing body, board, panel  
2 ADJ [ADJ n] (房屋或公寓)地方政府出租的 Council houses or flats are owned by the local council, and people pay rent to live in them. (房屋或公寓)地方政府出租的 [British]

  • There is a shortage of council housing.

    廉租公房房源不足。

  • Council tenants around the country are planning a mass lobby of Parliament.

    全国各地的市政住房租户正在计划组建一个大的议会游说团。

3 N-COUNT-COLL [with singular or plural verb, usu in names] (用于组织名称)委员会 Council is used in the names of some organizations. (用于组织名称)委员会

  • ...the National Council for Civil Liberties.

    全国公民自由委员会

  • ...the Arts Council.

    艺术委员会

  • ...community health councils.

    社区健康委员会

4 N-COUNT-COLL [with singular or plural verb] [usually singular] (组织的)管理者，委员 In some organizations, the council is the group of people that controls or governs it. (组织的)管理者，委员

  • The permanent council of the Organization of American States meets today here in Washington. [+ of]

    美洲国家组织的常务委员们今天在华盛顿这里会面。

  • ...the Bundesbank's central council.

    德意志联邦银行的中央委员会

5 N-COUNT (特定人群参加的正式)会议 A council is a specially organized, formal meeting that is attended by a particular group of people. (特定人群参加的正式)会议

  • President Najibullah said he would call a grand council of all Afghans. [+ of]

    纳吉布拉总统称他将召开阿富汗全民大会。

  • The president also meets ministers at inter-ministerial councils held at the Elysée Palace.

    总统还在爱丽舍宫接见了参加部长级会议的各部长。
