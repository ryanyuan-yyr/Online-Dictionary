extremity
ɪkstremɪti ɪkˈstrɛmɪtɪ ɛkˈstrɛməti ɪkˈstrɛməti
Word forms: plural extremities
1 N-COUNT 尽头;末端 The extremity of something is its furthest end or edge. 尽头;末端 [formal]

  • ...a small port on the north-western extremity of the Iberian peninsula. [+ of]

    伊比利亚半岛西北端的小港口

  • ...the extremities of the aeroplane. [+ of]

    飞机的末端

SYN limit, end, edge, border  
2 N-PLURAL [oft with poss] （身体的）末端，末梢;（尤指）手脚 Your extremities are the end parts of your body, especially your hands and feet. （身体的）末端，末梢;（尤指）手脚

  • He found that his extremities grew cold.

    他发现手脚变冷了。

  • Exercise is very important as it keeps the circulation moving and warms the extremities.

    锻炼非常重要，它可以促进血液循环，保持手脚温暖。

3 N-UNCOUNT 极度;极端 The extremity of a situation or of someone's behaviour is the degree to which it is severe, unusual, or unacceptable. 极度;极端

  • In spite of the extremity of her seclusion she was sane. [+ of]

    尽管完全与世隔绝，她依然心智健全。

  • In the past, the region had been protected by its forbidding geography and the extremities of its climate. [+ of]

    过去，这个地区令人生畏的地理条件和极端的天气状况使它受到保护。

  • Only in extremity, when his defences had been reduced by six weeks of bombing, was he ready to leave.

    经过6周的轰炸，他的防御已经不堪一击，在这种极端情况下他才愿意离开。
