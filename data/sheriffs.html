sheriff
ʃerɪf ˈʃɛrɪf
Word forms: plural sheriffs
1 N-COUNT; N-TITLE (美国由选举产生的)县治安官 In the United States, a sheriff is a person who is elected to make sure that the law is obeyed in a particular county. (美国由选举产生的)县治安官

  • ...the local sheriff.

    当地的县治安官

  • ...Sheriff Bob Cahill.

    县治安官鲍勃·卡希尔

2 N-COUNT; N-TITLE (苏格兰的)郡法官,区法官 In Scotland, a sheriff is a legal officer whose chief duty is to act as judge in a Sheriff Court. These courts deal with all but the most serious crimes and with most civil actions. (苏格兰的)郡法官,区法官

  • ...the presiding judge, Sheriff John Mowatt.

    首席法官—郡法官约翰·莫厄特

3 N-COUNT (英格兰和威尔士境内挂名的名誉)郡长，郡督 In England and Wales, the Sheriff of a city or county is a person who is elected or appointed to carry out mainly ceremonial duties. (英格兰和威尔士境内挂名的名誉)郡长，郡督

  • ...the Sheriff of Oxford. [+ of]

    牛津的郡长
