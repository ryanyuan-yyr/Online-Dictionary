point of order
Word forms: plural points of order
N-COUNT [usually singular] （正式辩论中质疑议事规则遭到破坏而提出的）程序问题 In a formal debate, a point of order is an official complaint that someone makes because the rules about how the debate is meant to be organized have been broken. （正式辩论中质疑议事规则遭到破坏而提出的）程序问题 [formal]

  • A point of order was raised in parliament by Mr Ben Morris.

    本·莫里斯先生在议会就程序问题提出质询。

  • The postponement was demanded and won on a point of order.

    因对程序问题的异议而提出了推迟要求并获得了通过。
