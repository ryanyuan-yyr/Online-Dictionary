play up
1 PHRASAL VERB 强调…的重要性；夸大；渲染 If you play up something, you emphasize it and try to make people believe that it is important. 强调…的重要性；夸大；渲染 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • The media played up the prospects for a settlement. [VERB PREP. noun]

    媒体夸大了达成和解的可能性。

  • His Japanese ancestry has been played up by some of his opponents. [VERB PREP. noun

    他的日本血统被一些对手大肆渲染。

2 PHRASAL VERB [usually cont, no passive] （机器、身体部位等）（给…）制造麻烦，（使）痛苦 If something such as a machine or a part of your body is playing up or is playing you up, it is causing problems because it is not working properly. （机器、身体部位等）（给…）制造麻烦，（使）痛苦 [British, informal]

  • The engine had been playing up. [VERB PREP.]

    发动机一直有问题。

  • It was his back playing him up. [VERB noun PREP.]

    是他的后背在作痛。

3 PHRASAL VERB （小孩）淘气，不听话 When children play up, they are naughty and difficult to control. （小孩）淘气，不听话 [British, informal]

  • Patrick often plays up when he knows I'm in a hurry. [VERB PREP.]

    帕特里克常常明知我手忙脚乱还要调皮捣蛋。
