noisy
nɔɪzi ˈnɔɪzi
Word forms: comparative noisier, superlative noisiest
1 ADJ-GRADED 喧闹的;嗓门大的;聒噪的 A noisy person or thing makes a lot of loud or unpleasant noise. 喧闹的;嗓门大的;聒噪的

  • ...my noisy old typewriter.

    我那台噪音极大的旧打字机

  • His daughter was very active and noisy in the mornings.

    他的女儿一到上午就很好动，吵吵闹闹的。

noisily

  • The students on the grass bank cheered noisily.

    学生们在河岸的草滩上吵闹地欢呼着。

  • She sat by the window, noisily gulping her morning coffee.

    清晨，她坐在窗边，咕咚咕咚地喝着咖啡。

2 ADJ-GRADED 充满噪音的;嘈杂的 A noisy place is full of a lot of loud or unpleasant noise. 充满噪音的;嘈杂的

  • It's a noisy place with film clips showing constantly on one of the cafe's giant screens.

    这个咖啡馆很嘈杂，室内的一个大屏幕上不停地播放着电影片断。

  • The baggage hall was crowded and noisy.

    行李大厅拥挤嘈杂。

  • Benitses is the noisiest resort on Corfu, with bars, discos and tavernas.

    拜尼采斯是科孚岛上最热闹的度假胜地，满是酒吧、迪斯科舞厅和餐馆。

3 ADJ-GRADED (为引起注意)吵吵嚷嚷的;哗众取宠的 If you describe someone as noisy, you are critical of them for trying to attract attention to their views by frequently and forcefully discussing them. (为引起注意)吵吵嚷嚷的;哗众取宠的 [disapproval]

  • It might, at last, silence the small but noisy intellectual clique.

    它可能最终会使这个聒噪的知识分子小圈子安静下来。

  • ...the noisy and unpopular fringe groups that are attempting to change the culture of their society.

    企图变革社会文化的哗众取宠、不受欢迎的边缘群体
