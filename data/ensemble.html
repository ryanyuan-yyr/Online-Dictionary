ensemble
ɒnsɒmbəl ɑ̃sɑ̃blə ɑnˈsɑmbəl
Word forms: plural ensembles
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 合唱组;乐团;全体演员;舞蹈团 An ensemble is a group of musicians, actors, or dancers who regularly perform together. 合唱组;乐团;全体演员;舞蹈团

  • ...an ensemble of young musicians. [+ of]

    青年音乐家乐团

  • He has also formed an exciting ensemble from his dancers.

    他还将他的舞蹈演员们组成了一个人们非常喜欢的舞蹈团。

SYN group, company, band, troupe  
2 ADJ [ADJ n] 合奏的;合演的;合奏技巧的;合演技巧的 In the arts, ensemble acting or playing is the fact or technique of playing or performing well together. 合奏的;合演的;合奏技巧的;合演技巧的 [technical]

  • Foote's most recent play, 'Dividing the Estate,' is an ensemble piece.

    福特的最新戏剧《分房产》是个合演剧目。

3 N-COUNT [usually singular] 整体;总体;全体 An ensemble of things or people is a group of things or people considered as a whole rather than as separate individuals. 整体;总体;全体 [formal]

  • The state is an ensemble of political and social structures. [+ of]

    国家是一个政治结构和社会结构的组合体。

SYN collection, set, body, whole  
4 N-COUNT [usually singular] 配套服装;套装 An ensemble is a set of clothes which have been chosen to look nice together. 配套服装;套装 [formal]

  • ...elegant designs, including navy and white ensembles and extravagant feathered hats.

    一些优雅的设计，包括海军蓝和白色的套装以及带羽毛的奢华帽子
