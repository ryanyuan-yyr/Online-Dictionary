retaliate
rɪtælieɪt rɪˈtæliˌeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense retaliates, present participle retaliating, past tense , past participle retaliated
VERB 报复;反击 If you retaliate when someone harms or annoys you, you do something which harms or annoys them in return. 报复;反击 [Also + for]

  • I was sorely tempted to retaliate. [VERB]

    我非常想报复。

  • Christie retaliated by sending his friend a long letter detailing Carl's utter incompetence. [VERB + by]

    为了报复，克里斯蒂给他朋友寄了一封长信，细述卡尔完全不称职。

  • The militia responded by saying it would retaliate against any attacks. [VERB + against]

    民兵组织回复说他们会对任何进攻予以反击。

  • They may retaliate with sanctions on other products if the bans are disregarded. [VERB + with]

    如果这些禁令被置之不理，他们可能会采取报复措施，对其他商品进行制裁。

SYN pay someone back, hit back, strike back, reciprocate  
retaliation

  • Police said they believed the attack was in retaliation for the death of the drug trafficker.

    警察说他们认为这次袭击是针对那个毒犯的死实施的报复。
