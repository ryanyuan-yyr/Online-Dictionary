sore
sɔːʳ sɔr
Word forms: comparative sorer, superlative sorest, plural sores
1 ADJ-GRADED 疼的;疼痛的 If part of your body is sore, it causes you pain and discomfort. 疼的;疼痛的

  • It's years since I've had a sore throat like I did last night.

    我的喉咙已经好几年没有像昨晚那样疼了。

  • My chest is still sore from the surgery.

    手术过后我的胸口还在作痛。

SYN painful, smarting, raw, tender  
soreness

  • The soreness lasted for about six weeks.

    这种疼痛持续了大约 6 周时间。

2 ADJ-GRADED [verb-link ADJ] 恼火的;恼怒的 If you are sore about something, you are angry and upset about it. 恼火的;恼怒的 [mainly US, informal]

  • The result is that they are now all feeling very sore at you. [+ at/about]

    结果就是，现在他们都对你感到十分恼火。

  • They are sore about losing to England in the quarter-finals.

    他们对在四分之一决赛里输给英格兰队这件事感到非常懊恼。

SYN annoyed, cross, angry, pained  
3 N-COUNT 皮肤发炎疼痛处;疮肿;溃疡 A sore is a painful place on the body where the skin is infected. 皮肤发炎疼痛处;疮肿;溃疡
SYN abscess, boil, ulcer, inflammation  
4See also:cold sore 
5 PHRASE 痛处;惹人生气的话题;令人难堪的话题 If something is a sore point with someone, it is likely to make them angry or embarrassed if you try to discuss it. 痛处;惹人生气的话题;令人难堪的话题

  • The continuing presence of American troops on Korean soil remains a very sore point with these students. [+ with/for/between]

    美国军队继续驻扎在韩国领土上这一事实仍然让这些学生深感气愤。

6 to stick out like a sore thumb→see: thumb
