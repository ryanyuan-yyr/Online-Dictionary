cruise
kruːz kruz
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense cruises, present participle cruising, past tense , past participle cruised
1 N-COUNT 乘船游览;航游 A cruise is a holiday during which you travel on a ship or boat and visit a number of places. 乘船游览;航游

  • He and his wife were planning to go on a world cruise.

    他和太太计划乘船环游世界。

  • The next stop on this cruise is likely to be in Cornwall.

    此次航游的下一站很可能是在康沃尔。

SYN sail, voyage, boat trip, sea trip  
2 VERB 乘船游览;航游 If you cruise a sea, river, or canal, you travel around it or along it on a cruise. 乘船游览;航游

  • She wants to cruise the canals of France in a barge. [VERB noun]

    她想乘驳船游览法国的运河。

  • During their summer holidays they cruised further afield to Normandy and Brittany. [VERB prep./adv.]

    暑期他们乘船到更远的诺曼底和布列塔尼游览。

SYN sail, coast, voyage  
cruising

  • ...a 51ft cruising yacht.

    长为51英尺的大游艇

3 VERB (车、船或飞机)以平稳且舒适的速度行驶，缓慢行进 If a car, ship, or aircraft cruises somewhere, it moves there at a steady comfortable speed. (车、船或飞机)以平稳且舒适的速度行驶，缓慢行进

  • A black and white police car cruised past. [VERB prep./adv.]

    一辆黑白相间的警车缓慢驶过。

SYN travel along, coast, drift, keep a steady pace  
4 VERB （球队或运动员）轻而易举赢得，轻取 If a team or sports player cruises to victory, they win easily. （球队或运动员）轻而易举赢得，轻取 [journalism]

  • Graf looked in awesome form as she cruised to an easy victory. [VERB + to]

    格拉芙轻松取胜，看上去状态极佳。

5 VERB (在公共场所，尤指男同性恋者)寻找性伙伴，猎艳 If a gay man is cruising, he is searching in public places for a sexual partner. (在公共场所，尤指男同性恋者)寻找性伙伴，猎艳

  • ...gay men cruising on Clapham Common. [VERB]

    在克拉珀姆公地寻找性伙伴的男同性恋者
