expropriate
eksproʊprieɪt ɛksˈproʊpriˌeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense expropriates, present participle expropriating, past tense , past participle expropriated
1 VERB 征收，征用（私人财产） If a government or other authority expropriates someone's property, they take it away from them for public use. 征收，征用（私人财产） [law]

  • The Bolsheviks expropriated the property of the landowners. [VERB noun]

    布尔什维克征收了地主的财产。

SYN seize, take, appropriate, confiscate  
expropriation

  • ...the expropriation of property.

    征用财产

  • Ownership is not clear because of expropriations in the Nazi era.

    纳粹时期的征用致使所有权不明确。
