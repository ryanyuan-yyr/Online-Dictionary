disable
dɪseɪbəl dɪsˈeɪbəl ˈdɪsˈeɪbəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense disables, present participle disabling, past tense , past participle disabled
1 VERB 使丧失能力;使伤残 If an injury or illness disables someone, it affects them so badly that it restricts the way that they can live their life. 使丧失能力;使伤残

  • She did all this tendon damage and it really disabled her. [VERB noun]

    她遭受这么严重的肌腱损伤，这实际上让她残废了。

  • Although disabled by polio during the Second World War, Proctor was also a first-rate helmsman. [VERB-ed]

    二战期间尽管身患小儿麻痹症，普罗克特仍不失为一流的舵手。

disabling

  • ...skin ulcers which are disfiguring and sometimes disabling.

    有损容貌甚至会致残的皮肤溃疡

2 VERB （通常指暂时地）使无效，使不能正常运转 If someone or something disables a system or mechanism, they stop it working, usually temporarily. （通常指暂时地）使无效，使不能正常运转

  • ...if you need to disable a car alarm. [VERB noun]

    如果你需要暂停汽车报警功能

SYN deactivate, disarm, defuse, make safe
