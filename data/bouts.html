bout
baʊt baʊt
Word forms: plural bouts
1 N-COUNT （疾病或不愉快情绪的）发作 If you have a bout of an illness or of an unpleasant feeling, you have it for a short period. （疾病或不愉快情绪的）发作

  • He was recovering from a severe bout of flu. [+ of]

    他患了严重的流感，尚未痊愈。

  • I was suffering with a bout of nerves. [+ of]

    我感到一阵紧张。

SYN period, time, term, fit  
2 N-COUNT （不愉快之事大量发生的）一阵，一小段时间 A bout of something that is unpleasant is a short time during which it occurs a great deal. （不愉快之事大量发生的）一阵，一小段时间

  • The latest bout of violence has claimed twenty four lives. [+ of]

    最近发生的一起暴力事件造成24人死亡。

  • A half-hour daily walk can be more beneficial than one hard bout of exercise a week. [+ of]

    每天散步半小时会比一周剧烈运动一次对身体更有益。

SYN round, run, course, series  
3 N-COUNT 拳击比赛；摔跤比赛 A bout is a boxing or wrestling match. 拳击比赛；摔跤比赛

  • This will be his eighth title bout in 19 months.

    这将是他19个月里的第8次卫冕赛。

SYN fight, match, battle, competition  
4 （当about的第一个音节不发音时，有些作家以’bout或bout替代about） Some writers use 'bout or bout to represent about when the first syllable is not pronounced. （当about的第一个音节不发音时，有些作家以’bout或bout替代about）

  • How 'bout some coffee?.

    来点儿咖啡吗？

  • I just felt the need to write bout it, I guess.

    我想我就是觉得有必要对此写点儿什么。
