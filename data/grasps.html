grasp
grɑːsp græsp græsp grɑsp
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense grasps, present participle grasping, past tense , past participle grasped
1 VERB 抓紧；握牢；握紧 If you grasp something, you take it in your hand and hold it very firmly. 抓紧；握牢；握紧

  • He grasped both my hands. [VERB noun]

    他紧紧抓住我的双手。

  • She was trying to grasp at something. [VERB + at]

    她正试图抓住什么东西。

SYN grip, hold, catch, grab  
2 See also: grasping 
3 N-SING 紧抓；紧握 A grasp is a very firm hold or grip. 紧抓；紧握

  • His hand was taken in a warm, firm grasp.

    他的手被热情地紧紧握住了。

4 N-SING [with poss, oft in/from N] 掌握；控制 If you say that something is in someone's grasp, you disapprove of the fact that they possess or control it. If something slips from your grasp, you lose it or lose control of it. 掌握；控制

  • The people in your grasp are not guests, they are hostages.

    你所控制的这些人不是客人，而是人质。

  • She allowed victory to slip from her grasp.

    她让胜利从手中溜走了。

  • ...the task of liberating a number of states from the grasp of tyrants.

    将多个国家从暴君手中解放出来的任务

5 VERB 理解；领悟；明白 If you grasp something that is complicated or difficult to understand, you understand it. 理解；领悟；明白

  • The Government has not yet grasped the seriousness of the crisis. [VERB noun]

    政府当局还没有意识到这场危机的严重性。

  • He instantly grasped that Stephen was talking about his wife. [VERB that]

    他马上就明白斯蒂芬在说他的妻子。

SYN understand, realize, take in, get  
6 N-SING 理解；掌握 A grasp of something is an understanding of it. 理解；掌握

  • They have a good grasp of foreign languages. [+ of]

    他们外语掌握得很好。

SYN understanding, knowledge, grip, perception  
7 PHRASE 为…所能实现；为…所能及 If you say that something is within someone's grasp, you mean that it is very likely that they will achieve it. 为…所能实现；为…所能及

  • Peace is now within our grasp.

    现在和平在望。
