leaflet
liːflət ˈliflɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense leaflets, present participle leafleting, past tense , past participle leafleted
1 N-COUNT 传单；小册子 A leaflet is a little book or a piece of paper containing information about a particular subject. 传单；小册子

  • Campaigners handed out leaflets on passive smoking. [+ on]

    活动人士派发了有关被动吸烟的宣传页。

  • ...a leaflet called 'Sexual Harassment at Work'.

    名为《工作中的性骚扰》的小册子

SYN booklet, notice, advert [British , informal] , brochure  
2 VERB 在（某处）散发传单（或小册子） If you leaflet a place, you distribute leaflets there, for example by handing them to people, or by putting them through letter boxes. 在（某处）散发传单（或小册子）

  • We've leafleted the university today to try to drum up some support. [VERB noun]

    我们今天在大学散发了传单，希望能争取到支持。

  • The only reason we leafleted on the Jewish New Year was because more people than usual go to the synagogue on that day. [VERB]

    我们在犹太新年派传单，纯粹是因为那天去犹太教堂的人比平日多。
