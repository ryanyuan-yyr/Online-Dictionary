desecrate
desɪkreɪt ˈdɛsɪˌkreɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense desecrates, present participle desecrating, past tense , past participle desecrated
VERB 亵渎，玷污(神圣或特别的事物) If someone desecrates something which is considered to be holy or very special, they deliberately damage or insult it. 亵渎，玷污(神圣或特别的事物)

  • She shouldn't have desecrated the picture of a religious leader. [VERB noun]

    她不该亵渎宗教领袖的画像。

  • The earth is to be honoured; it is not to be desecrated. [VERB noun]

    大地应该受到敬仰，而不该被亵渎。

SYN profane, dishonour, defile, violate  
desecration

    对墓地的亵渎震惊了整个地区。
