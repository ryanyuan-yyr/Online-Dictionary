chirp
tʃɜːʳp tʃɜrp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense chirps, present participle chirping, past tense , past participle chirped
1 VERB (鸟、昆虫)啾啾叫，发唧唧声 When a bird or an insect such as a cricket or grasshopper chirps, it makes short high-pitched sounds. (鸟、昆虫)啾啾叫，发唧唧声

  • The crickets chirped faster and louder. [VERB]

    蟋蟀叫得更欢了。

Chirp is also a noun.

  • The chirps of the small garden birds sounded distant.

    远处传来庭园小鸟的鸣啭。

chirping

  • ...the chirping of birds.

    鸟儿的啁啾

2 VERB （人）欢快地高声说话，叽叽喳喳 You say that a person chirps when they say something in a cheerful, high-pitched voice. （人）欢快地高声说话，叽叽喳喳 [written]

  • 'See you soon, I hope!' chirped my mother. [VERB with quote]

    “希望很快能再见！”我妈开心地大声说。
