invert
ɪnvɜːʳt ɪnˈvɜrt ˈɪnˌvɜrt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense inverts, present participle inverting, past tense , past participle inverted
1 VERB 使反向;使倒置 If you invert something, you turn it the other way up or back to front. 使反向;使倒置 [formal]

  • Invert the cake onto a cooling rack. [VERB noun]

    把蛋糕倒扣在冷却架上。

  • ...a black inverted triangle. [VERB-ed]

    一个黑色的倒三角

SYN overturn, upturn, turn upside down, upset  
2 VERB 使颠倒 If you invert something, you change it to its opposite. 使颠倒 [formal]

  • They may be hoping to invert the presumption that a defendant is innocent until proved guilty. [VERB noun]

    他们可能有望颠覆“被告在证实有罪之前是无辜的”这一假设。

  • ...a telling illustration of inverted moral values. [VERB-ed]

    是非颠倒的道德观的有力证明
