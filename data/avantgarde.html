avant-garde
ævɒŋ gɑːʳd ˌævɒŋˈɡɑːd avɑ̃ɡard əˌvɑntˈgɑrd ˌ ɑˌvɑntˈgɑrd ˌ æˌvɑntˈgɑrd avɑ̃ˈgaʀd
(also avant garde)
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] （艺术、音乐、戏剧、文学）先锋派的，前卫的，实验性的 Avant-garde art, music, theatre, and literature is very modern and experimental. （艺术、音乐、戏剧、文学）先锋派的，前卫的，实验性的

  • ...avant-garde concert music.

    音乐会演奏的前卫音乐

SYN progressive, pioneering, way-out [informal] , experimental  
Avant-garde is also a noun.

  • He was an enthusiast for the avant-garde.

    他是个先锋艺术的狂热拥护者。

2 N-SING [the NOUN] 先锋派；前卫派 You can refer to the artists, writers, and musicians who introduce new and very modern ideas as the avant-garde . 先锋派；前卫派

  • In Paris he made friends among the avant-garde.

    他在巴黎结交先锋人士。
