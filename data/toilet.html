toilet
tɔɪlət ˈtɔɪlɪt
Word forms: plural toilets
1 N-COUNT 抽水马桶；便池 A toilet is a large bowl with a seat, or a platform with a hole, which is connected to a water system and which you use when you want to get rid of urine or faeces from your body. 抽水马桶；便池

  • She made Tina flush the pills down the toilet.

    她让蒂娜把药丸扔进马桶里冲走。

SYN lavatory, bathroom, loo [British , informal] , bog [slang]  
2 N-COUNT 厕所;卫生间;洗手间 A toilet is a room in a house or public building that contains a toilet. 厕所;卫生间;洗手间 [British]

  • Annette ran and locked herself in the toilet.

    安妮特跑向洗手间，把自己锁在里面。

  • Fred never uses public toilets.

    弗雷德从不使用公共厕所。

in AM, use 美国英语用 bathroom, rest room
3 PHRASE 上厕所;上洗手间 You can say that someone goes to the toilet to mean that they get rid of waste substances from their body, especially when you want to avoid using words that you think may offend people. 上厕所;上洗手间 [mainly British]
in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 go to the bathroom
