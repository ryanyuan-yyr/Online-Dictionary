scuffle
skʌfəl ˈskʌfəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense scuffles, present participle scuffling, past tense , past participle scuffled
1 N-COUNT (短暂的)扭打,混战 A scuffle is a short, disorganized fight or struggle. (短暂的)扭打,混战

  • Violent scuffles broke out between rival groups demonstrating for and against independence.

    支持和反对独立的两支游行队伍间爆发了激烈的冲突。

SYN fight, set-to [informal] , scrap [informal] , disturbance  
2 V-RECIP 扭打;混战 If people scuffle, they fight for a short time in a disorganized way. 扭打;混战

  • Police scuffled with some of the protesters. [VERB + with]

    警察与一些抗议者发生了冲突。

  • He and Hannah had been scuffling in the yard outside his house. [VERB]

    他和汉娜一直在他房外的院子里扭打。

SYN fight, struggle, clash, contend
