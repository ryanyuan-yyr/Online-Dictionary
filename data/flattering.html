flattering
flætərɪŋ ˈflætərɪŋ
1 ADJ-GRADED 使人显得更漂亮的 If something is flattering, it makes you appear more attractive. 使人显得更漂亮的

  • Some styles are so flattering that they instantly become classics.

    有些款式特别衬人，很快就成了经典。

  • ...a refreshing new look that's flattering to skins of all ages.

    令人耳目一新、能使所有年龄段的皮肤更添靓丽的新妆

  • It wasn't a very flattering photograph.

    这张相片显得人不是十分漂亮。

SYN becoming, kind, effective, enhancing  
flatteringly

  • The bold necklace flatteringly lightens her skin tone.

    这条光彩夺目的项链衬得她的肤色亮了起来。

2 ADJ-GRADED 谄媚的;奉承的;讨好的 If someone's remarks are flattering, they praise you and say nice things about you. 谄媚的;奉承的;讨好的

  • There were pleasant and flattering obituaries about him.

    讣告里全是对他的客套溢美之词。

  • The press was flattering.

    新闻界正在大肆吹捧。

SYN ingratiating, complimentary, gratifying, fawning  
3 ADJ-GRADED [oft it verb-link ADJ to-infinitive] 奉承的;令人感到荣幸的 If you describe something as flattering, you mean that it pleases you and makes you feel important or special. 奉承的;令人感到荣幸的

  • It is all terribly flattering and she is trying to reply to every single letter personally.

    一切都让她感到无比荣幸，她尽量亲自回复每一封信。

  • Colin chuckled at the flattering response.

    这一奉承的回应让科林暗自发笑。

  • It was flattering to be told how indispensable his taste and talent were.

    被告知自己的品味和才能如此不可或缺，他心里非常高兴。
