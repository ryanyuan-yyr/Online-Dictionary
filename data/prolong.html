prolong
prəlɒŋ prəʊˈlɒŋˌɡeɪt -lɔːŋ proʊˈlɔŋ prəˈlɔŋ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense prolongs, present participle prolonging, past tense , past participle prolonged
VERB 延长；拉长；拖长 To prolong something means to make it last longer. 延长；拉长；拖长

  • Mr Chesler said foreign military aid was prolonging the war. [VERB noun]

    切斯勒先生说国外的军事援助拉长了战事。

  • The actual action of the drug can be prolonged significantly. [VERB noun]

    这种药物实际起作用的时间可以大大延长。

SYN lengthen, continue, perpetuate, draw out  
prolongation

  • ...the prolongation of productive human life.

    人类生育年龄的延长
