foundation stone
Word forms: plural foundation stones
1 N-COUNT [oft with poss] (常指建筑物奠基典礼时放置的)基石,奠基石 A foundation stone is a large block of stone built into a large public building near the bottom. It is often involved in a ceremony for the opening of the building, and has writing on it recording this. (常指建筑物奠基典礼时放置的)基石,奠基石 [mainly British]

  • The Princess of Wales laid the foundation stone for the extension to the Cathedral.

    威尔士王妃为大教堂的扩建奠基。

in AM, use 美国英语用 cornerstone
2 N-COUNT (存在或成功的)根基，根本 The foundation stone of something is the basic, important thing which its existence or success depends on. (存在或成功的)根基，根本

  • ...these foundation stones of the future: education, training, research, development. [+ of]

    决定未来的一些根基：教育、培训、研究和发展
