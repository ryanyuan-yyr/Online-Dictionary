posting
poʊstɪŋ
Word forms: plural postings
1 N-COUNT 派任；外派 If you get a posting to a different town or country, your employers send you to work there, usually for several years. 派任；外派 [mainly British]

  • He was rewarded with a posting to New York. [+ to]

    作为奖励，他被派往纽约工作。

  • Relevant work experience is required for overseas postings.

    派驻海外要求有相关的工作经验。

2See also:post 
in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 assignment
3 N-COUNT （军队的）派驻，派遣 If a member of an armed force gets a posting to a particular place, they are sent to live and work there for a period. （军队的）派驻，派遣

  • ...awaiting his posting to a field ambulance corps in early 1941. [+ to]

    他1941年年初等待被派往野战救护队

  • ...a prestigious military posting.

    享有威望的军事委派

4 N-COUNT （因特网上的）帖子 A posting is a message that is placed on the Internet, for example on a bulletin board or website, for everyone to read. （因特网上的）帖子 [computing]

  • Postings on the Internet can be accessed from anywhere in the world.

    因特网上的帖子在世界任何一个地方都能读到。
