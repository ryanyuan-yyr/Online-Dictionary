joy
dʒɔɪ dʒɔɪ
Word forms: plural joys
1 N-UNCOUNT 欢欣；愉快；喜悦 Joy is a feeling of great happiness. 欢欣；愉快；喜悦

  • Salter shouted with joy.

    索尔特欣喜地叫喊着。

  • ...tears of joy.

    喜悦的泪水

SYN delight, pleasure, triumph, satisfaction  
2 N-COUNT 令人高兴的事（或人）；乐事；乐趣 A joy is something or someone that makes you feel happy or gives you great pleasure. 令人高兴的事（或人）；乐事；乐趣

  • One can never learn all there is to know about cooking, and that is one of the joys of being a chef. [+ of]

    钻研烹饪之道是永无止境的，这也是当厨师的乐趣之一。

  • It was a joy to see her looking so well.

    看到她气色这么好真叫人高兴。

SYN treasure, wonder, treat, prize  
3 N-UNCOUNT 成功；好运 If you get no joy, you do not have success or luck in achieving what you are trying to do. 成功；好运 [British, informal]

  • They expect no joy from the vote itself. [+ from]

    他们对投票本身不抱任何希望。

  • If you don't get any joy, get in touch with your local councillor.

    如果你没得到任何结果，请和本地议员联系。

4 PHRASE 欢欣鼓舞；欣喜若狂 If you say that someone is jumping for joy, you mean that they are very pleased or happy about something. 欢欣鼓舞；欣喜若狂

  • He jumped for joy on being told the news.

    得知这个消息，他高兴得手舞足蹈。
