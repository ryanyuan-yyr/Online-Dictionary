throw off
1 PHRASAL VERB 摆脱，抛掉(束缚，苦恼) If you throw off something that is restricting you or making you unhappy, you get rid of it. 摆脱，抛掉(束缚，苦恼)

  • ...a country ready to throw off the shackles of its colonial past. [VERB PREP. noun]

    就要摆脱过去殖民枷锁的国家

  • One day depression descended upon him, and wherever he went after that he could never throw it off. [VERB noun PREP.]

    有一天，他突然开始消沉起来，从那以后，无论他走到哪儿也摆脱不了这种情绪。

SYN cast off, shake off, rid yourself of, free yourself of  
2 PHRASAL VERB (向空中)释放，散发出 If something throws off a substance, it produces it and releases it into the air. (向空中)释放，散发出

  • The belt may make a squealing noise and throw off sooty black particles of rubber. [VERB PREP. noun]

    传送带会发出刺耳的噪音并释放出乌黑的橡胶颗粒。

  • The star grew 30% brighter and threw off huge amounts of radiation. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    那颗星的亮度增加了 30%并释放出大量辐射。

3 PHRASAL VERB 甩掉，摆脱(追赶者) If you throw off people who are chasing you or trying to find you, you do something unexpected that makes them unable to catch you or find you. 甩掉，摆脱(追赶者) [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • He is said to have thrown off pursuers by pedaling across the Wisconsin state line. [VERB PREP. noun]

    据说他骑车越过威斯康星州界，摆脱了追捕他的人。

  • He tried to throw police off the track of his lover. [VERB noun PREP. noun]

    他努力让警方无法跟踪他的情人。

SYN escape from, lose, leave behind, get away from
