stand up
1 →see: stand 1  →see: 2 
2 PHRASAL VERB (要求或证据)经得起检验，能够成立 If something such as a claim or a piece of evidence stands up, it is accepted as true or satisfactory after being carefully examined. (要求或证据)经得起检验，能够成立

  • He made wild accusations that did not stand up. [VERB PREP.]

    他的指控毫无根据，根本站不住脚。

  • How well does this thesis stand up to close examination? [VERB PREP. + to]

    这个命题经得起推敲吗？

SYN be plausible, be convincing, hold up, hold water  
3 PHRASAL VERB (男友或女友)爽约，放…的鸽子 If a boyfriend or girlfriend stands you up, they fail to keep an arrangement to meet you. (男友或女友)爽约，放…的鸽子 [informal] [Also VERB PREPOSITION n (not pron)]

  • We were to have had dinner together yesterday evening, but he stood me up. [VERB noun PREP.]

    我们昨晚本来要一起吃晚饭的，但他爽约了。

  • He was in a foul mood because he had been stood up. [VERB noun PREP.]

    他因女友爽约而心情不好。

SYN let down, leave stranded, leave in the lurch  
stand-up ˈstændʌp ˈstændˌʌp
(also standup)
1 ADJ [ADJ n] (喜剧演员)单人表演的 A stand-up comic or comedian stands alone in front of an audience and tells jokes. (喜剧演员)单人表演的

  • He does all kinds of accents, he can do jokes—he could be a stand-up comic.

    他会模仿各种口音，还会讲笑话—他可以当个独角喜剧演员。

  • Women do not normally break into the big time by doing stand-up comedy.

    女性靠演单人喜剧通常无法成名。

Stand-up is also a noun.

  • ...one of the worst stand-ups alive.

    在世的演技最差的单人喜剧演员之一

2 N-UNCOUNT 单人滑稽表演;单口相声 Stand-up is stand-up comedy. 单人滑稽表演;单口相声

  • ...getting by on likeability, professionalism and the kind of nerve you need to do stand-up.

    凭借个人魅力、精湛的技艺和进行单人喜剧表演所需要的勇气过活

3 ADJ [ADJ n] (争吵、打斗)激烈的 If people have a stand-up argument or fight, they stand up and shout at each other or hit each other violently. (争吵、打斗)激烈的
