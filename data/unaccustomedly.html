unaccustomed
ʌnəkʌstəmd ˌʌnəˈkʌstəmd ˌʌnəˈkʌstəmd
1 ADJ-GRADED 不习惯的；不熟悉的；不适应的 If you are unaccustomed to something, you do not know it very well or have not experienced it very often. 不习惯的；不熟悉的；不适应的 [written]

  • They were unaccustomed to such military setbacks. [+ to]

    他们还不习惯这样的军事挫败。

  • It is a part of Britain as yet largely unaccustomed to tourists. [+ to]

    英国的这一块地方还很不为游客所熟悉。

SYN not used to, unfamiliar with, unused to, not given to  
2 ADJ-GRADED [ADJ n] 不寻常的；奇怪的 If you describe someone's behaviour or experiences as unaccustomed, you mean that they do not usually behave like this or have experiences of this kind. 不寻常的；奇怪的 [written]

  • He began to comfort me with such unaccustomed gentleness.

    他开始以那种少有的温柔安慰我。

  • His nose had been reddened by unaccustomed exposure to the California sun.

    经过加州阳光不同寻常的照射，他的鼻子晒红了。

SYN unfamiliar, unusual, unexpected, new
