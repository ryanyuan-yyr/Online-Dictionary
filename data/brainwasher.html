brainwash
breɪnwɒʃ ˈbreɪnˌwɔʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense brainwashes, present participle brainwashing, past tense , past participle brainwashed
VERB 给…洗脑；向…强行灌输 If you brainwash someone, you force them to believe something by continually telling them that it is true, and preventing them from thinking about it properly. 给…洗脑；向…强行灌输 [Also VERB n]

  • They brainwash people into giving up all their money. [VERB noun + into]

    他们向人们强行灌输放弃所有钱财的思想。

  • I'd been brainwashed into believing I was worthless. [V n into -ing]

    我被洗了脑，认为自己一文不值。

  • We were brainwashed to believe we were all equal. [be VERB-ed to-infinitive]

    我们被洗了脑，认为人人都是平等的。

SYN indoctrinate, condition, drill, pressurize
