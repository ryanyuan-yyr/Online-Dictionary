dispensation
dɪspenseɪʃən ˌdɪspənˈseɪʃən
Word forms: plural dispensations
1 N-VAR 特许 A dispensation is special permission to do something that is normally not allowed. 特许

  • A special dispensation may be obtained from the domestic union concerned.

    从国内相关的工会那儿或许能得到一项特许。

  • They were promised dispensation from military service. [+ from]

    他们得到许诺，可以免服兵役。

  • The committee was not prepared to use its discretion and grant special dispensation.

    委员会无意动用它的决定权来给予特批。

SYN exemption, licence, exception, permission  
2 N-UNCOUNT 分配;分发;执行;实施 Dispensation of something is the issuing of it, especially from a position of authority. 分配;分发;执行;实施 [formal]

  • ...our application of consistent standards in the dispensation of justice. [+ of]

    我们始终如一的执法标准

3 N-COUNT [usually supplement NOUN] （某一特定时期内宗教或政治上的）制度 A dispensation is a religious or political system that has authority at a particular time. （某一特定时期内宗教或政治上的）制度

  • The new dispensation proved a success, certainly with the business community.

    新的制度收到了成效，当然也包括商业界。
