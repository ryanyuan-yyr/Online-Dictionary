jut
dʒʌt dʒʌt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense juts, present participle jutting, past tense , past participle jutted
1 VERB 凸出；伸出 If something juts out, it sticks out above or beyond a surface. 凸出；伸出

  • The northern end of the island juts out like a long, thin finger into the sea. [VERB adv./prep.]

    岛屿的北端像一根细长的手指一样伸入海中。

  • Tombstones jutted out of the ground in broken clusters. [VERB adv./prep.]

    墓碑三三两两地竖立在地面上。

  • He had clear blue eyes and a jutting chin. [VERB-ing]

    他有一双清澈的蓝眼睛和凸出的下巴。

SYN stick out, project, extend, protrude  
2 V-ERG （使）（使身体部位）凸出；（尤指）翘起（下巴） If you jut a part of your body, especially your chin, or if it juts, you push it forward in an aggressive or determined way. （使）（使身体部位）凸出；（尤指）翘起（下巴） [Also VERB n]

  • His jaw jutted stubbornly forward; he would not be denied. [VERB adv./prep.]

    他的下巴倔强地翘着；他不会甘心被拒绝。

  • Gwen jutted her chin forward and did not answer the teacher. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    格温下巴翘得高高的，没有回答老师的问题。

  • Ken's jaw jutted with determination. [VERB]

    肯坚定地扬起下颌。
