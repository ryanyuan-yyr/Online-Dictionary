hollow
hɒloʊ ˈhɑloʊ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense hollows, present participle hollowing, past tense , past participle hollowed
1 ADJ 空的;空心的;中空的 Something that is hollow has a space inside it, as opposed to being solid all the way through. 空的;空心的;中空的

  • ...a hollow tree.

    空心的树

  • ...a hollow cylinder.

    中空圆筒

SYN empty, vacant, void, unfilled  
2 ADJ-GRADED 凹的;凹陷的 A surface that is hollow curves inwards. 凹的;凹陷的

  • He looked young, dark and sharp-featured, with hollow cheeks.

    他看起来很年轻，肤色黝黑，五官分明，双颊凹陷。

3 N-COUNT 树洞 A hollow is a hole inside a tree. 树洞

  • I made my home there, in the hollow of a dying elm.

    我在一棵快要枯死的榆树的树洞里安了家。

4 N-COUNT 凹陷处;低洼地 A hollow is an area that is lower than the surrounding surface. 凹陷处;低洼地

  • Below him the town lay warm in the hollow of the hill. [+ of/in/between]

    在他下方，小镇卧在温暖的山谷里。

  • ...where water gathers in a hollow and forms a pond.

    在那里水积聚在低洼处形成了池塘

SYN cavity, cup, hole, bowl  
5 ADJ [usually ADJ n] 空洞的;无用的;无价值的 If you describe a statement, situation, or person as hollow, you mean they have no real value, worth, or effectiveness. 空洞的;无用的;无价值的

  • Any threat to bring in the police is a hollow one.

    任何威胁说要叫警察来的话都是虚声恫吓。

  • ...a hollow man who's coasted on charm for far too long.

    长久以来一直依靠个人魅力取胜的肤浅之人

SYN worthless, empty, useless, vain  
hollowness

  • One month before the deadline we see the hollowness of these promises.

    离最后期限只有一个月时，我们认识到了这些许诺都是空头支票。

6 ADJ [ADJ n] （笑声等）虚伪的，不真诚的 If someone gives a hollow laugh, they laugh in a way that shows that they do not really find something amusing. （笑声等）虚伪的，不真诚的

  • Murray Pick's hollow laugh had no mirth in it.

    默里·皮克的干笑里没有一点快乐。

SYN insincere, false, artificial, cynical  
7 ADJ [ADJ n] （声音）空洞的，沉闷回荡的 A hollow sound is dull and echoing. （声音）空洞的，沉闷回荡的

  • ...the hollow sound of a gunshot.

    空洞的枪响

SYN dull, low, deep, flat  
8 VERB [usually passive] 使凹陷;使下陷 If something is hollowed, its surface is made to curve inwards or downwards. 使凹陷;使下陷

  • The mule's back was hollowed by the weight of its burden. [be VERB-ed]

    骡背被重负压得塌下了。

  • ...her high, elegantly hollowed cheekbones. [VERB-ed]

    她凹陷有致的高颧骨

SYN scoop out, dig out, excavate, gouge out
