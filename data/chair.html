chair
tʃeəʳ tʃɛə tʃɛr
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense chairs, present participle chairing, past tense , past participle chaired
1 N-COUNT 椅子 A chair is a piece of furniture for one person to sit on. Chairs have a back and four legs. 椅子

  • He rose from his chair and walked to the window.

    他从椅子上站起来，朝窗口走去。

2 N-COUNT [usually singular] (大学的)教授职位 At a university, a chair is the post of professor. (大学的)教授职位

  • He has been appointed to the chair of sociology at Southampton University. [+ of/in]

    他被任命担任南安普敦大学的社会学教授一职。

  • He gave London University £100,000 to establish a chair in Islamic art.

    他资助伦敦大学10万英镑，以设立伊斯兰艺术教授一职。

3 N-COUNT [usually singular] (委员会、会议的)主席 The person who is the chair of a committee or meeting is the person in charge of it. (委员会、会议的)主席

  • She is the chair of the Defense Advisory Committee on Women in the Military. [+ of]

    她担任军队女性权益保护顾问委员会主席。

4 VERB 主持(会议);担任(委员会的)主席 If you chair a meeting or a committee, you are the person in charge of it. 主持(会议);担任(委员会的)主席

  • He was about to chair a meeting in Venice of EU foreign ministers.

    他将主持在威尼斯召开的欧盟外长会议。

  • The declaration was drafted by a committee chaired by Dr Robert Song. [VERB noun]

    宣言由罗伯特·桑博士领导的委员会起草。

5 N-SING 同 electric chair The chair is the same as the electric chair . 同 electric chair [US]
6 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 主持(会议) If you are in the chair or take the chair at a meeting, you are the person in charge of it. 主持(会议)

  • In the chair was Morien Morgan.

    会议由莫里恩·摩根主持。

  • Wheeler took the chair of this sub-committee.

    惠勒担任该小组委员会会议主席。

  • Finance Ministers are meeting today in Brussels with Britain in the chair.

    财政部部长们今天在布鲁塞尔碰头，会议由英国主持。
