authoritative
ɔːθɒrɪtətɪv əθɔːrɪteɪtɪv əˈθɔrəˌteɪtɪv əˈθɑrəˌteɪtɪv əˈθɔrətəˌtɪv əˈθɑrətəˌtɪv
1 ADJ-GRADED 权威的;威严的;有威信的 Someone or something that is authoritative gives an impression of power and importance and is likely to be obeyed. 权威的;威严的;有威信的

  • He has a commanding presence and deep, authoritative voice.

    他仪态威严，声音低沉，说话有威信。

  • Her smile was warm but authoritative.

    她的笑容很和蔼，同时又透着威严。

SYN commanding, lordly, masterly, imposing  
authoritatively

  • The man pushed his way authoritatively through the crowd.

    那个人从人群中硬挤出一条路。

2 ADJ-GRADED （在某一方面）权威性的，可信的 Someone or something that is authoritative has a lot of knowledge of a particular subject. （在某一方面）权威性的，可信的

  • The first authoritative study of polio was published in 1840.

    关于小儿麻痹症的第一篇权威性研究报告发表于1840年。

  • The evidence she uses is usually highly authoritative.

    她采用的证据通常都具有很高的可信度。

SYN reliable, learned, sound, true  
authoritatively

  • My own life is the only thing I can speak authoritatively about.

    我只对自己的生活有发言权。
