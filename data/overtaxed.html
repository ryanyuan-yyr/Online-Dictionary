overtax
oʊvəʳtæks ˌoʊvərˈtæks
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense overtaxes, present participle overtaxing, past tense , past participle overtaxed
1 VERB 使负担过重；使超负荷工作；过度使用 If you overtax someone or something, you force them to work harder than they can really manage, and may do them harm as a result. 使负担过重；使超负荷工作；过度使用

  • ...a contralto who has overtaxed her voice. [VERB noun]

    用嗓过度的女低音

2 VERB 对…征税过重 If you say that a government is overtaxing its people, you mean that it is making them pay more tax than you think they should pay. 对…征税过重

  • You can't help Britain by overtaxing its people. [VERB noun]

    向国民过重课税对英国于事无补。
