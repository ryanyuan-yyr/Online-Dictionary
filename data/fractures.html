fracture
fræktʃəʳ ˈfræktʃər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense fractures, present participle fracturing, past tense , past participle fractured
1 N-COUNT 破裂;(尤指)骨折 A fracture is a slight crack or break in something, especially a bone. 破裂;(尤指)骨折

  • At least one-third of all women over ninety have sustained a hip fracture.

    超过90岁的所有女性中至少有1/3已经出现髋骨骨折。

2 V-ERG (使)(轻微)骨折;(使)断裂 If something such as a bone is fractured or fractures, it gets a slight crack or break in it. (使)(轻微)骨折;(使)断裂

  • You've fractured a rib, maybe more than one. [VERB noun]

    你的一根肋骨断了，可能不止一根。

  • One strut had fractured and been crudely repaired in several places. [VERB]

    一根撑木出现了裂痕，有几处已进行了粗略的修补。

  • He suffered a fractured skull. [VERB-ed]

    他的头骨开裂了。

SYN break, split, crack, rupture  
3 V-ERG (使)分裂;(使)解散 If something such as an organization or society is fractured or fractures, it splits into several parts or stops existing. (使)分裂;(使)解散 [formal]

  • His policy risks fracturing the coalition. [VERB noun]

    他的政策有分裂联盟的风险。

  • It might be a society that could fracture along class lines. [VERB]

    它可能成为一个阶级分化的社会。

SYN split, separate, divide, rend [literary]
