rappel
ræpel ræˈpɛl ræˈpɛl rəˈpɛl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense rappels, present participle rappelling, past tense , past participle rappelled
VERB 绕绳滑下;绕绳下降 To rappel down a cliff or rock face means to slide down it in a controlled way using a rope, with your feet against the cliff or rock. 绕绳滑下;绕绳下降 [US]

  • They learned to rappel down a cliff. [VERB prep.]

    他们学会了绕绳从绝壁上滑下。
