pick on
1 PHRASAL VERB 找…的岔子；故意刁难 If someone picks on you, they repeatedly criticize you unfairly or treat you unkindly. 找…的岔子；故意刁难 [informal]

  • Bullies pick on younger children. [VERB PREP. noun]

    坏孩子专挑年纪更小的孩子欺负。

  • Mr Adams was repeatedly bullied and picked on by manageress Elizabeth Archer. [VERB PREP. noun]

    女经理伊丽莎白·阿彻多次胁迫和刁难亚当斯先生。

SYN torment, bully, bait, tease  
2 PHRASAL VERB 挑中；选中 If someone picks on a particular person or thing, they choose them, for example for special attention or treatment. 挑中；选中 [mainly British]

  • When you have made up your mind, pick on a day when you will not be under much stress. [VERB PREP. noun]

    你什么时候下决心了，就挑一个压力不大的日子。

  • I picked on simple things — rice and peas, meat and bread. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我选了些简单的：豌豆米饭和夹肉面包。
