adjunct
ædʒʌŋkt ˈæˌdʒʌŋkt
Word forms: plural adjuncts
1 N-COUNT 附属品;辅助物 Something that is an adjunct to something larger or more important is connected with it or helps to perform the same task. 附属品;辅助物 [Also + of]

  • Physical therapy is an important adjunct to drug treatments. [+ to]

    物理疗法是戒毒治疗中的一种重要辅助性疗法。

  • The Party was certainly not an official adjunct of the police department.

    该党派肯定不是公安部门的官方附属机构。

SYN addition, supplement, accessory, complement  
2 N-COUNT （语法中的）修饰成分 In grammar, an adjunct is a word or group of words which indicates the circumstances of an action, event, or situation. An adjunct is usually a prepositional phrase or an adverb group. （语法中的）修饰成分
