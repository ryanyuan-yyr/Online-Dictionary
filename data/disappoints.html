disappoint
dɪsəpɔɪnt ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪnt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense disappoints, present participle disappointing, past tense , past participle disappointed
VERB 使失望 If things or people disappoint you, they are not as good as you had hoped, or do not do what you hoped they would do. 使失望

  • She knew that she would disappoint him. [VERB noun]

    她知道她会令他失望的。

  • A spokesman for UNHCR said he was surprised and disappointed by the decision. [VERB noun]

    联合国难民署的一位发言人说他对这一决定既震惊又失望。

SYN let down, dismay, fail, dash
