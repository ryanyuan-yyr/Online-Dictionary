revile
rɪvaɪl rɪˈvaɪl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense reviles, present participle reviling, past tense , past participle reviled
VERB 憎恨;辱骂;谩骂;痛斥 If someone or something is reviled, people hate them intensely or show their hatred of them. 憎恨;辱骂;谩骂;痛斥 [formal]

  • He was just as feared and reviled as his tyrannical parents. [be VERB-ed]

    他和他专横残暴的父母一样为人惧怕和憎恨。

  • What right had the crowd to revile the England players for something they could not help...? [VERB noun]

    观众有什么权力因为英格兰队员们无能为力的事情而斥责他们？

SYN malign, abuse, knock [informal] , rubbish [informal]
