relocate
riːloʊkeɪt ˌriːləʊˈkeɪt -loʊkeɪt riˈloʊˌkeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense relocates, present participle relocating, past tense , past participle relocated
V-ERG （使）迁移;（使）搬迁;重新安置 If people or businesses relocate or if someone relocates them, they move to a different place. （使）迁移;（使）搬迁;重新安置

  • If the company was to relocate, most employees would move. [VERB]

    如果这个公司要搬迁，多数员工也要搬家。

  • Ray Bonner first relocated to Africa several years ago. [VERB]

    雷·邦纳在几年前先搬到了非洲。

  • There will be the problem of where to relocate the returning troops. [VERB noun]

    重新安置退伍军人将成为问题。

  • Its headquarters will soon be relocated from Westminster to the Greenwich site. [VERB noun]

    其总部很快将从威斯敏斯特迁往格林尼治。

SYN move house, move, change residence, leave
