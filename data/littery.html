litter
lɪtəʳ ˈlɪtər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense litters, present participle littering, past tense , past participle littered
1 N-UNCOUNT 废弃物；被胡乱扔掉的东西；垃圾 Litter is rubbish that is left lying around outside. 废弃物；被胡乱扔掉的东西；垃圾

  • If you see litter in the corridor, pick it up.

    在过道里看到垃圾的话请捡起来。

  • On Wednesday we cleared a beach and woodland of litter.

    星期三我们清理了一块海滨林地的垃圾。

SYN rubbish, refuse, waste, fragments  
2 N-UNCOUNT 杂乱的一堆 A litter of things is a quantity of them that are lying around in a disorganized way. 杂乱的一堆

  • He pushed aside the litter of books and papers and laid two places at the table. [+ of]

    他把桌子上凌乱堆放着的书本和报纸推开，摆了两套餐具。

SYN jumble, mess, disorder, confusion  
3 VERB 使凌乱 If a number of things litter a place, they are scattered untidily around it or over it. 使凌乱

  • Glass from broken bottles litters the pavement. [VERB noun]

    人行道上满是破碎瓶子的玻璃渣。

SYN clutter, mess up, clutter up, be scattered about  
littered

  • The entrance hall is littered with toys and wellington boots.

    门厅里到处乱扔着玩具和雨靴。

  • Concrete purpose-built resorts are littered across the mountainsides.

    山坡上到处都是专门建造的水泥度假屋。

4 ADJ [verb-link ADJ with n] 充满的 If something is littered with things, it contains many examples of it. 充满的

  • History is littered with men and women spurred into achievement by a father's disregard. [+ with]

    历史上因为被父亲漠视而发奋成功的儿女数不胜数。

  • Charles' speech is littered with lots of marketing buzzwords like 'package' and 'product'. [+ with]

    查尔斯的演讲充斥着“包装”、“产品”之类的营销领域流行用语。

5 N-COUNT 一窝（幼崽） A litter is a group of animals born to the same mother at the same time. 一窝（幼崽）

  • ...a litter of pups. [+ of]

    一窝小狗

6 N-UNCOUNT 猫沙 Litter is a dry substance that you put in the container where you want your cat to go to the toilet. 猫沙
