dispense
dɪspens dɪˈspɛns dɪˈspɛns
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dispenses, present participle dispensing, past tense , past participle dispensed
1 VERB 分配;分发;施与 If someone dispenses something that they own or control, they give or provide it to a number of people. 分配;分发;施与 [formal]

  • The Union had already dispensed £40,000 in grants. [VERB noun]

    工会已经拨款4万英镑。

  • The local welfare office is where government dispenses many of its services. [VERB noun]

    地方福利部门就是政府贯彻实施其众多服务的窗口。

  • I thought of myself as a patriarch, dispensing words of wisdom to all my children. [VERB noun + to]

    我把自己当作在向子女传授至理名言的一家之长。

SYN distribute, assign, allocate, allot  
2 VERB (机器自动)提供(货物等) If you obtain a product by getting it out of a machine, you can say that the machine dispenses the product. (机器自动)提供(货物等)

  • For two weeks, the cash machine was unable to dispense money. [VERB noun]

    该自动取款机已有两个星期不能提供现金。

  • The lotion is dispensed by a handy pump action spray. [VERB noun]

    洗液由一个使用方便的按压式皂液器提供。

3 VERB 配(药);配(方);发(药) When a chemist dispenses medicine, he or she prepares it, and gives or sells it to the patient or customer. 配(药);配(方);发(药) [Also VERB n + to]

  • Some shops gave wrong or inadequate advice when dispensing homeopathic medicines. [VERB noun]

    有些药店在配发顺势疗法的药物时给出了错误或不当的建议。

  • Four out of five prescriptions are dispensed free to people who are exempt. [be V-ed to n]

    五分之四的处方是免费配给那些免除了医疗费的病人的。

  • Doctors confine themselves to prescribing rather than dispensing. [VERB]

    医生仅仅是开药而不配药。
