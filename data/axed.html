axe
æks
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense axes, present participle axing, past tense , past participle axed
in AM, use 美国英语用 ax
1 N-COUNT 斧;斧子 An axe is a tool used for cutting wood. It consists of a heavy metal blade which is sharp at one edge and attached by its other edge to the end of a long handle. 斧;斧子
2 VERB [usually passive] 解雇;裁减;削减;撤销 If someone's job or something such as a public service or a television programme is axed, it is ended suddenly and without discussion. 解雇;裁减;削减;撤销

  • Community projects are being axed by hard-pressed social services departments. [be VERB-ed]

    处于困境的社会服务部门被迫砍掉社区项目。

SYN abandon, end, pull, eliminate  
3 N-SING （通常为节省资金而进行的）解雇，撤销，关闭 If a person or institution is facing the axe, that person is likely to lose their job or that institution is likely to be closed, usually in order to save money. （通常为节省资金而进行的）解雇，撤销，关闭 [journalism]

  • St Bartholomew's is one of four London hospitals facing the axe.

    圣巴塞洛缪医院是伦敦4个面临关闭的医院之一。

4 PHRASE 有私心；有个人打算；另有企图 If someone has an axe to grind, they are doing something for selfish reasons. 有私心；有个人打算；另有企图 [informal, disapproval]

  • He seems like a decent bloke and I've got no axe to grind with him. [+ with]

    他看上去像是个正派的家伙，而且我对他没有任何企图。
