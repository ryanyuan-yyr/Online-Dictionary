precursor
priːkɜːʳsəʳ prɪˈkɜːsə priˈkɜrsər prɪˈkɜrsər ˈpriˌkɜrsər
Word forms: plural precursors
N-COUNT 先驱；先锋；前身 A precursor of something is a similar thing that happened or existed before it, often something which led to the existence or development of that thing. 先驱；先锋；前身

  • Real tennis, an ancient precursor of the modern game, originated in the eleventh century.

    室内网球，现代网球运动的古老前身，起源于11世纪。

  • He said that the deal should not be seen as a precursor to a merger. [+ to]

    他说该笔交易不应该被看作是合并的前兆。

SYN forerunner, pioneer, predecessor, forebear
