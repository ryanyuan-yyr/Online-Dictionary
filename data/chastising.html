chastise
tʃæstaɪz tʃæsˈtaɪz ˈtʃæsˌtaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense chastises, present participle chastising, past tense , past participle chastised
VERB 严厉训斥;责罚 If you chastise someone, you speak to them angrily or punish them for something wrong that they have done. 严厉训斥;责罚 [formal]

  • Thomas Rane chastised Peters for his cruelty. [VERB noun + for]

    托马斯·拉纳严厉训斥彼得斯的残酷行为。

  • The Securities Commission chastised the firm but imposed no fine. [VERB noun]

    证券委员会严厉批评了那家公司，不过没有处以罚款。

  • I just don't want you to chastise yourself. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    我只是不想要你过于自责。
