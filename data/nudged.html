nudge
nʌdʒ nʌdʒ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense nudges, present participle nudging, past tense , past participle nudged
1 VERB (通常指用肘)轻推，轻碰(以引起注意) If you nudge someone, you push them gently, usually with your elbow, in order to draw their attention to something. (通常指用肘)轻推，轻碰(以引起注意)

  • I nudged Stan and pointed again. [VERB noun]

    我轻轻推了推斯坦，又指了一下。

  • 'Stop it,' he said, and nudged the boy lightly with his knee. [VERB noun]

    “别这么干了，”他用膝盖轻轻碰了碰那个男孩说道。

SYN push, touch, dig, jog  
Nudge is also a noun.

  • She slipped her arm under his and gave him a nudge.

    她挽住他的胳膊，轻轻推了他一下。

2 VERB 轻轻地推动(或推向) If you nudge someone or something into a place or position, you gently push them there. 轻轻地推动(或推向)

  • Edna Swinson nudged him into the sitting room. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    埃德娜·斯温森把他推进了起居室。

  • The civil servant nudged him forward. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    那个文职人员向前推了他一把。

Nudge is also a noun.

  • McKinnon gave the wheel another slight nudge.

    麦金农又轻轻推了一下轮子。

3 VERB 劝说;鼓动 If you nudge someone into doing something, you gently persuade them to do it. 劝说;鼓动

  • Bit by bit Bob had nudged Fritz into selling his controlling interest. [VERB noun + into]

    鲍勃一步一步劝说弗里茨卖掉了他的控股权益。

  • Foreigners must use their power to nudge the country towards greater tolerance. [V n + towards]

    外国人必须动用他们的力量促使这个国家变得更加宽容。

  • British tour companies are nudging clients to travel further afield. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    英国旅游公司正鼓动客户到更远的地方去旅游。

SYN prompt, influence, urge, persuade  
Nudge is also a noun.

  • I had a feeling that the challenge appealed to him. All he needed was a nudge.

    我感觉这个挑战对他颇具吸引力，他所需要的就是一点推动。

4 VERB [usually cont] 几乎达到;接近 If someone or something is nudging a particular amount, level, or state, they have almost reached it. 几乎达到;接近

  • ...a little-known stage play writer and actress who was nudging 40 and going nowhere. [VERB noun]

    一个年近四旬、一事无成、寂寂无闻的舞台剧作者兼演员

  • The temperature when we were there was nudging 80 degree F. [VERB noun]

    我们在的时候，那里温度接近80华氏度。

5 PHRASE [oft PHR n] 性关系;(尤指)性丑闻 If you refer to a nudge and a wink or to nudge-nudge wink-wink, you mean that someone is referring to sex, especially immoral sex, in an indirect way. 性关系;(尤指)性丑闻 [mainly British, journalism]

  • The article then listed a series of nudge-nudge, wink-wink rumors that have appeared in newspapers over the last two years.

    文章接下来列举了过去两年间报纸上出现过的一连串性丑闻传言。
