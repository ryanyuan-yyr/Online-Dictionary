bring out
1 PHRASAL VERB 生产（新产品）；（尤指）出版，推出（图书或CD） When a person or company brings out a new product, especially a new book or CD, they produce it and put it on sale. 生产（新产品）；（尤指）出版，推出（图书或CD） [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • A journalist all his life, he's now brought out a book. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他当了一辈子新闻记者，如今已出了一本书。

SYN publish, issue, produce, print  
2 PHRASAL VERB 使（某种通常不易显露的行为或情感）显现；激发 Something that brings out a particular kind of behaviour or feeling in you causes you to show it, especially when it is something you do not normally show. 使（某种通常不易显露的行为或情感）显现；激发 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • He is totally dedicated and brings out the best in his pupils. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他全身心地投入，最大限度地激发学生的潜能。
