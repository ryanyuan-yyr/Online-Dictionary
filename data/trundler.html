trundle
trʌndəl ˈtrʌndəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense trundles, present participle trundling, past tense , past participle trundled
1 VERB (车辆)缓缓行进，艰难移动，曲折开进 If a vehicle trundles somewhere, it moves there slowly, often with difficulty or an irregular movement. (车辆)缓缓行进，艰难移动，曲折开进

  • The truck was trundling along the escarpment of the Zambesi valley… [VERB prep./adv.]

    卡车沿着赞比西山谷的绝壁缓缓行进。

  • A few horse-drawn carts still trundle through the dilapidated mining villages… [VERB prep./adv.]

    几架马车仍然在破落的矿村中曲折行进。

  • The train eventually trundled in at 7.54. [VERB prep./adv.]

    火车最终在 7 点 54 分缓缓到站。

2 VERB 慢慢移动，缓慢滚动(尤指带轮的小体积重物) If you trundle something somewhere, especially a small, heavy object with wheels, you move or roll it along slowly. 慢慢移动，缓慢滚动(尤指带轮的小体积重物)

  • The old man lifted the barrow and trundled it away. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    那个老头提起手推车，慢慢推走了。

  • They trundled his mowers outside and dumped them. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    他们把他的除草机全都推到外面丢掉了。

3 VERB 慢步走;迈着沉重的脚步走 If you say that someone is trundling somewhere, you mean that they are walking slowly, often in a tired way or with heavy steps. 慢步走;迈着沉重的脚步走

  • Girls trundle in carrying heavy book bags. [VERB adv./prep.]

    背着沉重书包的女孩们慢慢走进来。
