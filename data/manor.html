manor
mænəʳ ˈmænər
Word forms: plural manors
1 N-COUNT [oft in names] （通常指中世纪）庄园，领地 A manor is a large private house in the country, usually built in the Middle Ages, and also includes the land and smaller buildings around it. （通常指中世纪）庄园，领地 [British]

  • Thieves broke into the manor at night.

    小偷晚上闯进了庄园。

  • ...Bawdsey Manor on the Suffolk coast.

    萨福克郡海岸边的鲍德西庄园

SYN manor house, seat, hall, mansion  
2 N-SING [usually poss NOUN] 警区;警察管辖区 Some people, especially police officers, refer to the area where they work as their manor . 警区;警察管辖区 [British, informal]

  • The Chief Constable deeply resented any intrusions into his manor.

    警察局局长对侵入其辖区的行为深恶痛绝。

  • Thank God they're not on my manor any more.

    谢天谢地，他们再也不在我的辖区里了。
