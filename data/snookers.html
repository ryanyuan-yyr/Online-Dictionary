snooker
snuːkəʳ snʊk- ˈsnʊkər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense snookers, present participle snookering, past tense , past participle snookered
1 N-UNCOUNT 斯诺克 Snooker is a game involving balls on a large table. The players use a long stick to hit a white ball, and score points by knocking coloured balls into the pockets at the sides of the table. 斯诺克

  • ...a game of snooker.

    一局斯诺克比赛

  • They were playing snooker.

    他们在打斯诺克。

2 VERB [usually passive] 阻止…采取行动;使…不如愿 If you are snookered by something, it is difficult or impossible for you to take action or do what you want to do. 阻止…采取行动;使…不如愿 [British, informal]

  • The President has been snookered on this issue. [be VERB-ed]

    在这个问题上，总统未能如愿。
