warn
wɔːʳn wɔrn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense warns, present participle warning, past tense , past participle warned
1 VERB 提醒;警告 If you warn someone about something such as a possible danger or problem, you tell them about it so that they are aware of it. 提醒;警告

  • When I had my first baby friends warned me that children were expensive. [VERB noun that]

    当我有了第一个孩子时，朋友们提醒我说养孩子是很花钱的。

  • They warned him of the dangers of sailing alone. [V n + of/about]

    他们警告他说独自航行危险重重。

  • Analysts warned that Europe's most powerful economy may be facing trouble. [VERB that]

    分析家警告说，欧洲最强大的经济体可能将面临困境。

  • He also warned of a possible anti-Western backlash. [VERB + of]

    他还警告有可能会出现对西方的强烈抵制。

SYN notify, tell, remind, inform  
2 VERB 警告，告诫(不要做…) If you warn someone not to do something, you advise them not to do it so that they can avoid possible danger or punishment. →see usage note at: avoid 警告，告诫(不要做…)

  • Mrs. Blount warned me not to interfere. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    布朗特夫人警告我不要插手。

  • Children must be warned to stay away from main roads. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    必须警告孩子们远离交通要道。

  • 'Don't do anything yet,' he warned. 'Too risky.'. [VERB with quote]

    “暂时不要采取行动，”他警告说，“太危险。”

  • 'Keep quiet, or they'll all come out,' they warned him. [VERB noun with quote]

    “别作声，否则他们全都出来了，”他们警告他。

  • I wish I'd listened to the people who warned me against having the operation. [VERB noun + against]

    我真希望自己当时听从了别人的劝告，没去做手术。

  • Mr Lowe warned against complacency. [V against n/-ing]

    洛先生告诫说不能自满。

SYN advise, urge, recommend, counsel  
3 CONVENTION 小心;提防 If someone says to you 'be warned', they are advising you to be cautious, because there are risks that you may not have thought about. 小心;提防

  • But be warned: this is not a cheap option.

    但要慎重：这个选择代价不菲。

Phrasal verbs:
warn away warn off
