grubby
grʌbi ˈgrʌbi
Word forms: comparative grubbier, superlative grubbiest
1 ADJ-GRADED 肮脏的；污秽的 A grubby person or object is rather dirty. 肮脏的；污秽的

  • His white coat was grubby and stained.

    他的白色外套污迹斑斑，简直脏透了。

  • ...kids with grubby faces.

    脸上脏兮兮的孩子

SYN dirty, soiled, filthy, squalid  
2 ADJ-GRADED 卑鄙的；可鄙的 If you call an activity or someone's behaviour grubby, you mean that it is not completely honest or respectable. 卑鄙的；可鄙的 [disapproval]

  • ...the grubby business of politics.

    肮脏的政治活动
