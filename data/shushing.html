shush
ʃʊʃ ʃʌʃ ʃʌʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense shushes, present participle shushing, past tense , past participle shushed
1 CONVENTION (要某人安静)嘘，别出声 You say shush when you are telling someone to be quiet. (要某人安静)嘘，别出声

  • Shush! Here he comes. I'll talk to you later.

    嘘！他来了。我一会儿再跟你说。

2 VERB (通过嘘声等)使安静 If you shush someone, you tell them to be quiet by saying 'shush' or 'sh', or by indicating in some other way that you want them to be quiet. (通过嘘声等)使安静 [Also VERB]

  • Frannie shushed her with a forefinger to the lips. [VERB noun]

    弗兰妮把食指放在嘴唇上让她安静。
