ruck
rʌk rʌk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense rucks, present participle rucking, past tense , past participle rucked
1 N-COUNT 群架;纷争 A ruck is a situation where a group of people are fighting or struggling. 群架;纷争 [British]

  • There'll be a huge ruck with the cops as they try to take photographs.

    他们试图拍照时将会和警察发生大规模的肢体冲突。

  • Ministers will find their budgets being decided in a political ruck with their colleagues.

    部长们将发现他们的预算会在和同僚的政治纷争中敲定。

SYN battle, fight, conflict, clash  
2 N-COUNT (橄榄球赛中的)自由密集争球 In the sport of rugby, a ruck is a situation where a group of players struggle for possession of the ball. (橄榄球赛中的)自由密集争球
3 N-COUNT (衣服的)皱，褶 A ruck is a fold or crease in cloth or clothing. (衣服的)皱，褶

  • ...a small ruck in the carpet.

    地毯上的小褶皱
