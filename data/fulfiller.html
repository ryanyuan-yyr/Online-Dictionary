fulfil
fʊlfɪl
(also fulfill)Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense fulfils, 3rd person singular present tense fulfills, present participle fulfilling, past tense , past participle fulfilled
1 VERB 履行;实现;使应验 If you fulfil something such as a promise, dream, or hope, you do what you said or hoped you would do. 履行;实现;使应验

  • President Kaunda fulfilled his promise of announcing a date for the referendum. [VERB noun]

    卡翁达总统兑现了他的承诺，宣布了全民公决的日期。

2 VERB 实行;执行;完成 To fulfil a task, role, or requirement means to do or be what is required, necessary, or expected. 实行;执行;完成

  • Without them you will not be able to fulfil the tasks you have before you. [VERB noun]

    没有他们你将无法完成面临的任务。

  • All the necessary conditions were fulfilled. [be VERB-ed]

    所有必要的条件都满足了。

SYN comply with, meet, fill, satisfy  
3 VERB 满足;使满意;使有成就感 If something fulfils you, or if you fulfil yourself, you feel happy and satisfied with what you are doing or with what you have achieved. 满足;使满意;使有成就感

  • The war was the biggest thing in her life and nothing after that quite fulfilled her. [VERB noun]

    这场战争是她生命中最重大的事件，从那以后再没有什么让她特别有成就感的了。

  • They don't like the idea that women can fulfil themselves without the assistance of a man. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    他们不喜欢那种女人没有男人的帮助仍会有所成就的想法。

SYN satisfy, please, content, cheer
