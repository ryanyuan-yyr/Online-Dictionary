estate
ɪsteɪt ɪˈsteɪt əˈsteɪt ɪˈsteɪt
Word forms: plural estates
1 N-COUNT 庄园;地产；（在乡村的）大片私有土地 An estate is a large area of land in the country which is owned by a person, family, or organization. 庄园;地产；（在乡村的）大片私有土地

  • ...a shooting party on Lord Wyville's estate in Yorkshire.

    在怀维尔勋爵位于约克郡的庄园里举行的狩猎会

SYN lands, property, area, grounds  
2 N-COUNT 住宅区;工业区 People sometimes use estate to refer to a housing estate or an industrial estate. 住宅区;工业区 [British]

  • He used to live on the estate.

    他过去曾住在那个住宅区。

3 N-COUNT [oft poss NOUN] 遗产 Someone's estate is all the money and property that they leave behind them when they die. 遗产 [law]

  • His estate was valued at $150,000.

    他的遗产被估价为15万美元。

SYN property, capital, assets, fortune  
4 N-COUNT 同estate car An estate is the same as an estate car . 同estate car [British]
in AM, use 美国英语用 station wagon
