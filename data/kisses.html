kiss
kɪs kɪs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense kisses, present participle kissing, past tense , past participle kissed
1 V-RECIP 吻；亲吻 If you kiss someone, you touch them with your lips to show affection or sexual desire, or to greet them or say goodbye. 吻；亲吻

  • She leaned up and kissed him on the cheek. [VERB noun + on]

    她倾身向前亲吻了他的面颊。

  • She kissed me hard on the mouth. [NON-RECIP: V n]

    她深深地吻了我的嘴。

  • Her parents kissed her goodbye as she set off from their home. [VERB noun noun]

    她动身离开父母家时父母和她吻别。

  • They kissed for almost half a minute. [VERB]

    他们吻了将近半分钟。

  • We kissed goodbye. [VERB noun]

    我们吻别了。

SYN peck [informal] , osculate, snog [British , slang] , neck [informal]  
Kiss is also a noun.

  • I put my arms around her and gave her a kiss.

    我抱着她吻了她一下。

  • We gave each other hugs and kisses every morning.

    我们每天早晨都拥抱亲吻对方。

2 VERB 轻吻（以示尊敬） If you kiss something, you touch it lightly with your lips, usually as a sign of respect. 轻吻（以示尊敬）

  • The men stepped forward to kiss the hand of their mentor. [VERB noun]

    这些男子走上前去轻吻他们导师的手。

  • She bowed her head and kissed the Archbishop's ring. [VERB noun]

    她低下头轻吻大主教的戒指。

3 VERB 轻拂；轻触 If you say that something kisses another thing, you mean that it touches that thing very gently. 轻拂；轻触

  • The wheels of the aircraft kissed the runway. [VERB noun]

    飞机的轮子轻触跑道。

SYN brush, touch, shave, scrape  
4 PHRASE 飞吻 If you blow someone a kiss or blow a kiss, you touch the palm of your hand lightly with your lips, and then blow across your hand towards the person, in order to show them your affection. 飞吻

  • Maria blew him a kiss.

    玛丽亚给了他一个飞吻。

  • Amy blew a kiss from the door.

    埃米在门口做了个飞吻的动作。

5 PHRASE 任其失去；放弃；承认对…无能为力 If you say that you kiss something goodbye or kiss goodbye to something, you accept the fact that you are going to lose it, although you do not want to. 任其失去；放弃；承认对…无能为力 [informal]

  • I felt sure I'd have to kiss my dancing career goodbye.

    我明确感觉到我将不得不与我的舞蹈生涯说再见了。
