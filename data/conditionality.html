conditional
kəndɪʃənəl kənˈdɪʃənəl kənˈdɪʃənəl
1 ADJ 有条件的;有前提的 If a situation or agreement is conditional on something, it will only happen or continue if this thing happens. 有条件的;有前提的

  • Their support is conditional on his proposals meeting their approval. [+ on]

    他们的支持是以他的提案得到他们的批准为条件的。

  • ...a conditional offer.

    有条件要约

  • ...a conditional ceasefire.

    有条件停火

SYN dependent, limited, qualified, contingent  
conditionally

  • Mr Smith has conditionally agreed to buy a shareholding in the club.

    史密斯先生有条件地同意购买这家俱乐部的股份。

2 ADJ [ADJ n] (从句)条件的 In grammar, a conditional clause is a subordinate clause which refers to a situation which may exist or whose possible consequences you are considering. Most conditional clauses begin with 'if' or 'unless', for example 'If that happens, we'll be in big trouble' and 'You don't have to come unless you want to'. (从句)条件的
