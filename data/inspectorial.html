inspector
ɪnspektəʳ ɪnˈspɛktər
Word forms: plural inspectors
1 N-COUNT 检查员;视察员;督察员 An inspector is a person, usually employed by a government agency, whose job is to find out whether people are obeying official regulations. 检查员;视察员;督察员

  • The mill was finally shut down by state safety inspectors.

    工厂终于遭国家安全检查员关闭了。

SYN examiner, investigator, supervisor, monitor  
2 N-COUNT; N-TITLE; N-VOC (英国警察中职位低于警司高于巡佐的)巡官,督察 In Britain, an inspector is an officer in the police who is higher in rank than a sergeant and lower in rank than a superintendent. (英国警察中职位低于警司高于巡佐的)巡官,督察

  • Last week gunmen attacked Mogadishu airport killing a police inspector.

    上周持枪歹徒袭击了摩加迪沙机场，杀死了一名警察巡官。

  • I got on the phone to Inspector Joplin at Scotland Yard.

    我与伦敦警察厅的乔普林巡官通过电话了。

3 N-COUNT; N-TITLE; N-VOC (美国警察中仅次于总警监的)督察长 In the United States, an inspector is an officer in the police who is next in rank to a superintendent or police chief. (美国警察中仅次于总警监的)督察长

  • ...San Francisco police inspector Tony Camileri.

    旧金山警察局督察长托尼·卡米莱里
