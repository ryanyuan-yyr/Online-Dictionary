dying
daɪɪŋ ˈdaɪɪŋ
1 Dying is the present participle of die .
2 ADJ [ADJ n] 垂死的;濒死的;临死的 A dying person or animal is very ill and likely to die soon. 垂死的;濒死的;临死的

  • ...a dying man.

    垂死的男子

SYN near death, going, failing, fading  
The dying are people who are dying.

  • The dead and the dying were everywhere.

    到处都是死尸和奄奄一息的人。

3 ADJ [ADJ n] 临终的;垂死的 You use dying to describe something which happens at the time when someone dies, or is connected with that time. 临终的;垂死的

  • It'll stay in my mind till my dying day.

    它将长存我心，直到生命的终点。

  • She was compelled to fulfil the dying wishes of her mother.

    她不得不去实现她母亲的临终遗愿。

SYN final, last, parting, departing  
4 ADJ [ADJ n] 行将结束的;最后时刻的;末期的 The dying days or dying minutes of a state of affairs or an activity are its last days or minutes. 行将结束的;最后时刻的;末期的

  • The islands were seized by the Soviet army in the dying days of the second world war.

    这些岛屿是在二战末期被苏联军队占领的。

  • A penalty by Thierry Lacroix broke the deadlock in the dying minutes of the game.

    蒂里·拉克鲁瓦的点球在比赛结束前的最后时刻打破了僵局。

5 ADJ [ADJ n] 行将消亡的;没落的 A dying tradition or industry is becoming less important and is likely to disappear completely. 行将消亡的;没落的

  • Shipbuilding is a dying business.

    造船业是夕阳产业。

SYN failing, declining, sinking, foundering  
6 ADJ [ADJ n] (火焰)行将熄灭的 A dying fire is no longer hot and bright and will not burn for much longer. (火焰)行将熄灭的
