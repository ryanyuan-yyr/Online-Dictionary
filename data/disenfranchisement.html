disenfranchise
dɪsɪnfræntʃaɪz ˌdɪsɪnˈfrænˌtʃaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense disenfranchises, present participle disenfranchising, past tense , past participle disenfranchised
VERB 剥夺…的选举权 To disenfranchise a group of people means to take away their right to vote, or their right to vote for what they really want. 剥夺…的选举权

  • ...fears of an organized attempt to disenfranchise supporters of Father Aristide. [VERB noun]

    对剥夺阿里斯蒂德神父支持者选举权这一有组织企图的担心

  • ...the helplessness of disenfranchised minorities. [VERB-ed]

    被剥夺了选举权的少数民族的无助
