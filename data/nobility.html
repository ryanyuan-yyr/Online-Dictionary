nobility
noʊbɪlɪti noʊˈbɪləti
1 N-SING-COLL [with singular or plural verb] 贵族(阶层) The nobility of a society are all the people who have titles and belong to a high social class. 贵族(阶层)

  • They married into the nobility and entered the highest ranks of state administration.

    他们同贵族阶层联姻，进入了国家最高行政层。

  • Despite its lack of formal power the nobility was not powerless.

    尽管没有正式的权力，贵族阶层并非没有权势。

SYN aristocracy, lords, elite, nobles  
2 N-UNCOUNT 高尚的品质;高贵的举止 A person's nobility is their noble character and behaviour. 高尚的品质;高贵的举止 [formal]

  • ...his nobility of character, and his devotion to his country. [+ of]

    他的高尚品格和对祖国的忠诚

Quotations
New nobility is but the act of power, but ancient nobility is the act of timeFrancis Bacon Essays
