reverberate
rɪvɜːʳbəreɪt rɪˈvɜrbəˌreɪt rɪˈvɜrbərɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense reverberates, present participle reverberating, past tense , past participle reverberated
1 VERB 发出回声;回响;回荡 When a loud sound reverberates through a place, it echoes through it. 发出回声;回响;回荡

  • The sound of the tank guns reverberated through the little Bavarian town. [VERB prep.]

    坦克炮声在巴伐利亚小镇回荡。

  • A woman's shrill laughter reverberated in the courtyard. [VERB]

    一个女人的尖笑声在院子里回荡。

SYN echo, ring, resound, vibrate  
2 VERB (事情、思想等)产生巨大反响，引起轰动，影响深远 You can say that an event or idea reverberates when it has a powerful effect which lasts a long time. (事情、思想等)产生巨大反响，引起轰动，影响深远

  • The controversy surrounding the take-over yesterday continued to reverberate around the television industry. [VERB prep.]

    昨天围绕接管展开的争论在电视业仍然有巨大的反响。

  • The news sent shock waves through the community that have continued to reverberate to this day. [VERB]

    这则消息震惊了整个社区，直到现在还波澜未息。
