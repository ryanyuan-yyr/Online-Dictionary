ambiguity
æmbɪgjuːɪti ˌæmbɪˈɡjuːɪtɪ ˌæmbəˈgjuəti
Word forms: plural ambiguities
1 N-VAR 含糊不清；不明确；模棱两可 If you say that there is ambiguity in something, you mean that it is unclear or confusing, or it can be understood in more than one way. 含糊不清；不明确；模棱两可

  • There is considerable ambiguity about what this part of the agreement actually means. [+ about]

    协议这一部分的真正含义很不明确。

  • ...the ambiguities of language.

    语言的模棱两可

2 N-VAR 暧昧;不协调 If you say that there is an ambiguity in a situation or in someone's character, you mean that it contains several different qualities or attitudes which do not fit well together. 暧昧;不协调

  • The author's style suggests a certain ambiguity in his moral view.

    该作家的风格表明其道德观有点暧昧不明。
