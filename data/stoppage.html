stoppage
stɒpɪdʒ ˈstɑpɪdʒ
Word forms: plural stoppages
1 N-COUNT 停工;罢工 When there is a stoppage, people stop working because of a disagreement with their employers. 停工;罢工 [business]

  • Mineworkers in the Ukraine have voted for a one-day stoppage next month.

    乌克兰矿工们已投票决定下个月罢工一天。

SYN strike, industrial action, walkout, closure  
2 N-COUNT (足球等体育运动中的)中断比赛，暂停 In football and some other sports, when there is a stoppage, the game stops for a short time, for example because a player is injured. The referee may add some extra time at the end of the game because of this. (足球等体育运动中的)中断比赛，暂停 [mainly British]
