widen
waɪdən ˈwaɪdən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense widens, present participle widening, past tense , past participle widened
1 V-ERG （使）变宽;（把…）加宽 If you widen something or if it widens, it becomes greater in measurement from one side or edge to the other. （使）变宽;（把…）加宽

  • He had an operation last year to widen a heart artery. [VERB noun]

    去年他接受了一个扩张心脏动脉的手术。

  • The river widens considerably as it begins to turn east. [VERB]

    河流转向东流时河道大幅度变宽。

SYN get wider, spread, extend, expand  
widening

  • They have ordered the widening of the road where the incident took place.

    他们已下令拓宽出事路段。

2 V-ERG (使)扩大;(使)扩展;(使)增加 If you widen something or if it widens, it becomes greater in range or it affects a larger number of people or things. (使)扩大;(使)扩展;(使)增加

  • U.S. prosecutors have widened a securities-fraud investigation. [VERB noun]

    美国检察官扩大了对一起证券欺诈案的调查范围。

  • The search for my brother widened. [VERB]

    对我弟弟的搜索范围扩大了。

  • Newspapers enjoyed a widening circle of readers. [VERB-ing]

    报纸拥有越来越广泛的读者群。

3 VERB (眼睛)睁大 If your eyes widen, they open more. (眼睛)睁大

  • His eyes widened as he spoke the words. [VERB]

    他说这番话时眼睛瞪大了。

4 V-ERG (使)(差距)拉大 If a difference or gap widens or if something widens it, it becomes greater. (使)(差距)拉大

  • Wage differences in the two areas are widening. [VERB]

    两个地区的工资差距正在拉大。

  • The US trade deficit widened to $7.59 billion in November.

    11月美国的贸易赤字扩大到75.9亿美元。

  • ...policies that widen the gap between the rich and the poor. [VERB noun]

    拉大贫富差距的政策
