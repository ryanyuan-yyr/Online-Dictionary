lashing
læʃɪŋ ˈlæʃɪŋ
Word forms: plural lashings
1 QUANT 大量；许多 Lashings of something means a large quantity or amount of it. 大量；许多 [mainly British, informal]

  • Serve by cutting the scones in half and spreading with jam and lashings of clotted cream. [+ of]

    吃时将司康饼切半，抹上果酱和大量凝脂奶油。

  • ...lashings of slapstick comedy.

    连场粗俗闹剧

2 N-COUNT [usually plural] 捆绑用的绳子；系索 Lashings are ropes or cables that are used to tie one thing to another. 捆绑用的绳子；系索

  • We made a tour of the yacht, checking lashings and emergency gear.

    我们在那艘游艇上四处看了看，检查了它的系索和应急装置。

3 N-COUNT 猛烈抨击；严厉批评 If you refer to someone's comments as a lashing, you mean that they are very critical and angry. 猛烈抨击；严厉批评

  • He never grew used to the lashings he got from the critics.

    他始终无法忍受评论界对他的刻薄抨击。

4 N-COUNT 笞刑；鞭打 A lashing is a punishment in which a person is hit with a whip. 笞刑；鞭打
