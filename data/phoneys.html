phoney
foʊni ˈfoʊni ˈfəʊnɪ
(also phony)Word forms: plural phoneys
1 ADJ-GRADED 伪造的；假的 If you describe something as phoney, you disapprove of it because it is false rather than genuine. 伪造的；假的 [informal, disapproval]

  • He'd telephoned with some phoney excuse she didn't believe for a minute.

    他找了某个她根本不会相信的借口给她打电话。

  • He didn't really have that moustache. It was phoney.

    他并没有真的长胡子，那是假的。

  • He used a phoney accent.

    他的口音是装出来的。

SYN fake, affected, assumed, trick  
2 ADJ-GRADED 虚伪的；做作的 If you say that someone is phoney, you disapprove of them because they are pretending to be someone that they are not in order to deceive people. 虚伪的；做作的 [informal, disapproval]

  • He looks totally phoney to me.

    在我看来，他非常虚伪。

  • ...phoney 'experts'.

    伪专家

SYN bogus, false, fake, pseudo [informal]  
Phoney is also a noun.

  • 'He's false, a phoney,' Harry muttered.

    “他是冒充的，是个骗子，”哈里咕哝道。
