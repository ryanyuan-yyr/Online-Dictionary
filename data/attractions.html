attraction
ətrækʃən əˈtrækʃən
Word forms: plural attractions
1 N-UNCOUNT 爱慕;喜欢;性吸引 Attraction is a feeling of liking someone, and often of being sexually interested in them. 爱慕;喜欢;性吸引

  • Our level of attraction to the opposite sex has more to do with our inner confidence than how we look.

    我们对异性的吸引程度更多的来自内在的自信，而不是长相。

  • His love for her was not just physical attraction.

    他对她的爱慕不只是肉体的吸引。

2 N-COUNT 吸引力；诱惑力 An attraction is a feature which makes something interesting or desirable. 吸引力；诱惑力

  • ...the attractions of living on the waterfront. [+ of]

    令人神往的滨水生活

3 N-COUNT 有吸引力的地方（或事物） An attraction is something that people can go to for interest or enjoyment, for example a famous building. 有吸引力的地方（或事物）

  • The walled city is an important tourist attraction.

    这座由城墙围绕的城市是个重要的旅游胜地。

SYN entertainment, feature, delight, distraction
