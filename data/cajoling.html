cajole
kədʒoʊl kəˈdʒoʊl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense cajoles, present participle cajoling, past tense , past participle cajoled
VERB 哄骗;劝诱;说服 If you cajole someone into doing something, you get them to do it after persuading them for some time. 哄骗;劝诱;说服

  • It was he who had cajoled Garland into doing the film. [VERB noun + into]

    是他劝服嘉兰拍这部片子的。

  • He cajoled Mr Dobson to stand for mayor. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    他说服多布森先生竞选市长。

SYN persuade, tempt, lure, flatter
