ascertain
æsəʳteɪn ˌæsərˈteɪn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense ascertains, present participle ascertaining, past tense , past participle ascertained
1 VERB （尤指经过努力后）查明，弄清，确定 If you ascertain the truth about something, you find out what it is, especially by making a deliberate effort to do so. （尤指经过努力后）查明，弄清，确定 [formal]

  • Through doing this, the teacher will be able to ascertain the extent to which the child understands what he is reading. [VERB noun]

    这样一来，老师就能确定孩子们对他所读的内容理解多少了。

  • Once they had ascertained that he was not a spy, they agreed to release him. [VERB that]

    一旦确认他并非间谍，他们即同意将他释放。

  • Take time to ascertain what services your bank is providing, and at what cost. [VERB wh]

    花些时间确认一下你的银行会提供什么样的服务，费用是多少。

SYN find out, learn, discover, determine
