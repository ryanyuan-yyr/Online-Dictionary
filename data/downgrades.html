downgrade
daʊngreɪd ˈdaʊnˌgreɪd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense downgrades, present participle downgrading, past tense , past participle downgraded
1 VERB [usually passive] 贬低;降低;低估 If something is downgraded, it is given less importance than it used to have or than you think it should have. 贬低;降低;低估

  • The boy's condition has been downgraded from critical to serious. [be VERB-ed]

    男孩已脱离生命危险，但病情仍很严重。

  • The female role has been downgraded altogether in the drive for greater equality. [be VERB-ed]

    在争取更多平等的运动中，女性角色完全被贬低了。

2 VERB 使降职;使降级 If someone is downgraded, their job or status is changed so that they become less important or receive less money. 使降职;使降级

  • There was no criticism of her work until after she was downgraded. [be VERB-ed]

    在被降职前她的工作从未受过批评。

  • His superiors suspended him, and then downgraded him. [VERB noun]

    他的上司先将他暂时停职，随后把他降职。

SYN demote, degrade, take down a peg [informal] , lower or reduce in rank
