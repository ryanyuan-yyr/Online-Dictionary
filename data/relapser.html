relapse
rɪlæps riːlæps rɪˈlæps ˈrilæps
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense relapses, present participle relapsing, past tense , past participle relapsed
The noun can be pronounced /rɪ'læps/ or /'riːlæps/. 名词可读作/rɪ'læps/或/'riːlæps/。
1 VERB 故态复萌;重新陷入;倒退 If you say that someone relapses into a way of behaving that is undesirable, you mean that they start to behave in that way again. 故态复萌;重新陷入;倒退

  • 'I wish I did,' said Phil Jordan, relapsing into his usual gloom. [VERB + into]

    “我要是那样做了就好了，”菲尔·乔丹说道，又像往常一样消沉起来。

  • It is by no means clear that the financial markets will not relapse into panic. [VERB into noun]

    现在还不能确定金融市场不会重新陷入恐慌。

Relapse is also a noun.

  • ...a relapse into the nationalism of the nineteenth century.

    倒退回19世纪的民族主义

2 VERB 旧病复发;再次发病 If a sick person relapses, their health suddenly gets worse after it had been improving. 旧病复发;再次发病

  • In 90 per cent of cases the patient will relapse within six months. [VERB]

    90%的病人在6个月内会再次发病。

SYN worsen, deteriorate, sicken, weaken  
Relapse is also a noun.

  • The treatment is usually given to women with a high risk of relapse after surgery.

    这种治疗方法通常针对术后复发风险高的女性。

  • The sufferer can experience frequent relapses.

    患者可能会频繁发病。
