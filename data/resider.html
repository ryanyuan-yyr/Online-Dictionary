reside
rɪzaɪd rɪˈzaɪd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense resides, present participle residing, past tense , past participle resided
1 VERB 居住;定居 If someone resides somewhere, they live there or are staying there. 居住;定居 [formal]

  • Margaret resides with her invalid mother in a London suburb. [VERB prep./adv.]

    玛格丽特同她病弱的母亲住在伦敦郊区。

  • All single full-time students must reside in university residence halls. [VERB prep./adv.]

    所有单身的全日制学生都必须住大学宿舍。

SYN live, lodge, dwell [formal , literary] , have your home  
2 VERB [no cont] 存在于;属于 If a quality resides in something, the thing has that quality. 存在于;属于 [formal]

  • Happiness does not reside in strength or money. [VERB + in]

    幸福不是由实力或金钱决定的。

  • The military is based on the principle that the ultimate authority resides in the armed forces. [VERB in noun]

    军队建立的基本原则就是最高权力属于武装部队。

SYN be present, lie, exist, consist
