glut
glʌt glʌt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense gluts, present participle glutting, past tense , past participle glutted
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 供过于求；供应过剩 If there is a glut of something, there is so much of it that it cannot all be sold or used. 供过于求；供应过剩

  • There's a glut of agricultural products in Western Europe.

    西欧的农产品供过于求。

  • ...a world oil glut.

    全球石油供应过剩

SYN surfeit, excess, surplus, plethora  
2 VERB 充斥；使供应过剩 If a market is glutted with something, there is a glut of that thing. 充斥；使供应过剩 [business]

  • The region is glutted with hospitals. [be VERB-ed + with]

    该地区的医院数量已经饱和。

  • Soldiers returning from the war had glutted the job market. [VERB noun]

    从战场返乡的士兵挤爆了劳动力市场。

SYN saturate, flood, choke, clog
