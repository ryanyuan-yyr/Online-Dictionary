repertoire
repəʳtwɑːʳ ˈrɛpərˌtwɑr ˈrɛpəˌtwɑr
Word forms: plural repertoires
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] （一个演员所能表演的）全部节目，曲目 A per-former's repertoire is all the plays or pieces of music that he or she has learned and can perform. （一个演员所能表演的）全部节目，曲目

  • Meredith D'Ambrosio has thousands of songs in her repertoire.

    梅雷迪思·丹布罗西奥会唱几千首歌曲。

2 N-SING 全部技能（或才能） The repertoire of a person or thing is all the things of a particular kind that the person or thing is capable of doing. 全部技能（或才能）

  • ...Mike's impressive repertoire of funny stories. [+ of]

    迈克会讲大量有趣故事的非凡才能

  • This has been one of the most successful desserts in my repertoire.

    这是我会做的甜点中做得最成功的一种。

SYN range, list, stock, supply  
3 N-SING [with supplement] （某个种类的）全部剧目(或曲目) You can refer to all the plays or music of a particular kind as, for example, the classical repertoire or the jazz repertoire . （某个种类的）全部剧目(或曲目)

  • It is no coincidence that the works in the 'standard repertoire' tend to have names.

    在“标准剧目”中的作品有各自的名字并非偶然。
