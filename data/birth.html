birth
bɜːʳθ bɜrθ
Word forms: plural births
1 N-VAR 出生；降生；出世 When a baby is born, you refer to this event as his or her birth . 出生；降生；出世

  • It was the birth of his grandchildren which gave him greatest pleasure. [+ of]

    孙子孙女的出生给他带来了极大的快乐。

  • She concealed her pregnancy right up to the moment of birth.

    她隐瞒了自己怀孕的事，直到孩子出世。

  • She weighed 5lb 7oz at birth.

    她出生时体重是5磅7盎司。

  • ...premature births.

    早产

SYN childbirth, birthing, delivery, confinement  
2 N-UNCOUNT [with poss] 诞生；产生；出现；起源 You can refer to the beginning or origin of something as its birth . 诞生；产生；出现；起源

  • ...the birth of popular democracy. [+ of]

    人民民主的诞生

SYN beginning, start, rise, source  
3 N-UNCOUNT 出身；家世；门第 Some people talk about a person's birth when they are referring to the social position of the person's family. 出身；家世；门第

  • ...men of low birth.

    出身卑微的人

  • His birth, background and career show that you can make it in this country on merit alone.

    他的出身、背景和职业表明，在这个国家仅凭自己的能力就可以取得成功。

SYN ancestry, line, race, stock  
4 See also: date of birth  home birth 
5 PHRASE 在血统上；生来 If, for example, you are French by birth, you are French because your parents are French, or because you were born in France. 在血统上；生来

  • Sadrudin was an Iranian by birth.

    萨德尔丁是伊朗血统。

6 PHRASE 生（孩子）；分娩；生育 When a woman gives birth, she produces a baby from her body. 生（孩子）；分娩；生育

  • She's just given birth to a baby girl.

    她刚生了一个女孩。

  • She's due to give birth at any moment.

    她随时可能分娩。

SYN deliver, have, mother, produce  
7 PHRASE 使产生；使诞生 To give birth to something such as an idea means to cause it to start to exist. 使产生；使诞生

  • In 1980, strikes at the Lenin Shipyards gave birth to the Solidarity trade union.

    1980年，列宁造船厂的罢工致使团结工会得以成立。

8 PHRASE 出生的（地方） The country, town, or village of your birth is the place where you were born. 出生的（地方）
