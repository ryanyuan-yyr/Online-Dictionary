include
ɪnkluːd ɪnˈklud
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense includes, present participle including, past tense , past participle included
1 VERB 包括;包含 If one thing includes another thing, it has the other thing as one of its parts. 包括;包含

  • A good British breakfast always includes sausages. [VERB noun]

    一顿丰盛的英式早餐总会包括香肠。

  • The trip has been extended to include a few other events. [VERB noun]

    旅程被延长，其他几项活动也被包括进来。

  • The list includes many British internationals. [VERB noun]

    名单中包括了许多英国国际级选手。

SYN contain, involve, incorporate, cover  
2 VERB 把…列为…的一部分;把…算入 If someone or something is included in a large group, system, or area, they become a part of it or are considered a part of it. 把…列为…的一部分;把…算入

  • I had worked hard to be included in a project like this. [be VERB-ed + in]

    为参与这样的项目,我过去一直努力工作。

  • The President is expected to include this idea in his education plan. [VERB noun + in]

    人们期望总统把这一意见列入他的教育计划当中。

SYN count, introduce, make a part of, number among  
+−Usage
Usageinclude
Saying that a group includes one or more people or things implies that the group has additional people or things in it. For instance, the sentence: Cities in Japan include Tokyo and Kyoto implies that Japan has additional cities.
