portray
pɔːʳtreɪ pɔrˈtreɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense portrays, present participle portraying, past tense , past participle portrayed
1 VERB 扮演；饰演 When an actor or actress portrays someone, he or she plays that person in a play or film. 扮演；饰演

  • In 1975 he portrayed the king in a Los Angeles revival of 'Camelot'. [VERB noun]

    他1975年在洛杉矶重新上演的剧目《卡米洛》中扮演国王。

  • ...the busty and rumbustious Mrs Hall, excellently portrayed by Toni Palmer. [VERB-ed]

    由托妮·帕默精彩演绎的胸部丰满、吵吵嚷嚷的霍尔太太

SYN play, take the role of, act the part of, represent  
2 VERB 描绘；描述 When a writer or artist portrays something, he or she writes a description or produces a painting of it. 描绘；描述

  • ...this northern novelist, who accurately portrays provincial domestic life. [VERB noun]

    这位准确描述外省家庭生活的北方小说家

  • ...the landscape as portrayed by painters such as Claude and Poussin. [VERB-ed]

    克劳德和普桑等画家所描绘的风景

SYN represent, draw, paint, illustrate  
3 VERB 表现；刻画 If a film, book, or television programme portrays someone in a certain way, it represents them in that way. 表现；刻画

  • She says the programme portrayed her as a 'lady of easy virtue'. [VERB noun + as]

    她说这个节目把她刻画成了一个“水性杨花的女人”。

  • ...complaints about the way women are portrayed in adverts. [be VERB-ed]

    抗议在广告中如此刻画女性

SYN characterize, describe, represent, depict
