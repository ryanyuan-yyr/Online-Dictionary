dislike
dɪslaɪk dɪsˈlaɪk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense dislikes, present participle disliking, past tense , past participle disliked
1 VERB 不喜欢;厌恶 If you dislike someone or something, you consider them to be unpleasant and do not like them. 不喜欢;厌恶

  • We don't serve liver often because so many people dislike it. [VERB noun]

    我们不常做肝这道菜，因为很多人吃不惯。

  • David began to dislike all his television heroes who smoked. [VERB noun]

    戴维开始讨厌所有他曾喜欢的吸烟的电视偶像了。

SYN hate, object to, loathe, despise  
2 N-UNCOUNT 不喜欢;反感 Dislike is the feeling that you do not like someone or something. 不喜欢;反感 [Also + for]

  • He made no attempt to conceal his dislike of me. [+ of]

    他毫不掩饰对我的厌恶。

  • Years of dislike boiled over and blows were exchanged.

    多年的厌恶之情终于爆发，双方拳脚相加。

3 N-COUNT [usually plural] 厌恶的事物 Your dislikes are the things that you do not like. 厌恶的事物

  • Consider what your likes and dislikes are about your job.

    想想工作中哪些部分是自己喜欢的，哪些是不喜欢的。

  • Strong irrational dislikes of other people can easily be picked up from others.

    对他人无端的厌恶之情别人可以轻易察觉到。

4 PHRASE 厌恶;嫌憎;讨厌 If you take a dislike to someone or something, you decide that you do not like them. 厌恶;嫌憎;讨厌

  • I took a violent dislike to him.

    我非常烦他。

  • He may suddenly take a dislike to foods that he's previously enjoyed.

    对以前爱吃的食物，他兴许突然就没了胃口。
