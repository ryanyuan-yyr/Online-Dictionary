consumption
kənsʌmpʃən kənˈsʌmpʃən
1 N-UNCOUNT (燃料、自然资源的)消耗量，消费量 The consumption of fuel or natural resources is the amount of them that is used or the act of using them. (燃料、自然资源的)消耗量，消费量

  • The laws have led to a reduction in fuel consumption in the US.

    这些法律已经使美国燃料消费量有所减少。

  • ...a tax on the consumption of non-renewable energy resources. [+ of]

    非再生能源消费税

SYN using up, use, loss, waste  
2 N-UNCOUNT 吃;喝;饮用 The consumption of food or drink is the act of eating or drinking something, or the amount that is eaten or drunk. 吃;喝;饮用 [formal]

  • Most of the wine was unfit for human consumption.

    这些酒中大部分都不适于人类饮用。

  • The average daily consumption of fruit and vegetables is around 200 grams. [+ of]

    每日平均果蔬摄入量约为200克。

  • Excessive alcohol consumption is clearly bad.

    过度饮酒显然是有害的。

3 N-UNCOUNT 消费 Consumption is the act of buying and using things. 消费

  • Recycling the waste from our increased consumption is better than burning it.

    对我们增加消费产生的垃圾进行回收要好过将其焚烧。

  • The production and consumption of goods and services is the ultimate aim of all economic endeavour.

    生产并消费商品和服务是一切经济活动的终极目标。

4 PHRASE [usually verb-link PHRASE, PHRASE after verb] 供(某人或某个群体)使用，享用 If you do or say something for a particular person's or group's consumption, you do or say it especially for them, although your private thoughts or plans may be very different. 供(某人或某个群体)使用，享用

  • The hard-line speech appears to be mostly for domestic consumption.

    强硬路线的讲话似乎主要是说给国内人听的。

  • The report was obviously designed for the consumption of members of the War Committee.

    该报告显然是为战争委员会的委员们撰写的。

5 N-UNCOUNT 同 tuberculosis Consumption is the same as tuberculosis . 同 tuberculosis [old-fashioned]
6 See also: conspicuous consumption
