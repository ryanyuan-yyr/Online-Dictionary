founder
faʊndəʳ ˈfaʊndər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense founders, present participle foundering, past tense , past participle foundered
1 N-COUNT [usually with poss] 创办人;创立者;奠基者 The founder of an institution, organization, or building is the person who got it started or caused it to be built, often by providing the necessary money. 创办人;创立者;奠基者

  • He was one of the founders of the university's medical faculty.

    他是该大学医学院的创建人之一。

SYN initiator, father, establisher, author  
2 VERB 失败;崩溃;垮掉 If something such as a plan or project founders, it fails because of a particular point, difficulty, or problem. 失败;崩溃;垮掉

  • The talks have foundered, largely because of the reluctance of some members of the government to do a deal with criminals. [VERB]

    对话已经破裂了，原因主要是因为一些政府成员不愿意和犯罪分子达成协议。

3 VERB (船只)沉没 If a ship founders, it fills with water and sinks. (船只)沉没

  • Three ships foundered in heavy seas. [VERB]

    三艘船在波涛汹涌的海面上沉没了。

SYN sink, go down, be lost, submerge
