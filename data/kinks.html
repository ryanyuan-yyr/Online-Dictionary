kink
kɪŋk kɪŋk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense kinks, present participle kinking, past tense , past participle kinked
1 N-COUNT （直线物体上的）弯，结 A kink is a curve or twist in something which is otherwise or normally straight. （直线物体上的）弯，结

  • ...a tiny black kitten with tufted ears and a kink in her tail. [+ in]

    长着毛茸茸的耳朵、尾巴卷曲的小黑猫

SYN twist, bend, wrinkle, knot  
2 V-ERG （使）弯曲；（使）扭结；（使）绞缠 If something kinks or is kinked, it has, or it develops a curve or twist in it. （使）弯曲；（使）扭结；（使）绞缠

  • ...her wet hair kinking in the breeze. [VERB]

    她那在微风中缠结的湿发

  • Care is needed when loading the roll to prevent twisting or kinking the film. [VERB noun]

    装胶卷时要小心，别使胶片扭结。

  • The kinked line in chart 1 represents this pattern. [VERB-ed]

    图表1中的曲线表现了这一模式。

3 N-COUNT （尤指古怪）念头;奇想，怪癖 A kink is a particular quality or feature of a person's mind or character, especially one which is thought to be unusual or abnormal. （尤指古怪）念头;奇想，怪癖 [informal]

  • What kink did he have in his character?

    他性格中有哪些怪癖？

SYN quirk, eccentricity, foible, idiosyncrasy  
4 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 解决问题 If someone works out the kinks in a situation, they resolve the problems associated with it. 解决问题

  • ...working out the kinks of a potential trade agreement.

    解决有望达成的一个贸易协定的若干问题

  • ...joint sessions with her, to work out the kinks in the marriage.

    为解决婚姻中存在的问题而与她进行的交谈
