fine-tune
ˈfaɪnˈtun ˈfaɪnˈtjun
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense fine-tunes, present participle fine-tuning, past tense , past participle fine-tuned
VERB 对…进行微调;细调 If you fine-tune something, you make very small and precise changes to it in order to make it as successful or effective as it possibly can be. 对…进行微调;细调

  • We do not try to fine-tune the economy on the basis of short-term predictions. [VERB noun]

    我们不会根据短期预测就试图对经济进行微调。

  • Computers allow the plans to be fine-tuned and to be altered quickly. [VERB noun]

    计算机使得这些图样能够得到细调，并且可以迅速更改。
