beast
biːst bist
Word forms: plural beasts
1 N-COUNT 兽；野兽 You can refer to an animal as a beast, especially if it is a large, dangerous, or unusual one. 兽；野兽 [literary]

  • ...the threats our ancestors faced from wild beasts.

    我们祖先面对的来自野兽的威胁

  • ...a centaur: half man, half beast.

    半人半马怪物：一半人身，一半兽形

SYN animal, creature, brute  
2 N-COUNT [usually with supplement] 兽性的人；畜生；禽兽 If you refer to a man as a beast, you mean that his behaviour, especially his sexual behaviour, is very violent and uncontrolled. 兽性的人；畜生；禽兽 [journalism, disapproval]

  • ...a sex beast who subjected two sisters to a terrifying ordeal.

    使两姐妹遭受恐怖摧残的色魔

3 N-COUNT; N-VOC 讨厌鬼；王八蛋 If you call someone a beast, you think that they are behaving in a selfish, unkind, or unpleasant way. 讨厌鬼；王八蛋 [informal, old-fashioned, disapproval]

  • Bully! Hooligan! Beast! Let me go, let go!

    混蛋！流氓！王八蛋！放开我，放开！

4 N-COUNT [usually adjective NOUN] （含诙谐意）有特定性质的事物，性格特别的人 You can use beast to refer to something or someone in a light-hearted way, and to mention that they have a particular quality. （含诙谐意）有特定性质的事物，性格特别的人

  • ...that rare beast, a sports movie that isn't boring.

    这场体育电影还挺有意思，真难得
