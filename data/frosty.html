frosty
frɒsti frɔːsti ˈfrɔsti
Word forms: comparative frostier, superlative frostiest
1 ADJ-GRADED 霜冻的;严寒的 If the weather is frosty, the temperature is below freezing. 霜冻的;严寒的

  • ...sharp, frosty nights.

    寒冷刺骨的夜晚

SYN cold, frozen, icy, chilly  
2 ADJ-GRADED 结霜的;被霜覆盖的 You describe the ground or an object as frosty when it is covered with frost. 结霜的;被霜覆盖的

  • The street was deserted except for a cat lifting its paws off the frosty stones.

    街上空无一人，只有一只猫在结霜的石砖上拾爪而行。

SYN icy, ice-capped, icicled, hoar [rare]  
3 ADJ-GRADED 冷淡的;冷若冰霜的 If you describe someone's behaviour as frosty, you think it is unfriendly. 冷淡的;冷若冰霜的

  • The president may get a frosty reception in New Hampshire.

    总统在新罕布什尔州可能会受到冷遇。

  • The relationship has obviously become frosty.

    很明显关系已经变冷了。

SYN unfriendly, cold, discouraging, icy  
frostily

  • The Prime Minister smiled again, this time a trifle frostily.

    首相又笑了笑，这回有点儿冷冰冰的。

frostiness

  • There was a certain frostiness in his smile.

    他的笑容带着些许冷漠。
