downshift
daʊnʃɪft ˈdaʊnˌʃɪft ˈdaʊnˌʃɪft
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense downshifts, present participle downshifting, past tense , past participle downshifted
1 VERB 放慢生活节奏（调换强度较低工作以减轻压力) If someone downshifts, they leave a job that is well-paid but stressful for a less demanding job and a more enjoyable way of life. 放慢生活节奏（调换强度较低工作以减轻压力) [British]

  • Lynda now sees many of her clients downshifting in search of a new way of living. [VERB]

    琳达现在发现她的很多客户都在调换工作减轻压力，寻求一种新的生活方式。

downshifting

  • The latest lifestyle trend is downshifting.

    最新的生活方式趋于放慢生活节奏。

downshifter

  • Downshifters are being tempted to leave the sophisticated city and go simple.

    追求放慢生活节奏的人群渴望告别喧嚣的城市，过简单的生活。

2 VERB (汽车)调低速挡 If you downshift while driving, you change to a lower gear. (汽车)调低速挡 [mainly US] [Also V + to]

  • He downshifted and turned the steering wheel. [VERB]

    他调到低速挡，转动方向盘。
