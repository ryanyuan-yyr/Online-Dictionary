plastered
plɑːstəʳd plæs- ˈplæstərd
1 ADJ 黏附的；紧贴的 If something is plastered to a surface, it is sticking to the surface. 黏附的；紧贴的

  • His hair was plastered down to his scalp by the rain. [+ to]

    他的头发淋雨后紧贴在头皮上。

2 ADJ [verb-link ADJ] 沾满…的；涂满…的 If something or someone is plastered with a sticky substance, they are covered with it. 沾满…的；涂满…的

  • My hands, boots and trousers were plastered with mud. [+ with/in]

    我的双手、靴子和裤子上都沾满了泥巴。

3 ADJ 大幅（或醒目）地刊载（于头版）的 If a story or photograph is plastered all over the front page of a newspaper, it is given a lot of space on the page and made very noticeable. 大幅（或醒目）地刊载（于头版）的

  • His picture was plastered all over the newspapers on the weekend.

    他的照片占据了周末报纸的头版。

4 ADJ [verb-link ADJ] 烂醉如泥的；酩酊大醉的 If someone gets plastered, they get very drunk. 烂醉如泥的；酩酊大醉的 [informal]

  • With gin at 9p a tot, getting plastered is cheap and easy.

    一小杯杜松子酒只要9便士，灌醉自己即便宜又容易。

5 ADJ 用熟石膏处理过的；打上石膏的 If someone's broken arm or leg is plastered, it has a hard cover of plaster of Paris around it to protect the broken bone while it is mending. 用熟石膏处理过的；打上石膏的

  • She was sitting in a hospital bed, her plastered leg up in the air.

    她坐在病床上，一条打了石膏的腿悬在空中。
