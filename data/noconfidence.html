no-confidence
1 N-UNCOUNT [usu n of N, NOUN n] (组织成员的)不信任投票 If members of an organization pass a vote or motion of no-confidence in someone, they take a vote which shows that they no longer support that person or their ideas. (组织成员的)不信任投票

  • A call for a vote of no-confidence in the president was rejected. [+ in]

    对总统进行不信任投票的请求被否决。

  • The students passed a motion of no-confidence in the college principal.

    学生们通过了对校长进行不信任投票的动议。

  • ...a no-confidence motion.

    不信任投票的动议

2 N-UNCOUNT [usu n of N] 不信任;不支持 You can refer to something people say or do as a vote of no-confidence when it shows that they no longer support a particular person or organization. 不信任;不支持

  • Many police officers view this action as a vote of no-confidence in their service. [+ in]

    很多警官把这种举动看作是对他们服务的不信任。
