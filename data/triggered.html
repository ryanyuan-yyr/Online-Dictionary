trigger
trɪgəʳ ˈtrɪgər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense triggers, present participle triggering, past tense , past participle triggered
1 N-COUNT (枪的)扳机 The trigger of a gun is a small lever which you pull to fire it. (枪的)扳机

  • A man pointed a gun at them and pulled the trigger.

    一名男子拿枪指着他们，接着扣动了扳机。

2 N-COUNT [oft NOUN n] (炸弹的)引爆器 The trigger of a bomb is the device which causes it to explode. (炸弹的)引爆器

  • ...trigger devices for nuclear weapons.

    核武器的引爆器

3 VERB 引爆;启动;触发 To trigger a bomb or system means to cause it to work. 引爆;启动;触发

  • The thieves must have deliberately triggered the alarm and hidden inside the house. [VERB noun]

    盗贼肯定是故意触发了报警器，然后躲在房子里。

  • The one thousand pound bomb was triggered by a wire. [VERB noun]

    那颗重达 1,000 磅的炸弹是由一根导线引爆的。

  • …nuclear triggering devices. [VERB-ing]

    核引爆装置

SYN set off, trip, activate, set going  
4 VERB 引起;发动;促使 If something triggers an event or situation, it causes it to begin to happen or exist. 引起;发动;促使 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • ...the incident which triggered the outbreak of the First World War. [VERB noun]

    导致第一次世界大战爆发的事件

  • The current recession was triggered by a slump in consumer spending… [VERB noun]

    目前的经济衰退是由消费支出骤跌引起的。

  • Even a problem as simple as a bad back often has an underlying triggering factor. [VERB-ing]

    甚至像背疼这样的小问题都常常有潜在的诱发因素。

SYN bring about, start, cause, produce  
Trigger off means the same as trigger.trigger off 同 trigger

  • It is still not clear what events triggered off the demonstrations.

    现在仍然不清楚是什么事件引发了示威活动。

5 N-COUNT 诱因;起因;引发…的原因 If something acts as a trigger for another thing such as an illness, event, or situation, the first thing causes the second thing to begin to happen or exist. 诱因;起因;引发…的原因

  • Stress may act as a trigger for these illnesses. [+ for]

    压力可能会成为引发这些疾病的原因。
