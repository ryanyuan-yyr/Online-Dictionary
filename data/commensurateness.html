commensurate
kəmensərət kəˈmɛnsərɪt kəˈmɛnʃʊrɪt kəˈmɛnsərɪt
ADJ [ADJ n] (比例)相当的，相称的 If the level of one thing is commensurate with another, the first level is in proportion to the second. (比例)相当的，相称的 [formal] [Also + to]

  • Employees are paid salaries commensurate with those of teachers. [+ with]

    给员工的薪水与老师拿的薪水相当。

  • Managers saw a commensurate fall in their revenues.

    经理们发现自己的收入相应地减少了。

SYN equivalent, consistent, corresponding, comparable  
commensurately

  • Japanese real wages have not yet increased commensurately with the wealth of Japan.

    日本人的实际工资并没有随着国家财富的增长而相应增加。

  • The gain will be commensurately modest.

    相应地，收益不会太多。
