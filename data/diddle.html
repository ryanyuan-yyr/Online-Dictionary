diddle
dɪdəl ˈdɪdəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense diddles, present participle diddling, past tense , past participle diddled
1 VERB 欺骗;骗取…的钱财 If someone diddles you, they take money from you dishonestly or unfairly. 欺骗;骗取…的钱财 [mainly British, informal]

  • They diddled their insurance company by making a false claim. [VERB noun]

    他们进行虚假索赔，骗取了保险公司的赔偿金。

2 VERB 闲混;浪费时间 If someone diddles, they waste time and do not achieve anything. 闲混;浪费时间 [US, informal]

  • ...if Congress were to just diddle around and not take any action at all. [V around]

    如果国会只是想要闲混而不采取任何行动
