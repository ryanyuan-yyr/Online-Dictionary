fizz
fɪz fɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense fizzes, present participle fizzing, past tense , past participle fizzed
1 VERB 嘶嘶地冒泡;发出嘶嘶声 If a drink fizzes, it produces lots of little bubbles of gas and makes a sound like a long 's'. 嘶嘶地冒泡;发出嘶嘶声

  • After a while their mother was back, holding a tray of glasses that fizzed. [VERB]

    过了一会儿他们的母亲回来了，用托盘端着一杯杯嘶嘶冒泡的饮料。

SYN bubble, froth, fizzle, effervesce  
Fizz is also a noun.

  • I wonder if there's any fizz left in the lemonade.

    我琢磨着柠檬汁里不知道还有没有嘶嘶作响的气泡。

2 VERB (引擎等)发出长长的嘶声，嘶嘶地响 If something such as an engine fizzes, it makes a sound like a long 's'. (引擎等)发出长长的嘶声，嘶嘶地响

  • When I started the engine it sparked, fizzed and went dead. [VERB]

    我刚发动引擎时，引擎冒出火星，发出长长的嘶声，然后就熄火了。

SYN sputter, buzz, sparkle, hiss  
3 N-UNCOUNT 趣味;激情;活力 If you say that someone puts fizz into something, you mean that they make it more interesting or exciting. 趣味;激情;活力

  • A Brazilian public relations firm has brought some fizz into his campaign.

    一家巴西公关公司给他的竞选活动带来了些许活力。

4 N-UNCOUNT 香槟;起泡沫的葡萄酒 Champagne or sparkling wine is sometimes called fizz . 香槟;起泡沫的葡萄酒 [informal]

  • ...a bottle of fizz.

    一瓶香槟

SYN champagne, sparkling wine
