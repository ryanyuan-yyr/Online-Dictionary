get up
1 PHRASAL VERB 起立；站起来 When someone who is sitting or lying down gets up, they rise to a standing position. 起立；站起来

  • I got up and walked over to where he was. [VERB PREP.]

    我起身走到他那边。

SYN arise [old-fashioned] , stand (up), rise, get to your feet  
2 PHRASAL VERB 起床 When you get up, you get out of bed. 起床

  • They have to get up early in the morning. [VERB PREP.]

    他们大清早就得起床。

3 PHRASAL VERB 穿…服装；打扮；装扮 If you get yourself up in a particular kind of clothing, you dress in that clothing. 穿…服装；打扮；装扮

  • They knew how to get themselves up in those days. [VERB pronoun-reflexive PREP.]

    那时候，他们懂得打扮。

  • ...an old tramp got up in a velvet smoking-jacket. [VERB-ed PREP.]

    身穿一件天鹅绒吸烟服的老流浪汉

get-up ˈɡɛtʌp ˈgɛtˌʌp
Word forms: plural get-ups
N-COUNT （奇特或滑稽的）服装，装束 If you refer to a set of clothes as a get-up, you think that they are unusual or ridiculous. （奇特或滑稽的）服装，装束 [informal, disapproval]

  • Naturally he couldn't work in this get-up.

    他当然不能穿这么滑稽的衣服上班。

SYN outfit, costume, clothes, gear [informal]
