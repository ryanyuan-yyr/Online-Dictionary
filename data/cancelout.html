cancel out
PHRASAL VERB 抵消 If one thing cancels out another thing, the two things have opposite effects, so that when they are combined no real effect is produced. 抵消 [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • He wonders if the different influences might not cancel each other out. [VERB noun PREP.]

    他在想,各种影响能不能相互抵消。

  • The goal was cancelled out just before half-time by Craig McLurg. [be V-ed P + by]

    中场还没到,比分就被克雷格·麦克勒格扳平了。

SYN counterbalance, offset, make up for, compensate for
