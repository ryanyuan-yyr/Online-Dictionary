glimmer
glɪməʳ ˈglɪmər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense glimmers, present participle glimmering, past tense , past participle glimmered
1 VERB 发微光；隐约闪烁 If something glimmers, it produces or reflects a faint, gentle, often unsteady light. 发微光；隐约闪烁

  • The moon glimmered faintly through the mists. [VERB]

    月亮透过薄雾洒下微光。

  • ...the glimmering ocean. [VERB-ing]

    波光粼粼的大海

SYN gleam, shine, glow, sparkle  
2 N-COUNT （常指闪烁的）微光 A glimmer is a faint, gentle, often unsteady light. （常指闪烁的）微光

  • In the east there is the slightest glimmer of light. [+ of]

    东方闪现出一丝微光。

SYN glow, ray, sparkle, gleam  
3 N-COUNT 一丝；少许 A glimmer of something is a faint sign of it. 一丝；少许

  • Despite an occasional glimmer of hope, this campaign has not produced any results. [+ of]

    虽然偶尔会闪现一丝希望，但是这场宣传活动目前并无任何效果。

  • He is celebrating his first glimmer of success. [+ of]

    他正为看到一线成功的曙光而欢欣鼓舞。
