slur
slɜːʳ slɜr
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense slurs, present participle slurring, past tense , past participle slurred
1 N-COUNT 毁谤;诋毁;侮辱性的言辞 A slur is an insulting remark which could damage someone's reputation. 毁谤;诋毁;侮辱性的言辞

  • This is yet another slur on the integrity of the Metropolitan Police. [+ on]

    这是对伦敦市警察局刚正廉明作风的又一次诽谤。

  • ...racial slurs.

    种族毁谤的言论

SYN insult, stain, smear, stigma  
2 V-ERG (由于醉酒、生病或困倦等)含糊不清地说，大着舌头说 If someone slurs their speech or if their speech slurs, they do not pronounce each word clearly, because they are drunk, ill, or sleepy. (由于醉酒、生病或困倦等)含糊不清地说，大着舌头说

  • He repeated himself and slurred his words more than usual. [VERB noun]

    他重复了一遍，说得更含糊了。

  • The newscaster's speech began to slur. [VERB]

    新闻广播员的话音变得含糊不清起来。

  • 'Hey, you're gorgeous,' he slurred. [VERB with quote]

    “嗨，你真漂亮，”他含混地说。

SYN mumble, stammer, stutter, stumble over  
slurred
