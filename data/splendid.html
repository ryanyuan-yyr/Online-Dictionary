splendid
splendɪd ˈsplɛndɪd
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 极好的;绝妙的;出色的 If you say that something is splendid, you mean that it is very good. 极好的;绝妙的;出色的

  • The book includes a wealth of splendid photographs.

    该书中有大量精彩的照片。

  • Our house has got a splendid view across to the Cotswolds.

    从我们的房子里向外望去，景色宜人，一直能看到科茨沃尔德丘陵。

  • I found him to be splendid company during the hour of our acquaintance.

    在我们相处的那段时间里我发现他是个很好的同伴。

SYN excellent, wonderful, marvellous, mean [slang]  
splendidly

  • I have heard him tell people that we get along splendidly.

    我听到他跟别人说我们相处得很好。

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (建筑、艺术作品)壮丽的，华丽的，美妙的 If you describe a building or work of art as splendid, you mean that it is beautiful, impressive, and extremely well made. (建筑、艺术作品)壮丽的，华丽的，美妙的

  • ...a splendid Victorian mansion.

    华丽的维多利亚式宅第

SYN magnificent, grand, imposing, impressive  
splendidly

  • The young women are splendidly dressed, some in floor-length ball gowns.

    年轻女子们都穿得非常漂亮，有些还穿着拖地的舞裙。

  • Its historic buildings are being slowly and splendidly renovated.

    此处的历史建筑正被缓慢且精心地修复着。

  • ...this splendidly readable and robust autobiography.

    这部可读性很强的、引人入胜的自传

3 EXCLAM 好极了(表示赞同) You can say 'splendid' in a conversation to indicate that you approve of a particular situation or something that someone has said. 好极了(表示赞同) [feelings]

  • 'I was thinking I might do a lemon cream sauce and baked potatoes.' 'Splendid!' Midge applauded.

    “我在考虑做一道浇柠檬奶油汁的烤马铃薯。”“好极了！”米基表示赞同。
