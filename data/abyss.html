abyss
æbɪs əˈbɪs
Word forms: plural abysses
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 深渊 An abyss is a very deep hole in the ground. 深渊 [literary]
2 N-COUNT [usually singular] 险恶的境地 If someone is on the edge or brink of an abyss, they are about to enter into a very frightening or threatening situation. 险恶的境地 [literary]

  • He felt he was on the edge of an abyss; one false move and he was done for.

    他觉得如临深渊，一步不慎便会永劫不复。

  • The country was on the brink of an abyss.

    国家岌岌可危。

SYN cataclysm, collapse, disaster, catastrophe  
3 N-COUNT [usually singular, with supplement] 极大的差异；鸿沟 A very great difference between two people, things, or groups can be referred to as an abyss . 极大的差异；鸿沟 [literary]

  • How big is the abyss between what you think you are and what you actually are?

    你对自己的认识和真实的你之间差别到底有多大？

SYN gap, difference, gulf, split
