line up
1 PHRASAL VERB-ERG （使）（某一批人）排成行；（使）列队 If people line up or if you line them up, they move so that they are standing in a line. （使）（某一批人）排成行；（使）列队

  • The senior leaders lined up behind him in orderly rows. [VERB PREP.]

    高级将领整齐有序地在他身后站成几排。

  • The gym teachers lined us up against the cement walls. [VERB noun PREP.]

    体育老师让我们靠着水泥墙排好。

  • Four members of a family were lined up and shot. [VERB noun PREP.]

    一家的四名成员被排成一排枪杀了。

  • When he came back the sergeant had lined up the terrorists. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他回来时，中士已经让恐怖分子列队站好了。

SYN queue up, file, fall in, form a queue  
2 PHRASAL VERB 使（某一批物）排列成行 If you line things up, you move them into a straight row. 使（某一批物）排列成行

  • I would line up my toys on this windowsill and play. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我会把玩具排在这个窗台上来玩。

  • He finished polishing the cocktail glasses and lined them up behind the bar. [VERB noun PREP.]

    他擦完了鸡尾酒杯，把它们成排摆放在吧台后面。

SYN align, order, range, arrange  
3 PHRASAL VERB-RECIP-ERG （使）对齐 If you line one thing up with another, or one thing lines up with another, the first thing is moved into its correct position in relation to the second. You can also say that two things line up, or are lined up. （使）对齐

  • You have to line the car up with the ones beside you. [VERB noun PREP. + with]

    你得把车子和旁边的车停齐。

  • Gas cookers are adjustable in height to line up with your kitchen work top. [VERB PREP. + with]

    燃气灶的高度可调整，使之与厨房操作台齐平。

  • Mahoney had lined up two of the crates. [VERB PREP. noun]

    马奥尼已经摆好了其中两个板条箱。

  • When the images line up exactly, the projectors should be fixed in place. [VERB PREP.]

    图像完全对齐时，投影仪的位置就应该固定下来。

  • All we have to do is to get the two pieces lined up properly. [VERB-ed PREP.]

    我们所要做的就是将这两个部件对齐。

4 PHRASAL VERB 组织；邀集 If you line up an event or activity, you arrange for it to happen. If you line someone up for an event or activity, you arrange for them to be available for that event or activity. 组织；邀集

  • She lined up executives, politicians and educators to serve on the board of directors. [V P n to-inf]

    她邀请了高管、政坛人物和教育家加入董事会。

  • Bob Dylan is lining up a two-week UK tour for the New Year.

    鲍勃·迪伦正在组织一个为期两周的英国新年巡回演唱会。

SYN prepare, schedule, organize, secure  
5 PHRASAL VERB 支持/反对 If you line up with, behind, or alongside a person or group, you support them. If you line up against a person or group, you oppose them. 支持/反对

  • Some surprising names have lined up behind the idea. [VERB PREP. prep.]

    有些出人意料的名人也在支持这个想法。

  • We Spaniards have lined up against each other all too often. [VERB-ed PREP.]

    我们西班牙人太爱窝里斗了。

6 See also: line-up 
line-up
Word forms: plural line-ups
1 N-COUNT 一组人；一批东西 A line-up is a group of people or a series of things that have been gathered together to be part of a particular event. 一组人；一批东西

  • Ryan Giggs is likely to be in Wales's starting line-up for their World Cup qualifying match.

    瑞恩·吉格斯可能出现在威尔士队世界杯预选赛的首发阵容中。

  • The programme is back for a new series with a great line-up of musicians and comedy acts. [+ of]

    该节目重新开播了，新系列中汇集了一大批音乐家和喜剧剧目。

SYN arrangement, team, row, selection  
2 N-COUNT （为识别嫌疑犯）警方安排有待辨认的一列人 At a line-up, a witness to a crime tries to identify the criminal from among a line of people. （为识别嫌疑犯）警方安排有待辨认的一列人

  • He failed to identify Graham from photographs, but later picked him out of a police line-up.

    他没能从疑犯照片中指认出格雷厄姆，但后来在警方安排的列队辨认中把他挑了出来。
