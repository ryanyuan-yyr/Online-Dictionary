parry
pæri ˈpæri
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense parries, present participle parrying, past tense , past participle parried
1 VERB （巧妙地）回避，闪避 If you parry a question or argument, you cleverly avoid answering it or dealing with it. （巧妙地）回避，闪避

  • In an awkward press conference, Mr King parried questions on the allegations. [VERB noun]

    在一场尴尬的新闻发布会上，金先生回避了有关指控的问题。

2 VERB 挡开；架开 If you parry a blow from someone who is attacking you, you push aside their arm or weapon so that you are not hurt. 挡开；架开

  • I did not want to wound him, but to restrict myself to defence, to parry his attacks. [VERB noun]

    我不想伤害他，只是想自我防卫，挡开他的攻击。

  • I parried, and that's when my sword broke. [VERB]

    我挡了一下，把我的剑挡断了。
