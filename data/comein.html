come in
1 PHRASAL VERB (信息)传来;(报告或电话)接收到 If information, a report, or a telephone call comes in, it is received. (信息)传来;(报告或电话)接收到

  • Reports are now coming in of trouble at yet another jail. [VERB PREP.]

    另一所监狱也发生了骚乱的消息正在不断传来。

2 PHRASAL VERB [usually cont] (钱款)定期收到;有收入 If you have some money coming in, you receive it regularly as your income. (钱款)定期收到;有收入

  • She had no money coming in and no funds. [VERB PREP.]

    她没有收入，也没有存款。

3 PHRASAL VERB 加入，参与(讨论、安排或任务) If someone comes in on a discussion, arrangement, or task, they join it. 加入，参与(讨论、安排或任务)

  • Can I come in here too, on both points?. [VERB PREP. + on]

    我能否也加入进来，说说这两点呢？

  • He had a designer come in and redesign the uniforms. [VERB PREP.]

    他请来一名设计师重新设计制服。

4 PHRASAL VERB (新的观点、时装或产品)流行起来，上市 When a new idea, fashion, or product comes in, it becomes popular or available. (新的观点、时装或产品)流行起来，上市

  • It was just when geography was really beginning to change and lots of new ideas were coming in. [VERB PREP.]

    那时正是地理学真正开始发生改变、许多新观念兴起的时候。

  • I wouldn't be sorry to see proportional representation and I think it's a thing which will gradually come in. [VERB PREP.]

    我不会为比例代表制的出现而难过，我认为这种制度将逐渐流行起来。

5 PHRASAL VERB (在某件事中)起作用，发挥作用 If you ask where something or someone comes in, you are asking what their role is in a particular matter. (在某件事中)起作用，发挥作用

  • Rose asked again, 'But where do we come in, Henry?' [VERB PREP.]

    罗丝又问：“但是需要我们干什么，亨利？”

  • Finally, he could do no more, which is where Jacques came in. [VERB PREP.]

    最后，他再也无能为力了，这也正是雅克发挥作用的时候。

6 PHRASAL VERB (潮)涨 When the tide comes in, the water in the sea gradually moves so that it covers more of the land. (潮)涨
