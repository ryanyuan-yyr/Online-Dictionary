anoint
ənɔɪnt əˈnɔɪnt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense anoints, present participle anointing, past tense , past participle anointed
1 VERB （常指在宗教仪式中）涂油（或水）于（人体） To anoint someone means to put oil or water on a part of their body, usually for religious reasons. （常指在宗教仪式中）涂油（或水）于（人体）

  • He anointed my forehead. [VERB noun]

    他给我的前额涂上了油。

  • The Pope has anointed him as Archbishop. [VERB noun + as]

    教皇给他施了涂油礼，任命其为大主教。

  • ...the anointed king. [VERB-ed]

    神权帝王

SYN consecrate, bless, sanctify, hallow  
2 VERB 选定;指定 If a person in a position of authority anoints someone, they choose them to do a particular important job. 选定;指定 [journalism]

  • The populist party anointed him as its candidate. [VERB noun + as]

    平民党选定他做候选人。

  • Mr. Olsen has always avoided anointing any successor. [VERB noun]

    奥尔森先生总是回避指定继任者。

  • His message about moderation and the middle class captured the attention of the press, which anointed him front-runner. [VERB noun noun]

    他关于中庸和中产阶级的观点引起媒体的注意，使他成为获胜的热门人选。
