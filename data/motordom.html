motor
moʊtəʳ ˈmoʊtər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense motors, present participle motoring, past tense , past participle motored
1 N-COUNT 发动机;引擎 The motor in a machine, vehicle, or boat is the part that uses electricity or fuel to produce movement, so that the machine, vehicle, or boat can work. 发动机;引擎

  • She got in and started the motor.

    她坐上车，发动了引擎。

SYN engine, machine, mechanism  
2 ADJ [ADJ n] 机动的;由发动机驱动的 Motor vehicles and boats have a petrol or diesel engine. 机动的;由发动机驱动的

  • Theft of motor vehicles is up by 15.9%.

    机动车辆偷盗案上升了15.9%。

3 ADJ [ADJ n] 汽车的;车辆的 Motor is used to describe activities relating to vehicles such as cars and buses. 汽车的;车辆的 [mainly British]

  • ...the future of the British motor industry.

    英国汽车工业的未来

  • He worked as a motor mechanic.

    他是一名汽车修理工。

in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 automotive, automobile
4 N-COUNT 汽车;轿车 Some people refer to a car as a motor . 汽车;轿车 [British, informal]
5 VERB 自驾游览;乘汽车旅行 If you motor somewhere, you travel there in a car, usually for pleasure. 自驾游览;乘汽车旅行 [old-fashioned]

  • I had motored down from Cheshire. [VERB adv./prep.]

    我从柴郡一路南下开过来。

6 VERB 使用发动机驾驶帆船 If people in a small sailing boat motor somewhere, they use the boat's motor rather than the power of the wind to get there. 使用发动机驾驶帆船

  • Restarting the engine, we motored downriver. [VERB adv./prep.]

    我们重新启动马达，沿河而下。
