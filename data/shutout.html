shut out
1 PHRASAL VERB 把…关在门外;使…不能进入 If you shut something or someone out, you prevent them from getting into a place, for example by closing the doors. 把…关在门外;使…不能进入 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • 'I shut him out of the bedroom,' says Maureen. [VERB noun PREP. + of]

    “我把他关在了卧室外面，”莫琳说。

  • I was set to shut out anyone else who came knocking. [VERB PREP. noun]

    其他任何人来敲门，我都决意将其拒之门外。

SYN exclude, bar, keep out, black  
2 PHRASAL VERB 摒弃，阻断(念头、情感等) If you shut out a thought or a feeling, you prevent yourself from thinking or feeling it. 摒弃，阻断(念头、情感等)

  • I shut out the memory which was too painful to dwell on. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我不再去回忆那些痛苦不堪的往事了。

  • The figures represent such overwhelming human misery that the mind wants to shut it out. [VERB noun PREP.]

    这些数据表明人类经受了如此深重的苦难，对此人们都不愿想起。

SYN block out, screen, hide, cover  
3 PHRASAL VERB 把…排除在外 If you shut someone out of something, you prevent them from having anything to do with it. 把…排除在外

  • She is very reclusive, to the point of shutting me out of her life. [VERB noun PREP. + of]

    她遁世索居，以至将我排除在她生活之外。

  • They refused to allow Republicans to offer amendments, effectively shutting them out of the process. [V n P of n]

    他们拒不允许共和党人提出修正案，实际上是将他们排除在了进程之外。

  • She had effectively shut him out by refusing to listen. [VERB noun PREP.]

    她拒不听他说话，并以此有效地将他排除在外。
