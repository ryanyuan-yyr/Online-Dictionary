extradite
ekstrədaɪt ˈɛkstrəˌdaɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense extradites, present participle extraditing, past tense , past participle extradited
1 VERB 引渡 If someone is extradited, they are officially sent back to their own or another country to be tried for a crime that they have been accused of. 引渡 [formal]

  • He was extradited to Britain from the Irish Republic to face explosives charges. [be V-ed + to/from]

    他被从爱尔兰共和国引渡到英国，面临爆炸罪的指控。

  • The authorities refused to extradite him. [VERB noun]

    当局拒绝引渡他。

SYN deport, exile, hand over, expel
