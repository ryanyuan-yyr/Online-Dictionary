unify
juːnɪfaɪ ˈjunəˌfaɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense unifies, present participle unifying, past tense , past participle unified
V-RECIP-ERG （使）成一体；（使）联合；（使）统一 If someone unifies different things or parts, or if the things or parts unify, they are brought together to form one thing. （使）成一体；（使）联合；（使）统一

  • A flexible retirement age is being considered by Ministers to unify men's and women's pension rights. [VERB noun]

    大臣们正在考虑一个灵活的退休年龄机制，这样可以把男性和女性的养老金权益统一起来。

  • He said he would seek to unify the Conservative Party and win the next general election. [VERB noun]

    他说他将寻求团结保守党，赢得下届大选。

  • The plan has been for the rival armies to demobilise, to unify, and then to hold elections to decide who rules. [VERB]

    这个计划是要让各派敌对武装遣散军队、联合起来，然后通过选举决定谁来统治。

  • Both sides say they want to re-unify their country, which has been divided since the end of the Second World War. [VERB noun with noun]

    双方都表示想重新统一自第二次世界大战结束以来分裂的国家。

  • ...the benefits of unifying with the West. [VERB + with]

    联合西方的好处

SYN unite, join, combine, merge
