spillover
spɪloʊvəʳ ˈspɪləʊvə ˈspɪlˌoʊvər
Word forms: plural spillovers
N-COUNT 伴随的结果(或影响) A spillover is a situation or feeling that starts in one place but then begins to happen or have an effect somewhere else. 伴随的结果(或影响)

  • Some jobs are quite likely to have a negative spillover into family life.

    有些工作很可能会对家庭生活产生不良影响。

  • Spillover damage from the building's demolition was confined to some broken glass.

    拆除这座大楼带来的损失仅仅是一些玻璃被震碎了。
