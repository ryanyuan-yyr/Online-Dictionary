defile
dɪfaɪl diˈfaɪl dɪˈfaɪl ˈdiˌfaɪl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense defiles, present participle defiling, past tense , past participle defiled
1 VERB 污损;玷污;亵渎 To defile something that people think is important or holy means to do something to it or say something about it which is offensive. 污损;玷污;亵渎 [literary]

  • He had defiled the sacred name of the Holy Prophet. [VERB noun]

    他亵渎了这位神圣先知的圣名。

2 N-COUNT (通常指山中的)峡谷，峡道，隘路 A defile is a very narrow valley or passage, usually through mountains. (通常指山中的)峡谷，峡道，隘路 [formal]
SYN gorge, pass, passage, way through
