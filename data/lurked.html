lurk
lɜːʳk lɜrk
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense lurks, present participle lurking, past tense , past participle lurked
1 VERB （通常指意图不轨地）潜伏，潜藏，埋伏 If someone lurks somewhere, they wait there secretly so that they cannot be seen, usually because they intend to do something bad. （通常指意图不轨地）潜伏，潜藏，埋伏

  • He thought he saw someone lurking above the chamber during the address. [VERB]

    他觉得自己看见有人在演讲时潜藏在会议厅顶上。

SYN hide, sneak, crouch, prowl  
2 VERB （危险、疑虑、恐惧等）暗藏，潜在 If something such as a danger, doubt, or fear lurks somewhere, it exists but is not obvious or easily recognized. （危险、疑虑、恐惧等）暗藏，潜在

  • Hidden dangers lurk in every family saloon car. [VERB]

    每部家庭式双排座小轿车都存在隐患。

  • Around every corner lurked doubt and uncertainty. [VERB]

    每个角落都暗藏着疑虑和不安。
