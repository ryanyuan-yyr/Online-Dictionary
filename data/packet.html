packet
pækɪt ˈpækɪt
Word forms: plural packets
1 N-COUNT 包装盒；包装袋 A packet is a small container in which a quantity of something is sold. Packets are either small boxes made of thin cardboard, or bags or envelopes made of paper or plastic. 包装盒；包装袋 [mainly British]

  • Cook the rice according to instructions on the packet.

    按照包装袋上的说明煮饭。

  • ...a cigarette packet.

    香烟盒

A packet of something is an amount of it contained in a packet.（一）包；（一）盒；（一）袋

  • He had smoked half a packet of cigarettes.

    他已经吸了半包香烟。

  • Elinor bought her a packet of biscuits.

    埃莉诺给她买了一袋饼干。

in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 package
2 N-COUNT （扁平的）小包裹 A packet is a small flat parcel. （扁平的）小包裹 [mainly British]

  • ...to send letters and packets abroad.

    将信和包裹寄往国外

  • ...a packet of photographs. [+ of]

    一包照片

3 N-SING 一大笔钱 You can refer to a lot of money as a packet . 一大笔钱 [British, informal]

  • It'll cost you a packet.

    那会花你一大笔钱。

  • You could save yourself a packet.

    你能为自己省一大笔钱。

  • Someone's making a packet out of it.

    有人从中狠赚了一笔。

SYN a fortune, lot(s), pot(s) [informal] , a bomb [British , slang]  
in AM, use 美国英语用 bundle
