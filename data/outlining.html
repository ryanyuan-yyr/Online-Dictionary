outline
aʊtlaɪn ˈaʊtˌlaɪn
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense outlines, present participle outlining, past tense , past participle outlined
1 VERB 概述；概括 If you outline an idea or a plan, you explain it in a general way. 概述；概括

  • The mayor outlined his plan to clean up the town's image. [VERB noun]

    市长概述了他整顿市容的计划。

  • The methods outlined in this book are only suggestions. [VERB noun]

    本书概括的一些方法仅供参考。

SYN summarize, review, draft, plan  
2 N-COUNT [oft in NOUN] 概要；梗概 An outline is a general explanation or description of something. 概要；梗概

  • Following is an outline of the survey findings. [+ of]

    以下是调查结果概要。

  • The proposals were given in outline by the Secretary of State.

    国务卿对提议进行了简要的概述。

SYN summary, review, résumé, abstract  
3 V-PASSIVE （因背后的光线）显示…的轮廓（或外形） You say that an object is outlined when you can see its general shape because there is light behind it. （因背后的光线）显示…的轮廓（或外形）

  • The Ritz was outlined against the lights up there. [be VERB-ed]

    高悬的灯光映照出里兹饭店的轮廓。

  • It was a beautiful sight outlined above the starry sky. [VERB-ed]

    星空之上映衬出美景。

SYN silhouette, etch, delineate  
4 N-COUNT （尤指模糊的）轮廓，外形 The outline of something is its general shape, especially when it cannot be clearly seen. （尤指模糊的）轮廓，外形

  • He could see only the hazy outline of the goalposts. [+ of]

    他只能看见球门柱模糊的轮廓。

SYN shape, lines, form, figure
