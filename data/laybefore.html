lay before
PHRASAL VERB 把…提交至…面前；把…向…详细陈述 If you lay an idea or piece of information before someone, you present it to them in detail, usually in order to obtain their approval or advice. 把…提交至…面前；把…向…详细陈述 [formal]

  • The majority party would be unwise to deviate far from the principles it had laid before the electors. [VERB noun PREP. noun]

    多数党如果远远背离原先向选民立下的原则，那将是不明智的。

  • Now what we need is definite proof that we can lay before the prime minister. [V P n n]

    如今，我们需要的是可呈交给首相的确凿证据。
