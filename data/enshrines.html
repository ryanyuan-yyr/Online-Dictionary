enshrine
ɪnʃraɪn ɛnˈʃraɪn ɪnˈʃraɪn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense enshrines, present participle enshrining, past tense , past participle enshrined
VERB 视…为神圣;珍藏;使…神圣不可侵犯 If something such as an idea or a right is enshrined in something such as a constitution or law, it is protected by it. 视…为神圣;珍藏;使…神圣不可侵犯

  • His new relationship with Germany is enshrined in a new non-aggression treaty. [be VERB-ed + in]

    他与德国的新关系受新的互不侵犯条约的保护。

  • The apartheid system which enshrined racism in law still existed. [VERB noun prep.]

    以法律保护种族主义的种族隔离制度依旧存在。

SYN preserve, protect, treasure, cherish
