forge
fɔːʳdʒ fɔrdʒ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense forges, present participle forging, past tense , past participle forged
1 V-RECIP 缔造;建立 If one person or institution forges an agreement or relationship with another, they create it with a lot of hard work, hoping that it will be strong or lasting. 缔造;建立

  • The Prime Minister is determined to forge a good relationship with America's new leader. [VERB noun + with]

    首相决意要和美国的新任领导人建立良好的关系。

  • They agreed to forge closer economic ties. [VERB noun]

    他们同意建立更加紧密的经济联系。

  • The programme aims to forge links between higher education and small businesses. [V n between]

    这个项目旨在为高等教育和小企业之间建立联系。

  • The Community was trying to forge a common foreign and security policy. [VERB noun]

    这个共同体试图形成共同的外交和安全政策。

SYN form, build, create, establish  
2 VERB 创造;开创 If you say that a person has forged something that you approve of, you mean that you admire them for having done something difficult. 创造;开创 [approval]

  • The project will help inmates forge new careers. [VERB noun]

    这项计划会帮助犯人开始新的职业。

  • Tito forged a unique model of communism after breaking with Stalin in 1948. [VERB noun]

    1948 年铁托在和斯大林决裂后创建了一种独特的共产主义模式。

3 VERB 伪造，仿造(钞票、文件、绘画作品等) If someone forges something such as a banknote, a document, or a painting, they copy it or make it so that it looks genuine, in order to deceive people. 伪造，仿造(钞票、文件、绘画作品等)

  • He admitted seven charges including forging passports. [VERB noun]

    他承认了7项罪名，其中包括伪造护照。

  • She alleged that Taylor had forged her signature on the form. [VERB noun]

    她声称泰勒在表格上伪造了她的签名。

  • They used forged documents to leave the country. [VERB-ed]

    他们使用伪造的证明文件离开了这个国家。

forger

  • ...the most prolific art forger in the country.

    这个国家最多产的艺术品仿造者

4 N-COUNT [oft in names] 铁匠铺;锻造厂 A forge is a place where someone makes metal goods and equipment by heating pieces of metal and then shaping them. 铁匠铺;锻造厂

  • ...the blacksmith's forge.

    铁匠铺

  • ...Woodbury Blacksmith & Forge Co.

    伍德伯里锻造公司

5 VERB 炼;锻造 If someone forges an object out of metal, they heat the metal and then hammer and bend it into the required shape. 炼;锻造

  • To forge a blade takes great skill. [VERB noun]

    锻造刀刃需要很高超的技巧。

SYN create, make, work, found  
6 PHRASAL VERB 取得进展;稳步推进 If you forge ahead with something, you continue with it and make a lot of progress with it. 取得进展;稳步推进

  • He again pledged to forge ahead with his plans for reform.

    他再次承诺会稳步推进自己的改革计划。

  • The two companies forged ahead, innovating and expanding.

    两家公司加快发展，改革创新，不断扩张。
