texture
tekstʃəʳ ˈtɛkstʃər
Word forms: plural textures
1 N-VAR （摸上去的）质地，质感，纹理 The texture of something is the way that it feels when you touch it, for example how smooth or rough it is. （摸上去的）质地，质感，纹理

  • Aloe Vera is used in moisturisers to give them a wonderfully silky texture.

    芦荟用于润肤霜中，使之具有美妙的丝般润滑感。

  • Her skin is pale, the texture of fine wax.

    她肤色苍白，肤质细腻如蜡。

SYN feel, quality, character, consistency  
2 N-VAR (尤指食品、土壤的)结构，构造 The texture of something, especially food or soil, is its structure, for example whether it is light with lots of holes, or very heavy and solid. (尤指食品、土壤的)结构，构造

  • This cheese has an open, crumbly texture with a strong flavour.

    这种奶酪多孔松脆，风味浓郁。

  • Earthworms consume large amounts of soil, and produce a rich humus, perfect in texture.

    蚯蚓吃下大量土壤，排出肥力丰富、物理结构理想的腐殖质。

3 N-VAR [usually with supplement] (音乐、文学作品的)神韵，谐和统一（感） The texture of a piece of music or a work of literature is the impression that it makes on you as a result of the way that its different elements are combined. (音乐、文学作品的)神韵，谐和统一（感） [technical]

  • The very texture of his prose bears the influence of his familiarity with drugs.

    他独特的散文风韵显示出他常常在毒品的影响下进行创作的痕迹。
