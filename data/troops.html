troop
truːp trup
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense troops, present participle trooping, past tense , past participle trooped
1 N-PLURAL 部队;军队;士兵 Troops are soldiers, especially when they are in a large organized group doing a particular task. 部队;军队;士兵

  • The next phase of the operation will involve the deployment of more than 35,000 troops from a dozen countries.

    下一阶段的军事行动将要对来自 12 个国家的 35,000多名士兵进行部署。

  • There were reports of troop movements.

    有报道称将要调动军队。

SYN soldiers, men, armed forces, servicemen  
2 N-COUNT-COLL [with singular or plural verb] 骑兵队;装甲团 A troop is a group of soldiers within a cavalry or armoured regiment. 骑兵队;装甲团

  • ...a troop of enemy cavalry trotting towards the Dutch right flank. [+ of]

    敌方一支骑兵队正冲向荷兰军右翼

3 N-COUNT [usually with supplement] 童子军中队 A troop of boy scouts is a local group of them that meets regularly. 童子军中队

  • …a Scout troop.

    一队童子军

4 N-COUNT 一群;一队 A troop of people or animals is a group of them. 一群;一队

  • Amy was aware of the little troop of travellers watching the two of them. [+ of]

    埃米知道那一小队旅行者正在看着她们俩。

  • Out of beams and cracks came troops of beetles, ants and spiders.

    从横梁上和裂缝里爬出来了一群群甲虫、蚂蚁和蜘蛛。

SYN group, company, team, body  
5 VERB (常指疲惫或忧伤地)成群移动，结队前行 If people troop somewhere, they walk there in a group, often in a sad or tired way. (常指疲惫或忧伤地)成群移动，结队前行 [informal]

  • They all trooped back to the house for a rest. [VERB adv./prep.]

    他们都成群结队地回到房子里休息。

  • The men trooped into work with resignation. [VERB adv./prep.]

    男人们被迫只好一伙伙地开始工作。

SYN plod, trail, tramp, trudge
