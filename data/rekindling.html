rekindle
riːkɪndəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense rekindles, present participle rekindling, past tense , past participle rekindled
1 VERB 重新点燃；使重新活跃 If something rekindles an interest, feeling, or thought that you used to have, it makes you think about it or feel it again. 重新点燃；使重新活跃

  • Ben Brantley's article on Sir Ian McKellen rekindled many memories. [VERB noun]

    本·布兰特利的那篇关于伊恩·麦凯伦爵士的文章唤起了许多记忆。

  • Her interest was rekindled when she saw herbs in everyday medicinal use there. [VERB noun]

    在那里看到日常医疗中广泛使用草药的时候，她的兴趣被重新点燃了。

2 VERB 再次引起，再次引发(使人不悦的状况) If something rekindles an unpleasant situation, it makes the unpleasant situation happen again. 再次引起，再次引发(使人不悦的状况)

  • There are fears that the series could rekindle animosity between the two countries. [VERB noun]

    有人担忧这一系列事件会再次引发两国之间的敌对。
