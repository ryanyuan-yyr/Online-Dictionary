pivot
pɪvət ˈpɪvət
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense pivots, present participle pivoting, past tense , past participle pivoted
1 N-COUNT 中枢；枢纽；要点；中心点 The pivot in a situation is the most important thing which everything else is based on or arranged around. 中枢；枢纽；要点；中心点

  • Forming the pivot of the exhibition is a large group of watercolours. [+ of]

    整个展览的重点是一大组水彩画。

SYN hub, centre, heart, hinge  
2 VERB 在枢轴上转动 If something pivots, it balances or turns on a central point. 在枢轴上转动

  • The boat pivoted on its central axis and pointed straight at the harbour entrance. [VERB prep./adv.]

    小船原地掉过头来，直指海港入口。

  • She pivots gracefully on the stage. [VERB prep./adv.]

    她在舞台上优雅地旋转。

  • He pivoted his whole body through ninety degrees. [VERB noun prep.]

    他整个身体转过90度。

SYN turn, spin, revolve, rotate  
3 N-COUNT [usually singular] 枢轴；支点 A pivot is the pin or the central point on which something balances or turns. 枢轴；支点

  • The pedal had sheared off at the pivot.

    踏板在枢轴处断裂了。

SYN axis, swivel, axle, spindle  
Phrasal verbs:
pivot on
