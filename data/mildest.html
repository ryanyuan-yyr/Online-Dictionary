mild
maɪld maɪld
Word forms: comparative milder, superlative mildest
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 温和的；不强烈的；不严重的 Mild is used to describe something such as a feeling, attitude, or illness that is not very strong or severe. 温和的；不强烈的；不严重的

  • Teddy turned to Mona with a look of mild confusion.

    泰迪表情略带困惑地向莫娜求助。

  • Anna put up a mild protest.

    安娜提出了温和的抗议。

  • If you have only mild symptoms, try an over-the-counter treatment.

    如果症状较轻，试试用非处方药来治疗。

SYN faint, slight, vague, minimal  
mildly

  • I'm only mildly surprised.

    我只是有点惊讶。

  • Josephine must have had the disease very mildly as she showed no symptoms.

    约瑟芬没有表现出任何症状，所以她的病情一定还很轻。

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (人)温和的，温良的 A mild person is gentle and does not get angry easily. (人)温和的，温良的

  • He is a mild man, who is reasonable almost to the point of blandness.

    他为人温和，几乎通情达理到了令人乏味的地步。

SYN gentle, kind, easy, soft  
mildly

  • 'I'm not meddling,' Kenworthy said mildly, 'I'm just curious.'

    “我不是在多管闲事，”肯沃西温和地说，“我只是好奇罢了。”

3 ADJ-GRADED (天气)温暖的，和煦的 Mild weather is pleasant because it is neither extremely hot nor extremely cold. →see usage note at: hot (天气)温暖的，和煦的

  • The area is famous for its very mild winter climate.

    这个地区因冬季气候十分温和而著名。

4 ADJ-GRADED (味道)温和的，清淡的，不刺激的 You describe food as mild when it does not taste or smell strong, sharp, or bitter, especially when you like it because of this. (味道)温和的，清淡的，不刺激的

  • This cheese has a soft, mild flavour.

    这种奶酪味道淡而不腻。

  • ...a mild curry powder.

    不太辣的咖喱粉

SYN bland, thin, smooth, tasteless  
5 ADJ-GRADED (肥皂、洗涤液)软性的，不伤皮肤的，温和的 Mild soap or washing-up liquid feels pleasant on your skin and does not contain any substances that might make your skin sore. (肥皂、洗涤液)软性的，不伤皮肤的，温和的

  • Wash your face thoroughly with a mild soap and warm water.

    用刺激性不强的肥皂和热水把脸彻底洗干净。

SYN soothing, mollifying, emollient, demulcent  
6 N-UNCOUNT 淡味黑啤酒 Mild is a clear, dark-coloured beer. 淡味黑啤酒 [British]
7 See also: mildly
