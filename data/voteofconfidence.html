vote of confidence vəʊt əv ˈkɒnfɪdəns
Word forms: plural votes of confidence
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] （通常指对政府的）信任投票 A vote of confidence is a vote in which members of a group are asked to indicate that they still support the person or group in power, usually the government. （通常指对政府的）信任投票

  • The Indian Prime Minister, V P Singh, lost a vote of confidence in the Indian parliament. [+ in]

    印度总理辛格未能通过印度议会的信任投票。

2 N-COUNT [usually singular] 赞同的表示;支持的表示 A vote of confidence is something that you say or do which shows that you approve of or support a person or a group. 赞同的表示;支持的表示

  • The ten-year deal is a vote of confidence in coal-fired power stations. [+ in]

    这份为期10年的协议是对燃煤电站的支持。
