intent
ɪntent ɪnˈtɛnt ˈɪnˌtɛnt
Word forms: plural intents
1 ADJ-GRADED 坚决的;下决心的 If you are intent on doing something, you are eager and determined to do it. 坚决的;下决心的

  • The rebels are obviously intent on keeping up the pressure. [+ on/upon]

    叛乱分子显然决心继续施压。

  • The play starred a well-known retired actress who was intent on a come-back.

    该剧由一名志在复出的著名隐退女演员主演。

2 ADJ-GRADED 专心的;专注的 If someone does something in an intent way, they pay great attention to what they are doing. 专心的;专注的 [written]

  • She looked from one intent face to another.

    她看着一张张专注的面孔。

  • Rodney had been intent on every word.

    罗德尼字字留心。

SYN absorbed, focused, fixed, earnest  
intently

  • He listened intently, then slammed down the phone.

    他专心地听着,然后砰地挂上电话。

3 N-VAR 意图;目的 A person's intent is their intention to do something. 意图;目的 [formal]

  • ...a strong statement of intent on arms control.

    决心实施军备控制的有力声明

  • Two men will appear before Birmingham magistrates' court today charged with possession of arms with intent to endanger life.

    两名男子今天将在伯明翰地方法庭受审,他们被控持有武器，企图危害他人生命。

SYN intention, aim, purpose, meaning  
4 PHRASE 差不多等于;几乎在一切方面 You say to all intents and purposes to suggest that a situation is not exactly as you describe it but the effect is the same as if it were. 差不多等于;几乎在一切方面

  • To all intents and purposes he was my father.

    他几乎可以称得上是我的父亲。
