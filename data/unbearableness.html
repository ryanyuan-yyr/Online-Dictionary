unbearable
ʌnbeərəbəl ʌnˈbɛrəbəl
ADJ-GRADED 不堪忍受的；无法容忍的；难以承受的 If you describe something as unbearable, you mean that it is so unpleasant, painful, or upsetting that you feel unable to accept it or deal with it. 不堪忍受的；无法容忍的；难以承受的

  • War has made life almost unbearable for the civilians remaining in the capital. [+ for]

    对留在首都的平民来说，战争已使生活变得几乎不堪忍受。

  • I was in terrible, unbearable pain.

    我当时疼痛难忍。

SYN intolerable, insufferable, unendurable, too much [informal]  
unbearably

  • By the evening it had become unbearably hot.

    到了傍晚时分，天气已经变得酷热难耐。
