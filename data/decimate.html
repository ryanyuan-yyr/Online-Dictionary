decimate
desɪmeɪt ˈdɛsəˌmeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense decimates, present participle decimating, past tense , past participle decimated
1 VERB 大批杀死;大量毁灭 To decimate something such as a group of people or animals means to destroy a very large number of them. 大批杀死;大量毁灭

  • The pollution could decimate the river's thriving population of kingfishers. [VERB noun]

    污染可能会造成河边大量繁殖的翠鸟大批死亡。

  • British forces in the Caribbean were being decimated by disease. [VERB noun]

    加勒比海地区的英国部队有大批的人病死。

SYN destroy, devastate, wipe out, ravage  
decimation

  • ...the decimation of the great rain forests.

    广阔雨林大片的毁灭

2 VERB 严重削弱，大幅缩减(系统或机构) To decimate a system or organization means to reduce its size and effectiveness greatly. 严重削弱，大幅缩减(系统或机构)

  • ...a recession which decimated the nation's manufacturing industry. [VERB noun]

    使该国制造业元气大伤的经济衰退

SYN reduce, diminish, decrease, weaken  
decimation

  • Government policies have resulted in a decimation of essential services used by the poor.

    政府的政策导致穷人所能享受到的基础服务大大减少。
