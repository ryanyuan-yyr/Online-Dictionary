antitrust
æntitrʌst ˌæntɪˈtrʌst ˌæntaɪˈtrʌst ˌæntiˈtrʌst
ADJ [ADJ n] （指美国法律）反托拉斯的，反垄断的 In the United States, antitrust laws are intended to stop large firms taking over their competitors, fixing prices with their competitors, or interfering with free competition in any way. （指美国法律）反托拉斯的，反垄断的

  • The jury found that the NFL had violated antitrust laws.

    陪审团裁决全国橄榄球联盟触犯了反垄断法。
