cliché
kliːʃeɪ kliːʃeɪ kliˈʃeɪ
Word forms: plural clichés
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 cliche
N-COUNT 陈词滥调; A cliché is an idea or phrase which has been used so much that it is no longer interesting or effective or no longer has much meaning. 陈词滥调; [disapproval]

  • I've learned that the cliché about life not being fair is true. [+ about]

    我已经体会到有关生活不公的老调是对的.

SYN platitude, stereotype, commonplace, banality
