commute
kəmjuːt kəˈmjut
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense commutes, present participle commuting, past tense , past participle commuted
1 VERB (经很远的路程)上下班往返;通勤 If you commute, you travel a long distance every day between your home and your place of work. (经很远的路程)上下班往返;通勤

  • Mike commutes to London every day. [V + to/from]

    迈克每天都去伦敦上班。

  • McLaren began commuting between Paris and London. [V + between]

    麦克拉伦开始往返于巴黎与伦敦之间。

  • He's going to commute. [VERB]

    他打算乘长途车往返上下班。

SYN travel  
commuter

  • The number of commuters to London has dropped by 100,000.

    往返于伦敦的上班族减少了10万。

  • ...a commuter train.

    通勤列车

2 N-COUNT 上下班路程 A commute is the journey that you make when you commute. 上下班路程 [mainly US]

  • The average Los Angeles commute is over 60 miles a day.

    洛杉矶的平均上下班路程为每天60多英里。

3 VERB [usually passive] 减轻(刑罚) If a death sentence or prison sentence is commuted to a less serious punishment, it is changed to that punishment. 减轻(刑罚)

  • His death sentence was commuted to life imprisonment. [be VERB-ed + to]

    他的死刑被减为无期徒刑。

  • Prison sentences have been commuted. [be VERB-ed]

    服刑期得以减短。

SYN reduce, cut, modify, shorten
