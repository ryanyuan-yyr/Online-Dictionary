reconsider
riːkənsɪdəʳ ˌriːkənˈsɪdə ˌrikənˈsɪdər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense reconsiders, present participle reconsidering, past tense , past participle reconsidered
VERB 重新考虑;重新斟酌 If you reconsider a decision or opinion, you think about it and try to decide whether it should be changed. 重新考虑;重新斟酌

  • We want you to reconsider your decision to resign from the board. [VERB noun]

    我们希望你重新考虑辞去董事会职务的决定。

  • This has forced the United States to seriously reconsider its position. [VERB noun]

    这迫使美国重新认真考虑自己的立场。

  • If at the end of two years you still feel the same, we will reconsider. [VERB]

    如果两年后你还是同样的感觉，我们会重新考虑。

SYN rethink, review, revise, think again  
reconsideration

  • The report urges reconsideration of the decision.

    这个报告强烈要求重新考虑这一决定。
