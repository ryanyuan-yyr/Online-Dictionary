tradition
trədɪʃən trəˈdɪʃən
Word forms: plural traditions
1 N-VAR 传统;传说 A tradition is a custom or belief that has existed for a long time. 传统;传说

  • ...the rich traditions of Afro-Cuban music, and dance. [+ of]

    非裔古巴人丰富的音乐、舞蹈传统

  • Mary has carried on the family tradition of giving away plants. [+ of]

    玛丽承袭了向别人赠送植物的家族传统。

  • The story of King Arthur became part of oral tradition.

    亚瑟王的故事成为口头传说的一部分。

SYN established practice, custom, convention, habit  
2 PREP-PHRASE 承袭…风格的;具有…特点的 If you say that something or someone is in the tradition of a person or thing from the past, you mean that they have many features that remind you of that person or thing. 承袭…风格的;具有…特点的

  • They're marvellous pictures in the tradition of Gainsborough. [+ of]

    这些是具有庚斯博罗风格的绝妙画作。

  • …a Catholic novelist in the tradition of Graham Greene.

    与格雷厄姆·格林一脉相承的天主教小说家
