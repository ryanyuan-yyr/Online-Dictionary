subtlety
sʌtəlti ˈsʌtəlti
Word forms: plural subtleties
1 N-COUNT [usually plural] 细微差别;微妙之处 Subtleties are very small details or differences which are not obvious. 细微差别;微妙之处

  • His fascination with the subtleties of human behaviour makes him a good storyteller. [+ of]

    他对人们行为上的细微差别极感兴趣，这使他成了一个很会讲故事的人。

  • When a book goes into translation, all those linguistic subtleties get lost.

    当一部书被翻译成另一种语言后，所有那些语言上的精妙之处就都丢失了。

SYN fine point, refinement, nicety, sophistication  
2 N-UNCOUNT 精妙;精细;细微;微妙 Subtlety is the quality of being not immediately obvious or noticeable, and therefore difficult to describe. 精妙;精细;细微;微妙

  • African dance is vigorous, but full of subtlety, requiring great strength and control.

    非洲舞蹈充满活力，但不乏精妙之处，需要舞者有很强的力量和控制能力。

  • Many of the resulting wines lack the subtlety of the original model. [+ of]

    许多如此酿制的葡萄酒都不像最初的葡萄酒那样耐人品味。

SYN delicacy, softness, delicateness, subtleness  
3 N-UNCOUNT 细致;敏锐 Subtlety is the ability to notice and recognize things which are not obvious, especially small differences between things. 细致;敏锐

  • She analyses herself with great subtlety.

    她非常敏锐地分析自己。

SYN skill, acumen, astuteness, ingenuity  
4 N-UNCOUNT 诡秘;狡猾;巧妙 Subtlety is the ability to use indirect methods to achieve something, rather than doing something that is obvious. 诡秘;狡猾;巧妙

  • They had obviously been hoping to approach the topic with more subtlety.

    他们显然一直希望能更巧妙地切入这一话题。

SYN sensitivity, diplomacy, discretion, delicacy
