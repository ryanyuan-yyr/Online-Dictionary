way of life weɪ əv laɪf
Word forms: plural ways of life
1 N-COUNT [usually singular, oft poss NOUN, adjective NOUN] 典型行为模式;特有生活方式 A way of life is the behaviour and habits that are typical of a particular person or group, or that are chosen by them. 典型行为模式;特有生活方式

  • Mining activities have totally disrupted the traditional way of life of the Yanomami Indians. [+ of]

    采矿已经彻底扰乱了扬诺马米印第安人传统的生活方式。

2 N-COUNT [usually singular] 生活的一部分 If you describe a particular activity as a way of life for someone, you mean that it has become a very important and regular thing in their life, rather than something they do or experience occasionally. 生活的一部分

  • She likes it so much it's become a way of life for her. [+ for]

    她太喜欢它了，它已经成为了她生活的一部分。

  • ...cities where violence is a way of life.

    暴力随处可见的城市
