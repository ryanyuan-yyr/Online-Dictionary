tutor
tjuːtəʳ tuːt- ˈtutər ˈtjutər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense tutors, present participle tutoring, past tense , past participle tutored
1 N-COUNT (英国大学或学院中的)导师;(某些美国大学或学院中的)助教 A tutor is a teacher at a British university or college. In some American universities or colleges, a tutor is a teacher of the lowest rank. (英国大学或学院中的)导师;(某些美国大学或学院中的)助教

  • He is course tutor in archaeology at the University of Southampton. [+ in]

    他是南安普敦大学考古学课程导师。

  • Liam surprised his tutors by twice failing a second year exam.

    利亚姆两次都没能通过大二的一门考试，这让他的导师们很吃惊。

SYN teacher, coach, instructor, educator  
2 N-COUNT 家庭教师;私人教师 A tutor is someone who gives private lessons to one pupil or a very small group of pupils. 家庭教师;私人教师
3 VERB 教授;指导;辅导 If someone tutors a person or a subject, they teach that person or subject. 教授;指导;辅导 [Also VERB]

  • The old man was tutoring her in the stringed instruments. [VERB noun + in]

    那个老人在教她弹弦乐器。

  • ...at the college where I tutored a two-day Introduction to Chairmaking course. [VERB noun]

    在我曾教过为期两天的椅子制作入门课的那个学院

  • I tutored in economics. [VERB + in]

    我教经济学。

SYN teach, educate, school, train
