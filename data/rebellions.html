rebellion
rɪbeliən rɪˈbɛljən
Word forms: plural rebellions
1 N-VAR 谋反；叛乱；反叛 A rebellion is a violent organized action by a large group of people who are trying to change their country's political system. 谋反；叛乱；反叛

  • The British soon put down the rebellion.

    英国人很快平息了叛乱。

  • ...the ruthless and brutal suppression of rebellion.

    对谋反无情、残酷的镇压

SYN resistance, rising, revolution, revolt  
2 N-VAR 内讧；（来自政党内部的）反抗 A situation in which politicians show their opposition to their own party's policies can be referred to as a rebellion . 内讧；（来自政党内部的）反抗

  • There was a Labour rebellion when some left-wing MPs voted against the Chancellor's tax cuts.

    工党内部发生内讧，一些左翼国会议员对财政大臣提出的减税政策投了反对票。
