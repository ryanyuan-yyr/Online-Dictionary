gait
geɪt geɪt
Word forms: plural gaits
N-COUNT [usually singular] 步态；步法 A particular kind of gait is a particular way of walking. 步态；步法 [written]

  • ...a tubby little man in his fifties, with sparse hair and a rolling gait.

    一位50多岁的矮胖男子，头发稀疏，走路晃晃悠悠

  • His movements were clumsy, and his gait peculiarly awkward.

    他动作笨拙，步态尤其难看。

SYN walk, step, bearing, pace
