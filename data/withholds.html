withhold
wɪðhoʊld wɪðˈhəʊld wɪðˈhoʊld wɪθˈhoʊld
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense withholds, present participle withholding, past tense , past participle withheld/wɪðheld/
1 VERB 拒绝给予;扣留 If you withhold something that someone wants, you do not let them have it. 拒绝给予;扣留 [formal] [Also V from n/-ing]

  • Police withheld the dead boy's name yesterday until relatives could be told. [VERB noun]

    警方昨天拒绝在通知亲属前透露死亡男孩的姓名。

  • Financial aid for Britain has been withheld. [VERB noun]

    向英国提供的经济援助已经被扣。

  • The captain decided to withhold the terrible news even from his officers. [VERB noun from noun]

    船长决定哪怕是对高级船员也要封锁这个可怕的消息。

SYN keep secret, keep, refuse, hide
