sarcasm
sɑːʳkæzəm ˈsɑrˌkæzəm
N-UNCOUNT 讽刺;嘲笑;挖苦 Sarcasm is speech or writing which actually means the opposite of what it seems to say. Sarcasm is usually intended to mock or insult someone. 讽刺;嘲笑;挖苦

  • 'What a pity,' Graham said with a hint of sarcasm.

    “太遗憾了，”格雷厄姆略带挖苦地说道。

  • His voice was heavy with sarcasm.

    他的话语里明显有讽刺意味。

  • Fred ignored the sarcasm.

    弗雷德对讽刺挖苦置之不理。

SYN irony, satire, cynicism, contempt
