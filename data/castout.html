cast out
PHRASAL VERB 赶走;抛弃;丢弃 To cast out something or someone means to get rid of them because you do not like or need them, or do not want to take responsibility for them. 赶走;抛弃;丢弃 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • One of the roles which science plays is that of casting out superstition. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    科学发挥的一个作用就是破除迷信。

  • To be cast out from civilization was the worst fate that could possibly befall me. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    被文明社会抛弃是可能降临在我身上的最悲惨的命运。
