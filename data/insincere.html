insincere
ɪnsɪnsɪəʳ ˌɪnsɪnˈsɪr
ADJ-GRADED 不诚恳的;虚伪的 If you say that someone is insincere, you are being critical of them because they say things they do not really mean, usually pleasant, admiring, or encouraging things. 不诚恳的;虚伪的 [disapproval]

  • Some people are so terribly insincere you can never tell if they are telling the truth.

    有些人非常虚伪,你根本说不准他们是否在讲实话。

  • John found himself surrounded by insincere flattery.

    约翰发现自己为虚情假意的奉承阿谀所包围。

SYN deceitful, lying, false, pretended  
insincerity

  • Too many superlatives lend a note of insincerity.

    太多的“最如何如何”让人觉得不够真诚。
