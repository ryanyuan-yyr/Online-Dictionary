twang
twæŋ twæŋ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense twangs, present participle twanging, past tense , past participle twanged
1 V-ERG (使)发出拨弦声;(使)发出嘣的一声 If you twang something such as a tight string or elastic band, or if it twangs, it makes a fairly loud, ringing sound because it has been pulled and then released. (使)发出拨弦声;(使)发出嘣的一声

  • ...people who sat at the back of class and twanged an elastic band. [VERB noun]

    坐在教室后面弹橡皮筋的这些人

  • His guitar makes a noise like a wooden ruler being twanged upon a desk… [VERB noun]

    他的吉他发出一声仿佛木尺弹在课桌上的噪音。

  • The song is a fiery mix of twanging guitar with relentless drumming. [VERB-ing]

    那是一首夹杂着撩拨的吉他声和密集的鼓点的激情四射的歌曲。

  • The fiddle began to twang. [VERB]

    小提琴声渐起。

Twang is also a noun.

  • Something gave a loud discordant twang.

    什么东西突然发出了响亮刺耳的“嘣”的一声。

  • He fitted a pebble into the catapult and pulled back the elastic. Twang!

    他把石子放进弹弓里，然后往回拉动橡皮筋。“嘣”！

2 N-COUNT [usually singular] 鼻音 A twang is a quality in someone's way of speaking in which sound seems to be coming through the nose. 鼻音

  • …the nasal twang of his voice.

    他说话时的鼻音

  • ...her broad Australian twang.

    她浓重的澳大利亚鼻音
