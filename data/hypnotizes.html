hypnotize
hɪpnətaɪz ˈhɪpnəˌtaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense hypnotizes, present participle hypnotizing, past tense , past participle hypnotized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 hypnotise
1 VERB 对…施催眠术;使进入催眠状态 If someone hypnotizes you, they put you into a state in which you seem to be asleep but can still see, hear, or respond to things said to you. 对…施催眠术;使进入催眠状态

  • A hypnotherapist will hypnotize you and will stop you from smoking. [VERB noun]

    一位催眠治疗师将对你实施催眠，从而帮你戒烟。

  • Surprisingly, the ability to hypnotize yourself can be learnt in a single session. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    出人意料的是，自我催眠术只要上一次课就可以学会。

SYN mesmerize, put in a trance, put to sleep  
2 VERB [usually passive] 使着迷;使沉醉 If you are hypnotized by someone or something, you are so fascinated by them that you cannot think of anything else. 使着迷;使沉醉

  • He's hypnotized by that black hair and that white face. [be VERB-ed]

    他为那乌黑的头发和白皙的面庞所倾倒。

  • Davey sat as if hypnotized by the sound of Nick's voice. [VERB-ed]

    戴维坐在那里，好像被尼克的声音迷住了似的。

SYN fascinate, absorb, entrance, magnetize
