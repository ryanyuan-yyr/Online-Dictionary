interpose
ɪntəʳpoʊz ˌɪntərˈpoʊz ˈɪntərˌpoʊz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense interposes, present participle interposing, past tense , past participle interposed
1 VERB 插入;使介于…之间 If you interpose something between two people or things, you place it between them. 插入;使介于…之间 [formal]

  • Police had to interpose themselves between the two rival groups. [V pron-refl + between]

    警方不得不对这两个对立团体进行干预。

  • The work interposes a glass plate between two large circular mirrors. [V n + between]

    该艺术品在两面圆形大镜子中间插入一块玻璃板。

SYN intervene, step in, interfere, intermediate  
2 VERB 插话 If you interpose, you interrupt with a comment or question. 插话 [formal] [Also VERB n]

  • 'He rang me just now,' she interposed. [VERB with quote]

    “他刚刚给我打过电话,”她插嘴说。

  • Jacob was silent so long that Livvy interposed. [VERB]

    雅各布沉默的时间太长了,莉薇只好插话了。

SYN interrupt, insert, interject, put in
