bell
bel bɛl
Word forms: plural bells
1 N-COUNT 铃 A bell is a device that makes a ringing sound and is used to give a signal or to attract people's attention. 铃

  • I had just enough time to finish eating before the bell rang and I was off to my first class.

    我刚吃完饭铃声就响了，于是我赶去上第一堂课。

  • I've been ringing the door bell, there's no answer.

    我一直按门铃，但是没有人应门。

2 N-COUNT 钟 A bell is a hollow metal object shaped like a cup which has a piece hanging inside it that hits the sides and makes a sound. 钟

  • My brother, Neville, was born on a Sunday, when all the church bells were ringing.

    我弟弟内维尔是星期天出生的，当时所有的教堂钟声齐鸣。

3 PHRASE 十分清晰；非常清楚 If something is as clear as a bell, it is very clear indeed. 十分清晰；非常清楚

  • There are 80 of these pictures and they're all as clear as a bell.

    这些照片一共有80张，都非常清晰。

4 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 给…打电话 If you give someone a bell, you telephone them. 给…打电话 [British, informal]

  • I was going to give you a bell tomorrow.

    我明天给你打电话。

5 PHRASE 听起来耳熟（但记不真切） If you say that something rings a bell, you mean that it reminds you of something, but you cannot remember exactly what it is. 听起来耳熟（但记不真切） [informal]

  • The description of one of the lads is definitely familiar. It rings a bell.

    对其中一个小伙子的描述确实似曾熟悉。听起来挺耳熟。

6 PHRASE [verb-link PHRASE] 非常健康的；完好无损的 If something is as sound as a bell it is healthy and not damaged in any way. 非常健康的；完好无损的

  • The horse returned sound as a bell.

    这匹马安然无恙地回来了。
