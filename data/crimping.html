crimp
krɪmp krɪmp
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense crimps, present participle crimping, past tense , past participle crimped
1 VERB 把(织物或面团)压出褶皱 If you crimp something such as a piece of fabric or pastry, you make small folds in it. 把(织物或面团)压出褶皱

  • Crimp the edges to seal them tightly. [VERB noun]

    把边缘折起来以便封紧。

2 VERB 限制;减少 To crimp something means to restrict or reduce it. 限制;减少 [US]

  • The dollar's recent strength is crimping overseas sales and profits. [VERB noun]

    美元最近的坚挺影响了国外的销量和利润。
