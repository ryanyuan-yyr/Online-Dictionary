lordship
ˈlɔːdʃɪp ˈlɔrdˌʃɪp
N-VOC; N-PROPER （对法官、主教和男性贵族的尊称）阁下，大人 You use the expressions Your Lordship ,His Lordship, or Their Lordships when you are addressing or referring to a judge, bishop, or male member of the nobility. （对法官、主教和男性贵族的尊称）阁下，大人

  • My name is Richard Savage, your Lordship.

    尊敬的阁下，我叫理查德·萨维奇。

  • His Lordship expressed the hope that the Law Commission might look at the subject.

    大人表达了他的愿望，希望法律委员会研究一下这一问题。
