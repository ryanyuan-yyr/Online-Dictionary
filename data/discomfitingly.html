discomfit
dɪskʌmfɪt dɪsˈkʌmfɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense discomfits, present participle discomfiting, past tense , past participle discomfited
VERB 使困惑;使窘迫;使尴尬 If you are discomfited by something, it causes you to feel slightly embarrassed or confused. 使困惑;使窘迫;使尴尬 [written]

  • He will be particularly discomfited by the minister's dismissal of his plan. [be VERB-ed]

    部长对他计划的不理会将使他特别尴尬。

  • The opposition leader has regularly discomfited him in parliament. [VERB noun]

    反对派领导人常搞得他在国会中下不来台。

SYN frustrate, beat, defeat, worst  
discomfited

  • Will wanted to do likewise, but felt too discomfited.

    威尔也想照着做，但觉得太难堪了。
