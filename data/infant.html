infant
ɪnfənt ˈɪnfənt
Word forms: plural infants
1 N-COUNT [oft NOUN n] 婴儿 An infant is a baby or very young child. 婴儿 [formal]

  • ...young mums with infants in prams.

    用婴儿车推着宝宝的年轻妈妈

  • The family were forced to flee with their infant son.

    那家人被迫带着他们尚在襁褓中的儿子逃走了。

  • ...the infant mortality rate in Britain.

    英国的婴儿夭折率

SYN baby, child, babe, toddler  
2 N-COUNT [usually plural] (5-7岁的)幼儿 Infants are children between the ages of five and seven, who go to an infant school. (5-7岁的)幼儿 [British]
You use the infants to refer to a school or class for such children.幼儿园;幼儿班

  • You've been my best friend ever since we started in the infants.

    自从我们上幼儿园起，你就是我最好的朋友。

3 ADJ [ADJ n] 供婴儿用的 Infant means designed especially for very young children. 供婴儿用的

  • ...an infant carrier in the back of a car.

    汽车后部的婴儿座椅

4 ADJ [ADJ n] (组织、运动等)初期的,初创的 An infant organization or system is new and has not developed very much. (组织、运动等)初期的,初创的

  • The infant company was based in Germany.

    这家新公司总部在德国。

  • ...the infant health service.

    处于初期阶段的公共医疗卫生服务

SYN early, new, developing, young
