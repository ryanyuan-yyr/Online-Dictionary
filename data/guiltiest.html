guilty
gɪlti ˈgɪlti
Word forms: comparative guiltier, superlative guiltiest
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 内疚的；感到愧疚的 If you feel guilty, you feel unhappy because you think that you have done something wrong or have failed to do something which you should have done. 内疚的；感到愧疚的 [Also + about]

  • I feel so guilty, leaving all this to you.

    把这些事都留给你去做，我真感到过意不去。

  • When she saw me she looked guilty.

    她见到我时一脸歉疚。

SYN ashamed, sorry, rueful, sheepish  
guiltily

  • He glanced guiltily over his shoulder.

    他很内疚地回头一瞥。

2 ADJ [ADJ n] 罪恶的 Guilty is used of an action or fact that you feel guilty about. guilty conscience→see: conscience  罪恶的

  • Many may be keeping it a guilty secret.

    许多人或许保守着这个罪恶的秘密。

  • I leave with a guilty sense of relief.

    我带着一种罪恶的解脱感离开。

3 ADJ 犯罪的；有罪的 If someone is guilty of a crime or offence, they have committed that crime or offence. 犯罪的；有罪的

  • They were found guilty of murder. [+ of]

    他们被判谋杀罪成立。

  • He pleaded guilty to causing actual bodily harm.

    他承认确实犯有人身伤害罪。

SYN culpable, responsible, convicted, to blame  
4 ADJ 有过失的 If someone is guilty of doing something wrong, they have done that thing. 有过失的

  • He claimed Mr Brooke had been guilty of a 'gross error of judgment'. [+ of]

    他宣称布鲁克先生犯有“判断失误的重大过错”。

  • They will consider whether or not he has been guilty of serious professional misconduct. [+ of]

    他们将考虑他是否犯有玩忽职守的重大过失。
