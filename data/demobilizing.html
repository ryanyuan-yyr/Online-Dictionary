demobilize
diːmoʊbɪlaɪz diˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense demobilizes, present participle demobilizing, past tense , past participle demobilized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 demobilise
1 V-ERG 遣散，解散(部队) If a country or armed force demobilizes its troops, or if its troops demobilize, its troops are released from service and allowed to go home. 遣散，解散(部队)

  • Both sides have agreed to demobilize 70% of their armies. [VERB noun]

    双方都同意各自遣散70%的军队。

  • It is unlikely that the rebels will agree to demobilise. [VERB]

    叛军不太可能会同意解散武装。

SYN disband, separate, break up, scatter  
demobilization

  • ...the demobilisation of a 100,000 strong army.

    一支10万人部队的遣散

2 VERB [usually passive] 使复员;使退伍 If someone has been demobilized, they have completed their service in the armed forces of their country. 使复员;使退伍 [US]

  • I spent one year in the service and did not come back to the United States until after I was demobilized. [be VERB-ed]

    我在部队里呆了一年，直到复员以后才回到美国。
