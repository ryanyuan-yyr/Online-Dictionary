oblique
oʊbliːk oʊˈblik əˈblik oʊˈblaɪk
1 ADJ-GRADED 不直截了当的;拐弯抹角的 If you describe a statement as oblique, you mean that is not expressed directly or openly, making it difficult to understand. 不直截了当的;拐弯抹角的

  • It was an oblique reference to his mother.

    这影射到了他的母亲。

  • Mr Golding delivered an oblique warning, talking of the danger of sudden action.

    戈尔丁先生谈及草率行事的危险，这是他在委婉地发出警告。

SYN indirect, implied, roundabout, backhanded  
obliquely

  • He obliquely referred to the US, Britain and Saudi Arabia.

    他旁敲侧击地提到美国、英国和沙特阿拉伯。

2 ADJ [usually ADJ n] （线或角）斜的，倾斜的，偏斜的 An oblique line is a straight line that is not horizontal or vertical. An oblique angle is any angle other than a right angle. （线或角）斜的，倾斜的，偏斜的

  • It lies between the plain and the sea at an oblique angle to the coastline.

    它位于平原与大海之间，跟海岸线形成一个斜角。

SYN slanting, angled, sloped, sloping  
obliquely

  • This muscle runs obliquely downwards inside the abdominal cavity.

    这块肌肉在腹腔内斜向下延伸。
