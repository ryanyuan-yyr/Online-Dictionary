endear
ɪndɪəʳ ɛnˈdɪr ɪnˈdɪr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense endears, present participle endearing, past tense , past participle endeared
VERB 使受喜爱;使受青睐 If something endears you to someone or if you endear yourself to them, you become popular with them and well liked by them. 使受喜爱;使受青睐

  • Their taste for gambling has endeared them to Las Vegas casino owners. [VERB noun + to]

    他们因好赌而深受拉斯维加斯赌场老板的欢迎。

  • He has endeared himself to the American public. [VERB pronoun-reflexive + to]

    他已经赢得了美国民众的好感。

SYN attract, draw, bind, engage
