contest
kɒntest kəntest kənˈtɛst ˈkɑnˌtɛst
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense contests, present participle contesting, past tense , past participle contested
The noun is pronounced /'kɒntest/. The verb is pronounced /kən'test/. 名词读作 /'kɒntest/，动词读作 /kən'test/。
1 N-COUNT 竞赛;比赛 A contest is a competition or game in which people try to win. 竞赛;比赛

  • Few contests in the recent history of British boxing have been as thrilling.

    英国近年的拳击比赛中很少有如此激动人心的。

  • ...a writing contest.

    写作比赛

SYN competition, game, match, trial  
2See also:beauty contest 
3 N-COUNT (权力或控制权的)争夺，竞争 A contest is a struggle to win power or control. (权力或控制权的)争夺，竞争

  • The state election due in November will be the last such ballot before next year's presidential contest.

    定于11月举行的州选举将是明年总统竞选前最后一次此类投票选举。

  • ...a clear contest between church and state. [+ between]

    教会与政府之间明显的争斗

4 VERB 争取赢得(选举、比赛等);角逐 If someone contests an election or competition, they take part in it and try to win it. 争取赢得(选举、比赛等);角逐 [mainly British]

  • He quickly won his party's nomination to contest the elections. [VERB noun]

    他很快获得了本党提名,角逐大选。

  • ...a closely contested regional flower show. [VERB-ed]

    各方争奇斗艳、难分高下的地区花卉展

SYN compete in, take part in, fight in, go in for  
5 VERB 争辩;对…提出异议 If you contest a statement or decision, you object to it formally because you think it is wrong or unreasonable. 争辩;对…提出异议

  • Your former employer has to reply within 14 days in order to contest the case. [VERB noun]

    你的前任雇主若对本案存有异议，必须在14天内予以答复。

  • Gender discrimination is a hotly contested issue. [VERB-ed]

    性别歧视是一个争论激烈的话题。

SYN oppose, question, challenge, argue
