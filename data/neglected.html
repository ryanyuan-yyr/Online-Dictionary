neglect
nɪglekt nɪˈglɛkt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense neglects, present participle neglecting, past tense , past participle neglected
1 VERB 忽视;忽略;疏于照看 If you neglect someone or something, you fail to look after them properly. 忽视;忽略;疏于照看

  • The woman denied that she had neglected her child. [VERB noun]

    那位女士否认疏于照管自己的孩子。

  • Feed plants and they grow, neglect them and they suffer. [VERB noun]

    人勤地生宝;人懒地长草。

  • ...an ancient and neglected church. [VERB-ed]

    疏于维护的古教堂

SYN disregard, ignore, leave alone, turn your back on  
Neglect is also a noun.

  • The town's old quayside is collapsing after years of neglect.

    镇上老码头区多年来疏于维护，快要塌陷了。

  • Niwano's business began to suffer from neglect.

    尼瓦诺的生意因为疏于管理而开始出现问题。

2 VERB 不予重视;忽视 If you neglect someone or something, you fail to give them the amount of attention that they deserve. 不予重视;忽视

  • He'd given too much to his career, worked long hours, neglected her. [VERB noun]

    他为事业付出太多，长时间地工作，忽视了她。

  • If you are not careful, children tend to neglect their homework. [VERB noun]

    如果你不注意的话，孩子们往往会不重视他们的家庭作业。

SYN shirk, forget, overlook, omit  
neglected

  • The fact that she is not coming today makes her grandmother feel lonely and neglected.

    她今天不来，这让她奶奶感到孤独，觉得受了冷落。

  • ... a neglected aspect of London's forgotten history.

    伦敦湮没的历史中被忽略了的一个方面

  • The journal she had begun lay neglected on her bedside table.

    她早就开始看的那本杂志搁在床头柜上，好久没翻了。

3 VERB 疏忽;疏漏 If you neglect to do something that you ought to do or neglect your duty, you fail to do it. 疏忽;疏漏

  • We often neglect to make proper use of our bodies. [VERB to-infinitive]

    我们经常忘记该合理地使用我们的身体。

  • They never neglect their duties. [VERB noun]

    他们从未玩忽职守。
