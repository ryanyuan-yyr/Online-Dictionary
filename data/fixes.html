fix
fɪks fɪks
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense fixes, present participle fixing, past tense , past participle fixed
1 VERB 固定;安装 If something is fixed somewhere, it is attached there firmly or securely. 固定;安装

  • It is fixed on the wall. [be VERB-ed prep./adv.]

    它固定在墙上。

  • Most blinds can be fixed directly to the top of the window-frame. [be VERB-ed prep./adv.]

    大多数窗帘都可直接安装在窗框上边。

  • He fixed a bayonet to the end of his rifle. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他在步枪口上安好了刺刀。

2 VERB 确定，决定，选定(日期、价格、政策等) If you fix something, for example a date, price, or policy, you decide and say exactly what it will be. 确定，决定，选定(日期、价格、政策等)

  • He's going to fix a time when I can see him. [VERB noun]

    他会确定一个我和他会面的时间。

  • The date of the election was fixed. [VERB noun]

    选举日期定下来了。

  • The prices of milk and cereals are fixed annually. [be VERB-ed]

    牛奶和谷类食物的价格每年确定一次。

SYN decide, set, name, choose  
3 VERB 安排;弄妥 If you fix something for someone, you arrange for it to happen or you organize it for them. 安排;弄妥

  • I've fixed it for you to see Bonnie Lachlan. [VERB noun + for]

    你去见邦尼·拉克伦的事我已经安排好了。

  • It's fixed. He's going to meet us at the airport. [be VERB-ed]

    已经安排好了，他会在机场接我们。

  • They thought that their relatives would be able to fix the visas. [VERB noun]

    他们以为他们的亲戚们会把签证办好。

  • He vanished after you fixed him with a job. [VERB noun + with]

    你给他安排好工作后他就突然不见了。

  • We fixed for the team to visit our headquarters. [VERB + for]

    我们安排了该工作组参观我们的总部。

  • They'd fixed yesterday that Mike'd be in late today. [VERB that]

    他们昨天已经确定迈克今天会来晚一些。

SYN arrange, organize, sort out, see to  
4 VERB 修理;维修 If you fix something which is damaged or which does not work properly, you repair it. 修理;维修

  • He cannot fix the electricity. [VERB noun]

    他不会修电路。

  • If something is broken, we get it fixed. [get/have n V-ed]

    如果什么东西坏了，我们就找人来修理。

SYN repair, mend, service, sort  
5 VERB 处理，解决(问题等) If you fix a problem or a bad situation, you deal with it and make it satisfactory. 处理，解决(问题等)

  • It's not too late to fix the problem, although time is clearly getting short. [VERB noun]

    虽然时间越来越紧迫，但现在处理这个问题还为时未晚。

  • Fixing a 40-year-old wrong does not mean, however, that history can be undone. [VERB-ing]

    但是，平反一件40年的冤案并不意味着历史可以重写。

6 N-COUNT [usually adjective NOUN] 解决方案(或措施) You can refer to a solution to a problem as a fix . 解决方案(或措施) [informal]

  • Many of those changes could just be a temporary fix.

    那些变革中可能有很多只是权宜之计。

SYN solution, result, answer, resolution  
7See also:quick fix 
8 V-ERG (目光)专注于，凝视;目不转睛地看 If you fix your eyes on someone or something or if your eyes fix on them, you look at them with complete attention. (目光)专注于，凝视;目不转睛地看

  • She fixes her steel-blue eyes on an unsuspecting local official. [VERB noun + on]

    她那灰蓝色的眼睛紧盯着一个毫无戒心的地方官员。

  • Her soft brown eyes fixed on Kelly. [VERB + on]

    她那双温柔的棕色眼睛紧盯着凯利。

  • The child kept her eyes fixed on the wall behind him. [VERB-ed]

    这个小女孩眼睛一直紧盯着他身后的那堵墙。

9 VERB [no passive] (用某种表情)看着，望着 If you fix someone with a particular kind of expression, you look at them in that way. (用某种表情)看着，望着 [literary]

  • He took her hand and fixed her with a look of deep concern. [VERB noun with noun]

    他抓住她的手，表情极其关切地看着她。

  • He fixed me with a lopsided grin. [VERB noun with noun]

    他冲我歪嘴笑了笑。

10 VERB 将(注意力)集中在;专心考虑 If you fix your attention on someone or something, you think about them with complete attention. 将(注意力)集中在;专心考虑

  • Fix your attention on the practicalities of financing your schemes. [VERB noun on noun]

    重点考虑为你的计划融资是否可行。

  • Attention is fixed on the stock market. [VERB noun on noun]

    重点关注的是证券市场。

  • She kept her mind fixed on the practical problems which faced her. [VERB-ed]

    她专心思考面临的一些实际问题。

SYN focus, direct at, level at, fasten on  
11 VERB 牢记，铭记，镌刻(在头脑中) If someone or something is fixed in your mind, you remember them well, for example because they are very important, interesting, or unusual. 牢记，铭记，镌刻(在头脑中)

  • Leonard was now fixed in his mind. [be VERB-ed + in]

    伦纳德现在已经令他无法忘怀。

  • Amy watched the child's intent face eagerly, trying to fix it in her mind. [VERB noun + in]

    埃米急切地看着孩子那张神情专注的脸，想把它记在脑海里。

12 VERB 把(枪、相机、雷达)对准(或瞄准、指向) If someone fixes a gun, camera, or radar on something, they point it at that thing. 把(枪、相机、雷达)对准(或瞄准、指向)

  • The US crew fixed its radar on the Turkish ship.

    美国船员将雷达对准那艘土耳其船。

  • The bore of the gun remained fixed on me. [VERB noun on noun]

    枪口依然指着我。

13 VERB (通常用雷达或电子设备)找到，弄清，确定(确切位置) If you fix the position of something, you find out exactly where it is, usually by using radar or electronic equipment. (通常用雷达或电子设备)找到，弄清，确定(确切位置)

  • He had not been able to fix his position. [VERB noun]

    他一直未能确定他所在的位置。

  • The satellite fixes positions by making repeated observations of each star. [VERB noun]

    卫星通过对每颗恒星反复观察进行定位。

  • ...accurate position fixing. [VERB-ing]

    准确定位

Fix is also a noun.

  • The army hasn't been able to get a fix on the transmitter.

    军方一直未能确定发射台的准确位置。

14 N-SING 了解;理解;认识 If you get a fix on someone or something, you have a clear idea or understanding of them. 了解;理解;认识 [informal]

  • It's been hard to get a steady fix on what's going on.

    要对正在发生的事情保持持续的关注和了解并不容易。

15 VERB 做，准备(食物或饮料) If you fix some food or a drink for someone, you make it or prepare it for them. 做，准备(食物或饮料)

  • Sarah fixed some food for us. [VERB noun + for]

    萨拉为我们准备了一些吃的。

  • Let me fix you a drink. [VERB noun noun]

    我给你弄杯饮料吧。

  • Scotty stayed behind to fix lunch. [VERB noun]

    斯科蒂留下来做午饭。

SYN prepare, make, cook, put together  
16 VERB [no passive] 整理，收拾(头发、衣服或妆容) If you fix your hair, clothes, or make-up, you arrange or adjust them so you look neat and tidy, showing you have taken care with your appearance. 整理，收拾(头发、衣服或妆容) [informal]

  • 'I've got to fix my hair,' I said and retreated to my bedroom. [VERB noun]

    “我得把头发梳理一下，”我说罢回到了卧室。

  • She called a cab, fixed her face, and scrawled a hasty note to Brian. [VERB noun]

    她叫了辆出租车，补了一下妆，匆匆地给布莱恩写了张便条。

SYN neaten, arrange, adjust, tidy  
17 VERB [usually passive] 矫正(牙齿);整形 If you have your teeth fixed, you have treatment from a dentist to make your teeth even, straight, and white. 矫正(牙齿);整形 [informal]

  • The PR man suggested that I might benefit from getting my teeth fixed. [VERB-ed]

    公关顾问建议说，整牙也许对我有好处。

  • I wonder if Tom ever had his teeth fixed anywhere else. [VERB-ed]

    我在想汤姆是不是在别的地方整过牙。

18 VERB 在(比赛、竞选等活动中)作弊，做手脚，操纵 If someone fixes a race, election, contest, or other event, they make unfair or illegal arrangements or use deception to affect the result. 在(比赛、竞选等活动中)作弊，做手脚，操纵 [disapproval]

  • They offered opposing players bribes to fix a decisive league match against Valenciennes. [VERB noun]

    他们贿赂对方队员，在联赛对阵瓦朗谢讷队的一场决定性比赛中作弊。

  • We didn't 'fix' anything. It'll be seen as it happens. [VERB noun]

    我们没做任何手脚，该什么样就会什么样。

  • The debate seems, in retrospect, to have been fixed from the beginning. [VERB noun]

    回想起来，这场辩论好像从一开始就被人操纵了。

  • ...this week's report of match-fixing. [VERB-ing]

    本周有关比赛作弊的报道

SYN rig, set up [informal] , influence, manipulate  
Fix is also a noun.

  • It's all a fix, a deal they've made.

    这完全是一场骗局，是他们之间的交易。

19 VERB 操纵，操控(价格) If you accuse someone of fixing prices, you accuse them of making unfair arrangements to charge a particular price for something, rather than allowing market forces to decide it. 操纵，操控(价格) [business, disapproval]

  • ...a suspected cartel that had fixed the price of steel for the construction market. [VERB noun]

    涉嫌操控建筑市场钢材价格的卡特尔

  • The company is currently in dispute with the government over price fixing. [VERB-ing]

    该公司目前与政府在是否存在价格操控问题上存在分歧。

21 N-COUNT [n NOUN] (维持身体或心理需要所需的)一个剂量，一定数量 You can use fix to refer to an amount of something which a person gets or wants and which helps them physically or psychologically to survive. (维持身体或心理需要所需的)一个剂量，一定数量 [informal]

    她的问题在于她每天都想要在媒体上曝一曝光。

  • ...a quick energy fix.

    快速能量增补剂

SYN dose, hit, shot, injection  
22 N-SING [a NOUN, usu in/into N] (尤指由自己引起的)困境，窘境 If you are in a fix, you are in a difficult situation, especially one that you have caused for yourself. (尤指由自己引起的)困境，窘境 [informal]
