billow
bɪloʊ ˈbɪloʊ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense billows, present participle billowing, past tense , past participle billowed
1 VERB （布制品）鼓起 When something made of cloth billows, it swells out and moves slowly in the wind. （布制品）鼓起

  • The curtains billowed in the breeze. [VERB]

    窗帘在微风中鼓了起来。

  • Her pink dress billowed out around her. [V out]

    她粉红色的连衣裙鼓了起来。

  • ...the billowing sails. [VERB-ing]

    鼓起的风帆

SYN surge, roll, expand, swell  
2 VERB （烟或云）翻滚，滚滚向上 When smoke or cloud billows, it moves slowly upwards or across the sky. （烟或云）翻滚，滚滚向上

  • ...thick plumes of smoke billowing from factory chimneys. [VERB prep./adv.]

    工厂烟囱冒出的滚滚浓烟

  • Steam billowed out from under the bonnet. [VERB prep./adv.]

    引擎盖下喷出滚滚的蒸汽。

  • ...billowing clouds of cigarette smoke. [VERB-ing]

    烟雾缭绕

SYN flow, course, rush, stream  
3 N-COUNT 腾起的一大团（烟、尘等） A billow of smoke or dust is a large mass of it rising slowly into the air. 腾起的一大团（烟、尘等）

  • ...smoke stacks belching billows of almost solid black smoke. [+ of]

    喷出滚滚黑烟的烟囱

SYN surge, wave, flow, rush
