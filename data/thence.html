thence
ðens ðɛns θɛns
1 ADV [oft ADV before v] 从那里;由此 Thence means from a particular place, especially when you are giving directions about how to get somewhere. 从那里;由此 [formal, old-fashioned]

  • I ran straight up to Columbia County, then turned East, came down the Harlem Valley and thence home.

    我一直跑到哥伦比亚县，然后向东转，沿着哈勒姆山谷，从那里回到家。

  • The mosaics were sent to Munich, and thence to Geneva.

    那些马赛克被送到慕尼黑，从那里又送到日内瓦。

2 ADV [oft ADV before v] 然后，接着（指状态的变化） Thence is used to say that something changes from one state or condition to another. 然后，接着（指状态的变化） [formal, old-fashioned]

  • ...the conversion of sunlight into heat and thence into electricity.

    将太阳能转化成热能，然后再转化成电能
