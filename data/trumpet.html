trumpet
trʌmpɪt ˈtrʌmpɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense trumpets, present participle trumpeting, past tense , past participle trumpeted
1 N-VAR 小号;喇叭 A trumpet is a musical instrument of the brass family which plays quite high notes. 小号;喇叭
2 VERB 大力鼓吹;积极宣扬;大力宣传 If someone trumpets something that they are proud of or that they think is important, they speak about it publicly in a very forceful way. 大力鼓吹;积极宣扬;大力宣传 [Also VERB n]

  • The government has been trumpeting tourism as a growth industry. [VERB noun + as]

    政府一直积极宣扬旅游业是快速发展型产业。

  • …Mark Morris, who is trumpeted as the dance talent of his generation… [VERB noun as noun]

    马克·莫里斯，被吹捧为他那一代的舞蹈奇才

  • Nobody should be trumpeting about chemical weapons. [VERB + about]

    任何人都不应大肆鼓吹化学武器。

  • It was trumpeted that the nation's health was improving. [be VERB-ed that]

    人们大力宣传该国的健康状况正在改善。

  • …the much trumpeted 'tax cuts' in the 1980s. [VERB-ed]

    20 世纪 80 年代被大肆宣扬的“减税”

SYN proclaim, advertise, extol, tout [informal]  
3 VERB (象)吼叫 When an elephant trumpets, it makes a loud sound. (象)吼叫

  • The elephants trumpeted and stamped their feet at their approach. [VERB]

    当他们靠近时，大象又吼又跺脚。

4 blow your own trumpet→see: blow
