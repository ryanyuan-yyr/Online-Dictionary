merchandising
mɜːʳtʃəndaɪzɪŋ ˈmɜːtʃənˌdaɪzɪŋ ˈmɜrtʃənˌdaɪzɪŋ
1 N-UNCOUNT (与影片、运动队、流行乐队等相关的)附带商品，文化衍生品 Merchandising consists of goods such as toys and clothes that are linked with something such as a film, sports team, or pop group. (与影片、运动队、流行乐队等相关的)附带商品，文化衍生品

  • We are selling the full range of World Cup merchandising.

    我们出售世界杯的所有纪念商品。

  • The club says it will make increasing amounts from merchandising.

    俱乐部称会从衍生商品中获得越来越多的利润。

SYN products, goods, produce, stock  
2 N-UNCOUNT 推销;商品拓销;销售规划 Merchandising is used to refer to the way shops and businesses organize the sale of their products, for example the way they are displayed and the prices that are chosen. 推销;商品拓销;销售规划 [mainly US, business]

  • Company executives say revamped merchandising should help Macy's earnings to grow.

    公司主管说销售规划改进后应该能够帮助梅西公司提高收益。
