appointment
əpɔɪntmənt əˈpɔɪntmənt
Word forms: plural appointments
1 N-VAR [usually with poss] 任命；委派；指派 The appointment of a person to a particular job is the choice of that person to do it. 任命；委派；指派

  • His appointment to the Cabinet would please the right-wing.

    他被任命为内阁成员会让右翼党派很高兴。

  • ...his appointment as foreign minister in 1985.

    在1985年他被委任为外交部长

SYN selection, naming, election, choosing  
2 N-COUNT 职位；职务 An appointment is a job or position of responsibility. 职位；职务

  • Mr Fay is to take up an appointment as a researcher with the Royal Society.

    费伊先生将担任皇家学会研究员的职务。

SYN job, office, position, post  
3 N-COUNT [NOUN to-infinitive] 约会；约定 If you have an appointment with someone, you have arranged to see them at a particular time, usually in connection with their work or for a serious purpose. 约会；约定

  • She has an appointment with her accountant. [+ with]

    她和她的会计约好了见面。

  • I made an appointment to see a specialist.

    我约好去见一位专家。

  • ...a dental appointment.

    看牙医的预约

SYN meeting, interview, date, session  
4 PHRASE 经过预约 If something can be done by appointment, people can arrange in advance to do it at a particular time. 经过预约

  • Viewing is by appointment only.

    参观须事先预约。
