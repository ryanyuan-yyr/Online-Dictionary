pre-empt priːempt prɪˈɛmpt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense pre-empts, present participle pre-empting, past tense , past participle pre-empted
in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 preempt
VERB 抢先行动；预先制止；先发制人 If you pre-empt an action, you prevent it from happening by doing something which makes it unnecessary or impossible. 抢先行动；预先制止；先发制人

  • You can pre-empt pain by taking a painkiller at the first warning sign. [VERB noun]

    刚出现征兆时，就服用止痛片来预防疼痛。

  • He pre-empted any decision to sack him. [VERB noun]

    他预先阻止了所有解雇他的决定。

  • The government pre-empted a threatened strike at the state-owned copper company. [VERB noun]

    政府先发制人，制止了国有铜矿公司可能发生的一次罢工。

SYN forestall, anticipate, prevent, steal a march on
