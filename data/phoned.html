phone
foʊn foʊn
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense phones, present participle phoning, past tense , past participle phoned
1 N-SING [oft by NOUN] 电话 The phone is an electrical system that you use to talk to someone else in another place, by dialling a number on a piece of equipment and speaking into it. 电话

  • You can buy insurance over the phone.

    你可以通过电话购买保险。

  • She looked forward to talking to her daughter by phone.

    她盼望着能打电话和女儿交谈。

  • Do you have an address and phone number for him?

    你有他的地址和电话号码么？

2 N-COUNT 电话机 The phone is the piece of equipment that you use when you dial someone's phone number and talk to them. 电话机

  • Two minutes later the phone rang.

    两分钟后电话响了。

  • Doug's 14-year-old son Jamie answered the phone.

    道格14岁的儿子杰米接了电话。

3See also:cellular phone  mobile phone 
4 N-SING 电话听筒 If you say that someone picks up or puts down the phone, you mean that they lift or replace the receiver. 电话听筒

  • She picked up the phone, and began to dial Maurice Campbell's number.

    她拿起电话听筒，开始拨打莫里斯·坎贝尔的号码。

  • Trembling, she put the phone down. It rang again almost immediately.

    她颤抖着放下电话听筒，电话几乎立刻又响了起来。

5 VERB 给…打电话 When you phone someone, you dial their phone number and speak to them by phone. 给…打电话

  • He'd phoned Laura to see if she was better. [VERB noun]

    他打电话给劳拉看她是不是好些了。

  • I got more and more angry as I waited for her to phone. [VERB]

    我等她电话的时候，越等越生气。

SYN call, telephone, ring (up) [informal , mainly British] , give someone a call  
6 PHRASE 在通话 If you say that someone is on the phone, you mean that they are speaking to someone else by phone. 在通话

  • She's always on the phone, wanting to know what I've been up to.

    她老来电话，想知道我在做什么。

7 PHRASE 装了电话的；可用电话联系的 If you are on the phone, you have a phone in your home or place of work, so that you can be contacted by phone. 装了电话的；可用电话联系的

  • The Frosts were not on the phone.

    福罗斯特家没装电话。
