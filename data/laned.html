lane
leɪn leɪn
Word forms: plural lanes
1 N-COUNT （尤指乡间的）小径，小路 A lane is a narrow road, especially in the country. （尤指乡间的）小径，小路

  • ...a quiet country lane.

    宁静的乡间小径

  • Follow the lane to the river. [+ to]

    沿着小路走到河边。

SYN road, street, track, path  
2 N-IN-NAMES （用于路名）道，路，巷 Lane is also used in the names of roads, either in cities or in the country. （用于路名）道，路，巷

  • ...The Dorchester Hotel, Park Lane.

    公园路多切斯特酒店

3 N-COUNT [usually adjective NOUN] 车道 A lane is a part of a main road which is marked by the edge of the road and a painted line, or by two painted lines. 车道

  • The lorry was travelling at 20mph in the slow lane.

    卡车在慢车道上以20英里的时速行驶。

  • I pulled out into the eastbound lane of Route 2.

    我转入2号公路东向车道。

SYN track, way, road, channel  
4 N-COUNT 泳道；跑道 At a swimming pool or athletics track, a lane is a long narrow section which is marked by lines or ropes. 泳道；跑道

  • The pool is divided into three sections with a crawler lane for beginners.

    泳池分三部分，有为初学者而设的慢泳道。

  • Drawn in lane three, she had all her rivals in sight.

    她抽到第三道，所有对手尽收眼底。

5 N-COUNT [usually n NOUN] 航道；航线 A lane is a route that is frequently used by aircraft or ships. 航道；航线

  • The collision took place in the busiest shipping lanes in the world.

    撞船事故发生在全世界最繁忙的航道上。

6 PHRASE [usu in PHR] 紧张而刺激的生活 If you say that someone lives their life in the fast lane, you mean that they live in a way which seems full of activity and excitement but which often involves a lot of pressure. 紧张而刺激的生活

  • ...the young criminals' lust for life in the fast lane.

    青少年罪犯对紧张刺激生活的贪求
