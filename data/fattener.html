fatten
fætən ˈfætən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense fattens, present participle fattening, past tense , past participle fattened
1 V-ERG (动物)长膘，变肥;催肥，养肥 If an animal is fattened, or if it fattens, it becomes fatter as a result of eating more. (动物)长膘，变肥;催肥，养肥 [Also VERB n]

  • The cattle are being fattened for slaughter. [be VERB-ed]

    喂肥牛群以宰杀。

  • The creature continued to grow and fatten. [VERB]

    这个家伙继续长大、变肥。

SYN grow fat, spread, expand, swell  
2 VERB 使(企业)发财;使(利润)增长;为…牟利 If you say that someone is fattening something such as a business or its profits, you mean that they are increasing the value of the business or its profits, in a way that you disapprove of. 使(企业)发财;使(利润)增长;为…牟利 [business, disapproval] [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • They have kept the price of sugar artificially high and so fattened the company's profits. [VERB noun]

    他们一直人为地使糖的价格居高不下，以此为公司牟利。

Fatten up means the same as fatten .fatten up 同 fatten

  • The Government is making the taxpayer pay to fatten up a public sector business for private sale.

    政府正在用纳税人的钱壮大一家公有企业，再进行私人认购。
