compound
kɒmpaʊnd kəmpaʊnd ˈkɑmˌpaʊnd kɑmˈpaʊnd kəmˈpaʊnd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense compounds, present participle compounding, past tense , past participle compounded
The noun is pronounced /'kɒmpaʊnd/. The verb is pronounced /kəm'paʊnd/. 名词读作 /'kɒmpaʊnd/，动词读作 /kəm'paʊnd/。
1 N-COUNT (作特殊用途的)大院，有围墙的场地 A compound is an enclosed area of land that is used for a particular purpose. (作特殊用途的)大院，有围墙的场地

  • Police fired on them as they fled into the embassy compound.

    当他们逃进使馆大院时，警察向他们开了枪。

  • ...a military compound.

    军营

2 N-COUNT (化学中的)化合物 In chemistry, a compound is a substance that consists of two or more elements. (化学中的)化合物

  • Organic compounds contain carbon in their molecules.

    有机化合物的分子中含碳。

SYN combination, mixture, blend, composite  
3 N-COUNT [usually singular] 混合物;组合物 If something is a compound of different things, it consists of those things. 混合物;组合物 [formal]

  • Honey is basically a compound of water, two types of sugar, vitamins and enzymes. [+ of]

    蜂蜜基本上是水、两种糖、几种维生素和酶的混合物。

4 ADJ [ADJ n] 复合的;组合的;混合的 Compound is used to indicate that something consists of two or more parts or things. 复合的;组合的;混合的

  • ...a tall shrub with shiny compound leaves.

    长有油光闪亮的复叶的高株灌木

  • ...the compound microscope.

    复式显微镜

SYN complex, multiple, composite, conglomerate  
5 ADJ [ADJ n] (单词)复合的 In grammar, a compound noun, adjective, or verb is one that is made up of two or more words, for example 'fire engine', 'bottle-green', and 'force-feed'. (单词)复合的
6 ADJ (句子)复合的 In grammar, a compound sentence is one that is made up of two or more main clauses. (句子)复合的
7 VERB 使恶化；使复杂化;加重 To compound a problem, difficulty, or mistake means to make it worse by adding to it. 使恶化；使复杂化;加重 [formal]

  • Additional bloodshed and loss of life will only compound the tragedy. [VERB noun]

    额外的流血和牺牲只会加重这场悲剧。

  • The problem is compounded by the medical system here. [be VERB-ed + by]

    这儿的医疗体制使问题进一步恶化。

SYN intensify, add to, complicate, worsen
