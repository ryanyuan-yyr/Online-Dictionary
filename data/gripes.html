gripe
graɪp graɪp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense gripes, present participle griping, past tense , past participle griped
1 VERB 抱怨；发牢骚 If you say that someone is griping, you mean they are annoying you because they keep on complaining about something. 抱怨；发牢骚 [informal, disapproval]

  • Why are football players griping when the average salary is half a million dollars?. [VERB]

    足球运动员的平均薪水已达50万美金，他们为什么还要抱怨呢？

  • They were always griping about high prices. [VERB + about]

    他们总是抱怨物价太高。

griping

  • Still, the griping went on.

    怨声依旧未止。

2 N-COUNT 抱怨；牢骚 A gripe is a complaint about something. 抱怨；牢骚 [informal]

  • My only gripe is that one main course and one dessert were unavailable.

    我唯一不满的就是连一道主菜和一份甜点都没有。

SYN complaint, protest, objection, beef [slang]
