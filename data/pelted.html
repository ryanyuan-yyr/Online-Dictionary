pelt
pelt pɛlt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense pelts, present participle pelting, past tense , past participle pelted
1 N-COUNT [usually plural] （可用来制衣或制毯的）毛皮 The pelt of an animal is its skin, which can be used to make clothing or rugs. （可用来制衣或制毯的）毛皮

  • ...a bed covered with beaver pelts.

    铺着海狸毛皮的床

  • ...rapidly diminishing suppliers of furs and pelts.

    数量锐减的毛皮供应商

SYN coat, fell, skin, hide  
2 VERB 向…投掷 If you pelt someone with things, you throw things at them. 向…投掷

  • Some of the younger men began to pelt one another with snowballs. [VERB noun + with]

    一些年轻人开始相互投掷雪球。

  • Crowds started to pelt police cars with stones. [VERB noun with noun]

    人群开始向警车扔石块。

SYN shower, beat, strike, pepper  
3 VERB [usually cont] 倾泻；倾盆而下 If the rain is pelting down, or if it is pelting with rain, it is raining very hard. 倾泻；倾盆而下 [informal]

  • The rain now was pelting down. [VERB adv.]

    此刻大雨滂沱。

  • It's pelting with rain. [VERB with noun]

    大雨倾盆。

  • We drove through pelting rain. [VERB-ing]

    我们在倾盆大雨中驾车前行。

SYN pour, teem, rain hard, bucket down [informal]  
4 VERB 快速奔跑；飞奔 If you pelt somewhere, you run there very fast. 快速奔跑；飞奔 [informal]

  • Without thinking, she pelted down the stairs in her nightgown. [VERB prep.]

    她想都没想，穿着睡衣就飞奔下了楼。

SYN rush, charge, shoot, career  
5 PHRASE 全速；飞快地 If you do something full pelt or at full pelt, you do it very quickly indeed. 全速；飞快地 [informal]

  • Alice leapt from the car and ran full pelt towards the emergency room.

    艾丽斯跳下车，拼命奔向急诊室。

  • He drove his car through the gates at full pelt.

    他驾车急速通过大门。
