two-faced
ˈtuˌfeɪst
ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 两面派的;言行不一的;口是心非的 If you describe someone as two-faced, you are critical of them because they say they do or believe one thing when their behaviour or words show that they do not do it or do not believe it. 两面派的;言行不一的;口是心非的 [disapproval]

  • He had been devious and two-faced.

    他从前向来都是阴险狡猾，口是心非。

  • The scientists saw the public as being particularly two-faced about animal welfare in view of the way domestic animals are treated.

    鉴于家养动物所受的待遇，科学家认为公众在动物福利的问题上尤为言行不一。

SYN hypocritical, false, deceiving, treacherous
