gang
gæŋ gæŋ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense gangs, present participle ganging, past tense , past participle ganged
1 N-COUNT 一帮，一伙（尤指滋事的年轻人） A gang is a group of people, especially young people, who go around together and often deliberately cause trouble. 一帮，一伙（尤指滋事的年轻人）

  • During the fight with a rival gang he lashed out with his flick knife.

    在与敌对帮派的打斗中，他挥舞着弹簧折刀一通猛刺。

  • Gang members were behind a lot of the violence.

    很多暴力事件是流氓团伙成员暗中策划的。

  • He was attacked by a gang of youths. [+ of]

    他遭到一帮小混混的袭击。

SYN group, crowd, pack, company  
2 N-COUNT 一帮，一伙（罪犯） A gang is a group of criminals who work together to commit crimes. 一帮，一伙（罪犯）

  • Police were hunting for a gang who had allegedly stolen fifty-five cars.

    警方正在追捕一个被控盗窃了55辆汽车的犯罪团伙。

  • ...an underworld gang.

    地下犯罪团伙

  • ...a gang of masked robbers. [+ of]

    一帮蒙面劫匪

SYN ring, firm [slang] , organization, band  
3 N-SING 一伙（常聚会的朋友） The gang is a group of friends who frequently meet. 一伙（常聚会的朋友） [informal]

  • Come on over, we've got lots of the old gang here.

    过来吧，好多老朋友都在这儿。

SYN circle, group, crowd, company  
4 N-COUNT 一群，一队，一组（一起干体力活的人） A gang is a group of workers who do physical work together. 一群，一队，一组（一起干体力活的人）

  • ...a gang of labourers. [+ of]

    一群工人

SYN squad, team, troop, force
