haste
heɪst heɪst
1 N-UNCOUNT 急速;仓促;匆忙 Haste is the quality of doing something quickly, sometimes too quickly so that you are careless and make mistakes. 急速;仓促;匆忙

  • In their haste to escape the rising water, they dropped some expensive equipment.

    他们在河水上涨仓促逃离时，丢掉了一些值钱的装备。

  • The translations bear the signs of inaccuracy and haste.

    这些译文中有不够准确、下笔仓促的痕迹。

2 PHRASE 匆忙地;急忙地;草率地 If you do something in haste, you do it quickly and hurriedly, and sometimes carelessly. 匆忙地;急忙地;草率地

  • Don't act in haste or be hot-headed.

    不可草率行事，不可头脑发热。

SYN hastily, rashly, too quickly, impetuously  
3 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 赶快;赶紧 If someone is told to make haste, they are told to do something quickly and not waste time. 赶快;赶紧 [old-fashioned]

  • As Simon was under orders to make haste, some days they covered thirty miles.

    西蒙接到命令要求抓紧行动，有些日子他们一走就是30英里。

SYN hurry (up), speed up, hasten, get a move on [informal]
