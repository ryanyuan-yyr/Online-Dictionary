assemble
əsembəl əˈsɛmbəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense assembles, present participle assembling, past tense , past participle assembled
1 V-ERG （使）集合;（使）聚集;（使）集会 When people assemble or when someone assembles them, they come together in a group, usually for a particular purpose such as a meeting. （使）集合;（使）聚集;（使）集会

  • There wasn't even a convenient place for students to assemble between classes. [VERB]

    在课间，学生们连一个方便凑在一起的地方都没有。

  • Thousands of people, mainly Zulus, assembled in a stadium in Thokoza. [V + in/at]

    数以千计的人，主要是祖鲁族人，聚集在托考查的一个体育场里。

  • He has assembled a team of experts to handle queries. [VERB noun]

    他召集了一班专家来答疑解惑。

  • The assembled multitude cheered and whistled as the political leaders arrived. [VERB-ed]

    当政治领袖到达的时候，聚集的人群欢呼起来,并吹起了口哨。

SYN bring together, collect, gather, rally  
2 VERB 集合;组装;装配 To assemble something means to collect it together or to fit the different parts of it together. 集合;组装;装配

  • Greenpeace managed to assemble enough boats to waylay the ship at sea. [VERB noun]

    绿色和平组织设法聚集起足够数量的小船在海上拦截该船。

  • She had been trying to assemble the bomb when it went off in her arms. [VERB noun]

    在她试图组装炸弹时，炸弹在她怀里爆炸了。

  • He is assembling evidence concerning a murder. [VERB noun]

    他正在收集一桩谋杀案的证据。

SYN put together, make, join, set up
