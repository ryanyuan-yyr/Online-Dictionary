harvest
hɑːʳvɪst ˈhɑrvɪst
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense harvests, present participle harvesting, past tense , past participle harvested
1 N-SING 收获;收割 The harvest is the gathering of a crop. 收获;收割

  • There were about 300 million tons of grain in the fields at the start of the harvest.

    收获伊始地里大概有3亿吨粮食。

SYN harvesting, picking, gathering, collecting  
2 N-COUNT 收成;收获量 A harvest is the crop that is gathered in. 收成;收获量

  • ...a bumper potato harvest.

    马铃薯大丰收

  • Millions of people are threatened with starvation as a result of drought and poor harvests.

    几百万人因干旱和歉收而受到饥饿的威胁。

SYN crop, yield, year's growth, produce  
3 VERB 收获;收割 When you harvest a crop, you gather it in. 收获;收割

  • Many farmers are refusing to harvest the cane. [VERB noun]

    很多农民正拒绝收割甘蔗。

  • ...freshly harvested beetroot. [VERB-ed]

    刚挖的甜菜根

SYN gather, pick, collect, bring in  
harvesting

  • ...war is hampering harvesting and the distribution of food aid.

    战争阻碍了庄稼的收割和粮食援助的分发。

4 VERB 获取;收获 If you harvest a large number of things, you collect them, often by making great efforts. 获取;收获 [literary]

  • In his new career as a restaurateur he has blossomed and harvested many awards. [VERB noun]

    转行经营餐饮之后，他在事业上大获成功并获得了许多奖项。

SYN collect, get, gain, earn  
5 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 获得成果;尝到苦果 If you reap the harvest, you benefit or suffer from the results of your past actions or of someone else's past actions. 获得成果;尝到苦果 [written]

  • The Israeli Prime Minister must be convinced that he will reap a richer harvest of votes by going to the electorate well before October.

    必须说服以色列总理，若在10月份前就尽早走近选民，他会赢得更多选票。
