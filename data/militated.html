militate
mɪlɪteɪt ˈmɪləˌteɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense militates, present participle militating, past tense , past participle militated
VERB 妨碍;阻碍 To militate against something means to make it less possible or likely. To militate against someone means to prevent them from achieving something. 妨碍;阻碍 [formal]

  • Her background militates against her. [VERB + against]

    她的背景对她不利。

  • We can never promise to sail anywhere in particular, because the weather might militate against it. [VERB + against]

    天有不测风云，所以我们永远都无法保证会航行到哪个地方。
