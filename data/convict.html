convict
kənvɪkt kɒnvɪkt kənˈvɪkt ˈkɑnˌvɪkt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense convicts, present participle convicting, past tense , past participle convicted
The verb is pronounced /kən'vɪkt/. The noun is pronounced /'kɒnvɪkt/. 动词读作 /kən'vɪkt/，名词读作 /'kɒnvɪkt/。
1 VERB 给…定罪;宣判…有罪 If someone is convicted of a crime, they are found guilty of that crime in a law court. 给…定罪;宣判…有罪 [Also V n of n]

  • In 1977 he was convicted of murder and sentenced to life imprisonment. [be V-ed of n/v-ing]

    1977年，他被判谋杀罪名成立，判处终身监禁。

  • There was insufficient evidence to convict him. [VERB noun]

    没有足够证据给他定罪。

  • ...a convicted drug dealer. [VERB-ed]

    已定罪的毒品贩子

SYN find guilty, sentence, condemn, imprison  
2 N-COUNT 已决犯;囚犯 A convict is someone who is in prison. 已决犯;囚犯 [journalism]
SYN prisoner, criminal, con [slang] , lag [slang]
