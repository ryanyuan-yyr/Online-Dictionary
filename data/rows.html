ARRANGEMENT OR SEQUENCE 排列；序列

1 N-COUNT 一排;一行;一列 A row of things or people is a number of them arranged in a line. 一排;一行;一列

  • ...a row of pretty little cottages. [+ of]

    一排漂亮的小村舍

  • Several men are pushing school desks and chairs into neat rows.

    几个人正挪动学校的桌椅，将它们排整齐。

SYN line, bank, range, series  
2 N-COUNT (电影院或飞机里的)一排座位 In a theatre or cinema, or on a plane, each line of seats is called a row . (电影院或飞机里的)一排座位

  • She was sitting in the front row.

    她坐在前排。

3 N-IN-NAMES [n NOUN] (用于街道名称)…街，…路 Row is sometimes used in the names of streets. (用于街道名称)…街，…路

  • ...the house at 236 Larch Row.

    拉奇街236号的房子

4 See also: death row  skid row 
5 PHRASE 连续地;接连不断地;不间断地 If something happens several times in a row, it happens that number of times without a break. If something happens several days in a row, it happens on each of those days. 连续地;接连不断地;不间断地

  • They have won five championships in a row.

    他们已经5次蝉联冠军。

  • If I'm inside for three days in a row, I go crazy.

    如果连续3天不出来，我会发疯的。

  • It is the sixth month in a row in which imports have fallen.

    这是进口量持续下降的第6个月了。

SYN consecutively, running, in turn, one after the other  
6 a hard row to hoe→see: hoe 

MAKING A BOAT MOVE 使船移动

1 VERB 划船;划船运送 When you row, you sit in a boat and make it move through the water by using oars. If you row someone somewhere, you take them there in a boat, using oars. 划船;划船运送

  • He rowed as quickly as he could to the shore. [VERB prep.]

    他尽快地把船划到岸边。

  • We could all row a boat and swim almost before we could walk. [VERB noun]

    我们所有人几乎在还不会走之前就已经会划船和游泳了。

  • The boatman refused to row him back. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    船夫拒绝划船把他送回去。

Row is also a noun.

  • I took Daniel for a row.

    我带着丹尼尔去划船了。

2See also:rowing 
Phrasal verbs:
row back

DISAGREEMENT OR NOISE 分歧；喧闹

1 N-COUNT [oft adjective NOUN] 严重分歧;纠纷 A row is a serious disagreement between people or organizations. 严重分歧;纠纷 [British, informal]

  • This is likely to provoke a further row about the bank's role in the affair.

    这有可能会加深关于银行在此事中的作用的分歧。

  • The ministers must have realized that they risked what could be a major diplomatic row with France.

    部长们应该已经认识到了他们这样做可能会与法国产生严重的外交纠纷。

2 N-COUNT 争吵;争执;争论 If two people have a row, they have a noisy argument. 争吵;争执;争论 [British, informal]

  • We never seem to stay together for very long before we have a dreadful row.

    我们总是在一起呆不了多久就会大吵一架。

  • A man had been stabbed to death in a family row.

    一位男子在一场家庭纠纷中被刺死。

SYN quarrel, dispute, argument, squabble  
3 V-RECIP 争吵;争执;争论 If two people row or if one person rows with another, they have a noisy argument. 争吵;争执;争论 [British, informal]

  • They rowed all the time. [VERB]

    他们总是争吵不休。

  • He had earlier rowed with his girlfriend. [VERB + with]

    他早先和女友吵过架。

SYN quarrel, fight, argue, dispute  
4 N-SING 叫嚷;吵闹;喧嚣 If you say that someone is making a row, you mean that they are making a loud, unpleasant noise. 叫嚷;吵闹;喧嚣 [British, informal]

  • 'Whatever is that row?' she demanded. 'Pop festival,' he answered.

    “那闹哄哄的究竟是在做什么？”她问道。“是流行音乐节，”他回答说。

SYN disturbance, noise, racket, uproar
