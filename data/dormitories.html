dormitory
dɔːʳmɪtri ˈdɔːmɪtərɪ -tɔːri ˈdɔrməˌtɔri
Word forms: plural dormitories
1 N-COUNT (寄宿学校等的)大寝室，集体寝室 A dormitory is a large bedroom where several people sleep, for example in a boarding school. (寄宿学校等的)大寝室，集体寝室

  • ...the boys' dormitory.

    男生寝室

  • The latest refugees were housed in makeshift dormitories.

    新来的难民被安置在临时的集体寝室里。

2 N-COUNT (大学等的)学生宿舍 A dormitory is a building in a college or university where students live. (大学等的)学生宿舍 [US]

  • She lived in a college dormitory.

    她住在大学宿舍里。

in BRIT, use 英国英语用 hall of residence
3 ADJ [ADJ n] (在大城市工作者居住的)邻镇住宅区的，郊外住宅区的 If you refer to a place as a dormitory suburb or town, you mean that most of the people who live there travel to work in another, larger town a short distance away. (在大城市工作者居住的)邻镇住宅区的，郊外住宅区的 [British]

  • It had become almost a dormitory suburb of the city.

    它几乎成为了城市的一个市郊住宅区。
