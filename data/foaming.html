foam
foʊm foʊm
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense foams, present participle foaming, past tense , past participle foamed
1 N-UNCOUNT 泡沫;气泡 Foam consists of a mass of small bubbles that are formed when air and a liquid are mixed together. 泡沫;气泡

  • The water curved round the rocks in great bursts of foam.

    水流撞击岩石，产生大量泡沫。

SYN froth, spray, bubbles, lather  
2 N-MASS 泡沫剂;泡沫产品 Foam is used to refer to various kinds of manufactured products which have a soft, light texture like a thick liquid. 泡沫剂;泡沫产品

  • ...shaving foam.

    剃须泡沫

3 N-MASS 泡沫橡胶;海绵式橡胶 Foam or foam rubber is soft rubber full of small holes which is used, for example, to make mattresses and cushions. 泡沫橡胶;海绵式橡胶

  • ...modern three-piece suites filled with foam rubber.

    有泡沫橡胶填充物的现代家具三件套

  • We had given him a large foam mattress to sleep on.

    我们给了他一张大海绵床垫用来睡觉。

4 VERB (液体)产生泡沫，起沫 If a liquid foams, it is full of small bubbles and keeps moving slightly. (液体)产生泡沫，起沫

  • I let the water run into it and we watched as it foamed and bubbled. [VERB]

    我把水引进去，然后我们看着它起沫、冒泡。

  • ...ravines with foaming rivers rushing through them. [VERB-ing]

    泛着白沫的河水奔腾而过的沟壑

SYN bubble, boil, fizz, froth  
5 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 大怒;暴怒;大发雷霆 If you say that someone is foaming at the mouth, you mean that they are very angry. 大怒;暴怒;大发雷霆

  • Stewart was foaming at the mouth about an incident the previous afternoon.

    斯图尔特正为头天下午的事件而大发脾气。
