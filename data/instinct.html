instinct
ɪnstɪŋkt ˈɪnˌstɪŋkt ɪnˈstɪŋkt
Word forms: plural instincts
1 N-VAR 本能;天性 Instinct is the natural tendency that a person or animal has to behave or react in a particular way. 本能;天性

  • I didn't have as strong a maternal instinct as some other mothers.

    我不像别的母亲那样有那么强烈的母性本能。

  • The basis for training relies on the dog's natural instinct to hunt and retrieve.

    训练是基于狗搜寻和衔回的天生本能。

  • He always knew what time it was, as if by instinct.

    他总能知道几点了,仿佛是出于本能。

SYN natural inclination, feeling, urge, talent  
2 N-COUNT [also N to-inf] 天分;天资;天赋的才能 If you have an instinct for something, you are naturally good at it or able to do it. 天分;天资;天赋的才能

  • Farmers are increasingly losing touch with their instinct for managing the land. [+ for]

    农民正在逐渐丢失经营土地的本领。

  • Irene is so incredibly musical and has a natural instinct to perform.

    艾琳有着极高的音乐天分和天生的表演才能。

SYN talent, skill, gift, capacity  
3 N-VAR [usually with poss, oft NOUN to-infinitive] 本能反应;心里的话 If it is your instinct to do something, you feel that it is right to do it. 本能反应;心里的话

  • I should've gone with my first instinct, which was not to do the interview.

    我本该听从自己的第一反应,不接受这次采访的。

  • She hadn't followed her instinct and because of this Frank was dead.

    她没有听从自己心里的话,弗兰克因此死了。

SYN intuition, feeling, impulse, gut feeling [informal]  
4 N-VAR [oft NOUN that] 直觉 Instinct is a feeling that you have that something is the case, rather than an opinion or idea based on facts. 直觉

  • There is scientific evidence to support our instinct that being surrounded by plants is good for health.

    我们觉得周围植物环绕会有益健康,这种直觉有科学证据支持。

  • He seems so honest and genuine and my every instinct says he's not.

    他看起来这么老实诚恳,但我的全部直觉告诉我他一点都不。
