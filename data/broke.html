broke
broʊk broʊk
1 Broke is the past tense of break .
2 ADJ-GRADED [verb-link ADJ] 身无分文的；一文不名的 If you are broke, you have no money. 身无分文的；一文不名的 [informal]

  • What do you mean, I've got enough money? I'm as broke as you are.

    你是什么意思，我的钱还富余吗？我已和你一样分文不剩了。

SYN penniless, short, ruined, bust [informal]  
3 PHRASE 破产 If a company or person goes broke, they lose money and are unable to continue in business or to pay their debts. 破产 [informal, business]

  • Balton went broke twice in his career.

    巴尔顿一生中经历了两次破产。

4 PHRASE 孤注一掷 If you go for broke, you take the most extreme or risky of the possible courses of action in order to try and achieve success. 孤注一掷 [informal]

  • It was a sharp disagreement about whether to go for broke or whether to compromise.

    是放手一搏还是保守妥协，在这一问题上的分歧很尖锐。
