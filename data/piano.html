piano
piænoʊ piɑːnoʊ pɪˈænəʊ ˈpjɑːnəʊ piˈænoʊ piˈɑnoʊ pjˈænoʊ
Word forms: plural pianos
Pronounced /pi'ænəʊ/ for meaning 1, and /pi'ɑːnəʊ/ for meaning 2. 义项1读作/pi'ænəʊ/，义项2读作/pi'ɑːnəʊ/。
1 N-VAR 钢琴 A piano is a large musical instrument with a row of black and white keys. When you press these keys with your fingers, little hammers hit wire strings inside the piano which vibrate to produce musical notes. 钢琴

  • I taught myself how to play the piano.

    我自学了弹钢琴。

  • He started piano lessons at the age of 7.

    他7岁开始上钢琴课。

  • ...sonatas for cello and piano.

    大提琴和钢琴奏鸣曲

  • ...Rachmaninov's Fourth Piano Concerto.

    《拉赫玛尼诺夫第四钢琴协奏曲》

2See also:grand piano  upright piano 
3 ADV [ADVERB after verb] 轻轻地；微弱地 A piece of music that is played piano is played quietly. 轻轻地；微弱地 [technical]
