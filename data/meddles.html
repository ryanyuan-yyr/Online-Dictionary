meddle
medəl ˈmɛdəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense meddles, present participle meddling, past tense , past participle meddled
VERB 管闲事;干涉;干预 If you say that someone meddles in something, you are criticizing the fact that they try to influence or change it without being asked. 管闲事;干涉;干预 [disapproval]

  • Already some people are asking whether scientists have any right to meddle in such matters. [V + in/with]

    已经有人在问科学家是否有权干预这类事情。

  • If only you hadn't felt compelled to meddle. [VERB]

    要是你没有觉得非插手不可就好了。

  • ...the inept and meddling bureaucrats. [VERB-ing]

    既无能又爱管闲事的官僚老爷们

SYN interfere, intervene, tamper, intrude  
meddler

  • They view activists as little more than meddlers.

    在他们眼中，积极分子不过是爱管闲事的人罢了。
