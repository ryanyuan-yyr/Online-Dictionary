devious
diːviəs ˈdiviəs
1 ADJ-GRADED 奸诈的;阴险的;狡猾的 If you describe someone as devious you do not like them because you think they are are dishonest and like to keep things secret, often in a complicated way. 奸诈的;阴险的;狡猾的 [disapproval]

  • Newman was devious, prepared to say one thing in print and another in private.

    纽曼阴险得很，明里一套，暗里一套。

  • By devious means she tracked down the other woman.

    她通过不正当的手段追查到了另外那个女人。

SYN sly, scheming, calculating, tricky  
deviousness

  • ...the deviousness of drug traffickers.

    毒贩的狡猾奸诈

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] （路线或道路）曲折的，迂回的 A devious route or path to a place involves many changes in direction, rather than being as straight and direct as possible. （路线或道路）曲折的，迂回的 [formal]

  • He followed a devious route.

    他走了一条迂回路线。

SYN indirect, roundabout, wandering, crooked
