deathly
deθli ˈdɛθli
1 ADV [ADVERB adjective] 死一般地 If you say that someone is deathly pale or deathly still, you are emphasizing that they are very pale or still, like a dead person. 死一般地 [literary, emphasis]

  • Bernadette turned deathly pale.

    贝尔纳黛特脸色变得死一般地惨白。

  • She lay deathly still.

    她像死了一样一动不动地躺在那里。

2 ADV-GRADED [ADVERB adjective] 极其;非常 If you say that someone is deathly afraid, you are emphasizing that they are very afraid. You can also say that someone or something is deathly silent, dull, boring, cold, or tired. 极其;非常 [literary, emphasis]

  • He is deathly afraid of black crows.

    他非常害怕黑乌鸦。

  • I took a deathly dull job.

    我接受了一份极其枯燥的工作。

3 ADJ [ADJ n] （沉默或寂静）死一般的 If you say that there is a deathly silence or a deathly hush, you are emphasizing that it is very quiet. （沉默或寂静）死一般的 [literary, emphasis]

  • A deathly silence hung over the square.

    广场上死一般地寂静。
