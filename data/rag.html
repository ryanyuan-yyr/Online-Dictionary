rag
ræg ræg
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , plural rags, present participle ragging, past tense , past participle ragged
1 N-VAR 抹布；破布；碎布 A rag is a piece of old cloth which you can use to clean or wipe things. 抹布；破布；碎布

  • He was wiping his hands on an oily rag.

    他正在用一块满是油污的破布擦手。

  • ...a bundle of old rags.

    一捆破布

  • It looked like a piece of rag.

    它看上去像块破布。

SYN cloth, piece of cloth  
2 N-PLURAL 破旧衣服；破烂衣裳 Rags are old torn clothes. 破旧衣服；破烂衣裳

  • There were men, women and small children, some dressed in rags.

    有男人、女人和小孩，其中一些人衣衫褴褛。

3 N-COUNT （格调低下的）报纸，小报 People refer to a newspaper as a rag when they have a poor opinion of it. （格调低下的）报纸，小报 [informal, disapproval]

  • 'This man Tom works for a local rag,' he said.

    “这个叫汤姆的人在一家地方小报社工作，”他说。

4 VERB 嘲弄；拿…开玩笑；对…恶作剧 To rag someone means to make fun of them in an unkind way. 嘲弄；拿…开玩笑；对…恶作剧 [British]

  • She was about thirty, ten years older than the youngsters ragging her. [VERB noun]

    她大概有30岁，比那些嘲弄她的年轻人大10岁。

SYN tease, provoke, needle [informal] , mock  
5 See also: ragged 
6 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 突然生气；勃然大怒 If you lose your rag, you suddenly become so angry that you are not in control of yourself. 突然生气；勃然大怒 [British, informal]

  • I've only once seen him lose his rag.

    我只见他发过一次脾气。

7 PHRASE 从赤贫到巨富;白手起家 You use rags to riches to describe the way in which someone quickly becomes very rich after they have been quite poor. 从赤贫到巨富;白手起家

  • His was a rags-to-riches story and people admire that.

    他白手起家，人们对他都很钦佩。

8 PHRASE 激怒人的东西 If you describe something as a red rag to a bull, you mean that it is certain to make a particular person or group very angry. 激怒人的东西 [mainly British]

  • This sort of information is like a red rag to a bull for the tobacco companies.

    烟草公司知道了这种消息一定会大怒。
