jot
dʒɒt dʒɑt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense jots, present participle jotting, past tense , past participle jotted
1 VERB 草草记下，匆匆写下（地址等） If you jot something short such as an address somewhere, you write it down so that you will remember it. 草草记下，匆匆写下（地址等）

  • Could you just jot his name on there? [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    你能把他的名字先记在那上面吗？

Jot down means the same as jot .jot down同jot

  • Keep a pad handy to jot down queries as they occur.

    手边放一本便笺簿，以便有问题时随时记下。

  • Listen carefully to the instructions and jot them down.

    仔细听指示，并记录下来。

2 QUANT 一点儿；少量 If you say that there is not a jot or not one jot of something, you are emphasizing that there is not even a very small amount of it. 一点儿；少量 [old-fashioned, emphasis]

  • There is not a jot of evidence to say it does them any good. [+ of]

    没有丝毫的证据显示这对他们有任何好处。

  • It makes not one jot of difference. [+ of]

    这一点区别也没有。

3 PHRASE [with brd-neg, PHRASE after verb] （用于强调否定陈述）丝毫，一点儿 Expressions such as a jot ,one jot ,not a jot are used to emphasize negative statements. （用于强调否定陈述）丝毫，一点儿 [old-fashioned, emphasis]

  • It doesn't affect my judgement one jot.

    这丝毫不会影响我的判断。

  • It matters not a jot.

    这完全无关紧要。
