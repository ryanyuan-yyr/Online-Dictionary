wrinkle
rɪŋkəl ˈrɪŋkəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense wrinkles, present participle wrinkling, past tense , past participle wrinkled
1 N-COUNT [usually plural] (脸上的)皱纹 Wrinkles are lines which form on someone's face as they grow old. (脸上的)皱纹

  • His face was covered with wrinkles.

    他的脸上布满了皱纹。

  • Some deep wrinkles furrow his lower forehead.

    他额头下方出现了几道深深的皱纹。

SYN line, fold, crease, furrow  
2 V-ERG (使)（皮肤）起皱纹 When someone's skin wrinkles or when something wrinkles it, lines start to form in it because the skin is getting old or damaged. (使)（皮肤）起皱纹

  • The skin on her cheeks and around her eyes was beginning to wrinkle. [VERB]

    她两颊和双眼周围开始起了皱纹。

  • ...protection against the sun's rays that age and wrinkle the skin. [VERB noun]

    防晒以免皮肤老化起皱

wrinkled

  • I did indeed look older and more wrinkled than ever.

    我的确比从前显老, 皱纹也多了。

3 N-COUNT （布或纸的）褶皱，皱痕，褶子 A wrinkle is a raised fold in a piece of cloth or paper that spoils its appearance. （布或纸的）褶皱，皱痕，褶子

  • He noticed a wrinkle in her stocking. [+ in]

    他注意到她长筒袜上有一道褶儿。

SYN crease, gather, fold, crumple  
4 V-ERG （使）（衣服）起皱，起褶 If cloth wrinkles, or if someone or something wrinkles it, it gets folds or lines in it. （使）（衣服）起皱，起褶

  • Her stockings wrinkled at the ankles. [VERB]

    她的长筒袜在脚踝处起了褶儿。

  • I wrinkled the velvet. [VERB noun]

    我把天鹅绒布弄皱了。

SYN crease, line, gather, fold  
wrinkled

  • His suit was wrinkled and he looked very tired.

    他的西服皱巴巴的，整个人显得非常疲惫。

5 V-ERG (使)(鼻子、额头等)起皱 When you wrinkle your nose or forehead, or when it wrinkles, you tighten the muscles in your face so that the skin folds. (使)(鼻子、额头等)起皱

  • Frannie wrinkled her nose at her daughter. [VERB noun]

    弗兰妮朝她女儿皱了皱鼻子。

  • Ellen's face wrinkles as if she is about to sneeze. [VERB]

    艾伦的脸皱了起来，好像要打喷嚏。
