observe
əbzɜːʳv əbˈzɜrv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense observes, present participle observing, past tense , past participle observed
1 VERB （尤指为了解而）观察，观测 If you observe a person or thing, you watch them carefully, especially in order to learn something about them. （尤指为了解而）观察，观测

  • Stern also studies and observes the behaviour of babies. [VERB noun]

    斯特恩还研究观察婴儿的行为。

  • Are there any classes I could observe?. [VERB noun]

    有我可以观摩的课吗？

  • Our sniper teams observed them manning an anti-aircraft gun. [VERB noun verb-ing]

    我们的狙击队观看他们操作高射炮。

SYN watch, study, view, look at  
2 VERB 看到；注意到 If you observe someone or something, you see or notice them. 看到；注意到 [formal]

  • In 1664 Hooke observed a reddish spot on the surface of the planet. [VERB noun]

    1664年，胡克注意到这颗行星表面有一块微红的斑点。

SYN notice, see, note, mark  
3 VERB （尤指就注意到或深思熟虑之事）说，评论，评述 If you observe that something is the case, you make a remark or comment about it, especially when it is something you have noticed and thought about a lot. （尤指就注意到或深思熟虑之事）说，评论，评述 [formal]

  • We observe that the first calls for radical transformation did not begin until the period of the industrial revolution. [VERB that]

    我们认为直到工业革命时期才出现了要求彻底变革的第一波呐喊。

  • 'He is a fine young man,' observed Stephen. [VERB with quote]

    “他是个很不错的小伙子，”斯蒂芬评论道。

4 VERB 遵守，奉行（法律、习俗等） If you observe something such as a law or custom, you obey it or follow it. 遵守，奉行（法律、习俗等）

  • Imposing speed restrictions is easy, but forcing motorists to observe them is trickier. [VERB noun]

    限制车速容易，但强制司机遵守这些限速规定就难办多了。

  • The army was observing a ceasefire. [VERB noun]

    军队正遵守停火协议。

  • American forces are observing Christmas quietly. [VERB noun]

    美国部队正在悄悄地庆祝圣诞节。

SYN comply with, keep, follow, mind
