fun
fʌn fʌn
1 N-UNCOUNT 趣事;乐趣 You refer to an activity or situation as fun if you think it is pleasant and enjoyable and it causes you to feel happy. 趣事;乐趣

  • This year promises to be terrifically good fun.

    今年一定会非常有意思。

  • It was such a success and we had so much fun doing it.

    它大获成功，而我们也从中得到了很多乐趣。

  • It could be fun to watch them.

    看着他们就很有意思。

  • You still have time to join in the fun.

    现在加入其中享受快乐还来得及。

SYN amusement, sport, treat, pleasure  
2 N-UNCOUNT 有趣的人;好玩的人 If you say that someone is fun, you mean that you enjoy being with them because they say and do interesting or amusing things. 有趣的人;好玩的人 [approval]

  • Liz was wonderful fun to be with.

    莉兹是个非常有趣的伙伴。

3 ADJ-GRADED [ADJ n] 令人愉快的;让人开心的;有趣的 If you describe something as a fun thing, you mean that you think it is enjoyable. If you describe someone as a fun person, you mean that you enjoy being with them. 令人愉快的;让人开心的;有趣的 [informal]

  • It was a fun evening.

    那个晚上令人愉快。

  • What a fun person he is!

    他多么有趣啊！

4 PHRASE 荒唐可笑的人;引人嘲笑的人 Someone who is a figure of fun is considered ridiculous, so that people laugh at them or make jokes about them. 荒唐可笑的人;引人嘲笑的人
5 PHRASE 为了好玩 If you do something for fun or for the fun of it, you do it in order to enjoy yourself rather than because it is important or necessary. 为了好玩

  • I took my M. A. just for fun really.

    我拿文科硕士学位其实就是觉得好玩儿。

  • He had just come for the fun of it.

    他来只是为了好玩。

6 PHRASE (尤指不负责任的)玩笑，嬉闹，恶作剧 You can refer to playful behaviour as fun and games, especially if you disapprove of it because you think it is irresponsible. (尤指不负责任的)玩笑，嬉闹，恶作剧 [informal, disapproval]

  • Police suspected that the boys, whose fun and games hurt a lot of people, were on drugs.

    警察怀疑这些经常胡为瞎闹令不少人受害的男孩子在吸毒。

SYN horseplay, clowning, romping, pranks  
7 PHRASE 开玩笑地;闹着玩地 If you do something in fun, you do it as a joke or for amusement, without intending to cause any harm. 开玩笑地;闹着玩地

  • Don't say such things, even in fun.

    不要说这些话，就算是开玩笑也不行。

8 PHRASE 取笑;戏弄;拿…开玩笑 If you make fun of someone or something or poke fun at them, you laugh at them, tease them, or make jokes about them in a way that causes them to seem ridiculous. 取笑;戏弄;拿…开玩笑

  • Don't make fun of me.

    不要取笑我。

  • She poked fun at people's shortcomings.

    她拿别人的缺点开玩笑。
