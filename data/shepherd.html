shepherd
ʃepəʳd ˈʃɛpərd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense shepherds, present participle shepherding, past tense , past participle shepherded
1 N-COUNT (尤指男性)牧羊人，羊倌 A shepherd is a person, especially a man, whose job is to look after sheep. (尤指男性)牧羊人，羊倌
SYN drover, stockman, herdsman [British] , grazier  
2 VERB [usually passive] 带领;引领;护送 If you are shepherded somewhere, someone takes you there to make sure that you arrive at the right place safely. 带领;引领;护送

  • She was shepherded by her guards up the rear ramp of the aircraft. [be VERB-ed prep./adv.]

    她在警卫的护送下上了飞机尾部的舷梯。

SYN guide, conduct, lead, steer  
Quotations
The Lord is my shepherd; I shall not want Bible: Psalm 23
