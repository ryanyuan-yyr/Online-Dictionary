unfamiliar
ʌnfəmɪlɪəʳ ˌʌnfəˈmɪljə ˌʌnfəˈmɪljər
1 ADJ-GRADED 不熟悉的；陌生的 If something is unfamiliar to you, you know nothing or very little about it, because you have not seen or experienced it before. 不熟悉的；陌生的

  • She grew many wonderful plants that were unfamiliar to me. [+ to]

    她种了很多我不熟悉的奇妙植物。

  • I was alone in an unfamiliar city.

    我在一个陌生的城市里形单影只。

SYN strange, new, unknown, different  
unfamiliarity

  • ...problems which arise from the newness of the approach and its unfamiliarity to prisoners.

    由于做法新、囚犯不熟悉而产生的问题

2 ADJ-GRADED [verb-link ADJ with n] 不熟知的；不了解的 If you are unfamiliar with something, it is unfamiliar to you. 不熟知的；不了解的

  • She speaks no Japanese and is unfamiliar with Japanese culture. [+ with]

    她不会说日语，也不了解日本文化。

SYN unacquainted with, a stranger to, unaccustomed to, inexperienced in  
unfamiliarity

  • ...her unfamiliarity with the politics of the region.

    她对该地区政治的不熟悉
