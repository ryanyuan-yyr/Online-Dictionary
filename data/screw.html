screw
skruː skru
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense screws, present participle screwing, past tense , past participle screwed
1 N-COUNT 螺(丝)钉 A screw is a metal object similar to a nail, with a raised spiral line around it. You turn a screw using a screwdriver so that it goes through two things, for example two pieces of wood, and fastens them together. 螺(丝)钉

  • Each bracket is fixed to the wall with just three screws.

    每个托架只用了 3 颗螺钉固定在墙上。

SYN nail, pin, tack, rivet  
2 V-ERG 用螺钉固定 If you screw something somewhere or if it screws somewhere, you fix it in place by means of a screw or screws. 用螺钉固定

  • I had screwed the shelf on the wall myself. [VERB noun prep.]

    我自己用螺钉把架子固定在墙上。

  • Screw down any loose floorboards. [VERB noun with adv.]

    用螺钉固定住所有松动的地板。

  • I particularly like the type of shelving that screws to the wall. [VERB prep./adv.]

    我尤其喜欢那种用螺钉固定在墙上的搁板。

SYN fasten, fix, attach, bolt  
3 ADJ [ADJ n] (盖子、配件)带螺旋的 A screw lid or fitting is one that has a raised spiral line on the inside or outside of it, so that it can be fixed in place by twisting. (盖子、配件)带螺旋的

  • ...an ordinary jam jar with a screw lid.

    带螺旋盖的普通果酱罐

4 V-ERG 旋紧;拧紧 If you screw something somewhere or if it screws somewhere, you fix it in place by twisting it round and round. 旋紧;拧紧

  • Kelly screwed the silencer onto the pistol. [VERB noun prep.]

    凯利把消音器拧到手枪上。

  • Screw down the lid fairly tightly. [VERB noun with adv.]

    把盖子拧紧。

  • This device screws into the shutter release button. [VERB prep./adv.]

    这个装置能旋进快门释放按钮。

  • ...several aluminium poles that screw together to give a maximum length of 10 yards. [VERB prep./adv.]

    几根旋接在一起、最长为 10 码的铝杆

SYN turn, wind, twist, tighten  
5 VERB 把(纸等)揉成一团，拧成一团 If you screw something such as a piece of paper into a ball, you squeeze it or twist it tightly so that it is in the shape of a ball. 把(纸等)揉成一团，拧成一团 [British]

  • He screwed the paper into a ball and tossed it into the fire. [VERB noun + into]

    他把纸揉成一团抛进火里。

SYN crumple, crush, squash, crunch  
in AM, use 美国英语用 crush
6 VERB 扭曲(脸);眯起(眼睛) If you screw your face or your eyes into a particular expression, you tighten the muscles of your face to form that expression, for example because you are in pain or because the light is too bright. 扭曲(脸);眯起(眼睛)

  • He screwed his face into an expression of mock pain. [VERB noun + into]

    他面部扭曲，假装疼痛。

SYN contort, twist, distort, contract  
7 V-RECIP (与…)性交 If someone screws someone else or if two people screw, they have sex together. (与…)性交 [rude]
8 VERB 去(你、它)的;见(你、它)的鬼 Some people use screw in expressions such as screw you or screw that to show that they are not concerned about someone or something or that they feel contempt for them. 去(你、它)的;见(你、它)的鬼 [rude, feelings]
9 VERB [usually passive] 欺骗;诈骗(钱财) If someone says that they have been screwed, they mean that someone else has cheated them, especially by getting money from them dishonestly. 欺骗;诈骗(钱财) [informal, rude]

  • They haven't given us accurate information. We've been screwed. [be VERB-ed]

    他们没有提供准确的消息。我们被骗了。

  • The consumer is getting screwed by cover charges as well. [get VERB-ed]

    这名消费者也被诈取了服务费。

10 VERB 敲诈，勒索(钱财等) If someone screws something, especially money, out of you, they get it from you by putting pressure on you. 敲诈，勒索(钱财等) [mainly British, informal]

  • For decades rich nations have been screwing money out of poor nations. [V n + out of]

    富国压榨穷国长达数十载。

SYN squeeze, extort, wring, extract  
11 N-COUNT (囚犯用语)狱警 Prisoners often refer to prison officers as screws . (囚犯用语)狱警 [informal]
12 N-COUNT 螺旋桨 A screw is a propeller on a ship or an aircraft. 螺旋桨 [technical]
13 PHRASE 对…增加压力 If you turn or tighten the screw on someone, you increase the pressure which is already on them, for example by using threats, in order to force them to do a particular thing. 对…增加压力

  • Parisian taxi drivers are threatening to mount a blockade to turn the screw on the government.

    巴黎出租车司机们正威胁阻塞交通以对政府进一步施压。

14 PHRASE 愈逼愈紧的压力(或行动) You can refer to each of a series of threats or actions which are intended to force someone to do a particular thing as another turn of the screw . 愈逼愈紧的压力(或行动)

  • Every rebel raid is another turn of the screw, increasing the pressure on the President.

    每次叛乱分子发动袭击就是又往前逼进了一步，使得总统的压力越来越大。
