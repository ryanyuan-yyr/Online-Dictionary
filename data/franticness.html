frantic
fræntɪk ˈfræntɪk
1 ADJ-GRADED (因害怕或担心)发狂的，狂躁的 If you are frantic, you are behaving in a wild and uncontrolled way because you are frightened or worried. (因害怕或担心)发狂的，狂躁的

  • A bird had been locked in and was by now quite frantic.

    一只鸟被关进了笼子，到现在它都十分狂躁。

SYN frenzied, wild, mad [informal] , raging  
frantically

  • She clutched frantically at Emily's arm.

    她疯狂地紧紧抓住埃米莉的胳膊。

2 ADJ-GRADED 匆忙混乱的;紧张忙乱的 If an activity is frantic, things are done quickly and in an energetic but disorganized way, because there is very little time. 匆忙混乱的;紧张忙乱的

  • A busy night in the restaurant can be frantic in the kitchen.

    餐馆晚上生意好,厨房里可能会忙成一片。

SYN hectic, desperate, frenzied, fraught [informal]  
frantically

  • We have been frantically trying to save her life.

    我们一直在极力想办法挽救她的生命。
