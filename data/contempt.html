contempt
kəntempt kənˈtɛmpt
1 N-UNCOUNT 鄙视;蔑视;轻视 If you have contempt for someone or something, you have no respect for them or think that they are unimportant. 鄙视;蔑视;轻视

  • He has contempt for those beyond his immediate family circle. [+ for]

    他鄙视直系亲属圈子以外的人。

  • I hope voters will treat his advice with the contempt it deserves.

    我希望选民们给予其建议应有的鄙视。

SYN scorn, despite [archaic] , disdain, mockery  
2 N-UNCOUNT 同 contempt of court Contempt means the same as contempt of court . 同 contempt of court [law]

  • Mr. Kelly was sentenced to six months in prison for contempt.

    凯利先生因藐视法庭被判6个月监禁。

3 PHRASE 鄙视;蔑视;轻视 If you hold someone or something in contempt, you feel contempt for them. familiarity breeds contempt→see: familiarity  鄙视;蔑视;轻视

  • Small wonder that many voters hold their politicians in contempt.

    无怪乎很多选民看不起他们的政治家。
