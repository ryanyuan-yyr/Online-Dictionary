uphold
ʌphoʊld ʌpˈhoʊld
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense upholds, present participle upholding, past tense , past participle upheld
1 VERB 拥护；支持；维护 If you uphold something such as a law, a principle, or a decision, you support and maintain it. 拥护；支持；维护

  • Our policy has been to uphold the law. [VERB noun]

    拥护法律是我们一贯的方针。

  • It is the responsibility of every government to uphold certain basic principles. [VERB noun]

    各政府均有责任拥护某些基本原则。

  • ...upholding the artist's right to creative freedom. [VERB noun]

    维护艺术家的创作自由

SYN support, back, defend, aid  
2 VERB 维持，坚持（原判） If a court of law upholds a legal decision that has already been made, it decides that it was the correct decision. 维持，坚持（原判）

  • The crown court, however, upheld the magistrate's decision. [VERB noun]

    然而，最高法院维持了地方法院的判决。

SYN confirm, support, sustain, endorse
