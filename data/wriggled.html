wriggle
rɪgəl ˈrɪgəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense wriggles, present participle wriggling, past tense , past participle wriggled
1 VERB 蠕动，扭动(身体的一部分) If you wriggle or wriggle part of your body, you twist and turn with quick movements, for example because you are uncomfortable. 蠕动，扭动(身体的一部分)

  • The babies are wriggling on their tummies. [VERB]

    婴儿们趴着，扭来扭去。

  • They were fidgeting and wriggling in their seats. [VERB]

    他们在座位上焦躁不安，扭来扭去。

  • She pulled off her shoes and stockings and wriggled her toes. [VERB noun]

    她脱下鞋袜，活动活动脚趾头。

  • ...wriggling worms. [VERB-ing]

    蠕动的虫子

SYN wiggle, jerk, wag, jiggle  
2 VERB 扭动着行进;蠕动着行进 If you wriggle somewhere, for example through a small gap, you move there by twisting and turning your body. 扭动着行进;蠕动着行进

  • He clutched the child tightly as she again tried to wriggle free. [VERB adv./prep.]

    孩子一扭一扭地又想挣脱，他紧紧地抓住了她。

  • Bauman wriggled into the damp coverall. [VERB adv./prep.]

    鲍曼扭着身子穿上潮湿的工作服。
