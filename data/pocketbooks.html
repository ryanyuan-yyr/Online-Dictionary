pocketbook
pɒkɪtbʊk ˈpɒkɪtˌbʊk ˈpɑkɪtˌbʊk
Word forms: plural pocketbooks
1 N-COUNT 经济来源；进账；收入 You can use pocketbook to refer to people's concerns about the money they have or hope to earn. 经济来源；进账；收入 [US, journalism]

  • People feel pinched in their pocketbooks and insecure about their futures.

    人们感到荷包拮据，对未来缺乏安全感。

  • ...the voters' concerns over pocketbook issues.

    选民对收入问题的关注

2 N-COUNT 女用手提包 A pocketbook is a small bag which a woman uses to carry things such as her money and keys in when she goes out. 女用手提包 [US]
in BRIT, use 英国英语用 handbag, bag
3 N-COUNT 皮夹子；钱包 A pocketbook is a small flat folded case, usually made of leather or plastic, where you can keep banknotes and credit cards. 皮夹子；钱包 [mainly US]
in BRIT, usually use 英国英语通常用 wallet
