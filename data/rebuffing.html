rebuff
rɪbʌf rɪˈbʌf
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense rebuffs, present participle rebuffing, past tense , past participle rebuffed
VERB 拒绝;回绝 If you rebuff someone or rebuff a suggestion that they make, you refuse to do what they suggest. 拒绝;回绝 [Also be V-ed in n]

  • He wanted sex with Julie but she rebuffed him. [VERB noun]

    他想和朱莉上床但被她拒绝了。

  • His proposals have already been rebuffed by the Prime Minister. [be VERB-ed + by]

    他的提议已经被首相否决了。

SYN reject, decline, refuse, turn down  
Rebuff is also a noun.

  • The results of the poll dealt a humiliating rebuff to Mr Jones.

    民意调查的结果对琼斯先生很不利，让他颜面尽失。
