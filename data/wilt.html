wilt
wɪlt wɪlt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense wilts, present participle wilting, past tense , past participle wilted
1 VERB 枯萎;凋谢;发蔫 If a plant wilts, it gradually bends downwards and becomes weak because it needs more water or is dying. 枯萎;凋谢;发蔫

  • The roses wilted the day after she bought them. [VERB]

    她买的玫瑰第二天就凋谢了。

  • Remove any damaged or wilted leaves. [VERB-ed]

    去掉烂叶和枯叶。

SYN droop, wither, sag, shrivel  
2 VERB 变得萎靡不振;打蔫儿;消沉 If someone wilts, they become weak or tired, or lose confidence. 变得萎靡不振;打蔫儿;消沉

  • She soon wilted in the morning heat. [VERB]

    在早晨的热浪中她很快就蔫儿了。

  • The government wilted in the face of such powerful pressure. [VERB]

    政府面对如此大的压力，失去了信心。

SYN wane, fail, sink, flag
