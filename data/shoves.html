shove
ʃʌv ʃʌv
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense shoves, present participle shoving, past tense , past participle shoved
1 VERB 猛推;乱挤;推撞 If you shove someone or something, you push them with a quick, violent movement. 猛推;乱挤;推撞

  • He shoved her out of the way. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他一把将她推开。

  • He was then shoved face down on the pavement. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    接着他被一把推倒，脸朝下趴在了人行道上。

  • He's the one who shoved me. [VERB noun]

    他就是那个推撞我的人。

  • She shoved as hard as she could. [VERB]

    她竭尽全力猛地一推。

SYN push, shoulder, thrust, elbow  
Shove is also a noun.

  • She gave Gracie a shove towards the house.

    她猛地将格蕾西朝房子那边推了一把。

2 VERB 乱放;乱塞;随便放;胡乱丢 If you shove something somewhere, you push it there quickly and carelessly. 乱放;乱塞;随便放;胡乱丢

  • We shoved a copy of the newsletter beneath their door. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    我们往他们门下塞了一份时事通讯。

  • He shoved a cloth in my hand. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他往我手中塞了块布。

SYN stick, push, thrust, ram  
3 PHRASE 情况不妙时;别无选择时;形势十分糟糕时 If you talk about what you think will happen if push comes to shove, you are talking about what you think will happen if a situation becomes very bad or difficult. 情况不妙时;别无选择时;形势十分糟糕时 [informal]

  • If push comes to shove, if you should lose your case in the court, what will you do?.

    如果情况不妙，法庭上败诉，你会怎么办？

  • When push comes to shove, you are on your own.

    如果形势糟糕，你就只能靠自己了。
