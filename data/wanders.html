wander
wɒndəʳ ˈwɑndər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense wanders, present participle wandering, past tense , past participle wandered
1 VERB 漫步;闲逛;游荡 If you wander in a place, you walk around there in a casual way, often without intending to go in any particular direction. 漫步;闲逛;游荡

  • When he got bored he wandered around the fair. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他烦了就去集市上逛逛。

  • They wandered off in the direction of the nearest store. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他们信步朝最近的商店走去。

  • Those who do not have relatives to return to are left to wander the streets and sleep rough. [VERB noun]

    举目无亲的人只好四处游荡，露宿街头。

Wander is also a noun.

  • A wander around any market will reveal stalls piled high with vegetables.

    随便到哪个市场逛逛，都能看见蔬菜堆得老高的菜摊。

2 VERB 迷路;迷失方向 If a person or animal wanders from a place where they are supposed to stay, they move away from the place without going in a particular direction. 迷路;迷失方向

  • Because Mother is afraid we'll get lost, we aren't allowed to wander far. [VERB adv./prep.]

    妈妈生怕我们迷路，不准我们走远。

  • To keep their bees from wandering, beekeepers feed them sugar solutions. [VERB]

    为了防止蜜蜂迷路 ，养蜂人给它们喝糖水。

3 VERB 走神;开小差 If your mind wanders or your thoughts wander, you stop concentrating on something and start thinking about other things. 走神;开小差

  • His mind would wander, and he would lose track of what he was doing. [VERB]

    他会走神，忘了自己正在干什么。

  • Jarvis found his attention wandering. [VERB]

    贾维斯发现自己走神了。

  • Grace allowed her mind to wander to other things. [VERB prep./adv.]

    格雷丝任由自己的思绪游走。

SYN stray, roam, digress, get sidetracked  
4 VERB (目光)游离 If your eyes wander, you stop looking at one thing and start looking around at other things. (目光)游离

  • His eyes wandered restlessly around the room. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他的眼睛不安地转来转去，打量着房间。

  • His eyes kept wandering to the picture. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他的眼神不停地瞄到这幅画上。

  • Read their body language. Are their eyes wandering or are they drumming the desk with their fingers? [VERB]

    解读他们的肢体语言。他们的眼睛游离不定吗，抑或他们在用手指不停地敲打桌子？
