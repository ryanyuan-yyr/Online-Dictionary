shovel
ʃʌvəl ˈʃʌvəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense shovels, present participle shovelling, past tense , past participle shovelled
in AM, use 美国英语用 shoveling, shoveled
1 N-COUNT 铲;铁锹 A shovel is a tool with a long handle that is used for lifting and moving earth, coal, or snow. 铲;铁锹

  • ...a coal shovel.

    一把煤铲

  • She dug the foundation with a pick and shovel.

    她用镐和平锹挖地基。

SYN spade, scoop  
2 VERB 铲;铲起;用铲挖 If you shovel earth, coal, or snow, you lift and move it with a shovel. 铲;铲起;用铲挖

  • He has to get out and shovel snow. [VERB noun]

    他得出去铲雪。

  • Pendergood had shovelled the sand out of the caravan. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    彭德古德把旅行拖车里的沙子铲了出来。

SYN move, scoop, dredge, shift  
3 VERB 迅速把…大量送入 If you shovel something somewhere, you push a lot of it quickly into that place. 迅速把…大量送入

  • Randall was shoveling food into his mouth. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    兰德尔将食物大口大口地塞进嘴里。
