seizure
siːʒəʳ ˈsiʒər
Word forms: plural seizures
1 N-COUNT (尤指心脏病或脑部疾病的)突然发作，侵袭 If someone has a seizure, they have a sudden violent attack of an illness, especially one that affects their heart or brain. (尤指心脏病或脑部疾病的)突然发作，侵袭

  • ...a mild cardiac seizure.

    轻度的心脏病发作

  • I was prescribed drugs to control seizures.

    医生给我开了些控制病情发作的药。

SYN attack, fit, spasm, convulsion  
2 N-COUNT (对权力、土地的)夺取;(突然的)占领，控制 If there is a seizure of power or a seizure of an area of land, a group of people suddenly take control of the place, using force. (对权力、土地的)夺取;(突然的)占领，控制

  • ...the seizure of territory through force. [+ of]

    对领土的武装占领

SYN taking, grabbing, annexation, confiscation  
3 N-COUNT (对非法物品的)起获，查封 When an organization such as the police or customs service makes a seizure of illegal goods, they find them and take them away. (对非法物品的)起获，查封

  • Police have made one of the biggest seizures of heroin there's ever been in Britain. [+ of]

    警方这次缴获的海洛因是英国迄今为止数量最大的几宗之一。

  • ...arms seizures.

    缴获武器

SYN confiscation, taking away, appropriation, commandeering  
4 N-COUNT (因欠款未还而采取的)没收，充公 If a financial institution or a government makes a seizure of someone's assets, they take their money or property from them because they have not paid money that they owe. (因欠款未还而采取的)没收，充公

  • A court ordered the seizure of two ships for non-payment of the debt. [+ of]

    因未付债款，法庭下令没收两艘船。
