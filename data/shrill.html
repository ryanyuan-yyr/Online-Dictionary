shrill
ʃrɪl ʃrɪl
Word forms: comparative shriller, superlative shrillest, 3rd person singular present tense shrills, present participle shrilling, past tense , past participle shrilled
1 ADJ-GRADED (声音)刺耳的，尖厉的 A shrill sound is high-pitched and unpleasant. (声音)刺耳的，尖厉的

  • Shrill cries and startled oaths flew up around us as pandemonium broke out.

    骚乱爆发时，我们周围充斥着尖厉的哭喊声和难以入耳的咒骂声。

  • ...the shrill whistle of the engine.

    引擎刺耳的呼啸声

  • Mary Ann's voice grew shrill.

    玛丽·安的声音变得尖厉了。

SYN piercing, high, sharp, acute  
shrilly

  • 'What are you doing?' she demanded shrilly.

    “你在干什么？”她厉声责问。

shrillness

  • ...that ugly shrillness in her voice.

    她的嗓音尖厉刺耳

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (要求、抗议、声明等)尖刻的，强硬的，不依不饶的 If you describe a demand, protest, or statement as shrill, you disapprove of it and do not like the strong, forceful way it is said. (要求、抗议、声明等)尖刻的，强硬的，不依不饶的 [disapproval]

  • Shrill voices on both sides of the Atlantic are advocating protectionism.

    大西洋两岸都在大肆鼓吹贸易保护主义。

3 VERB (铃、哨)发出尖锐的声音，发出刺耳的声音 If a bell or whistle shrills, it makes a loud, high-pitched sound. (铃、哨)发出尖锐的声音，发出刺耳的声音 [written]

  • The phone shrilled, making her jump. [VERB]

    电话铃声刺耳地响起，惊得她跳了起来。

SYN ring, buzz, trill  
4 VERB 尖声说出;尖声喊出 If someone with a high-pitched voice shrills something, they say it loudly. 尖声说出;尖声喊出 [written]

  • 'No, no, no,' she shrilled. [VERB with quote]

    “不，不，不，”她尖叫道。
