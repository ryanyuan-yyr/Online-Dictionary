blubber
blʌbəʳ ˈblʌbər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense blubbers, present participle blubbering, past tense , past participle blubbered
1 N-UNCOUNT 鲸脂；海兽脂 Blubber is the fat of whales, seals, and similar sea animals. 鲸脂；海兽脂

  • The baby whale develops a thick layer of blubber to protect it from the cold sea.

    幼鲸长出一层厚厚的鲸脂，以抵御寒冷的海水。

SYN fat, fatty tissue, adipose tissue  
2 VERB 哭闹；号啕大哭 If someone blubbers, they cry noisily and in an unattractive way. 哭闹；号啕大哭 [informal]

  • She started to blubber like a child. [VERB]

    她开始像个小孩似的放声大哭。

SYN weep, sob, wail, whimper
