trot
trɒt trɑt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense trots, present participle trotting, past tense , past participle trotted
1 VERB 快步走;小步跑 If you trot somewhere, you move fairly fast at a speed between walking and running, taking small quick steps. 快步走;小步跑

  • I trotted down the steps and out to the shed. [VERB prep./adv.]

    我小步跑下台阶，奔向外面的小屋。

  • They trotted along behind him… [VERB prep./adv.]

    他们跟在他身后疾走。

  • A small shabby man was trotting beside Bardi trying to get his attention. [VERB]

    一个衣衫褴褛的矮个子男人在巴迪身边碎步急行而过，想要引起他的注意。

SYN run, jog, scamper, lope  
Trot is also a noun.

  • He walked briskly, but without breaking into a trot.

    他轻快地走着，但没有突然小跑起来。

2 VERB (马等)小跑;骑马小跑 When an animal such as a horse trots, it moves fairly fast, taking quick small steps. You can also say that the rider of the animal is trotting . (马等)小跑;骑马小跑

  • Alan took the reins and the small horse started trotting. [VERB]

    艾伦抓住缰绳，小马开始慢跑起来。

  • Pete got on his horse and started trotting across the field. [VERB prep./adv.]

    皮特跨上马，开始在田野间小跑起来。

Trot is also a noun.

  • As they started up again, the horse broke into a brisk trot.

    当他们又重新骑上马时，马突然轻快地小跑起来。

3 PHRASE 接连地;持续地 If something happens several times on the trot, it happens that number of times without a break. 接连地;持续地 [British, informal]

  • She lost five games on the trot.

    她接连输了 5 场比赛。

  • Sales of new cars rose in May for the second month on the trot.

    5 月份新车的销售量连续第二个月攀升。

SYN one after the other, in a row, in succession, without break
