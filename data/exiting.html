exit
egzɪt eksɪt ˈɛɡzɪt ˈɛksɪt ˈɛgzɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense exits, present participle exiting, past tense , past participle exited
1 N-COUNT 出口；通道 The exit is the door through which you can leave a public building. 出口；通道

  • He picked up the case and walked towards the exit.

    他提起箱子，向出口走去。

  • There's a fire exit by the downstairs ladies room.

    楼下女洗手间旁边有个消防出口。

2 N-COUNT （高速路、公路等的）出口，换道口 An exit on a motorway or highway is a place where traffic can leave it. （高速路、公路等的）出口，换道口

  • Take the A422 exit at Old Stratford.

    从旧斯特拉福特的A422号出口出来。

3 N-COUNT [usually adjective NOUN] 离开;离去 If you refer to someone's exit, you are referring to the way that they left a room or building, or the fact that they left it. 离开;离去 [formal] [formal]

  • I made a hasty exit and managed to open the gate.

    我匆忙离开并设法打开了大门。

4 N-COUNT [usually adjective NOUN] 离开;退出;退场 If you refer to someone's exit, you are referring to the way that they left a situation or activity, or the fact that they left it. 离开;退出;退场 [formal] [formal]

  • ...after England's exit from the European Championship. [+ from]

    英格兰退出欧锦赛后

  • They suggested that she make a dignified exit in the interest of the party.

    他们建议她为了政党的利益体面地下台。

5 VERB 出去;离去;退出 If you exit from a room or building, you leave it. 出去;离去;退出 [formal]

  • She exits into the tropical storm. [VERB]

    她走了出去，消失在热带风暴中。

  • As I exited the final display, I entered a hexagonal room. [VERB noun]

    我从最后一个展厅里出来，走进一个六角形的房间。

  • She walked into the front door of a store and exited from the rear. [VERB + from]

    她走进了一家商店的前门，又从后门离开。

6 VERB 退出，停止运行（计算机程序、系统等） If you exit a computer program or system, you stop running it. 退出，停止运行（计算机程序、系统等） [computing]

  • I can open other applications without having to exit WordPerfect. [VERB noun]

    我不必退出WordPerfect程序就可以打开其他应用程序。

Exit is also a noun.

  • Press Exit to return to your document.

    按“退出”返回文档。
