doctrine
dɒktrɪn ˈdɑktrɪn
Word forms: plural doctrines
1 N-VAR 信条;主义;学说;（尤指）宗教教义 A doctrine is a set of principles or beliefs, especially religious ones. 信条;主义;学说;（尤指）宗教教义

  • ...the doctrine of reincarnation.

    轮回转世说

  • I disagree with the doctrine that the writer's life and intention have no bearing on his texts.

    我不同意作者的生活和意图与他的文本没有联系的学说。

SYN teaching, principle, belief, opinion  
2 N-COUNT (尤指政府外交政策的)正式声明 A doctrine is a statement of official government policy, especially foreign policy. (尤指政府外交政策的)正式声明 [US]

  • Following World War II, the first U.S. commitment to Europe came in the form of the 1947 Truman Doctrine.

    二战后，美国对欧洲作出的第一个承诺便是在 1947 年提出杜鲁门主义。
