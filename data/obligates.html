obligate
ɒblɪgeɪt ˈɑblɪˌgeɪt ˈɑbləgɪt ˈɑbləˌgeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense obligates, present participle obligating, past tense , past participle obligated
VERB 强迫;迫使 If something obligates you to do a particular thing, it creates a situation where you have to do it. 强迫;迫使 [formal]

  • The ruling obligates airlines to release information about their flight delays. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    这一裁决规定航空公司有义务公布航班晚点信息。

  • Under a separation agreement, he was obligated to pay his ex-wife £50,000 a year for life. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    根据分居协议，他必须每年向前妻支付5万英镑。
