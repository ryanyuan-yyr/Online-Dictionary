uproar ʌprɔːʳ ˈʌpˌrɔr
1 N-UNCOUNT [oft a NOUN, oft in NOUN] 喧闹；吵闹；鼓噪；骚动 If there is uproar, there is a lot of shouting and noise because people are very angry or upset about something. 喧闹；吵闹；鼓噪；骚动

  • The announcement caused uproar in the crowd.

    公告在人群中引起了骚动。

  • The courtroom was in an uproar.

    法庭上一片哗然。

SYN commotion, noise, racket, riot  
2 N-UNCOUNT [oft a NOUN] 骚乱；骚动 You can also use uproar to refer to a lot of public criticism and debate about something that has made people angry. 骚乱；骚动

  • The town is in uproar over the dispute.

    整个城市因为这次争端而陷入了骚动。

  • The surprise announcement could cause an uproar in the United States.

    这一突然发布的公告可能会在美国引起骚乱。

SYN protest, outrage, criticism, complaint
