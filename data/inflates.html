inflate
ɪnfleɪt ɪnˈfleɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense inflates, present participle inflating, past tense , past participle inflated
1 V-ERG (使)充气;(使)膨胀;(使)胀大 If you inflate something such as a balloon or tyre, or if it inflates, it becomes bigger as it is filled with air or a gas. (使)充气;(使)膨胀;(使)胀大

  • Stuart jumped into the sea and inflated the liferaft. [VERB noun]

    斯图尔特跳到海里给救生艇充气。

  • Don's lifejacket had failed to inflate. [VERB]

    唐的救生衣未能充气膨胀。

SYN blow up, pump up, swell, balloon  
2 V-ERG 抬高(物价);(使)(物价)涨高 If you say that someone inflates the price of something, or that the price inflates, you mean that the price increases. 抬高(物价);(使)(物价)涨高

  • The promotion of a big release can inflate a film's final cost. [VERB noun]

    大规模发行所需的广告宣传会增加一部电影的最终成本。

  • Clothing prices have not inflated as much as automobiles. [VERB]

    服装价格的涨幅不及汽车那么高。

SYN increase, boost, expand, enlarge  
inflated

  • They had to buy everything at inflated prices at the ranch store.

    他们不得不在农场商店以高价购买所有东西。

3 VERB 夸张;夸大;吹嘘 If someone inflates the amount or effect of something, they say it is bigger, better, or more important than it really is, usually so that they can profit from it. 夸张;夸大;吹嘘

  • They inflated clients' medical treatment to defraud insurance companies. [VERB noun]

    他们夸大客户伤势的严重性和治疗情形以欺骗保险公司。

  • Even his war record was fraudulently inflated. [VERB noun]

    甚至他的参战履历也是夸大其词，充满谎言。

SYN exaggerate, embroider, embellish, emphasize
