slim
slɪm slɪm
Word forms: comparative slimmer, superlative slimmest, 3rd person singular present tense slims, present participle slimming, past tense , past participle slimmed
1 ADJ-GRADED 苗条的;纤细的;修长的 A slim person has an attractively thin and well-shaped body. 苗条的;纤细的;修长的 [approval]

  • The young woman was tall and slim.

    那个年轻的女子身材苗条修长。

  • Jean is pretty, of slim build, with blue eyes.

    珍妮非常漂亮，身材苗条，有一双蓝色的眼睛。

SYN slender, slight, trim, thin  
2 VERB (通过节食)减肥，变苗条 If you are slimming, you are trying to make yourself thinner and lighter by eating less food. (通过节食)减肥，变苗条 [Also VERB n]

  • Some people will gain weight, no matter how hard they try to slim. [VERB]

    有些人无论多么努力地减肥，都会长肉。

  • It makes sense to eat a reasonably balanced diet when slimming. [VERB]

    在减肥过程中保持饮食的营养相对均衡是明智的。

SYN lose weight, diet, get thinner, get into shape

3 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (书、钱包等)薄的 A slim book, wallet, or other object is thinner than usual. (书、钱包等)薄的

  • The slim booklets describe a range of services and facilities.

    这些薄薄的小册子介绍了一系列服务和设施。

  • He published only three slim volumes of verse in his short life.

    在他短暂的一生里，他只出版过 3 卷薄薄的诗集。

4 ADJ-GRADED (可能性)不大的，微乎其微的 A slim chance or possibility is a very small one. (可能性)不大的，微乎其微的

  • There's still a slim chance that he may become Prime Minister.

    他仍然有一丝希望当上首相。

5 VERB 减少(产品);缩减(人员) If an organization slims its products or workers, it reduces the number of them that it has. 减少(产品);缩减(人员) [business]

  • The company recently slimmed its product line. [VERB noun]

    公司最近压缩了产品线。

SYN reduce, cut, cut down, trim
