unwilling
ʌnwɪlɪŋ ʌnˈwɪlɪŋ ʌnˈwɪlɪŋ
1 ADJ-GRADED [usu v-link, usually ADJ to-infinitive] 不愿意的；不情愿的；不乐意的 If you are unwilling to do something, you do not want to do it and will not agree to do it. 不愿意的；不情愿的；不乐意的

  • Initially the government was unwilling to accept the defeat.

    最初政府不愿意承认失败。

  • For months I had been either unwilling or unable to go through with it.

    数月来，我一直不是不愿意就是没办法做这件事。

SYN disinclined, reluctant, averse, loath  
unwillingness

  • ...their unwillingness to accept responsibility for mistakes.

    他们不愿意为错误承担责任

  • ...the unwillingness of banks to grant loans.

    银行的“惜贷”心理

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 不愿意的；勉强的 You can use unwilling to describe someone who does not really want to do the thing they are doing. 不愿意的；勉强的

  • A youthful teacher, he finds himself an unwilling participant in school politics.

    作为一名年轻教师，他发现自己并不情愿地参与到了学校的权力争斗中。

  • She was certainly an unwilling victim of circumstances.

    她成为情势的牺牲品绝对不是自己愿意的。

SYN reluctant, grudging, unenthusiastic, resistant  
unwillingly

  • My beard had started to grow, and I had unwillingly complied with the order to shave it off.

    我的胡子开始长出来，而我不得不服从命令把胡子刮掉。

  • Unwillingly, she moved aside.

    她很不情愿地挪到一边。
