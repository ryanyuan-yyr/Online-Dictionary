strew
struː stru
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense strews, present participle strewing, past tense strewed, past participle strewn
VERB 撒;散播;在…上布满(或撒满) To strew things somewhere, or to strew a place with things, means to scatter them there. 撒;散播;在…上布满(或撒满)

  • The racoons knock over the rubbish bins in search of food, and strew the contents all over the ground. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    浣熊弄翻垃圾箱找吃的，把垃圾弄得满地都是。

  • An elderly woman was strewing the floor with French chalk so that the dancing shoes would not slip. [VERB noun + with]

    一位老妇人正在往地板上撒滑石粉以防止舞鞋打滑。

  • By the end, bodies were strewn all round the headquarters building. [be VERB-ed]

    最终，总部大楼四周遍布着死尸。

SYN scatter, spread, litter, toss
