eccentricity
eksentrɪsɪti ˌɛksɛnˈtrɪsɪtɪ ˌɛksɛnˈtrɪsəti ˌɛksənˈtrɪsəti
Word forms: plural eccentricities
1 N-UNCOUNT 古怪 Eccentricity is unusual behaviour that other people consider strange. 古怪

  • He was known as Mad Shelley partly because of his eccentricity and partly because of his violent temper.

    大家都叫他“疯子谢利”，部分因为他的古怪，部分因为他火爆的脾气。

  • She is unusual to the point of eccentricity.

    她特立独行到了古怪的地步。

SYN oddity, peculiarity, strangeness, irregularity  
2 N-COUNT [usually plural, oft with poss] 古怪行为(或想法);怪癖 Eccentricities are ways of behaving that people think are strange, or habits or opinions that are different from those of most people. 古怪行为(或想法);怪癖

  • We all have our eccentricities.

    我们每个人都有自己的怪癖。

  • ...the eccentricities of British life.

    英国人生活中的种种怪癖

SYN foible, anomaly, abnormality, quirk
