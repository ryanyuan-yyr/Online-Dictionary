overtake
oʊvəʳteɪk ˌoʊvərˈteɪk
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense overtakes, present participle overtaking, past tense overtook, past participle overtaken
1 VERB 超（车）；追上（前面的人） If you overtake a vehicle or a person that is ahead of you and moving in the same direction, you pass them. 超（车）；追上（前面的人） [mainly British]

  • When he eventually overtook the last truck he pulled over to the inside lane. [VERB noun]

    他终于超过了最后那辆卡车后便驶回了内车道。

  • The red car was pulling out ready to overtake. [VERB]

    那辆红色汽车驶离车列准备超车。

SYN pass, leave behind, overhaul, catch up with  
in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 pass
2 VERB 赶上，超越（对手） If someone or something overtakes a competitor, they become more successful than them. 赶上，超越（对手）

  • It's the first time at these games that the Americans have overtaken the Cubans. [VERB noun]

    这是美国人在这一运动会上首次超越古巴人。

  • Lung cancer has now overtaken breast cancer as a cause of death for women in the US.

    肺癌现在已经取代乳腺癌成为美国女性的一大杀手。

  • Sales are booming in Japan, which has overtaken Britain as the Mini's biggest market. [VERB noun]

    迷你车在日本的销量迅猛增长，使日本超过英国成为该车最大的消费市场。

SYN outdo, top, exceed, eclipse  
3 VERB （事件）出人意料地降临于…，突然发生在…身上 If an event overtakes you, it happens unexpectedly or suddenly. （事件）出人意料地降临于…，突然发生在…身上

  • Tragedy was shortly to overtake him, however. [VERB noun]

    然而，不幸将很快降临到他的头上。

SYN befall [archaic , literary] , hit, happen to, come upon  
4 VERB （情感等）压倒，使难以承受 If a feeling overtakes you, it affects you very strongly. （情感等）压倒，使难以承受 [literary]

  • Something like panic overtook me. [VERB noun]

    一种恐慌的感觉袭裹了我。

  • From the moment Edward had told her of the escape attempt, she had been overtaken by a sense of impending doom. [VERB noun]

    自从爱德华告诉她打算逃跑的那一刻起，她便强烈感到厄运在一步步逼近。
