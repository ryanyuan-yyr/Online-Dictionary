ease up
1 PHRASAL VERB （在程度、速度、强度等上）缓和，减轻，减弱 If something eases up, it is reduced in degree, speed, or intensity. （在程度、速度、强度等上）缓和，减轻，减弱

  • The rain had eased up. [VERB PREP.]

    雨小了。

  • New figures indicate the recession may be easing up. [VERB PREP.]

    新的数据显示经济衰退可能有所缓和。

2 PHRASAL VERB 放松;松懈 If you ease up, you start to make less effort. 放松;松懈

  • He told supporters not to ease up even though he's leading in the presidential race. [VERB PREP.]

    他告诉支持者们即使眼下他在总统选举中领先也不要松懈。

  • Christie was easing up over the last 10m to finish third. [VERB PREP.]

    克里斯蒂在最后10英里慢了下来，最终获得了第3名。

3 PHRASAL VERB 宽待;宽容;对…放松 If you ease up on someone or something, your behaviour or attitude towards them becomes less severe or strict. 宽待;宽容;对…放松 [informal]

  • The manager does not intend to ease up on his players for some time. [V P on n]

    主教练并不打算在一段时间内放松对队员的要求。

  • Officials have eased up on the press restrictions. [VERB PREP. + on]

    官员们已经放松了对新闻界的限制。
