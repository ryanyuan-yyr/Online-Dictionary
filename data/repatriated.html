repatriate
riːpætrieɪt -peɪt- riˈpeɪtriˌeɪt riˈpeɪtriɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense repatriates, present participle repatriating, past tense , past participle repatriated
1 VERB 遣返;遣送回国 If a country repatriates someone, it sends them back to their home country. 遣返;遣送回国

  • It was not the policy of the government to repatriate genuine refugees. [VERB noun]

    遣返真正的难民回国并非政府的政策。

  • About 300 French hostages are to be repatriated. [VERB noun]

    大约300名法国人质即将被遣送回国。

repatriation

  • ...the forced repatriation of Vietnamese boat people.

    强制遣返越南船民

2 VERB 把(海外利润）调回国内 If a company repatriates profits that it has made in another country, it brings them back into its home country. 把(海外利润）调回国内

  • Foreign investors are to be allowed to repatriate profits over one billion rupees. [VERB noun]

    外国投资者将被允许将利润额超过10亿卢比的部分调回国内。

repatriation

  • ...penalties on the repatriation of profits.

    对调利润回国的惩罚
