regain
rɪgeɪn rɪˈgeɪn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense regains, present participle regaining, past tense , past participle regained
1 VERB 恢复；失而复得；重新得到 If you regain something that you have lost, you get it back again. 恢复；失而复得；重新得到

  • Troops have regained control of the city. [VERB noun]

    军队重新控制了该市。

  • It took him a while to regain his composure. [VERB noun]

    他过了一会儿才恢复镇定。

SYN recover, get back, retrieve, redeem  
2 VERB 重返 If you regain a place that you have left, you succeed in getting back there. 重返 [formal]

  • Davis went to regain his carriage. [VERB noun]

    戴维斯回到他的马车上。

SYN get back to, return to, reach again, reattain
