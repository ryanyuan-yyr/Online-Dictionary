recapitulate
riːkəpɪtʃʊleɪt ˌrikəˈpɪtʃəˌleɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense recapitulates, present participle recapitulating, past tense , past participle recapitulated
VERB 重述要点；简要回顾 You can say that you are going to recapitulate the main points of an explanation, argument, or description when you want to draw attention to the fact that you are going to repeat the most important points as a summary. 重述要点；简要回顾

  • Let's just recapitulate the essential points. [VERB noun]

    我们再来回顾一下要点。

  • It will shortly be put up for sale under the terms already communicated to you, which, to recapitulate, call for a very minimum of publicity. [VERB]

    不久它就可以上市，按照我们与你商定的条款进行销售，重申一遍，这些条款尽量不要对外公开。

SYN restate, review, repeat, outline  
recapitulation

  • Chapter 9 provides a valuable recapitulation of the material already presented.

    第9章对已经讲过的内容作了很有用的扼要回顾。
