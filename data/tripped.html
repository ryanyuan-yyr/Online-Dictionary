trip
trɪp trɪp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense trips, present participle tripping, past tense , past participle tripped
1 N-COUNT 旅行;出行;旅游 A trip is a journey that you make to a particular place. →see usage note at: journey 旅行;出行;旅游

  • On the Thursday we went out on a day trip.

    星期四我们出去玩了一天。

  • Mark was sent to the Far East on a business trip.

    马克被派往远东出差。

SYN journey, outing, excursion, day out  
2See also:round trip 
3 VERB 绊倒;绊 If you trip when you are walking, you knock your foot against something and fall or nearly fall. 绊倒;绊

  • She tripped and fell last night and broke her hip. [VERB]

    她昨晚绊倒了，摔坏了髋骨。

  • He tried to follow Jack's footsteps in the snow and tripped on a rock. [V + on/over]

    他想要跟着杰克在雪中留下的脚印走，却让一块石头绊倒了。

  • The cables are all bright yellow to prevent you tripping over them. [V on/over n]

    电缆都是亮黄色，以防绊倒人。

SYN stumble, fall, fall over, slip  
Trip up means the same as trip .trip up 同 trip

  • I tripped up and hurt my foot.

    我绊了一下，伤到了脚。

  • Make sure trailing flexes are kept out of the way so you don't trip up over them.

    别让拖地的皮线挡了道，这样你就不会被绊倒。

4 VERB 使绊;将…绊倒; 使跌倒 If you trip someone who is walking or running, you put your foot or something else in front of them, so that they knock their own foot against it and fall or nearly fall. 使绊;将…绊倒; 使跌倒

  • One guy stuck his foot out and tried to trip me. [VERB noun]

    一个家伙把脚伸出来想要绊倒我。

Trip up means the same as trip .trip up 同 trip

  • He made a sudden dive for Uncle Jim's legs to try to trip him up.

    他突然扑向吉姆叔叔的双腿，想要把他绊倒。

  • He was tripped up by a passer-by.

    他被一个路人绊倒了。

5 N-COUNT (对权力等的)迷恋;(内疚、怀旧感等的)困扰 If you say that someone is, for example, on a power trip, a guilt trip, or a nostalgia trip, you mean that their behaviour is motivated by power, guilt, or nostalgia. (对权力等的)迷恋;(内疚、怀旧感等的)困扰 [informal, disapproval]

  • There's such pressure to be happy in Hawaii, if you're unhappy you're on a guilt trip.

    就好像去夏威夷就一定要开心似的，如果你不开心，你会一个劲儿地感到愧疚。

  • The biggest star perk, and the biggest power trip, must be the private plane.

    顶级的明星待遇和无上的权力体验肯定要数私人飞机了。

6 N-COUNT (吸毒所致的)幻觉，迷幻感受 A trip is an experience that someone has when their mind is affected by a drug such as LSD. (吸毒所致的)幻觉，迷幻感受 [informal]

  • An anxious or depressed person can experience a really bad trip.

    焦虑或抑郁的人吸毒后可能会有非常糟糕的幻觉。

7 VERB [usually cont] (服用毒品后)产生幻觉 If someone is tripping, they are having an experience in which their mind is affected by a drug such as LSD. (服用毒品后)产生幻觉 [informal] [Also VERB]

  • One night I was tripping on acid. [VERB + on]

    有一天晚上我服用了迷幻药后正在幻游。

SYN take drugs, get high [informal] , get stoned [slang] , get loved-up [informal]  
8 VERB 轻快地走 If someone trips somewhere, they walk there with light, quick steps. 轻快地走 [literary]

  • A girl in a red smock tripped down the hill. [VERB prep./adv.]

    一个身穿红色罩衫的女孩迈着轻快的步子下山。

  • They tripped along with scarcely a care in the world. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他们轻快地走着，几乎没有世间的烦恼。

SYN skip, dance, spring, hop
