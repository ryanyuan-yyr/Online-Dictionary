goof
guːf guf
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense goofs, present participle goofing, past tense , past participle goofed
1 VERB 犯愚蠢的错误；出大差错 If you goof or goof up, you make a silly mistake. 犯愚蠢的错误；出大差错 [informal] [Also V adv]

  • We goofed last week at the end of our interview with singer Annie Ross. [VERB]

    上周我们采访歌手安妮·罗斯，结果到快结束时犯了个愚蠢的错误。

Goof is also a noun.

  • But was it, in fact, a hideous goof?

    但事实上这是否是个弥天大错？

2 N-COUNT 傻瓜；笨蛋 If you call someone a goof, you think they are silly. 傻瓜；笨蛋 [informal, disapproval]

  • I could write for TV as well as any of those goofs.

    我写的电视剧肯定一点儿不比那帮笨蛋写得差。

Phrasal verbs:
goof around goof off
