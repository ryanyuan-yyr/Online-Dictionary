accede
æksiːd ækˈsid
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense accedes, present participle acceding, past tense , past participle acceded
1 VERB 答应；同意 If you accede to someone's request, you do what they ask. 答应；同意 [formal]

  • Britain would not accede to France's request. [VERB + to]

    英国不肯同意法国的要求。

  • I never understood why he didn't just accede to our demands at the outset. [VERB to noun]

    我一直搞不懂他为什么不一开始就同意我们的要求。

2 VERB （君主）即位 When a member of a royal family accedes to the throne, they become king or queen. （君主）即位 [formal] [Also VERB]

  • ...when Henry VIII acceded to the throne. [VERB to noun]

    当亨利八世即位时
