acknowledgements
ækˈnɒlɪdʒmənts
1 N-SING （对事实、局面等的）承认 An acknowledgement is a statement or action which recognizes that something exists or is true. （对事实、局面等的）承认

  • The President's resignation appears to be an acknowledgment that he has lost all hope of keeping the country together.

    总统的辞职似乎表明他对维护国家稳定已不抱任何希望。

  • He appreciated her acknowledgement of his maturity.

    她承认他的成熟，他对此心怀感激。

2 N-PLURAL （作者的）致谢，鸣谢 The acknowledgements in a book are the section in which the author thanks all the people who have helped him or her. （作者的）致谢，鸣谢
3 N-UNCOUNT 打招呼的表示（如微笑等） A gesture of acknowledgement, such as a smile, shows someone that you have seen and recognized them. 打招呼的表示（如微笑等）

  • Farling smiled in acknowledgement and gave a bow.

    法林微笑示意，又躬身行了个礼。

  • Mitzi nodded a perfunctory acknowledgement as her glass was filled.

    杯子被倒满时米芝敷衍性地点头示意。

4 N-VAR 公开致谢 An acknowledgement is a published statement in which you express your gratitude for or appreciation of something. 公开致谢

  • The benefit to the donor was an acknowledgment of the donation in a printed bulletin.

    捐赠者被印入捐赠公示榜以示感谢。

  • Grateful acknowledgment is made for permission to reprint.

    为获准重印致以深切感谢。

5 N-COUNT （表示已收到信函等的）确认通知，收件复函 An acknowledgement is a letter or message that you receive from someone, telling you that something you have sent to them has arrived. （表示已收到信函等的）确认通知，收件复函

  • I have received neither an acknowledgment nor a reply.

    我未收到任何复函或答复。
