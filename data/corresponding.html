correspond
kɒrɪspɒnd ˌkɒrɪˈspɒnd kɔːr- ˌkɔrəˈspɑnd ˌ kɑrəˈspɑnd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense corresponds, present participle corresponding, past tense , past participle corresponded
1 V-RECIP 相类似;相关;相对应 If one thing corresponds to another, there is a close similarity or connection between them. You can also say that two things correspond . 相类似;相关;相对应

  • Racegoers will be given a number which will correspond to a horse running in a race. [V + to/with]

    观看赛马比赛的观众将获得一个与某一匹赛马相对应的号码。

  • A 22 per cent increase in car travel corresponds with a 19 per cent drop in cycle mileage per person. [V to/with n]

    乘车出行者的比率上升了22%，与之相对应，人均骑自行车的行驶里程下降了19%。

  • The two maps of London correspond closely. [VERB]

    这两张伦敦地图很相似。

  • Her expression is concerned but her body-language does not correspond. [VERB]

    她满脸关切之情，但她的肢体语言并非如此。

SYN be consistent, match, agree, accord  
corresponding

  • March and April sales this year were up 8 per cent on the corresponding period in 1992.

    与1992年同期相比，今年3月和4月的销售额增长了8%。

  • ...the inexorable rise in Britain's fortunes and the corresponding decline of France as an international power.

    英国时来运转，势不可挡，与之相对，法国的国际影响力不断下降

2 V-RECIP (与…)通信 If you correspond with someone, you write letters to them. You can also say that two people correspond . (与…)通信

  • She still corresponds with American friends she met in Majorca nine years ago. [VERB + with]

    她仍与9年前在马略卡岛结识的美国朋友们通信。

  • We corresponded regularly. [VERB]

    我们定期通信。

SYN communicate, write, keep in touch, exchange letters
