bulge
bʌldʒ bʌldʒ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense bulges, present participle bulging, past tense , past participle bulged
1 VERB 凸出；鼓胀 If something such as a person's stomach bulges, it sticks out. 凸出；鼓胀

  • Jiro waddled closer, his belly bulging and distended. [VERB]

    伊罗步履蹒跚地走近，肚子鼓胀凸出。

  • He bulges out of his black T-shirt. [VERB adv./prep.]

    他的肚子在黑色T恤衫下鼓鼓地挺着。

  • He is 6ft 3ins with bulging muscles. [VERB-ing]

    他身高6英尺3英寸，肌肉发达。

SYN swell out, project, expand, swell  
2 VERB （眼睛或血管）凸出 If someone's eyes or veins are bulging, they seem to stick out a lot, often because the person is making a strong physical effort or is experiencing a strong emotion. （眼睛或血管）凸出

  • His eyes seemed to bulge like those of a toad. [VERB]

    他的眼睛鼓得像癞蛤蟆。

  • He shouted at his brother, his neck veins bulging. [VERB]

    他朝他的兄弟吼叫，脖子上青筋暴突。

  • ...bulging eyes. [VERB-ing]

    凸出的眼睛

SYN stick out, project, stand out, protrude  
3 VERB [oft cont] 充满；塞满 If you say that something is bulging with things, you are emphasizing that it is full of them. 充满；塞满 [emphasis] [Also VERB]

  • They returned home with the car bulging with boxes. [VERB + with]

    他们车里满载大包小盒，回到了家。

  • ...a bulging briefcase. [VERB-ing]

    一个塞得满满的公文包

SYN overflow, be full, be packed, be stuffed  
4 N-COUNT 肿块；疙瘩 Bulges are lumps that stick out from a surface which is otherwise flat or smooth. 肿块；疙瘩

  • Why won't those bulges on your hips and thighs go?

    你屁股和大腿上的肿块怎么还不消下去？

SYN lump, swelling, bump, projection  
5 N-COUNT [usually singular] 激增；猛涨 If there is a bulge in something, there is a sudden large increase in it. 激增；猛涨 [Also + of]

  • ...a bulge in aircraft sales. [+ in]

    飞机销量的猛增

  • ...the huge bulge of payments due over the next two years.

    今后两年陆续到期的巨额支付款项

SYN increase, rise, boost, surge
