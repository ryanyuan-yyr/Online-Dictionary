speaking
spiːkɪŋ ˈspikɪŋ
1 N-UNCOUNT 发表讲话;演讲 Speaking is the activity of giving speeches and talks. 发表讲话;演讲

  • It would also train women union members in public speaking and decision-making.

    它还将培养工会的女性成员公开演讲和决策的能力。

  • His work schedule still includes speaking engagements and other public appearances.

    他的工作日程表上还包括演讲安排和在其他公开场合的露面。

2 PREP-PHRASE (表示说话者身份)作为…来说 You can say 'speaking as a parent' or 'speaking as a teacher', for example, to indicate that the opinion you are giving is based on your experience as a parent or as a teacher. (表示说话者身份)作为…来说

  • Well, speaking as a journalist I'm dismayed by the amount of pressure there is for pictures of combat. [+ as]

    嗯，作为一名新闻记者，拍摄战争场面的巨大压力让我深感不安。

3 PREP-PHRASE (接过话头引入新话题)说到…，谈到… You can say speaking of something that has just been mentioned as a way of introducing a new topic which has some connection with that thing. (接过话头引入新话题)说到…，谈到…

  • There's plenty of time to drop hints for Christmas presents! And speaking of presents, we have 100 exclusive fragrance collections to give away. [+ of]

    还有足够的时间去暗示自己想要的圣诞礼物！说到礼物，我们有 100 份限量版香水要赠送。

4 PHRASE 一般说来/从技术角度说来 You use speaking in expressions such as generally speaking and technically speaking to indicate which things or which particular aspect of something you are talking about. 一般说来/从技术角度说来

  • Generally speaking there was no resistance to the idea.

    一般说来，没有人会反对这个观点。

  • Politically speaking, do you think that these moves have been effective?

    从政治角度来说，你认为这些举措有效吗？

-speaking -spiːkɪŋ
COMB in ADJ [ADJ n] (与表示语言的名词连用构成形容词)表示“讲…语言的” -speaking combines with nouns referring to languages to form adjectives which indicate what language someone speaks, or what language is spoken in a particular region. (与表示语言的名词连用构成形容词)表示“讲…语言的”

  • Lessons with English-speaking instructors can be booked and paid for in the resort.

    想上英语外教授课班可以提前预订，当场交费。

  • ...in the mainly French-speaking province of Quebec.

    在主要讲法语的魁北克省
