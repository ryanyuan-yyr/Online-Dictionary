broaden
brɔːdən ˈbrɔdən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense broadens, present participle broadening, past tense , past participle broadened
1 VERB 变宽 When something broadens, it becomes wider. 变宽 [Also VERB]

  • The trails broadened into roads. [VERB + into]

    羊肠小道变成了宽阔的公路。

  • The smile broadened to a grin. [VERB + to]

    笑容变得更加灿烂。

SYN widen, spread, extend, stretch  
2 V-ERG 扩大…的范围；增长，增加（经验、受欢迎程度） When you broaden something such as your experience or popularity or when it broadens, the number of things or people that it includes becomes greater. 扩大…的范围；增长，增加（经验、受欢迎程度）

  • We must broaden our appeal. [VERB noun]

    我们必须进行更广泛的呼吁。

  • I thought you wanted to broaden your horizons. [VERB noun]

    我想你是想拓宽自己的视野。

  • The political spectrum has broadened. [VERB]

    政治范围得到了扩大。

SYN expand, increase, develop, spread  
3 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 开阔（心胸） If an experience broadens your mind, it makes you more willing to accept other people's beliefs and customs. 开阔（心胸）

  • They say that travel broadens the mind.

    他们说旅行能够开阔心胸。
