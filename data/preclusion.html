preclude
prɪkluːd priˈklud prɪˈklud
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense precludes, present participle precluding, past tense , past participle precluded
1 VERB 排除；杜绝；防止 If something precludes an event or action, it prevents the event or action from happening. 排除；杜绝；防止 [formal]

  • At 84, John feels his age precludes too much travel. [VERB noun/verb-ing]

    84岁的约翰感到自己年事已高，不能进行太多的旅行。

  • He would rebuff enquiries in such a way as to preclude any further discussion. [VERB noun/-ing]

    他会断然拒绝询问，使得无法进行进一步的讨论。

SYN rule out, put a stop to, obviate [formal] , make impossible  
2 VERB 阻止；妨碍 If something precludes you from doing something or going somewhere, it prevents you from doing it or going there. 阻止；妨碍 [formal]

  • A constitutional amendment precludes any president from serving more than two terms. [VERB noun + from]

    一项宪法修正案使得任何总统的任职不得超过两届。

  • In some cases poor English precluded them from ever finding a job. [V n from -ing/n]

    英语不好有时会让他们找不到工作。

SYN prevent, stop, check, exclude
