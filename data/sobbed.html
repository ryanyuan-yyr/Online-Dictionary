sob
sɒb ˌɛsˌoʊˈbi sɑb
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense sobs, present participle sobbing, past tense , past participle sobbed
1 VERB 抽噎;呜咽;啜泣 When someone sobs, they cry in a noisy way, breathing in short breaths. 抽噎;呜咽;啜泣

  • She began to sob again, burying her face in the pillow. [VERB]

    她把脸埋在枕头里，又开始抽噎起来。

  • Her sister broke down, sobbing into her handkerchief. [VERB]

    她姐姐再也控制不住自己的情绪，捂着手绢呜咽起来。

SYN cry, weep, blubber, greet [Scottish]  
sobbing

  • The room was silent except for her sobbing.

    房间里静悄悄的，只有她的啜泣声。

2 VERB 呜咽着说;哭诉 If you sob something, you say it while you are crying. 呜咽着说;哭诉

  • 'Everything's my fault,' she sobbed. [VERB with quote]

    她呜咽着说：“一切都是我的错。”

3 N-COUNT 呜咽声 A sob is one of the noises that you make when you are crying. 呜咽声
SYN cry, whimper, howl
