panhandle
pænhændəl ˈpænˌhændəl ˈpænˌhændəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense panhandles, present participle panhandling, past tense , past participle panhandled
1 N-COUNT （伸展到另一地区的）狭长土地，锅柄状地区 A panhandle is a narrow strip of land joined to a larger area of land. （伸展到另一地区的）狭长土地，锅柄状地区 [US]

  • ...the Texas panhandle.

    得克萨斯州的锅柄状地区

2 VERB 在街上乞讨；街头行乞 If someone panhandles, they stop people in the street and ask them for food or money. 在街上乞讨；街头行乞 [mainly US, informal] [Also VERB n]

  • Many of these street people seemed to support themselves by panhandling and doing odd jobs. [VERB]

    这些无家可归者很多似乎都靠街头行乞或者干零工养活自己。

  • There was also a guy panhandling for quarters. [VERB + for]

    还有一个沿街乞讨的家伙。
