trough
trɒf trɔːf trɔf trɑf
Word forms: plural troughs
1 N-COUNT (喂家畜的)饲料槽，饮水槽 A trough is a long narrow container from which farm animals drink or eat. (喂家畜的)饲料槽，饮水槽

  • The old stone cattle trough still sits by the main entrance.

    陈旧的石制牛食槽仍然放在大门口。

SYN manger, crib, water trough  
2 N-COUNT (两个大浪之间的)波谷 A trough is a low area between two big waves on the sea. (两个大浪之间的)波谷

  • The boat rolled heavily in the troughs between the waves. [+ between]

    船在波涛起伏的海浪中剧烈摇摆。

3 N-COUNT (商业活动等的)淡季，低谷 A trough is a low point in a process that has regular high and low points, for example a period in business when people do not produce as much as usual. (商业活动等的)淡季，低谷

  • Looking back afterwards you will see that this was not a terminal trough in your career. [+ in]

    今后回想起来你就会明白，这并非你事业中一蹶不振的低谷。

  • American bank shares have risen by 60% since their trough last October.

    美国的银行股自从去年 10 月跌入低谷以来已经上涨了 60%。

4 N-COUNT 低压槽 A trough of low pressure is a long narrow area of low air pressure between two areas of higher pressure. 低压槽 [technical]

  • The trough of low pressure extended over about 1000 kilometres.

    低压槽延伸约 1,000 公里。
