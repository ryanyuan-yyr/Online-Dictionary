become
bɪkʌm biˈkʌm bɪˈkʌm
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense becomes, present participle becoming, past tense became
The form become is used in the present tense and is the past participle. become 的过去分词与原形相同。
1 V-LINK 变成；成为；变得 If someone or something becomes a particular thing, they start to change and develop into that thing, or start to develop the characteristics mentioned. 变成；成为；变得

  • I first became interested in Islam while I was doing my nursing training. [VERB adj.ive]

    我在接受护理培训的时候开始对伊斯兰教产生了兴趣。

  • The cocoa industry dwindled because it became increasingly difficult to cover costs. [VERB adj.ive]

    由于越来越难以收回成本，可可产业日渐衰落。

  • During the 1980s the world's financial systems became more open. [VERB adj.ive]

    在20世纪80年代，世界金融体系变得更加开放。

  • The pilot decided to land, but as we lost altitude the wind became stronger. [VERB adj.ive]

    飞行员决定着陆，但随着我们越飞越低，风变得越来越大。

  • As she reached the age of thirty she became convinced she would remain single all her life. [VERB adj.ive]

    步入而立之年时，她确信自己会独身一辈子。

  • After leaving school, he became a professional footballer. [VERB noun]

    走出校门后，他成了一名职业足球运动员。

  • In 1823 Honduras became a part of the United Provinces of Central America. [VERB noun]

    1823年，洪都拉斯成为了中美洲联邦的一部分。

SYN come to be, develop into, be transformed into, grow into  
2 VERB [no passive, no cont] 适合；适宜；与…相称 If something becomes someone, it makes them look attractive or it seems right for them. 适合；适宜；与…相称

  • Does khaki become you?. [VERB noun]

    卡其布适合你吗？

  • Don't be crude tonight, Bernard, it doesn't become you. [VERB noun]

    今晚别说荤话，伯纳德，说荤话可不好。

SYN suit, look good on, fit, enhance  
3 PHRASE …怎么样了；…会怎么样 If you wonder what has become of someone or something, you wonder where they are and what has happened to them. …怎么样了；…会怎么样

  • She thought constantly about her family; she might never know what had become of them.

    她一直挂念着家人；她也许再也听不到他们的消息了。

  • Where will he go to school now he's been thrown out of Eton? What will become of him?

    现在他被伊顿公学开除了，他去哪儿上学呢？他将来又会怎样呢？

+−Usage
Usagebecome
Become is a linking verb and may be followed by a noun: I'd like to become a teacher. or by an adjective: In the summer the weather becomes hot.
