plead
pliːd plid
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense pleads, present participle pleading, past tense , past participle pleaded
1 VERB 恳求；请求 If you plead with someone to do something, you ask them in an intense, emotional way to do it. 恳求；请求 [Also V, V that]

  • The lady pleaded with her daughter to come back home. [VERB + with]

    这位女士恳求她的女儿回家。

  • He was kneeling on the floor pleading for mercy. [VERB + for]

    他跪在地板上请求宽恕。

  • 'Do not say that,' she pleaded. [VERB with quote]

    她恳求道：“别那么说。”

  • I pleaded to be allowed to go. [VERB to-infinitive]

    我请求准许离开。

SYN appeal, ask, request, beg  
2 VERB 作（有罪或无罪的）答辩 When someone charged with a crime pleads guilty or not guilty in a court of law, they officially state that they are guilty or not guilty of the crime. 作（有罪或无罪的）答辩

  • Morris had pleaded guilty to robbery. [VERB adj.ive]

    莫里斯已承认犯有抢劫罪。

3 VERB 为…辩护；维护；声援 If you plead the case or cause of someone or something, you speak out in their support or defence. 为…辩护；维护；声援

  • He appeared before the Committee to plead his case. [VERB noun]

    他出现在委员会面前为自己辩护。

  • He would plead the cause of Russian unity. [VERB noun]

    他会公开支持俄罗斯的统一。

4 VERB 提出…为理由；把…作为借口 If you plead a particular thing as the reason for doing or not doing something, you give it as your excuse. 提出…为理由；把…作为借口

  • Mr Burke, pleading poverty, changed his mind. [VERB noun]

    伯克先生以自己穷为由改变了主意。

  • Mr Giles pleads ignorance as his excuse. [VERB noun]

    贾尔斯先生把不知情当作自己的借口。

  • It was no defence to plead that they were only obeying orders. [VERB that]

    辩称他们只是在服从命令站不住脚。

SYN allege, claim, argue, maintain
