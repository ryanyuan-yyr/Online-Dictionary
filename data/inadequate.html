inadequate
ɪnædɪkwət ɪnˈædɪkwət
1 ADJ-GRADED 不充分的;不足的;不够好的 If something is inadequate, there is not enough of it or it is not good enough. 不充分的;不足的;不够好的

  • Supplies of food and medicines are inadequate.

    食物和药品供应不足。

  • The problem goes far beyond inadequate staffing.

    问题远非人手不足那么简单。

SYN insufficient, short, scarce, meagre  
inadequately

  • The projects were inadequately funded.

    这些项目资金不足。

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 不够格的;不能胜任的;不适合的 If someone feels inadequate, they feel that they do not have the qualities and abilities necessary to do something or to cope with life in general. 不够格的;不能胜任的;不适合的

  • I still feel inadequate, useless and mixed up.

    我仍然感到自己能力不够,毫无用处,整个儿一团糟。

  • Mary Ann felt painfully inadequate in the crisis.

    意识到自己在这场危机中无能为力，玛丽·安感到很痛苦。

SYN incapable, incompetent, ineffective, weak
