ennoble
ɪnnoʊbəl ɛˈnoʊbəl ɪˈnoʊbəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense ennobles, present participle ennobling, past tense , past participle ennobled
1 VERB 使崇高;使高尚 To ennoble someone or something means to make them more dignified and morally better. 使崇高;使高尚 [literary]

  • ...the enduring fundamental principles of life that ennoble mankind. [VERB noun]

    使人类彰显高尚的经久不衰的人生基本准则

SYN dignify, honour, enhance, elevate  
ennobling

  • ...the ennobling and civilizing power of education.

    教育使人高尚、具有教养的力量

2 VERB [usually passive] 封…为贵族 If someone is ennobled, they are made a member of the nobility. 封…为贵族 [formal]

  • ...the son of a financier who had been ennobled. [be VERB-ed]

    被封为贵族的金融家之子

  • ...the newly ennobled Lord Archer. [VERB-ed]

    新近被封为贵族的阿彻男爵
