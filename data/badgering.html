badger
bædʒəʳ ˈbædʒər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense badgers, present participle badgering, past tense , past participle badgered
1 N-COUNT 獾 A badger is a wild animal which has a white head with two wide black stripes on it. Badgers live underground and usually come up to feed at night. 獾
2 VERB 纠缠；烦扰；对…软磨硬泡 If you badger someone, you repeatedly tell them to do something or repeatedly ask them questions. 纠缠；烦扰；对…软磨硬泡

  • She badgered her doctor time and again, pleading with him to do something. [VERB noun]

    她屡屡缠着她的医生不放，求他做点什么。

  • They kept phoning and writing, badgering me to go back. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    他们不停地给我打电话、写信，软磨硬泡要我回去。

  • Richard's mother badgered him into taking a Spanish wife. [VERB noun + into]

    理查德的母亲磨破嘴皮子，终于说服他娶了个西班牙太太。

SYN pester, worry, harry, bother
