surpass
səʳpɑːs -pæs sərˈpæs sərˈpɑs
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense surpasses, present participle surpassing, past tense , past participle surpassed
1 VERB 超过;胜过;优于 If one person or thing surpasses another, the first is better than, or has more of a particular quality than, the second. 超过;胜过;优于

  • He was determined to surpass the achievements of his older brothers. [VERB noun]

    他决心超越几个哥哥的成就。

  • Warwick Arts Centre is the second largest Arts Centre in Britain, surpassed in size only by London's Barbican. [VERB-ed]

    沃里克艺术中心是英国第二大艺术中心，规模仅次于伦敦的巴比肯。

SYN outdo, top, beat, best  
2 VERB 远远超出(预期) If something surpasses expectations, it is much better than it was expected to be. 远远超出(预期)

  • Conrad Black gave an excellent party that surpassed expectations. [VERB noun]

    康拉德·布莱克举办了一次远超人们预期的精彩聚会。

3 VERB 超出(理解范围) If something surpasses understanding, it is too difficult to understand. 超出(理解范围)

  • ...a clever and spectacularly successful system, the detailed complexity of which surpasses our full understanding. [VERB noun]

    一个设计巧妙且极其成功的系统，其复杂的细节非我辈所能完全理解
