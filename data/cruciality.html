crucial
kruːʃəl ˈkruʃəl
1 ADJ-GRADED 至关重要的;关键性的 If you describe something as crucial, you mean it is extremely important. 至关重要的;关键性的

  • He had administrators under him but took the crucial decisions himself.

    他手下有管理人员，但重要的决策仍由他自己来做。

  • ...the most crucial election campaign for years.

    几年来最重要的竞选活动

  • Improved consumer confidence is crucial to an economic recovery. [+ to]

    消费者信心的提升对经济的复苏至关重要。

SYN vital, important, pressing, essential  
crucially

  • Chewing properly is crucially important.

    正确的咀嚼方式是极其重要的。

  • Crucially, though, it failed to secure the backing of the banks.

    然而重要的是，它没能获得银行的支持。
