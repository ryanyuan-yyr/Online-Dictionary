startle
stɑːʳtəl ˈstɑrtəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense startles, present participle startling, past tense , past participle startled
VERB 惊吓;使吓一跳 If something sudden and unexpected startles you, it surprises and frightens you slightly. 惊吓;使吓一跳

  • The telephone startled him. [VERB noun]

    电话铃声吓了他一跳。

  • Sorry, I didn't mean to startle you. [VERB noun]

    对不起，我不是故意想吓你一跳的。

  • The news will startle the City. [VERB noun]

    这个消息会让伦敦商业区的人们大吃一惊。

SYN surprise, shock, alarm, frighten
