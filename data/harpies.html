harpy
hɑːʳpi ˈhɑrpi
Word forms: plural harpies
1 N-COUNT [usually plural] 哈比（古典神话中的鸟身人面女妖，性残忍而贪婪） In classical mythology, the harpies were creatures with the bodies of birds and the faces of women. They flew quickly and were cruel and greedy. 哈比（古典神话中的鸟身人面女妖，性残忍而贪婪）
2 N-COUNT 凶悍贪婪的妇人;泼妇;恶妇 If you refer to a woman as a harpy, you mean that she is very cruel or violent. 凶悍贪婪的妇人;泼妇;恶妇 [literary, disapproval]

  • ...a snobby, scheming harpy who sells off the family silverware.

    低价变卖家传银器的势利狡诈的泼妇
