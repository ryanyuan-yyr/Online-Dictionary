prance
prɑːns præns præns prɑns
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense prances, present participle prancing, past tense , past participle pranced
1 VERB 大摇大摆地行走；神气活现地行走 If someone prances around, they walk or move around with exaggerated movements, usually because they want people to look at them and admire them. 大摇大摆地行走；神气活现地行走 [disapproval]

  • He was horrified at the thought of his son prancing about on a stage in tights. [VERB adv./prep.]

    一想到儿子身穿紧身衣在舞台上神气活现地走来走去，他就感到震惊。

2 VERB （马）腾跃，奔驰 When a horse prances, it moves with quick, high steps. （马）腾跃，奔驰

  • Their horses pranced and whinnied. [VERB]

    他们的马奔腾着、嘶鸣着。

  • ...as the carriage horses pranced through the bustling thoroughfares. [VERB prep./adv.]

    当几匹马拉着车在熙熙攘攘的大道上疾驰而过时

  • ...a prancing light-footed mare named Princess. [VERB-ing]

    一匹脚步轻盈地疾跑着的名叫“公主”的母马
