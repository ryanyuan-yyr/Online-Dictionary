await
əweɪt əˈweɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense awaits, present participle awaiting, past tense , past participle awaited
1 VERB 等候；等待 If you await someone or something, you wait for them. 等候；等待 [formal]

  • Very little was said as we awaited the arrival of the chairman. [VERB noun]

    我们等候主席到来的时候都没怎么说话。

  • He's awaiting trial, which is expected to begin early next year. [VERB noun]

    他在等候审判，预计明年初开始。

SYN wait for, expect, look for, look forward to  
2 VERB 即将发生于；将降临到…身上 Something that awaits you is going to happen or come to you in the future. 即将发生于；将降临到…身上 [formal]

  • A nasty surprise awaited them in Rosemary Lane. [VERB noun]

    在罗斯玛丽巷，一起严重的意外事件正等待着他们。

SYN be in store for, wait for, be ready for, lie in wait for
