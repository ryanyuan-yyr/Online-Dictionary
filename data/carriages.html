carriage
kærɪdʒ ˈkærɪdʒ ˈkæriˌɪdʒ
Word forms: plural carriages
1 N-COUNT [oft by NOUN] (旧时的)马车 A carriage is an old-fashioned vehicle, usually for a small number of passengers, which is pulled by horses. (旧时的)马车

  • The President-elect followed in an open carriage drawn by six beautiful gray horses.

    新当选的总统紧随其后，乘坐一辆由6匹漂亮的灰马拉着的敞篷马车。

SYN vehicle, coach, trap, gig  
2 N-COUNT (客运列车的)车厢 A carriage is one of the separate, long sections of a train that carries passengers. (客运列车的)车厢 [British]
in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 car
3 N-COUNT 同 baby carriage A carriage is the same as a baby carriage . 同 baby carriage [US]
4 N-UNCOUNT 运费;运输 Carriage is the cost or action of transporting or delivering goods. 运费;运输 [British, formal]

  • It costs £10.86 for one litre including carriage.

    连运费在内,每升花费10.86英镑。

  • If the Government introduces a carbon tax on road haulage, then carriage by water will become more attractive.

    如果政府开始对公路运输征收碳税,那么水运就会变得更加具有吸引力。

SYN transportation, transport, delivery, conveying  
in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 delivery charge
5 N-UNCOUNT [usually with poss] 仪态;姿态 Your carriage is the way you hold your body and head when you are walking, standing, or sitting. 仪态;姿态 [literary]

  • Her legs were long and fine, her hips slender, her carriage erect.

    她长腿细臀，身姿笔直。

SYN bearing, posture, gait, deportment
