abseil
æbseɪl ˈɑpˌzaɪl ˈɑpˌsaɪl ˈɑbˌzaɪl ˈɑbˌsaɪl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense abseils, present participle abseiling, past tense , past participle abseiled
VERB 缘绳索滑下（峭壁、崖面等）；绕绳下降 To abseil down a cliff or rock face means to slide down it in a controlled way using a rope, with your feet against the cliff or rock. 缘绳索滑下（峭壁、崖面等）；绕绳下降 [British]

  • The man and his son Mark had abseiled down the sheer cliffs. [V down n]

    这个男人和他的儿子马克曾沿绳索爬下过这些陡峭的悬崖。
