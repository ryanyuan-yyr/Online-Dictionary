sprint
sprɪnt sprɪnt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense sprints, present participle sprinting, past tense , past participle sprinted
1 N-SING 短跑 The sprint is a short, fast running race. 短跑

  • Rob Harmeling won the sprint in Bordeaux.

    罗布·哈梅林在波尔多获得短跑冠军。

  • ...the women's 100-metres sprint.

    女子 100 米短跑

2 N-COUNT 短程竞速赛 A sprint is a short race in which the competitors run, drive, ride, or swim very fast. 短程竞速赛

  • Lewis will compete in both sprints in Stuttgart.

    刘易斯将参加在斯图加特举行的两个短跑项目的比赛。

  • I knew there were other riders who could beat me in a sprint.

    我知道还有其他骑手可能在短程赛马中打败我。

3 N-SING 冲刺;快跑 A sprint is a fast run that someone does, either at the end of a race or because they are in a hurry. 冲刺;快跑

  • Gilles Delion, of France, won the Tour of Lombardy in a sprint finish at Monza yesterday.

    法国选手吉勒斯·德利翁昨天在蒙扎凭借最后的冲刺获得伦巴第巡回赛冠军。

  • The police retreated at a sprint.

    警方快速撤退。

  • I broke into a sprint.

    我开始飞快地跑了起来。

4 VERB 全速奔跑;(短距离)冲刺 If you sprint, you run or ride as fast as you can over a short distance. 全速奔跑;(短距离)冲刺

  • Sergeant Horne sprinted to the car. [VERB adv./prep.]

    霍恩中士全力冲向那辆汽车。

SYN run, race, shoot, tear
