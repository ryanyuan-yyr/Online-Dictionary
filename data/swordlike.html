sword
sɔːʳd sɔrd
Word forms: plural swords
1 N-COUNT 剑;刀 A sword is a weapon with a handle and a long sharp blade. 剑;刀
SYN blade, brand [archaic] , trusty steel  
2 PHR-RECIP 与…交锋;与…争论 If you cross swords with someone, you disagree with them and argue with them about something. 与…交锋;与…争论

  • ...a candidate who's crossed swords with Labor by supporting the free-trade pact. [+ with]

    因为支持自由贸易协定而与工党交锋的候选人

  • The fiercest of rivals, the last time they crossed swords was during the 1980s.

    这对争得你死我活的竞争对手的最后一次交锋是在 20 世纪 80 年代。

SYN fight, argue, dispute, disagree  
3 PHRASE (指兼有正面和负面影响的)双刃剑 If you say that something is a double-edged sword or a two-edged sword, you mean that it has negative effects as well as positive effects. (指兼有正面和负面影响的)双刃剑
4 Sword of Damocles→see: Damocles 
Quotations
The pen is mightier than the swordE.G. Bulwer-Lytton Richelieu
All they that take the sword shall perish with the sword Bible: St. Matthew
