splutter
splʌtəʳ ˈsplʌtər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense splutters, present participle spluttering, past tense , past participle spluttered
1 VERB (因窘迫或生气而)结结巴巴地说，语无伦次地说，气急败坏地说 If someone splutters, they make short sounds and have difficulty speaking clearly, for example because they are embarrassed or angry. (因窘迫或生气而)结结巴巴地说，语无伦次地说，气急败坏地说

  • 'But it cannot be,' he spluttered. [VERB with quote]

    “但这不可能，”他气急败坏地说道。

  • Molly leapt to her feet, spluttering and howling with rage. [VERB]

    莫利跳了起来，气急败坏地大吼起来。

Splutter is also a noun.

  • He gave a brief splutter of laughter.

    他干笑了几声。

2 VERB 发出噼啪声 If something splutters, it makes a series of short, sharp sounds. 发出噼啪声

  • Suddenly the engine coughed, spluttered and died. [VERB]

    突然发动机噗噗响起来，噼啪几下就熄火了。
