padlock
pædlɒk ˈpædˌlɑk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense padlocks, present participle padlocking, past tense , past participle padlocked
1 N-COUNT 挂锁；扣锁 A padlock is a lock which is used for fastening two things together. It consists of a block of metal with a U-shaped bar attached to it. One end of the bar is released by turning a key in the lock. 挂锁；扣锁

  • They had put a padlock on the door of his flat.

    他们已经在他的公寓门上上了把挂锁。

SYN lock, fastening, clasp  
2 VERB （用挂锁）锁住 If you padlock something, you lock it or fasten it to something else using a padlock. （用挂锁）锁住 [Also VERB n + to]

  • Eddie parked his cycle against a lamp post and padlocked it. [VERB noun]

    埃迪把他的自行车靠在路灯柱上，用挂锁锁好。

  • An old mailbox has been padlocked shut. [VERB noun]

    一个旧信箱被挂锁锁着。

SYN secure, lock, chain, bolt
