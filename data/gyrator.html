gyrate
dʒaɪreɪt dʒaɪreɪt ˈdʒaɪˌreɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense gyrates, present participle gyrating, past tense , past participle gyrated
1 VERB （人）旋转，转动 If you gyrate, you dance or move your body quickly with circular movements. （人）旋转，转动

  • The woman began to gyrate to the music. [VERB]

    这位女子开始随着音乐旋转。

  • ...a room stuffed full of gasping, gyrating bodies. [VERB-ing]

    挤满了气喘吁吁、不停旋转的人的房间

SYN dance, move, twist, writhe  
gyration

  • Prince continued his enthusiastic gyrations on stage.

    王子继续在舞台上充满激情地旋转。

2 VERB 旋转；回旋 To gyrate means to turn round and round in a circle, usually very fast. 旋转；回旋 [Also VERB]

  • The aeroplane was gyrating about the sky in a most unpleasant fashion. [VERB prep.]

    飞机在空中盘旋，令人不堪忍受。

SYN rotate, circle, spin, spiral  
3 VERB （价格、货币等）剧烈波动，起伏不定 If things such as prices or currencies gyrate, they move up and down in a rapid and uncontrolled way. （价格、货币等）剧烈波动，起伏不定 [journalism] [Also VERB]

  • Interest rates began to gyrate up towards 20 per cent in 1980 and then down and up again. [VERB adv./prep.]

    1980年利率开始攀升至20%，随后又起伏不定。

gyration

  • ...the gyrations of the currency markets.

    货币市场的剧烈波动
