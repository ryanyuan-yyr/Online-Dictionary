oath
oʊθ oʊθ
Word forms: plural oaths
1 N-COUNT （尤指对个人或国家的）誓言，誓约 An oath is a formal promise, especially a promise to be loyal to a person or country. （尤指对个人或国家的）誓言，誓约

  • He took an oath of loyalty to the government. [+ of]

    他宣誓效忠政府。

  • He swore an oath promising to uphold and protect the country's laws and constitution.

    他宣誓捍卫国家的法律与宪法。

2See also:Hippocratic oath 
3 N-SING [oft on/under N] （在法庭上承诺说实话的）誓言 In a court of law, when someone takes the oath, they make a formal promise to tell the truth. You can say that someone is on oath or under oath when they have made this promise. （在法庭上承诺说实话的）誓言

  • His girlfriend had gone into the witness box and taken the oath.

    他的女朋友进入证人席并立了誓。

  • Under oath, Aston finally admitted that he had lied.

    在宣誓的约束下，阿斯顿终于承认自己撒了谎。

  • Three officers gave evidence on oath against him.

    3位警官经宣誓提出了对他不利的证据。

4 N-COUNT （表示愤怒、震惊的）咒骂，诅咒 An oath is an offensive or emphatic word or expression which you use when you are angry or shocked. （表示愤怒、震惊的）咒骂，诅咒 [written]

  • Wellor let out a foul oath and hurled himself upon him.

    韦勒骂了一句粗话，然后向他猛扑过去。

SYN swear word, curse, obscenity, blasphemy
