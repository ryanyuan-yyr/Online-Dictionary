absorption
əbzɔːʳpʃən əbˈsɔːpʃən æbˈsɔrpʃən æbˈzɔrpʃən əbˈzɔrpʃən əbˈsɔrpʃən
1 N-UNCOUNT (液体、气体等的）吸收 The absorption of a liquid, gas, or other substance is the process of it being soaked up or taken in. (液体、气体等的）吸收

  • This controls the absorption of liquids.

    这控制着液体的吸收。

  • Vitamin C increases the absorption of iron from food. [+ of]

    维生素C促进食物中铁的吸收。

SYN soaking up, consumption, digestion, sucking up  
2 N-UNCOUNT [usually with poss] 合并;兼并;同化 The absorption of a group into a larger group is the process of it becoming part of the larger group. 合并;兼并;同化

  • Two new camps were readied for the absorption of refugees.

    为收容难民又备好了两个新营地。

SYN assimilation, integration, inclusion, fusion  
3 N-UNCOUNT [poss N in/with n] 专注；着迷 Someone's absorption in something is the fact that they are very interested in it and that it takes up all their attention and energy. 专注；着迷

  • He was struck by the artists' total absorption in their work.

    他被艺术家对创作的全身心投入所打动。

SYN immersion, holding, involvement, concentration
