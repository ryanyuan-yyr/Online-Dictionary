butter up
PHRASAL VERB 奉承；巴结；讨好 If someone butters you up, they try to please you because they want you to help or support them. 奉承；巴结；讨好 [British, informal]

  • The bank has to butter up investors because it is in a fiercely competitive market. [VERB PREP. noun]

    由于市场竞争激烈，银行不得不去讨好投资者。

  • I tried buttering her up. 'I've always admired people with these sorts of talents.' [VERB noun PREP.]

    我拼命奉承她：“我一直钦佩有如此才能的人。”

SYN flatter, coax, cajole, pander to
