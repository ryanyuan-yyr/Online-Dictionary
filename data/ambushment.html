ambush
æmbʊʃ ˈæmˌbʊʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense ambushes, present participle ambushing, past tense , past participle ambushed
1 VERB 打埋伏；伏击 If a group of people ambush their enemies, they attack them after hiding and waiting for them. 打埋伏；伏击

  • The Guatemalan army says rebels ambushed and killed 10 patrolmen. [VERB noun]

    危地马拉军方说叛乱分子伏击打死了10名巡逻兵。

SYN trap, attack, surprise, deceive  
2 N-VAR 埋伏；伏击 An ambush is an attack on someone by people who have been hiding and waiting for them. 埋伏；伏击

  • A policeman has been shot dead in an ambush.

    一次伏击中一名警察中弹身亡。

SYN trap, snare, attack, lure  
3 PHRASE 埋伏着 If someone is lying in ambush, they are hiding and waiting for someone, usually to attack them. 埋伏着

  • The gunmen, lying in ambush, opened fire, killing the driver.

    埋伏的枪手开枪打死了司机。
