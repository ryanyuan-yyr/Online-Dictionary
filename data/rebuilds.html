rebuild
riːbɪld riːˈbɪld riˈbɪld
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense rebuilds, present participle rebuilding, past tense , past participle rebuilt
1 VERB 重建；重修 When people rebuild something such as a building or a city, they build it again after it has been damaged or destroyed. 重建；重修

  • They say they will stay to rebuild their homes rather than retreat to refugee camps. [VERB noun]

    他们说他们将留下重建家园而不是躲到难民营里。

  • The castle was rebuilt by his great grandson in 1859. [VERB noun]

    城堡在1859年由他的曾孙重建。

SYN reconstruct, restore, revamp, renovate  
2 VERB 重建，重组，复原（机构、制度或生活的某些方面） When people rebuild something such as an institution, a system, or an aspect of their lives, they take action to bring it back to its previous condition. 重建，重组，复原（机构、制度或生活的某些方面）

  • Everyone would have to work hard together to rebuild the economy. [VERB noun]

    所有人都应该一起努力以重建经济。

  • Jane began to rebuild her social life which had been non-existent since her marriage. [VERB noun]

    简开始重新建立自己自结婚以来便陷于沉寂的社交生活。

  • The agency has been rebuilding under new management. [VERB]

    这个机构在新的管理机制下一直在进行重建。

3 VERB 复原，修复（身体某一部分） If doctors rebuild part of someone's body that has been damaged, they operate on that person to make them well again. 复原，修复（身体某一部分）

  • Two weeks later, surgeons carried out the operation to rebuild his face. [VERB noun]

    两周后，外科医生实施了手术以复原他的面部。
