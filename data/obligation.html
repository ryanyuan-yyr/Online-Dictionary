obligation
ɒblɪgeɪʃən ˌɑblɪˈgeɪʃən
Word forms: plural obligations
1 N-VAR [usually NOUN to-infinitive] 义务;责任 If you have an obligation to do something, it is your duty to do that thing. 义务;责任

  • When teachers assign homework, students usually feel an obligation to do it.

    老师布置作业时，学生通常认为完成作业是一种义务。

  • Ministers are under no obligation to follow the committee's recommendations.

    部长不必非得听从委员会的建议。

2 N-VAR (照顾某人或保护某人利益的)义务，责任 If you have an obligation to a person, it is your duty to look after them or protect their interests. (照顾某人或保护某人利益的)义务，责任

  • The United States will do that which is necessary to meet its obligations to its own citizens. [+ to]

    美国将为自己的公民尽其应尽的义务。

  • I have an ethical and a moral obligation to my client. [+ to]

    我对我的当事人负有道义上的责任。

SYN responsibility, duty, liability, accountability  
3 PHRASE (广告中产品或服务)免费试用地，免费体验地 In advertisements, if a product or a service is available without obligation, you do not have to pay for that product or service until you have tried it and are satisfied with it. (广告中产品或服务)免费试用地，免费体验地

  • If you are selling your property, why not call us for a free valuation without obligation?.

    如果您打算出售房产，何不打电话让我们作免费估价呢？

  • You can review your policy in detail for a full 15 days without obligation.

    您可以在15天的免费体验期内仔细审核保单细则。

SYN free, for free [informal] , for nothing, unpaid
