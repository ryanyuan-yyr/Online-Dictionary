dawdle
dɔːdəl ˈdɔdəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dawdles, present participle dawdling, past tense , past participle dawdled
1 VERB 闲逛;游荡 If you dawdle, you spend more time than is necessary going somewhere. 闲逛;游荡

  • Eleanor will be back any moment, if she doesn't dawdle. [VERB]

    如果不闲荡的话，埃莉诺随时都会回来。

  • They dawdled arm in arm past the shopfronts. [VERB]

    他们手挽手慢悠悠地逛过一家家店面。

SYN waste time, potter, trail, lag  
2 VERB 慢吞吞地做;（在…上）磨蹭 If you dawdle over something, you spend more time than is necessary doing something. 慢吞吞地做;（在…上）磨蹭

  • He got fed up as bank staff dawdled over cashing him a cheque. [V over n/-ing]

    银行职员慢吞吞地给他兑现支票，他憋了一肚子气。

  • She downed two glasses of white wine while I dawdled over a draught beer. [V over n/-ing]

    我慢悠悠地呷着一杯散装啤酒的工夫她已经灌下了两杯白葡萄酒。
