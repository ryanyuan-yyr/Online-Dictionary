snub
snʌb snʌb
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense snubs, present participle snubbing, past tense , past participle snubbed
1 VERB 冷落;怠慢;不理睬 If you snub someone, you deliberately insult them by ignoring them or by behaving or speaking rudely towards them. 冷落;怠慢;不理睬

  • He snubbed her in public and made her feel an idiot. [VERB noun]

    他当众冷落她，让她觉得自己像一个白痴似的。

  • They snubbed his invitation to a meeting of foreign ministers at the UN.

    他们没有理睬他发出的出席联合国外交部长会议的邀请。

SYN insult, slight, put down, humiliate  
2 N-COUNT 冷落;怠慢 If you snub someone, your behaviour or your remarks can be referred to as a snub . 冷落;怠慢

  • Ryan took it as a snub.

    瑞安觉得这是一种怠慢。

  • The German move was widely seen as a deliberate snub to Mr Cook.

    德国方面的举动被普遍视为对库克先生的有意怠慢。

SYN insult, put-down, humiliation, affront  
3 ADJ [ADJ n] (鼻子)短而略向上翘的 Someone who has a snub nose has a short nose which points slightly upwards. (鼻子)短而略向上翘的
