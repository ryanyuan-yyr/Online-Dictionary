thank
θæŋk θæŋk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense thanks, present participle thanking, past tense , past participle thanked
1 CONVENTION 谢谢您;谢谢;多谢 You use thank you or, in more informal English, thanks to express your gratitude when someone does something for you or gives you what you want. 谢谢您;谢谢;多谢 [formulae]

  • Thank you very much for your call.

    非常感谢您的来电。

  • Thanks for the information. [+ for]

    谢谢提供这个信息。

  • Oh thank you so much! They're so pretty!.

    哦，太感谢你了！它们真漂亮！

  • Thanks a lot, Suzie. You've been great.

    多谢了，苏兹。你太好了。

2 CONVENTION 谢谢你，多谢(用于有礼貌地接受或拒绝) You use thank you or, in more informal English, thanks to politely accept or refuse something that has just been offered to you. 谢谢你，多谢(用于有礼貌地接受或拒绝) [formulae]

  • 'You'd like a cup as well, would you, Mr Secombe?' — 'Thank you, Jane, I'd love one.'.

    “塞科姆先生，你也想来一杯吧？”—“谢谢，简，我确实想来一杯。”

  • 'Would you like a cigarette?' — 'No thank you.'.

    “要来支烟吗？”—“不了，谢谢。”

  • 'A whisky?' — 'I'd better not, thanks.'

    “来杯威士忌？”—“还是不要了，谢谢。”

3 CONVENTION 谢谢你，多谢(尤用于礼貌地感谢对方的回答或所说的令人开心的话) You use thank you or, in more informal English, thanks to politely acknowledge what someone has said to you, especially when they have answered your question or said something nice to you. 谢谢你，多谢(尤用于礼貌地感谢对方的回答或所说的令人开心的话) [formulae]

  • The policeman smiled at her. 'Pretty dog.' — 'Oh well, thank you.'.

    警察冲她笑了笑，“这狗真漂亮。”—“哦，谢谢。”

  • 'His eyes were glassy?' — 'And dilated. They were watery.' — 'Thank you.'.

    “他的眼神呆滞？”—“还双眼圆睁，泪汪汪的。”—“谢谢！”

  • 'It's great to see you.' — 'Thanks. Same to you.'

    “看见你很高兴。”—“谢谢，我也是。”

4 CONVENTION 谢谢，非常感谢(用于坚决回绝别人的帮助或表示不喜欢别人的举动) You use thank you or thank you very much in order to say firmly that you do not want someone's help or to tell them that you do not like the way that they are behaving towards you. 谢谢，非常感谢(用于坚决回绝别人的帮助或表示不喜欢别人的举动) [emphasis]

  • I can stir my own tea, thank you.

    我的茶我自己会搅，谢谢。

  • We know where we can get it, thank you very much.

    我们知道在哪儿能买到，谢谢。

5 VERB 向…表示谢意;感谢 When you thank someone for something, you express your gratitude to them for it. 向…表示谢意;感谢

  • I thanked them for their long and loyal service. [VERB noun + for]

    我感谢他们长期以来忠诚的效力。

  • When the decision was read out Mrs Gardner thanked the judges. [VERB noun]

    判决书宣读完，加德纳夫人向法官们表示感谢。

SYN say thank you to, express gratitude to, show gratitude to, show your appreciation to  
6 N-PLURAL 感谢;谢意;感激 When you express your thanks to someone, you express your gratitude to them for something. 感谢;谢意;感激

  • They accepted their certificates with words of thanks.

    他们一边道谢，一边接过证书。

SYN say thank you to, express gratitude to, show gratitude to, show your appreciation to  
7 See also: thankyou 
8 PHRASE [VERB inflects, oft PHR for n] 感谢(上帝);感恩 When people give thanks, they thank God for something good that has happened. 感谢(上帝);感恩

  • We give thanks for this food.

    感谢上帝赐给我们食物。

9 PHRASE 谢天谢地;感谢上帝 You say 'Thank God', 'Thank Goodness', or 'Thank heavens' when you are very relieved about something. 谢天谢地;感谢上帝 [feelings]

  • I was wrong, thank God.

    原来我错了，感谢上帝。

  • Thank heavens we have you here.

    谢天谢地，我们可等到你了。

10 PHRASE 要向…表示感谢;多亏 If you say that you have someone to thank for something, you mean that you are grateful to them because they caused it to happen. 要向…表示感谢;多亏

  • I have her to thank for my life. [+ for]

    我这条命多亏了她。

  • For all this I have only you to thank.

    这一切我都得感谢你才是。

11 PHRASE 幸亏;归因于 If you say that something happens thanks to a particular person or thing, you mean that they are responsible for it happening or caused it to happen. 幸亏;归因于

  • It is thanks to this committee that many new sponsors have come forward. [+ to]

    又有许多赞助商找上门来，这要归功于这个委员会。

  • Thanks to recent research, effective treatments are available.

    多亏近来的研究才有了有效的疗法。

SYN because of, through, due to, as a result of  
12 PHRASE 并非由于…才(发生);并不归功于 If you say that something happens no thanks to a particular person or thing, you mean that they did not help it to happen, or that it happened in spite of them. 并非由于…才(发生);并不归功于

  • It is no thanks to the Government that net assets did rise. [+ to]

    净资产的确增长了，但这与政府无关。
