resonate
rezəneɪt ˈrɛzəˌneɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense resonates, present participle resonating, past tense , past participle resonated
1 VERB 发出嘹亮的声音;回响;回荡 If something resonates, it vibrates and produces a deep, strong sound. 发出嘹亮的声音;回响;回荡

  • The bass guitar began to thump so loudly that it resonated in my head. [VERB]

    低音电吉他开始砰砰作响，巨大的声音一直在我的脑海里回响。

SYN reverberate, echo, resound, vibrate  
2 VERB 有特殊意义;引起共鸣 You say that something resonates when it has a special meaning or when it is particularly important to someone. 有特殊意义;引起共鸣

  • London is confident and alive, resonating with all the qualities of a civilised city. [VERB + with]

    伦敦洋溢着自信和活力，呈现出一个文明城市的所有特性。
