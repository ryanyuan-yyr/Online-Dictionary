tie-dye
ˈtaɪˌdaɪ ˈtaɪˌdaɪ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense tie-dyes, present participle tie-dyeing, past tense , past participle tie-dyed
1 VERB [usually passive] 扎染(指将布料打结后用染料染色) If a piece of cloth or a garment is tie-dyed, it is tied in knots and then put into dye, so that some parts become more deeply coloured than others. 扎染(指将布料打结后用染料染色)

  • He wore a T-shirt that had been tie-dyed in bright colours. [be VERB-ed]

    他身穿一件颜色鲜艳的扎染的 T 恤。

  • I bought a great tie-dyed silk scarf. [VERB-ed]

    我买了条非常好的扎染丝巾。

2 N-VAR [usually NOUN n] 扎染衣服;扎染布料 A tie-dye is a garment or piece of cloth that has been tie-dyed. 扎染衣服;扎染布料

  • They wore tie-dyes and ponchos.

    他们身穿扎染衣服和披风。

  • ...a hideous tie-dye shirt.

    丑陋的扎染衬衫
