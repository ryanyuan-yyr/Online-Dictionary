intimidate
ɪntɪmɪdeɪt ɪnˈtɪməˌdeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense intimidates, present participle intimidating, past tense , past participle intimidated
VERB 恫吓;恐吓;威胁 If you intimidate someone, you deliberately make them frightened enough to do what you want them to do. 恫吓;恐吓;威胁

  • Jones had set out to intimidate and dominate Paul. [VERB noun]

    琼斯开始恐吓并控制保罗。

  • Attempts to intimidate people into voting for the governing party did not work. [VERB noun + into]

    胁迫人们为执政党投票的企图没有成功。

SYN frighten, pressure, threaten, alarm  
intimidation

  • ...an inquiry into allegations of intimidation during last week's vote.

    对上周投票期间出现胁迫投票的指控的调查
