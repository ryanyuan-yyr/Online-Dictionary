stew
stjuː stuː stu stju
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense stews, present participle stewing, past tense , past participle stewed
1 N-VAR 炖煮的菜肴 A stew is a meal which you make by cooking meat and vegetables in liquid at a low temperature. 炖煮的菜肴

  • She served him a bowl of beef stew.

    她为他端上了一碗炖牛肉。

  • They made a stew.

    他们做了一道炖菜。

SYN hash, goulash, ragout, olla  
2 VERB 炖;煨;焖 When you stew meat, vegetables, or fruit, you cook them slowly in liquid in a closed dish. 炖;煨;焖

  • Stew the apple and blackberries to make a thick pulp. [VERB noun]

    将苹果和黑莓炖成稠浆。

  • ...stewed prunes. [VERB-ed]

    炖李子

SYN braise, boil, simmer, casserole  
3 PHRASE [PHRASE after verb, verb-link PHRASE] 深感不安的;焦虑的;担心的 If you are in a stew, you feel very worried. 深感不安的;焦虑的;担心的 [informal]

  • He's been in a stew since early this morning.

    他从今天一大早就焦虑不安。

  • Highly charged emotions have you in a stew.

    高度紧张的情绪会让人陷入不安。

SYN troubled, concerned, anxious, worried  
4 PHRASE [VERB inflects] (故意)让…独自烦恼(而不加劝慰) If you let a person stew or if you leave them to stew, you deliberately allow them to worry about something for a while, rather than telling them something which would make them feel better. (故意)让…独自烦恼(而不加劝慰)

  • I'd rather let him stew.

    我宁可让他一个人去烦恼。

  • Leave them to stew in their own juice.

    让他们自食其果吧。
