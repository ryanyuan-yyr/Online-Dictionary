flirt
flɜːʳt flɜrt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense flirts, present participle flirting, past tense , past participle flirted
1 V-RECIP (和…)调情;(和…)打情骂俏 If you flirt with someone, you behave as if you are sexually attracted to them, in a playful or not very serious way. (和…)调情;(和…)打情骂俏

  • Dad's flirting with all the ladies, or they're all flirting with him, as usual. [VERB + with]

    和平常一样，爸爸在和所有的女士调情，或者说所有的女士都在和他调情。

  • He flirts outrageously. [VERB]

    他肆无忌惮地调情。

SYN chat up, lead on [informal] , dally with, make advances at  
flirtation

  • ...a professor who has a flirtation with a student.

    和一名学生打情骂俏的教授

  • She was aware of his attempts at flirtation.

    她知道他想要挑逗她。

2 N-COUNT 调情老手 Someone who is a flirt likes to flirt a lot. 调情老手
SYN tease, philanderer, coquette, heart-breaker  
3 VERB 不认真地考虑;有…的念头(或想法) If you flirt with the idea of something, you consider it but do not do anything about it. 不认真地考虑;有…的念头(或想法)

  • Di Pietro, 45, has been flirting with the idea of a political career. [VERB + with]

    迪彼得罗，45 岁，一直有从政的念头。

  • They flirted with the idea of making records throughout the 1980s. [VERB with noun]

    他们想过要在20世纪80年代不停地出唱片。
