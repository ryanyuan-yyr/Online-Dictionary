dodge
dɒdʒ dɑdʒ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense dodges, present participle dodging, past tense , past participle dodged
1 VERB 闪开;躲开;避开 If you dodge, you move suddenly, often to avoid being hit, caught, or seen. 闪开;躲开;避开

  • He dodged amongst the seething crowds of men. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他在川流不息的人群中东躲西闪。

  • We dodged behind a pillar out of sight of the tourists. [VERB prep./adv.]

    我们躲到柱子后面，避开了观光者的视线。

SYN duck [informal] , dart, swerve, sidestep  
2 VERB 闪身躲开;避开 If you dodge something, you avoid it by quickly moving aside or out of reach so that it cannot hit or reach you. 闪身躲开;避开

  • He desperately dodged a speeding car trying to run him down. [VERB noun]

    他拼命地闪身躲开一辆试图撞倒他的疾驰的汽车。

3 VERB (常指用欺诈的方式故意)躲避，逃避 If you dodge something, you deliberately avoid thinking about it or dealing with it, often by being deceitful. (常指用欺诈的方式故意)躲避，逃避

  • He boasts of dodging military service by feigning illness. [VERB noun]

    他炫耀自己通过装病来逃避服兵役。

  • Many struggling firms are ready to break the law by dodging tax.

    很多挣扎求生的企业不惜违法逃税。

Dodge is also a noun.

  • This was not just a tax dodge.

    这不仅仅是逃税。
