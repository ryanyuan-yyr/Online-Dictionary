stock-in-trade ˌstɒkɪnˈtreɪd
(also stock in trade)
N-SING [with poss] 习惯性行为;惯用伎俩 If you say that something is someone's stock-in-trade, you mean that it is a usual part of their behaviour or work. 习惯性行为;惯用伎俩

  • Patriotism is every politician's stock-in-trade.

    爱国主义是政客们的惯用伎俩。

  • Delicious potted shrimps and prawns were once the stock-in-trade of the harbourside cafe.

    美味的火锅虾曾经是海港周围咖啡馆的招牌菜。
