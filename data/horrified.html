horrify
hɒrɪfaɪ hɔːr- ˈhɔrəˌfaɪ ˈhɑrəfaɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense horrifies, present participle horrifying, past tense , past participle horrified
VERB 使感到震惊;使毛骨悚然;使产生反感 If someone is horrified, they feel shocked or disgusted, usually because of something that they have seen or heard. 使感到震惊;使毛骨悚然;使产生反感

  • His family were horrified by the change. [be VERB-ed]

    他的家人对这一变化感到震惊。

  • ...a crime trend that will horrify all parents. [VERB noun]

    会让所有父母感到毛骨悚然的犯罪趋势

SYN terrify, alarm, frighten, scare
