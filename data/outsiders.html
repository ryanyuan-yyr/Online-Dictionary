outsider
aʊtsaɪdəʳ ˌaʊtˈsaɪdə ˌaʊtˈsaɪdər ˈaʊtˌsaɪdər
Word forms: plural outsiders
1 N-COUNT （某个团体或组织）外部的人 An outsider is someone who does not belong to a particular group or organization. （某个团体或组织）外部的人

  • The most likely outcome may be to subcontract much of the work to an outsider.

    最可能的结果是将大量的工作分包出去。

2 N-COUNT （不为某一团体所接受或感觉不属其中的）外人 An outsider is someone who is not accepted by a particular group, or who feels that they do not belong in it. （不为某一团体所接受或感觉不属其中的）外人

  • Malone, a cop, felt as much an outsider as any of them.

    马隆是个警察，他跟他们一样都觉得自己像个外人。

3 N-COUNT （在竞赛中）不大可能获胜的人（或队伍） In a competition, an outsider is a competitor who is unlikely to win. （在竞赛中）不大可能获胜的人（或队伍）

  • He was an outsider in the race to be the new UN Secretary-General.

    在新一任联合国秘书长的角逐中，他的胜算很小。
