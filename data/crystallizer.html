crystallize
krɪstəlaɪz ˈkrɪstəlˌaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense crystallizes, present participle crystallizing, past tense , past participle crystallized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 crystallise
1 V-ERG (使)(意见或想法)明确;(使)具体化 If you crystallize an opinion or idea, or if it crystallizes, it becomes fixed and definite in someone's mind. (使)(意见或想法)明确;(使)具体化

  • He has managed to crystallise the feelings of millions of ordinary Russians. [VERB noun]

    他弄清了百万普通俄罗斯人的感受。

  • Now my thoughts really began to crystallise. [VERB]

    现在我的想法开始真正成形了。

SYN take shape, form, become clear, come together  
crystallization

  • ...encouraging the crystallization of new values.

    鼓励形成新的价值观

2 V-ERG (使)形成晶体;(使)结晶 If a substance crystallizes, or something crystallizes it, it turns into crystals. (使)形成晶体;(使)结晶

  • Don't stir or the sugar will crystallise. [VERB]

    不要搅，否则糖会结晶。

  • ...a 19th century technique that actually crystallizes the tin. [VERB noun]

    19世纪已然能使锡结晶的技术

crystallization

  • ...experiments on the crystallisation of glass.

    研究玻璃结晶的实验
