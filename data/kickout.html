kick out
1 PHRASAL VERB 强使离开；赶走 To kick someone out of a place means to force them to leave it. 强使离开；赶走 [informal] [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • The country's leaders kicked five foreign journalists out of the country. [VERB noun PREP. + of]

    该国领导将5名外国记者驱逐出境。

  • Her family kicked her out. [VERB noun PREP.]

    她的家人把她赶了出来。

SYN dismiss, remove, reject, get rid of
