depress
dɪpres diˈprɛs dɪˈprɛs
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense depresses, present participle depressing, past tense , past participle depressed
1 VERB 使抑郁;使沮丧;使消沉 If someone or something depresses you, they make you feel sad and disappointed. 使抑郁;使沮丧;使消沉

  • I must admit the state of the country depresses me. [VERB noun]

    我必须承认国家的现状让我倍感沮丧。

  • I know he is too optimistic but I don't want to depress him. [VERB noun]

    我知道他太过乐观了，但我不想令他灰心丧气。

SYN sadden, upset, distress, chill  
2 VERB 降低;减少 If something depresses prices, wages, or figures, it causes them to become less. 降低;减少

  • The stronger U.S. dollar depressed sales. [VERB noun]

    美元走强导致销售额下降。
