intransigent
ɪntrænsɪdʒənt ɪnˈtrænsədʒənt ɪnˈtrænzədʒənt
ADJ-GRADED 不妥协的;不让步的;不调和的 If you describe someone as intransigent, you mean that they refuse to behave differently or to change their attitude to something. 不妥协的;不让步的;不调和的 [formal, disapproval]

  • They put pressure on the Government to change its intransigent stance.

    他们向政府施压以迫使其改变不妥协的立场。

  • The worry is that the radicals will grow more intransigent.

    现在人们担忧激进分子会变得更加不妥协。

SYN uncompromising, intractable, tough, stubborn
