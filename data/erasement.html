erase
ɪreɪz ɪreɪs iˈreɪs ɪˈreɪs
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense erases, present participle erasing, past tense , past participle erased
1 VERB 忘却,抹去,消除(想法或感受) If you erase a thought or feeling, you destroy it completely so that you can no longer remember something or no longer feel a particular emotion. 忘却,抹去,消除(想法或感受)

  • They are desperate to erase the memory of that last defeat in Cardiff. [VERB noun]

    他们急于抹去上次在卡迪夫遭遇失败的记忆。

  • Love was a word he'd erased from his vocabulary since Susan's going. [VERB noun + from]

    自从苏珊离开后，他再也不提“爱情”这个词了。

SYN delete, cancel out, wipe out, remove  
2 VERB 抹掉（磁带录音）；删除（计算机文件） If you erase sound which has been recorded on a tape or information which has been stored in a computer, you completely remove or destroy it. 抹掉（磁带录音）；删除（计算机文件） [Also V n + from]

  • He was in the studio tearfully erasing all the tapes he'd slaved over. [VERB noun]

    他在录音棚里流着眼泪抹掉他辛苦录制的所有磁带。

  • It appears the names were accidentally erased from computer disks. [be VERB-ed + from]

    看起来那些名字被意外地从计算机磁盘上删除了。

3 VERB 擦掉;抹去 If you erase something such as writing or a mark, you remove it, usually by rubbing it with a cloth. 擦掉;抹去

  • It was unfortunate that she had erased the message. [VERB noun]

    不幸的是她已经删除了那条短信。
