hallmark
hɔːlmɑːʳk ˈhɔlˌmɑrk
Word forms: plural hallmarks
1 N-COUNT [usually with poss] 标志;特征;特点 The hallmark of something or someone is their most typical quality or feature. 标志;特征;特点

  • It's a technique that has become the hallmark of Amber Films. [+ of]

    这已经成为安伯电影公司的标志性技术。

  • The killing had the hallmarks of a professional assassination.

    这起谋杀案具有职业暗杀的特征。

  • His designs show a love of simplicity which is very much his hallmark.

    他的设计显示出对简约风格的偏爱，这正是他一贯的特点。

SYN trademark, indication, badge, emblem  
2 N-COUNT （金、银、铂制品上标明纯度、产地、制造者的）印记 A hallmark is an official mark put on things made of gold, silver, or platinum that indicates the quality of the metal, where the object was made, and who made it. （金、银、铂制品上标明纯度、产地、制造者的）印记
