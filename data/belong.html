belong
bɪlɒŋ -lɔːŋ biˈlɔŋ bɪˈlɔŋ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense belongs, present participle belonging, past tense , past participle belonged
1 VERB [no cont] 属于；归…所有 If something belongs to you, you own it. 属于；归…所有

  • The house had belonged to her family for three or four generations. [VERB + to]

    这套房子归她们家有三四代人了。

2 VERB [no cont] 与…有关；是…的一部分 You say that something belongs to a particular person when you are guessing, discovering, or explaining that it was produced by or is part of that person. 与…有关；是…的一部分

  • The handwriting belongs to a male. [VERB + to]

    这是男性的笔迹。

  • They established that the body belonged to a 15-year-old girl. [VERB to noun]

    他们确定这是一具15岁女孩的尸体。

3 VERB [no cont] 是…的成员；属于（某团体） If someone belongs to a particular group, they are a member of that group. 是…的成员；属于（某团体）

  • I used to belong to a youth club. [VERB + to]

    我过去是一个青年俱乐部的成员。

4 VERB [no cont] 属于（某个类别、类型或群体） If something or someone belongs in or to a particular category, type, or group, they are of that category, type, or group. 属于（某个类别、类型或群体）

  • The judges could not decide which category it belonged in. [V + in/to]

    评委们无法判定它属于哪一类。

  • I realized that he and I belonged to different worlds. [V in/to n]

    我意识到我和他不是一路人。

SYN go with, fit into, be part of, relate to  
5 VERB [no cont] 源于，来自于（某个时代） If something belongs to a particular time, it comes from that time. 源于，来自于（某个时代）

  • The pictures belong to an era when there was a preoccupation with high society. [VERB + to]

    这些画出自一个人人向往上流社会的时代。

6 VERB [no cont] （权利）属于（某人） If you say that something belongs to someone, you mean that person has the right to it. （权利）属于（某人）

  • ...but the last word belonged to Rosanne. [VERB + to]

    但是最终决定权在罗莎娜手里

7 VERB [no cont] 以…为特点；以…为特征 If you say that a time belongs to a particular system or way of doing something, you mean that that time is or will be characterized by it. 以…为特点；以…为特征

  • The future belongs to democracy. [VERB + to]

    未来属于民主。

8 VERB [no cont] （孩子）是…的，为…所拥有（或监护） If a baby or child belongs to a particular adult, that adult is his or her parent or the person who is looking after him or her. （孩子）是…的，为…所拥有（或监护）

  • He deduced that the two children belonged to the couple. [VERB + to]

    他推断这俩小孩是这对夫妇的。

9 V-RECIP [no cont] 属于彼此（恋人用来表示亲密关系） When lovers say that they belong together, they are expressing their closeness or commitment to each other. 属于彼此（恋人用来表示亲密关系）

  • I really think that we belong together. [V together]

    我真的觉得我们属于彼此。

  • He belongs with me. [VERB + with]

    他是我的。

10 VERB [no cont] 归属于（某地或某一情形） If a person or thing belongs in a particular place or situation, that is where they should be. 归属于（某地或某一情形）

  • You don't belong here. [VERB adv./prep.]

    你不属于这里。

  • This piece really belongs in the concert hall. [VERB adv./prep.]

    这曲子很适合在音乐厅演奏。

  • I'm so glad to see you back where you belong. [VERB adv./prep.]

    我真高兴看到你又回到了属于你自己的天地。

  • They need to feel they belong. [VERB]

    他们需要有归属感。

SYN go, fit in, have a home, have a rightful place  
belonging

  • ...a man utterly without a sense of belonging.

    完全没有归属感的男人
