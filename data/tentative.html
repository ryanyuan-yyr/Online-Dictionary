tentative
tentətɪv ˈtɛntətɪv
1 ADJ-GRADED (协议、计划、安排)暂行的，临时的，试探性的 Tentative agreements, plans, or arrangements are not definite or certain, but have been made as a first step. (协议、计划、安排)暂行的，临时的，试探性的

  • Political leaders have reached a tentative agreement to hold a preparatory conference next month.

    政治领导人已就下个月举行预备会议达成初步协定。

  • Such theories are still very tentative.

    这种理论仍然是探索性的。

SYN unconfirmed, provisional, indefinite, test  
tentatively

  • The next round of talks is tentatively scheduled to begin October 21st in Washington.

    下一轮会谈暂定于10月21日在华盛顿开始。

2 ADJ-GRADED 踌躇不决的;犹豫的 If someone is tentative, they are cautious and not very confident because they are uncertain or afraid. 踌躇不决的;犹豫的

  • My first attempts at complaining were rather tentative.

    我刚开始投诉时很犹豫不决。

  • She did not return his tentative smile.

    她并没有对他怯生生的微笑作出回应。

SYN hesitant, cautious, uncertain, doubtful  
tentatively

  • Perhaps, he suggested tentatively, they should send for Dr Band.

    他小心地建议道，也许他们应该让人去请班德大夫。
