concur
kənkɜːʳ kənˈkɜr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense concurs, present participle concurring, past tense , past participle concurred
V-RECIP 意见一致;同意 If one person concurs with another person, the two people agree. You can also say that two people concur . 意见一致;同意 [formal] [Also VERB]

  • Local feeling does not necessarily concur with the press. [VERB + with]

    当地人的感受未必与媒体一致。

  • Daniels and Franklin concurred in an investigator's suggestion that the police be commended. [VERB + in]

    丹尼尔斯和富兰克林一致同意一位调查人员的建议，即警方应该受到赞扬。

  • Butler and Stone concur that the war threw people's lives into a moral relief. [VERB that]

    巴特勒和斯通一致认为这场战争使人们的生命得到了道义上的解脱。

  • Four other judges concurred. [VERB]

    还有四位法官表示同意。

  • After looking at the jug, Faulkner concurred that it was late Roman, third or fourth century. [VERB that]

    看了这个罐子之后，福克纳赞成罐子属于罗马帝国晚期的说法，即3或4世纪。

  • 'It's not an agreeable state of affairs,' concurs Heitham. [VERB with quote]

    “目前的事态并不令人满意。”海瑟姆赞同道。

SYN agree, accord, approve, assent
