conjure
kʌndʒəʳ kɑːn- ˈkʌndʒər ˈkɑndʒər kənˈdʒʊr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense conjures, present participle conjuring, past tense , past participle conjured
1 VERB 变魔术般地创造出 If you conjure something out of nothing, you make it appear as if by magic. 变魔术般地创造出 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Thirteen years ago she found herself having to conjure a career from thin air. [V n + from/out of]

    13年前，她认识到自己得白手起家创造出一番事业来。

  • They managed to conjure a victory. [VERB noun]

    他们出人意料地取得了胜利。

SYN produce, generate, bring about, give rise to  
Conjure up means the same as conjure .conjure up 同 conjure

  • Every day a different chef will be conjuring up delicious dishes in the restaurant.

    每天，饭店里会有一位不同的大厨像变戏法似的奉上可口的菜肴。

  • He conjured up a smile and reached out to squeeze her hand.

    他马上露出笑脸，伸手去捏她的手。

2 PHRASE [NOUN inflects, verb-link PHRASE] 重要人物(或组织)的名字;有影响力的名字 If you say that the name of a particular person or organization is a name to conjure with, you mean that that person or organization is very important and influential in the field you are discussing. 重要人物(或组织)的名字;有影响力的名字

  • By 1920, Fox and Universal were already names to conjure with.

    到1920年，福克斯和环球已经声名显赫。
