vault
vɔːlt vɔlt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense vaults, present participle vaulting, past tense , past participle vaulted
1 N-COUNT 金库;保险库 A vault is a secure room where money and other valuable things can be kept safely. 金库;保险库

  • Most of the money was in storage in bank vaults.

    大部分钱都存放在银行的保险库里。

  • The gold recovered so far is being held in a vault at a secret location.

    到目前为止，找回来的金子都保存在一个秘密地点的金库中。

SYN strongroom, repository, depository  
2 N-COUNT （通常指安葬同一家族死者的）教堂地下室，墓穴,墓室 A vault is a room underneath a church or in a cemetery where people are buried, usually the members of a single family. （通常指安葬同一家族死者的）教堂地下室，墓穴,墓室

  • He ordered that Matilda's body should be buried in the family vault.

    他指示马蒂尔达的遗体必须葬在家族墓穴里。

SYN crypt, tomb, catacomb, cellar  
3 N-COUNT 拱顶;穹窿 A vault is an arched roof or ceiling. 拱顶;穹窿

  • ...the vault of a great cathedral. [+ of]

    一座大教堂的拱顶

SYN arch, roof, ceiling, span  
4 VERB （尤指用手支撑）跳跃,跳过 If you vault something or vault over it, you jump quickly onto or over it, especially by putting a hand on top of it to help you balance while you jump. （尤指用手支撑）跳跃,跳过

  • He could easily vault the wall. [VERB noun]

    他可以轻松地翻过墙去。

  • Ned vaulted over a fallen tree. [VERB prep.]

    内德跳过一棵倒下的树。

  • He ran to the door and vaulted into the saddle. [VERB prep.]

    他跑到门口，飞身跃上马背。
