move in
1 PHRASAL VERB 迁入;搬来 When you move in somewhere, you begin to live there as your home. 迁入;搬来

  • Her house was in perfect order when she moved in. [VERB PREP.]

    她搬进来时，屋子里井井有条。

  • Her husband had moved in with a younger woman. [VERB PREP. + with]

    她丈夫和一个比她年轻的女人同居了。

  • We'd been seeing each other for a year when he suggested we should move in together. [V P together]

    我们已经交往一年了，这时他提议搬到一起住。

2 PHRASAL VERB (警察、士兵或袭击者)逼近，进逼 If police, soldiers, or attackers move in, they go towards a place or person in order to deal with or attack them. (警察、士兵或袭击者)逼近，进逼

  • Police moved in to disperse the crowd. [VERB PREP.]

    警察开进来驱散人群。

  • Forces were moving in on the town of Knin. [VERB PREP. + on]

    军队正向克宁镇进逼。

3 PHRASAL VERB 插手;涉足 If someone moves in on an area of activity which was previously only done by a particular group of people, they start becoming involved with it for the first time. 插手;涉足 [Also VERB PREPOSITION]

  • These black models are moving in on what was previously white territory: the lucrative cosmetic contracts. [VERB PREP. + on]

    黑人模特正逐步涉足曾是白人主宰的领地：签约获利丰厚的化妆品行业。
