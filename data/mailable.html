mail
meɪl meɪl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense mails, present participle mailing, past tense , past participle mailed
1 N-SING [oft by NOUN] 邮政;邮政系统 The mail is the public service or system by which letters and parcels are collected and delivered. 邮政;邮政系统

  • Your check is in the mail.

    您的支票正在邮递中。

  • People had to renew their motor vehicle registrations through the mail.

    人们必须以书信方式重新登记机动车辆。

  • The firm has offices in several large cities, but does most of its business by mail.

    公司在几座大城市都设有办事处，不过大部分业务都通过邮政系统来实现。

SYN postal service, post, postal system  
2 N-UNCOUNT [oft the NOUN] 邮件;信件;邮包 You can refer to letters and parcels that are delivered to you as mail . 邮件;信件;邮包

  • There was no mail except the usual junk addressed to the occupier.

    除了寄给业主的常见垃圾信件之外，再没有其他邮件了。

  • Nora looked through the mail.

    诺拉大致看了一下那封信。

SYN letters, post, packages, parcels  
3 VERB 寄出;邮寄 If you mail a letter or parcel to someone, you send it to them by putting it in a post box or taking it to a post office. 寄出;邮寄 [mainly US] [Also VERB n]

  • Last year, he mailed the documents to French journalists. [VERB noun + to]

    他去年把这些文件寄给了法国记者。

  • He mailed me the contract. [VERB noun noun]

    他把合同邮寄给了我。

  • The Government has already mailed some 18 million households with details of the public offer. [VERB noun + with]

    政府已经向大约1,800万个家庭寄发了公开出售的详细说明。

SYN post, send, forward, dispatch  
in BRIT, usually use 英国英语通常用 post
4 VERB 发送(电子邮件) To mail a message to someone means to send it to them by means of e-mail or a computer network. 发送(电子邮件) [Also VERB n]

  • ...if a report must be electronically mailed to an office by 9 am the next day. [be VERB-ed prep.]

    如果一份报告必须在次日早晨9时之前以电子邮件的形式发送至办公室

Mail is also a noun.

  • If you have any problems then send me some mail.

    如有任何疑问，就给我发邮件。
