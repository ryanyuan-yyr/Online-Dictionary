cascade
kæskeɪd kæsˈkeɪd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense cascades, present participle cascading, past tense , past participle cascaded
1 N-COUNT 大量;许多 If you refer to a cascade of something, you mean that there is a large amount of it. 大量;许多 [literary]

  • The women have lustrous cascades of black hair. [+ of]

    那些女子拥有瀑布般乌黑亮泽的秀发。

  • A cascade of mail arrived from friends.

    朋友寄来的邮件如雪片一般。

2 N-COUNT 瀑布 A cascade is a waterfall. 瀑布 [literary]
3 VERB (水)飞流直下，倾泻而下 If water cascades somewhere, it pours or flows downwards very fast and in large quantities. (水)飞流直下，倾泻而下 [Also VERB]

  • She hung on as the freezing, rushing water cascaded past her. [VERB adv./prep.]

    她抓得紧紧的，任凭冰冷湍急的水流从她身上倾泻而下。

  • A waterfall cascades down the cliff from the hills behind. [VERB adv./prep.]

    一处瀑布从身后山崖上飞流直下。

SYN flow, fall, flood, pour  
4 VERB 垂落;悬挂 If one thing cascades over another, it falls or hangs over it. 垂落;悬挂 [literary]

  • Vivid red and pink geraniums cascade over my balcony. [VERB prep.]

    鲜艳的红色和粉色的天竺葵从我家的阳台上直挂下去。

  • From her tiny waist a crinolined skirt cascaded in three deep tiers. [VERB]

    裙架撑起的三层大裥裙从她的纤腰上款款垂下。
