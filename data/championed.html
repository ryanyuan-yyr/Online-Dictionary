champion
tʃæmpiən ˈtʃæmpiən
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense champions, present participle championing, past tense , past participle championed
1 N-COUNT 冠军 A champion is someone who has won the first prize in a competition, contest, or fight. 冠军

  • ...a former Olympic champion.

    前奥运会冠军

  • Kasparov became world champion.

    卡斯帕罗夫成为世界冠军。

  • ...a champion boxer and skier.

    拳击和滑雪双料冠军

SYN winner, hero, victor, conqueror  
2 N-COUNT 支持者；拥护者；捍卫者 If you are a champion of a person, a cause, or a principle, you support or defend them. 支持者；拥护者；捍卫者

  • He received acclaim as a champion of the oppressed.

    他因维护被压迫者的利益而备受称赞。

  • He was once known as a champion of social reform. [+ of]

    他曾经是社会改革的拥护者。

SYN defender, guardian, patron, backer  
3 VERB 支持；拥护；捍卫 If you champion a person, a cause, or a principle, you support or defend them. 支持；拥护；捍卫

  • He passionately championed the poor. [VERB noun]

    他满腔热情地捍卫穷人的利益。

  • The amendments had been championed by pro-democracy activists. [be VERB-ed + by]

    修正案一直受到亲民主的活跃分子的支持。

SYN support, back, defend, promote
