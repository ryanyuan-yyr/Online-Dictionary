euphemism
juːfəmɪzəm ˈjufəˌmɪzəm
Word forms: plural euphemisms
N-COUNT 委婉语;委婉说法 A euphemism is a polite word or expression that is used to refer to things which people may find upsetting or embarrassing to talk about, for example sex, the human body, or death. 委婉语;委婉说法

  • The term 'early retirement' is nearly always a euphemism for redundancy nowadays. [+ for]

    现在“提前退休”几乎就是裁员的委婉说法。

SYN polite term, substitute, understatement, alternative word
