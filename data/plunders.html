plunder
plʌndəʳ ˈplʌndər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense plunders, present participle plundering, past tense , past participle plundered
1 VERB 劫掠；掠夺；窃取 If someone plunders a place or plunders things from a place, they steal things from it. 劫掠；掠夺；窃取 [literary]

  • They plundered and burned the market town of Leominster. [VERB noun]

    他们洗劫并焚烧了集镇莱姆斯特。

  • She faces charges of helping to plunder her country's treasury of billions of dollars. [VERB noun + of]

    她面临协助侵吞数十亿美元国家财产的指控。

  • This has been done by plundering £4 billion from the Government reserves. [VERB noun + from]

    这是通过侵占40亿英镑的政府储备金完成的。

SYN loot, strip, sack, rob  
Plunder is also a noun.

  • ...a guerrilla group infamous for torture and plunder.

    因酷刑和劫掠而臭名昭著的游击队

2 N-UNCOUNT 劫掠物；侵吞物；赃物 Plunder is property that is stolen. 劫掠物；侵吞物；赃物 [literary]

  • The thieves are often armed and in some cases have killed for their plunder.

    这些小偷常携带凶器，有时会为赃物杀人。
