cluck
klʌk klʌk
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense clucks, present participle clucking, past tense , past participle clucked
1 VERB (母鸡)咯咯叫 When a hen clucks, it makes short, low noises. (母鸡)咯咯叫

  • Chickens clucked in the garden. [VERB]

    鸡在花园里咯咯叫。

2 VERB （对…）大惊小怪，过分关心 To cluck over someone or something means to behave in a fussy or protective way. （对…）大惊小怪，过分关心 [disapproval]

  • I've never been one to cluck over babies of either sex. [V over/around n]

    不管是男孩还是女孩，我从来都对小孩没什么兴趣。

  • The seamstresses cluck around a dummy, discussing a tuck here and there. [V over/around n]

    这些女裁缝围着模特儿衣架叽叽嘎嘎地讨论衣服这里或那里要做的小改动。

3 VERB 发咂咂声(以示不满) To cluck at someone or something means to make disapproving noises or say things in a disapproving way. 发咂咂声(以示不满)

  • Superintendent Fairbairn was still clucking at the photographers, warning them he'd be speaking to their editor. [VERB at noun]

    费尔贝恩警长还在表达对摄影师们的不满，警告说他会和他们的主编谈谈。

  • He clucks in disapproval. [VERB]

    他不满地咂咂嘴。

  • Teddy clucked his tongue like a disapproving English matron. [VERB noun]

    特迪咂了咂舌头，活像个不以为然的英国女舍监。
