abscond
æbskɒnd æbˈskɑnd əbˈskɑnd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense absconds, present participle absconding, past tense , past participle absconded
1 VERB （从监狱等处）逃走，逃脱 If someone absconds from somewhere such as a prison, they escape from it or leave it without permission. （从监狱等处）逃走，逃脱 [formal]

  • He was ordered to appear the following day, but absconded. [VERB]

    他被传唤于第二天出庭，可是他逃跑了。

  • A dozen inmates have absconded from Forest Jail in the past year. [VERB + from]

    去年共有12名犯人从福雷斯特监狱逃脱。

SYN escape, flee, get away, bolt  
2 VERB （携…）潜逃 If someone absconds with something, they leave and take it with them, although it does not belong to them. （携…）潜逃 [formal]

  • Unfortunately, his partners were crooks and absconded with the funds. [VERB + with]

    不幸的是，他的合伙人都是些骗子，已卷款逃之夭夭了。

Quotations
