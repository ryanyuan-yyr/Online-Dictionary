nobody
noʊbɒdi ˈnoʊˌbɑdi ˈnoʊˌbʌdi ˈnoʊbədi
Word forms: plural nobodies
1 PRON-INDEF-NEG 没有人;无人 Nobody means not a single person, or not a single member of a particular group or set. 没有人;无人

  • They were shut away in a little room where nobody could overhear.

    他们被关在一个小屋里，没人能听到他们的动静。

  • Nobody realizes how bad things are.

    没人意识到事情是多么糟糕。

  • Nobody ever spoke to me at press conferences.

    记者招待会上一直没人和我说话。

  • Nobody else in the neighbourhood can help.

    邻居中找不到谁还能帮忙。

SYN no-one  
2 N-COUNT 无名之辈;小人物 If someone says that a person is a nobody, they are saying in an unkind way that the person is not at all important. 无名之辈;小人物 [disapproval]

  • A man in my position has nothing to fear from a nobody like you.

    处在我这样位置的人，一点都不害怕像你这种小人物。

SYN nonentity, nothing [informal] , lightweight [informal] , non-person  
Usage Note :

  • You do not use nobody or no one in front of of to talk about a particular group of people. The word you need is none. None of his companions answered.

    不能将 nobody 或 no one 置于介词 of 前谈论特定的人群，需要使用的词是 none。如，None of his companions answered (他的同伴中没人回答)。
