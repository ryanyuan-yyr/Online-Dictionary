hose
hoʊz hoʊz
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense hoses, present participle hosing, past tense , past participle hosed
1 N-COUNT （橡胶或塑料制的）水管，水龙带 A hose is a long, flexible pipe made of rubber or plastic. Water is directed through a hose in order to do things such as put out fires, clean cars, or water gardens. （橡胶或塑料制的）水管，水龙带

  • You've left the garden hose on.

    你忘了关花园里的水管了。

  • The firemen unwrapped their hoses and began dousing the scorched grain silos.

    消防员展开水龙带，开始冲着烧焦的谷仓喷水。

2 N-COUNT （引导液体或气体的）橡胶（或塑料）软管，导管 A hose is a pipe made of rubber or plastic, along which a liquid or gas flows, for example from one part of an engine to another. （引导液体或气体的）橡胶（或塑料）软管，导管

  • Water in the engine compartment is sucked away by a hose.

    发动机舱里的水由一条软管抽走。

3 VERB 用水管冲洗（或浇灌） If you hose something, you wash or water it using a hose. 用水管冲洗（或浇灌） [Also VERB]

  • We wash our cars and hose our gardens without even thinking of the water that uses. [VERB noun]

    我们洗车和浇灌花园时根本就没有想过用了多少水。

4 N-UNCOUNT 袜类（包括连裤袜、长筒袜、短袜） Hose is used to refer to tights, socks, and stockings. 袜类（包括连裤袜、长筒袜、短袜） [technical, or old-fashioned]

  • If you have varicose veins, consider wearing elastic support hose.

    如果患了静脉曲张，可以考虑穿弹力护腿袜。

5See also:panty hose 
6 N-UNCOUNT （旧式的）男式紧身裤 Hose is an old-fashioned men's garment that looks like a pair of very tight trousers. （旧式的）男式紧身裤
