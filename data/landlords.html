landlord
lændlɔːʳd ˈlændˌlɔrd
Word forms: plural landlords
1 N-COUNT （男）房东 Someone's landlord is the man who allows them to live or work in a building which he owns, in return for rent. （男）房东

  • His landlord doubled the rent.

    他的房东把租金提高了一倍。

SYN owner, landowner, proprietor, freeholder  
2 N-COUNT （酒吧的）店主，老板 The landlord of a pub is the man who owns or runs it, or the husband of the woman who owns or runs it. （酒吧的）店主，老板 [mainly British]

  • The landlord refused to serve him because he considered him too drunk.

    酒吧老板觉得他已经喝多了，拒绝再给他添酒。

SYN innkeeper, host, hotelier, hotel-keeper  
3 N-COUNT （家庭旅馆或客栈的）店主 A landlord is the man who owns or runs a boarding house or inn. （家庭旅馆或客栈的）店主 [US, also British, old-fashioned]
