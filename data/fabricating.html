fabricate
fæbrɪkeɪt ˈfæbrɪˌkeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense fabricates, present participle fabricating, past tense , past participle fabricated
1 VERB 编造;捏造 If someone fabricates information, they invent it in order to deceive people. 编造;捏造

  • All four claim that officers fabricated evidence against them. [VERB noun]

    4 人全都声称警察捏造证据陷害他们。

  • Eleven key officials were hanged on fabricated charges. [VERB-ed]

    11位高官因莫须有的罪名被处以绞刑。

SYN make up, invent, concoct, falsify  
fabrication

  • She described the interview with her in an Italian magazine as a 'complete fabrication'.

    她将意大利一家杂志对她的采访报道称为“子虚乌有”。

  • This story is total fabrication.

    这种说法纯属捏造。

2 VERB 制造;制作 If something is fabricated from different materials or substances, it is made out of those materials or substances. 制造;制作 [Also V n + from]

  • All the tools are fabricated from high quality steel. [be VERB-ed + from]

    所有工具均由精钢制成。

  • ...a plant which fabricates airplane components. [VERB noun]

    一家制造飞机配件的工厂

SYN manufacture, make, build, form  
fabrication

  • More than 200 improvements, many of them major, were made in the design and fabrication of the shuttle.

    在航天飞机的设计及制造过程中，作了两百多项改进，其中很多是大的改动。
