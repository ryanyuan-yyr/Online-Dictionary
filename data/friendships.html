friendship
frendʃɪp ˈfrɛndˌʃɪp
Word forms: plural friendships
1 N-VAR (相互之间的)友谊，友情，朋友关系 A friendship is a relationship between two or more friends. (相互之间的)友谊，友情，朋友关系

  • Giving advice when it's not called for is the quickest way to end a good friendship.

    在别人不需要的时候提供建议是葬送好友关系的最快方式。

  • She struck up a close friendship with Desiree during the week of rehearsals. [+ with]

    在排练的一周里她和德西蕾建立了亲密的友谊。

  • After seven years of friendship, she still couldn't tell when he was kidding.

    他们已有 7 年的交情，但她还是搞不清他什么时候是在开玩笑。

2 N-UNCOUNT (泛指的)友谊，友情，朋友情义 You use friendship to refer in a general way to the state of being friends, or the feelings that friends have for each other. (泛指的)友谊，友情，朋友情义

  • ...a hobby which led to a whole new world of friendship and adventure.

    打开了一片充满友谊和冒险的全新天地的爱好

SYN friendliness, affection, harmony, goodwill  
3 N-SING [with poss] (某人的)友谊，友情 If you have someone's friendship, they are your friend. (某人的)友谊，友情

  • He had the friendship of Terry Jones and the respect of John Cleese.

    他得到了特里·琼斯的友谊和约翰·克利斯的尊敬。

  • Steve really values your friendship more than anything else.

    史蒂夫真的把你的友谊看得比其他任何东西都重要。

4 N-VAR (两国之间的)友好关系，亲善 Friendship is a relationship between two countries in which they help and support each other. (两国之间的)友好关系，亲善

  • The President set the targets for the future to promote friendship with East Europe. [+ with]

    总统确定了在未来加强同东欧的互助的目标。

  • They were reaching out the hand of friendship to their former adversaries of the Cold War.

    他们向从前冷战时期的对手伸出友谊之手。

  • Restoring ties with Israel would not affect Bulgaria's traditional friendships with other countries.

    和以色列恢复关系不会影响保加利亚和其他国家的传统友谊。
