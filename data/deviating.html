deviate
diːvieɪt ˈdiviˌeɪt diviɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense deviates, present participle deviating, past tense , past participle deviated
VERB 偏离;背离 To deviate from something means to start doing something different or not planned, especially in a way that causes problems for others. 偏离;背离 [Also VERB]

  • They stopped you as soon as you deviated from the script. [VERB + from]

    一旦你偏离了剧本的内容，他们就会喊停。

  • He planned his schedule far in advance, and he didn't deviate from it. [VERB from noun]

    他提前很早就制订出了自己的进度表，然后从未偏离过它。

  • From very early on he believed a military revolution was necessary and he has never deviated from that ideological path. [VERB from noun]

    他一早就认为军事革命是必要的，并且从未背离过这一思想路线。

SYN differ, vary, depart, part
