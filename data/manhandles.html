manhandle
mænhændəl ˈmænˌhændəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense manhandles, present participle manhandling, past tense , past participle manhandled
1 VERB 粗暴地对待;推搡 If someone is manhandled, they are physically held or pushed, for example when they are being taken somewhere. 粗暴地对待;推搡 [Also VERB n]

  • Foreign journalists were manhandled by armed police, and told to leave. [be VERB-ed]

    外国记者遭到了武警的粗暴对待和口头驱逐。

  • They manhandled the old man along the corridor. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他们沿着走廊把老人拖走。

SYN rough up, pull, push, paw [informal]  
2 VERB 用人力搬动（大或重的物体） If you manhandle something big or heavy somewhere, you move it there by hand. 用人力搬动（大或重的物体）

  • The three of us manhandled the uncovered dinghy out of the shed. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    我们三个人把无篷小划艇推出了棚子。

SYN haul, carry, pull, push
