stick
stɪk stɪk
Word forms: plural sticks
  • 1. NOUN USES 名词用法
  • 2. VERB USES 动词用法

NOUN USES 名词用法

1 N-COUNT 枯枝;柴枝 A stick is a thin branch which has fallen off a tree. 枯枝;柴枝

  • ...people carrying bundles of dried sticks to sell for firewood.

    背着成捆的干树枝去当薪柴卖的人们

SYN twig, branch, birch, offshoot  
2 N-COUNT 杖;棍;棒 A stick is a long thin piece of wood which is used for supporting someone's weight or for hitting people or animals. 杖;棍;棒

  • He looks old and walks with a stick.

    他看起来很老，走路时拄着一根拐棍。

  • Crowds armed with sticks and stones took to the streets.

    人群手持棍棒和石块走上了街头。

SYN cane, staff, pole, rod  
3See also:carrot and stick 
4 N-COUNT [usually n NOUN] (用于某一特定用途的)木条，木棍 A stick is a long thin piece of wood which is used for a particular purpose. (用于某一特定用途的)木条，木棍

  • ...kebab sticks.

    烤肉扦

  • ...lolly sticks.

    冰棍棍

  • ...drum sticks.

    鼓槌

5 N-COUNT [usually n NOUN] (体育运动中使用的)杖，棍，棒 Some long thin objects that are used in sports are called sticks . (体育运动中使用的)杖，棍，棒

  • ...lacrosse sticks.

    兜网球棍

  • ...hockey sticks.

    曲棍球棍

  • ...ski-sticks.

    滑雪杖

6 N-COUNT [n NOUN] 条状物;棒状物;棍状物 A stick of something is a long thin piece of it. 条状物;棒状物;棍状物

  • ...a stick of celery. [+ of]

    一根芹菜

  • ...cinnamon sticks.

    肉桂条

7 N-UNCOUNT 严厉的批评;大肆的取笑 If you give someone some stick, you criticize them or tease them roughly. 严厉的批评;大肆的取笑 [British, informal]

  • It's not motorists who give you the most stick, it's the general public.

    对你批评最严厉的并不是驾车人，而是普通民众。

  • I get some stick from the lads because of my faith but I don't mind.

    我由于自己的信仰遭到了伙伴们的取笑，但我不在乎。

SYN abuse, criticism, flak [informal] , blame  
8 N-PLURAL 偏远乡村;边远地区 If you say that someone lives in the sticks, you mean that they live a long way from any large cities. 偏远乡村;边远地区 [informal, disapproval]

  • He lived out in the sticks somewhere.

    他住在某个偏远的乡村。

9 PHRASE 批评别人的依据 If you say that something is a stick to beat someone with, you mean that it is used, or could be used, as a basis for criticism. 批评别人的依据 [journalism]

  • Unfortunately historic American fiction is constantly being used as a stick to beat contemporary British writers with.

    不幸的是，经典的美国小说经常被用来作为批评当代英国作家的依据。

10 PHRASE (完全)误解;错误地理解 If someone gets the wrong end of the stick or gets hold of the wrong end of the stick, they do not understand something correctly and get the wrong idea about it. (完全)误解;错误地理解 [informal]
11 PHRASE 很多 If you say that there are more things than you could or can shake a stick at, you are emphasizing in a humorous way that there are a lot of them. 很多 [informal, emphasis]

  • ...a man with more medals than you can shake a stick at.

    拥有很多奖章的人

VERB USES 动词用法

1 VERB (随便地)放，搁，塞 If you stick something somewhere, you put it there in a rather casual way. (随便地)放，搁，塞 [informal]

  • He folded the papers and stuck them in his desk drawer. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他将文件叠起来，塞进自己的书桌抽屉里。

  • Jack opened his door and stuck his head out. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    杰克打开门，探出头来。

SYN put, place, set, position  
2 V-ERG 刺;扎;戳 If you stick a pointed object in something, or if it sticks in something, it goes into it or through it by making a cut or hole. 刺;扎;戳

  • They sent in loads of male nurses and stuck a needle in my back. [V n in/into/through n]

    他们派来了很多男护士，在我的背上扎了一针。

  • Some punk stuck a knife in her last night. [V n + in/into/through]

    有个小流氓昨晚扎了她一刀。

  • The soldiers went at once to the mound and began to stick their bayonets through it. [V n + in/into/through]

    士兵们马上赶到土丘处，开始用刺刀往里戳。

  • The knife stuck in the ground at his feet. [VERB + in]

    刀扎在了他脚边的地上。

SYN poke, dig, stab, insert  
3 VERB 突出/扎进 If something is sticking out from a surface or object, it extends up or away from it. If something is sticking into a surface or object, it is partly in it. 突出/扎进

  • They lay where they had fallen from the crane, sticking out of the water. [VERB adv./prep.]

    它们还在从起重机上掉落的地方，一部分露出水面。

  • Something was sticking from the pocket of the little man's grimy shorts. [VERB adv./prep.]

    小个子男人脏乎乎的短裤口袋里鼓鼓的，伸出来个什么东西。

  • His hair sticks up in half a dozen directions. [VERB adv./prep.]

    他的头发胡乱支棱着。

  • ...when we see her with lots of tubes and needles sticking into her little body. [VERB adv./prep.]

    当我们看到她瘦小的身体上插着很多导管和针头时

4 VERB (用胶水、胶带等)粘，粘贴 If you stick one thing to another, you attach it using glue, sticky tape, or another sticky substance. (用胶水、胶带等)粘，粘贴

  • Don't forget to clip the token and stick it on your card. [VERB noun prep.]

    别忘了剪下标志，贴到你的卡上。

  • We just stuck it to the window. [VERB noun prep.]

    我们就把它贴在窗户上了。

  • He has nowhere to stick up his posters. [VERB noun with adv.]

    他没地方贴海报了。

  • Stick down any loose bits of flooring. [VERB noun with adv.]

    把松动的地板块都粘好。

SYN fasten, fix, bind, hold  
5 VERB 黏附;附着在 If one thing sticks to another, it becomes attached to it and is difficult to remove. 黏附;附着在

  • The soil sticks to the blade and blocks the plough. [VERB to noun]

    土粘在犁铧上，卡住了犁。

  • Peel away the waxed paper if it has stuck to the bottom of the cake. [VERB + to]

    如果蜡纸已粘在蛋糕的底部，就把它撕掉。

  • If left to stand, cooked pasta sticks together. [V together]

    煮好的面放置时间长了会变坨。

SYN adhere, cling, cleave, become joined  
6 VERB 铭记在心;经久不忘 If something sticks in your mind, you remember it for a long time. 铭记在心;经久不忘

  • The incident stuck in my mind because it was the first example I had seen of racism in that country. [VERB + in]

    这件事一直萦绕在我的脑海里，因为这是我在那个国家看到的首起种族主义实例。

  • That song has stuck in my head for years. [VERB in noun]

    那首歌多年来一直铭记在我心中。

SYN stay, remain, linger, persist  
7 VERB (名字)流传开去，被众人采用 If you give someone or something a name and the name sticks, it becomes the name which most people use to refer to that person or thing. (名字)流传开去，被众人采用

  • A friend dubbed it 'The Sanctuary' and the name stuck. [VERB]

    有位朋友戏谑地称它为“避难所”，然后这个名称就沿用了下来。

8 VERB [no cont, with brd-neg] (证明…)属实,成立 If someone manages to make a charge or accusation stick, they show that the person accused is guilty of the crime or action they are accused of. (证明…)属实,成立

  • I don't see how they'll make the charges stick. [VERB]

    我不知道他们将如何证明这些指控属实。

  • But legal experts are not sure if such a charge can stick. [VERB]

    但法律专家们不能肯定这一指控是否成立。

9 VERB 固定不动 If something which can usually be moved sticks, it becomes fixed in one position. 固定不动

  • The needle on the dial went right round to fifty feet, which was as far as it could go, and there it stuck. [VERB]

    仪表盘上的指针一下转到了 50 英尺处，也就是到头了，然后就停在那里不动了。

  • The dagger stuck tightly in the silver scabbard. [VERB]

    匕首牢牢地卡在银质刀鞘中。

SYN catch, lodge, jam, stop  
10 VERB 忍受;容忍 If you are in an unpleasant or difficult situation and you say that you cannot stick it, you mean that you cannot bear to remain there long. 忍受;容忍 [British, informal]

  • Got a job bottle-washing at the brewery. I lasted a fortnight. I couldn't stick it. [VERB noun]

    我找了份在啤酒厂洗瓶子的工作。干了两星期，就再也受不了了。

  • How long did you stick it for? [VERB noun]

    你坚持了多久？

SYN tolerate, take, stand, stomach  
11 See also: stuck 
12 PHRASE 去他的(工作);杵一边去 If you say that someone can stick something, especially a job, or if you tell them where to stick it, you are rudely refusing it or emphasizing that you do not want it or like it. 去他的(工作);杵一边去 [informal, rude, feelings]

  • It's a rotten play, so they can stick it.

    这部剧糟透了，他们趁早拉倒吧。

  • She then stormed out in a temper telling him to 'stick his job'.

    她接着怒气冲冲地走了出去，告诉他“去他妈的工作”。
