buffet
bʌfeɪ bʌfɪt bʊfeɪ bəˈfeɪ ˈbʌfɪt bʊˈfeɪ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense buffets, present participle buffeting, past tense , past participle buffeted
Pronounced /'bʌfeɪ, am bʊ'feɪ/ for meanings 1 to 3, and /'bʌfɪt/ for meanings 4 and 5. 义项1至3读作 /'bʌfeɪ, 美 bʊ'feɪ/，义项4和5读作 /'bʌfɪt/。
1 N-COUNT [oft NOUN n] 自助餐 A buffet is a meal of cold food that is displayed on a long table at a party or public occasion. Guests usually serve themselves from the table. 自助餐

  • ...a buffet lunch.

    自助午餐

  • A cold buffet had been laid out in the dining-room.

    餐厅里已经摆好了自助冷食。

SYN smorgasbord, counter, cold table  
2 N-COUNT [oft n NOUN] （通常指旅馆或车站的）餐饮部，小餐馆 A buffet is a café, usually in a hotel or station. （通常指旅馆或车站的）餐饮部，小餐馆

  • We sat in the station buffet sipping tea.

    我们坐在车站的自助餐厅里喝茶。

SYN snack bar, café, cafeteria, brasserie  
3 N-COUNT [usually singular] （火车上的）餐车 On a train, the buffet or the buffet car is the carriage or car where meals and snacks are sold. （火车上的）餐车 [British]
in AM, use 美国英语用 dining car
4 VERB （大风或海浪）反复拍打，猛击 If something is buffeted by strong winds or by stormy seas, it is repeatedly struck or blown around by them. （大风或海浪）反复拍打，猛击

  • Their plane had been severely buffeted by storms. [be VERB-ed]

    他们的飞机遭受到风暴的重创。

  • Storms swept the country, closing roads, buffeting ferries and killing as many as 30 people. [VERB noun]

    暴风雨席卷全国，使道路无法通行，渡船受袭，多达30人遇难。

SYN knock, push, bang, rap  
buffeting

  • ...the buffetings of the winds.

    风的袭击

5 VERB （严峻局势）冲击，打击 If an economy or government is buffeted by difficult or unpleasant situations, it experiences many of them. （严峻局势）冲击，打击

  • The whole of Africa had been buffeted by social and political upheavals. [be VERB-ed]

    整个非洲都经受了社会和政治动荡之苦。

SYN beset, trouble, plague, harass
