snack
snæk snæk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense snacks, present participle snacking, past tense , past participle snacked
1 N-COUNT 快餐;便餐 A snack is a simple meal that is quick to cook and to eat. 快餐;便餐

  • Lunch was a snack in the fields.

    午饭是在野外吃的便餐。

SYN light meal, bite, refreshment(s), nibble  
2 N-COUNT 小吃;零嘴 A snack is something such as a chocolate bar that you eat between meals. 小吃;零嘴

  • Do you eat sweets, cakes or sugary snacks?

    你吃糖果、蛋糕或甜点吗？

3 VERB 吃小吃;吃点心 If you snack, you eat snacks between meals. 吃小吃;吃点心

  • Instead of snacking on crisps and chocolate, nibble on celery or carrot. [VERB + on]

    不要把薯片和巧克力当零食来吃，要吃些芹菜和胡萝卜。

  • She would improve her diet if she ate less fried food and snacked less. [VERB]

    如果她少吃油炸食品和零食的话，她的饮食结构就会有所改观。

SYN eat between meals, graze [informal] , nibble
