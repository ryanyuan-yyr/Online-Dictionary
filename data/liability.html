liability
laɪəbɪlɪti ˌlaɪəˈbɪləti
Word forms: plural liabilities
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 累赘；负累；负担 If you say that someone or something is a liability, you mean that they cause a lot of problems or embarrassment. 累赘；负累；负担

  • As the president's prestige continues to fall, they're clearly beginning to consider him a liability.

    随着总统的声望持续下跌，他们显然开始视他为负累。

  • ...what was once a vote catching policy, is now a political liability.

    这一曾经吸引选票的政策，如今成了政治上的负累。

SYN disadvantage, burden, drawback, inconvenience  
2 N-COUNT [usually plural] 负债；债务 A company's or organization's liabilities are the sums of money which it owes. 负债；债务 [business, or law]

  • The company had assets of $138 million and liabilities of $120.5 million.

    这家公司资产为1.38亿美元，负债为1.205亿美元。

3 See also: liable
