bark
bɑːʳk bɑrk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense barks, present participle barking, past tense , past participle barked
1 VERB （犬）吠，狺狺 When a dog barks, it makes a short, loud noise, once or several times. （犬）吠，狺狺

  • Don't let the dogs bark. [VERB]

    让那些狗别叫。

  • A small dog barked at a seagull he was chasing. [VERB + at]

    小狗对着它正在追赶的一只海鸥吠叫。

SYN yap, bay, howl, snarl  
Bark is also a noun.

  • The Doberman let out a string of roaring barks.

    短毛猎犬一阵狂吠。

2 VERB 大声嚷；吼叫；厉声叫喊 If you bark at someone, you shout at them aggressively in a loud, rough voice. 大声嚷；吼叫；厉声叫喊 [Also VERB with quote]

  • I didn't mean to bark at you. [VERB + at]

    我不是成心对你嚷嚷的。

  • A policeman held his gun in both hands and barked an order. [VERB noun]

    警察双手握枪，厉声发出指令。

SYN shout, snap, yell, snarl  
3 N-UNCOUNT 树皮 Bark is the tough material that covers the outside of a tree. 树皮
4 PHRASE 刀子嘴，豆腐心；冷面孔，菩萨心 If you say that someone's bark is worse than their bite, you mean that they seem much more unpleasant or hostile than they really are. 刀子嘴，豆腐心；冷面孔，菩萨心 [informal]

  • She can be a bit tetchy but her bark is worse than her bite.

    她脾气可能有点暴躁，但她是刀子嘴，豆腐心。

5 to be barking up the wrong tree→see: tree
