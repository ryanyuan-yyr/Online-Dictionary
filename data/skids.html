skid
skɪd skɪd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense skids, present participle skidding, past tense , past participle skidded
1 VERB (车辆)侧滑，打滑;(刹车时)滑行 If a vehicle skids, it slides sideways or forwards while moving, for example when you are trying to stop it suddenly on a wet road. (车辆)侧滑，打滑;(刹车时)滑行

  • The car pulled up too fast and skidded on the dusty shoulder of the road. [VERB]

    由于刹车太急，车子侧滑到了满是尘土的路肩上。

  • The plane skidded off the runway while taking off in a snow storm. [VERB prep.]

    飞机在暴风雪中起飞的时候滑出了跑道。

SYN slide, slip, slither, coast  
Skid is also a noun.

  • I slammed the brakes on and went into a skid.

    我猛地一踩刹车，结果车子打滑了。

2 PHRASE [verb-link PHRASE] 无法挽救的;注定要失败的 If you say that something is on the skids, you believe that it is out of control and certain to fail. 无法挽救的;注定要失败的 [informal]

  • His career is on the skids.

    他的事业每况愈下。

  • ...my marriage was on the skids.

    我的婚姻注定要失败。

SYN in trouble, breaking down, on the rocks, going downhill
