shriek
ʃriːk ʃrik
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense shrieks, present participle shrieking, past tense , past participle shrieked
1 VERB (因惊讶或疼痛等)尖叫;尖声笑 When someone shrieks, they make a short, very loud cry, for example because they are suddenly surprised, are in pain, or are laughing. (因惊讶或疼痛等)尖叫;尖声笑

  • She shrieked and leapt from the bed. [VERB]

    她尖叫一声从床上跳了起来。

  • Miranda shrieked with laughter. [VERB + with]

    米兰达尖声大笑。

SYN scream, cry, yell, howl  
Shriek is also a noun.

  • Sue let out a terrific shriek and leapt out of the way.

    休发出一声尖叫，跳到了一边。

  • ...the groans of the wounded, or the shrieks of the dying.

    伤者的呻吟，或者垂死者的尖叫

  • ...a shriek of joy.

    兴奋的尖叫

2 VERB 尖声说出;尖声喊出 If you shriek something, you shout it in a loud, high-pitched voice. 尖声说出;尖声喊出

  • 'Stop it! Stop it!' shrieked Jane. [VERB with quote]

    “停下！停下！”简尖叫道。

  • He was shrieking obscenities and weeping. [VERB noun]

    他一边哭一边尖声咒骂着脏话。
