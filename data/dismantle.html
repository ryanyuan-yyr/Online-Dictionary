dismantle
dɪsmæntəl dɪsˈmæntəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dismantles, present participle dismantling, past tense , past participle dismantled
1 VERB 拆卸;拆开;拆除 If you dismantle a machine or structure, you carefully separate it into its different parts. 拆卸;拆开;拆除

  • He asked for immediate help from the United States to dismantle the warheads. [VERB noun]

    他请求美国立即提供援助，拆除这批弹头。

SYN take apart, strip, demolish, raze  
2 VERB （逐渐）废除，撤销，破坏 To dismantle an organization or system means to cause it to stop functioning by gradually reducing its power or purpose. （逐渐）废除，撤销，破坏

  • ...opposition to the president's policy of dismantling apartheid. [VERB noun]

    反对总统废除种族隔离的政策

  • Public services of all kinds are being dismantled. [VERB noun]

    林林总总的公共设施正被逐渐废除。

SYN abolish, end, overturn, suppress
