project
prɒdʒekt prədʒekt ˈprɑˌdʒɛkt ˈprɑdʒɪkt proʊˈdʒɛkt prəˈdʒɛkt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense projects, present participle projecting, past tense , past participle projected
The noun is pronounced /'prɒdʒekt/. The verb is pronounced /prə'dʒekt/ and is hyphenated pro|ject. 名词读作 /'prɒdʒekt/。动词读作 /prə'dʒekt/，分音节形式为pro|ject。
1 N-COUNT 项目；工程；计划；规划 A project is a task that requires a lot of time and effort. 项目；工程；计划；规划

  • Money will also go into local development projects in Vietnam.

    钱也会用于越南的地方发展项目当中。

  • ...an international science project.

    国际科学项目

  • Besides film and record projects, I have continued to work in the theater.

    除了电影和唱片工作外，我还继续从事戏剧表演。

2 N-COUNT （学生的）课题，研究项目 A project is a detailed study of a subject by a pupil or student. （学生的）课题，研究项目

  • Students complete projects for a personal tutor, working at home at their own pace.

    学生完成了做私人家教的课题，在家里按自己的步调工作。

SYN assignment, task, homework, piece of research  
3 VERB 规划；计划；预计 If something is projected, it is planned or expected. 规划；计划；预计

  • Africa's mid-1993 population is projected to more than double by 2025. [be VERB-ed to-infinitive]

    预计到2025年，非洲的人口会是1993年年中人口的两倍多。

  • The government had been projecting a 5% consumer price increase for the entire year. [VERB noun]

    政府预测全年消费价格将增长5%。

  • ...a projected deficit of $1.5 million. [VERB-ed]

    预计赤字为150万美元

SYN forecast, expect, estimate, predict  
4 VERB （使）呈现；（使）表现；（使）展现 If you project someone or something in a particular way, you try to make people see them in that way. If you project a particular feeling or quality, you show it in your behaviour. （使）呈现；（使）表现；（使）展现

  • Bradley projects a natural warmth and sincerity. [VERB noun]

    布拉德利表现出一种自然的热情和真诚。

  • He just hasn't been able to project himself as the strong leader. [V pron-refl + as]

    他根本没能表现出一个强有力的领导者的样子。

  • His first job will be to project Glasgow as a friendly city. [VERB noun + as]

    他的第一项任务将是树立格拉斯哥作为一座友好城市的形象。

  • The initial image projected was of a caring, effective president. [VERB-ed]

    他最初表现出的是一个充满爱心、办事有效的总统形象。

5 VERB 把（情感、想法）投射转移（到他人身上） If you project feelings or ideas on to other people, you imagine that they have the same ideas or feelings as you. 把（情感、想法）投射转移（到他人身上）

  • He projects his own thoughts and ideas onto her. [V n + on/onto/upon]

    他以为她有着和自己相同的想法和观点。

SYN displace, move, shift, attribute  
6 VERB 投射；放映 If you project a film or picture onto a screen or wall, you make it appear there. 投射；放映

  • The team tried projecting the maps with two different projectors onto the same screen. [VERB noun]

    这个小组想用两台不同的投影仪把这些地图投射到同一个屏幕上。

7 VERB 伸出；凸出 If something projects, it sticks out above or beyond a surface or edge. 伸出；凸出 [formal]

  • ...the remains of a war-time defence which projected out from the shore. [VERB prep./adv.]

    海滨凸立着的战时防御工事的遗迹

  • ...a piece of projecting metal. [VERB-ing]

    一块凸立着的金属

SYN stick out, extend, stand out, bulge  
8 See also: housing project
