doctor
dɒktəʳ ˈdɑktər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense doctors, present participle doctoring, past tense , past participle doctored
1 N-COUNT; N-TITLE; N-VOC 医生;大夫 A doctor is someone who is qualified in medicine and treats people who are ill. 医生;大夫

  • Do not discontinue the treatment without consulting your doctor.

    未经医嘱不要擅自中断治疗。

  • Doctor Paige will be here right after lunch to see her.

    佩奇医生一吃完午饭就会来这儿看她。

SYN physician, medic [informal] , clinician, general practitioner  
2 N-COUNT; N-TITLE; N-VOC 牙医;兽医 A dentist or veterinarian can also be called doctor . 牙医;兽医 [US]
3 N-COUNT [usually singular] 诊所 The doctor's is used to refer to the surgery or office where a doctor works. 诊所

  • I have an appointment at the doctors.

    我在诊所有个预约。

4 N-COUNT; N-TITLE 博士 A doctor is someone who has been awarded the highest academic or honorary degree by a university. 博士

  • He is a doctor of philosophy. [+ of]

    他是哲学博士。

5 VERB 篡改;伪造 If someone doctors something, they change it in order to deceive people. 篡改;伪造

  • They doctored the prints to make her look as awful as possible. [VERB noun]

    他们修改了照片，尽可能地丑化她的容貌。

  • ...a cleverly doctored photograph. [VERB-ed]

    巧妙加工的照片

doctoring

  • The doctoring of the document has become a live political issue.

    对文件的篡改伪造已成为当下让人关注的政治问题。

6 VERB (将毒药、麻药等)掺入(食物或饮料中) If someone doctors food or drink, they add a poison or drug to it. (将毒药、麻药等)掺入(食物或饮料中) [written]

  • She had no doubt that it was he who had doctored her milk. [VERB noun]

    她毫不怀疑是他在她的牛奶里下了药。

  • ...doctored wine. [VERB-ed]

    掺了药的酒
