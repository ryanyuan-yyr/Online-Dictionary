upside down
ʌpsaɪd daʊn
(also upside-down)
1 ADV [ADVERB after verb, n ADVERB] 倒置地；颠倒过来 If something has been moved upside down, it has been turned round so that the part that is usually lowest is above the part that is usually highest. 倒置地；颠倒过来

  • The painting was hung upside down.

    油画挂倒了。

  • Salter held the bag by the corners and shook it upside down.

    索尔特捏住包的外角，把它倒过来晃了晃。

SYN wrong side up, bottom up, on its head  
Upside down is also an adjective.

  • His eyes were open and everything he saw was upside down.

    他的眼睛睁开了，看到的一切都是颠倒的。

  • Tony had an upside-down map of Britain on his wall.

    托尼的墙上倒挂了一张英国地图。

2 to turn something upside down→see: turn
