waive
weɪv weɪv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense waives, present participle waiving, past tense , past participle waived
1 VERB 放弃（合法权利等） If you waive your right to something, for example legal representation, you choose not to have it or do it. 放弃（合法权利等）

  • He pleaded guilty to the murders of three boys and waived his right to appeal. [VERB noun]

    他承认谋杀了3个男孩，并放弃上诉。

SYN give up, relinquish [formal] , renounce, forsake  
2 VERB （特定情况下）不强求执行(规则等) If someone waives a rule, they say that people do not have to obey it in a particular situation. （特定情况下）不强求执行(规则等)

  • The art gallery waives admission charges on Sundays. [VERB noun]

    美术馆周日免收门票。

  • The authorities had agreed to waive normal requirements for permits to cross the border. [VERB noun]

    当局已经同意免除跨境所需办理的常规手续。
