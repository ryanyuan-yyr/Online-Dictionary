resound
rɪzaʊnd rɪˈzaʊnd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense resounds, present participle resounding, past tense , past participle resounded
1 VERB 回响;鸣响 When a noise resounds, it is heard very loudly and clearly. 回响;鸣响 [literary]

  • A roar of approval resounded through the Ukrainian parliament. [VERB prep.]

    一片赞成声在乌克兰议会中回响。

  • The soldiers' boots resounded in the street. [VERB prep.]

    士兵的军靴踏在地面上的声音在大街上回响。

SYN echo, resonate, reverberate, fill the air  
2 VERB 充满，回荡着（声音） If a place resounds with or to particular noises, it is filled with them. 充满，回荡着（声音） [literary]

  • The whole place resounded with music. [VERB + with]

    整个场地回荡着音乐。

  • Kabul resounded to the crack of Kalashnikov fire and a flood of artillery. [VERB + to]

    卡拉什尼科夫冲锋枪的声音和连续不断的大炮声在喀布尔回荡。

SYN ring, be filled, vibrate, resonate
