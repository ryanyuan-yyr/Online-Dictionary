prefigure
priːfɪgəʳ priːˈfɪɡjʊˌreɪt -gjər priˈfɪgjər priˈfɪgjʊr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense prefigures, present participle prefiguring, past tense , past participle prefigured
VERB 预示；预兆 If one thing prefigures another, it is a first indication which suggests or determines that the second thing will happen. 预示；预兆 [formal]

  • The wall through Berlin was finally ruptured, prefiguring the reunification of Germany. [VERB noun]

    柏林墙终于倒塌了，预示着德国的重新统一。
