underrate
ʌndəʳreɪt ˌʌndərˈreɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense underrates, present participle underrating, past tense , past participle underrated
VERB 低估；看轻 If you underrate someone or something, you do not recognize how clever, important, or significant they are. 低估；看轻

  • We women have a lot of good business skills, although we tend to underrate ourselves. [VERB noun]

    我们女性拥有很多出色的商业技巧，只是我们往往会看轻自己。

  • He underrated the seriousness of William's head injury. [VERB noun]

    他低估了威廉头部伤势的严重性。

SYN underestimate, discount, undervalue, belittle  
underrated

  • He is a very underrated poet.

    他是一名才华被严重低估的诗人。

  • The most important and underrated Alpine wine country is Austria.

    最重要、最受低估的阿尔卑斯山葡萄酒生产国乃是奥地利。
