wallow
wɒloʊ ˈwɑloʊ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense wallows, present participle wallowing, past tense , past participle wallowed
1 VERB 沉溺（于不快的境况） If you say that someone is wallowing in an unpleasant situation, you are criticizing them for being deliberately unhappy. 沉溺（于不快的境况） [disapproval]

  • His tired mind continued to wallow in self-pity. [VERB + in]

    他疲惫的心继续沉溺于自哀自怜之中。

  • I wanted only to wallow in my own grief. [VERB in noun]

    我只想沉湎于自己的悲伤中。

2 VERB (在水或泥浆中)躺卧，打滚 If a person or animal wallows in water or mud, they lie or roll about in it slowly for pleasure. (在水或泥浆中)躺卧，打滚

  • Never have I had such a good excuse for wallowing in deep warm baths. [VERB + in]

    我从来没找到过如此好的理由，可以美美地泡个热水澡。

  • Dogs love splashing in mud and hippos wallow in it. [VERB in noun]

    狗喜欢在泥水洼里扑腾，河马则喜欢在其中打滚撒欢儿。
