phone in
1 PHRASAL VERB 打进电话 If you phone in to a radio or television show, you telephone the show in order to give your opinion on a matter that the show has raised. 打进电话

  • Listeners have been invited to phone in to pick the winner. [VERB PREP.]

    听众被邀请打进电话选择赢家。

2 PHRASAL VERB 打电话给（某地） If you phone in to a place, you make a telephone call to that place. 打电话给（某地）

  • He has phoned in to say he is thinking over his options. [VERB PREP. to-infinitive]

    他打来电话说他正在仔细考虑他的选择。

3 PHRASAL VERB 电话预订 If you phone in an order for something, you place the order by telephone. 电话预订 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Just phone in your order three or more days prior to departure. [VERB PREP. noun]

    在出发的三天之前打电话预订即可。

4 PHRASE 打电话请病假 If you phone in sick, you telephone your workplace to say that you will not come to work because you are ill. 打电话请病假

  • On Monday I was still upset and I phoned in sick to work.

    星期一，我还是感觉不适，便打电话到单位请了病假。

phone-in

N-COUNT （电台或电视台）听众（或观众）来电参与的节目 A phone-in is a programme on radio or television in which people telephone with questions or opinions and their calls are broadcast. （电台或电视台）听众（或观众）来电参与的节目 [mainly British]

  • She took part in a BBC radio phone-in programme.

    她参加了英国广播公司听众来电参与节目。
