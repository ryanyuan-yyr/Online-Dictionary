indicate
ɪndɪkeɪt ˈɪndɪˌkeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense indicates, present participle indicating, past tense , past participle indicated
1 VERB 表明;说明 If one thing indicates another, the first thing shows that the second is true or exists. 表明;说明

  • A survey of retired people has indicated that most are independent and enjoying life. [VERB that]

    对退休人员的调查表明，大部分人都自食其力,享受生活。

  • Our vote today indicates a change in United States policy. [VERB noun]

    我们今天的选举表明了美国政策的一种变化。

  • This indicates whether remedies are suitable for children. [VERB wh]

    由此可以看出治疗方法是否适合儿童。

SYN show, suggest, reveal, display  
2 VERB 暗示 If you indicate an opinion, an intention, or a fact, you mention it in an indirect way. 暗示

  • Mr Rivers has indicated that he may resign. [VERB that]

    里弗斯先生暗示他可能会辞职。

  • U.S. authorities have not yet indicated their monetary policy plans. [VERB noun]

    美国当局尚未透露他们的货币政策计划。

SYN imply, suggest, hint, intimate  
3 VERB 指示;指出 If you indicate something to someone, you show them where it is, especially by pointing to it. 指示;指出 [formal]

  • He indicated a chair. 'Sit down.' [VERB noun]

    他指着一把椅子说，“坐下。”

  • Pelham moved across to indicate a wall chart. [VERB noun]

    佩勒姆走过去指着一张挂图。

SYN point to, point out, specify, gesture towards  
4 VERB 是…的标志;象征;反映 If one thing indicates something else, it is a sign of that thing. 是…的标志;象征;反映

  • Dreams can help indicate your true feelings. [VERB noun]

    梦可以反映你的真实感情。

  • His language indicates a poor education. [VERB noun]

    他的言语反映出他没受过多少教育。

5 VERB (仪器等)指示，显示 If a technical instrument indicates something, it shows a measurement or reading. (仪器等)指示，显示

  • The needles that indicate your height are at the top right-hand corner. [VERB noun]

    显示你身高的指针在右上角。

  • The temperature gauge indicated that it was boiling. [VERB that]

    温度计显示它在沸腾。

SYN register, show, record, mark  
6 VERB (驾车时)打转向灯指示转弯 When drivers indicate, they make lights flash on one side of their vehicle to show that they are going to turn in that direction. (驾车时)打转向灯指示转弯 [mainly British] [Also VERB n]

  • He told us when to indicate and when to change gear. [VERB]

    他告诉我们何时打转向灯、何时换挡。
