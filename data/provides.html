provide
prəvaɪd prəˈvaɪd proʊˈvaɪd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense provides, present participle providing, past tense , past participle provided
1 VERB 供给；提供；给予 If you provide something that someone needs or wants, or if you provide them with it, you give it to them or make it available to them. 供给；提供；给予

  • I'll be glad to provide a copy of this. [VERB noun]

    我很愿意提供它的一个副本。

  • They would not provide any details. [VERB noun]

    他们不愿提供任何细节。

  • The government was not in a position to provide them with food. [VERB noun + with]

    政府不可能给他们提供食物。

SYN supply, give, contribute, provision  
provider

  • They remain the main providers of sports facilities.

    他们仍然是体育设施的主要提供商。

2 VERB （法律或协议）规定 If a law or agreement provides that something will happen, it states that it will happen. （法律或协议）规定 [formal]

  • The treaty provides that, by the end of the century, the United States must have removed its bases. [VERB that]

    条约规定，到本世纪末，美国必须撤除其基地。

  • The Act provides that only the parents of a child have a responsibility for that child's financial support. [VERB that]

    法令规定只有孩子的父母才有责任为孩子提供经济支持。

SYN stipulate, state, require, determine
