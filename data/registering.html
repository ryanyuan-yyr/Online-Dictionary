register
redʒɪstəʳ ˈrɛdʒɪstər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense registers, present participle registering, past tense , past participle registered
1 N-COUNT 登记本；注册簿；（官方）记录 A register is an official list or record of people or things. 登记本；注册簿；（官方）记录

  • ...registers of births, deaths and marriages.

    出生、死亡和婚姻记录

  • He signed the register at the hotel.

    他在酒店的登记簿上签了名。

  • She calls the register for her class of thirty 12 year olds.

    她按照花名册上的名单对她班上30个12岁大的孩子进行点名。

SYN list, record, roll, file  
2 VERB 登记；注册 If you register to do something, you put your name on an official list, in order to be able to do that thing or to receive a service. 登记；注册

  • Have you come to register at the school?. [VERB]

    你来学校注册了吗？

  • Thousands lined up to register to vote. [VERB to-infinitive]

    数千选民排队登记投票。

  • Many students register for these courses to widen skills for use in their current job . [VERB + for]

    许多学生报名学习这些课程是为增加技能，以适应目前工作的需要。

  • About 26 million people are not registered with a dentist. [VERB-ed]

    大约2,600万人没有在牙医处登记。

  • ...registered voters. [VERB-ed]

    登记选民

SYN enrol, sign on or up, enlist, list  
3 VERB 登记;正式记录;给…注册 If you register something, such as the name of a person who has just died or information about something you own, you have these facts recorded on an official list. 登记;正式记录;给…注册

  • In order to register a car in Japan, the owner must have somewhere to park it. [VERB noun]

    在日本要登记一辆汽车，车主必须先要有车位。

  • We registered his birth. [VERB noun]

    我们给他进行了出生登记。

  • The house is registered in her name, not her husband's. [VERB noun]

    房子登记在她而不是她丈夫的名下。

  • ...a registered charity. [VERB-ed]

    正式注册的慈善组织

SYN record, catalogue, chronicle, take down  
4 V-ERG （仪表等）显示，指示 When something registers on a scale or measuring instrument, it shows on the scale or instrument. You can also say that something registers a certain amount or level on a scale or measuring instrument. （仪表等）显示，指示

  • It will only register on sophisticated X-ray equipment. [VERB + on]

    只有精密的X射线仪器才能显示出来。

  • The earthquake registered 5.3 points on the Richter scale. [VERB noun]

    地震的震级达到里氏5.3级。

  • The scales registered a gain of 1.3 kilograms. [VERB noun]

    称重显示增加了1.3千克。

5 VERB 表示，表达（情绪或意见） If you register your feelings or opinions about something, you do something that makes them clear to other people. 表示，表达（情绪或意见）

  • Voters wish to register their dissatisfaction with the ruling party. [VERB noun]

    选民希望表达他们对执政党的不满。

  • Workers stopped work to register their protest. [VERB noun]

    工人罢工以示抗议。

SYN express, say, show, reveal  
6 VERB （表情）流露，显露，显出 If a feeling registers on someone's face, their expression shows clearly that they have that feeling. （表情）流露，显露，显出

  • Surprise again registered on Rodney's face. [VERB + on]

    罗德尼的脸上再次露出诧异的神情。

7 V-ERG 引起注意;（被）意识到;（使）留下印象 If a piece of information does not register or if you do not register it, you do not really pay attention to it, and so you do not remember it or react to it. 引起注意;（被）意识到;（使）留下印象 [Also VERB that]

  • What I said sometimes didn't register in her brain. [VERB]

    有时我说的话她根本没听进去。

  • The sound was so familiar that she didn't register it. [VERB noun]

    这个声音如此耳熟，她根本没怎么留意。

SYN have an effect, get through, sink in, make an impression  
8 N-COUNT （嗓音或乐器的）音域 If you sing or play something in a high or low register, you sing, or play it using high or low notes. If you say something in a high or low register, you say it in a high or low voice. （嗓音或乐器的）音域
9 N-VAR （语言学中的）语域 In linguistics, the register of a piece of speech or writing is its level and style of language, which is usually appropriate to the situation or circumstances in which it is used. （语言学中的）语域 [technical]
10 See also: cash register  electoral register
