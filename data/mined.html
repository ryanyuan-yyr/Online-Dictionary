mine
maɪn maɪn
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense mines, present participle mining, past tense , past participle mined

PRONOUN USE 代词用法

PRON-POSS (第一人称单数所有格代词)我的(东西) Mine is the first person singular possessive pronoun. A speaker or writer uses mine to refer to something that belongs or relates to himself or herself. (第一人称单数所有格代词)我的(东西)

  • Her right hand is inches from mine.

    她的右手和我的手挨得很近。

  • That wasn't his fault, it was mine.

    那不是他的错，是我的错。

  • I'm looking for a friend of mine who lives here.

    我正在寻找一位住在这里的朋友。

NOUN AND VERB USES 名词和动词用法

1 N-COUNT [oft n NOUN] 矿;矿山;矿井 A mine is a place where deep holes and tunnels are dug under the ground in order to obtain a mineral such as coal, diamonds, or gold. 矿;矿山;矿井

  • ...coal mines.

    煤矿

2 VERB [usually passive] 开采;采掘 When a mineral such as coal, diamonds, or gold is mined, it is obtained from the ground by digging deep holes and tunnels. 开采;采掘

  • The pit is being shut down because it no longer has enough coal that can be mined economically. [be VERB-ed]

    这座煤矿即将关闭，因为从经济上考虑，矿井里已经没有足够的煤可供开采。

  • ...the finest gems, mined from all corners of the world. [VERB-ed]

    从世界各地采掘来的最精美的宝石

3 N-COUNT 地雷;水雷 A mine is a bomb which is hidden in the ground or in water and which explodes when people or things touch it. 地雷;水雷
4 VERB 在…布雷 If an area of land or water is mined, mines are placed there which will explode when people or things touch them. 在…布雷 [Also VERB n]

  • The approaches to the garrison have been heavily mined. [be VERB-ed]

    通往要塞的道路都已布满地雷。

SYN lay mines in or under, sow with mines  
5 PHRASE 知识宝库；信息源泉 If you say that someone is a mine of information, you mean that they know a great deal about something. 知识宝库；信息源泉
