wade in
(also wade into)
PHRASAL VERB (常指不计后果地)介入，干涉 If someone wades in or wades into something, they get involved in a very determined and forceful way, often without thinking enough about the consequences of their actions. (常指不计后果地)介入，干涉

  • They don't just listen sympathetically, they wade in with remarks like, 'If I were you...'. [VERB PREP.]

    他们不是只同情地倾听，而是还时不时地插话进来：“如果我是你…”

  • I waded in to help, but got kicked to the ground. [VERB PREP.]

    我本想插进去帮忙，结果却被踢倒在地。

  • Police waded into a crowd of protesters. [VERB PREP. noun]

    警察介入，企图驱散抗议人群。

SYN move in, pitch in, dive in [informal] , set to work
