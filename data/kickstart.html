kick-start
ˈkɪkˌstɑːt ˈkɪkˌstɑrt
(also kickstart)Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense kick-starts, present participle kick-starting, past tense , past participle kick-started
1 VERB 使快速重新启动;使…重新开始 To kick-start a process that has stopped working or progressing is to take a course of action that will quickly start it going again. 使快速重新启动;使…重新开始

  • The President has chosen to kick-start the economy by slashing interest rates. [VERB noun]

    总统选择通过大幅降低利率来重新拉动经济增长。

SYN stimulate, encourage, prompt, spur  
Kick-start is also a noun.

  • The housing market needs a kick-start.

    房地产市场需要刺激。

2 VERB 用脚踏启动（摩托车） If you kick-start a motorcycle, you press the lever that starts it with your foot. 用脚踏启动（摩托车）

  • He lifted the bike off its stand and kick-started it. [VERB noun]

    他将摩托车撑脚架往上一提，开始蹬车启动。
