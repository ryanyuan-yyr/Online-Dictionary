redraw
riːdrɔː riːˈdrɔː
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense redraws, present participle redrawing, past tense redrew, past participle redrawn
1 VERB 重划（边境或边界） If people in a position of authority redraw the boundaries or borders of a country or region, they change the borders so that the country or region covers a slightly different area than before. 重划（边境或边界）

  • They have redrawn the country's boundaries along ethnic lines. [VERB noun]

    他们已经按照民族界线重新划定了国家的边界。

  • The map of post-war Europe was redrawn. [VERB noun]

    战后欧洲的版图被重新划定。

SYN change, review, correct, edit  
2 VERB 改变，改动（安排、计划等） If people redraw something, for example an arrangement or plan, they change it because circumstances have changed. 改变，改动（安排、计划等）

  • With both countries experiencing economic revolutions, it might be time to redraw the traditional relationship. [VERB noun]

    两国都在经历经济革命，现在可能是重新描绘两国传统关系的时候。

  • The Treaty of Union should be redrawn. [VERB noun]

    应该重新制定《联盟条约》。

SYN revise
