schlep
ʃlep ʃlɛp ʃlɛp
(also schlepp)Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense schleps, present participle schlepping, past tense , past participle schlepped
1 VERB (费力或不方便地)携带,搬运，拖曳 If you schlep something somewhere, you take it there although this is difficult or inconvenient. (费力或不方便地)携带,搬运，拖曳 [US, informal]

  • You didn't just schlep your guitar around from folk club to folk club. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    你不只是背着吉他艰难地从一家乡村俱乐部转到另一家。

2 VERB 去;赶往 If you schlep somewhere, you go there. 去;赶往 [US, informal]

  • It's too cold to schlepp around looking at property. [VERB adv./prep.]

    天太冷，不适合到处去看房子。

3 N-COUNT 蠢货;笨蛋;笨手笨脚的人 If you describe someone as a schlep, you mean that they are stupid or clumsy. 蠢货;笨蛋;笨手笨脚的人 [US, informal, disapproval]
