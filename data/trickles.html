trickle
trɪkəl ˈtrɪkəl ˈtrɪkəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense trickles, present participle trickling, past tense , past participle trickled
1 V-ERG (使)滴;(使)淌 When a liquid trickles, or when you trickle it, it flows slowly in very small amounts. (使)滴;(使)淌 [Also VERB]

  • A tear trickled down the old man's cheek. [VERB prep./adv.]

    一滴眼泪沿着老人的脸颊淌了下来。

  • Trickle water gently over the back of your baby's head. [VERB noun]

    往婴儿的脑后部轻撩些水。

  • The trickling stream glistened in the sunlight. [VERB-ing]

    涓涓溪流在阳光下闪闪发亮。

SYN dribble, run, drop, stream  
Trickle is also a noun.

  • There was not so much as a trickle of water.

    连一道细流都没有。

2 VERB (小批地)慢慢移动 When people or things trickle in a particular direction, they move there slowly in small groups or amounts, rather than all together. (小批地)慢慢移动

  • Some donations are already trickling in. [VERB adv./prep.]

    一些捐赠已经陆续到来。

Trickle is also a noun.

  • The flood of cars has now slowed to a trickle.

    汹涌的车流现在已经变得稀稀拉拉。

  • A trickle of refugees began to flee the country.

    难民开始三五成群地陆续逃离该国。
