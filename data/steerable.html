steer
stɪəʳ stɪr
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense steers, present participle steering, past tense , past participle steered
1 VERB 驾驶(车、船、飞机等) When you steer a car, boat, or plane, you control it so that it goes in the direction that you want. 驾驶(车、船、飞机等) [Also VERB]

  • What is it like to steer a ship this size?. [VERB noun]

    驾驶这么大的船感觉怎么样？

  • When I was a kid, about six or seven, she would often let me steer the car along our driveway. [VERB noun prep.]

    在我小时候，大概六七岁时，她经常让我在自家的车道上开车。

SYN drive, control, direct, handle  
2 VERB 引导，指导(某人采取某种行动或态度) If you steer people towards a particular course of action or attitude, you try to lead them gently in that direction. 引导，指导(某人采取某种行动或态度)

  • The new government is seen as one that will steer the country in the right direction. [VERB noun prep.]

    新政府被认为能将国家引向正确的方向。

  • I think you are perfectly correct in trying to steer your mother towards increased independence. [VERB noun prep.]

    我认为你试图引导你母亲变得更加独立是完全正确的。

3 VERB 引导，带领(某人去某地) If you steer someone in a particular direction, you guide them there. 引导，带领(某人去某地)

  • Nick steered them into the nearest seats. [VERB noun prep.]

    尼克将他们领到最近的座位上。

SYN direct, lead, guide, conduct  
4 VERB 选择某种做法;采取某种方式 If you steer a particular course, you take a particular line of action. 选择某种做法;采取某种方式

  • Prime Minister Hun Sen has sought to steer a course between the two groups. [VERB noun prep.]

    洪森首相设法游走于两个集团之间。

  • In nearly all these issues the British steered a middle course. [VERB noun]

    英国人几乎在所有这些问题上都选择了中间路线。

5 N-COUNT 犍牛 A steer is a bull that has been castrated. 犍牛
6 See also: steering 
7 PHRASE 避开;躲避;绕开 If you steer clear of someone or something, you deliberately avoid them. 避开;躲避;绕开

  • I think a lot of people, women in particular, steer clear of these sensitive issues.

    我认为很多人，特别是女性，都会回避这些敏感问题。
