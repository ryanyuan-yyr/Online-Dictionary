ostracize
ɒstrəsaɪz ˈɑstrəˌsaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense ostracizes, present participle ostracizing, past tense , past participle ostracized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 ostracise
VERB [usually passive] 排斥；排挤 If someone is ostracized, people deliberately behave in an unfriendly way towards them and do not allow them to take part in any of their social activities. 排斥；排挤 [formal]

  • She claims she's being ostracized by some members of her local community. [be VERB-ed]

    她声称受到当地社区一些人的排挤。

SYN exclude, reject, boycott, avoid
