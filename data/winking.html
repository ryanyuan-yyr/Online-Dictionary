wink
wɪŋk wɪŋk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense winks, present participle winking, past tense , past participle winked
1 VERB 向…眨眼；使眼色 When you wink at someone, you look towards them and close one eye very briefly, usually as a signal that something is a joke or a secret. 向…眨眼；使眼色

  • Brian winked at his bride-to-be. [VERB + at]

    布赖恩向他的准新娘使了个眼色。

  • He smiled, winked and nodded, giving his seal of approval. [VERB]

    他笑了，使了个眼色，然后点点头表示同意。

SYN blink, bat, flutter, nictate  
Wink is also a noun.

  • I gave her a wink.

    我朝她使了个眼色。

2 VERB (灯)闪亮，闪烁 If a lamp winks, it shines or reflects light in short flashes. (灯)闪亮，闪烁

  • From the hotel window, they could see lights winking on the bay. [VERB]

    透过宾馆的窗子，他们能看见海湾一带灯光摇曳。

SYN twinkle, flash, shine, sparkle  
3 PHRASE 无法入睡;没合一下眼 If you say that you did not sleep a wink or did not get a wink of sleep, you mean that you tried to go to sleep but could not. 无法入睡;没合一下眼 [informal]

  • I didn't get a wink of sleep on the aeroplane.

    我在飞机上没合一下眼。
