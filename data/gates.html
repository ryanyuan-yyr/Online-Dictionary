gate
geɪt geɪt
Word forms: plural gates
1 N-COUNT 大门；门 A gate is a structure like a door which is used at the entrance to a field, a garden, or the grounds of a building. 大门；门

  • He opened the gate and started walking up to the house.

    他打开大门，迈步朝房子走去。

SYN barrier, opening, door, access  
2 N-COUNT 登机门；登机口 In an airport, a gate is a place where passengers leave the airport and get on their aeroplane. 登机门；登机口

  • Passengers with hand luggage can go straight to the departure gate to check in there.

    携带手提行李的乘客可直接到登机口办理登机手续。

3 N-IN-NAMES …门（在英国用于街道名） Gate is used in the names of streets in Britain that are in a place where there once was a gate into a city. …门（在英国用于街道名）

  • ...9 Palace Gate.

    王宫门9号

4 N-COUNT （体育比赛的）观众总人数 The gate at a sporting event such as a football match or baseball game is the total number of people who attend it. （体育比赛的）观众总人数

  • Their average gate is less than 23,000.

    他们的平均上座率不到23,000人。
