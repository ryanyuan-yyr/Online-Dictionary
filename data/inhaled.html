inhale
ɪnheɪl ɪnˈheɪl ˈɪnˌheɪl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense inhales, present participle inhaling, past tense , past participle inhaled
VERB 吸气;吸入(烟等) When you inhale, you breathe in. When you inhale something such as smoke, you take it into your lungs when you breathe in. 吸气;吸入(烟等)

  • He took a long slow breath, inhaling deeply. [VERB]

    他缓缓地深吸了一口气。

  • He was treated for the effects of inhaling smoke. [VERB noun]

    他因吸入烟尘而接受治疗。

SYN breathe in, gasp, draw in, suck in
