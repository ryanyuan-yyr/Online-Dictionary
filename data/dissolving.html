dissolve
dɪzɒlv dɪˈzɑlv dɪˈzɔlv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dissolves, present participle dissolving, past tense , past participle dissolved
1 V-ERG （使）溶解 If a substance dissolves in liquid or if you dissolve it, it becomes mixed with the liquid and disappears. （使）溶解

  • Heat gently until the sugar dissolves. [VERB]

    慢慢加热直到糖溶解为止。

  • Dissolve the salt in a little boiled water. [VERB noun]

    将盐溶解在少许开水中。

SYN melt, break down, disintegrate, soften  
2 VERB 解散(团体或机构) When an organization or institution is dissolved, it is officially ended or broken up. 解散(团体或机构)

  • The committee has been dissolved. [be VERB-ed]

    委员会已经解散了。

  • The King agreed to dissolve the present commission. [VERB noun]

    国王同意解散目前的委员会。

SYN end, dismiss, suspend, axe [informal]  
3 VERB 解散(议会) When a parliament is dissolved, it is formally ended, so that elections for a new parliament can be held. 解散(议会)

  • The present assembly will be dissolved on April 30th. [be VERB-ed]

    现在的议会将于 4 月 30 日解散。

  • Kaifu threatened to dissolve the Parliament and call an election. [VERB noun]

    卡夫威胁要解散议会并举行选举。

4 VERB [usually passive] 解除(婚姻关系）；终止（商业协议) When a marriage or business arrangement is dissolved, it is officially ended. 解除(婚姻关系）；终止（商业协议)

  • The marriage was dissolved in 1976. [be VERB-ed]

    这段婚姻于1976年结束。

5 V-ERG （使）消失;消除;消散 If something such as a problem or feeling dissolves or is dissolved, it becomes weaker and disappears. （使）消失;消除;消散

  • His new-found optimism dissolved. [VERB]

    他刚刚萌生的乐观情绪消失了。

  • Lenny still could not dissolve the nagging lump of tension in his chest. [VERB noun]

    莱尼仍不能消除令他不安的紧张感。

SYN disappear, fade, vanish, break down
