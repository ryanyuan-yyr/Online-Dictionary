sear
sɪəʳ sɪr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense sears, present participle searing, past tense , past participle seared
1 VERB 烧掉…的表面;烧焦 To sear something means to burn its surface with a sudden intense heat. 烧掉…的表面;烧焦

  • Grass fires have seared the land near the farming village of Basekhai. [VERB noun]

    草地野火烧焦了巴斯克亥农庄附近的土地。

SYN wither, burn, blight, brand  
2 VERB 烧灼;灼伤;灼痛 If something sears a part of your body, it causes a painful burning feeling there. 烧灼;灼伤;灼痛 [literary]

  • I distinctly felt the heat start to sear my throat. [VERB noun]

    我分明感觉到嗓子开始烧得难受。
