ratify
rætɪfaɪ ˈrætəˌfaɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense ratifies, present participle ratifying, past tense , past participle ratified
VERB 正式签署;批准;认可 When national leaders or organizations ratify a treaty or written agreement, they make it official by giving their formal approval to it, usually by signing it or voting for it. 正式签署;批准;认可

  • The parliaments of Australia and Indonesia have yet to ratify the treaty. [VERB noun]

    澳大利亚和印度尼西亚的国会尚未批准该条约。

SYN approve, sign, establish, confirm
