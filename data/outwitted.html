outwit
aʊtwɪt ˌaʊtˈwɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense outwits, present participle outwitting, past tense , past participle outwitted
VERB 智胜；用计击败（或超过） If you outwit someone, you use your intelligence or a clever trick to defeat them or to gain an advantage over them. 智胜；用计击败（或超过）

  • To win the presidency he had first to outwit his rivals within the Socialist Party. [VERB noun]

    要想赢得总统之位，他必须首先智胜其在社会党内的竞选对手。

  • Seeing how cleverly he had been outwitted he made no complaint. [VERB noun]

    看到自己在计谋上完全不是对手，他甘拜下风。

SYN outsmart [informal] , get the better of, circumvent, outperform
