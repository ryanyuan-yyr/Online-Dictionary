sequence
siːkwəns ˈsikwəns ˈsiˌkwɛns
Word forms: plural sequences
1 N-COUNT 一系列;一连串 A sequence of events or things is a number of events or things that come one after another in a particular order. 一系列;一连串

  • ...the sequence of events which led to the murder. [+ of]

    导致谋杀发生的一连串事件

  • ...a dazzling sequence of novels by John Updike. [+ of]

    约翰·厄普代克令人惊叹的系列小说

SYN succession, course, series, order  
2 N-COUNT 顺序;次序;序列 A particular sequence is a particular order in which things happen or are arranged. 顺序;次序;序列

  • ...the colour sequence yellow, orange, purple, blue, green and white.

    黄、橙、紫、蓝、绿、白的颜色顺序

  • The chronological sequence gives the book an element of structure.

    时间顺序让这本书有了一定的结构。

SYN order, structure, arrangement, ordering  
3 N-COUNT (电影中描述某一组动作的)连续镜头,片段 A film sequence is a part of a film that shows a single set of actions. (电影中描述某一组动作的)连续镜头,片段

  • The best sequence in the film occurs when Roth stops at a house he used to live in.

    电影中最棒的一组镜头出现于罗思在他曾住过的一幢屋子前驻足时。

SYN part, scene, section, episode  
4 N-COUNT (基因)排列顺序，序列 A gene sequence or a DNA sequence is the order in which the elements making up a particular gene are combined. (基因)排列顺序，序列

  • The project is nothing less than mapping every gene sequence in the human body.

    这个项目就是要绘制人体所有基因序列的图谱。

  • ...the complete DNA sequence of the human genome. [+ of]

    人体基因组的完整 DNA 序列
