mushroom
mʌʃruːm ˈmʌʃruːm ˈmʌʃˌrum ˈmʌʃˌrʊm
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense mushrooms, present participle mushrooming, past tense , past participle mushroomed
1 N-VAR 蘑菇;伞菌;蕈 Mushrooms are fungi that you can eat. 蘑菇;伞菌;蕈

  • There are many types of wild mushrooms.

    野生蘑菇有很多种。

  • ...eggs, bacon, sausage, and mushrooms.

    鸡蛋、腊肉、香肠和蘑菇

  • ...mushroom omelette.

    蘑菇煎蛋饼

2See also:button mushroom 
3 VERB 迅速成长;大量涌现 If something such as an industry or a place mushrooms, it grows or comes into existence very quickly. 迅速成长;大量涌现

  • The media training industry has mushroomed over the past decade. [VERB]

    在过去10年里，媒体培训产业迅速成长。

  • A sleepy capital of a few hundred thousand people has mushroomed to a crowded city of 2 million. [V + to/into]

    几十万人的沉寂首府迅速发展成为拥有两百万人口的拥挤都市。

SYN expand, increase, spread, boom  
mushrooming

  • ...the mushrooming of commercial art galleries in Barcelona and Madrid.

    巴塞罗那和马德里两地大量涌现的商业美术馆
