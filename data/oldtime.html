old-time ˈoʊldˈtaɪm ˈoʊldˌtaɪm
1 ADJ [ADJ n] 老式的；旧式的；过时的 If you describe something as old-time, you mean that it was common or popular in the past but is not common or popular now. 老式的；旧式的；过时的

  • ...an old-time dance hall which still has a tea dance on Monday afternoons.

    每周一下午依旧举办茶话舞会的老式舞厅

2 ADJ [ADJ n] （用于职业名称前）过去的，昔日的 You can use old-time before the name of someone's job to show that they do their job in the way it was done in the past. （用于职业名称前）过去的，昔日的

  • The effect of that singing, as the old-time reporters used to say, was electrical.

    正如从前的记者常说的那样，歌声直击人心。

  • ...like an old-time sailor climbing the rigging.

    像过去爬帆索的水手
