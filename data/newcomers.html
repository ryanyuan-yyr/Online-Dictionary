newcomer
njuːkʌməʳ nuː- ˈnuˌkʌmər ˈnjuˌkʌmər
Word forms: plural newcomers
1 N-COUNT 新来的人;新参加的人;新手 A newcomer is a person who has recently arrived in a place, joined an organization, or started a new activity. 新来的人;新参加的人;新手

  • He must be a newcomer to town and he obviously didn't understand our local customs. [+ to]

    他一定是刚到镇上来的，明显不了解我们这儿的风俗。

  • The candidates are both relative newcomers to politics. [+ to]

    两位候选人相对而言都是政坛新人。

SYN beginner, stranger, outsider, novice  
2 N-COUNT 新近出现的事物;新得到的东西 A newcomer is something which has not existed before or been available before. 新近出现的事物;新得到的东西

  • The company's latest newcomer is a 4 x 4 estate with a 2.2 litre petrol engine.

    公司最近新到了一辆有2.2升汽油发动机的4 x 4客货两用车。

  • ...last year's aggressive price slashing by campsite newcomer, Airtours.

    野营服务新军英国空中旅游公司去年咄咄逼人的大幅削价
