bunker
bʌŋkəʳ ˈbʌŋkər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , plural bunkers, present participle bunkering, past tense , past participle bunkered
1 N-COUNT （通常建于地下的）掩体；地堡 A bunker is a place, usually underground, that has been built with strong walls to protect it against heavy gunfire and bombing. （通常建于地下的）掩体；地堡

  • ...an extensive network of fortified underground bunkers.

    四通八达的地下防御工事

2 N-COUNT 煤箱；燃料箱 A bunker is a container for coal or other fuel. 煤箱；燃料箱
3 N-COUNT （高尔夫球场上的）沙坑 On a golf course, a bunker is a large area filled with sand, which is deliberately put there as an obstacle that golfers must try to avoid. （高尔夫球场上的）沙坑
4 VERB （高尔夫球运动中）把球击入沙坑 In golf, if you bunker a shot, you hit your ball into the bunker. （高尔夫球运动中）把球击入沙坑

  • She bunkered her second shot. [VERB noun]

    她第二次击球把球打进了沙坑。
