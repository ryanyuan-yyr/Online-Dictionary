payoff
peɪɒf ˈpeɪɒf ˈpeɪˌɔf
(also pay-off)Word forms: plural payoffs
1 N-COUNT 优势；收益；收效；回报 The payoff from an action is the advantage or benefit that you get from it. 优势；收益；收效；回报

  • If such materials became generally available to the optics industry the payoffs from such a breakthrough would be enormous.

    如果这些材料能够普遍用于光学产业，那么这一突破带来的收益将非常巨大。

  • You're doing what you really love to do, which is making music — that's the payoff.

    你现在做着你真正喜欢做的事情，也就是音乐制作—这就是回报。

2 N-COUNT （用于避免麻烦的）封口费，贿赂 A payoff is a payment which is made to someone, often secretly or illegally, so that they will not cause trouble. （用于避免麻烦的）封口费，贿赂

  • Soldiers in both countries supplement their incomes with payoffs from drugs exporters. [+ from]

    两国士兵都用毒品走私贩的贿赂来贴补收入。

3 N-COUNT 解约金；遣散费 A payoff is a large payment made to someone by their employer when the person has been forced to leave their job. 解约金；遣散费

  • The ousted chairman received a £1.5 million payoff from the loss-making oil company.

    被解职的主席从亏损的石油公司那里拿到了150万英镑的解约金。
