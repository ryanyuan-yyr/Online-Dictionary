boss
bɒs bɔs bɑs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense bosses, present participle bossing, past tense , past participle bossed
1 N-COUNT [oft poss NOUN] 老板；上司 Your boss is the person in charge of the organization or department where you work. 老板；上司

  • He cannot stand his boss.

    他无法忍受他的老板。

  • Occasionally I have to go and ask the boss for a rise.

    有时我不得不找老板要求加薪。

SYN manager, head, leader, director  
2 N-COUNT 领导；头儿；发号施令者 If you are the boss in a group or relationship, you are the person who makes all the decisions. 领导；头儿；发号施令者 [informal]

  • He thinks he's the boss.

    他认为自己是头儿。

3 VERB 对…颐指气使；对…指手画脚 If you say that someone bosses you, you mean that they keep telling you what to do in a way that is irritating. 对…颐指气使；对…指手画脚 [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • We cannot boss them into doing more. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    我们不能再差遣他们做更多的事了。

  • 'You are not to boss me!' she shouted. [VERB noun]

    “你不能指挥我！”她喊道。

SYN be in charge, run, control, manage  
Boss around, or in British English boss about, means the same as boss .boss around,boss about（英国英语）同boss

  • He started bossing people around and I didn't like what was happening.

    他开始把别人差来遣去，而我不喜欢他这样。

4 PHRASE 为自己打工；自己拿主意 If you are your own boss, you work for yourself or make your own decisions and do not have anyone telling you what to do. 为自己打工；自己拿主意

  • I'm very much my own boss and no one interferes with what I do.

    我自己很有主见，没有人干预我做的事。
