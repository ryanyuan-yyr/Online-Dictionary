civilize
sɪvɪlaɪz ˈsɪvəˌlaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense civilizes, present participle civilizing, past tense , past participle civilized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 civilise
VERB 教化;使开化 To civilize a person or society means to educate them and improve their way of life. 教化;使开化

  • ...a comedy about a man who tries to civilise a woman—but she ends up civilising him. [VERB noun]

    一出有关一个男人试图教化一个女人，结果反而被那个女人教化的喜剧

  • It exerts a civilizing influence on mankind. [VERB-ing]

    这产生了教化人类的影响。

SYN cultivate, improve, polish, educate
