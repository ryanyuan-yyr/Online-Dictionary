lampoon
læmpuːn læmˈpun
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense lampoons, present participle lampooning, past tense , past participle lampooned
1 VERB 讥讽；冷嘲热讽；极力挖苦 If you lampoon someone or something, you criticize them very strongly, using humorous means. 讥讽；冷嘲热讽；极力挖苦

  • He entertained his readers by lampooning the pretensions of the rich. [VERB noun]

    他以冷嘲热讽自以为是的有钱人来愉悦读者。

  • He was lampooned for his short stature and political views. [be VERB-ed]

    他的矮小身材和政治观点使他成了受奚落的对象。

SYN ridicule, mock, mimic, parody  
2 N-VAR 辛辣的讽刺文章（或言论） A lampoon is a piece of writing or speech which criticizes someone or something very strongly, using humorous means. 辛辣的讽刺文章（或言论）

  • ...his scathing lampoons of consumer culture. [+ of]

    他对消费文化的尖刻讥讽

  • The style Shelley is using here is that of popular lampoon.

    这里雪莱用的是通俗的嘲讽手法。

SYN satire, parody, caricature, send-up [British , informal]
