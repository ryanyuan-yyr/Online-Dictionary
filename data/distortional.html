distortion
dɪstɔːʳʃən dɪˈstɔːʃən dɪˈstɔrʃən
Word forms: plural distortions
1 N-VAR 歪曲;曲解 Distortion is the changing of something into something that is not true or not acceptable. 歪曲;曲解 [disapproval]

  • I think it would be a gross distortion of reality to say that they were motivated by self-interest. [+ of]

    我认为说他们是受自身利益的驱使是对事实的严重歪曲。

  • He later accused reporters of wilful distortion and bias.

    他后来指责记者有意歪曲事实并带有偏见。

SYN misrepresentation, bias, slant, perversion  
2 N-VAR 失真;变形 Distortion is the changing of the appearance or sound of something in a way that makes it seem strange or unclear. 失真;变形

  • Audio signals can be transmitted along cables without distortion.

    声音信号可以通过电缆传送而不失真。
