dazzle
dæzəl ˈdæzəl ˈdæzəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dazzles, present participle dazzling, past tense , past participle dazzled
1 VERB 使惊叹;使叹服;使倾倒 If someone or something dazzles you, you are extremely impressed by their skill, qualities, or beauty. 使惊叹;使叹服;使倾倒 [Also VERB n]

  • George dazzled her with his knowledge of the world. [VERB noun + with]

    乔治的洞察世情令她叹服。

  • The movie's special effects fail to dazzle. [VERB]

    这部电影的特效未能出彩。

SYN impress, amaze, fascinate, overwhelm  
2 N-SING [with poss] 辉煌;灿烂;艳丽;令人赞叹的特质 The dazzle of something is a quality it has, such as beauty or skill, which is impressive and attractive. 辉煌;灿烂;艳丽;令人赞叹的特质

  • The dazzle of stardom and status attracts them.

    明星的光彩和地位吸引着他们。

SYN splendour, sparkle, glitter, flash  
3 VERB （强光等）使目眩，使眼花 If a bright light dazzles you, it makes you unable to see properly for a short time. （强光等）使目眩，使眼花

  • The sun, glinting from the pool, dazzled me. [VERB noun]

    在太阳的照射下，池面上泛起粼粼波光，让我一时睁不开眼。

  • Kelly was dazzled by the lights. [VERB noun]

    灯光照得凯莉有些眼晕。

SYN blind, confuse, daze, bedazzle  
4 N-UNCOUNT (光的）炫目，耀眼，闪耀 The dazzle of a light is its brightness, which makes it impossible for you to see properly for a short time. (光的）炫目，耀眼，闪耀

  • The sun's dazzle on the water hurts my eyes.

    水面上耀眼的阳光刺痛了我的眼睛。

  • ...a filter that can cut dazzle.

    可以去除眩光的滤光片

SYN brilliance, intensity, sparkle, blaze  
5 See also: razzle-dazzle
