scatter
skætəʳ ˈskætər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense scatters, present participle scattering, past tense , past participle scattered
1 VERB 撒;撒播;撒于…上 If you scatter things over an area, you throw or drop them so that they spread all over the area. 撒;撒播;撒于…上

  • She tore the rose apart and scattered the petals over the grave. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    她掰开玫瑰花，将花瓣撒在坟墓上。

  • They've been scattering toys everywhere. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他们总是把玩具扔得到处都是。

  • He began by scattering seed and putting in plants. [VERB noun]

    他从播种和栽苗开始。

SYN throw about, spread, sprinkle, strew  
2 V-ERG (使)(人群)分散;(使)散布在各处;(使)散开 If a group of people scatter or if you scatter them, they suddenly separate and move in different directions. (使)(人群)分散;(使)散布在各处;(使)散开

  • After dinner, everyone scattered. [VERB]

    用餐后，大家各奔东西。

  • The cavalry scattered them and chased them off the field. [VERB noun]

    骑兵驱散了他们并把他们逐出了战场。

SYN disperse, separate, break up, dispel  
3 N-SING [usually NOUN of n] (散放的)摊，堆 A scatter of things is a number of them spread over an area in an irregular way. (散放的)摊，堆 [literary]

  • On the table was a pile of books and a scatter of papers.

    桌子上放着一摞书和零落的几份报纸。
