personalize
pɜːʳsənəlaɪz ˈpɜrsənəˌlaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense personalizes, present participle personalizing, past tense , past participle personalized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 personalise
1 VERB [usually passive] 在…上标明主人姓名 If an object is personalized, it is marked with the name or initials of its owner. 在…上标明主人姓名

  • The clock has easy-to-read numbers and is personalised with the child's name and birth date. [be VERB-ed]

    钟表上的数字一目了然，并且标有孩子的姓名和出生日期。

SYN customize, individualize, make to order, make distinctive  
personalized

  • ...a unique collection of personalised presents, colourful toys & games.

    独一无二的标有主人姓名的礼物、五彩缤纷的玩具和游戏的汇集

  • ...a Rolls-Royce with a personalised number plate.

    车牌上标有车主姓名的劳斯莱斯汽车

2 VERB 为个人特制（或专设） If you personalize something, you do or design it specially according to the needs of an individual or to your own needs. 为个人特制（或专设）

  • Personalising your car has never been cheaper. [VERB noun]

    定制汽车从来都没这么便宜过。

  • ...an ideal centre for professional men or women who need intensive, personalised French courses. [VERB-ed]

    面向需要个性化法语强化课程的专业人士的理想培训中心

3 VERB 使（论点、讨论、思想、事情）针对个人；使个人化 If you personalize an argument, discussion, idea, or issue, you consider it from the point of view of individual people and their characters or relationships, rather than considering the facts in a general or abstract way. 使（论点、讨论、思想、事情）针对个人；使个人化 [Also VERB]

  • Women tend to personalise rejection more than men. [VERB noun]

    一旦遭拒，女性比男性更容易将事情个人化。

  • I hope they won't make the mistake of personalising the issue. [VERB noun]

    我希望他们不要犯对人不对事的错误。

  • The contest has become personalised, if not bitter. [VERB-ed]

    这场角逐开始针对个人了，甚至话语也变得尖刻起来。
