blanch
blɑːntʃ blæntʃ blæntʃ blɑntʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense blanches, present participle blanching, past tense , past participle blanched
1 VERB 突然变得煞白 If you blanch, you suddenly become very pale. 突然变得煞白

  • His face blanched as he looked at Sharpe's blood-drenched uniform. [VERB]

    他一看到夏普浸满鲜血的制服，脸色顿时变得煞白。

  • She felt herself blanch at the unpleasant memories. [VERB + at]

    一回想起那些不愉快的事情，她觉得自己的脸刷地就白了。

SYN turn pale, fade, pale, drain  
2 VERB 退缩；回避 If you say that someone blanches at something, you mean that they find it unpleasant and do not want to be involved with it. 退缩；回避

  • Everything he had said had been a mistake. He blanched at his miscalculations. [VERB + at]

    他所说的一切都是错的。他回避了自己的判断错误。

  • There are places you can take physically handicapped children where staff don't blanch at the sight of a wheelchair. [VERB at noun]

    有些地方你能带残疾儿童去，那里的工作人员不会一看到轮椅就退缩。

SYN recoil, start, withdraw, flee  
3 VERB 焯 If you blanch vegetables, fruit, or nuts, you put them into boiling water for a short time, usually in order to remove their skins, or to prepare them for freezing. 焯

  • Skin the peaches by blanching them. [VERB noun]

    把桃子焯一下去皮。

SYN boil, scald, dunk
