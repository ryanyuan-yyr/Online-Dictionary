awaken
əweɪkən əˈweɪkən əˈweɪkən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense awakens, present participle awakening, past tense , past participle awakened
1 VERB 唤起，激起（情感） To awaken a feeling in a person means to cause them to start having this feeling. 唤起，激起（情感） [literary]

  • The aim of the cruise was to awaken an interest in and an understanding of foreign cultures. [VERB noun]

    此次巡游旨在唤起我们对外国文化的兴趣和理解。

2 V-ERG （使）意识到；（使）认识到 When you awaken to a fact or when someone awakens you to it, you become aware of it. （使）意识到；（使）认识到 [literary]

  • The British never awaken to peril until it is almost too late. [VERB to noun]

    英国人从来不懂得防患于未然。

  • ...the picture of the Earth, so blue and fragile, that awakened a generation to the Earth's mortality. [VERB noun to noun]

    照片上的地球，如此的忧郁而脆弱，唤起了一代人对地球生命的意识

3 V-ERG 醒来;唤醒;叫醒 When you awaken, or when something or someone awakens you, you wake up. 醒来;唤醒;叫醒 [literary]

  • Unfortunately, Grandma always seems to awaken at awkward moments. [VERB]

    不幸的是，奶奶好像总是醒来的不是时候。

  • He was snoring when Desmond awakened him. [VERB noun]

    德斯蒙德叫醒他的时候他还在打呼噜呢。

SYN awake, wake, wake up, revive
