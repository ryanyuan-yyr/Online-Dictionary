vitiate
vɪʃieɪt ˈvɪʃiˌeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense vitiates, present participle vitiating, past tense , past participle vitiated
VERB 削弱…的效能;损害;使失效;使变质 If something is vitiated, its effectiveness is spoiled or weakened. 削弱…的效能;损害;使失效;使变质 [formal]

  • Strategic policy during the War was vitiated because of a sharp division between 'easterners' and 'westerners'. [be VERB-ed]

    由于“东方人”和“西方人”之间存在巨大的分歧，战争期间的战略方针没有得到切实的执行。

  • But this does not vitiate his scholarship. [VERB noun]

    但这不会威胁到他的奖学金。
