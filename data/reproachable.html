reproach
rɪproʊtʃ rɪˈproʊtʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense reproaches, present participle reproaching, past tense , past participle reproached
1 VERB 责备;斥责;指责;责怪 If you reproach someone, you say or show that you are disappointed, upset, or angry because they have done something wrong. 责备;斥责;指责;责怪

  • She is quick to reproach anyone who doesn't live up to her own high standards. [VERB noun]

    只要有人不符合她自己的高标准，她马上就会加以指责。

  • She had not even reproached him for breaking his promise. [VERB noun + for]

    她甚至没有责怪他违背诺言。

SYN blame, criticize, rebuke, reprimand  
2 N-VAR 责备;斥责;指责;责怪 If you look at or speak to someone with reproach, you show or say that you are disappointed, upset, or angry because they have done something wrong. 责备;斥责;指责;责怪

  • He looked at her with reproach.

    他用责备的目光看着她。

  • Women in public life must be beyond reproach.

    女性参与公众生活无可非议。

SYN censure, blame, abuse, contempt  
3 VERB 自责 If you reproach yourself, you think with regret about something you have done wrong. 自责

  • You've no reason to reproach yourself, no reason to feel shame. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    你没有理由自责，也没有理由感到惭愧。

  • We begin to reproach ourselves for not having been more careful. [V pron-refl + for]

    我们开始自责起来，怪自己不够小心。

4 N-SING [usu N to n] （某群体的）耻辱 If you consider someone's actions or behaviour to be a reproach to a group of people, you consider them to be harmful or insulting to that group. （某群体的）耻辱

  • The shootings and bombings were 'a scandal and reproach to all of us in Europe'.

    这些枪杀和轰炸事件是“我们所有欧洲人的丑闻和耻辱”。

SYN disgrace, shame, slight, stain
