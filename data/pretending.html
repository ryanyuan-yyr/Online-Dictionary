pretend
prɪtend priˈtɛnd prɪˈtɛnd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense pretends, present participle pretending, past tense , past participle pretended
1 VERB 假装；伪装；装作 If you pretend that something is the case, you act in a way that is intended to make people believe that it is the case, although in fact it is not. 假装；伪装；装作

  • I pretend that things are really okay when they're not. [VERB that]

    当情况不好的时候我假装一切顺利。

  • Sometimes the boy pretended to be asleep. [VERB to-infinitive]

    有时这个男孩会假装睡着了。

  • I had no option but to pretend ignorance. [VERB noun]

    我别无他法，只能假装不知道。

2 VERB （做游戏等时）假装，假扮，装扮 If children or adults pretend that they are doing something, they imagine that they are doing it, for example as part of a game. （做游戏等时）假装，假扮，装扮

  • She can sunbathe and pretend she's in Spain. [VERB that]

    她可以晒晒太阳，假装自己是在西班牙。

  • The children pretend to be different animals dancing to the music. [VERB to-infinitive]

    孩子们扮成不同的动物，随着音乐起舞。

SYN make believe, suppose, imagine, play  
3 VERB [with neg] 自诩；自称；自以为 If you do not pretend that something is the case, you do not claim that it is the case. 自诩；自称；自以为

  • We do not pretend that the past six years have been without problems for us. [VERB that]

    我们并不自夸在过去的6年中没有遇到任何问题。

  • Within this lecture I cannot pretend to deal adequately with dreams. [VERB to-infinitive]

    在这一次讲座中，我不敢自诩能对梦境作透彻的分析。
