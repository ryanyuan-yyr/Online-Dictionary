vindicate
vɪndɪkeɪt ˈvɪndəˌkeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense vindicates, present participle vindicating, past tense , past participle vindicated
VERB 证实;证明…正确;证明…无辜 If a person or their decisions, actions, or ideas are vindicated, they are proved to be correct, after people have said that they were wrong. 证实;证明…正确;证明…无辜 [formal] [Also VERB n]

  • The director said he had been vindicated by the experts' report. [be VERB-ed]

    主任说专家们的报告证明了他是正确的。

  • Ministers and officials are confident their decision will be vindicated. [be VERB-ed]

    部长们和官员们相信他们的决定会被证明是正确的。

SYN clear, acquit, exonerate, absolve  
vindication

  • He called the success a vindication of his party's free-market economic policy.

    他认为这次成功证明了他的政党所实行的自由市场经济政策是正确的。
