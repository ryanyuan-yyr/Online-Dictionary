materialize
mətɪəriəlaɪz məˈtɪriəlˌaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense materializes, present participle materializing, past tense , past participle materialized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 materialise
1 VERB 变为现实;实现;发生;出现 If a possible or expected event does not materialize, it does not happen. 变为现实;实现;发生;出现

  • A rebellion by radicals failed to materialize. [VERB]

    激进分子的叛乱没有得逞。

  • None of the anticipated difficulties materialized. [VERB]

    预想中的困难全都没有出现。

SYN occur, happen, take place, turn up  
2 VERB 突然出现;显形;现身 If a person or thing materializes, they suddenly appear, after they have been invisible or in another place. 突然出现;显形;现身

  • Tamsin materialized at her side, notebook at the ready. [VERB]

    塔姆辛从她身旁冒了出来，且备好了笔记本。

  • A moment or two later champagne in an ice-bucket materialized beside them. [VERB]

    片刻之后，他们身边出现了一只装着香槟的冰桶。

SYN appear, arrive, emerge, surface
