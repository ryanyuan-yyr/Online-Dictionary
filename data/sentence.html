sentence
sentəns ˈsɛntəns
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense sentences, present participle sentencing, past tense , past participle sentenced
1 N-COUNT 句子 A sentence is a group of words which, when they are written down, begin with a capital letter and end with a full stop, question mark, or exclamation mark. Most sentences contain a subject and a verb. 句子
2 N-VAR 判决;宣判;判刑 In a law court, a sentence is the punishment that a person receives after they have been found guilty of a crime. 判决;宣判;判刑

  • They are already serving prison sentences for their part in the assassination.

    他们因参与这起刺杀已经开始在监狱服刑了。

  • He was given a four-year sentence.

    他被判了4年徒刑。

  • The offences carry a maximum sentence of 10 years.

    这些罪行最高可判 10 年徒刑。

  • ...demands for tougher sentences.

    从严量刑的要求

  • The court is expected to pass sentence later today.

    预计法院今天晚些时候会宣布判决。

SYN punishment, prison sentence, jail sentence, prison term  
3See also:death sentence  life sentence  suspended sentence 
4 VERB 判决;宣判;判刑 When a judge sentences someone, he or she states in court what their punishment will be. 判决;宣判;判刑 [Also VERB n to-infinitive]

  • A military court sentenced him to death in his absence. [VERB noun + to]

    一所军事法庭在他缺席的情况下判处他死刑。

  • She was sentenced to nine years in prison. [VERB noun to noun]

    她被判 9 年徒刑。

  • He has admitted the charge and will be sentenced later. [be VERB-ed]

    他已经认罪，会在晚些时候宣判。

SYN condemn, doom
