unreadable
ʌnriːdəbəl ʌnˈridəbəl
1 ADJ-GRADED 没有可读性的；不值得一读的 If you use unreadable to describe a book or other piece of writing, you are criticizing it because it is very boring, complicated, or difficult to understand. 没有可读性的；不值得一读的 [disapproval]

  • For some this is the greatest novel in the world. For others it is unreadable.

    对于一些人来说这是世界上最伟大的小说，但对于另一些人来说，它不值得一读。

  • Most computer ads used to be loaded with technical specifications, virtually unreadable to the average consumer.

    以前的大多数电脑广告里都会有很多专业说明，普通消费者几乎看不懂。

SYN turgid, heavy going, badly written, dry as dust  
2 ADJ [usually verb-link ADJ] 难以辨认的；模糊不清的 If a piece of writing is unreadable, it is impossible to read because the letters are unclear, especially because it has been damaged in some way. 难以辨认的；模糊不清的

  • ...if contracts are unreadable because of the microscopic print.

    如果合同因为字体太小而无法阅读的话

  • I always cover my licence with sticky-backed plastic, but even then it can turn mouldy and unreadable.

    我总是在执照上贴一层塑膜，但即便如此它还是会发霉并致使没法辨认。

3 ADJ-GRADED （表情）难以捉摸的，难以猜测的 If someone's face or expression is unreadable, it is impossible to tell what they are thinking or feeling. （表情）难以捉摸的，难以猜测的 [literary]

  • He looked back at the woman for approval, but her face was unreadable.

    他回头看那个女人以求得到赞许，但是她的表情让人读不懂。
