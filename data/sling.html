sling
slɪŋ slɪŋ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense slings, present participle slinging, past tense , past participle slung
1 VERB 摔;扔;投;掷 If you sling something somewhere, you throw it there carelessly. 摔;扔;投;掷

  • Marla was recently seen slinging her shoes at Trump. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    最近有人看见马拉朝特朗普扔鞋子。

  • I saw him take off his anorak and sling it into the back seat. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    我看见他把带帽防寒服脱下来扔在了后面的座位上。

2 VERB (往肩膀上)挎;搭(在椅背上) If you sling something over your shoulder or over something such as a chair, you hang it there loosely. (往肩膀上)挎;搭(在椅背上)

  • She slung her coat over her desk chair. [VERB noun prep.]

    她把外套搭在书桌前的椅子上。

  • He had a small green rucksack slung over one shoulder. [V-ed prep]

    他单肩挎着一个绿色的帆布小背包。

  • ...a police informer with a rifle slung across his back. [VERB noun prep.]

    一名挎着步枪的警方线人

SYN throw, cast, toss, hurl  
3 VERB [usually passive] (在两点之间)吊起，悬挂 If a rope, blanket, or other object is slung between two points, someone has hung it loosely between them. (在两点之间)吊起，悬挂

  • ...two long poles with a blanket slung between them. [be VERB-ed prep.]

    两根长竿之间悬挂着一张毯子

  • We slept in hammocks slung beneath the roof. [be VERB-ed prep.]

    我们睡在悬在房顶下的吊床上。

SYN hang, swing, suspend, string  
4 N-COUNT (用以吊运物品的)吊索，吊链 A sling is an object made of ropes, straps, or cloth that is used for carrying things. (用以吊运物品的)吊索，吊链

  • They used slings of rope to lower us from one set of arms to another. [+ of]

    他们用吊索把我们从高处的一组吊臂上放到低处的另一组吊臂上。

5 N-COUNT (保护骨折或受伤的胳膊的)吊腕带，三角巾 A sling is a piece of cloth which supports someone's broken or injured arm and is tied round their neck. (保护骨折或受伤的胳膊的)吊腕带，三角巾

  • She was back at work with her arm in a sling.

    她胳膊上还吊着三角巾，就回来工作了。

SYN harness, support, bandage, strap  
6 N-COUNT (用于把婴儿背在背上或托在胸前的)背带，背巾 A baby sling is a device in which you carry a baby, either on your back or across your front. (用于把婴儿背在背上或托在胸前的)背带，背巾
7 See also: mud-slinging 
8 PHRASE 无妄之灾;飞来横祸;不愉快的事 Slings and arrows are unpleasant things that happen to you and that are not your fault. 无妄之灾;飞来横祸;不愉快的事 [written]

  • She had suffered her own share of slings and arrows in the quest for publicity.

    在追求成名的道路上，她经历了许许多多的坎坷不平。
