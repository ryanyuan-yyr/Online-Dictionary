laze
leɪz leɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense lazes, present participle lazing, past tense , past participle lazed
VERB 懒散；消闲 If you laze somewhere for a period of time, you relax and enjoy yourself, not doing any work or anything else that requires effort. 懒散；消闲

  • Fred lazed in an easy chair. [VERB]

    弗雷德优哉游哉地躺在安乐椅上。

  • They used the swimming-pool, rode, lazed in the deep shade of the oaks in the heat of the day. [VERB prep.]

    他们去游泳池游泳，去骑马，一天之中最热之时在橡树的浓阴下消磨时间。

SYN idle, lounge, hang around, loaf  
Laze around or laze about means the same as laze .laze around或laze about同laze

  • He went to Spain for nine months, to laze around and visit relations.

    他到西班牙呆了9个月，在那里消闲度日，探访亲戚。

  • I was happy enough to laze about on the beach.

    可以在沙滩上懒洋洋地躺着，我高兴极了。
