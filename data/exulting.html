exult
ɪgzʌlt ɪɡˈzʌlt ɛgˈzʌlt ɪgˈzʌlt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense exults, present participle exulting, past tense , past participle exulted
VERB 欢欣鼓舞；兴高采烈 If you exult in a triumph or success that you have had, you feel and show great happiness and pleasure because of it. 欢欣鼓舞；兴高采烈 [written]

  • He was exulting in a win at the show earlier that day. [VERB + in]

    他正在为当天早些时候在节目中胜出而欢欣鼓舞。

  • Some individual investors exulted at the record. [VERB + at]

    一些个人投资者为这一记录欢呼雀跃。

  • I exulted and wept for joy. [VERB]

    我欢天喜地，喜极而泣。

  • 'This is what I've longed for during my entire career,' Kendall exulted. [VERB with quote]

    “这是我整个职业生涯中梦寐以求的，”肯德尔欣喜地说。
