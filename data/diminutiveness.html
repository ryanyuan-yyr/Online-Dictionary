diminutive
dɪmɪnjʊtɪv dəˈmɪnjutɪv dəˈmɪnjətɪv
Word forms: plural diminutives
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 很小的;微小的 A diminutive person or object is very small. 很小的;微小的

  • She noticed a diminutive figure standing at the entrance.

    她注意到入口处站着一个小小的身影。

SYN small, little, tiny, minute  
2 N-COUNT 昵称;爱称 A diminutive is an informal form of a name. For example, 'Jim' and 'Jimmy' are diminutives of 'James'. 昵称;爱称
3 N-COUNT 指小词缀(表示亲昵或某东西很小的后缀，如 -ie 和 -ette) A diminutive is a suffix which is added to a word to show affection or to indicate that something is small. For example, '-ie' and '-ette' are diminutives, for example in 'doggie' and 'statuette'. 指小词缀(表示亲昵或某东西很小的后缀，如 -ie 和 -ette)
