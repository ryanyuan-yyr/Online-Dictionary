jangle
dʒæŋgəl ˈdʒæŋɡəl ˈdʒæŋgəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense jangles, present participle jangling, past tense , past participle jangled
1 V-ERG （使）发出刺耳声；（使）发出丁零当啷的声音 When objects strike against each other and make an unpleasant ringing noise, you can say that they jangle or are jangled. （使）发出刺耳声；（使）发出丁零当啷的声音

  • Her bead necklaces and bracelets jangled as she walked. [VERB]

    她的珠子项链和手镯在她走动时叮当作响。

  • Jane took out her keys and jangled them. [VERB noun]

    简掏出钥匙丁零当啷地晃着。

  • ...her jangling bracelets. [VERB-ing]

    她叮当作响的手镯

SYN rattle, ring, clash, clatter  
Jangle
is also a noun.

  • ...a jangle of bells.

    丁零当啷的铃声

2 V-ERG （神经）紧张；刺激，烦扰（神经） If your nerves are jangling or if something jangles them, you are very anxious. （神经）紧张；刺激，烦扰（神经）

  • Behind that quietness his nerves are jangling, he's in a terrible state. [VERB]

    表面看上去沉默不语，其实内心非常烦躁不安，他的情况糟透了。

  • The caffeine in coffee can jangle the nerves. [VERB noun]

    咖啡中的咖啡因会使神经紧张。

SYN disturb, worry, trouble, excite
