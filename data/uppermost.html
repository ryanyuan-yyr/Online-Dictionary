uppermost
ʌpəʳmoʊst ˈʌpərˌmoʊst
1 ADJ [usually ADJ n] 最高的；最上面的 The uppermost part of something is the part that is higher than the rest of it. The uppermost thing is the highest one of a group of things. 最高的；最上面的

  • John was on the uppermost floor of the three-storey gatehouse.

    约翰在三层门房的最高层。

  • The rain spattered on the uppermost leaves.

    雨溅落在最上面的树叶上。

SYN top, highest, topmost, upmost  
Uppermost is also an adverb.

  • She placed her hands palm uppermost in her lap.

    她把手掌心向上放在大腿上。

  • Lift the fish and carefully place it on a large board, flat side uppermost.

    拿起鱼，小心地放在一块平放的大板上。

2 ADJ [usually verb-link ADJ] 最重要的；最主要的 If something is uppermost in a particular situation, it is the most important thing in that situation. 最重要的；最主要的

  • The economy appears to be uppermost in people's minds.

    大家心里最关心的似乎是经济。

  • Protection of sites, habitats and landscapes is of uppermost priority.

    保护遗址、栖息地和风景区是重中之重。

SYN supreme, greatest, chief, leading
