economy
ɪkɒnəmi ɪˈkɑnəmi iˈkɑnəmi
Word forms: plural economies
1 N-COUNT （国家或地区的）经济，经济体制 An economy is the system according to which the money, industry, and trade of a country or region are organized. （国家或地区的）经济，经济体制

  • Zimbabwe boasts Africa's most industrialised economy.

    津巴布韦是非洲工业化程度最高的经济体。

2 N-COUNT 经济情况;经济状况 A country's economy is the wealth that it gets from business and industry. 经济情况;经济状况

  • The Japanese economy grew at an annual rate of more than 10 per cent.

    日本经济以年增长率超过10%的速度增长。

3 N-UNCOUNT 节约;节俭 Economy is the use of the minimum amount of money, time, or other resources needed to achieve something, so that nothing is wasted. 节约;节俭

  • ...improvements in the fuel economy of cars.

    汽车节油方面的改进

  • There was mostly silence. I have never known such economy with words.

    大部分时候都是沉默，我还从未见过如此惜字如金的情形。

4 N-COUNT [usually plural] 精打细算 If you make economies, you try to save money by not spending money on unnecessary things. 精打细算

  • They will make economies by hiring fewer part-time workers.

    他们将通过减少雇用兼职工人来节省开支。

5 ADJ [ADJ n] 经济的;便宜的 Economy services such as travel are cheap and have no luxuries or extras. →see: economy class  经济的;便宜的
6 ADJ [ADJ n] （比标准包装大的）经济装的 Economy is used to describe large packs of goods which are cheaper than normal sized packs. （比标准包装大的）经济装的

  • ...an economy pack containing 150 assorted screws.

    内含150枚各种螺丝的经济装

7 PHRASE 假节约;看似省钱的做法 If you describe an attempt to save money as a false economy, you mean that you have not saved any money as you will have to spend a lot more later. 假节约;看似省钱的做法

  • A cheap bed can be a false economy.

    便宜的床看似省钱，其实可能不然。

  • It seems a false economy to me to cut down on libraries.

    在我看来，减少图书馆的数量并不是真正的节约。
