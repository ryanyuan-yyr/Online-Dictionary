countenance
kaʊntɪnəns ˈkaʊntənəns
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense countenances, present participle countenancing, past tense , past participle countenanced
1 VERB 支持;赞同;同意 If someone will not countenance something, they do not agree with it and will not allow it to happen. 支持;赞同;同意 [formal]

  • Jake would not countenance Janis's marrying while still a student. [VERB noun]

    杰克不会同意贾尼斯还在上学就结婚。

  • ...the military men who refused to countenance the overthrow of the president. [VERB noun]

    拒绝支持推翻总统的军人们

SYN tolerate, sanction, endorse, condone  
2 N-COUNT 脸庞;面容 Someone's countenance is their face. 脸庞;面容 [literary]

  • He met each inquiry with an impassive countenance.

    他面无表情地接受每一个盘问。

SYN face, features, expression, look
