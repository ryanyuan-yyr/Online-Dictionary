enrich
ɪnrɪtʃ ɛnˈrɪtʃ ɪnˈrɪtʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense enriches, present participle enriching, past tense , past participle enriched
1 VERB 充实;使丰富 To enrich something means to improve its quality, usually by adding something to it. 充实;使丰富

  • An extended family enriches life in many ways. [VERB noun]

    大家庭在很多方面会使生活更加丰富多彩。

  • It is important to enrich the soil prior to planting. [VERB noun]

    在种植前给土壤增肥很重要。

-enriched

  • ...nutrient-enriched water.

    营养液

2 VERB 使富有;使富余 To enrich someone means to increase the amount of money that they have. 使富有;使富余

  • He will drain, rather than enrich, the country. [VERB noun]

    他会耗尽而不是增加该国的财富。

  • Those who know how to use the system can enrich themselves at the expense of the very people who work for those companies. [VERB noun]

    那些懂得如何利用现行体制的人使自己富有起来，而代价是牺牲真正为那些公司工作的人。

SYN make rich, make wealthy, make affluent, make prosperous  
3 VERB 浓缩（核燃料） To enrich a nuclear fuel such as uranium means to increase the number of atoms of a particular kind in it, so that it can be used to produce more energy or a greater explosion. 浓缩（核燃料） [technical]

  • It was actually used for enriching uranium to weapons-grade levels. [VERB noun]

    实际上，它是用来将铀浓缩以制造武器。
