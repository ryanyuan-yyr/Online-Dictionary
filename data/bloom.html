bloom
bluːm blum
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense blooms, present participle blooming, past tense , past participle bloomed
1 N-COUNT 花；花朵 A bloom is the flower on a plant. 花；花朵 [literary, technical]

  • ...the sweet fragrance of the white blooms.

    白色花朵的芳香

  • Harry carefully picked the bloom.

    哈里小心翼翼地摘下那朵花。

SYN flower, bud, blossom  
2 PHRASE 正开花的；花朵绽放的 A plant or tree that is in bloom has flowers on it. 正开花的；花朵绽放的

  • ...a pink climbing rose in full bloom.

    盛开的粉色藤蔓玫瑰

  • ...the sweet smell of the blackberry in bloom.

    绽放的黑莓花的芬芳

3 VERB 开花；（花朵）绽放 When a plant or tree blooms, it produces flowers. When a flower blooms, it opens. 开花；（花朵）绽放

  • This plant blooms between May and June. [VERB]

    这种植物在5月至6月间开花。

SYN flower, blossom, open, bud  
-blooming

  • ...the scent of night-blooming flowers.

    夜间开放的花的香味

4 VERB 繁荣；兴盛；茁壮成长；变漂亮 If someone or something blooms, they develop good, attractive, or successful qualities. 繁荣；兴盛；茁壮成长；变漂亮

  • Not many economies bloomed in 1990, least of all gold exporters like Australia. [VERB]

    1990年经济繁荣的国家并不多，尤其是像澳大利亚这样的黄金出口国。

  • She bloomed into an utterly beautiful creature. [VERB + into]

    她出落得亭亭玉立。

SYN succeed, flourish, thrive, prosper  
5 N-UNCOUNT [oft a NOUN] （肤色的）红润，健康的光彩；容光 If something such as someone's skin has a bloom, it has a fresh and healthy appearance. （肤色的）红润，健康的光彩；容光

  • The skin loses its youthful bloom.

    皮肤失去了年轻时的光泽。

SYN glow, flush, blush, freshness
