deed
diːd did
Word forms: plural deeds
1 N-COUNT （做的）事情;作为;（尤指）事迹，功绩，劣迹 A deed is something that is done, especially something that is very good or very bad. （做的）事情;作为;（尤指）事迹，功绩，劣迹 [literary]

  • His heroic deeds were celebrated in every corner of India.

    他的英勇事迹在印度各地广为传颂。

  • ...the warm feeling one gets from doing a good deed.

    做了好事后心头暖暖的感觉

  • The perpetrators of this evil deed must be brought to justice.

    必须将参与这一恶行的凶手绳之以法。

SYN action, act, performance, achievement  
2 N-COUNT 契约;证书;(尤指)地契，房契 A deed is a document containing the terms of an agreement, especially an agreement concerning the ownership of land or a building. 契约;证书;(尤指)地契，房契 [law]

  • He asked if I had the deeds to his father's property.

    他问我是否有他父亲房产的房契。

SYN document, title, contract, title deed
