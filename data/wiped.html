wipe
waɪp waɪp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense wipes, present participle wiping, past tense , past participle wiped
1 VERB 拭;擦;揩 If you wipe something, you rub its surface to remove dirt or liquid from it. 拭;擦;揩

  • I'll just wipe the table. [VERB noun]

    我就擦擦桌子。

  • When he had finished washing he began to wipe the basin clean. [VERB noun with adj.ive]

    他清洗完后开始把洗脸池擦拭干净。

  • Lainey wiped her hands on the towel. [VERB noun + on]

    莱内用毛巾把手擦干。

SYN clean, dry, polish, brush  
Wipe is also a noun.

  • She gave the table a quick wipe and disappeared behind the counter.

    她很快地擦了擦桌子，然后就消失到柜台后面去了。

2 VERB 擦去;擦掉 If you wipe dirt or liquid from something, you remove it, for example by using a cloth or your hand. 擦去;擦掉

  • Gleb wiped the sweat from his face. [VERB noun prep.]

    格莱布擦去脸上的汗水。

  • He shook his head and wiped his tears with a tissue. [VERB noun]

    他摇摇头，用纸巾擦掉眼泪。

SYN erase, remove, take off, get rid of  
3 N-COUNT （一次性）湿巾 A wipe is a small moist cloth for cleaning things and is designed to be used only once. （一次性）湿巾

  • ...antiseptic wipes.

    消毒湿巾

SYN rub, clean, polish, brush  
4 PHRASE [V and Ns inflect] 让…笑容顿失;使…兴致全无;使…丧失优势 If you say that something wipes the smile off someone's face, you mean that it suddenly spoils their enjoyment or removes an advantage that they had and that you are pleased about it. 让…笑容顿失;使…兴致全无;使…丧失优势 [informal]

  • Tony Holmes wiped the smile off the faces of his rivals with a solo 30-second win.

    托尼·福尔摩斯以30秒的领先优势获得了这一赛段的个人胜利，使对手再也笑不起来。
