desensitize
diːsensɪtaɪz diˈsɛnsəˌtaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense desensitizes, present participle desensitizing, past tense , past participle desensitized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 desensitise
VERB 使不敏感;使麻木;使无情 To desensitize someone to things such as pain, anxiety, or other people's suffering, means to cause them to react less strongly to them. 使不敏感;使麻木;使无情 [Also VERB n]

  • ...the language that is used to desensitize us to the terrible reality of war. [VERB noun + to]

    旨在让我们对战争的残酷现实无动于衷的语言

  • Your immune system has been desensitized because it has become used to the substance. [be VERB-ed]

    你的免疫系统已经因习惯了这种物质而不再敏感。
