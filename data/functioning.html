function
fʌŋkʃən ˈfʌŋkʃən
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense functions, present participle functioning, past tense functioned
1 N-COUNT 功能;作用;职责 The function of something or someone is the useful thing that they do or are intended to do. 功能;作用;职责

  • The main function of the merchant banks is to raise capital for industry. [+ of]

    商业银行的主要职能是为产业融资。

SYN purpose, business, job, concern  
2 VERB 工作;运转;运行 If a machine or system is functioning, it is working or operating. 工作;运转;运行

  • The authorities say the prison is now functioning normally. [VERB]

    当局称监狱现在运转正常。

  • Conservation programs cannot function without local support. [VERB]

    没有地方的支持，自然环境保护项目就无法运作。

SYN work, run, operate, perform  
3 VERB 发挥功能;起作用;行使职责 If someone or something functions as a particular thing, they do the work or fulfil the purpose of that thing. 发挥功能;起作用;行使职责

  • On weekdays, one third of the room functions as workspace. [VERB + as]

    在工作日，房间的1/3用作办公区域。

SYN act, serve, operate, perform  
4 N-COUNT (计算机的)功能(指执行的一系列操作) A function is a series of operations that a computer performs, for example when a single key or a combination of keys is pressed. (计算机的)功能(指执行的一系列操作)
5 N-COUNT [usually singular] 应变量;随他物变化而变化的事物 If you say that one thing is a function of another, you mean that its amount or nature depends on the other thing. 应变量;随他物变化而变化的事物 [formal]

  • Investment is a function of the interest rate. [+ of]

    投资随着利率的变化而变。

6 N-COUNT (正式的)宴会，大型聚会 A function is a large formal dinner or party. (正式的)宴会，大型聚会
