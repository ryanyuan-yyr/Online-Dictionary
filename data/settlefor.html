settle for
PHRASAL VERB 选择;(尤指)勉强接受,将就 If you settle for something, you choose or accept it, especially when it is not what you really want but there is nothing else available. 选择;(尤指)勉强接受,将就

  • Virginia was a perfectionist. She was just not prepared to settle for anything mediocre. [VERB PREP. noun]

    弗吉尼娅是个完美主义者，她根本不愿意拿任何普普通通毫无特色的东西将就。

  • England will have to settle for third or fourth place. [VERB PREP. noun]

    英格兰将只好接受第三或第四的排名。

SYN accept, take, stand for, tolerate
