indignation
ɪndɪgneɪʃən ɪnˈdɪɡnəns ˌɪndɪgˈneɪʃən
N-UNCOUNT 愤怒;愤慨;义愤 Indignation is the feeling of shock and anger which you have when you think that something is unjust or unfair. 愤怒;愤慨;义愤

  • She was filled with indignation at the conditions under which miners were forced to work. [+ at]

    矿工们被迫在如此恶劣的条件下工作,她感到异常愤怒。

  • No wonder he could hardly contain his indignation.

    难怪他几乎抑制不住自己的愤怒。

SYN resentment, anger, rage, fury
