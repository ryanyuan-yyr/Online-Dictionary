gulp
gʌlp gʌlp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense gulps, present participle gulping, past tense , past participle gulped
1 VERB 吞食；大口吃；狼吞虎咽 If you gulp something, you eat or drink it very quickly by swallowing large quantities of it at once. 吞食；大口吃；狼吞虎咽

  • She quickly gulped her tea. [VERB noun]

    她将茶一饮而尽。

SYN swallow, bolt, devour, gobble  
2 VERB （因紧张或兴奋）倒吸气 If you gulp, you swallow air, often making a noise in your throat as you do so, because you are nervous or excited. （因紧张或兴奋）倒吸气 [written]

  • I gulped, and then proceeded to tell her the whole story. [VERB]

    我倒吸了一口气，接着把事情的原委都讲给她听。

  • 'I'm sorry,' he gulped. [VERB with quote]

    “很抱歉，”他倒吸了一口气说。

3 VERB 大口大口地吸（气） If you gulp air, you breathe in a large amount of air quickly through your mouth. 大口大口地吸（气）

  • She gulped air into her lungs. [VERB noun + into]

    她大口大口地吸着气。

  • He slumped back, gulping for air. [VERB + for]

    他一屁股坐了回去，大口地喘着气。

SYN gasp, swallow, choke  
4 N-COUNT 吞咽的量；一大口 A gulp of air, food, or drink, is a large amount of it that you swallow at once. 吞咽的量；一大口

  • I took in a large gulp of air. [+ of]

    我深吸了一大口气。

  • When his whisky came he drank half of it in one gulp.

    威士忌端上来后，他一口气就喝掉了一半。

SYN swallow, draught, mouthful, swig [informal]  
Phrasal verbs:
gulp down
