launder
lɔːndəʳ ˈlɔndər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense launders, present participle laundering, past tense , past participle laundered
1 VERB 洗熨（衣物） When you launder clothes, sheets, and towels, you wash and iron them. 洗熨（衣物） [old-fashioned]

  • How many guests who expect clean towels every day in an hotel launder their own every day at home?. [VERB noun]

    希望在酒店里天天有干净毛巾用的客人，有多少会在家里每天洗烫自己的毛巾呢？

  • She wore a freshly laundered and starched white shirt. [VERB-ed]

    她穿一件刚刚浆洗熨烫过的白色衬衣。

SYN wash, clean, dry-clean, tub  
2 VERB 洗（钱） To launder money that has been obtained illegally means to process it through a legitimate business or to send it abroad to a foreign bank, so that when it comes back nobody knows that it was illegally obtained. 洗（钱）

  • The House voted today to crack down on banks that launder drug money. [VERB noun]

    众议院今天表决打击为毒资洗钱的银行。

SYN process, doctor, manipulate
