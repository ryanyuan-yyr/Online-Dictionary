freely
friːli
1 ADV-GRADED [ADVERB after verb, ADVERB adjective] 经常地;大量地 Freely means many times or in large quantities. 经常地;大量地

  • We have referred freely to his ideas.

    我们频繁地提到了他的观点。

  • George was spending very freely.

    乔治花钱大手大脚。

  • ...the United States, where consumer goods are freely available.

    消费品供应充足的美国

SYN abundantly, liberally, lavishly, like water  
2 ADV-GRADED [ADVERB after verb] 坦率地;无顾忌地 If you can talk freely, you can talk without needing to be careful about what you say. 坦率地;无顾忌地

  • She wondered whether he had someone to whom he could talk freely.

    她不知道他是否有可以倾心交谈的对象。

SYN openly, frankly, plainly, candidly  
3 ADV-GRADED [ADVERB with verb] 自愿地;心甘情愿地 If someone gives or does something freely, they give or do it willingly, without being ordered or forced to do it. 自愿地;心甘情愿地

  • Danny shared his knowledge freely with anyone interested.

    丹尼十分乐意地和所有感兴趣的人分享他的知识。

  • Williams freely admits he lives for racing.

    威廉斯坦承他为赛车而活。

SYN willingly, readily, voluntarily, spontaneously  
4 ADV-GRADED [ADVERB after verb] 无阻碍地;顺畅地 If something or someone moves freely, they move easily and smoothly, without any obstacles or resistance. 无阻碍地;顺畅地

  • The clay court was slippery and he was unable to move freely.

    红土网球场很滑，他不能自如地移动。

  • You must allow the clubhead to swing freely.

    必须让杆头一气呵成地挥出。

SYN easily, cleanly, loosely, smoothly
