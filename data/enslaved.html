enslave
ɪnsleɪv ɛnˈsleɪv ɪnˈsleɪv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense enslaves, present participle enslaving, past tense , past participle enslaved
1 VERB 使成为奴隶;奴役 To enslave someone means to make them into a slave. 使成为奴隶;奴役

  • They've been enslaved and had to do what they were told. [be VERB-ed]

    他们已经沦为奴隶，不得不听从命令做事。

  • I'd die myself before I'd let anyone enslave your folk ever again. [VERB noun]

    我死也不会让任何人再次奴役你们。

  • George was born to an enslaved African mother. [VERB-ed]

    乔治的母亲是个非洲奴隶。

2 VERB 束缚;约束;使受控制 To enslave a person or society means to trap them in a situation from which they cannot escape. 束缚;约束;使受控制 [Also VERB n + to]

  • ...the various cultures, cults and religions that have enslaved human beings for untold years. [VERB noun]

    束缚人类已久的各种文化、异教和宗教

  • It would be a tragedy if both sexes were enslaved to the god of work. [be VERB-ed + to]

    如果男人和女人都被工作所束缚，那将会是一场悲剧。
