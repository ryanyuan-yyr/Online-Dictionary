ignite
ɪgnaɪt ɪgˈnaɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense ignites, present participle igniting, past tense , past participle ignited
1 V-ERG 点燃;(使)燃烧;着火 When you ignite something or when it ignites, it starts burning or explodes. 点燃;(使)燃烧;着火

  • The bombs ignited a fire which destroyed some 60 houses. [VERB noun]

    炸弹引发的大火烧毁了大约 60 栋房屋。

  • The blasts were caused by pockets of methane gas that ignited. [VERB]

    爆炸是由甲烷气囊被引燃造成的。

SYN set fire to, light, set alight, torch  
2 VERB 激起;使激动 If something or someone ignites your feelings, they cause you to have very strong feelings about something. 激起;使激动 [literary]

  • There was one teacher who really ignited my interest in words. [VERB noun]

    曾经有一位老师真正激起了我对文字的兴趣。

  • The recent fighting in the area could ignite regional passions far beyond the borders. [VERB noun]

    最近该地区的战斗可能激发边境以外整个地区的激动情绪。
