liken
laɪkən ˈlaɪkən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense likens, present participle likening, past tense , past participle likened
VERB 把…比作；把…比拟为 If you liken one thing or person to another thing or person, you say that they are similar. 把…比作；把…比拟为

  • She likens marriage to slavery. [V n to n/-ing]

    她把婚姻比作奴役。

  • The pain is often likened to being drilled through the side of the head. [be VERB-ed + to]

    这种疼痛常被比作钻头钻入头部的感觉。

SYN compare, match, relate, parallel
