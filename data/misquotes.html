misquote
mɪskwoʊt mɪsˈkwoʊt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense misquotes, present participle misquoting, past tense , past participle misquoted
VERB 错误地引用；误引 If someone is misquoted, something that they have said or written is repeated incorrectly. 错误地引用；误引

  • He claimed that he had been misquoted and he threatened to sue the magazine for libel. [be VERB-ed]

    他声称自己的话被错误地引用，并威胁说要以诽谤罪起诉这家杂志。

  • The case was brought by a psychoanalyst who says a journalist misquoted him in a series of magazine articles. [VERB noun]

    这起官司是由一位心理分析学家起诉的，他声称一名记者发表在杂志上面的一系列文章里错误地引用了他的话。

SYN misrepresent, twist, distort, pervert
