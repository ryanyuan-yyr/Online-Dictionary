collision course
kəˈlɪʒən kɔːs
1 N-SING [usu on a N] 有可能发生严重冲突 If two or more people or things are on a collision course, there is likely to be a sudden and violent disagreement between them. 有可能发生严重冲突

  • The two communities are now on a collision course.

    这两个社区之间现在有可能会发生冲突。

  • Britain's universities are set on a collision course with the government. [+ with]

    英国的大学势必将与政府发生冲突。

2 N-SING [usu on a N] 可能发生碰撞 If two or more people or things are on a collision course, they are likely to meet and crash into each other violently. 可能发生碰撞

  • There is an asteroid on a collision course with the Earth. [+ with]

    有一颗小行星可能会与地球发生碰撞。
