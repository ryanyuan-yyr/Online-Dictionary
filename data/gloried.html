glory
glɔːri ˈglɔri
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense glories, present participle glorying, past tense , past participle gloried
1 N-UNCOUNT 光荣；荣耀 Glory is the fame and admiration that you gain by doing something impressive. 光荣；荣耀

  • Walsham had his moment of glory when he won a 20km race.

    沃尔沙姆夺得20公里比赛冠军的那一刻无比荣耀。

  • ...we were still basking in the glory of our Championship win.

    我们依然沉浸在夺得锦标赛冠军的荣耀中。

SYN honour, praise, fame, celebrity  
2 N-PLURAL 辉煌时刻；巅峰 A person's glories are the occasions when they have done something people greatly admire which makes them famous. 辉煌时刻；巅峰

  • The album sees them reliving past glories but not really breaking any new ground.

    这张专辑使他们重温了昔日的辉煌，但实际上没有任何新的突破。

  • ...the military glories of Frederick the Great.

    腓特烈大帝军事上的巅峰时刻

3 N-UNCOUNT [with poss] 壮丽；壮观 The glory of something is its great beauty or impressive nature. 壮丽；壮观

  • The glory of the idea blossomed in his mind. [+ of]

    在他的脑海中这一想法变得越来越宏大。

4 N-COUNT [usually plural] 引以为荣的事物 The glories of a culture or place are the things that people admire most about it. 引以为荣的事物

  • ...a tour of Florence, to enjoy the artistic glories of the Italian Renaissance. [+ of]

    领略意大利文艺复兴时期伟大艺术成就的佛罗伦萨之旅

  • One of the glories of the island has always been its bird population.

    鸟儿的数量一直是该岛的一大骄傲。

5 VERB 因…而欣喜；为…而喜悦 If you glory in a situation or activity, you enjoy it very much. 因…而欣喜；为…而喜悦

  • The workers were glorying in their new-found freedom. [VERB + in]

    工人们因才获得的自由而无比喜悦。

  • He does not glory in his past successes and looks forward to achieving more. [VERB in noun]

    他没有陶醉于过去的成功，而是期望取得更大的成就。

SYN triumph, boast, relish, revel  
6 PHRASE 一举成名 If you go out in a blaze of glory, you do something very dramatic at the end of your career or your life which makes you famous. 一举成名

  • I am never going back to prison. I am going to make national news headlines and go out in a blaze of glory.

    我将永远告别监狱，我会成为全国报纸的头条新闻，从此扬名天下。
