turn in
1 PHRASAL VERB 上床睡觉 When you turn in, you go to bed. 上床睡觉 [informal]

  • Would you like some tea before you turn in? [VERB PREP.]

    你睡前想喝杯茶吗？

SYN go to bed, go to sleep, hit the sack [slang] , retire for the night  
2 PHRASAL VERB 把（犯罪嫌疑人）交给警方;告发;检举;自首 If you turn someone in, you take them to the police or tell the police where they are because they are suspected of committing a crime. If you turn yourself in, you go to the police because you have been involved in a crime. 把（犯罪嫌疑人）交给警方;告发;检举;自首

  • He has been given until noon today to turn himself in to authorities. [VERB noun PREP. + to]

    他被要求在今天中午前向当局投案自首。

  • There would be strong incentives to turn someone in. [VERB noun PREP.]

    向警方举报某人将获重赏。

  • He seems to be making all the arrangements necessary so that he can continue trafficking in drugs when he turns himself in… [VERB noun PREP.]

    他似乎正在做好各种必要安排，以便他自首后还能继续贩毒。

  • I might today hesitate to turn in a burglar. [VERB PREP. noun]

    现在让我把入室窃贼送交警方，我可能会有所顾虑。

SYN hand someone over, denounce, inform on, blow the whistle on [informal]  
3 PHRASAL VERB 上交;提交;交付 When you turn in a completed piece of work, especially written work, you give it to the person who asked you to do it. 上交;提交;交付

  • Now we wait for them to turn in their essays. [VERB PREP. noun]

    现在我们等着他们交上作文。

  • I want everybody to turn a report in. [VERB noun PREP.]

    我希望每个人都上交一份报告。

4 PHRASAL VERB 交还;返还 If you turn something in, you return it to the place or person you borrowed it from. 交还;返还 [mainly US] [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • I went back to the station-house to turn in my badge and gun. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我回到警局交还警徽和佩枪。

  • The official showed up to tell her to turn in her library books. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    那名管理人员出面让她返还所借图书馆的书。

SYN hand in, return, deliver, give back
