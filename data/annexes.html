annex
æneks əˈnɛks æˈnɛks ˈænˌɛks
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense annexes, present participle annexing, past tense , past participle annexed
VERB 并吞，占领（国家或地域） If a country annexes another country or an area of land, it seizes it and takes control of it. 并吞，占领（国家或地域）

  • Rome annexed the Nabatean kingdom in 106 AD. [VERB noun]

    公元106年罗马吞并了纳巴泰王国。

  • Hitler was determined to annex Austria to Germany. [VERB noun + to]

    希特勒决定将奥地利并入德国。

SYN seize, take over, appropriate, acquire  
annexation

  • ...Indonesia's annexation of East Timor.

    印度尼西亚对东帝汶的吞并

annexe æneks ˈænˌɛks
(also annex)Word forms: plural annexes
1 N-COUNT 附属建筑物;侧楼;配楼 An annexe is a building which is joined to or is next to a larger main building. 附属建筑物;侧楼;配楼

  • ...setting up a museum in an annexe to the theatre.

    在剧院的侧楼中建一个博物馆

SYN extension, wing, ell, supplementary building  
2 N-COUNT 附件；附录 An annexe to a document is a section added to it at the end. 附件；附录

  • The Annex lists and discusses eight titles.

    附录列举并讨论了8本书。

SYN appendix, addition, supplement, attachment
