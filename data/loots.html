loot
luːt lut
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense loots, present participle looting, past tense , past participle looted
1 VERB （战争、暴乱等时）洗劫，劫掠（商店或民宅） If people loot shops or houses, they steal things from them, for example during a war or riot. （战争、暴乱等时）洗劫，劫掠（商店或民宅）

  • The trouble began when gangs began breaking windows and looting shops. [VERB noun]

    一些团伙开始破窗抢劫商店时发生了骚乱。

  • There have been reports of youths taking advantage of the general confusion to loot and steal. [VERB]

    有报道说一些年轻人乘局势一片混乱之际抢劫盗窃。

SYN plunder, rob, raid, sack  
looting

  • In the country's largest cities there has been rioting and looting.

    该国的大城市有暴乱和抢劫发生。

2 VERB （战争、暴乱等时）掠夺，抢劫（财物） If someone loots things, they steal them, for example during a war or riot. （战争、暴乱等时）掠夺，抢劫（财物）

  • The town has been plagued by armed thugs who have looted food supplies and terrorized the population. [VERB noun]

    武装暴徒抢掠食物、恐吓群众，该镇居民深受其苦。

  • ...lists of looted material ranging from tanks to office fittings. [VERB-ed]

    被劫物资清单上，从油桶到办公设施都有。

3 N-UNCOUNT （盗抢而来的）贼赃，赃物 Loot is stolen money or goods. （盗抢而来的）贼赃，赃物 [informal]

  • Most criminals steal in order to sell their loot for cash on the black market.

    罪犯盗窃大多是为了把赃物在黑市上卖掉，换取现金。

SYN plunder, goods, prize, haul
