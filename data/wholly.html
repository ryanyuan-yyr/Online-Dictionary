wholly
hoʊlli ˈhəʊllɪ ˈhoʊlli ˈhoʊli
ADV [ADVERB adjective] 完全地;完整地 You use wholly to emphasize the extent or degree to which something is the case. 完全地;完整地 [emphasis]

  • While the two are only days apart in age they seem to belong to wholly different generations.

    尽管这两人年龄上只相差几天，他们却好像是属于完全不同时代的人。

  • For urban areas this approach was wholly inadequate.

    对于市区来说，这种办法根本不会奏效。

  • The accusation is wholly without foundation.

    这项指控完全没有依据。

SYN completely, totally, perfectly, fully
