collective
kəlektɪv kəˈlɛktɪv
Word forms: plural collectives
1 ADJ [ADJ n] 集体的;共同的 Collective actions, situations, or feelings involve or are shared by every member of a group of people. 集体的;共同的

  • It was a collective decision.

    这是集体的决定。

  • The country's politicians are already heaving a collective sigh of relief.

    该国的政界人士均已松了口气。

SYN joint, united, shared, common  
collectively

  • They collectively decided to recognize the changed situation.

    他们集体决定承认形势已经发生改变。

  • The Cabinet is collectively responsible for policy.

    内阁集体对政策负责。

2 ADJ [ADJ n] 总的;集合的 A collective amount of something is the total obtained by adding together the amounts that each person or thing in a group has. 总的;集合的

  • Their collective volume wasn't very large.

    他们的总量不是太大。

SYN combined, aggregate, composite, cumulative  
collectively

  • In 1968 the states collectively spent $2 billion on it.

    1968年，各州在这上面总共花费了20亿美元。

3 ADJ [ADJ n] 集合的;总括的 The collective term for two or more types of thing is a general word or expression which refers to all of them. 集合的;总括的

  • Social science is a collective name, covering a series of individual sciences.

    社会科学是一个统称，涵盖一系列的独立学科。

collectively

  • ...other sorts of cells (known collectively as white corpuscles).

    其他类型的细胞(统称为白细胞)

4 N-COUNT 集体企业;合作农场 A collective is a business or farm which is run, and often owned, by a group of people who take an equal share of any profits. 集体企业;合作农场 [business]

  • He will see that he is participating in all the decisions of the collective.

    他会确保自己参与集体企业的各项决策。
