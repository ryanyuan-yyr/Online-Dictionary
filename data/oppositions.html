opposition
ɒpəzɪʃən ˌɑpəˈzɪʃən
Word forms: plural oppositions
1 N-UNCOUNT （强烈的）反对，不赞成 Opposition is strong, angry, or violent disagreement and disapproval. （强烈的）反对，不赞成

  • The government is facing a new wave of opposition in the form of a student strike.

    政府面临着学生通过罢课掀起的新一轮反对浪潮。

  • Much of the opposition to this plan has come from the media. [+ to]

    该计划的主要反对声音来自媒体。

SYN hostility, resistance, resentment, disapproval  
2 N-COUNT-COLL [with singular or plural verb, usually singular, oft NOUN n] （与政府意见相左的）反对党，在野党，反对派 The opposition is the political parties or groups that are opposed to a government. （与政府意见相左的）反对党，在野党，反对派

  • The main opposition parties boycotted the election, saying it would not be conducted fairly.

    主要反对党联合抵制这次选举，认为该选举将缺乏公正性。

3 N-COUNT-COLL [with singular or plural verb] [usually singular] （议会或立法机构中的）反对党，在野党，反对派 In a country's parliament or legislature, the opposition refers to the politicians or political parties that form part of the parliament or legislature, but are not the government. （议会或立法机构中的）反对党，在野党，反对派

  • ...the Leader of the Opposition.

    反对党领袖

4 N-SING-COLL [with singular or plural verb] （体育比赛中的）对手，竞争者，竞争队伍 The opposition is the person or team you are competing against in a sports event. （体育比赛中的）对手，竞争者，竞争队伍

  • Poland provide the opposition for the Scots' last warm-up match at home. [+ for]

    苏格兰队在国内最后一场热身赛的对手是波兰队。
