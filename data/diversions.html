diversion
daɪvɜːʳʃən dɪvɜːrʒən dəˈvɜrʒən dəˈvɜrʃən daɪˈvɜrʒən daɪˈvɜrʃən
Word forms: plural diversions
1 N-COUNT 转移注意力的行为;令人分心的事情 A diversion is an action or event that attracts your attention away from what you are doing or concentrating on. 转移注意力的行为;令人分心的事情

  • The robbers threw smoke bombs to create a diversion.

    劫匪扔烟幕弹来转移人们的视线。

  • The whole argument is a diversion.

    这场辩论分散了人们的注意力。

SYN distraction, deviation, deflection, digression  
2 N-COUNT 消遣;娱乐 A diversion is an activity that you do for pleasure. 消遣;娱乐 [formal]

  • Finger painting is very messy but an excellent diversion.

    用手指画画是很脏，但却是一项极好的消遣。

SYN pastime, play, game, sport  
3 N-COUNT 临时绕行路;临时支路 A diversion is a special route arranged for traffic to follow when the normal route cannot be used. 临时绕行路;临时支路 [British]

  • They turned back because of traffic diversions.

    因为交通要临时绕行，他们掉头了。

SYN detour, deviation, circuitous route, roundabout way  
in AM, use 美国英语用 detour
4 N-UNCOUNT 转向;转移;偏离 The diversion of something involves changing its course or destination. 转向;转移;偏离

  • ...the illegal diversion of profits from secret arms sales. [+ of]

    秘密军火交易所获利润的非法转移

  • ...the diversion of a ship to Lebanon with $8m worth of aluminium on board.

    前往黎巴嫩的装有价值800万美元铝的船只改换航道
