page
peɪdʒ peɪdʒ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense pages, present participle paging, past tense , past participle paged
1 N-COUNT （书、杂志、报纸等的）页，面，版 A page is one side of one of the pieces of paper in a book, magazine, or newspaper. Each page usually has a number printed at the top or bottom. （书、杂志、报纸等的）页，面，版

  • Where's your book? Take it out and turn to page 4.

    你的书呢？拿出来，翻到第4页。

  • ...the front page of the Guardian. [+ of]

    《卫报》的头版

  • ...1,400 pages of top-secret information. [+ of]

    1,400页绝密资料

2 N-COUNT （书、杂志、报纸等的）纸页 The pages of a book, magazine, or newspaper are the pieces of paper it consists of. （书、杂志、报纸等的）纸页

  • He turned the pages of his notebook. [+ of]

    他翻着笔记本。

  • Over the page you can read all about the six great books on offer.

    在下一页上，你可以看到有关6本在售好书的全部信息。

3 N-COUNT （历史的）一页（指重大事件或重要时期） You can refer to an important event or period of time as a page of history. （历史的）一页（指重大事件或重要时期） [literary]

  • ...a new page in the country's political history. [+ in]

    该国政治史上崭新的一页

SYN period, chapter, phase, era  
4 VERB （在公共场合通过广播）呼叫 If someone who is in a public place is paged, they receive a message, often over a speaker, telling them that someone is trying to contact them. （在公共场合通过广播）呼叫

  • He was paged repeatedly as the flight was boarding. [be VERB-ed]

    航班登机时，广播里反复呼叫他。

  • I'll have them paged and tell them you're here. [have noun VERB-ed]

    我会叫人呼他们，告诉他们你在这儿。

SYN call, seek, summon, call out for  
5 N-COUNT （婚礼上新娘的）男伴童，小男傧相 A page is a small boy who accompanies the bride at a wedding. （婚礼上新娘的）男伴童，小男傧相 [mainly US]
in BRIT, use 英国英语用 pageboy
6 N-COUNT （美国国会或州立法会的）青年助理 A page is a young person who takes messages or does small jobs for members of the United States Congress or state legislatures. （美国国会或州立法会的）青年助理 [US]
7 N-COUNT [oft poss NOUN] （旧时做骑士侍从的）实习骑士 In former times, a page was a young boy who was a knight's servant and was learning to be a knight. （旧时做骑士侍从的）实习骑士
