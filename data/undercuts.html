undercut
ʌndəʳkʌt ˈʌndərˌkʌt ˌ ʌndərˈkʌt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense undercuts, present participle undercutting
The form undercut is used in the present tense and is also the past tense and past participle. undercut 的过去式和过去分词与原形相同。
1 VERB 压低(价格)；以低于…的价格销售 If you undercut someone or undercut their prices, you sell a product more cheaply than they do. 压低(价格)；以低于…的价格销售 [business]

  • The firm will be able to undercut its competitors whilst still making a profit. [VERB noun]

    该公司将得以把价格压得低于竞争对手而仍能盈利。

  • ...promises to undercut air fares on some routes by 40 per cent. [VERB noun]

    承诺将一些航线的票价降低40%

  • Prices were undercut and profits collapsed. [VERB noun]

    价格被压低后，利润暴跌。

SYN underprice, sell cheaply, sell at a loss, undersell  
2 VERB [usually passive] 削弱；破坏 If your attempts to achieve something are undercut by something, that thing prevents your attempts from being effective. 削弱；破坏

  • The appeal in Miller's pictures of Indian women is undercut at times by what the artist writes about them. [be VERB-ed]

    米勒画作中印第安妇女的魅力有时会被艺术家的文字描述削弱。

  • Popular support would be undercut by political developments. [be VERB-ed]

    民众的支持率将会随政治势态的发展而下降。

SYN weaken, undermine, subvert, threaten
