catch out
PHRASAL VERB 使原形毕露;抓住…的短处 To catch someone out means to cause them to make a mistake that reveals that they are lying about something, do not know something, or cannot do something. 使原形毕露;抓住…的短处 [mainly British]

  • Detectives followed him for months hoping to catch him out in some deception. [V n P prep]

    侦探们跟踪了他几个月，希望能当场抓住他行骗。

  • He did not like to be caught out on details. [V n P prep]

    他不喜欢在细节上被人抓住把柄。

  • The government has been caught out by the speed of events. [be V-ed P + by]

    事情发展之迅速暴露了政府的无能。
