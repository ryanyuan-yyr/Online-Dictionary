entail
ɪnteɪl ɛnˈteɪl ɪnˈteɪl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense entails, present participle entailing, past tense , past participle entailed
VERB 使…必然发生;势必造成；使…成为必需 If one thing entails another, it involves it or causes it. 使…必然发生;势必造成；使…成为必需 [formal]

  • Such a decision would entail a huge political risk. [VERB noun]

    这样的决定势必带来巨大的政治风险。

  • The changed outlook entails higher economic growth than was previously assumed. [VERB noun]

    观念的改变势必会使得经济增长高于之前预计的水平。

  • The job of a choreologist entails teaching dancers the technique and performance of dance movements. [V -ing]

    舞谱学家的工作包括教舞蹈演员舞蹈动作的技巧和演绎。

  • I'll never accept parole because that entails me accepting guilt. [VERB noun verb-ing]

    我决不会接受假释，因为这就等于我承认自己有罪。

SYN involve, require, cause, produce
