discern
dɪsɜːʳn dɪˈsɜrn dɪˈzɜrn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense discerns, present participle discerning, past tense , past participle discerned
1 VERB 觉察出;知道;了解;辨明 If you can discern something, you are aware of it and know what it is. 觉察出;知道;了解;辨明 [formal] [Also VERB that]

  • You need a long series of data to be able to discern such a trend. [VERB noun]

    你需要一长串的数据才能看出这一趋势。

  • It was hard to discern why this was happening. [VERB wh]

    很难弄清楚这为什么会发生。

2 VERB 依稀看出;分辨出 If you can discern something, you can just see it, but not clearly. 依稀看出;分辨出 [formal]

  • Below the bridge we could just discern a narrow, weedy ditch. [VERB noun]

    我们仅能依稀辨出桥下一条杂草丛生的窄沟。

SYN see, perceive, make out, notice
