chat
tʃæt tʃæt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense chats, present participle chatting, past tense , past participle chatted
1 V-RECIP 聊天;闲聊 When people chat, they talk to each other in an informal and friendly way. 聊天;闲聊

  • The women were chatting. [VERB]

    女人们在聊天。

  • I was chatting to him the other day. [V + to/with]

    前几天我和他聊了一阵。

  • He's chatting with his dad. [V to/with n]

    他在和他爸爸闲聊。

  • We chatted about old times. [VERB + about]

    我们聊到过去的时光。

SYN talk, gossip, jaw [slang] , natter  
Chat is also a noun.

  • I had a chat with John.

    我和约翰聊了一会儿。

chat up
