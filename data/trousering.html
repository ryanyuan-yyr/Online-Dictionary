trouser
traʊzəʳ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense trousers, present participle trousering, past tense , past participle trousered
VERB 捞取，得到(不义之财) If you say that someone trousers a sum of money, you mean that they receive it, usually when they do not deserve it or should not take it. 捞取，得到(不义之财) [British, informal]

  • Many people think that ex-ministers are trousering £25,000 in fees simply for going to a few board meetings a year. [VERB noun]

    很多人认为前任部长们一年仅参加几次理事会就轻而易举地捞取了 25,000 英镑的报酬。
