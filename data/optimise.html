optimize
ɒptɪmaɪz ˈɑptəˌmaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense optimizes, present participle optimizing, past tense , past participle optimized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 optimise
1 VERB 使最优化；使最有效率；使尽可能完善 To optimize a plan, system, or machine means to arrange or design it so that it operates as smoothly and efficiently as possible. 使最优化；使最有效率；使尽可能完善 [formal]

  • Doctors are concentrating on understanding the disease better, and on optimizing the treatment. [VERB noun]

    医生正致力于更好地了解这种疾病，并使治疗方法尽可能完善。

  • The new systems have been optimised for running Microsoft Windows. [be VERB-ed]

    已经对这些新系统进行了优化设置，以便运行微软视窗。

2 VERB 充分利用 To optimize a situation or opportunity means to get as much advantage or benefit from it as you can. 充分利用 [formal]

  • What can you do to optimize your family situation? [VERB noun]

    你如何能充分利用自己的家庭条件？
