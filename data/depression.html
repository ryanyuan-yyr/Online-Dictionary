depression
dɪpreʃən diˈprɛʃən dɪˈprɛʃən
Word forms: plural depressions
1 N-VAR 抑郁;沮丧;消沉 Depression is a mental state in which you are sad and feel that you cannot enjoy anything, because your situation is so difficult and unpleasant. 抑郁;沮丧;消沉

  • Mr Thomas was suffering from depression.

    托马斯先生患有抑郁症。

  • I slid into a depression and became morbidly fascinated with death.

    我陷入消沉，开始对死亡有种病态的迷恋。

SYN despair, misery, sadness, the dumps [informal]  
2 N-COUNT 萧条（期）；不景气 A depression is a time when there is very little economic activity, which causes a lot of unemployment and poverty. 萧条（期）；不景气

  • He never forgot the hardships he witnessed during the Great Depression of the 1930s. [+ of]

    他永远不会忘记 20 世纪 30 年代经济大萧条时期他所亲眼目睹的困苦情形。

SYN recession, slump, economic decline, credit crunch  
3 N-COUNT 凹陷;凹坑;洼地 A depression in a surface is an area which is lower than the parts surrounding it. 凹陷;凹坑;洼地

  • ...an area pockmarked by rain-filled depressions.

    密布着雨水洼的地区

SYN hollow, pit, dip, bowl  
4 N-COUNT 低气压 A depression is a mass of air that has a low pressure and that often causes rain. 低气压
Quotations
