rumoured ruːməʳd
in AM, use 美国英语用 rumored
V-PASSIVE 据传闻;据说 If something is rumoured to be the case, people are suggesting that it is the case, but they do not know for certain. 据传闻;据说 [Also there be V-ed to-inf, be V-ed]

  • Her parents are rumoured to be on the verge of splitting up. [be VERB-ed to-infinitive]

    据说她的父母快要离婚了。

  • It was rumoured that he had been interned in an asylum for a while. [be VERB-ed that]

    据说他曾在一家精神病院关过一段时间。

  • ...his rumoured relationship with a young singer. [VERB-ed]

    他与一位年轻歌手的绯闻
