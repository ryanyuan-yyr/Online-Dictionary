provincial
prəvɪnʃəl prəˈvɪnʃəl proʊˈvɪnʃəl
Word forms: plural provincials
1 ADJ [ADJ n] 省份的；外省的；地方的 Provincial means connected with the parts of a country away from the capital city. 省份的；外省的；地方的

  • Jeremy Styles, 34, was the house manager for a provincial theatre for ten years.

    杰里米·斯泰尔斯，34岁，在一家地方剧院担任了10年经理。

  • ...in Rasht, the provincial capital of Gilan province.

    在吉兰省省会拉什特

2 ADJ-GRADED 过时的；守旧的；迂腐的 If you describe someone or something as provincial, you disapprove of them because you think that they are old-fashioned and boring. 过时的；守旧的；迂腐的 [disapproval]

  • He decided to revamp the company's provincial image.

    他决心一改公司保守落后的形象。

  • The audience was dull and very provincial.

    观众既呆板又非常守旧。

SYN parochial, insular, narrow-minded, unsophisticated  
3 N-COUNT 外省人；地方居民 A provincial is someone who comes from a part of a country away from the capital city. 外省人；地方居民

  • Owen died an unknown provincial.

    欧文死的时候是一个不为人知的外乡人。

SYN yokel, hick [informal , mainly US , Canadian] , rustic, country cousin
