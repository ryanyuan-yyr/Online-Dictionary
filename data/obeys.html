obey
oʊbeɪ oʊˈbeɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense obeys, present participle obeying, past tense , past participle obeyed
1 VERB 服从；顺从；听从；遵守 If you obey a person, a command, or an instruction, you do what you are told to do. 服从；顺从；听从；遵守

  • Cissie obeyed her mother without question. [VERB noun]

    茜茜对她母亲言听计从。

  • Most people obey the law. [VERB noun]

    大多数人都遵纪守法。

  • It was still Baker's duty to obey. [VERB]

    贝克依然有义务服从。

SYN submit to, surrender (to), give way to, succumb to
