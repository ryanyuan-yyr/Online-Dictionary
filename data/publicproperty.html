public property ˈpʌblɪk ˈprɒpətɪ
1 N-UNCOUNT 公共财产；公产；公物 Public property is land and other assets that belong to the general public and not to a private owner. 公共财产；公产；公物

  • ...vandals who wrecked public property.

    蓄意破坏公共财产的人

  • Half of the north-west's timber land is public property.

    西北部的一半林地是公共财产。

2 N-UNCOUNT 公众性的人（或事物） If you describe a person or thing as public property, you mean that information about them is known and discussed by everyone. 公众性的人（或事物）

  • He couldn't handle being public property.

    当一个公众人物，他无法应付。

  • She complained that intimate aspects of her personal life had been made public property.

    她抱怨说自己私生活中的隐私都成了家喻户晓的事。
