enfold
ɪnfoʊld ɛnˈfoʊld ɪnˈfoʊld
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense enfolds, present participle enfolding, past tense , past participle enfolded
1 VERB 盖住;裹住;包住 If something enfolds an object or person, they cover, surround, or are wrapped around that object or person. 盖住;裹住;包住 [literary] [Also V n + in]

  • Aurora felt the opium haze enfold her. [VERB noun]

    奥萝拉感觉自己被笼罩在鸦片烟霾中。

  • Wood was now comfortably enfolded in a woolly dressing-gown. [be VERB-ed + in]

    伍德正舒服地穿着件羊毛晨衣。

SYN wrap, surround, enclose, wrap up  
2 VERB 拥抱;抱住 If you enfold someone or something, you hold them close in a very gentle, loving way. 拥抱;抱住 [literary] [Also VERB n]

  • Thack came up behind him, enfolding him in his arms. [VERB noun + in]

    撒克走到他身后，伸开双臂拥抱他。
