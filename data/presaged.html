presage
presɪdʒ ˈprɛsɪdʒ priˈseɪdʒ prɪˈseɪdʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense presages, present participle presaging, past tense , past participle presaged
1 VERB 预示；预兆；警示 If something presages a situation or event, it is considered to be a warning or sign of what is about to happen. 预示；预兆；警示 [formal]

  • ...the dawn's loud chorus that seemed to presage a bright hot summer's day. [VERB noun]

    似乎预示着今天会是一个阳光灿烂的炎炎夏日的清晨嘹亮的鸟叫声
