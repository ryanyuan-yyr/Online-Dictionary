resident
rezɪdənt ˈrɛzɪdənt ˈrɛzɪˌdɛnt
Word forms: plural residents
1 N-COUNT [usually plural] 居民;住户;居住者 The residents of a house or area are the people who live there. 居民;住户;居住者

  • The Archbishop called upon the government to build more low cost homes for local residents.

    大主教呼吁政府为当地居民建造更多低成本的住宅。

  • More than 10 percent of Munich residents live below the poverty line.

    超过10％的慕尼黑居民生活在贫困线以下。

SYN inhabitant, citizen, native, householder  
2 ADJ [verb-link ADJ] （在某地）居住的，居留的 Someone who is resident in a country or a town lives there. （在某地）居住的，居留的

  • He moved to Belgium in 1990 to live with his son, who had been resident in Brussels since 1967. [+ in]

    他于1990年搬到比利时和儿子一起住。他儿子自1967年起就住在布鲁塞尔。

SYN inhabiting, living, staying, settled  
3 ADJ [usually ADJ n] (医生或教师)住在任职地的 A resident doctor or teacher lives in the place where he or she works. (医生或教师)住在任职地的 [British]

  • The morning after your arrival, you meet with the resident physician for a private consultation.

    到达后的第二天早上，你就去见住院医生，进行私人问诊。

4 N-COUNT 实习医生 A resident or a resident doctor is a doctor who is receiving a period of specialized training in a hospital after leaving university. 实习医生 [US]
5 ADJ [ADJ n] 特聘的;专聘的 If an institution has a resident specialist, that specialist works for the institution. 特聘的;专聘的

  • Having begun her career at Gray's Pottery, she stayed there as resident designer for seven years.

    在格雷陶器场开始自己的职业生涯后，她在那里做了7年的特聘设计师。
