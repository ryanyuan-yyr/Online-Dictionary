tumult
tjuːmʌlt tuː- ˈtumʌlt ˈtjumʌlt
1 N-SING 混乱;骚乱 A tumult is a state of great confusion or excitement. 混乱;骚乱

  • A tumult of feelings inside her fought for supremacy. [+ of]

    她内心百感交集。

  • ...the recent tumult in global financial markets.

    最近全球金融市场的混乱

SYN disturbance, trouble, chaos, turmoil  
2 N-SING 吵闹;喧哗 A tumult is a lot of noise made by a crowd of people. 吵闹;喧哗

  • Round one ends, to a tumult of whistles, screams and shouts. [+ of]

    第一局比赛结束了，口哨声、尖叫声与呼喊声一片喧哗。

SYN clamour, row, outbreak, racket
