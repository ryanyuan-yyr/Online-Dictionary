plump
plʌmp plʌmp
Word forms: comparative plumper, superlative plumpest, 3rd person singular present tense plumps, present participle plumping, past tense , past participle plumped
1 ADJ-GRADED 丰满的；胖乎乎的；圆滚滚的 You can describe someone or something as plump to indicate that they are rather fat or rounded. 丰满的；胖乎乎的；圆滚滚的

  • Maria was small and plump with a mass of curly hair.

    玛丽亚身材矮小丰满，一头卷发。

  • He pushed a plump little hand towards me.

    他把一只胖胖的小手朝我伸了过来。

  • ...red pears, ripe peaches and plump nectarines.

    红色的梨、熟了的桃和圆润的油桃

plumpness
→see usage note at: fat

  • There was a sturdy plumpness about her hips.

    她的臀部浑圆结实。

2 VERB （通过摇抖或拍打）使膨松，使松软鼓起 If you plump a pillow or cushion, you shake it and hit it gently so that it goes back into a rounded shape. （通过摇抖或拍打）使膨松，使松软鼓起 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • She panics when people pop in unexpectedly, rushing round plumping cushions. [VERB noun]

    一群人冷不防地冒出来，跑来跑去地拍松垫子，这把她吓坏了。

Plump up means the same as plump .plump up 同 plump

  • 'You need to rest,' she told her reassuringly as she moved to plump up her pillows.

    “你需要休息，”她一边安慰她一边走过去拍打枕头。

3 VERB （常指经过犹豫或深思熟虑后）选择，选中 If you plump for someone or something, you choose them, often after hesitating or thinking carefully. （常指经过犹豫或深思熟虑后）选择，选中 [mainly British]

  • I think Tessa should plump for Malcolm, her long-suffering admirer. [VERB + for]

    我想特莎应该选择长期默默爱慕她的马尔科姆。
