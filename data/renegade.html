renegade
renɪgeɪd ˈrɛnɪˌɡeɪd ˈrɛnəˌgeɪd
Word forms: plural renegades
1 N-COUNT 叛徒;变节者;改变宗教信仰（或政见）者 A renegade is a person who abandons the religious, political, or philosophical beliefs that he or she used to have, and accepts opposing or different beliefs. 叛徒;变节者;改变宗教信仰（或政见）者
SYN deserter, rebel, betrayer, dissident  
2 ADJ [ADJ n] 离经叛道的;叛逆的;变节的 Renegade is used to describe a member of a group or profession who behaves in a way that is opposed to the normal behaviour or beliefs of that group or profession. 离经叛道的;叛逆的;变节的

  • Three men were shot dead by a renegade policeman.

    3名男子被一个变节警察开枪打死。
