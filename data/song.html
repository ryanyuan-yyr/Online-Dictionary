song
sɒŋ sɔːŋ sɔŋ
Word forms: plural songs
1 N-COUNT 歌;歌曲 A song is words sung to a tune. 歌;歌曲

  • ...a voice singing a Spanish song.

    正在演唱一首西班牙歌曲的声音

  • ...a love song.

    情歌

SYN ballad, air, tune, lay  
2 N-UNCOUNT 歌唱艺术 Song is the art of singing. 歌唱艺术

  • ...dance, music, mime and song.

    舞蹈、音乐、哑剧以及歌唱

  • ...the history of American popular song.

    美国流行音乐史

3 N-COUNT (鸟儿的)鸣啭，鸣叫 A bird's song is the pleasant, musical sounds that it makes. (鸟儿的)鸣啭，鸣叫

  • It's been a long time since I heard a blackbird's song in the evening.

    我很久没有听见乌鸫的夜鸣了。

SYN birdsong, call, warbling, chirp  
4 See also: birdsong  song and dance  songbird  swan song 
5 PHRASE 放声歌唱 If someone bursts into song or breaks into song, they start singing. 放声歌唱

  • I feel as if I should break into song.

    我觉得自己应该放声歌唱。

6 PHRASE [after v] 用很便宜的价钱(买下);非常便宜地 If you buy something for a song, you buy it for much less than its real value. 用很便宜的价钱(买下);非常便宜地 [informal]

  • We found two second-hand chairs that were going for a song.

    我们发现了两把准备低价出售的二手椅子。

7 PHRASE [usually verb-link PHRASE] 竭尽全力，鼓足劲头，处于良好的竞技状态(新闻用语，形容运动员) Journalists sometimes describe sports players as being on song when they are playing really well. 竭尽全力，鼓足劲头，处于良好的竞技状态(新闻用语，形容运动员) [British]

  • Ward is not the most consistent of players, but when he is on song he looks a world-beater.

    沃德并不是发挥最稳定的球员，可是一旦进入状态，他似乎就是天下无敌的王者。

SYN on form, fit, in good shape, in good condition
