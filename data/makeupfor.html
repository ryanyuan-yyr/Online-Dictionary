make up for
PHRASAL VERB 弥补;补足 To make up for a bad experience or the loss of something means to make the situation better or make the person involved happier. 弥补;补足

  • Ask for an extra compensation payment to make up for the stress you have been caused. [VERB PREP. PREP. noun]

    为补偿你为此承受的压力，你要申请额外的补偿金。

  • A conservatory would make up for the fact that we were refused planning permission for a roof terrace. [VERB PREP. PREP. noun]

    我们建屋顶平台的计划被驳回了，要是能盖一间温室也算一种补偿。

  • The semi-finalists had to play twice in the day to make up for time lost to bad weather. [VERB PREP. PREP. noun]

    为了补回恶劣天气耽误的时间，半决赛选手不得不一日双赛。

SYN compensate for, redress, make amends for, atone for
