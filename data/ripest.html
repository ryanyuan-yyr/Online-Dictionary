ripe
raɪp raɪp
Word forms: comparative riper, superlative ripest
1 ADJ-GRADED (水果、谷物等)成熟的 Ripe fruit or grain is fully grown and ready to eat. (水果、谷物等)成熟的

  • Always choose firm, but ripe fruit.

    一定要挑选硬实但已成熟的水果。

  • ...fields of ripe wheat.

    成熟的麦田

SYN ripened, seasoned, ready, mature  
ripeness

  • Test the figs for ripeness.

    看看这些无花果熟了没有。

2 ADJ-GRADED (时机)成熟的，适宜的 If a situation is ripe for a particular development or event, you mean that development or event is likely to happen soon. (时机)成熟的，适宜的

  • Conditions were ripe for an outbreak of cholera. [+ for]

    霍乱即将暴发。

  • This society is ripe for change.

    发生社会变革的时机已经成熟。

SYN right, fit, ready, suitable  
3 PHRASE (活到)高龄 If someone lives to a ripe old age, they live until they are very old. (活到)高龄

  • He lived to the ripe old age of 95.

    他活到了95岁高龄。

4 PHRASE [usually verb-link PHRASE] 唾手可得;得到…易如反掌 If something is ripe for picking or ripe for plucking, it will be very easy to obtain. 唾手可得;得到…易如反掌

  • Haig's message was that victory was ripe for picking.

    黑格传达的意思是说胜利唾手可得。

5 PHRASE [VERB inflects, oft PHR for n, PHRASE to-infinitive] 时机已经成熟 If you say the time is ripe, you mean that a suitable time has arrived for something to be done. 时机已经成熟

  • He told reporters that he thought the time was ripe for a normalisation of relations.

    他告诉记者他认为是时候恢复两国间正常关系。

  • The British Foreign Office has decided the time is ripe to send its first female ambassador to the region.

    英国外交部作出决定现在可以向该地区派驻第一位女大使。
