observation
ɒbzəʳveɪʃən ˌɑbzərˈveɪʃən
Word forms: plural observations
1 N-UNCOUNT 观察；观测 Observation is the action or process of carefully watching someone or something. 观察；观测

  • ...careful observation of the movement of the planets. [+ of]

    对行星运动的仔细观测

  • In hospital she'll be under observation all the time.

    医院会对她24小时密切观察。

SYN watching, study, survey, review  
2 N-COUNT 观察所得；观察资料；观测数据 An observation is something that you have learned by seeing or watching something and thinking about it. 观察所得；观察资料；观测数据

  • This book contains observations about the causes of addictions. [+ about]

    该书包括有关成瘾原因的观察发现。

SYN comment, finding, thought, note  
3 N-COUNT （通常指观察后发表的）评论，言论 If a person makes an observation, they make a comment about something or someone, usually as a result of watching how they behave. （通常指观察后发表的）评论，言论

  • 'You're an obstinate man,' she said. 'Is that a criticism,' I said, 'or just an observation?'.

    “你是个很执拗的人，”她说道。“你这是批评呢，”我说，“还是只是发表评论而已？”

SYN remark, thought, comment, statement  
4 N-UNCOUNT 观察力 Observation is the ability to pay a lot of attention to things and to notice more about them than most people do. 观察力

  • She has good powers of observation.

    她的观察力很敏锐。
