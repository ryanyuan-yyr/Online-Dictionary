lifetime
laɪftaɪm ˈlaɪfˌtaɪm
Word forms: plural lifetimes
1 N-COUNT [usually singular, oft poss NOUN] 一生；终生 A lifetime is the length of time that someone is alive. 一生；终生

  • During my lifetime I haven't got around to much travelling.

    我一生中没怎么去旅行过。

  • ...a trust fund to be administered throughout his wife's lifetime.

    为他妻子终生管理的信托基金

  • ...an extraordinary lifetime of achievement. [+ of]

    成就斐然的一生

SYN existence, time, day(s), course  
2 N-SING [with poss] （事物的）存在期，使用期 The lifetime of a particular thing is the period of time that it lasts. （事物的）存在期，使用期

  • ...the lifetime of a parliament. [+ of]

    议会的届期

  • ...a satellite's lifetime.

    卫星的使用期

3 PHRASE 终生难遇的；千载难逢的 If you describe something as the chance or experience of a lifetime, you are emphasizing that it is the best or most important chance or experience that you are ever likely to have. 终生难遇的；千载难逢的 [emphasis]

  • This could be not just the trip of a lifetime but the experience of a lifetime.

    这可能不仅是一趟终生难遇的旅行，也将是一次终生难遇的体验。
