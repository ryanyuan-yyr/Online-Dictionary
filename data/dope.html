dope
doʊp doʊp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense dopes, present participle doping, past tense , past participle doped
1 N-UNCOUNT 麻醉剂;毒品 Dope is a drug, usually an illegal drug such as marijuana or cocaine. 麻醉剂;毒品 [informal]

  • A man asked them if they wanted to buy some dope.

    一名男子问他们想不想买些毒品。

  • He has failed a dope test for cocaine.

    他未能通过可卡因药检。

  • You get dope dealers on every corner.

    到处都是毒品贩子。

SYN drugs, narcotics, illegal drugs, opiates  
2 VERB 给…服麻醉剂（或毒品）；在…中掺麻醉剂（或毒品） If someone dopes a person or animal or dopes their food, they put drugs into their food or force them to take drugs. 给…服麻醉剂（或毒品）；在…中掺麻醉剂（或毒品） [Also V n with n]

  • Anyone could have got in and doped the wine. [VERB noun]

    任何人都可能会进来在酒中掺麻醉剂。

  • I'd been doped with Somnolin. [be VERB-ed + with]

    我被人下了安眠药。

  • They've got him doped to the eyeballs. [VERB-ed]

    他们给他服用了大量毒品。

  • ...recent cases of horse doping. [VERB-ing]

    最近给马匹服用兴奋剂的事件

SYN drug, doctor, knock out, inject  
3 N-COUNT 笨蛋;呆子;蠢货 If someone calls a person a dope, they think that the person is stupid. 笨蛋;呆子;蠢货 [informal, disapproval]

  • I'm more comfortable with them. I don't feel I'm such a dope.

    我和他们在一起更自在，也觉得自己没那么傻。

SYN idiot, fool, jerk [slang , mainly US , Canadian] , plank [British , slang]  
4 N-UNCOUNT 情报;密报 Dope is information which you have been given illegally or secretly. 情报;密报 [informal]

  • The government had plenty of dope on him.

    政府掌握大量关于他的内部消息。

SYN information, facts, details, material
