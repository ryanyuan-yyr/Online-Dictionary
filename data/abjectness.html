abject
æbdʒekt ˈæbˌdʒɛkt æbˈdʒɛkt
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 凄惨的;糟透的;蹩脚的 You use abject to emphasize that a situation or quality is extremely bad. 凄惨的;糟透的;蹩脚的 [emphasis]

  • Both of them died in abject poverty.

    他们两人都于穷困潦倒中死去。

  • This scheme was an abject failure.

    这项计划彻底失败了。

SYN wretched, miserable, hopeless, dismal  
abjectly

  • Both have failed abjectly.

    两人都遭遇了惨败。

2 ADJ-GRADED 卑躬屈节的；自卑的；怯懦的 If you describe someone as abject, you think they have no courage or respect for themselves. 卑躬屈节的；自卑的；怯懦的 [literary]

  • He sounded abject and eager to please.

    他低声下气，急于讨好别人。

  • He looked back at the abject, silent girl and repeated his question.

    他转过头看着这个噤若寒蝉的女孩，又重复了一遍他的问题。

SYN servile, humble, humiliating, craven
