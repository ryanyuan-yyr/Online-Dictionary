libel
laɪbəl ˈlaɪbəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense libels, present participle libelling, past tense , past participle libelled
in AM, use 美国英语用 libeling, libeled
1 N-VAR （以文字进行的）诽谤（罪） Libel is a written statement which wrongly accuses someone of something, and which is therefore against the law. （以文字进行的）诽谤（罪） [law]

  • Warren sued him for libel over the remarks.

    沃伦就此言论起诉他诽谤。

  • If the jury decided there was a libel, it would have to consider its effect on Miss Smith's position.

    如果陪审团裁定诽谤罪成立，那就必须考虑其对史密斯小姐的职位的影响。

  • ...a libel action against the paper.

    对该报提出的诽谤讼诉

SYN defamation, slander, misrepresentation, denigration  
2 VERB （在书报、杂志上）对…进行诽谤 To libel someone means to write or print something in a book, newspaper, or magazine which wrongly damages that person's reputation and is therefore against the law. （在书报、杂志上）对…进行诽谤 [law]

  • The newspaper which libelled him had already offered compensation. [VERB noun]

    诽谤他的报纸已经向他提出支付赔偿金。

SYN defame, smear, slur, blacken
