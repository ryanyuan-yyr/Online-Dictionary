indignant
ɪndɪgnənt ɪnˈdɪgnənt
1 ADJ-GRADED [ADJ that] 愤怒的;愤慨的 If you are indignant, you are shocked and angry, because you think that something is unjust or unfair. 愤怒的;愤慨的

  • He is indignant at suggestions that they were secret agents. [+ at/about]

    有人暗示他们可能是特务,这让他非常气愤。

  • MPs were indignant that the government had not consulted them.

    政府没有事先征求他们的意见,下院议员们感到很愤怒。

  • Sheena gave her an indignant look.

    希娜愤怒地看了她一眼。

SYN resentful, angry, mad [informal] , heated  
indignantly

  • 'That is not true,' Erica said indignantly.

    “那不是真的,”埃丽卡愤怒地说。
