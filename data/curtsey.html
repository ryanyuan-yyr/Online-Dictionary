curtsy
kɜːʳtsi ˈkɜrtsi
(also curtsey)Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense curtsies, present participle curtsying, past tense , past participle curtsied
VERB (女子行的)屈膝礼 If a woman or a girl curtsies, she lowers her body briefly, bending her knees and sometimes holding her skirt with both hands, as a way of showing respect for an important person. (女子行的)屈膝礼

  • We were taught how to curtsy to the Queen. [VERB + to]

    有人教我们如何向女王行屈膝礼。

  • Ingrid shook the Duchess's hand and curtsied. [VERB]

    英格丽德和公爵夫人握了握手并行了屈膝礼。

Curtsy is also a noun.

  • She gave a curtsy.

    她行了个屈膝礼。
