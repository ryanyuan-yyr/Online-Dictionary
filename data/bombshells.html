bombshell
bɒmʃel ˈbɑmˌʃɛl
Word forms: plural bombshells
1 N-COUNT 意外的坏消息；出乎意料的消息 A bombshell is a sudden piece of bad or unexpected news. 意外的坏消息；出乎意料的消息

  • His resignation after thirteen years is a political bombshell.

    他任职13年后提出辞职，这在政治上轰动一时。

  • The police told me. It was a bombshell. I had no idea.

    是警察告诉我的。我很震惊，完全没想到。

If someone drops a bombshell, they give you a sudden piece of bad or unexpected news.投下一枚重磅炸弹；带来令人意外的（坏）消息

  • He dropped the bombshell. He told me he was dying.

    他的话犹如一枚重磅炸弹。他告诉我他要死了。
