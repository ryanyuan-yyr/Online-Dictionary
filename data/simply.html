simply
sɪmpli ˈsɪmpli
1 ADV [ADVERB before verb] 简单地;只不过;仅仅;纯粹 You use simply to emphasize that something consists of only one thing, happens for only one reason, or is done in only one way. 简单地;只不过;仅仅;纯粹 [emphasis]

  • The table is simply a chipboard circle on a base.

    这张桌子只不过是在底座上安了一块圆形刨花板。

  • Most of the damage that's occurred was simply because of fallen trees.

    目前大部分损坏都是因为倒下的树木造成的。

  • Many people switch on the television simply to stave off boredom over the Christmas break.

    很多人打开电视仅仅是为了打发圣诞节休息期间的无聊时光而已。

  • A sitting room can be transformed into a guest bedroom simply by adding a sofabed.

    加上一张沙发床，客厅便变成了客房。

SYN just, only, merely, purely  
2 ADV [ADVERB before verb, ADVERB adjective] (用于强调)简直，根本 You use simply to emphasize what you are saying. (用于强调)简直，根本 [emphasis]

  • This sort of increase simply cannot be justified.

    这种增长根本就不合理。

  • So many of these questions simply don't have answers.

    这些问题有很多根本就没有答案。

  • In a poll of those leaving the theatre and nine out of ten thought it was simply marvellous.

    一项对那些刚在剧院看完这场戏的观众所作的调查显示，十有八九的人都认为它简直棒极了。
