hinder
hɪndəʳ ˈhaɪndə ˈhaɪndər ˈhɪndər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense hinders, present participle hindering, past tense , past participle hindered
1 VERB 阻碍;妨碍;阻止 If something hinders you, it makes it more difficult for you to do something or make progress. 阻碍;妨碍;阻止

  • Does the fact that your players are part-timers help or hinder you?. [VERB noun]

    队员们都是兼职的，这对你有利还是不利？

  • Further investigation was hindered by the loss of all documentation on the case. [VERB noun]

    由于案件卷宗全部丢失致使进一步的调查受阻。

2 VERB 牵制，束缚（动作） If something hinders your movement, it makes it difficult for you to move forward or move around. 牵制，束缚（动作）

  • A thigh injury increasingly hindered her mobility. [VERB noun]

    大腿受伤使她行动愈发不便。

  • Landslides and bad weather are continuing to hinder the arrival of relief supplies to the area. [VERB noun]

    滑坡和恶劣天气仍在阻碍救援物资运送到该地区。

SYN obstruct, stop, check, block
