nurse
nɜːʳs nɜrs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense nurses, present participle nursing, past tense , past participle nursed
1 N-COUNT; N-TITLE; N-VOC 护士 A nurse is a person whose job is to care for people who are ill. 护士

  • She had spent 29 years as a nurse.

    她做了29年的护士。

  • Patients were dying because of an acute shortage of nurses.

    因为护理人员严重不足，患者生命垂危。

SYN carer, caregiver, angel [informal]  
2 VERB 护理;照顾;照料 If you nurse someone, you care for them when they are ill. 护理;照顾;照料

  • All the years he was sick my mother had nursed him. [VERB noun]

    他生病的这些年来，我妈妈一直照料着他。

  • She rushed home to nurse her daughter back to health. [V n + back]

    她赶回家去，要照顾女儿恢复健康。

SYN look after, treat, tend, care for  
3 VERB 调养，调治(伤病) If you nurse an illness or injury, you allow it to get better by resting as much as possible. 调养，调治(伤病)

  • We're going to go home and nurse our colds. [VERB noun]

    我们打算回家调养感冒。

4 VERB 怀有，抱有(强烈的希望、愿望等) If you nurse an emotion or desire, you feel it strongly for a long time. 怀有，抱有(强烈的希望、愿望等)

  • Jane still nurses the pain of rejection. [VERB noun]

    简仍因遭到拒绝而痛苦。

  • He had nursed an ambition to lead his own big orchestra. [VERB noun]

    他一直有一个梦想，希望能指挥一支自己的顶级管弦乐队。

SYN harbour, have, maintain, preserve  
5 N-COUNT 保姆;保育员 A nurse is a person who is trained to look after young children. 保姆;保育员 [old-fashioned]

  • Every morning she got up early with the children and the nurse.

    每天早晨，她和孩子、保姆一起早早起床。

6 V-ERG (婴儿)吃奶;(母亲)给…喂奶 When a baby nurses or when its mother nurses it, it feeds by sucking milk from its mother's breast. (婴儿)吃奶;(母亲)给…喂奶 [old-fashioned]

  • Most authorities recommend letting the baby nurse whenever it wants. [VERB]

    大部分权威人士建议婴儿饿了就随时喂奶。

  • ...young women nursing babies. [VERB noun]

    给孩子喂奶的年轻妈妈

  • Young people and nursing mothers are exempted from charges. [VERB-ing]

    未成年人和哺乳期女性免予起诉。

7 See also: nursery nurse  nursing  wet nurse
