excel
ɪksel ɪkˈsɛl ɛkˈsɛl ɪkˈsɛl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense excels, present participle excelling, past tense , past participle excelled
VERB 突出;擅长;胜过（他人） If someone excels in something or excels at it, they are very good at doing it. 突出;擅长;胜过（他人）

  • Caine has always been an actor who excels in irony. [VERB + in]

    凯恩向来是个擅长讽刺的演员。

  • Mary was a better rider than either of them and she excelled at outdoor sports. [VERB + at]

    玛丽的骑术比他们俩都要好，而且她擅长户外运动。

  • Academically he began to excel. [VERB]

    他开始在学术上超越他人。

  • I think Krishnan excelled himself in all departments of his game. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    我认为克里舍南在比赛的各个环节都有超常的发挥。

SYN be superior, better, pass, eclipse
