overemphasize
oʊvəremfəsaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense overemphasizes, present participle overemphasizing, past tense , past participle overemphasized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 overemphasise
1 VERB 过分强调；过分注重 If you say that someone overemphasizes something, you mean that they give it more importance than it deserves or than you consider appropriate. 过分强调；过分注重

  • In the public discussion of nuclear policies, technology has usually been overemphasized and morality neglected. [be VERB-ed]

    核政策的公开讨论往往过分强调技术层面，却忽略了道德问题。

  • Democrats will complain he overemphasizes punishment at the expense of prevention and treatment. [VERB noun]

    民主党人会抱怨他过分看重惩罚，而忽视了预防和解决措施。

2 VERB 再怎么强调（都不为过） If you say that something cannot be overemphasized, you are emphasizing that you think it is very important. 再怎么强调（都不为过） [emphasis]

  • The importance of education cannot be overemphasised. [be VERB-ed]

    教育的重要性怎么强调都不为过。

  • I can't overemphasize the cleanliness of this place. [VERB noun]

    这个地方的清洁我再怎么说都不为过。
