reinstate
riːɪnsteɪt ˌriːɪnˈsteɪt ˌriɪnˈsteɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense reinstates, present participle reinstating, past tense , past participle reinstated
1 VERB 使复职;使复位 If you reinstate someone, you give them back a job or position which had been taken away from them. 使复职;使复位

  • The governor is said to have agreed to reinstate five senior workers who were dismissed. [VERB noun]

    据说州长已同意给5名被解雇的高级雇员复职。

  • Thailand's prime minister announced his resignation today, but he is widely expected to be reinstated within a few days. [VERB noun]

    泰国总理今天宣布辞职，但外界广泛猜测他会在几天之内复职。

SYN restore, recall, bring back, re-establish  
2 VERB 恢复(法律、做法等);修复（设施等） To reinstate a law, facility, or practice means to start having it again. 恢复(法律、做法等);修复（设施等）

  • ...the decision to reinstate the grant. [VERB noun]

    恢复此项补贴的决定

  • There have now been 139 executions since the death penalty was reinstated in 1976. [VERB noun]

    自1976年恢复死刑以来共有139人被处决。

SYN restore, re-establish
