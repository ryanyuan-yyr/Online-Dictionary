mystery
mɪstəri ˈmɪstəri ˈmɪstri
Word forms: plural mysteries
1 N-COUNT 谜;神秘的事物;难懂的事物 A mystery is something that is not understood or known about. 谜;神秘的事物;难懂的事物

  • The source of the gunshots still remains a mystery.

    枪炮声来自何处仍是个谜。

  • ...the mysteries of mental breakdown. [+ of]

    精神崩溃之谜

SYN puzzle, problem, question, secret  
2 N-UNCOUNT 神秘；谜一样的特质 If you talk about the mystery of someone or something, you are talking about how difficult they are to understand or know about, especially when this gives them a rather strange or magical quality. 神秘；谜一样的特质

  • She's a lady of mystery.

    她是个神秘的女人。

  • It is an elaborate ceremony, shrouded in mystery.

    仪式是精心筹划的，笼罩着一层神秘的色彩。

SYN secrecy, uncertainty, obscurity, mystique  
3 ADJ [ADJ n] 神秘的;身份不明的 A mystery person or thing is one whose identity or nature is not known. 神秘的;身份不明的

  • The mystery hero immediately alerted police after spotting a bomb.

    那个无名英雄发现炸弹后马上报了警。

  • ...a mystery prize of up to £1,000.

    高达1,000英镑的神秘奖品

4 N-COUNT 疑案小说;推理小说 A mystery is a story in which strange things happen that are not explained until the end. 疑案小说;推理小说

  • His fourth novel is a murder mystery set in London.

    他的第四部小说讲述的是一起发生在伦敦的神秘谋杀案。
