massage
mæsɑːʒ məsɑːʒ məˈsɑʒ məˈsɑdʒ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense massages, present participle massaging, past tense , past participle massaged
1 N-VAR 按摩;推拿 Massage is the action of squeezing and rubbing someone's body, as a way of making them relax or reducing their pain. 按摩;推拿

  • Alex asked me if I wanted a massage.

    亚力克斯问我是否想做按摩。

  • Massage isn't a long-term cure for stress.

    按摩对缓解压力并非长期有效。

SYN rub-down, rubbing, manipulation, kneading  
2 VERB 为…按摩;给…推拿 If you massage someone or a part of their body, you squeeze and rub their body, in order to make them relax or reduce their pain. 为…按摩;给…推拿

  • She continued massaging her right foot, which was bruised and aching. [VERB noun]

    她继续按摩又肿又疼的右脚。

  • ...if you wish to massage your family and friends yourself. [VERB noun]

    如果你希望亲自为家人及朋友按摩的话

SYN rub down, rub, manipulate, knead  
3 VERB 修改，改动,窜改(数据或证据以掩人耳目) If you say that someone massages statistics, figures, or evidence, you are criticizing them for changing or presenting the facts in a way that misleads people. 修改，改动,窜改(数据或证据以掩人耳目) [disapproval]

  • Their governments have no reason to 'massage' the statistics. [VERB noun]

    他们的政府没有理由“窜改”这些数据。

  • ...efforts to massage the unemployment figures for electoral purposes. [VERB noun]

    为了选举的目的而试图窜改失业数字

SYN manipulate, alter, distort, doctor
