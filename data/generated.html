generate
dʒenəreɪt ˈdʒɛnərˌeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense generates, present participle generating, past tense , past participle generated
1 VERB 造成；引起；导致 To generate something means to cause it to begin and develop. 造成；引起；导致

  • The Employment Minister said the reforms would generate new jobs. [VERB noun]

    劳工大臣表示，改革将带来新的就业机会。

  • ...the excitement generated by the changes in Eastern Europe. [VERB-ed]

    东欧变革引起的骚动

SYN produce, create, make, form  
2 VERB 产生（能量）；发（电） To generate a form of energy or power means to produce it. 产生（能量）；发（电）

  • The company, New England Electric, burns coal to generate power. [VERB noun]

    新英格兰电力公司用煤发电。
