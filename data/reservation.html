reservation
rezəʳveɪʃən ˌrɛzərˈveɪʃən
Word forms: plural reservations
1 N-VAR 保留意见;疑义 If you have reservations about something, you are not sure that it is entirely good or right. 保留意见;疑义

  • I told him my main reservation about his film was the ending. [+ about]

    我告诉他我主要是对他电影的结尾有保留意见。

  • After three days, the strikers' demands were met almost without reservation.

    3天以后，罢工人员的要求几乎全部得到了满足。

2 N-COUNT 预订;预约 If you make a reservation, you arrange for something such as a table in a restaurant or a room in a hotel to be kept for you. 预订;预约

  • He went to the desk to make a reservation.

    他到服务台进行了预订。

  • Accommodation is restricted so a reservation is essential.

    铺位有限，所以有必要进行预订。

SYN booking [British] , advance booking, prior arrangements  
3 N-COUNT 保留地;居留地 A reservation is an area of land that is kept separate for a particular group of people to live in. 保留地;居留地

  • Seventeen thousand Indians live in Arizona on a reservation.

    1.7万印第安人生活在亚利桑那州的保留地。

4 See also: central reservation
