opportunism
ɒpəʳtjuːnɪzəm -tuːn- ˌɑpərˈtunˌɪzəm ˌɑpərˈtjunˌɪzəm
N-UNCOUNT 机会主义；投机取巧 If you refer to someone's behaviour as opportunism, you are criticizing them for taking advantage of any opportunity that occurs in order to gain money or power, without thinking about whether their actions are right or wrong. 机会主义；投机取巧 [disapproval]

  • The Energy Minister responded by saying that the opposition's concern for the environment was political opportunism.

    能源部长回应说，反对派对环境问题的关切是政治上的机会主义。

SYN expediency, convenience, exploitation, realism
