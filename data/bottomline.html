bottom line
Word forms: plural bottom lines
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 最重要的因素；要旨 The bottom line in a decision or situation is the most important factor that you have to consider. 最重要的因素；要旨

  • The bottom line is that it's not profitable.

    最重要的一点是它无利可图。

  • The bottom line is that it did not get the best out of everybody.

    最关键的问题是这不能使每个人充分发挥自己的长处。

2 N-COUNT [usually singular, usually poss NOUN] （生意上达成交易的）底线，最低条件 The bottom line in a business deal is the least a person is willing to accept. （生意上达成交易的）底线，最低条件

  • She says £95,000 is her bottom line.

    她说95,000英镑是她的底线。

3 N-COUNT [oft poss NOUN] （公司在一段时期内的）盈亏总额，账本底线 The bottom line is the total amount of money that a company has made or lost over a particular period of time. （公司在一段时期内的）盈亏总额，账本底线 [business]

  • These small promotions were costly and they did nothing to increase his bottom line.

    这些小规模的促销活动成本很高，而且对增加他的最终效益没什么贡献。

  • ...to force chief executives to look beyond the next quarter's bottom line.

    迫使主管们将目光不要仅局限于下一个季度的盈亏
