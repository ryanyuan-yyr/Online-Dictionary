write in
1 PHRASAL VERB (向某一机构)写信，发信 If you write in to an organization, you send them a letter. (向某一机构)写信，发信

  • What's the point in writing in when you only print half the letter anyway?. [VERB PREP.]

    只打印出一半就把信寄出去到底有什么用呢？

  • So there's another thing that you might like to write in to this programme about. [VERB PREP. + to]

    所以，你或许还有另外一件事想写信告诉本节目。

2 PHRASAL VERB (美国选举中)在选票上加写(非候选人的姓名) In the United States, if someone who is voting in an election writes in a person whose name is not on the list of candidates, they write that person's name on the voting paper and vote for him or her. (美国选举中)在选票上加写(非候选人的姓名)

  • I think I'll write in Pat Wilson. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我想我要在选票上加写上帕特·威尔逊。

  • I'm going to write him in on my ballot next year. [VERB noun PREP.]

    明年我要在我的选票上写上他的名字。

3 See also: write-in 
write-in
ˈraɪtˌɪn raɪtˌɪn
Word forms: plural write-ins
N-COUNT [oft NOUN n] (美国选举中)投给非推荐候选人的票 In the US, a write-in is a vote that you make by writing the candidate's name on the ballot paper. (美国选举中)投给非推荐候选人的票

  • When Republican write-ins were included, Johnson's margin of victory was only 330 votes.

    将共和党非推荐候选人所获选票计入在内的话，约翰逊只多赢了330票。

  • The state does not permit write-in votes.

    该州不允许投票给非推荐候选人。

  • Four years ago, he was a write-in candidate in New Hampshire.

    4年前，他是新罕布什尔州一名非推荐候选人。
