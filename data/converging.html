converge
kənvɜːʳdʒ kənˈvɜrdʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense converges, present participle converging, past tense , past participle converged
1 VERB 汇集;集中;聚集 If people or vehicles converge on a place, they move towards it from different directions. 汇集;集中;聚集

  • Competitors from more than a hundred countries have converged on Sheffield for the Games. [VERB + on]

    来自100多个国家的选手齐聚设菲尔德参加此次运动会。

  • Hundreds of coaches will converge on the capital. [VERB on noun]

    数百辆长途汽车将会在首都汇集。

2 VERB (路、线等)交会，交叉，汇合 If roads or lines converge, they meet or join at a particular place. (路、线等)交会，交叉，汇合 [formal]

  • As they flow south, the five rivers converge. [VERB]

    5条大河在南边交汇。

SYN come together, meet, join, combine  
3 V-RECIP (不同的思想、社会等)趋同，趋于一致，融合 If different ideas or societies converge, they stop being different and become similar to each other. (不同的思想、社会等)趋同，趋于一致，融合

  • Speeches delivered by Mr Dewar and Mr Wallace indicated their views were converging. [VERB]

    杜瓦先生和华莱士先生发表的讲话表明他们的观点越来越接近了。

  • The views of the richest householders converged with those of the poorest and created a new consensus. [VERB + with]

    最富的房主和最穷的房主观点趋于一致，达成了新的共识。
