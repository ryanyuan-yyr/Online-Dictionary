old guard
N-SING-COLL [with singular or plural verb] （组织中的）保守派，守旧派 If you refer to a group of people as the old guard, you mean that they have worked in a particular organization for a very long time and are unwilling to accept new ideas or practices. （组织中的）保守派，守旧派 [disapproval]

  • The old guard did not like the changes that Brewer introduced.

    保守派不满布鲁尔推行的这些变革。

  • He belongs to the ruling Nationalist Party's old guard.

    他属于执政的国民党的保守派。

SYN traditionalists, conservatives, reactionaries
