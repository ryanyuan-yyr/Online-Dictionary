float
floʊt floʊt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense floats, present participle floating, past tense , past participle floated
1 V-ERG (使)漂浮;(使)浮动 If something or someone is floating in a liquid, they are in the liquid, on or just below the surface, and are being supported by it. You can also float something on a liquid. (使)漂浮;(使)浮动 [Also VERB n preposition/adverb]

  • They noticed fifty and twenty dollar bills floating in the water. [VERB + in]

    他们发现水中漂浮着50和20美元的纸币。

  • ...barges floating quietly by the grassy river banks. [VERB prep./adv.]

    青草萋萋的河岸边静静漂浮着的驳船

  • They'll spend some time floating boats in the creek. [VERB noun]

    他们打算花些时间在溪流里放船模。。

SYN glide, sail, drift, move gently  
2 VERB 漂;浮 Something that floats lies on or just below the surface of a liquid when it is put in it and does not sink. 漂;浮

  • Empty things float. [VERB]

    空的物体会在水中浮起。

SYN be buoyant, stay afloat, be or lie on the surface, rest on water  
3 N-COUNT 浮体;浮板;救生圈 A float is a light object that is used to help someone or something float. 浮体;浮板;救生圈
4 N-COUNT (钓鱼用的)鱼漂浮子 A float is a small object attached to a fishing line which floats on the water and moves when a fish has been caught. (钓鱼用的)鱼漂浮子
5 VERB (在空中)飘荡，飘浮 Something that floats in or through the air hangs in it or moves slowly and gently through it. (在空中)飘荡，飘浮

  • The white cloud of smoke floated away. [VERB prep./adv.]

    白色的烟雾飘远了。

  • ...the sun's rays lighting up the dust floating in the air. [VERB prep./adv.]

    阳光照亮了空气中浮动的灰尘

6 VERB (声音或气味)飘到，传到 If a sound or smell floats to a place quite far away, it can be heard or smelled there. (声音或气味)飘到，传到 [literary]

  • Sublime music floats on a scented summer breeze to the spot where you lie on the lush grass. [VERB prep./adv.]

    你躺在绿草如茵的草地上，涤荡心灵的乐声随着清香的夏日微风飘到你的身边。

  • The smells of delicious foods floated all around him. [VERB prep./adv.]

    美食的香味弥漫在他的四周。

SYN waft, coast, drift  
7 VERB 优雅地走动;轻盈地走动 If you float somewhere, you walk there very lightly and gracefully. 优雅地走动;轻盈地走动 [literary]

  • Caroline floated up the aisle on her father's arm. [VERB prep./adv.]

    卡罗琳挽着父亲的手臂从过道上优雅地走过。

8 VERB 提出(计划、方案或想法) If you float a project, plan, or idea, you suggest it for others to think about. 提出(计划、方案或想法)

  • The French had floated the idea of placing the diplomatic work in the hands of the UN.

    法国提出将外交工作交由联合国处理的想法。

SYN suggest, present, propose, recommend  
9 VERB 使(公司)发行股票;使上市 If a company director floats their company, they start to sell shares in it to the public. 使(公司)发行股票;使上市 [business]

  • He floated his firm on the stock market. [VERB noun + on]

    他让自己的公司上市了。

  • The advisers are delaying the key decision on whether to float 60 per cent or 100 per cent of the shares. [VERB noun]

    顾问们对于发行60%还是100%股份的关键问题迟迟未作决定。

SYN launch, offer, sell, set up  
10 V-ERG (使)(货币)自由浮动 If a government floats its country's currency or allows it to float, it allows the currency's value to change freely in relation to other currencies. (使)(货币)自由浮动 [business]

  • A decision by the Finns to float their currency sent a shudder through the foreign exchanges. [VERB noun]

    芬兰允许其货币自由浮动的决定在外汇市场上引起了震动。

  • 59 per cent of people believed the pound should be allowed to float freely. [VERB]

    59%的人认为应该允许英镑自由浮动。

11 N-COUNT (节日游行时的)彩车，花车 A float is a truck on which displays and people in special costumes are carried in a festival procession. (节日游行时的)彩车，花车
12See also:milk float 
13 N-SING (商店的)备用零钱 A float is a small amount of coins and notes of low value that someone has before they start selling things so that they are able to give customers change if necessary. (商店的)备用零钱 [British]
14 PHRASAL VERB 传开;流传 A rumour or idea that is floating around is often heard or talked about. 传开;流传

  • There are still some unfounded fears floating around out there about cancer being contagious.

    社会上还是有一些毫无根据的关于癌症会传染的恐惧。

Phrasal verbs:
float around
