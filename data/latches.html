latch
lætʃ lætʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense latches, present participle latching, past tense , past participle latched
1 N-COUNT 门闩；插销 A latch is a fastening on a door or gate. It consists of a metal bar which you lift in order to open the door. 门闩；插销

  • You left the latch off the gate and the dog escaped.

    你没把门闩插上，所以狗跑了。

SYN fastening, catch, bar, lock  
2 N-COUNT 碰锁；弹簧锁 A latch is a lock on a door which locks automatically when you shut the door, so that you need a key in order to open it from the outside. 碰锁；弹簧锁

  • ...a key clicked in the latch of the front door. [+ of]

    钥匙插进前门弹簧锁里发出咔嗒一声

If a door is on the latch or off the latch, the latch has been set so that it will not lock when you shut the door.（门）虚掩着，没上锁

  • Let yourself in; the door's on the latch.

    你自己进来吧，门没上锁。

  • They leave their back door off the latch when they're at home so that friends can pop in.

    他们在家时就让后门虚掩着，好让朋友们可以随时进来。

3 VERB 用闩闩上；用碰锁锁上 If you latch a door or gate, you fasten it by means of a latch. 用闩闩上；用碰锁锁上

  • He latched the door, tested it, and turned around to speak to Frank. [VERB noun]

    他把门锁上，又试了试，然后转身和弗兰克说话。

SYN fasten, bar, secure, lock
