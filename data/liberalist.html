liberalism
lɪbərəlɪzəm ˈlɪbərəlˌɪzəm ˈlɪbrəlˌɪzəm
1 N-UNCOUNT （主张通过修改法律逐步实现社会进步的）自由主义 Liberalism is a belief in gradual social progress by changing laws, rather than by revolution. （主张通过修改法律逐步实现社会进步的）自由主义

  • ...a democrat who has decided that economic liberalism is the best way to secure change.

    认定经济自由主义是促进变革的最好办法的民主党党员

  • ...the tradition of nineteenth-century liberalism.

    19世纪的自由主义传统

2 N-UNCOUNT （主张个体拥有广泛的政治和个人自由的）自由主义 Liberalism is the belief that people should have a lot of political and individual freedom. （主张个体拥有广泛的政治和个人自由的）自由主义

  • He was concerned over growing liberalism in the Church.

    他对教会中日益增强的自由主义倾向心存忧虑。

SYN progressivism, radicalism, humanitarianism, libertarianism  
Quotations
By liberalism I don't mean the creed of any party or any century. I mean a generosity of spirit, a tolerance of others, an attempt to comprehend otherness, a commitment to the rule of law, a high ideal of the worth and dignity of man, a repugnance for authoritarianism and a love of freedomAlan Paton Lecture at Yale University
