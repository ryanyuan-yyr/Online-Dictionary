appeal
əpiːl əˈpil
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense appeals, present participle appealing, past tense , past participle appealed
1 VERB 呼吁;恳请;恳求 If you appeal to someone to do something, you make a serious and urgent request to them. 呼吁;恳请;恳求

  • The Prime Minister appealed to young people to use their vote. [V + to/for]

    首相呼吁年轻人行使他们的投票权。

  • He will appeal to the state for an extension of unemployment benefits. [VERB + to]

    他将呼吁国家延长领取失业救济金的时限。

  • The United Nations has appealed for help from the international community. [VERB + for]

    联合国已经呼吁国际社会提供援助。

SYN plead, call, ask, apply  
2 N-COUNT [NOUN to-infinitive] 呼吁;恳请;恳求 An appeal is a serious and urgent request. 呼吁;恳请;恳求

  • He has a message from King Fahd, believed to be an appeal for Arab unity.

    法赫德国王传话给他，据信内容是呼吁整个阿拉伯世界团结起来。

  • Romania's government issued a last-minute appeal to him to call off his trip. [+ to]

    罗马尼亚政府最后一刻恳请他取消行程。

SYN plea, call, application, request  
3 N-COUNT [oft NOUN to-infinitive] （为慈善或正义事业的）募捐 An appeal is an attempt to raise money for a charity or for a good cause. （为慈善或正义事业的）募捐

  • ...an appeal to save a library containing priceless manuscripts.

    为挽救存有珍贵手稿的图书馆的一次募捐

  • This is not another appeal for famine relief.

    这不是为赈济饥荒的又一次募捐。

4 VERB 上诉;申诉 If you appeal to someone in authority against a decision, you formally ask them to change it. In British English, you appeal against something. In American English, you appeal something. 上诉;申诉

  • He said they would appeal against the decision. [VERB + against]

    他说他们将对该决议提出申诉。

  • We intend to appeal the verdict. [VERB noun]

    我们打算对裁决提出上诉。

  • Maguire has appealed to the Supreme Court to stop her extradition. [VERB + to]

    马圭尔已向最高法院提出上诉，要求停止对她的引渡。

5 N-VAR 上诉;申诉 An appeal is a formal request for a decision to be changed. 上诉;申诉

  • Heath's appeal against the sentence was later successful. [+ against]

    希思对判决不服提出上诉，后来胜诉了。

  • The jury agreed with her, but she lost the case on appeal.

    陪审团同意她的说法，但在上诉时她败诉了。

SYN retrial, reconsideration  
6See also:Court of Appeal 
7 VERB 对…有吸引力；使感兴趣 If something appeals to you, you find it attractive or interesting. 对…有吸引力；使感兴趣 [Also VERB]

  • On the other hand, the idea appealed to him. [VERB + to]

    从另一方面来说，这个想法又使他很感兴趣。

  • The range has long appealed to all tastes. [VERB to noun]

    该系列长期受到各类人士的欢迎。

8 N-UNCOUNT 吸引力;感染力 The appeal of something is a quality that it has which people find attractive or interesting. 吸引力;感染力

  • Its new title was meant to give the party greater public appeal.

    新的党名意在使该党对公众具有更强的吸引力。

  • Johnson's appeal is to people in all walks of life.

    约翰逊广受各界人士欢迎。

SYN attraction, charm, fascination, charisma  
9See also:sex appeal
