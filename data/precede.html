precede
prɪsiːd priˈsid prɪˈsid
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense precedes, present participle preceding, past tense , past participle preceded
1 VERB 发生在…之前；先于 If one event or period of time precedes another, it happens before it. 发生在…之前；先于 [formal]

  • Intensive negotiations between the main parties preceded the vote. [VERB noun]

    主要政党在选举前进行了集中的谈判。

  • The earthquake was preceded by a loud roar and lasted 20 seconds. [be VERB-ed + by]

    地震前有一阵巨大的轰隆声，持续了20秒钟。

  • Industrial orders had already fallen in the preceding months. [VERB-ing]

    工业订单在前几个月就已减少。

SYN go before, introduce, herald, pave the way for  
2 VERB （顺序、位置）先于，在…前面 If you precede someone somewhere, you go in front of them. （顺序、位置）先于，在…前面 [formal]

  • He gestured to Alice to precede them from the room. [VERB noun]

    他给艾丽斯做手势，让她在他们前面离开房间。

  • They were preceded by mounted cowboys. [be VERB-ed + by]

    他们前面是骑马的牛仔们。

SYN go ahead of, lead, head, go before  
3 VERB （句、段、章节）在…之前 A sentence, paragraph, or chapter that precedes another one comes just before it. （句、段、章节）在…之前

  • Look at the information that precedes the paragraph in question. [VERB noun]

    查看正在讨论的这段文字前面的信息。

  • Repeat the exercises described in the preceding section. [VERB-ing]

    把前一节要求的练习重做一遍。

SYN preface, introduce, go before, launch
