plucky
plʌki ˈplʌki
Word forms: comparative pluckier, superlative pluckiest
ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 有勇气的；有胆量的 If someone, for example a sick child, is described as plucky, it means that although they are weak, they face their difficulties with courage. 有勇气的；有胆量的 [journalism, approval]

  • The plucky schoolgirl amazed doctors by hanging on to life for nearly two months.

    这名勇敢的女生坚持不放弃生命近两个月的精神令医生感到震惊。

SYN courageous, spirited, brave, daring
