jog
dʒɒg dʒɑg
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense jogs, present participle jogging, past tense , past participle jogged
1 VERB （常作为锻炼）慢跑，慢步长跑 If you jog, you run slowly, often as a form of exercise. （常作为锻炼）慢跑，慢步长跑

  • I got up early the next morning to jog. [VERB]

    第二天早晨我一大早起来去慢跑。

  • He could scarcely jog around the block that first day. [VERB]

    第一天他勉强绕着整个街区跑了下来。

SYN run, trot, canter, lope  
Jog is also a noun.

  • He went for another early morning jog.

    他又去晨跑了。

jogging

  • It isn't the walking and jogging that got his weight down.

    他并不是因为散步和慢跑而瘦下来的。

2 VERB 轻推；轻撞；轻碰 If you jog something, you push or bump it slightly so that it moves. 轻推；轻撞；轻碰

  • Avoid jogging the camera. [VERB noun]

    请勿磕碰相机。

SYN nudge, push, shake, prod  
3 PHRASE 唤起…的记忆；提醒 If something or someone jogs your memory, they cause you to suddenly remember something that you had forgotten. 唤起…的记忆；提醒

  • Police have planned a reconstruction of the crime tomorrow in the hope this will jog the memory of passers-by.

    警方已计划明天再现犯罪现场，希望当时路过的人能想起些什么。
