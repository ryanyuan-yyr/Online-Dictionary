kindle
kɪndəl ˈkɪndəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense kindles, present participle kindling, past tense , past participle kindled
1 VERB 激起（感情）；使感受到 If something kindles a particular emotion in someone, it makes them start to feel it. 激起（感情）；使感受到

  • The second world war kindled his enthusiasm for politics. [VERB noun]

    第二次世界大战激起了他的政治热情。

  • These poems have helped kindle the imagination of generations of children. [VERB noun]

    这些诗激发了一代又一代孩子的想象力。

SYN arouse, excite, inspire, stir  
2 VERB 点燃 If you kindle a fire, you light paper or wood in order to start it. 点燃

  • I came in and kindled a fire in the stove. [VERB noun]

    我进来把炉子点着了。

SYN light, start, ignite, fire
