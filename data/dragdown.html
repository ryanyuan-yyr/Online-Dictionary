drag down
1 PHRASAL VERB 降低…的身份（或社会地位）；使堕落 To drag someone down means to reduce them to an inferior social status or to lower standards of behaviour. 降低…的身份（或社会地位）；使堕落

  • She dragged him down with her. [VERB noun PREP.]

    她把他拉下了水。

  • There were fears he would be dragged down by the scandal. [be V-ed P + by]

    有人担心他会因丑闻而辱没了身份。

2 PHRASAL VERB 使虚弱;使沮丧 Something that drags you down makes you feel weak or depressed. 使虚弱;使沮丧

  • I have had really bad bouts of flu that have really dragged me down. [VERB noun PREP.]

    我患上了重度流感，快被拖垮了。
