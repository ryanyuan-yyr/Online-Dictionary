enclose
ɪnkloʊz ɛnˈkloʊz ɪnˈkloʊz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense encloses, present participle enclosing, past tense , past participle enclosed
1 VERB 包围;围住;封闭 If a place or object is enclosed by something, the place or object is inside that thing or completely surrounded by it. 包围;围住;封闭 [Also VERB n]

  • The rules state that samples must be enclosed in two watertight containers. [be VERB-ed + in]

    规则要求样本必须装在两个水密容器中。

  • Enclose the pot in a clear polythene bag. [VERB noun + in]

    将罐子装在透明的塑胶袋中。

  • The surrounding land was enclosed by an eight foot wire fence. [VERB-ed]

    周围的土地围有8英尺高的铁丝栅栏。

  • ...the enclosed waters of the Baltic. [VERB-ed]

    波罗的海的封闭水域

SYN surround, cover, circle, bound  
2 VERB 把…封入信封;随函附上 If you enclose something with a letter, you put it in the same envelope as the letter. 把…封入信封;随函附上

  • I have enclosed a cheque for £10. [VERB noun]

    我已随信附上10英镑的支票。

  • He tore open the creamy envelope that had been enclosed in the letter. [VERB noun]

    他撕开了随信寄来的奶油色信封。

  • The enclosed leaflet shows how Service Care can ease all your worries. [VERB-ed]

    随信寄上的宣传页将说明“服保”如何能为您解除所有烦忧。

SYN send with, include, put in, insert
