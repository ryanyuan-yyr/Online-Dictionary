cognizance
kɒgnɪzəns ˈkɑgnəzəns ˈkɑnəzəns
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 cognisance
1 PHRASE 察觉;注意;承认 If you take cognizance of something, you take notice of it or acknowledge it. 察觉;注意;承认 [formal]

  • The government failed to take cognisance of their protest.

    政府没有察觉到他们的抗议。

2 N-UNCOUNT 认知;察知 Cognizance is knowledge or understanding. 认知;察知 [formal]

  • ...the teacher's developing cognizance of the child's intellectual activity. [+ of]

    老师对孩子智力活动的逐步认知

SYN knowledge, understanding, regard, notice
