mooch around
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 mooch about
PHRASAL VERB 徘徊;漫步;闲荡 If you mooch around or mooch about a place, you move around there slowly with no particular purpose. 徘徊;漫步;闲荡

  • Andrew was left to mooch around the house on his own. [VERB PREP. noun]

    只剩安德鲁一人在屋里走来走去。

  • He was awake at 3am, mooching about in the darkness. [VERB PREP.]

    他凌晨3点钟醒来，在黑暗中摸索。
