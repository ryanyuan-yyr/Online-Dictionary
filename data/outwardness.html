outward
aʊtwəʳd ˈaʊtwərd
1 ADJ [ADJ n] 向外的;外出的 An outward journey is a journey that you make away from a place that you are intending to return to later. 向外的;外出的

  • Tickets must be bought seven days in advance, with outward and return journey dates specified.

    购票需提前7天，并确定往返日期。

2 ADJ [ADJ n] 外表的；表面上的 The outward feelings, qualities, or attitudes of someone or something are the ones they appear to have rather than the ones that they actually have. 外表的；表面上的

  • In spite of my outward calm I was very shaken.

    我外表看似镇定，实则吓得不轻。

  • What the military rulers have done is to restore the outward appearance of order.

    军事统治者所做的只是为了恢复表面上的秩序。

SYN apparent, seeming, outside, surface  
3 ADJ [ADJ n] （特定方面）可见的，外部的 The outward features of something are the ones that you can see from the outside. （特定方面）可见的，外部的

  • Mark was lying unconscious but with no outward sign of injury.

    马克躺在那儿昏迷不醒，但看不出受伤的迹象。
