reputed
rɪpjuːtɪd rɪˈpjutɪd
V-PASSIVE 据说;相传;据称 If you say that something is reputed to be true, you mean that people say it is true, but you do not know if it is definitely true. 据说;相传;据称 [formal, vagueness]

  • The monster, which is reputed to live in the deep dark water of a Scottish loch. [be VERB-ed to-infinitive]

    相传生活在苏格兰一个漆黑深水湖中的妖怪

reputedly

  • He reputedly earns two million pounds a year.

    据说，他每年赚200万英镑。

  • Both women have dramatic dark looks and, reputedly, fiery temperaments.

    两个女人都皮肤黝黑，据说，脾气也很火暴。
