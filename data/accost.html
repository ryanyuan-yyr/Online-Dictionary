accost
əkɒst əkɔːst əˈkɔst əˈkɑst
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense accosts, present participle accosting, past tense , past participle accosted
VERB （唐突地或带有威胁性地）走近跟…攀谈，上前与…搭讪 If someone accosts another person, especially a stranger, they stop them or go up to them and speak to them in a way that seems rude or threatening. （唐突地或带有威胁性地）走近跟…攀谈，上前与…搭讪 [formal, disapproval]

  • A man had accosted me in the street. [VERB noun]

    一个男的在街上和我搭讪。

SYN confront, challenge, address, stop
