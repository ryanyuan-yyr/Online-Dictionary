contend
kəntend kənˈtɛnd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense contends, present participle contending, past tense , past participle contended
1 VERB 处理;对付;解决 If you have to contend with a problem or difficulty, you have to deal with it or overcome it. 处理;对付;解决

  • It is time, once again, to contend with racism. [VERB + with]

    又一次到了与种族主义战斗的时刻了。

  • American businesses could soon have a new kind of lawsuit to contend with. [VERB + with]

    美国公司可能很快就要有一种新型的官司去应付了。

2 VERB 声称;争辩;主张 If you contend that something is true, you state or argue that it is true. 声称;争辩;主张 [formal]

  • The government contends that he is fundamentalist. [VERB that]

    政府认定他是原教旨主义者。

  • 'You were just looking,' contends Samantha. 'I was the one doing all the work.' [VERB with quote]

    “你不过是看看而已。”萨曼莎争辩道，“什么活儿都是我来干。”

SYN argue, hold, maintain, allege  
3 V-RECIP 竞争;争夺 If you contend with someone for something such as power, you compete with them to try to get it. 竞争;争夺

  • ...the two main groups contending for power. [VERB + for]

    两大集团争权夺利

  • ...with 10 UK construction yards contending with rivals from Norway, Holland, Italy and Spain.

    10家英国建造厂与来自挪威、荷兰、意大利和西班牙的对手展开竞争

  • ...a binding political settlement between the contending parties. [VERB-ing]

    角力各派间一项具有约束力的政治协议

SYN compete, fight, struggle, clash
