embolden
ɪmboʊldən ɛmˈboʊldən ɪmˈboʊldən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense emboldens, present participle emboldening, past tense , past participle emboldened
VERB 使有胆量;使有信心;鼓励 If you are emboldened by something, it makes you feel confident enough to behave in a particular way. 使有胆量;使有信心;鼓励

  • The Prime Minister was steadily emboldened by the discovery that he faced no opposition. [be VERB-ed]

    发现自己并未遭到反对，首相渐渐有了信心。

  • Four days of non-stop demonstrations have emboldened the anti-government protesters. [VERB noun]

    持续4天不间断的示威使反政府抗议者士气高涨。

SYN encourage, cheer, stir, strengthen
