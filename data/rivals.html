rival
raɪvəl ˈraɪvəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense rivals, present participle rivalling, past tense , past participle rivalled
in AM, use 美国英语用 rivaling, rivaled
1 N-COUNT 竞争者;对手;敌手 Your rival is a person, business, or organization who you are competing or fighting against in the same area or for the same things. 竞争者;对手;敌手

  • The world champion finished more than two seconds ahead of his nearest rival.

    世界冠军领先第二名2秒多钟到达终点。

  • He eliminated his rivals in a brutal struggle for power.

    他在一场残酷的权力争斗中将对手一一铲除。

  • The police believe the fight was due to a dispute between rival teenage gangs.

    警察认为这场打斗是由两个敌对的青少年帮派之间的纠纷引起的。

SYN opponent, competitor, contender, challenger  
2 N-COUNT 没有敌手;独一无二;无人能及 If you say that someone or something has no rivals or is without rival, you mean that it is best of its type. 没有敌手;独一无二;无人能及

  • ...a wonderfully fragrant wine which has no rivals in the Rhone.

    在罗纳河地区独一无二的无比香醇的葡萄酒

  • He is a pastry chef without rival.

    他是最好的面点师。

SYN equal, match, fellow, equivalent  
3 VERB 能与…媲美;与…匹敌;与…不分高低 If you say that one thing rivals another, you mean that they are both of the same standard or quality. 能与…媲美;与…匹敌;与…不分高低

  • Cassette recorders cannot rival the sound quality of CDs. [VERB noun]

    卡式磁带录音机的音质无法和CD相媲美。

  • An epidemic to rival that which killed 26,000 in 1989 may hit the UK.

    一种流行病可能会袭击英国，其危害程度不亚于1989年导致26,000人丧生的疾病。

SYN compete with, match, equal, oppose
