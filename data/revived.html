revive
rɪvaɪv rɪˈvaɪv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense revives, present participle reviving, past tense , past participle revived
1 V-ERG (使)复苏;(使)复兴;(使)恢复;(使)再次流行 When something such as the economy, a business, a trend, or a feeling is revived or when it revives, it becomes active, popular, or successful again. (使)复苏;(使)复兴;(使)恢复;(使)再次流行

  • ...an attempt to revive the British economy. [VERB noun]

    试图复兴英国经济的努力

  • His trial revived memories of French suffering during the war. [VERB noun]

    他的受审重新勾起了在战争中受苦受难的法国人民的回忆。

  • There is no doubt that grades have improved and interest in education has revived. [VERB]

    无疑，成绩提高了，上学的兴趣也恢复了。

SYN revitalize, restore, rally, renew  
revived

  • Habib grimaced at the revived memories.

    重新涌上心头的记忆让哈比卜愁眉苦脸。

2 VERB 重新上演(戏剧、歌剧或芭蕾等) When someone revives a play, opera, or ballet, they present a new production of it. 重新上演(戏剧、歌剧或芭蕾等)

  • The Gaiety is reviving John B. Kean's comedy 'The Man from Clare'. [VERB noun]

    盖伊提剧院正在重新上演约翰·B. 基恩的喜剧《来自克莱尔的人》。

3 V-ERG (使)苏醒；(使)清醒 If you manage to revive someone who has fainted or if they revive, they become conscious again. (使)苏醒；(使)清醒

  • She and a neighbour tried in vain to revive him. [VERB noun]

    她和邻居想尽一切办法都没能让他苏醒过来。

  • With a glazed stare she revived for one last instant. [VERB]

    她最后清醒了一会儿，眼神呆滞。

SYN bring round, awaken, animate, rouse  
revived
