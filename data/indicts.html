indict
ɪndaɪt ɪnˈdaɪt ɪnˈdaɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense indicts, present participle indicting, past tense , past participle indicted
VERB [usually passive] 控告;告发;起诉 If someone is indicted for a crime, they are officially charged with it. 控告;告发;起诉 [mainly US, law]

  • He was later indicted on corruption charges. [be V-ed + on]

    他后来被指控贪污。

  • She has been indicted for possessing cocaine. [be VERB-ed + for]

    她被指控藏有可卡因。

  • The police said he'd been formally indicted on Saturday. [be VERB-ed]

    警方称他星期六已被正式起诉。

  • Attorneys for the indicted officers tried to delay the trial. [VERB-ed]

    被起诉官员的律师试图延迟审判。

SYN charge, accuse, prosecute, summon
