jettison
dʒetɪsən ˈdʒɛtəsən ˈdʒɛtəzən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense jettisons, present participle jettisoning, past tense , past participle jettisoned
1 VERB 放弃，拒绝接受（想法、计划等） If you jettison something, for example an idea or a plan, you deliberately reject it or decide not to use it. 放弃，拒绝接受（想法、计划等）

  • The Government seems to have jettisoned the plan. [VERB noun]

    政府似乎已经放弃了这个计划。

SYN abandon, reject, desert, dump  
2 VERB 丢弃；处理掉；摆脱 To jettison something that is not needed or wanted means to throw it away or get rid of it. 丢弃；处理掉；摆脱

  • The crew jettisoned excess fuel and made an emergency landing. [VERB noun]

    机组人员丢弃了多余燃料，紧急着陆。

SYN expel, dump, unload, throw overboard
