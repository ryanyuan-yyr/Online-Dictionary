foreground
fɔːʳgraʊnd ˈfɔrˌgraʊnd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense foregrounds, present participle foregrounding, past tense , past participle foregrounded
1 N-VAR [oft in the N] (照片、景物等的)前景 The foreground of a picture or scene you are looking at is the part or area of it that appears nearest to you. (照片、景物等的)前景

  • He is the bowler-hatted figure in the foreground of Orpen's famous painting.

    他是奥彭那幅名画中位于前景的戴圆顶硬礼帽的那个人物。

2 N-SING [oft in/to N] 受到关注;引人注目 If something or someone is in the foreground, or comes to the foreground, they receive a lot of attention. 受到关注;引人注目

  • This is another worry that has come to the foreground in recent years.

    这是近年来开始备受关注的另一个令人忧虑的问题。

SYN prominence, limelight, fore, forefront  
3 VERB 强调;使突出 To foreground certain features of a situation means to make them the most important part of a description or account. 强调;使突出 [formal]

  • His book foregrounds three events in which police relations with the media were central. [VERB noun]

    他的书重点讲了 3 个事件，以警察和媒体的关系为中心展开。
