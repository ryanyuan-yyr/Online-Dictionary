condense
kəndens kənˈdɛns
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense condenses, present participle condensing, past tense , past participle condensed
1 VERB 压缩,精简(尤指文章或讲话) If you condense something, especially a piece of writing or speech, you make it shorter, usually by including only the most important parts. 压缩,精简(尤指文章或讲话) [Also VERB n]

  • We have learnt how to condense serious messages into short, self-contained sentences. [VERB noun + into]

    我们已经学会了如何把重要的信息压缩成简短独立的句子。

  • The English translation may have been condensed into a single more readable book. [VERB noun into noun]

    英译本可能被压缩成了更具可读性的单行本。

SYN abridge, contract, concentrate, compact  
2 V-ERG (使)冷凝;(使)凝结 When a gas or vapour condenses, or is condensed, it changes into a liquid. (使)冷凝;(使)凝结 [Also VERB n]

  • Water vapour condenses to form clouds. [VERB]

    水蒸气凝结形成云。

  • The compressed gas is cooled and condenses into a liquid. [V + into/out of]

    压缩气体经冷却凝结成液态。

  • As the air rises it becomes colder and moisture condenses out of it. [V into/out of n]

    随着空气上升，其温度降低，凝结出水分。

SYN concentrate, reduce, precipitate [chemistry] , thicken
