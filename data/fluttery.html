flutter
flʌtəʳ ˈflʌtər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense flutters, present participle fluttering, past tense , past participle fluttered
1 V-ERG 颤动;飘动;挥动 If something thin or light flutters, or if you flutter it, it moves up and down or from side to side with a lot of quick, light movements. 颤动;飘动;挥动

  • Her chiffon skirt was fluttering in the night breeze. [VERB]

    她的雪纺裙在晚风中飘动着。

  • ...a butterfly fluttering its wings. [VERB noun]

    挥动着翅膀的蝴蝶

  • ...the fluttering white lace handkerchief. [VERB-ing]

    飘动的白色蕾丝手绢

SYN beat, bat, flap, tremble  
Flutter is also a noun.

  • ...a flutter of white cloth.

    飘扬的白布

2 VERB (小鸟)振翅飞翔;(纸片)飘飞，翩翩飞舞 If something light such as a small bird or a piece of paper flutters somewhere, it moves through the air with small quick movements. (小鸟)振翅飞翔;(纸片)飘飞，翩翩飞舞

  • The paper fluttered to the floor. [VERB adv./prep.]

    纸飘落到地板上。

  • The birds were active, whirring and fluttering among the trees. [VERB]

    鸟儿们很活跃，在树林中呼呼地飞来飞去。

SYN flit, hover, flitter  
3 N-COUNT 小赌注 If you have a flutter, you have a small bet on something such as a horse race. 小赌注 [British, informal]

  • I had a flutter on five horses. [+ on]

    我在5匹马身上下了小赌注。

SYN bet, gamble, punt [mainly British] , wager  
4 VERB 匆忙急赶;奔走 If you say that someone flutters somewhere, you mean that they walk there with quick, light movements, often in a silly way or in a way which suggests that they are nervous. 匆忙急赶;奔走

  • She'd been fluttering about in the kitchen. [VERB adv./prep.]

    她一直在厨房里忙前忙后。

5 VERB (心)扑通扑通地跳，怦怦直跳 If your heart or stomach flutters, you experience a strong feeling of excitement or anxiety. (心)扑通扑通地跳，怦怦直跳

  • The look in his eyes made my heart flutter. [VERB]

    他的眼神让我心里扑通扑通直跳。
