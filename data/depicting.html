depict
dɪpɪkt diˈpɪkt dɪˈpɪkt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense depicts, present participle depicting, past tense , past participle depicted
1 VERB 描绘;描画 To depict someone or something means to show or represent them in a work of art such as a drawing or painting. 描绘;描画

  • ...a gallery of pictures depicting Nelson's most famous battles. [VERB noun]

    描绘纳尔逊指挥的那些最著名战役的一组画作

SYN illustrate, portray, picture, paint  
2 VERB 描写;描述;刻画 To depict someone or something means to describe them or give an impression of them in writing. 描写;描述;刻画

  • Margaret Atwood's novel depicts a gloomy, futuristic America. [VERB noun]

    玛格丽特·阿特伍德的小说描述了一个黑暗无望的未来美国。

  • Children's books often depict farmyard animals as gentle, lovable creatures. [VERB noun + as]

    儿童图书常常把农场的动物描写得温和而可爱。

SYN describe, present, represent, detail
