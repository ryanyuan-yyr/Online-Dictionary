monitor
mɒnɪtəʳ ˈmɑnɪtər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense monitors, present participle monitoring, past tense , past participle monitored
1 VERB 监控;监视;监督 If you monitor something, you regularly check its development or progress, and sometimes comment on it. 监控;监视;监督

  • Officials had not been allowed to monitor the voting. [VERB noun]

    以前不允许官员监督投票。

  • You need feedback to monitor progress. [VERB noun]

    你需要利用反馈信息来监控进展。

SYN check, follow, record, watch  
monitoring
2 VERB 监听(外国无线电广播等) If someone monitors radio broadcasts from other countries, they record them or listen carefully to them in order to obtain information. 监听(外国无线电广播等)

  • Peter Murray is in London and has been monitoring reports out of Monrovia. [VERB noun]

    彼得·默里一直在伦敦监听来自蒙罗维亚的新闻报道。

3 N-COUNT [usually n NOUN] 监测仪器;监控器 A monitor is a machine that is used to check or record things, for example processes or substances inside a person's body. 监测仪器;监控器

  • The heart monitor shows low levels of consciousness.

    心脏监测器显示患者神志不清。

SYN scanner, recorder, detector  
4 N-COUNT [oft NOUN n] 显示屏;监视器 A monitor is a screen which is used to display certain kinds of information, for example in airports or television studios. 显示屏;监视器

  • He was watching a game of tennis on a television monitor.

    他在看电视转播的网球赛。

SYN screen, VDU, visual display unit  
5 N-COUNT 监督员;监控人;核查员 You can refer to a person who checks that something is done correctly, or that it is fair, as a monitor . 监督员;监控人;核查员

  • Government monitors will continue to accompany reporters.

    政府监督员将继续陪同记者。

  • ...UN monitors overseeing Namibian independence.

    监督纳米比亚独立情况的联合国核查员

SYN guide, observer, supervisor, overseer
