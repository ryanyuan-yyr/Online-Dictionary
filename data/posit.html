posit
pɒzɪt ˈpɑzɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense posits, present participle positing, past tense , past participle posited
VERB 假定；假设 If you posit something, you suggest or assume it as the basis for an argument or calculation. 假定；假设 [formal]

  • Several writers have posited the idea of a universal consciousness. [VERB noun]

    有几个作者都假设存在普遍意识。

  • Callahan posits that chemical elements radiate electromagnetic signals. [VERB that]

    卡拉汉假定化学元素放射电磁信号。

SYN put forward, advance, submit, state
