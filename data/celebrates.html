celebrate
selɪbreɪt ˈsɛləˌbreɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense celebrates, present participle celebrating, past tense , past participle celebrated
1 VERB 庆祝 If you celebrate, you do something enjoyable because of a special occasion or to mark someone's success. 庆祝

  • I was in a mood to celebrate. [VERB]

    我很想庆祝一下。

  • Tom celebrated his 24th birthday two days ago. [VERB noun]

    汤姆两天前庆祝了他的24岁生日。

SYN rejoice, party, enjoy yourself, carouse  
2 VERB 成立(…年);庆祝(周年) If an organization or country is celebrating an anniversary, it has existed for that length of time and is doing something special because of it. 成立(…年);庆祝(周年)

  • The Society is celebrating its tenth anniversary this year. [VERB noun]

    这个协会今年将举办成立10周年庆典。

3 VERB 主持(圣餐或弥撒) When priests celebrate Holy Communion or Mass, they officially perform the actions and ceremonies that are involved. 主持(圣餐或弥撒)

  • Pope John Paul celebrated mass today in a city in central Poland. [VERB noun]

    教皇保罗二世今天在波兰中部的一个城市主持了弥撒。

SYN perform, observe, preside over, officiate at
