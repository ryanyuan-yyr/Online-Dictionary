highway patrol
ˈhaɪˌweɪ pəˈtrəʊl
Word forms: highway patrols
N-COUNT [usu sing; oft N n] 公路巡逻队In the United States, the highway patrol is the part of the police force within a particular state that is responsible for making sure that the roads are safe and for dealing with drivers who break the law. 公路巡逻队

  • The Florida Highway Patrol shut down the 20-mile stretch of the interstate.

    弗罗里达公路巡逻队关闭了20英里的州际公路段。

  • ...highway patrol officers.

    ...公路巡逻队员。
