peculiar
pɪkjuːliəʳ pɪˈkjuljər
1 ADJ-GRADED 奇怪的；古怪的；不寻常的 If you describe someone or something as peculiar, you think that they are strange or unusual, sometimes in an unpleasant way. 奇怪的；古怪的；不寻常的

  • Mr Kennet has a rather peculiar sense of humour.

    肯尼特先生有种很奇怪的幽默感。

  • Rachel thought it tasted peculiar.

    雷切尔觉得它味道古怪。

SYN odd, strange, unusual, bizarre  
peculiarly

  • His face had become peculiarly expressionless.

    奇怪的是，他的脸已经变得毫无表情。

2 ADJ 特有的；特别的；独特的 If something is peculiar to a particular thing, person, or situation, it belongs or relates only to that thing, person, or situation. 特有的；特别的；独特的

  • The problem is by no means peculiar to America. [+ to]

    这个问题绝对不只限于美国。

peculiarly

  • Cricket is so peculiarly English.

    板球是颇具英国特色的运动。

3 ADJ-GRADED [verb-link ADJ] 有点不舒服的；稍感眩晕的 If you say that you feel peculiar, you mean that you feel slightly ill or unsteady. 有点不舒服的；稍感眩晕的

  • All this has made me feel quite peculiar.

    这一切令我觉得有点儿不舒服。

SYN ill, poorly [informal] , sick, crook [Australian , New Zealand , informal]
