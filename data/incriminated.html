incriminate
ɪnkrɪmɪneɪt ɪnˈkrɪmɪˌneɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense incriminates, present participle incriminating, past tense , past participle incriminated
VERB 暗示…有罪;牵连;连累 If something incriminates you, it suggests that you are responsible for something bad, especially a crime. 暗示…有罪;牵连;连累

  • He claimed that the drugs had been planted to incriminate him. [VERB noun]

    他声称毒品是别人为了陷害他而故意放在那里的。

  • They are afraid of incriminating themselves and say no more than is necessary. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    他们害怕受牵连,一句话也不肯多说。

SYN implicate, involve, accuse, blame  
incriminating

  • Police had reportedly searched his flat and found incriminating evidence.

    据传闻,警方已经搜查了他的公寓并发现了他涉案的证据。
