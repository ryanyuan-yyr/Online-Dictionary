lynch
lɪntʃ lɪntʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense lynches, present participle lynching, past tense , past participle lynched
VERB 以私刑绞死（某人） If an angry crowd of people lynch someone, they kill that person by hanging them, without letting them have a trial, because they believe that that person has committed a crime. 以私刑绞死（某人）

  • They were about to lynch him when reinforcements from the army burst into the room and rescued him. [VERB noun]

    他们正准备动私刑绞死他时，援军闯入房间把他救下了。

SYN hang, kill, execute, put to death  
lynching

  • Some towns found that lynching was the only way to drive away bands of outlaws.

    一些城镇发现，私处绞刑是赶走匪帮的唯一办法。
