normalize
nɔːʳməlaɪz ˈnɔrməˌlaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense normalizes, present participle normalizing, past tense , past participle normalized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 normalise
1 V-ERG (使)正常化;(使)变得正常 When you normalize a situation or when it normalizes, it becomes normal. (使)正常化;(使)变得正常

  • Meditation tends to lower or normalize blood pressure. [VERB noun]

    冥想有助于降低血压或使血压恢复正常。

  • There may be some deep-seated emotional reason which has to be dealt with before your eating habits normalize. [VERB]

    要使饮食习惯正常，你也许要先处理好一些根深蒂固的情感问题。

2 V-RECIP-ERG (使)(关系)正常化 If people, groups, or governments normalize relations, or when relations normalize, they become normal or return to normal. (使)(关系)正常化

  • The two governments were close to normalizing relations. [VERB noun]

    两国政府即将恢复正常邦交。

  • The United States says they are not prepared to join in normalizing ties with their former enemy. [VERB noun + with]

    美国表示他们不准备加入到和宿敌恢复正常关系的行动中。

  • If relations between Hanoi and Washington begin to normalise, anything is possible. [VERB]

    如果河内和华盛顿之间的关系开始恢复正常，任何事情都有可能。
