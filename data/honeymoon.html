honeymoon
hʌnimuːn ˈhʌniˌmun
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense honeymoons, present participle honeymooning, past tense , past participle honeymooned
1 N-COUNT （新婚夫妇的）蜜月 A honeymoon is a holiday taken by a man and a woman who have just got married. （新婚夫妇的）蜜月
2 VERB 度蜜月 When a recently-married couple honeymoon somewhere, they go there on their honeymoon. 度蜜月

  • They honeymooned in Venice. [VERB]

    他们在威尼斯度了蜜月。

  • ...honeymooning Japanese tourists. [VERB-ing]

    正在度蜜月的日本游客

3 N-COUNT （新工作开始或新政府成立后的）和谐时期，蜜月期 You can use honeymoon to refer to a period of time after the start of a new job or new government when everyone is pleased with the person or people concerned and is nice to them. （新工作开始或新政府成立后的）和谐时期，蜜月期

  • Austrians voted for EU membership by a two-to-one majority in a June 1994 referendum, but the honeymoon is definitely over.

    奥地利在1994年6月举行的全民公决中以2比1的多数票支持加入欧盟，但这一蜜月期明显已经结束了。

  • Brett is enjoying a honeymoon period with both press and public.

    布雷特和报界及公众之间都正处在蜜月期。
