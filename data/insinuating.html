insinuating
ɪnsɪnjueɪtɪŋ ɪnˈsɪnjʊeɪtɪŋ
ADJ 含沙射影的;旁敲侧击的 If you describe someone's words or voice as insinuating, you mean that they are saying in an indirect way that something bad is the case. 含沙射影的;旁敲侧击的 [disapproval]

  • Marcus kept making insinuating remarks.

    马库斯一直在说些含沙射影的话。

  • 'Yes.' My tone was insinuating. 'I heard she was a real friendly girl.'

    “是啊。”我语带暗示地说：“我听说她是个非常友善的女孩。”
