wistful
wɪstfʊl ˈwɪstfəl
ADJ-GRADED (因求而不得)失意的，伤感的，惆怅的 Someone who is wistful is rather sad because they want something and know that they cannot have it. (因求而不得)失意的，伤感的，惆怅的

  • I can't help feeling slightly wistful about the perks I'm giving up. [+ about]

    我不禁对自己将要放弃的津贴感到有点儿不舍。

  • He has a wistful look.

    他神情沮丧。

SYN melancholy, longing, dreaming, sad  
wistfully

  • 'I wish I had a little brother,' said Daphne wistfully.

    “我希望有个小弟弟。”达夫妮伤感地说。

wistfulness

  • I sensed her wistfulness when she talked about vacations her relatives took.

    她谈起她的亲戚度假时，我觉得她心里酸酸的。
