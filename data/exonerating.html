exonerate
ɪgzɒnəreɪt ɪɡˈzɒnəˌreɪt ɛgˈzɑnərˌeɪt ɪgˈzɑnəˌreɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense exonerates, present participle exonerating, past tense , past participle exonerated
VERB 使免于罪责;证明…无罪;宣布…无罪 If a court, report, or person in authority exonerates someone, they officially say or show that that person is not responsible for something wrong or unpleasant that has happened. 使免于罪责;证明…无罪;宣布…无罪 [formal]

  • The official report basically exonerated everyone. [VERB noun]

    官方报道基本上宣布所有人无罪。

  • An investigation exonerated the school from any blame. [VERB noun + from]

    一项调查证明该学校没有任何过失。

SYN acquit, clear, excuse, pardon
