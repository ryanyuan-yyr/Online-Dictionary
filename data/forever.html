forever
fərevəʳ fɔːˈrɛvə fɔrˈɛvər fərˈɛvər
(also for ever)
The spelling for ever is also used for meanings 1, 2, and 3. 拼写 for ever 亦用于义项1，2和3。
1 ADV [ADVERB with verb] 永远;永恒;永久 If you say that something will happen or continue forever, you mean that it will always happen or continue. 永远;永恒;永久

  • I think that we will live together forever.

    我想我们会永远生活在一起。

  • It was great fun but we knew it wouldn't go on for ever.

    这很开心，但是我们知道不会永远这样。

  • I will forever be grateful for his considerable input.

    我将永远感激他的大量投入。

2 ADV [ADVERB after verb] 完全地;永恒地 If something has gone or changed forever, it has gone or changed completely and permanently. 完全地;永恒地

  • The old social order was gone forever.

    旧的社会秩序一去不复返了。

  • Their lives changed forever.

    他们的生活完全改变了。

3 ADV [ADVERB after verb] 长久地;无尽头地 If you say that something takes forever or lasts forever, you are emphasizing that it takes or lasts a very long time, or that it seems to. 长久地;无尽头地 [informal, emphasis]

  • The drive seemed to take forever.

    车似乎永远也开不到头。

  • They didn't cost anything and they lasted forever.

    它们花不了多少钱，却非常耐用。

4 ADV [ADV before v-cont] 老是;没完没了地 If you say that someone is forever doing a particular thing, especially something which annoys or amuses you, you are emphasizing that they do it very often. 老是;没完没了地 [informal, emphasis]

  • He was forever attempting to arrange deals.

    他老是想撮合交易。

  • I was forever dragging him away from the fireplace.

    我老是要把他从壁炉边拉开。

SYN constantly, always, all the time, continually  
5 ADV [ADVERB adjective] 总是;始终 You use forever to emphasize that someone always has or shows the quality mentioned. 总是;始终 [emphasis]

  • Katherine was forever secretive.

    凯瑟琳总是神神秘秘的。

  • To this end the young child is forever watchful.

    这个年纪不大的孩子为此始终保持警惕。
