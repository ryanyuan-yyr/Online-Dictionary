plaster
plɑːstəʳ plæs- ˈplæstər ˈplɑstər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense plasters, present participle plastering, past tense , past participle plastered
1 N-UNCOUNT 灰泥；灰浆；熟石膏 Plaster is a smooth paste made of sand, lime, and water which goes hard when it dries. Plaster is used to cover walls and ceilings and is also used to make sculptures. 灰泥；灰浆；熟石膏

  • There were huge cracks in the plaster, and the green shutters were faded.

    灰泥上出现多条大裂缝，绿色百叶窗也褪色了。

  • In the Mus d'Orsay in Paris is a sculpture in plaster by Rodin.

    巴黎的奥赛博物馆里有一件罗丹的石膏雕塑作品。

SYN mortar, stucco, gypsum, plaster of Paris  
2 VERB 涂灰泥于；在…上抹灰浆 If you plaster a wall or ceiling, you cover it with a layer of plaster. 涂灰泥于；在…上抹灰浆

  • The ceiling he had just plastered fell in and knocked him off his ladder. [VERB noun]

    他刚抹上灰泥的天花板塌了，把他从梯子上砸了下来。

3 VERB 在…上大量粘贴；贴满 If you plaster a surface or a place with posters or pictures, you stick a lot of them all over it. 在…上大量粘贴；贴满

  • They plastered the city with posters condemning her election. [VERB noun + with]

    他们在城里到处张贴谴责她当选的海报。

  • His room is plastered with pictures of Porsches and Ferraris. [be VERB-ed + with]

    他的房间里贴满保时捷和法拉利车的图片。

4 VERB （给自己）涂上，抹上 If you plaster yourself in some kind of sticky substance, you cover yourself in it. （给自己）涂上，抹上

  • She plastered herself from head to toe in high factor sun lotion. [V pron-refl + in]

    她给自己从头到脚都抹上了强效防晒露。

5 N-COUNT 膏药；橡皮膏；创可贴 A plaster is a strip of sticky material used for covering small cuts or sores on your body. 膏药；橡皮膏；创可贴 [British]
in AM, usually 美国英语通常用 Band-Aid
6 See also: plastered 
7 PHRASE 打着石膏；上着筒形石膏夹 If you have a leg or arm in plaster, you have a cover made of plaster of Paris around your leg or arm, in order to protect a broken bone and allow it to mend. 打着石膏；上着筒形石膏夹 [mainly British]
in AM, use 美国英语用 in a cast
