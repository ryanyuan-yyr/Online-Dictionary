carouse
kəraʊz kəˈraʊz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense carouses, present participle carousing, past tense caroused
VERB 喝酒狂欢;狂欢作乐 If you say that people are carousing, you mean that they are behaving very noisily and drinking a lot of alcohol as they enjoy themselves. 喝酒狂欢;狂欢作乐

  • They told him to stay home with his wife instead of going out and carousing with friends. [VERB + with]

    他们叫他在家陪老婆,不要出去和朋友们饮酒作乐。

SYN drink, booze [informal] , revel, imbibe [formal]  
carousing

  • The singing and carousing did not end until after midnight.

    人们把酒纵歌,一直闹腾到后半夜。
