sign off
1 PHRASAL VERB (在信的结尾)写结束语;(挂电话、广播节目结束时)就说到这，到此为止 If someone signs off, they write a final message at the end of a letter or they say a final message at the end of a telephone conversation. You can say that people such as entertainers sign off when they finish a broadcast. (在信的结尾)写结束语;(挂电话、广播节目结束时)就说到这，到此为止

  • O.K. I'll sign off. We'll talk at the beginning of the week. [VERB PREP.]

    好，就说到这儿吧，下周初我们再谈。

  • He would sign off each week with the catch-phrase, 'I'll see thee!' [VERB PREP.]

    每周结束节目时他都会说那句经典的话，“下周见！”。

2 PHRASAL VERB 注销(领取失业救济金的)登记 When someone who has been unemployed signs off, they officially inform the authorities that they have found a job, so that they no longer receive money from the government. 注销(领取失业救济金的)登记 [British] [Also VERB PREPOSITION]

  • If you work without signing off the dole you are breaking the law. [VERB PREP. noun]

    如果就业后未注销领取失业救济金的登记视为触犯法律。
