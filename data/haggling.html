haggle
hægəl ˈhægəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense haggles, present participle haggling, past tense , past participle haggled
V-RECIP （尤指就价格）争论;讨价还价 If you haggle, you argue about something before reaching an agreement, especially about the cost of something that you are buying. （尤指就价格）争论;讨价还价

  • Ella taught her how to haggle with used furniture dealers. [VERB + with]

    埃拉教她如何和旧家具经销商砍价。

  • Meanwhile, as the politicians haggle, the violence worsens. [VERB]

    在政客们大打嘴仗的同时，暴力事件愈演愈烈。

  • Of course he'll still haggle over the price. [VERB]

    他一准还会杀价。

SYN bargain, barter, beat down, drive a hard bargain  
Haggle is also a noun.

  • She laughed again, enjoying the haggle.

    她又大笑起来，享受着砍价的乐趣。

haggling

  • After months of haggling, they recovered only three-quarters of what they had lent.

    经过几个月的讨价还价后，他们只收回了贷出款项的3/4。
