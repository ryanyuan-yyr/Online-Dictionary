relish
relɪʃ ˈrɛlɪʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense relishes, present participle relishing, past tense , past participle relished
1 VERB 喜爱;玩味;欣赏;品味 If you relish something, you get a lot of enjoyment from it. 喜爱;玩味;欣赏;品味

  • I relish the challenge of doing jobs that others turn down. [VERB noun]

    我喜欢挑战别人拒绝做的工作。

  • He ate quietly, relishing his meal. [VERB noun]

    他安静地吃着，细细品味着食物。

SYN enjoy, like, prefer, taste  
Relish is also a noun.

  • The three men ate with relish.

    这3个人吃得津津有味。

2 VERB 盼望;期待;享受 If you relish the idea, thought, or prospect of something, you are looking forward to it very much. 盼望;期待;享受

  • Jacqueline is not relishing the prospect of another spell in prison. [VERB noun]

    杰奎琳可不想再蹲一阵子监狱。

  • He relished the idea of getting some cash. [VERB noun]

    他盼望得到一些现金。

SYN look forward to, fancy [informal] , long for, delight in  
3 N-MASS 开胃小菜;调味品 Relish is a sauce or pickle that you eat with other food in order to give the other food more flavour. 开胃小菜;调味品
