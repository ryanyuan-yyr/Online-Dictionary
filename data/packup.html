pack up
1 PHRASAL VERB 打点（行装） If you pack up or if you pack up your things, you put your possessions or the things you have been using in a case or bag, because you are leaving. 打点（行装） [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • They packed up and went home. [VERB PREP.]

    他们打点行囊，回家了。

  • He began packing up his things. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他开始收拾行李。

SYN put away, store, tidy up  
2 PHRASAL VERB （机器）出故障，停止工作；（身体部位）衰竭 If a machine or a part of the body packs up, it stops working. （机器）出故障，停止工作；（身体部位）衰竭 [British, informal]

  • In the end it was his stomach and lungs that packed up. [VERB PREP.]

    最后，他的胃和肺衰竭了。

  • Our car packed up. [VERB PREP.]

    我们的车抛锚了。

SYN break down, stop, fail, stall
