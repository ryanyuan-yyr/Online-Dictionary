terrace
terɪs ˈtɛrəs
Word forms: plural terraces
1 N-COUNT; N-IN-NAMES 排屋(指相互连接、式样相似的房屋) A terrace is a row of similar houses joined together by their side walls. 排屋(指相互连接、式样相似的房屋) [British]

  • ...a terrace of stylish Victorian houses. [+ of]

    一排雅致的维多利亚风格的房屋

  • ...3 Queensborough Terrace.

    昆斯伯勒排屋 3 号

2 N-COUNT (房屋外的)草坪，平台 A terrace is a flat area of stone or grass next to a building where people can sit. (房屋外的)草坪，平台

  • Some guests recline in lounge chairs on the sea-facing terrace.

    在面向大海的平台上一些客人斜躺在躺椅上。

3 N-COUNT [usually plural] 梯田;阶地 Terraces are a series of flat areas built like steps on the side of a hill so that crops can be grown there. 梯田;阶地

  • ...massive terraces of maize and millet carved into the mountainside like giant steps. [+ of]

    山坡上开垦出的像巨型台阶一样的大块大块梯田，上面种着玉米和小米

4 N-PLURAL (足球赛观众可站立的)阶梯看台 The terraces at a football ground are wide steps that people can stand on when they are watching a game. (足球赛观众可站立的)阶梯看台 [British]
