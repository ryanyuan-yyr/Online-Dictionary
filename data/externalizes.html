externalize
ɪkstɜːʳnəlaɪz ɪkˈstɜːnəˌlaɪz ɪkˈstɪərɪəˌraɪz ɛkˈstɜrnəlˌaɪz ɪkˈstɜrnəlˌaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense externalizes, present participle externalizing, past tense , past participle externalized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 externalise
VERB 表露;公开表达 If you externalize your ideas or feelings, you express them openly, in words or actions. 表露;公开表达 [formal]

  • For the Prime Minister, externalising the problem would divert attention from his domestic troubles. [VERB noun]

    对于首相而言，公开这一问题将可转移对他所面临的国内问题的注意力。

  • These are people who tend to externalize blame when anything goes wrong at work. [VERB noun]

    这是些工作中一出现问题就往往归诸外因的人。
