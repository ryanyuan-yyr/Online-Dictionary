strike down
1 PHRASAL VERB [usually passive] 摧垮;使病倒;使丧命 If someone is struck down, especially by an illness, they are killed or severely harmed by it. 摧垮;使病倒;使丧命 [written]

  • Frank had been struck down by a massive heart attack. [be VERB-ed PREP.]

    弗兰克的身体已经被一场严重的心脏病拖垮了。

  • ...a great sporting hero, struck down at 49. [VERB-ed PREP.]

    在 49 岁时因病逝世的一个了不起的体坛英雄人物

SYN kill [informal] , destroy, slay [archaic , literary] , ruin  
2 PHRASAL VERB 废止，取缔，终结(非法的法律或规定) If a judge or court strikes down a law or regulation, they say that it is illegal and end it. 废止，取缔，终结(非法的法律或规定) [US]

  • The Supreme Court today struck down a law that prevents criminals from profiting from books or movies about their crimes. [VERB PREP. noun]

    最高法院今天废止了一项关于禁止罪犯把其罪行写成书或拍成电影而从中牟利的法律。
