corral
kərɑːl kɒˈrɑːl -ræl kəˈræl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense corrals, present participle corralling, past tense , past participle corralled
1 N-COUNT (北美的)畜栏，围栏 In North America, a corral is a space surrounded by a fence where cattle or horses are kept. (北美的)畜栏，围栏
SYN enclosure, yard, pen, confine  
2 VERB 把(动物)关进畜栏;把(人)关起来 To corral a person or animal means to capture or confine them. 把(动物)关进畜栏;把(人)关起来 [mainly US]

  • Within hours, police corralled the three men Lewis had named. [VERB noun]

    几小时之内，警察就抓到了刘易斯提到的那三个人。

SYN enclose, confine, cage, mew
