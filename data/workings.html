working
wɜːʳkɪŋ ˈwɜrkɪŋ
Word forms: plural workings
1 ADJ [ADJ n] 有工作的;有职业的 Working people have jobs which they are paid to do. 有工作的;有职业的

  • Like working women anywhere, Asian women are buying convenience foods.

    像其他地方的职业女性一样，亚洲女性也购买方便食品。

SYN employed, labouring, in work, in a job  
2 ADJ [ADJ n] 从事普通工作的 Working people are ordinary people who do not have professional or very highly paid jobs. 从事普通工作的

  • The needs and opinions of ordinary working people were ignored.

    普通劳动者的需求和意见遭到忽视。

  • One or two, in blue suits, might have been bank officials. Others were clearly working men.

    有一两个穿蓝色西装的人可能是银行职员，其他的显然都是普通工人。

3 ADJ [ADJ n] 上班时间的;工作时间的 A working day or week is the amount of time during a normal day or week which you spend doing your job. 上班时间的;工作时间的 [mainly British]

  • For doctors the working day often has no end.

    医生常常没有下班的时候。

  • Automation would bring a shorter, more flexible working week.

    自动化会使工作周缩短，也更为灵活。

in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 workday, work week
4 ADJ [ADJ n] 工作日的;上班的 A working day is a day on which people go to work. 工作日的;上班的 [mainly British]

  • The full effect will not be apparent until Tuesday, the first working day after the three day holiday weekend.

    周二是周末3天假期后的第一个工作日，到那时效果才会完全显现出来。

in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 workday
5 ADJ [ADJ n] （人生阶段）工作的，处于就业年龄的 Your working life is the period of your life in which you have a job or are of a suitable age to have a job. （人生阶段）工作的，处于就业年龄的

  • He started his working life as a truck driver.

    他的工作生涯是从当卡车司机开始的。

6 ADJ [ADJ n] (某一地区的人口)就业的，处于就业年龄的 The working population of an area consists of all the people in that area who have a job or who are of a suitable age to have a job. (某一地区的人口)就业的，处于就业年龄的

  • Almost 13 per cent of the working population is already unemployed.

    就业人口中有将近13%已失业。

7 ADJ [ADJ n] （条件或常规做法）工作的，工作上的 Working conditions or practices are ones which you have in your job. （条件或常规做法）工作的，工作上的

  • The strikers are demanding higher pay and better working conditions.

    罢工者要求加薪并改善工作环境。

8 ADJ [ADJ n] （衣服）工作时穿的 Working clothes are designed for doing work in, and are intended to be practical rather than attractive. （衣服）工作时穿的
9 ADJ [ADJ n] （关系）工作上的，同事间的 A working relationship is the relationship you have with someone when you work with them. （关系）工作上的，同事间的

  • A working relationship turned into a very close friendship.

    同事关系变成了非常亲密的友谊。

  • The vice-president seems to have a good working relationship with the president.

    副总统似乎与总统有着良好的工作关系。

10 ADJ [ADJ n] (农场或企业)经营性的，营利性的 A working farm or business exists to do normal work and make a profit, and not only for tourists or as someone's hobby. (农场或企业)经营性的，营利性的
11 ADJ [ADJ n] (机器零部件)能运转的，用于操作的 The working parts of a machine are the parts which move and operate the machine, in contrast to the outer case or container in which they are enclosed. (机器零部件)能运转的，用于操作的
12 ADJ [ADJ n] (模型)活动的，可操作的 A working model is one that has parts that move. (模型)活动的，可操作的
13 ADJ [ADJ n] (知识)够用的;（多数票）有效的 A working knowledge or majority is not very great, but is enough to be useful. (知识)够用的;（多数票）有效的

  • This book was designed in order to provide a working knowledge of finance and accounts.

    本书旨在提供金融和会计方面的实用知识。

  • Neither candidate won a working majority.

    两位候选人都没有赢得有效的多数票。

14 ADJ [ADJ n] (题目或定义)暂用的，暂定的 A working title or definition is one which you use when starting to make or do something, but which you are likely to change or improve. (题目或定义)暂用的，暂定的

  • His working title for the script was 'Trust the People'.

    他给这个剧本暂定的标题是《相信人民》。

15 N-PLURAL (设备、组织或系统的)工作方式，运行方式 The workings of a piece of equipment, an organization, or a system are the ways in which it operates and the processes which are involved in it. (设备、组织或系统的)工作方式，运行方式

  • Neural networks are computer systems which mimic the workings of the brain. [+ of]

    神经网络是模拟大脑工作方式的计算机系统。

  • The bill would give people the right to much more information about the workings of government.

    该法案将赋予人们了解更多有关政府运作方面信息的权利。

SYN operation, running, action, method  
16 N-PLURAL 矿坑;巷道 You can use workings to refer to deep tunnels or holes which have been dug in the ground in order to remove coal, metal, or stone. 矿坑;巷道

  • ...housing which was built above old mine workings.

    修建在老矿坑上的房屋
