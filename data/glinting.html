glint
glɪnt glɪnt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense glints, present participle glinting, past tense , past participle glinted
1 VERB 闪闪发光；闪亮 If something glints, it produces or reflects a quick flash of light. 闪闪发光；闪亮 [written] [Also V + off]

  • The sea glinted in the sun. [VERB]

    阳光下的海面波光粼粼。

  • Sunlight glinted on his spectacles. [VERB + on]

    阳光照射下，他的眼镜片闪着光。

  • ...the glinting ripples of water. [VERB-ing]

    水面上泛着波光的涟漪

SYN gleam, flash, shine, sparkle  
2 N-COUNT 闪光；闪烁 A glint is a quick flash of light. 闪光；闪烁 [written]

  • ...a glint of silver. [+ of]

    银光闪闪

  • ...glints of sunlight. [+ of]

    阳光闪耀

SYN gleam, flash, shine, sparkle  
3 VERB （眼睛）闪光，闪动，闪亮 If someone's eyes glint, they shine and express a particular emotion. （眼睛）闪光，闪动，闪亮 [written]

  • Her eyes glinted wildly. [VERB]

    她眼里流露出激动的神情。

  • A mischievous spark glinted in his eyes. [VERB]

    他眼中闪现出一丝顽皮的神情。

4 N-SING （眼神的）闪光，闪动，闪亮 A glint in someone's eyes is a brightness that expresses a particular emotion. （眼神的）闪光，闪动，闪亮

  • He came up to me with the glint of triumph in his eye.

    他向我走来，眼里闪现出一丝胜利的神情。
