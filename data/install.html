install
ɪnstɔːl ɪnˈstɔl
(also instal)Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense installs, present participle installing, past tense , past participle installed
1 VERB 安装;设置 If you install a piece of equipment, you fit it or put it somewhere so that it is ready to be used. 安装;设置

  • They had installed a new phone line in the apartment. [VERB noun]

    他们已经在公寓里装上了新的电话线。

SYN set up, put in, place, position  
installation

  • Hundreds of lives could be saved if the installation of alarms was more widespread.

    如果报警器得到更广泛的安装,成百上千的生命就可能被挽救。

2 VERB 使就职;任命 If someone is installed in a new job or important position, they are officially given the job or position, often in a special ceremony. 使就职;任命 [Also V n as n]

  • A new Catholic bishop was installed in Galway yesterday. [be VERB-ed]

    昨天在戈尔韦任命了一位新的天主教主教。

  • Professor Sawyer was formally installed as President last Thursday. [be VERB-ed + as]

    索耶教授上周四被正式任命为校长。

  • The army has promised to install a new government within a week. [VERB noun]

    军队已经许诺在一周内任命新一届政府。

SYN institute, establish, introduce, invest  
installation

  • He sent a letter inviting Naomi to attend his installation as chief of his tribe.

    他给娜奥米寄了封信,邀请她来参加他就任部落酋长的仪式。

3 VERB 安顿;安置 If you install yourself in a particular place, you settle there and make yourself comfortable. 安顿;安置 [formal]

  • Before her husband's death she had installed herself in a modern villa. [VERB pronoun-reflexive prep./adv.]

    丈夫去世之前她已经在一所现代化别墅里安顿下来了。

SYN settle, position, plant, establish
