mellow
meloʊ ˈmɛloʊ
Word forms: comparative mellower, superlative mellowest, 3rd person singular present tense mellows, present participle mellowing, past tense , past participle mellowed
1 ADJ-GRADED (颜色)柔和的,温暖的 Mellow is used to describe things that have a pleasant, soft, rich colour, usually red, orange, yellow, or brown. (颜色)柔和的,温暖的

  • ...a classic Queen Anne house of mellow red brick with a white portico.

    一座典型的安妮女王时代的房屋，有着色彩柔和的红砖和白色的门廊

  • ...this mellow Cotswold manor house.

    这座古色古香的科茨沃尔德庄园宅邸

  • ...the softer, mellower light of evening.

    夜晚更为温和、柔媚的光线

2 ADJ-GRADED (声音)悦耳的，圆润的;(味道)香醇的 A mellow sound or flavour is pleasant, smooth, and rich. (声音)悦耳的，圆润的;(味道)香醇的

  • His voice was deep and mellow and his speech had a soothing and comforting quality.

    他的嗓音低沉悦耳，他的讲话能抚慰人心。

  • ...the mellow background music.

    舒缓的背景音乐

  • ...a delightfully mellow, soft and balanced wine.

    口感芳醇、柔和、恰到好处的葡萄酒

SYN tuneful, full, rich, soft  
3 V-ERG (使)变平和;(使)变得成熟 If someone mellows or if something mellows them, they become kinder or less extreme in their behaviour, especially as a result of growing older. (使)变平和;(使)变得成熟

  • When the children married and had children of their own, he mellowed a little. [VERB]

    等孩子们结了婚又有了自己的小孩以后，他变得平和了一些。

  • Marriage had not mellowed him. [VERB noun]

    婚姻并没有让他成熟起来。

Mellow is also an adjective.

  • Is she more mellow and tolerant?

    她更成熟、更宽容吗？

4 ADJ-GRADED (尤指喝过酒或品尝过美食后)怡然自得的 If someone is mellow, they feel very relaxed and cheerful, especially as the result of alcohol or good food. (尤指喝过酒或品尝过美食后)怡然自得的 [informal]

  • He'd had a few glasses of champagne himself and was fairly mellow.

    他自己也已几杯香槟下肚，很是飘飘然。

  • At the other tables couples were now in a mellow mood, chattering happily and drinking.

    其他桌上的情侣们正一边饮酒一边畅谈，兴致很高。
