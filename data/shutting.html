shut
ʃʌt ʃʌt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense shuts, present participle shutting
The form shut is used in the present tense and is the past tense and past participle. shut 的过去式和过去分词与原形相同。
1 V-ERG 关闭;关上;合上 If you shut something such as a door or if it shuts, it moves so that it fills a hole or a space. 关闭;关上;合上

  • Just make sure you shut the gate after you. [VERB noun]

    进来后务必关上大门。

  • The screen door shut gently. [VERB]

    纱门轻轻关上了。

SYN close, secure, fasten, bar  
Shut is also an adjective.

  • They have warned residents to stay inside and keep their doors and windows shut.

    他们业已提醒居民呆在家里，将门窗关好。

  • The exit doors were locked shut.

    紧急出口被锁上了。

2 VERB 闭上(眼睛) If you shut your eyes, you lower your eyelids so that you cannot see anything. 闭上(眼睛)

  • Lucy shut her eyes so she wouldn't see it happen. [VERB noun]

    露西闭上了眼睛，这样就不会看到它发生了。

Shut is also an adjective.

  • His eyes were shut and he seemed to have fallen asleep.

    他眼睛闭着，似乎睡着了。

3 V-ERG 闭上(嘴巴);(嘴巴)合上 If your mouth shuts or if you shut your mouth, you place your lips firmly together. 闭上(嘴巴);(嘴巴)合上

  • Daniel's mouth opened, and then shut again. [VERB]

    丹尼尔嘴巴张了张又合上了。

  • He opened and shut his mouth, unspeaking. [VERB noun]

    他张了张嘴，欲言又止。

Shut is also an adjective.

  • She was silent for a moment, lips tight shut, eyes distant.

    她沉默了一会儿，嘴唇紧闭，眼神茫然。

4 V-ERG (使)(商店、酒吧等公共场所)关门，停止营业，休息 When a store, bar, or other public building shuts or when someone shuts it, it is closed and you cannot use it until it is open again. (使)(商店、酒吧等公共场所)关门，停止营业，休息

  • There is a tendency to shut museums or shops at a moment's notice. [VERB noun]

    现在博物馆或商店有一种说关就关的趋势。

  • Shops usually shut from noon-3pm, and stay open late. [VERB]

    商店通常中午到下午 3 点关门，然后营业到很晚。

  • What time do the pubs shut? [VERB]

    酒吧什么时候打烊？

Shut is also an adjective.

  • Make sure you have food to tide you over when the local shop may be shut.

    要确保在当地商店可能关门时也有食物可以果腹。

5 PHRASE 故意忽视;故意不予理会;对…视若罔闻 If you say that someone shuts their eyes to something, you mean that they deliberately ignore something which they should deal with. 故意忽视;故意不予理会;对…视若罔闻 [disapproval]

  • We shut our eyes to the plainest facts, refusing to admit the truth.

    我们对最浅显的事实视而不见，拒绝承认真相。

  • She was shutting her eyes to reality, just as she had done after Matthew died.

    她不肯正视现实，跟她在马修去世后的表现如出一辙。

6 PHRASE 保密;三缄其口 If someone tells you to keep your mouth shut about something, they are telling you not to let anyone else know about it. 保密;三缄其口

  • I don't have to tell you how important it is for you to keep your mouth shut about all this.

    我不用告诉你，你自己也知道对所有这些事保密有多重要了吧。

  • He paid my brother to kill Norton and keep his mouth shut.

    他花钱雇我兄弟去杀了诺顿并给了他封口费。

7 PHRASE 保持沉默;不发表意见 If you keep your mouth shut, you do not express your opinions about something, even though you would like to. 保持沉默;不发表意见

  • If she had kept her mouth shut she would still have her job now.

    如果她当时保持沉默的话，她现在工作也就不会丢。

8 PHRASE [NOUN inflects] 住口;闭嘴 If someone tells you to shut your mouth or shut your face, they are telling you very rudely to stop talking. 住口;闭嘴 [informal, rude, disapproval]

  • 'Oi, shut your mouth and have respect for elders,' Langda said to the boy.

    “喂， 闭嘴，对长者要尊重，”兰达对那男孩说。
