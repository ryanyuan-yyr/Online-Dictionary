descent
dɪsent diˈsɛnt dɪˈsɛnt
Word forms: plural descents
1 N-VAR 下降;降落 A descent is a movement from a higher to a lower level or position. 下降;降落

  • Sixteen of the youngsters set off for help, but during the descent three collapsed in the cold and rain.

    16 名年轻人出发去寻求帮助，但在下山过程中有 3 人因寒冷和大雨晕倒了。

  • ...the crash of an Airbus A300 on its descent into Kathmandu airport. [+ into]

    空客 A300 在加德满都机场降落时的坠毁

SYN fall, drop, plunge, coming down  
2 N-COUNT 斜坡;斜面 A descent is a surface that slopes downwards, for example the side of a steep hill. 斜坡;斜面

  • On the descents, cyclists spin past cars, freewheeling downhill at tremendous speed.

    在坡道上，自行车手飞驰着超越了一辆辆汽车，靠着惯性一路疾冲下山。

SYN slope, drop, dip, incline  
3 N-SING (形势的)恶化;(人的)堕落，沦落 When you want to emphasize that a situation becomes very bad, you can talk about someone's or something's descent into that situation. (形势的)恶化;(人的)堕落，沦落 [emphasis]

  • The only lasting solution lies in a political settlement. Without it, their descent into chaos will be guaranteed.

    唯一的长久之计是通过政治手段解决。否则，他们势必会陷入一片混乱。

  • ...his swift descent from respected academic to struggling small businessman. [+ from/to]

    他从一名受人尊敬的学者到一个度日艰难的小商人的瞬间沦落

SYN decline, deterioration, degradation, decadence  
4 N-UNCOUNT [usu of adj N] 血统;家世;出身 You use descent to talk about a person's family background, for example their nationality or social status. 血统;家世;出身 [formal]

  • All the contributors were of African descent.

    所有的撰稿人都是非裔人士。

SYN origin, extraction, ancestry, lineage
