possibly
pɒsɪbli ˈpɒsɪblɪ ˈpɑsəbli
1 ADV [ADVERB with verb] 或许；也许 You use possibly to indicate that you are not sure whether something is true or might happen. 或许；也许 [vagueness]

  • Exercise will not only lower blood pressure but possibly protect against heart attacks.

    锻炼不仅会降低血压，而且可能对心脏病有预防效果。

  • They were smartly but casually dressed; possibly students.

    他们衣着时尚，但很休闲，也许是学生。

  • Do you think that he could possibly be right?

    你觉得他有可能是对的吗？

SYN perhaps, maybe, God willing, perchance [archaic]  
2 ADV [ADVERB before verb] （表示对所见或所闻感到惊讶、困惑或震惊） You use possibly to emphasize that you are surprised, puzzled, or shocked by something that you have seen or heard. （表示对所见或所闻感到惊讶、困惑或震惊） [emphasis]

  • It was the most unexpected piece of news one could possibly imagine.

    这条新闻最出人意料了，真是让人想象不到。

  • I mean, how could they possibly eat that stuff?.

    我的意思是，他们怎么可能吃那东西呢？

  • What could this possibly mean?

    这到底是什么意思？

3 ADV [ADVERB before verb] 尽量；尽可能地 You use possibly to emphasize that someone has tried their hardest to do something, or has done it as well as they can. 尽量；尽可能地 [emphasis]

  • They've done everything they can possibly think of.

    凡是能想到的，他们都去做了。

  • It's one of the nicest feelings you can possibly have.

    这是一种极其美妙的感觉。

4 ADV [ADVERB before verb] （加强语气）无论如何也 You use possibly to emphasize that something definitely cannot happen or definitely cannot be done. （加强语气）无论如何也 [emphasis]

  • No I really can't possibly answer that!

    不，我无论如何也回答不了那个问题。

  • There's nothing more they can possibly do right.

    他们除此之外啥也做不了。

  • We cannot possibly risk so many innocent lives.

    我们绝不可能拿这么多无辜者的生命冒险。

  • That can't possibly be right.

    那绝不可能是对的。

SYN at all, in any way, conceivably, by any means
