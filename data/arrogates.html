arrogate
ærəgeɪt ˈærəˌɡeɪt ˈærəˌgeɪt ˈɛrəˌgeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense arrogates, present participle arrogating, past tense , past participle arrogated
VERB 擅取;篡夺;霸占;越权 If someone arrogates to themselves something such as a responsibility or privilege, they claim or take it even though they have no right to do so. 擅取;篡夺;霸占;越权 [formal, disapproval]

  • The assembly arrogated to itself the right to make changes. [VERB + to]

    大会越权作出变更。

  • He arrogated the privilege to himself alone. [VERB noun + to]

    他为自己僭取特权。

SYN seize, demand, assume, appropriate
