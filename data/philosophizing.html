philosophize
fɪlɒsəfaɪz fɪˈlɑsəˌfaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense philosophizes, present participle philosophizing, past tense , past participle philosophized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 philosophise
VERB 谈论；思考 If you say that someone is philosophizing, you mean that they are talking or thinking about important subjects, sometimes instead of doing something practical. 谈论；思考 [Also VERB with quote]

  • He philosophized, he admitted, not because he was certain of establishing the truth, but because it gave him pleasure. [VERB]

    他承认自己高谈阔论不是因为他确定找到了真理，而是因为那给了他很多乐趣。

  • ...a tendency to philosophize about racial harmony. [V + about/on]

    空谈种族和谐的倾向
