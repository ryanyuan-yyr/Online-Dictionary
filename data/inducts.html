induct
ɪndʌkt ɪnˈdʌkt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense inducts, present participle inducting, past tense , past participle inducted
1 VERB 使正式就任;赋予头衔;给予职位 If someone is inducted into a particular job, rank, or position, they are given the job, rank, or position in a formal ceremony. 使正式就任;赋予头衔;给予职位 [formal] [Also VERB n]

  • Six new members have been inducted into the Provincial Cabinet. [be VERB-ed + into]

    6 名新成员已经正式加入省内阁班子。

  • She inducts Nina into the cult. [VERB noun + into]

    她诱导尼娜加入这一邪教。

SYN install, admit, introduce, allow  
2 VERB [usually passive] 征召…入伍 If someone is inducted into the army, they are officially made to join the army. 征召…入伍 [US]

  • In December he was inducted into the army. [be V-ed into n]

    12 月他应征入伍了。
