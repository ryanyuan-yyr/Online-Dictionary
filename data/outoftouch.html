out of touch aʊt əv tʌtʃ
1 ADJ-GRADED [verb-link ADJ] 不了解最新情况的 Someone who is out of touch with a situation is not aware of recent changes in it. 不了解最新情况的

  • Washington politicians are out of touch with the American people. [+ with]

    华盛顿的政客们脱离了美国民众。

  • If they really believe in this then they must be completely out of touch.

    如果他们真的相信这一点，那么他们肯定是完全与现实脱节了。

2 ADJ [verb-link ADJ] （与某人）未有联系的，失去音讯的 If you are out of touch with someone, you have not been in contact with them recently and are not familiar with their present situation. （与某人）未有联系的，失去音讯的 [Also + with]

  • James wasn't invited. We've been out of touch for years. [+ for]

    没有邀请詹姆斯，我们已经多年未联系了。
