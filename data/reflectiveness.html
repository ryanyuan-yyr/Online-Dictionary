reflective
rɪflektɪv rɪˈflɛktɪv
1 ADJ-GRADED 沉思的；深思的 If you are reflective, you are thinking deeply about something. 沉思的；深思的 [written]

  • I walked on in a reflective mood to the car.

    我心事重重地向车子走去。

  • Mike is a quiet, reflective man.

    迈克是一个文静、喜欢沉思的人。

SYN thoughtful, contemplative, meditative, pensive  
reflectively

  • 'The first part of her life hasn't been all that good,' he said reflectively.

    “她早年的生活并非一帆风顺，”他若有所思地说。

  • He gazed reflectively at his companion.

    他盯着自己的同伴，若有所思。

2 ADJ-GRADED 反映…的;表现…的;代表性的 If something is reflective of a particular situation or attitude, it is typical of that situation or attitude, or is a consequence of it. 反映…的;表现…的;代表性的

  • The German government's support of the US is not entirely reflective of German public opinion.

    德国政府对美国的支持不完全是德国公众意见的反映。

  • The pupil's answers may not have been reflective of what the class as a whole had understood.

    这个学生的回答也许并不能代表该班级整体的理解。

3 ADJ-GRADED 反射的；反光的 A reflective surface or material sends back light or heat. 反射的；反光的 [formal]

  • ...a garden of flowing streams, water basins, waterfalls, and a reflective pool.

    园里有流动的小溪、水池、瀑布和波光粼粼的游泳池

  • Avoid pans with a shiny, reflective base as the heat will be reflected back.

    不要使用锅底光亮、能反射热的平底锅，因为热量会被反射回去。
