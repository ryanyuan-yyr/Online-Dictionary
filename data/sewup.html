sew up
1 PHRASAL VERB 缝合;缝补 If you sew up pieces of cloth or tears in cloth or skin, you join them together using a needle and thread. 缝合;缝补 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Next day, Miss Stone decided to sew up the rip. [VERB PREP. noun]

    第二天，斯通小姐决定将撕破的地方缝好。

  • This material was then put into cotton bags which were weighed and then sewn up. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    然后这些材料被放入棉布袋子里称重，接着再缝好。

SYN mend, repair, patch up, darn  
2 PHRASAL VERB 安排妥帖;办好;使万无一失 If someone sews up something such as a business deal, an election, or a game, they make sure that they will get the result they want. 安排妥帖;办好;使万无一失 [informal] [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • If they didn't move fast, Johnson could sew this deal up within days. [VERB noun PREP.]

    如果他们还不赶快行动的话，约翰逊几天之内就会搞定这笔交易。

  • The Italians think they've got it all sewn up. [VERB-ed PREP.]

    意大利人以为他们已稳操胜券。
