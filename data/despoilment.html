despoil
dɪspɔɪl dɪˈspɔɪl diˈspɔɪl dɪˈspɔɪl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense despoils, present participle despoiling, past tense , past participle despoiled
VERB 掠夺;洗劫;破坏;蹂躏 To despoil a place means to make it less attractive, valuable, or important by taking things away from it or by destroying it. 掠夺;洗劫;破坏;蹂躏 [formal]

  • ...people who despoil the countryside. [VERB noun]

    破坏乡村景致的人

  • ...a landscape despoiled by coal-mining and heavy industry. [VERB-ed]

    遭到煤矿开采和重工业破坏的景观

SYN plunder, destroy, strip, rob
