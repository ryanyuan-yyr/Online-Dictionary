flounce
flaʊns flaʊns
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense flounces, present participle flouncing, past tense , past participle flounced
1 VERB 怒气冲冲地走;暴跳 If you flounce somewhere, you walk there quickly with exaggerated movements, in a way that shows you are annoyed or upset. 怒气冲冲地走;暴跳

  • She flounced out of my room in a huff. [VERB adv./prep.]

    她愤愤地冲出我的房间。

  • She will flounce and argue when asked to leave the room. [VERB]

    要是让她离开房间，她会暴跳如雷，吵闹不休。

SYN bounce, storm, stamp, go quickly  
2 N-COUNT (裙子、桌布、窗帘等的)荷叶边 A flounce is a piece of cloth that has been sewn into folds and put around the edge of something, for example a skirt, dress, tablecloth, or curtain. (裙子、桌布、窗帘等的)荷叶边

  • ...a gown with a flounce round the hem.

    镶有荷叶边的睡衣

SYN ruffle, gathering, tuck, frill
