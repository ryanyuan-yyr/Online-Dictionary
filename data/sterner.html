stern
stɜːʳn stɜrn
Word forms: comparative sterner, superlative sternest, plural sterns
1 ADJ-GRADED (话语或行动)严厉的，严正的 Stern words or actions are very severe. (话语或行动)严厉的，严正的

  • Mr Straw issued a stern warning to those who persist in violence.

    斯特劳先生向那些坚持进行暴力活动的人发出了严正警告。

  • He said stern measures would be taken against the killers.

    他说将严惩凶手。

  • Michael gave the dog a stern look.

    迈克尔狠狠瞪了那只狗一眼。

SYN strict, harsh, rigorous, hard  
sternly

  • 'We will take the necessary steps,' she said sternly.

    “我们将采取必要措施，”她厉声说道。

2 ADJ-GRADED (人)严厉的，严格的，苛刻的 Someone who is stern is very serious and strict. (人)严厉的，严格的，苛刻的

  • Her father was stern and hard to please.

    她父亲很苛刻，让他满意很难。

SYN severe, serious, forbidding, steely  
3 N-COUNT 船尾;艉部 from stem to stern The stern of a boat is the back part of it. →see: stem  船尾;艉部 from stem to stern
4 PHRASE (人)性格坚强的，坚忍的 If you say that someone is made of sterner stuff, you mean that they have a strong personality and are capable of overcoming difficulties and problems. (人)性格坚强的，坚忍的

  • Whoever this woman is, she's made of sterner stuff than I am.

    不管这个女人是谁，她都比我坚强。
