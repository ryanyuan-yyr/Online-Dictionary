consummate

Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense consummates, present participle consummating, past tense , past participle consummated
The adjective is pronounced /'kɒnsʌmət/. The verb is pronounced /'kɒnsəmeɪt/. 形容词读作 /'kɒnsʌmət/,动词读作 /'kɒnsəmeɪt/。
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 造诣极高的;技艺精湛的;炉火纯青的 You use consummate to describe someone who is extremely skilful. 造诣极高的;技艺精湛的;炉火纯青的 [formal]

  • He acted the part with consummate skill.

    他以精湛的演技饰演了这一角色。

  • Those familiar with Sanders call him a consummate politician.

    那些熟悉桑德斯的人都称他为手段高明的政治家。

SYN skilled, perfect, supreme, polished  
consummately

  • The film is a well made, atmospheric, consummately acted piece.

    这部电影是一部制作精良、很有味道、表演臻于完美的作品。

2 VERB 圆房;通过性交使(婚姻或两人关系)圆满 If two people consummate a marriage or relationship, they make it complete by having sex. 圆房;通过性交使(婚姻或两人关系)圆满 [formal]

  • They consummated their passion only after many hesitations and delays. [VERB noun]

    他们几经踌躇和拖延后才尽情宣泄了激情。

consummation

  • ...the morning after the consummation of their marriage.

    他们结婚圆房后的早晨

3 VERB 完成，达成(协议等) To consummate an agreement means to complete it. 完成，达成(协议等) [formal]

  • There have been several close calls, but no one has been able to consummate a deal. [VERB noun]

    有好几次死里逃生，却没有人做成一笔交易。
