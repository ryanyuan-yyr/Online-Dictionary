equip
ɪkwɪp iˈkwɪp ɪˈkwɪp
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense equips, present participle equipping, past tense , past participle equipped
1 VERB 装备;配备 If you equip a person or thing with something, you give them the tools or equipment that are needed. 装备;配备

  • They become obsessed with trying to equip their vehicles with gadgets to deal with every possible contingency. [VERB noun + with]

    他们变得痴迷于给自己的车辆装备小配件，以应付可能出现的各种紧急情况。

  • Owners of restaurants would have to equip them to admit disabled people.

    餐厅老板将必须在餐厅里配备能够接纳残疾人的设施。

  • The country did not possess the modern guns to equip the reserve army properly. [VERB noun]

    该国没有现代枪炮来给预备役部队提供适当装备。

SYN supply, provide, stock, dress  
equipped

    设施齐全的研究大楼

2 VERB （尤指通过教育）使做好准备，使有能力，使有资格 If something equips you for a particular task or experience, it gives you the skills and attitudes you need for it, especially by educating you in a particular way. （尤指通过教育）使做好准备，使有能力，使有资格 [Also VERB n + for]

  • Relative poverty, however, did not prevent Martin from equipping himself with an excellent education. [VERB noun + with]

    然而，较为贫困的状况并没有阻止马丁让自己接受一流的教育。

  • A basic two-hour first aid course would equip you to deal with any of these incidents. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    两小时的基础急救课程能够让你有能力处理这类事件。
