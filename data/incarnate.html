incarnate
ɪnkɑːʳnɪt ɪnkɑːʳneɪt ɪnˈkɑrnɪt ɪnˈkɑrˌneɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense incarnates, present participle incarnating, past tense , past participle incarnated
The adjective is pronounced /ɪn'kɑːnɪt/. The verb is pronounced /'ɪnkɑːneɪt/. 形容词读作 /ɪn'kɑːnɪt/,动词读作 /'ɪnkɑːneɪt/。
1 ADJ [n ADJ] 化身的;典型的 If you say that someone is a quality incarnate, you mean that they represent that quality or are typical of it in an extreme form. 化身的;典型的

  • She is evil incarnate.

    她是邪恶的化身。

  • He is cynicism incarnate.

    他是愤世嫉俗的极端体现。

2 ADJ [verb-link ADJ, n ADJ, ADJ n] (神灵)现身的，显形的 You use incarnate to say that something, especially a god or spirit, is represented in human form. (神灵)现身的，显形的

  • Why should God become incarnate as a male?.

    为什么上帝以男性之身显现呢？

  • The pharaoh is Osiris, the moon bull incarnate.

    这位法老就是俄西里斯，丰产神的化身。

SYN made flesh, in the flesh, in human form, in bodily form  
3 VERB [n ADJ] 表现;典型体现 If you say that a quality is incarnated in a person, you mean that they represent that quality or are typical of it in an extreme form. 表现;典型体现

  • The iniquities of the regime are incarnated in one man. [be VERB-ed + in]

    该政权的种种劣行集中体现于一人。

  • ...a writer who incarnates the changing consciousness of the Americas. [VERB noun]

    体现了美洲观念变化的作家

4 VERB [usually passive] 化身为;显形为 If you say that someone or something is incarnated in a particular form, you mean that they appear on earth in that form. 化身为;显形为

  • The god Vishnu was incarnated on earth as a king. [be VERB-ed prep.]

    守护之神毗湿奴化身为下界的一位国王。
