scupper
skʌpəʳ ˈskʌpər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense scuppers, present participle scuppering, past tense , past participle scuppered
VERB 彻底破坏(计划、企图);使成泡影 To scupper a plan or attempt means to spoil it completely. 彻底破坏(计划、企图);使成泡影 [mainly British, journalism]

  • If Schneider had seen him that would have scuppered all his plans. [VERB noun]

    如果施奈德看到了他，那他的所有计划都泡汤了。

  • If the Commission has its way, the entire deal will be scuppered.

    如果委员会我行我素的话，整个交易将化为泡影。

SYN destroy, ruin, wreck, defeat
