parliament
pɑːʳləmənt ˈpɑrləmənt
(also Parliament)Word forms: plural parliaments
1 N-COUNT; N-PROPER 议会；国会 The parliament of some countries, for example Britain, is the group of people who make or change its laws, and decide what policies the country should follow. →see usage note at: parliament 议会；国会

  • Parliament today approved the policy, but it has not yet become law.

    议会于今天批准了这项政策，但尚未成为法律。

2See also:Houses of Parliament  Member of Parliament 
3 N-COUNT 一届议会（会期） A particular parliament is a particular period of time in which a parliament is doing its work, between two elections or between two periods of holiday. 一届议会（会期）

  • The legislation is expected to be passed in the next parliament.

    预计这项法案将在下一届议会中通过。

SYN sitting, diet
