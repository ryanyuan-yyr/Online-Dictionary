reckoning
rekənɪŋ ˈrɛkənɪŋ ˈrɛkənɪŋ
Word forms: plural reckonings
1 N-VAR [usually poss NOUN] （尤指并不十分准确的）预计，估计，估算 Some-one's reckoning is a calculation they make about something, especially a calculation that is not very exact. （尤指并不十分准确的）预计，估计，估算

  • By my reckoning we were seven or eight kilometres from Borj Mechaab.

    据我估计我们离梅卡巴堡有七八公里远。

SYN count, working, estimate, calculation  
2 N-COUNT [usually singular] 报应;清算 If you say that you will have a reckoning with someone, you mean that you will face them at some time in the future and punish them for something they have done. 报应;清算

  • She knew their truce would not last. There would be a reckoning. There would be another fight.

    她早知道他们之间的休战持续不了多长时间。他们之间的账迟早要清算。还将会有一场争斗。

SYN retribution, punishment, revenge, repayment
