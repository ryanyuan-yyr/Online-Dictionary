disperse
dɪspɜːʳs dɪˈspɜːs dɪˈspɜrs
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense disperses, present participle dispersing, past tense , past participle dispersed
1 V-ERG （使）分散;（使）散布 When something disperses or when you disperse it, it spreads over a wide area. （使）分散;（使）散布

  • The oil appeared to be dispersing. [VERB]

    油好像在扩散。

  • The intense currents disperse the sewage. [VERB noun]

    急流冲散了污水。

  • Because the town sits in a valley, air pollution is not easily dispersed. [VERB noun]

    因为小镇坐落在山谷中，空气污染不太容易散去。

SYN scatter, spread, distribute, circulate  
2 V-ERG （使）散开;分散;驱散 When a group of people disperses or when someone disperses them, the group splits up and the people leave in different directions. （使）散开;分散;驱散

  • Police fired shots and used teargas to disperse the demonstrators. [VERB noun]

    警方鸣枪并使用催泪弹来驱散示威者。

  • The crowd dispersed peacefully after prayers. [VERB]

    祈祷仪式过后，人们静静散去。

SYN break up, separate, dismiss, disappear
