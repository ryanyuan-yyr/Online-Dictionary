bleak
bliːk blik
Word forms: comparative bleaker, superlative bleakest
1 ADJ-GRADED （情形）没有希望的，前景暗淡的 If a situation is bleak, it is bad, and seems unlikely to improve. （情形）没有希望的，前景暗淡的

  • The immediate outlook remains bleak.

    近期前景依然暗淡。

  • Many predicted a bleak future.

    很多人都预测前景暗淡。

SYN dismal, black, dark, depressing  
bleakness

  • The continued bleakness of the American job market was blamed.

    美国就业市场的持续低迷被视为罪魁祸首。

2 ADJ-GRADED （地方）凄清的，荒凉的，萧瑟的 If you describe a place as bleak, you mean that it looks cold, empty, and unattractive. （地方）凄清的，荒凉的，萧瑟的

  • The island's pretty bleak.

    这座岛屿一片荒凉。

  • ...bleak inner-city streets.

    凄清的旧城区街道

SYN exposed, open, empty, raw  
3 ADJ-GRADED （天气）阴冷的，寒冷的 When the weather is bleak, it is cold, dull, and unpleasant. （天气）阴冷的，寒冷的

  • The weather can be quite bleak on the coast.

    沿海地区的天气会非常阴冷。

SYN stormy, cold, severe, bitter  
4 ADJ-GRADED （人）无精打采的，沮丧的，阴郁的 If someone looks or sounds bleak, they look or sound depressed, as if they have no hope or energy. （人）无精打采的，沮丧的，阴郁的

  • His face was bleak.

    他脸色阴郁。

  • Alberg gave him a bleak stare.

    阿尔伯格沮丧地凝视着他。

bleakly
“什么也没有留下，”她沮丧地说。 'There is nothing left,' she says bleakly. “什么也没有留下，”她沮丧地说。
