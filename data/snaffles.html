snaffle
snæfəl ˈsnæfəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense snaffles, present participle snaffling, past tense , past participle snaffled
1 N-COUNT (马的)嚼子;马衔铁 A snaffle is an object consisting of two short joined bars of metal that is put in a horse's mouth and attached to the straps that the rider uses to control the horse. (马的)嚼子;马衔铁
2 VERB 偷窃;扒取;攫取 If you snaffle something, you take it for yourself. 偷窃;扒取;攫取 [British, informal]

  • Michael Stich then proceeded to snaffle the $2 million first prize. [VERB noun]

    接着，迈克尔·斯蒂克又一举拿下了两百万美元的头奖。
