deposit
dɪpɒzɪt diˈpɑzɪt dɪˈpɑzɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense deposits, present participle depositing, past tense , past participle deposited
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 订金;保证金 A deposit is a sum of money which is part of the full price of something, and which you pay when you agree to buy it. 订金;保证金

  • A £50 deposit is required when ordering, and the balance is due upon delivery.

    订货时需要交50英镑的订金，货到后余款结清。

SYN down payment, security, stake, pledge  
2 N-COUNT [usually singular] (租赁的)押金 A deposit is a sum of money which you pay when you start renting something. The money is returned to you if you do not damage what you have rented. (租赁的)押金

  • It is common to ask for the equivalent of a month's rent as a deposit.

    要求交一个月的房租作押金是很常见的。

3 N-COUNT (尤指定期)存款 A deposit is a sum of money which is in a bank account or savings account, especially a sum which will be left there for some time. (尤指定期)存款
4 N-COUNT [oft poss NOUN] 竞选保证金(参加议会或欧洲选举时提交，如获一定比例选票便可返还) A deposit is a sum of money which you have to pay if you want to be a candidate in a parliamentary or European election. The money is returned to you if you receive more than a certain percentage of the votes. 竞选保证金(参加议会或欧洲选举时提交，如获一定比例选票便可返还) [British]

  • The Tory candidate lost his deposit.

    这名托利党候选人连竞选保证金都搭进去了。

5 N-COUNT 沉淀物;沉积物 A deposit is an amount of a substance that has been left somewhere as a result of a chemical or geological process. 沉淀物;沉积物

  • After 10 minutes the surplus material is washed away and any remaining deposit examined with ultra violet light.

    10分钟后剩余的物质被冲洗掉，任何残留的沉淀物则会用紫外线进行检测。

  • ...underground deposits of gold and diamonds.

    地下金矿和金刚石矿

  • ...mineral deposits.

    矿藏

SYN accumulation, growth, mass, build-up  
6 VERB 放下;放置;丢下 To deposit someone or something somewhere means to put them or leave them there. 放下;放置;丢下

  • Someone was seen depositing a packet. [VERB noun]

    有人看见一个人放下了件小包裹。

  • Fritz deposited a glass and two bottles of beer in front of Wolfe. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    弗里茨把一个玻璃杯和两瓶啤酒放在沃尔夫面前。

  • Imagine if you were suddenly swept up and deposited in Morocco. How well could you cope with the language, the weather, the people, and so on? [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    设想一下你突然被带走丢到了摩洛哥。你能多好地适应那里的语言、天气和人等等？

SYN put, place, lay, drop  
7 VERB 存放;寄存 If you deposit something somewhere, you put it where it will be safe until it is needed again. 存放;寄存

  • You are advised to deposit valuables in the hotel safe. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    建议把贵重物品存放在宾馆的保险箱里。

SYN store, keep, put, bank  
8 VERB 将（钱）存入银行（或储蓄）账户 If you deposit a sum of money, you pay it into a bank account or savings account. 将（钱）存入银行（或储蓄）账户

  • The customer has to deposit a minimum of £100 monthly. [VERB noun]

    顾客必须每月至少存 100 英镑。

9 VERB [usually passive] 使沉淀;使沉积 If a substance is deposited somewhere, it is left there as a result of a chemical or geological process. 使沉淀;使沉积

  • The phosphate was deposited by the decay of marine microorganisms. [be VERB-ed]

    海洋微生物腐烂后沉积形成磷酸盐。
