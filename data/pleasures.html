pleasure
pleʒəʳ ˈplɛʒər
Word forms: plural pleasures
1 N-UNCOUNT 愉快；高兴；快乐；满足 If something gives you pleasure, you get a feeling of happiness, satisfaction, or enjoyment from it. 愉快；高兴；快乐；满足 [Also + from]

  • Watching sport gave him great pleasure.

    观看体育比赛给他以极大的愉悦。

  • Everybody takes pleasure in eating. [+ in]

    每个人都从吃中得到满足感。

  • He gets huge pleasure from ballet and contemporary dance.

    他从芭蕾和现代舞中得到极大的快乐。

2 N-UNCOUNT （尤指工作、职责之外的）娱乐，消遣 Pleasure is the activity of enjoying yourself, especially rather than working or doing what you have a duty to do. （尤指工作、职责之外的）娱乐，消遣

  • He mixed business and pleasure in a perfect and dynamic way.

    他以互动的方式将工作和娱乐完美地结合起来。

  • I read for pleasure.

    我读书消遣。

3 N-COUNT 乐趣；乐事；给人愉快的事物 A pleasure is an activity, experience or aspect of something that you find very enjoyable or satisfying. 乐趣；乐事；给人愉快的事物

  • Watching TV is our only pleasure.

    看电视是我们唯一的乐事。

  • ...the pleasure of seeing a smiling face. [+ of]

    看到一张微笑的面孔的愉悦

  • ...the conveniences and pleasures of modern life.

    现代生活的种种便利和乐趣

SYN amusement, joy, recreation, diversion  
4 CONVENTION 很高兴见到…/…莅临的荣幸 If you meet someone for the first time, you can say, as a way of being polite, that it is a pleasure to meet them. You can also ask for the pleasure of someone's company as a polite and formal way of inviting them somewhere. 很高兴见到…/…莅临的荣幸 [politeness]

  • 'A pleasure to meet you, sir,' he said.

    他说：“先生，很高兴见到您。”

  • Mr and Mrs James Stephens request the pleasure of your company at the marriage of their daughter Caroline Mary to Mr David Smith.

    詹姆斯·斯蒂芬斯夫妇敬请您莅临小女卡罗琳·玛丽与戴维·史密斯先生的婚礼。

5 CONVENTION （受到感谢时的一种礼貌回答）别客气，不用谢，是我的荣幸 You can say 'It's a pleasure ' or 'My pleasure ' as a polite way of replying to someone who has just thanked you for doing something. （受到感谢时的一种礼貌回答）别客气，不用谢，是我的荣幸 [formulae]

  • 'Thanks very much anyhow.' — 'It's a pleasure.'

    “无论怎样，非常感谢。”—“别客气。”

  • 'Thanks for your call.' — 'My pleasure.'

    “谢谢你打来电话。”—“不用谢。”

6 CONVENTION 非常乐意；愿意效劳 You can say 'With pleasure ' as a polite way of saying that you are very willing to do something. 非常乐意；愿意效劳 [formulae]

  • 'Could you photocopy the advert and put it in the post to us?' — 'With pleasure John.'

    “你能把广告影印一下寄给我们吗？”—“当然可以，约翰。”

SYN gladly, of course, happily, readily
