botch
bɒtʃ bɑtʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense botches, present participle botching, past tense , past participle botched
1 VERB 把…搞糟；笨手笨脚地做 If you botch something that you are doing, you do it badly or clumsily. 把…搞糟；笨手笨脚地做 [informal]

  • It is a silly idea and he has botched it. [VERB noun]

    这是个愚蠢的想法，他已经把事情弄糟了。

  • ...a botched job. [VERB-ed]

    拙劣的工作

SYN spoil, mar, bungle, fumble  
Botch up means the same as botch .botch up 同 botch

  • I hate having builders botch up repairs on my house.

    我不愿意让维修工人把我的房子弄得一团糟。

  • Hemingway complained that Nichols had 'botched everything up'.

    海明威抱怨尼科尔斯“把一切都搞砸了”。

2 N-COUNT [usually singular] 拙劣的工作；粗制滥造 If you make a botch of something that you are doing, you botch it. 拙劣的工作；粗制滥造 [informal]

  • I rather made a botch of that whole thing.

    我结果把那整件事弄得一团糟。

SYN mess, failure, blunder, miscarriage
