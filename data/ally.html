ally
ælaɪ əlaɪ əˈlaɪ æˈlaɪ ˈælaɪ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense allies, present participle allying, past tense , past participle allied
The noun is pronounced /'ælaɪ/. The verb is pronounced /ə'laɪ/. 名词读作/'ælaɪ/，动词读作/ə'laɪ/。
1 N-COUNT （尤指战时的）同盟国 A country's ally is another country that has an agreement to support it, especially in war. （尤指战时的）同盟国

  • Washington would not take such a step without its allies' approval.

    没有盟国的同意，华盛顿将不会采取这样一种行动。

  • The United States is a close ally of South Korea. [+ of]

    美国是韩国的亲密盟友。

2 N-PLURAL （第二次世界大战时的）同盟国 The Allies were the armed forces that fought against Germany and Japan in the Second World War. （第二次世界大战时的）同盟国
3 N-COUNT 盟友；支持者 If you describe someone as your ally, you mean that they help and support you, especially when other people are opposing you. 盟友；支持者

  • He is a close ally of the Prime Minister. [+ of]

    他是首相的亲密支持者。

  • She will regret losing a close political ally.

    她将为失去一个亲密的政治盟友而懊悔。

SYN partner, friend, colleague, associate  
4 VERB 与…结盟；支持 If you ally yourself with someone or something, you give your support to them. 与…结盟；支持

  • He will have no choice but to ally himself with the new movement. [VERB pronoun-reflexive + with]

    他除了与新运动结盟别无选择。

SYN combine, unite, join, link
