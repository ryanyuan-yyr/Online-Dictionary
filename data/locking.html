lock
lɒk lɑk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense locks, present participle locking, past tense , past participle locked
1 VERB 锁；锁上；锁住 When you lock something such as a door, drawer, or case, you fasten it, usually with a key, so that other people cannot open it. 锁；锁上；锁住

  • Are you sure you locked the front door?. [VERB noun]

    你确定锁了前门吗？

  • Wolfgang moved along the corridor towards the locked door at the end. [VERB-ed]

    沃尔夫冈走向过道尽头锁着的那扇门。

SYN fasten, close, secure, shut  
2 N-COUNT 锁 The lock on something such as a door or a drawer is the device which is used to keep it shut and prevent other people from opening it. Locks are opened with a key. 锁

  • At that moment he heard Gill's key turning in the lock of the door. [+ of]

    那一刻，他听见吉尔的钥匙开启门锁的声音。

  • An intruder forced open a lock on French windows at the house.

    有人强行撬开落地窗上的锁闯入屋里。

SYN fastening, catch, bolt, clasp  
3 VERB 把… 锁起妥藏；把…关起来 If you lock something or someone in a place, room, or container, you put them there and fasten the lock. 把… 锁起妥藏；把…关起来

  • Her maid locked the case in the safe. [V n + in/into]

    她的女佣把箱子锁进保险柜里。

  • They beat them up and locked them in a cell. [V n + in/into]

    他们殴打了他们，并把他们锁在一间囚室里。

4 V-ERG 扣住；拴住；（使）固定 If you lock something in a particular position or if it lock there, it is held or fitted firmly in that position. 扣住；拴住；（使）固定

  • He leaned back in the swivel chair and locked his fingers behind his head. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他仰靠在转椅上，十指交叉枕在脑后。

  • There was a whine of hydraulics as the undercarriage locked into position. [VERB prep./adv.]

    随着液压系统嘎吱一声响，起落架进入了锁定位置。

SYN unite, join, link, engage  
5 N-COUNT （运河或河流上的）船闸，水闸 On a canal or river, a lock is a place where walls have been built with gates at each end so that boats can move to a higher or lower section of the canal or river, by gradually changing the water level inside the gates. （运河或河流上的）船闸，水闸
6 N-COUNT 一绺（头发） A lock of hair is a small bunch of hairs on your head that grow together and curl or curve in the same direction. 一绺（头发）

  • She brushed a lock of hair off his forehead. [+ of]

    她撩开他脑门上的一绺头发。

SYN strand, curl, tuft, tress  
7 N-PLURAL [usually supplement NOUN] 头发 Your locks are your hair. 头发 [literary]

  • ...women with long, wavy locks.

    留着长长卷发的女人们

8 PHRASE [PHRASE after verb] 妥善锁藏着；被关押着 If something or someone is kept under lock and key, they are in a container or room which has been securely locked. 妥善锁藏着；被关押着

  • The books were normally kept under lock and key in the library vault.

    这些书一般妥善锁藏在图书馆的地窖里。

  • He is currently under lock and key at Eastmoor secure unit in Leeds.

    他眼下被关押在利兹的伊斯特穆尔保安部。
