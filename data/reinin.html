rein in
1 PHRASAL VERB 控制;约束 To rein in something means to control it. 控制;约束

  • His administration's economic policy would focus on reining in inflation. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他的政府的经济政策将侧重于控制通货膨胀。

  • Mary spoiled both her children, then tried too late to rein them in. [VERB noun PREP.]

    玛丽惯坏了她的两个孩子，想要管束他们的时候已经来不及了。

SYN control, hold, limit, check  
2 PHRASAL VERB 拉紧(马的)缰绳;勒缰（使马停步） If you rein in a horse, you stop it or cause it to go more slowly by pulling its reins. 拉紧(马的)缰绳;勒缰（使马停步） [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • The horsemen reined in and shouted at the men behind to turn back. [VERB PREP.]

    骑手们勒缰停住，对身后的人大嚷，让他们回去。

  • Mrs Glick reined in the horse and stopped at the crossroads. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    格利克夫人勒住缰绳在十字路口停了下来。
