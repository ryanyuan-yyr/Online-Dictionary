unusually
ʌnjuːʒuəli
1 ADV-GRADED [ADVERB adjective] 出奇地；不寻常地 You use unusually to emphasize that someone or something has more of a particular quality than is usual. 出奇地；不寻常地 [emphasis]

  • He was an unusually complex man.

    他是个极其复杂的人。

  • ...this year's unusually harsh winter.

    今年异常寒冷的冬天

SYN exceptionally, especially, remarkably, extraordinarily  
2 ADV-GRADED 异常地；不常见地 You can use unusually to suggest that something is not what normally happens. 异常地；不常见地 [Also + for]

  • Unusually among British prime ministers, he was not a man of natural authority.

    他不是那种天生一副官派的人，这在英国首相中并不常见。
