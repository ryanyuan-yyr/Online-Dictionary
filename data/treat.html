treat
triːt trit
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense treats, present participle treating, past tense , past participle treated
1 VERB 对待;看待;处理 If you treat someone or something in a particular way, you behave towards them or deal with them in that way. 对待;看待;处理 [Also V n like]

  • Artie treated most women with indifference. [VERB noun + with]

    阿蒂对大多数女人都漠不关心。

  • Police say they're treating it as a case of attempted murder. [VERB noun + as]

    警方称他们正把它当作一起谋杀未遂案件来处理。

  • She adored Paddy but he didn't treat her well. [VERB noun adv.]

    她深爱帕迪，但他对她却并不好。

  • The issues should be treated separately. [VERB noun adv.]

    这些问题应该分别对待。

SYN behave towards, deal with, handle, act towards  
2 VERB 治疗;医治 When a doctor or nurse treats a patient or an illness, he or she tries to make the patient well again. 治疗;医治

  • Doctors treated her with aspirin. [VERB noun + with]

    医生用阿司匹林为她治疗。

  • The boy was treated for a minor head wound. [VERB noun + for]

    那个男孩因头部轻伤接受了治疗。

  • An experienced nurse treats all minor injuries. [VERB noun]

    一位有经验的护士处理所有的轻伤。

SYN take care of, minister to, attend to, give medical treatment to  
3 VERB 处理;给…上保护层 If something is treated with a particular substance, the substance is put onto or into it in order to clean it, to protect it, or to give it special properties. 处理;给…上保护层

  • About 70% of the cocoa acreage is treated with insecticide. [be VERB-ed + with]

    大约 70% 的可可树林地喷过杀虫剂。

  • It was many years before the city began to treat its sewage. [VERB noun]

    很多年以后这个城市才开始处理污水。

SYN prime, cover, process, prepare  
4 VERB 请(客);招待;款待 If you treat someone to something special which they will enjoy, you buy it or arrange it for them. 请(客);招待;款待 [Also VERB n]

  • She was always treating him to ice cream. [VERB noun + to]

    她总是请他吃冰激凌。

  • Tomorrow I'll treat myself to a day's gardening. [VERB pronoun-reflexive + to]

    明天我将犒劳一下自己—享受一天的园艺乐趣。

  • If you want to treat yourself, the Malta Hilton offers high international standards. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    如果您想犒劳一下自己，马耳他希尔顿酒店将为您提供高水准的国际化服务。

SYN provide, give, buy, stand [informal]  
5 N-COUNT 请客;款待 If you give someone a treat, you buy or arrange something special for them which they will enjoy. 请客;款待

  • Lettie had never yet failed to return from town without some special treat for him. [+ for]

    莱蒂每次从城里回来都必定会给他带点特别的礼物。

SYN present, gift, luxury, indulgence  
6 N-SING 请客;做东 If you say that something is your treat, you mean that you are paying for it as a treat for someone else. 请客;做东 [spoken]
7 PHRASE 很出色的东西 If you say, for example, that something looks or works a treat, you mean that it looks very good or works very well. 很出色的东西 [British, informal]

  • The first part of the plan works a treat.

    计划的第一部分天衣无缝。

  • The apricots would go down a treat.

    杏会非常好吃。

8 to treat someone like dirt→see: dirt
