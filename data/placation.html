placate
pləkeɪt pleɪkeɪt ˈpleɪˌkeɪt pleɪˈkeɪt ˈplækˌeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense placates, present participle placating, past tense , past participle placated
1 VERB 安抚；抚慰 If you placate someone, you do or say something to make them stop feeling angry. 安抚；抚慰 [formal] [Also VERB]

  • He smiled, trying to placate me. [VERB noun]

    他微笑着，想要安抚我。

  • 'I didn't mean to upset you,' Agnew said in a placating voice. [VERB-ing]

    阿格纽用抚慰的语气说：“我不是有意让你不高兴。”

SYN calm, satisfy, humour, soothe
