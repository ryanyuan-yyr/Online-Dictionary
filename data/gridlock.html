gridlock
grɪdlɒk ˈɡrɪdˌlɒk ˈgrɪdˌlɑk
1 N-UNCOUNT 交通瘫痪；交通大堵塞 Gridlock is the situation that exists when all the roads in a particular place are so full of vehicles that none of them can move. 交通瘫痪；交通大堵塞

  • The streets are wedged solid with near-constant traffic gridlock.

    交通堵塞丝毫不见缓解，街道被堵得水泄不通。

SYN traffic jam  
2 N-UNCOUNT 僵局 You can use gridlock to refer to a situation in an argument or dispute when neither side is prepared to give in, so no agreement can be reached. 僵局

  • He agreed that these policies will lead to gridlock in the future.

    他认为这些政策将来会导致僵局。

SYN deadlock, halt, stalemate, impasse
