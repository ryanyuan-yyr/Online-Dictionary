get on
1 PHRASAL VERB-RECIP （同…）和睦相处 If you get on with someone, you like them and have a friendly relationship with them. （同…）和睦相处

  • The host fears the guests won't get on.

    主人担心客人们会相处得不愉快。

  • What are your neighbours like? Do you get on with them? [VERB PREP. + with]

    你的邻居怎么样？跟他们相处得好吗？

SYN be friendly, agree, get along, concur  
2 PHRASAL VERB 继续做；开始做 If you get on with something, you continue doing it or start doing it. 继续做；开始做

  • Jane got on with her work. [VERB PREP. + with]

    简继续干她的工作。

  • Let's get on. [VERB PREP.]

    我们开始干吧。

3 PHRASAL VERB 取得进展；有起色 If you say how someone is getting on, you are saying how much success they are having with what they are trying to do. 取得进展；有起色

  • Livy's getting on very well in Russian. She learns very quickly. [VERB PREP. adv.]

    莉薇的俄语很有起色，她学得非常快。

  • When he came back to see me I asked how he had got on. [VERB PREP. adv.]

    他回来看我的时候，我问他进展如何。

SYN progress, manage, cope, fare  
4 PHRASAL VERB 发迹；取得成功；出人头地 If you try to get on, you try to be successful in your career. 发迹；取得成功；出人头地 [mainly British]

  • Politics is seen as a man's world. It is very difficult for women to get on. [VERB PREP.]

    政治被视为男人的领域，女人很难出头。

5 PHRASAL VERB [usually cont] 上年纪 If someone is getting on, they are getting old. 上年纪 [informal]

  • I'm nearly 31 and that's getting on a bit for a footballer. [VERB PREP.]

    我快31了，作为足球运动员也算是高龄了。
