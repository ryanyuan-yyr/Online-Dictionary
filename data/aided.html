aid
eɪd eɪd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense aids, present participle aiding, past tense , past participle aided
1 N-UNCOUNT 援助;救助;资助 Aid is money, equipment, or services that are provided for people, countries, or organizations who need them but cannot provide them for themselves. 援助;救助;资助

  • ...regular flights carrying humanitarian aid to Cambodia. [+ to]

    向柬埔寨运送人道援助物资的定期航班

  • They have already pledged billions of dollars in aid.

    他们已经许诺援助几十亿美元。

  • ...food aid convoys.

    救援食品护送车队

SYN financial assistance, help, relief, support  
2 VERB 援助;救助;资助 To aid a country, organization, or person means to provide them with money, equipment, or services that they need. 援助;救助;资助

  • ...US efforts to aid Kurdish refugees.

    美国对库尔德难民的援助行动

  • ...a charitable organization that has spent millions aiding pharmaceutical research. [VERB noun]

    已投入巨资资助药物研究的一家慈善机构

-aided

  • ...grant-aided factories.

    政府资助的工厂

  • ...state-aided schools.

    享受国家补助的学校

3 VERB 帮助;援助 To aid someone means to help or assist them. 帮助;援助 [written] [Also VERB n to-infinitive]

  • ...a software system to aid managers in advanced decision-making. [VERB noun]

    帮助经理作出高级决策的软件系统

  • The hunt for her killer will continue, with police aided by the army and air force. [VERB-ed]

    警方将在陆军和空军的帮助下，继续追踪谋杀她的凶手。

SYN help, second, support, serve  
Aid is also a noun.

  • He was forced to turn for aid to his former enemy.

    他曾被迫向以前的敌人求助。

4 N-UNCOUNT （在…的）帮助（下）；（借）助（于） If you perform a task with the aid of something, you need or use that thing to perform that task. （在…的）帮助（下）；（借）助（于）

  • He succeeded with the aid of a completely new method he discovered. [+ of]

    借助于自己发现的一种全新的方法，他获得了成功。

  • Gently raise your upper body to a sitting position, without the aid of your hands.

    在不用手扶的前提下，慢慢抬起上身直到坐起。

5 N-COUNT 辅助物;辅助用具;辅助性技术 An aid is an object, device, or technique that makes something easier to do. 辅助物;辅助用具;辅助性技术

  • The new law gives authorities a responsibility to provide aids to the disabled.

    新法律规定当局有责任为残疾人提供帮助。

  • The book is an invaluable aid to teachers of literature. [+ to]

    这本书对于教授文学的老师来说帮助非常大。

  • Colonel Hardy would like to see every tank with a computerized aid.

    哈迪上校希望看到每辆坦克都配有电脑化辅助装置。

6 VERB 促进;有助于 If something aids a process, it makes it easier or more likely to happen. 促进;有助于

  • The export sector will aid the economic recovery. [VERB noun]

    出口产业将促进经济复苏。

  • Calcium may aid in the prevention of colon cancer. [VERB + in]

    钙可能有助于预防结肠癌。

SYN promote, help, further, forward  
7 See also: Band-Aid  first aid  hearing aid  legal aid 
8 PHRASE 为帮助;为给…筹资 An activity or event in aid of a particular cause or charity is intended to raise money for that cause or charity. 为帮助;为给…筹资 [mainly British]

  • ...a charity performance in aid of Great Ormond Street Children's Hospital.

    为给大奥蒙德街儿童医院筹资而举办的慈善演出

SYN in support of, backing, helping, in promotion of  
9 PHRASE 援助；救助 If you come or go to someone's aid, you try to help them when they are in danger or difficulty. 援助；救助

  • We're coming to the aid of the people of Somalia at the request of the United Nations.

    我们应联合国的要求前去救援索马里的民众。

  • Horrified neighbours rushed to his aid as he fell.

    他摔下来时，惊恐的邻居们赶忙冲过去救他。
