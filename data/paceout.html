pace out
(also pace off)
PHRASAL VERB 步测 If you pace out or pace off a distance, you measure it by walking from one end of it to the other. 步测

  • Nash saw Colin pace out the length of the field in which he had landed to ensure that he could fly safely out of it. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    纳什看见科林步测了他着陆场地的长度，以确保自己能够安全飞离。

  • I marked the ground and then paced it out to be sure. [VERB noun PREP.]

    我在地面上作了标记，然后为了确保起见步测了一下。

SYN measure, determine, mark out
