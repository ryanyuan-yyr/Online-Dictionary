quack
kwæk kwæk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense quacks, present participle quacking, past tense , past participle quacked
1 N-COUNT [oft NOUN n] 庸医；江湖郎中；冒牌医生 If you call someone a quack or a quack doctor, you mean that they claim to be skilled in medicine but are not. 庸医；江湖郎中；冒牌医生 [disapproval]

  • I went everywhere for treatment, tried all sorts of quacks.

    我四处求医，看过了各种各样的江湖郎中。

SYN doctor, GP, physician, medical practitioner  
2 ADJ [ADJ n] 江湖医术;骗人的医术 Quack remedies or quack cures are medical treatments that you think are unlikely to work because they are not scientific. 江湖医术;骗人的医术 [disapproval]

  • Why do intelligent people find quack remedies so appealing?

    为什么聪明人会被江湖医术所吸引？

3 VERB (鸭子)发出嘎嘎声 When a duck quacks, it makes the noise that ducks typically make. (鸭子)发出嘎嘎声

  • There were ducks quacking on the lawn. [VERB]

    草地上有几只鸭子在嘎嘎叫。

Quack is also a noun.

  • Suddenly he heard a quack.

    他突然听见一声嘎嘎的叫声。
