embellish
ɪmbelɪʃ ɛmˈbɛlɪʃ ɪmˈbɛlɪʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense embellishes, present participle embellishing, past tense , past participle embellished
1 VERB 装饰;修饰 If something is embellished with decorative features or patterns, it has those features or patterns on it and they make it look more attractive. 装饰;修饰

  • The stern was embellished with carvings in red and blue. [be VERB-ed + with]

    船尾饰有红色和蓝色的雕刻图案。

  • Ivy leaves embellish the front of the dresser. [VERB noun]

    梳妆台正面饰有常春藤叶子。

  • Embellish basic covers and curtains with borders, ties and fringing. [VERB noun]

    用镶边、系带和流苏装饰朴素的床罩和窗帘。

SYN decorate, enhance, adorn, dress  
2 VERB 给（故事）润色;渲染;润饰 If you embellish a story, you make it more interesting by adding details which may be untrue. 给（故事）润色;渲染;润饰

  • I launched into the parable, embellishing the story with invented dialogue and extra details. [VERB noun]

    我开始讲那则寓言，并编造了些对话，加了些细节对其进行润色。

  • Irving popularized the story in a dramatic and embellished account. [VERB-ed]

    欧文凭借其生动细致的描写使这篇小说广受欢迎。

SYN elaborate, colour, exaggerate, dress up
