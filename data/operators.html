operator
ɒpəreɪtəʳ ˈɑpəˌreɪtər
Word forms: plural operators
1 N-COUNT 电话接线员 An operator is a person who connects telephone calls at a telephone exchange or in a place such as an office or hotel. 电话接线员

  • He dialled the operator and put in a call for Rome.

    他拨了接线员的号，让他接通一个到罗马的电话。

2 N-COUNT [usually n NOUN] （机器）操作员，技工 An operator is a person who is employed to operate or control a machine. （机器）操作员，技工

  • ...computer operators.

    计算机操作员

3 N-COUNT （企业的）经营者；运营公司 An operator is a person or a company that runs a business. （企业的）经营者；运营公司 [business]

  • ...'Tele-Communications', the nation's largest cable TV operator.

    全国最大的有线电视运营商“电视通信公司”

SYN contractor, dealer, trader, administrator  
4 N-COUNT [usually adjective NOUN] 精明圆滑的人；善于取巧的人 If you call someone a good operator, you mean that they are skilful at achieving what they want, often in a slightly dishonest way. 精明圆滑的人；善于取巧的人 [informal]

  • He was a smart operator. Don't underestimate him.

    他这个人精明圆滑，可别小瞧了他。

  • ...one of the shrewdest political operators in the Arab World. [+ in]

    阿拉伯世界最精明狡猾的政客之一

SYN manipulator, worker, mover, Machiavellian  
5 See also: tour operator
