brassy
brɑːsi bræsi ˈbræsi
Word forms: comparative brassier, superlative brassiest
1 ADJ-GRADED （音乐）刺耳的，吵闹的 Brassy music is bold, harsh, and loud. （音乐）刺耳的，吵闹的

  • Musicians blast their brassy jazz from street corners.

    玩音乐的人在街角大声弹奏起尖锐刺耳的爵士乐。

SYN strident, loud, harsh, piercing  
2 ADJ-GRADED （女子）没有品位的，打扮花哨的，俗气的 If you describe a woman's appearance or her behaviour as brassy, you think that she does not have good taste, and that she dresses or behaves in a way that is too loud or vulgar. （女子）没有品位的，打扮花哨的，俗气的 [disapproval]

  • ...Alec and his brassy blonde wife.

    亚历克和他俗气的金发太太

  • Those ladies were brassy and busty, with pudgy fingers and painted eyes.

    那些女人们打扮花哨，体态丰满，手指肥肥的，眼睛都涂了眼影。

3 ADJ-GRADED 黄铜色的 Something that is brassy has a yellow metallic colour and sometimes looks cheap. 黄铜色的

  • a woman with big brassy earrings.

    戴着黄铜色大耳环的女人

SYN flashy, loud, blatant, vulgar
