relegate
relɪgeɪt ˈrɛləˌgeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense relegates, present participle relegating, past tense , past participle relegated
1 VERB 贬黜;把…降级;把…降格 If you relegate someone or something to a less important position, you give them this position. 贬黜;把…降级;把…降格

  • Might it not be better to relegate the King to a purely ceremonial function?. [VERB noun + to]

    让国王变成纯粹发挥典礼仪式上象征作用的元首会不会更好？

  • Other newspapers relegated the item to the middle pages. [VERB noun to noun]

    其他的报纸把这条消息降格报道，放到了中间版面。

SYN demote, degrade, downgrade, declass  
2 VERB [usually passive] 使(球队)降级 If a sports team that competes in a league is relegated, it has to compete in a lower division in the next competition, because it was one of the least successful teams in the higher division. 使(球队)降级 [British]

  • If Leigh lose, they'll be relegated. [be VERB-ed]

    如果利失球，他们将会降级。

  • ...a team about to be relegated to the second division. [be V-ed to n]

    即将被降到乙级的球队。

SYN banish, exile, expel, throw out  
relegation
