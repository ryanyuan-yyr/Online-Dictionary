clearance
klɪərəns ˈklɪrəns
Word forms: plural clearances
1 N-VAR 清除;清理 Clearance is the removal of old buildings, trees, or other things that are not wanted from an area. 清除;清理

  • ...a slum clearance operation in Nairobi.

    内罗毕拆除贫民窟的行动

  • The UN pledged to help supervise the clearance of mines.

    联合国承诺协助监督扫雷工作。

  • ...widespread clearance of jungle land.

    丛林地带的大面积砍伐

2 N-VAR (官方的)批准，许可 If you get clearance to do or have something, you get official approval or permission to do or have it. (官方的)批准，许可

  • Thai Airways said the plane had been given clearance to land. [+ to]

    泰国航空公司说，该飞机已经得到降落许可。

  • He has a security clearance that allows him access to classified information.

    他持有安全许可，可以接触机密信息。

SYN permission, consent, endorsement, green light  
3 N-VAR (路面、河面与上方桥梁之间的)净空，间隙，间距 The clearance of a bridge is the distance between the lowest point of the bridge and the road or the water under the bridge. (路面、河面与上方桥梁之间的)净空，间隙，间距

  • The lowest fixed bridge has 12.8m clearance.

    最低的固定桥下方通行高度为12.8米。

SYN space, gap, margin, allowance
