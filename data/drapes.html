drape
dreɪp dreɪp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense drapes, present participle draping, past tense , past participle draped
1 VERB 将(织物)悬挂;披 If you drape a piece of cloth somewhere, you place it there so that it hangs down in a casual and graceful way. 将(织物)悬挂;披

  • Natasha took the coat and draped it over her shoulders. [VERB noun prep.]

    娜塔莎拿起外套披在肩上。

  • A soft white robe had been draped over a chair for Joanna's use. [VERB noun prep.]

    一件柔软的白袍子搭在椅子上，供乔安娜使用。

  • She had a towel draped around her neck. [V-ed prep]

    她把一条毛巾挂在脖子上。

SYN lay, put, place, spread  
2 VERB 遮盖;盖住 If someone or something is draped in a piece of cloth, they are loosely covered by it. 遮盖;盖住

  • The coffin had been draped in a Union Jack. [be V-ed + in/with]

    灵柩上覆盖着英国国旗。

  • He draped himself in the Canadian flag and went round the track. [V n + in/with]

    他身披加拿大国旗绕跑道奔跑。

  • She opened her front door draped in a towel. [VERB-ed]

    她身裹毛巾打开了前门。

SYN cover, wrap, fold, array  
3 VERB 将(身体的某部位)随意搭在…上 If you drape a part of your body somewhere, you lay it there in a relaxed and graceful way. 将(身体的某部位)随意搭在…上

  • Nicola slowly draped herself across the couch. [V pron-refl prep]

    妮古拉慢慢地靠在了长沙发上。

  • He draped his arm over Daniels' shoulder. [VERB noun prep.]

    他把手臂搭在丹尼尔的肩膀上。

  • They sprawl at ease across the sofa, arms draped over the back. [V-ed prep]

    他们惬意地躺在沙发上，胳膊搭在后面。

4 N-COUNT [usually plural] (厚的)帘子，帷幕 Drapes are pieces of heavy fabric that you hang from the top of a window and can close to keep the light out or stop people looking in. (厚的)帘子，帷幕 [US]

  • He pulled the drapes shut, locked the door behind him.

    他把帘子拉严，转身把门锁好。
