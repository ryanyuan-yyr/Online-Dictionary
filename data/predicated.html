predicate
predɪkət predɪkeɪt ˈprɛdɪˌkeɪt & ˈprɛdɪkɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense predicates, present participle predicating, past tense , past participle predicated
The noun is pronounced /'predɪkət/. The verb is pronounced /'predɪkeɪt/. 名词读作 /'predɪkət/。动词读作 /'predɪkeɪt/。
1 N-COUNT 谓语 In some systems of grammar, the predicate of a clause is the part of it that is not the subject. For example, in 'I decided what to do', 'decided what to do' is the predicate. 谓语
2 VERB [usually passive] 基于；取决于；以…为基础 If you say that one situation is predicated on another, you mean that the first situation can be true or real only if the second one is true or real. 基于；取决于；以…为基础 [formal]

  • Financial success is usually predicated on having money or being able to obtain it. [be V-ed + on]

    先要有钱或者能弄到钱才能够赚钱。
