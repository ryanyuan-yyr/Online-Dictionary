contrivance
kəntraɪvəns kənˈtraɪvəns
Word forms: plural contrivances
1 N-VAR 不自然的东西;矫揉造作之物 If you describe something as a contrivance, you disapprove of it because it is unnecessary and artificial. 不自然的东西;矫揉造作之物 [formal, disapproval]

  • They wear simple clothes and shun modern contrivances.

    他们衣着简朴，不使用那些现代的发明物。

  • music with a tendency towards contrivance and lack of substance.

    矫揉造作而缺乏实质性内容的音乐

SYN device, machine, equipment, gear  
2 N-COUNT 诡计;计谋;阴谋 A contrivance is an unfair or dishonest scheme or trick to gain an advantage for yourself. 诡计;计谋;阴谋

  • ...some contrivance to raise prices.

    提高价格的计谋
