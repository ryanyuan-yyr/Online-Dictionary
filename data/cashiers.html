cashier
kæʃɪəʳ kæˈʃɪr
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , plural cashiers, present participle cashiering, past tense , past participle cashiered
1 N-COUNT 出纳(员);收银员 A cashier is a person who customers pay money to or get money from in places such as shops or banks. 出纳(员);收银员
SYN teller, accountant, clerk, treasurer  
2 VERB [usually passive] 开除，革除(军职) If a person in the armed forces is cashiered, he or she is forced to leave because they have done something seriously wrong. 开除，革除(军职)

  • The government had to recall many officers who had been cashiered on political grounds. [be VERB-ed]

    政府不得不召回许多由于政治原因被革职的军官。

  • ...a cashiered army colonel. [VERB-ed]

    被革职的陆军上校

SYN dismiss, discharge, expel, cast off
