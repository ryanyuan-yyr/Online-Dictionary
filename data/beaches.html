beach
biːtʃ bitʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense beaches, present participle beaching, past tense , past participle beached
1 N-COUNT 海滩；海滨 A beach is an area of sand or stones beside the sea. 海滩；海滨

  • ...a beautiful sandy beach.

    美丽的沙滩

  • I just want to lie on the beach in the sun.

    我只想躺在海滩上晒晒太阳。

SYN shore, coast, sands, margin  
2 V-ERG 把（船）拖上岸；（船等）搁浅 If something such as a boat beaches, or if it is beached, it is pulled or forced out of the water and onto land. 把（船）拖上岸；（船等）搁浅

  • We beached the canoe, running it right up the bank. [VERB noun]

    我们把独木舟径直划到岸边，并拖上岸。

  • The boat beached on a mud flat. [VERB]

    船搁浅在一片淤泥滩上。

  • Experts are unable to explain why the whales beached themselves. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    专家们无法解释为什么这些鲸会搁浅在海滩上。

SYN land, founder, be stuck, be stranded  
Usage Note :

  • You can use beach, coast, and shore to talk about the piece of land beside a stretch of water. A beach is a flat area of sand or pebbles next to the sea. The coast is the area of land that lies alongside the sea. You may be referring just to the land close to the sea, or to a wider area that extends further inland. The shore is the area of land along the edge of the sea, a lake, or a wide river.

    beach, coast和shore都可以用来指大片水域的岸边陆地。beach是指海边的沙滩或碎石滩。coast是沿海陆地区域，既可以指紧靠海边的区域，也可以泛指沿海地区。而shore是指海洋、湖泊或宽广的河流的岸边陆地。
