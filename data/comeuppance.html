comeuppance
kʌmʌpəns ˌkʌmˈʌpəns
(also come-uppance)
N-SING [usually poss NOUN] 应得的惩罚;报应 If you say that someone has got their comeuppance, you approve of the fact that they have been punished or have suffered for something wrong that they have done. 应得的惩罚;报应 [informal, approval]

  • The central character is a bad man who shoots people and gets his comeuppance.

    主要人物是一个持枪杀人的坏蛋，最终恶贯满盈。

SYN punishment, dues, deserts, retribution
