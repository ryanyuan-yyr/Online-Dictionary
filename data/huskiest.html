husky
hʌski ˈhʌski
Word forms: comparative huskier, superlative huskiest, plural huskies
1 ADJ-GRADED （嗓音）沙哑的，深沉的 If someone's voice is husky, it is low and rather rough, often in an attractive way. （嗓音）沙哑的，深沉的

  • His voice was husky with grief.

    他因伤心而声音沙哑。

  • ...Dietrich's deep, husky voice.

    迪特里希低沉沙哑的噪音

SYN hoarse, rough, harsh, raucous  
huskily

  • 'Ready?' I asked huskily.

    “准备好了吗？”我声音沙哑地问道。

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] （男人）魁梧健美的，高大英俊的 If you describe a man as husky, you think that he is tall, strong, and attractive. （男人）魁梧健美的，高大英俊的 [informal]

  • ...a very husky young man, built like a football player.

    非常魁梧的帅气小伙，身材像橄榄球运动员

SYN muscular, powerful, strapping, rugged [US , Canadian]  
3 N-COUNT （用来拉雪橇的）爱斯基摩犬 A husky is a strong, furry dog, which is used to pull sledges across snow. （用来拉雪橇的）爱斯基摩犬
