capitulate
kəpɪtʃʊleɪt kəˈpɪtʃjʊˌleɪt kəˈpɪtʃəˌleɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense capitulates, present participle capitulating, past tense , past participle capitulated
1 VERB 不再反抗;屈服 If you capitulate, you stop resisting and do what someone else wants you to do. 不再反抗;屈服

  • The club eventually capitulated and now grants equal rights to women. [VERB]

    那家俱乐部最终还是让了步,现在赋予女性以平等权利。

  • In less than two hours Cohen capitulated to virtually every demand. [VERB + to]

    还不到两个小时,科恩就屈服了,接受了几乎一切要求。

SYN give in, yield, concede, submit
