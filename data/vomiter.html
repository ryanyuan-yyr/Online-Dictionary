vomit
vɒmɪt ˈvɑmɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense vomits, present participle vomiting, past tense , past participle vomited
1 VERB 吐;呕吐 If you vomit, food and drink comes back up from your stomach and out through your mouth. 吐;呕吐

  • Any product made from cow's milk made him vomit. [VERB]

    任何乳制品都会让他呕吐。

  • She began to vomit blood a few days before she died. [VERB noun]

    她临死前几天开始吐血。

  • He vomited up all he had just eaten. [V n with up]

    他把刚才吃下的所有东西都吐了出来。

SYN bring up, throw up, regurgitate, chuck (up) [slang , mainly US]  
vomiting

  • Nausea, diarrhoea, and vomiting may accompany migraine.

    伴随偏头痛可能会出现恶心、腹泻和呕吐症状。

2 N-UNCOUNT 呕吐物 Vomit is partly digested food and drink that has come back up from someone's stomach and out through their mouth. 呕吐物
