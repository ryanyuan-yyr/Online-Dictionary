inclined
ɪnklaɪnd ɪnˈklaɪnd
1 ADJ-GRADED [verb-link ADJ, ADJ to-infinitive, so ADJ] 愿意…的;有…意向的 If you are inclined to behave in a particular way, you often behave in that way, or you want to do so. 愿意…的;有…意向的

  • Nobody felt inclined to argue with Smith.

    没人愿意与史密斯争辩。

  • He was inclined to self-pity.

    他总是自怜自叹。

  • If you are so inclined, you can watch TV.

    如果你这么想看电视，你就看吧。

SYN disposed, given, prone, likely  
2 ADJ-GRADED 倾向于…的;赞成…的 If you say that you are inclined to have a particular opinion, you mean that you hold this opinion but you are not expressing it strongly. 倾向于…的;赞成…的 [vagueness]

  • I am inclined to think that the ancient Greeks understood this better than we do.

    我倾向于认为古希腊人对此比我们了解的更多。

  • I am inclined to agree with Alan.

    我倾向于赞同艾伦的观点。

SYN willing, minded, ready, disposed  
3 ADJ [adverb ADJ] 有…天分的;有…天赋的 Someone who is mathematically inclined or artistically inclined, for example, has a natural talent for mathematics or art. 有…天分的;有…天赋的

  • Bratby's grandfather had been artistically inclined.

    布拉特比的祖父很有艺术天分。

  • ...the needs of academically inclined pupils.

    对在学术上有天赋的学生的需求

4 See also: incline
