let off
1 PHRASAL VERB 准许…不必做（或不必工作） If someone in authority lets you off a task or duty, they give you permission not to do it. 准许…不必做（或不必工作） [mainly British]

  • The theatre management kindly let me off a couple of performances to go to Yorkshire. [V n P n/-ing]

    剧院领导很体谅地同意我休演几场抽身到约克郡去。

  • In those days they didn't let you off work to go home very often. [V n P n/v-ing]

    在当时，他们并不经常准许请假回家。

2 PHRASAL VERB 对…宽大处理 If you let someone off, you give them a lighter punishment than they expect or no punishment at all. 对…宽大处理

  • Because he was a Christian, the judge let him off. [VERB noun PREP.]

    由于他是基督徒，法官便对他从轻发落。

  • When police realised who he was, they asked for an autograph and let him off with a warning. [V n P prep/adv]

    警察知道他是谁后，便向他要了签名，然后只给了他个警告就放他走了。

SYN excuse, release, discharge, pardon  
3 PHRASAL VERB 使（炸药等）爆炸；开（枪、炮等） If you let off an explosive or a gun, you explode or fire it. 使（炸药等）爆炸；开（枪、炮等） [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • A resident of his neighbourhood had let off fireworks to celebrate the Revolution. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他家附近的一个居民燃放了烟花庆祝革命胜利。

SYN fire, explode, set off, discharge
