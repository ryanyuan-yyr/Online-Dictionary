misbehave
mɪsbɪheɪv ˌmɪsbiˈheɪv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense misbehaves, present participle misbehaving, past tense , past participle misbehaved
VERB (尤指儿童)行为不当，举止失礼 If someone, especially a child, misbehaves, they behave in a way that is not acceptable to other people. (尤指儿童)行为不当，举止失礼

  • When the children misbehaved she was unable to cope. [VERB]

    孩子们调皮捣蛋的时候她管不了。

SYN be naughty, be bad, act up [informal] , muck about [British , slang]
