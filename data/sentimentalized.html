sentimentalize
sentɪmentəlaɪz ˌsɛntəˈmɛntəlˌaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense sentimentalizes, present participle sentimentalizing, past tense , past participle sentimentalized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 sentimentalise
VERB 使感伤;使带有感情色彩 If you sentimentalize something, you make it seem sentimental or think about it in a sentimental way. 使感伤;使带有感情色彩

  • He seems either to fear women or to sentimentalize them. [VERB noun]

    他似乎要么怕女人要么就对她们怀有浪漫想法。

  • He's the kind of filmmaker who doesn't hesitate to over-sentimentalize. [VERB]

    他是那种煽起情来不遗余力的电影制作人。

  • ...Rupert Brooke's sentimentalised glorification of war. [VERB-ed]

    鲁珀特·布鲁克对战争的带有个人感情色彩的颂扬
