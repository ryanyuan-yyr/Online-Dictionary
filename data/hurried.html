hurried
hʌrid hɜːr- ˈhɜrid
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] （行动）匆忙的，迅速的 A hurried action is done quickly, because you do not have much time to do it in. （行动）匆忙的，迅速的

  • ...a hurried breakfast.

    匆忙塞了几口的早餐

hurriedly

  • ...students hurriedly taking notes.

    匆忙记笔记的学生

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] （行动）仓促的，突然的，急于应对的 A hurried action is done suddenly, in reaction to something that has just happened. （行动）仓促的，突然的，急于应对的

  • Downing Street denied there had been a hurried overnight redrafting of the text.

    唐宁街否认连夜赶工改拟了文本。

SYN rushed, perfunctory, hectic, speedy  
hurriedly

  • The moment she saw it, she blushed and hurriedly left the room.

    她一看到这一幕就脸红了，连忙匆匆走出了房间。

3 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] （人）做事匆忙的，行动仓促的 Someone who is hurried does things more quickly than they should because they do not have much time to do them. （人）做事匆忙的，行动仓促的

  • Parisians on the street often looked worried, hurried and unfriendly.

    巴黎街上的路人往往看上去忧心忡忡、匆匆忙忙而且不太友善。
