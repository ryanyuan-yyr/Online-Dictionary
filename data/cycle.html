cycle
saɪkəl ˈsaɪkəl ˈsɪkəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense cycles, present participle cycling, past tense , past participle cycled
1 VERB 骑(自行车) If you cycle, you ride a bicycle. 骑(自行车)

  • He cycled to Ingwold. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他骑车去英格伍德。

  • Britain could save £4.6 billion a year in road transport costs if more people cycled. [VERB]

    如更多的人骑自行车，英国一年能在道路交通上节省46亿英镑的开销。

  • Over 1000 riders cycled 100 miles around the Vale of York. [VERB noun]

    有1,000多人围着约克谷地骑行了100英里。

cycling

  • The quiet country roads are ideal for cycling.

    宁静的乡间道路是骑自行车的理想场所。

2 N-COUNT 自行车 A cycle is a bicycle. 自行车

  • ...an eight-mile cycle ride.

    骑自行车8英里

3 N-COUNT 摩托车 A cycle is a motorcycle. 摩托车 [US]
4 N-COUNT 循环；周期 A cycle is a series of events or processes that is repeated again and again, always in the same order. 循环；周期

  • ...the life cycle of the plant. [+ of]

    植物的生命周期

  • The figures marked the final low point of the present economic cycle.

    这些数据显示了目前经济周期的最低点。

  • They must break out of the cycle of violence.

    他们必须打破暴力循环。

5 N-COUNT [usually plural] (运转的)一周，一圈;一个工作循环 A cycle is a single complete series of movements in an electrical, electronic, or mechanical process. (运转的)一周，一圈;一个工作循环

  • ...10 cycles per second.

    每秒10转

6 N-COUNT [usually with supplement] 组歌;组诗 A cycle is a series of songs or poems that are intended to be performed or read one after the other. 组歌;组诗

  • ...Wagner's Ring cycle.

    瓦格纳的《指环》组歌
