intermingle
ɪntəʳmɪŋgəl ˌɪntərˈmɪŋgəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense intermingles, present participle intermingling, past tense , past participle intermingled
V-RECIP 混合;混在一起 When people or things intermingle, they mix with each other. 混合;混在一起 [formal]

  • This allows the two cultures to intermingle without losing their separate identities. [VERB]

    这使得两种文化能相互融合，却又不失其个性。

  • ...an opportunity for them to intermingle with the citizens of other countries. [VERB + with]

    与其他国家的公民打成一片的机会

SYN mix, combine, blend, merge  
intermingled

  • The ethnic populations are so intermingled that there's bound to be conflict.

    各民族人口如此杂居,肯定会发生冲突。
