desolation
desəleɪʃən ˌdɛsəˈleɪʃən
1 N-UNCOUNT 凄凉;孤寂;悲怆;绝望 Desolation is a feeling of great unhappiness and hopelessness. 凄凉;孤寂;悲怆;绝望

  • Kozelek expresses his sense of desolation absolutely without self-pity.

    科泽莱克不带一丝顾影自怜地表达了自己的凄凉悲怆之感。

SYN misery, distress, despair, gloom  
2 N-UNCOUNT 荒凉;荒芜;荒废;满目疮痍 If you refer to desolation in a place, you mean that it is empty and frightening, for example because it has been destroyed by a violent force or army. 荒凉;荒芜;荒废;满目疮痍 [disapproval]

  • We looked out upon a scene of desolation and ruin.

    我们放眼望去，眼前是一片荒凉的废墟。

  • She says the army left desolation and death through the whole of northern Morazan.

    她说军队在整个莫拉桑省北部留下了满目疮痍和遍野横尸。

SYN bleakness, isolation, loneliness, solitude
