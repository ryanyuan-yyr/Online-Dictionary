bath
bɑːθ bæθ bɑːðz bæθs bæðz bɑːθs bæθ bɑːθ bæθ bɑθ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense baths, present participle bathing, past tense , past participle bathed
When the form baths is the plural of the noun it is pronounced /bɑːðz/ or /bæθs/ in British English, and /bæðz/ in American English. When it is used in the present tense of the verb, it is pronounced /bɑːθs/ or /bæθs/. baths 用作名词的复数形式时在英国英语中读作 /bɑːðz/ 或 /bæθs/，在美国英语中读作 /bæðz/。用作动词的一般现在时形式时读作 /bɑːθs/ 或者 /bæθs/。
1 N-COUNT 浴盆；浴缸 A bath is a container, usually a long rectangular one, which you fill with water and sit in while you wash your body. 浴盆；浴缸 [British]

  • In those days, only quite wealthy families had baths of their own.

    那时候，只有非常富有的家庭才有自己的浴缸。

in AM, use 美国英语用 bathtub
2 N-COUNT （在浴缸中的）洗澡，沐浴 When you have or take a bath, or when you are in the bath, you sit or lie in a bath filled with water in order to wash your body. （在浴缸中的）洗澡，沐浴

  • ...if you have a bath every morning.

    如果你每天早上泡澡

  • Take a shower instead of a bath.

    洗淋浴吧，别用浴缸了。

  • ...a bath and shower gel.

    沐浴凝胶

3 VERB 给（小孩）洗澡 If you bath someone, especially a child, you wash them in a bath. 给（小孩）洗澡 [British]

  • Don't feel you have to bath your child every day. [VERB noun]

    不用每天给孩子洗澡。

SYN clean, wash, soap, shower  
Bath is also a noun.

  • The midwife gave him a warm bath.

    助产士给他洗了个热水澡。

in AM, use 美国英语用 bathe
4 VERB 洗澡；沐浴 When you bath, you have a bath. 洗澡；沐浴 [British]

  • The three children all bath in the same bath water. [VERB prep./adv.]

    这三个小孩用同一盆水洗澡。

in AM, use 美国英语用 bathe
5 N-COUNT 澡堂；（附带泳池的）公共浴室 A bath or a baths is a public building containing a swimming pool, and sometimes other facilities that people can use to have a wash or a bath. 澡堂；（附带泳池的）公共浴室

  • One of the most important buildings in this ruined city is a public bath.

    在这座被毁坏的城市里，最重要的建筑物之一就是公共澡堂。

  • As well as a Roman amphitheatre and baths, the town has two superb museums.

    除了罗马式圆形剧场和公共浴池，这个镇上还有两座非常好的博物馆。

6 N-COUNT （盛放溶液的）容器 A bath is a container filled with a particular liquid, such as a dye or an acid, in which particular objects are placed, usually as part of a manufacturing or chemical process. （盛放溶液的）容器

  • ...a developing photograph placed in a bath of fixer.

    放在定影液中冲洗的照片
