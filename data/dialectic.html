dialectic
daɪəlektɪk ˌdaɪəˈlɛktɪk
Word forms: plural dialectics
1 N-COUNT 辩证关系;(两种力量或因素之间的)对立统一 People refer to the dialectic or dialectics of a situation when they are referring to the way in which two very different forces or factors work together, and the way in which their differences are resolved. 辩证关系;(两种力量或因素之间的)对立统一 [technical, formal]

  • ...the intricate dialectic of these two contrasting concepts.

    这两个相互对立的概念之间错综复杂的辩证关系

  • ...the dialectics of class struggle and of socio-economic change.

    阶级斗争的对立统一和社会经济变革的对立统一

2 N-UNCOUNT 辩证法 In philosophy, dialectics is a method of reasoning and reaching conclusions by considering theories and ideas together with ones that contradict them. 辩证法 [technical]
