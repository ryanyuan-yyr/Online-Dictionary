advocate
ædvəkeɪt ædvəkət ˈædvəkɪt ˈædvəˌkeɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense advocates, present participle advocating, past tense , past participle advocated
The verb is pronounced /'ædvəkeɪt/. The noun is pronounced /'ædvəkət/. 动词读作 /'ædvəkeɪt/，名词读作 /'ædvəkət/。
1 VERB 拥护；提倡；主张 If you advocate a particular action or plan, you recommend it publicly. 拥护；提倡；主张 [formal]

  • Mr Williams is a conservative who advocates fewer government controls on business. [VERB noun]

    威廉斯先生是个保守派，主张政府应减少对商业的控制。

  • ...the tax policy advocated by the Opposition. [VERB-ed]

    反对党提倡的税收政策

SYN recommend, support, champion, encourage  
2 N-COUNT 拥护者；提倡者 An advocate of a particular action or plan is someone who recommends it publicly. 拥护者；提倡者 [formal]

  • He was a strong advocate of free market policies and a multi-party system. [+ of]

    他是自由市场政策和多党派制度的坚决拥护者。

SYN supporter, spokesman, champion, defender  
3 N-COUNT 辩护律师 An advocate is a lawyer who speaks in favour of someone or defends them in a court of law. →see usage note at: lawyer 辩护律师
4 N-COUNT （为某一类人）谋利益者 An advocate for a particular group is a person who works for the interests of that group. （为某一类人）谋利益者 [US]

  • ...advocates for the homeless. [+ for]

    为无家可归的人谋利益者

  • Animal rights advocates argue that zoos are really animal prisons and very often cruel.

    动物权利保护者指出动物园实际上是动物的牢笼，经常很残酷地对待动物。

5See also:devil's advocate
