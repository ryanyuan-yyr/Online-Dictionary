squeal
skwiːl skwil
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense squeals, present participle squealing, past tense , past participle squealed
VERB 发出长而尖的叫声 If someone or something squeals, they make a long, high-pitched sound. 发出长而尖的叫声 [Also VERB with quote]

  • Jennifer squealed with delight and hugged me. [VERB + with]

    珍妮弗高兴地尖叫起来，然后拥抱了我。

  • The car's tires squealed again as it sped around the corner. [VERB]

    汽车快速驶过拐角时，轮胎又一次发出嘎吱声。

SYN scream, yell, shriek, screech  
Squeal is also a noun.

  • At that moment there was a squeal of brakes and the angry blowing of a car horn.

    当时就听见刺耳的刹车声和愤怒的汽车喇叭声。
