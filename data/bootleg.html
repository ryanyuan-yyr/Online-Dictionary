bootleg
buːtleg ˈbuːtˌlɛɡ ˈbutˌlɛg
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense bootlegs, present participle bootlegging, past tense , past participle bootlegged
1 ADJ [ADJ n] 非法制造贩卖的 Bootleg is used to describe something that is made secretly and sold illegally. 非法制造贩卖的

  • ...a bootleg recording of the band's 1977 tour of Scandinavia.

    该乐队1977年斯堪的纳维亚巡回演唱会的盗版录音带

  • ...bootleg liquor.

    私酒

SYN illicit, illegal, outlawed, pirate  
2 VERB 非法制造贩卖（唱片等） To bootleg something such as a recording means to make and sell it illegally. 非法制造贩卖（唱片等）

  • He has sued a fan for bootlegging his concerts. [VERB noun]

    他已经起诉一个歌迷非法录制他音乐会的节目。

  • Avid Bob Dylan fans treasure bootlegged recordings. [VERB-ed]

    鲍勃·迪伦的疯狂歌迷们对他的盗版唱片视若珍宝。

SYN pirate  
Bootleg is also a noun.

  • The record was a bootleg.

    这张唱片是盗版的。

bootlegger

  • Bootleggers sold 75 million dollars-worth of copies.

    非法书商们售出了价值7,500万美元的盗版书。
