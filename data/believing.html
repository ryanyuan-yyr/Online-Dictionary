believe
bɪliːv bəˈliv biˈliv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense believes, present participle believing, past tense , past participle believed
1 VERB 相信；认为 If you believe that something is true, you think that it is true, but you are not sure. 相信；认为 [formal] [Also V n adj]

  • Experts believe that the coming drought will be extensive. [VERB that]

    专家们认为即将发生的旱灾将侵袭大片地区。

  • I believe you have something of mine. [VERB that]

    我想你拿了我什么东西。

  • The main problem, I believe, lies elsewhere. [VERB that]

    主要的问题，我认为，出在别的地方。

  • We believe them to be hidden here in this apartment. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    我们相信他们就被藏在这套公寓里。

  • 'You've never heard of him?' — 'I don't believe so.' [V so]

    “你从来没有听说过他？”—“我认为不是这样的。”

SYN think, consider, judge, suppose  
2 VERB 相信（某人或某人的话） If you believe someone or if you believe what they say or write, you accept that they are telling the truth. 相信（某人或某人的话）

  • He did not sound as if he believed her. [VERB noun]

    他听起来似乎并不相信她。

  • Never believe anything a married man says about his wife. [VERB noun]

    永远不要相信一个已婚男人说的任何有关他妻子的话。

  • Don't believe what you read in the papers. [VERB noun]

    不要相信报纸上看到的东西。

SYN accept, hold, buy [slang] , trust  
3 VERB 相信有…；认为…存在 If you believe in fairies, ghosts, or miracles, you are sure that they exist or happen. If you believe in a god, you are sure of the existence of that god. 相信有…；认为…存在 [Also VERB]

  • I don't believe in ghosts. [VERB + in]

    我不相信有鬼。

  • Do you believe in magic? [VERB + in]

    你相信魔法真的存在吗？

4 VERB 认为…是应该的（或正确的） If you believe in a way of life or an idea, you are in favour of it because you think it is good or right. 认为…是应该的（或正确的）

  • He believed in marital fidelity. [VERB + in]

    他认为婚内应该忠诚。

  • ...decent candidates who believed in democracy. [VERB in noun]

    信奉民主政治的正派候选人

5 VERB 对…有信心；信任；信赖 If you believe in someone or what they are doing, you have confidence in them and think that they will be successful. 对…有信心；信任；信赖

  • If you believe in yourself you can succeed. [VERB + in]

    只要自信，就能成功。

Usage Note :

  • Note that when you are using the verb believe with that-clause in order to state a negative opinion or belief, you normally make believe negative, rather than the verb. For instance, it is more usual to say 'He didn't believe she could do it...' than 'He believed she couldn't do it'. The same applies to other verbs with a similar meaning, such as consider, suppose, and think. I don't consider that you kept your promise... I don't suppose he ever saw it... I don't think he saw me.

    注意，当 believe 和 that 从句连用陈述否定的观点或看法时，通常对 believe 而不是从句中的动词加以否定。例如，通常说 He didn't believe she could do it（他认为她做不到），而很少说 He believed she couldn't do it。同样的句型结构也适用于 consider, suppose 和 think 等意思相近的动词：I don't consider that you kept your promise（我认为你没有遵守诺言），I don't suppose he ever saw it（我想他从没见过它），I don't think he saw me（我认为他没有看到我）。

6 VERB （表示惊奇）难以置信 Believe is used in expressions such as I can't believe how or it's hard to believe that in order to express surprise, for example because something bad has happened or something very difficult has been achieved. （表示惊奇）难以置信 [feelings] [Also VERB that]

  • Many officers I spoke to found it hard to believe what was happening around them. [VERB wh]

    很多和我谈过的官员都觉得发生在他们周围的事情令人难以置信。

  • I was a physical and emotional wreck — I still can't believe how I ever got any work done. [VERB wh]

    我那时身心俱疲—现在还难以相信我是怎么把一些事情做成的。

7 PHRASE [VERB inflects, with brd-neg] （表示对所见所闻惊讶不已）相信自己的眼睛/耳朵 If you say that you cannot believe your eyes or cannot believe your ears, you are emphasizing that you are very surprised about something you have seen or heard. （表示对所见所闻惊讶不已）相信自己的眼睛/耳朵 [emphasis]

  • I could not believe my eyes. She was far more beautiful than I had imagined.

    我简直不敢相信自己的眼睛。她比我想象的漂亮多了。

8 PHRASE （强调刚才所述之事令人吃惊）信不信由你 You can use believe it or not to emphasize that what you have just said is surprising. （强调刚才所述之事令人吃惊）信不信由你 [emphasis]

  • That's normal, believe it or not.

    那很正常，信不信由你。

9 PHRASE （强调对某事感到惊讶）你会相信吗？ If you say would you believe it, you are emphasizing your surprise about something. （强调对某事感到惊讶）你会相信吗？ [emphasis]

  • And would you believe it, he's younger than me!

    你信吗？他比我年轻！

10 PHRASE （强调所述属实）相信我 You can use believe you me to emphasize that what you are saying is true. （强调所述属实）相信我 [emphasis]

  • It's absolutely amazing, believe you me.

    这太不可思议了，真的。
