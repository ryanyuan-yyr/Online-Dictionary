slaver
slævəʳ ˈsleɪvə ˈsleɪvər ˈslævər ˈslɑvər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense slavers, present participle slavering, past tense , past participle slavered
1 VERB (动物)流口水，垂涎 If an animal slavers, liquid comes from its mouth, for example because it is about to attack and eat something. (动物)流口水，垂涎

  • Mad guard dogs slavered at the end of their chains. [VERB]

    拴在链子上的看门狗狂躁地流着口水。

  • ...the wolf's slavering jaws. [VERB-ing]

    狼流着口水的嘴巴

SYN dribble, drool, salivate, slobber  
2 VERB 兴奋地无法自制;狂喜 If you say that someone is slavering, you mean that they are so excited by something that they cannot control themselves, and that you find this disgusting. 兴奋地无法自制;狂喜 [disapproval] [Also VERB]

  • I found myself skipping these passages, though no doubt many readers will slaver over them. [V over n]

    我发现自己跳过了这些章节，尽管有许多读者无疑会为这些描写兴奋不已。

SYN drool, salivate, slobber, dribble
