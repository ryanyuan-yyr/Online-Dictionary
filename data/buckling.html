buckle
bʌkəl ˈbʌkəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense buckles, present participle buckling, past tense , past participle buckled
1 N-COUNT （皮带等的）搭扣，搭钩 A buckle is a piece of metal or plastic attached to one end of a belt or strap, which is used to fasten it. （皮带等的）搭扣，搭钩

  • He wore a belt with a large brass buckle.

    他系了条带有一个大的铜搭扣的皮带。

SYN fastener, catch, clip, clasp  
2 VERB 扣住；扣紧 When you buckle a belt or strap, you fasten it. 扣住；扣紧

  • A door slammed in the house and a man came out buckling his belt. [VERB noun]

    房子里的一扇门砰地关上，一个男子边扣腰带边走了出来。

SYN fasten, close, secure, hook  
3 V-ERG （因受热或受力）（使）弯曲，（使）变形 If an object buckles or if something buckles it, it becomes bent as a result of very great heat or force. （因受热或受力）（使）弯曲，（使）变形

  • The door was beginning to buckle from the intense heat. [VERB]

    门在高温高热下开始变形。

  • A freak wave had buckled the deck. [VERB noun]

    突然掀起的巨浪打弯了甲板。

SYN distort, bend, warp, crumple  
4 VERB （双腿或膝盖因虚弱、劳累）发软 If your legs or knees buckle, they bend because they have become very weak or tired. （双腿或膝盖因虚弱、劳累）发软

  • Mcanally's knees buckled and he crumpled down onto the floor. [VERB]

    麦卡纳利双膝发软，瘫倒在地板上。

  • His right leg buckled under him. [VERB]

    他的右腿发软打战。

SYN collapse, bend, twist, fold  
Phrasal verbs:
