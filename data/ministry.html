ministry
mɪnɪstri ˈmɪnɪstri
Word forms: plural ministries
1 N-COUNT [n NOUN] (政府的)部 In Britain and some other countries, a ministry is a government department which deals with a particular thing or area of activity, for example trade, defence, or transport. (政府的)部

  • ...the Ministry of Justice. [+ of]

    司法部

  • ...a spokesman for the Agriculture Ministry.

    农业部发言人

SYN department, office, bureau, government department  
2 N-COUNT [usually singular, usually with poss] 圣职;神职 The ministry of a religious person is the work that they do that is based on or inspired by their religious beliefs. 圣职;神职

  • His ministry is among the poor.

    他是穷人的牧师。

3 N-SING-COLL [the NOUN] （基督教的）全体牧师 Members of the clergy belonging to some branches of the Christian church are referred to as the ministry. （基督教的）全体牧师

  • So what prompted him to enter the ministry?

    那么，是什么促使他成为牧师的呢？

SYN the priesthood, the church, the cloth, the pulpit
