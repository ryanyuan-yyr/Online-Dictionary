acknowledgment
æknɒlɪdʒmənt əkˈnɒlɪdʒmənt ækˈnɑlɪdʒmənt əkˈnɑlɪdʒmənt
(also acknowledgement)Word forms: acknowledgments
1 N-SING [also no det] (对某物存在或属实的) 承认An acknowledgment is a statement or action which recognizes that something exists or is true. (对某物存在或属实的) 承认

  • The president's resignation appears to be an acknowledgment that he has lost all hope of keeping the country together.

    该总统的辞职看起来是对他已经放弃将国家团结起来的所有希望的一种承认。

2 N-PLURAL 致谢辞The acknowledgments in a book are the section in which the author thanks all the people who have helped him or her. 致谢辞

  • ...two whole pages of acknowledgments.

    …整整两页的致谢辞。

3 N-UNCOUNT [also a N] 致意A gesture of acknowledgment, such as a smile, shows someone that you have seen and recognized them. 致意

  • Farling smiled in acknowledgment and gave a bow.

    法林微笑致意并鞠了一躬。
