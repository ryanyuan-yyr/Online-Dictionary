street
striːt strit
Word forms: plural streets
1 N-COUNT; N-IN-NAMES 大街;街道 A street is a road in a city, town, or village, usually with houses along it. 大街;街道

  • He lived at 66 Bingfield Street.

    他住在宾菲尔德大街 66 号。

  • Boppard is a small, quaint town with narrow streets.

    博帕德是一个有着许多深巷窄道、古香古色的小镇。

SYN road, lane, avenue, terrace  
2 N-COUNT [usu on/off N] 户外;街头 You can use street or streets when talking about activities that happen out of doors in a town rather than inside a building. 户外;街头

  • Changing money on the street is illegal — always use a bank.

    在街头兑换货币是违法的—无论何时都应该去银行。

  • Their aim is to raise a million pounds to get the homeless off the streets.

    他们的目标是筹集 100 万英镑，使那些无家可归者不再流浪街头。

  • ...a New York street gang.

    纽约街头帮派

3 See also: back street  civvy street  Downing Street  Fleet Street  high street  Wall Street 
4 PHRASE (比…)好得多 If someone is streets ahead of you, they are much better at something than you are. (比…)好得多

  • He was streets ahead of the other contestants. [+ of]

    他比其他参赛选手水平高出一大截。

5 PHRASE 普通人;平民百姓 If you talk about the man in the street or the man or woman in the street, you mean ordinary people in general. 普通人;平民百姓

  • The average man or woman in the street doesn't know very much about immune disorders.

    普通人一般不是很了解何谓免疫机能紊乱。

6 PHRASE 中…的意;和…对口 If a job or activity is up your street, it is the kind of job or activity that you are very interested in. 中…的意;和…对口 [British]

  • She loved it, this was just up her street.

    她喜欢这个，这正中她的意。
