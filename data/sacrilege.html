sacrilege
sækrɪlɪdʒ ˈsækrəˌlɪdʒ
1 N-UNCOUNT [oft a NOUN] (对圣地或圣物的)亵渎,渎圣 Sacrilege is behaviour that shows great disrespect for a holy place or object. (对圣地或圣物的)亵渎,渎圣

  • Stealing from a place of worship was regarded as sacrilege.

    从礼拜场所偷东西是对神灵的亵渎。

SYN desecration, violation, blasphemy, mockery  
2 N-UNCOUNT [oft a NOUN] (对名人或普遍信仰的)大不敬,冒犯,无礼 You can use sacrilege to refer to disrespect that is shown for someone who is widely admired or for a belief that is widely accepted. (对名人或普遍信仰的)大不敬,冒犯,无礼

  • It is a sacrilege to offend democracy.

    违反民主是冒天下之大不韪。

SYN disrespect, contempt, disregard, dishonour
