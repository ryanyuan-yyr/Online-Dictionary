salute
səluːt səˈlut
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense salutes, present participle saluting, past tense , past participle saluted
1 VERB 向…致意;向…致敬;向…行军礼 If you salute someone, you greet them or show your respect with a formal sign. Soldiers usually salute officers by raising their right hand so that their fingers touch their forehead. 向…致意;向…致敬;向…行军礼

  • One of the company stepped out and saluted the General. [VERB noun]

    人群中走出一人向将军敬礼。

  • I stood to attention and saluted. [VERB]

    我立正敬礼。

SYN greet, welcome, acknowledge, address  
Salute is also a noun.

  • The soldier gave the clenched-fist salute.

    士兵行握拳礼。

  • He raised his hand in salute.

    他举手致意。

2 VERB 赞扬;颂扬 To salute a person or their achievements means to publicly show or state your admiration for them. 赞扬;颂扬

  • I salute him for the leadership role that he is taking. [VERB noun]

    我对他发挥的领导作用表示赞扬。

  • The statement salutes the changes of the past year. [VERB noun]

    这一表态称赞了过去一年的变革。

SYN honour, celebrate, acknowledge, recognize  
Salute is also a noun.

  • ...a special salute to Anne Scargill for her plucky underground protest.

    对安妮·斯卡吉尔勇敢开展地下抗议活动致以特别的敬意
