warrant
wɒrənt wɔːr- ˈwɔrənt ˈwɑrənt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense warrants, present participle warranting, past tense , past participle warranted
1 VERB 使显得必要(或正当) If something warrants a particular action, it makes the action seem necessary or appropriate for the circumstances. 使显得必要(或正当)

  • The allegations are serious enough to warrant an investigation. [VERB noun]

    这些指控很严重，有必要进行一番调查。

  • No matter was too small to warrant his attention. [VERB noun]

    再小的事也会引起他的注意。

SYN call for, demand, require, merit  
warranted

  • Do you think this fear is warranted?

    你认为有必要这样害怕吗？

2 N-COUNT [oft by NOUN] 搜查令;拘捕令；令状 A warrant is a legal document that allows someone to do something, especially one that is signed by a judge or magistrate and gives the police permission to arrest someone or search their house. 搜查令;拘捕令；令状

  • Police confirmed that they had issued a warrant for his arrest. [+ for]

    警方证实他们已经签发了对他的拘捕令。

  • ...a search warrant.

    搜查令

  • Equipment is allocated by warrant.

    设备已经按照令状分配好了。

SYN authorization, permit, licence, permission  
3 N-UNCOUNT [oft with brd-neg, usu N for n/-ing] 正当理由 If you say that there is no warrant for something, you mean that there is no good reason to justify it. 正当理由 [formal]

  • There is some warrant for holding back on full-scale aid.

    有理由不进行全面援助。

SYN justification, reason, grounds, defence  
4 VERB 保证;担保 If you warrant that something is true or will happen, you say officially that it is true, or guarantee that it will happen. 保证;担保 [formal]

  • All entrants must warrant that their entry is entirely their own work. [VERB that]

    所有参赛者必须保证其参赛作品完全是自己创作的。

  • The contract warrants that an experienced person is on board all the time. [VERB that]

    合同确保一位富有经验的人会一直呆在船上。

5 See also: death warrant
