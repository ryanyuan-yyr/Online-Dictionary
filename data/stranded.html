strand
strænd strænd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense strands, present participle stranding, past tense , past participle stranded
1 N-COUNT (头发、电线或纱线等的)股，缕 A strand of something such as hair, wire, or thread is a single thin piece of it. (头发、电线或纱线等的)股，缕

  • She tried to blow a gray strand of hair from her eyes. [+ of]

    她竭力想把眼前的一缕白发吹开。

  • ...high fences, topped by strands of barbed-wire. [+ of]

    顶部装有一束束带刺铁丝的高围栏

  • He began to feed in the spaghetti, carefully separating the strands.

    他开始放入意大利面，小心翼翼地将面一缕缕分开。

SYN filament, fibre, thread, length  
2 N-COUNT (计划或理论的)部分，方面 A strand of a plan or theory is a part of it. (计划或理论的)部分，方面

  • There had been two strands to his tactics.

    他的策略分两个部分。

  • He's trying to bring together various strands of radical philosophic thought.

    他正试图把各种激进的哲学思想综合在一起。

SYN component, part, element, ingredient  
3 VERB (坏天气等)使滞留，使搁浅 If you are stranded, you are prevented from leaving a place, for example because of bad weather. (坏天气等)使滞留，使搁浅

  • The climbers had been stranded by a storm. [be VERB-ed]

    登山者被暴风雨困住了。

  • The airport had to be closed, stranding tourists. [VERB noun]

    机场被迫关闭，造成游客滞留。
