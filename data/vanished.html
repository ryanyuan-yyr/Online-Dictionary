vanish
vænɪʃ ˈvænɪʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense vanishes, present participle vanishing, past tense , past participle vanished
1 VERB 消失;失踪;突然不见 If someone or something vanishes, they disappear suddenly or in a way that cannot be explained. 消失;失踪;突然不见

  • He just vanished and was never seen again. [VERB]

    他就这么消失了，再也没人见到过他。

  • The aircraft vanished without trace. [VERB]

    飞机消失得无影无踪。

  • Anne vanished from outside her home last Wednesday. [VERB + from]

    安妮上周三突然在家门外失踪了。

  • The gunmen paused only to cut the wires to the house, then vanished into the countryside. [VERB + into]

    那些持枪歹徒停下来只是切断了连接到房子里的电线，然后消失在乡间。

SYN disappear, become invisible, be lost to sight, dissolve  
2 VERB (物种）灭绝;（传统）消亡 If something such as a species of animal or a tradition vanishes, it stops existing. (物种）灭绝;（传统）消亡

  • Near the end of Devonian times, thirty percent of all animal life vanished. [VERB]

    在泥盆纪末期，30％的动物物种都灭绝了。

  • ...all the species of birds that had vanished from Iowa.

    艾奥瓦州所有消失了的鸟类物种
