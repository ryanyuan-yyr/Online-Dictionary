pinpoint
pɪnpɔɪnt ˈpɪnˌpɔɪnt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense pinpoints, present participle pinpointing, past tense , past participle pinpointed
1 VERB 确认；准确解释（或说明） If you pinpoint the cause of something, you discover or explain the cause exactly. 确认；准确解释（或说明）

  • It was almost impossible to pinpoint the cause of death. [VERB noun]

    几乎不可能确认死因。

  • ...if you can pinpoint exactly what the anger is about. [VERB wh]

    如果你能确切地解释愤怒从何而来

  • The commission pinpoints inadequate housing as a basic problem threatening village life. [VERB noun + as]

    该委员会明确指出住房不足是威胁村民生活的根本问题。

SYN identify, discover, spot, define  
2 VERB 精确地确定（或指明）…的位置 If you pinpoint something or its position, you discover or show exactly where it is. 精确地确定（或指明）…的位置

  • I could pinpoint his precise location on a map. [VERB noun]

    我能在地图上指明他的准确位置。

  • Computers pinpointed where the shells were coming from. [VERB wh]

    计算机确定了炮弹发射的位置。

3 ADJ [ADJ n] 定点的；定位精确的；非常精确的 If something is placed with pinpoint accuracy, it is placed in exactly the right place or position. 定点的；定位精确的；非常精确的

  • ...the pinpoint accuracy of the bombing campaigns.

    战略性轰炸的精确定位

SYN precise, scientific, careful, strict
