outvote
aʊtvoʊt ˌaʊtˈvoʊt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense outvotes, present participle outvoting, past tense , past participle outvoted
VERB 通过投票击败；以多数票击败 If you are outvoted, more people vote against what you are suggesting than vote for it, so that your suggestion is defeated. 通过投票击败；以多数票击败

  • They walked out in protest after being outvoted by the National Salvation Front majority. [be VERB-ed]

    他们被救国阵线以多数票否决后，退席以示抗议。

  • Twice his colleagues have outvoted him. [VERB noun]

    他的同事已经两次否决了他的建议。
