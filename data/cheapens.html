cheapen
tʃiːpən ˈtʃipən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense cheapens, present participle cheapening, past tense , past participle cheapened
VERB 使有失身价;使降低身份;使贬值 If something cheapens a person or thing, it lowers their reputation or position. 使有失身价;使降低身份;使贬值

  • When America boycotted the Moscow Olympics it cheapened the medals won. [VERB noun]

    美国抵制莫斯科奥运会的行为使金牌的含金量降低了。

  • Love is a word cheapened by overuse. [VERB-ed]

    “爱”这个字眼用得太滥不值钱了。

SYN degrade, lower, discredit, devalue
