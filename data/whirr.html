whirr
hwɜːʳ
(also whir)Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense whirrs, present participle whirring, past tense , past participle whirred
VERB (机器、昆虫翅膀等)发出呼呼声，嗡嗡作响 When something such as a machine or an insect's wing whirrs, it makes a series of low sounds so quickly that they seem like one continuous sound. (机器、昆虫翅膀等)发出呼呼声，嗡嗡作响

  • The camera whirred and clicked. [VERB]

    相机发出嗡嗡声和咔嚓声。

  • ...the whirring sound of the film projector. [VERB-ing]

    电影放映机的嗡嗡声

Whirr is also a noun.

  • He could hear the whirr of a vacuum cleaner.

    他能听到真空吸尘器的嗡嗡声。

  • Whirr, click, whirr, click — step by step the scan probed deeper.

    嗡嗡，咔嗒，嗡嗡，咔嗒—扫描探查一步步地深入。

whirring

  • The silence was broken by the whirring of a helicopter.

    直升机的嗡嗡声打破了沉寂。
