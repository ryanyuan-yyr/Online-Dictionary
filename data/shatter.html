shatter
ʃætəʳ ˈʃætər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense shatters, present participle shattering, past tense , past participle shattered
1 V-ERG (使)粉碎;(使)破碎;(使)碎裂 If something shatters or is shattered, it breaks into a lot of small pieces. (使)粉碎;(使)破碎;(使)碎裂 [Also V n into n]

  • ...safety glass that won't shatter if it's broken. [VERB]

    被打破后也不会碎裂的安全玻璃

  • The car shattered into a thousand burning pieces in a 200mph crash. [VERB + into]

    汽车在时速为 200 英里时发生撞车，炸成了数以千计燃烧的碎片。

  • One bullet shattered his skull. [VERB noun]

    一颗子弹打穿了他的头颅。

SYN smash, break, burst, split  
shattering

  • ...the shattering of glass.

    玻璃的破碎

2 VERB 使(梦想、希望或信念)粉碎;(使)破灭 If something shatters your dreams, hopes, or beliefs, it completely destroys them. 使(梦想、希望或信念)粉碎;(使)破灭

  • A failure would shatter the hopes of many people. [VERB noun]

    一次失败会使很多人希望破灭。

  • Something like that really shatters your confidence. [VERB noun]

    那样的事情会让人的信心严重受挫。

  • ...broken hearts and shattered dreams. [VERB-ed]

    破碎的心和破灭的梦想

SYN destroy, ruin, wreck, blast  
3 VERB 使非常惊愕;使受到极大打击 If someone is shattered by an event, it shocks and upsets them very much. 使非常惊愕;使受到极大打击

  • He had been shattered by his son's death. [be VERB-ed]

    儿子的死让他大受打击。

  • ...the tragedy which had shattered his life. [VERB noun]

    给他的生活造成重创的悲剧

SYN devastate [informal] , shock, stun, crush
