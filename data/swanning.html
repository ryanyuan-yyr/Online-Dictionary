swan
swɒn swɑn swɔn
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense swans, present participle swanning, past tense , past participle swanned
1 N-COUNT 天鹅 A swan is a large bird with a very long neck. Swans live on rivers and lakes and are usually white. 天鹅
2 VERB 逍遥自在;悠然自得 If you describe someone as swanning around or swanning off, you mean that they go and have fun, rather than working or taking care of their responsibilities. 逍遥自在;悠然自得 [British, informal]

  • She spends her time swanning around the world. [VERB prep./adv.]

    她一个人自由自在地环游世界。

  • The mother was widowed and had swanned off. [VERB prep./adv.]

    这位母亲成了寡妇，已经悠然闲逛去了。
