visitation
vɪzɪteɪʃən ˌvɪzəˈteɪʃən
Word forms: plural visitations
1 N-COUNT （上帝等神灵的）显现，出现 A visitation is an event in which God or another non-human being seems to appear to someone or contact them. （上帝等神灵的）显现，出现

  • The young people have claimed almost daily visitations from the Virgin Mary. [+ from]

    这些年轻人说几乎每天都会看到圣母马利亚显圣。

2 N-COUNT 视察;巡视;光临;到访（幽默的说法） People sometimes refer humorously to a visit from someone, especially from someone in authority, as a visitation . 视察;巡视;光临;到访（幽默的说法）

  • They had another visitation from Essex police. [+ from]

    埃塞克斯的警察再次拜访了他们。

3 N-UNCOUNT （正式的）访问,探访，视察 Visitation is the act of officially visiting someone. （正式的）访问,探访，视察 [formal]

  • House-to-house visitation has been carried on, under the regulations of the General Board of Health.

    根据卫生委员会的规定，挨家挨户的探访持续进行了下去。

  • I had visitation rights.

    我有探视权。

SYN inspection, survey, examination, visit
