bifurcate
baɪfɜːkeɪt ˈbaɪfərˌkeɪt baɪˈfɜrˌkeɪt ˈbaɪfərkɪt baɪˈfɜrkɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense bifurcates, present participle bifurcating, past tense , past participle bifurcated
V-ERG （使）分叉；（使）分成两支 If something such as a line or path bifurcates or is bifurcated, it divides into two parts which go in different directions. （使）分叉；（使）分成两支 [Also VERB n]

  • The blood supply bifurcates between eight and thirty times before reaching each particular location in the body. [VERB]

    血液要分流8到30次才能到达身体的各个部位。

  • ...the bifurcation between high art and popular culture.
