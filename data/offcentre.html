off-centre
in AM, use 美国英语用 off-center
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 偏离中心的；中心错位的 If something is off-centre, it is not exactly in the middle of a space or surface. 偏离中心的；中心错位的

  • The pedals seem a bit off-centre and the clutch is rather stiff.

    踏板似乎有点偏心，离合器也相当紧。

  • If the blocks are placed off-centre, they will fall down.

    积木如果搭得中心错位就会倒塌。

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 异常的；古怪的；非传统的 If you describe someone or something as off-centre, you mean that they are less conventional than other people or things. 异常的；古怪的；非传统的

  • Davies's writing is far too off-centre to be commercial.

    戴维斯的作品由于太过怪诞而难以获得商业成功。
