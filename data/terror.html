terror
terəʳ ˈtɛrər
Word forms: plural terrors
1 N-UNCOUNT 恐惧;惊恐;恐慌 Terror is very great fear. 恐惧;惊恐;恐慌

  • I shook with terror whenever I was about to fly in an aeroplane.

    每次坐飞机前，我都吓得直发抖。

  • The day of terror ended after police used teargas and stormed the house.

    警察使用了催泪瓦斯攻入屋子以后，恐惧的一天结束了。

SYN fear, alarm, dread, fright  
2 N-UNCOUNT [oft NOUN n] (尤指有政治目的的)恐怖活动，恐怖主义 Terror is violence or the threat of violence, especially when it is used for political reasons. (尤指有政治目的的)恐怖活动，恐怖主义

  • The bomb attack on the capital could signal the start of a pre-election terror campaign.

    对首都的炸弹袭击可能标志着选举前的恐怖活动开始了。

3 N-COUNT 恐怖的事物 A terror is something that makes you very frightened. 恐怖的事物

  • As a boy, he had a real terror of facing people. [+ of]

    小时候，他特别害怕见人。

  • ...the terrors of violence. [+ of]

    恐怖的暴力行为

4 N-COUNT 讨厌的小孩;淘气鬼;小捣蛋 If someone describes a child as a terror, they think that he or she is naughty and difficult to control. 讨厌的小孩;淘气鬼;小捣蛋 [informal, disapproval]

  • He was a terror. He had been a difficult child for as long as his parents could remember.

    他是个淘气鬼，从小就不老实。

SYN rascal, devil, monkey, scamp  
5 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 完全吓不倒；让人丝毫不焦虑 If something holds no terrors for you, you are not at all frightened or worried by it. 完全吓不倒；让人丝毫不焦虑

  • Childbirth now held fewer terrors for her than it once had.

    她现在不像原来那样害怕分娩了。
