deserve
dɪzɜːʳv dɪˈzɜrv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense deserves, present participle deserving, past tense , past participle deserved
1 VERB 应得;应受;值得 If you say that a person or thing deserves something, you mean that they should have it or receive it because of their actions or qualities. 应得;应受;值得

  • Government officials clearly deserve some of the blame as well. [VERB noun]

    政府官员显然也应当承担部分责任。

  • They know the sport inside out, and we treat them with the respect they deserve. [VERB noun]

    他们对这项体育运动了如指掌，我们也给予了他们应有的尊重。

  • These people deserve to make more than the minimum wage. [VERB to-infinitive]

    这些人应该得到比最低工资更高的报酬。

  • His children's books are classics that deserve to be much better known. [VERB to-infinitive]

    他的儿童书非常经典，应该为更多人所知晓。

  • I felt I deserved better than that. [VERB noun]

    我觉得自己不应受到那样的对待。

  • The Park Hotel has a well-deserved reputation. [VERB-ed]

    帕克旅馆的名声当之无愧。

SYN merit, warrant, be entitled to, have a right to  
2 PHRASE 罪有应得;活该 If you say that someone got what they deserved, you mean that they deserved the bad thing that happened to them, and you have no sympathy for them. 罪有应得;活该 [feelings]

  • One of them said the two dead joy riders got what they deserved.

    他们中的一个人说这两名开偷来的车去兜风的人死得活该。
