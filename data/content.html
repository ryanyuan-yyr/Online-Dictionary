content
kəntent kɒntent ˈkɑnˌtɛnt kənˈtɛnt
Word forms: plural contents
  • 1. NOUN USES 名词用法
  • 2. ADJECTIVE AND VERB USES 形容词和动词用法

NOUN USES 名词用法

1 N-PLURAL 所容纳的东西;所含之物 The contents of a container such as a bottle, box, or room are the things that are inside it. 所容纳的东西;所含之物

  • Empty the contents of the pan into the sieve. [+ of]

    将锅里的东西倒到筛子上。

  • I emptied the contents of the fridge into carrier bags.

    我把冰箱里的东西全都腾到手提袋里。

  • Sandon Hall and its contents will be auctioned by Sotheby's on October 6.

    桑登府及府内所存物品将由苏富比拍卖行于10月6日进行拍卖。

2 N-UNCOUNT (书、演讲、电视节目等的)内容 If you refer to the content or contents of something such as a book, speech, or television programme, you are referring to the subject that it deals with, the story that it tells, or the ideas that it expresses. (书、演讲、电视节目等的)内容

  • She is reluctant to discuss the content of the play. [+ of]

    她不愿意谈该剧的内容。

  • Stricter controls were placed on the content of video films.

    对录像片内容实行了更为严格的监管。

  • The letter's contents were not disclosed.

    信件内容并未透露。

SYN subject matter, ideas, matter, material  
3 N-PLURAL (书的)目录 The contents of a book are its different chapters and sections, usually shown in a list at the beginning of the book. (书的)目录

  • There is no initial list of contents.

    没有章首目录。

4 N-UNCOUNT (教育项目或课程的)内容 The content of something such as an educational course or a programme of action is the elements that it consists of. (教育项目或课程的)内容

  • Previous students have had nothing but praise for the course content and staff.

    往届学生对课程内容与教员全是赞扬之词。

  • This is how we see our tasks, and the substance and content of our work for the forthcoming period.

    这就是我们对工作任务的看法，以及下一阶段我们工作的实质与内容。

5 N-SING [n NOUN] 含量;成分 You can use content to refer to the amount or proportion of something that a substance contains. 含量;成分

  • Sunflower margarine has the same fat content as butter.

    向日葵所制人造黄油的脂肪含量与黄油脂肪含量相同。

  • He was astonished at the high gold content in the 340 million-year-old rock.

    他对这块有3.4亿年历史的岩石的高含金量大为惊异。

SYN amount, measure, size, load  

ADJECTIVE AND VERB USES 形容词和动词用法

1 ADJ-GRADED [verb-link ADJ, ADJ to-infinitive] 满足的;知足的 If you are content with something, you are willing to accept it, rather than wanting something more or something better. 满足的;知足的

  • I am content to admire the mountains from below.

    我满足于从山脚下观赏山景。

  • I'm perfectly content with the way the campaign has gone. [+ with]

    我对竞选活动的进展十分满意。

  • Not content with rescuing one theatre, Sally Green has taken on another. [+ with]

    萨莉·格林不满足于挽救一家剧院，她又接手了另外一家。

SYN satisfied, happy, pleased, contented  
2 ADJ-GRADED [verb-link ADJ] 满意的;惬意的 If you are content, you are fairly happy or satisfied. 满意的;惬意的

  • He says his daughter is quite content.

    他说他女儿非常满意。

3 VERB 使(自己)满足(于…) If you content yourself with something, you accept it and do not try to do or have other things. 使(自己)满足(于…)

  • He wisely contented himself with his family and his love of nature. [VERB pronoun-reflexive + with]

    他非常明智，拥有家庭和对自然的热爱便知足了。

  • Most manufacturers content themselves with updating existing models. [V pron-refl + with/by]

    大多数制造商都满足于对现有款型进行更新。
