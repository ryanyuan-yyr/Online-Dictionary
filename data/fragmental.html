fragment
frægmənt frægment ˈfrægmənt ˈfrægˌmɛnt frægˈmɛnt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense fragments, present participle fragmenting, past tense , past participle fragmented
The noun is pronounced /'frægmənt/. The verb is pronounced /fræg'ment/. 名词读作/'frægmənt/。动词读作/fræg'ment/。
1 N-COUNT 碎片;片断;小部分 A fragment of something is a small piece or part of it. 碎片;片断;小部分

  • ...fragments of metal in my shoulder. [+ of]

    我肩膀里的几块金属碎片

  • She read everything, digesting every fragment of news. [+ of]

    她什么都看，对新闻的每个片段都细细品味。

  • ...glass fragments.

    玻璃碎片

SYN piece, part, bit, scrap  
2 V-ERG (使)成碎片;(使)分裂;(使)分化 If something fragments or is fragmented, it breaks or separates into small pieces or parts. (使)成碎片;(使)分裂;(使)分化

  • The clouds fragmented and out came the sun. [VERB]

    乌云散开，太阳出来了。

  • Fierce rivalries have traditionally fragmented the region. [VERB noun]

    激烈的竞争已经如从前一样使得该地区四分五裂。

  • By the first century BC, Buddhism was in danger of fragmenting into small sects. [VERB into noun]

    到公元前1世纪时，佛教面临着被分化成一些小派别的危险。

fragmentation

  • ...the extraordinary fragmentation of styles on the music scene.

    音乐界极其多元化的风格

fragmented

  • Europe had become infinitely more unstable and fragmented.

    欧洲已经变得相当不稳定，也越来越分化。
