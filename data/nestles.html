nestle
nesəl ˈnɛsəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense nestles, present participle nestling, past tense , past participle nestled
1 V-ERG (使)(舒适地)安顿下来，安卧，偎依 If you nestle or are nestled somewhere, you move into a comfortable position, usually by pressing against someone or against something soft. (使)(舒适地)安顿下来，安卧，偎依

  • John took one child into the crook of each arm and let them nestle against him. [VERB prep.]

    约翰一只臂弯搂着一个孩子，让他们舒服地靠着他。

  • Jade nestled her first child in her arms. [VERB noun prep.]

    杰德怀里抱着她的第一个孩子。

2 V-ERG (使)置于，坐落在(安全、有遮蔽之处) If something such as a building nestles somewhere or if it is nestled somewhere, it is in that place and seems safe or sheltered. (使)置于，坐落在(安全、有遮蔽之处)

  • Nearby, nestling in the hills, was the children's home. [VERB prep.]

    孩子们的家就在附近的山里。

  • She nestled the eggs safely in the straw in Jim's basket. [VERB noun prep.]

    她把鸡蛋轻轻地放在吉姆篮子里的稻草上。
