decoy
diːkɔɪ diˈkɔɪ dɪkɔɪ ˈdiˌkɔɪ
Word forms: plural decoys
1 N-COUNT 诱饵;诱惑物;用作诱饵的人 If you refer to something or someone as a decoy, you mean that they are intended to attract people's attention and deceive them, for example by leading them into a trap or away from a particular place. 诱饵;诱惑物;用作诱饵的人

  • Nick acted as a decoy after the dog attacked a group of children swimming in the river.

    那只狗攻击了在河里游泳的一群孩子后，尼克作诱饵要将其捉住。

  • He was booked on a flight leaving that day, but that was just a decoy.

    他订了那天起飞的航班，但那不过是个诱饵。

SYN lure, attraction, bait, trap  
2 N-COUNT (用于吸引野鸟的)假鸟 A decoy is a model of a bird that is used to attract wild birds towards it so that people can study them or shoot them. (用于吸引野鸟的)假鸟
