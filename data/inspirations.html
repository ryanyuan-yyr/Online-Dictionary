inspiration
ɪnspɪreɪʃən ˌɪnspɪˈreɪʃən ˌɪnspəˈreɪʃən
Word forms: plural inspirations
1 N-UNCOUNT 灵感;启示;启发 Inspiration is a feeling of enthusiasm you get from someone or something, which gives you new and creative ideas. 灵感;启示;启发

  • My inspiration comes from poets like Baudelaire and Jacques Prévert.

    我的灵感源于像波德莱尔和雅克·普雷韦这样的诗人。

  • What better way of finding inspiration for your own garden than by visiting others.

    还有什么比参观别人花园更好的获取灵感、扮靓自家花园的方法呢。

2 N-SING [an N] 激励(者);鼓舞(者) If you describe someone or something good as an inspiration, you mean that they make you or other people want to do or achieve something. 激励(者);鼓舞(者) [approval]

  • Powell's unusual journey to high office is an inspiration to millions. [+ to]

    鲍威尔升任高官的不寻常历程对数以百万计的人都是一个激励。

  • She was a great inspiration. She was the person who impressed us with the importance of research.

    她极大地鼓舞了我们，让我们深刻了解到研究的重要性。

SYN motivation, example, influence, model  
3 N-SING 灵感来源;原型 If something or someone is the inspiration for a particular book, work of art, or action, they are the source of the ideas in it or act as a model for it. 灵感来源;原型

  • India's myths and songs are the inspiration for her books. [+ for]

    印度的神话与歌曲是她那些书的灵感来源。

  • The inspiration behind the reforms was a paper written in 1985.

    改革的想法源于 1985 年的一篇论文。

SYN influence, spur, stimulus, muse  
4 N-COUNT 妙计;好办法 If you suddenly have an inspiration, you suddenly think of an idea of what to do or say. 妙计;好办法

  • Alison had an inspiration.

    艾莉森有了条妙计。
