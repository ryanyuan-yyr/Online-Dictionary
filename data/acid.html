acid
æsɪd ˈæsɪd
Word forms: plural acids
1 N-MASS 酸 An acid is a chemical substance, usually a liquid, which contains hydrogen and can react with other substances to form salts. Some acids burn or dissolve other substances that they come into contact with. 酸

  • ...citric acid.

    柠檬酸

  • Acids in the stomach destroy the virus.

    胃酸能杀死病毒。

2 ADJ-GRADED （物质）含酸的，酸性的 An acid substance contains acid. （物质）含酸的，酸性的

  • These shrubs must have an acid, lime-free soil.

    这些灌木必须生长在不含石灰的酸性土壤环境。

acidity

  • ...the acidity of rainwater.

    雨水的酸性

3 ADJ-GRADED （水果或饮料）酸味的，酸的 An acid fruit or drink has a sour or sharp taste. （水果或饮料）酸味的，酸的

  • These wines may taste rather hard and somewhat acid.

    这些酒可能喝着比较冲并且有股酸味。

SYN sour, sharp, tart, pungent  
acidity

  • ...a finely balanced wine with ripe acidity and soft fruit flavours.

    融合了芳醇的酸味和柔和的水果口味的调味精致的酒

4 ADJ-GRADED 尖刻的；刻薄的 An acid remark, or acid humour, is very unkind or critical. 尖刻的；刻薄的

  • This comedy of contemporary manners is told with compassion and acid humour.

    这部当代风尚喜剧杂糅了悲悯的情怀和尖酸的幽默。

SYN sharp, cutting, biting, severe  
acidly

  • 'You don't know how to be a mother and you never did,' she said acidly.

    “你不知道怎样做一位母亲，而且你从来就没有知道过，”她刻薄地说。

5 N-UNCOUNT 麦角酰二乙胺 The drug LSD is sometimes referred to as acid . 麦角酰二乙胺 [informal]
