denude
dɪnjuːd -nuːd diˈnud diˈnjud dɪˈnud dɪˈnjud
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense denudes, present participle denuding, past tense , past participle denuded
1 VERB 使（地表）裸露;使光秃 To denude an area means to destroy the plants in it. 使（地表）裸露;使光秃 [formal] [Also V n of n]

  • Mining would pollute the lake and denude the forest. [VERB noun]

    采矿会污染湖水，毁掉森林。

  • Many hillsides had been denuded of trees. [be V-ed of n]

    许多山坡上都已经光秃秃的，没有任何树木了。

SYN strip, expose, bare, uncover  
2 VERB 剥夺;夺去 To denude someone or something of a particular thing means to take it away from them. 剥夺;夺去 [formal]

  • The Embassy is now denuded of all foreign and local staff. [VERB noun + of]

    现在大使馆里所有的外国和当地工作人员都清空了。

  • In such areas we see villages denuded of young people. [VERB-ed]

    在这些地区，我们在村子里根本看不到年轻人。
