inadequacy
ɪnædɪkwəsi ɪnˈædɪkwəsi
Word forms: plural inadequacies
1 N-VAR 不足;不完备 The inadequacy of something is the fact that there is not enough of it, or that it is not good enough. 不足;不完备

  • ...the inadequacy of the water supply. [+ of]

    水供应不足

  • The inadequacies of the current system have already been recognised.

    人们已经认识到现行体制的不足。

  • This man drank rather heavily in an effort to come to terms with his own inadequacies.

    这个人狂饮一通,努力让自己去接受自身的不完美。

SYN shortage, poverty, dearth, paucity [formal]  
2 N-UNCOUNT 不够格;能力不足;无法胜任 If someone has feelings of inadequacy, they feel that they do not have the qualities and abilities necessary to do something or to cope with life in general. 不够格;能力不足;无法胜任

  • The feeling of inadequacy was overpowering.

    无法胜任的感觉非常强烈。

  • ...his deep-seated sense of inadequacy.

    他内心那种根深蒂固的不自信

SYN incompetence, inability, deficiency, incapacity
