cross-examine
ˈkrɔsɪgˌzæmɪn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense cross-examines, present participle cross-examining, past tense , past participle cross-examined
VERB (律师就某人所提供的证据进行)盘问，反诘 When a lawyer cross-examines someone during a trial or hearing, he or she questions them about the evidence that they have already given. (律师就某人所提供的证据进行)盘问，反诘 [Also V n + about]

  • The accused's lawyers will get a chance to cross-examine him. [VERB noun]

    被告律师将有机会盘问他。

  • You know you are liable to be cross-examined mercilessly about the assault. [be V-ed + about]

    你要清楚他们可能会就这起人身侵犯案毫不留情地盘问你。

SYN question, grill [informal] , quiz, interrogate  
cross-examination

  • ...during the cross-examination of a witness in a murder case.

    在盘问一桩谋杀案的证人时

  • Under cross-examination, he admitted the state troopers used more destructive ammunition than usual.

    在反复盘问下，他承认该州警察使用了比平常更具破坏性的弹药。
