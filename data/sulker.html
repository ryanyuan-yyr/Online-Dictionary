sulk
sʌlk sʌlk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense sulks, present participle sulking, past tense , past participle sulked
VERB 生闷气;面有愠色;闷闷不乐 If you sulk, you are silent and bad-tempered for a while because you are annoyed about something. 生闷气;面有愠色;闷闷不乐

  • He turned his back and sulked. [VERB]

    他转过身去，面带愠色。

SYN be sullen, brood, be in a huff, pout  
Sulk is also a noun.

  • He went off in a sulk.

    他闷闷不乐地离开了。

  • Now she must be tired of my sulks.

    现在她肯定受够了我的坏脾气。
