lay aside
1 PHRASAL VERB 把…放在一边；把…搁置一边 If you lay something aside, you put it down, usually because you have finished using it or want to save it to use later. 把…放在一边；把…搁置一边

  • He finished the tea and laid the cup aside. [VERB noun PREP.]

    他喝完茶后把杯子搁在一边。

  • This allowed Ms. Kelley to lay aside money to start her business. [VERB PREP. noun]

    这样凯利女士就可以把钱存起来创业。

2 PHRASAL VERB 放下，抛开（情感或信仰） If you lay aside a feeling or belief, you reject it or give it up in order to progress with something. 放下，抛开（情感或信仰） [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Perhaps the opposed parties will lay aside their sectional interests and rise to this challenge. [VERB PREP. noun]

    也许各反对党会撇开派系利益，迎接这一挑战。

  • All animosities were laid aside for the moment. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    一切仇恨都被暂时抛开。

SYN abandon, reject, dismiss, postpone
