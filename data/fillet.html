fillet
fɪlɪt fɪleɪ ˈfɪlɪt fɪˈleɪ ˈfɪlˌeɪ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense fillets, present participle filleting, past tense , past participle filleted
1 N-VAR [oft NOUN n] (尤指牛肉)无骨肉片 Fillet is a strip of meat, especially beef, that has no bones in it. (尤指牛肉)无骨肉片

  • ...fillet of beef with shallots.

    葱香无骨牛肉片

  • ...chicken breast fillets.

    鸡脯肉片

  • ...fillet steak.

    无骨牛排

2 N-COUNT 去骨鱼片 A fillet of fish is the side of a fish with the bones removed. 去骨鱼片

  • ...anchovy fillets.

    去骨鳀鱼片

  • I ordered a fine fillet of salmon.

    我点了一份精品去骨鲑鱼片。

3 VERB 剔除(鱼或肉)的骨头 When you fillet fish or meat, you prepare it by taking the bones out. 剔除(鱼或肉)的骨头

  • Don't be afraid to ask your fishmonger to fillet flat fish. [VERB noun]

    尽管叫鱼贩子把比目鱼的鱼刺剔掉。
