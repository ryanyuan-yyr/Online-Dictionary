commitment
kəmɪtmənt kəˈmɪtmənt
Word forms: plural commitments
1 N-UNCOUNT 信奉;忠诚 Commitment is a strong belief in an idea or system. 信奉;忠诚

  • ...commitment to the ideals of Bolshevism. [+ to]

    对布尔什维克主义理想的信奉

SYN dedication, loyalty, devotion, adherence  
2 N-COUNT 承诺;责任;义务 A commitment is something which regularly takes up some of your time because of an agreement you have made or because of responsibilities that you have. 承诺;责任;义务

  • I've got a lot of commitments.

    我承担了很多责任。

  • Work commitments forced her to uproot herself and her son from Reykjavik.

    她的工作迫使她和儿子从雷克雅未克搬走。

3 N-COUNT [usually NOUN to-infinitive] 承诺;许诺 If you make a commitment to do something, you promise that you will do it. 承诺;许诺 [formal]

  • We made a commitment to keep working together.

    我们承诺继续合作。

  • They made a commitment to peace. [+ to]

    他们承诺要维护和平。

SYN pledge, promise, guarantee, undertaking  
4 N-VAR 收监;送入医院 Commitment is the process of officially sending someone to a prison or to hospital. 收监;送入医院 [US]
in BRIT, use 英国英语用 committal
