gratify
grætɪfaɪ ˈgrætɪˌfaɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense gratifies, present participle gratifying, past tense , past participle gratified
1 VERB 使高兴；令人满意 If you are gratified by something, it gives you pleasure or satisfaction. 使高兴；令人满意 [formal] [Also VERB n]

  • Mr. Dambar was gratified by his response. [be VERB-ed]

    丹巴尔先生对他的回答感到满意。

SYN please, delight, satisfy, thrill  
gratified

  • He was gratified to hear that his idea had been confirmed.

    听到自己的想法得到了认可，他感到很开心。

  • They were gratified that America kept its promise.

    美国政府信守承诺，这使他们感到高兴。

gratifying

  • We took a chance and we've won. It's very gratifying.

    我们决定搏上一搏，结果赢了，真是令人心花怒放。

  • ...a gratifying development.

    令人欣喜的发展

gratification

  • He is waiting for them to recognise him and eventually they do, much to his gratification.

    他等着他们认出他来，让他倍感欣慰的是他们终于认出来了。

2 VERB 满足（愿望等） If you gratify your own or another person's desire, you do what is necessary to please yourself or them. 满足（愿望等） [formal]

  • We gratified our friend's curiosity. [VERB noun]

    我们满足了朋友的好奇心。

  • Every whim will be gratified. [VERB noun]

    每一个突发的念头都会得到满足。
