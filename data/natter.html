natter
nætəʳ ˈnætər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense natters, present participle nattering, past tense , past participle nattered
V-RECIP 瞎扯;闲聊 When people natter, they talk casually for a long time about unimportant things. 瞎扯;闲聊 [mainly British, informal]

  • If something dramatic has happened during the day, we'll sit and natter about it. [VERB]

    如果白天发生了什么出人意料的事情，我们就会坐下来聊聊。

  • Susan and the girl were still nattering away in German. [V away/on]

    苏姗和那个女孩还在用德语聊个没完。

  • Ahead of you is a day of nattering with fellow farmers at the local market. [VERB + with]

    你将会有一天时间在当地市场和农民兄弟们聊天。

  • You natter all day long at the hospital. [VERB]

    你在医院白话了一整天。

  • His mother would natter to anyone. [VERB + to]

    他妈妈跟随便哪个人都会说个不停。

Natter is also a noun.

  • What's the topic of conversation when a group of new mums get together for a natter?

    一群年轻的妈妈聚在一起聊天时会聊什么呢？
