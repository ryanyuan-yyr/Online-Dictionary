honourable
ɒnrəbəl ˈɒnərəbəl
in AM, use 美国英语用 honorable
1 ADJ-GRADED （人）值得尊敬的，正直的;（行为）光荣的，体面的 If you describe people or actions as honourable, you mean that they are good and deserve to be respected and admired. （人）值得尊敬的，正直的;（行为）光荣的，体面的

  • I believe he was an honourable man, dedicated to the people and his union.

    我认为他是个品行高洁的人，一心奉献于人民和工会的事业。

  • He argued that the only honorable course of action was death.

    他认为唯一体面的行为就只有一死。

  • However, their intentions are honourable.

    然而，他们的初衷是高尚的。

SYN proper, right, respectable, righteous  
honourably

  • He also felt she had not behaved honorably in the leadership election.

    他还认为她在领导人选举中有不端行为。

2 ADJ （用于某些贵族和法官等官员名字前的尊称）尊敬的，阁下 Honourable is used as a title before the names of some members of the nobility, judges, and some other officials. （用于某些贵族和法官等官员名字前的尊称）尊敬的，阁下

  • ...the Honourable Mr Justice Swinton Thomas.

    尊敬的斯温顿·托马斯法官先生

3 ADJ [ADJ n] （英国议会辩论时议员彼此间的尊称）尊敬的，阁下 In debates in the British parliament, one member of parliament refers to another as the honourable member, the honourable gentleman, the honourable lady, or their honourable friend. （英国议会辩论时议员彼此间的尊称）尊敬的，阁下

  • ...the Honourable Member for Billericay.

    比勒里基区的议员阁下
