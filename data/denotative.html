denote
dɪnoʊt diˈnoʊt dɪˈnoʊt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense denotes, present participle denoting, past tense , past participle denoted
1 VERB 预示;是…的征兆;表示;是…的标志 If one thing denotes another, it is a sign or indication of it. 预示;是…的征兆;表示;是…的标志 [formal]

  • Red eyes denote strain and fatigue. [VERB noun]

    眼睛充血表明压力过大、身心疲惫。

  • There was a message waiting, denoting that someone had been here ahead of her. [VERB that]

    有了一条留言，这表示有人已先于她到了这儿。

SYN indicate, show, mean, mark  
2 VERB （符号等）代表，是…的标记 What a symbol denotes is what it represents. （符号等）代表，是…的标记 [formal]

  • In figure 24 'D' denotes quantity demanded and 'S' denotes quantity supplied. [VERB noun]

    在图 24 中，D 代表需求量而 S 代表供应量。

3 VERB 意思是;指代 What a word or name denotes is what it means or refers to. 意思是;指代 [formal]

  • In the Middle Ages the term 'drab' denoted a very simple type of woollen cloth which was used by peasants to make their clothes. [VERB noun]

    中世纪时，drab（“本色布”）一词指的是一种农民用来制衣的非常普通的羊毛织物。

SYN refer to, mean, signify, suggest
