derive
dɪraɪv dɪˈraɪv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense derives, present participle deriving, past tense , past participle derived
1 VERB 获得;取得;得到 If you derive something such as pleasure or benefit from a person or from something, you get it from them. 获得;取得;得到 [formal]

  • Mr Ying is one of those happy people who derive pleasure from helping others. [VERB noun + from]

    英先生属于那种助人为乐的快活人。

SYN obtain, get, receive, draw  
2 V-ERG （使）起源于;（使）来自 If you say that something such as a word or feeling derives or is derived from something else, you mean that it comes from that thing. （使）起源于;（使）来自 [Also VERB n from n]

  • Anna's strength is derived from her parents and her sisters. [be VERB-ed + from]

    安娜的坚强源自于她的父母和姐姐们。

  • The word Easter derives from Eostre, the pagan goddess of spring. [VERB + from]

    Easter (复活节)一词由Eostre(多神教的春天女神)衍生而来。
