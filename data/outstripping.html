outstrip
aʊtstrɪp ˌaʊtˈstrɪp
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense outstrips, present participle outstripping, past tense , past participle outstripped
VERB 超过；胜过 If one thing outstrips another, the first thing becomes larger in amount, or more successful or important, than the second thing. 超过；胜过

  • In the mid-eighteenth century the production of food far outstripped the rise in population. [VERB noun]

    18世纪中叶食物的产量远远超过人口的增长。

  • In 1989 and 1990 demand outstripped supply, and prices went up by more than a third. [VERB noun]

    1989年和1990年出现了供不应求的状况，物价上涨了三分之一还要多。

SYN exceed, eclipse, overtake, top
