inflexible
ɪnfleksɪbəl ɪnˈflɛksəbəl
1 ADJ-GRADED 不可改变的;不容变更的 Something that is inflexible cannot be altered in any way, even if the situation changes. 不可改变的;不容变更的

  • Workers insisted the new system was too inflexible.

    工人们坚持认为新制度太不灵活。

  • Charles was a man of settled habits and inflexible routine.

    查尔斯是一个恪守习惯、生活规律不容打乱的人。

SYN fixed, set, established, rooted  
inflexibility

  • The snag about an endowment mortgage is its inflexibility.

    人寿保险抵押的问题在于其缺乏灵活性。

2 ADJ-GRADED 顽固的;僵化的 If you say that someone is inflexible, you are criticizing them because they refuse to change their mind or alter their way of doing things. 顽固的;僵化的 [disapproval]

  • His opponents viewed him as stubborn, dogmatic, and inflexible.

    他的反对者们认为他固执、教条、僵化。

SYN obstinate, strict, relentless, firm  
inflexibility

  • Joyce was irritated by the inflexibility of her colleagues.

    乔伊斯被她同事的顽固激怒了。
