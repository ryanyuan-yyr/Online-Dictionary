suggest
sədʒest səgdʒ- səgˈdʒɛst səɡˈdʒɛst səˈdʒɛst
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense suggests, present participle suggesting, past tense , past participle suggested
1 VERB 提议;建议 If you suggest something, you put forward a plan or idea for someone to think about. 提议;建议

  • He suggested a link between class size and test results of seven-year-olds. [VERB noun]

    他认为 7 岁大的学生的测试分数和班级的规模有关系。

  • I suggest you ask him some specific questions about his past. [VERB that]

    我建议你问他一些有关他的过去的具体问题。

  • I suggested to Mike that we go out for a meal with his colleagues. [VERB + to]

    我向迈克提议，我们和他的同事们一起出去吃饭。

  • No one has suggested how this might occur. [VERB wh]

    没人说得清这事是怎么发生的。

  • 'Could he be suffering from amnesia?' I suggested. [VERB with quote]

    “他是不是患了健忘症?”我试探着问道。

  • So instead I suggested taking her out to dinner for a change. [VERB verb-ing]

    于是我又提议，带她出去吃饭换换口味。

SYN recommend, propose, advise, move  
2 VERB 推荐;举荐 If you suggest the name of a person or place, you recommend them to someone. 推荐;举荐

  • Could you suggest someone to advise me how to do this?. [VERB noun]

    这该怎么办？你能不能推荐一个人帮我出个主意？

  • They can suggest where to buy one. [V wh to-inf]

    他们可以推荐去哪里购买。

3 VERB 意思是;言下之意是 If you suggest that something is the case, you say something which you believe is the case. 意思是;言下之意是

  • I'm not suggesting that is what is happening. [VERB that]

    我没有说那就是事实。

  • It is wrong to suggest that there are easy alternatives. [VERB that]

    如果认为有更容易的选择，那就错了。

  • Their success is conditional, I suggest, on this restriction. [VERB that]

    我认为他们能否成功取决于这一限制因素。

SYN indicate, show, demonstrate, give the impression  
4 VERB 暗示;表明;显示 If one thing suggests another, it implies it or makes you think that it might be the case. 暗示;表明;显示

  • Earlier reports suggested that a meeting would take place on Sunday. [VERB that]

    早先的报道暗示周日可能会召开会议。

  • Its hairy body suggests a mammal. [VERB noun]

    它毛茸茸的身体表明它是哺乳动物。

SYN bring to mind, evoke, remind you of, smack of  
5 VERB 使联想到;使想起 If one thing suggests another, it brings it to your mind through an association of ideas. 使联想到;使想起

  • This onomatopoeic word suggests to me the sound a mousetrap makes when it snaps shut. [VERB noun]

    这个拟声词让我想起捕鼠夹子突然合上时的声音。

Usage Note :

  • Note that suggest cannot usually be followed directly by a noun or pronoun referring to a person. You generally have to put the preposition to in front of it. You do not 'suggest someone something', you 'suggest something to someone'. John Caskey first suggested this idea to me. Nor do you 'suggest someone to do something' unless suggest means recommend. You 'suggest that someone does something'. Beatrice suggested that he spend the summer at their place. Do not confuse suggest and advise. If you suggest something, you mention it as an idea or plan for someone to think about. If you advise someone to do something, you tell them what you think they should do. I advised him to leave as soon as possible.

    注意 suggest 后通常不能直接跟指人的名词或代词，一般要在这类名词或代词之前加介词 to。不能说 suggest someone something，而说 suggest something to someone，例如：John Caskey first suggested this idea to me (约翰·卡斯基首先向我提出了这一想法)。一般也不说suggest someone to do something，除非 suggest 表示推荐;一般会说 suggest that someone does something，例如：Beatrice suggested that he spend the summer at their place (比阿特丽斯提议他在他们那里度夏)。不要混淆suggest 和 advise。suggest 表示提出供他人考虑的想法或计划。advise 表示建议某人做某事，例如：I advised him to leave as soon as possible (我建议他尽快离开)。
