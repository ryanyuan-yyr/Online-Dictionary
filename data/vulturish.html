vulture
vʌltʃəʳ ˈvʌltʃər
Word forms: plural vultures
1 N-COUNT 兀鹫;秃鹫 A vulture is a large bird which lives in hot countries and eats the flesh of dead animals. 兀鹫;秃鹫
2 N-COUNT 趁火打劫的人;乘人之危的人 If you describe a person as a vulture, you disapprove of them because you think they are trying to gain from another person's troubles. 趁火打劫的人;乘人之危的人 [journalism, disapproval]

  • With no buyer in sight for the company as a whole, the vultures started to circle.

    由于公司目前找不到买主，趁火打劫者开始围攻。
