swill
swɪl swɪl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense swills, present participle swilling, past tense , past participle swilled
1 VERB 大口喝;狂饮 If you swill an alcoholic drink, you drink a lot of it. 大口喝;狂饮

  • A crowd of men were standing around swilling beer. [VERB noun]

    一群人正站在一起痛饮啤酒。

SYN drink, gulp, swig [informal] , guzzle  
2 V-ERG (使)(液体)晃动,摇晃;(使)来回流动 If a liquid swills around, or if you swill it around, it moves around the area that it is contained in. (使)(液体)晃动,摇晃;(使)来回流动

  • Gallons of sea water had rushed into the cabin and were now swilling about in the bilges. [V around/about]

    大量海水冲入了船舱，正在船底来回晃荡。

  • She swilled the whisky around in her glass. [V n around/about]

    她晃动着杯中的威士忌。

3 VERB 冲刷;冲洗;涮 To swill out something means to clean it by pouring water over it. 冲刷;冲洗;涮 [mainly British]

  • Having finished his coffee, he swilled out the mug and left it on the draining board. [V n with out]

    喝完咖啡后，他涮了涮杯子然后把它放在滴水板上。

SYN rinse, wash out, sluice, flush  
in AM, use 美国英语用 rinse
4 N-UNCOUNT 泔脚饲料;猪食 Swill is a liquid mixture containing waste food that is given to pigs to eat. 泔脚饲料;猪食
SYN waste, slops, mash, mush
