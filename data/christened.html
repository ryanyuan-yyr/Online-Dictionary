christen
krɪsən ˈkrɪsən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense christens, present participle christening, past tense , past participle christened
1 VERB [usually passive] 给…施洗礼;(施洗时)为…命名 When a baby is christened, he or she is given a name during the Christian ceremony of baptism. 给…施洗礼;(施洗时)为…命名

  • She was born in March and christened in June. [be VERB-ed]

    她3月出生，6月受洗。

  • She was christened Susan. [be VERB-ed]

    她受洗时取名苏珊。

SYN baptize, name  
2 VERB 开始称呼…为;给…取名为 You say that you christen a person, place, or object a particular name if you choose a name for them and start calling them by that name. 开始称呼…为;给…取名为 [informal]

  • The pair were christened 'The Women in Black' after they both wore black dresses at a party. [be VERB-ed]

    那两人在一次聚会上都穿了黑裙子，从此就被叫做“黑衣姐妹花”。

  • We had a tiny little room which was christened Hades. [VERB noun noun]

    我们有一个非常小的房间，取名哈得斯。

3 VERB 启用；正式使用 You say that you christen something new when you use it for the first time, especially if you do something special to mark the occasion. 启用；正式使用 [informal]

  • To christen the new hall, a number of great orchestras have been invited to play. [VERB noun]

    几个著名交响乐团受邀演出，以庆祝新音乐厅的启用。
