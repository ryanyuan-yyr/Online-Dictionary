fluke
fluːk fluk
Word forms: plural flukes
N-COUNT [usually singular, oft by NOUN] 侥幸;意外;偶然 If you say that something good is a fluke, you mean that it happened accidentally rather than by being planned or arranged. 侥幸;意外;偶然 [informal] [Also + of]

  • The discovery was something of a fluke.

    这个发现多少有点机缘巧合。

  • By sheer fluke, one of the shipowner's employees was in the city.

    非常凑巧的是，船主的一个雇员正在城里。

SYN stroke of luck, accident, coincidence, chance occurrence
