mess up
1 PHRASAL VERB 搞砸;弄糟 If you mess something up or if you mess up, you cause something to fail or be spoiled. 搞砸;弄糟 [informal]

  • When politicians mess things up, it is the people who pay the price. [VERB noun PREP.]

    政客们把事情搞砸的时候，埋单的是老百姓。

  • He had messed up one career. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他把一份工作弄砸了。

  • If I messed up, I would probably be fired. [VERB PREP.]

    要是我搞砸了，很可能会被炒鱿鱼。

SYN botch, bungle, make a hash of [informal] , make a nonsense of  
2 PHRASAL VERB 弄脏;弄乱 If you mess up a place or a thing, you make it untidy or dirty. 弄脏;弄乱 [informal] [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • I hope they haven't messed up your video tapes. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我希望他们没把您的录像带弄乱。

SYN dirty, foul, litter, pollute  
3 PHRASAL VERB 干扰;搅乱;使不得安宁 If something messes someone up, it causes them to be very confused or worried, or to have psychological problems. 干扰;搅乱;使不得安宁 [informal] [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • That really messed them up, especially the boys. [VERB noun PREP.]

    那件事真把他们搅得天翻地覆，男孩子们尤受其害。
