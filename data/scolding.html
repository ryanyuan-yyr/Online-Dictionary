scold
skoʊld skoʊld
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense scolds, present participle scolding, past tense , past participle scolded
VERB 训斥;责骂 If you scold someone, you speak angrily to them because they have done something wrong. 训斥;责骂 [formal] [Also VERB]

  • If he finds out, he'll scold me. [VERB noun]

    如果他知道了，肯定会责骂我的。

  • Later she scolded her daughter for having talked to her father like that. [VERB noun + for]

    后来她训斥女儿不应该那样对父亲说话。

  • 'You should be at school,' he scolded. [VERB with quote]

    “你不是应该在学校吗，”他责骂道。

SYN reprimand, censure, rebuke, rate
