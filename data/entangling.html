entangle
ɪntæŋgəl ɛnˈtæŋgəl ɪnˈtæŋgəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense entangles, present participle entangling, past tense , past participle entangled
1 VERB 缠住;套住 If one thing entangles itself with another, the two things become caught together very tightly. 缠住;套住 [Also VERB n]

  • The blade of the oar had entangled itself with something in the water. [V n + with/in]

    船桨的桨叶和水里的什么东西缠住了。

SYN tangle, catch, trap, twist  
2 VERB 使陷入，使卷入（问题、困难等） If something entangles you in problems or difficulties, it causes you to become involved in problems or difficulties from which it is hard to escape. 使陷入，使卷入（问题、困难等）

  • Bureaucracy can entangle ventures for months. [VERB noun]

    官僚体制会使风险投资搁置好几个月。

  • His tactics were to entangle the opposition in a web of parliamentary procedure. [V n + in/with]

    他的策略是使反对派陷入错综复杂的议会程序之中。

SYN embroil, involve, complicate, mix up
