spice
spaɪs spaɪs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense spices, present participle spicing, past tense , past participle spiced
1 N-MASS 香料，佐料，调料，调味品(如肉桂、姜、辣椒粉等) A spice is a part of a plant, or a powder made from that part, which you put in food to give it flavour. Cinnamon, ginger, and paprika are spices. 香料，佐料，调料，调味品(如肉桂、姜、辣椒粉等)

  • ...herbs and spices.

    香草和香料

  • ...a row of spice jars.

    一排调料罐

SYN seasoning, condiment  
2 VERB 使活泼生动;给…增添趣味 If you spice something that you say or do, you add excitement or interest to it. 使活泼生动;给…增添趣味

  • They spiced their conversations and discussions with intrigue. [VERB noun + with]

    他们的谈话和讨论透着些许的神秘。

  • ...a boring film spiced with the occasional funny moment. [VERB-ed]

    仅有几处笑点的无聊影片

Spice up means the same as spice .spice up 同 spice

  • Her publisher wants her to spice up her stories with sex.

    她的出版商想让她在小说中增添些性描写以吸引读者。

  • ...a discovery which spiced the conversation up quite a bit.

    让谈话变得有趣不少的发现

3 N-UNCOUNT 给生活增添情趣的事 Spice is something which makes life more exciting. 给生活增添情趣的事

  • Variety is the spice of life!.

    多样化使生活更有情趣！

  • To add spice to the debate, they disagreed about method and ideology.

    他们在方法和观念上都持不同意见，这使辩论精彩了很多。
