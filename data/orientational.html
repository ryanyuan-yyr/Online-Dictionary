orientation
ɔːriənteɪʃən ˌɔriɛnˈteɪʃən ˌ oʊriɛnˈteɪʃən
Word forms: plural orientations
1 N-VAR （组织、国家等的）方向，目标，定位 If you talk about the orientation of an organization or country, you are talking about the kinds of aims and interests it has. （组织、国家等的）方向，目标，定位

  • ...a marketing orientation.

    营销定位

  • To a society which has lost its orientation he has much to offer.

    对于一个迷失了方向的社会，他能做的有很多。

  • The movement is liberal and social democratic in orientation.

    这一运动以自由和社会民主为目标。

SYN inclination, tendency, bias, leaning  
2 N-VAR （某人在信仰或偏好上基本的）态度，取向 Someone's orientation is their basic beliefs or preferences. （某人在信仰或偏好上基本的）态度，取向

  • ...legislation that would have made discrimination on the basis of sexual orientation illegal.

    本来会将性取向歧视定为非法的法规

3 N-UNCOUNT [oft NOUN n] （岗前、学前、课前等的）情况介绍，培训，训练 Orientation is basic information or training that is given to people starting a new job, school, or course. （岗前、学前、课前等的）情况介绍，培训，训练

  • They give their new employees a day or two of perfunctory orientation.

    他们对新员工进行了一两天例行公事的培训。

  • ...a one-day orientation session.

    为期一天的迎新会

4 N-COUNT [usually with poss] （建筑物等的）朝向 The orientation of a structure or object is the direction it faces. （建筑物等的）朝向

  • Farnese had the orientation of the church changed so that the front would face a square. [+ of]

    法尔内塞改变了教堂的朝向，这样它的正面就可以对着广场。

SYN position, situation, location, site
