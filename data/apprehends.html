apprehend
æprɪhend ˌæpriˈhɛnd ˌæprɪˈhɛnd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense apprehends, present participle apprehending, past tense , past participle apprehended
1 VERB 逮捕；拘押 If the police apprehend someone, they catch them and arrest them. 逮捕；拘押 [formal]

  • Police have not apprehended her killer. [VERB noun]

    警察还未抓获谋杀她的凶手。

SYN arrest, catch, lift [slang] , nick [slang , mainly British]  
2 VERB 了解;明白 If you apprehend something, you understand it fully. 了解;明白 [literary]

  • Only now can I begin to apprehend the power of these forces. [VERB noun]

    直到现在我才真正了解这些队伍的力量。

SYN understand, know, think, believe
