servile
sɜːʳvaɪl -vəl ˈsɜrvəl ˈsɜrˌvaɪl
ADJ-GRADED 奴性的;逢迎的;恭顺的 If you say that someone is servile, you disapprove of them because they are too eager to obey someone or do things for them. 奴性的;逢迎的;恭顺的 [formal, disapproval]

  • He was subservient and servile.

    他低声下气、卑躬屈膝。

  • They said she had a servile attitude to her employer.

    他们说她对她的老板阿谀逢迎。

SYN subservient, cringing, grovelling, mean  
servility

  • She's a curious mixture of stubbornness and servility.

    她既固执又恭顺，令人捉摸不透。
