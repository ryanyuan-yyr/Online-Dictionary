fast track
fɑːst træk
(also fast-track)Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense fast tracks, present participle fast tracking, past tense , past participle fast tracked
1 N-SING [NOUN n] 捷径;快速成功之道 The fast track to a particular goal, especially in politics or in your career, is the quickest route to achieving it. 捷径;快速成功之道

  • Many Croats and Slovenes saw independence as the fast track to democracy. [+ to]

    很多克罗地亚人和斯洛文尼亚人把独立看作通往民主的捷径。

  • He was told that, at 28, he was too old for fast-track promotion.

    有人告诉他，28 岁的他已经过了破格提升的年龄。

2 VERB 使加速;使提前 To fast track something means to make it happen or progress faster or earlier than normal. 使加速;使提前

  • A Federal Court case had been fast tracked to Wednesday. [be VERB-ed]

    联邦法庭的一桩案件提前到星期三审理。

  • Woodward has fast-tracked a number of youngsters into the line-up since he became coach. [VERB noun]

    伍德沃德担任教练后破格起用了一些年轻人出场。
