apiece
əpiːs əˈpis
1 ADV 每人 If people have a particular number of things apiece, they have that number each. 每人

  • He and I had two fish apiece.

    他和我各有两条鱼。

  • The World Series between the Atlanta Braves and Toronto Blue Jays is tied at one game apiece.

    世界系列赛上，亚特兰大勇士队和多伦多蓝鸟队双方各赢一场，战成平局。

SYN each, individually, separately, for each  
2 ADV 每件；每个 If a number of similar things are for sale at a certain price apiece, that is the price for each one of them. 每件；每个

  • Entire roast chickens were sixty cents apiece.

    烤全鸡每只60美分。
