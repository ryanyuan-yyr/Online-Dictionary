garrison
gærɪsən ˈgærəsən
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense garrisons, present participle garrisoning, past tense , past participle garrisoned
1 N-COUNT-COLL [with singular or plural verb] 卫戍部队；守备部队；警卫部队 A garrison is a group of soldiers whose task is to guard the town or building where they live. 卫戍部队；守备部队；警卫部队

  • ...a five-hundred man French army garrison.

    一支500人的法国陆军卫戍部队

SYN troops, group, unit, section  
2 N-COUNT 卫戍区；驻防地 A garrison is the buildings which the soldiers live in. 卫戍区；驻防地

  • The approaches to the garrison have been heavily mined.

    通往驻防地的道路上地雷密布。

SYN fort, fortress, camp, base  
3 VERB 派（兵）驻守；驻防 To garrison a place means to put soldiers there in order to protect it. You can also say that soldiers are garrisoned in a place. 派（兵）驻守；驻防

  • British troops still garrisoned the country. [VERB noun]

    英军依然驻守在该国。

  • No other soldiers were garrisoned there. [be VERB-ed]

    那里没有其他驻防士兵。

  • ...the large, heavily garrisoned towns. [VERB-ed]

    重兵驻防的大城市

SYN station, position, post, mount
