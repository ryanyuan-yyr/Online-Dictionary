behave
bɪheɪv biˈheɪv bɪˈheɪv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense behaves, present participle behaving, past tense , past participle behaved
1 VERB 表现 The way that you behave is the way that you do and say things, and the things that you do and say. 表现

  • I couldn't believe these people were behaving in this way. [VERB prep./adv.]

    我难以相信这些人竟会有这种举动。

  • He'd behaved badly. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他表现得很糟糕。

SYN act, react, conduct yourself, acquit yourself  
2 VERB 守规矩；举止得体；行为检点 If you behave or behave yourself, you act in the way that people think is correct and proper. 守规矩；举止得体；行为检点

  • You have to behave. [VERB]

    你得规矩点。

  • They were expected to behave themselves. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    他们应该守规矩。

SYN be well-behaved, be good, be polite, mind your manners  
3 VERB （在科学领域）运动，起作用，作出反应 In science, the way that something behaves is the things that it does. （在科学领域）运动，起作用，作出反应

  • Under certain conditions, electrons can behave like waves rather than particles. [VERB prep./adv.]

    在一定条件下，电子可以作波状运动，而不是粒子状运动。
