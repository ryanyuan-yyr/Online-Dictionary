entomb
ɪntuːm ɛnˈtum ɪnˈtum
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense entombs, present participle entombing, past tense , past participle entombed
1 VERB 使深陷于;埋藏;掩埋 If something is entombed, it is buried or permanently trapped by something. 使深陷于;埋藏;掩埋 [formal] [Also V n + in]

  • The city was entombed in volcanic lava. [be VERB-ed + in]

    城市被火山熔岩掩埋。

  • The Tel, an artificial mountain, entombs Jericho's ancient past. [VERB noun]

    泰勒山作为一座人造山掩藏着杰里科过去古老的历史。

2 VERB [usually passive] 埋葬 When a person's dead body is entombed, it is buried in a grave or put into a tomb. 埋葬 [formal]

  • Neither of them had any idea how long the body had been entombed. [be VERB-ed]

    他们俩谁也不知道这具尸体被埋葬多久了。
