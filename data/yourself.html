yourself
jɔːʳself jʊəʳ- jɔːˈsɛlf jʊrˈsɛlf jərˈsɛlf
Word forms: plural yourselves
Yourself is the second person reflexive pronoun. yourself是第二人称反身代词。
1 PRON-REFL 你自己;您自己;你们自己 A speaker or writer uses yourself to refer to the person that they are talking or writing to. Yourself is used when the object of a verb or preposition refers to the same person as the subject of the verb. 你自己;您自己;你们自己

  • Have the courage to be honest with yourself and about yourself.

    要有勇气对自己坦诚，同时对别人也要开诚布公。

  • Your baby depends on you to look after yourself properly while you are pregnant.

    你怀孕的时候，你的孩子指望着你好好照顾自己。

  • Treat yourselves to a glass of wine to help you relax at the end of the day.

    一天下来，喝上杯葡萄酒，有助于放松自己。

2 PRON-REFL-EMPH （用于强调所指对象）你自己，你本人，你们自己，你们本人 You use yourself to emphasize the person that you are referring to. （用于强调所指对象）你自己，你本人，你们自己，你们本人 [emphasis]

  • They mean to share the business between them, after you yourself are gone, Sir.

    先生，他们两个打算等您本人离开后瓜分这个企业。

  • I've been wondering if you yourselves have any idea why she came.

    我一直在想，你们自己是否知道她为什么来。

3 PRON-REFL-EMPH （代替作宾语的you，用于强调或表示礼貌）你（自己），你们（自己） You use yourself instead of 'you' for emphasis or in order to be more polite when 'you' is the object of a verb or preposition. by yourself→see: by  （代替作宾语的you，用于强调或表示礼貌）你（自己），你们（自己） [politeness]

  • A wealthy man like yourself is bound to make an enemy or two along the way.

    像你这样的有钱人，一路走来注定会结下一两个仇人的。

  • I wouldn't want to cause such important people as yourselves any bother.

    我不想给你们这样的大人物添任何麻烦。
