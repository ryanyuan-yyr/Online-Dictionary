tinkle
tɪŋkəl ˈtɪŋkəl ˈtɪŋkəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense tinkles, present participle tinkling, past tense , past participle tinkled
1 VERB 叮当作响;发出叮当声 If something tinkles, it makes a clear, high-pitched, ringing noise, especially as small parts of it strike a surface. 叮当作响;发出叮当声 [Also VERB]

  • A fresh cascade of splintered glass tinkled to the floor. [VERB prep./adv.]

    大量刚裂开的玻璃碎片叮叮当当地落到地板上。

  • We strolled past tinkling fountains and perfumed gardens. [VERB-ing]

    我们漫步经过泉水叮咚的喷泉和芬芳四溢的花园。

SYN splash, babble, burble, purl  
Tinkle is also a noun.

  • ...a tinkle of broken glass.

    碎玻璃的叮当声

2 V-ERG (钟、铃)作响;敲响(钟、铃) If a bell tinkles or if you tinkle it, it makes a quiet ringing noise as you shake it. (钟、铃)作响;敲响(钟、铃)

  • An old-fashioned bell tinkled as he pushed open the door. [VERB]

    当他推开门时，一口旧式的钟丁零零地响了起来。

  • Miss Peel tinkled her desk bell and they all sat down again. [VERB noun]

    皮尔小姐摇了摇她桌子上的铃铛，他们全都又坐下了。

SYN ring, chime, jingle, ping  
Tinkle is also a noun.

  • ...the tinkle of goat bells.

    山羊所系铃铛的叮当声
