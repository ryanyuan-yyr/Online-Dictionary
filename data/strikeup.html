strike up
1 PHRASAL VERB 开始(交谈);建立(友谊);开始(来往) When you strike up a conversation or friendship with someone, you begin one. 开始(交谈);建立(友谊);开始(来往) [written]

  • I trailed her into Penney's and struck up a conversation. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我跟着她进了彭尼家然后开始交谈起来。

  • James struck up a friendship with a small boy who owned a pony on the island. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    詹姆斯和小岛上一个养了一匹小马的小男孩儿交上了朋友。

SYN establish, start, begin, initiate  
2 PHRASAL VERB-ERG 开始演奏 When musicians strike up a piece of music, or when music strikes up, the music begins. 开始演奏

  • And then the orchestra struck up the National Anthem. [VERB PREP. noun]

    然后管弦乐队奏起了国歌。

  • Music struck up in one of the big old buildings along the seafront. [VERB PREP.]

    音乐从海滨区一栋古老的大楼里响起。

  • The band struck up, and riders paraded round the ring. [VERB PREP.]

    乐队开始奏乐，骑手们列队绕场行进。

SYN start to play, start playing, embark on
