inconsistency
ɪnkənsɪstənsi ˌɪnkənˈsɪstənsi
Word forms: plural inconsistencies
1 N-UNCOUNT (行为的)反复无常,易变 If you refer to someone's inconsistency, you are criticizing them for not behaving in the same way every time a similar situation occurs. (行为的)反复无常,易变 [disapproval]

  • His worst fault was his inconsistency.

    他最大的缺点就是反复无常。

  • The report accuses the judicial system of inconsistency and partiality.

    报告指责司法系统执法前后不一且有失公正。

SYN unreliability, instability, unpredictability, fickleness  
2 N-VAR 前后矛盾;不一致 If there are inconsistencies in two statements, one cannot be true if the other is true. 前后矛盾;不一致

  • We were asked to investigate the alleged inconsistencies in his evidence. [+ in]

    我们被要求对他证词中的前后矛盾之处进行调查。

  • ...the complete inconsistency of his argument.

    他的论点全然自相矛盾

SYN incompatibility, paradox, discrepancy, disparity
