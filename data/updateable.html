update
ʌpdeɪt ʌpdeɪt ʌpˈdeɪt ˈʌpˌdeɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense updates, present participle updating, past tense , past participle updated
The verb is pronounced /ʌp'deɪt/. The noun is pronounced /'ʌpdeɪt/. 动词读作/ʌp'deɪt/，名词读作/'ʌpdeɪt/。
1 VERB 更新；使现代化 If you update something, you make it more modern, usually by adding new parts to it or giving new information. 更新；使现代化

  • He was back in the office, updating the work schedule on the computer. [VERB noun]

    他已回到办公室，正在电脑上更新工作日程。

  • Airlines would prefer to update rather than retrain crews. [VERB]

    航空公司更愿意换新员工而不是对旧员工重新进行培训。

  • ...an updated edition of the book. [VERB-ed]

    该书的新版

SYN bring up to date, improve, correct, renew  
2 N-COUNT 最新消息；快讯；速递 An update is a news item containing the latest information about a particular situation. 最新消息；快讯；速递

  • She had heard the news-flash on a TV channel's news update.

    她在一家电视台的新闻快讯中听到了这则简明新闻。

  • ...a weather update.

    最新天气报告

  • ...football results update.

    最新足球战况

3 VERB 为…提供最新消息；把最新进展告诉（某人） If you update someone on a situation, you tell them the latest developments in that situation. 为…提供最新消息；把最新进展告诉（某人）

  • We'll update you on the day's top news stories. [VERB noun + on]

    我们将为您提供当天的重要新闻。

  • I would just update them on any news we might have. [VERB noun on noun]

    我们一有新消息我就会告诉他们。

SYN brief, advise, inform, report to
