distrust
dɪstrʌst dɪsˈtrʌst
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense distrusts, present participle distrusting, past tense , past participle distrusted
1 VERB 不信任;怀疑 If you distrust someone or something, you think they are not honest, reliable, or safe. 不信任;怀疑

  • I don't have any particular reason to distrust them. [VERB noun]

    我没有任何特别的理由怀疑他们。

SYN suspect, doubt, discredit, be wary of  
2 N-UNCOUNT [oft a NOUN] 不信任;怀疑 Distrust is the feeling of doubt that you have towards someone or something you distrust. 不信任;怀疑

  • What he saw there left him with a profound distrust of all political authority. [+ of]

    他在那儿的所见让他对一切政治当局产生了一种深深的不信任感。

  • ...a decision that should help to dispel much of the atmosphere of distrust.

    有助于极大地消除不信任氛围的决定

SYN suspicion, question, doubt, disbelief
