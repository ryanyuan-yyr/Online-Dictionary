flog
flɒg flɑg flɔg
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense flogs, present participle flogging, past tense , past participle flogged
1 VERB 出售;卖掉 If someone tries to flog something, they try to sell it. 出售;卖掉 [British, informal]

  • They are trying to flog their house. [VERB noun]

    他们正试图卖掉房子。

SYN sell, market, trade, dispose of  
2 VERB 鞭笞;棒打 If someone is flogged, they are hit very hard with a whip or stick as a punishment. 鞭笞;棒打

  • In these places people starved, were flogged, were clubbed to death. [be VERB-ed]

    在这些地方，人们忍饥挨饿，遭受鞭打，甚至会被乱棍打死。

  • Flog them soundly. [VERB noun]

    好好鞭打他们一顿。

SYN beat, whip, lash, thrash  
flogging

  • He was sentenced to a flogging and life imprisonment.

    他被判鞭笞和终身监禁。

  • He urged the restoration of hanging and flogging.

    他敦促恢复绞刑和鞭笞刑罚。

3 PHRASE 做徒劳无益的事;白忙活 If you say that someone is flogging a dead horse, you mean that they are trying to achieve something impossible. 做徒劳无益的事;白忙活 [informal]
