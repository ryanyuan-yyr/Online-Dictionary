dilute
daɪluːt dɪˈlut daɪˈlut
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dilutes, present participle diluting, past tense , past participle diluted
1 V-ERG （使）稀释;（使）冲淡 If a liquid is diluted or dilutes, it is added to or mixes with water or another liquid, and becomes weaker. （使）稀释;（使）冲淡 [Also VERB n]

  • If you give your baby juice, dilute it well with cooled, boiled water. [VERB noun prep.]

    如果给婴儿喝果汁，要用凉开水充分稀释。

  • The liquid is then diluted. [be VERB-ed]

    然后液体被稀释。

  • The poisons seeping from Hanford's contaminated land quickly dilute in the water. [VERB]

    从汉福德受污染的土地里渗出的有毒物质很快就在水中稀释了。

dilution

  • ...ditches dug for sewage dilution.

    用来稀释污水的沟渠

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 稀释过的;冲淡的 A dilute liquid is very thin and weak, usually because it has had water added to it. 稀释过的;冲淡的

  • ...a dilute solution of bleach.

    经稀释的漂白剂

SYN watered down, weak, diluted, watery  
3 VERB 削弱;降低;减轻 If someone or something dilutes a belief, quality, or value, they make it weaker and less effective. 削弱;降低;减轻

  • There was a clear intention to dilute black voting power. [VERB noun]

    明显有意图要削弱黑人的选举力量。

  • Serious attention is being given to diluting the value of personal tax allowances. [VERB noun]

    人们正密切关注降低个人收入免税额度一事。

SYN reduce, weaken, diminish, temper  
dilution

  • ...a potentially devastating dilution of earnings per share.

    每股收益可能的大幅摊薄
