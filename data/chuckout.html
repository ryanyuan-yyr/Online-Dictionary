chuck out
1 PHRASAL VERB 扔掉;抛弃 If you chuck something out, you throw it away, because you do not need it or cannot use it. 扔掉;抛弃 [informal] [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Many companies have struggled valiantly to use less energy and chuck out less rubbish. [VERB PREP. noun]

    许多公司都勇敢地努力尝试节约能源，减少垃圾。

2 PHRASAL VERB 解雇;把…赶走 If a person is chucked out of a job, a place, or their home, they are forced by other people to leave. 解雇;把…赶走 [informal]

  • Any head teacher who made errors like this would be chucked out. [be VERB-ed PREP.]

    哪个校长只要犯了这样的错误都会被开除。

  • I was chucked out of my London flat in 1960. [be V-ed P + of]

    1960年，我被从伦敦公寓里赶了出去。

  • Her parents are going to chuck her out on the street. [VERB noun PREP.]

    她父母要把她撵到大街上去。
