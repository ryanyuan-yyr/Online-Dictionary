centre
sentəʳ ˈsɛntər ˈsɛntə
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense centres, present participle centring, past tense , past participle centred
in AM, use 美国英语用 center
1 N-COUNT [oft in names] (会议、活动、服务等的)中心 A centre is a building where people have meetings, take part in a particular activity, or get help of some kind. (会议、活动、服务等的)中心

  • We went to a party at the leisure centre.

    我们去休闲中心参加一个聚会。

  • She now also does pottery classes at a community centre.

    她现在还在社区中心上陶艺课。

  • ...the National Exhibition Centre.

    国家展览中心

2 N-COUNT (工业、活动等的)中心 If an area or town is a centre for an industry or activity, that industry or activity is very important there. (工业、活动等的)中心

  • London is also the major international insurance centre.

    伦敦也是重要的国际保险中心。

3 N-COUNT [usually singular] 中心;中央;中部 The centre of something is the middle of it. 中心;中央;中部

  • A large wooden table dominates the centre of the room. [+ of]

    一张大木桌摆在房间正中央。

  • Bake until light golden and crisp around the edges and slightly soft in the centre.

    烤至边缘浅黄松脆，中间略软。

SYN middle, heart, focus, mid [archaic]  
4 N-COUNT [usually singular] (市镇的)商业中心，闹市区，繁华地带 The centre of a town or city is the part where there are the most shops and businesses and where a lot of people come from other areas to work or shop. (市镇的)商业中心，闹市区，繁华地带

  • ...the city centre.

    市中心

5 N-COUNT [usually singular] 中心;核心 If something or someone is at the centre of a situation, they are the most important thing or person involved. 中心;核心

  • ...the man at the centre of the controversy. [+ of]

    处于争论焦点的人

  • At the centre of the inquiry has been concern for the pensioners involved. [+ of]

    调查的核心一直是对于牵涉其中的领取养老金者的关注。

6 N-COUNT [usually singular] (关注的)中心，焦点;中心人物 If someone or something is the centre of attention or interest, people are giving them a lot of attention. (关注的)中心，焦点;中心人物

  • The rest of the cast was used to her being the centre of attention. [+ of]

    她总是人们关注的焦点，其他演职人员对此已经习以为常。

  • The centre of attraction was Pierre Auguste Renoir's oil painting. [+ of]

    最吸引人注意的是皮埃尔·奥古斯特·雷诺阿的油画。

7 N-SING [oft NOUN n] (政党)中间派，中立派 In politics, the centre refers to groups and their beliefs, when they are considered to be neither left-wing nor right-wing. (政党)中间派，中立派

  • The Democrats have become a party of the centre.

    民主党成了中立的党派。

  • ...the centre parties.

    中间党派

8 VERB 将…放在正中央 If you centre something, you move it so that it is at the centre of something else. 将…放在正中央 [Also VERB n]

  • Centre the design on the cloth before you start. [VERB noun on noun]

    在开始前，将图案放在布的正中间。

9 V-ERG （使）以…为中心;（使）注意力集中于 If something centres or is centred on a particular thing or person, that thing or person is the main subject of attention. （使）以…为中心;（使）注意力集中于 [Also V n on/around n]

  • ...a plan which centred on academic achievement and personal motivation. [V + on/around]

    重点强调学术成就和个人兴趣的计划

  • All his concerns were centred around himself rather than Rachel. [V on/around n]

    他所关心的全是他自己，而不是雷切尔。

  • When working with patients, my efforts are centred on helping them to overcome illness. [be V-ed + on/around]

    在治疗过程中，我的主要精力是帮助病人战胜疾病。

-centred

  • ...a child-centred approach to teaching.

    以孩子为中心的教学方法

  • ...patient-centred care.

    以病人为本的护理

10 V-ERG (使)主要发生在；（使）集中于 If an industry or event is centred in a place, or if it centres there, it takes place to the greatest extent there. (使)主要发生在；（使）集中于

  • The fighting has been centred around the town of Vucovar. [be VERB-ed prep.]

    战斗主要集中在武科瓦尔镇周围。

  • The disturbances have centred round the two main university areas. [VERB prep.]

    骚乱主要发生在两所大学附近的区域。

  • Between 100 and 150 travellers' vehicles were scattered around the county, with the largest gathering centred on Ampfield. [VERB-ed]

    大约100到150辆旅游车分散在这个县里，其中数量最多的一拨集中在安帕菲尔德。
