dressed up
drɛst ʌp
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 穿上盛装的;装扮成…的 If someone is dressed up, they are wearing special clothes, in order to look smarter than usual or in order to disguise themselves. 穿上盛装的;装扮成…的

  • You're all dressed up. Are you going somewhere?.

    你打扮得这么漂亮,要去哪？

  • You don't have to get dressed up for this party.

    你没必要为这次聚会而着盛装。

2 ADJ 装扮成;伪装成 If you say that something is dressed up as something else, you mean that someone has tried to make it more acceptable or attractive by making it seem like that other thing. 装扮成;伪装成 [disapproval] [Also + in]

  • He tried to organise things so that the trip would be dressed up as a UN mission.

    他竭力筹备好各项事宜以便这次出行看上去像联合国代表团出访一样。
