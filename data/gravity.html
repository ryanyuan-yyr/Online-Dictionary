gravity
grævɪti ˈgrævɪti
1 N-UNCOUNT 重力；地球引力 Gravity is the force which causes things to drop to the ground. 重力；地球引力

  • Arrows would continue to fly forward forever in a straight line were it not for gravity, which brings them down to earth.

    要不是重力使箭落向地面，它们将永远以直线往前飞行。

2See also:centre of gravity 
3 N-UNCOUNT （事态的）严重性，重大 The gravity of a situation or event is its extreme importance or seriousness. （事态的）严重性，重大

  • They deserve punishment which matches the gravity of their crime. [+ of]

    应当根据罪行的严重性来惩治他们。

  • Not all acts of vengeance are of equal gravity.

    并非所有的报复行为都同等恶劣。

4 N-UNCOUNT （言谈举止的）庄重，严肃 The gravity of someone's behaviour or speech is the extremely serious way in which they behave or speak. （言谈举止的）庄重，严肃

  • There was an appealing gravity to everything she said.

    她说的每句话都带着一种庄严，很能吸引人。

SYN solemnity, gloom, seriousness, gravitas
