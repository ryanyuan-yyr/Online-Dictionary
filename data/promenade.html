promenade
prɒmənɑːd ˌprɒməˈnɑːd -neɪd ˌprɑməˈneɪd ˌprɑməˈnɑd
Word forms: plural , plural , 3rd person singular present tense promenades, present participle promenading, past tense , past participle promenaded
1 N-COUNT 滨海大道；海滨散步的道路 In a seaside town, the promenade is the road by the sea where people go for a walk. 滨海大道；海滨散步的道路
2 N-COUNT 步行区；供散步的区域 A promenade is an area that is used for walking, for example a wide road or a deck on a ship. 步行区；供散步的区域 [mainly US]
3 VERB 散步 If someone promenades somewhere, for example along a beach, the edge of a lake, or a main street, they go for a walk there. 散步 [old-fashioned]

  • People came out in smarter clothes to promenade along the front. [VERB prep.]

    人们穿上更加时髦漂亮的衣服，沿着海滨散步。

SYN stroll, walk, saunter, take a walk  
4 N-COUNT （学校在学年末举行的）正式舞会 A promenade is a formal dance at a school or college which is usually held at the end of the academic year. （学校在学年末举行的）正式舞会 [US]
