swank
swæŋk swæŋk
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense swanks, present participle swanking, past tense , past participle swanked
1 VERB 炫耀;吹嘘;卖弄 If someone is swanking, they are speaking about things they own or things they have achieved, in order to impress other people. 炫耀;吹嘘;卖弄 [mainly British, informal, disapproval] [Also VERB]

  • I have always been against swanking about all the things I have been lucky enough to win. [VERB + about]

    我一向不喜欢和别人炫耀自己因为幸运而取得的那些成就。

SYN show off, swagger, give yourself airs, posture [informal]  
Swank is also a noun.

  • There was no swank in Martin.

    马丁从不炫耀自己。

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 同 swanky Swank means the same as swanky . 同 swanky [informal]

  • ...a swank new shop on the outskirts of Beijing.

    北京郊区新开的一家高档时尚商店
