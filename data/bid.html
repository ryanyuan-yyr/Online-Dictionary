bid
bɪd bɪd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense bids, present participle bidding
  • 1. ATTEMPTING OR OFFERING 尝试或出价
  • 2. SAYING SOMETHING 说话

ATTEMPTING OR OFFERING 尝试或出价

The form bid is used in the present tense and is the past tense and past participle. bid 的过去式和过去分词与原形相同。
1 N-COUNT [NOUN to-infinitive] 努力；尝试；企图 A bid for something or a bid to do something is an attempt to obtain it or do it. 努力；尝试；企图 [journalism]

  • ...Sydney's successful bid for the 2000 Olympic Games. [+ for]

    悉尼对2000年奥运会的成功申办

  • The Government has already closed down two newspapers in a bid to silence its critics.

    政府已经查禁了两家报纸，试图压制批评人士的言论。

SYN attempt, try, effort, venture  
2 N-COUNT 投标；出价 A bid is an offer to pay a particular amount of money for something that is being sold. 投标；出价

  • Hanson made an agreed takeover bid of £351 million.

    汉森按协商出价3.51亿英镑接管该公司。

SYN offer, price, attempt, amount  
3 VERB 努力争取；尝试 If you bid for something or bid to do something, you try to obtain it or do it. 努力争取；尝试 [literary] [Also VERB n to-infinitive]

  • Singapore Airlines is rumoured to be bidding for a management contract to run both airports. [VERB + for]

    据传，新加坡航空公司正在争取签下两家机场的经营权。

  • I don't think she is bidding to be Prime Minister again. [VERB to-infinitive]

    我想她不会再次竞选首相。

SYN make a bid for, attempt, seek, strive  
4 VERB 投标；竞标；出价 If you bid for something that is being sold, you offer to pay a particular amount of money for it. 投标；竞标；出价

  • She decided to bid for a Georgian dressing table. [VERB + for]

    她决定参与一个乔治王朝时期风格梳妆台的竞价。

  • The bank announced its intention to bid. [VERB]

    这家银行宣布了参加竞标的意向。

  • He certainly wasn't going to bid $18 billion for this company. [VERB noun]

    他肯定不会出180亿美元来收购这家公司。

bidding

  • The bidding starts at £2 million.

    竞标底价是200万英镑。

Phrasal verbs:
bid up

SAYING SOMETHING 说话

American English sometimes uses the form bid for the past tense. 美国英语中，过去式有时用 bid。
1 VERB 道（别、晚安等） If you bid someone farewell, you say goodbye to them. If you bid them goodnight, you say goodnight to them. 道（别、晚安等） [formal]

  • She bade farewell to her son. [VERB noun + to]

    她和儿子道了别。

  • I bade her goodnight. [VERB noun noun]

    我向她道了晚安。

SYN wish, say, call, tell  
2 VERB 要求；邀请 If you bid someone do something, you ask or invite them to do it. 要求；邀请 [literary] [Also VERB n to-infinitive]

  • They all smiled at him and bade him eat. [VERB noun infinitive]

    他们都朝他微笑，请他吃饭。

  • I dare say he did as he was bidden. [be VERB-ed]

    我敢说他按照要求做了。

SYN tell, call, ask, order
