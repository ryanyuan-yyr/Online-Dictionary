whiff
hwɪf hwɪf wɪf
Word forms: plural whiffs
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 一阵(气味) If there is a whiff of a particular smell, you smell it only slightly or only for a brief period of time, for example as you walk past someone or something. 一阵(气味)

  • He caught a whiff of her perfume. [+ of]

    他闻到了她身上的一股香水味。

SYN smell, hint, scent, sniff  
2 N-COUNT [usually singular] 轻微的征兆;迹象 A whiff of something bad or harmful is a slight sign of it. 轻微的征兆;迹象

  • Not a whiff of scandal has ever tainted his private life. [+ of]

    他的私生活没有爆出过一丁点儿的丑闻。

  • The TV show had the whiff of hypocrisy and pomposity.

    这个电视节目有些伪善和夸耀的嫌疑。

SYN trace, suggestion, hint, suspicion
