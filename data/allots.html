allot
əlɒt əˈlɑt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense allots, present participle allotting, past tense , past participle allotted
VERB [usually passive] 分配;分派;划拨 If something is allotted to someone, it is given to them as their share. 分配;分派;划拨

  • The seats are allotted to the candidates who have won the most votes. [be VERB-ed + to]

    席位被分配给了赢得选票最多的候选人。

  • We were allotted half an hour to address the committee. [be VERB-ed noun]

    给了我们半个小时向委员会作陈述。

SYN assign, allocate, designate, set aside
