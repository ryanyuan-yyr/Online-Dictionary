chamber
tʃeɪmbəʳ ˈtʃeɪmbər
Word forms: plural chambers
1 N-COUNT 大房间;(尤指)大会议室 A chamber is a large room, especially one that is used for formal meetings. 大房间;(尤指)大会议室

  • We are going to make sure we are in the council chamber every time he speaks.

    每次他发表讲话我们都会确保赶到市议会会议厅去。

SYN hall, room  
2 N-COUNT 议会;议院 You can refer to a country's parliament or to one section of it as a chamber . 议会;议院

  • More than 80 parties are contesting seats in the two-chamber parliament.

    有80多个党派在竞争两院的议席。

  • Signor Amato's government has only a 16-seat majority in the Chamber of Deputies. [+ of]

    阿马托先生的政府在下议院仅以16席的优势占有多数席位。

3 N-COUNT (作特殊用途的)房间 A chamber is a room designed and equipped for a particular purpose. (作特殊用途的)房间

  • For many, the dentist's surgery remains a torture chamber.

    对许多人来说，牙医的治疗室一直是间受刑室。

4See also:gas chamber 
5 N-COUNT (人体、动植物体内的)腔，室 A chamber is a hollow place inside the body of a person or animal, or inside a plant. (人体、动植物体内的)腔，室
6 N-PLURAL （法官）办公室;(律师)事务所 The offices used by judges and barristers are referred to as chambers. （法官）办公室;(律师)事务所
