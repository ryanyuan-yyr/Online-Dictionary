grief
griːf grif
Word forms: plural griefs
1 N-VAR 悲伤；悲痛 Grief is a feeling of extreme sadness. 悲伤；悲痛

  • ...a huge outpouring of national grief for the victims of the shootings. [+ for]

    举国上下对枪击事件中的遇害者所表现出的万分悲痛之情

  • Their grief soon gave way to anger.

    他们的悲痛迅速转化为愤怒。

SYN sadness, suffering, pain, regret  
2 PHRASE （事情）失败；（人）受挫，遭到不幸 If something comes to grief, it fails. If someone comes to grief, they fail in something they are doing, and may be hurt. （事情）失败；（人）受挫，遭到不幸

  • So many marriages have come to grief over lack of money.

    许多婚姻都因缺钱而以失败告终。

  • He was driving a Mercedes racer at 100 mph and almost came to grief.

    他当时驾驶着一辆梅塞德斯赛车以每小时100英里时速度飞驰，险些出事。

SYN fail, founder, break down, come unstuck  
3 EXCLAM （表示惊奇或震惊）哎呀，天哪 Some people say 'Good grief' when they are surprised or shocked. （表示惊奇或震惊）哎呀，天哪 [feelings]

  • 'He's been arrested for theft and burglary.' — 'Good grief!'

    “他因为偷窃和入室盗窃而被逮捕了。”—“我的天哪！”

Quotations
Grief is a species of idlenessSamuel Johnson letter to Mrs Thrale
