fawn
fɔːn fɔn
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense fawns, present participle fawning, past tense , past participle fawned
1 COLOUR 浅黄褐色 Fawn is a pale yellowish-brown colour. 浅黄褐色

  • ...a light fawn coat.

    浅黄褐色大衣

2 N-COUNT 幼鹿 A fawn is a very young deer. 幼鹿

  • The fawn ran to the top of the ridge.

    小鹿跑上山脊。

3 VERB 逢迎;巴结;讨好;拍…的马屁 If you say that someone fawns over a powerful or rich person, you disapprove of them because they flatter that person and like to be with him or her. 逢迎;巴结;讨好;拍…的马屁 [disapproval]

  • People fawn over you when you're famous. [V + over/on/around]

    你出了名，大家就会来讨好你。

  • Nauseatingly fawning journalism that's all it is. [VERB-ing]

    那只不过是以令人作呕的溜须拍马为能事的新闻报道。

SYN ingratiate yourself, court, flatter, pander to
