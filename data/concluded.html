conclude
kənkluːd kənˈklud
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense concludes, present participle concluding, past tense , past participle concluded
1 VERB 推断出;推论出;断定 If you conclude that something is true, you decide that it is true using the facts you know as a basis. 推断出;推论出;断定

  • Larry had concluded that he had no choice but to accept Paul's words as the truth. [VERB that]

    拉里断定自己别无选择，只能当保罗的话是真的。

  • So what can we conclude from this debate?. [VERB noun + from]

    那么从这场辩论中我们能得出什么结论？

  • 'The situation in the inner cities is bad and getting worse,' she concluded. [VERB with quote]

    “内城的情况很糟，而且是越来越糟。”她断言道。

2 VERB 结束(讲话);总结 When you conclude, you say the last thing that you are going to say. 结束(讲话);总结 [formal]

  • 'It's a waste of time,' he concluded. [VERB with quote]

    “这是浪费时间。”他总结道。

  • I would like to conclude by saying that I do enjoy your magazine. [VERB]

    最后，我想说我的确很喜欢你们的杂志。

concluding

  • On the radio I caught Mr Hague's concluding remarks at the Blackpool conference.

    我在收音机中听到了黑格先生在布莱克浦会议上的总结发言。

3 V-ERG 结束;终止 When something concludes, or when you conclude it, you end it. 结束;终止 [formal]

  • The evening concluded with dinner and speeches. [VERB adv./prep.]

    这个夜晚在宴会和讲话中结束。

  • The Group of Seven major industrial countries concluded its annual summit meeting today. [VERB noun]

    由主要工业国家组成的七国集团今天结束了其年度峰会。

SYN come to an end, end, close, finish  
4 V-RECIP 达成(协议等);缔结(条约) If one person or group concludes an agreement, such as a treaty or business deal, with another, they arrange it. You can also say that two people or groups conclude an agreement. 达成(协议等);缔结(条约) [formal]

  • Mexico and the Philippines have both concluded agreements with their commercial bank creditors. [VERB noun + with]

    墨西哥和菲律宾均已与商业银行债权人达成了协议。

  • If the clubs cannot conclude a deal, an independent tribunal will decide. [VERB noun]

    如果俱乐部之间无法达成协议，将由一个独立的仲裁委员会来裁定。

SYN accomplish, effect, settle, bring about
