hold over
1 PHRASAL VERB 以…威胁;以…要挟 If you hold something over someone, you use it in order to threaten them or make them do what you want. 以…威胁;以…要挟

  • Did Laurie know something, and hold it over Felicity? [VERB noun PREP. noun]

    劳丽是否知道点什么，并以此要挟费莉西蒂呢？

2 PHRASAL VERB 推迟;搁置 If something is held over, it does not happen or it is not dealt with until a future date. 推迟;搁置 [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • Further voting might be held over until tomorrow. [be VERB-ed PREP.]

    下一步投票可能要推到明天。

  • We would have held the story over until the next day. [VERB noun PREP.]

    我们原本是要把报道推迟到第二天的。

SYN postpone, delay, suspend, put off
