junk
dʒʌŋk dʒʌŋk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense junks, present participle junking, past tense , past participle junked
1 N-UNCOUNT [oft NOUN n] 废弃物；废旧物 Junk is old and used goods that have little value and that you do not want any more. 废弃物；废旧物

  • Rose finds her furniture in junk shops.

    罗丝从旧货店买来了家具。

  • What are you going to do with all that junk, Larry?

    这一大堆破烂儿你打算怎么处理，拉里？

SYN rubbish, refuse, waste, scrap  
2 VERB 丢弃；抛弃；废弃 If you junk something, you get rid of it or stop using it. 丢弃；抛弃；废弃 [informal]

  • Consumers will not have to junk their old cassettes to use the new format. [VERB noun]

    消费者将不必丢弃他们的旧盒带而采用新的制式。

  • The Socialists junked dogma when they came to office in 1982. [VERB noun]

    社会党人1982年上台执政后，就把其政治信条弃之不顾。

SYN get rid of, drop, remove, reject  
3 N-COUNT 中国式（平底）帆船；舢板 A junk is a Chinese sailing boat that has a flat bottom and square sails. 中国式（平底）帆船；舢板
