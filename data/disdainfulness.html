disdainful
dɪsdeɪnfʊl dɪsˈdeɪnfəl
ADJ-GRADED 轻蔑的;鄙视的 To be disdainful means to dislike something or someone because you think they are unimportant or not worth your attention. 轻蔑的;鄙视的

  • He is highly disdainful of anything to do with the literary establishment. [+ of]

    他对任何与文学界权威沾边的事都嗤之以鼻。

  • Edgar cast a disdainful look at his twin.

    埃德加朝他的双胞胎兄弟投去鄙夷的目光。

SYN contemptuous, scornful, arrogant, superior  
disdainfully

  • 'We know all about you,' she said disdainfully.

    “我们了解你的一切情况，”她轻蔑地说道。
