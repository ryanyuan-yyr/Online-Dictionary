norm
nɔːʳm nɔrm
Word forms: plural norms
1 N-COUNT [usually plural] 行为准则;规范 Norms are ways of behaving that are considered normal in a particular society. 行为准则;规范

  • ...the commonly accepted norms of democracy. [+ of]

    被人们普遍接受的民主准则

  • ...a social norm that says drunkenness is inappropriate behaviour.

    认为醉酒是不当行为的社会规范

2 N-SING 通例;常规 If you say that a situation is the norm, you mean that it is usual and expected. 通例;常规 [Also + for]

  • Families of six or seven are the norm in Borough Park. [+ in]

    在区公园住宅区六口或七口之家十分普遍。

  • The changes will lead to more flexible leases, and leases nearer to 15 years than the present norm of 25 years. [+ of]

    这些变化将带来更灵活的、接近15年的租期，而不是现行的常规为25年的租期。

3 N-COUNT 标准;规范 A norm is an official standard or level that organizations are expected to reach. 标准;规范

  • ...an agency which would establish European norms and co-ordinate national policies to halt pollution.

    确立各项欧洲环保标准、协调各国政策以阻遏污染的机构
