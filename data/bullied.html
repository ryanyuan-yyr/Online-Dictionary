bully
bʊli ˈbʊli
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense bullies, present participle bullying, past tense , past participle bullied
1 N-COUNT 恃强凌弱者；恶霸 A bully is someone who uses their strength or power to hurt or frighten other people. 恃强凌弱者；恶霸

  • I fell victim to the office bully.

    我成了这个办公室霸王欺侮的对象。

  • He's a coward and a bully who confuses physical strength with manhood.

    他是一个懦夫，一个分不清蛮力和大丈夫气概的恃强凌弱者。

SYN persecutor, tough, oppressor, tormentor  
2 VERB 欺负；恐吓；欺凌 If someone bullies you, they use their strength or power to hurt or frighten you. 欺负；恐吓；欺凌

  • I wasn't going to let him bully me. [VERB noun]

    我可不打算任由他欺负。

  • I asked her if she was bullied by the other children. [VERB noun]

    我问她有没有被别的孩子欺负。

SYN persecute, intimidate, torment, hound  
bullying

  • ...schoolchildren who were victims of bullying.

    受大孩子欺负的小学生

3 VERB 迫使；胁迫 If someone bullies you into something, they make you do it by using force or threats. 迫使；胁迫 [Also VERB n]

  • We think an attempt to bully them into submission would be counterproductive. [VERB noun + into]

    我们认为试图迫使他们屈服会适得其反。

  • She used to bully me into doing my schoolwork. [VERB noun + into]

    她过去总是强迫我写作业。

  • The government says it will not be bullied by the press. [be VERB-ed]

    政府声称不会屈服于新闻媒体的压力。

SYN force, coerce, railroad [informal] , bulldoze [informal]
