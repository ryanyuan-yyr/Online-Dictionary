handy
hændi ˈhændi
Word forms: comparative handier, superlative handiest
1 ADJ-GRADED 有用的;方便的 Something that is handy is useful. 有用的;方便的

  • The book gives handy hints on looking after indoor plants.

    这本书提供了一些关于如何照料室内花草的有用信息。

  • Credit cards can be handy — they mean you do not have to carry large sums of cash.

    信用卡很方便，有了它你就不用携带大量现金了。

SYN useful, practical, helpful, neat  
2 PHRASE 派上用场 If something comes in handy, it is useful in a particular situation. 派上用场

  • That key will come in handy if you lock yourself out.

    要是你把自己锁在了屋外，那把钥匙就派上用场了。

  • The $20 check came in very handy.

    那张20美元的支票正好派上了用场。

3 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 手边的;附近的 A thing or place that is handy is nearby and therefore easy to get or reach. 手边的;附近的

  • It would be good to have a pencil and paper handy.

    最好在手边准备好纸和笔。

  • Keep handy a lightweight sweater or cardigan.

    手边备一件薄的套头毛衣或开襟毛衫。

  • This lively town is handy for Londoners.

    这个热闹的小城离伦敦很近。

SYN convenient, close, near, available  
4 ADJ-GRADED [verb-link ADJ with n] 手巧的;有手艺的 Someone who is handy with a particular tool is skilful at using it. 手巧的;有手艺的 [informal]

  • If you're handy with a needle you could brighten up your sweater with daisies. [+ with]

    如果你针线活做得好，可以在自己的毛衣上绣几朵雏菊做点缀。

SYN skilful, skilled, expert, clever
