resurface
riːsɜːʳfɪs riːˈsɜːfɪs riˈsɜrfɪs
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense resurfaces, present participle resurfacing, past tense , past participle resurfaced
1 VERB （思想、问题等）再次变得重要，重新引人注意 If something such as an idea or problem resurfaces, it becomes important or noticeable again. （思想、问题等）再次变得重要，重新引人注意

  • These ideas resurfaced again in the American civil rights movement. [VERB]

    这些观念在美国民权运动中重新抬头。

  • The disease was said to have resurfaced in three countries. [VERB]

    据说，这种疾病在3个国家重新出现了。

2 VERB 重新露面;再次出现 If someone who has not been seen for a long time resurfaces, they suddenly appear again. 重新露面;再次出现 [informal]

  • It is likely that they would go into hiding for a few weeks, and resurface when the publicity has died down. [VERB]

    他们很可能会躲藏几个星期，等风声过了以后再露面。

3 VERB 重新浮出水面 If someone or something that has been under water resurfaces, they come back to the surface of the water again. 重新浮出水面

  • George struggled wildly, going under and resurfacing at regular intervals. [VERB]

    乔治疯狂地挣扎，不断在水面上浮浮沉沉。

4 VERB 给…重铺路面 To resurface something such as a road means to put a new surface on it. 给…重铺路面

  • Meanwhile the race is on to resurface the road before next Wednesday. [VERB noun]

    与此同时正赶着在下周三之前把路面重新铺好。
