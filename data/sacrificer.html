sacrifice
sækrɪfaɪs ˈsækrəˌfaɪs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense sacrifices, present participle sacrificing, past tense , past participle sacrificed
1 VERB 牺牲;舍弃;献出 If you sacrifice something that is valuable or important, you give it up, usually to obtain something else for yourself or for other people. 牺牲;舍弃;献出

  • She sacrificed family life to her career. [V n + to/for]

    她为事业舍弃了家庭。

  • Her husband's pride was a small thing to sacrifice for their children's security. [V to/for n]

    为了孩子们的安全，舍弃她丈夫的尊严不算什么。

  • Kitty Aldridge has sacrificed all for her first film. [VERB noun]

    姬蒂·奥尔德里奇为自己的首部影片牺牲了一切。

  • He sacrificed himself and so saved his country. [VERB pronoun-reflexive]

    他牺牲自己，以此拯救了他的祖国。

SYN give up, abandon, relinquish [formal] , lose  
Sacrifice is also a noun.

  • She made many sacrifices to get Anita a good education.

    为了让安妮塔接受良好的教育，她作出了很多牺牲。

  • He was willing to make any sacrifice for peace.

    他愿意为和平作出任何牺牲。

2See also:self-sacrifice 
3 VERB 献祭;以…为祭品 To sacrifice an animal or person means to kill them in a special religious ceremony as an offering to a god. 献祭;以…为祭品 [Also V n to n]

  • The priest sacrificed a chicken. [VERB noun]

    祭司用一只鸡作祭品。

  • Two white bulls were sacrificed and a feast was held. [VERB noun]

    献祭了两头白牛，并举行了盛宴。

SYN offer, slaughter, offer up, immolate [literary]  
Sacrifice is also a noun.

  • ...animal sacrifices to the gods.

    祭神的牺牲
