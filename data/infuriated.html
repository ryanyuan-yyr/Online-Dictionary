infuriate
ɪnfjʊərieɪt ɪnˈfjʊriˌeɪt ɪnˈfjʊriɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense infuriates, present participle infuriating, past tense , past participle infuriated
VERB 使十分恼火;使大怒;激怒 If something or someone infuriates you, they make you extremely angry. 使十分恼火;使大怒;激怒 [Also V n that]

  • Jimmy's presence had infuriated Hugh. [VERB noun]

    吉米的到场激怒了休。

  • The champion was infuriated by the decision. [VERB noun]

    冠军获得者对这一决定十分恼火。

  • It infuriates us to have to deal with this particular mayor. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    不得不跟这位市长打交道让我们大为光火。

SYN enrage, anger, provoke, irritate  
infuriated

  • I was absolutely infuriated with him.

    我对他极为生气。
