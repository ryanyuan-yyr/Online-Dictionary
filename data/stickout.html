stick out
1 PHRASAL VERB 伸出;探出 If you stick out part of your body, you extend it away from your body. to stick your neck out→see: neck  伸出;探出

  • She made a face and stuck out her tongue at him. [VERB PREP. noun]

    她做了个鬼脸，向他吐了吐舌头。

  • He stuck his hand out and he said, 'Good evening.' [VERB noun PREP.]

    他伸出手说：“晚上好。”

2 PHRASAL VERB 显眼;显得突出 If something sticks out, it is very noticeable because it is unusual. to stick out a mile→see: mile  to stick out like a sore thumb→see: thumb  显眼;显得突出

  • The things that stuck out were his cockiness and his four-letter words. [VERB PREP.]

    与众不同之处在于他的骄傲自大和满嘴脏话。

  • What had Cutter done to make him stick out from the crowd? [VERB PREP. + from]

    卡特做了些什么，让他从众人之中脱颖而出？

3 PHRASE (在逆境中)坚持到底，忍耐到底 If someone in an unpleasant or difficult situation sticks it out, they do not leave or give up. (在逆境中)坚持到底，忍耐到底

  • I really didn't like New York, but I wanted to stick it out a little bit longer.

    我真的不喜欢纽约，但我想再坚持一段时间。
