drench
drentʃ drɛntʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense drenches, present participle drenching, past tense , past participle drenched
VERB 使湿透;使浸湿 To drench something or someone means to make them completely wet. 使湿透;使浸湿

  • They turned fire hoses on the people and drenched them. [VERB noun]

    他们将消防水管对着人们，把他们浇了个透。

  • They were getting drenched by icy water. [get VERB-ed]

    他们被冰冷的水浇透了。

  • We were completely drenched and cold. [VERB-ed]

    我们浑身湿透了，冻得冰冷。

  • I reached Kilmarnock in the early morning in drenching rain. [VERB-ing]

    我一大早就冒着瓢泼大雨到了基尔曼诺克。

SYN soak, flood, wet, duck  
-drenched

  • ...the rain-drenched streets of the capital.

    首都积满雨水的街道
