reject
rɪdʒekt riːdʒekt rɪˈdʒɛkt ˈridʒɛkt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense rejects, present participle rejecting, past tense , past participle rejected
The verb is pronounced /rɪ'dʒekt/. The noun is pronounced /'riːdʒekt/. 动词读作/rɪ'dʒekt/。名词读作/'riːdʒekt/。
1 VERB 拒绝考虑;不接受;不同意 If you reject something such as a proposal, a request, or an offer, you do not accept it or you do not agree to it. 拒绝考虑;不接受;不同意

  • The British government is expected to reject the idea of state subsidy for a new high speed railway. [VERB noun]

    预计英国政府不会考虑为修建一条新的高速铁路提供国家补贴的设想。

  • Seventeen publishers rejected the manuscript before Jenks saw its potential.

    17家出版社回绝了这部手稿，直到詹克斯看到了它的潜在价值。

SYN discard, decline, eliminate, scrap  
rejection

  • The rejection of such initiatives indicates that voters are unconcerned about the environment.

    此类倡议遇冷表明选民对环境并不关心。

2 VERB 摈弃，抛弃，不接受(信仰或政治制度) If you reject a belief or a political system, you refuse to believe in it or to live by its rules. 摈弃，抛弃，不接受(信仰或政治制度)

  • ...the children of Eastern European immigrants who had rejected their parents' political and religious beliefs. [VERB noun]

    抛弃了父母一代政治和宗教信仰的东欧移民子女

rejection

  • ...his rejection of our values.

    他对我们的价值观的摒弃

3 VERB 不录用;不录取 If someone is rejected for a job or course of study, it is not offered to them. 不录用;不录取 [Also VERB n]

  • One of my most able students was rejected by another university. [be VERB-ed]

    我的一个最有能力的学生申请另一所大学落选了。

rejection

  • Be prepared for lots of rejections before you land a job.

    在找到一份工作之前，要做好屡次碰壁的准备。

4 VERB 冷漠对待;拒绝…的爱意 If someone rejects another person who expects affection from them, they are cold and unfriendly towards them. 冷漠对待;拒绝…的爱意

  • You make friends with people and then make unreasonable demands so that they reject you. [VERB noun]

    你和别人交朋友，然后向人家提出无理要求，结果被人家拒绝。

  • ...people who had been rejected by their lovers. [be VERB-ed]

    被爱人拒绝的人

SYN rebuff, drop, jilt, desert  
rejection

  • These feelings of rejection and hurt remain.

    被拒绝和受伤的感觉还在。

5 VERB 排斥(移植器官)；对…产生排斥反应 If a person's body rejects something such as a new heart that has been transplanted into it, it tries to attack and destroy it. 排斥(移植器官)；对…产生排斥反应

  • It was feared his body was rejecting a kidney he received in a transplant four years ago. [VERB noun]

    令人担心的是，他的身体可能对4年前移植的肾产生了排斥反应。

rejection

  • ...a special drug which stops rejection of transplanted organs.

    一种可以阻止身体对移植器官产生排异反应的特殊药物

6 VERB (投币设备)拒绝接受(硬币) If a machine rejects a coin that you put in it, the coin comes out and the machine does not work. (投币设备)拒绝接受(硬币)
7 N-COUNT 残次品;等外品 A reject is a product that has not been accepted for use or sale, because there is something wrong with it. 残次品;等外品
