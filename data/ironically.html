ironically
aɪrɒnɪkli
1 ADV-GRADED 具有讽刺意味的是;令人哭笑不得的是 You use ironically to draw attention to a situation which is odd or amusing because it involves a contrast. 具有讽刺意味的是;令人哭笑不得的是

  • Ironically, for a man who hated war, he would have made a superb war cameraman.

    具有讽刺意味的是,他这个痛恨战争的人曾有可能成为一个优秀的战地摄影师。

SYN paradoxically, absurdly, incongruously, ambiguously  
2 ADV-GRADED [ADVERB with verb] 讽刺挖苦地;说反话地 If you say something ironically, you do not mean it and are saying it as a joke. 讽刺挖苦地;说反话地

  • Classmates at West Point had ironically dubbed him Beauty.

    西点军校的同学们曾挖苦地戏称他为“美人”。
