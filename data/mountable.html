mount maʊnt maʊnt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense mounts, present participle mounting, past tense , past participle mounted
1 VERB 组织;发起;开展 If you mount a campaign or event, you organize it and make it take place. 组织;发起;开展

  • The ANC announced it was mounting a major campaign of mass political protests. [VERB noun]

    非洲人国民大会宣布他们正在开展一次大规模政治抗议活动。

  • ...a security operation mounted by the army. [VERB noun]

    军队开展的维护安全军事行动

SYN launch, stage, prepare, deliver  
2 VERB 增强;加剧 If something mounts, it increases in intensity. 增强;加剧

  • For several hours, tension mounted. [VERB]

    有几个小时，紧张局势有所加剧。

  • The decibel level was mounting. [VERB]

    噪音在增强。

  • There was mounting concern in her voice. [VERB-ing]

    她的声音里流露出越来越多的担心。

  • ...the mounting heat of the stadium. [VERB-ing]

    体育场不断上升的热度

SYN increase, build, grow, swell  
3 VERB 增多;增加 If something mounts, it increases in quantity. 增多;增加

  • The uncollected garbage mounts in city streets. [VERB]

    未收的垃圾在市区街道上越积越多。

  • He ignored his mounting debts. [VERB-ing]

    他对日益增加的债务置之不理。

SYN accumulate, increase, collect, gather  
To mount up means the same as to mount .同 mount

  • If you hide away your problems and pretend that they don't exist they will just continue to mount up.

    如果你把问题藏起来，假装它们不存在，问题只会越积越多。

  • Her medical bills mounted up.

    她的医疗费不断增加。

4 VERB 爬上，登上(台阶、梯级) If you mount the stairs or a platform, you go up the stairs or go up onto the platform. 爬上，登上(台阶、梯级) [formal]

  • Llewelyn was mounting the stairs up into the keep. [VERB noun]

    卢埃林登上台阶，走进城堡主楼。

  • The vehicle mounted the pavement. [VERB noun]

    机动车开上了人行道。

SYN ascend, scale, climb (up), go up  
5 VERB 骑上(马、自行车等) If you mount a horse or cycle, you climb on to it so that you can ride it. 骑上(马、自行车等)

  • He mounted his horse and rode away. [VERB noun]

    他骑上马走了。

  • A man in a crash helmet was mounting a motorbike. [VERB noun]

    一位戴头盔的男子正跨上摩托车。

  • He went to the small stable where his horse was, harnessed it, mounted, and rode out to the beach. [VERB]

    他走进拴马的小马厩，套上马具，骑上马奔向海滩。

SYN get (up) on, jump on, straddle, climb onto  
6 N-COUNT 坐骑;马 A mount is a horse. 坐骑;马 [formal]

  • ...the number of owners who care for older mounts.

    愿意照料老马的马主的数量

SYN horse, steed [literary]  
7 VERB 安装;安置;裱 If you mount an object on something, you fix it there firmly. 安装;安置;裱 [Also VERB n]

  • Her husband mounts the work on velour paper and makes the frame. [VERB noun + on]

    她丈夫把作品装裱在绒纸上，并制作了画框。

  • The support for the fence is mounted on an extension to the table. [VERB noun on noun]

    篱笆的支撑部分安装在桌子延长出的地方。

  • ...a specially mounted horse shoe. [VERB-ed]

    特别安装的马蹄铁

SYN fit, place, set, position  
-mounted

  • ...a wall-mounted electric fan.

    安装在墙上的电扇

  • ...pickup trucks with side-mounted fuel tanks.

    油箱置于侧面的轻型货车

8 VERB 组织，举办(展览或展示) If you mount an exhibition or display, you organize and present it. 组织，举办(展览或展示)

  • The gallery has mounted an exhibition of art by Irish women painters. [VERB noun]

    画廊已举办了一场爱尔兰女画家的作品展。

9 N-IN-NAMES (用于山的名称中)山 Mount is used as part of the name of a mountain. (用于山的名称中)山

  • ...Mount Kilimanjaro.

    乞力马扎罗山
