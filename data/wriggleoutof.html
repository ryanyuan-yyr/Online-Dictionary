wriggle out of
PHRASAL VERB 设法摆脱，逃避(应做的事情) If you say that someone has wriggled out of doing something, you disapprove of the fact that they have managed to avoid doing it, although they should have done it. 设法摆脱，逃避(应做的事情) [disapproval] [Also V P P v-ing]

  • The Government has tried to wriggle out of any responsibility for providing childcare for working parents. [VERB PREP. PREP. noun]

    政府试图推脱为在职父母提供儿童保育服务的责任。

SYN twist, avoid, duck [informal] , dodge
