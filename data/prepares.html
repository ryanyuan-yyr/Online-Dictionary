prepare
prɪpeəʳ prɪˈpɛə priˈpær prɪˈpær
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense prepares, present participle preparing, past tense , past participle prepared
1 VERB 把…准备好；使预备好 If you prepare something, you make it ready for something that is going to happen. 把…准备好；使预备好

  • Two technicians were preparing a videotape recording of last week's programme. [VERB noun]

    两个技术人员正在准备上周节目的录像带。

  • On average each report requires 1,000 hours to prepare. [VERB noun]

    准备一份报告平均需要1,000小时。

  • The crew of the Iowa has been preparing the ship for storage. [VERB noun + for]

    “艾奥瓦号”的船员一直都在为货物储存准备船只。

SYN equip, fit, adapt, adjust  
2 VERB 为…做准备；筹备 If you prepare for an event or action that will happen soon, you get yourself ready for it or make the necessary arrangements. 为…做准备；筹备 [Also VERB]

  • The Party leadership is using management consultants to help prepare for the next election. [VERB + for]

    该党的领导层正在管理顾问的帮助下筹备下一届的选举。

  • President Castro has warned Cubans to prepare for a profound economic emergency. [VERB for noun]

    卡斯特罗主席已经提醒古巴人民为严重的经济危急状况做准备。

  • He had to go back to his hotel and prepare to catch a train for New York. [VERB to-infinitive]

    他不得不回到酒店，准备赶火车去纽约。

  • His doctor had told him to prepare himself for surgery. [V pron-refl + for]

    他的医生已经告诉他做好动手术的准备。

SYN train, guide, prime, direct  
3 VERB 准备（食物）；做（饭菜） When you prepare food, you get it ready to be eaten, for example by cooking it. 准备（食物）；做（饭菜）

  • She made her way to the kitchen, hoping to find someone preparing dinner. [VERB noun]

    她走到厨房，希望看到有人在准备晚饭。

  • The best way of preparing the nuts is to rehydrate them by soaking overnight. [VERB noun]

    做这些坚果的最好方法就是用水浸泡一个晚上，为其补充水分。
