calculate
kælkjʊleɪt ˈkælkjuˌleɪt ˈkælkjəˌleɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense calculates, present participle calculating, past tense , past participle calculated
1 VERB 计算 If you calculate a number or amount, you discover it from information that you already have, by using arithmetic, mathematics, or a special machine. 计算 [Also VERB wh]

  • From this you can calculate the total mass in the Galaxy. [VERB noun]

    由此你可以算出银河系的总质量。

  • We calculate that the average size farm in Lancaster County is 65 acres. [VERB that]

    我们计算出兰开斯特郡的农场面积平均为65英亩。

  • A computer calculates by switching currents on or off. [VERB]

    计算机通过电路的开合来进行运算。

SYN work out, value, judge, determine  
2 VERB 估算;估计;推算;预料 If you calculate the effects of something, especially a possible course of action, you think about them in order to form an opinion or decide what to do. 估算;估计;推算;预料

  • I believe I am capable of calculating the political consequences accurately. [VERB noun]

    我觉得我能准确预料此事的政治后果。

  • The President is calculating that this will somehow relieve the international pressure on him. [VERB that]

    总统预计这可以从某一方面缓解自己所承受的国际社会压力。
