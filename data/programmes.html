programme
proʊgræm ˈprəʊɡræm
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense programmes, present participle programming, past tense , past participle programmed
in AM, use 美国英语用 program
1 N-COUNT 方案；计划；安排 A programme of actions or events is a series of actions or events that are planned to be done. 方案；计划；安排

  • The general argued that the nuclear programme should still continue.

    将军认为核计划应该继续进行。

  • The programme of sell-offs has been implemented by the new chief executive.

    新任总裁已经执行了抛售方案。

2 N-COUNT [oft n NOUN] （电视或广播）节目 A television or radio programme is something that is broadcast on television or radio. （电视或广播）节目

  • ...a series of TV programmes on global environment.

    有关全球环境的一系列电视节目

  • ...local news programmes.

    地方新闻节目

SYN show, performance, production, broadcast  
3 N-COUNT （剧院或音乐会的）节目单 A theatre or concert programme is a small book or sheet of paper which gives information about the play or concert you are attending. （剧院或音乐会的）节目单
4 VERB 为（机器或系统）设定程序 When you programme a machine or system, you set its controls so that it will work in a particular way. 为（机器或系统）设定程序 [Also VERB n]

  • Parents can programme the machine not to turn on at certain times. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    父母可以设定这台机器的程序，使它在特定时间段无法开启。

5 VERB [usually passive] 按特定的方式行事 If a living creature is programmed to behave in a particular way, they are likely to behave in that way because of social or biological factors that they cannot control. 按特定的方式行事 [Also be VERB-ed]

  • We are all genetically programmed to develop certain illnesses. [be VERB-ed to-infinitive]

    基因决定了我们都会患上某些疾病。
