hard luck
hɑːd lʌk
1 N-UNCOUNT （非自身造成的）坏运气，噩运 If you say that someone had some hard luck, or that a situation was hard luck on them, you mean that something bad happened to them and you are implying that it was not their fault. （非自身造成的）坏运气，噩运 [informal]

  • We had a bit of hard luck this season.

    我们本季运气不太好。

  • It was jolly hard luck on him, wasn't it?

    他可真倒霉，不是吗？

2 N-UNCOUNT （自身造成的）不幸;不走运 If someone says that a bad situation affecting you is just your hard luck, they do not care about it or think you should be helped, often because they think it is your fault. （自身造成的）不幸;不走运 [informal]

  • The shop assistants didn't really want to discuss the matter, saying it was just my hard luck.

    店员根本不想谈论此事，说这完全是我运气太差。

3 CONVENTION （表示同情）真不幸，真不走运 You can say 'hard luck' to someone to show that you are sorry they have not got or done something that they had wanted to get or do. （表示同情）真不幸，真不走运 [informal, feelings]

  • Hard luck, chaps, but don't despair too much.

    真不走运，伙计们，不过别太灰心。
