counterpoint
kaʊntəʳpɔɪnt ˈkaʊntərˌpɔɪnt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , plural counterpoints, present participle counterpointing, past tense , past participle counterpointed
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 有趣的对比 Something that is a counterpoint to something else contrasts with it in a satisfying way. 有趣的对比 [journalism]

  • Paris is just a short train journey away, providing the perfect counterpoint to the peace and quiet of Reims. [+ to]

    从这儿到巴黎坐火车一会儿就到，那里与兰斯的宁静平和相比却又是另一番景象。

2 VERB 与…形成有趣的对比 If one thing counterpoints another, it contrasts with it in a satisfying way. 与…形成有趣的对比 [journalism]

  • A good sharp dressing counterpointed the sweetness of the dried fruit. [VERB noun]

    味道浓烈的上好调味品和香甜的干果形成有趣的对比。

3 N-UNCOUNT (音乐的)对位法，对位 In music, counterpoint is a technique in which two or more different tunes are played together at the same time. (音乐的)对位法，对位

  • ...lessons in counterpoint and harmony.

    对位及和声课
