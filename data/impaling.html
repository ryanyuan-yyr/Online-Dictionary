impale
ɪmpeɪl ɪmˈpeɪl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense impales, present participle impaling, past tense , past participle impaled
VERB (以尖物)刺穿,刺入 To impale something on a pointed object means to cause the point to go into it or through it. (以尖物)刺穿,刺入 [Also VERB n]

  • Researchers observed one bird impale a rodent on a cactus. [VERB noun + on]

    研究人员观察到一只鸟把一只啮齿动物钉在一棵仙人掌上。

  • Lenny swayed for a moment, then dropped to the ground, impaling himself on his switchblade. [VERB noun on noun]

    伦尼晃了一下,然后倒在了地上,他的弹簧刀刺穿了自己的身体。

  • The boy died after being impaled on railings when he slipped while playing. [VERB noun on noun

    小男孩玩耍时失足滑倒,被栏杆刺穿身体致死。

SYN pierce, stick, run through, spike
