stretcher
stretʃəʳ ˈstrɛtʃər
Word forms: plural stretchers, past tense , past participle stretchered
1 N-COUNT 担架 A stretcher is a long piece of canvas with a pole along each side, which is used to carry an injured or sick person. 担架

  • The two ambulance attendants quickly put Plover on a stretcher and got him into the ambulance.

    两名救护人员迅速地将普洛弗放到担架上抬进救护车里。

2 V-PASSIVE 用担架抬 If someone is stretchered somewhere, they are carried there on a stretcher. 用担架抬

  • I was close by as Lester was stretchered into the ambulance. [be VERB-ed prep./adv.]

    莱斯特被用担架抬进救护车时我就在旁边。

  • The goalkeeper was stretchered off just before half-time with a rib injury. [be VERB-ed prep./adv.]

    就在中场休息前，该守门员由于肋骨受伤被用担架抬下场。
