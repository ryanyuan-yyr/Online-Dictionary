tranche
trɑːnʃ trɑnʃ
Word forms: plural tranches
1 N-COUNT (股票的)(一)份，(一)部分 In economics, a tranche of shares in a company, or a tranche of a company, is a number of shares in that company. (股票的)(一)份，(一)部分 [mainly British, business]

  • On February 12th he put up for sale a second tranche of 32 state-owned companies. [+ of]

    2 月 12 日他把第二批 32 家国有企业的股票抛向市场。

2 N-COUNT (一)片;(一)部分;(一)组 A tranche of something is a piece, section, or part of it. A tranche of things is a group of them. (一)片;(一)部分;(一)组 [formal]

  • They risk losing the next tranche of funding. [+ of]

    他们冒着失去下一笔资金的风险。

  • The next tranche of managers consists of assistant general managers and board directors.

    下一批经理人包括副总经理和董事会董事。
