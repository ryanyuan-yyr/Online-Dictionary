catalyse
kætəlaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense catalyses, present participle catalysing, past tense , past participle catalysed
in AM, use 美国英语用 catalyze
1 VERB 促成;激发;引发 If something catalyses a thing or a situation, it makes it active. 促成;激发;引发 [formal]

  • Any unexpected circumstance that arises may catalyze a sudden escalation of violence. [VERB noun]

    任何突如其来的情况都可能造成暴力骤然升级。

2 VERB (化学)催化 In chemistry, if something catalyses a reaction or event, it causes it to happen. (化学)催化 [technical]

  • It had been known for some time that chemical reactions can be catalyzed by materials with large surface areas. [be VERB-ed]

    人们早已知道，如果材料的表面积足够大，可引起催化反应。

  • The wires do not have a large enough surface to catalyse a big explosion. [VERB noun]

    这些导线覆盖的面积不够大，不足以引发大爆炸。
