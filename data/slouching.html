slouch
slaʊtʃ slaʊtʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense slouches, present participle slouching, past tense , past participle slouched
1 VERB 无精打采地坐着(或站着) If someone slouches, they sit or stand with their shoulders and head bent so they look lazy and unattractive. 无精打采地坐着(或站着)

  • Try not to slouch when you are sitting down. [VERB]

    坐的时候尽量不要无精打采的。

  • She has recently begun to slouch over her typewriter. [VERB prep./adv.]

    最近，她坐在打字机前就开始变得无精打采的。

SYN lounge, slump, flop, sprawl  
Slouch is also a noun.

  • He straightened himself from a slouch.

    他一改刚才的无精打采，挺直了腰杆。

slouched

  • The men were slouched on sofas and chairs.

    男人们无精打采地坐在沙发和椅子上。

  • She had been slouched against the counter.

    她无精打采地倚着柜台。

2 VERB 无精打采地走;懒洋洋地走 If someone slouches somewhere, they walk around slowly with their shoulders and head bent looking lazy or bored. 无精打采地走;懒洋洋地走

  • Most of the time, they slouch around in the fields. [VERB adv./prep.]

    他们绝大多数时间都无精打采地走在田间。

  • Scowling, the lad slouched over. [VERB adv./prep.]

    这个小家伙愁眉苦脸，一副垂头丧气的样子。

3 PHRASE [NOUN inflects, usually verb-link PHRASE] 擅长;愿意卖力工作;手脚勤快麻利 If you say that someone is no slouch at a particular activity, you mean that they are skilful at it or are willing to work hard at it. 擅长;愿意卖力工作;手脚勤快麻利

  • The Welsh are no slouches at cooking.

    威尔士人在烹饪方面可是好手。
