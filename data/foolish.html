foolish
fuːlɪʃ ˈfulɪʃ
1 ADJ-GRADED (举止或行为)愚蠢的，傻的，笨的 If someone's behaviour or action is foolish, it is not sensible and shows a lack of good judgment. (举止或行为)愚蠢的，傻的，笨的

  • It would be foolish to raise hopes unnecessarily.

    无谓地燃起人们的希望是愚蠢的。

  • It is foolish to risk skin cancer.

    冒患上皮肤癌的风险是愚蠢的。

SYN unwise, silly, absurd, rash  
foolishly

  • He admitted that he had acted foolishly.

    他承认自己的行为很愚蠢。

foolishness

  • They don't accept any foolishness when it comes to spending money.

    在花钱的问题上，他们向来很谨慎。

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 傻的;蠢的;显得难堪的 If you look or feel foolish, you look or feel so silly or ridiculous that people are likely to laugh at you. 傻的;蠢的;显得难堪的

  • I just stood there feeling foolish and watching him.

    我只是傻傻地站在那里看着他。

  • I didn't want him to look foolish and be laughed at.

    我不希望见到他出丑，被别人嘲笑。

foolishly

  • He saw me standing there, grinning foolishly at him.

    他看见我站在那里，傻乎乎地咧着嘴朝他笑。
