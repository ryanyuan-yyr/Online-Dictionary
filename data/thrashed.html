thrash
θræʃ θræʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense thrashes, present participle thrashing, past tense , past participle thrashed
1 VERB (在比赛、竞赛中)轻松击败，以大比分战胜 If one player or team thrashes another in a game or contest, they defeat them easily or by a large score. (在比赛、竞赛中)轻松击败，以大比分战胜 [informal] [Also VERB n]

  • Second-placed Rangers thrashed St Johnstone 5-nil. [V n amount]

    排名第二的流浪者队以 5: 0 大胜圣约翰斯通队。

2 VERB (作为惩罚)连续击打，接连殴打，痛打 If you thrash someone, you hit them several times as a punishment. (作为惩罚)连续击打，接连殴打，痛打

  • 'Liar!' Sarah screamed, as she thrashed the child. 'You stole it.' [VERB noun]

    “叫你撒谎！”萨拉一边揍着孩子，一边尖叫道，“就是你偷的。”

SYN beat, wallop, whip, hide [informal]  
3 V-ERG 猛烈摆动;翻来覆去;剧烈扭动(四肢) If someone thrashes about, or thrashes their arms or legs about, they move in a wild or violent way, often hitting against something. You can also say that someone's arms or legs thrash about . 猛烈摆动;翻来覆去;剧烈扭动(四肢)

  • Many of the crew died a terrible death as they thrashed about in shark-infested waters. [VERB adv./prep.]

    很多船员在鲨鱼成群的水域中拼命挣扎，死状惨不忍睹。

  • ...dreams so vivid that I thrash inside my sleeping bag and cry out. [VERB adv./prep.]

    梦境太逼真了，我在睡袋里翻来滚去，喊出了声

  • Jimmy collapsed on the floor, thrashing his legs about like an injured racehorse. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    吉米瘫倒在地板上，像匹受伤的赛马一样拼命踢蹬着双腿。

SYN thresh, flail, jerk, plunge  
4 VERB 连续击打;猛烈抽打 If a person or thing thrashes something, or thrashes at something, they hit it continually in a violent or noisy way. 连续击打;猛烈抽打

  • ...a magnificent paddle-steamer on the mighty Mississippi, her huge wheel thrashing the muddy water. [VERB noun]

    广阔的密西西比河上行驶着一艘豪华庞大的明轮船，她巨大的轮子拍打着浑浊的河水

  • Three shaggy-haired men thrash tunelessly at their guitars. [VERB + at]

    3 个头发蓬乱的男人没腔没调地乱弹着吉他。

Thrash is also a noun.

  • ...the thrash of the horses' hooves.

    马蹄的猛烈踢蹬

5 N-COUNT 派对;聚会 A thrash is a party. 派对;聚会 [British, informal]

  • Harry always invited Charlie when he threw a thrash in his office.

    哈里在办公室开派对时总不忘叫上查利。
