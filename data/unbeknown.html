unbeknown
ʌnbɪnoʊn ʌnbɪnoʊnst ˌʌnbɪˈnəʊnst ˌʌnbiˈnoʊn
The form unbeknownst /ˌʌnbɪ'nəʊnst/ is also used. 亦使用 unbeknownst.
PREP-PHRASE 在…不知情的情况下；瞒着… If something happens unbeknown to you or unbeknownst to you, you do not know about it. 在…不知情的情况下；瞒着…

  • I am appalled that children can mount up debts unbeknown to their parents. [+ to]

    让我深感震惊的是，孩子们竟会背着父母欠债累累。

  • Unbeknownst to her father, she began taking dancing lessons. [+ to]

    她瞒着父亲开始去上舞蹈课。
