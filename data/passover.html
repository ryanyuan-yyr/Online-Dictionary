pass over
1 （任命或晋升时）跳过，不考虑 If someone is passed over for a job or position, they do not get the job or position and someone younger or less experienced is chosen instead. （任命或晋升时）跳过，不考虑

  • She claimed she was repeatedly passed over for promotion. [be V-ed P + for]

    她声称在晋升时自己被一次又一次地排除在外。

  • They've been rejected, disappointed, ignored, passed over. [be VERB-ed PREP.]

    他们遭到拒绝，满心失望，备受冷落，无人理会。

2 （在谈话或演讲中）不提及，忽略，跳过 If you pass over a topic in a conversation or speech, you do not talk about it. （在谈话或演讲中）不提及，忽略，跳过

  • He largely passed over the government's record. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他基本没有提及政府政绩。

  • They seem to think her crimes should be passed over in silence. [be VERB-ed PREP.]

    他们似乎认为应该对她的罪行保持沉默。
