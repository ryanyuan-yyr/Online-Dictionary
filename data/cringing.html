cringe
krɪndʒ krɪndʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense cringes, present participle cringing, past tense , past participle cringed
VERB 为…觉得难为情;对…感到厌恶；退缩 If you cringe at something, you feel embarrassed or disgusted, and perhaps show this feeling in your expression or by making a slight movement. 为…觉得难为情;对…感到厌恶；退缩

  • Molly had cringed when Ann started picking up the guitar. [VERB]

    安开始弹吉他时，莫莉感到有些难堪。

  • Chris had cringed at the thought of using her own family for publicity. [VERB + at]

    一想到要拿自己的家庭作宣传，克丽丝就感到厌烦。

  • I cringed in horror. [VERB + in]

    我吓得往后缩。

SYN shrink, flinch, quail, recoil
