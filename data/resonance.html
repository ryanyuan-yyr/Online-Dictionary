resonance
rezənəns ˈrɛzənəns
Word forms: plural resonances
1 N-VAR 共鸣;特殊意义 If something has a resonance for someone, it has a special meaning or is particularly important to them. 共鸣;特殊意义

  • The ideas of order, security, family, religion and country had the same resonance for them as for Michael. [+ for]

    秩序、安全、家庭、宗教和国家这些概念对于他们和迈克尔来说有着同样特殊的意义。

2 N-UNCOUNT 嘹亮;洪亮 If a sound has resonance, it is deep, clear, and strong. 嘹亮;洪亮

  • His voice had lost its resonance; it was tense and strained.

    他的声音不再洪亮，而是显得紧张，不太自然。

3 N-VAR 共鸣;共振;谐振 A resonance is the sound which is produced by an object when it vibrates at the same rate as the sound waves from another object. 共鸣;共振;谐振 [technical]

  • The ear has a set of filaments to vibrate in resonance with incoming sound-waves.

    耳朵里有一组细丝能和进入的声波产生共振。
