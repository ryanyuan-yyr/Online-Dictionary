sir
sɜːʳ sɜr
Word forms: plural sirs
1 N-VOC (对不知其姓名的男士或上级的尊称)先生，长官 People sometimes say sir as a very formal and polite way of addressing a man whose name they do not know or a man of superior rank. For example, a shop assistant might address a male customer as sir . (对不知其姓名的男士或上级的尊称)先生，长官 [politeness]

  • Excuse me sir, but would you mind telling me what sort of car that is?.

    先生，请您告诉我那是什么类型的车好吗？

  • Good afternoon to you, sir.

    下午好，先生。

2 N-TITLE (用于爵士或准男爵的姓名前)爵士 Sir is the title used in front of the name of a knight or baronet. (用于爵士或准男爵的姓名前)爵士

  • She introduced me to Sir Tobias and Lady Clarke.

    她把我引见给了托拜厄斯爵士和克拉克夫人。

3 CONVENTION (正式信函或商务信函开头的称呼语)先生，阁下(写信给某组织时使用 Dear sirs) You use the expression Dear sir at the beginning of a formal letter or a business letter when you are writing to a man. You use Dear sirs when you are writing to an organization. (正式信函或商务信函开头的称呼语)先生，阁下(写信给某组织时使用 Dear sirs)

  • Dear Sir, Your letter of the 9th October has been referred to us.

    尊敬的先生，您 10 月 9 日的来信已转交我处。
