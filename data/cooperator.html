co-operate
(also cooperate)Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense co-operates, present participle co-operating, past tense , past participle co-operated
1 V-RECIP 合作;协作 If you co-operate with someone, you work with them or help them for a particular purpose. You can also say that two people co-operate . 合作;协作 [Also V (non-recip)]

  • The UN had been co-operating with the State Department on a plan to find countries willing to take the refugees. [VERB + with]

    联合国一直都在与美国国务院合作推进一项计划，寻找愿意接纳难民的国家。

  • The couple spoke about how they would co-operate in the raising of their child. [VERB]

    这对夫妇讲述了他们将如何共同抚养孩子。

  • The French and British are co-operating more closely than they have for years. [plural-noun VERB]

    法国和英国现在的合作比往年更加密切。

co-operation

  • A deal with Japan could indeed open the door to economic co-operation with East Asia.

    和日本的一宗交易可能真的能够开启同东亚的经济合作之门。

2 VERB 合作;配合 If you co-operate, you do what someone has asked or told you to do. 合作;配合

  • He agreed to co-operate with the police investigation. [VERB + with]

    他同意配合警方的调查。

  • The plan failed because the soldiers refused to co-operate. [VERB]

    因为士兵们不肯合作，这项计划宣告失败。

co-operation

  • The police underlined the importance of the public's co-operation in the hunt for the bombers.

    警方强调在搜捕爆炸制造者的过程中，公众的配合至关重要。
