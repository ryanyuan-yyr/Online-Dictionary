culminate
kʌlmɪneɪt ˈkʌlməˌneɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense culminates, present participle culminating, past tense , past participle culminated
VERB (以…)结束，告终 If you say that an activity, process, or series of events culminates in or with a particular event, you mean that event happens at the end of it. (以…)结束，告终

  • They had an argument, which culminated in Tom getting drunk. [V + in/with]

    他们争论了一番，最后汤姆喝醉了。

  • The celebration of the centenary will culminate with a dinner on November 20. [V in/with n]

    百年庆典活动将在11月20日的宴会中宣告结束。

SYN end up, end, close, finish
