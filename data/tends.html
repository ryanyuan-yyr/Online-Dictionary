tend
tend tɛnd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense tends, present participle tending, past tense , past participle tended
1 VERB 往往会；经常就 If something tends to happen, it usually happens or it often happens. 往往会；经常就

  • A problem for manufacturers is that lighter cars tend to be noisy. [VERB to-infinitive]

    制造商们遇到的一个问题是，重量较轻的汽车往往噪音大。

  • In older age groups women predominate because men tend to die younger. [VERB to-infinitive]

    在老年群体里女性占大多数，因为男性通常不易高寿。

  • They tend to buy cheap processed foods like canned chicken and macaroni. [VERB to-infinitive]

    他们往往会买便宜的加工食品，像鸡肉罐头和通心粉之类的。

SYN be inclined, be likely, be liable, have a tendency  
2 VERB 倾向于，容易表现出(某种特性) If you tend towards a particular characteristic, you often display that characteristic. 倾向于，容易表现出(某种特性) [Also V + to]

  • Artistic and intellectual people tend towards left-wing views. [V + towards]

    搞艺术的人和知识分子容易持“左倾”观点。

SYN favour, lean, be biased, gravitate  
3 VERB 个人认为;个人觉得 You can say that you tend to think something when you want to give your opinion, but do not want it to seem too forceful or definite. 个人认为;个人觉得 [vagueness]

  • I tend to think that members of parliament by and large do a good job. [VERB to-infinitive]

    我个人觉得，议会议员总体上工作还是不错的。

4 VERB 护理;照料;照看 If you tend someone or something, you do what is necessary to keep them in a good condition or to improve their condition. 护理;照料;照看 [formal]

  • For years he tended her in her painful illness. [VERB noun]

    多年来她一直受到病痛的折磨，都是他在照料。

  • He tends the flower beds and evergreens that he has planted in the driveway. [VERB noun]

    他打理着他栽在车道两旁的花坛和常青树。

SYN take care of, look after, care for, keep  
5 VERB 照顾;照管;伺候 If you tend to someone or something, you pay attention to them and deal with their problems and needs. 照顾;照管;伺候

  • In our culture, girls are brought up to tend to the needs of others. [VERB + to]

    在我们的文化里，女孩从小就学着照料别人。

  • She hurried away to pour more coffee and tend to the grill. [VERB to noun]

    她匆忙走开，去添加咖啡和照看烤架。
