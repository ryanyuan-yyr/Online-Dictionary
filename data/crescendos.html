crescendo
krɪʃendoʊ krɪˈʃɛnˌdoʊ
Word forms: plural crescendos
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 逐渐增强的喧闹声;噪声的最高点 A crescendo is a noise that gets louder and louder. Some people also use crescendo to refer to the point when a noise is at its loudest. 逐渐增强的喧闹声;噪声的最高点

  • She spoke in a crescendo: 'You are a bad girl! You are a wicked girl! You are evil!'.

    她嗓门越来越高地说道：“你是个坏女孩！你是个妖女！你是个魔鬼！”

  • The crescendo of noise was continuous.

    噪声越来越大。

  • The applause rose to a crescendo and cameras clicked.

    掌声雷动，照相机咔嚓咔嚓响个不停。

2 N-COUNT [usually singular] 最大强度;渐强;顶点 People sometimes describe an increase in the intensity of something, or its most intense point, as a crescendo . 最大强度;渐强;顶点 [journalism]

  • There was a crescendo of parliamentary and press criticism. [+ of]

    来自议会和新闻界的批评越来越多。

  • And now the story reaches a crescendo.

    现在故事进行到了高潮。

3 N-COUNT [usually singular] (音乐的)渐强部分，高潮 In music, a crescendo is a section of a piece of music in which the music gradually gets louder and louder. (音乐的)渐强部分，高潮
