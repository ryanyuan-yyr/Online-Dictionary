collaborate
kəlæbəreɪt kəˈlæbəˌreɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense collaborates, present participle collaborating, past tense , past participle collaborated
1 V-RECIP 合作，协作(尤指著书或做研究) When one person or group collaborates with another, they work together, especially on a book or on some research. 合作，协作(尤指著书或做研究) [Also pl-n V to-inf, V with n to-inf]

  • He collaborated with his son Michael on the English translation of a text on food production. [VERB + with]

    他和儿子迈克尔合作，把一篇介绍食品生产的文章译成了英文。

  • The government is urging Japan's firms to collaborate with foreigners. [VERB + with]

    政府正在督促日本的公司进行更多的海外合作。

  • ...a place where professionals and amateurs collaborated in the making of music. [V + on/in]

    一个专业人士和业余爱好者合作创作音乐的地方

  • The two men met and agreed to collaborate. [VERB]

    这两个人见了面，并同意进行合作。

2 VERB 通（敌）;勾结(敌人) If someone collaborates with an enemy that is occupying their country during a war, they help them. 通（敌）;勾结(敌人) [disapproval] [Also VERB]

  • He was accused of having collaborated with the secret police. [VERB + with]

    他被控与秘密警察勾结。

SYN conspire, cooperate, collude, fraternize
