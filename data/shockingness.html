shocking
ʃɒkɪŋ ˈʃɑkɪŋ
1 ADJ-GRADED 糟透了的;极坏的 You can say that something is shocking if you think that it is very bad. 糟透了的;极坏的 [informal]

  • The media coverage was shocking.

    当时媒体的报道糟透了。

  • I must have been in a shocking state last night.

    昨晚我的情况一定很糟糕。

SYN terrible, appalling, dreadful, bad  
shockingly

  • His memory was becoming shockingly bad.

    他的记忆力变得糟透了。

2 ADJ-GRADED 令人震惊的;令人愤慨的;不道德的 You can say that something is shocking if you think that it is morally wrong. 令人震惊的;令人愤慨的;不道德的

  • It is shocking that nothing was said.

    当时竟然什么都没说,真让人愤慨。

  • This was a shocking invasion of privacy.

    这是对隐私的公然侵犯。

SYN appalling, outrageous, disgraceful, offensive  
shockingly

  • Shockingly, this useless and dangerous surgery did not end until the 1930s.

    令人震惊的是，这种既无效又危险的手术直到 20 世纪 30 年代才被废止。
