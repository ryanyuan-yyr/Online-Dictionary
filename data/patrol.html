patrol
pətroʊl pəˈtroʊl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense patrols, present participle patrolling, past tense , past participle patrolled
1 VERB 巡逻；巡查 When soldiers, police, or guards patrol an area or building, they move around it in order to make sure that there is no trouble there. 巡逻；巡查

  • Prison officers continued to patrol the grounds within the jail. [VERB noun]

    狱警继续巡视监狱。

SYN police, guard, keep watch (on), pound  
Patrol is also a noun.

  • He failed to return from a patrol.

    他去巡逻，然后没再回来。

2 PHRASE 巡逻 Soldiers, police, or guards who are on patrol are patrolling an area. 巡逻

  • The army is now on patrol in Srinagar and a curfew has been imposed.

    军队目前在斯利那加巡逻，且已实行了宵禁。

  • Security forces remained on patrol until late into the night.

    保安部队继续巡逻至深夜。

SYN during a vigil, policing, watching, protecting  
3 N-COUNT 巡逻队；巡逻车队 A patrol is a group of soldiers or vehicles that are patrolling an area. 巡逻队；巡逻车队

  • Guerrillas attacked a patrol with hand grenades.

    游击队用手榴弹袭击了巡逻队。

  • ...a border patrol operating near the Burmese frontier.

    在缅甸边界巡逻的边境巡逻队

SYN guard, watch, garrison, watchman
