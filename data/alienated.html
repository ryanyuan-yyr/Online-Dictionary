alienate
eɪliəneɪt ˈeɪljəˌneɪt ˈeɪljənˌeɪt ˈeɪliənˌeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense alienates, present participle alienating, past tense , past participle alienated
1 VERB 使疏远;使不友好 If you alienate someone, you make them become unfriendly or unsympathetic towards you. 使疏远;使不友好

  • The government cannot afford to alienate either group. [VERB noun]

    疏远两个团体中的任何一方都是政府承受不起的。

SYN antagonize, anger, annoy, offend  
2 VERB （感情上、思想上）使疏远，离间 To alienate a person from someone or something that they are normally linked with means to cause them to be emotionally or intellectually separated from them. （感情上、思想上）使疏远，离间

  • His second wife, Alice, was determined to alienate him from his two boys. [VERB noun + from]

    他的第二任妻子艾丽斯，决意要疏远他和两个儿子的感情。

alienated

  • He felt alienated from his peers.

    他感觉和同僚疏远了。

alienation

  • ...the alienation of many from the political process.

    许多人与政治活动脱节

  • Her sense of alienation from the world disappeared.

    她与世界脱节的感觉消失了。
