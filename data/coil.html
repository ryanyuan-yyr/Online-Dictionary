coil
kɔɪl kɔɪl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense coils, present participle coiling, past tense , past participle coiled
1 N-COUNT (绳子、金属丝等绕成的)卷，圈 A coil of rope or wire is a length of it that has been wound into a series of loops. (绳子、金属丝等绕成的)卷，圈

  • Tod shook his head angrily and slung the coil of rope over his shoulder. [+ of]

    托德气愤地摇了摇头，然后把那卷绳子往肩上一搭。

  • The steel arrives at the factory in coils.

    钢材被成卷地运到工厂。

SYN loop, twist, curl, spiral  
2 N-COUNT 一卷;一圈 A coil is one loop in a series of loops. 一卷;一圈

  • Pythons kill by tightening their coils so that their victim cannot breathe.

    大蟒蛇紧紧盘绕猎物使之无法呼吸，从而杀死猎物。

3 N-COUNT (电路的)线圈 A coil is a thick spiral of wire through which an electrical current passes. (电路的)线圈
4 N-COUNT (汽车发动机的)盘管 In a vehicle, the coil is the part on a petrol engine that sends electricity to the spark plugs. (汽车发动机的)盘管
5 N-COUNT 子宫节育环;避孕环 The coil is a contraceptive device used by women. It is fitted inside a woman's womb, usually for several months or years. 子宫节育环;避孕环
6 V-ERG 卷;盘绕 If you coil something, you wind it into a series of loops or into the shape of a ring. If it coils around something, it forms loops or a ring. 卷;盘绕 [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • He turned off the water and began to coil the hose. [VERB noun]

    他关掉水龙头，开始盘水管。

  • Louisa was dancing, spinning by herself, her skirt flying out and coiling around her feet. [VERB prep./adv.]

    路易莎打着圈儿翩翩起舞，裙摆时而肆意飞扬，时而盘绕脚边。

  • A huge rattlesnake lay coiled on the blanket. [VERB-ed]

    一条巨大的响尾蛇盘绕在毯子上。

SYN wind, twist, curl, loop  
Coil up means the same as coil .coil up 同 coil

  • Once we have the wire, we can coil it up into the shape of a spring.

    一旦有了金属丝，我们就能把它卷成弹簧的形状。

  • Her hair was coiled up on top of her head.

    她的头发盘在头顶上。
