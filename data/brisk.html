brisk
brɪsk brɪsk
Word forms: comparative brisker, superlative briskest
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 轻快的；生气勃勃的 A brisk activity or action is done quickly and in an energetic way. 轻快的；生气勃勃的

  • Taking a brisk walk can often induce a feeling of well-being.

    快步行走常常会使人感到身心舒畅。

  • The horse broke into a brisk trot.

    马突然快步小跑起来。

SYN quick, lively, energetic, active  
briskly

  • Eve walked briskly down the corridor to her son's room.

    伊芙步履轻快地穿过走廊来到儿子的房间。

briskness

  • With determined briskness, Amy stood up and put their cups back on the tray.

    埃米干脆利落地站起身来，把他们的杯子放回到盘子里。

2 ADJ-GRADED （生意）兴隆的，红火的 If trade or business is brisk, things are being sold very quickly and a lot of money is being made. （生意）兴隆的，红火的 [business]

  • Vendors were doing a brisk trade in souvenirs.

    商贩们的纪念品生意很红火。

  • Its sales had been brisk since July.

    自7月以来销售一直很旺。

SYN busy, good, rewarding, lucrative  
briskly

  • A trader said gold sold briskly on the local market.

    一个商人说当地市场上黄金热销。

3 ADJ-GRADED （天气）寒冷而清新的 If the weather is brisk, it is cold and fresh. （天气）寒冷而清新的

  • ...a typically brisk winter's day on the South Coast.

    南部海岸一个典型的清冷冬日

  • The breeze was cool, brisk and invigorating.

    微风凉爽、清新，令人精神振奋。

SYN invigorating, fresh, biting, sharp  
4 ADJ-GRADED 爽快干脆的；精干利落的；麻利的 Someone who is brisk behaves in a busy, confident way which shows that they want to get things done quickly. 爽快干脆的；精干利落的；麻利的

  • The Chief summoned me downstairs. He was brisk and businesslike.

    主管叫我到楼下。他做事干练、务实。

  • She is noted for her brisk handling of business.

    她处理事务时的干脆利落是出了名的。

briskly

  • 'Anyhow,' she added briskly, 'it's none of my business.'

    “不管怎样”，她轻松地补充说，“这不关我的事。”

briskness

  • He felt her familiar briskness, and he knew that it was all over.

    他感觉到了她一贯的爽快作风，他明白一切都结束了。
