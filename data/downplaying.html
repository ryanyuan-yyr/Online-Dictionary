downplay
daʊnpleɪ ˈdaʊnˌpleɪ ˈdaʊnˌpleɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense downplays, present participle downplaying, past tense , past participle downplayed
VERB 对…轻描淡写;使轻视;贬低 If you downplay a fact or feature, you try to make people think that it is less important or serious than it really is. 对…轻描淡写;使轻视;贬低

  • The government is trying to downplay the violence. [VERB noun]

    政府试图对暴力行为低调处理。

  • ...to downplay the dangers of nuclear accidents. [VERB noun]

    对核事故的危险轻描淡写

SYN soft-pedal, play down
