shoe
ʃuː ʃu
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , plural shoes, present participle shoeing, past tense , past participle shoed, past tense shod
1 N-COUNT 鞋;鞋子 Shoes are objects which you wear on your feet. They cover most of your foot and you wear them over socks or stockings. 鞋;鞋子

  • ...a pair of shoes.

    一双鞋

  • Low-heeled comfortable shoes are best.

    舒服的低跟鞋子最好了。

  • You don't mind if I take my shoes off, do you?

    你不介意我把鞋子脱了吧？

2See also:snowshoe  training shoe 
3 N-COUNT 同 horseshoe A shoe is the same as a horseshoe . 同 horseshoe
4 VERB 给(马)钉蹄铁 When a blacksmith shoes a horse, they fix horseshoes onto its feet. 给(马)钉蹄铁

  • Blacksmiths spent most of their time repairing tools and shoeing horses. [VERB noun]

    铁匠大部分时间都在修理工具和钉马掌。

  • He helped his father by holding the horses steady while they were being shod. [VERB noun]

    他父亲给马钉掌时他就帮忙将马抓牢。

5 See also: shod 
6 PHRASE 接替…的位置(或工作) If you fill someone's shoes or step into their shoes, you take their place by doing the job they were doing. 接替…的位置(或工作)

  • No one has been able to fill his shoes.

    目前还没人能接替他的位置。

  • Now that Chris is gone she wants me to step into his shoes.

    既然克里斯走了，她就想让我接替他的位置。

7 PHRASE 处在…的境地;设身处地 If you talk about being in someone's shoes, you talk about what you would do or how you would feel if you were in their situation. 处在…的境地;设身处地

  • I wouldn't want to be in his shoes.

    我可不想处在他那样的处境。
