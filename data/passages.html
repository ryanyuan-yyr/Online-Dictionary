passage
pæsɪdʒ ˈpæsɑːʒ ˈpæsɪdʒ
Word forms: plural passages
1 N-COUNT 通道；过道；走廊 A passage is a long narrow space with walls or fences on both sides, which connects one place or room with another. 通道；过道；走廊

  • Harry stepped into the passage and closed the door behind him.

    哈里走进过道，随手关上了门。

  • ...up some stairs and along a narrow passage towards a door.

    上几级楼梯，沿着一条狭窄的走廊来到一个门口

2 N-COUNT （书、演讲、乐曲等的）段，节，章 A passage in a book, speech, or piece of music is a section of it that you are considering separately from the rest. （书、演讲、乐曲等的）段，节，章

  • He reads a passage from Milton. [+ from]

    他读了一节弥尔顿的诗。

  • ...the passage in which Blake spoke of the world of imagination.

    布莱克谈及幻想世界的那一段

  • Compare the following passages.

    比较下列片段。

3 N-COUNT （人体中的）道，通道 A passage is a long narrow hole or tube in your body, which air or liquid can pass along. （人体中的）道，通道

  • ...cells that line the air passages.

    气管内壁上的细胞

  • ...blocked nasal passages.

    堵塞了的鼻腔

SYN tube, opening, hole, aperture  
4 N-COUNT （人群等中的）通道，通路 A passage through a crowd of people or things is an empty space that allows you to move through them. （人群等中的）通道，通路

  • He cleared a passage for himself through the crammed streets. [+ through]

    他在拥挤的街道上挤出了一条通路。

  • Two men suddenly elbowed a passage through the shoppers.

    两名男子突然用肘在购物者中挤出一条路。

SYN way, route, path, course  
5 N-UNCOUNT [usually with poss] 通过；经过 The passage of someone or something is their movement from one place to another. 通过；经过

  • Germany had not requested Franco's consent for the passage of troops through Spain. [+ of]

    德国没有征求佛朗哥的同意便让军队通过了西班牙。

  • The passage of heat through rock is extremely slow.

    热在岩石中传导得极慢。

SYN movement, passing, advance, progress  
6 N-UNCOUNT [oft with poss] 过渡 The passage of someone or something is their progress from one situation or one stage in their development to another. 过渡

  • ...to ease their passage to a market economy.

    使他们更顺利地过渡到市场经济

  • ...the passage from school to college. [+ from/to]

    从中学到大学的过渡

SYN transition, change, move, development  
7 N-UNCOUNT [oft NOUN of n] （议案等的）通过 The passage of a bill or act is the official acceptance of it by a parliament or legislature. （议案等的）通过

  • It's been 200 years since the passage of the Bill of Rights.

    《人权法案》通过已经200年了。

SYN establishment, passing, legislation, sanction  
8 N-SING （时间的）推移，流逝 The passage of a period of time is its passing. （时间的）推移，流逝

  • An asset that increases in value with the passage of time. [+ of]

    价值与日俱增的资产

  • ...after the passage of eighteen months.

    18个月过去后

SYN passing, course, march, advance  
9 N-COUNT 航行；航程 A passage is a journey by ship. 航行；航程

  • We'd arrived the day before after a 10-hour passage from Swansea. [+ from]

    从斯旺西出发航行10小时后，我们于前天到达。

SYN journey, crossing, tour, trip  
10 N-UNCOUNT 通行权；通行许可 If you are granted passage through a country or area of land, you are given permission to go through it. 通行权；通行许可

  • Mr Thomas would be given safe passage to and from Jaffna. [+ to/from]

    托马斯先生可以安全地往来于贾夫纳。

  • You may have free passage across our territory whenever you require it.

    无论你什么时候需要，都可以自由穿过我们的领地。
