correspondence
kɒrɪspɒndəns ˌkɒrɪˈspɒndəns kɔːr- ˌkɔrəˈspɑndəns ˌkɑrəˈspɑndəns
Word forms: plural correspondences
1 N-UNCOUNT [oft a NOUN] 通信 Correspondence is the act of writing letters to someone. 通信

  • The judges' decision is final and no correspondence will be entered into.

    法官的裁决是最终裁决，再写信也不会予以审议。

  • His interest in writing came from a long correspondence with a close college friend. [+ with]

    与一位大学密友的长期通信培养了他写作的兴趣。

SYN communication, writing, contact  
2 N-UNCOUNT 往来书信 Some-one's correspondence is the letters that they receive or send. 往来书信

  • He always replied to his correspondence.

    来信他总是会回。

  • She virtually never mentions him in her correspondence or notebooks.

    她在书信和笔记中几乎从不提及他。

SYN letters, post, mail  
3 N-COUNT 相似;关联 If there is a correspondence between two things, there is a close similarity or connection between them. 相似;关联

  • In African languages there is a close correspondence between sounds and letters. [+ between]

    非洲语言中的发音与字母关系密切。

  • ...correspondences between Eastern religions and Christianity.

    东方宗教与基督教的关联

SYN relation, match, agreement, fitness
