2 PHRASAL VERB-ERG （使）（地方、经济、兴趣领域等）开放 If a place, economy, or area of interest opens up, or if someone opens it up, more people can go there or become involved in it. （使）（地方、经济、兴趣领域等）开放 [Also V n P, V n P to n]

  • As the market opens up, I think people are going to be able to spend more money on consumer goods. [VERB PREP.]

    随着市场的放开，我认为人们将能够增加用于消费品的支出。

  • He said he wanted to see how Albania was opening up to the world. [VERB PREP. + to]

    他说他想看看阿尔巴尼亚是如何对外开放的。

  • These programmes will open up markets for farmers. [VERB PREP. noun]

    这些计划将为农民打开市场。

  • The money could open up music to more children. [V P n to n]

    这笔款项能让更多的孩子受到音乐的熏陶。

3 PHRASAL VERB-ERG （使）（机会、可能性等）出现；（使）产生 If something opens up opportunities or possibilities, or if they open up, they are created. （使）（机会、可能性等）出现；（使）产生 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • It was also felt that the collapse of the system opened up new possibilities. [VERB PREP. noun]

    人们也认为这一制度的崩溃带来了各种新的可能性。

  • New opportunities are opening up for investors who want a more direct stake in overseas companies. [VERB PREP.]

    希望更加直接地参股海外公司的投资者获得了新的机会。

4 PHRASAL VERB （通常指大幅度地在比赛中）取得（领先地位） If you open up a lead in a race or competition, you get yourself into a position where you are leading, usually by quite a long way. （通常指大幅度地在比赛中）取得（领先地位）

  • The Chinese team had opened up a lead of more than two minutes. [VERB PREP. noun]

    中国队取得了两分多钟的领先优势。

5 PHRASAL VERB 开…的门 When you open up a building, you unlock and open the door so that people can get in. 开…的门

  • Three armed men were waiting when the postmaster and his wife arrived to open up the shop. [VERB PREP. noun]

    当邮政局长和妻子到邮局开门营业时，3名持械男子正等着他们。

  • Open up, or I break in! [VERB PREP.]

    把门打开，不然我要砸门了！

6 PHRASAL VERB 畅谈；无拘无束地谈；自由自在地谈 If someone opens up, they start to say exactly what they think or feel. 畅谈；无拘无束地谈；自由自在地谈

  • Lorna found that people were willing to open up to her. [VERB PREP. + to]

    洛娜发现人们愿意对她畅所欲言。
