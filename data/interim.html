interim
ɪntərɪm ˈɪntərɪm
1 ADJ [ADJ n] 暂时的;临时的;过渡期间的 Interim is used to describe something that is intended to be used until something permanent is done or established. 暂时的;临时的;过渡期间的

  • She was sworn in as head of an interim government in March.

    她3月份宣誓就任过渡政府首脑。

  • ...an interim report.

    临时报告

SYN temporary, provisional, makeshift, acting  
2 PHRASE 在过渡期间;在间歇期 In the interim means until a particular thing happens or until a particular thing happened. 在过渡期间;在间歇期 [formal]

  • But, in the interim, we obviously have a duty to maintain law and order.

    但是,在过渡期间,我们显然有责任维护治安。

  • He was to remain in jail in the interim.

    他在此期间将会继续被关押。
