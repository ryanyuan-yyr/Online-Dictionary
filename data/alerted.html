alert
əlɜːʳt əˈlɜrt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense alerts, present participle alerting, past tense , past participle alerted
1 ADJ-GRADED 警觉的；警惕的；留神的；机敏的 If you are alert, you are paying full attention to things around you and are able to deal with anything that might happen. 警觉的；警惕的；留神的；机敏的

  • We all have to stay alert.

    我们大家都必须保持警惕。

  • He had been spotted by an alert neighbour.

    一个警觉的邻居发现了他。

SYN attentive, careful, awake, wary  
alertness

  • The drug improved mental alertness.

    该药有助于提神。

2 ADJ-GRADED 充分意识到…的;完全感觉到…的 If you are alert to something, you are fully aware of it. 充分意识到…的;完全感觉到…的

  • The bank is alert to the danger. [+ to]

    银行充分意识到了危险。

3 N-COUNT 警戒（状态）；戒备（状态） An alert is a situation in which people prepare themselves for something dangerous that might happen soon. 警戒（状态）；戒备（状态）

  • Due to a security alert, this train will not be stopping at Oxford Circus.

    由于安全戒备的原因，该次列车在“牛津街口广场”站将不停留。

SYN warning, signal, alarm, siren  
4 VERB 向…报警；使警觉；使警惕；使注意 If you alert someone to a situation, especially a dangerous or unpleasant situation, you tell them about it. 向…报警；使警觉；使警惕；使注意

  • He wanted to alert people to the activities of the group. [VERB noun + to]

    他想提醒人们小心该团体的活动。

  • I was hoping he'd alert the police. [VERB noun]

    我当时希望他会报警。

SYN warn, signal, inform, alarm  
5 See also: red alert 
6 PHRASE 处于警戒状态;保持戒备 If soldiers or police are on alert, they are ready to deal with anything that may happen. 处于警戒状态;保持戒备

  • Soldiers and police have been put on alert.

    士兵和警方都已进入警戒状态。

7 PHRASE 准备就绪；防备着 If you are on the alert for something, you are ready to deal with it if it happens. 准备就绪；防备着

  • They want to be on the alert for similar buying opportunities.

    他们希望随时掌握有关类似购买机会的信息。
