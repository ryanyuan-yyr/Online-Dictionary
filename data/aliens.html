alien
eɪliən ˈeɪljən ˈeɪljən ˈeɪliən
Word forms: plural aliens
1 ADJ [usually ADJ n] 外国的;异族的 Alien means belonging to a different country, race, or group, usually one you do not like or are frightened of. 外国的;异族的 [formal, disapproval]

  • He said they were opposed to the presence of alien forces in the region.

    他说他们反对外国军队驻扎在该地区。

SYN foreign, outside, strange, imported  
2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 怪异的；可怕的；异己的 You use alien to describe something that seems strange and perhaps frightening, because it is not part of your normal experience. 怪异的；可怕的；异己的

  • His work offers an insight into an alien culture.

    他的作品能让人们对一种异域文化产生较深入的了解。

SYN strange, new, foreign, novel  
3 ADJ-GRADED 陌生的；格格不入的；不相容的 If something is alien to you or to your normal feelings or behaviour, it is not the way you would normally feel or behave. 陌生的；格格不入的；不相容的 [formal]

  • Such an attitude is alien to most businessmen. [+ to]

    大多数商界人士都会对这样的态度感到陌生。

4 N-COUNT 外国人 An alien is someone who is not a legal citizen of the country in which they live. 外国人 [formal, law]

  • Both women had hired illegal aliens for child care.

    两个女人都曾雇过非法入境者照看孩子。

  • When war broke out, he was interned as an enemy alien.

    战争爆发时，他作为敌国人士遭到关押。

SYN foreigner, incomer, immigrant, stranger  
5 N-COUNT 外星人 In science fiction, an alien is a creature from outer space. 外星人
