lecture
lektʃəʳ ˈlɛktʃər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense lectures, present participle lecturing, past tense , past participle lectured
1 N-COUNT （通常指大学或学院里的）演讲，讲座，讲课 A lecture is a talk someone gives in order to teach people about a particular subject, usually at a university or college. （通常指大学或学院里的）演讲，讲座，讲课

  • ...a series of lectures by Professor Eric Robinson. [+ by]

    埃里克·鲁宾逊教授的系列讲座

  • In his lecture Riemann covered an enormous variety of topics.

    里曼的演讲涵盖了各种各样的主题。

SYN talk, address, speech, lesson  
2 VERB （就…）作演讲（或讲座） If you lecture on a particular subject, you give a lecture or a series of lectures about it. （就…）作演讲（或讲座）

  • She then invited him to Atlanta to lecture on the history of art. [V + on/in]

    她于是邀请他去亚特兰大讲艺术史。

  • She has danced, choreographed, lectured and taught all over the world. [VERB]

    她到过世界各地跳舞、编舞、演讲和教学。

  • Wendy Rigby was recently invited to lecture a group of doctors on the benefits of aromatherapy. [VERB noun on noun]

    温迪·里格比最近应邀给一帮医生讲芳香疗法的疗效。

SYN talk, speak, teach, address  
3 VERB 训诫；教训 If someone lectures you about something, they criticize you or tell you how they think you should behave. 训诫；教训 [Also VERB n to-infinitive]

  • He used to lecture me about getting too much sun. [V n + about/on]

    他以前常唠叨我太阳晒得太多。

  • Chuck would lecture me, telling me to get a haircut. [VERB noun]

    查克就会数落我，让我去理一下发。

  • She was no longer interrogating but lecturing. [VERB]

    她已经不是在审问而是在教训人了。

SYN tell off [informal] , berate, scold, reprimand  
Lecture is also a noun.

  • Our captain gave us a stern lecture on safety.

    船长就安全问题严厉地训斥了我们一顿。
