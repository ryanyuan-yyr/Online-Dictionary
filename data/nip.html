nip
nɪp nɪp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense nips, present participle nipping, past tense , past participle nipped
1 VERB [no passive] 迅速前往;急忙赶往 If you nip somewhere, usually somewhere nearby, you go there quickly or for a short time. 迅速前往;急忙赶往 [British, informal]

  • Should I nip out and get some groceries?. [VERB adv./prep.]

    要我赶紧出去一趟买些食品吗？

  • Wayne is always nipping down to the corner shop for him. [VERB adv./prep.]

    韦恩总是一溜烟跑到街角商店找他。

  • Beasley told me he'd seen you, so I nipped straight home. [VERB adv./prep.]

    比斯利告诉我他看到你了，所以我就赶快直接回家了。

SYN pop, go, run, rush  
2 VERB 轻咬;轻掐;轻捏 If an animal or person nips you, they bite you lightly or squeeze a piece of your skin between their finger and thumb. 轻咬;轻掐;轻捏 [Also V at n, V]

  • I have known cases where dogs have nipped babies. [VERB noun]

    我知道有一些狗咬婴儿的事情。

  • He nipped Billy's cheek with two rough fingers. [VERB noun]

    他用两个粗糙的手指捏了捏比利的脸蛋。

SYN pinch, catch, grip, squeeze  
Nip is also a noun.

  • Some ants can give you a nasty nip.

    一些蚂蚁会咬得你很疼。

3 N-COUNT (烈性酒的)一小口，一呷，少量 A nip is a small amount of a strong alcoholic drink. (烈性酒的)一小口，一呷，少量

  • She had a habit of taking an occasional nip from a flask of cognac. [+ from]

    她有偶尔喝一小口干邑的习惯。

SYN dram, shot [informal] , drop, taste  
4 to nip something in the bud→see: bud
