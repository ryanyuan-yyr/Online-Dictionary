squall
skwɔːl skwɔl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense squalls, present participle squalling, past tense , past participle squalled
1 N-COUNT 飑;突起的大风(常引起短暂暴雨或暴雪) A squall is a sudden strong wind which often causes a brief, violent rain storm or snow storm. 飑;突起的大风(常引起短暂暴雨或暴雪)

  • The boat was hit by a squall north of the island.

    船在岛的北边遇到了狂飑。

SYN storm, gale, flurry, blow  
2 VERB 嚎叫;大声啼哭 If a person or animal squalls, they make a loud unpleasant noise like the noise made by a crying baby. 嚎叫;大声啼哭

  • There was an infant squalling in the back of the church. [VERB]

    有个婴儿在教堂后部大声啼哭。

  • ...squalling guitars. [VERB-ing]

    刺耳的吉他声

SYN wail, cry, howl, bawl
