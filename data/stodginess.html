stodgy
stɒdʒi ˈstɑdʒi
Word forms: comparative stodgier, superlative stodgiest
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (食物)硬实的，难消化的，使人胀饱的 Stodgy food is very solid and heavy. It makes you feel very full, and is difficult to digest. (食物)硬实的，难消化的，使人胀饱的

  • He was disgusted with the stodgy pizzas on sale in London.

    他讨厌吃伦敦卖的难以下咽的比萨饼。

SYN heavy, filling, substantial, leaden  
2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 古板的;令人厌烦的;枯燥乏味的 If you describe someone or something as stodgy, you dislike them or are bored by them because they are very old-fashioned or serious. 古板的;令人厌烦的;枯燥乏味的 [disapproval]

  • They're not cultured or interesting, they are boring stodgy old things.

    他们没修养，很乏味，是一群无聊古板的老家伙。
