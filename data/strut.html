strut
strʌt strʌt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense struts, present participle strutting, past tense , past participle strutted
1 VERB 趾高气扬地走;昂首阔步 Someone who struts walks in a proud way, with their head held high and their chest out, as if they are very important. 趾高气扬地走;昂首阔步 [disapproval]

  • He struts around town like he owns the place. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他在城里大摇大摆地晃荡，好像那是他的地盘。

If you strut your stuff, you act in a proud way and show off.炫耀;卖弄

  • He got up to strut his stuff on the dance-floor.

    他起身到舞池里炫耀自己的舞技。

2 N-COUNT 撑杆;支杆;支柱;支撑 A strut is a piece of wood or metal which holds the weight of other pieces in a building or other structure. 撑杆;支杆;支柱;支撑

  • ...the struts of a suspension bridge.

    吊桥的支柱
