kick off
1 PHRASAL VERB （在足球比赛中）开球 In football, when the players kick off, they start a game by kicking the ball from the centre of the pitch. （在足球比赛中）开球

  • Liverpool kicked off an hour ago. [VERB PREP.]

    利物浦队一小时前开的球。

2 PHRASAL VERB-ERG (事件、比赛或讨论等)开始 If an event, game, series, or discussion kicks off, or is kicked off, it begins. (事件、比赛或讨论等)开始 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • The shows kick off on October 24th. [VERB PREP.]

    演出10月24日开始。

  • The Mayor kicked off the party. [VERB PREP. noun]

    市长宣布晚会开始。

  • We kicked off with a slap-up dinner. [VERB PREP. + with]

    我们先是享用了一顿考究的晚餐。

SYN begin, start, open, commence  
3 PHRASAL VERB 踢掉（鞋） If you kick off your shoes, you shake your feet so that your shoes come off. 踢掉（鞋） [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • She stretched out on the sofa and kicked off her shoes. [VERB PREP. noun]

    她四肢平摊躺在沙发上，踢掉了鞋子。

4 PHRASAL VERB 强使离开；赶走 To kick someone off an area of land means to force them to leave it. 强使离开；赶走 [informal] [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • We can't kick them off the island. [VERB noun PREP. noun]

    我们不能把他们从岛上赶走。

SYN dismiss from, ban from, exclude from, bar from  
kick-off
ˈkɪkˌɒf
Word forms: plural kick-offs
in AM, use 美国英语用 kickoff
1 N-VAR （足球赛的）开赛 In football, the kick-off is the time at which a particular game starts. （足球赛的）开赛 [British]

  • The kick-off is at 1.30.

    开赛时间是1点30分。

2 N-COUNT （橄榄球赛的）开球 In American football, a kickoff is the kick that begins a play, for example at the beginning of a half or after a touchdown or field goal. （橄榄球赛的）开球 [US]
3 N-SING （事件或活动的）开始，开幕 The kick-off of an event or activity is its beginning. （事件或活动的）开始，开幕 [informal]

  • People stood waiting for the kick-off of the parade. [+ of]

    人们站着等候游行的开始。

SYN start, opening, beginning, commencement
