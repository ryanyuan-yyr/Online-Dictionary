start off
1 PHRASAL VERB 从…开始;从…着手 If you start off by doing something, you do it as the first part of an activity. 从…开始;从…着手

  • She started off by accusing him of blackmail but he more or less ignored her. [V P + by]

    她先是指控他勒索，但他几乎不理睬她。

  • Joe Loss started off playing piano background music for silent films in the 1920s. [VERB PREP. verb-ing]

    乔·洛斯先是于 20 世纪 20 年代为无声电影弹奏钢琴背景音乐。

2 PHRASAL VERB 使(某人)开始(做某事) To start someone off means to cause them to begin doing something. 使(某人)开始(做某事)

  • Her mother started her off acting in children's theatre. [VERB noun PREP.]

    她母亲先是让她在儿童剧院里演出。

SYN set off, start, cause, produce  
3 PHRASAL VERB 使开始 To start something off means to cause it to begin. 使开始

  • He became more aware of the things that started that tension off. [VERB noun PREP.]

    他越来越清楚地认识到引起紧张局势的原因。

  • Best results are obtained by starting the plants off in a warm greenhouse. [VERB noun PREP.]

    要想取得最理想的结果，一开始就应该让植株在温暖的温室里生长。

SYN set off, start, cause, produce
