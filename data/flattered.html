flattered
flætəʳd ˈflætəd
1 ADJ-GRADED [verb-link ADJ, ADJ that/to-inf] (因受重视而)感到满意的，觉得荣幸的 If you are flattered by something that has happened, you are pleased about it because it makes you feel important or special. (因受重视而)感到满意的，觉得荣幸的

  • She was flattered by Roberto's long letter. [+ by]

    罗伯托的一封长信让她很是高兴。

  • I am flattered that they should be so supportive.

    他们这么帮忙，我深感荣幸。

  • She didn't know whether to feel flattered or indignant.

    她不知道应该感到高兴还是愤怒。

SYN pleased, delighted, honoured, gratified  
2 ADJ-GRADED [verb-link ADJ, oft ADJ that/to-inf] (在正式场合表示感谢)不胜荣幸的，深感荣幸的 People sometimes use flattered when they are expressing thanks in formal situations. (在正式场合表示感谢)不胜荣幸的，深感荣幸的

  • We're flattered and honoured to receive this Doris Day Award.

    能获得多丽丝·戴奖，我们深感荣幸和光荣。
