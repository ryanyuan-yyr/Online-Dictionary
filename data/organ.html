organ
ɔːʳgən ˈɔrgən
Word forms: plural organs
1 N-COUNT 器官 An organ is a part of your body that has a particular purpose or function, for example your heart or lungs. 器官

  • ...damage to the muscles and internal organs.

    对肌肉与内脏的损伤

  • ...the reproductive organs.

    生殖器官

  • ...organ transplants.

    器官移植

SYN body part, part of the body, member, element  
2See also:sense organ 
3 N-COUNT 风琴；管风琴 An organ is a large musical instrument with pipes of different lengths through which air is forced. It has keys and pedals rather like a piano. 风琴；管风琴
4See also:barrel organ  mouth organ 
5 N-COUNT （政府、集团等的）喉舌，机构 You refer to a newspaper or organization as the organ of the government or another group when it is used by them as a means of giving information or getting things done. （政府、集团等的）喉舌，机构

  • The Security Service is an important organ of the State. [+ of]

    军情五处是国家的重要机构。

  • The most powerful organ of government in Scotland is the Scottish Office.

    苏格兰职权最大的政府部门是苏格兰事务部。
