bribe
braɪb braɪb
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense bribes, present participle bribing, past tense , past participle bribed
1 N-COUNT 贿赂 A bribe is a sum of money or something valuable that one person offers or gives to another in order to persuade him or her to do something. 贿赂

  • He was being investigated for receiving bribes.

    他因收受贿赂正在接受调查。

SYN inducement, incentive, pay-off [informal] , graft [informal]  
2 VERB 向…行贿 If one person bribes another, they give them a bribe. 向…行贿

  • He was accused of bribing a senior bank official. [VERB noun]

    他被指控贿赂一名高级银行官员。

  • The government bribed the workers to be quiet. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    政府收买工人让其保持沉默。

SYN buy off, reward, pay off [informal] , lure
