erode
ɪroʊd iˈroʊd ɪˈroʊd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense erodes, present participle eroding, past tense , past participle eroded
1 V-ERG （使）侵蚀;(使)腐蚀;(使)风化 If rock or soil erodes or is eroded by the weather, sea, or wind, it cracks and breaks so that it is gradually destroyed. （使）侵蚀;(使)腐蚀;(使)风化 [Also VERB n]

  • By 1980, Miami beach had all but totally eroded. [VERB]

    到1980年，迈阿密海滩已经完全风蚀了。

  • Once exposed, soil is quickly eroded by wind and rain. [be VERB-ed]

    一旦暴露在外，土壤很快就会被风雨侵蚀。

SYN destroy, consume, spoil, crumble  
eroded

  • ...the deeply eroded landscape.

    被严重侵蚀的地形

2 V-ERG （权威、权利、信心等）逐渐丧失；削弱 If someone's authority, right, or confidence erodes or is eroded, it is gradually destroyed or removed. （权威、权利、信心等）逐渐丧失；削弱 [formal]

  • His critics say his fumbling of the issue of reform has eroded his authority. [VERB noun]

    批评家们说他在改革问题上的蹩脚做法已经削弱了他的权威。

  • America's belief in its own God-ordained uniqueness started to erode. [VERB]

    美国人心中上帝赋予了他们独一无二的品质的信念开始逐渐丧失。

SYN weaken, destroy, undermine, diminish  
3 V-ERG （使）贬值;（使）降价 If the value of something erodes or is eroded by something such as inflation or age, its value decreases. （使）贬值;（使）降价

  • Competition in the financial marketplace has eroded profits. [VERB noun]

    金融市场的竞争降低了利润。

  • The value of the dollar began to erode rapidly just around this time. [VERB]

    刚好在这个时候，美元开始迅速贬值。
