disgorge
dɪsgɔːʳdʒ dɪsˈgɔrdʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense disgorges, present participle disgorging, past tense , past participle disgorged
1 VERB 排出;倾倒;喷吐 If something disgorges its contents, it empties them out. 排出;倾倒;喷吐 [written]

  • The ground had opened to disgorge a boiling stream of molten lava. [VERB noun]

    大地开裂，汩汩冒出炽热的熔岩。

SYN emit, discharge, send out, expel  
2 VERB (车、船、飞机等)下（客）；(从建筑物中)涌出(人流) If you say that a vehicle or building disgorges people, especially a lot of people, you mean that the people leave the vehicle or building. (车、船、飞机等)下（客）；(从建筑物中)涌出(人流) [written]

  • The bus drew up in the village square and disgorged its passengers. [VERB noun]

    公共汽车驶达乡村广场后停车下客。

3 VERB (动物)吐出 If an animal disgorges something it has swallowed, it produces it again from its mouth. (动物)吐出

  • They pursue other birds, forcing them to disgorge the fish they have caught. [VERB noun]

    它们追赶别的鸟儿，强迫它们吐出抓到的鱼。
