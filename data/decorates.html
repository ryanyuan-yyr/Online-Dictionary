decorate
dekəreɪt ˈdɛkəˌreɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense decorates, present participle decorating, past tense , past participle decorated
1 VERB 装饰;装点;点缀 If you decorate something, you make it more attractive by adding things to it. 装饰;装点;点缀

  • He decorated his room with pictures of all his favorite sports figures. [VERB noun + with]

    他用自己最喜欢的几位体育明星的照片装饰房间。

  • Use shells to decorate boxes, trays, mirrors or even pots. [VERB noun]

    用贝壳来装点盒子、托盘、镜子甚至是花盆。

2 VERB 粉刷;油漆;裱糊 If you decorate a room or the inside of a building, you put new paint or wallpaper on the walls and ceiling, and paint the woodwork. 粉刷;油漆;裱糊

  • When they came to decorate the rear bedroom, it was Jemma who had the final say. [VERB noun]

    他们装修后面的卧室时，是耶马最后说了算。

  • The boys are planning to decorate when they get the time. [VERB]

    小伙子们正打算有时间油漆一下。

  • I had the flat decorated quickly so that Philippa could move in. [have noun VERB-ed]

    我很快地把公寓粉刷了一遍，这样菲利帕就能搬进来了。

  • ...a small, badly decorated office. [VERB-ed]

    装修简陋的小办公室

SYN do up, paper, paint, wallpaper  
decorating

  • I did a lot of the decorating myself.

    大部分油漆活儿都是我自己做的。

decoration

  • The renovation process and decoration took four months.

    翻新和粉刷总共耗时 4 个月。

3 VERB 美化 If something decorates a place or an object, it makes it look more attractive. 美化 [written]

  • Constable posters decorate the walls. [VERB noun]

    康斯太布尔风景画的海报装点着那些墙面。

4 VERB [usually passive] 授予…勋章(或其他荣誉) If someone is decorated, they are given a medal or other honour as an official reward for something that they have done. 授予…勋章(或其他荣誉)

  • He was decorated for bravery in battle. [be VERB-ed]

    他因为作战英勇而被授予奖章。
