interview
ɪntəʳvjuː ˈɪntərˌvju
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense interviews, present participle interviewing, past tense , past participle interviewed
1 N-VAR 面试;面谈 An interview is a formal meeting at which someone is asked questions in order to find out if they are suitable for a job or a course of study. 面试;面谈

  • When I went for my first interview for this job I arrived extremely early.

    在参加这份工作的初次面试时,我到得非常早。

  • The interview went well.

    面试进行得非常顺利。

  • Not everyone who writes in can be invited for interview.

    并非每一个写来求职信的人都会被邀请参加面试。

SYN meeting, examination, evaluation, oral (examination)  
2 VERB [usually passive] 对…进行面试 If you are interviewed for a particular job or course of study, someone asks you questions about yourself to find out if you suitable for it. 对…进行面试

  • When Wardell was interviewed, he was impressive, and on that basis, he was hired. [be VERB-ed]

    沃德尔参加面试的时候给人留下了深刻印象,所以基于这点,他被雇用了。

  • He was among the three candidates interviewed for the job. [VERB-ed]

    他是3个来参加求职面试的人选之一。

SYN examine, talk to, sound out  
3 N-COUNT 采访;访谈 An interview is a conversation in which a journalist puts questions to someone such as a famous person or politician. 采访;访谈

  • Allan gave an interview to the Chicago Tribune newspaper last month.

    艾伦上个月接受了《芝加哥论坛报》的采访。

  • There'll be an interview with Mr Brown after the news.

    新闻过后播出的是对布朗先生的访谈。

SYN audience, talk, conference, exchange  
4 VERB 采访;访谈 When a journalist interviews someone such as a famous person, they ask them a series of questions. 采访;访谈

  • I seized the chance to interview Chris Hani about this issue. [VERB noun]

    我抓住机会就这一问题采访了克里斯·哈尼。

5 VERB 讯问;审讯 When the police interview someone, they ask them questions about a crime that has been committed. 讯问;审讯

  • The police interviewed the driver, but had no evidence to go on. [VERB noun]

    警方讯问了司机,但是没有获得什么有用的证据。

SYN question, interrogate, examine, investigate
