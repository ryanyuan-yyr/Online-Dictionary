malign
məlaɪn məˈlaɪn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense maligns, present participle maligning, past tense , past participle maligned
1 VERB 诽谤;中伤;污蔑 If you malign someone, you say unpleasant and untrue things about them. 诽谤;中伤;污蔑 [formal]

  • We maligned him dreadfully when you come to think of it. [VERB noun]

    回头想想，我们狠狠地中伤了他。

  • Either the managers have been maligned or they are not telling the truth. [VERB noun]

    要么是经理们受到了污蔑，要么就是他们在撒谎。

SYN disparage, abuse, run down, libel  
2 ADJ-GRADED [ADJ n] 有害的;不良的 If something is malign, it causes harm. 有害的;不良的 [formal]

  • ...the malign influence jealousy had on their lives.

    妒忌给他们的生活造成的恶劣影响

  • Reliance on sponsorship can have a malign effect on theatre groups.

    对赞助的依赖会给剧团带来不良的影响。

SYN evil, bad, destructive, harmful  
3 See also: much-maligned
