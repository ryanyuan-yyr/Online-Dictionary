acquiesce
ækwies ˌækwiˈɛs
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense acquiesces, present participle acquiescing, past tense , past participle acquiesced
VERB 同意；默认；默许；顺从 If you acquiesce in something, you agree to do what someone wants or to accept what they do. 同意；默认；默许；顺从 [formal]

  • Steve seemed to acquiesce in the decision. [V + in/to]

    史蒂夫好像默认了这个决定。

  • He has gradually acquiesced to the demands of the opposition. [V in/to n]

    他渐渐顺从了反对派的要求。

  • When her mother suggested that she stay, Alice willingly acquiesced. [VERB]

    当母亲建议她留下时，艾丽斯欣然允之。

SYN submit, allow, agree, accept
