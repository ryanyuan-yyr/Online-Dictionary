allocation
æləkeɪʃən ˌæləˈkeɪʃən ˌæloʊˈkeɪʃən ˌ æləˈkeɪʃən
Word forms: plural allocations
1 N-COUNT 分配物；分配额；（尤指）划拨款项 An allocation is an amount of something, especially money, that is given to a particular person or used for a particular purpose. 分配物；分配额；（尤指）划拨款项

  • The aid allocation for Pakistan was still under review. [+ for]

    拨给巴基斯坦的援助物资仍在审核当中。

  • During rationing we had a sugar allocation.

    定量配给时期，糖是限量配给的。

SYN allowance, share, measure, grant  
2 N-UNCOUNT 分配；分派 The allocation of something is the decision that it should be given to a particular person or used for a particular purpose. 分配；分派

  • His sons quarrelled bitterly over the allocation of family resources.

    他的儿子们为家产如何分配而吵得很凶。

  • Town planning and land allocation had to be coordinated.

    必须协调城市规划和土地分配之间的关系。

SYN assignment, allowance, rationing, allotment
