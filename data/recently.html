recently
riːsəntli
1 ADV-GRADED [ADVERB with verb] 近来；最近 If you have done something recently or if something happened recently, it happened only a short time ago. 近来；最近

  • The bank recently opened a branch in Germany.

    这家银行最近在德国开设了分行。

  • It is only fairly recently that historians have begun to investigate the question.

    只是到最近历史学家才开始探究这个问题。

  • He was until very recently the most powerful banker in the city.

    到最近为止，他一直都是这个城市最有实力的银行家。

SYN not long ago, newly, lately, currently  
+−Usage
Usagerecently
Recently and lately can both be used to express that something began in the past and continues into the present: Recently/Lately I've been considering going back to school to get a master's degree. Recently, but not lately, is also used to describe a completed action: I recently graduated from university.
