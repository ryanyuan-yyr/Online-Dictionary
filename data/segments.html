segment
segmənt segment ˈsɛgmənt ˈsɛgmɛnt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense segments, present participle segmenting, past tense , past participle segmented
The noun is pronounced /'segmənt/. The verb is pronounced /seg'ment/. 名词读作 /'segmənt/。动词读作 /seg'ment/。
1 N-COUNT 部分;片段 A segment of something is one part of it, considered separately from the rest. 部分;片段

  • ...the poorer segments of society. [+ of]

    社会中的较贫穷阶层

  • ...the third segment of his journey. [+ of]

    他此行的第三程

SYN section, part, piece, division  
2 N-COUNT (橘子、葡萄柚等水果的)瓣 A segment of fruit such as an orange or grapefruit is one of the sections into which it is easily divided. (橘子、葡萄柚等水果的)瓣
3 N-COUNT 圆缺;弓形 A segment of a circle is one of the two parts into which it is divided when you draw a straight line through it. 圆缺;弓形
4 N-COUNT (市场)细分的部分 A segment of a market is one part of it, considered separately from the rest. (市场)细分的部分

  • Three-to-five day cruises are the fastest-growing segment of the market. [+ of]

    3 至 5 天的乘船游览是该市场中发展最快的部分。

  • Women's tennis is the market leader in a growing market segment — women's sports.

    女子网球正引领着一个逐渐壮大的市场—女子运动市场。

SYN section, part, piece, division  
5 VERB 细分，划分(市场) If a company segments a market, it divides it into separate parts, usually in order to improve marketing opportunities. 细分，划分(市场) [business]

  • The big six record companies are multinational, and thus can segment the world market into national ones. [VERB noun + into]

    六大唱片公司都是跨国经营，因此能够将世界市场按国家划分。

SYN divide, split, break up, split up
