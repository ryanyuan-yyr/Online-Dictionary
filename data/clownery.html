clown
klaʊn klaʊn
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense clowns, present participle clowning, past tense , past participle clowned
1 N-COUNT (马戏团的)小丑 A clown is a performer in a circus who wears funny clothes and bright make-up, and does silly things in order to make people laugh. (马戏团的)小丑
2 VERB 扮小丑，装傻，作蠢相（逗人发笑） If you clown, you do silly things in order to make people laugh. 扮小丑，装傻，作蠢相（逗人发笑）

  • Bruno clowned and won affection everywhere. [VERB]

    布鲁诺很会耍活宝，到哪儿都受到大家的喜爱。

Clown around and clown about mean the same as clown .clown around 和 clown about 同 clown

  • Bev made her laugh, the way she was always clowning around.

    贝夫用她一贯滑稽耍宝的方式逗她发笑。

clowning

  • She senses that behind the clowning there is a terrible sense of anguish.

    她感觉到在这种滑稽可笑行为的背后隐藏着极大的痛苦。

3 N-COUNT 爱开玩笑的人;诙谐的人;活宝 If you say that someone is a clown, you mean that they say funny things or do silly things to amuse people. 爱开玩笑的人;诙谐的人;活宝

  • He was laughing, the clown of the twosome.

    他在笑，二人搭档中逗人乐的那个。

4 N-COUNT 傻瓜;蠢货 If you describe someone as a clown, you disapprove of them and have no respect for them. 傻瓜;蠢货 [informal, disapproval]

  • I still think I could do a better job than those clowns in Washington.

    我还是认为自己会比华盛顿的那些蠢货干得好。

SYN fool, dope [informal] , jerk [slang , mainly US , Canadian] , idiot
