disparage
dɪspærɪdʒ dɪˈspærɪdʒ dɪˈspærɪdʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense disparages, present participle disparaging, past tense , past participle disparaged
VERB 贬低;轻视 If you disparage someone or something, you speak about them in a way which shows that you do not have a good opinion of them. 贬低;轻视 [formal]

  • ...Larkin's tendency to disparage literature. [VERB noun]

    拉金贬低文学的倾向

  • The tax cut is widely disparaged by senators from both parties as a budget gimmick. [VERB noun]

    两个党派的参议员们普遍对作为预算噱头的税收削减嗤之以鼻。

SYN run down, dismiss, put down, criticize
