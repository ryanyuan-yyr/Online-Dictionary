parcel
pɑːʳsəl ˈpɑːsəl ˈpɑrsəl
Word forms: plural , plural , 3rd person singular present tense parcels, present participle parcelling, past tense , past participle parcelled
in AM, use 美国英语用 parceling, parceled
1 N-COUNT 包裹；邮包 A parcel is something wrapped in paper, usually so that it can be sent to someone by post. 包裹；邮包 [mainly British]

  • ...parcels of food and clothing. [+ of]

    装着食品和衣物的包裹

  • He had a large brown paper parcel under his left arm.

    他左臂下夹着一个大牛皮纸邮包。

SYN package, case, box, pack  
in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 package
2 N-COUNT 地块 A parcel of land is a piece of land. 地块

  • These small parcels of land were purchased for the most part by local people. [+ of]

    这些小块土地大多数是当地人买下的。

SYN plot, area, property, section  
3 N-COUNT [NOUN of n] 一批；一群 A parcel of things or people is a quantity of them. 一批；一群 [mainly British]

  • The woman who became his wife would be acquiring not just a husband but a run-down house and a parcel of financial worries.

    女人嫁给他,得到的不仅是一个丈夫，还会有一栋破烂的房子和一大堆经济问题。

4 PHRASE …的内容；…的必要部分 If you say that something is part and parcel of something else, you are emphasizing that it is involved or included in it. …的内容；…的必要部分 [emphasis]

  • Payment was part and parcel of carrying on insurance business within the UK.

    赔付是在英国开展保险业务必不可少的一部分。

  • It's all part and parcel — just a day's work really you know.

    这只是一部分—才一天的工作，你要知道。

Phrasal verbs:
parcel out
