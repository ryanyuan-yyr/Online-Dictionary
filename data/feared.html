fear
fɪəʳ fɪr
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense fears, present participle fearing, past tense , past participle feared
1 N-VAR 恐惧;惧怕 Fear is the unpleasant feeling you have when you think that you are in danger. 恐惧;惧怕

  • I was sitting on the floor shivering with fear.

    我坐在地板上，吓得发抖。

  • ...boyhood memories of sickness and fear of the dark. [+ of]

    儿时记忆中的病痛和对黑暗的恐惧

  • London Zoo is running hypnosis programmes to help people overcome their fear of spiders.

    伦敦动物园正在搞催眠活动，帮助人们克服对蜘蛛的恐惧。

2 VERB 害怕;惧怕 If you fear someone or something, you are frightened because you think that they will harm you. 害怕;惧怕

  • Many people fear change because they do not like the old ways to be disrupted. [VERB noun]

    很多人惧怕变化，因为他们不喜欢旧有的生活方式遭到破坏。

3 N-VAR [NOUN that] 担心;担忧;忧心 A fear is a thought that something unpleasant might happen or might have happened. 担心;担忧;忧心

  • These youngsters are motivated not by a desire to achieve, but by fear of failure. [+ of]

    激发这些年轻人的不是对成就的渴望，而是对失败的担心。

  • Then one day his worst fears were confirmed.

    后来有一天，他最担心的事终于发生了。

  • His fears might be groundless.

    他的担心也许毫无根据。

  • ...the fear that once a war began it would soon pass beyond the ability of either side to manage it.

    担心一旦开战，双方很快都将无法驾驭战势

SYN anxiety, concern, worry, doubt  
4 VERB 担心;担忧;忧虑 If you fear something unpleasant or undesirable, you are worried that it might happen or might have happened. 担心;担忧;忧虑

  • She had feared she was going down with pneumonia or bronchitis. [VERB that]

    她担心会得肺炎或支气管炎。

  • More than two million refugees have fled the area, fearing attack by loyalist forces. [VERB noun]

    两百多万难民担心受到效忠旧政府的势力的攻击而逃离该地区。

SYN worry, suspect, anticipate, be afraid  
5 N-VAR [oft NOUN that] (坏事发生的)可能性，机会，几率 If you say that there is a fear that something unpleasant or undesirable will happen, you mean that you think it is possible or likely. (坏事发生的)可能性，机会，几率

  • There was no fear that anything would be misunderstood.

    并无引起误会的可能性。

  • There is a fear that the freeze on bank accounts could prove a lasting deterrent to investors.

    人们担心冻结银行账户可能会长期阻碍投资。

6 VERB (替…)担心;(为…)担忧 If you fear for someone or something, you are very worried because you think that they might be in danger. (替…)担心;(为…)担忧

  • Carla fears for her son. [VERB + for]

    卡拉为儿子担心。

  • He fled on Friday, saying he feared for his life. [VERB + for]

    他星期五逃走了，说是担心自己有生命危险。

7 N-VAR 担心;担忧 If you have fears for someone or something, you are very worried because you think that they might be in danger. 担心;担忧

  • He also spoke of his fears for the future of his country's culture. [+ for]

    他也谈到了对祖国文化前途的担忧。

  • ...fear for her own safety.

    对她自己安全的担心

8 VERB 害怕做;不愿做 If you fear to do something, you are afraid to do it or you do not wish to do it. 害怕做;不愿做

  • She pursed her lips together, as though fearing to betray her news. [VERB to-infinitive]

    她闭紧双唇，仿佛害怕透露出她的消息。

  • Old people fear to leave their homes. [VERB to-infinitive]

    老年人都不愿背井离乡。

9 VERB 恐怕 You say that you fear that a situation is the case when the situation is unpleasant or undesirable, and when you want to express sympathy, sorrow, or regret about it. 恐怕 [formal]

  • I fear that a land war now looks very probable. [VERB that]

    恐怕陆地战爆发的可能性很大。

  • 'Is anything left at all?' — 'I fear not.' [V so/not]

    “留下什么东西了吗?” —“恐怕没有。”

SYN regret, feel, suspect, have a feeling  
10 PHRASE 在对…的担心之中;在对…的恐惧之中 If you are in fear of doing or experiencing something unpleasant or undesirable, you are very worried that you might have to do it or experience it. 在对…的担心之中;在对…的恐惧之中

  • The elderly live in fear of assault and murder.

    老人们生活在对袭击和谋杀的恐惧之中。

11 PHRASE 以免;免得 If you take a particular course of action for fear of something, you take the action in order to prevent that thing happening. 以免;免得

  • She was afraid to say anything to them for fear of hurting their feelings.

    她什么都不敢对他们说，以免伤害他们的感情。

  • No one dared shoot for fear of hitting Pete.

    没人敢开枪，怕伤着皮特。

12 PHRASE [usually PHRASE with cl] 别担心;不用怕 You say 'fear not' or 'never fear' to someone when you are telling them not to worry or be frightened. 别担心;不用怕 [old-fashioned]

  • Fear not, Darlene will protect me.

    别担心，达琳会保护我的。

  • You'll get the right training, never fear.

    你会受到正规训练的，别担心。

13 CONVENTION 绝对不;当然不 You use 'no fear' to emphasize that you do not want to do something. 绝对不;当然不 [British, informal, emphasis]

  • When I asked him if he wanted to change his mind, William said 'No fear.'

    我问威廉是否想改主意，他说：“绝不会。”

14 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 故意吓唬;故意使…担心 If someone or something puts the fear of God into you, they frighten or worry you, often deliberately. 故意吓唬;故意使…担心

  • At some time or other Eve had obviously put the fear of God into her.

    显然伊芙时不时有意吓唬她一下。
