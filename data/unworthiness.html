unworthy
ʌnwɜːʳði ʌnˈwɜrði
1 ADJ-GRADED [ADJ to-infinitive] 不值得的；配不上的 If a person or thing is unworthy of something good, they do not deserve it. 不值得的；配不上的 [literary]

  • You may feel unworthy of the attention and help people offer you.

    你可能会觉得自己不值得别人关心和帮助。

  • He felt unworthy of being married to such an attractive woman. [+ of]

    他觉得自己不配娶这么迷人的女人。

SYN undeserving, not good enough, not fit, not worth  
2 ADJ-GRADED 不相称的；与（某人）身份不相符的 If you say that an action is unworthy of someone, you mean that it is not a nice thing to do and someone with their reputation or position should not do it. 不相称的；与（某人）身份不相符的 [literary]

  • Miss Melville could not resist asking, although she knew it was unworthy of her.

    梅尔维尔小姐忍不住问起来，虽然她知道自己不该问。

  • His accusations are unworthy of a prime minister. [+ of]

    他的指责与其首相身份不相称。

SYN unbefitting, beneath, unfitting to, unsuitable for
