inferior
ɪnfɪəriəʳ ɪnˈfɪriər
Word forms: plural inferiors
1 ADJ-GRADED (质量等)差的,次的 Something that is inferior is not as good as something else. (质量等)差的,次的

  • The cassettes were of inferior quality.

    那些磁带质量较差。

  • This resulted in overpriced and often inferior products.

    这就导致了标价过高而往往质量低劣的产品的出现。

  • If children were made to feel inferior to other children their confidence declined. [+ to]

    如果让孩子感觉他们不如其他孩子,他们的信心就会减弱。

SYN substandard, bad, poor, mean  
2 ADJ-GRADED (地位、能力等)低等的,低于…的;下级的 If one person is regarded as inferior to another, they are regarded as less important because they have less status or ability. (地位、能力等)低等的,低于…的;下级的

  • He preferred the company of those who were intellectually inferior to himself. [+ to]

    他喜欢与那些智力水平不如他的人共处。

  • ...the inferior status of women in pre-revolutionary Russia.

    革命之前俄国妇女的低微地位

  • Most career women make me feel inferior.

    大部分职业女性让我感到自己不如她们。

SYN lower, junior, minor, secondary  
Inferior is also a noun.

  • A gentleman should always be civil, even to his inferiors.

    绅士应该总是彬彬有礼,即便是对地位不及自己者。
