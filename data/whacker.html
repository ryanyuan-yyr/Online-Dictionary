whack
hwæk hwæk wæk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense whacks, present participle whacking, past tense , past participle whacked
1 VERB 重打;重击 If you whack someone or something, you hit them hard. 重打;重击 [informal]

  • You really have to whack the ball. [VERB noun]

    你真的需要猛击球才行。

  • Someone whacked him on the head. [VERB noun prep.]

    有人狠狠地打了他的头。

SYN strike, hit, beat, box  
Whack is also a noun.

  • He gave the donkey a whack across the back with his stick.

    他拿棍子朝驴背上狠狠打了一下。

  • He took his ax and struck the trunk of the tree. Whack. Whack. Whack.

    他举起斧子砍树干，咔，咔，咔。

2 N-SING [oft poss NOUN] 份额；一份 Your whack of something is your share of it. 份额；一份 [British, informal]

  • The majority of people in this country pay their whack.

    该国大部分人尽了他们的一份力。

  • We need to win a fair whack of the contracts. [+ of]

    我们需要赢得这些合同中的相当大的份额。

3 VERB (轻松地)打败，击败 If someone whacks you, they defeat you easily in a game or competition. (轻松地)打败，击败 [British, informal] [Also VERB n]

  • We got whacked. On paper we should have wiped the floor with them. [get VERB-ed]

    我们被轻易地击败了。按理说我们本应该大败他们。

4 VERB 谋杀 To whack someone means to kill them. 谋杀 [US, informal] [Also VERB n]

  • His mother was murdered. Yeah, she got whacked. Snuffed out. [get VERB-ed]

    他母亲是被谋杀的。是的，是被人干掉的，弄死的。

5 PHRASE [PHRASE after verb, oft verb-link PHRASE] 不正常的;紊乱的；出故障的 If something is out of whack, it is not working properly, often because its natural balance has been upset. 不正常的;紊乱的；出故障的 [mainly US, informal]

  • The ecosystem will be thrown out of whack.

    生态系统将会失去平衡。
