guide
gaɪd gaɪd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense guides, present participle guiding, past tense , past participle guided
1 N-COUNT; N-IN-NAMES 指南；手册 A guide is a book that gives you information or instructions to help you do or understand something. 指南；手册

  • Our 10-page guide will help you to change your life for the better.

    我们这本10页的指南有助于您提高生活品质。

  • ...the Pocket Guide to Butterflies of Britain and Europe. [+ to]

    《英国和欧洲大陆蝴蝶袖珍指南》

SYN handbook, manual, guidebook, instructions  
2 N-COUNT; N-IN-NAMES 旅行指南 A guide is a book that gives tourists information about a town, area, or country. 旅行指南

  • The Rough Guide to Paris lists accommodation for as little as £25 a night. [+ to]

    《巴黎旅行概览手册》列出了价格低至每晚25英镑的住宿信息。

SYN directory, street map  
3 N-COUNT 导游 A guide is someone who shows tourists around places such as museums or cities. 导游

  • We've arranged a walking tour of the city with your guide.

    我们已安排大家在导游的带领下步行游览这座城市。

4 VERB 给…当导游；带…参观 If you guide someone around a city, museum, or building, you show it to them and explain points of interest. 给…当导游；带…参观

  • ...a young Egyptologist who guided us through tombs and temples with enthusiasm. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    热心陪同我们参观陵墓和庙宇的年轻埃及古物学家

  • There will be guided walks around the site. [VERB-ed]

    将有导游陪同步行观光该遗址。

5See also:guided tour 
6 N-COUNT 向导 A guide is someone who shows people the way to a place in a difficult or dangerous region. 向导

  • The mountain people say that, with guides, the journey can be done in fourteen days.

    山民们说，有人带路的话这段行程可以在14天内完成。

SYN escort, leader, controller, attendant  
7 N-COUNT [usually singular] 指导原则；准则 A guide is something that can be used to help you plan your actions or to form an opinion about something. 指导原则；准则

  • As a rough guide, a horse needs 2.5 per cent of his body weight in food every day.

    马每天需要的食物相当于其体重的2.5%，这是一个大致的标准。

  • When selecting fresh fish, let your taste buds be your guide.

    挑选鲜鱼时，要靠味觉才行。

8 VERB 给…带路；为…引路 If you guide someone somewhere, you go there with them in order to show them the way. 给…带路；为…引路

  • He took the bewildered Elliott by the arm and guided him out. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    他抓着不知所措的埃利奥特的胳膊，把他带了出来。

  • Khandoo guided me through the dark alleys until the smell told me we had arrived. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    康多带着我在漆黑的小巷中穿行，直到闻到那种味道，我知道我们到了。

SYN lead, direct, escort, conduct  
9 VERB 驾驶（交通工具） If you guide a vehicle somewhere, you control it carefully to make sure that it goes in the right direction. 驾驶（交通工具）

  • Captain Shelton guided his plane down the runway and took off. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    谢尔顿机长驾驶飞机沿跑道滑行起飞。

SYN steer, control, manage, direct  
10 VERB 给…指明方向 If something guides you somewhere, it gives you the information you need in order to go in the right direction. 给…指明方向

  • They sailed across the Baltic and North Seas with only a compass to guide them. [VERB noun]

    他们仅凭一个罗盘的指引驶过了波罗的海和北海。

11 VERB 指导；影响 If something or someone guides you, they influence your actions or decisions. 指导；影响

  • He should have let his instinct guide him. [VERB noun]

    他本该相信他的直觉。

  • Development has been guided by a concern for the ecology of the area. [VERB noun]

    该地区的发展以注重生态为指导原则。

  • My mother, whose guiding principle in life was doing right, had a far greater influence on me. [VERB-ing]

    我母亲的生活原则是做事堂堂正正，她对我有着更为深刻的影响。

SYN supervise, train, rule, teach  
12 VERB 指导；指引 If you guide someone through something that is difficult to understand or to achieve, you help them to understand it or to achieve success in it. 指导；指引

  • ...a free helpline to guide businessmen through the maze of EU grants.

    帮助商业人士处理纷繁复杂的欧盟拨款事宜的免费热线电话

  • The 42-year-old Scot has guided the team to victory in three of their last five games. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    这位42岁的苏格兰人带领球队在最近5场比赛中赢了3场。
