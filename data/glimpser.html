glimpse
glɪmps glɪmps
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense glimpses, present participle glimpsing, past tense , past participle glimpsed
1 N-COUNT 一瞥；扫视 If you get a glimpse of someone or something, you see them very briefly and not very well. 一瞥；扫视

  • Some of the fans had waited 24 hours outside the Hyde Park Hotel to catch a glimpse of their heroine. [+ of]

    为了一睹女主角的风采，一些追星族已经在海德公园饭店外守候了24个小时。

SYN look, sighting, sight, glance  
2 VERB 瞥见 If you glimpse someone or something, you see them very briefly and not very well. 瞥见

  • She glimpsed a group of people standing on the bank of a river. [VERB noun]

    她瞥见一群人站在河岸边。

SYN catch sight of, spot, sight, view  
3 N-COUNT 短暂的感受；初步的认识 A glimpse of something is a brief experience of it or an idea about it that helps you understand or appreciate it better. 短暂的感受；初步的认识

  • As university campuses become increasingly multi-ethnic, they offer a glimpse of the conflicts society will face tomorrow. [+ of]

    大学校园变得日益多种族化，通过校园可以对未来社会将要面对的诸多冲突有一个初步的认识。

  • ...a glimpse into the future.

    对前途的大致展望
