juvenile
dʒuːvənaɪl ˈdʒuːvɪˌnaɪl ˈdʒuvəˌnaɪl ˈdʒuvənəl
Word forms: plural juveniles
1 N-COUNT 少年 A juvenile is a child or young person who is not yet old enough to be regarded as an adult. 少年 [formal]

  • The number of juveniles in the general population has fallen by a fifth in the past 10 years.

    总人口中少年所占比例在过去10年中下降了1/5。

SYN child, youth, minor, girl  
2 ADJ [ADJ n] 少年的；未成年的 Juvenile activity or behaviour involves young people who are not yet adults. 少年的；未成年的

  • Juvenile crime is increasing at a terrifying rate.

    青少年犯罪正在以惊人的速度增长。

  • ...a scheme to lock up persistent juvenile offenders.

    将青少年惯犯关入监狱的计划

SYN young, junior, adolescent, youthful  
3 ADJ-GRADED （行为）幼稚的，不成熟的，孩子气的 If you describe someone's behaviour as juvenile, you are critical of it because you think that it is silly or childish. （行为）幼稚的，不成熟的，孩子气的 [disapproval]

  • He's a typical male, as he gets older he becomes more juvenile.

    他是个典型的男人，年龄越大越孩子气。

SYN immature, childish, infantile, puerile  
4 N-COUNT 幼仔；幼兽;雏鸟 Young animals are sometimes referred to as juveniles . 幼仔；幼兽;雏鸟
