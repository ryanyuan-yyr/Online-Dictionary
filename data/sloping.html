slope
sloʊp sloʊp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense slopes, present participle sloping, past tense , past participle sloped
1 N-COUNT 山坡 A slope is the side of a mountain, hill, or valley. 山坡

  • Saint-Christo is perched on a mountain slope.

    圣克里斯托教堂坐落在一个山坡上。

  • ...the lower slopes of the Himalayas. [+ of]

    喜马拉雅山脉较低处的山坡

2 N-COUNT [usually singular] 斜坡 A slope is a surface that is at an angle, so that one end is higher than the other. 斜坡

  • The street must have been on a slope.

    这条街一定是建在一个斜坡上。

SYN slant, incline, drop away, fall  
3 VERB (表面)倾斜 If a surface slopes, it is at an angle, so that one end is higher than the other. (表面)倾斜

  • The bank sloped down sharply to the river. [VERB adv./prep.]

    河岸到河面的斜坡非常陡。

  • The garden sloped quite steeply. [VERB]

    花园的斜坡颇为陡峭。

SYN slant, incline, drop away, fall  
sloping

  • ...a brick building, with a sloping roof.

    有着斜屋顶的砖房

  • ...the gently sloping beach.

    坡度很缓的海滩

4 VERB 歪向一边;倾斜 If something slopes, it leans to the right or to the left rather than being upright. 歪向一边;倾斜

  • The writing sloped backwards. [VERB adv./prep.]

    笔迹朝右倾斜。

  • He wonders why the digits on his calculator slope to the right. [VERB adv./prep.]

    他不明白为什么他的计算器上面的数字向右倾斜。

5 N-COUNT [usually singular] 斜度;坡度 The slope of something is the angle at which it slopes. 斜度;坡度

  • The slope increases as you go up the curve.

    上了弯道以后，路越来越陡。

  • ...a slope of ten degrees. [+ of]

    一段坡度为 10° 的斜坡

6 VERB 潜入/潜出 If someone slopes into or out of a place, they enter or leave it quickly and quietly, especially because they are trying to avoid or escape something. 潜入/潜出 [informal]

  • She sloped off quietly on Saturday afternoon. [VERB adv./prep.]

    星期六下午，她脚底抹油，溜之大吉。

  • They sloped into their hotel at 6am. [VERB adv./prep.]

    凌晨 6 点，他们悄悄地潜入他们下榻的饭店。
