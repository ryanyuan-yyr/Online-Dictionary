ferocious
fəroʊʃəs fəˈroʊʃəs
1 ADJ-GRADED 凶猛的;凶暴的 A ferocious animal, person, or action is very fierce and violent. 凶猛的;凶暴的

  • ...a ferocious guard-dog.

    凶恶的看门狗

  • The police had had to deal with some of the most ferocious violence ever seen on the streets of London.

    警方不得不去处理伦敦街头发生的一些极其严重的暴力事件。

ferociously

  • She kicked out ferociously.

    她狠命地踢着。

2 ADJ-GRADED (战争、争论等冲突)激烈的，猛烈的，酷烈的 A ferocious war, argument, or other form of conflict involves a great deal of anger, bitterness, and determination. (战争、争论等冲突)激烈的，猛烈的，酷烈的

  • Fighting has been ferocious.

    战斗十分激烈。

  • A ferocious battle to select a new parliamentary candidate is in progress.

    推选一位新的议会候选人的激战正酣。

SYN cruel, bitter, brutal, vicious  
ferociously

  • These days he is ferociously competitive.

    这些天来他极其争强好胜。

3 ADJ-GRADED (行动或感情)强烈的，决然的 If you describe actions or feelings as ferocious, you mean that they are intense and determined. (行动或感情)强烈的，决然的

  • Lindbergh was startled at the ferocious depth of anti-British feeling.

    反英情绪之深之烈令林德伯格大为震惊。

ferociously

  • He set himself ferociously tough standards; and he was ardently devoted to ballet.

    他给自己制定了极为苛刻的标准，满腔热忱地献身于芭蕾事业。
