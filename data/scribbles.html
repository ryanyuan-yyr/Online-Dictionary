scribble
skrɪbəl ˈskrɪbəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense scribbles, present participle scribbling, past tense , past participle scribbled
1 VERB 潦草地写;匆匆地写 If you scribble something, you write it quickly and roughly. 潦草地写;匆匆地写

  • She scribbled a note to tell Mum she'd gone out. [VERB noun]

    她匆匆写了个便条告诉妈妈她已外出。

  • As I scribbled in my diary the light went out. [VERB prep./adv.]

    我正草草地写着日记，灯灭了。

SYN scrawl, write, jot, pen  
2 VERB 乱涂;乱画 To scribble means to make meaningless marks or rough drawings using a pencil or pen. 乱涂;乱画 [Also VERB]

  • When Caroline was five she scribbled on a wall. [VERB prep./adv.]

    卡罗琳 5 岁时在墙上乱涂乱画。

SYN draw, write, scrawl, doodle  
3 N-VAR 潦草的字迹;涂鸦 Scribble is something that has been written or drawn quickly and roughly. 潦草的字迹;涂鸦

  • I'm sorry what I wrote was such a scribble.

    很抱歉我写得如此潦草。

SYN mess, state, jumble, confusion  
Phrasal verbs:
scribble down
