rhapsodize
ræpsədaɪz ˈræpsəˌdaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense rhapsodizes, present participle rhapsodizing, past tense , past participle rhapsodized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 rhapsodise
VERB 热烈地赞美;热情称颂 If you rhapsodize about someone or something, you express great delight or enthusiasm about them. 热烈地赞美;热情称颂 [formal]

  • The critics rhapsodized over her performance in 'Autumn Sonata'. [V + over/about]

    评论家对于她在电影《秋日奏鸣曲》中的表演大加赞赏。

  • 'Orchards would take the place of the jungle,' he rhapsodized. [VERB with quote]

    “要让这片丛林成为果园，”他无比兴奋地说。

SYN enthuse, rave [informal] , gush, drool
