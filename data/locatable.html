locate
loʊkeɪt loʊkeɪt ˈloʊˌkeɪt loʊˈkeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense locates, present participle locating, past tense , past participle located
1 VERB 找到；探明；确定…的位置 If you locate something or someone, you find out where they are. 找到；探明；确定…的位置 [formal]

  • The scientists want to locate the position of the gene on a chromosome. [VERB noun]

    科学家们希望找出该基因在染色体中的位置。

  • We've simply been unable to locate him. [VERB noun]

    我们就是没办法找到他。

SYN find, discover, detect, come across  
2 VERB 放置；设立；建立 If you locate something in a particular place, you put it there or build it there. 放置；设立；建立 [formal]

  • Atlanta was voted the best city in which to locate a business by more than 400 chief executives. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    逾400名行政总裁投票推选亚特兰大为最适宜开办公司的城市。

  • Tudor Court represents your opportunity to locate at the heart of the new Birmingham. [VERB prep./adv.]

    都铎宛，您在新伯明翰中心区置业的好机会。

SYN place, put, set, position  
3 VERB 迁居；开办公司 If you locate in a particular place, you move there or open a business there. 迁居；开办公司 [mainly US, business]

  • ...tax breaks for businesses that locate in run-down neighbor-hoods. [VERB]

    针对开办在老旧街区的商户的减税措施
