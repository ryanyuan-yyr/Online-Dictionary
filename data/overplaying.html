overplay
oʊvəʳpleɪ ˌoʊvərˈpleɪ ˈoʊvərˌpleɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense overplays, present participle overplaying, past tense , past participle overplayed
1 VERB 夸大…的重要性；过分强调 If you say that someone is overplaying something such as a problem, you mean that they are making it seem more important than it really is. 夸大…的重要性；过分强调

  • ...overplaying the depth of the economic crisis. [VERB noun]

    夸大经济危机的严重性

  • I think the historical factor is overplayed, that it really doesn't mean much. [be VERB-ed]

    我认为历史因素被过分强调了，它实际上并不太重要。

2 PHRASE 过高估计自己的形势 If someone overplays their hand, they act more confidently than they should because they believe that they are in a stronger position than they actually are. 过高估计自己的形势

  • The United States has to be careful it doesn't overplay its hand.

    美国必须谨防高估自身实力。
