overdue
oʊvəʳdjuː -duː ˌoʊvərˈdu ˌoʊvərˈdju
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] （变化或事件）早该发生的 If you say that a change or an event is overdue, you mean that you think it should have happened before now. （变化或事件）早该发生的

  • This debate is long overdue.

    这场辩论早该进行了。

  • I'll go home and pay an overdue visit to my mother.

    我将回家看望母亲，其实我早该回去了。

SYN delayed, belated, late, late in the day  
2 ADJ-GRADED （钱款）到期未付的，拖欠的 Overdue sums of money have not been paid, even though it is later than the date on which they should have been paid. （钱款）到期未付的，拖欠的

  • Teachers have joined a strike aimed at forcing the government to pay overdue salaries and allowances.

    教师加入了旨在迫使政府支付拖欠薪水和津贴的罢工。

SYN unpaid, outstanding, owing, due  
3 ADJ-GRADED （图书馆的书）逾期未还的 An overdue library book has not been returned to the library, even though the date on which it should have been returned has passed. （图书馆的书）逾期未还的
