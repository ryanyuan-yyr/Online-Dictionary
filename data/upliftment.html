uplift
ʌplɪft ʌplɪft ʌpˈlɪft ˈʌpˌlɪft
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense uplifts, present participle uplifting, past tense , past participle uplifted
The verb is pronounced /ʌp'lɪft/. The noun is pronounced /'ʌplɪft/. 动词读作/ʌp'lɪft/，名词读作/'ʌplɪft/。
1 VERB 使振奋；提升 If something uplifts people, it helps them to have a better life, for example by making them feel happy or by improving their social conditions. 使振奋；提升 [literary]

  • We need a little something to help sometimes, to uplift us and make us feel better. [VERB noun]

    有时候，我们需要一些小东西来激励自己，使自己感觉更好。

  • Art was created to uplift the mind and the spirit. [VERB noun]

    艺术创作旨在陶冶情操。

SYN improve, better, raise, advance  
Uplift is also a noun.

  • This victory was a massive uplift for us.

    这个胜利对我们来说是巨大的鼓舞。

2 N-COUNT [usually singular] 上扬；上涨；增长 In economics, an uplift in something such as the price of shares is an increase in their value. 上扬；上涨；增长 [business]

  • ...an uplift in the stock market. [+ in]

    股市上涨

  • Its shares were down across the first quarter, but are now showing a 20 per cent uplift.

    它的股票在第一季度陷入低迷，但现在累计已有20%的涨幅。

SYN improvement, enlightenment, advancement, cultivation
