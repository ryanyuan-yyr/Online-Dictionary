throb
θrɒb θrɑb
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense throbs, present participle throbbing, past tense , past participle throbbed
1 VERB (身体局部)抽动，阵痛 If part of your body throbs, you feel a series of strong and usually painful beats there. (身体局部)抽动，阵痛

  • His head throbbed. [VERB]

    他的头阵阵作痛。

  • Presently George's ankle began to throb with pain. [VERB with noun]

    不一会儿，乔治的脚踝就开始一阵阵地疼。

  • ...the throbbing tooth whose pain had woken her. [VERB-ing]

    把她疼醒的阵阵牙痛

SYN pulsate, pound, beat, pulse  
Throb is also a noun.

  • The bruise on his stomach ached with a steady throb.

    他腹部的擦伤一直阵阵作痛。

2 VERB 震动作响;有规律地颤动 If something throbs, it vibrates and makes a steady noise. 震动作响;有规律地颤动 [literary]

  • The engines throbbed. [VERB]

    发动机轰隆隆地震动着。

  • The music throbbed hypnotically. [VERB]

    音乐咚咚作响，叫人昏昏欲睡。

  • The gardens blazed with colour and throbbed with birdsong. [VERB with noun]

    花园里万紫千红，鸟鸣嘤嘤。

SYN vibrate, pulse, resonate, pulsate  
Throb is also a noun.

  • Jake's head jerked up at the throb of the engine.

    听到发动机的轰鸣声，杰克猛地抬起了头。
