gloss
glɒs glɔːs glɔs glɑs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense glosses, present participle glossing, past tense , past participle glossed
1 N-SING 光泽；色泽；光亮 A gloss is a bright shine on the surface of something. 光泽；色泽；光亮

  • Rain produced a black gloss on the asphalt.

    雨后的柏油路面显得黑亮黑亮的。

SYN shine, gleam, sheen, polish  
2 N-UNCOUNT 虚假的外表；假象；虚饰 Gloss is an appearance of attractiveness or good quality which sometimes hides less attractive features or poor quality. 虚假的外表；假象；虚饰

  • Television commercials might seem more professional but beware of mistaking the gloss for the content.

    电视广告看上去或许更加专业，但要当心，不要把表象误以为真。

3 N-SING 粉饰；掩饰 If you put a gloss on a bad situation, you try to make it seem more attractive or acceptable by giving people a false explanation or interpretation of it. 粉饰；掩饰

  • He used his diary to put a fine gloss on the horrors the regime perpetrated. [+ on]

    他假借日记对该政权犯下的恐怖罪行巧加粉饰。

  • The whole idea was to give history a happy gloss.

    整个想法就是要粉饰历史。

SYN façade, show, front [informal] , surface  
4 N-MASS 同gloss paint Gloss is the same as gloss paint . 同gloss paint
5 N-MASS 唇彩；亮彩化妆品 Gloss is a type of shiny make-up. 唇彩；亮彩化妆品

  • She brushed gloss on to her eyelids.

    她在眼睑上涂了些亮彩眼影。

  • ...lip glosses.

    唇彩

6 VERB 解释；诠释；说明 If you gloss a difficult word or idea, you provide an explanation of it. 解释；诠释；说明 [Also VERB n]

  • Older editors glossed 'drynke' as 'love-potion'. [VERB noun + as]

    老编辑将drynke注释为love-potion（春药）。

SYN interpret, explain, comment, translate
