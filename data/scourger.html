scourge
skɜːʳdʒ skɜrdʒ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense scourges, present participle scourging, past tense , past participle scourged
1 N-COUNT 灾难;祸害 A scourge is something that causes a lot of trouble or suffering to a group of people. 灾难;祸害

  • ...the best chance in 20 years to end the scourge of terrorism. [+ of]

    20 年来消灭恐怖主义祸害的最佳机会

  • Drugs are a scourge that is devastating our society.

    毒品是破坏我们社会的一大祸害。

SYN affliction, plague [informal] , curse, terror  
2 VERB 使痛苦;危害;折磨 If something scourges a place or group of people, it causes great pain and suffering to people. 使痛苦;危害;折磨

  • Economic anarchy scourged the post-war world. [VERB noun]

    经济混乱使得战后的世界陷入水深火热之中。

SYN afflict, plague, curse, torment
