boggle
bɒgəl ˈbɑgəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense boggles, present participle boggling, past tense , past participle boggled
1 V-ERG （思想）困惑；使无法想象；使难以理解 If you say that the mind boggles at something or that something boggles the mind, you mean that it is so strange or amazing that it is difficult to imagine or understand. （思想）困惑；使无法想象；使难以理解

  • The mind boggles at the possibilities that could be in store for us. [VERB + at]

    我们简直无法想象将来面临的种种可能性。

  • The good grace with which they face the latest privations makes the mind boggle. [VERB]

    他们面对最近的贫困所表现出的坦然令人难以理解。

  • The management group's decision still boggled his mind. [VERB noun]

    管理层的决定仍让他丈二和尚摸不着头脑。

SYN confuse, surprise, shock, amaze
