testament
testəmənt ˈtɛstəmənt
Word forms: plural testaments
1 N-VAR 证据;证明 If one thing is a testament to another, it shows that the other thing exists or is true. 证据;证明 [formal]

  • Braka's house, just off Sloane Square, is a testament to his Gothic tastes. [+ to]

    布拉卡那幢紧靠斯隆广场的房子是他喜好哥特式建筑的一个证据。

  • The fact that these scandals are now public is testament to the relative openness of America's government.

    这些丑闻如今公之于众的事实说明了美国政府的相对透明。

SYN proof, evidence, testimony, witness  
2 PHRASE [usually with poss] 最新遗嘱;（尤指）临终遗嘱,临终遗言 Someone's last will and testament is the most recent will that they have made, especially the last will that they make before they die. 最新遗嘱;（尤指）临终遗嘱,临终遗言 [law]
