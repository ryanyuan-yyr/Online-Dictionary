demoralize
dɪmɒrəlaɪz -mɔːr- diˈmɔrəˌlaɪz dɪˈmɔrəlaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense demoralizes, present participle demoralizing, past tense , past participle demoralized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 demoralise
VERB 使士气低落;使失去信心;使泄气 If something demoralizes someone, it makes them lose so much confidence in what they are doing that they want to give up. 使士气低落;使失去信心;使泄气

  • Clearly, one of the objectives is to demoralize the enemy troops in any way they can. [VERB noun]

    很明显，目标之一就是要用一切可能的手段打击敌军的士气。

SYN dishearten, undermine, discourage, shake  
demoralized

  • The ship's crew were now exhausted and utterly demoralized.

    这艘船上的船员现在都已精疲力竭，彻底丧失了信心。

  • ...legitimate grievances raised by a demoralized police force.

    士气低落的警察队伍提出的合理申诉

demoralization

  • ...the lingering demoralization that followed defeat in World War I.

    第一次世界大战战败之后挥之不去的消沉情绪
