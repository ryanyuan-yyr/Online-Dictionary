stand in
1 PHRASAL VERB (在别人生病或不在时)替代，代替 If you stand in for someone, you take their place or do their job, because they are ill or away. (在别人生病或不在时)替代，代替

  • I had to stand in for her on Tuesday when she didn't show up. [VERB PREP. + for]

    她周二没来，我只好顶替她。

  • ...the acting president, who's standing in while she's out of the country. [VERB PREP.]

    她出国时代替她的代理主席

2See also:stand-in 
stand-in
ˈstændɪn ˈstændˌɪn
Word forms: plural stand-ins
N-COUNT (别人生病或不在时的)临时替代者，顶替者 A stand-in is a person who takes someone else's place or does someone else's job for a while, for example because the other person is ill or away. (别人生病或不在时的)临时替代者，顶替者

  • He was a stand-in for my regular doctor.

    他临时代替平常给我看病的那位医生。

SYN substitute, deputy, replacement, reserve
