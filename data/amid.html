amid
əmɪd əˈmɪd
The form amidst is also used, but is more literary. 亦使用amidst，但文学性更强。
1 PREP 在…过程中；在…中 If something happens amid noises or events of some kind, it happens while the other things are happening. 在…过程中；在…中

  • A senior leader cancelled a trip to Britain yesterday amid growing signs of a possible political crisis.

    爆发政治危机的迹象日趋明显，一位高级领导人昨天取消了预定的英国之行。

  • Children were changing classrooms amid laughter and shouts.

    孩子们在欢笑声和喊叫声中换教室。

SYN during, among, at a time of, in an atmosphere of  
2 PREP 在…中间；在…之中 If something is amid other things, it is surrounded by them. 在…中间；在…之中 [literary]

  • ...a tiny bungalow amid clusters of trees.

    在树丛中的一间小平房

SYN in the middle of, among, surrounded by, amongst
