prohibit
prəhɪbɪt proʊ- proʊˈhɪbɪt prəˈhɪbɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense prohibits, present participle prohibiting, past tense , past participle prohibited
VERB （法律或掌权者）禁止，不允许 If a law or someone in authority prohibits something, they forbid it or make it illegal. （法律或掌权者）禁止，不允许 [formal]

  • ...a law that prohibits tobacco advertising in newspapers and magazines. [VERB noun]

    禁止在报刊杂志上刊登香烟广告的法律

  • Fishing is prohibited. [VERB noun]

    禁止钓鱼。

  • Federal law prohibits foreign airlines from owning more than 25% of any U.S. airline. [VERB noun + from]

    联邦法律禁止外国航空公司拥有任何一家美国航空公司超过25%的股权份额。
