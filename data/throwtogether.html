throw together
1 PHRASAL VERB 仓促做成;快速张罗出 If you throw something together, for example a meal or a costume, you make it quickly and not very carefully. 仓促做成;快速张罗出 [informal] [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Too often, picnic preparation consists of throwing together some sandwiches and grabbing an apple. [VERB PREP. noun]

    准备野餐往往不过是随便做几个三明治，再来一个苹果。

2 PHR-V-RECIP 使…相遇相识 If people are thrown together by a situation or event, it causes them to get to know each other, even though they may not want to. 使…相遇相识

  • The cast and crew were thrown together for 12 hours a day, six days a week, until the filming was completed. [be VERB-ed PREP.]

    演员和剧组人员每周 6 天，每天 12 小时都呆在一起，一直到影片杀青为止。

  • If you have men and women thrown together in inhospitable surroundings, you are going to get some sexual tension. [VERB-ed PREP.]

    你要是让男人和女人一起呆在不友好的环境里，男女关系肯定会紧张。

  • My husband is constantly thrown together with young people through his work. [be VERB-ed PREP. + with]

    我丈夫由于工作关系经常遇到年轻人。
