execute
eksɪkjuːt ˈɛksɪˌkjut
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense executes, present participle executing, past tense , past participle executed
1 VERB 将…处死;将…正法 To execute someone means to kill them as a punishment for a serious crime. 将…处死;将…正法

  • He was executed by lethal injection earlier today. [be VERB-ed]

    他于今天早些时候被注射处死。

  • One group claimed to have executed the American hostage. [VERB noun]

    一个组织声称已经处决了那名美国人质。

  • This boy's father had been executed for conspiring against the throne. [be VERB-ed + for]

    这个男孩的父亲因为密谋反对君主而被处死。

2 VERB 执行;实施;贯彻 If you execute a plan, you carry it out. 执行;实施;贯彻 [formal]

  • We are going to execute our campaign plan to the letter. [VERB noun]

    我们将严格施行我们的竞选计划。

SYN carry out, effect, finish, complete

  • I executed the hairpin turn high on the sheer western face of the mountains. [VERB noun]

    我在山脉西侧陡峭的高处成功地急转弯。
