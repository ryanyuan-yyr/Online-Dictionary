elective
ɪlektɪv iˈlɛktɪv ɪˈlɛktɪv
Word forms: plural electives
1 ADJ [usually ADJ n] 由选举产生的;选任的 An elective post or committee is one to which people are appointed as a result of winning an election. 由选举产生的;选任的 [formal]

  • Buchanan has never held elective office.

    布坎南从未就任过经选举产生的职位。

2 ADJ [usually ADJ n] （外科手术）非急需的，可选择的 Elective surgery is surgery that you choose to have before it becomes essential. （外科手术）非急需的，可选择的 [formal]
3 N-COUNT 选修课 An elective is a subject which a student can choose to study as part of his or her course. 选修课 [US]

  • Electives are offered in Tai Chi and advanced dance exercise.

    选修课包括太极拳和高级舞蹈练习。
