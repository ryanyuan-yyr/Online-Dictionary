wrong-foot
ˈrɔŋˌfʊt
(also wrong foot)Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense wrong-foots, present participle wrong-footing, past tense , past participle wrong-footed
VERB 使措手不及;使仓皇失措;使方寸大乱 If you wrong-foot someone, you surprise them by putting them into an unexpected or difficult situation. 使措手不及;使仓皇失措;使方寸大乱 [mainly British]

  • He has surprised his supporters and wrong-footed his opponents with his latest announcement. [VERB noun]

    他最新发布的声明让支持者大呼意外,令反对者方寸大乱。
