abolish
əbɒlɪʃ əˈbɑlɪʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense abolishes, present participle abolishing, past tense , past participle abolished
1 VERB 废除，废止（制度、习俗等） If someone in authority abolishes a system or practice, they formally put an end to it. 废除，废止（制度、习俗等）

  • The following year Parliament voted to abolish the death penalty for murder. [VERB noun]

    议会于翌年表决对谋杀罪废除死刑。

  • The whole system should be abolished.

    整个制度应该被废除。

SYN do away with, end, destroy, eliminate
