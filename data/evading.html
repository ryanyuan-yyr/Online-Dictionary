evade
ɪveɪd iˈveɪd ɪˈveɪd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense evades, present participle evading, past tense , past participle evaded
1 VERB 规避;逃避 If you evade something, you find a way of not doing something that you really ought to do. 规避;逃避

  • By his own admission, he evaded taxes as a Florida real-estate speculator. [VERB noun]

    他亲口承认自己在佛罗里达从事房地产投机生意时逃过税。

  • Delegates accused them of trying to evade responsibility for the failures of the past five years. [VERB noun]

    代表们指责他们企图逃避过去5年中多次失败应负的责任。

2 VERB 回避，避开（问题、话题等） If you evade a question or a topic, you avoid talking about it or dealing with it. 回避，避开（问题、话题等）

  • Mr Portillo denied he was evading the question. [VERB noun]

    波蒂略先生否认自己正在回避那个问题。

  • Too many companies, she says, are evading the issue. [VERB noun]

    她说太多公司都在回避这个问题。

SYN avoid answering, parry, circumvent, fend off  
3 VERB 躲避;避开 If you evade someone or something, you move so that you can avoid meeting them or avoid being touched or hit. 躲避;避开

  • Under the pretence of lighting a candle, she evades him and disappears. [VERB noun]

    她假装点蜡烛，避开他消失了。

  • She turned and gazed at the river, evading his eyes. [VERB noun]

    她转身凝视着那条河，避开他的目光。

  • He managed to evade capture because of the breakdown of a police computer. [VERB noun]

    因为警方一台电脑瘫痪，他成功地躲避了抓捕。

SYN avoid, escape, dodge, get away from  
4 VERB （成功、光荣、爱情等）与…无缘 If something such as success, glory, or love evades you, you do not manage to have it. （成功、光荣、爱情等）与…无缘 [literary]

  • Happiness, which had been so elusive in Henry's life, still evaded him. [VERB noun]

    亨利生活中过去一直难以捕捉的幸福仍旧与他无缘。

  • When she sat down at her desk she found that the words evaded her. [VERB noun]

    当她在桌前坐下时，却发现自己不知道该说些什么了。
