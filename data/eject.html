eject
ɪdʒekt iˈdʒɛkt ɪdʒɛkt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense ejects, present participle ejecting, past tense , past participle ejected
1 VERB 驱逐;把…逐出 If you eject someone from a place, you force them to leave. 驱逐;把…逐出

  • Officials used guard dogs to eject the protesters. [VERB noun]

    官员们用警犬驱散抗议人群。

  • He was ejected from a restaurant. [be VERB-ed + from]

    他被逐出餐厅。

SYN throw out, remove, turn out, expel [slang]  
ejection

  • ...the ejection of hecklers from the meeting.

    对会议上捣乱分子的驱逐

2 VERB 弹出;喷出;射出 To eject something means to remove it or push it out forcefully. 弹出;喷出;射出

  • He aimed his rifle, fired a single shot, then ejected the spent cartridge. [VERB noun]

    他用步枪瞄准，射出一枚子弹，然后退出空弹壳。

SYN discharge, expel, emit, give off  
3 VERB （通常指飞行员从即将坠毁的飞机中）弹射出来 When a pilot ejects from an aircraft, he or she leaves the aircraft quickly using an ejector seat, usually because the plane is about to crash. （通常指飞行员从即将坠毁的飞机中）弹射出来 [Also VERB]

  • The pilot ejected from the plane and escaped injury. [VERB + from]

    飞行员从飞机中弹出，安然无恙。

SYN bail out, escape, get out
