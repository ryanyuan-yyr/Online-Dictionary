tow
toʊ təʊ toʊ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense tows, present participle towing, past tense , past participle towed
1 VERB 拖;拉;牵引 If one vehicle tows another, it pulls it along behind it. 拖;拉;牵引

  • He had been using the vehicle to tow his work trailer. [VERB noun]

    他一直用这辆车牵引他的活动工作室。

  • They threatened to tow away my car. [VERB noun with adv.]

    他们威胁要拖走我的车。

  • The British navy boarded the vessel and towed it to New York. [VERB noun prep.]

    英国海军登上船，把它拖到纽约。

SYN drag, draw, pull, trail  
Tow is also a noun.

  • I can give you a tow if you want.

    如果你愿意，我可以拖带你的车。

2 PHRASE 跟随;陪伴 If you have someone in tow, they are following you closely because you are looking after them or you are leading them somewhere. 跟随;陪伴 [informal]

  • There she was on my doorstep with child in tow.

    她站在我门口，身后跟着一个孩子。

  • She had a reporter and a photographer in tow.

    她有一个记者和一名摄影师追随着。

SYN accompanying, following, in attendance, by your side
