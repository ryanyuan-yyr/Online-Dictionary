casual
kæʒuəl ˈkæʒuəl
1 ADJ-GRADED 漫不经心的;(故作)随意的 If you are casual, you are, or you pretend to be, relaxed and not very concerned about what is happening or what you are doing. 漫不经心的;(故作)随意的

  • It's difficult for me to be casual about anything. [+ about]

    要我轻松处事很难。

  • He's an easy-going, friendly young man with a casual sort of attitude towards money.

    他是个随和、友善的年轻人，不太在意金钱。

SYN careless, relaxed, informal, indifferent  
casually

  • 'No need to hurry,' Ben said casually.

    “不必急，”本漫不经心地说。

casualness

  • Bayldon asked the question with studied casualness.

    贝尔顿故作轻松地问了那个问题。

2 ADJ [ADJ n] (事件或情况)偶发的，偶然的，碰巧的 A casual event or situation happens by chance or without planning. (事件或情况)偶发的，偶然的，碰巧的

  • What you mean as a casual remark could be misinterpreted.

    你随便说的一句话，可能就被曲解了。

  • Even a casual observer could hardly have failed to notice the heightening of an already tense atmosphere.

    即便是不经意的旁观者也一定已经觉察到本就紧张的气氛进一步加剧了。

3 ADJ-GRADED [ADJ n] (服装)休闲的，便装的 Casual clothes are ones that you normally wear at home or on holiday, and not on formal occasions. (服装)休闲的，便装的

  • I also bought some casual clothes for the weekend.

    我还买了些周末穿的休闲装。

SYN informal, leisure, sporty, non-dressy  
casually

  • They were smartly but casually dressed.

    他们穿得很漂亮，但又都很随意。

4 ADJ [ADJ n] (工作)临时的，短期的 Casual work is done for short periods and not on a permanent or regular basis. (工作)临时的，短期的

  • ...establishments which employ people on a casual basis, such as pubs and restaurants.

    酒吧、饭店等雇用临时工的地方

  • It became increasingly expensive to hire casual workers.

    雇用临时工越来越贵了。
