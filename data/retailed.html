retail
riːteɪl riːteɪl riːteɪl ˈriˌteɪl rɪˈteɪl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense retails, present participle retailing, past tense , past participle retailed
The pronunciation is /'riːteɪl/ for meanings 1 to 3, and /riː'teɪl/ for meaning 4. 义项1到3读作/'riːteɪl/。义项4读作/riː'teɪl/。
1 N-UNCOUNT 零售;零卖 Retail is the activity of selling goods direct to the public, usually in small quantities. 零售;零卖

  • ...retail stores.

    零售店

  • Retail sales grew just 3.8 percent last year.

    去年零售额只上升了3.8%。

2 ADV [ADVERB after verb] 以零售方式 If something is sold retail, it is sold in ordinary shops direct to the public. 以零售方式 [business]
3 VERB （以…价格）零售，零卖 If an item in a shop retails at or for a particular price, it is on sale at that price. （以…价格）零售，零卖 [business]

  • It originally retailed at £23.50. [V + at/for]

    它原来的零售价格是23.50英镑。

4 VERB （热情而详细地）讲述;详述 If someone retails a story or event, they tell it to someone else, often in detail and in an exciting way. （热情而详细地）讲述;详述 [literary]

  • Mr Hastings gleefully retailed the story to Mr Anderson over lunch. [VERB noun]

    黑斯廷斯先生在午宴上兴致勃勃地把这个故事向安德森先生详述了一遍。
