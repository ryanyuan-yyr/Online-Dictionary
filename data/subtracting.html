subtract
səbtrækt səbˈtrækt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense subtracts, present participle subtracting, past tense , past participle subtracted
1 VERB 减;减去 If you subtract one number from another, you do a calculation in which you take it away from the other number. For example, if you subtract 3 from 5, you get 2. 减;减去

  • Mandy subtracted the date of birth from the date of death. [VERB noun + from]

    曼迪用去世的日期减去出生的日期。

  • We have subtracted $25 per adult to arrive at a basic room rate. [VERB noun]

    我们已给每位成人房客减去了 25 美元，这就是基本的房费价格了。

SYN take away, take off, deduct, remove
