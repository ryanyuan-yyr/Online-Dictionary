electrode
ɪlektroʊd iˈlɛkˌtroʊd ɪˈlɛkˌtroʊd
Word forms: plural electrodes
N-COUNT 电极 An electrode is a small piece of metal or other substance that is used to take an electric current to or from a source of power, a piece of equipment, or a living body. 电极

  • The patient's brain activity is monitored via electrodes taped to the skull.

    病人的大脑活动通过固定在其头骨上的电极进行监控。
