editorial
edɪtɔːriəl ˌɛdɪˈtɔriəl
Word forms: plural editorials
1 ADJ [ADJ n] 编辑的；编者的；主编的 Editorial means involved in preparing a newspaper, magazine, or book for publication. 编辑的；编者的；主编的

  • He has been on the editorial staff of 'Private Eye' since 1963.

    自1963年以来，他一直是《第三只眼》杂志的编辑。

  • I went to the editorial board meetings when I had the time.

    我一有空就去参加编委会会议。

editorially

  • Rosie Boycott was not involved editorially with Virago.

    罗西·博伊科特不属于维拉戈出版公司的编辑队伍。

2 ADJ [ADJ n] （报纸、杂志、电视节目等）立场（或观点等）的;社论（式）的 Editorial means involving the attitudes, opinions, and contents of something such as a newspaper, magazine, or television programme. （报纸、杂志、电视节目等）立场（或观点等）的;社论（式）的

  • We are not about to change our editorial policy.

    我们不会改变我们的社论方针。

editorially

  • Editorially, they never really became a unique distinct product.

    就立场和观点来说，它们从未真正做到树立起自己独具一格的视角。

3 N-COUNT （报纸的）社论 An editorial is an article in a newspaper which gives the opinion of the editor or owner on a topic or item of news. （报纸的）社论

  • In an editorial, The Independent suggests the victory could turn nasty.

    《独立报》在一篇社论中暗示这场胜利可能会变味。

4 N-COUNT （电视或电台）评论 An editorial on television or radio is an item which gives the opinion of the network or radio station. （电视或电台）评论 [US]
