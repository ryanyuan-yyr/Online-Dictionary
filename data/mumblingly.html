mumble
mʌmbəl ˈmʌmbəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense mumbles, present participle mumbling, past tense , past participle mumbled
VERB 咕哝;含糊地说 If you mumble, you speak very quietly and not at all clearly with the result that the words are difficult to understand. 咕哝;含糊地说

  • Her grandmother mumbled in her sleep. [VERB]

    她祖母含混不清地说着梦话。

  • He mumbled a few words. [VERB noun]

    他小声咕哝了一句。

  • 'Today of all days,' she mumbled. [VERB with quote]

    她低声咕哝了一句：“为什么偏偏是今天。”

SYN mutter, whisper, murmur, drone  
Mumble is also a noun.

  • He could hear the low mumble of Navarro's voice.

    他能听到纳瓦罗在小声咕哝。
