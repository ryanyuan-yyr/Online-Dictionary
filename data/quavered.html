quaver
kweɪvəʳ ˈkweɪvər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense quavers, present participle quavering, past tense , past participle quavered
1 VERB (声音因紧张或害怕而)颤抖 If someone's voice quavers, it sounds unsteady, usually because they are nervous or uncertain. (声音因紧张或害怕而)颤抖

  • Her voice quavered and she fell silent. [VERB]

    她声音发颤然后便默不作声了。

SYN tremble, shake, quiver, thrill  
Quaver is also a noun.

  • There was a quaver in Beryl's voice.

    贝丽尔的声音有一丝颤抖。

2 N-COUNT 八分音符 A quaver is a musical note that is half as long as a crotchet. 八分音符 [mainly British]
SYN trembling, break, shake, sob
