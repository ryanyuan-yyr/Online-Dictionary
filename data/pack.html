pack pæk pæk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense packs, present participle packing, past tense , past participle packed
1 VERB 把…装进行囊；打点（行装） When you pack a bag, you put clothes and other things into it, because you are leaving a place or going on holiday. 把…装进行囊；打点（行装）

  • When I was 17, I packed my bags and left home. [VERB noun]

    17岁时，我背起行囊离开了家。

  • I decided to pack a few things and take the kids to my Mum's. [VERB noun]

    我决定收拾些东西，带孩子们去我妈妈家。

  • I packed and said goodbye to Charlie. [VERB]

    我收拾好东西告别了查利。

packing

  • She left Frances to finish her packing.

    她让弗朗西丝把她的行装收拾完。

2 VERB 将…打包；把…装箱 When people pack things, for example in a factory, they put them into containers or parcels so that they can be transported and sold. 将…打包；把…装箱

  • They offered me a job packing goods in a warehouse. [VERB noun]

    他们给了我一份在货栈打包的工作。

  • Machines now exist to pack olives in jars. [VERB noun + in]

    现在可用机器将橄榄装进罐子里。

  • ...sardines packed in oil. [VERB-ed]

    油浸沙丁鱼罐头

SYN package, load, store, bundle  
packing

  • His onions cost 9p a lb wholesale; packing and transport costs 10p.

    他的洋葱批发价每磅9便士；打包送货的话要10便士。

3 VERB 挤满；塞满 If people or things pack into a place or if they pack a place, there are so many of them that the place is full. 挤满；塞满

  • Hundreds of thousands of people packed into the mosque. [VERB + into]

    几十万人挤进了那座清真寺。

  • Seventy thousand people will pack the stadium. [VERB noun]

    将有7万人挤满整个体育场。

4 N-COUNT 包；盒 A pack of things is a collection of them that is sold or given together in a box or bag. 包；盒

  • The club will send a free information pack.

    这家俱乐部会邮寄一个免费资料包。

  • ...a pack of cigarettes. [+ of]

    一包烟

  • She read the back of the pack and said it had the same ingredients.

    她看了看盒子背面，说成分一样。

SYN packet, box, package, carton  
5 N-COUNT 背包；旅行包 A pack is a bag containing your possessions that you carry on your back when you are travelling. 背包；旅行包

  • I hid the money in my pack.

    我把钱藏在旅行包里。

SYN bundle, kit, parcel, load  
6 N-COUNT 一群；一伙；一帮 You can refer to a group of people who go around together as a pack, especially when it is a large group that you feel threatened by. 一群；一伙；一帮

  • ...a pack of journalists eager to question him. [+ of]

    一群急着要问他问题的记者

  • Sal was the leader of the pack.

    萨尔是这伙人的头儿。

SYN group, crowd, collection, company  
7 N-COUNT （狼或狗的）一群 A pack of wolves or dogs is a group of them that hunt together. （狼或狗的）一群
8 N-COUNT （纸牌的）一副 A pack of playing cards is a complete set of playing cards. （纸牌的）一副 [mainly British]

  • ...a pack of cards.

    一副纸牌

  • ...shuffle the pack.

    洗牌

in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 deck
9 VERB 带（枪）；佩（枪） If someone packs a gun, they carry it. 带（枪）；佩（枪） [informal]

  • ...eight bodyguards, at least one of them packing a pistol. [VERB noun]

    8名保镖，其中至少有1人佩枪

10 VERB 在（陪审团、委员会、会议等）中安插支持者 If someone packs a jury, committee, or meeting, they make sure that it includes people who support them. 在（陪审团、委员会、会议等）中安插支持者

  • Opposition parties have boycotted the proceedings, saying the government has packed the conference with its own supporters. [VERB noun with noun]

    反对党派抵制了该议程，声称政府在大会上安插了亲信。

  • John Major will not try to pack the House of Lords. [VERB noun]

    约翰·梅杰不会在上议院安插亲信。

11 See also: packed  packing 
12 PHRASE 一派谎言 If you say that an account is a pack of lies, you mean that it is completely untrue. 一派谎言

  • You told me a pack of lies.

    你跟我说的都是一派胡言。

13 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 产生巨大影响；震撼 If something packs a punch, it has a very powerful effect. 产生巨大影响；震撼

  • W. Somerset Maugham's novel still packs an emotional punch.

    威廉·萨默塞特·毛姆的小说仍具有强烈的情感冲击力。

  • ...drinks that pack a punch.

    烈酒

14 PHRASE 叫…卷铺盖；撵…走；叫…滚蛋 If you send someone packing, you make them go away. 叫…卷铺盖；撵…走；叫…滚蛋 [informal]

  • I decided I wanted to live alone and I sent him packing.

    我决定要一个人生活，于是叫他走人。

15 PHRASE （在竞赛中）领先其他对手 You can say that someone is ahead of the pack or leading the pack if they are ahead of everyone else in a race or competition. （在竞赛中）领先其他对手

  • The Socialists may still finish ahead of the pack.

    社会党最终仍可能会领先。

  • Europe has got used to following rather than leading the pack.

    欧洲已经习惯了跟从，而不是领头儿。
