stub
stʌb stʌb
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense stubs, present participle stubbing, past tense , past participle stubbed
1 N-COUNT 残根;残端;(铅笔)头;(烟)蒂 The stub of a cigarette or a pencil is the last short piece of it which remains when the rest has been used. 残根;残端;(铅笔)头;(烟)蒂

  • He pulled the stub of a pencil from behind his ear. [+ of]

    他从耳后拿出一小段铅笔头。

  • ...an ashtray of cigarette stubs.

    一烟缸的烟蒂

SYN butt, end, stump, tail [informal]  
2 N-COUNT [usually n NOUN] 票根;存根 A ticket stub is the part that you keep when you go in to watch a performance. 票根;存根

  • Fans who still have their original ticket stubs should contact Sheffield Arena by July 3.

    仍保留原始票根的球迷请于 7 月 3 号前和谢菲尔德体育馆联系。

SYN counterfoil, tab, receipt, coupon  
3 N-COUNT [usually n NOUN] (支票的)存根 A cheque stub is the small part that you keep as a record of what you have paid. (支票的)存根
4 VERB 不小心踢到(脚趾) If you stub your toe, you hurt it by accidentally kicking something. 不小心踢到(脚趾)

  • I stubbed my toes against a table leg. [VERB noun]

    我不小心踢到桌子腿上。
