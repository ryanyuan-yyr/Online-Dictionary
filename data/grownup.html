grown-up ˈgroʊnˈʌp ˈgroʊnˌʌp
Word forms: plural grown-ups
The spelling grownup is also used. The syllable up is not stressed when it is a noun. 亦拼作grownup，用作名词时up不重读。
1 N-COUNT （用作儿语）成年人，大人 A grown-up is an adult; used by or to children. （用作儿语）成年人，大人

  • Jan was almost a grown-up.

    简都快是个大人了。

  • Tell children to tell a grown-up if they're being bullied.

    告诉孩子们，如果被人欺负就告诉大人。

SYN adult, man, woman  
2 ADJ 成熟的；成年的；长大的 Someone who is grown-up is physically and mentally mature and no longer depends on their parents or another adult. 成熟的；成年的；长大的

  • I have grown-up children who're doing well.

    我的几个孩子已经长大成人并且事业有成。

3 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 似成人的；成熟的 If you say that someone is grown-up, you mean that they behave in an adult way, often when they are in fact still a child. 似成人的；成熟的

  • She's very grown-up.

    她表现得很像个大人。

4 ADJ-GRADED 适于成人的；成人的 Grown-up things seem suitable for or typical of adults. 适于成人的；成人的 [informal]

  • Her songs tackle grown-up subjects.

    她的歌曲唱的都是成年人的事情。

  • She talked in a grown-up manner.

    她说起话来像个大人。
