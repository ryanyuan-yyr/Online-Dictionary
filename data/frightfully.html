frightful
fraɪtfʊl ˈfraɪtfəl
1 ADJ-GRADED 讨厌的;令人不快的;可怕的 Frightful means very bad or unpleasant. 讨厌的;令人不快的;可怕的 [old-fashioned]

  • My father was unable to talk about the war, it was so frightful.

    我父亲没法谈论那场战争，那太可怕了。

2 ADJ [ADJ n] 非常的;极度的 Frightful is used to emphasize the extent or degree of something, usually something bad. 非常的;极度的 [informal, old-fashioned, emphasis]

  • He got himself into a frightful muddle.

    他把自己卷进了一个不可收拾的乱摊子。

  • What frightful gossips people are, even one's best friends.

    人们最爱散布流言蜚语，哪怕是自己最好的朋友也不例外。

SYN dreadful, great, terrible, extreme  
frightfully

  • I'm most frightfully sorry about this.

    我对此感到万分抱歉。

  • You're frightfully good at this sort of thing.

    你做这种事特别拿手。
