swagger
swægəʳ ˈswæɡɪ ˈswægər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense swaggers, present participle swaggering, past tense , past participle swaggered
VERB 趾高气扬地走;大摇大摆地走 If you swagger, you walk in a very proud, confident way, holding your body upright and swinging your hips. 趾高气扬地走;大摇大摆地走 [Also VERB]

  • A broad shouldered man wearing a dinner jacket swaggered confidently up to the bar. [VERB prep./adv.]

    穿晚礼服的宽肩膀男子神气十足地走向吧台。

  • The burly brute swaggered forward, towering over me, and shouted. [VERB prep./adv.]

    五大三粗的恶汉趾高气扬地走过来，居高临下地对我咆哮着。

  • John Steed was an arrogant, swaggering young man. [VERB-ing]

    约翰·斯蒂德是一个傲慢狂妄的年轻人。

SYN stride, parade, strut, prance  
Swagger is also a noun.

  • He walked with something of a swagger.

    他趾高气扬地走着。
