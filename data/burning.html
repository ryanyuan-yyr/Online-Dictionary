burning
bɜːʳnɪŋ ˈbɜrnɪŋ
1 ADJ 灼热的；炙热的 You use burning to describe something that is extremely hot. 灼热的；炙热的

  • ...the burning desert of Central Asia.

    炙热的中亚沙漠

SYN very hot, boiling, baking, flaming  
Burning is also an adverb.

  • He touched the boy's forehead. It was burning hot.

    他摸了摸男孩的额头，烧得烫手。

2 ADJ [ADJ n] 热烈的；热切的；强烈的 If you say that someone has burning eyes, you mean they look at you in an intense way or have bright eyes because of a strong feeling. 热烈的；热切的；强烈的 [literary]

  • She glared at both of them with burning, reproachful eyes.

    她瞪着他们俩，目光中充满怒火和责备。

SYN flashing, blazing, flaming, gleaming  
3 ADJ [ADJ n] （兴趣）浓厚的；（欲望）强烈的 If you have a burning interest in something or a burning desire to do something, you are extremely interested in it or want to do it very much. （兴趣）浓厚的；（欲望）强烈的

  • I had a burning ambition to become a journalist.

    我热切期盼成为一名记者。

  • She had a burning desire to wreak revenge.

    她复仇心切。

SYN intense, passionate, earnest, eager  
4 ADJ-GRADED [ADJ n] 急迫的；重要的 A burning issue or question is a very important or urgent one that people feel very strongly about. 急迫的；重要的

  • The burning question in this year's debate over the federal budget is: whose taxes should be raised?

    今年联邦财政预算辩论中的一个重要问题就是：哪些人的纳税额应该提高？
