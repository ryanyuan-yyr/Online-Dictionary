intervene
ɪntəʳviːn ˌɪntərˈvin
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense intervenes, present participle intervening, past tense , past participle intervened
1 VERB 干涉;干预;介入 If you intervene in a situation, you become involved in it and try to change it. 干涉;干预;介入

  • The situation calmed down when police intervened. [VERB]

    警方干预后，局势平息下来。

  • The Government is doing nothing to intervene in the crisis. [VERB + in]

    政府没有采取任何行动对这次危机进行干预。

SYN step in [informal] , interfere, mediate, intrude  
2 VERB 插话;打岔 If you intervene, you interrupt a conversation in order to add something to it. 插话;打岔

  • Hattie intervened and told me to stop it. [VERB]

    哈蒂打断我，叫我停下来。

  • 'I've told you he's not here,' Irena intervened. [VERB with quote]

    “我告诉你了他不在这里,”伊雷娜插话说。

SYN interrupt, involve yourself, put your oar in, interpose yourself  
3 VERB 干扰;阻挠;打扰 If an event intervenes, it happens suddenly in a way that stops, delays, or prevents something from happening. 干扰;阻挠;打扰

  • The South African mailboat arrived on Friday mornings unless bad weather intervened. [VERB]

    除非受恶劣天气干扰,南非邮船一般在星期五上午抵达。

  • I pray that death may not intervene to prevent our meeting with my darling children. [VERB to-infinitive]

    我祈祷死亡不要阻挠我们与我亲爱的孩子们见一面。
