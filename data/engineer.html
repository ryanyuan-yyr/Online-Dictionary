engineer
endʒɪnɪəʳ ˌɛndʒəˈnɪr
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense engineers, present participle engineering, past tense , past participle engineered
1 N-COUNT 工程师；设计师 An engineer is a person who uses scientific knowledge to design, construct, and maintain engines and machines or structures such as roads, railways, and bridges. 工程师；设计师
2See also:chemical engineer  civil engineer  electrical engineer  sound engineer 
3 N-COUNT 机械修理工;电气修理工；技师 An engineer is a person who repairs mechanical or electrical devices. 机械修理工;电气修理工；技师

  • They send a service engineer to fix the disk drive.

    他们派出维修人员来修磁盘驱动器。

SYN worker, specialist, operator, practitioner  
4 N-COUNT （船上的）轮机手 An engineer is a person who is responsible for maintaining the engine of a ship while it is at sea. （船上的）轮机手
5 VERB [usually passive] 设计制造（车辆、桥梁、建筑物等） When a vehicle, bridge, or building is engineered, it is planned and constructed using scientific methods. 设计制造（车辆、桥梁、建筑物等）

  • Many of Kuwait's spacious freeways were engineered by W S Atkins. [be VERB-ed]

    科威特有很多宽阔的高速公路是阿特金斯集团设计建造的。

  • ...the car's better designed and engineered rivals.

    比那款轿车的设计和制造更为精良的竞争车型

SYN design, plan, create, construct  
6 VERB （巧妙或间接地）安排，筹划 If you engineer an event or situation, you arrange for it to happen, in a clever or indirect way. （巧妙或间接地）安排，筹划

  • He could stand no more and engineered an escape. [VERB noun]

    他无法再忍受，便策划了出逃。

  • This boom has been engineered by the Chancellor for short-term political reasons.

    这片繁荣景象是总理为了短期政治利益而精心安排的。

SYN bring about, plan, control, cause
