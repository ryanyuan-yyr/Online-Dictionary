preliminary
prɪlɪmɪnri -neri priˈlɪməˌnɛri prɪˈlɪməˌnɛri
Word forms: plural preliminaries
1 ADJ [usually ADJ n] 预备的；初步的 Preliminary activities or discussions take place at the beginning of an event, often as a form of preparation. 预备的；初步的

  • Preliminary results show the Republican party with 11 percent of the vote.

    初步结果显示共和党获得了11%的选票。

  • Preliminary talks on the future of the bases began yesterday.

    昨天开始了关于基地未来的初步讨论。

2 N-COUNT 准备工作；初步行动 A preliminary is something that you do at the beginning of an activity, often as a form of preparation. 准备工作；初步行动

  • It had taken about ten minutes to cover the preliminaries.

    准备工作大概花了10分钟。

  • A background check is normally a preliminary to a presidential appointment.

    总统任命前通常要进行背景审查的准备工作。

3 N-COUNT 预赛；初赛；初试 A preliminary is the first part of a competition to see who will go on to the main competition. 预赛；初赛；初试

  • The winner of each preliminary goes through to the final.

    每场初赛的获胜者进入决赛。
