mountain
maʊntɪn -tən ˈmaʊntən
Word forms: plural mountains
1 N-COUNT 山;山岳;高山 A mountain is a very high area of land with steep sides. 山;山岳;高山

  • Ben Nevis, in Scotland, is Britain's highest mountain.

    苏格兰境内的本尼维斯山是英国最高的山。

  • ...a lovely little mountain village.

    怡人的小山村

SYN peak, mount, height, ben [Scottish]  
2 QUANT 大量;大堆 If you talk about a mountain of something, or mountains of something, you are emphasizing that there is a large amount of it. 大量;大堆 [informal, emphasis]

  • They are faced with a mountain of bureaucracy.

    他们面对一大堆繁文缛节。

  • They have mountains of coffee to sell.

    他们有大量的咖啡要卖。

3 PHRASE 极大的困难;难办的事情；棘手的难题 If you say that someone has a mountain to climb, you mean that it will be difficult for them to achieve what they want to achieve. 极大的困难;难办的事情；棘手的难题 [journalism]

  • 'We had a mountain to climb after the second goal went in,' said Crosby.

    克罗斯比说：“进第二个球之后，我们面临着严峻的挑战。”

4 to make a mountain out of a molehill→see: molehill
