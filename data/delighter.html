delight
dɪlaɪt dɪˈlaɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense delights, present participle delighting, past tense , past participle delighted
1 N-UNCOUNT 高兴;愉快;快乐 Delight is a feeling of very great pleasure. 高兴;愉快;快乐

  • Throughout the house, the views are a constant source of surprise and delight.

    从房子各处看到的景色不断给人以惊奇和欣喜。

  • Andrew roared with delight when he heard Rachel's nickname for the baby.

    安德鲁听到雷切尔给婴儿起的绰号时，不禁高兴得大笑起来。

  • To my great delight, it worked perfectly.

    让我非常高兴的是，它的效果奇佳。

SYN pleasure, joy, satisfaction, comfort  
2 PHRASE （以…）为乐;喜欢;爱好 If someone takes delight or takes a delight in something, they get a lot of pleasure from it. （以…）为乐;喜欢;爱好

  • Haig took obvious delight in proving his critics wrong.

    黑格显然以证明他的批评者是错误的为乐。

  • I enjoy seeing your parents take such a delight in the boys.

    看到你父母如此喜欢这些男孩子，我很高兴。

3 N-COUNT [oft NOUN to-infinitive] 令人高兴的人(或物);乐事;乐趣 You can refer to someone or something that gives you great pleasure or enjoyment as a delight . 令人高兴的人(或物);乐事;乐趣 [approval]

  • Isn't she a delight?.

    她真讨人喜欢，不是吗？

  • The aircraft was a delight to fly.

    驾驶这架飞机是一件乐事。

  • Sampling the local cuisine is one of the delights of a holiday abroad.

    品尝当地菜肴是去国外度假的一大享受。

4 VERB 使高兴;使愉快;使快乐 If something delights you, it gives you a lot of pleasure. 使高兴;使愉快;使快乐

  • She has created a style of music that has delighted audiences all over the world. [VERB noun]

    她创造出了一种能令全世界的听众都感到快乐的音乐风格。

  • The report has delighted environmentalists. [VERB noun]

    这份报告让环境保护主义者欢欣不已。

SYN please, satisfy, content, thrill  
5 VERB （以…）为乐;喜欢;爱好 If you delight in something, you get a lot of pleasure from it. （以…）为乐;喜欢;爱好

  • Generations of adults and children have delighted in the story. [VERB + in]

    一代又一代的成年人和儿童都很喜欢这个故事。

  • He delighted in sharing his love of birds with children. [VERB + in]

    他喜欢与孩子们分享他对鸟类的热爱。
