subside
səbsaɪd səbˈsaɪd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense subsides, present participle subsiding, past tense , past participle subsided
1 VERB (感情)平息，缓和，趋于平静;(噪音)减弱，降低 If a feeling or noise subsides, it becomes less strong or loud. (感情)平息，缓和，趋于平静;(噪音)减弱，降低

  • The pain had subsided during the night. [VERB]

    晚间疼痛已经减轻了。

  • Catherine's sobs finally subsided. [VERB]

    凯瑟琳终于慢慢停止了抽泣。

SYN decrease, diminish, lessen, ease  
2 VERB (争斗)平息，平静 If fighting subsides, it becomes less intense or general. (争斗)平息，平静

  • Violence has subsided following two days of riots. [VERB]

    两天的暴乱过后，暴力事件开始平息。

3 VERB (地面、建筑物)下沉，沉降，下陷 If the ground or a building is subsiding, it is very slowly sinking to a lower level. (地面、建筑物)下沉，沉降，下陷

  • Does that mean the whole house is subsiding? [VERB]

    那意味着整个房子正在下陷吗？

SYN collapse, sink, cave in, drop  
4 VERB (水位)回落;(尤指洪水)减退，消退 If a level of water, especially flood water, subsides, it goes down. (水位)回落;(尤指洪水)减退，消退

  • Local officials say the flood waters have subsided. [VERB]

    当地官员称洪水已经退去了。

SYN drop, fall, decline, ebb
