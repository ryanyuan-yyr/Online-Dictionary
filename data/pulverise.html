pulverize
pʌlvəraɪz ˈpʌlvərˌaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense pulverizes, present participle pulverizing, past tense , past participle pulverized
in AM, also use 美国英语亦用 pulverise
1 VERB 严重损坏；彻底摧毁 To pulverize something means to do great damage to it or to destroy it completely. 严重损坏；彻底摧毁

  • ...the economic policies which pulverised the economy during the 1980s. [VERB noun]

    20世纪80年代使经济遭受了重创的经济政策

  • A factory making armaments had been bombed the night before and a residential area not far away had been pulverized. [VERB noun]

    前天晚上，一家兵工厂被炸，不远处的居民区也被夷为平地。

SYN destroy, wreck, demolish, flatten  
2 VERB 彻底击败；彻底战胜 If someone pulverizes an opponent in an election or competition, they thoroughly defeat them. 彻底击败；彻底战胜 [informal]

  • He is set to pulverise his two opponents in the race for the presidency. [VERB noun]

    他决心在总统竞选中彻底击垮他的两个对手。

SYN defeat, stuff [slang] , tank [slang] , annihilate  
3 VERB 把…磨成粉 If you pulverize something, you make it into a powder by crushing it. 把…磨成粉

  • Using a pestle and mortar, pulverise the bran to a coarse powder. [VERB noun]

    用杵和臼，将麸皮磨成粗粉。

  • The fries are made from pellets of pulverised potato. [VERB-ed]

    这些炸土豆是土豆泥球做成的。

SYN crush, pound, grind, mill
