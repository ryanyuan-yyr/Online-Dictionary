swear
sweəʳ swɛə swɛr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense swears, present participle swearing, past tense swore, past participle sworn
1 VERB 咒骂;诅咒;说脏话 If someone swears, they use language that is considered to be rude or offensive, usually because they are angry. 咒骂;诅咒;说脏话

  • It's wrong to swear and shout. [VERB]

    破口大骂是不对的。

  • They swore at them and ran off. [VERB + at]

    他们骂了他们后就跑掉了。

SYN curse, cuss [informal] , blaspheme, turn the air blue [informal]  
2 VERB 发誓要;郑重承诺 If you swear to do something, you promise in a serious way that you will do it. 发誓要;郑重承诺

  • Alan swore that he would do everything in his power to help us. [VERB that]

    艾伦许诺要尽其所能帮助我们。

  • We have sworn to fight cruelty wherever we find it. [VERB to-infinitive]

    我们发誓要见恶除恶。

  • The police are the only civil servants who have to swear allegiance to the Crown. [VERB noun]

    警察是唯一要宣誓效忠君主的公务员。

  • I have sworn an oath to defend her. [VERB noun]

    我发誓要保护她。

SYN vow, promise, take an oath, warrant  
3 VERB 发誓，郑重声明，保证(所说属实) If you say that you swear that something is true or that you can swear to it, you are saying very firmly that it is true. 发誓，郑重声明，保证(所说属实) [emphasis]

  • I swear I've told you all I know. [VERB that]

    我发誓我已经把我所知道的全都告诉你了。

  • I swear on all I hold dear that I had nothing to do with this. [V + on/by]

    我以身家性命担保，我和这件事无关。

  • Behind them was a confusion of noise, perhaps even a shot, but he couldn't swear to it. [VERB + to]

    他们身后响起一片嘈杂声，也许还有枪击声，但他不能确定。

SYN declare, assert, affirm, swear blind  
4 VERB [usually passive] 使发誓，使承诺(保守秘密) If someone is sworn to secrecy or is sworn to silence, they promise another person that they will not reveal a secret. 使发誓，使承诺(保守秘密)

  • She was bursting to announce the news but was sworn to secrecy. [be VERB-ed + to]

    她急不可待想宣布这个消息，但却发过誓要守口如瓶。
