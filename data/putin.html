put in
1 PHRASAL VERB 花费，投入（时间或精力） If you put in an amount of time or effort doing something, you spend that time or effort doing it. 花费，投入（时间或精力）

  • Wade was going to be paid a salary, instead of by the hour, whether he put in forty hours or not. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    会按月给韦德开工资，而不是按小时，无论他是否工作了40个小时。

  • They've put in time and effort to keep the strike going. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他们花费了很多时间和精力以使罢工持续下去。

  • If we don't put money in we will lose our investment. [VERB noun PREP.]

    如果我们不再投入资金，我们会失掉本金。

SYN submit, present, enter, file  
2 PHRASAL VERB 申请；要求 If you put in a request or put in for something, you formally request or apply for that thing. 申请；要求

  • The ministry ordered 113 of these and later put in a request for 21 more. [VERB PREP. noun]

    这些东西该部订购了113个，后来又追加了21个。

  • I decided to put in for a job as deputy secretary. [VERB PREP. + for]

    我决定申请副书记一职。

3 PHRASAL VERB 插（话）；作补充 If you put in a remark, you interrupt someone or add to what they have said with the remark. 插（话）；作补充

  • 'He was a lawyer before that,' Mary Ann put in. [VERB PREP. with quote]

    “在那之前他是个律师，”玛丽·安补充道。

  • 'Helen had something to eat before she left,' put in Cecil anxiously. [VERB PREP. with quote]

    “海伦走之前吃了点儿东西，”塞西尔急忙插了一句。

4 PHRASAL VERB （船）停靠码头，入港 When a ship puts in or puts into a port, it goes into the port for a short stop. （船）停靠码头，入港

  • It's due to put in at Aden and some other ports before arriving in Basra. [VERB PREP. adv./prep.]

    按计划在到达巴士拉之前它会停靠在亚丁或其他港口。

  • They had asked Hong Kong for permission to put into port there. [VERB PREP. adv./prep.]

    他们请求香港允许他们在那里入港停留。
