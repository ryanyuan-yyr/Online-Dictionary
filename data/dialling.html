dial
daɪəl ˈdaɪəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense dials, present participle dialling, past tense , past participle dialled
in AM, use 美国英语用 dialing, dialed
1 N-COUNT 钟面;表盘;（仪表等的）刻度盘，标度盘 A dial is the part of a machine or instrument such as a clock or watch which shows you the time or a measurement that has been recorded. 钟面;表盘;（仪表等的）刻度盘，标度盘

  • The luminous dial on the clock showed five minutes to seven.

    夜光钟面显示时间为 6点 55 分。

  • The dials of most barometers are inscribed with weather terms.

    大多数气压计的盘面都刻有天气术语。

2 N-COUNT （装置或设备的）调节器，控制器 A dial is a control on a device or piece of equipment which you can move in order to adjust the setting, for example to select or change the frequency on a radio or the temperature of a heater. （装置或设备的）调节器，控制器

  • He turned the dial on the radio.

    他转动收音机的电台旋钮。

  • The heat dial was set at 150 degrees.

    温度调节器设定在 150 度。

3 N-COUNT (尤指老式电话机的)拨号盘 On some telephones, especially older ones, the dial is the disc on the front that you turn with your finger to choose the number that you want to call. The disc has holes in it, and numbers or letters behind the holes. (尤指老式电话机的)拨号盘

  • ...turning the dial on the phone.

    打转盘拨号电话

4 VERB 拨(电话号码) If you dial or if you dial a number, you turn the dial or press the buttons on a telephone in order to phone someone. 拨(电话号码)

  • He lifted the phone and dialled her number. [VERB noun]

    他拿起电话，拨打了她的号码。

  • He dialled, and spoke briefly to the duty officer. [VERB]

    他拨通了电话，跟值班员简短地说了几句。
