certify
sɜːʳtɪfaɪ ˈsɜrtəˌfaɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense certifies, present participle certifying, past tense , past participle certified
1 VERB 证明;出具证明;证实 If someone in an official position certifies something, they officially state that it is true. 证明;出具证明;证实 [Also V n adj, V n as adj]

  • ...if the president certified that the project would receive at least $650m from overseas sources. [VERB that]

    如果总统证明这个工程将获得至少6.5亿美元的海外资金

  • The National Election Council is supposed to certify the results of the election. [VERB noun]

    全国选举委员会应对选举结果出具证明。

  • It has been certified as genuine. [be V-ed as adj]

    这已经被证明是真品。

  • Mrs Simpson was certified dead. [be VERB-ed adj.ive]

    辛普森夫人被证实已经死亡。

certification

  • An employer can demand written certification that the relative is really ill.

    雇主可以要求出具书面证明，证实员工的亲人确实病了。

2 VERB [usually passive] 给…颁发合格（或结业）证书 If someone is certified as a particular kind of worker, they are given a certificate stating that they have successfully completed a course of training in their profession. 给…颁发合格（或结业）证书

  • They wanted to get certified as divers. [get V-ed + as]

    他们想拿到潜水员资格证。

  • ...a certified accountant. [VERB-ed]

    持证会计

  • All three doctors are certified as addictions specialists. [VERB-ed]

    3个医生都是持有执业证书的瘾症专家。

certification

  • Pupils would be offered on-the-job training leading to the certification of their skill in a particular field.

    学生将得到在岗培训的机会，从而获得某一行业的技能合格证书。
