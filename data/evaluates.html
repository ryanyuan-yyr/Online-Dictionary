evaluate
ɪvæljueɪt iˈvæljuˌeɪt ɪˈvæljuˌeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense evaluates, present participle evaluating, past tense , past participle evaluated
VERB 评估;评价 If you evaluate something or someone, you consider them in order to make a judgment about them, for example about how good or bad they are. 评估;评价

  • They will first send in trained nurses to evaluate the needs of the individual situation. [VERB noun]

    他们首先会派一些训练有素的护士来评估每个病人的需求。

  • The market situation is difficult to evaluate. [VERB noun]

    市场状况难以评价。

SYN assess, rate, value, judge  
evaluation

  • ...the opinions and evaluations of college supervisors.

    学院督学的意见和评价

  • Evaluation is standard practice for all training arranged through the school.

    对学校组织的所有培训进行评估是例行的做法。
