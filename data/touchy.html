touchy
tʌtʃi ˈtʌtʃi
Word forms: comparative touchier, superlative touchiest
1 ADJ-GRADED 易怒的;过于敏感的;暴躁的 If you describe someone as touchy, you mean that they are easily upset, offended, or irritated. 易怒的;过于敏感的;暴躁的 [disapproval]

  • She is very touchy about her past. [+ about]

    她对自己的过去非常敏感。

  • Don't be so touchy.

    别这么小心眼。

SYN oversensitive, irritable, bad-tempered, cross  
2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 棘手的;难办的;需谨慎处理的 If you say that something is a touchy subject, you mean that it is a subject that needs to be dealt with carefully and in a sensitive way, because it might upset or offend people. 棘手的;难办的;需谨慎处理的

  • ...the touchy question of political reform.

    政治改革这一棘手问题
