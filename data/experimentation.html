experiment
ɪksperɪmənt ɪksperɪment ɛkˈspɛrəmənt ɪkˈspɛrəmənt ɛkˈspɛrəmɛnt ɪkˈspɛrəmɛnt ɛkˈspɪrəmɛnt ɪkˈspɪrəmɛnt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense experiments, present participle experimenting, past tense , past participle experimented
The noun is pronounced /ɪk'sperɪmənt/. The verb is pronounced /ɪk'sperɪment/. 名词读作 /ɪk'sperɪmənt/。动词读作 /ɪk'sperɪment/。
1 N-VAR 实验 An experiment is a scientific test which is done in order to discover what happens to something in particular conditions. 实验

  • The astronauts are conducting a series of experiments to learn more about how the body adapts to weightlessness.

    那些宇航员正在进行一系列实验，以更多地了解身体如何适应失重状态。

  • ...a proposed new law on animal experiments.

    有关动物实验的最新法规提案

  • This question can be answered only by experiment.

    只有实验才能够解决这个问题。

SYN test, trial, investigation, examination  
2 VERB （用…）做实验 If you experiment with something or experiment on it, you do a scientific test on it in order to discover what happens to it in particular conditions. （用…）做实验

  • In 1857 Mendel started experimenting with peas in his monastery garden. [V + with/on]

    1857年，孟德尔开始在修道院的菜园里用豌豆做实验。

  • The scientists have experimented on the tiny neck arteries of rats. [V with/on n]

    科学家们已经在老鼠细小的颈动脉上做了实验。

  • The scientists have already experimented at each other's test sites. [VERB]

    科学家已经在彼此的实验场所进行了实验。

experimentation

  • ...the ethical aspects of animal experimentation.

    动物实验涉及的伦理问题

experimenter

  • When the experimenters repeated the tests on themselves, they observed an exactly opposite effect.

    当实验者在自己身上重复测试时，他们观察到了完全相反的效果。

3 N-VAR 试验;试用 An experiment is the trying out of a new idea or method in order to see what it is like and what effects it has. 试验;试用

  • As an experiment, we bought Ted a watch.

    我们试着给特德买了一块手表。

  • ...the country's five year experiment in democracy.

    该国5年来对民主的尝试

  • She needs plenty of room for experiment in her life.

    她一生需要很多尝试的机会。

4 VERB 进行试验;试用 To experiment means to try out a new idea or method to see what it is like and what effects it has. 进行试验;试用

  • ...if you like cooking and have the time to experiment. [VERB]

    如果你喜欢烹饪而且有时间尝试的话

  • He believes that students should be encouraged to experiment with bold ideas. [VERB + with]

    他认为应该鼓励学生们将大胆的观念付诸试验。

experimentation

  • Decentralization and experimentation must be encouraged.

    必须鼓励权力下放和大胆尝试。

  • His stories about his sexual experimentation were more bravado than fact.

    他讲述的那些自己进行性尝试的事，更多的是吹嘘而非事实。
