pepper
pepəʳ ˈpɛpər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense peppers, present participle peppering, past tense , past participle peppered
1 N-UNCOUNT 胡椒粉 Pepper is a hot-tasting spice which is used to flavour food. 胡椒粉

  • Season with salt and pepper.

    用盐和胡椒调味。

  • ...freshly ground black pepper.

    新磨的黑胡椒粉

SYN seasoning, flavour, spice  
2 N-COUNT 甜椒；柿子椒；灯笼椒 A pepper, or in American English a bell pepper, is a hollow green, red, or yellow vegetable with seeds inside it. 甜椒；柿子椒；灯笼椒
3 VERB [usually passive] （小物体）密集地击打，连续击打 If something is peppered with small objects, a lot of those objects hit it. （小物体）密集地击打，连续击打

  • He was wounded in both legs and severely peppered with shrapnel. [be VERB-ed + with]

    他两条腿都挂彩了，人也被如雨的弹片炸成重伤。

  • Suddenly the garden was peppered with pellets. [be V-ed with n]

    突然间花园遭到扫射。

SYN pelt, hit, shower, scatter  
4 VERB 布满；充满 If something is peppered with things, it has a lot of those things in it or on it. 布满；充满

  • While her English was correct, it was peppered with French phrases. [be VERB-ed + with]

    她的英语语法上没错，但是里面夹杂着很多法语短语。

  • Outside, the road was peppered with glass. [be V-ed with n]

    屋外，马路上尽是些玻璃碴。

  • Yachts peppered the tranquil waters of Botafogo Bay. [VERB noun]

    博塔福古湾宁静的水面上挤满了游艇。

SYN sprinkle, spot, scatter, dot
