attempt
ətempt əˈtɛmpt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense attempts, present participle attempting, past tense , past participle attempted
1 VERB 尝试，试图（尤指做困难的事） If you attempt to do something, especially something difficult, you try to do it. 尝试，试图（尤指做困难的事）

  • The only time that we attempted to do something like that was in the city of Philadelphia. [VERB to-infinitive]

    只有在费城那次我们曾试着做那样的事。

  • Before I could attempt a reply he added over his shoulder: 'Wait there.' [VERB noun]

    还没等我回答他就回头又说了一句：“在那儿等着。”

SYN try, seek, aim, struggle  
2 N-COUNT [oft NOUN to-infinitive] （通常指不成功的）尝试，企图，试图 If you make an attempt to do something, you try to do it, often without success. （通常指不成功的）尝试，企图，试图

  • ...a deliberate attempt to destabilise the defence.

    经过深思熟虑的破坏该防御的尝试

  • It was one of his rare attempts at humour. [+ at]

    那是他极不常见的想要表现幽默的一次。

  • ...their involvement in a coup attempt in September.

    他们参与了9月的那场失败的政变

SYN try, go [informal] , shot [informal] , effort  
3 N-COUNT 杀人企图 An attempt on some-one's life is an attempt to kill them. 杀人企图

  • ...an attempt on the life of the former Iranian Prime Minister. [+ on]

    试图杀害伊朗前总理

SYN attack, assault, strike, offensive  
4 N-COUNT （运动员创造纪录的）尝试，冲击 In British English, if a sportsman or sportswoman makes an attempt on a sporting record, they try to beat it. In American English you say that they make an attempt to break it. （运动员创造纪录的）尝试，冲击

  • Everything is almost ready for me to make another attempt on the record.

    一切几乎都已准备就绪，就等我向纪录发起再一次冲击了。
