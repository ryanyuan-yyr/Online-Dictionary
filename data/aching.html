ache
eɪk eɪk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense aches, present participle aching, past tense , past participle ached
1 VERB （持续并较强烈地）疼痛 If you ache or a part of your body aches, you feel a steady, fairly strong pain. （持续并较强烈地）疼痛

  • Her head was throbbing and she ached all over. [VERB adv./prep.]

    她的脑袋嗡嗡作响并且浑身疼痛。

  • My leg still aches when I sit down. [VERB]

    坐下时我的腿还会痛。

  • The weary walkers soothed their aching feet in the sea. [VERB-ing]

    走累了的人们把脚伸进海水中浸泡以缓解疼痛。

SYN hurt, suffer, burn, pain  
2 N-COUNT [n NOUN] （身体某部位持续且较强烈的)疼痛 An ache is a steady, fairly strong pain in a part of your body. （身体某部位持续且较强烈的)疼痛

  • You feel nausea and aches in your muscles.

    症状为恶心和肌肉疼痛。

  • Poor posture can cause neck ache, headaches and breathing problems.

    姿势不当会导致颈部疼痛、头痛和呼吸困难。

SYN pain, discomfort, suffering, hurt  
3See also:backache  headache  heartache  stomach ache 
4 VERB 渴望（且因得不到而痛苦） If you ache for something or your heart aches, you want something very much, and feel very unhappy because you cannot have it. 渴望（且因得不到而痛苦） [written]

  • She still ached for the lost intimacy and sexual contact of marriage. [VERB + for]

    她仍旧渴望拥有婚姻中逝去的那份缱绻和肌肤之亲。

  • But Spain was a country aching to get away from its past. [VERB to-infinitive]

    但西班牙是一个渴望摆脱以往形象的国家。

  • It was quite an achievement to keep smiling when his heart must have been aching. [VERB]

    虽然经受着内心的煎熬，但他仍强作欢颜，这实属不易。

SYN suffer, hurt, grieve, sorrow  
Ache is also a noun.

  • You also feel an overwhelming ache for support from others which you cannot put into words.

    你还会感受到一种无以言表的想要获得他人支持的渴望。

5 PHRASE （泛指较轻微的）身体疼痛 You can use aches and pains to refer in a general way to any minor pains that you feel in your body. （泛指较轻微的）身体疼痛

  • It seems to ease all the aches and pains of a hectic and tiring day.

    繁忙劳碌了一天所带来的周身酸痛似乎被一扫而空。
