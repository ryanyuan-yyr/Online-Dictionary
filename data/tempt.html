tempt
tempt tɛmpt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense tempts, present participle tempting, past tense , past participle tempted
1 VERB 引诱;诱惑;吸引 Something that tempts you attracts you and makes you want it, even though it may be wrong or harmful. 引诱;诱惑;吸引

  • Reducing the income will further impoverish these families and could tempt an offender into further crime. [VERB noun + into]

    降低收入只会使这些家庭更加贫穷，而且可能诱使不法分子进一步犯罪。

  • It is the fresh fruit that tempts me at this time of year. [VERB noun]

    一年当中这个时节，新鲜水果最能吸引我了。

  • Can I tempt you with a little puff pastry?. [VERB noun + with]

    我能给你尝点泡芙点心吗？

  • The fact that she had become wealthy did not tempt her to alter her frugal way of life. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    她有钱了，可这丝毫没能让她改变节俭的生活习惯。

SYN attract, draw, appeal to, allure  
2 VERB 怂恿;利诱;诱惑 If you tempt someone, you offer them something they want in order to encourage them to do what you want them to do. 怂恿;利诱;诱惑

  • ...a million dollar marketing campaign to tempt American tourists back to Britain. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    耗资百万美元试图把美国游客重新吸引到英国来的营销活动

  • Having spent so long at a great club like Rangers, no other Scottish team could tempt him away. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    在流浪者队这样优秀的俱乐部呆了这么久之后，其他苏格兰球队都吸引不了他了。

  • Don't let credit tempt you to buy something you can't afford. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    不要因为可以赊购，就买超过自己支付能力的东西。

  • She will be offering a package worth about 40 million dollars, to tempt the rebels into agreeing to disarm. [VERB noun + into]

    她将提出价值 4, 000 万美元的一揽子条件，以诱使反叛分子答应解除武装。

SYN entice, lure, lead on, invite  
3 See also: tempted 
4 PHRASE 说晦气话，玩过火，冒险（使好运结束） If someone says that something they say or do is tempting fate or is tempting providence, they mean they are worried that it may cause the good luck they have had so far to end. 说晦气话，玩过火，冒险（使好运结束）

  • As soon as you start to talk about never having played on a losing side, it is tempting fate.

    你刚说从来没输过，晦气就来了。
