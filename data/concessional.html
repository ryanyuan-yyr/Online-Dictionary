concession
kənseʃən kənˈsɛʃən
Word forms: plural concessions
1 N-COUNT (尤指为平息争端或冲突而作出的)让步，妥协 If you make a concession to someone, you agree to let them do or have something, especially in order to end an argument or conflict. (尤指为平息争端或冲突而作出的)让步，妥协 [Also + to/from]

  • The King made major concessions to end the confrontation with his people.

    为了结束与其臣民的冲突，国王作出了很大的让步。

2 N-COUNT 特许权;优惠 A concession is a special right or privilege that is given to someone. 特许权;优惠

  • The government has granted concessions to three private telephone companies.

    政府已经向3家私营电话公司授予了特许权。

  • ...tax concessions for mothers who stay at home with their children. [+ for]

    针对在家照顾孩子的母亲的纳税优惠

SYN privilege, right, permit, licence  
3 N-COUNT (通常指针对老人、学生和失业人员的)优惠价格 A concession is a special price which is lower than the usual price and which is often given to old people, students, and the unemployed. (通常指针对老人、学生和失业人员的)优惠价格 [British]

  • Open daily; admission £1.10 with concessions for children and OAPs. [+ for]

    每天开放;门票1.10英镑，儿童和退休人士可享受票价优惠。

in AM, use 美国英语用 reduction
4 N-COUNT (尤指在别家公司大楼内的)销售权(或经营权)协议 A concession is an arrangement where someone is given the right to sell a product or to run a business, especially in a building belonging to another business. (尤指在别家公司大楼内的)销售权(或经营权)协议 [mainly US, business]
in BRIT, usually use 英国英语通常用 franchise
