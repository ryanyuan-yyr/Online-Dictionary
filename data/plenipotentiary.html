plenipotentiary
plenɪpətenʃəri -ʃieri ˌplɛnɪpoʊˈtɛnʃiˌɛri ˌplɛnɪpoʊˈtɛnʃəri
(also Plenipotentiary)Word forms: plural plenipotentiaries
1 N-COUNT （尤指在国外代表政府的）全权代表，全权大使 A plenipotentiary is a person who has full power to make decisions or take action on behalf of their government, especially in a foreign country. （尤指在国外代表政府的）全权代表，全权大使 [formal]

  • ...the Polish government in exile's plenipotentiary in Warsaw.

    波兰流亡政府在华沙的全权代表

  • ...the British Plenipotentiary to the UN conference.

    英国在联合国大会的全权代表

SYN ambassador, minister, envoy, emissary  
2 ADJ [n ADJ] （大使、公使等）全权的 An ambassador plenipotentiary or minister plenipotentiary has full power or authority to represent their country. （大使、公使等）全权的 [formal]

  • She became Her Britannic Majesty's new ambassador plenipotentiary to the Republic of Lebanon.

    她成为了英国女王陛下驻黎巴嫩共和国的新任全权大使。

  • Edwin H. Conger was envoy extraordinary and Minister Plenipotentiary.

    埃德温·H. 康格是特命全权公使。

3 ADJ [ADJ n] 全（权）的 If someone such as an ambassador has plenipotentiary powers, they have full power or authority to represent their country. 全（权）的 [formal]

  • The Constitution grants the president plenipotentiary powers to use military force to protect our national security.

    宪法赋予总统全权来使用武力保卫国家安全。
