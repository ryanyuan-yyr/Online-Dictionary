bias
baɪəs ˈbaɪəs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense biases, present participle biasing, past tense , past participle biased
1 N-VAR 偏见；偏心；偏袒 Bias is a tendency to prefer one person or thing to another, and to favour that person or thing. 偏见；偏心；偏袒

  • Bias against women permeates every level of the judicial system.

    各级司法机构普遍存在对女性的偏见。

  • There were fierce attacks on the BBC for alleged political bias.

    英国广播公司因被指具有政治偏见而遭到猛烈抨击。

SYN prejudice, leaning, bent, tendency  
2 N-VAR 偏好；偏爱 Bias is a concern with or interest in one thing more than others. 偏好；偏爱

  • The Department has a strong bias towards neuroscience.

    这个系特别偏重神经科学。

3 VERB 使有偏见；使偏向 To bias someone means to influence them in favour of a particular choice. 使有偏见；使偏向

  • We mustn't allow it to bias our teaching. [VERB noun]

    我们决不允许它影响我们的教学。

SYN influence, colour, weight, prejudice  
4 PHRASE 斜裁 A dress or skirt that is cut on the bias or that is bias-cut has been cut diagonally across the material so that it hangs down in a particular way. 斜裁

  • The fabric, cut on the bias, hangs as light as a cobweb off a woman's body.

    这块斜裁料如蛛网一般轻盈地从一女子身上垂下来。

  • ...a bias-cut dress.

    斜裁裙装
