twig
twɪg twɪg
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense twigs, present participle twigging, past tense , past participle twigged
1 N-COUNT 细枝;嫩枝 A twig is a very small thin branch that grows out from a main branch of a tree or bush. 细枝;嫩枝
SYN branch, stick, sprig, offshoot  
2 VERB 顿悟;突然醒悟;豁然开窍 If you twig, you suddenly realize or understand something. 顿悟;突然醒悟;豁然开窍 [informal] [Also VERB]

  • Then I twigged that they were illegal immigrants. [VERB that]

    然后我突然意识到他们是非法移民。

  • By the time she'd twigged what it was all about it was too late. [VERB wh]

    等到她把一切都想通时，已为时太晚。

SYN understand, get, see, find out
