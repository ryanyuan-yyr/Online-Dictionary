muddy
mʌdi ˈmʌdi
Word forms: comparative muddier, superlative muddiest, 3rd person singular present tense muddies, present participle muddying, past tense , past participle muddied
1 ADJ-GRADED 沾满泥的;多泥的;泥泞的 Something that is muddy contains mud or is covered in mud. 沾满泥的;多泥的;泥泞的

  • ...a muddy track.

    泥泞的小道

  • The ground was still very muddy.

    地上还很泥泞。

  • ...his muddy boots.

    他沾满泥的靴子

SYN boggy, swampy, marshy, miry  
2 VERB 使沾上泥 If you muddy something, you cause it to be muddy. 使沾上泥

  • The ground still smelled of rain and they muddied their shoes. [VERB noun]

    地面还散发着雨后的气息，他们把鞋上弄得全是泥。

  • His new grey jacket was torn and muddied. [VERB-ed]

    他新买的灰色夹克被撕破了，还弄上了泥巴。

3 ADJ-GRADED [ADJ n] 暗褐色的;土黄色的 Muddy is used to describe a colour which is dull and brownish. 暗褐色的;土黄色的

  • The paper has turned a muddy colour.

    报纸已变成土黄色了。

SYN dull, flat, blurred, unclear  
4 VERB 使更为复杂;使难以理解 If someone or something muddies a situation or issue, they cause it to seem less clear and less easy to understand. 使更为复杂;使难以理解

  • It's difficult enough without muddying the issue with religion. [VERB noun]

    即使不把宗教牵扯进去，这个问题也已经够棘手的了。

  • ...the mixed motives that muddied Mr Crane's efforts. [VERB noun]

    使克兰先生的努力变得难以理解的复杂动机

muddied
PHRASE

  • Overseas the legal issues are more muddied.

    在海外，法律问题更为繁复。

  • If someone or something muddies the waters, they cause a situation or issue to seem less clear and less easy to understand.搅浑水;使局势(或问题)更不明朗

    • They keep on muddying the waters by raising other political issues.

      他们不停地提出其他政治问题，力图把水搅浑。
