laud
lɔːd lɔd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense lauds, present participle lauding, past tense , past participle lauded
VERB 嘉许；赞扬；称赞 If people laud someone, they praise and admire them. 嘉许；赞扬；称赞 [journalism]

  • He lauded the work of the UN High Commissioner for Refugees.

    他高度赞扬了联合国难民事务高级专员的工作。

  • They lauded the former president as a hero. [VERB noun + as]

    他们颂扬前总统为英雄。

  • Dickens was lauded for his social and moral sensitivity. [VERB noun + for]

    狄更斯以其敏锐的社会和道德触觉为人称道。

SYN praise, celebrate, honour, acclaim  
lauded

  • ...the most lauded actress in New York.

    纽约最受赞誉的女演员
