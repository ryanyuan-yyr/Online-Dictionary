imbibe
ɪmbaɪb ɪmˈbaɪb
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense imbibes, present participle imbibing, past tense , past participle imbibed
1 VERB 饮,喝(酒) To imbibe alcohol means to drink it. 饮,喝(酒) [formal, often humorous]

  • They were used to imbibing enormous quantities of alcohol. [VERB noun]

    他们过去习惯于狂饮烈酒。

  • No one believes that current nondrinkers should be encouraged to start imbibing. [VERB]

    没人认为应该鼓励现在滴酒不沾的人去狂喝豪饮。

SYN drink, consume, knock back [informal] , neck [slang]  
2 VERB 吸收,接受(思想或观点) If you imbibe ideas or arguments, you listen to them, accept them, and believe that they are right or true. 吸收,接受(思想或观点) [formal]

  • As a clergyman's son he'd imbibed a set of mystical beliefs from the cradle. [VERB noun]

    作为牧师的儿子,他从尚在襁褓时起就接受了一套神秘主义信仰。

SYN absorb, receive, take in, gain
