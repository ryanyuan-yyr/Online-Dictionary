moan
moʊn moʊn
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense moans, present participle moaning, past tense , past participle moaned
1 VERB 呻吟;呜咽 If you moan, you make a low sound, usually because you are unhappy or in pain. 呻吟;呜咽

  • Tony moaned in his sleep and then turned over on his side. [VERB]

    托尼在睡梦中发出呻吟声，然后翻了个身。

  • 'My head, my head,' he moaned. 'I can't see.' [VERB with quote]

    “我的头，我的头，”他呻吟着说，“我看不见了。”

SYN groan, sigh, sob, whine  
Moan is also a noun.

  • Suddenly she gave a low, choking moan and began to tremble violently.

    突然，她发出了一声低沉而哽咽的呻吟，然后剧烈地颤抖起来。

  • ...her moan of sorrow.

    她难过的呻吟声

2 VERB 抱怨;发牢骚 To moan means to complain or speak in a way which shows that you are very unhappy. 抱怨;发牢骚 [disapproval]

  • I used to moan if I didn't get at least six hours' sleep at night. [VERB]

    过去如果晚上的睡眠时间不足6小时的话，我就会抱怨。

  • ...moaning about the weather. [VERB prep./adv.]

    抱怨天气

  • They moan on a lot about money. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他们对钱的事情抱怨不已。

  • Meg moans, 'I hated it!'. [VERB with quote]

    梅格抱怨道：“我不想这样！”

  • The gardener was moaning that he had another garden to do later that morning. [VERB that]

    园丁当时抱怨说，上午晚些时候他还要到另一个花园干活。

SYN grumble, complain, groan [informal] , whine  
3 N-COUNT 抱怨 A moan is a complaint. 抱怨 [informal]

  • They have been listening to people's gripes, moans and praise.

    他们一直在倾听人们的牢骚、抱怨和表扬。

SYN complaint, protest, grumble, beef [slang]  
4 PHRASE 抱怨；发牢骚 If you have a moan, you complain about something. 抱怨；发牢骚 [informal]

  • You can go see him and have a good old moan.

    你可以去看看他，好好地诉诉苦。

5 N-COUNT 低沉的声音 A moan is a low noise. 低沉的声音 [literary]

  • ...the occasional moan of the wind round the corners of the house. [+ of]

    风刮过屋角时间或发出的呼啸声

  • ...the moan of distant traffic.

    远处车流传来的低沉的嘈杂声

SYN sigh, whisper, murmur
