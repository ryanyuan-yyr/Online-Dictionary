impel
ɪmpel ɪmˈpɛl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense impels, present participle impelling, past tense , past participle impelled
VERB 激励;驱策;迫使 When something such as an emotion impels you to do something, it affects you so strongly that you feel forced to do it. 激励;驱策;迫使

  • ...the courage and competitiveness which impels him to take risks. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    促使他冒险的勇气和竞争精神

  • I felt impelled to go on speaking. [VERB-ed]

    我感到不得不继续说下去。

SYN force, move, compel, drive
