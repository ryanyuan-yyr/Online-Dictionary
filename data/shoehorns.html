shoehorn
ʃuːhɔːʳn ʃuˌhɔrn
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , plural shoehorns, present participle shoehorning, past tense , past participle shoehorned
1 N-COUNT 鞋拔 A shoehorn is a piece of metal or plastic with a slight curve that you put in the back of your shoe so that your heel will go into the shoe easily. 鞋拔
2 VERB 把…硬塞进 If you shoehorn something into a tight place, you manage to get it in there even though it is difficult. 把…硬塞进

  • Their cars are shoehorned into tiny spaces. [be VERB-ed + into]

    他们的车硬被塞进了狭小的空间。

  • I was shoehorning myself into my skin-tight ball gown. [VERB noun + into]

    我正拼命把自己塞进我的紧身晚礼服里。
