outpace
aʊtpeɪs ˌaʊtˈpeɪs
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense outpaces, present participle outpacing, past tense , past participle outpaced
VERB 比…快；超过；胜过 To outpace someone or something means to perform a particular action faster or better than they can. 比…快；超过；胜过

  • These hovercraft can easily outpace most boats. [VERB noun]

    这些气垫船在速度上能轻而易举地超过大多数船只。

  • The Japanese economy will continue to outpace its foreign rivals for years to come. [VERB noun]

    在未来几年里，日本的经济发展将继续快过其国外的对手。

  • She outpaced the former Olympic champion Evelyn Ashford. [VERB noun]

    她超过了前奥运冠军伊芙琳·阿什福德。

SYN outdistance, leave behind, outstrip, lose
