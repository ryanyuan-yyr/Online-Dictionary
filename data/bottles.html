bottle
bɒtəl ˈbɑtəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense bottles, present participle bottling, past tense , past participle bottled
1 N-COUNT 瓶子 A bottle is a glass or plastic container in which drinks and other liquids are kept. Bottles are usually round with straight sides and a narrow top. 瓶子

  • There were two empty beer bottles on the table.

    桌子上有两个空啤酒瓶。

  • He was pulling the cork from a bottle of wine.

    他正在启一瓶葡萄酒的瓶塞。

  • ...Victorian scent bottles.

    维多利亚时代风格的香水瓶

SYN flask, pitcher, decanter, carafe  
A bottle of something is an amount of it contained in a bottle.一瓶之量

  • He had drunk half a bottle of whisky.

    他已经喝掉了半瓶威士忌。

2 VERB 用瓶装；把…装入瓶中 To bottle a drink or other liquid means to put it into bottles after it has been made. 用瓶装；把…装入瓶中

  • This is a large truck which has equipment to automatically bottle the wine. [VERB noun]

    这是一辆大型卡车，拥有自动将葡萄酒装瓶的设备。

  • ...bottled water. [VERB-ed]

    瓶装水

3 N-COUNT 奶瓶 A bottle is a drinking container used by babies. It has a special rubber part at the top through which they can suck their drink. 奶瓶

  • Gary was holding a bottle to the baby's lips.

    加里正拿着一个奶瓶给婴儿喂奶。

A bottle of milk or other drink is an amount of it contained in a baby's bottle.（牛奶或其他饮品的）一瓶之量
4 VERB 将（水果）装罐瓶保存 To bottle fruit means to put it into special jars, in order to preserve it. 将（水果）装罐瓶保存

  • Did she do things like bottling fruit or making jam? [VERB noun]

    她干过将水果装瓶或做果酱之类的活计吗？

  • ...bottled plums. [VERB-ed]

    装罐贮藏的李子

5 N-UNCOUNT 勇气；胆量 Bottle is used to refer to courage or boldness. 勇气；胆量 [British, informal]

  • But will anyone have the bottle to go through with it?

    但是谁会有胆量经受这件事？

SYN nerve, will, daring, courage  
6 See also: bottled  feeding bottle  hot-water bottle  water bottle 
7 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 酗酒 If someone hits the bottle, they drink a lot of alcohol. 酗酒 [informal]

  • After my mother died my father started hitting the bottle.

    我母亲死后父亲开始酗酒。

SYN start drinking, take to drink, go on a bender [informal] , drown your sorrows  
8 PHRASE [VERB inflects] （在最后时刻）因胆怯而放弃，打退堂鼓 If you say that someone has bottled it, you mean that they have lost their courage at the last moment and have not done something they intended to do. （在最后时刻）因胆怯而放弃，打退堂鼓 [British, informal]

  • He was scheduled to appear on the Russell Harty Show, but bottled it at the last minute.

    他原定参加拉塞尔·哈蒂主持的节目，但在最后时刻打了退堂鼓。
