modulate
mɒdʒʊleɪt ˈmɑdʒəˌleɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense modulates, present participle modulating, past tense , past participle modulated
1 VERB 改变，调节(嗓音或声音) If you modulate your voice or a sound, you change or vary its loudness, pitch, or tone in order to create a particular effect. 改变，调节(嗓音或声音) [written] [Also VERB]

  • He carefully modulated his voice. [VERB noun]

    他小心地压低了声音。

SYN adjust, balance, vary, tone  
modulated

  • 'Who's this?' asked a well-modulated voice.

    一个优美的声音问道：“谁呀？”

2 VERB 调节;调整 To modulate an activity or process means to alter it so that it is more suitable for a particular situation. 调节;调整 [formal]

  • These chemicals modulate the effect of potassium. [VERB noun]

    这些化学物质可以调节钾的功效。

modulation

  • The famine turned the normal modulation of climate into disaster.

    正常的气候变化因饥荒而成了灾难。
