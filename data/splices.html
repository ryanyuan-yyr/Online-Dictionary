splice
splaɪs splaɪs
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense splices, present participle splicing, past tense , past participle spliced
1 VERB 绞接(绳子);粘接(胶片、磁带等) If you splice two pieces of rope, film, or tape together, you join them neatly at the ends so that they make one continuous piece. 绞接(绳子);粘接(胶片、磁带等)

  • He taught me to edit and splice film. [VERB noun]

    他教我剪辑和粘接胶片。

  • The film will be spliced with footage of Cypress Hill to be filmed in America. [be VERB-ed]

    这部电影要和将在美国拍摄的柏树山乐队的音乐片段粘接在一起。

SYN join, unite, graft, marry  
2 V-PASSIVE 结婚 When two people get spliced, they get married. 结婚 [informal, old-fashioned]

  • An old friend of mine, newly spliced, recently invited me to dinner in his new marital home. [VERB-ed]

    我的一位刚结婚不久的老朋友最近邀请我到他的婚房去吃晚饭。
