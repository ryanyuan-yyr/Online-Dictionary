manage
mænɪdʒ ˈmænɪdʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense manages, present participle managing, past tense , past participle managed
1 VERB 管理;经营;掌管 If you manage an organization, business, or system, or the people who work in it, you are responsible for controlling them. 管理;经营;掌管

  • Within two years he was managing the store. [VERB noun]

    不出两年他就开始管理这家店了。

  • Most factories in the area are obsolete and badly managed. [be VERB-ed]

    这个地区的绝大多数工厂都十分陈旧，经营状况也非常糟糕。

  • There is a lack of confidence in the government's ability to manage the economy. [VERB noun]

    人民对该政府管理经济的能力缺乏信心。

  • Professors are notoriously difficult to manage. [VERB noun]

    教授们很难管理是出了名的。

SYN be in charge of, run, handle, rule  
2 VERB 谨慎地使用，好好地利用(时间、金钱等) If you manage time, money, or other resources, you deal with them carefully and do not waste them. 谨慎地使用，好好地利用(时间、金钱等)

  • In a busy world, managing your time is increasingly important. [VERB noun]

    在忙碌的世界里，善于利用时间变得越来越重要。

  • Josh expects me to manage all the household expenses on very little. [VERB noun]

    乔希指望我对每笔家庭支出都精打细算。

SYN organize, use, handle, govern  
3 VERB 设法做到;成功完成 If you manage to do something, especially something difficult, you succeed in doing it. 设法做到;成功完成

  • Somehow, he'd managed to persuade Kay to buy one for him. [VERB to-infinitive]

    不知用了什么方法，他成功说服凯给他买了一个。

  • I managed to pull myself up onto a wet, sloping ledge. [VERB to-infinitive]

    我终于爬上了一块潮湿倾斜的暗礁。

  • Over the past 12 months the company has managed a 10 per cent improvement. [VERB noun]

    在过去的12个月里，公司的业绩提高了10%。

4 VERB 成功对付;设法应对 If you manage, you succeed in coping with a difficult situation. 成功对付;设法应对

  • She had managed perfectly well without medication for three years. [VERB]

    3年来，她一直没有服药却挺了过来。

  • I am managing, but I could not possibly give up work. [VERB]

    我正在努力应付，但我不可能放弃工作。

  • How did your mother manage when your father left? [VERB]

    你父亲走了以后，你母亲是怎么熬过来的呢？

SYN cope, survive, shift, succeed  
5 VERB 抽出，腾出，安排出(时间、金钱等) If you say that you can manage an amount of time or money for something, you mean that you can afford to spend that time or money on it. 抽出，腾出，安排出(时间、金钱等)

  • This makes it ideal for those who can only manage a few hours in the morning or evening. [VERB noun]

    对于那些只能在早晚抽出几个小时的人来说，这是最理想不过的了。

  • 'All right, I can manage a fiver,' McMinn said with reluctance. [VERB noun]

    麦克明不情愿地说：“好吧，我可以出5镑。”

SYN perform, do, deal with, achieve  
6 VERB 勉强做出（微笑、寒暄等);强作 If you say that someone managed a particular response, such as a laugh or a greeting, you mean that it was difficult for them to do it because they were feeling sad or upset. 勉强做出（微笑、寒暄等);强作

  • He looked dazed as he spoke to reporters, managing only a weak smile. [VERB noun]

    跟记者谈话的时候，他只是勉强挤出一丝淡淡的微笑，显得非常恍惚。

  • He managed a few sentences about his visit to the prison. [VERB noun]

    他勉强描述了一下探监情况。

  • Now is the time to forge ahead with all the enthusiasm and optimism that you can manage. [VERB noun]

    现在到了拿出你最大的热情和积极性，努力进取的时候了。

7 CONVENTION （对他人给予帮助的婉拒）我能对付，我能行 You say 'I can manage' or 'I'll manage' as a way of refusing someone's offer of help and insisting on doing something by yourself. （对他人给予帮助的婉拒）我能对付，我能行

  • I know you mean well, but I can manage by myself.

    我知道您是一番好意，不过我一个人能行。

  • 'I'll do it, Eleanor,' Angus said, quickly jumping up. But this time she only shook her head. 'I'll manage,' she said firmly.

    安格斯一跃而起道：“让我来，埃莉诺。”可是，这一次她只是摇了摇头，坚定地说：“我能应付。”
