caper
keɪpəʳ ˈkeɪpər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense capers, present participle capering, past tense , past participle capered
1 N-COUNT [usually plural] 刺山柑花蕾 Capers are the small green buds of caper plants. They are usually sold preserved in vinegar. 刺山柑花蕾
2 VERB 欢跃;雀跃 If you caper about, you run and jump around because you are happy or excited. 欢跃;雀跃

  • They were capering about, shouting and laughing. [VERB adv./prep.]

    他们欢呼雀跃，放声大笑。

  • Painted musicians capered behind gorgeous banners. [VERB adv./prep.]

    涂脂抹粉的音乐家们在绚丽的横幅后面欢跳雀跃。

SYN dance, trip, spring, jump  
3 N-COUNT [usually supplement NOUN] 欺骗行为;不法活动 A dishonest or illegal activity can be referred to as a caper . 欺骗行为;不法活动 [informal]

  • She served six months in prison for the helicopter caper.

    她因为直升机一案坐了6个月牢。

4 N-COUNT [usually supplement NOUN] 恶作剧;玩笑;胡闹 Activities or behaviour that are not at all serious can be referred to as capers . 恶作剧;玩笑;胡闹 [informal]

  • Jack would have nothing to do with such capers.

    杰克不愿扯上这种荒唐的事。

  • What a mess we were in at the end of this caper — hair, eyes, cheeks and neck covered in treacle.

    胡闹了一通后,我们浑身脏得一塌糊涂—头发、眼睛、脸颊、脖子上,全是糖浆。

SYN escapade, sport, stunt, mischief
