muffle
mʌfəl ˈmʌfəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense muffles, present participle muffling, past tense , past participle muffled
VERB 使(声音)低沉;使(音量)减弱 If something muffles a sound, it makes it quieter and more difficult to hear. 使(声音)低沉;使(音量)减弱

  • Blake held his handkerchief over the mouthpiece to muffle his voice. [VERB noun]

    布莱克把手绢包在话筒上，以使自己的声音听起来模糊不清。

  • She heard a muffled cough behind her. [VERB-ed]

    她听到身后一声低沉的咳嗽。

SYN deaden, suppress, gag, stifle
