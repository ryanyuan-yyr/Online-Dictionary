migrate
maɪgreɪt maɪgreɪt ˈmaɪˌgreɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense migrates, present participle migrating, past tense , past participle migrated
1 VERB 移居，迁徙(尤指到外地寻找工作或暂住一段时间) If people migrate, they move from one place to another, especially in order to find work or to live somewhere for a short time. 移居，迁徙(尤指到外地寻找工作或暂住一段时间)

  • People migrate to cities like Jakarta in search of work. [VERB prep./adv.]

    人们为找工作而迁移到雅加达这类城市里。

  • Farmers have learned that they have to migrate if they want to survive. [VERB]

    农民们已经认识到，想要生存就必须外出打工。

SYN move, travel, journey, wander  
migration

  • ...the migration of Soviet Jews to Israel.

    苏联犹太人向以色列的迁徙

2 VERB (鸟类、鱼类、兽类等随季节变化)迁徙 When birds, fish, or animals migrate, they move at a particular season from one part of the world or from one part of a country to another, usually in order to breed or to find new feeding grounds. (鸟类、鱼类、兽类等随季节变化)迁徙

  • Most birds have to fly long distances to migrate. [VERB]

    绝大多数鸟类为了迁徙都不得不进行长距离的飞行。

  • ...a dam system that kills the fish as they migrate from streams to the ocean. [VERB prep./adv.]

    导致鱼类在从溪流向海洋迁徙途中死亡的水坝体系

migration

  • ...the migration of animals in the Serengeti.

    塞伦盖蒂地区的动物迁徙
