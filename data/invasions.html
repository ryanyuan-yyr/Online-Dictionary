invasion
ɪnveɪʒən ɪnˈveɪʒən
Word forms: plural invasions
1 N-VAR [oft adjective NOUN] 武装入侵;侵略 If there is an invasion of a country, a foreign army enters it by force. 武装入侵;侵略

  • ...seven years after the Roman invasion of Britain. [+ of]

    罗马人入侵英国7年以后

  • He was commander in chief during the invasion of Panama.

    在侵略巴拿马的战役中他是总司令。

SYN attack, assault, capture, takeover  
2 N-VAR (令人不快的或难以对付的人或事物的)大批进入，涌入 If you refer to the arrival of a large number of people or things as an invasion, you are emphasizing that they are unpleasant or difficult to deal with. (令人不快的或难以对付的人或事物的)大批进入，涌入

  • ...this year's annual invasion of flies, wasps and ants. [+ of]

    今年，苍蝇、黄蜂和蚂蚁同往年一样的大批进犯

  • Seaside resorts such as Blackpool and Brighton are preparing for a tourist invasion.

    像布莱克浦与布赖顿这样的海滨胜地正为游客的大批涌入做准备。

SYN flood, flow, rush, arrival  
3 N-VAR 侵扰;干扰 If you describe an action as an invasion, you disapprove of it because it affects someone or something in a way that is not wanted. 侵扰;干扰 [disapproval]

  • Is reading a child's diary always a gross invasion of privacy? [+ of]

    偷看孩子的日记一定是对个人隐私的严重侵犯吗？

  • ...the first skirmish in the invasion of the British Parliament by Brussels.

    布鲁塞尔干扰英国议会的首次摩擦

SYN intrusion, breach, violation, disturbance
