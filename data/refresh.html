refresh
rɪfreʃ rɪˈfrɛʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense refreshes, present participle refreshing, past tense , past participle refreshed
1 VERB 使精神抖擞;使精神振奋;使重新充满活力 If something refreshes you when you have become hot, tired, or thirsty, it makes you feel cooler or more energetic. 使精神抖擞;使精神振奋;使重新充满活力

  • The lotion cools and refreshes the skin. [VERB noun]

    这种润肤液凉爽清新，能够焕发肌肤的活力。

  • They had stopped by a spring to refresh themselves. [VERB noun]

    他们曾在一处泉边停歇休息。

SYN revive, cool, freshen, revitalize  
refreshed

  • He awoke feeling completely refreshed.

    他醒来感到彻底恢复了精力。

2 VERB 翻新;使变新 If you refresh something old or dull, you make it as good as it was when it was new. 翻新;使变新

  • Many view these meetings as an occasion to share ideas and refresh friendship. [VERB noun]

    许多人把这些会面看成是交流想法和保持友谊长青的机会。

  • Lettie appeared, her make-up refreshed. [VERB-ed]

    莱蒂出现了，她重新上过妆。

SYN replenish, restore, repair, renew  
3 VERB 使记起;提醒;提示 If someone refreshes your memory, they tell you something that you had forgotten. 使记起;提醒;提示

  • Allow me to refresh your memory. [VERB noun]

    让我帮你回忆吧。

  • He walked on the opposite side of the street to refresh his memory of the building. [VERB noun]

    他走在街的对面，努力回忆这座建筑的原貌。

SYN stimulate, prompt, renew, jog  
4 VERB 刷新（网页） If you refresh a web page, you click a button in order to get the most recent version of the page. 刷新（网页） [computing]

  • Press the 'reload' button on your web browser to refresh the site and get the most current version. [VERB noun]

    点击网页浏览器上的“刷新”按钮来刷新页面，显示最新的版本。
