interfere
ɪntəʳfɪəʳ ˌɪntərˈfɪr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense interferes, present participle interfering, past tense , past participle interfered
1 VERB 干涉;干预;介入 If you say that someone interferes in a situation, you mean they get involved in it although it does not concern them and their involvement is not wanted. 干涉;干预;介入 [disapproval]

  • I wish everyone would stop interfering and just leave me alone. [VERB]

    我希望大家都别再来干涉我,让我一个人静一静。

  • The UN cannot interfere in the internal affairs of any country.

    联合国不能干涉任何国家的内政。

SYN meddle, intervene, intrude, butt in  
2 VERB 妨碍;冲突;抵触 Something that interferes with a situation, activity, or process has a damaging effect on it. 妨碍;冲突;抵触

  • Drug problems frequently interfered with his work. [VERB with noun]

    吸毒问题频频干扰他的工作。

  • Alexander wasn't going to let a lack of space interfere with his plans. [VERB with noun]

    亚历山大不会让空间不够的问题妨碍他的计划。

  • Smoking and drinking interfere with your body's ability to process oxygen. [VERB + with]

    抽烟与酗酒有碍身体处理氧气的能力。
