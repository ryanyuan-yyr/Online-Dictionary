terrible
terɪbəl ˈtɛrəbəl
1 ADJ-GRADED 非常严重的;骇人的;糟糕的 A terrible experience or situation is very serious or very unpleasant. 非常严重的;骇人的;糟糕的

  • Tens of thousands more suffered terrible injuries in the world's worst industrial disaster.

    还有成千上万的人在那场全世界最严重的工业灾难中受了重伤。

  • I often have the most terrible nightmares.

    我常做很吓人的噩梦。

  • Prison life, he told me, was terrible.

    他告诉我，监狱生活很可怕。

SYN bad, awful, dreadful, beastly [informal]  
terribly

  • My son has suffered terribly. He has lost his best friend.

    我儿子遭受了巨大痛苦，他失去了最要好的朋友。

2 ADJ-GRADED [feel/look ADJ] (感觉或看起来)病得很重的，心情很糟的 If you feel terrible, you feel extremely ill or unhappy. If you tell someone that they look terrible, you mean that they look as if they are extremely ill or unhappy. (感觉或看起来)病得很重的，心情很糟的

  • He did feel terrible at the time but seems to be fine now.

    他当时确实很难受，但现在似乎好了。

  • Are you all right? You look terrible. Are you sick?

    你没事儿吧？你的脸色很难看，生病了吗？

SYN ill, sick, unwell, poorly [informal]  
3 ADJ-GRADED 质量极差的;很糟糕的 If something is terrible, it is very bad or of very poor quality. 质量极差的;很糟糕的

  • She admits her French is terrible.

    她承认自己的法语很蹩脚。

SYN very bad, poor, inadequate, awful  
4 ADJ [ADJ n] (用来加强语气)十足的，极度的，不折不扣的 You use terrible to emphasize the great extent or degree of something. (用来加强语气)十足的，极度的，不折不扣的 [emphasis]

  • I was a terrible fool, you know. I remember that now.

    你知道，我那时愚蠢至极。我至今还记得。

  • Her death is a terrible waste.

    她死得太不值了。

terribly

  • I'm terribly sorry to bother you at this hour.

    这个时候打搅您，我万分抱歉。

  • I suffered terribly when she died.

    她死的时候，我伤心透了。
