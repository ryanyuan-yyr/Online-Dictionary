fuel
fjuːəl ˈfjuəl fjul
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense fuels, present participle fuelling, past tense , past participle fuelled
in AM, use 美国英语用 fueling, fueled
1 N-MASS 燃料 Fuel is a substance such as coal, oil, or petrol that is burned to provide heat or power. 燃料

  • They ran out of fuel.

    他们的燃料用完了。

  • ...industrial research into cleaner fuels.

    对更清洁燃料的工业研究

SYN petrol, oil, gas [US , Canadian , New Zealand] , gasoline [US]  
2 VERB 刺激;使变得更糟;使加剧 To fuel a situation means to make it become worse or more intense. 刺激;使变得更糟;使加剧

  • The result will inevitably fuel speculation about the Prime Minister's future. [VERB noun]

    这一结果将不可避免地加剧人们对首相未来的揣测。

  • The economic boom was fueled by easy credit. [be VERB-ed]

    宽松的信贷政策刺激了经济繁荣。

SYN inflame, power, charge, fire  
3 PHRASE (对…)火上浇油 If something adds fuel to a conflict or debate, or adds fuel to the fire, it makes the conflict or debate more intense. (对…)火上浇油

  • His comments are bound to add fuel to the debate.

    他的话必将为争论推波助澜。

  • The decision to raise tariffs on imports will only add fuel to the fire.

    上调进口关税的决定只会火上浇油。
