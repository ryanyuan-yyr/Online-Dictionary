inscribe
ɪnskraɪb ɪnˈskraɪb
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense inscribes, present participle inscribing, past tense , past participle inscribed
1 VERB 写;刻;雕 If you inscribe words on an object, you write or carve the words on the object. 写;刻;雕

  • Some galleries commemorate donors by inscribing their names on the walls. [VERB noun + on]

    一些美术馆把捐赠者的姓名镌刻在墙上以示纪念。

  • She and Mark read the words inscribed on the inner walls of the monument. [V-ed on/with n]

    她和马克读了刻在纪念碑内墙上的文字。

  • ...stone slabs inscribed with Buddhist texts. [V-ed + with]

    镌刻着佛经的石板

  • ...a silver cigarette-case, inscribed 'To Laura, with all my love, Leonard'. [V-ed quote]

    刻有“献给劳拉,深爱你的伦纳德”字样的一个银质烟盒

SYN carve, cut, etch, engrave  
2 VERB 题写;题献;题赠 If you inscribe something in the front of a book or on a photograph, you write it there, often before giving it to someone. 题写;题献;题赠

  • On the back I had inscribed the words: 'Here's to Great Ideas! John'. [VERB noun]

    在背后我题写了这样的话：“愿奇思妙想不绝！约翰”。

  • The book is inscribed: To John Arlott from Laurie Lee. [V-ed quote]

    书的题赠是：献给约翰·阿洛特—劳丽·李。

SYN dedicate, sign, address
