override
oʊvəʳraɪd oʊvəʳraɪd ˌəʊvəˈraɪd ˌoʊvərˈraɪd ˈoʊvərˌraɪd
(also over-ride)Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense overrides, present participle overriding, past tense overrode, past participle overridden
The verb is pronounced /ˌəʊvə'raɪd/. The noun is pronounced /'əʊvəraɪd/. 动词读作 /ˌəʊvə'raɪd/。名词读作 /'əʊvəraɪd/。
1 VERB 凌驾于；优先于；比…更重要 If one thing in a situation overrides other things, it is more important than them. 凌驾于；优先于；比…更重要

  • The welfare of a child should always override the wishes of its parents. [VERB noun]

    孩子的幸福安康应该永远比父母的愿望来得更重要。

  • Their work is frequently an obsession that overrides all other considerations. [VERB noun]

    他们对工作的痴迷常常超乎其他一切。

SYN outweigh, overcome, eclipse, supersede  
2 VERB （以更大的权力）撤销，推翻，否决 If someone in authority overrides a person or their decisions, they cancel their decisions. （以更大的权力）撤销，推翻，否决

  • The president vetoed the bill, and the Senate failed by a single vote to override his veto. [VERB noun]

    总统否决了该议案，而参议院以一票之差未能推翻他的否决。

  • I'm applying in advance for the authority to override him. [VERB noun]

    我提前申请当局对他进行否决。

  • Big companies think they can sometimes override local opinion. [VERB noun]

    大公司认为它们对地方舆论有时候可以置之不理。

SYN overrule, reverse, cancel, overturn  
3 N-COUNT （对决定等的）撤销，推翻，否决 An override is an attempt to cancel someone's decisions by using your authority over them or by gaining more votes than them in an election or contest. （对决定等的）撤销，推翻，否决 [US]

  • The bill now goes to the House where an override vote is expected to fail.

    该议案现在提交到众议院，在这里否决投票预计不会获得通过。

  • An override of the veto appears unlikely.

    看来推翻这项否决的可能性很小。
