suffice
səfaɪs səˈfaɪs səˈfaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense suffices, present participle sufficing, past tense , past participle sufficed
1 VERB [no cont] 足够;充足 If you say that something will suffice, you mean it will be enough to achieve a purpose or to fulfil a need. 足够;充足 [formal] [Also VERB to-infinitive]

  • A cover letter should never exceed one page; often a far shorter letter will suffice. [VERB]

    附信不应超过一页，通常来说，一封相对非常简短的信就足够了。

SYN be enough, do, be sufficient, be adequate  
2 PHRASE (用于句首)无须多说，只需说…就够了 Suffice it to say or suffice to say is used at the beginning of a statement to indicate that what you are saying is obvious, or that you will only give a short explanation. (用于句首)无须多说，只需说…就够了

  • Suffice it to say that afterwards we never met again.

    简单地说，此后我们再也没有见过面。

  • Suffice to say, it was more than a couple of years ago!

    我只想说，那是几年前的事了！
