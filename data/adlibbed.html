ad-lib
ˌædˈlɪb
(also ad lib)Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense ad-libs, present participle ad-libbing, past tense , past participle ad-libbed
1 VERB 即兴演出，临时插入（脚本或讲稿中没有的台词） If you ad-lib something in a play or a speech, you say something which has not been planned or written beforehand. 即兴演出，临时插入（脚本或讲稿中没有的台词）

  • He began comically ad-libbing a script. [VERB noun]

    他即兴调侃起了一段剧本。

  • He's good at ad-libbing his way out of trouble. [VERB noun]

    他善于为自己妙语解围。

  • He is rather disjointed when he ad-libs. [VERB]

    他即兴表演时，有些语无伦次。

  • ...ad-libbed phrases. [VERB-ed]

    临时插入的话

SYN improvise, speak off the cuff, vamp, busk  
2 N-COUNT 即兴（或临时插入）的台词 An ad-lib is something which is said without having been planned or written beforehand. 即兴（或临时插入）的台词

  • Every time I fluffed a line Lenny got me out of trouble with a brilliant ad-lib.

    每次我台词出错时，伦尼都通过机智的临场发挥帮我解围。

SYN improvisation, makeshift, impromptu comment, expedient  
Ad lib is also an adverb.

  • I spoke from the pulpit ad lib.

    我在讲坛上发表即兴演说。
