smoulder
smoʊldəʳ ˈsmoʊldər ˈsməʊldə
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense smoulders, present participle smouldering, past tense , past participle smouldered
in AM, use 美国英语用 smolder
1 VERB (有烟、无火焰地)闷烧，阴燃 If something smoulders, it burns slowly, producing smoke but not flames. (有烟、无火焰地)闷烧，阴燃

  • A number of buildings around the Parliament were still smouldering today. [VERB]

    国会周围的一些大楼今天还在冒烟。

  • Whole blocks had been turned into smouldering rubble. [VERB-ing]

    整座整座的街区都变成了一堆冒着青烟的瓦砾。

SYN smoke, burn slowly  
2 VERB (愤怒、仇恨等)郁积，憋在心里 If a feeling such as anger or hatred smoulders inside you, you continue to feel it but do not show it. (愤怒、仇恨等)郁积，憋在心里

  • Baxter smouldered as he drove home for lunch. [VERB]

    巴克斯特开车回家吃午饭的路上，心里一直憋着一团火。

  • That's a lot of people smouldering with resentment. [VERB]

    许多人的心里都在愤愤不平。

  • There is a smouldering anger in the black community. [VERB-ing]

    在黑人社区里，人们的心中都揣着一团怒火。

SYN seethe, rage, fume, burn  
3 VERB 性感迷人 If you say that someone smoulders, you mean that they are sexually attractive, usually in a mysterious or very intense way. 性感迷人

  • Melanie Griffith seems to smoulder with sexuality. [VERB + with]

    梅拉妮·格里菲思显得很性感。

  • His darkly smouldering eyes never left her face. [VERB-ing]

    他那双性感迷人的黑眼睛一直盯着她的面庞。
