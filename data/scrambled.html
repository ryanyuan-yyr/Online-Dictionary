scramble
skræmbəl ˈskræmbəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense scrambles, present participle scrambling, past tense , past participle scrambled
1 VERB (迅速地)爬,攀登 If you scramble over rocks or up a hill, you move quickly over them or up it using your hands to help you. (迅速地)爬,攀登

  • Tourists were scrambling over the rocks looking for the perfect camera angle. [VERB prep./adv.]

    游客们在正爬上岩石寻找最佳的拍摄角度。

  • He scrambled up a steep bank. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他攀上了一个陡峭的河堤。

SYN struggle, climb, clamber, push  
2 VERB 仓促行动;慌忙前往 If you scramble to a different place or position, you move there in a hurried, awkward way. 仓促行动;慌忙前往

  • Ann threw back the covers and scrambled out of bed. [VERB prep./adv.]

    安掀开被子，急忙下了床。

  • He scrambled to his feet. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他赶忙站起身来。

3 VERB 争夺;抢夺 If a number of people scramble for something, they compete energetically with each other for it. 争夺;抢夺

  • More than three million fans are expected to scramble for tickets. [VERB + for]

    预计会有超过 300 万的球迷抢购门票。

  • Business is booming and foreigners are scrambling to invest. [VERB to-infinitive]

    商业繁荣，外国人争相投资。

SYN strive, rush, contend, vie  
Scramble is also a noun.

  • ...the scramble for jobs.

    对工作机会的争夺

  • ...a scramble to get a seat on the early morning flight.

    争订凌晨班机的座位

4 VERB 炒(蛋) If you scramble eggs, you break them, mix them together and then heat and stir the mixture in a pan. 炒(蛋)

  • Make the toast and scramble the eggs. [VERB noun]

    把面包烤一下并炒些鸡蛋。

scrambled

  • ...scrambled eggs and bacon.

    炒蛋和熏肉

5 VERB 倒换(无线电或电话信号)的频率(使只有持特殊仪器的人才能接收) If a device scrambles a radio or telephone message, it interferes with the sound so that the message can only be understood by someone with special equipment. 倒换(无线电或电话信号)的频率(使只有持特殊仪器的人才能接收)

  • The latest machines scramble the messages so that the conversation cannot easily be intercepted. [VERB noun]

    最新的机器对信号作扰频处理，通话就不易被侦听了。

SYN jumble, mix up, muddle, shuffle
