merge
mɜːʳdʒ mɜrdʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense merges, present participle merging, past tense , past participle merged
1 V-RECIP-ERG (使)合并;(使)融合 If one thing merges with another, or is merged with another, they combine or come together to make one whole thing. You can also say that two things merge, or are merged . (使)合并;(使)融合 [Also VERB n + into]

  • My life merged with his. [VERB with noun]

    我和他的生活合而为一。

  • Bank of America merged with a rival bank. [VERB + with]

    美国银行与一家竞争银行合并了。

  • The rivers merge just north of a vital irrigation system. [VERB]

    就在一个重要的灌溉系统的北边，这些河流交汇在一起。

  • The two countries merged into one. [VERB + into]

    两国合而为一。

  • He sees sense in merging the two agencies while both are new. [VERB noun]

    他觉得将这两家建立不久的机构合而为一是明智的。

  • Then he showed me how to merge the graphic with text on the same screen. [VERB noun + with]

    然后他教我如何将图表和文字合并在同一个屏幕上。

SYN combine, blend, fuse, amalgamate  
2 V-RECIP 渐渐消失于;融入;相融 If one sound, colour, or object merges into another, the first changes so gradually into the second that you do not notice the change. 渐渐消失于;融入;相融

  • Like a chameleon, he could merge unobtrusively into the background. [VERB + into]

    他就像一条变色龙，可以神不知鬼不觉地隐藏在背景中间。

  • His features merged with the darkness. [VERB + with]

    他的面容消失在黑暗之中。

  • Night and day begin to merge. [VERB]

    夜尽昼来。

SYN melt, blend, incorporate, mingle
