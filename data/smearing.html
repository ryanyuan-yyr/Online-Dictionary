smear
smɪəʳ smɪr
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense smears, present participle smearing, past tense , past participle smeared
1 VERB (用润滑、黏腻之物)涂，抹 If you smear a surface with an oily or sticky substance or smear the substance onto the surface, you spread a layer of the substance over the surface. (用润滑、黏腻之物)涂，抹

  • My sister smeared herself with suntan oil and slept by the swimming pool. [VERB noun + with]

    我姐姐在身上抹了防晒油，躺在游泳池边睡着了。

  • Smear a little olive oil over the inside of the salad bowl. [VERB noun prep.]

    在色拉碗的内壁上抹一点橄榄油。

SYN spread over, daub, rub on, cover  
2 N-COUNT 污迹;油迹 A smear is a dirty or oily mark. 污迹;油迹

  • There was a smear of gravy on his chin. [+ of]

    他的下巴上粘着肉汁。

3 VERB 中伤;诽谤;污蔑 To smear someone means to spread unpleasant and untrue rumours or accusations about them in order to damage their reputation. 中伤;诽谤;污蔑 [journalism]

  • ...an attempt to smear the director-general of the BBC. [VERB noun]

    污蔑英国广播公司主管的企图

4 N-COUNT [oft NOUN n] 污蔑;诽谤;中伤 A smear is an unpleasant and untrue rumour or accusation that is intended to damage someone's reputation. 污蔑;诽谤;中伤 [journalism]

  • He puts all the accusations down to a smear campaign by his political opponents.

    他把所有的指责都归结为政治对手们的造谣攻势。

5 N-COUNT 涂片检查(对妇女子宫颈取样检查癌变的一种方法) A smear or a smear test is a medical test in which a few cells are taken from a woman's cervix and examined to see if any cancer cells are present. 涂片检查(对妇女子宫颈取样检查癌变的一种方法) [British]
