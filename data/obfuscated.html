obfuscate
ɒbfʌskeɪt ˈɒbfʌsˌkeɪt ˈɑbfəsˌkeɪt ɑbˈfʌsˌkeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense obfuscates, present participle obfuscating, past tense , past participle obfuscated
VERB （故意地）混淆，使混乱，使困惑 To obfuscate something means to deliberately make it seem confusing and difficult to understand. （故意地）混淆，使混乱，使困惑 [formal]

  • They are obfuscating the issue, as only insurance companies can. [VERB noun]

    他们在此问题上混淆视听，这事只有保险公司才做得出来。

  • Macdonald accepted that such information could be used to manipulate, to obfuscate, and to mislead. [VERB]

    麦克唐纳相信这样的信息可以用来操纵舆论，蒙蔽误导。

SYN obscure, confuse, cloud, blur  
obfuscation

  • He gave a more lucid exposition of the party's strategy than Bush has managed in months of obfuscation.

    相对于布什几个月来的语焉不详，他对该党的策略作了较为清晰的阐述。
