batten
bætən ˈbætən
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense battens, present participle battening, past tense battened
1 N-COUNT （固定用的）板条，压条 A batten is a long strip of wood that is fixed to something to strengthen it or to hold it firm. （固定用的）板条，压条

  • ...a batten to support the base timbers.

    用来支撑底部木头的板条

SYN rod, bar, stick, stake  
2 VERB [usually passive] 用板条（或压条）固定 If something is battened in place, it is made secure by having battens fixed across it or being closed firmly. 用板条（或压条）固定

  • The roof was never securely battened down. [be VERB-ed adv./prep.]

    屋顶板条一直没钉牢。

  • ...big pine shutters that could be battened against the noonday sun. [be VERB-ed adv./prep.]

    钉上大块的松木窗板可以遮挡中午的太阳

SYN fasten, unite, fix, secure  
3 to batten down the hatches→see: hatch 
Phrasal verbs:
batten on
