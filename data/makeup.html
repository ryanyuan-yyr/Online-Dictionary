make up
1 PHRASAL VERB 组成;构成 The people or things that make up something are the members or parts that form that thing. 组成;构成 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • North Africans make up the largest and poorest immigrant group in the country. [VERB PREP. noun]

    北非人构成了该国最大的、也是最贫困的移民群。

  • Women officers make up 13 per cent of the police force. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    女警占警力的13%。

  • Insects are made up of tens of thousands of proteins. [be V-ed P + of]

    昆虫由数万种蛋白质构成。

SYN form, account for, constitute, compose  
2 PHRASAL VERB 编造;虚构 If you make up something such as a story or excuse, you invent it, sometimes in order to deceive people. 编造;虚构

  • I think it's very unkind of you to make up stories about him. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我认为你编造他的谣言很不厚道。

  • I'm not making it up. The character exists in real life. [VERB noun PREP.]

    我没有凭空捏造，这种人现实生活中确实存在。

3 PHRASAL VERB （给…）化妆;（为…）打扮 If you make yourself up or if someone else makes you up, make-up such as powder or lipstick is put on your face. （给…）化妆;（为…）打扮

  • She spent too much time making herself up. [VERB noun PREP.]

    她在化妆上花去了太多时间。

  • She chose Maggie to make her up for her engagement photographs. [VERB noun PREP.]

    她选择玛吉为她拍订婚照的时候化妆。

  • I can't be bothered to make up my face. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我懒得化妆。

4 PHRASAL VERB 凑足;补齐 If you make up an amount, you add something to it so that it is as large as it should be. 凑足;补齐

  • Less than half of the money that students receive is in the form of grants, and loans have made up the difference. [VERB PREP. noun]

    学生领到的钱有不到一半是助学金，差额部分由贷款补足。

  • The team had six professionals and made the number up with five amateurs. [VERB noun PREP.]

    该队有6名职业运动员和凑数的5名业余运动员。

  • For every £100 you invest into a pension plan the Inland Revenue makes it up to £125. [VERB noun PREP. + to]

    你每向养老金计划中投入100英镑，国内税收署就会将其补加至125英镑。

SYN complete, meet, supply, fill  
5 PHRASAL VERB (加班加点)补足(时间) If you make up time or hours, you work some extra hours because you have previously taken some time off work. (加班加点)补足(时间) [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • They'll have to make up time lost during the strike. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他们不得不加班弥补罢工耽误的时间。

6 PHR-V-RECIP 和解;和好 If two people make up or make it up after a quarrel or disagreement, they become friends again. 和解;和好

  • She came back and they made up. [VERB PREP.]

    她回来之后他们又和好如初。

  • They never made up the quarrel. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他们再也没能和解。

  • They should make up with their ex-enemy in the West. [VERB PREP. + with]

    他们应该和西方的老对头和解。

  • I'll make it up with him again. [VERB noun PREP. + with]

    我会与他重归于好。

SYN settle your differences, come to terms, shake hands, make peace  
7 PHRASAL VERB 配(菜);配(药) If you make up something such as food or medicine, you prepare it by mixing or putting different things together. 配(菜);配(药) [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Prepare the souffle dish before making up the souffle mixture. [VERB PREP. noun]

    在搅拌做蛋奶酥的各种原料之前先要把盘子准备好。

8 PHRASAL VERB 铺(床);搭(铺) If you make up a bed, you put sheets and blankets on it so that someone can sleep there. 铺(床);搭(铺)

  • Her mother made up a bed in her old room. [VERB PREP. noun]

    她母亲在她曾经住过的房间里铺好了一张床。

make-up
(also makeup)
1 N-UNCOUNT 化妆品 Make-up consists of things such as lipstick, eye shadow, and powder which some women put on their faces to make themselves look more attractive or which actors use to change or improve their appearance. 化妆品

  • Normally she wore little make-up.

    她一般不怎么化妆。

  • She checked her makeup one last time, and then walked out with a spring in her step.

    她最后检查了一下妆容，然后步履轻盈地走了出去。

SYN cosmetics, paint [informal] , powder, face [informal]  
2 N-UNCOUNT [usually poss NOUN] 天性;性格 Some-one's make-up is their nature and the various qualities in their character. 天性;性格

  • There was some fatal flaw in his makeup, and as time went on he lapsed into long silences or became off-hand.

    他的性格有致命的缺陷，随着时间的推移，他陷入了久久的沉默，或者变得漠然。

SYN nature, character, constitution, temperament  
3 N-UNCOUNT 组成成分;构成方式 The make-up of something consists of its different parts and the way these parts are arranged. 组成成分;构成方式

  • The ideological make-up of the unions is now radically different from what it had been. [+ of]

    协会的思想观念与过去截然不同。

  • ...the chemical make-up of the oceans and atmosphere.

    海洋与大气的化学构造

SYN structure, organization, arrangement, form
