incline
ɪnklaɪn ɪnklaɪn ɪnˈklaɪn ˈɪnˌklaɪn
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense inclines, present participle inclining, past tense , past participle inclined
The verb is pronounced /ɪn'klaɪn/. The noun is pronounced /'ɪnklaɪn/. 动词读作 /ɪn'klaɪn/,名词读作 /'ɪnklaɪn/。
1 V-ERG (使)倾向;(使)趋向 If you incline to think or act in a particular way, or if something inclines you to it, you are likely to think or act in that way. (使)倾向;(使)趋向 [formal]

  • I incline to the view that he is right. [V + to/towards]

    我倾向于认为他是正确的。

  • ...the factors which incline us towards particular beliefs. [V n + to/towards]

    使我们倾向于相信某些特定信仰的因素

  • Many end up as team leaders, which inclines them to co-operate with the bosses. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    很多人最后成为团队领袖,这使得他们易于和上司合作。

  • Those who fail incline to blame the world for their failure. [VERB to-infinitive]

    那些失败者往往将他们的失败归咎于社会。

SYN predispose, influence, tend, persuade  
2 VERB 点(头) If you incline your head, you bend your neck so that your head is leaning forward. 点(头) [written]

  • Jack inclined his head very slightly. [VERB noun]

    杰克轻轻点了点头。

SYN bend, lower, nod, bow  
3 N-COUNT 斜坡;斜面 An incline is land that slopes at an angle. 斜坡;斜面 [formal]

  • He came to a halt at the edge of a steep incline.

    他在一个陡坡边上停住了。

SYN slope, rise, dip, grade
