she'd
ʃiːd ʃɪd ʃiːd ʃid
1 (尤其当had为助动词时, she had 的口语形式) She'd is the usual spoken form of 'she had', especially when 'had' is an auxiliary verb. (尤其当had为助动词时, she had 的口语形式)

  • She'd rung up to discuss the divorce.

    她打过电话来协商离婚事宜。

  • She would go for a swim when she'd unpacked.

    她打开行李取出衣物后就会去游泳。

2 (she would 的口语形式) She'd is a spoken form of 'she would'. (she would 的口语形式)

  • She'd do anything for a bit of money.

    为了一点儿钱，她什么都愿意做。

  • I got the impression she'd like a word with you, sir.

    我觉得她可能想和你谈一谈，先生。

shed
ʃed ʃɛd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense sheds, present participle shedding
The form shed is used in the present tense and in the past tense and past participle of the verb. shed 的过去式和过去分词与原形相同。
1 (用于储存园艺工具等的)简易房，小棚屋，小屋 A shed is a small building that is used for storing things such as garden tools. (用于储存园艺工具等的)简易房，小棚屋，小屋

  • ...a garden shed.

    园艺工艺棚

2 (车站、港口、工厂等里面的)棚式建筑物，厂房,工棚,库房 A shed is a large shelter or building, for example at a railway station, port, or factory. (车站、港口、工厂等里面的)棚式建筑物，厂房,工棚,库房

  • ...disused railway sheds.

    废弃的铁路工棚

3 落(叶);蜕(皮);脱(毛) When a tree sheds its leaves, its leaves fall off in the autumn. When an animal sheds hair or skin, some of its hair or skin drops off. 落(叶);蜕(皮);脱(毛)

  • Some of the trees were already beginning to shed their leaves. [VERB noun]

    有些树已经开始落叶了。

  • ...a snake who has shed its skin. [VERB noun]

    已经蜕皮的蛇

4 摆脱;去除;抛弃 To shed something means to get rid of it. 摆脱;去除;抛弃

  • The firm is to shed 700 jobs. [VERB noun]

    公司将裁员 700 人。

  • He had maintained a rigid diet, shedding some twenty pounds. [VERB noun]

    他严格地坚持节食，减了差不多 20 磅。

  • ...a city trying to shed its rough image. [VERB noun]

    试图摆脱其治安混乱的形象的城市

5 (卡车等)意外地掉落(货物) If a lorry sheds its load, the goods that it is carrying accidentally fall onto the road. (卡车等)意外地掉落(货物)

  • A lorry piled with scrap metal had shed its load. [VERB noun]

    一辆载满废金属的卡车掉货了。

6 流，洒,落(泪) If you shed tears, you cry. 流，洒,落(泪)

  • They will shed a few tears at their daughter's wedding. [VERB noun]

    他们会在女儿的婚礼上落泪的。

7 屠杀;导致惨死;(通常指在战争中)流血死亡,惨死 To shed blood means to kill people in a violent way. If someone sheds their blood, they are killed in a violent way, usually when they are fighting in a war. 屠杀;导致惨死;(通常指在战争中)流血死亡,惨死

  • Gunmen in Ulster shed the first blood of the new year. [VERB noun]

    阿尔斯特的持枪歹徒制造了新一年的首起流血事件。

  • Others promised to 'shed our blood and sacrifice our lives to oppose the invaders'. [VERB noun]

    其他人承诺要“抛头颅、洒热血、抗击侵略者”。
