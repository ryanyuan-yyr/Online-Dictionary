supreme
suːpriːm səˈprim sʊˈprim suˈprim
1 ADJ [ADJ n] 最高的;至高无上的 Supreme is used in the title of a person or an official group to indicate that they are at the highest level in a particular organization or system. 最高的;至高无上的

  • MacArthur was Supreme Commander for the allied powers in the Pacific.

    麦克阿瑟将军曾是太平洋战区盟军的最高统帅。

  • ...the Supreme Court.

    最高法院

  • ...the Supreme Being.

    上帝

2 ADJ 最大的;最多的 You use supreme to emphasize that a quality or thing is very great. 最大的;最多的 [emphasis]

  • Her approval was of supreme importance.

    她的认可是至关重要的。

  • The lady conspired to seize supreme power.

    该女士图谋攫取最高权力。

SYN paramount, surpassing, superlative, prevailing  
supremely

  • We are supremely confident because we have the right mixture of skill and experience.

    我们有必备的技巧和经验，因此自信满满。

  • She gets on with her job and does it supremely well.

    她继续她的工作,并且做得非常出色。
