capsule
kæpsjuːl kæpsəl ˈkæpsəl ˈkæpsjul
Word forms: plural capsules
1 N-COUNT (口服)胶囊 A capsule is a very small tube containing powdered or liquid medicine, which you swallow. (口服)胶囊

  • ...cod liver oil capsules.

    鳕鱼肝油胶囊

  • You can also take red ginseng in convenient tablet or capsule form.

    你也可以选择便于服用的红参片或红参胶囊。

SYN pill, tablet, lozenge, bolus  
2 N-COUNT (医用或科学研究用的)密封小容器 A capsule is a small container with a drug or other substance inside it, which is used for medical or scientific purposes. (医用或科学研究用的)密封小容器

  • They first implanted capsules into the animals' brains.

    他们首先将密封管植入动物的大脑。

  • The clear capsules start dissolving as soon as they are immersed in the lake.

    那些透明密闭容器一浸入湖水中就开始溶解。

3 N-COUNT [oft n NOUN] (植物的)蒴果，荚膜，孢蒴 In some plants, a capsule is a part which forms a case or container for seeds, fruit, or spores. (植物的)蒴果，荚膜，孢蒴 [technical]

  • ...a large shiny brown nut, enclosed in a large spiny seed capsule.

    一个硕大发亮的褐色坚果，包裹在一个多刺的大种子荚内

4 N-COUNT 航天舱;密封舱;太空舱 A space capsule is the part of a spacecraft in which people travel, and which often separates from the main rocket. 航天舱;密封舱;太空舱

  • A Russian space capsule is currently orbiting the Earth.

    一个俄罗斯太空舱目前正在绕地球轨道飞行。
