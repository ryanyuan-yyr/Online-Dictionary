complaint
kəmpleɪnt kəmˈpleɪnt
Word forms: plural complaints
1 N-VAR 投诉;意见;抱怨 A complaint is a statement in which you express your dissatisfaction with a particular situation. 投诉;意见;抱怨

  • There's been a record number of complaints about the standard of service on Britain's railways. [+ about]

    有关英国铁路服务水平的投诉空前的多。

  • People have been reluctant to make formal complaints to the police.

    人们不愿向警方作出正式投诉。

  • If you feel you have any cause for complaint about the service you should write to the Hospital Administrator.

    如果你觉得对服务有任何不满，应该写信给医院院长。

SYN protest, accusation, objection, grievance  
2 N-COUNT 投诉的原因;不满的原因 A complaint is a reason for complaining. 投诉的原因;不满的原因

  • If you have a complaint about shoes bought from a shop covered by the Footwear Code, there are several ways of putting the matter right.

    如果对在Footwear Code旗下零售店里买的鞋有不满意的地方，有几种解决问题的方式。

  • I've got no complaints about them.

    对他们我没什么意见。

  • My main complaint is that we can't go out on the racecourse anymore.

    我投诉的主要问题是我们没法再去外面的赛道了。

3 N-COUNT (尤指不严重的)疾病 You can refer to an illness as a complaint, especially if it is not very serious. (尤指不严重的)疾病

  • Eczema is a common skin complaint which often runs in families.

    湿疹是一种常见的皮肤病，通常具遗传性。

SYN disorder, problem, trouble, disease
