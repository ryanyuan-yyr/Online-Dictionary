darken
dɑːʳkən ˈdɑrkən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense darkens, present participle darkening, past tense , past participle darkened
1 V-ERG (使)变黑;(使)变深;(使)变暗 If something darkens or if a person or thing darkens it, it becomes darker. (使)变黑;(使)变深;(使)变暗

  • The sky darkened abruptly. [VERB]

    天突然暗了下来。

  • She had put on her make-up and darkened her eyelashes. [VERB noun]

    她已化好妆，并涂黑了睫毛。

2 V-ERG (使)变得阴郁;(使)心情沉重 If someone's mood darkens or if something darkens their mood, they suddenly become rather unhappy. (使)变得阴郁;(使)心情沉重 [literary]

  • My sunny mood suddenly darkened. [VERB]

    我的好心情突然变得阴郁起来。

  • Nothing was going to darken his mood today. [VERB noun]

    今天什么都不会影响到他的心情。

SYN sadden, upset, cloud, blacken  
3 VERB (脸色)变阴沉;(面)有愠色 If someone's face darkens, they suddenly look angry. (脸色)变阴沉;(面)有愠色 [literary]

  • Rawley's face darkened again. [VERB]

    罗利的脸色又阴沉下来。
