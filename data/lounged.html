lounge
laʊndʒ laʊndʒ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense lounges, present participle lounging, past tense , past participle lounged
1 N-COUNT （私宅中的）起居室，客厅 In a house, a lounge is a room where people sit and relax. （私宅中的）起居室，客厅 [mainly British]

  • The Holmbergs were sitting before a roaring fire in the lounge, sipping their cocoa.

    霍姆伯格一家坐在客厅里，对着熊熊炉火呷着可可。

SYN sitting room [British] , living room, parlour [old-fashioned] , drawing room  
2 N-COUNT （酒店、俱乐部或其他公共场所的）休息厅，休息室 In a hotel, club, or other public place, a lounge is a room where people can sit and relax. （酒店、俱乐部或其他公共场所的）休息厅，休息室

  • I spoke to her in the lounge of a big Johannesburg hotel where she was attending a union meeting. [+ of]

    我在约翰内斯堡一家大酒店的休息大厅跟她打过招呼，她当时在那里参加一个工会会议。

3 N-COUNT 候机厅；候机大堂 In an airport, a lounge is a very large room where people can sit and wait for aircraft to arrive or leave. 候机厅；候机大堂

  • Instead of taking me to the departure lounge they took me right to my seat on the plane.

    他们没有带我去候机厅，而是直接领我上飞机到了我的座位上。

4 VERB （懒洋洋地）坐，躺 If you lounge somewhere, you sit or lie there in a relaxed or lazy way. （懒洋洋地）坐，躺

  • They ate and drank and lounged in the shade. [VERB prep.]

    他们在阴凉处吃吃喝喝懒懒地闲坐着。

  • If you don't want to lounge on the beach, you can go on a guided walk along the nature trail. [VERB prep.]

    如果不想闲躺在沙滩上，可以让导游带你走一走观光小径。

SYN relax, pass time, hang out [informal] , idle  
Phrasal verbs:
lounge around
