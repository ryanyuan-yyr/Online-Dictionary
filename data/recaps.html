recap
riːkæp ˈriˌkæp riˈkæp
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense recaps, present participle recapping, past tense , past participle recapped
VERB 概括;重述(要点)；简要回顾 You can say that you are going to recap when you want to draw people's attention to the fact that you are going to repeat the main points of an explanation, argument, or description, as a summary of it. 概括;重述(要点)；简要回顾

  • To recap briefly, an agreement negotiated to cut the budget deficit was rejected 10 days ago. [VERB]

    简要概括起来，一项关于削减预算赤字的协议在10天前遭到了否决。

  • Can you recap the points included in the regional conference proposal? [VERB noun]

    你能扼要说明一下地区会议上提案的重点吗？

Recap is also a noun.

  • Each report starts with a recap of how we did versus our projections.

    每份报告开头都扼要回顾了我们的表现和我们的预期目标。
