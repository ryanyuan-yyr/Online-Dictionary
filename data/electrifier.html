electrify
ɪlektrɪfaɪ iˈlɛktrəˌfaɪ ɪˈlɛktrəfaɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense electrifies, present participle electrifying, past tense , past participle electrified
1 VERB [usually passive] 使极为兴奋;使激动不已;使震惊 If people are electrified by an event or experience, it makes them feel very excited and surprised. 使极为兴奋;使激动不已;使震惊

  • The world was electrified by his courage and resistance. [be V-ed +by]

    世人为他的勇气和反抗精神震惊不已。

SYN thrill, shock, excite, amaze  
electrifying

  • He gave an electrifying performance.

    他的演出令人激动不已。

2 VERB [usually passive] 使电气化;使通电 When a railway system or railway line is electrified, electric cables are put over the tracks, or electric rails are put beside them, so that the trains can be powered by electricity. 使电气化;使通电

  • The west-coast line was electrified as long ago as 1974. [be VERB-ed]

    西海岸铁路线早在1974年就实现了电气化。

  • ...the electrified section of the Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway. [VERB-ed]

    兰开夏郡至约克郡铁路的电气化路段

SYN wire up, wire, supply electricity to, convert to electricity  
electrification

  • ...the electrification of the Oxted to Uckfield line.

    奥克斯特德至阿克菲尔德铁路线的电气化
