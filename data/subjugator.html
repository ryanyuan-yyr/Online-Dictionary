subjugate
sʌbdʒʊgeɪt ˈsʌbdʒəˌgeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense subjugates, present participle subjugating, past tense , past participle subjugated
1 VERB (尤指在战争中)征服，制服，使屈服 If someone subjugates a group of people, they take complete control of them, especially by defeating them in a war. (尤指在战争中)征服，制服，使屈服 [formal]

  • Their costly and futile attempt to subjugate the Afghans lasted just 10 years. [VERB noun]

    他们大动干戈想要控制阿富汗地区，但只持续了10年就无果而终了。

SYN conquer, master, overcome, defeat  
subjugation

  • ...the brutal subjugation of native tribes.

    对当地部落的残忍的征服

2 VERB [usually passive] 使(愿望、渴望)退居其次;使让位于 If your wishes or desires are subjugated to something, they are treated as less important than that thing. 使(愿望、渴望)退居其次;使让位于 [formal] [Also be VERB-ed]

  • After having been subjugated to ambition, your maternal instincts are at last starting to assert themselves. [be VERB-ed + to]

    你那被雄心壮志压制已久的母性本能终于开始展现出来。
