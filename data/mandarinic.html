mandarin
mændərɪn ˈmændərɪn
Word forms: plural mandarins
1 N-COUNT （新闻用语）政界要人，政府高官 Journalists sometimes use mandarin to refer to someone who has an important job in the Civil Service. （新闻用语）政界要人，政府高官 [British]

  • ...Foreign Office mandarins.

    外交部高官

  • ...the latest evidence of the mandarins' power over their ministers.

    证明政府高官对部长们的影响力的最新证据

2 N-UNCOUNT 汉语普通话 Mandarin is the commonly-used language of China. 汉语普通话
3 N-COUNT 柑橘 A mandarin or a mandarin orange is a small orange whose skin comes off easily. 柑橘
4 N-COUNT (中国古代的)官僚，大臣 A mandarin was, in former times, an important government official in China. (中国古代的)官僚，大臣
