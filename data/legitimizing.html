legitimize
lɪdʒɪtɪmaɪz lɪˈdʒɪtɪməˌtaɪz ləˈdʒɪtəˌmaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense legitimizes, present participle legitimizing, past tense , past participle legitimized
The spellings legitimise in British English, and legitimatize in American English are also used. 亦拼作legitimise（英国英语）和legitimatize（美国英语）。
VERB 使（尤其是坏事）合法化；正式批准（或认可）；证明…为有理（或正当） To legitimize something, especially something bad, means to officially allow it, approve it, or make it seem acceptable. 使（尤其是坏事）合法化；正式批准（或认可）；证明…为有理（或正当） [formal]

  • They will accept no agreement that legitimizes the ethnic division of the country. [VERB noun]

    他们不会接受任何使国家民族分裂合法化的协议。

  • Images which glorify violence and cruelty, serve to legitimise such behaviour. [VERB noun]

    这些美化暴力和暴行的图片，就是要使此等行为合理化。

SYN legalize, permit, sanction, legitimate
