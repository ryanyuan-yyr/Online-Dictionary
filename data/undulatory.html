undulate
ʌndʒʊleɪt ˈʌndʒəˌleɪt ˈʌndjəˌleɪt ˈʌndjəlɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense undulates, present participle undulating, past tense , past participle undulated
VERB 波动；起伏；摇曳 Something that undulates has gentle curves or slopes, or moves gently and slowly up and down or from side to side in an attractive manner. 波动；起伏；摇曳 [literary] [Also VERB n]

  • As we travel south, the countryside begins to undulate as the rolling hills sweep down to the riverbanks. [VERB]

    我们一路南行，乡间开始变得起伏不平，绵延的山峦一直延伸到河岸。

  • His body slowly undulated in time to the music. [VERB]

    他的身体随着音乐的节拍慢慢摆动。

SYN wave, roll, surge, swell  
undulating

  • ...gently undulating hills.

    微微起伏的山丘
