assign
əsaɪn əˈsaɪn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense assigns, present participle assigning, past tense , past participle assigned
1 VERB 分派（工作）；布置（作业） If you assign a piece of work to someone, you give them the work to do. 分派（工作）；布置（作业） [Also VERB n to-infinitive]

  • When I taught, I would assign a topic to children which they would write about. [VERB noun + to]

    我教课时会给孩子们布置一个题目，让他们就这个题目写点东西。

  • Later in the year, she'll assign them research papers. [VERB noun noun]

    今年晚些时候，她将给他们指定研究论文。

  • When teachers assign homework, students usually feel an obligation to do it. [VERB noun]

    当老师布置家庭作业的时候，学生们通常觉得做作业是分内之事。

SYN give, set, grant, allocate  
2 VERB 分配;配给 If you assign something to someone, you say that it is for their use. 分配;配给

  • The selling broker is then required to assign a portion of the commission to the buyer broker. [VERB noun + to]

    卖方经纪人须随后将部分佣金分给买方经纪人。

  • He assigned her all his land in Ireland. [VERB noun noun]

    他把自己在爱尔兰的所有土地都转给了她。

SYN allocate, give, determine, fix  
3 VERB [usually passive] 委派;派往;指派 If someone is assigned to a particular place, group, or person, they are sent there, usually in order to work at that place or for that person. 委派;派往;指派

  • I was assigned to Troop A of the 10th Cavalry. [be VERB-ed + to]

    我被派往第10骑兵队的A连。

  • Did you choose Russia or were you simply assigned there?. [be V-ed adv]

    你是自己选择去的俄罗斯还是就是被派到那里的？

  • Each of us was assigned a minder, someone who looked after us. [be VERB-ed noun]

    我们每个人都被派了一个看护人来照顾我们。

SYN select for, post, commission, elect  
4 VERB 赋予，给予（功能或价值） If you assign a particular function or value to someone or something, you say they have it. 赋予，给予（功能或价值）

  • Under Mr. Harel's system, each business must assign a value to each job. [VERB noun + to]

    根据哈雷尔先生的体系，每个企业必须赋予每份工作一定的价值。

  • Assign the letters of the alphabet their numerical values — A equals 1, B equals 2, etc. [VERB noun noun]

    给字母表里的每个字母赋予相应的数值—A等于1，B等于2，等等。

SYN attribute, credit, put down, set down
