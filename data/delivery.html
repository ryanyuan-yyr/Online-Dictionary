delivery
dɪlɪvəri dɪˈlɪvəri
Word forms: plural deliveries
1 N-VAR (信件、包裹等的)递送，投递，运送 Delivery or a delivery is the bringing of letters, parcels, or other goods to someone's house or to another place where they want them. (信件、包裹等的)递送，投递，运送

  • Please allow 28 days for delivery.

    递送将需要 28 天的时间。

  • It is available at £108, including VAT and delivery.

    其价格为 108 英镑，包括增值税和送货费在内。

  • ...the delivery of goods and resources.

    货物和资源的运送

SYN handing over, transfer, distribution, transmission  
2 N-COUNT 递送之物;投递之物;运送之物 A delivery of something is the goods that are delivered. 递送之物;投递之物;运送之物

  • I got a delivery of fresh eggs this morning.

    我今天早上收到了送来的新鲜鸡蛋。

SYN consignment, goods, shipment, batch  
3 ADJ [ADJ n] 递送的;投递的;运送的 A delivery person or service delivers things to a place. 递送的;投递的;运送的

  • ...a pizza delivery man.

    比萨饼递送员

  • ...Interflora, the flower delivery service.

    国际花商联盟，鲜花速递服务机构

4 N-UNCOUNT [usually poss NOUN] 发言(或演说)的方式 You talk about someone's delivery when you are referring to the way in which they give a speech or lecture. 发言(或演说)的方式

  • His speeches were magnificently written but his delivery was hopeless.

    他的发言稿写得非常精彩，但他的演讲风格真是无可救药。

SYN speech, speaking, expression, pronunciation  
5 N-VAR 分娩;生产 Delivery is the process of giving birth to a baby. 分娩;生产

  • In the end, it was an easy delivery: a fine baby boy.

    分娩过程最终非常顺利，生了个健康的男婴。

  • Premature birth is three times more likely for twins, and delivery at 36 to 38 weeks is normal.

    双胞胎的早产率要比一般的高出两倍，在36到38周之间分娩很正常。
