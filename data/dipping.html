dipDip.
dip
dɪp dɪp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense dips, present participle dipping, past tense , past participle dipped
1 VERB 浸;蘸 If you dip something in a liquid, you put it into the liquid for a short time, so that only part of it is covered, and take it out again. 浸;蘸

  • They dip the food into the sauce. [V n into/in n]

    他们把食物放到调味汁里蘸了蘸。

  • Quickly dip the base in and out of cold water. [V n + into/in]

    把底部在冷水中快速地浸一下后拿出来。

SYN plunge, immerse, bathe, duck  
Dip is also a noun.

  • One dip into the bottle should do an entire nail.

    往瓶子里蘸一下的量就应该能涂满整个指甲。

2 VERB 把(手)伸入 If you dip your hand into a container or dip into the container, you put your hand into it in order to take something out of it. 把(手)伸入

  • She dipped a hand into the jar of sweets and pulled one out. [VERB noun + into]

    她把手伸进糖果罐，摸出了一颗糖。

  • Watch your fingers as you dip into the pot. [VERB + into]

    把手伸进锅里时当心你的手指头。

  • Ask the children to guess what's in each container by dipping their hands in. [V n with in]

    让孩子们伸手进去摸摸，猜猜每个容器里面是什么。

3 VERB (通常指迅速地)下降，下落，下沉 If something dips, it makes a downward movement, usually quite quickly. (通常指迅速地)下降，下落，下沉

  • Blake jumped in expertly; the boat dipped slightly under his weight. [VERB]

    布莱克很熟练地跳上了船，他的重量使小船微微向下沉了沉。

  • The sun dipped below the horizon. [VERB prep.]

    太阳落到了地平线下。

SYN drop (down), set, fall, lower  
Dip is also a noun.

  • I noticed little things, a dip of the head, a twitch in the shoulder.

    我注意到了一些细节：头的往下一沉，肩膀的突然颤动。

4 VERB 陡然下降;凹下去;突然下倾 If an area of land, a road, or a path dips, it goes down quite suddenly to a lower level. 陡然下降;凹下去;突然下倾

  • The road dipped and rose again. [VERB]

    道路陡然沉下去又升上来。

  • ...a path which suddenly dips down into a tunnel. [VERB adv./prep.]

    突然下降进入一条隧道的小路

SYN slope, drop (down), descend, fall  
Dip is also a noun.

  • Where the road makes a dip, turn right.

    在下坡处，向右转。

5 VERB 给(羊等家畜)洗药浴 When farmers dip sheep or other farm animals, they put them into a container of liquid with chemicals in it, in order to kill harmful insects which live on the animals' bodies. 给(羊等家畜)洗药浴

  • Their father was helping to dip the sheep. [VERB noun]

    他们的父亲在帮忙给羊群洗药浴。

dipping

  • He digs potatoes and helps with the sheep dipping.

    他挖土豆并帮忙给羊群洗药浴。

6 N-UNCOUNT [also NOUN in plural, usually supplement NOUN] 药浴液;浸液 Dip is a liquid with chemicals in it which animals or objects can be dipped in to disinfect or clean them. 药浴液;浸液

  • ...sheep dip.

    羊群用的药浴液

7 VERB (数量或水平，通常短暂地)下降，下跌，减少 If the amount or level of something dips, it becomes smaller or lower, usually only for a short period of time. (数量或水平，通常短暂地)下降，下跌，减少

  • Unemployment dipped to 6.9 per cent last month. [VERB prep./adv.]

    上个月失业率降到了 6.9%。

  • The president became more cautious as his popularity dipped. [VERB]

    由于支持率有所下降，总统变得更加谨慎了。

SYN drop, fall, lower, decline  
Dip is also a noun.

  • ...the current dip in farm spending.

    目前农场开支的减少

8 N-VAR 蘸酱;(蘸蔬菜或饼干吃的)调味酱，果酱 A dip is a thick creamy sauce. You dip pieces of raw vegetable or biscuits into the sauce and then eat them. 蘸酱;(蘸蔬菜或饼干吃的)调味酱，果酱

  • Maybe we could just buy some dips.

    或许我们可以只买一点儿蘸酱。

  • ...prawns with avocado dip.

    配鳄梨酱的对虾

SYN sauce, dressing, relish  
9 N-COUNT (短时的)游泳，泡水 If you have or take a dip, you go for a quick swim in the sea, a river, or a swimming pool. (短时的)游泳，泡水

  • She flicked through a romantic paperback between occasional dips in the pool.

    用在泳池里游一阵后歇一阵的几个空当，她翻完了一本平装本言情小说。

10 VERB 将(汽车前灯)调为近光 If you are driving a car and dip the headlights, you operate a switch that makes them shine downwards, so that they do not shine directly into the eyes of other drivers. 将(汽车前灯)调为近光 [British]

  • He dipped his headlights as they came up behind a slow-moving van. [VERB noun]

    当他们靠近一辆缓慢行驶的货车时，他把汽车前灯调成了近光。

  • This picture shows the view from a car using normal dipped lights. [VERB-ed]

    这幅图片显示的是从一辆使用正常近光灯的汽车上所看到的景色。

in AM, use 美国英语用 dim
11 VERB 浏览;翻看 If you dip into a book, you have a brief look at it without reading or studying it seriously. 浏览;翻看

  • ...a chance to dip into a wide selection of books on Buddhism. [VERB + into]

    博览佛教群书的机会

12 VERB 动用(款项等) If you dip into a sum of money that you had intended to save, you use some of it to buy something or pay for something. 动用(款项等)

  • Just when she was ready to dip into her savings, Greg hastened to her rescue. [VERB + into]

    正当她准备动用自己的存款时，格雷格赶来给她救了急。
