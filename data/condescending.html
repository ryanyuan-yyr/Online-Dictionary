condescending
kɒndɪsendɪŋ ˌkɑndɪˈsɛndɪŋ
ADJ-GRADED 盛气凌人的;居高临下的 If you say that someone is condescending, you are showing your disapproval of the fact that they talk or behave in a way which shows that they think they are superior to other people. 盛气凌人的;居高临下的 [disapproval]

  • I'm fed up with your money and your whole condescending attitude.

    我讨厌你的钱，也受够了你居高临下的态度。

  • They can be a bit condescending.

    他们可能有点儿架子。

SYN patronizing, lordly, superior, lofty  
condescendingly

  • 'The practical work you did for us in Brazil was of great value,' he went on condescendingly.

    “你在巴西为我们所做的实际工作非常有价值。”他继续以高人一等的语气说道。
