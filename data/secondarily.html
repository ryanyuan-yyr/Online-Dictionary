secondary
sekəndri -deri ˈsɛkənˌdɛri
1 ADJ [usually ADJ n, Also v-link ADJ to n] 第二位的;次要的;从属的 If you describe something as secondary, you mean that it is less important than something else. 第二位的;次要的;从属的

  • The street erupted in a huge explosion, with secondary explosions in the adjoining buildings.

    街上发生了剧烈爆炸，邻近的建筑里也发生了程度较轻的爆炸。

  • They argue that human rights considerations are now of only secondary importance.

    他们认为人权方面的考虑现在只是第二位的。

  • The actual damage to the brain cells is secondary to the damage caused to the blood supply.

    与对血液供应的损害相比，对大脑细胞的实际损害不那么严重。

2 ADJ [usually ADJ n] (疾病、感染)继发性的，第二期的 Secondary diseases or infections happen as a result of another disease or infection that has already happened. (疾病、感染)继发性的，第二期的

  • He had kidney cancer, with secondary tumours in the brain and lungs.

    他患有肾癌，还有脑部和肺部的继发性肿瘤。

3 ADJ (教育)中等的，中学的 Secondary education is given to pupils between the ages of 11 or 12 and 17 or 18. (教育)中等的，中学的

  • Examinations for the GCSE are taken after about five years of secondary education.

    在接受中等教育约5年后便可参加普通中等教育证书的考试。
