coddle
kɒdəl ˈkɑdəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense coddles, present participle coddling, past tense , past participle coddled
VERB 溺爱;娇纵 To coddle someone means to treat them too kindly or protect them too much. 溺爱;娇纵 [disapproval]

  • She coddled her youngest son madly. [VERB noun]

    她对小儿子过于溺爱。

  • The warders' union has been denouncing the government for coddling prisoners. [VERB noun]

    狱警联盟一直谴责政府姑息犯人。

SYN pamper, spoil, indulge, cosset
