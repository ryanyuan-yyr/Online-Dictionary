disquiet
dɪskwaɪət dɪsˈkwaɪət ˈdɪsˈkwaɪət
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense disquiets, present participle disquieting, past tense , past participle disquieted
1 N-UNCOUNT 不安;焦急;忧虑 Disquiet is a feeling of worry or anxiety. 不安;焦急;忧虑 [formal]

  • There is growing public disquiet about the cost of such policing.

    公众对这种治安管理费用的不满情绪与日俱增。

SYN uneasiness, concern, fear, worry  
2 VERB 使不安;使忧虑 If something disquiets you, it makes you feel anxious. 使不安;使忧虑 [formal]

  • This information disquieted him. [VERB noun]

    这则消息让他焦虑不安。

SYN make uneasy, concern, worry, trouble  
disquieting

  • He found her letter disquieting.

    他觉得她的信令人不安。
