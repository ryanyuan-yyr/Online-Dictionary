counterfeit
kaʊntəʳfɪt ˈkaʊntəfɪt ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense counterfeits, present participle counterfeiting, past tense , past participle counterfeited
1 ADJ [usually ADJ n] (钱、货物或文件)仿造的，假冒的 Counterfeit money, goods, or documents are not genuine, but have been made to look exactly like genuine ones in order to deceive people. (钱、货物或文件)仿造的，假冒的

  • He admitted possessing and delivering counterfeit currency.

    他供认持有并散播假币。

SYN fake, copied, false, forged  
Counterfeit is also a noun.

  • Levi Strauss says counterfeits of the company's jeans are flooding Europe.

    利维·施特劳斯称仿冒其公司品牌的牛仔装正充斥着欧洲市场。

2 VERB 仿造;假冒 If someone counterfeits something, they make a version of it that is not genuine but has been made to look genuine in order to deceive people. 仿造;假冒

  • ...the coins Davies is alleged to have counterfeited. [VERB noun]

    据称是戴维斯伪造的硬币

SYN fake, copy, forge, imitate  
counterfeiting

  • The business of counterfeiting appears to be expanding.

    假冒伪造业似乎正日益膨胀。

counterfeiter

  • ...a gang of counterfeiters.

    制假团伙
