forefront
fɔːʳfrʌnt ˈfɔrˌfrʌnt
1 N-SING [usu at/in/to N] 显著地位;重要位置;最前沿 If you are at the forefront of a campaign or other activity, you have a leading and influential position in it. 显著地位;重要位置;最前沿

  • They have been at the forefront of the campaign for political change. [+ of/in]

    他们一直处于政治变革运动的风口浪尖上。

SYN lead, centre, front, fore  
2 N-SING [usu at/in/to N of n] (思考或关注的)重心,焦点 If something is at the forefront of people's minds or attention, they think about it a lot because it is particularly important to them. (思考或关注的)重心,焦点

  • The pension issue was not at the forefront of his mind in the spring of 1985.

    在 1985 年春，养老金问题并非他关注的重点。
