chill
tʃɪl tʃɪl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense chills, present participle chilling, past tense , past participle chilled
1 V-ERG (使)冷却;(使)凉下来;冷藏 When you chill something or when it chills, you lower its temperature so that it becomes colder but does not freeze. (使)冷却;(使)凉下来;冷藏

  • Chill the fruit salad until serving time. [VERB noun]

    把水果色拉冰镇一下，吃的时候再拿出来。

  • These doughs can be rolled out while you wait for the pastry to chill. [VERB]

    在等油酥糕饼凉的时候，可以把这些面团擀薄些。

  • ...a glass of chilled champagne. [VERB-ed]

    一杯冰镇香槟

SYN cool, refrigerate, freeze  
2 VERB 使变冷;使冰凉 When cold weather or something cold chills a person or a place, it makes that person or that place feel very cold. 使变冷;使冰凉

  • The marble floor was beginning to chill me. [VERB noun]

    大理石地面开始让我觉得发冷。

  • An exposed garden may be chilled by cold winds. [be VERB-ed]

    露天花园容易被冷风吹透。

  • Wade placed his chilled hands on the radiator and warmed them. [VERB-ed]

    韦德把冰凉的双手放在暖气片上取暖。

  • The boulder sheltered them from the chilling wind. [VERB-ing]

    那块大石头替他们挡住了寒风。

3 VERB 惊吓;使恐惧 If you say that something you see, hear, or feel chills you, you mean that it frightens you. 惊吓;使恐惧 [written]

  • There was a coldness in her that chilled him. [VERB noun]

    她身上透着一股冷漠，让他不寒而栗。

  • Some films chill you to the marrow of your bones. [VERB noun to noun]

    有些电影让人毛骨悚然。

SYN dishearten, depress, discourage, dismay  
4 N-COUNT 害怕;焦虑;紧张 If something sends a chill through you, it gives you a sudden feeling of fear or anxiety. 害怕;焦虑;紧张

  • The violence used against the students sent a chill through Indonesia.

    针对学生的暴力使得整个印度尼西亚不寒而栗。

  • He smiled, an odd, dreamy smile that sent chills up my back.

    他笑了，笑容古怪迷离，叫我后背发凉。

SYN shiver, frisson, goose pimples, goose flesh  
5 N-COUNT 着凉;伤寒 A chill is a mild illness which can give you a slight fever and headache. 着凉;伤寒

  • He caught a chill while performing at a rain-soaked open-air venue.

    他在大雨瓢泼的露天场地演出时受了寒。

6 ADJ [ADJ n] (天气)阴冷的，寒冷的 Chill weather is cold and unpleasant. (天气)阴冷的，寒冷的

  • ...chill winds, rain and choppy seas.

    凄风、冷雨和波浪起伏的大海

Chill is also a noun.

  • September is here, bringing with it a chill in the mornings.

    9月到了，早晨已有了寒意。

  • ...the cold chill of the night.

    夜晚的寒冷
