centralize
sentrəlaɪz ˈsɛntrəlˌaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense centralizes, present participle centralizing, past tense , past participle centralized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 centralise
VERB 使权力中央化;对…集权控制 To centralize a country, state, or organization means to create a system in which one central group of people gives instructions to regional groups. 使权力中央化;对…集权控制

  • In the mass production era multinational firms tended to centralize their operations. [VERB noun]

    在大规模生产的时代，跨国公司往往实行集权化经营。

  • The economy of the times made it difficult to support centralized rule. [VERB-ed]

    当时的经济状况不利于实行集权统治。

SYN unify, concentrate, incorporate, compact
