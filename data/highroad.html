high road haɪ rəʊd
Word forms: plural high roads
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 大路;大街;交通要道 A high road is a main road. 大路;大街;交通要道 [British]

  • The gutters either side of the paved high road were gurgling and even flooding.

    铺砌的大路两旁的排水沟在汩汩作响，甚至漫出水来。

in AM, use 美国英语用 highway
2 N-SING [usu the N to n] （可能导致…的）途径，方式 You say that something is the high road to a particular situation when it seems likely to lead to that situation. （可能导致…的）途径，方式

  • This is the high road to disaster.

    这很可能会导致灾难。

3 N-SING 最好（或最有把握）的途径;最保险的方式 If you say that someone is taking the high road in a situation, you mean that they are taking the most positive and careful course of action. 最好（或最有把握）的途径;最保险的方式 [mainly US]

  • US diplomats say the president is likely to take the high road in his statements about trade.

    美国的外交官们说，总统在贸易问题的声明中可能会采取最保险的立场。
