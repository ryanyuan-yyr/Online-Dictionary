cricket
krɪkɪt ˈkrɪkɪt
Word forms: plural crickets
1 N-UNCOUNT 板球(运动) Cricket is an outdoor game played between two teams. Players try to score points, called runs, by hitting a ball with a wooden bat. 板球(运动)

  • During the summer term we would play cricket at the village ground.

    在夏季学期，我们会在村里的空地上打板球。

  • ...the Yorkshire County Cricket Club.

    约克郡板球俱乐部

2 PHRASE [verb-link PHRASE] 不公正;不光明正大;不光彩 If you say that someone's behaviour is not cricket, you mean that they have not behaved fairly. 不公正;不光明正大;不光彩 [British, old-fashioned]

  • Their treatment of staff is definitely not cricket.

    他们对待员工的方式绝对不公正。

3 N-COUNT 蟋蟀;蛐蛐 A cricket is a small jumping insect that produces short, loud sounds by rubbing its wings together. 蟋蟀;蛐蛐
