cry
kraɪ kraɪ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense cries, present participle crying, past tense , past participle cried
1 VERB 哭;哭泣 When you cry, tears come from your eyes, usually because you are unhappy or hurt. 哭;哭泣

  • I hung up the phone and started to cry. [VERB]

    我挂断电话，开始哭了起来。

  • Please don't cry. [VERB]

    拜托不要哭。

  • He cried with anger and frustration. [VERB + with]

    他又生气又沮丧，不禁哭了起来。

  • ...a crying baby. [VERB-ing]

    啼哭的婴儿

SYN weep, sob, bawl, shed tears  
Cry is also a noun.

  • A nurse patted me on the shoulder and said, 'You have a good cry, dear.'

    一位护士拍了拍我的肩膀说，“痛快地哭一场吧，宝贝儿。”

crying

  • She had been unable to sleep for three days because of her 13-week-old son's crying.

    她13周大的儿子老是哭，她已经有3天没睡了。

2 VERB 喊叫;呼喊;高声说 If you cry something, you shout it or say it loudly. 喊叫;呼喊;高声说 [Also VERB that] [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • 'Nancy Drew,' she cried, 'you're under arrest!'. [VERB with quote]

    “南茜·朱尔，”她喊道，“你被捕了！”

  • I cried: 'It's wonderful news!' [VERB with quote]

    我叫道：“真是个好消息！”

Cry out means the same as cry .cry out 同 cry

  • 'You're wrong, quite wrong!' Henry cried out, suddenly excited.

    “你错了，大错特错！”亨利突然激动地大喊道。

  • According to the legend, she cried out that no storm was going to stop her from finishing her ride.

    据传，她当时大声宣布，即使有再大的暴风雨她也要骑到终点。

3 N-COUNT (表达强烈感情的)叫喊，叫声 A cry is a loud, high sound that you make when you feel a strong emotion such as fear, pain, or pleasure. (表达强烈感情的)叫喊，叫声

  • A cry of horror broke from me. [+ of]

    我吓得大叫了一声。

  • Her brother gave a cry of recognition.

    她哥哥认了出来，惊呼了一声。

  • With a cry, she rushed forward.

    她大喊一声，向前冲去。

SYN shout, call, bell, scream  
4 N-COUNT 喊叫;呼喊 A cry is a shouted word or phrase, usually one that is intended to attract someone's attention. 喊叫;呼喊

  • Thousands of Ukrainians burst into cries of 'bravo' on the steps of the parliament. [+ of]

    成千上万的乌克兰人聚集在议会门前高喊“好啊”。

  • Passers-by heard his cries for help. [+ for]

    路过的人听到了他求救的呼喊。

5See also:battle cry  rallying cry 
6 N-COUNT 抗议;请求 You can refer to a public protest about something or an appeal for something as a cry of some kind. 抗议;请求 [journalism] [Also+ for]

  • There have been cries of outrage about this expenditure. [+ of]

    这项开支遭到了一些人愤怒的指责。

  • Many other countries have turned a deaf ear to their cries for help.

    很多其他国家对他们的求助置若罔闻。

SYN appeal, prayer, plea, petition  
7 N-COUNT (鸟的)鸣叫，啼叫;(动物的)嗥叫，吠 A bird's or animal's cry is the loud, high sound that it makes. (鸟的)鸣叫，啼叫;(动物的)嗥叫，吠

  • ...the cry of a seagull. [+ of]

    海鸥的鸣叫

8 See also: crying 
9 PHRASE (与…)大相径庭，相去甚远 Something that is a far cry from something else is very different from it. (与…)大相径庭，相去甚远

  • Their lives are a far cry from his own poor childhood.

    他们的生活与他那贫苦的童年生活相比大相径庭。

10 PHRASE [verb-link PHRASE] 大声疾呼;激情呐喊 When someone is in full cry, they are expressing their views very strongly or are very active. 大声疾呼;激情呐喊

  • The main opposition party is already in full cry over this mishandling of security.

    主要反对党已经对这起安全事故的错误处理进行了猛烈抨击。

11 EXCLAM 天啊，哎呀，岂有此理(用来表示恼怒或不耐烦，或加强提问或请求的语气) You use the expression for crying out loud in order to show that you are annoyed or impatient, or to add force to a question or request. 天啊，哎呀，岂有此理(用来表示恼怒或不耐烦，或加强提问或请求的语气) [informal, spoken, feelings]

  • I mean, what's he ever done in his life, for crying out loud?

    我的意思是，天啊，他一生中到底做过什么呀？
