muster
mʌstəʳ ˈmʌstər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense musters, present participle mustering, past tense , past participle mustered
1 VERB 聚集(支持力量);奋(力);集中(精力) If you muster something such as support, strength, or energy, you gather as much of it as you can in order to do something. 聚集(支持力量);奋(力);集中(精力)

  • He travelled around West Africa trying to muster support for his movement. [VERB noun]

    他在西非四处奔走，为自己的运动争取支持。

  • Mustering all her strength, Nancy pulled hard on both oars. [VERB noun]

    南希使出所有力气，用力划动双桨。

SYN summon up, collect, call up, marshal  
2 V-ERG 召集，集合(士兵) When soldiers muster or are mustered, they gather together in one place in order to take part in a military action. 召集，集合(士兵)

  • The men mustered before their clan chiefs. [VERB]

    男人们聚集在族长们面前。

  • The general had mustered his troops north of the Hindu Kush. [VERB noun]

    将军在兴都库什山脉以北集结军队。

SYN rally, group, gather, assemble  
3 PHRASE 合格;符合要求 If someone or something passes muster, they are good enough for the thing they are needed for. 合格;符合要求

  • I could not pass muster in his language.

    我说不好他的语言。

  • If it doesn't pass muster, a radio station could have its license challenged.

    如果不合要求，电台可能会被吊销执照。

SYN be acceptable, qualify, measure up, make the grade
