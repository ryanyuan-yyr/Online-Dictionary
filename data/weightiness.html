weighty
weɪti weɪti
Word forms: comparative weightier, superlative weightiest
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 重要的;事关重大的 If you describe something such as an issue or a decision as weighty, you mean that it is serious or important. 重要的;事关重大的 [formal]

  • Surely such weighty matters merit a higher level of debate?

    这样重大的事件谅必应进行更高级别的讨论吧？

SYN important, serious, significant, critical  
2 ADJ-GRADED (尤指书)重的，沉甸甸的 You use weighty to describe something, especially a book, that is heavy or heavier than you would expect. (尤指书)重的，沉甸甸的 [literary]

  • Simon lifted a weighty volume from the shelf.

    西蒙从书架上取下一本厚重的书。

SYN heavy, massive, dense, hefty [informal]
