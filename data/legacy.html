legacy
legəsi ˈlɛgəsi
Word forms: plural legacies
1 N-COUNT 遗产；遗赠 A legacy is money or property which someone leaves to you when they die. 遗产；遗赠

  • You could make a real difference to someone's life by leaving them a generous legacy.

    慷慨留下您的遗产，改写他人的人生。

SYN bequest, inheritance, endowment, gift  
2 N-COUNT [n NOUN] （事件或历史的）遗留问题，后遗症 A legacy of an event or period of history is something which is a direct result of it and which continues to exist after it is over. （事件或历史的）遗留问题，后遗症

  • ...a programme to overcome the legacy of inequality and injustice created by Apartheid. [+ of]

    旨在消除种族隔离政策所遗留的不平等与不公正问题的项目

  • The old system has left a mixed legacy.

    旧制度留下的遗产有好有坏。

SYN repercussion, result, fruit, consequences
