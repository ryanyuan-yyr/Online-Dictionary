suffuse
səfjuːz səˈfjuz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense suffuses, present participle suffusing, past tense , past participle suffused
1 VERB (尤指色彩或感情)弥漫于，布满，充满 If something, especially a colour or feeling, suffuses a person or thing, it gradually spreads over or through them. (尤指色彩或感情)弥漫于，布满，充满 [literary]

  • A dull red flush suffused Selby's face. [VERB noun]

    塞尔比的脸庞泛起了淡淡的红晕。

SYN spread through or over, flood, infuse, cover  
2 VERB 使充满，使洋溢着(某种品质) If something such as a book, film, or piece of music is suffused with a quality, it is full of that quality. 使充满，使洋溢着(某种品质) [formal]

  • This book is suffused with Shaw's characteristic wry Irish humour. [be VERB-ed + with]

    这本书充满了萧伯纳独特的爱尔兰式幽默与嘲讽。

  • Kingdon's broad experience, as writer and scholar, suffuses this important book. [VERB noun]

    金登身为作家和学者的丰富阅历在这部重要的著作中体现得淋漓尽致。
