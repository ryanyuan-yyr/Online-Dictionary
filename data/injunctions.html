injunction
ɪndʒʌŋkʃən ɪnˈdʒʌŋkʃən
Word forms: plural injunctions
1 N-COUNT (法院下的)强制令，禁制令 An injunction is a court order, usually one telling someone not to do something. (法院下的)强制令，禁制令 [law]

  • He took out a court injunction against the newspaper demanding the return of the document. [+ against]

    他取得了法院强制令，要求报社归还那份文件。

SYN order, ruling, command, instruction  
2 N-COUNT [NOUN to-infinitive] 命令;指令;训谕 An injunction to do something is an order or strong request to do it. 命令;指令;训谕 [formal]

  • We hear endless injunctions to build a sense of community among staff.

    我们听到上面没完没了地讲要在员工中培养一种集体归属感。
