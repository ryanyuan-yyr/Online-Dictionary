moralize
mɒrəlaɪz mɔːr- ˈmɔrəlˌaɪz ˈmɑrəlaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense moralizes, present participle moralizing, past tense , past participle moralized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 moralise
VERB 进行训诫，进行说教，教化 If you say that someone is moralizing, you are critical of them for telling people what they think is right or wrong, especially when they have not been asked their opinion. 进行训诫，进行说教，教化 [disapproval]

  • As a dramatist I hate to moralize. [VERB]

    作为一名剧作家，我憎恨说教。

  • Society in general moralized about 'loose women'. [VERB]

    社会通常会对那些“浪荡女子”指指点点。

moralizing

  • We have tried to avoid any moralising.

    我们力图避免任何形式的说教。
