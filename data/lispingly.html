lisp
lɪsp lɪsp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense lisps, present participle lisping, past tense , past participle lisped
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 咬舌（指将s和z读作th，如把sing念作thing等） If someone has a lisp, they pronounce the sounds 's' and 'z' as if they were 'th'. For example, they say 'thing' instead of 'sing'. 咬舌（指将s和z读作th，如把sing念作thing等）

  • He has a slight lisp.

    他说话有点咬舌。

2 VERB 咬着舌说；口齿不清地说 If someone lisps, they say something with a lisp or speak with a lisp. 咬着舌说；口齿不清地说

  • The little man, upset, was lisping badly. [VERB]

    那个矮个子因为非常生气，所以说话咬舌咬得很厉害。

  • Bochmann lisped his congratulations. [VERB noun]

    博克曼口齿不清地表示祝贺。

  • ...her low, lisping voice. [VERB-ing]

    她低而不清的声音
