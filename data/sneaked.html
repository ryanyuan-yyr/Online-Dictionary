sneak
sniːk snik
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense sneaks, present participle sneaking, past tense , past participle sneaked
The form snuck is also used in American English for the past tense and past participle. 在美国英语里 snuck 也用作过去式和过去分词。
1 VERB 潜行;偷偷地走 If you sneak somewhere, you go there very quietly on foot, trying to avoid being seen or heard. 潜行;偷偷地走

  • Sometimes he would sneak out of his house late at night to be with me. [VERB adv./prep.]

    有时候，他会在深夜里偷偷溜出家与我相会。

  • Don't sneak away and hide. [VERB adv./prep.]

    别溜走藏起来。

SYN slink, slip, steal, creep  
2 VERB 偷带;私运 If you sneak something somewhere, you take it there secretly. 偷带;私运

  • He smuggled papers out each day, photocopied them, and snuck them back. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他每天都把文件偷偷地带出去复印，然后再偷偷地拿回来。

  • You even snuck me a cigarette. [VERB noun noun]

    你居然偷偷塞给我一支烟。

SYN slip, smuggle, spirit  
3 VERB 偷(看) If you sneak a look at someone or something, you secretly have a quick look at them. 偷(看)

  • You sneak a look at your watch to see how long you've got to wait. [VERB noun prep.]

    你偷偷地望一眼手表，看看要等多长时间。
