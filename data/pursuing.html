pursue
pəʳsjuː -suː pərˈsu pərˈsju
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense pursues, present participle pursuing, past tense , past participle pursued
1 VERB 实行；执行；贯彻 If you pursue an activity, interest, or plan, you carry it out or follow it. 实行；执行；贯彻 [formal]

  • It became harder for women married to diplomats to pursue their own interests. [VERB noun]

    与外交官结婚的女性要追求自己的爱好就变得更加困难了。

  • He said Japan would continue to pursue the policies laid down at the London summit. [VERB noun]

    他说日本将继续贯彻伦敦峰会所制定的政策。

  • She had come to England to pursue an acting career. [VERB noun]

    她来英格兰投身演艺事业。

SYN engage in, follow, perform, conduct  
2 VERB 寻求；追求 If you pursue a particular aim or result, you make efforts to achieve it, often over a long period of time. 寻求；追求 [formal]

  • The implication seems to be that it is impossible to pursue economic reform and democracy simultaneously. [VERB noun]

    言外之意似乎是经济改革和民主化进程是不可能同时进行的。

  • Mr. Menendez has aggressively pursued new business. [VERB noun]

    梅嫩德斯先生积极开拓新业务。

SYN try for, seek, desire, search for  
3 VERB 追查；追问；追究 If you pursue a particular topic, you try to find out more about it by asking questions. 追查；追问；追究 [formal]

  • If your original request is denied, don't be afraid to pursue the matter. [VERB noun]

    如果你最初的请求遭到拒绝，不要害怕，要继续追问下去。

SYN continue, maintain, carry on, keep on  
4 VERB 追赶；追逐；追捕 If you pursue a person, vehicle, or animal, you follow them, usually in order to catch them. 追赶；追逐；追捕 [formal]

  • She pursued the man who had stolen a woman's bag. [VERB noun]

    她追赶那个偷了一个妇女提包的男人。

SYN follow, track, hunt, chase
