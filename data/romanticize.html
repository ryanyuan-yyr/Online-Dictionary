romanticize
roʊmæntɪsaɪz roʊˈmæntəˌsaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense romanticizes, present participle romanticizing, past tense , past participle romanticized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 roman-ticise
VERB 使浪漫化;使传奇化;使具有浪漫色彩 If you romanticize someone or something, you think or talk about them in a way which is not at all realistic and which makes them seem better than they really are. 使浪漫化;使传奇化;使具有浪漫色彩

  • He romanticized the past as he became disillusioned with his present. [VERB noun]

    当他对现在不再抱任何幻想时，便把过去想得非常浪漫。

romanticized

  • Mr. Lane's film takes a highly romanticized view of life on the streets.

    莱恩先生的电影从一个非常理想化的视角描述流浪街头的生活。
