grandeur
grændʒəʳ ˈɡrændʒə ˈgrændʒər ˈgrænˌdʒʊr ˈgrænˌdjʊr
1 N-UNCOUNT 宏伟；堂皇；壮丽 If something such as a building or a piece of scenery has grandeur, it is impressive because of its size, its beauty, or its power. 宏伟；堂皇；壮丽

  • ...the grandeur and natural beauty of South America.

    南美洲壮丽的自然美景

  • Only inside do you appreciate the church's true grandeur.

    只有深入其中，你才能真正欣赏教堂的富丽堂皇。

SYN splendour, glory, majesty, nobility  
2 N-UNCOUNT [oft poss NOUN] 高贵；显赫；自命不凡 Someone's grandeur is the great importance and social status that they have, or think they have. 高贵；显赫；自命不凡

  • He is wholly concerned with his own grandeur.

    他只关心自己的显赫地位。

SYN importance, status, prestige, standing  
3 delusions of grandeur→see: delusion
