image
ɪmɪdʒ ˈɪmɪdʒ
Word forms: plural images
1 N-COUNT (头脑中的)形象,概念 If you have an image of something or someone, you have a picture or idea of them in your mind. (头脑中的)形象,概念

  • The image of art theft as a gentleman's crime is outdated. [+ of]

    把盗窃艺术品看作是一种绅士犯罪的观念已经过时了。

  • The words 'Cote d'Azur' conjure up images of sunny days in Mediterranean cafes.

    Cote d'Azur (蓝色海岸)这个词让人联想到地中海咖啡馆阳光明媚的日子。

SYN thought, idea, vision, concept  
2 N-COUNT [oft with poss] (个人、团队或组织的)形象,声誉 The image of a person, group, or organization is the way that they appear to other people. (个人、团队或组织的)形象,声誉

  • The Prime Minister knows that his personal image is his greatest political asset.

    总理知道他的个人形象是他最重要的政治资产。

  • He has cultivated the image of an elder statesman.

    他已树立起一个政界元老的形象。

  • The tobacco industry has been trying to improve its image.

    烟草行业一直在试图改善其形象。

SYN profile, face, front [informal] , role  
3 N-COUNT 图像；映像；影像 An image is a picture of someone or something. 图像；映像；影像 [formal]

  • ...photographic images of young children. [+ of]

    小孩子们的照片

  • A computer in the machine creates an image on the screen.

    机器上的计算机在屏幕上产生了影像。

SYN picture, photo, photograph, representation  
4 N-COUNT 诗意的描绘;生动的描绘 An image is a poetic description of something. 诗意的描绘;生动的描绘 [formal]

  • The natural images in the poem are meant to be suggestive of realities beyond themselves.

    诗中描绘的自然景象意在让人联想到景象以外的现实。

SYN figure of speech, metaphor, simile, conceit [archaic]  
5 PHRASE 是…的翻版;长相酷似… If you are the image of someone else, you look very much like them. 是…的翻版;长相酷似…

  • Marianne's son was the image of his father.

    玛丽安娜的儿子长得酷似他父亲。

6 See also: mirror image  spitting image→see: spit
