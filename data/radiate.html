radiate
reɪdieɪt ˈreɪdiˌeɪt ˈreɪdiɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense radiates, present participle radiating, past tense , past participle radiated
1 VERB 辐射；(从中心)发散 If things radiate out from a place, they form a pattern that is like lines drawn from the centre of a circle to various points on its edge. 辐射；(从中心)发散

  • ...the various walks which radiate from the Heritage Centre. [VERB + from]

    从遗产中心向四周延伸出的许许多多步行道

  • From here, contaminated air radiates out to the open countryside. [VERB prep./adv.]

    被污染的空气从这里扩散到开阔的乡村地区。

SYN emanate, shine, beam, be diffused  
2 VERB 流露，显示（情感） If you radiate an emotion or quality or if it radiates from you, people can see it very clearly in your face and in your behaviour. 流露，显示（情感）

  • She radiates happiness and health. [VERB noun]

    她看起来幸福而健康。

  • Her voice hadn't changed but I felt the anger that radiated from her. [VERB + from]

    尽管她声音没变，但我还是感受到了她所流露出的怒气。

SYN show, display, demonstrate, exhibit  
3 VERB 散发（热）；发射（光） If something radiates heat or light, heat or light comes from it. 散发（热）；发射（光）

  • Stoves are meant to radiate heat. [VERB noun]

    炉子就是要发热的。
