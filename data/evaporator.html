evaporate
ɪvæpəreɪt iˈvæpəˌreɪt ɪˈvæpəˌreɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense evaporates, present participle evaporating, past tense , past participle evaporated
1 V-ERG （使）蒸发;（使）挥发 When a liquid evaporates, or is evaporated, it changes from a liquid state to a gas, because its temperature has increased. （使）蒸发;（使）挥发 [Also VERB n]

  • Moisture is drawn to the surface of the fabric so that it evaporates. [VERB]

    湿气被吸到织物表面从而蒸发。

  • The water is evaporated by the sun. [be VERB-ed]

    水在阳光照射下蒸发了。

SYN dry up, dry, dehydrate, vaporize  
evaporation

  • High temperatures also result in high evaporation from the plants.

    高温也导致了植物水分的大量蒸发。

  • The soothing, cooling effect is caused by the evaporation of the sweat on the skin.

    这种放松而又凉爽的感觉是由于皮肤表面汗液的蒸发而产生的。

2 VERB （情绪）逐渐消失；（计划）逐渐落空；（活动）逐渐停止 If a feeling, plan, or activity evaporates, it gradually becomes weaker and eventually disappears completely. （情绪）逐渐消失；（计划）逐渐落空；（活动）逐渐停止

  • My anger evaporated and I wanted to cry. [VERB]

    我的怒气渐渐消失，想大哭一场。

  • Your dreams always seem to evaporate, and nothing ever quite matches expectations. [VERB]

    你的梦想似乎总是逐渐破灭，从来没有什么事情能完全符合你的预期。

  • The project evaporated and Harry was left high and dry. [VERB]

    那个项目逐渐叫停，哈里陷入了困境。

SYN fade away, disappear, fade, melt
