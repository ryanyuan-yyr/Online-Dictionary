leverage
liːvərɪdʒ lev- ˈlɛvərɪdʒ ˈlivərɪdʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense leverages, present participle leveraging, past tense , past participle leveraged
1 N-UNCOUNT （能够操控形势的）影响力，手段，优势 Leverage is the ability to influence situations or people so that you can control what happens. （能够操控形势的）影响力，手段，优势

  • His function as a Mayor affords him the leverage to get things done through attending committee meetings.

    他的市长身份使他有能力通过出席委员会会议来达成一些事情。

SYN influence, authority, pull [informal] , weight  
2 N-UNCOUNT 杠杆力；杠杆作用 Leverage is the force that is applied to an object when something such as a lever is used. 杠杆力；杠杆作用

  • The spade and fork have longer shafts, providing better leverage.

    锹和耙的手柄较长，可发挥较好的杠杆作用。

SYN force, hold, pull, strength  
3 VERB 举债经营；为…融资 To leverage a company or investment means to use borrowed money in order to buy it or pay for it. 举债经营；为…融资 [business]

  • He might feel that leveraging the company at a time when he sees tremendous growth opportunities would be a mistake. [VERB noun]

    他也许觉得，在他认为大有可为的时候让公司借贷经营是一个错误。
