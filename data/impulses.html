impulse
ɪmpʌls ˈɪmˌpʌls
Word forms: plural impulses
1 N-VAR [oft NOUN to-infinitive] 冲动;一时兴起的念头 An impulse is a sudden desire to do something. 冲动;一时兴起的念头

  • Unable to resist the impulse, he glanced at the sea again.

    他抑制不住冲动,又向海面瞥了一眼。

  • He still couldn't understand the impulse that had made him confide in Cassandra.

    他仍然难以理解是什么样的冲动使他向卡桑德拉吐露了秘密。

  • Wade resisted an impulse to smile.

    韦德强忍住想笑的冲动。

SYN urge, longing, desire, drive  
2 N-COUNT 脉冲 An impulse is a short electrical signal that is sent along a wire or nerve or through the air, usually as one of a series. 脉冲
3 ADJ [ADJ n] 一时兴起的;随兴的 An impulse buy or impulse purchase is something that you decide to buy when you see it, although you had not planned to buy it. 一时兴起的;随兴的

  • The curtains were an impulse buy.

    窗帘是一时兴起买下的。

4 PHRASE 凭冲动;一时兴起 If you do something on impulse, you suddenly decide to do it, without planning it. 凭冲动;一时兴起

  • Sean's a fast thinker, and he acts on impulse.

    肖恩是个思维敏捷、行事随兴的人。

  • After lunch she decided on impulse to take a bath.

    午饭后她突然兴起,决定洗个澡。

SYN impulsively, of your own accord, freely, voluntarily
