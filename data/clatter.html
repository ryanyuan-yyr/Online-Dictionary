clatter
klætəʳ ˈklætər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense clatters, present participle clattering, past tense , past participle clattered
1 VERB (移动时)发出声响 If you say that people or things clatter somewhere, you mean that they move there noisily. (移动时)发出声响

  • He turned and clattered down the stairs. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他转过身，噔噔噔地下了楼梯。

2 VERB (硬物)发出碰撞声 If something hard clatters, it makes repeated short noises as it hits against another hard thing. (硬物)发出碰撞声 [literary]

  • His hobnail boots clattered on the stone floor. [VERB prep.]

    他那双钉了铁掌的靴子在石地板上嗒嗒响。

  • She set her cup down, and it clattered against the saucer. [VERB prep.]

    她放下杯子时在茶碟上咣当碰了一下。

Clatter is also a noun.

  • From somewhere distant he heard the clatter of a typewriter.

    他听见从远处什么地方传来打字机的嗒嗒声。
