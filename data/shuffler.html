shuffle
ʃʌfəl ˈʃʌfəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense shuffles, present participle shuffling, past tense , past participle shuffled
1 VERB 拖着脚走 If you shuffle somewhere, you walk there without lifting your feet properly off the ground. 拖着脚走

  • Moira shuffled across the kitchen. [VERB prep./adv.]

    莫伊拉拖着脚穿过厨房。

  • They shuffled along somewhat reluctantly. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他们有些不情愿地拖着脚走着。

SYN shamble, stagger, stumble, lumber  
Shuffle is also a noun.

  • She noticed her own proud walk had become a shuffle.

    她意识到自己趾高气扬的步伐已变成了拖着脚走路了。

2 VERB (通常因不舒服或难为情)挪动双脚，坐立不安 If you shuffle around, you move your feet about while standing or you move your bottom about while sitting, often because you feel uncomfortable or embarrassed. (通常因不舒服或难为情)挪动双脚，坐立不安

  • He shuffles around in his chair. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他在椅子上如坐针毡。

  • He grinned and shuffled his feet. [VERB noun]

    他咧嘴笑了，脚动来动去。

3 VERB 洗(牌) If you shuffle playing cards, you mix them up before you begin a game. 洗(牌) [Also VERB]

  • There are various ways of shuffling and dealing the cards. [VERB noun]

    有各种不同的洗牌和发牌方式。

4 VERB 将(纸张等)变换位置;打乱…的次序 If you shuffle things such as pieces of paper, you move them around so that they are in a different order. 将(纸张等)变换位置;打乱…的次序

  • The silence lengthened as Thorne unnecessarily shuffled some papers. [VERB noun]

    索恩整理着一些本无须整理的文件，沉默还在延续。

SYN rearrange, jumble, reorganize, mix
