appal
əpɔːl əˈpɔl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense appals, present participle appalling, past tense , past participle appalled
in AM, use 美国英语用 appall
VERB 使厌恶；使惊骇 If something appals you, it disgusts you because it seems so bad or unpleasant. 使厌恶；使惊骇 [Also VERB]

  • His ignorance appals me. [VERB noun]

    他的无知让我震惊。

  • My wife now looks like her mother, which appals me. [VERB noun]

    我老婆现在看着就跟丈母娘似的，可吓人了。

SYN horrify, shock, alarm, frighten
