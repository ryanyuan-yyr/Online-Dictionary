filthy
fɪlθi ˈfɪlθi
Word forms: comparative filthier, superlative filthiest
1 ADJ-GRADED 肮脏的;污秽的 Something that is filthy is very dirty indeed. 肮脏的;污秽的

  • He never washed, and always wore a filthy old jacket.

    他总是穿着一件从来不洗的脏兮兮的旧夹克。

SYN grimy, black, muddy, smoky  
2 ADJ-GRADED 卑劣的;淫秽的;猥亵的 If you describe something as filthy, you mean that you think it is morally very unpleasant and disgusting, sometimes in a sexual way. 卑劣的;淫秽的;猥亵的 [disapproval]

  • Apparently, well known actors were at these filthy parties.

    听说有些知名演员也参加了这些下流的聚会。

  • The play was full of filthy foul language.

    这部戏充斥着猥亵粗俗的语言。

SYN obscene, foul, corrupt, coarse  
3 ADJ-GRADED (天气)阴冷潮湿的，恶劣的 Filthy weather is very cold, wet, and windy. (天气)阴冷潮湿的，恶劣的 [informal, old-fashioned]

  • ...a filthy wet night.

    糟糕的雨夜

SYN stormy, bad, wild, rough
