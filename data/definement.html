define
dɪfaɪn diˈfaɪn dɪˈfaɪn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense defines, present participle defining, past tense , past participle defined
1 VERB 阐明;规定;限定;使明确 If you define something, you show, describe, or state clearly what it is and what its limits are, or what it is like. 阐明;规定;限定;使明确

  • We were unable to define what exactly was wrong with him. [VERB wh]

    我们说不清楚他到底哪里不对劲。

  • He was asked to define his concept of cool. [VERB noun]

    他被要求说明自己关于“酷”的定义。

defined

  • ...a party with a clearly defined programme and strict rules of membership.

    纲领明确、准入条件严格的政党

2 VERB 给…下定义;解释 If you define a word or expression, you explain its meaning, for example in a dictionary. 给…下定义;解释

  • When people are asked 'What is intelligence?' they tend to reply: 'I don't know how to define it, but I can certainly recognize it when I see it.'. [VERB noun]

    当被问及“智力是什么”的时候，人们往往会回答说：“我不知道该如何定义它，但我见到了肯定就能认得出。”

  • Collins English Dictionary defines a workaholic as 'a person obsessively addicted to work'. [VERB noun + as]

    《柯林斯英语词典》给“工作狂”一词下的定义是“一个过分沉溺于工作中的人”。
