socialize
soʊʃəlaɪz ˈsoʊʃəˌlaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense socializes, present participle socializing, past tense , past participle socialized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 socialise
1 VERB 进行社交活动;与人交往 If you socialize, you meet other people socially, for example at parties. 进行社交活动;与人交往

  • ...an open meeting, where members socialized and welcomed any new members. [VERB]

    会员相互交流，同时也欢迎新成员加入的开放型会议

  • It distressed her that she and Charles no longer socialized with old friends. [VERB + with]

    她和查尔斯不再跟老朋友们来往，这让她非常苦恼。

socializing

  • The hours were terrible, so socialising was difficult.

    时机不对，因此交往也有困难。

2 VERB [usually passive] 使社会化;使合群 When people, especially children, are socialized, they are made to behave in a way which is acceptable in their culture or society. 使社会化;使合群 [technical]

  • You may have been socialized to do as you are told. [be VERB-ed]

    也许你已经被同化了—让你做什么，你就做什么。

  • From the time you are born you have to be socialised into being a good father. [be VERB-ed]

    从出生的那一刻起，你就得学习融入群体，最后变成一个好父亲。

SYN condition, train, teach, rear  
3 VERB [usually passive] 使…为社会所有;使社会主义化 When something is socialized, it is made to operate on socialist principles. 使…为社会所有;使社会主义化 [technical]

  • ...a socialist system in which the ownership of the means of production will be socialized. [be VERB-ed]

    生产资料所有权社会化的社会主义制度

  • ...the debate over how a fully socialized economy might work. [VERB-ed]

    关于全面的社会化经济如何运行的辩论
