froth
frɒθ frɔːθ frɔθ frɑθ frɔð frɑð
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense froths, present participle frothing, past tense , past participle frothed
1 N-UNCOUNT (液体表面的)泡,泡沫 Froth is a mass of small bubbles on the surface of a liquid. (液体表面的)泡,泡沫

  • ...the froth of bubbles on the top of a glass of beer.

    一杯啤酒表面的啤酒沫

  • The froth is blown away.

    泡沫给吹开了。

SYN foam, head, bubbles, lather  
2 VERB 起泡沫;生泡沫 If a liquid froths, small bubbles appear on its surface. 起泡沫;生泡沫

  • The sea froths over my feet. [VERB prep.]

    海水在我的脚上泛起泡沫。

  • Add a little of the warmed milk and allow to froth a little. [VERB]

    加一点温牛奶，并让它起点儿沫。

SYN fizz, foam, come to a head, lather  
3 VERB (因非常愤怒或激动而)嘴喷唾沫，口吐白沫 If you say that someone is frothing, or that they are frothing at the mouth, you are emphasizing that they are very angry or excited about something. (因非常愤怒或激动而)嘴喷唾沫，口吐白沫 [written, emphasis] [Also VERB]

  • 'No! No! Never!' he froths. [VERB with quote]

    “不！不！绝不!”他气得口吐白沫。

  • This story has many ingredients which make any news editor froth at the mouth with excitement. [VERB prep.]

    这篇报道包含了众多元素，任何一个新闻编辑都会激动得嘴上唾沫横飞。

4 N-UNCOUNT 迷人但没有实际价值的事物;虚浮的东西 If you refer to an activity or object as froth, you disapprove of it because it appears exciting or attractive, but has very little real value or importance. 迷人但没有实际价值的事物;虚浮的东西 [disapproval]

  • No substance at all, just froth.

    根本没有任何实质，只是一些虚浮的东西。

  • Falling in love the first time is all froth and fantasy.

    初恋完全是一场虚幻的美梦。

SYN trivia, trifles, frivolity, trivialities
