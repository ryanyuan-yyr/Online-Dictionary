beacon
biːkən ˈbikən
Word forms: plural beacons
1 N-COUNT 灯塔 A beacon is a light or a fire, usually on a hill or tower, which acts as a signal or a warning. 灯塔
2 N-COUNT 指路明灯；指引者；楷模 If someone acts as a beacon to other people, they inspire or encourage them. 指路明灯；指引者；楷模

  • Our Parliament has been a beacon of hope to the peoples of Europe. [+ of]

    我们的国会一直是欧洲各族人民的希望之灯。

  • General Rudnicki was a moral beacon for many exiled Poles. [+ for]

    鲁德尼茨基将军是众多被放逐的波兰人精神上的引路人。
