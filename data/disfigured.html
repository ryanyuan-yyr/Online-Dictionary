disfigure
dɪsfɪgəʳ -gjər dɪsˈfɪgjər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense disfigures, present participle disfiguring, past tense , past participle disfigured
1 VERB [usually passive] 使变丑;毁损…的容貌 If someone is disfigured, their appearance is spoiled. 使变丑;毁损…的容貌

  • Many of the wounded had been badly disfigured. [be VERB-ed]

    很多伤者面容严重受损。

disfigured

  • She tried not to look at the scarred, disfigured face.

    她尽量不去看那张带有疤痕的丑陋脸庞。

  • Two dogs attacked him, leaving him horribly disfigured.

    两条狗袭击了他，导致他面容全毁。

2 VERB 破坏…的外形;毁损…的外观 To disfigure an object or a place means to spoil its appearance. 破坏…的外形;毁损…的外观

  • Wind turbines are large and noisy and they disfigure the landscape. [VERB noun]

    风力涡轮机个头大、噪音响，还会破坏周边风景。
