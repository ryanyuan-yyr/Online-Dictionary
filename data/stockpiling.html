stockpile
stɒkpaɪl ˈstɑkˌpaɪl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense stockpiles, present participle stockpiling, past tense , past participle stockpiled
1 VERB 大量贮备，囤积(食品或武器) If people stockpile things such as food or weapons, they store large quantities of them for future use. 大量贮备，囤积(食品或武器)

  • People are stockpiling food for the coming winter. [VERB noun]

    人们正在为即将到来的冬天贮备大量食物。

SYN store up, hoard, put away, save  
2 N-COUNT 大量的储备;囤积 A stockpile of things is a large quantity of them that have been stored for future use. 大量的储备;囤积

  • The two leaders also approved treaties to cut stockpiles of chemical weapons. [+ of]

    两位领导人还同意签署削减化学武器储备的几项条约。

SYN stock, store, reserve, bank
