jurisdiction
dʒʊərɪsdɪkʃən ˌdʒʊrɪsˈdɪkʃən
Word forms: plural jurisdictions
1 N-UNCOUNT 司法权；审判权；管辖权 Jurisdiction is the power that a court of law or an official has to carry out legal judgments or to enforce laws. 司法权；审判权；管辖权 [formal]

  • The British police have no jurisdiction over foreign bank accounts. [+ over]

    外国银行账户不在英国警方的管辖权限之内。

SYN authority, say, power, control  
2 N-COUNT 管辖区域；管辖范围 A jurisdiction is a state or other area in which a particular court and system of laws has authority. 管辖区域；管辖范围 [law]
