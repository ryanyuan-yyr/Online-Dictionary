poke
poʊk poʊk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense pokes, present participle poking, past tense , past participle poked
1 VERB （用手指或尖物）戳，捅，杵 If you poke someone or something, you quickly push them with your finger or with a sharp object. （用手指或尖物）戳，捅，杵

  • Lindy poked him in the ribs. [VERB noun]

    琳迪戳了戳他的肋部。

SYN jab, hit, push, stick  
Poke is also a noun.

  • John smiled at them and gave Richard a playful poke.

    约翰冲着他们笑了笑，开玩笑地捅了理查德一下。

2 VERB 把…戳进；把…插入 If you poke one thing into another, you push the first thing into the second thing. 把…戳进；把…插入

  • He poked his finger into the hole. [VERB noun + into]

    他把手指插入孔中。

3 VERB （从后面或下面）露出，冒出 If something pokes out of or through another thing, you can see part of it appearing from behind or underneath the other thing. （从后面或下面）露出，冒出

  • He saw the dog's twitching nose poke out of the basket. [V + out of]

    他看到那只狗一抽一抽的鼻子从篮子里冒出来。

  • His fingers poked through the worn tips of his gloves. [VERB + through]

    他的手指从手套的破指尖处露了出来。

SYN protrude, stick, thrust, jut  
4 V-ERG （使）（头）伸出；（使）探出 If you poke your head through an opening or if it pokes through an opening, you push it through, often so that you can see something more easily. （使）（头）伸出；（使）探出

  • Julie tapped on my door and poked her head in. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    朱莉轻轻地敲了敲我的门，探进头来。

  • We hadn't been able to poke our heads out and see what was going on. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    我们无法伸出头去，看看发生了什么。

  • Raymond's head poked through the doorway. [VERB prep./adv.]

    雷蒙德的头从门口探了出来。
