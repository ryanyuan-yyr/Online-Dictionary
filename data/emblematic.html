emblematic
embləmætɪk ˌɛmbləˈmætɪk
1 ADJ [usually verb-link ADJ] 象征（性）的;标志（性）的 If something, such as an object in a picture, is emblematic of a particular quality or an idea, it symbolically represents the quality or idea. 象征（性）的;标志（性）的

  • Dogs are emblematic of faithfulness. [+ of]

    狗象征着忠诚。

  • In some works, flowers take on a powerful emblematic quality.

    在一些作品中，花具有强烈的象征意义。

SYN symbolic, significant, figurative, allegorical  
2 ADJ 代表（性）的;典型（性）的 If you say that something is emblematic of a state of affairs, you mean that it is characteristic of it and represents its most typical features. 代表（性）的;典型（性）的

  • The killing in Pensacola is emblematic of a lot of the violence that is happening around the world. [+ of]

    彭萨柯拉的杀人事件是世界各地发生的大量暴力事件的典型代表。
