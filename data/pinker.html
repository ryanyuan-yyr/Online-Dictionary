pink
pɪŋk pɪŋk
Word forms: comparative pinker, superlative pinkest, plural pinks
1 COLOUR 粉红色；淡红色 Pink is the colour between red and white. 粉红色；淡红色

  • ...pink lipstick.

    粉红色的口红

  • ...white flowers edged in pink.

    带粉边的白花

  • ...sweaters in a variety of pinks and blues.

    各种各样粉色和蓝色的毛衣

pinkish

  • Her nostrils were pinkish, as though she had a cold.

    她的鼻孔略带粉红，好像患了感冒似的。

pinkness

  • Meat which has been cooked thoroughly shows no traces of pinkness.

    彻底煮熟的肉不会带有血色。

2 COLOUR （因窘迫、愤怒或剧烈活动而）脸色发红 If you go pink, your face turns a slightly redder colour than usual because you are embarrassed or angry, or because you are doing something energetic. （因窘迫、愤怒或剧烈活动而）脸色发红

  • She went pink again as she remembered her mistake.

    想起自己的错误，她又脸红了。

3 ADJ 与同性恋者有关的 Pink is used to refer to things relating to or connected with homosexuals. 与同性恋者有关的

  • Businesses are now more aware of the importance of the 'pink pound'.

    现在商家更加意识到同性恋族群消费力、即所谓“粉红英镑”的重要性。

SYN rosy, rose, salmon, flushed  
4 N-COUNT [usually plural] 石竹 Pinks are small plants that people grow in their gardens. They have sweet-smelling pink, white, or red flowers. 石竹
SYN best, summit, height, peak  
5 PHRASE [verb-link PHRASE, PHRASE after verb] 非常健康；满面红光；容光焕发 If you are in the pink, you are fit, healthy, and happy. 非常健康；满面红光；容光焕发

  • A glass of red wine keeps you in the pink.

    一杯红酒有益健康。
