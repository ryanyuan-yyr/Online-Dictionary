flit
flɪt flɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense flits, present participle flitting, past tense , past participle flitted
1 VERB 辗转;穿梭 If you flit around or flit between one place and another, you go to lots of places without staying for very long in any of them. 辗转;穿梭

  • Laura flits about New York hailing taxis at every opportunity. [VERB prep./adv.]

    劳拉穿梭于纽约各处，一有机会就打车。

  • He spends his time flitting between Florence, Rome and Bologna. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他辗转于佛罗伦萨、罗马和博洛尼亚三地之间。

2 VERB (在不同情况间)转变，转换 If someone flits from one thing or situation to another, they move or turn their attention from one to the other very quickly. (在不同情况间)转变，转换

  • She flits from one dance partner to another. [VERB from noun to noun]

    她不断地换舞伴。

  • He's prone to flit between subjects with amazing ease. [VERB prep.]

    他往往会轻松自如地转换话题。

3 VERB 飞过;掠过;闪过 If something such as a bird or a bat flits about, it flies quickly from one place to another. 飞过;掠过;闪过

  • ...the parrot that flits from tree to tree. [VERB prep./adv.]

    在树木间飞来飞去的鹦鹉

SYN fly, dash, dart, skim  
4 VERB (表情或念头)闪现，闪过 If an expression flits across your face or an idea flits through your mind, it is there for a short time and then goes again. (表情或念头)闪现，闪过

  • He was unable to prevent a look of interest from flitting across his features. [V + across]

    他脸上忍不住掠过一丝感兴趣的神色。

  • Images and memories of the evening flitted through her mind. [VERB + through]

    关于那个晚上的影像和记忆在她脑海中一一闪现。
