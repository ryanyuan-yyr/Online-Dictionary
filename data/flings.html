fling
flɪŋ flɪŋ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense flings, present participle flinging, past tense , past participle flung
1 VERB 猛掷;用力抛 If you fling something somewhere, you throw it there using a lot of force. 猛掷;用力抛

  • The woman flung the cup at him. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    那个女人使劲把杯子朝他扔去。

  • He once seized my knitting, flinging it across the room. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    有一次他抓过我织的东西，把它扔到了屋子的另一头。

SYN throw, toss, hurl, chuck [informal]  
2 VERB 突然冲向;突然跳向 If you fling yourself somewhere, you move or jump there suddenly and with a lot of force. 突然冲向;突然跳向

  • He flung himself to the floor. [VERB pronoun-reflexive prep./adv.]

    他突然跳到地板上。

3 VERB 急伸，猛动(尤指头或手臂) If you fling a part of your body in a particular direction, especially your arms or head, you move it there suddenly. 急伸，猛动(尤指头或手臂)

  • She flung her arms around my neck and kissed me. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    她突然张开手臂搂住我的脖子，然后吻了我一下。

4 VERB 把…摔倒 If you fling someone to the ground, you push them very roughly so that they fall over. 把…摔倒

  • The youth got him by the front of his shirt and flung him to the ground. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    年轻人抓住他的衬衫前襟，把他摔在了地上。

5 VERB 扔;摔;丢 If you fling something into a particular place or position, you put it there in a quick or angry way. 扔;摔;丢

  • Peter flung his shoes into the corner. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    彼得把他的鞋子甩到角落里。

  • He flung it down on the desk. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他把东西摔在桌子上。

6 VERB 投身于;致力于 If you fling yourself into a particular activity, you do it with a lot of enthusiasm and energy. 投身于;致力于

  • She flung herself into her career. [V pron-refl + into]

    她一心扑在事业上。

  • I flung myself into poetry. [V pron-refl into n]

    我全身心投入诗歌创作。

7 N-COUNT 风流韵事 If two people have a fling, they have a brief sexual relationship. 风流韵事 [informal]

  • She claims she had a brief fling with him 30 years ago. [+ with]

    她声称在 30 年前曾和他有过一段短暂的风流韵事。

SYN affair, relationship, involvement, liaison  
8 N-SING 短暂的放纵;一时的尽情享乐 A fling is a short period of enjoyment, especially the last one that you will get an opportunity to have. 短暂的放纵;一时的尽情享乐

  • ...that last fling before you finally give up and take up a job.

    你最终收心并开始工作之前的最后一次放纵

SYN binge [informal] , good time, bash, bit of fun  
9 (在许多短语中可代替 throw） Fling can be used instead of 'throw' in many expressions that usually contain 'throw'. (在许多短语中可代替 throw）
