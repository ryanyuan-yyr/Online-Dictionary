call for
1 PHRASAL VERB 接(人) If you call for someone, you go to the building where they are, so that you can both go somewhere. 接(人)

  • I shall be calling for you at seven o'clock. [VERB PREP. noun]

    7点钟我来接你。

SYN fetch, pick up, collect, uplift [Scottish]  
2 PHRASAL VERB 要求;呼吁 If you call for something, you demand that it should happen. 要求;呼吁

  • They angrily called for Robinson's resignation. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他们愤怒地要求鲁宾逊辞职。

  • The ceasefire resolution calls for the release of all prisoners of war. [VERB PREP. noun]

    停火决议要求释放所有战俘。

SYN demand, order, request, insist on  
3 PHRASAL VERB 需要;要求 If something calls for a particular action or quality, it needs it or makes it necessary. 需要;要求

  • It's a situation that calls for a blend of delicacy and force. [VERB PREP. noun]

    应付这种局面需要刚柔并济。

SYN require, need, involve, demand
