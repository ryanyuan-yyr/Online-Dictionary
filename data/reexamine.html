re-examine ˌriːɪɡˈzæmɪn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense re-examines, present participle re-examining, past tense , past participle re-examined
in AM, also use 美国英语亦用 reexamine
VERB 重新审视（观点、信仰或态度） If a person or group of people re-examines their ideas, beliefs, or attitudes, they think about them carefully because they are no longer sure if they are correct. 重新审视（观点、信仰或态度）

  • We may reexamine our goals or our methods for attaining our goals. [VERB noun]

    我们可能会重新审视我们的目标或我们实现目标的方法。

  • Her husband and children will also have to re-examine their expectations. [VERB noun]

    她的丈夫和孩子也都要重新审视他们的期望。

SYN reconsider, review, take a fresh look at, re-analyse  
re-examination

  • It was time for a re-examina-tion of the situation.

    该重新审视一下局势了。

  • Energy policy is badly in need of radical re-examination.

    能源政策亟需进行彻底的重新审视。
