snare
sneəʳ snɛə snɛr
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense snares, present participle snaring, past tense , past participle snared
1 N-COUNT (捕鸟或小野兽的)陷阱，罗网 A snare is a trap for catching birds or small animals. It consists of a loop of wire or rope which pulls tight around the animal. (捕鸟或小野兽的)陷阱，罗网
2 N-COUNT 陷阱;圈套 If you describe a situation as a snare, you mean that it is a trap from which it is difficult to escape. 陷阱;圈套 [formal]

  • Given data which are free from bias there are further snares to avoid in statistical work.

    即使数据本身不存在偏差在统计工作中仍需要避免其他陷阱。

  • Worldly success could prove a snare unless used for the good of others.

    若不用于为他人谋福祉，俗世的成功到头来不过是个枷锁而已。

3 VERB 用陷阱(或罗网)捕捉 If someone snares an animal, they catch it using a snare. 用陷阱(或罗网)捕捉

  • He'd snared a rabbit earlier in the day. [VERB noun]

    那天早一点的时候，他设套捉了一只兔子。

SYN trap, catch, net, wire  
4 VERB [usually passive] 诱…入圈套;诱惑 If someone is snared, they are caught in a trap. 诱…入圈套;诱惑

  • A motorcyclist was seriously injured when she was snared by a rope stretched across the road.

    一个骑摩托的女子被横扯在马路上的一根绳子给绊倒了，伤势严重。

5 VERB 谋得;诱获 If someone snares something, they get it in a clever way, perhaps by deceiving people. 谋得;诱获

  • Most of all I want to snare a husband. [VERB noun]

    最重要的是，我想钓得一个金龟婿。

  • Possessed of such qualities, how do you bring them to the fore and snare the job? [VERB noun]

    既然具备了这些优秀素质，你该怎样把它们充分展示出来并谋得这份工作呢？
