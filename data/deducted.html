deduct
dɪdʌkt diˈdʌkt dɪˈdʌkt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense deducts, present participle deducting, past tense , past participle deducted
VERB 扣除;减去 When you deduct an amount from a total, you subtract it from the total. 扣除;减去 [Also VERB n]

  • The company deducted this payment from his compensation. [VERB noun + from]

    公司从他获得的赔偿金中扣去了这笔付款。

  • Up to 5% of marks in the exams will be deducted for spelling mistakes. [be VERB-ed]

    试卷中最多将会有 5% 的分数因为拼写错误而被扣掉。

SYN subtract, remove, take off, withdraw
