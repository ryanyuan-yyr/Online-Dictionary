tuck into
(also tuck in)
PHRASAL VERB 尽情地吃;大口地吃 If someone tucks into a meal or tucks in, they start eating enthusiastically or hungrily. 尽情地吃;大口地吃 [British, informal]

  • She tucked into a breakfast of bacon and eggs. [VERB PREP. noun]

    她大口地吃着咸肉和鸡蛋作成的早餐。

  • Tuck in, it's the last hot food you'll get for a while. [VERB PREP.]

    尽情地吃吧，一段时间里你都吃不到这么一顿热饭了。

SYN devour, dig into [informal] , get stuck into [informal] , shift [British , informal]
