fussy
fʌsi ˈfʌsi
Word forms: comparative fussier, superlative fussiest
1 ADJ-GRADED 过分注重细节的;爱挑剔的;难以取悦的 Someone who is fussy is very concerned with unimportant details and is difficult to please. 过分注重细节的;爱挑剔的;难以取悦的 [disapproval]

  • She is not fussy about her food. [+ about]

    她不挑食。

  • Her aunt was small, with a rather fussy manner.

    她的姨妈个子矮小，为人相当挑剔。

SYN particular, difficult, exacting, discriminating  
fussily

  • She adjusted her coloured head scarf fussily.

    她不厌其烦地调弄着自己的彩色头巾。

  • He was once described as a fussily accurate test pilot.

    他一度被称为过分讲究精准的试飞员。

fussiness

  • There's a lack of fussiness about the way he works.

    他工作时不会过分注重细节。

2 ADJ-GRADED 过分装饰的;过于繁琐的 If you describe things such as clothes and furniture as fussy, you are criticizing them because they are too elaborate or detailed. 过分装饰的;过于繁琐的 [disapproval]

  • We are not very keen on floral patterns and fussy designs.

    我们不是很喜欢花卉图案和过于繁琐的设计。

SYN overelaborate, busy, cluttered, rococo  
fussily

  • ...the fussily ornate Jubilee Room at the House of Commons.

    装饰过分华丽的下议院庆典大厅
