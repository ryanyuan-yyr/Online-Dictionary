unbecoming
ʌnbɪkʌmɪŋ ˌʌnbɪˈkʌmɪŋ ˌʌnbiˈkʌmɪŋ
1 ADJ-GRADED 不好看的；不吸引人的 If you describe things such as clothes as unbecoming, you mean that they look unattractive. 不好看的；不吸引人的 [old-fashioned]

  • ...the unbecoming dress hurriedly stitched from cheap cloth.

    用廉价布料匆匆缝制的难看的连衣裙

SYN unattractive, unflattering, unsightly, unsuitable  
2 ADJ-GRADED 不得体的；不合宜的；不恰当的 If you describe a person's behaviour or remarks as unbecoming, you mean that they are shocking and unsuitable for that person. 不得体的；不合宜的；不恰当的 [formal]

  • His conduct was totally unbecoming to an officer in the British armed services. [+ to]

    作为一名英军军官，他的行为极不得体。

  • Those involved had performed acts unbecoming of university students. [+ of]

    那些参与者的所作所为有失大学生的体面。

SYN unseemly, inappropriate, unfit, offensive
