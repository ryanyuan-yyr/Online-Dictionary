bed
bed bɛd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , plural beds, present participle bedding, past tense , past participle bedded
1 N-COUNT 床；床铺 A bed is a piece of furniture that you lie on when you sleep. 床；床铺

  • She went into her bedroom and lay down on the bed.

    她走进卧室，躺在床上。

  • We finally went to bed at about 4am.

    我们最后大概是凌晨4点上的床。

  • By the time we got back from dinner, Nona was already in bed.

    我们吃完饭回来时，诺娜已经睡下了。

  • When she had gone Sam and Robina put the children to bed.

    她走了以后，萨姆和罗宾娜就安顿孩子们上床睡觉了。

SYN bedstead, couch, berth, cot  
2 N-COUNT （旅馆、医院等的）床位 If a place such as a hospital or a hotel has a particular number of beds, it is able to hold that number of patients or guests. （旅馆、医院等的）床位
3 N-COUNT [usually n NOUN] 畦；苗床；花圃；花坛 A bed in a garden or park is an area of ground that has been specially prepared so that plants can be grown in it. 畦；苗床；花圃；花坛

  • The geraniums in the flower bed looked bedraggled from the heavy rain.

    花坛里的天竺葵被大雨浇得七零八落。

  • ...beds of strawberries and rhubarb.

    种有草莓和大黄的苗圃

SYN plot, area, row, strip  
4 N-COUNT （海洋或湖泊中某种贝类和植物密集分布的）场，层，带 A bed of shellfish or plants is an area in the sea or in a lake where a particular type of shellfish or plant is found in large quantities. （海洋或湖泊中某种贝类和植物密集分布的）场，层，带

  • Fishermen fear valuable oyster and mussel beds could be decimated.

    渔民们害怕宝贵的牡蛎和贻贝层会被破坏。

  • The whole lake was rimmed with thick beds of reeds.

    整片湖都被密密层层的芦苇围了起来。

5 N-COUNT [usually singular] 海床；河床 The sea bed or a river bed is the ground at the bottom of the sea or of a river. 海床；河床

  • For three weeks a big operation went on to recover the wreckage from the sea bed.

    打捞海底残骸这一浩大工程持续了三周。

  • ...the bare bed of a dry stream.

    干涸小河裸露的河床

SYN bottom, ground, floor  
6 N-COUNT （岩）层 A bed of rock is a layer of rock that is found within a larger area of rock. （岩）层

  • Between the white limestone and the greyish pink limestone is a thin bed of clay.

    在白色石灰石和粉灰色石灰石之间是一层薄薄的黏土。

  • ...a sandstone bed.

    砂岩层

SYN base, footing, basis, bottom  
7 N-COUNT [usually singular] 底部食物；底层食物 If a recipe or a menu says that something is served on a bed of a food such as rice or vegetables, it means it is served on a layer of that food. 底部食物；底层食物

  • Heat the curry thoroughly and serve it on a bed of rice. [+ of]

    把咖喱热透后浇在米饭上。

SYN layer, mass, pile, heap

9 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 把…搞上床；诱使…发生性关系 To get someone into bed means to persuade them to have sex with you. 把…搞上床；诱使…发生性关系
10 PHR-RECIP 和…上床；和…发生性关系 To go to bed with someone means to have sex with them. 和…上床；和…发生性关系
SYN retire, turn in [informal] , go to sleep, hit the sack [slang]  
11 PHRASE [verb-link PHRASE, PHRASE after verb] [verb-link PHRASE, PHRASE after verb] 正在和…性交 If you say that someone is in bed with someone else, you mean that they are having sex in bed. 正在和…性交
12 PHRASE 在做爱方面；就床上功夫而言 If you say that someone is good in bed, you mean that they are a skilful lover. 在做爱方面；就床上功夫而言
13 PHRASE 自作自受；自食其果 If you say that someone has made their bed and must lie in it, you mean that since they have chosen to do a particular thing, they must now accept the unpleasant results of their action. 自作自受；自食其果
14 PHRASE 铺床 When you make the bed, you neatly arrange the sheets and covers of a bed so that it is ready to sleep in. 铺床
15 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 自打起床心情就不好 If you say that someone got out of bed on the wrong side, you mean that they have been bad-tempered ever since they woke up that morning. 自打起床心情就不好
