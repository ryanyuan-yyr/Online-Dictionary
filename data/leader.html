leader
liːdəʳ ˈlidər
Word forms: plural leaders
1 N-COUNT [n NOUN] 领袖；领导者 The leader of a group of people or an organization is the person who is in control of it or in charge of it. 领袖；领导者

  • We now need a new leader of the party and a new style of leadership. [+ of]

    我们现在需要一位新的政党领袖和一种新的领导作风。

  • We are going to hold a rally next month to elect a new leader.

    我们打算下月举行集会，选举一位新的领导人。

2 N-COUNT （比赛或竞赛中的）领先者，占先者 The leader at a particular point in a race or competition is the person who is winning at that point. （比赛或竞赛中的）领先者，占先者

  • The leaders came in two minutes clear of the field.

    优胜者较其他参赛者快两分钟。

  • The world drivers' championship leader crossed the line ahead of the Swede.

    在世界车手锦标赛上，领先选手比瑞典人快一步冲线。

3 N-COUNT （产品或公司中的）首屈一指者，佼佼者 The leader among a range of products or companies is the one that is most successful. （产品或公司中的）首屈一指者，佼佼者

  • Procter & Gamble is the leader in the mass market cosmetics industry. [+ in]

    宝洁是大众化妆品行业中的领头羊。

SYN front runner, pioneer, leading light, leading company  
4 N-COUNT （交响乐团的）首席小提琴手 The leader of an orchestra is the most senior violin player, who acts as a deputy to the conductor. （交响乐团的）首席小提琴手 [British]
in AM, use 美国英语用 concertmaster
5 N-COUNT （交响乐团的）指挥 The leader of an orchestra is the conductor. （交响乐团的）指挥 [US]
6 N-COUNT （报纸上的）社评，社论 A leader in a newspaper is a piece of writing which gives the editor's opinion on an important news item. （报纸上的）社评，社论 [British]
in AM, use 美国英语用 editorial
7 N-COUNT （报纸的）要闻，头条 A leader in a newspaper is the most important story in it. （报纸的）要闻，头条 [US]
in BRIT, use 英国英语用 lead, lead story
