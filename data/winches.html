winch
wɪntʃ wɪntʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense winches, present participle winching, past tense , past participle winched
1 N-COUNT 绞车;卷扬机 A winch is a machine which is used to lift heavy objects or people who need to be rescued. It consists of a drum around which a rope or chain is wound. 绞车;卷扬机
2 VERB 用绞车吊起 If you winch an object or person somewhere, you lift or lower them using a winch. 用绞车吊起

  • He would attach a cable around the chassis of the car and winch it up on to the canal bank. [V n with adv/prep]

    他会把钢丝绳拴在汽车底盘上，用卷扬机把汽车吊到运河岸上。

  • The crew members were winched to safety by helicopters. [V n with adv/prep]

    船员们被直升机吊到安全的地方。
