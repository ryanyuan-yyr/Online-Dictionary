repudiate
rɪpjuːdieɪt rɪˈpjudiˌeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense repudiates, present participle repudiating, past tense , past participle repudiated
VERB 批判;驳斥;与…断绝关系 If you repudiate something or someone, you show that you strongly disagree with them and do not want to be connected with them in any way. 批判;驳斥;与…断绝关系 [formal, or written]

  • Leaders urged people to turn out in large numbers to repudiate the violence. [VERB noun]

    领导们力劝人们集体出面反对暴力。

  • The Prime Minister has repudiated racist remarks made by a member of the Conservative Party. [VERB noun]

    首相已经驳斥了一个保守党成员的种族主义言论。
