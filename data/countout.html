count out
1 PHRASAL VERB 数(钱) If you count out a sum of money, you count the notes or coins as you put them in a pile one by one. 数(钱) [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Mr. Rohmbauer counted out the money and put it in an envelope. [VERB PREP. noun]

    罗姆保先生把钱数好放在了一个信封里。

2 PHRASAL VERB [usually imper] 不把…算入;不包括 If you tell someone to count you out, you mean that you do not want to be included in an activity. 不把…算入;不包括

  • If this is the standard to which I have to drop to gain membership, then count me out! [VERB noun PREP.]

    如果要加入就得降低到这一标准的话，那我就不参加了。
