window
wɪndoʊ ˈwɪndoʊ
Word forms: plural windows
1 N-COUNT 窗;窗户 A window is a space in the wall of a building or in the side of a vehicle, which has glass in it so that light can come in and you can see out. 窗;窗户

  • He stood at the window, moodily staring out.

    他站在窗前，忧郁地凝视着外面。

  • The room felt very hot and she wondered why someone did not open a window.

    房间里感觉很热，她纳闷怎么没人开窗子。

  • ...my car window.

    我的车窗

2 N-COUNT (商店的)玻璃橱窗，陈列窗 A window is a large piece of glass along the front of a shop, behind which some of the goods that the shop sells are displayed. (商店的)玻璃橱窗，陈列窗

  • I stood for a few moments in front of the nearest shop window.

    我在最近的一家商店的橱窗前站了一会儿。

3 N-COUNT (银行、邮局、火车站、博物馆等处柜台的)窗口 A window is a glass-covered opening above a counter, for example in a bank, post office, railway station, or museum, which the person serving you sits behind. (银行、邮局、火车站、博物馆等处柜台的)窗口

  • The woman at the ticket window told me that the admission fee was $17.50.

    售票窗口那个女的告诉我入场费是17.50美元。

4 N-COUNT 窗口;视窗 On a computer screen, a window is one of the work areas that the screen can be divided into. 窗口;视窗 [computing]
5 N-COUNT [usually singular] (日程安排等中的)空当，空隙 If you have a window in your diary for something, or if you can make a window for it, you are free at a particular time and can do it then. (日程安排等中的)空当，空隙

  • Tell her I've got a window in my diary later on this week. [+ in]

    告诉她我这周晚些时候有空儿。

SYN space, opening, gap, blank  
6 See also: French window  picture window  rose window 
7 PHRASE 消失殆尽;烟消云散;化为乌有 If you say that something such as a plan or a particular way of thinking or behaving has gone out of the window or has flown out of the window, you mean that it has disappeared completely. 消失殆尽;烟消云散;化为乌有

  • By now all logic had gone out of the window.

    到这时根本就不讲什么逻辑了。

  • When he went, our happiness and our security flew out of the window.

    他走后，我们幸福安稳的好日子便到头了。

8 PHRASE 转瞬即逝的机会;稍纵即逝的机会 If you say that there is a window of opportunity for something, you mean that there is an opportunity to do something but that this opportunity will only last for a short time and so it needs to be taken advantage of quickly. 转瞬即逝的机会;稍纵即逝的机会 [journalism]

  • The king said there was now a window of opportunity for peace. [+ for]

    国王说现在有寻求和平的短暂机会。
