banish
bænɪʃ ˈbænɪʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense banishes, present participle banishing, past tense , past participle banished
1 VERB 放逐；驱逐；流放 If someone or something is banished from a place or area of activity, they are sent away from it and prevented from entering it. 放逐；驱逐；流放

  • John was banished from England. [be V-ed from/to n]

    约翰被逐出了英格兰。

  • I was banished to the small bedroom upstairs. [be V-ed + from/to]

    我被赶到了楼上的小卧室。

  • They tried to banish him from politics. [V n + from/to]

    他们试图把他逐出政界。

SYN exclude, bar, ban, dismiss  
2 VERB 消除；排除；摆脱 If you banish something unpleasant, you get rid of it. 消除；排除；摆脱

  • ...a public investment programme intended to banish the recession. [VERB noun]

    旨在摆脱经济衰退的公共投资项目

  • ...diseases like malaria that have been banished for centuries. [VERB noun]

    已经被根除数世纪之久的疟疾之类的疾病

SYN get rid of, remove, eliminate, eradicate  
3 VERB 不再去想；打消…的念头 If you banish the thought of something, you stop thinking about it. 不再去想；打消…的念头

  • He has now banished all thoughts of retirement. [VERB noun]

    他现在已经完全打消了退休的念头。

  • The past few days had been banished from his mind. [be V-ed + from/to]

    他现在已经不再去想过去的这几天了。

SYN dismiss, drop, ban, reject
