understate
ʌndəʳsteɪt ˌʌndəˈsteɪt ˌʌndərˈsteɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense understates, present participle understating, past tense , past participle understated
VERB 不充分如实地陈述；对…轻描淡写；打折扣地说 If you understate something, you describe it in a way that suggests that it is less important or serious than it really is. 不充分如实地陈述；对…轻描淡写；打折扣地说

  • The government chooses deliberately to understate the increase in prices. [VERB noun]

    政府故意对物价上涨轻描淡写。

  • That understates my commitment to the orchestra. [VERB noun]

    那没有充分表达我对管弦乐队的热爱。

SYN play down, diminish, minimize, downgrade
