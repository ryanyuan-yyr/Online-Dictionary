superstitious
suːpəʳstɪʃəs ˌsupərˈstɪʃəs
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 迷信的 People who are superstitious believe in things that are not real or possible, for example magic. 迷信的

  • Jean was extremely superstitious and believed the colour green brought bad luck.

    琼非常迷信，认为绿色会带来厄运。

SYN prone to superstition, naive, gullible  
2 ADJ-GRADED [ADJ n] (恐惧、信念)非理性的，盲目的 Superstitious fears or beliefs are irrational and not based on fact. (恐惧、信念)非理性的，盲目的

  • A wave of superstitious fear spread among the townspeople.

    盲目的恐惧感在市民中蔓延。

  • In the countryside as a young doctor she encountered ancient superstitious beliefs and prejudices.

    作为一名年轻医生，她在乡下见识了古老的迷信思想和偏见。

SYN irrational, unfounded, groundless, unprovable
