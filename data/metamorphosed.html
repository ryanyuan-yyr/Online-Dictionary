metamorphose
metəmɔːʳfoʊz ˌmɛtəmɔrˌfoʊz ˌmɛtəmɔrˌfoʊs
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense metamorphoses, present participle metamorphosing, past tense , past participle metamorphosed
1 V-ERG (使)完全变形;(使)彻底改变 To metamorphose or be metamorphosed means to develop and change into something completely different. (使)完全变形;(使)彻底改变 [formal] [Also VERB n]

  • ...hysterical laughter which gradually metamorphoses into convulsive sobs. [V + into/from]

    歇斯底里的笑声渐渐地变成了剧烈的抽泣

  • The group is having to metamorphose from a loose collection of businesses into a fully integrated multinational. [V into/from n]

    该集团将不得不由一个松散的企业联合组织转变成一个全面整合的跨国集团。

  • I was going to metamorphose the movies into an art. [V n into/from n]

    我准备把电影业转变为一门艺术。

  • The actors were metamorphosed into a living tapestry of color at the dress rehearsal. [V n into/from n]

    带妆彩排的时候，演员们变幻出一幅活生生的锦绣画卷。

  • The tadpoles metamorphose and emerge onto land. [VERB]

    蝌蚪蜕变了，登上陆地。

  • She had been metamorphosed by the war. [be VERB-ed]

    她被这场战争彻底改变了。

SYN transform, change, alter, remake
