streamline
striːmlaɪn ˈstrimˌlaɪn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense streamlines, present participle streamlining, past tense , past participle streamlined
VERB 使(机构或过程)效率更高;精简 To streamline an organization or process means to make it more efficient by removing unnecessary parts of it. 使(机构或过程)效率更高;精简

  • They're making efforts to streamline their normally cumbersome bureaucracy. [VERB noun]

    他们正努力精简本来繁冗复杂的官僚体制。

  • They say things should be better now that they have streamlined application procedures. [VERB noun]

    他们称鉴于他们已经简化了申请手续，情况应该会有所好转。

SYN rationalize, restructure, reorganize, modernize
