illuminate
ɪluːmɪneɪt ɪˈluməˌneɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense illuminates, present participle illuminating, past tense , past participle illuminated
1 VERB 照亮;照明;照射 To illuminate something means to shine light on it and to make it brighter and more visible. 照亮;照明;照射 [formal]

  • No streetlights illuminated the street. [VERB noun]

    这条街上没有照明的路灯。

  • The black sky was illuminated by forked lightning. [VERB noun]

    叉状的闪电照亮了黑沉沉的天空。

SYN light up, light, brighten, irradiate  
2 VERB 阐释;说明 If you illuminate something that is unclear or difficult to understand, you make it clearer by explaining it carefully or giving information about it. 阐释;说明 [formal]

  • They use games and drawings to illuminate their subject. [VERB noun]

    他们用游戏和图画来阐明他们的主题。

SYN explain, interpret, make clear, clarify  
illuminating

  • His autobiography provides an illuminating insight into his mind.

    他的自传可以让读者深入洞察他的精神世界。
