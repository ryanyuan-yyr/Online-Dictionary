run away with
1 PHRASAL VERB (想象、情绪等)失去控制，按捺不住 If you let your imagination or your emotions run away with you, you fail to control them and cannot think sensibly. (想象、情绪等)失去控制，按捺不住

  • You're letting your imagination run away with you. [VERB PREP. PREP. noun]

    你这是在想入非非。

  • Radford sometimes allows his enthusiasm to run away with him. [V P P pron]

    雷德福有时会任由自己热情澎湃。

2 PHRASAL VERB 轻松取胜;轻易赢得 If someone runs away with a competition, race, or prize, they win it easily. 轻松取胜;轻易赢得

  • Theresa Zabell ran away with the women's gold medal. [VERB PREP. PREP. noun]

    特雷莎·扎贝尔轻松获得了女子金牌。

3 PHRASAL VERB 轻率接受(某种错误想法) If you run away with a particular idea, you accept it without thinking about it carefully, even though it is wrong. 轻率接受(某种错误想法)

  • It's very easy for us to run away with the idea that we can control everything. [VERB PREP. PREP. noun]

    我们轻易就会误以为自己可以控制一切。
