opponent
əpoʊnənt əˈpoʊnənt
Word forms: plural opponents
1 N-COUNT [usually with poss] （政治上的）对手，敌手 A politician's opponents are other politicians who belong to a different party or who have different aims or policies. （政治上的）对手，敌手

  • ...Mr Kennedy's opponent in the leadership contest. [+ in]

    与肯尼迪先生争夺领导权的对手

  • He described the detention without trial of political opponents as a cowardly act.

    他称未经审判就将政敌关押起来是懦夫之举。

SYN adversary, rival, enemy, the opposition  
2 N-COUNT [usually poss NOUN] （体育竞赛中的）对手，竞争者 In a sporting contest, your opponent is the person who is playing against you. （体育竞赛中的）对手，竞争者

  • Norris twice knocked down his opponent in the early rounds of the fight.

    诺里斯在前几个回合中两次击倒对手。

  • He's the best opponent I've come across this season, a great player.

    他是我本赛季遇到的最出色的对手，一位了不起的运动员。

3 N-COUNT （观点、政策等的）反对者 The opponents of an idea or policy do not agree with it and do not want it to be carried out. （观点、政策等的）反对者

  • ...opponents of the spread of nuclear weapons. [+ of]

    反对核武器扩散的人士

  • He became an outspoken opponent of the old Soviet system.

    他成为旧的苏联体制直言不讳的抨击者。

SYN opposer, dissident, objector, dissentient
