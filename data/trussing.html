truss
trʌs trʌs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense trusses, present participle trussing, past tense , past participle trussed
1 VERB 扎紧;绑紧;捆紧 To truss someone means to tie them up very tightly so that they cannot move. 扎紧;绑紧;捆紧 [written] [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • She trussed him quickly with stolen bandage, and gagged his mouth. [VERB noun]

    她用偷来的绷带迅速把他绑紧，并塞住了他的嘴。

SYN tie, secure, bind, strap  
Truss up means the same as truss .truss up 同 truss

  • She was trussed up with yellow nylon rope.

    她被人用黄尼龙绳子捆得动弹不得。

2 VERB (烹调前)把（鸡等的）腿和翅膀扎紧 If you truss a bird such as a chicken, you prepare it for cooking by tying its legs and wings. (烹调前)把（鸡等的）腿和翅膀扎紧 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Put stuffing into the cavity and truss the bird. [VERB noun]

    把填料放到腔内，把鸟的腿和翅膀扎紧。

Truss up means the same as truss.truss up 同 truss

  • Some people like to truss up the turkey.

    有些人喜欢烹饪前把火鸡的腿和翅膀扎紧。

3 N-COUNT （治疗疝气用的）疝带 A truss is a special belt with a pad that a man wears when he has a hernia in order to prevent it from getting worse. （治疗疝气用的）疝带
