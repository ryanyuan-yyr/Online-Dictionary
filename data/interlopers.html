interloper
ɪntəʳloʊpəʳ ˈɪntərˌloʊpər
Word forms: plural interlopers
N-COUNT 闯入者;干涉他人事务者 If you describe someone as an interloper, you mean that they have come into a situation or a place where they are not wanted or do not belong. 闯入者;干涉他人事务者 [disapproval]

  • She had no wish to share her father with any outsider and regarded us as interlopers.

    她不愿与任何外人分享父亲的爱,并把我们视为闯入者。

SYN trespasser, intruder, gate-crasher [informal] , uninvited guest
