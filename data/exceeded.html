exceed
ɪksiːd ɪkˈsiːd ɛkˈsid ɪkˈsid
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense exceeds, present participle exceeding, past tense , past participle exceeded
1 VERB 超过，超出（某数量、数字等） If something exceeds a particular amount or number, it is greater or larger than that amount or number. 超过，超出（某数量、数字等） [formal]

  • Its research budget exceeds $700 million a year. [VERB noun]

    其研究预算每年超过7亿美元。

  • The demand for places at some schools exceeds the supply. [VERB noun]

    某些学校的入学申请人数超过了招生。

  • His performance exceeded all expectations. [VERB noun]

    他的表演超出了所有人的期待。

SYN surpass, better, pass, eclipse  
2 VERB 超出，超越（限制、规定等） If you exceed a limit or rule, you go beyond it, even though you are not supposed to or it is against the law. 超出，超越（限制、规定等） [formal]

  • He accepts he was exceeding the speed limit. [VERB noun]

    他承认自己超速了。

  • I would be exceeding my powers if I ordered the march to be halted. [VERB noun]

    如果我命令停止游行，我就越权了。
