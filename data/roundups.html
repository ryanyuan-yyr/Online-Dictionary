roundup
raʊndʌp ˈraʊndʌp ˈraʊndˌʌp
(also round-up)Word forms: plural roundups
1 N-COUNT [adjective NOUN] (尤指电视或广播的)新闻摘要，内容提要 In journalism, especially television or radio, a roundup of news is a summary of the main events that have happened. (尤指电视或广播的)新闻摘要，内容提要

  • First, we have this roundup of the day's news. [+ of]

    首先来看今天的新闻摘要。

  • We'll also have an election roundup from the streets of New York to the Via Veneto.

    我们还有从纽约街头到威尼托大街带来的有关选举的新闻摘要。

SYN summary, review, survey, outline  
2 N-COUNT (强行对人的)驱集，聚拢，聚集 When there is a roundup of people, they are arrested or captured by the police or army and brought to one place. (强行对人的)驱集，聚拢，聚集

  • There are reports that round ups of westerners are still taking place. [+ of]

    据报道，强行将西方人驱集到一起的事件仍在发生。

3 N-COUNT (强行对动物的)驱集，驱赶，聚集 A roundup is an occasion when cattle, horses, or other animals are collected together so that they can be counted or sold. (强行对动物的)驱集，驱赶，聚集 [US]

  • What is it that keeps a cowboy looking strong, young and ready for another roundup?

    是什么使牛仔总是看起来强壮又年轻，而且随时能够把牛赶到一起？

SYN muster, collection, rally, rallying
