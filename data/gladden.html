gladden
glædən ˈglædən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense gladdens, present participle gladdening, past tense , past participle gladdened
1 PHRASE 使心花怒放；使充满希望 If you say that something gladdens someone's heart, you mean that it makes them feel pleased and hopeful. 使心花怒放；使充满希望 [written]

  • ...a conclusion that should gladden the hearts of all animal-rights activists.

    会使所有动物权益保护者欢欣鼓舞的结论

2 VERB 使高兴；使愉快 If something gladdens you, it makes you feel happy and pleased. 使高兴；使愉快 [literary]

  • Charles's visit surprised him and gladdened him. [VERB noun]

    查尔斯的来访让他惊喜交集。

SYN please, delight, cheer, exhilarate
