jiggle
dʒɪgəl ˈdʒɪɡəl ˈdʒɪgəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense jiggles, present participle jiggling, past tense , past participle jiggled
1 VERB 使上下急动；使左右晃动；抖动；拨动 If you jiggle something, you move it quickly up and down or from side to side. 使上下急动；使左右晃动；抖动；拨动 [informal]

  • He jiggled the doorknob noisily. [VERB noun]

    他使劲地晃动门把手，响声很大。

SYN shake, jerk, agitate, joggle  
2 VERB 晃动；急颤；抖动 To jiggle around means to move quickly up and down or from side to side. 晃动；急颤；抖动 [informal]

  • He tapped his feet, hummed tunes and jiggled about. [VERB adv.]

    他脚下打着拍子，嘴里哼着调子，身子晃来晃去的。

  • A roll of fat jiggled on the underside of her arm. [VERB]

    她胳膊下侧有一层肥肉一颤一颤的。

SYN jerk, bounce, jog, fidget
