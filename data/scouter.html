skaʊt skaʊt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense scouts, present participle scouting, past tense , past participle scouted
1 N-COUNT 侦察员 A scout is someone who is sent to an area of countryside to find out the position of an enemy army. 侦察员

  • They sent two men out in front as scouts.

    他们派两人在前面侦察。

SYN vanguard, lookout, precursor, outrider  
2 N-COUNT 同 talent scout A scout is the same as a talent scout . 同 talent scout
3 VERB (在某地)搜索,寻找 If you scout somewhere for something, you go through that area searching for it. (在某地)搜索,寻找

  • I wouldn't have time to scout the area for junk. [VERB noun + for]

    我没空在这个地区找没用的东西。

  • A team of four was sent to scout for a nuclear test site. [VERB + for]

    一个4人小分队被派出寻找核试验地。

  • I have people scouting the hills already. [VERB noun]

    我已经让人搜过那些山了。

SYN reconnoitre, investigate, check out, case [slang]
