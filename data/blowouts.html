blowout bloʊaʊt bloʊˌaʊt
(also blow-out)Word forms: plural blowouts
1 N-COUNT 盛宴；大餐 A blowout is a large meal, often a celebration with family or friends, at which people may eat too much. 盛宴；大餐 [informal]

  • Once in a while we had a major blowout.

    我们偶尔会吃顿大餐。

SYN binge [informal] , party, feast, rave [British , slang]  
2 N-COUNT 轮胎爆裂;爆胎 If you have a blowout while you are driving a car, one of the tyres suddenly bursts. 轮胎爆裂;爆胎

  • A lorry travelling south had a blow-out and crashed.

    一辆南行的卡车爆胎并撞车了。

SYN puncture, burst, flat, flat tyre  
3 N-COUNT （数量、价格的）突然上涨，骤升 A blowout in an amount or a price is a sudden increase in it. （数量、价格的）突然上涨，骤升 [Australian, journalism]

  • ...a blowout in surgery costs. [+ in]

    外科手术费的蹿升

  • ...a blow-out in the balance of payments.

    国际收支差额的井喷
