niggle
nɪgəl ˈnɪgəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense niggles, present participle niggling, past tense , past participle niggled
1 VERB (长时间地)使不安，使烦恼 If something niggles you, it causes you to worry slightly over a long period of time. (长时间地)使不安，使烦恼 [mainly British] [Also VERB]

  • I realise now that the things which used to niggle and annoy me just don't really matter. [VERB noun]

    我现在意识到那些曾令我烦恼不快的事情其实无关紧要。

  • It's been niggling at my mind ever since I met Neville in Nice. [VERB + at]

    自从在尼斯见到内维尔后，我一直忐忑不安。

  • The puzzle niggled away in Arnold's mind. [V away]

    这个谜团在阿诺德脑海里挥之不去。

SYN bother, concern, worry, trouble  
Niggle is also a noun.

  • So why is there a little niggle at the back of my mind?

    那为什么我潜意识里会有一丝不安呢?

2 VERB 对…吹毛求疵;挑剔 If someone niggles you, they annoy you by continually criticizing you for what you think are small or unimportant things. 对…吹毛求疵;挑剔 [mainly British]

  • I don't react anymore when opponents try to niggle me. [VERB noun]

    对手找茬惹我时，我不会再理他们。

  • You tend to niggle at your partner, and get hurt when he doesn't hug you. [VERB + at]

    你往往会对伴侣吹毛求疵，如果他不拥抱你，就感到受了伤害。

SYN criticize, provoke, annoy, plague  
Niggle is also a noun.

  • The life we have built together is more important than any minor niggle either of us might have.

    咱们共同建立的生活要比你我各自可能会有的任何小小的不满重要。
