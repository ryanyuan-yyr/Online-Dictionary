resolve
rɪzɒlv rɪˈzɑlv rɪˈzɔlv
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense resolves, present participle resolving, past tense , past participle resolved
1 VERB 解决 To resolve a problem, argument, or difficulty means to find a solution to it. 解决 [formal]

  • We must find a way to resolve these problems before it's too late. [VERB noun]

    我们必须及时找到解决这些问题的方法。

  • They hoped the crisis could be resolved peacefully. [VERB noun]

    他们希望这场危机能够得到和平解决。

SYN work out, answer, solve, find the solution to  
2 VERB 决定;下定决心 If you resolve to do something, you make a firm decision to do it. 决定;下定决心 [formal]

  • She resolved to report the matter to the hospital's nursing manager. [VERB to-infinitive]

    她决定把这件事汇报给医院的护士长。

  • She resolved that, if Mimi forgot this promise, she would remind her. [VERB that]

    她决定，如果米米忘记了这个承诺，她就提醒她。

SYN decide, determine, undertake, make up your mind  
3 N-VAR [oft NOUN to-infinitive] 决心;决意 Resolve is determination to do what you have decided to do. 决心;决意 [formal]

  • This will strengthen the American public's resolve to go to war.

    这会坚定美国公众诉诸战争的决心。

4 V-ERG （使）分解为 If you resolve something into a clearer form, or if it resolves into a clearer form, its shape or the different parts it contains become clear. （使）分解为 [formal]

  • ...like a musician resolving a confused mass of sound into melodic or harmonic order. [VERB noun into noun]

    就像音乐家那样将混乱嘈杂的声音变成优美和谐的旋律

  • Each of the spirals of light resolved into points. [VERB into noun]

    每一束螺旋光都分解成了光点。

SYN change, convert, transform, alter
