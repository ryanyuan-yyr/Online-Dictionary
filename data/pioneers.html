pioneer
paɪənɪəʳ ˌpaɪəˈnɪr
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense pioneers, present participle pioneering, past tense , past participle pioneered
1 N-COUNT [NOUN n] 先驱；先锋；创始人；倡导者 Someone who is referred to as a pioneer in a particular area of activity is one of the first people to be involved in it and develop it. 先驱；先锋；创始人；倡导者

  • ...one of the leading pioneers of British photo journalism. [+ of/in]

    英国新闻摄影界的领军人物之一

  • What they lacked in speed those pioneer pilots made up for by flying only a few feet above the ground.

    那些飞行员先驱通过离地仅几英尺的低空飞行，弥补了飞行速度的不足。

2 VERB 开创；倡导 Someone who pioneers a new activity, invention, or process is one of the first people to do it. 开创；倡导 [Also VERB]

  • ...Professor Alec Jeffreys, who invented and pioneered DNA tests. [VERB noun]

    发明和倡导DNA检测的亚历克·杰弗里斯教授

  • The campaigns are part of American-style innovations being pioneered by the new universities. [VERB noun]

    这些运动是几所新建大学倡导的美式创新的一部分。

  • ...the folk-tale writing style pioneered by Gabriel Garcia Marquez. [VERB-ed]

    由加夫列尔·加西亚·马尔克斯开创的民间传说写作风格

SYN develop, create, launch, establish  
3 N-COUNT 拓荒者；开拓者；开发者 Pioneers are people who leave their own country or the place where they were living, and go and live in a place that has not been lived in before. 拓荒者；开拓者；开发者

  • ...abandoned settlements of early European pioneers.

    已废弃的早期欧洲拓荒者的定居点

SYN settler, explorer, colonist, colonizer
