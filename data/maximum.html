maximum
mæksɪməm ˈmæksəməm
1 ADJ [ADJ n] 最高的;最大的;最多的 You use maximum to describe an amount which is the largest that is possible, allowed, or required. 最高的;最大的;最多的

  • Under planning law the maximum height for a fence or hedge is 2 metres.

    规划法中规定，围栏或树篱最高为2米。

  • China headed the table with maximum points.

    中国队以最高分位居榜首。

SYN greatest, highest, supreme, paramount  
Maximum is also a noun.

  • The law provides for a maximum of two years in prison.

    法律规定最高可处以两年监禁。

  • Twelve hours is the minimum, sixty hours the maximum.

    12小时为下限，60小时为上限。

2 ADJ [ADJ n] (数量)极大的,极多的 You use maximum to indicate how great an amount is. (数量)极大的,极多的

  • ...the maximum amount of information.

    海量信息

  • It was achieved with minimum fuss and maximum efficiency.

    事情事半功倍地完成了。

  • ...a maximum security prison.

    密不透风的监狱

3 ADV 最多;顶多;充其量 If you say that something is a particular amount maximum, you mean that this is the greatest amount it should be or could possibly be, although a smaller amount is acceptable or very possible. 最多;顶多;充其量

  • We need an extra 6g a day maximum.

    我们一天最多还需要6克。

4 PHRASE 最大限度地;尽可能充分地;竭尽全力地 If you say that someone does something to the maximum, you are emphasizing that they do it to the greatest degree possible. 最大限度地;尽可能充分地;竭尽全力地 [emphasis]

  • You have to develop your capabilities to the maximum.

    你必须尽你所能。
