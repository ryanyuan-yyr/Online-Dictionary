splinter
splɪntəʳ ˈsplɪntər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense splinters, present participle splintering, past tense , past participle splintered
1 N-COUNT (木头、玻璃等硬物的)尖细碎片，破片，裂片 A splinter is a very thin, sharp piece of wood, glass, or other hard substance, which has broken off from a larger piece. (木头、玻璃等硬物的)尖细碎片，破片，裂片

  • ...splinters of glass. [+ of]

    玻璃碎片

  • ...a splinter in the finger.

    扎进手指的木刺

SYN sliver, fragment, chip, needle  
2 V-ERG (使)裂成碎片 If something splinters or is splintered, it breaks into thin, sharp pieces. (使)裂成碎片

  • The ruler cracked and splintered into pieces. [VERB prep./adv.]

    尺子裂开了，碎成了小片。

  • The stone rocketed into the glass, splintering it. [VERB noun]

    石块击中了玻璃，将它打成了碎片。

SYN shatter, split, fracture, shiver
