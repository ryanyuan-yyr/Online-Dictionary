divorce
dɪvɔːʳs dəˈvɔrs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense divorces, present participle divorcing, past tense , past participle divorced
1 离婚 A divorce is the formal ending of a marriage by law. 离婚

  • Numerous marriages now end in divorce.

    现在许多婚姻都以离婚收场。

  • Their divorce became final this weekend.

    他们离婚一事在这周末尘埃落定。

  • The divorce proceedings have accelerated.

    离婚的诉讼程序已经加快。

2 (与…)离婚 If a man and woman divorce or if one of them divorces the other, their marriage is legally ended. (与…)离婚

  • My parents divorced when I was very young. [VERB]

    我很小的时候父母就离婚了。

  • He and Lillian had got divorced. [n get V-ed]

    他和莉莲已离婚了。

  • I am absolutely furious that he divorced me to marry her. [VERB noun]

    他为了娶她而和我离婚，我愤怒至极。

  • Mr Gold is divorcing for the second time. [VERB]

    戈尔德先生正准备第二次离婚。

  • I got divorced when I was about 31. [get VERB-ed]

    我在 31 岁左右离了婚。

3 分离;脱离 A divorce of one thing from another, or a divorce between two things is a separation between them which is permanent or is likely to be permanent. 分离;脱离

  • ...this divorce of Christian culture from the roots of faith. [+ from]

    基督教文化和信仰根基的脱离

  • This process is, in no sense, a divorce between the Labour Party and the trade union movement.

    这个进程绝不是工党和工会运动相脱离。

4 使分离;使分开 If you say that one thing cannot be divorced from another, you mean that the two things cannot be considered as different and separate things. 使分离;使分开

  • Good management in the police cannot be divorced from accountability. [be VERB-ed + from]

    对警察队伍的良好管理和应负的责任是分不开的。

  • We have been able to divorce sex from reproduction. [VERB noun + from]

    我们已经能够将性和生育完全分开了。
