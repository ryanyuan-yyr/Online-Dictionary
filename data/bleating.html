bleat
bliːt blit
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense bleats, present participle bleating, past tense , past participle bleated
1 VERB （羊）咩咩叫 When a sheep or goat bleats, it makes the sound that sheep and goats typically make. （羊）咩咩叫

  • From the slope below, the wild goats bleated faintly. [VERB]

    山坡下面，野山羊咩咩地轻声叫着。

  • ...a small flock of bleating ewes and lambs. [VERB-ing]

    一小群咩咩叫的母羊和羊羔

SYN baa, call, cry, maa  
Bleat is also a noun.

  • ...the faint bleat of a distressed animal.

    一只虚弱的动物微弱的呻吟

2 VERB 微弱地尖声诉说；羊叫般地说 If someone bleats, they speak in a weak, high voice. 微弱地尖声诉说；羊叫般地说 [written]

  • 'I don't want it,' Eric bleated. [VERB with quote]

    “我不想要它，”埃里克微弱地尖声说。

Bleat is also a noun.

  • She wanted to scream, but all that would come out was this faint bleat.

    她想尖叫，但最终只是发出了这么微弱的呻吟。

3 VERB 抱怨；低声诉苦 If you say that someone bleats about something, you mean that they complain about it in a way which makes them sound weak and irritating. 抱怨；低声诉苦 [disapproval] [Also VERB that]

  • They are always bleating about 'unfair' foreign competition. [VERB + about]

    他们总是抱怨“不公平的”外国竞争。

  • Don't come bleating to me every time something goes wrong. [VERB prep./adv.]

    不要一有问题就跑来向我哭诉。

SYN complain, carp, fuss, moan [informal]  
Phrasal verbs:
bleat on about
