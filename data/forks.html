fork
fɔːʳk fɔrk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense forks, present participle forking, past tense , past participle forked
1 N-COUNT 叉;餐叉 A fork is a tool used for eating food which has a row of three or four long metal points at the end. 叉;餐叉

  • ...knives and forks.

    刀叉

2 VERB 用叉子叉 If you fork food into your mouth or onto a plate, you put it there using a fork. 用叉子叉

  • Ann forked some fish into her mouth. [V n + into/onto]

    安用叉子叉了点儿鱼肉放进嘴里。

  • He forked an egg onto a piece of bread and folded it into a sandwich. [V n + into/onto]

    他用叉子叉了个鸡蛋放在面包上，然后把面包片折起做成了三明治。

3 N-COUNT 叉，耙(园艺工具) A garden fork is a tool used for breaking up soil which has a row of three or four long metal points at the end. 叉，耙(园艺工具)
4 VERB 叉运，叉掘(肥料、干草等) If you fork something such as manure or hay, you move it from one place to another using a large garden fork. 叉运，叉掘(肥料、干草等)

  • They started me off in the gardens as a handyman. Digging, forking manure, that kind of thing. [VERB noun]

    他们让我先在园子里干些杂活，挖土、叉粪肥之类的。

  • Farmers cut the hay, fork it on to a cart and then store it in barns. [VERB noun prep.]

    农夫们割下干草，用叉子把草叉到车上，然后拉到谷仓贮存。

5 N-COUNT (道路、河流等的)分岔处，分流处;分岔;岔路 A fork in a road, path, or river is a point at which it divides into two parts and forms a 'Y' shape. (道路、河流等的)分岔处，分流处;分岔;岔路

  • We arrived at a fork in the road. [+ in]

    我们到达了一个岔路口。

  • The road divides; you should take the right fork.

    路在这里分了岔;你应该走右边一条。

  • ...the fork of the Delaware and Lehigh rivers.

    特拉华河和利哈伊河的分岔口

6 VERB [no cont] (道路、河流等)分岔 If a road, path, or river forks, it forms a fork. (道路、河流等)分岔

  • Beyond the village the road forked. [VERB]

    过了村子之后路分了岔。

  • The path dipped down to a sort of cove, and then it forked in two directions. [VERB prep./adv.]

    这条路向下延伸到了一个山坳，然后在那里分了岔。

SYN branch, part, separate, split  
7 VERB 走岔路 If you fork in a particular direction when you are travelling along a road or path, you choose one of the forks in it and travel down it. 走岔路

  • Just before the town boundary fork left onto a minor road. [VERB prep./adv.]

    快到城镇边界时要走岔路的左边一条小路。

8See also:tuning fork 
9 PHRASAL VERB 付钱;大把花钱 If you fork out for something, you spend a lot of money on it. 付钱;大把花钱

  • He will have to fork out for private school fees for Nina.

    他得为尼娜付一大笔私立学校的学费。

  • You don't ask people to fork out every time they drive up the motorways.

    不能要求人们每次开车上高速公路都要交费。

  • Britons fork out more than a billion pounds a year on toys.

    英国人每年花在玩具上的钱超过 10 亿英镑。
