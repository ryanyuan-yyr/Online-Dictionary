discriminate
dɪskrɪmɪneɪt dɪˈskrɪmɪˌneɪt dɪˈskrɪmɪnɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense discriminates, present participle discriminating, past tense , past participle discriminated
1 VERB 区分;辨别 If you can discriminate between two things, you can recognize that they are different. 区分;辨别

  • He is incapable of discriminating between a good idea and a terrible one. [V + between]

    他分不清主意的好坏。

  • The device can discriminate between the cancerous and the normal cells. [V between pl-n]

    这台仪器可以辨别癌细胞和正常的细胞。

SYN differentiate, distinguish, discern, separate  
2 VERB 区别对待;歧视;偏袒 To discriminate against a group of people or in favour of a group of people means to unfairly treat them worse or better than other groups. 区别对待;歧视;偏袒

  • They believe the law discriminates against women. [VERB + against]

    他们认为该项法律歧视女性。

  • ...legislation which would discriminate in favour of racial minorities. [V + in favour of]

    偏袒少数种族的立法

  • The Commission for Racial Equality teaches organisations not to discriminate. [VERB]

    种族平等委员会告诫各家机构不要心存歧视。
