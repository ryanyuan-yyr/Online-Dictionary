tart
tɑːʳt tɑrt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense tarts, present participle tarting, past tense , past participle tarted
1 N-VAR (尤指甜心的)果馅饼 A tart is a shallow pastry case with a filling of food, especially sweet food. (尤指甜心的)果馅饼

  • ...jam tarts.

    果酱馅饼

  • ...a slice of home-made tart.

    一块自制的果馅饼

SYN pie, pastry, pasty, tartlet  
2 ADJ-GRADED (水果等)味道浓烈的，味儿冲的 If something such as fruit is tart, it has a sharp taste. (水果等)味道浓烈的，味儿冲的

  • The blackberries were a bit too tart on their own, so we stewed them gently with some apples.

    光黑莓味道有点儿冲，所以我们将它和几个苹果一起用小火炖了炖。

  • ...a slightly tart wine.

    味道有点儿冲的葡萄酒

SYN sharp, acid, sour, bitter  
3 ADJ-GRADED (言语)尖刻的，尖酸的，刻薄的 A tart remark or way of speaking is sharp and unpleasant, often in a way that is rather cruel. (言语)尖刻的，尖酸的，刻薄的

  • The words were more tart than she had intended.

    话听起来太刻薄，她本没想这么刻薄的。

  • Lyle, I'm sure, would have had a very tart comment to make about this new social trend.

    我敢肯定，莱尔会对这种社会新潮流发表非常尖刻的评论。

SYN cutting, biting, sharp, short  
tartly

  • 'There are other patients on the ward, Lovell,' the staff nurse reminded her tartly.

    “洛弗尔，病房里还有别的病人呢，”护士毫不客气地提醒她说。

4 N-COUNT 淫妇;荡妇;妓女 If someone refers to a woman or girl as a tart, they are criticizing her because they think she is sexually immoral or dresses in a way that makes her look sexually immoral. 淫妇;荡妇;妓女 [informal, offensive, disapproval]

  • He was nasty about my appearance. If I dressed up he'd say I looked like a tart.

    他对我的外表要求很苛刻，我要是打扮打扮，他就会说我像荡妇。

Phrasal verbs:
tart up
