tolerable
tɒlərəbəl ˈtɑlərəbəl
1 ADJ-GRADED 可忍受的;可容忍的 If you describe something as tolerable, you mean that you can bear it, even though it is unpleasant or painful. 可忍受的;可容忍的

  • The levels of tolerable pain vary greatly from individual to individual…

    对疼痛的耐受程度因人而异，差别很大。

  • He described their living conditions as tolerable.

    他把他们的生活条件描述为“尚可忍受”。

SYN bearable, acceptable, allowable, supportable  
tolerably

  • Their captors treated them tolerably well.

    抓他们的人对他们还算可以。

  • ...tolerably hot water.

    热得还能忍受的水

2 ADJ-GRADED 过得去的;还可以的 If you describe something as tolerable, you mean that it is fairly good and reasonably satisfactory, but not of the highest quality or standard. 过得去的;还可以的 [formal]

  • He fell asleep just past midnight with tolerable ease.

    他刚过午夜就睡着了，睡得还算安稳。

  • Is there anywhere tolerable to eat in town?

    城里有什么过得去的地方可以吃饭吗？

SYN fair, O.K. or okay [informal] , middling, average  
tolerably

  • He can see tolerably well and he can read.

    他的视力还算可以，还能看书。
