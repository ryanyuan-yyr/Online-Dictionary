put on
1 PHRASAL VERB 穿；戴；涂；抹 When you put on clothing or make-up, you place it on your body in order to wear it. 穿；戴；涂；抹

  • She put on her coat and went out. [VERB PREP. noun]

    她穿上大衣出去了。

  • Maximo put on a pair of glasses. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    马克西莫戴上了一副眼镜。

  • I haven't even put any lipstick on. [VERB noun PREP.]

    我甚至还没抹口红。

SYN don, dress in, slip into, pull on  
2 PHRASAL VERB 上演；举办；展出 When people put on a show, exhibition, or service, they perform it or organize it. 上演；举办；展出

  • The band are hoping to put on a UK show before the end of the year.

    那个乐队希望年底之前在英国举办一场演出。

  • British Airways is putting on an extra flight to London tomorrow. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    英国航空公司明天将增加一班到伦敦的航班。

  • We put it on and everybody said 'Oh it's a brilliant production'. [VERB noun PREP.]

    我们把它搬上舞台，大家都说：“噢，真是一部了不起的作品。”

SYN present, stage, perform, do  
3 PHRASAL VERB 增加（体重） If someone puts on weight, they become heavier. 增加（体重） [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • I can eat what I want but I never put on weight. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我能想吃什么就吃什么，但是体重从来不增加。

  • Luther's put on three stone. [VERB PREP. noun]

    卢瑟体重增加了3英石。

4 PHRASAL VERB 开动（设备、装置等）；使运转 If you put on a piece of equipment or a device, you make it start working, for example by pressing a switch or turning a knob. 开动（设备、装置等）；使运转

  • I put the radio on. [VERB noun PREP.]

    我打开了收音机。

  • I put on the light by the bed. [VERB noun PREP.]

    我打开了床边的灯。

SYN switch on, turn on, activate, flick on  
5 PHRASAL VERB 播放（唱片、磁带或CD光盘） If you put a record, tape, or CD on, you place it in a record, tape, or CD player and listen to it. 播放（唱片、磁带或CD光盘）

  • She poured them drinks, and put a record on loud. [VERB noun PREP.]

    她为他们倒上饮料，然后开大音量播放唱片。

  • Let's go into the study and put on some music. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我们到书房听点儿音乐吧。

6 PHRASAL VERB 烹调；加热 If you put something on, you begin to cook or heat it. 烹调；加热

  • She immediately put the kettle on. [VERB noun PREP.]

    她立刻烧了水。

  • Put some rice on now. [VERB noun PREP.]

    现在煮些米饭吧。

  • Put on a pan of water to simmer and gently poach the eggs. [VERB PREP. noun]

    放一锅水，用文火煮荷包蛋。

7 PHRASAL VERB 对…下赌注；押赌注于… If you put a sum of money on something, you make a bet about it. For example, if you put £10 on a racehorse, you bet £10 that it will win. 对…下赌注；押赌注于… [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • They each put £20 on Matthew scoring the first goal. [V n P n/v-ing]

    他们每人押了20英镑赌马修进第一个球。

  • He'll be back in an hour. I'd put money on it. [V n P n/-ing]

    他一个小时后会回来。我敢打赌。

  • I'll put a bet on for you. [VERB noun PREP.]

    我打赌你会赢。

SYN bet, back, place, chance  
8 PHRASAL VERB 提高…的成本（或价值） To put a particular amount on the cost or value of something means to add that amount to it. 提高…的成本（或价值）

  • The proposal could put 3p on a loaf of bread. [VERB noun PREP. noun]

    这项提议会使每个面包的成本增加3便士。

9 PHRASAL VERB （行为举止）做作，矫饰，刻意表现 If you put on a way of behaving, you behave in a way that is not natural to you or that does not express your real feelings. （行为举止）做作，矫饰，刻意表现

  • Stop putting on an act and be yourself. [VERB PREP. noun]

    别端着，自然一点。

  • She had hoped the couple would put on a show of unity. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    她曾经希望那对夫妇会表现出和睦相处的样子。

  • It was hard to believe she was ill, she was putting it on. [VERB noun PREP.]

    很难相信她真病了，她是在装病。
