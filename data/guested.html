guest
gest gɛst
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , plural guests, present participle guesting, past tense , past participle guested
1 N-COUNT 客人；宾客 A guest is someone who is visiting you or is at an event because you have invited them. 客人；宾客

  • She was a guest at the wedding.

    她是参加婚礼的客人。

  • Their guests sipped drinks on the veranda.

    他们的客人在游廊里浅酌着美酒。

SYN visitor, company, caller, visitant  
2 N-COUNT 特邀嘉宾；客串演员 A guest is someone who visits a place or organization or appears on a radio or television show because they have been invited to do so. 特邀嘉宾；客串演员

  • ...a frequent chat show guest.

    经常出现在访谈节目中的特邀嘉宾

  • Dr Gerald Jeffers is the guest speaker.

    杰拉尔德·杰弗斯博士是特邀演讲人。

  • They met when she made a guest appearance in the hit TV show Minder.

    他们是在她客串出演热播电视系列剧《守护者》时认识的。

3 N-COUNT （旅馆的）客人，房客 A guest is someone who is staying in a hotel. →see usage note at: client （旅馆的）客人，房客

  • I was the only hotel guest.

    我是宾馆里唯一的住客。

  • Hotels operate a collection service for their guests from the airports.

    宾馆为客人提供机场接机服务。

4 VERB 做特邀嘉宾；做特邀演员；客串 To guest means to take part in a performance or a match as a guest, rather than as a regular member. 做特邀嘉宾；做特邀演员；客串 [mainly British]

  • He guested for one or two League sides. [VERB prep.]

    他在一两支联赛队伍中做过外援。

  • The band have recently guested on records by Ringo Starr and Tears For Fears. [VERB prep.]

    这个乐队最近在林戈·斯塔尔和“恐惧之泪”乐队的唱片中客串过。

5 CONVENTION 请便；随便 If you say be my guest to someone, you are giving them permission to do something. 请便；随便

  • If anybody wants to work on this, be my guest.

    如果哪位想干这个，请便。
