mitigate
mɪtɪgeɪt ˈmɪtəˌgeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense mitigates, present participle mitigating, past tense , past participle mitigated
VERB 减轻;缓解;缓和 To mitigate something means to make it less unpleasant, serious, or painful. 减轻;缓解;缓和 [formal]

  • ...ways of mitigating the effects of an explosion. [VERB noun]

    减轻爆炸威力的种种方法

  • The cost of getting there is mitigated by Sydney's offer of a subsidy. [VERB noun]

    由于悉尼提供补助金，所以到那里的花费就减少了。

SYN ease, moderate, soften, check
