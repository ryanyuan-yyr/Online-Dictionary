profit
prɒfɪt ˈprɑfɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense profits, present participle profiting, past tense , past participle profited
1 N-VAR 利润；收益；盈利 A profit is an amount of money that you gain when you are paid more for something than it cost you to make, get, or do it. 利润；收益；盈利

  • The bank made pre-tax profits of £3.5 million.

    这家银行的税前利润为350万英镑。

  • You can improve your chances of profit by sensible planning.

    你可以通过合理计划来提高盈利的机会。

  • The profit motive is inherently at odds with principles of fairness and equity.

    盈利动机天生就与公平和公正的原则相抵触。

SYN earnings, winnings, return, revenue  
2 VERB 盈利；获取利润；赚钱 If you profit from something, you earn a profit from it. 盈利；获取利润；赚钱

  • Footballers are accustomed to profiting handsomely from bonuses. [V + from/by]

    足球运动员对获取丰厚的奖金已习以为常。

  • He has profited by selling his holdings to other investors. [V from/by n/-ing]

    他将自己的股票卖给其他投资者，从中获利。

  • The dealers profited shamefully at the expense of my family. [VERB]

    这些经销商通过损害我家人的利益不光彩地赚钱。

SYN make money, clear up, gain, earn  
3 V-ERG 得益（于）；对…有益；对…有好处 If you profit from something, or it profits you, you gain some advantage or benefit from it. 得益（于）；对…有益；对…有好处 [formal]

  • Jennifer wasn't yet totally convinced that she'd profit from a more relaxed lifestyle. [V + from/by]

    珍尼弗还不完全相信更轻松的生活方式会对她有好处。

  • So far the French alliance had profited the rebels little. [VERB noun]

    到目前为止叛军都没有从法国同盟那里获得什么好处。

  • Whom would it profit to terrify or to kill James Sinclair? [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    恐吓或者干掉詹姆斯·辛克莱对谁会有好处？

SYN benefit, help, serve, aid  
Profit is also a noun.

  • The artist found much to his profit in the Louvre.

    这位艺术家从卢浮宫受益颇多。
