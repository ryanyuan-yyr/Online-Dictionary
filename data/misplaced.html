misplaced
mɪspleɪst ˌmɪsˈpleɪst
ADJ-GRADED (感情、行动)不适当的，不得体的，搞错对象的 If you describe a feeling or action as misplaced, you are critical of it because you think it is inappropriate, or directed towards the wrong thing or person. (感情、行动)不适当的，不得体的，搞错对象的 [disapproval]

  • Lenders rely on the misplaced loyalty of existing borrowers to make their profit.

    放贷人指望现有借贷人的盲目忠诚来谋求利益。

  • I think your concern is misplaced. Ackroyd is no threat to anyone.

    我认为你多虑了，阿克罗伊德不会对任何人构成威胁。
