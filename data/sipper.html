sip
sɪp sɪp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense sips, present participle sipping, past tense , past participle sipped
1 VERB 小口地喝;抿 If you sip a drink or sip at it, you drink by taking just a small amount at a time. 小口地喝;抿

  • Jessica sipped her drink thoughtfully. [VERB noun]

    杰茜卡小口地喝着饮料，若有所思。

  • He sipped at the glass and then put it down. [V + at/from]

    他抿了一口杯中的酒，然后放了下来。

  • She sipped from her coffee mug, watching him over the rim. [V at/from n]

    她端着咖啡杯小口地喝着,目光越过杯沿注视着他。

  • He lifted the water-bottle to his lips and sipped. [VERB]

    他把水瓶举到嘴边，小口地喝着。

SYN drink, taste, sample, sup  
2 N-COUNT (饮料)一小口;一啜的量 A sip is a small amount of drink that you take into your mouth. (饮料)一小口;一啜的量

  • Harry took a sip of bourbon. [+ of]

    哈里抿了一口波旁威士忌。

  • Katherine took another sip from her glass to calm herself.

    凯瑟琳又抿了一口杯中的酒，竭力使自己镇静下来。

SYN swallow, mouthful, swig, drop
