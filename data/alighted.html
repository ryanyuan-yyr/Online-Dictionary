alight
əlaɪt əˈlaɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense alights, present participle alighting, past tense , past participle alighted
1 ADJ [verb-link ADJ] 燃烧着的；点亮着的 If something is alight, it is burning. 燃烧着的；点亮着的

  • Several buildings were set alight.

    几幢大楼着了火。

  • The gas fire was still alight.

    燃气引起的大火还在熊熊燃烧。

2 ADJ [verb-link ADJ] （脸色、眼神因激动、高兴）熠熠放光的，闪闪发亮的 If someone's eyes are alight or if their face is alight, the expression in their eyes or on their face shows that they are feeling a strong emotion such as excitement or happiness. （脸色、眼神因激动、高兴）熠熠放光的，闪闪发亮的 [literary]

  • Her eyes were alight with a girlish enjoyment of life.

    她的眼睛闪闪发亮，闪烁着少女般的幸福和满足。

  • She paused and turned, her face alight with happiness. [+ with]

    她停住转过身，脸上洋溢着幸福。

3 VERB （鸟或昆虫）飞落，降落 If a bird or insect alights somewhere, it lands there. （鸟或昆虫）飞落，降落 [literary]

  • A thrush alighted on a branch of the pine tree. [VERB prep./adv.]

    一只鸫落在松树的树枝上。

SYN land, light, settle, come down  
4 VERB 下（火车、公共汽车等） When you alight from a train, bus, or other vehicle, you get out of it after a journey. 下（火车、公共汽车等） [formal] [Also VERB]

  • Two men alighted from the vehicle. [VERB prep./adv.]

    两个男人从车上下来。

SYN get off, descend, get down, disembark  
5 VERB 偶然发现;偶然想到;突然对…产生兴趣 If someone alights on something, they suddenly see it, think of it, or take an interest in it. 偶然发现;偶然想到;突然对…产生兴趣 [literary]

  • He would then suddenly alight on the tune he really wanted to play. [VERB on/upon noun]

    他常会在那时突然想起他真正想弹的曲子。
