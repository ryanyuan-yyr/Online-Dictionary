hog
hɒg hɔːg hɔg hɑg
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense hogs, present participle hogging, past tense , past participle hogged
1 N-COUNT 猪（英国英语中常指阉公猪，而美国英语中泛指猪） A hog is a pig. In British English, hog usually refers to a large male pig that has been castrated, but in American English it can refer to any kind of pig. 猪（英国英语中常指阉公猪，而美国英语中泛指猪）

  • We picked the corn by hand and we fed it to the hogs and the cows.

    我们用手掰下玉米，把它喂给猪和牛吃。

  • Johnny Warrick has a hog farm in Alabama.

    约翰尼·沃里克在亚拉巴马州有个养猪场。

SYN pig, swine, porker  
2 VERB （贪心地）攫取，独占 If you hog something, you take all of it in a greedy or impolite way. （贪心地）攫取，独占 [informal]

  • Have you done hogging the bathroom?. [VERB noun]

    你要把浴室霸占到什么时候？

  • Now, Bert, quit hogging the limelight. [VERB noun]

    好了，伯特，别出风头。

SYN monopolize, dominate, tie up, corner  
3 See also: road hog 
4 PHRASE 彻底地干;一不做二不休 If you go the whole hog, you do something bold or extravagant in the most complete way possible. 彻底地干;一不做二不休 [informal]

  • Well, I thought, I've already lost half my job, I might as well go the whole hog and lose it completely.

    嗯，我想，反正我的工作已经丢了一半了，干脆一不做二不休，走人算了。
