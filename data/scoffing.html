scoff
skɒf skɑf skɔf
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense scoffs, present participle scoffing, past tense , past participle scoffed
1 VERB 嘲笑;讥讽;嘲弄 If you scoff at something, you speak about it in a way that shows you think it is ridiculous or inadequate. 嘲笑;讥讽;嘲弄

  • At first I scoffed at the notion. [VERB + at]

    刚开始我对那种想法嗤之以鼻。

  • You may scoff but I honestly feel I'm being cruel only to be kind. [VERB]

    你可能不以为然，但我真的认为我狠下心来只是出于一片好意。

  • 'You'll have to do better than that,' Joanna scoffed. [VERB with quote]

    “你可得做得比那好，”乔安娜讥讽地说。

SYN scorn, mock, laugh at, ridicule  
2 VERB 贪婪地吃;狼吞虎咽 If you scoff food, you eat it quickly and greedily. 贪婪地吃;狼吞虎咽 [British, informal]

  • The pancakes were so good that I scoffed the lot. [VERB noun]

    那些薄饼太好吃了，我狼吞虎咽地都吃下去了。
