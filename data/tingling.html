tingle
tɪŋgəl ˈtɪŋɡəl ˈtɪŋgəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense tingles, present participle tingling, past tense , past participle tingled
1 VERB 感到刺痛 When a part of your body tingles, you have a slight stinging feeling there. 感到刺痛

  • The backs of his thighs tingled. [VERB]

    他大腿后侧感到刺痛。

  • …a taste which is first sweet, then bitter, leaving a tingling sensation on the tongue. [VERB-ing]

    先甜后苦，在舌头上留下一丝刺痛感觉的味道

SYN prickle, sting, itch, tickle  
tingling

  • Its effects on the nervous system include weakness, paralysis, and tingling in the hands and feet.

    它对神经系统的影响包括虚弱、麻痹与手脚刺痛。

2 VERB 强烈地感到;激动 If you tingle with a feeling such as excitement, you feel it very strongly. 强烈地感到;激动

  • She tingled with excitement. [VERB + with]

    她激动不已。

  • When I look over and see Terry I tingle all over. [VERB]

    当我放眼望去见到特里时，我整个人兴奋不已。

Tingle is also a noun.

  • I felt a sudden tingle of excitement.

    我突然感到一阵激动。
