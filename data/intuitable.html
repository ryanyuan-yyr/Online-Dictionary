intuit
ɪntjuːɪt ɪnˈtjuːɪt -tuː- ɪnˈtuɪt ɪnˈtjuɪt ˈɪntʊwɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense intuits, present participle intuiting, past tense , past participle intuited
VERB 凭直觉感知 If you intuit something, you guess what it is on the basis of your intuition or feelings, rather than on the basis of knowledge. 凭直觉感知 [formal]

  • They would confidently intuit your very thoughts. [VERB noun]

    他们会很有把握地凭直觉猜出你的真实想法。

  • He was probably right to intuit that it was universal. [VERB that]

    他凭直觉认为它具有普遍性，也许他是正确的。
