list
lɪst lɪst
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense lists, present participle listing, past tense , past participle listed
1 N-COUNT 一览表；名单；目录 A list of things such as names or addresses is a set of them which all belong to a particular category, written down one below the other. 一览表；名单；目录

  • We are making a list of the top ten men we would not want to be married to. [+ of]

    我们正在列一份我们最不愿意嫁的十大男人的名单。

  • There were six names on the list.

    名单上有六个名字。

  • ...fine wine from the hotel's exhaustive wine list.

    酒店应有尽有的酒单上的美酒

SYN inventory, record, listing, series  
2See also:Civil List  hit list  honours list  laundry list  mailing list  shopping list  waiting list 
3 N-COUNT （特定顺序的）一系列，一长串 A list of things is a set of them that you think of as being in a particular order. （特定顺序的）一系列，一长串

  • High on the list of public demands is to end military control of broadcasting. [+ of]

    高居公众呼声前列的是要求结束军方对广播电视的控制。

  • I would have thought if they were looking for redundancies I would be last on the list.

    我本以为，要是他们打算裁员，我会是最不可能被裁的人选。

  • 'First City' joined a long list of failed banks. [+ of]

    “第一城市银行”加入了一长串破产银行之列。

4 VERB 列举；历数 To list several things such as reasons or names means to write or say them one after another, usually in a particular order. 列举；历数

  • The pupils were asked to list the sports they loved most and hated most. [VERB noun]

    小学生们被要求列出他们最喜欢和最讨厌的运动。

  • Manufacturers must list ingredients in order of the amount used. [VERB noun]

    生产商必须按用量多少的顺序将成分列出。

SYN itemize, record, note, enter  
5 VERB （以特定方式）把…列入单子（或报告）；登录 To list something in a particular way means to include it in that way in a list or report. （以特定方式）把…列入单子（或报告）；登录

  • A medical examiner has listed the deaths as homicides. [VERB noun prep.]

    一名验尸官将这些死亡事件列为凶杀。

  • He was not listed under his real name on the residents panel. [V-ed + under]

    他未以真名在居民名册上登记。

6 VERB （使）上市；把（证券）列入上市证券表 If a company is listed, or if it lists, on a stock exchange, it obtains an official quotation for its shares so that people can buy and sell them. （使）上市；把（证券）列入上市证券表 [business] [Also VERB n]

  • It will list on the London Stock Exchange next week with a value of 130 million pounds. [VERB]

    它将以1.3亿英镑的市值于下星期在伦敦证券交易所上市。

7 VERB （尤指船）倾侧 In sailing, if something, especially a ship, lists, it leans over to one side. （尤指船）倾侧 [technical]

  • The ship listed again, and she was thrown back across the bunk. [VERB]

    船再次倾侧了，她又被甩回床铺的另一头。

SYN lean, tip, heel, incline  
List is also a noun.

  • The ship's list was so strong now that almost at once she stumbled.

    船现在倾侧得太厉害，她几乎立刻就摔倒了。
