lodge
lɒdʒ lɑdʒ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense lodges, present participle lodging, past tense , past participle lodged
1 N-COUNT （乡间或山里的）度假屋，小舍（尤用于狩猎或钓鱼时暂住） A lodge is a house or hut in the country or in the mountains where people stay on holiday, especially when they want to shoot or fish. （乡间或山里的）度假屋，小舍（尤用于狩猎或钓鱼时暂住）

  • ...a Victorian hunting lodge.

    维多利亚式狩猎度假屋

  • ...a ski lodge.

    滑雪度假屋

SYN cabin, house, shelter, cottage  
2 N-COUNT （宅第大门口的）小屋，门房 A lodge is a small house at the entrance to the grounds of a large house. （宅第大门口的）小屋，门房

  • I drove out of the gates, past the keeper's lodge.

    我驶出大门，经过守门人的小屋。

3 N-COUNT 地方分会；地方分部；分会集会处 In some organizations, a lodge is a local branch or meeting place of the organization. 地方分会；地方分部；分会集会处

  • My father would occasionally go to his Masonic lodge.

    我爸爸偶尔会到他的共济会分会那里去。

SYN society, group, club, association  
4 VERB （正式）提出（投诉、抗议、指控、索赔等） If you lodge a complaint, protest, accusation, or claim, you officially make it. （正式）提出（投诉、抗议、指控、索赔等）

  • He has four weeks in which to lodge an appeal. [VERB noun]

    他有4个星期的时间提出上诉。

SYN register, put, place, set  
5 VERB 寄宿；租住 If you lodge somewhere, such as in someone else's house or if you are lodged there, you live there, usually paying rent. 寄宿；租住

  • ...the story of the farming family she lodged with as a young teacher. [VERB prep./adv.]

    她还是一名年轻教师时所寄宿的那个农家的故事

  • The building he was lodged in turned out to be a church. [be VERB-ed prep./adv.]

    原来，他租住的那栋建筑是一座教堂。

6 VERB 收容；安置；安顿 If someone lodges you somewhere, they give you a place to stay, for example because they are responsible for your safety or comfort. 收容；安置；安顿

  • They took me into custody, questioned me, then lodged me in a children's home. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他们把我关起来，问完我话后又将我安置在儿童院。

  • They lodged the delegates in different hotels. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他们把代表团的成员安顿在不同的酒店。

SYN accommodate, house, shelter, put up  
7 VERB 卡住；搁住；嵌入 If an object lodges somewhere, it becomes stuck there. 卡住；搁住；嵌入

  • The bullet lodged in the sergeant's leg, shattering his thigh bone. [VERB prep./adv.]

    子弹嵌进了中士的腿里，使其股骨碎裂。

  • His car has a bullet lodged in the passenger door. [VERB-ed]

    他的车在客座位的门上嵌着一颗子弹。

SYN stick, remain, catch, implant  
8 VERB 铭记；留存 If a fact or feeling lodges in your mind or is lodged there, you remember it for a long time. 铭记；留存

  • It just lodged in my mind as a very sentimental song. [VERB in noun]

    这首歌令人感怀不已，我已牢牢记住。

  • If you've got something to say it's got to be lodged in their brains at the end. [be V-ed in n]

    如果你有话要说，那就要让他们最终将这些话牢记于心。

  • The festival has lodged itself in the public mind. [V pron-refl in n]

    这个节日已经深入人心。

9 See also: lodging
