tone
toʊn toʊn
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense tones, present participle toning, past tense , past participle toned
1 N-COUNT [usually plural] 音质;音色;音调 The tone of a sound is its particular quality. 音质;音色;音调

  • Cross could hear him speaking in low tones to Sarah.

    克罗斯听得到他对萨拉低声说话。

  • ...the clear tone of the bell. [+ of]

    清亮的铃音

SYN volume, timbre, tonality  
2 N-COUNT 语气；口气；腔调 Someone's tone is a quality in their voice which shows what they are feeling or thinking. 语气；口气；腔调

  • I still didn't like his tone of voice. [+ of]

    我还是不喜欢他说话的语气。

  • Suddenly he laughed again, this time with a cold, sharp tone…

    突然他又笑了，这次笑得冷漠、刺耳。

  • Her tone implied that her patience was limited.

    她的口气暗示她的耐心是有限的。

3 N-SING [oft in NOUN] (演讲、文章的)语气，调子，基调 The tone of a speech or piece of writing is its style and the opinions or ideas expressed in it. (演讲、文章的)语气，调子，基调

  • The spokesman said the tone of the letter was very friendly. [+ of]

    发言人说信函的语气非常友好。

  • His comments to reporters were conciliatory in tone.

    他对记者们的讲话带着安抚的口吻。

  • The whole tone of President Suharto's speech was one of continuity and stability.

    苏哈托总统演讲的整个基调是强调连续性与稳定性。

SYN character, style, approach, feel  
4 N-SING 气氛;氛围;格调 The tone of a place or an event is its general atmosphere. 气氛;氛围;格调

  • The high tone of the occasion was assured by the presence of a dozen wealthy patrons…

    十几位富有资助人的出席确保了仪式的高格调。

  • The service desk at the entrance, with its friendly, helpful and efficient staff, sets the tone for the rest of the store.

    入口处设有服务台，加之友好、热心与高效的服务人员，营造了这家商店的整体氛围。

5 N-UNCOUNT （身体的）强健状况；(尤指肌肉的)紧实度，结实度 The tone of someone's body, especially their muscles, is its degree of firmness and strength. （身体的）强健状况；(尤指肌肉的)紧实度，结实度

  • ...stretch exercises that aim to improve muscle tone.

    旨在提高肌肉结实度的伸展锻炼

  • Keeping your muscles strong and in tone helps you to avoid back problems.

    保持肌肉强健有力有助于避免背部疾病。

6 VERB 使结实；使强健 Something that tones your body makes it firm and strong. 使结实；使强健 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • This movement lengthens your spine and tones the spinal nerves. [VERB noun]

    这一动作舒展脊椎，使脊神经更加强健。

  • Try these toning exercises before you start the day. [VERB-ing]

    在开始一天的活动之前，试着做做这些强身健体的体操。

  • ...finely toned muscular bodies. [VERB-ed]

    健美强壮的身体

Tone up means the same as tone.tone up 同 tone

  • Exercise tones up your body.

    锻炼使身体结实强健。

  • Although it's not strenuous exercise, you feel toned-up, supple and relaxed.

    虽然这不是剧烈运动，但会让人感到强健、柔韧与放松。

7 N-VAR 色调;明暗 A tone is one of the lighter, darker, or brighter shades of the same colour. 色调;明暗

  • Each brick also varies slightly in tone, texture and size.

    每块砖在色调、质地与大小上也都略有不同。

  • I'm a cheery sort of person, so I like cheerful tones.

    我是个活泼类型的人，因此喜欢明亮的色调。

  • …two-tone, striped wallpaper.

    两种色调、带条纹的墙纸

SYN colour, cast, shade, tint  
8 VERB (与…)协调(或相配) If one thing tones with another, the two things look nice together because their colours are similar in quality or brightness. (与…)协调(或相配) [mainly British] [mainly British]

  • Princess Margaret toned with her in a turquoise print dress. [VERB with noun]

    玛格丽特公主那条青绿色的印花裙子与她很相配。

SYN harmonize, match, blend, suit  
Tone in means the same as tone .tone in 同 tone

  • The bowls tone in cleverly with the mugs.

    那些碗与杯子巧妙相配。

9 N-SING 电话提示音 A tone is one of the sounds that you hear when you are using a telephone, for example the sound that tells you that a number is engaged or busy, or no longer exists. 电话提示音
10 N-COUNT 音;全音 A tone is a difference in pitch between two musical notes equal to two semitones. 音;全音
11 See also: dialling tone  ring tone 
12 PHRASE 降低格调 If you say that something lowers the tone of a place or event, you mean that it is not appropriate and makes the place or event seem less respectable. 降低格调

  • Councillors say plastic-framed windows lower the tone of the neighbourhood.

    市政会委员说塑料框架的窗户让这个社区掉份儿了。

13 PHRASAL VERB 使(颜色)柔和；使(味道)变淡;调和 If you tone down a colour or a flavour, you make it less bright or strong. 使(颜色)柔和；使(味道)变淡;调和

  • When Ken Hom wrote his first book for the BBC he was asked to tone down the spices and garlic in his recipes.

    当肯·霍姆为英国广播公司写第一本书的时候，他被要求在菜谱里少用些香料和大蒜。
