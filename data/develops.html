develop
dɪveləp dɪˈvɛləp
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense develops, present participle developing, past tense , past participle developed
1 VERB 发展;成长;发育;生长 When something develops, it grows or changes over a period of time and usually becomes more advanced, complete, or severe. 发展;成长;发育;生长

  • As children develop, some of the most important things they learn have to do with their sense of self. [VERB]

    在成长过程中，孩子学到的一些最重要的东西与他们的自我意识有关。

  • It's hard to say at this stage how the market will develop. [VERB]

    在现阶段，很难说市场将会如何发展。

  • These clashes could develop into open warfare. [VERB + into]

    这些冲突可能会发展成为公开的战争。

  • Society begins to have an impact on the developing child. [VERB-ing]

    社会开始对成长中的孩子产生影响。

developed

  • Their bodies were well-developed and super fit.

    他们的身体发育得很好，非常健壮。

2 VERB (问题或困难)出现，产生 If a problem or difficulty develops, it begins to occur. (问题或困难)出现，产生

  • A huge row has developed about the pollution emanating from a chemical plant. [VERB]

    一家化工厂造成的污染已经引发了激烈争论。

  • ...blood clots in his lungs, a problem which developed from a leg injury. [V + from/out of]

    由腿伤引起的他肺部的血块

SYN result, follow, arise, issue  
3 VERB (国家从贫穷的农业国向富有的工业国)发展 If you say that a country develops, you mean that it changes from being a poor agricultural country to being a rich industrial country. (国家从贫穷的农业国向富有的工业国)发展

  • All countries, it was predicted, would develop and develop fast. [VERB]

    据预测，所有国家都会实现发展而且发展速度会很快。

4See also:developed  developing 
5 V-ERG （使）（企业或行业）发展；（使）扩展；（使）壮大 If you develop a business or industry, or if it develops, it becomes bigger and more successful. （使）（企业或行业）发展；（使）扩展；（使）壮大 [business]

  • She won a grant to develop her own business. [VERB noun]

    她赢得了一笔拨款，用以扩展自己的企业。

  • Over the last few years tourism here has developed considerably. [VERB]

    在过去几年里，这里的旅游业获得了相当大的发展。

developed

  • Housing finance is less developed in continental Europe.

    住房金融业在欧洲大陆的发展程度较低。

6 VERB 开发(土地或地产) To develop land or property means to make it more profitable, by building houses or factories or by improving the existing buildings. 开发(土地或地产) [Also VERB]

  • Entrepreneurs developed fashionable restaurants and bars in the area. [VERB noun]

    实业家在这一地区建起了许多时尚的餐馆和酒吧。

  • ...the cost of acquiring or developing property. [VERB noun]

    获得或开发地产的成本

developed

  • Developed land was to grow from 5.3% to 6.9%.

    已开发土地的面积将从 5.3% 增加到 6.9%。

7 VERB 养成(习惯);获得(名声);形成(信仰) If you develop a habit, reputation, or belief, you start to have it and it then becomes stronger or more noticeable. 养成(习惯);获得(名声);形成(信仰)

  • She later developed a taste for expensive nightclubs. [VERB noun]

    她后来逐渐变得喜欢去消费高昂的夜总会。

  • Mr Robinson has developed the reputation of a ruthless cost-cutter. [VERB noun]

    大家都知道鲁宾逊先生在降低成本上毫不手软。

SYN form, start, begin, contract  
8 V-ERG （使）提高;（使）加强 If you develop a skill, quality, or relationship, or if it develops, it becomes better or stronger. （使）提高;（使）加强

  • Now you have an opportunity to develop a greater understanding of each other. [VERB noun]

    现在你们有一个好机会，可以大大增进对彼此的了解。

  • ...weekly workshops that are designed to develop acting and theatre skills. [VERB noun]

    为了提高表演和舞台技巧而设的每周一次的培训班

  • We must develop closer ties with Germany. [VERB noun]

    我们必须与德国建立更为密切的关系。

  • Their friendship developed through their shared interest in the Arts. [VERB]

    对艺术的共同爱好加深了他们之间的友谊。

developed

  • ...a highly developed instinct for self-preservation.

    很强的自我保护意识

9 V-ERG 患(病);得(病) If you develop an illness, or if it develops, you become affected by it. 患(病);得(病)

  • The test should identify which smokers are most prone to develop lung cancer. [VERB noun]

    这项检查应该可以确认哪些吸烟者更容易得肺癌。

  • A sharp ache developed in her back muscles. [VERB]

    她后背的肌肉疼得厉害。

10 VERB 发生(故障等) If a piece of equipment develops a fault, it starts to have the fault. 发生(故障等)

  • The aircraft made an unscheduled landing after developing an electrical fault. [VERB noun]

    飞机由于发生电力故障而作了临时降落。

11 VERB 研制(新产品) If someone develops a new product, they design it and produce it. 研制(新产品)

  • He claims that several countries have developed nuclear weapons secretly. [VERB noun]

    他声称有几个国家已经秘密研制出了核武器。

  • ...a computer system specially developed for the Coastguard service. [VERB-ed]

    专为海岸警卫队研发的计算机系统

SYN produce, make, create, turn out  
12 V-ERG 阐释(想法、理论、故事或主题)；（使）展开；（使）完善 If you develop an idea, theory, story, or theme, or if it develops, it gradually becomes more detailed, advanced, or complex. 阐释(想法、理论、故事或主题)；（使）展开；（使）完善

  • I would like to thank them for allowing me to develop their original idea. [VERB noun]

    我想感谢他们允许我深入完善他们最初的想法。

  • This point is developed further at the end of this chapter. [VERB noun]

    这一点在本章结尾处有进一步阐释。

  • The idea of weather forecasting developed incredibly quickly. [VERB]

    天气预报概念的发展速度快得令人难以置信。

SYN expand, improve, perfect, extend  
13 VERB 冲洗(胶片);使显影 To develop photographs means to make negatives or prints from a photographic film. 冲洗(胶片);使显影

  • ...after developing one roll of film. [VERB noun]

    冲洗了一卷胶卷后
