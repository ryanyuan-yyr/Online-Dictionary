muse
mjuːz mjuːz mjuz
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense muses, present participle musing, past tense , past participle mused
1 VERB 沉思;冥想;若有所思地说(或写) If you muse on something, you think about it, usually saying or writing what you are thinking at the same time. 沉思;冥想;若有所思地说(或写) [written]

  • Many of the papers muse on the fate of the President. [V + on/about/over]

    很多报纸都在揣测总统的命运。

  • 'As a whole,' she muses, 'the 'organized church' turns me off'. [VERB with quote]

    她沉思道：“总的来说，我不喜欢‘制度森严的教会’。”

  • He once mused that he would have voted Labour in 1964 had he been old enough. [VERB that]

    他曾思忖过，如果1964年时自己够年龄了，他就会投工党的票。

SYN ponder, consider, reflect, contemplate  
musing

  • His musings were interrupted by Montagu who came and sat down next to him.

    他的沉思被走过来挨着他坐下的蒙塔古打断了。

2 N-COUNT (给画家、诗人或音乐家带来灵感的)女神，创作女神 A muse is a person, usually a woman, who gives someone, usually a man, a desire to create art, poetry, or music, and gives them ideas for it. (给画家、诗人或音乐家带来灵感的)女神，创作女神

  • Once she was a nude model and muse to French artist Henri Matisse. [+ to]

    她曾是法国画家亨利·马蒂斯的人体模特和灵感女神。

  • ...the muses who fuel his inspiration.

    给他以灵感的人们
