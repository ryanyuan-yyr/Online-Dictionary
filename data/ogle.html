ogle
oʊgəl ˈoʊgəl ˈɑgəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense ogles, present participle ogling, past tense , past participle ogled
VERB （色迷迷地）盯视 If you say that one person is ogling another, you disapprove of them continually staring at that person in a way that indicates a strong sexual interest. （色迷迷地）盯视 [disapproval] [Also VERB]

  • All she did was hang around ogling the men in the factory. [VERB noun]

    她所做的就只是在工厂里荡来荡去，朝男人抛媚眼。

  • Paula is not used to everyone ogling at her while she undresses backstage. [VERB + at]

    葆拉还不习惯在后台换衣服时所有人都色迷迷地盯着她看。

SYN leer at, stare at, eye up [informal] , gawp at [British , slang]
