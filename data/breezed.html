breeze
briːz briz
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense breezes, present participle breezing, past tense , past participle breezed
1 N-COUNT 微风；清风 A breeze is a gentle wind. 微风；清风

  • ...a cool summer breeze.

    夏日凉爽的微风

SYN light wind, air, whiff, draught  
2 VERB 随意轻松地进入；漫步走入 If you breeze into a place or a position, you enter it in a very casual or relaxed manner. 随意轻松地进入；漫步走入

  • Lopez breezed into the quarter-finals of the tournament. [VERB prep./adv.]

    洛佩斯轻松打入了锦标赛的四分之一决赛。

  • 'Are you all right?' Francine asked as she breezed in with the mail. [VERB prep./adv.]

    “你没事吧？”芙朗辛拿着邮件踱步走进来，问道。

SYN sweep, move briskly, pass, trip  
3 VERB 轻松过关，轻易通过（比赛或测试） If you breeze through something such as a game or test, you cope with it easily. 轻松过关，轻易通过（比赛或测试）

  • John seems to breeze effortlessly through his many commitments at work. [VERB + through]

    约翰似乎不费吹灰之力就完成了很多工作任务。

SYN take something in your stride, have no trouble, make no bones about  
4 N-SING [a NOUN] 容易办的事 If you say that something is a breeze, you mean that it is very easy to do or to achieve. 容易办的事 [informal]

  • And after being an office manager for 20 people, handling my own tiny staff of three is a breeze!.

    我曾经是管理着20个人的办公室经理，现在领导区区3名员工不费吹灰之力！

  • Making the pastry is a breeze if you have a food processor.

    如果你有食品加工机，做这种点心轻而易举。

SYN child's play, piece of cake, pushover [informal] , doddle [British , informal]
