paw
pɔː pɔ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense paws, present participle pawing, past tense , past participle pawed
1 N-COUNT [oft with poss] （猫、狗、熊等的）爪子，脚掌 The paws of an animal such as a cat, dog, or bear are its feet, which have claws for gripping things and soft pads for walking on. （猫、狗、熊等的）爪子，脚掌

  • The kitten was black with white front paws and a white splotch on her chest.

    那只黑色小猫长着白色前爪，胸口上有一块白斑。

  • He removes a thorn from a lion's paw.

    他从狮子脚掌上拔下一根刺。

2 N-COUNT [oft poss NOUN, adjective NOUN] 爪子（指手，尤用于形容手非常大或非常笨拙） You can describe someone's hand as their paw, especially if it is very large or if they are very clumsy. 爪子（指手，尤用于形容手非常大或非常笨拙） [mainly humorous, informal]

  • He shook Keaton's hand with his big paw.

    他用他的大爪子握了握基顿的手。

SYN hand, palm, mitt [slang]  
3 VERB 用爪子抓 （或扒） If an animal paws something, it draws its foot over it or down it. 用爪子抓 （或扒）

  • Madigan's horse pawed the ground. [VERB noun]

    马迪根的马用蹄子扒着地面。

  • The dogs continued to paw and claw frantically at the chain mesh. [VERB + at]

    那些狗继续用爪子疯狂地抓铁丝网。

4 VERB （冒犯地）摸弄，动手动脚 If one person paws another, they touch or stroke them in a way that the other person finds offensive. （冒犯地）摸弄，动手动脚 [disapproval]

  • Stop pawing me, Giles!. [VERB noun]

    别碰我，贾尔斯！

  • He pawed at my jacket with his free hand. [VERB + at]

    他用闲着的那只手摸我的夹克。
