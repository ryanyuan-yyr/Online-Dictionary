excruciating
ɪkskruːʃieɪtɪŋ ɪkˈskruːʃɪˌeɪtɪŋ ɛksˈkruʃˈmætɪŋ ɪkˈskruʃiˌeɪtɪŋ
1 ADJ-GRADED 极痛苦的;折磨人的;剧痛的 If you describe something as excruciating, you are emphasizing that it is extremely painful, either physically or emotionally. 极痛苦的;折磨人的;剧痛的 [emphasis]

  • I was in excruciating pain and one leg wouldn't move.

    我忍受着剧痛，一条腿无法动弹。

  • Her search for love has often caused her excruciating misery and loneliness.

    她对爱情的寻觅常常给她带来极大的痛苦和孤独感。

SYN agonizing, acute, severe, extreme  
excruciatingly

  • He found the transition to boarding school excruciatingly painful.

    他发现转到寄宿学校极其痛苦。

  • The ball hit him excruciatingly in the most sensitive part of his anatomy.

    球打在他身体最敏感的部位，让他疼痛不已。

2 ADJ-GRADED 难以忍受的;令人苦恼的 If you describe something as excruciating, you mean that it is very unpleasant to experience, for example because it is very boring or embarrassing. 难以忍受的;令人苦恼的

  • Meanwhile, the boredom is excruciating.

    同时，又无聊得让人苦恼不已。

  • There was a moment of excruciating silence.

    一时间安静得令人难以忍受。

excruciatingly

  • The dialogue is excruciatingly embarrassing.

    对话十分难堪，让人不堪忍受。

  • ...the one where the children's chorus goes on excruciatingly about 'Grocer Jack'.

    《杂货商杰克》这首歌中儿童合唱队唱得十分难听的一段
