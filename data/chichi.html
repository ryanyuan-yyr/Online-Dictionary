chichi
ʃiʃi ˈʃiːˌʃiː ˈtʃiːˌtʃiː ˈʃiʃi
(also chi-chi)
1 ADJ 自认为时髦的If you say that something is chichi, you mean that it is pretty or stylish, especially in a way that you consider affected. 自认为时髦的 [mainly US, disapproval]

  • Two years ago glass bricks were all the rage in chichi homes.

    两年前，玻璃砖在住宅装饰中风靡一时。

  • Some of the shops were just a bit chichi, I must admit.

    我必须承认，有些商店太过时髦了。

2 N 华丽虚饰the quality of being affectedly pretty or stylish 华丽虚饰
3 N (旧)有英国和印度血统的人 (Also (less common) chee-chee)formerly, in India a person of mixed British and Indian descent; Anglo-Indian (旧)有英国和印度血统的人 (Also (less common) chee-chee)
