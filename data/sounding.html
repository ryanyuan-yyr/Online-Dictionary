sounding
saʊndɪŋ ˈsaʊndɪŋ
Word forms: plural soundings
1 N-SING （铃或号的）发声，鸣响 The sounding of a bell or a horn is the act of causing it to make a sound. （铃或号的）发声，鸣响

  • There were 15 minutes between the first air raid alert and the sounding of the all-clear signal.

    第一次空袭警报和空袭警报解除信号之间间隔了 15 分钟。

2 N-COUNT [usually plural] （收集或征询的）意见 If you take soundings, you try to find out people's opinions on a subject. （收集或征询的）意见

  • She will take soundings of the people's wishes before deciding on a course of action. [+ of]

    在决定行动方针之前，她会对人们的愿望作一番调查。

-sounding -saʊndɪŋ
1 COMB in ADJ-GRADED (与形容词连用)表示“有…声音的”，“听上去…的” -sounding combines with adjectives to indicate a quality that a word, phrase, or name seems to have. (与形容词连用)表示“有…声音的”，“听上去…的”

  • Many literary academics simply parrot a set of impressive-sounding phrases.

    许多文人只是机械地模仿一套听上去令人印象深刻的说法。

  • ...faraway places with strange-sounding names.

    地名听上去比较奇特的遥远地区
