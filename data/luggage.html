luggage
lʌgɪdʒ ˈlʌgɪdʒ
1 N-UNCOUNT 行李 Luggage is the suitcases and bags that you take with you when travel. 行李

  • Leave your luggage in the hotel.

    将您的行李放在酒店。

  • Each passenger was allowed two 30-kg pieces of luggage.

    每位乘客可带两件30公斤的行李。

SYN baggage, things, cases, bags  
2See also:left luggage 
Usage Note :

  • Luggage is an uncount noun. You can have a piece of luggage or some luggage but you cannot have 'a luggage' or 'some luggages'. In British English, people normally use luggage when they are talking about everything that travellers carry. Baggage is a more technical word and is used for example when discussing airports or travel insurance. In American English, luggage refers to empty bags and suitcases and baggage refers to bags and suitcases with their contents. Both British and American speakers can refer to everything that travellers carry as their bags. American speakers can also call an individual suitcase a bag.
