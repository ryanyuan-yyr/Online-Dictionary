lunacy
luːnəsi ˈlunəsi
1 N-UNCOUNT 荒诞；愚蠢 If you describe someone's behaviour as lunacy, you mean that it seems very strange or foolish. 荒诞；愚蠢 [disapproval]

  • ...the lunacy of the tax system. [+ of]

    税制的荒谬

  • It remains lunacy to produce yet more coal to add to power stations' stockpiles.

    煤越产越多，而发电站的煤堆越来越高，这始终是愚蠢的行为。

SYN foolishness, madness, folly, stupidity  
2 N-UNCOUNT 精神错乱 Lunacy is severe mental illness. 精神错乱 [old-fashioned]
