skirmish
skɜːʳmɪʃ ˈskɜrmɪʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense skirmishes, present participle skirmishing, past tense , past participle skirmished
1 N-COUNT 小规模战斗;接触战 A skirmish is a minor battle. 小规模战斗;接触战 [Also + with]

  • Border skirmishes between India and Pakistan were common. [+ between]

    印度和巴基斯坦边境上常常发生小规模战斗。

2 V-RECIP 发生小冲突;进行小规模战斗;打斗 If people skirmish, they fight. 发生小冲突;进行小规模战斗;打斗

  • They were skirmishing close to the minefield now. [VERB]

    他们当时正在雷区附近交火。

  • Police skirmished with youths on the estate last Friday. [VERB + with]

    上周五警方与一些青年在住宅区发生了冲突。

SYN fight, clash, come to blows, scrap [informal]  
skirmishing

  • On land there was minor skirmishing.

    陆地上发生了小规模的战斗。

3 N-COUNT 小争执;口角;争论;冲突 A skirmish is a short, sharp argument. 小争执;口角;争论;冲突

  • This difference in approach has led to several political skirmishes.

    这种方式上的差异已经导致了几场政治冲突。

SYN argument, fight, row, clash  
skirmishing
