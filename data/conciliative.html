conciliate
kənsɪlieɪt kənˈsɪliˌeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense conciliates, present participle conciliating, past tense , past participle conciliated
VERB 和解;调解;安抚 If you conciliate someone, you try to end a disagreement with them. 和解;调解;安抚 [formal]

  • His duty was to conciliate the people, not to provoke them. [VERB noun]

    他的职责是安抚那些人，而不是去激怒他们。

  • The President has a strong political urge to conciliate. [VERB]

    总统有进行安抚的强烈政治意愿。

  • He spoke in a low, nervous, conciliating voice. [VERB-ing]

    他说话时声音低沉、紧张，带着一种安抚的口吻。

SYN pacify, win over, soothe, reconcile  
conciliator

  • Douglas Hurd was widely seen as a conciliator, occupying the central ground of Conservative politics.

    道格拉斯·赫德占据着保守党政治的中心舞台，人们普遍认为其扮演着调解员的角色。
