pocket money
(also pocket-money)
1 N-UNCOUNT （父母给孩子的）零用钱，零花钱 Pocket money is money which children are given by their parents, usually every week. （父母给孩子的）零用钱，零花钱 [mainly British]

  • We agreed to give her £6 a week pocket money.

    我们同意每周给她6英镑零花钱。

in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 allowance
2 N-UNCOUNT 日常零用钱；挣得的小钱 Pocket money is a small amount of money which you earn, and which you can use for buying things that you want. 日常零用钱；挣得的小钱 [mainly British]

  • Volunteers receive £21 pocket money each week, accommodation and expenses.

    志愿者每周领到21英镑的零用钱，免费住宿，还得到一笔生活费。
