inverted commas
ɪnˈvɜːtɪd ˈkɒməz
1 N-PLURAL 引号(写作‘’或“”) Inverted commas are punctuation marks that are used in writing to show where speech or a quotation begins and ends. They are usually written or printed as ' ' or “ ”. Inverted commas are also sometimes used around the titles of books, plays, or songs, or around a word or phrase that is being discussed. 引号(写作‘’或“”) [British]
