plunk
plʌŋk plʌŋk plʌŋk
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense plunks, present participle plunking, past tense , past participle plunked
1 VERB 随意地放下；漫不经心地放下 If you plunk something somewhere, you put it there without great care. 随意地放下；漫不经心地放下 [US, informal]

  • Melanie plunked her cosmetic case down on a chair. [V n with down]

    梅拉妮把化妆盒砰的一声扔到椅子上。

  • She swept up a hat from where it had fallen on the ground, and plunked it on her hair. [VERB noun + on]

    她拾起掉在地上的一顶帽子，随意地往头上一扣。

in BRIT, use 英国英语用 plonk
2 VERB （使）砰地坐下；（使）重重地坐下 If you plunk yourself somewhere, or plunk down, you sit down heavily and clumsily. （使）砰地坐下；（使）重重地坐下 [US, informal]

  • I watched them go and plunked down on one of the small metal chairs. [V down]

    我目送他们离去，然后一屁股坐在其中一把金属小椅子上。

in BRIT, use 英国英语用 plonk
