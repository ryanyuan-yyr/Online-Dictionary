give up
1 PHRASAL VERB 放弃；戒除 If you give up something, you stop doing it or having it. 放弃；戒除 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Coastguards had given up all hope of finding the two divers alive. [VERB PREP. noun/verb-ing]

    海岸警卫队对两位潜水员生还已完全不抱希望。

  • ...smokers who give up before 30. [VERB PREP.]

    30岁以前戒烟的人

2 PHRASAL VERB 投降；认输；放弃 If you give up, you decide that you cannot do something and stop trying to do it. 投降；认输；放弃

  • After a fruitless morning sitting at his desk he had given up. [VERB PREP.]

    他在书桌前徒劳地坐了一上午之后便放弃了。

3 PHRASAL VERB 辞去（工作） If you give up your job, you resign from it. 辞去（工作） [Also V n P, V P]

  • She gave up her job to join her husband's campaign. [VERB PREP. noun/verb-ing]

    她辞去工作，加入到丈夫的宣传活动中。

  • He is thinking of giving up teaching. [VERB PREP. noun/verb-ing]

    他在考虑辞去教书的工作。

SYN quit, leave, resign, step down from [informal]  
4 PHRASAL VERB 把…让给 If you give up something that you have or that you are entitled to, you allow someone else to have it. 把…让给 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Georgia refuses to give up any territory. [VERB PREP. noun]

    格鲁吉亚拒绝出让任何领土。

  • One of the men with him gave up his place on the bench. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他的一个同伴将长椅上的位子让了出来。

SYN hand over, yield, surrender, relinquish [formal]  
5 PHRASAL VERB 自首；投案 If you give yourself up, you let the police or other people know where you are, after you have been hiding from them. 自首；投案 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • A 28-year-old man later gave himself up and will appear in court today. [VERB pronoun-reflexive PREP.]

    后来一位28岁的男子向警方自首了，他将于今天出庭。
