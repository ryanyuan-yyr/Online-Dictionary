slide
slaɪd slaɪd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense slides, present participle sliding, past tense , past participle slid
1 V-ERG 滑动;溜 When something slides somewhere or when you slide it there, it moves there smoothly over or against something. 滑动;溜 [Also VERB]

  • She slid the door open. [VERB noun with adj.ive]

    她滑开了门。

  • I slid the wallet into his pocket. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    我把钱包麻利地塞进了他的口袋。

  • Tears were sliding down his cheeks. [VERB prep./adv.]

    眼泪顺着他的脸颊往下淌。

2 VERB 麻利而无声地移动;悄悄地溜到 If you slide somewhere, you move there smoothly and quietly. 麻利而无声地移动;悄悄地溜到

  • He slid into the driver's seat. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他麻利地坐进驾驶员的座位。

  • 'Nice meeting you, Zoe,' I said and slid off. [VERB prep./adv.]

    “很高兴见到你，佐薇，”说完我便溜走了。

3 VERB 不自觉地陷入(某种情绪、态度或情境) To slide into a particular mood, attitude, or situation means to gradually start to have that mood, attitude, or situation often without intending to. 不自觉地陷入(某种情绪、态度或情境)

  • She had slid into a depression. [VERB + into]

    她不觉沮丧起来。

  • He needs them to stop the country sliding into chaos. [VERB into noun]

    他需要他们来防止这个国家陷入混乱。

SYN fall, drop, descend, decline  
4 VERB (货币)贬值;(物价)下跌 If currencies or prices slide, they gradually become worse or lower in value. (货币)贬值;(物价)下跌 [journalism]

  • The US dollar continued to slide.

    美元继续贬值。

  • The upset sent share prices sliding to their lowest level for almost 18 months. [VERB prep./adv.]

    这种不安情绪使股票价格跌落到几乎 18 个月以来的最低水平。

  • Shares slid 11p to 293p after brokers downgraded their profit estimates. [VERB amount]

    股票经纪人降低了他们的利润预期之后，股价下跌了 11 便士至 293 便士。

  • Its share slid from 24.24 per cent to 22.17 per cent. [V from/to/by amount]

    其份额从 24.24%降至 22.17%。

Slide is also a noun.

  • ...the dangerous slide in oil prices.

    石油价格的危险下跌

5 N-COUNT 幻灯片 A slide is a small piece of photographic film which you project onto a screen so that you can see the picture. 幻灯片

  • ...a slide show.

    幻灯片放映

6 N-COUNT (显微镜的)载玻片 A slide is a piece of glass on which you put something that you want to examine through a microscope. (显微镜的)载玻片
7 N-COUNT 滑梯 A slide is a piece of playground equipment that has a steep slope for children to go down for fun. 滑梯
8 PHRASE 放任…自流;任…恶化而不予处理 If you let something slide, you allow it to get into a worse state or condition by not attending to it. 放任…自流;任…恶化而不予处理

  • The company had let environmental standards slide.

    这家公司听凭环境标准的不断下降。
