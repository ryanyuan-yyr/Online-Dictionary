indictment
ɪndaɪtmənt ɪnˈdaɪtmənt
Word forms: plural indictments
1 N-COUNT 控诉;谴责 If you say that one thing is an indictment of another thing, you mean that it shows how bad the other thing is. 控诉;谴责

  • It's a sad indictment of society that policemen are regarded as easy targets by thugs. [+ of]

    警察很容易就成了暴徒下手袭击的对象,这是一个社会的悲哀。

2 N-VAR 控告;告发;起诉 An indictment is a formal accusation that someone has committed a crime. 控告;告发;起诉 [mainly US, law] [Also + against]

  • Prosecutors may soon seek an indictment on racketeering and fraud charges. [+ on]

    检举官可能不久就会以敲诈勒索与欺诈罪名提起诉讼。

  • The government's indictment against the three men alleged unlawful trading.

    政府在对这三个人的起诉书中指控他们从事非法贸易。

SYN charge, allegation, prosecution, accusation
