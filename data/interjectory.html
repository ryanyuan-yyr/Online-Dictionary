interject
ɪntəʳdʒekt ˌɪntərˈdʒɛkt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense interjects, present participle interjecting, past tense , past participle interjected
VERB (突然)插入 If you interject something, you say it and interrupt someone else who is speaking. (突然)插入 [formal] [Also VERB]

  • 'Surely there's something we can do?' interjected Palin. [VERB with quote]

    “我们一定可以做点儿什么吧？”佩林突然插话道。

  • He listened thoughtfully, interjecting only the odd word. [VERB noun]

    他仔细地听着,只是偶尔插句话。

SYN interrupt with, put in, interpose, introduce
