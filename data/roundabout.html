roundabout
raʊndəbaʊt ˈraʊndəˌbaʊt
Word forms: plural roundabouts
1 N-COUNT （交通）环岛;环形交叉路口 A roundabout is a circular structure in the road at a place where several roads meet. You drive round it until you come to the road that you want. （交通）环岛;环形交叉路口 [British]
in AM, use 美国英语用 traffic circle
2 N-COUNT 旋转木马 A roundabout at a fair is a large, circular mechanical device with seats, often in the shape of animals or cars, on which children sit and go round and round. 旋转木马 [British]
in AM, use 美国英语用 merry-go-round, carousel
3 N-COUNT （儿童坐的）旋转圆台 A roundabout in a park or school play area is a circular platform that children sit or stand on. People push the platform to make it spin round. （儿童坐的）旋转圆台 [British]
in AM, use 美国英语用 merry-go-round
4 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (路线)迂回的，绕弯的，绕远的 If you go somewhere by a roundabout route, you do not go there by the shortest and quickest route. (路线)迂回的，绕弯的，绕远的

  • He left today on a roundabout route for Jordan and is also due soon in Egypt.

    他今天启程绕道去约旦，用不了多久还会去埃及。

5 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (说话或做事)迂回的，转弯抹角的，不直截了当的 If you do or say something in a roundabout way, you do not do or say it in a simple, clear, and direct way. (说话或做事)迂回的，转弯抹角的，不直截了当的

  • We made a bit of a fuss in a roundabout way.

    我们拐弯抹角地发了点牢骚。

  • ...using indirect or roundabout language in place of a precise noun.

    使用间接的、旁敲侧击的语言，而不是准确的名词
