ooze
uːz uz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense oozes, present participle oozing, past tense , past participle oozed
1 V-ERG 渗出；冒出；分泌出 When a thick or sticky liquid oozes from something or when something oozes it, the liquid flows slowly and in small quantities. 渗出；冒出；分泌出

  • He saw there was a big hole in the back of the man's head, blood was still oozing from it. [VERB]

    他看到这个男人后脑勺上有道大口子，血还在往外渗。

  • The lava will just ooze gently out of the crater. [VERB adv.]

    岩浆就会从火山口缓缓涌流出来。

  • The wounds may heal cleanly or they may ooze a clear liquid. [VERB noun]

    这些伤口可能会利索地愈合，也可能会分泌出一种透明的液体。

SYN seep, well, drop, escape  
2 VERB 凸显，洋溢（特性或特点） If you say that someone or something oozes a quality or characteristic, or oozes with it, you mean that they show it very strongly. 凸显，洋溢（特性或特点）

  • Outwardly, Graham will ooze all his old confidence. [VERB noun]

    格雷厄姆表面上看起来会像过去一样满怀信心。

  • The Elizabethan house oozes charm. [VERB noun]

    这幢伊丽莎白时期的房子散发着迷人的魅力。

  • Manchester United were by now oozing with confidence. [VERB + with]

    曼彻斯特联队现在充满信心。

3 N-UNCOUNT （尤指河、湖、海底的）软泥，淤泥，泥浆 You can refer to any thick, sticky, liquid substance as ooze, especially the mud at the bottom of a river, lake, or the sea. （尤指河、湖、海底的）软泥，淤泥，泥浆

  • ...a primeval ooze.

    原始软泥

  • He grabbed into the ooze and came up clutching a large toad.

    他把手伸进淤泥里捞摸，结果抓上来一只巨大的蟾蜍。

SYN mud, clay, dirt, muck
