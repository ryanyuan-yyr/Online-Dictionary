diverge
daɪvɜːʳdʒ dɪ- daɪˈvɜrdʒ dɪˈvɜrdʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense diverges, present participle diverging, past tense , past participle diverged
1 V-RECIP 分化;偏离;背离 If one thing diverges from another similar thing, the first thing becomes different from the second or develops differently from it. You can also say that two things diverge . 分化;偏离;背离

  • His interests increasingly diverged from those of his colleagues. [VERB + from]

    他的兴趣和同事们越来越不一样了。

  • Scientists believe that man diverged from the apes between 5 and 7 million years ago. [VERB from noun]

    科学家们相信人类是在 500 至 700 万年前由类人猿分化而来。

  • When the aims of the partners begin to diverge, there's trouble. [VERB]

    当合作伙伴的目标开始出现分歧时，麻烦就产生了。

SYN separate, part, split, branch  
2 V-RECIP [no cont] (意见、观点等)存在分歧，相悖，相异 If one opinion or idea diverges from another, they contradict each other or are different. You can also say that two opinions or ideas diverge . (意见、观点等)存在分歧，相悖，相异

  • The view of the Estonian government does not diverge that far from Lipmaa's thinking. [VERB + from]

    爱沙尼亚政府的观点和李普马的想法相去不远。

  • Needless to say, theory and practice sometimes diverged. [VERB]

    不用说，理论和实践有时候并不相符。

  • ...the widely diverging ideologies of the two states. [VERB-ing]

    两国之间存在巨大分歧的意识形态

3 V-RECIP (道路)分开，分叉，叉开 If one road, path, or route diverges from another, they lead in different directions after starting from the same place. You can also say that roads, paths, or routes diverge . (道路)分开，分叉，叉开

  • ...a course that diverged from the Calvert Island coastline. [VERB + from]

    从卡尔弗特岛海岸线叉开的一条路

  • Where three roads diverge take the middle branch. [VERB]

    到三岔路口时走中间的道。

SYN deviate, depart, stray, wander
