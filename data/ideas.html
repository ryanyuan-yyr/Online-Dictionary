idea
aɪdiːə aɪˈdiə
Word forms: plural ideas
1 N-COUNT [oft adjective NOUN, NOUN to-infinitive] 计划;建议;提议 An idea is a plan, suggestion, or possible course of action. 计划;建议;提议

  • It's a good idea to plan ahead.

    提前做计划是个好主意。

  • I really like the idea of helping people. [+ of]

    我非常赞同帮助他人的提议。

  • She told me she'd had a brilliant idea.

    她告诉我她有个好主意。

SYN plan, scheme, proposal, design  
2 N-COUNT [NOUN that] 意见;看法;信念 An idea is an opinion or belief about what something is like or should be like. 意见;看法;信念 [Also + on/of]

  • Some of his ideas about democracy are entirely his own. [+ about]

    他关于民主的一些观点完全是他自己的创见。

  • ...the idea that reading too many books ruins your eyes.

    读书过多损害眼睛的看法

  • My idea of physical perfection is to be very slender.

    在我看来，要拥有完美身材就要非常苗条。

SYN notion, thought, view, understanding  
3 N-SING 大致的情况;大致概念 If someone gives you an idea of something, they give you information about it without being very exact or giving a lot of detail. 大致的情况;大致概念

  • This table will give you some idea of how levels of ability can be measured. [+ of]

    这张表格会让你大致了解能力水平可以怎样评估。

  • Could you give us an idea of the range of complaints you've been receiving?.

    你能跟我们大致讲一下今年收到的投诉的范围吗？

  • If you cannot remember the exact date give a rough idea of when it was. [+ of]

    如果你记不清确切日期，就说一下大致的时间吧。

SYN impression, estimate, guess, hint  
4 N-SING (对…的)一定了解，些许的认识 If you have an idea of something, you know about it to some extent. (对…的)一定了解，些许的认识

  • By the end of the week you will have a clear idea of what your eating habits are.

    到这周末你便会清楚自己的饮食习惯了。

  • No one has any real idea how much the company will make next year.

    没人真的知道该公司明年会赚多少钱。

SYN understanding, thought, view, sense  
5 N-SING [NOUN that] 猜测;猜想 If you have an idea that something is the case, you think that it may be the case, although you are not certain. 猜测;猜想 [vagueness]

  • I had an idea that he joined the army later, but I may be wrong.

    我猜他后来参军了，但也可能我猜得不对。

SYN suspicion, guess, impression, notion  
6 N-SING 目标;目的 The idea of an action or activity is its aim or purpose. 目标;目的

  • The idea is to encourage people to get to know their neighbours.

    其目的是促使人们结识邻居。

SYN intention, aim, purpose, object  
7 N-COUNT 打算;意图 If you have the idea of doing something, you intend to do it. 打算;意图

  • He sent for a number of books he admired with the idea of re-reading them. [+ of]

    他订购了一些自己喜欢的书，打算重新再读一遍。

  • I had to postpone ideas of a career and stay at home.

    我不得不暂缓就业打算，呆在家里。

8 N-SING (表示强调)我不知道/我一点儿都不知道 You can use idea in expressions such as I've no idea or I haven't the faintest idea to emphasize that you do not know something. (表示强调)我不知道/我一点儿都不知道 [emphasis]

  • 'Is she coming by coach?' — 'Well I've no idea.'

    “她坐长途汽车来吗?”—“哦，我不知道。”

  • We haven't the faintest idea where he is.

    我们根本不知道他在哪里。

9 PHRASE 理解;懂得;明白 If someone gets the idea, they understand how to do something or they understand what you are telling them. 理解;懂得;明白 [informal]

  • It isn't too difficult once you get the idea.

    你一旦弄懂了，就不太难了。

  • You're beginning to get the idea.

    你开始明白了。

10 PHRASE [VERB inflects, oft PHR wh] (强调好或糟糕的程度)你都意想不到，你想象不出 You can say you have no idea to emphasize how good or bad something is. (强调好或糟糕的程度)你都意想不到，你想象不出 [informal, emphasis]

  • We are both so happy, you have no idea.

    你肯定想象不出来，我们俩都这么幸福。

  • You have no idea how depressed it made me.

    你都想象不出这事令我多么沮丧。
