decency
diːsənsi ˈdisənsi
1 N-UNCOUNT 合宜;得体;正派;合乎礼仪 Decency is the quality of following accepted moral standards. 合宜;得体;正派;合乎礼仪

  • Unfortunately, on Friday night he showed neither decency nor dignity.

    不幸的是，周五晚上他表现得既不得体，也不庄重。

  • His sense of decency forced him to resign.

    他顾及颜面，不得不辞了职。

  • ...the threat of rampant materialism to common decency and enlightened values.

    物欲横流对起码的行为准则和文明的价值观所构成的威胁

SYN propriety, correctness, decorum, fitness  
2 PHRASE （出于礼貌）应该做（但没做）… If you say that someone did not have the decency to do something, you are criticizing them because there was a particular action which they did not do but which you believe they ought to have done. （出于礼貌）应该做（但没做）… [disapproval]

  • Nobody had the decency to inform me of what was planned.

    竟然没有一个人最起码地通知我一声计划了些什么。
