revival
rɪvaɪvəl rɪˈvaɪvəl
Word forms: plural revivals
1 N-COUNT 复苏;复兴;恢复;再次流行 When there is a revival of something, it becomes active or popular again. 复苏;复兴;恢复;再次流行

  • This return to realism has produced a revival of interest in a number of artists. [+ of]

    这种重回现实主义的趋势已经重新激起了人们对许多艺术家的兴趣。

  • There is little chance of a revival in new car sales until at least August next year.

    至少到明年8月之前，新车销量回升的希望都微乎其微。

SYN resurgence, picking up, improvement, rallying  
2 N-COUNT (戏剧、歌剧或芭蕾等的)重新上演 A revival is a new production of a play, an opera, or a ballet. (戏剧、歌剧或芭蕾等的)重新上演

  • ...John Clement's revival of Chekhov's 'The Seagull'. [+ of]

    约翰·克莱门特对于契诃夫的《海鸥》的重新演绎

3 N-UNCOUNT [usually NOUN n] （复兴基督教信仰的）奋兴布道会 A revival meeting is a public religious event that is intended to make people more interested in Christianity. （复兴基督教信仰的）奋兴布道会

  • He toured South Africa organizing revival meetings.

    他游历南非，组织奋兴布道会。
