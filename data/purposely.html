purposely
pɜːʳpəsli ˈpɜrpəsli
1 ADV [usually ADVERB with verb, oft ADVERB adjective] 特意地；故意地；有意地 If you do something purposely, you do it intentionally. 特意地；故意地；有意地 [formal]

  • They are purposely withholding information.

    他们故意隐瞒消息。

SYN deliberately, expressly, consciously, intentionally  
+−Usage
Usagepurposely and purposefully
Be careful not to confuse purposely and purposefully. If you do something purposely (or on purpose), you do it intentionally, not accidentally: Rodrigo purposely came late, just to make Robert mad. If you do something purposefully, you do it strongly and definitely, with an important goal in mind: Robert walked purposefully up to Rodrigo and punched him in the nose!
