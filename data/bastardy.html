bastard
bɑːstəʳd bæs- ˈbæstərd
Word forms: plural bastards
1 N-COUNT 杂种；坏蛋；混蛋 Bastard is an insulting word which some people use about a person, especially a man, who has behaved very badly. 杂种；坏蛋；混蛋 [informal, offensive, disapproval]
2 N-COUNT [usually adjective NOUN] （走运或倒霉的）家伙 Some people say things such as lucky bastard or poor bastard to refer to a person, usually a man, who they think is very lucky, or very unlucky. （走运或倒霉的）家伙 [informal, rude, feelings]
3 N-COUNT [oft NOUN n] 私生子（可能具冒犯意味） A bastard is a person whose parents were not married to each other at the time that he or she was born. This use could cause offence. 私生子（可能具冒犯意味） [old-fashioned]

  • ...King Arthur's bastard son, Mordred.

    亚瑟王的私生子莫德雷德

4 N-SING 极讨厌的事；极麻烦的事 If someone describes a problem or a situation as a bastard, they mean that it is extremely annoying or difficult to deal with. 极讨厌的事；极麻烦的事 [informal, rude]

  • Life can be a real bastard at times.

    有时生活会变得麻烦多多。

  • I had to go early 'cos it's a bastard to get your motor out of the car park.

    我得早点走，因为从停车场取车麻烦得要死。
