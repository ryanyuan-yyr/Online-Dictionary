incapacitate
ɪnkəpæsɪteɪt ˌɪnkəˈpæsəˌteɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense incapacitates, present participle incapacitating, past tense , past participle incapacitated
VERB 使无能力;使伤残 If something incapacitates you, it weakens you in some way, so that you cannot do certain things. 使无能力;使伤残 [formal]

  • A serious fall incapacitated the 68-year-old congressman. [VERB noun]

    一次严重跌伤使这位 68 岁的国会议员丧失了行动能力。

SYN disable, cripple, paralyse, scupper [British , slang]  
incapacitated

  • He is incapacitated and can't work.

    他受伤致残，失去了工作能力。
