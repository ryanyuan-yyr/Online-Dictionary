toil
tɔɪl tɔɪl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense toils, present participle toiling, past tense , past participle toiled
1 VERB 苦干;辛勤劳动 When people toil, they work very hard doing unpleasant or tiring tasks. 苦干;辛勤劳动 [literary] [Also V at/on n]

  • People who toiled in dim, dank factories were too exhausted to enjoy their family life. [VERB]

    在昏暗潮湿的工厂辛苦劳作的人们累得无力再去享受家庭生活。

  • Workers toiled long hours. [VERB noun]

    工人们长时间辛苦劳动。

Toil away means the same as toil.toil away 同 toil

  • She has toiled away at the violin for years.

    她多年来苦练小提琴。

  • Nora toils away serving burgers at the local cafe.

    诺拉在当地小餐馆干着一份为客人端送汉堡包的辛苦活儿。

2 VERB (通常指因疲倦而)费劲地行走，艰难地行动 If you toil somewhere, you move there slowly and with difficulty, usually because you are very tired. (通常指因疲倦而)费劲地行走，艰难地行动 [literary]

  • Arnold had his head down, gasping as he toiled up the hill. [VERB prep./adv.]

    阿诺德垂着头，一边费劲地往山上爬一边气喘吁吁。

SYN struggle, trek, slog, trudge  
3 N-UNCOUNT 苦工;苦活 Toil is unpleasant work that is very tiring physically. 苦工;苦活 [literary]
SYN hard work, industry, labour, effort
