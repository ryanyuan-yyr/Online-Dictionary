bounce
baʊns baʊns
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense bounces, present participle bouncing, past tense , past participle bounced
1 V-ERG （使)弹起；（使)反弹 When an object such as a ball bounces or when you bounce it, it moves upwards from a surface or away from it immediately after hitting it. （使)弹起；（使)反弹

  • I bounced a ball against the house. [VERB noun prep.]

    我对着房子打球。

  • My father would burst into the kitchen bouncing a football. [VERB noun]

    我的父亲会颠着足球冲进厨房。

  • ...a falling pebble, bouncing down the eroded cliff. [VERB prep./adv.]

    从饱受侵蚀的峭壁弹落下来的卵石

  • They watched the dodgem cars bang and bounce. [VERB]

    他们看着那些碰碰车互相碰撞并弹开。

Bounce is also a noun.

  • The wheelchair tennis player is allowed two bounces of the ball.

    轮椅网球选手可以等球落地两次再击球。

2 N-UNCOUNT [usually with supplement] （体育场地的）弹性，弹力 The bounce of a sports field is the condition of it, which determines how high a ball will bounce on it. （体育场地的）弹性，弹力
3 V-ERG （使）（声或光）反射 If sound or light bounces off a surface or is bounced off it, it reaches the surface and is reflected back. （使）（声或光）反射

  • Your arms and legs need protection from light bouncing off glass. [V + off]

    需要保护你的手臂和双腿免受玻璃反射光线的照射。

  • They work by bouncing microwaves off solid objects. [V n + off]

    它们的工作原理是通过固体来反射微波。

4 V-ERG （使）跳动；（使）上下晃动 If something bounces or if something bounces it, it swings or moves up and down. （使）跳动；（使）上下晃动

  • Her long black hair bounced as she walked. [VERB]

    她走路时长长的黑发随之飘动。

  • Then I noticed the car was bouncing up and down as if someone were jumping on it. [VERB adv.]

    然后我注意到那辆车在上下颠簸，好像有人在车上蹦跳似的。

  • The wind was bouncing the branches of the big oak trees. [VERB noun]

    一棵棵高大橡树的枝条随风摇摆。

5 VERB (在柔软物体表面）跳上跳下，蹦跳，弹跳 If you bounce on a soft surface, you jump up and down on it repeatedly. (在柔软物体表面）跳上跳下，蹦跳，弹跳 [Also VERB]

  • She lets us do anything, even bounce on our beds. [VERB prep./adv.]

    她放任我们随心所欲，甚至连我们在床上蹦跳她也不管。

6 VERB （用膝盖）颠动（孩子） If you bounce a child on your knee, you lift him or her up and down quickly and repeatedly for fun. （用膝盖）颠动（孩子）

  • Patsy had picked up the baby and was bouncing him on her knee. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    帕齐抱起婴儿放在膝上颠动着。

7 VERB 蹦蹦跳跳地去 If someone bounces somewhere, they move there in an energetic way, because they are feeling happy. 蹦蹦跳跳地去

  • Moira bounced into the office. [VERB prep./adv.]

    莫伊拉蹦蹦跳跳地进了办公室。

SYN bound, spring, jump, leap  
8 VERB 向…透露（想法）；与…探讨（想法） If you bounce your ideas off someone, you tell them to that person, in order to find out what they think about them. 向…透露（想法）；与…探讨（想法）

  • It was good to bounce ideas off another mind. [V n + off]

    与别人谈谈自己的想法是有益的。

  • Let's bounce a few ideas around. [V n around]

    咱们交流一下看法吧。

9 VERB 迫使…做（不情愿做的事） If someone bounces you into doing something you do not really want to do, they make you do it, usually by starting a process which cannot easily be stopped. 迫使…做（不情愿做的事） [British, journalism]

  • Attempts have been made to bounce member states into decisions with major financial implications. [V n into n/-ing]

    已经努力迫使成员国制定具有重大金融意义的决策。

10 V-ERG （支票）被拒付；（银行）拒付（支票） If a cheque bounces or if a bank bounces it, the bank refuses to accept it and pay out the money, because the person who wrote it does not have enough money in their account. （支票）被拒付；（银行）拒付（支票）

  • Our only complaint would be if the cheque bounced. [VERB]

    我们唯一的不满就是，如果支票被拒付…

  • His bank wrongly bounced cheques worth £75,000. [VERB noun]

    他的开户银行错误地拒付了票面金额为75,000英镑的支票。

11 VERB [VERB] （电子邮件等）被退回 If an e-mail or other electronic message bounces, it is returned to the person who sent it because the address was wrong or because of a problem with one of the computers involved in sending it. （电子邮件等）被退回 [computing]
Phrasal verbs:
bounce back
