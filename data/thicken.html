thicken
θɪkən ˈθɪkən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense thickens, present participle thickening, past tense , past participle thickened
1 V-ERG (使)(液体)变浓稠 When you thicken a liquid or when it thickens, it becomes stiffer and more solid. (使)(液体)变浓稠

  • Thicken the broth with the cornflour. [VERB noun]

    加玉米面使汤变稠。

  • Keep stirring until the sauce thickens. [VERB]

    不停地搅，一直搅到调味汁变稠。

SYN set, condense, congeal, cake  
2 VERB 变密集;变浓密 If something thickens, it becomes more closely grouped together or more solid than it was before. 变密集;变浓密

  • The crowds around him began to thicken. [VERB]

    他四周的人群密集起来。

  • As the ice sheet grows and thickens it chills the nearby air. [VERB]

    随着冰盖越来越大，越来越厚，周围的空气也冷了起来。

3 PHRASE 情况愈发复杂起来 People sometimes say 'the plot thickens' when a situation or series of events is getting more and more complicated and mysterious. 情况愈发复杂起来

  • 'Find anything?' he asked. 'Yeah. The plot thickens,' I said.

    “有什么发现没有？”他问。“嗯，情况越来越复杂了，”我说。
