handle
hændəl ˈhændəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense handles, present participle handling, past tense , past participle handled
1 N-COUNT （门上的）拉手，把手 A handle is a small round object or a lever that is attached to a door and is used for opening and closing it. （门上的）拉手，把手

  • I turned the handle and found the door was open.

    我转了一下门把手，发现门开着。

2 N-COUNT （工具、包、杯等的）柄，把手，提手 A handle is the part of an object such as a tool, bag, or cup that you hold in order to be able to pick up and use the object. （工具、包、杯等的）柄，把手，提手

  • The handle of a cricket bat protruded from under his arm. [+ of]

    一个板球拍柄从他的胳肢窝下面伸了出来。

  • ...a broom handle.

    扫帚柄

SYN grip, knob, hilt, haft  
3 VERB 有能力应付;（成功地）处理 If you say that someone can handle a problem or situation, you mean that they have the ability to deal with it successfully. 有能力应付;（成功地）处理

  • To tell the truth, I don't know if I can handle the job. [VERB noun]

    说实话，我不知道自己是否能做好这份工作。

  • She cannot handle pressure. [VERB noun]

    她承受不了压力。

  • You must learn how to handle your feelings. [VERB noun]

    你必须学会如何控制自己的感情。

SYN manage, deal with, tackle, cope with  
4 VERB （以…方式）应付，处理 If you talk about the way that someone handles a problem or situation, you mention whether or not they are successful in achieving the result they want. （以…方式）应付，处理

  • I think I would handle a meeting with Mr. Siegel very badly. [VERB noun adv.]

    我觉得和西格尔先生会谈我可能会搞得一团糟。

  • She admitted to herself she didn't know how to handle the problem. [VERB noun adv.]

    她心里承认自己不知道该如何处理这个问题。

handling

  • The family has criticized the military's handling of Robert's death.

    罗伯特的家人批评军方对罗伯特死亡的事情处理失当。

5 VERB 负责（某项工作） If you handle a particular area of work, you have responsibility for it. 负责（某项工作）

  • She handled travel arrangements for the press corps during the presidential campaign. [VERB noun]

    她负责了总统竞选活动期间新闻报道团的行程安排。

  • The investigation is being handled by Scotland Yard's anti-terrorist branch. [VERB noun]

    调查由伦敦警察厅反恐部门负责。

SYN deal with, manage, take care of, administer  
6 VERB 使用，操纵（武器、车辆等）;训练，使唤（动物） When you handle something such as a weapon, vehicle, or animal, you use it or control it, especially by using your hands. 使用，操纵（武器、车辆等）;训练，使唤（动物）

  • I had never handled an automatic. [VERB noun]

    我从未用过自动手枪。

7 VERB （车辆等容易）操纵，驾驶 If something such as a vehicle handles well, it is easy to use or control. （车辆等容易）操纵，驾驶

  • His ship had handled like a dream! [VERB adv./prep.]

    他的船操纵起来非常灵便。

8 VERB 拿;抓;搬动 When you handle something, you hold it or move it with your hands. 拿;抓;搬动

  • Wear rubber gloves when handling cat litter. [VERB noun]

    清理猫粪时要带上橡胶手套。

9 N-SING （理解、解决问题的）方法，途径 If you have a handle on a subject or problem, you have a way of approaching it that helps you to understand it or deal with it. （理解、解决问题的）方法，途径 [informal]

  • When you have got a handle on your anxiety you can begin to control it.

    一旦了解了自己焦虑的原因，你就开始能控制它了。

10 PHRASE 大发脾气;勃然大怒 If you fly off the handle, you suddenly and completely lose your temper. 大发脾气;勃然大怒 [informal]

  • He flew off the handle at the slightest thing.

    他为一点小事就大发脾气。

SYN lose your temper, explode, lose it [informal] , lose the plot [informal]
