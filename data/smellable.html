smell
smel smɛl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense smells, present participle smelling, past tense , past participle smelled, past tense , past participle smelt
American English usually uses the form smelled as the past tense and past participle. British English uses either smelled or smelt. 美国英语 smell 的过去式和过去分词通常为 smelled，英国英语使用 smelled 或 smelt。
1 N-COUNT 气味 The smell of something is a quality it has which you become aware of when you breathe in through your nose. 气味

  • ...the smell of freshly baked bread. [+ of]

    新鲜出炉的面包的香味

  • ...horrible smells.

    难闻的气味

  • What is your favourite smell?

    你喜欢哪种气味呢？

SYN odour, scent, fragrance, perfume  
2 N-UNCOUNT 嗅觉 Your sense of smell is the ability that your nose has to detect things. 嗅觉

  • ...people who lose their sense of smell.

    丧失了嗅觉的人

3 V-LINK 散发出…气味;有…气味 If something smells in a particular way, it has a quality which you become aware of through your nose. 散发出…气味;有…气味 [Also V as if]

  • The room smelled of lemons. [VERB + of]

    房间里有股柠檬的味道。

  • It smells delicious. [VERB adj.ive]

    闻起来很香。

  • ...a crumbly black substance that smells like fresh soil. [V + like]

    散发着新鲜泥土气息的黑色易碎物质

SYN reek, stink, have the fragrance of, whiff  
4 VERB 散发异味;气味难闻 If you say that something smells, you mean that it smells unpleasant. 散发异味;气味难闻

  • Ma threw that out. She said it smelled. [VERB]

    妈把那玩意扔出去了，她说它有股怪味。

  • Do my feet smell? [VERB]

    我的脚臭吗？

SYN stink, reek, pong [British , informal] , hum [slang]  
5 VERB 嗅到;闻到 If you smell something, you become aware of it when you breathe in through your nose. 嗅到;闻到

  • As soon as we opened the front door we could smell the gas. [VERB noun]

    我们一打开前门，就闻到了煤气的味道。

6 VERB (用鼻子凑过去)嗅，闻 If you smell something, you put your nose near it and breathe in, so that you can discover its smell. (用鼻子凑过去)嗅，闻

  • I took a fresh rose out of the vase on our table, and smelled it. [VERB noun]

    我从饭桌上的花瓶里抽了一只新鲜的玫瑰，闻了闻。

7 VERB (本能地)预感到，觉察到，嗅出 If you smell something, you feel that it is likely to happen or be true. (本能地)预感到，觉察到，嗅出

  • He knew virtually nothing about music but he could smell a hit. [VERB noun]

    他对音乐几乎一窍不通，却预感到它将会大受欢迎。
