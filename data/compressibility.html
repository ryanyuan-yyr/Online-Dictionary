compress
kəmpres kɒmpres kəmˈprɛs ˈkɑmˌprɛs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense compresses, present participle compressing, past tense , past participle compressed
The verb is pronounced /kəm'pres/. The noun is pronounced /'kɒmpres/. 动词读作 /kəm'pres/，名词读作 /'kɒmpres/。
1 V-ERG (使)压紧;(使)压缩 When you compress something or when it compresses, it is pressed or squeezed so that it takes up less space. (使)压紧;(使)压缩

  • Poor posture, sitting or walking slouched over, compresses the body's organs. [VERB noun]

    坐着或者走路的时候低头垂肩，这种不正确的姿势会压迫体内的器官。

  • Air will compress but the brake fluid won't. [VERB]

    空气会压缩，但是刹车油不会。

SYN squeeze, crush, squash, constrict  
compression

  • The compression of the wood is easily achieved.

    木头很容易压紧。

2 VERB 使简练;缩短 If you compress something such as a piece of writing or a description, you make it shorter. 使简练;缩短

  • He never understood how to organize or compress large masses of material. [VERB noun]

    他从来就不懂如何对大篇的材料进行整理或精简。

  • All those three books are compacted and compressed into one book. [VERB-ed]

    那三本书的内容全都压缩进一本书了。

3 VERB [usually passive] 压缩，缩短(时间) If an event is compressed into a short space of time, it is given less time to happen than normal or previously. 压缩，缩短(时间)

  • The four debates will be compressed into an unprecedentedly short eight-day period. [be VERB-ed + into]

    这4场辩论将被压缩到短短8天的时间，这是前所未有的。

  • Some courses such as engineering had to be compressed. [be VERB-ed]

    有些课程，比如工程学，必须减少课时。

4 N-COUNT (用以退烧的)敷布，压布 A compress is a pad of wet or dry cloth pressed on part of a patient's body to reduce fever. (用以退烧的)敷布，压布

  • Sore throats may be relieved by cold compresses.

    喉咙痛用冷敷布敷一下可能会好一些。
