compile
kəmpaɪl kəmˈpaɪl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense compiles, present participle compiling, past tense , past participle compiled
VERB 汇编;编制;编纂 When you compile something such as a report, book, or programme, you produce it by collecting and putting together many pieces of information. 汇编;编制;编纂

  • Councils were required to compile a register of all adults living in their areas. [VERB noun]

    各地政务委员会被要求编制一份该地区成年居民的名册。

  • The book took 10 years to compile. [VERB noun]

    编这本书花了10年时间。

  • A report compiled by the Fed's Philadelphia branch described the economy as weak. [VERB-ed]

    一份由联邦储备银行的费城分部汇编的报告说经济不景气。

SYN put together, collect, gather, organize  
compilation

  • There have been enormous advances in the compilation of data on suspected terrorists.

    恐怖嫌疑犯的资料收集工作已经取得了非常大的进展。
