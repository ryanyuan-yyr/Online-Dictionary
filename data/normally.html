normally
nɔːʳməli ˈnɔrməli
1 ADV [ADVERB with verb] 正常地;通常;平常 If you say that something normally happens or that you normally do a particular thing, you mean that it is what usually happens or what you usually do. 正常地;通常;平常

  • All airports in the country are working normally today.

    今天国内所有机场都运转正常。

  • Social progress is normally a matter of struggles and conflicts.

    社会进步通常是斗争和冲突的结果。

  • Normally, the transportation system in Paris carries 950,000 passengers a day.

    正常情况下，巴黎的交通系统每天运送95万乘客。

SYN usually, generally, commonly, regularly  
2 ADV [ADVERB after verb] 常规地;按惯例 If you do something normally, you do it in the usual or conventional way. 常规地;按惯例

  • She would apparently eat normally and then make herself sick.

    她显然还会照吃不误，然后把自己吃到恶心。

  • ...failure of the blood to clot normally.

    血液未能正常凝结

SYN as usual, naturally, properly, conventionally
