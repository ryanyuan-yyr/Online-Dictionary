write
raɪt raɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense writes, present participle writing, past tense wrote, past participle written
1 VERB 写;书写;写字 When you write something on a surface, you use something such as a pen or pencil to produce words, letters, or numbers on the surface. 写;书写;写字

  • If you'd like one, simply write your name and address on a postcard and send it to us. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    如果你想要一个的话，只要把姓名、住址写在一张明信片上寄给我们就行。

  • They were still trying to teach her to read and write. [VERB]

    他们还在试图教她读书写字。

  • He wrote the word 'pride' in huge letters on the blackboard. [VERB noun]

    他在黑板上写了一个大大的单词pride。

2 VERB 写(书、诗等);创作(歌曲) If you write something such as a book, a poem, or a piece of music, you create it and record it on paper or perhaps on a computer. 写(书、诗等);创作(歌曲)

  • I had written quite a lot of orchestral music in my student days. [VERB noun]

    我在学生时代创作过很多管弦乐作品。

  • Finding a volunteer to write the computer program isn't a problem. [VERB noun]

    找个志愿者编这个计算机程序不成问题。

  • Thereafter she wrote articles for papers and magazines in Paris. [VERB noun + for]

    此后她给巴黎的报纸和杂志撰稿。

  • Jung Lu wrote me a poem once. [VERB noun noun]

    荣路有一次给我写了一首诗。

SYN compose, create, author, draft  
3 VERB 写作，著述（通常以出版为目的） Someone who writes creates books, stories, or articles, usually for publication. 写作，著述（通常以出版为目的）

  • Jay wanted to write. [VERB]

    杰伊想要写作。

  • She writes for many papers, including the Sunday Times. [VERB + for]

    她给包括《星期日泰晤士报》在内的很多报纸撰稿。

  • He now works in industry and writes on science in his spare time. [V on/about n]

    他现在在企业工作，业余时间写点科学文章。

4 VERB 写(信);给…写信(美国英语亦可用 write someone) When you write to someone or write them a letter, you give them information, ask them something, or express your feelings in a letter. In American English, you can also write someone. nothing to write home about→see: home  写(信);给…写信(美国英语亦可用 write someone)

  • Many people have written to me on this subject. [VERB + to]

    很多人给我写信探讨这个问题。

  • She had written him a note a couple of weeks earlier. [VERB noun noun]

    她几周前给他写过一个便条。

  • I wrote a letter to the car rental agency, explaining what had happened. [VERB noun + to]

    我给租车行写了一封信说明发生的情况。

  • Why didn't you write, call, anything?. [VERB]

    你为什么不写信，不打电话，什么也不做？

  • He had written her in Italy but received no reply. [VERB noun]

    他在意大利给她写过信，但没有收到回音。

5 VERB (在信、书或文章中)写道，说道 If someone writes that something is the case, they say it in a letter, book, or article. (在信、书或文章中)写道，说道

  • 'Some six months later,' Freud writes, 'Hans had got over his jealousy.'. [VERB with quote]

    “大约6个月后，”弗洛伊德写道，“汉斯克服了他的嫉妒心理。”

  • A few days later he wrote that he had hopes of a staff job. [VERB that]

    几天后，他写信说他有望得到一份行政工作。

6 VERB 开具(支票、收据、处方等) When someone writes something such as a cheque, receipt, or prescription, they put the necessary information on it and usually sign it. 开具(支票、收据、处方等) [Also VERB n + for] [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Snape wrote a receipt with a gold fountain pen. [VERB noun]

    斯内普用金笔开了一张收据。

  • I'll write you a cheque in a moment. [VERB noun noun]

    我过会儿给你开张支票。

Write out means the same as write .write out 同 write

  • We went straight to the estate agent and wrote out a cheque.

    我们径直到房地产经纪人那里开了一张支票。

  • Get my wife to write you out a receipt before you leave.

    你走之前让我太太给你开个收据。

7 VERB (往计算机或磁盘)录入，写入 If you write to a computer or a disk, you record data on it. (往计算机或磁盘)录入，写入 [computing]

  • You should write-protect all disks that you do not usually need to write to. [V + to/onto]

    你应该对你通常不需要写入内容的磁盘进行写保护。
