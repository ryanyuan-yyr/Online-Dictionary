ace
eɪs eɪs
Word forms: plural aces
1 N-COUNT A纸牌 An ace is a playing card with a single symbol on it. In most card games, the ace of a particular suit has either the highest or the lowest value of the cards in that suit. A纸牌

  • ...the ace of hearts. [+ of]

    红桃A

SYN one, single point  
2 N-COUNT [oft n NOUN] （在运动等方面）成绩超群的人，佼佼者 If you describe someone such as a sports player as an ace, you mean that they are very good at what they do. （在运动等方面）成绩超群的人，佼佼者 [journalism]

  • ...former motor-racing ace Stirling Moss.

    前一流赛车手斯特林·莫斯

SYN expert, star, champion, authority  
Ace is also an adjective.

  • ...ace horror-film producer Lawrence Woolsey.

    王牌恐怖片制片人劳伦斯·伍尔西

3 ADJ 非常好的；顶呱呱的 If you say that something is ace, you think that it is good and you like it a lot. 非常好的；顶呱呱的 [informal, approval]

  • ...a really ace film.

    精彩绝伦的电影

SYN great [informal] , good, brilliant, mean [slang]  
4 N-COUNT （网球中的）发球得分，爱司球 In tennis, an ace is a serve which is so fast that the other player cannot reach the ball. （网球中的）发球得分，爱司球

  • The American served three aces in the opening game of the set.

    这位美国选手在该盘首局有三次发球直得。

5 PHRASE 占绝对优势；占尽天时地利人和 If you say that someone holds all the aces, you mean that they have all the advantages in a contest or situation. 占绝对优势；占尽天时地利人和

  • 'When I was an adolescent,' says a thirty-one-year-old man, 'I thought girls held all the aces.'

    “当我还是个毛头小伙儿时，”一个31岁的男人说，“我觉得完全是女孩子说了算。”

6 PHRASE [verb-link PHRASE, PHRASE after verb] 秘藏的王牌;应急的绝招;锦囊妙计 Something that is an ace in the hole is an advantage which you have over an opponent or rival, and which you can use if necessary. 秘藏的王牌;应急的绝招;锦囊妙计

  • Our superior technology is our ace in the hole.

    我们超众的技术是我们的法宝。

SYN advantage, benefit, edge, asset  
7 PHRASE [VERB inflects, PHR -ing/n] 差点;险些 If you come within an ace of doing something, or you are within an ace of doing something, you very nearly do or experience it. 差点;险些

  • He was intent on murder and he came within an ace of succeeding.

    他一心想杀人并且差点就得逞了。
