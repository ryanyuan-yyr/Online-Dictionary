detour
diːtʊəʳ ˈdiˌtʊr diˈtʊr dɪˈtʊr
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense detours, present participle detouring, past tense , past participle detoured
1 N-COUNT 绕道;绕路;迂回 If you make a detour on a journey, you go by a route which is not the shortest way, because you want to avoid something such as a traffic jam, or because there is something you want to do on the way. 绕道;绕路;迂回

  • He did not take the direct route to his home, but made a detour around the outskirts of the city.

    他没有直接回家，而是绕到市郊兜了个圈子。

SYN diversion [British] , bypass, deviation, circuitous route  
2 N-COUNT 绕行路线;迂回路线 A detour is a special route for traffic to follow when the normal route is blocked, for example because it is being repaired. 绕行路线;迂回路线 [US]
in BRIT, use 英国英语用 diversion
3 VERB 绕道;绕路;绕行 If you detour, you make a detour. 绕道;绕路;绕行 [Also VERB preposition]

  • On the way back, Jarvis detoured to check the time of services at the church. [VERB]

    回去的路上，贾维斯绕道去核实了一下教堂礼拜仪式的时间。
