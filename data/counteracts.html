counteract
kaʊntərækt ˌkaʊntərˈækt ˌkaʊntərˈækt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense counteracts, present participle counteracting, past tense , past participle counteracted
VERB 抵制;抵消;抵抗 To counteract something means to reduce its effect by doing something that produces an opposite effect. 抵制;抵消;抵抗

  • My husband has to take several pills to counteract high blood pressure. [VERB noun]

    我丈夫不得不吃几片药来降压。

  • This event will counteract such trends. [VERB noun]

    这一事件将会削弱此类趋势。

SYN offset, negate, neutralize, invalidate
