complete
kəmpliːt kəmˈplit
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense completes, present participle completing, past tense , past participle completed
1 ADJ [usually ADJ n] 完全的;十足的 You use complete to emphasize that something is as great in extent, degree, or amount as it possibly can be. 完全的;十足的 [emphasis]

  • The rebels had taken complete control.

    叛乱分子完全控制了局面。

  • It shows a complete lack of understanding by management.

    这表明管理层对此完全不了解。

  • The resignation came as a complete surprise.

    辞职来得十分意外。

  • He was the complete opposite of Raymond.

    他与雷蒙德完全相反。

completely

  • Dozens of flats had been completely destroyed.

    许多公寓已经被完全破坏了。

  • Make sure that you defrost it completely.

    一定要完全解冻。

  • ...something completely different.

    截然不同的事

2 ADJ [ADJ n] 完整的;全部的;整个的 You can use complete to emphasize that you are referring to the whole of something and not just part of it. 完整的;全部的;整个的 [emphasis]

  • A complete tenement block was burnt to the ground.

    整幢公寓大楼被付之一炬。

  • The job sheets eventually filled a complete book.

    工作指导卡最终装满了整整一个册子。

SYN whole, full, entire  
3 ADJ 齐全的;完整的 If something is complete, it contains all the parts that it should contain. 齐全的;完整的

  • The list may not be complete.

    这个单子可能不全。

  • ...a complete dinner service.

    一整套餐具

  • No garden is complete without a bed of rose bushes.

    没有玫瑰花圃的花园是不完整的。

completeness

  • ...the accuracy and completeness of the information obtained.

    所获信息的精确性和完整性

4 VERB [no cont] 使齐全;使完整 To complete a set or group means to provide the last item that is needed to make it a full set or group. 使齐全;使完整

  • Children don't complete their set of 20 baby teeth until they are two to three years old. [VERB noun]

    小孩要到两三岁才会长齐20颗乳牙。

  • ...the stickers needed to complete the collection. [VERB noun]

    集全一整套所需的贴纸

SYN perfect, accomplish, finish off, round off  
5 ADJ [ADJ n] (作品)全部的，完整的 The complete works of a writer are all their books or poems published together in one book or as a set of books. (作品)全部的，完整的

  • ...the Complete Works of William Shakespeare.

    《威廉·莎士比亚全集》

6 PREP-PHRASE 配有…的;兼有…的 If one thing comes complete with another, it has that thing as an extra or additional part. 配有…的;兼有…的

  • The diary comes complete with a gold-coloured ballpoint pen. [+ with]

    这个日记本配有一支金色的圆珠笔。

7 ADJ [verb-link ADJ] 完成的;结束的 If something is complete, it has been finished. 完成的;结束的

  • The work of restoring the farmhouse is complete.

    修复农舍的工作已经完成。

  • It'll be two years before the process is complete.

    这个过程需要两年才能完成。

SYN finished, done, ended, completed  
8 VERB 结束;完成 If you complete something, you finish doing, making, or producing it. 结束;完成

  • Peter Mayle has just completed his first novel. [VERB noun]

    彼得·梅尔刚刚完成他的第一部小说。

  • ...the rush to get the stadiums completed on time. [get n V-ed]

    为了让各个体育场按时完工进行的匆忙赶工

SYN finish, conclude, fulfil, accomplish  
completion

  • The project is nearing completion.

    这个项目即将结束。

  • House completions for the year should be up from 1,841 to 2,200.

    今年竣工的房屋将会从1,841幢增加到2,200幢。

9 VERB [no cont] 全部完成 If you complete something, you do all of it. 全部完成

  • She completed her degree in two years. [VERB noun]

    她用两年的时间修完了课程，拿到了学位。

  • This book took years to complete. [VERB noun]

    这本书耗时多年才写完。

10 VERB 填写(表格或问卷) If you complete a form or questionnaire, you write the answers or information asked for in it. 填写(表格或问卷)

  • Simply complete the coupon below. [VERB noun]

    填写下面的订货单即可。

  • We ask candidates to complete a psychometric questionnaire. [VERB noun]

    我们让候选人填写一份心理测试问卷。

  • Use the enclosed envelope to return your completed survey. [VERB-ed]

    请用随信附上的信封将填好的调查表寄回。

SYN fill in, fill out  
11 ADJ [ADJ n] 全面的;全能的 You can use complete to emphasize that someone is skilled at all aspects of a particular activity and is therefore the best example of that kind of person. 全面的;全能的 [emphasis]

  • He was the complete all-round journalist.

    他是一名全能的记者。
