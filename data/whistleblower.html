whistle-blower ˈhwɪsəlˌbloʊər ˈwɪsəlˌbloʊər
(also whistleblower)Word forms: plural whistle-blowers
N-COUNT 告发者;检举者 A whistle-blower is someone who finds out that the organization they are working for is doing something immoral or illegal and tells the authorities or the public about it. 告发者;检举者 [journalism]

  • He has been a prominent victim of alleged witch-hunts against whistle-blowers in the NHS.
