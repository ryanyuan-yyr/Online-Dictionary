flower
flaʊəʳ ˈflaʊər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense flowers, present participle flowering, past tense , past participle flowered
1 N-COUNT 花朵;鲜花 A flower is the part of a plant which is often brightly coloured, grows at the end of a stem, and only survives for a short time. 花朵;鲜花

  • Each individual flower is tiny.

    每一朵花都很小。

  • ...large, purplish-blue flowers.

    紫蓝色的大花朵

SYN bloom, blossom, efflorescence  
2 N-COUNT [usually plural] (已摘的)带梗的花 A flower is a stem of a plant that has one or more flowers on it and has been picked, usually with others, for example to give as a present or to put in a vase. (已摘的)带梗的花

  • ...a bunch of flowers sent by a new admirer.

    又一个仰慕者送来的一束鲜花

3 N-COUNT [usually plural] 花卉;开花植物 Flowers are small plants that are grown for their flowers as opposed to trees, shrubs, and vegetables. 花卉;开花植物

  • ...a lawned area surrounded by plants and flowers.

    周围种着植物和花卉的草地

  • The flower garden will be ablaze with colour every day.

    花园每天都将开满各色鲜花。

4 VERB 开花;盛开 When a plant or tree flowers, its flowers appear and open. 开花;盛开

  • Several of these rhododendrons will flower this year for the first time. [VERB]

    这些杜鹃花有些将在今年首度开花。

SYN bloom, open, mature, flourish  
5 VERB 繁荣;成熟;发展 When something flowers, for example a political movement or a relationship, it gets stronger and more successful. 繁荣;成熟;发展

  • Their relationship flowered. [VERB]

    他们的关系最终开花结果了。

SYN blossom, grow, develop, progress  
6 N-SING [the NOUN of n] 精华;精英;最好的部分 A person or thing that is described as the flower of something is the best part or example of it. 精华;精英;最好的部分 [literary]

  • Those killed have been described as the flower of Polish manhood.

    那些牺牲者被称为波兰男子中的精英。

7 PHRASE 开花;花朵绽放 When a plant is in flower or when it has come into flower, its flowers have appeared and opened. 开花;花朵绽放

  • Some of the daffodils are still in flower.

    有些水仙花仍在开放。

  • As one plant fades, another comes into flower.

    一株植物凋谢了，另一株又会盛开。

8See also:flowered
