valedictory
vælɪdɪktəri ˌvæləˈdɪktəri
Word forms: plural valedictories
1 ADJ [usually ADJ n] （致词、书信或演出）告别的，告别性质的 A valedictory speech, letter, or performance is one that is intended as a way of saying goodbye when someone leaves another person, a place, or a job. （致词、书信或演出）告别的，告别性质的 [formal]

  • ...Mr Walker, making his valedictory address after two years as chairman.

    沃克先生，在担任主席职务两年后发表了告别演说

  • ...her valedictory aria, sung as she leaves her lover.

    她离开情人时唱起的离别咏叹调

SYN farewell, leaving, parting, last  
2 N-COUNT [oft NOUN n] （优秀生在毕业典礼上作的）告别演说 A valedictory is a speech that is given by the student with the highest marks in their class at their graduation ceremony. （优秀生在毕业典礼上作的）告别演说 [US]

  • He listened to Howard give the valedictory address at high school graduation.

    他听了霍华德在中学毕业典礼上作的告别演讲。
