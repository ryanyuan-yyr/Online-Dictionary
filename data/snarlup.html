snarl up
PHRASAL VERB 阻塞;使…陷于混乱(或停顿) To snarl something up means to cause problems which prevent it continuing or making progress. 阻塞;使…陷于混乱(或停顿) [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • The ensuing row snarled up the work of the joint peace commission. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    随后的争吵把联合和平委员会的工作搅得一团糟。

SYN tangle, complicate, muddle, embroil  
snarl-up
Word forms: plural snarl-ups
N-COUNT (交通阻塞等)混乱局面 A snarl-up is a disorganized situation such as a traffic jam, in which things are unable to move or work normally. (交通阻塞等)混乱局面 [British, informal]
SYN tangle, confusion, muddle, entanglement
