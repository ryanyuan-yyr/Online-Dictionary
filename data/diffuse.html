diffuse
dɪfjuːz dɪfjuːs dɪˈfjus dɪˈfjuz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense diffuses, present participle diffusing, past tense , past participle diffused
The verb is pronounced /dɪ'fjuːz/. The adjective is pronounced /dɪ'fjuːs/. 动词读作 /dɪ'fjuːz/。形容词读作/dɪ'fjuːs/。
1 V-ERG (使）（知识、消息等)传播；（使）散布；（使）普及 If something such as knowledge or information is diffused, or if it diffuses somewhere, it is made known over a wide area or to a lot of people. (使）（知识、消息等)传播；（使）散布；（使）普及 [written]

  • Over time, the technology is diffused and adopted by other countries. [be VERB-ed]

    久而久之，这项技术传播到了其他国家并得到利用。

  • ...an attempt to diffuse new ideas. [VERB noun]

    传播新思想的尝试

  • As agriculture developed, agricultural ideas diffused across Europe. [VERB prep.]

    随着农业的发展，农业知识开始在欧洲普及。

diffusion

  • ...the development and diffusion of ideas.

    思想的发展和传播

2 VERB 宣泄，舒缓(尤指不良情绪) To diffuse a feeling, especially an undesirable one, means to cause it to weaken and lose its power to affect people. 宣泄，舒缓(尤指不良情绪)

  • The arrival of letters from the Pope did nothing to diffuse the tension. [VERB noun]

    教皇的来信并没有缓解紧张的气氛。

3 VERB 使(光)漫射;使发散 If something diffuses light, it causes the light to spread weakly in different directions. 使(光)漫射;使发散

  • Diffusing a light also reduces its power. [VERB noun]

    使灯光漫射也会降低其强度。

  • The sun slid behind trees, its last light diffused by wintry branches. [VERB noun]

    太阳躲到了树后，余晖透过冬日的树枝洒落下来。

4 V-ERG （使）扩散;（使）弥漫;（使）渗透 To diffuse or be diffused through something means to move and spread through it. （使）扩散;（使）弥漫;（使）渗透

  • It allows nicotine to diffuse slowly and steadily into the bloodstream. [VERB prep.]

    它能使尼古丁平稳缓慢地渗透到血液里。

  • It created a glowing centre of warmth that quickly diffused through my limbs. [VERB prep.]

    它形成了一个温暖的热源，热量迅速扩散至我的四肢。

  • The moisture present in all foods absorbs the flavour of the smoke and eventually diffuses that flavour into its interior. [VERB noun prep.]

    所有食物中都存在的水分会吸收烟味，并最终将其散播到食物内部。

diffusion

  • There are data on the rates of diffusion of molecules.

    有关于分子扩散速度的数据。

5 ADJ-GRADED 散布的;分散的;弥漫的 Something that is diffuse is not directed towards one place or concentrated in one place but spread out over a large area. 散布的;分散的;弥漫的 [written]

  • ...a diffuse community.

    分散的社群

  • A cold, diffuse light filtered in through the skylight.

    一道冰冷的光线透过天窗漫射进来。

SYN spread-out, scattered, dispersed, unconcentrated  
6 ADJ-GRADED 含糊不清的;难以理解的 If you describe something as diffuse, you mean that it is vague and difficult to understand or explain. 含糊不清的;难以理解的

  • His writing is so diffuse and obscure that it is difficult to make out what it is he is trying to say.

    他的文章含混晦涩，让人看后不知所云。

SYN rambling, loose, vague, meandering
