expostulate
ɪkspɒstʃʊleɪt ɪkˈspɒstjʊˌleɪt ɛkˈspɑstʃəˌleɪt ɪkˈspɑstʃəˌleɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense expostulates, present participle expostulating, past tense , past participle expostulated
VERB 争论;反驳 If you expostulate, you express strong disagreement with someone. 争论;反驳 [formal]

  • 'For heaven's sake!' Dot expostulated. 'They're cheap and they're useful.'. [VERB with quote]

    “拜托！”多特反驳道，“它们又便宜又管用。”

  • For a moment I thought she was going to expostulate. [VERB]

    那一刻，我以为她要提出异议。

  • His family expostulated with him. [VERB + with]

    家人和他意见相左。

SYN protest, reason (with), argue (with), dissuade
