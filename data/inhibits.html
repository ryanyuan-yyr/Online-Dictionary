inhibit
ɪnhɪbɪt ɪnˈhɪbɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense inhibits, present participle inhibiting, past tense , past participle inhibited
1 VERB 抑制;约束 If something inhibits an event or process, it prevents it or slows it down. 抑制;约束

  • Wine or sugary drinks inhibit digestion. [VERB noun]

    葡萄酒或含糖饮料抑制消化。

  • The high cost of borrowing is inhibiting investment by industry in new equipment. [VERB noun]

    借款的高成本抑制了企业对新设备的投资。

2 VERB 禁止;阻止 To inhibit someone from doing something means to prevent them from doing it, although they want to do it or should be able to do it. 禁止;阻止

  • It could inhibit the poor from getting the medical care they need. [VERB noun + from]

    那会使穷人得不到他们所需要的医疗保健。

  • Officers will be inhibited from doing their duty. [V n from -ing/n]

    官员们将无法履行职责。

SYN prevent, stop, bar, frustrate
