willy-nilly
wɪli nɪli ˌwɪlɪˈnɪlɪ ˈwɪliˈnɪli
(also willy nilly)
1 ADV [usually ADVERB with verb] 不管喜欢不喜欢;无可奈何地 If something happens to you willy-nilly, it happens whether you like it or not. 不管喜欢不喜欢;无可奈何地

  • The government were dragged willy-nilly into the confrontation.

    政府不管情愿不情愿，也被拖进了这场冲突。

SYN whether you like it or not, necessarily, of necessity, perforce [formal]  
2 ADV [usually ADVERB after verb] 随意地;胡乱地 If someone does something willy-nilly, they do it in a careless and disorganized way, without planning it in advance. 随意地;胡乱地

  • Clerks bundled papers into files willy-nilly.

    职员们随意地将文件塞进文件夹里。

SYN haphazardly, at random, randomly, without order
