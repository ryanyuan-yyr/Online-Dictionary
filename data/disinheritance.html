disinherit
dɪsɪnherɪt ˌdɪsɪnˈhɛrɪt ˌdɪsɪnˈhɛrɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense disinherits, present participle disinheriting, past tense , past participle disinherited
VERB 剥夺…的继承权 If you disinherit someone such as your son or daughter, you arrange that they will not become the owner of your money and property after your death, usually because they have done something that you do not approve of. 剥夺…的继承权

  • He threatened to disinherit her if she refused to obey. [VERB noun]

    他威胁说如果她拒绝服从就剥夺她的继承权。

SYN cut off, dispossess, disown, cut off without a penny
