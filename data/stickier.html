sticky
stɪki ˈstɪki
Word forms: comparative stickier, superlative stickiest
1 ADJ-GRADED 黏性的;涂有黏性物质的 A sticky substance is soft, or thick and liquid, and can stick to other things. Sticky things are covered with a sticky substance. 黏性的;涂有黏性物质的

  • ...sticky toffee.

    黏牙的太妃糖

  • If the dough is sticky, add more flour.

    如果面团太黏，就再加点面粉。

  • Peel away the sticky paper.

    将贴纸撕掉。

SYN adhesive, gummed, adherent, grippy  
stickiness

  • ...the stickiness of her hands.

    她黏糊糊的双手

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] (情况)棘手的，令人为难的 A sticky situation involves problems or is embarrassing. (情况)棘手的，令人为难的 [mainly British, informal]

  • Inevitably the transition will yield some sticky moments.

    过渡时期难免会出现一些困难的时候。

  • Her research was going through a sticky patch.

    她的研究工作正处于一个艰难的阶段。

3 ADJ-GRADED (天气)湿热的，闷热的 Sticky weather is unpleasantly hot and damp. (天气)湿热的，闷热的

  • ...four desperately hot, sticky days in the middle of August.

    8 月中旬极其闷热的 4 天

SYN humid, close, sultry, oppressive  
4 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 下场很悲惨;死得很惨 If someone comes to a sticky end or meets a sticky end, they suffer very badly or die in an unpleasant way. 下场很悲惨;死得很惨 [British, informal]

  • Arminius also came to a sticky end, murdered by his own troops.

    阿米尼乌斯也死得很惨，被自己的部下害死了。
