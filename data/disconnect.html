disconnect
dɪskənekt ˌdɪskəˈnɛkt ˈdɪskəˌnɛkt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense disconnects, present participle disconnecting, past tense , past participle disconnected
1 VERB 切断;断开 To disconnect a piece of equipment means to separate it from its source of power or to break a connection that it needs in order to work. 切断;断开

  • The device automatically disconnects the ignition when the engine is switched off. [VERB noun]

    当引擎关掉时，这个装置会自动切断点火开关。

  • Vicky Brown arrived home to find the men disconnecting her microwave. [VERB noun]

    维基·布朗到家时发现那些人正在断开她微波炉的开关。

  • She ran back to the phone. The line was disconnected.

    她跑回去接电话。但电话已挂了。

SYN deactivate, shut down, turn off, switch off  
2 VERB [usually passive] (通常指因欠费而)切断(煤气、水、电或者电话) If you are disconnected by a gas, electricity, water, or telephone company, they turn off the connection to your house, usually because you have not paid the bill. (通常指因欠费而)切断(煤气、水、电或者电话)

  • You will be given three months to pay before you are disconnected. [be VERB-ed]

    在被切断供应之前你有三个月的时间来付款。

3 VERB 使分离;使分开 If you disconnect something from something else, you separate the two things. 使分离;使分开

  • He disconnected the IV bottle from the overhead hook. [VERB noun + from]

    他把静脉滴注瓶从头顶的钩子上拿下来。

SYN detach, separate, undo, part
