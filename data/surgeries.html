surgery
sɜːʳdʒəri ˈsɜrdʒəri
Word forms: plural surgeries
1 N-UNCOUNT 外科手术 Surgery is medical treatment in which someone's body is cut open so that a doctor can repair, remove, or replace a diseased or damaged part. 外科手术

  • His father has just recovered from heart surgery.

    他父亲刚刚从心脏外科手术中康复过来。

  • Mr Clark underwent five hours of emergency surgery.

    克拉克先生经受了 5 个小时的外科急救手术。

SYN operation, treatment  
2See also:cosmetic surgery  keyhole surgery  plastic surgery 
3 N-COUNT 诊疗室;诊所 A surgery is the room or house where a doctor or dentist works. 诊疗室;诊所 [British]

  • Bill was in the doctor's surgery demanding to know what was wrong with him.

    比尔在医生的诊疗室里想要知道自己到底得了什么病。

in AM, use 美国英语用 doctor's office, dentist's office
4 N-COUNT [oft with poss] 应诊时间 A doctor's surgery is the period of time each day when a doctor sees patients at his or her surgery. 应诊时间 [British]

  • His surgery always ends at eleven.

    他的出诊时间总是到11点结束。

in AM, use 美国英语用 office hours
5 N-COUNT [usually singular] (议员对公众的)接待时间 In Britain, when someone such as an MP or a local councillor holds a surgery, they go to an office where members of the public can come and talk to them about problems or issues that concern them. (议员对公众的)接待时间
6 N-COUNT 手术室 A surgery is the room in a hospital where surgeons operate on their patients. 手术室 [US]
in BRIT, use 英国英语用 theatre, operating theatre
