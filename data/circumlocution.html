circumlocution
sɜːʳkəmloʊkjuːʃən ˌsɜrkəmloʊˈkjuʃən
Word forms: plural circumlocutions
1 N-VAR 婉转曲折的表达法;迂回表达 A circumlocution is a way of saying or writing something using more words than are necessary instead of being clear and direct. 婉转曲折的表达法;迂回表达 [formal]

  • It was always when you most wanted a direct answer that Greenfield came up with a circumlocution.

    总是在你最想得到一个直截了当的回答时，格林菲尔德却要绕上十八个弯。

SYN indirectness, redundancy, euphemism, beating about the bush [informal]
