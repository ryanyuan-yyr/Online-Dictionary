institutional
ɪnstɪtjuːʃənəl -tuː- ˌɪnstəˈtuʃənəl ˌɪnstəˈtjuʃənəl
1 ADJ [ADJ n] (大学、银行、教会等)(大型)机构的;具有机构特征的 Institutional means relating to a large organization, for example a university, bank, or church. (大学、银行、教会等)(大型)机构的;具有机构特征的

  • NATO remains the United States' chief institutional anchor in Europe.

    北大西洋公约组织仍然是美国在欧洲首要的支柱机构。

  • The share price will be determined by bidding from institutional investors.

    股票价格将由机构投资商的出价决定。

2 ADJ [ADJ n] 社会收容机构的 Institutional means relating to a building where people are looked after or held. 社会收容机构的

  • Outside the protected environment of institutional care he could not survive.

    离开福利机构的照顾，离开这种受保护的环境,他将难以生存下去。

3 ADJ [ADJ n] 习俗化了的;制度化了的 An institutional value or quality is considered an important and typical feature of a particular society or group, usually because it has existed for a long time. 习俗化了的;制度化了的

  • ...social and institutional values.

    社会与习俗价值观

SYN conventional, accepted, established, formal  
4 ADJ [usually ADJ n] 根深蒂固的 If someone accuses an organization of institutional racism or sexism, they mean that the organization is deeply racist or sexist and has been so for a long time. 根深蒂固的

  • ...the Macpherson report, which accused the Metropolitan Police of institutional racism.

    指控伦敦警察厅存在根深蒂固的种族主义的麦克弗森报告。

institutionally

  • ...the Government's policy still appeared to be institutionally racist.

    政府政策看来仍然存在根深蒂固的种族主义。
