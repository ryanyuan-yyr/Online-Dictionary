indicative
ɪndɪkətɪv ɪnˈdɪkətɪv
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 指示的;象征的;暗示的 If one thing is indicative of another, it suggests what the other thing is likely to be. 指示的;象征的;暗示的 [formal]

  • The result was indicative of a strong retail market. [+ of]

    结果表明零售业市场繁荣。

  • Often physical appearance is indicative of how a person feels.

    一个人的外表常能反映出他的感受。

SYN suggestive, significant, symptomatic, pointing to  
2 N-SING 陈述语气 In grammar, a clause that is in the indicative, or in the indicative mood, has a subject followed by a verb group. Examples are 'I'm hungry' and 'She was followed'. Clauses of this kind are typically used to make statements. 陈述语气
