lovely
lʌvli ˈlʌvli
Word forms: comparative lovelier, superlative loveliest
1 ADJ-GRADED 可爱的；迷人的；悦耳的 If you describe someone or something as lovely, you mean that they are very beautiful and therefore pleasing to look at or listen to. 可爱的；迷人的；悦耳的 [mainly British]

  • You look lovely, Marcia.

    你看上去真漂亮，马西娅。

  • He had a lovely voice.

    他的声音很动听。

  • It was just one of those lovely old English gardens.

    那正是一个那种古老的英式花园，非常迷人。

SYN beautiful, appealing, attractive, charming  
loveliness

  • You are a vision of loveliness.

    你真是漂亮极了。

2 ADJ-GRADED 令人愉快的；美好的 If you describe something as lovely, you mean that it gives you pleasure. 令人愉快的；美好的 [mainly British [mainly, mainly spoken]

  • Mary! How lovely to see you!.

    玛丽，见到你真是太高兴了！

  • It's a lovely day.

    今天天气真好。

  • What a lovely surprise!

    真是个惊喜！

SYN wonderful, pleasing, nice, pleasant  
3 ADJ-GRADED 亲切和善的；心地善良的 If you describe someone as lovely, you mean that they are friendly, kind, or generous. 亲切和善的；心地善良的 [mainly British]

  • Laura is a lovely young woman.

    劳拉是个亲切友好的姑娘。

  • She's a lovely child.

    她是个心地善良的孩子。
