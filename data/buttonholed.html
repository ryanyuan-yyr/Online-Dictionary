buttonhole
bʌtənhoʊl ˈbʌtənˌhoʊl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense buttonholes, present participle buttonholing, past tense , past participle buttonholed
1 N-COUNT 纽孔；扣眼 A buttonhole is a hole that you push a button through in order to fasten a shirt, coat, or other piece of clothing. 纽孔；扣眼
2 N-COUNT 别在衣服上的花；胸花 A buttonhole is a flower that you wear on your coat or dress. 别在衣服上的花；胸花 [British]
3 VERB 强留（某人）停下来听 If you buttonhole someone, you stop them and make them listen to you. 强留（某人）停下来听

  • Several people buttonholed television reporters to explain to them their reasons for not voting. [VERB noun]

    几个人拉住电视台记者非要向他们解释自己不投票的理由。

SYN detain, catch, grab, intercept
