proscribe
proʊskraɪb prəʊˈskraɪb proʊˈskraɪb
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense proscribes, present participle proscribing, past tense , past participle proscribed
VERB [usually passive] 禁止；排斥 If something is proscribed by people in authority, the existence or the use of that thing is forbidden. 禁止；排斥 [formal]

  • In some cultures surgery is proscribed. [be VERB-ed]

    在一些文化里，外科手术是被禁止的。

  • They are proscribed by federal law from owning guns. [be VERB-ed + from]

    根据联邦法律的规定，他们不准拥有枪支。

SYN prohibit, ban, forbid, boycott
