constrict
kənstrɪkt kənˈstrɪkt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense constricts, present participle constricting, past tense , past participle constricted
1 V-ERG (使)(身体部位，尤指喉咙)收紧，收缩 If a part of your body, especially your throat, is constricted or if it constricts, something causes it to become narrower. (使)(身体部位，尤指喉咙)收紧，收缩

  • Severe migraine can be treated with a drug which constricts the blood vessels. [VERB noun]

    严重的偏头疼可以用一种收缩血管的药物进行治疗。

  • My throat constricted, so that I had to concentrate on breathing. [VERB]

    我的嗓子发紧，所以我不得不把注意力集中在呼吸上。

SYN squeeze, contract, narrow, restrict  
constricted

  • His throat began to feel swollen and constricted.

    他开始觉得喉咙肿胀发紧。

constriction

  • The pain is produced by constriction of the blood vessels.

    这种疼痛是由于血管的收缩而引起的。

2 VERB 限制;束缚;约束 If something constricts you, it limits your actions so that you cannot do what you want to do. 限制;束缚;约束

  • She objects to the tests the Government's advisers have devised because they constrict her teaching style. [VERB noun]

    她反对政府顾问设计的考试，因为这些考试限制了她的教学风格。

  • Men and women alike have been constricted by traditional sexual roles. [VERB noun]

    男性和女性同样受到传统性别角色的束缚。

constricted

    我觉得办公室的环境太死板，限制太多。

  • The bill is filled with constricting amendments.

    该法案具有很多限制性修正条款。
