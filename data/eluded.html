elude
ɪluːd iˈlud ɪˈlud
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense eludes, present participle eluding, past tense , past participle eluded
1 VERB [no passive] 错过;未获得 If something that you want eludes you, you fail to obtain it. 错过;未获得

  • Sleep eluded her. [VERB noun]

    她失眠了。

  • At 62, Brian found the celebrity and status that had eluded him for so long. [VERB noun]

    布赖恩在62岁时获得了多年来未能得到的名望和地位。

2 VERB 躲避;避开;逃避 If you elude someone or something, you avoid them or escape from them. 躲避;避开;逃避

  • He eluded the police for 13 years. [VERB noun]

    他躲过了警方抓捕达13年。

3 VERB [no passive] 不为…所明白;不为…所记得 If a fact or idea eludes you, you do not succeed in understanding it, realizing it, or remembering it. 不为…所明白;不为…所记得

  • The appropriate word eluded him. [VERB noun]

    他找不到合适的字眼。

SYN escape, baffle, frustrate, puzzle
