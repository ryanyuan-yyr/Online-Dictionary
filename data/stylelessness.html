style
staɪl staɪl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense styles, present participle styling, past tense , past participle styled
1 N-COUNT [oft in adj N] 行为方式;作风;风格 The style of something is the general way in which it is done or presented, which often shows the attitudes of the people involved. 行为方式;作风;风格

  • Our children's different needs and learning styles created many problems.

    孩子们不同的需求和学习方式给我们带来了许多问题。

  • Belmont Park is a broad sweeping track which will suit the European style of running.

    贝尔蒙特跑马场有宽阔的弧形跑道，很适合举行欧式赛马。

  • Sam celebrated in fine style.

    萨姆精心庆祝了一番。

SYN manner, way, method, approach  
2 N-UNCOUNT [oft in NOUN] 风度;气派;格调 If people or places have style, they are smart and elegant. 风度;气派;格调

  • Bournemouth, you have to admit, has style.

    你不得不承认伯恩茅斯是个很有格调的地方。

  • Both love doing things in style.

    两人行事都讲究风度。

  • She had not lost her grace and style.

    她依然仪态优雅，不失风度。

SYN elegance, taste, chic, flair [informal]  
3 N-VAR 款式 The style of a product is its design. 款式

  • His 50 years of experience have given him strong convictions about style.

    50 年的经验使得他在款式上有自己的独到见解。

  • Several styles of hat were available.

    帽子有几种款式。

SYN design, form, cut  
4 N-COUNT (某群艺术家或某文艺时期的)风格 In the arts, a particular style is characteristic of a particular period or group of people. (某群艺术家或某文艺时期的)风格

  • ...six scenes in the style of a classical Greek tragedy.

    6 个古典希腊悲剧式的场景

  • ...a mixture of musical styles.

    音乐风格的混搭

  • ...the revival of the gothic style.

    哥特式风格的复兴

SYN type, sort, kind, spirit  
5 VERB [usually passive] 设计;使具有…风格 If something such as a piece of clothing, a vehicle, or someone's hair is styled in a particular way, it is designed or shaped in that way. 设计;使具有…风格

  • His thick blond hair had just been styled before his trip. [be VERB-ed]

    他在旅行前刚把浓密的金发修剪了一番。

  • ...classically styled clothes. [VERB-ed]

    式样传统的衣服

SYN design, cut, tailor, fashion  
6 See also: old-style  self-styled  styling 
7 PHRASE 不是…的风格;不符合…的喜好 If you say that something is not someone's style, you mean that it is not the way in which they usually do things, or it is different from the sort of thing they usually like. to cramp someone's style→see: cramp  不是…的风格;不符合…的喜好

  • Mr Blair was not off guard. That is not his style.

    布莱尔先生没有放松警惕，这不是他的风格。

  • To be honest, the house is not quite our style.

    说实话，这房子我们不是非常喜欢。
