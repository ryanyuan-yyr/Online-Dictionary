put down
1 PHRASAL VERB 写下；记录；键入 If you put something down somewhere, you write or type it there. 写下；记录；键入 [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • Never put anything down on paper which might be used in evidence against you at a later date. [V n P + in/on]

    一定不要写下任何书面的东西，以免日后被用作对你不利的证据。

  • We've put down on our staff development plan for this year that we would like some technology courses. [VERB PREP. that]

    我们已经在今年的员工发展计划中写到我们希望有一些技术培训课程。

  • I had prepared for the meeting by putting down what I wanted from them. [VERB PREP. wh]

    我已为会议做了准备，写下了我想从他们那里得到的东西。

SYN record, write down, list, enter  
2 PHRASAL VERB 付（定金） If you put down some money, you pay part of the price of something, and will pay the rest later. 付（定金）

  • He bought an investment property for $100,000 and put down $20,000. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他以10万美元买下一处投资性地产，并支付了2万美元的定金。

  • He's got to put cash down. [VERB noun PREP.]

    他必须先付一部分现金。

3 PHRASAL VERB 镇压（暴乱或叛乱） When soldiers, police, or the government put down a riot or rebellion, they stop it by using force. 镇压（暴乱或叛乱） [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Soldiers went in to put down a rebellion. [VERB PREP. noun]

    军队出动镇压叛乱。

SYN repress, crush, suppress, check  
4 PHRASAL VERB 贬低；奚落；使窘迫 If someone puts you down, they treat you in an unpleasant way by criticizing you in front of other people or making you appear foolish. 贬低；奚落；使窘迫

  • I know that I do put people down occasionally. [VERB noun PREP.]

    我知道我确实偶尔会奚落别人。

  • She learned to stop putting herself down and comparing herself to others. [VERB noun PREP.]

    她学会了不再自我贬低，拿自己与别人相比。

  • Racist jokes come from wanting to put down other kinds of people we feel threatened by. [VERB PREP. noun]

    开种族主义色彩的玩笑是因为我们想羞辱那些对我们构成了威胁的其他种族。

5See also:put-down 
6 PHRASAL VERB 杀死（具危险性或生病的动物） When an animal is put down, it is killed because it is dangerous or very ill. 杀死（具危险性或生病的动物） [mainly British] [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Magistrates ordered his dog Samson to be put down immediately. [be VERB-ed PREP.]

    治安官们勒令立即将他的狗萨姆森杀掉。

  • They think that any legislation that involved putting down dogs was wrong. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他们认为任何涉及捕杀狗的立法都是错误的。

SYN put to sleep, kill, destroy, do away with  
put-down
ˈpʊtdaʊn ˈpʊtˌdaʊn
(also put down)Word forms: plural put-downs
N-COUNT 贬低（或奚落）的语言（或行为） A put-down is something that you say or do to criticize someone or make them appear foolish. 贬低（或奚落）的语言（或行为） [informal]

  • I see the term as a put-down of women.

    我认为这个词是对女性的贬低。

  • She was getting very sick of Mick's put-downs.

    她越来越厌烦米克的奚落。

SYN humiliation, slight, snub, knock [informal]
