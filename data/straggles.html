straggle
strægəl ˈstrægəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense straggles, present participle straggling, past tense , past participle straggled
1 VERB 散乱地移动;零星地走 If people straggle somewhere, they move there slowly, in small groups with large, irregular gaps between them. 散乱地移动;零星地走

  • They came straggling up the cliff road. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他们零零散散地沿着悬崖小路走上来。

  • The other boys straggled away, some to their work in the kitchen, some to the great hall. [VERB prep./adv.]

    其他男孩散开了，有些去厨房干活去了，有些去大厅了。

SYN trail, drift, wander, range  
2 VERB 散乱地分布;散布 If a small quantity of things straggle over an area, they cover it in an uneven or untidy way. 散乱地分布;散布

  • Her grey hair straggled in wisps about her face. [VERB prep.]

    她那灰白的头发一缕缕地披散在脸旁。

  • They were beyond the last straggling suburbs now. [VERB-ing]

    他们现在已经远离了最远的零星分布的郊区了。

SYN hang (down), trail, dangle, sag
