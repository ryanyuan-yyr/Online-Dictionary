race
reɪs reɪs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense races, present participle racing, past tense , past participle raced
1 N-COUNT （速度）比赛；竞赛 A race is a competition to see who is the fastest, for example in running, swimming, or driving. （速度）比赛；竞赛

  • The women's race was won by the American, Patti Sue Plumer.

    女子赛跑的冠军被美国人帕蒂·休·普卢默夺得。

2 VERB 参赛;与…比赛 If you race, you take part in a race. 参赛;与…比赛 [Also V + against]

  • In the 10 years I raced in Europe, 30 drivers were killed. [VERB]

    在我10年的欧洲赛车生涯中，有30名车手丧生。

  • They may even have raced each other — but not regularly. [VERB noun]

    他们甚至可能曾经相互比试过，只不过不是经常性的。

SYN compete against, run against, try to beat, have a race with  
3 N-PLURAL 赛马会 The races are a series of horse races that are held in a particular place on a particular day. People go to watch and to bet on which horse will win. 赛马会

  • The high point of this trip was a day at the races.

    此行的高潮是去看赛马会的那一天。

4 N-COUNT [usually singular] 竞争;角逐 A race is a situation in which people or organizations compete with each other for power or control. 竞争;角逐

  • The race for the White House begins in earnest today.

    入主白宫的角逐今天正式开始。

  • The race is on to build up membership fast.

    举办这次竞赛是为了迅速招募成员。

SYN contest, competition, quest, rivalry  
5See also:arms race  rat race 
6 N-VAR 人种；种族 A race is one of the major groups which human beings can be divided into according to their physical features, such as the colour of their skin. 人种；种族

  • The College welcomes students of all races, faiths, and nationalities.

    学院欢迎所有的学生，无论其种族、宗教信仰和国籍是什么。

  • Discrimination by employers on the grounds of race and nationality was illegal.

    雇主以种族或国籍为由歧视员工是非法的。

SYN people, ethnic group, nation, blood  
7See also:human race  race relations 
8 VERB 赶奔；疾走 If you race somewhere, you go there as quickly as possible. 赶奔；疾走

  • He raced across town to the State House building. [VERB adv./prep.]

    他快速穿过市区赶往州议会大厦。

  • The hares raced away out of sight. [VERB adv./prep.]

    野兔飞奔而去，很快就看不见了。

SYN run, fly, career, speed  
9 VERB （向某种状态或位置）急速发展，快速移动 If something races towards a particular state or position, it moves very fast towards that state or position. （向某种状态或位置）急速发展，快速移动

  • Do they realize we are racing towards complete economic collapse?. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他们意识到我们的经济马上要彻底崩溃了吗？

  • American economic growth raced ahead. [VERB prep./adv.]

    美国的经济飞速增长。

10 VERB 使（动物或车辆）参赛 If you race a vehicle or animal, you prepare it for races and make it take part in races. 使（动物或车辆）参赛

  • He still raced sports cars as often as he could. [VERB noun]

    他仍然尽可能经常地参加赛车比赛。

11 VERB （尤指在面临困难或危险时大脑）飞速运转 If your mind races, or if thoughts race through your mind, you think very fast about something, especially when you are in a difficult or dangerous situation. （尤指在面临困难或危险时大脑）飞速运转

  • I made sure I sounded calm but my mind was racing. [VERB]

    我确保自己听上去很镇定，但脑筋却飞快地转着。

  • Already her mind was racing ahead to the hundred and one things she had to do. [VERB adv./prep.]

    她的脑子已经急速地转动起来，思考自己必须做的那一大堆事。

  • Bits and pieces of the past raced through her mind. [VERB adv./prep.]

    往昔的点点滴滴涌上她的心头。

12 VERB （心脏因兴奋或害怕）快速搏动，剧烈跳动 If your heart races, it beats very quickly because you are excited or afraid. （心脏因兴奋或害怕）快速搏动，剧烈跳动

  • Her heart raced uncontrollably. [VERB]

    她控制不住地心跳加速。

SYN pump, pound, hammer, thump  
13 See also: racing 
14 PHRASE 与时间赛跑;争分夺秒 You describe a situation as a race against time when you have to work very fast in order to do something before a particular time, or before another thing happens. 与时间赛跑;争分夺秒

  • An air force spokesman said the rescue operation was a race against time.

    一名空军发言人说这次营救行动是在和时间赛跑。
