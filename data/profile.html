profile
proʊfaɪl ˈproʊˌfaɪl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense profiles, present participle profiling, past tense , past participle profiled
1 N-COUNT 侧面像；侧影像 Your profile is the outline of your face as it is seen when someone is looking at you from the side. 侧面像；侧影像

  • His handsome profile was turned away from us.

    他英俊的侧影从我们眼前转开了。

SYN outline, lines, form, figure  
2 N-UNCOUNT [in NOUN] （从）侧面（看） If you see someone in profile, you see them from the side. （从）侧面（看）

  • This picture shows the girl in profile.

    这张照片照的是女孩的侧面。

3 N-COUNT 人物简介；传略 A profile of someone is a short article or programme in which their life and character are described. 人物简介；传略

  • A newspaper published profiles of the candidates' wives. [+ of]

    一家报纸刊登了几位候选人夫人的简介。

SYN biography, sketch, vignette, characterization  
4 VERB 为…作传略 To profile someone means to give an account of that person's life and character. 为…作传略 [journalism]

  • Tamar Golan, a Paris-based journalist, profiles the rebel leader. [VERB noun]

    驻巴黎记者塔玛·戈兰对反叛者的首领进行了介绍。

SYN describe, outline, write about, portray  
profiling

  • ...a former FBI agent who pioneered psychological profiling in the 1970s.

    在20世纪70年代开创了心理画像的前联邦调查局探员

  • DNA profiling has aided the struggle against crime.

    DNA分析对于打击犯罪有所帮助。

5 PHRASE 高调/低调；高姿态/低姿态 If someone has a high profile, people notice them and what they do. If you keep a low profile, you avoid doing things that will make people notice you. 高调/低调；高姿态/低姿态

  • ...a move that would give Egypt a much higher profile in the upcoming peace talks.

    能使埃及在未来的和谈中更加引人关注的举动

  • Football is a high profile business.

    足球是一个引人注目的产业。

  • The police deliberately kept a low profile in most places.

    警方在大多数地方有意保持低调。
