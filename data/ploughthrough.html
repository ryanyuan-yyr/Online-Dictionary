plough through
1 PHRASAL VERB [no passive] 终于完成；奋力完成 If you plough through something such as a large meal or a long piece of work, you finally finish it although it takes a lot of effort. 终于完成；奋力完成

  • Researchers have ploughed through 16,000 different pieces of classical, rock and jazz music. [VERB PREP. noun]

    研究者好不容易完成了对1.6万首不同的古典、摇滚和爵士乐曲的研究。

  • He used to watch body building videos and plough through a daily diet of a dozen eggs and four pints of milk. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他曾经观看健身录像，并且熬过了每天12个鸡蛋和4品脱牛奶的饮食。

2 PHRASAL VERB [no passive] （尤指费很大力气地）通过，冲破 If someone ploughs through a place or if a ship ploughs through water, they move through it, especially with great effort. （尤指费很大力气地）通过，冲破

  • Mr. Dambar watched her plough through the grass. [VERB PREP. noun]

    丹巴尔先生看着她艰难地涉草而过。

  • The ship ploughed through the waves. [VERB PREP. noun]

    那艘船破浪前进。

SYN forge, cut, drive, press  
3 PHRASAL VERB [no passive] （车辆或导弹）猛力穿过，失控冲过 If a vehicle or a missile ploughs through something, it goes violently or carelessly through it as though it is out of control. （车辆或导弹）猛力穿过，失控冲过

  • The car ploughed through three gardens and flattened a tree. [VERB PREP. noun]

    小轿车轰然穿过3个花园，还碾倒了一棵树。
