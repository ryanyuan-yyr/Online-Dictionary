rearrange
riːəreɪndʒ ˌriːəˈreɪndʒ ˌriəˈreɪndʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense rearranges, present participle rearranging, past tense , past participle rearranged
1 VERB 重整；重新安排；重新排列 If you rearrange things, you change the way in which they are organized or ordered. 重整；重新安排；重新排列

  • When she returned, she found Malcolm had rearranged all her furniture. [VERB noun]

    她回家时，发现马尔科姆把她所有的家具都重新摆放了。

  • A waiter was rapidly rearranging tables for the big group. [VERB noun]

    一个服务生正在麻利地为这一大帮人重新布置餐桌。

SYN reorganize, regroup, move round, reposition  
2 VERB 重新安排（会议或约会） If you rearrange a meeting or an appointment, you arrange for it to take place at a different time to that originally intended. 重新安排（会议或约会）

  • You may cancel or rearrange the appointment. [VERB noun]

    你可以取消约会或是重新安排约会时间。

  • The meeting on 31 October is rearranged for 30 January. [VERB noun]

    10月31日的会议重新安排到了1月30日。

SYN reschedule, alter, adjust, reshuffle
