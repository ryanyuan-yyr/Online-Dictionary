sledge
sledʒ slɛdʒ slɛd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense sledges, present participle sledging, past tense , past participle sledged
1 N-COUNT [oft by NOUN] 雪橇 A sledge is an object used for travelling over snow. It consists of a framework which slides on two strips of wood or metal. 雪橇 [British]

  • She travelled 14,000 miles by sledge across Siberia to Kamchatka.

    她乘雪橇穿越西伯利亚到达了堪察加半岛，行程共 1.4 万英里。

SYN bobsleigh, sled [US , Canadian] , sleigh, toboggan  
2 VERB 滑雪橇 If you sledge or go sledging, you ride on a sledge. 滑雪橇 [British] [Also VERB preposition]

  • Our hill is marvellous for sledging and we always have snow in January. [VERB-ing]

    在我们的那座小山上滑雪橇最棒了，而且1月我们那里总会下雪。

SYN bobsleigh, sled [US] , sleigh, toboggan
