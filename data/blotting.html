blot
blɒt blɑt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense blots, present participle blotting, past tense , past participle blotted
1 N-COUNT （名誉上的）污点，耻辱 If something is a blot on a person's or thing's reputation, it spoils their reputation. （名誉上的）污点，耻辱

  • ...a blot on the reputation of the architectural profession. [+ on]

    败坏建筑业名声的污点

  • This drugs scandal is another blot on the Olympics. [+ on]

    这次的兴奋剂丑闻又给奥林匹克运动会抹了黑。

SYN disgrace, spot, fault, stain  
2 N-COUNT 污渍；污痕；污点 A blot is a drop of liquid that has fallen on to a surface and has dried. 污渍；污痕；污点

  • ...an ink blot.

    墨迹

SYN spot, mark, patch, smear  
3 VERB （用软纸或布）吸干，擦干 If you blot a surface, you remove liquid from it by pressing a piece of soft paper or cloth onto it. （用软纸或布）吸干，擦干 [Also V n adj]

  • Before applying make-up, blot the face with a tissue to remove any excess oils. [VERB noun]

    上妆前，先用面巾纸把脸上多余的油吸干。

SYN soak up, take up, absorb, draw up  
4 PHRASE 破坏整体美的事物；煞风景的事物 If you describe something such as a building as a blot on the landscape, you mean that you think it is very ugly and spoils an otherwise attractive place. 破坏整体美的事物；煞风景的事物

  • The developers insist the 80m village will not leave a blot on the landscape.

    开发商们坚称这个耗资8,000万的居住区不会破坏风景。
