joker
dʒoʊkəʳ ˈdʒoʊkər
Word forms: plural jokers
1 N-COUNT 爱开玩笑的人；逗趣的人 Someone who is a joker likes making jokes or doing amusing things. 爱开玩笑的人；逗趣的人

  • He is, by nature, a joker, a witty man with a sense of fun.

    他天性是个爱开玩笑的人，说话诙谐，很有幽默感。

SYN comedian, comic, wit, clown  
2 N-COUNT （纸牌中的）百搭牌，王牌 The joker in a pack of playing cards is the card which does not belong to any of the four suits. （纸牌中的）百搭牌，王牌
3 N-COUNT 愚蠢的人；举止危险的人；小丑 You can call someone a joker if you think they are behaving in a stupid or dangerous way. 愚蠢的人；举止危险的人；小丑 [informal, disapproval]

  • Keep your eye on these jokers, you never know what they will come up with.

    留神那些活宝，你永远也不知道他们会想出什么样的主意。

4 PHRASE 与众不同的人（或事物）；难以捉摸的人（或事物） If you describe someone or something as the joker in the pack, you mean that they are different from the other people or things in their group, and can be unpredictable. 与众不同的人（或事物）；难以捉摸的人（或事物）
