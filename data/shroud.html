shroud
ʃraʊd ʃraʊd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense shrouds, present participle shrouding, past tense , past participle shrouded
1 N-COUNT 裹尸布;寿衣 A shroud is a cloth which is used for wrapping a dead body. 裹尸布;寿衣
2 N-COUNT 遮蔽物;覆盖物 You can refer to something that surrounds an object or situation as a shroud of something. 遮蔽物;覆盖物

  • ...a parked car huddled under a shroud of grey snow. [+ of]

    车身上覆着一层灰白色积雪的停放着的汽车

  • Ministers are as keen as ever to wrap their activities in a shroud of secrecy. [+ of]

    部长们都一如既往地迫切想要将他们的行动搞得神神秘秘的。

SYN covering, veil, mantle, screen  
3 VERB 隐瞒;保密;掩饰;掩盖 If something has been shrouded in mystery or secrecy, very little information about it has been made available. 隐瞒;保密;掩饰;掩盖

  • For years the teaching of acting has been shrouded in mystery. [be VERB-ed + in]

    多年以来表演教学一直蒙着神秘的面纱。

  • ...the secrecy which has shrouded the whole affair. [VERB noun]

    笼罩着整件事的诡秘气氛

shrouded

  • His demise is as shrouded in mystery as ever.

    他的死亡一直笼罩着神秘色彩。

4 VERB (黑暗、烟雾等)遮蔽，笼罩 If darkness, fog, or smoke shrouds an area, it covers it so that it is difficult to see. (黑暗、烟雾等)遮蔽，笼罩

  • Mist shrouded the outline of Buckingham Palace. [VERB noun]

    雾霭蒙蒙，白金汉宫若隐若现。

SYN conceal, cover, screen, hide  
shrouded

  • The area is shrouded in smoke.

    整个地区笼罩在烟雾之中。
