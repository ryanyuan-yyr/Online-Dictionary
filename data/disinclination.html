disinclination
dɪsɪnklɪneɪʃən ˌdɪsˌɪnkləˈneɪʃən
N-SING [usually NOUN to-infinitive] 不愿意;不喜欢;厌恶 A disinclination to do something is a feeling that you do not want to do it. 不愿意;不喜欢;厌恶 [formal]

  • They are showing a marked disinclination to pursue these opportunities.

    他们对争取这些机会明显表现出不乐意。

SYN reluctance, aversion, unwillingness, opposition
