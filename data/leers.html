leer
lɪəʳ lɪr
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense leers, present participle leering, past tense , past participle leered
VERB 不怀好意地笑；猥亵地笑；淫笑 If someone leers at you, they smile in an unpleasant way, usually because they are sexually interested in you. 不怀好意地笑；猥亵地笑；淫笑 [disapproval]

  • Men were standing around, swilling beer and occasionally leering at passing females. [VERB prep./adv.]

    男人们吊儿郎当地站着，大口大口地喝着啤酒，偶尔还对着路过的女性猥亵地笑。

  • He looked back at Kenworthy and leered. [VERB]

    他回头看了看肯沃西，色迷迷地笑了。

SYN grin, eye, stare, wink  
Leer is also a noun.

  • When I asked the clerk for my room key, he gave it to me with a leer.

    我向接待员要房间钥匙的时候，他边给边露出一个不怀好意的笑。
