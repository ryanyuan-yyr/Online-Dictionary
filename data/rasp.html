rasp
rɑːsp ræsp ræsp rɑsp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense rasps, present participle rasping, past tense , past participle rasped
1 VERB 发出难听（或刺耳）的声音;尖声叫嚷 If someone rasps, their voice or breathing is harsh and unpleasant to listen to. 发出难听（或刺耳）的声音;尖声叫嚷

  • 'Where've you put it?' he rasped. [VERB with quote]

    “你把它放在哪里了？”他尖声叫道。

  • He fell back into the water, his breath rasping in his heaving chest. [VERB]

    他仰面跌进了水中，胸部起伏，呼吸急促。

  • In a rasping voice, she told of her long and agonising battle. [VERB-ing]

    她用粗嘎的声音讲述了自己长期而痛苦的抗争。

SYN say hoarsely, croak, squawk  
Rasp is also a noun.

  • He was still laughing when he heard the rasp of Rennie's voice.

    当他听到伦尼尖叫的声音时仍然在笑。

2 V-ERG （物体摩擦时）发出刺耳的声音 If something rasps or if you rasp it, it makes a harsh, unpleasant sound as it rubs against something hard or rough. （物体摩擦时）发出刺耳的声音

  • Sabres rasped from scabbards and the horsemen spurred forward. [VERB prep.]

    马刀在刀鞘里咔嗒作响，骑手催马向前。

  • Foden rasped a hand across his chin. [VERB noun prep.]

    福登用手蹭得下巴刺刺响。

  • He pulled the cloth and it came away with a rasping sound. [VERB-ing]

    他用力一扯，那块布被刺啦一下扯了下来。

Rasp is also a noun.

  • ...the rasp of something being drawn across the sand.

    在沙地上拖物体发出的摩擦声

3 N-COUNT 锉子；锉刀 A rasp is a long metal tool with rough surfaces, used to rub solid objects and give them smooth surfaces. 锉子；锉刀
