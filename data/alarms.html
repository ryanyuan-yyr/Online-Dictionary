alarm
əlɑːʳm əˈlɑrm
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense alarms, present participle alarming, past tense , past participle alarmed
1 N-UNCOUNT [oft with/in N] 惊恐；忧虑；担心 Alarm is a feeling of fear or anxiety that something unpleasant or dangerous might happen. 惊恐；忧虑；担心 [Also + over/about]

  • The news was greeted with alarm by MPs.

    得知这个消息，议员们忧心忡忡。

  • She sat up in alarm.

    她惊慌得坐了起来。

  • The moves reflect growing alarm over recent events.

    这些举动表明人们愈加担心最近发生的事件。

SYN fear, horror, panic, anxiety  
2 VERB 使惊恐；使忧虑；使担心 If something alarms you, it makes you afraid or anxious that something unpleasant or dangerous might happen. 使惊恐；使忧虑；使担心

  • We could not see what had alarmed him. [VERB noun]

    我们搞不明白是什么使他忧虑不安。

SYN frighten, shock, scare, panic  
3 N-COUNT 警报器 An alarm is an automatic device that warns you of danger, for example by ringing a bell. 警报器

  • He heard the alarm go off.

    他听见警报响了。

  • ...an extremely sophisticated alarm system.

    极其先进的警报系统

  • The other man rang the alarm bell.

    另一名男子按响了警铃。

SYN danger signal, warning, bell, alert  
4 N-COUNT 同alarm clock An alarm is the same as an alarm clock . 同alarm clock
5 See also: alarming  alarmed  burglar alarm  car alarm  false alarm  fire alarm  smoke alarm 
6 PHRASE 危险信号;警报 If you say that something sets alarm bells ringing, you mean that it makes people feel worried or concerned about something. 危险信号;警报

  • This has set the alarm bells ringing in Moscow.

    这已给莫斯科拉响了警报。

  • Alarm bells are beginning to sound at Westminster.

    威斯敏斯特的警钟已开始敲响。

7 PHRASE 发出警报；敲警钟 If you raise the alarm or sound the alarm, you warn people of danger. 发出警报；敲警钟

  • His family raised the alarm when he had not come home by 9pm.

    等到晚上9点他还未回家，他的家人便报警了。
