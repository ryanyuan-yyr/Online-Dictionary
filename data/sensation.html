sensation
senseɪʃən sɛnˈseɪʃən
Word forms: plural sensations
1 N-COUNT (身体上的)感觉，知觉 A sensation is a physical feeling. (身体上的)感觉，知觉

  • Floating can be a very pleasant sensation.

    漂浮会是一种非常惬意的感觉。

  • A sensation of burning or tingling may be experienced in the hands. [+ of]

    双手可能会有种刺痛感或灼热感。

SYN feeling, sense, impression, perception  
2 N-UNCOUNT (尤指)触觉;感觉能力;知觉 Sensation is your ability to feel things physically, especially through your sense of touch. (尤指)触觉;感觉能力;知觉

  • The pain was so bad that she lost all sensation.

    太疼了，疼得她都失去了知觉。

  • ...nerve damage which can lead to loss of sensation in the limbs.

    会导致四肢麻木的神经损伤

3 N-COUNT [usually adjective NOUN] (某种经历所产生的)整体感觉，总体印象 You can use sensation to refer to the general feeling or impression caused by a particular experience. (某种经历所产生的)整体感觉，总体印象

  • It's a funny sensation to know someone's talking about you in a language you don't understand.

    知道有人正用一种你听不懂的语言谈论你，这种感觉很奇怪。

4 N-COUNT 引起轰动的人(或事件、情况) If a person, event, or situation is a sensation, it causes great excitement or interest. 引起轰动的人(或事件、情况)

  • ...the film that turned her into an overnight sensation.

    使她一夜间名声大噪的影片

SYN hit, success, wow [slang , mainly US] , crowd puller [informal]  
5 N-SING 轰动;哗然 If a person, event, or situation causes a sensation, they cause great interest or excitement. 轰动;哗然

  • She was just 14 when she caused a sensation at the Montreal Olympics.

    她在蒙特利尔奥运会上引起轰动时才 14 岁。

SYN excitement, surprise, thrill, stir
