mistress
mɪstrəs ˈmɪstrɪs
Word forms: plural mistresses
1 N-COUNT [usually with poss] 情妇 A married man's mistress is a woman who is not his wife and with whom he is having a sexual relationship. 情妇 [old-fashioned]

  • She was his mistress for three years.

    她曾做过他3年的情妇。

  • He has a wife and a mistress.

    他有妻子，还有一个情妇。

SYN lover, girlfriend, concubine [old-fashioned] , kept woman  
2 N-COUNT [usually n NOUN] 女教师 A mistress is a female teacher. 女教师 [British, old-fashioned]

  • My history mistress was extremely helpful.

    教我历史的那位女老师特别乐于助人。

3 N-COUNT [usually with poss] 女主人 A servant's mistress is the woman that he or she works for. 女主人 [old-fashioned]

  • It must be really bad, for her to ignore a summons from her mistress!

    她居然不听女主人的使唤，实在是太不像话了！

4 N-COUNT [usually poss NOUN] (狗的)女主人 A dog's mistress is the woman or girl who owns it. (狗的)女主人

  • The huge wolfhound danced in circles around his mistress.

    这条大狼犬围着女主人蹦蹦跳跳地绕圈子。

5 N-UNCOUNT [also NOUN in plural, usually NOUN of n] 绝对控制…的女人；主宰…的女人 If a woman is mistress of a situation, she has complete control over it. 绝对控制…的女人；主宰…的女人

  • She had always been mistress of her own destiny.

    她总是能够掌握自己的命运。

6 N-COUNT [NOUN of n] (精通某项活动的)女能手，女行家 If you say that a woman is a mistress of a particular activity, you mean that she is very skilled at it. (精通某项活动的)女能手，女行家

  • She is a mistress of disguise.

    她是伪装的高手。

  • ...another winner from the mistress of historical romance.

    来自这位历史爱情小说知名女作家又一部必定大受欢迎的作品
