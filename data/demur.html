demur
dɪmɜːʳ diˈmɜr dɪˈmɜr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense demurs, present participle demurring, past tense , past participle demurred
1 VERB 表示反对;提出异议 If you demur, you say that you do not agree with something or will not do something that you have been asked to do. 表示反对;提出异议 [formal]

  • Hunt wanted to know, would I be prepared to take over the whole operation and supervise it? At first I demurred. [VERB]

    亨特想要知道我是否有准备接手整个行动并对其进行监督。刚开始我表示反对。

  • The doctor demurred, but Piercey was insistent. [VERB]

    医生提出异议，但皮尔西却一再坚持。

SYN object, refuse, protest, doubt  
2 PHRASE 毫无异议地;毫不犹豫地 If you do something without demur, you do it immediately and without making any protest. 毫无异议地;毫不犹豫地 [formal]

  • When Scobie opened the door and stood aside for her to enter, she did so without demur.

    当斯科比打开门并站到一边让她进去时，她毫不犹豫地这样做了。
