crop
krɒp krɑp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense crops, present participle cropping, past tense , past participle cropped
1 N-COUNT 庄稼;作物 Crops are plants such as wheat and potatoes that are grown in large quantities for food. 庄稼;作物

  • Rice farmers here still plant and harvest their crops by hand.

    这里种植水稻的农场主仍是人工种植和收割庄稼。

  • The main crop is wheat and this is grown even on the very steep slopes.

    主要作物是小麦，就连很陡的坡地都种上了小麦。

2See also:cash crop 
3 N-COUNT (谷物或水果的)收成，产量 The plants or fruits that are collected at harvest time are referred to as a crop . (谷物或水果的)收成，产量

  • Each year it produces a fine crop of fruit. [+ of]

    这里每年水果都大丰收。

  • The US government says that this year's corn crop should be about 8 percent more than last year.

    美国政府称今年的玉米产量应该会比去年提高8%左右。

  • In the Middle Ages, years of crop failure were always followed by terrible disease.

    中世纪时，庄稼连年歉收后总会出现严重的疫情。

4 N-SING (在一起的)一群（人）;(同时发生的)一些（事情） You can refer to a group of people or things that have appeared together as a crop of people or things. (在一起的)一群（人）;(同时发生的)一些（事情） [informal]

  • The present crop of books and documentaries about Marilyn Monroe exploit the thirtieth anniversary of her death. [+ of]

    时下出现的这些有关玛丽莲·梦露的书和纪录片是借她逝世30周年之际推出的。

  • Next year's crop of undergraduates opting to take physics represents just 2 per cent of all undergraduates.

    明年选修物理的大学生仅占大学生总数的2%。

SYN batch, set, lot, pack  
5 VERB 结果实;有收成 When a plant crops, it produces fruits or parts which people want. 结果实;有收成

  • Although these vegetables adapt well to our temperate climate, they tend to crop poorly. [VERB]

    虽然这些蔬菜作物很适应我们这里温和的气候，它们的收成却很差。

6 VERB 收割;收获 When you crop something that you have planted, you collect the fruits or parts that you want from it. 收割;收获

  • I started cropping my beans in July. [VERB noun]

    我7月份开始收割豆类作物。

SYN harvest, pick, collect, gather  
7 VERB (牛、马等)啃吃(树叶或植物) When an animal such as a cow or horse crops leaves or plants, it eats them. (牛、马等)啃吃(树叶或植物)

  • I let the horse drop his head to crop the spring grass. [VERB noun]

    我让马低下头啃吃春天的青草。

SYN graze, eat, browse, feed on  
8 VERB 剪短(头发) To crop someone's hair means to cut it short. 剪短(头发)

  • She cropped her hair and dyed it blonde. [VERB noun]

    她把头发剪短并染成了金黄色。

SYN cut, reduce, trim, clip  
cropped

  • She had cropped grey hair.

    她有一头剪短了的灰白头发。

9 N-COUNT [usually singular] 短发 A crop is a short hairstyle. 短发

  • She had her long hair cut into a boyish crop.

    她把长发剪成了男孩子般的短发。

10 VERB 剪裁，裁切(照片) If you crop a photograph, you cut part of it off, in order to get rid of part of the picture or to be able to frame it. 剪裁，裁切(照片) [Also VERB n from n]

  • I decided to crop the picture just above the water line. [VERB noun]

    我决定把这张照片水印以下的部分裁掉。

  • Her husband was cropped from the photograph. [be VERB-ed + from]

    她丈夫被从照片上剪下去了。
