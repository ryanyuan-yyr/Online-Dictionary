treasure
treʒəʳ ˈtrɛʒər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense treasures, present participle treasuring, past tense , past participle treasured
1 N-UNCOUNT 金银财宝 Treasure is a collection of valuable old objects such as gold coins and jewels that has been hidden or lost. 金银财宝 [literary]

  • It was here, the buried treasure, she knew it was.

    就在这里，那些埋藏在地下的财宝，她知道就在这里。

SYN riches, money, gold, fortune  
2 N-COUNT [usually plural] 珍品，珍宝(尤指艺术品和文物) Treasures are valuable objects, especially works of art and items of historical value. 珍品，珍宝(尤指艺术品和文物)

  • The house was large and full of art treasures.

    房子很大，里面摆满了艺术珍品。

SYN objet d'art, masterpiece, work of art, valuable object  
3 VERB 珍藏;珍视;珍爱 If you treasure something that you have, you keep it or care for it carefully because it gives you great pleasure and you think it is very special. 珍藏;珍视;珍爱

  • She treasures her memories of those joyous days. [VERB noun]

    她珍视那段快乐时光的记忆。

SYN prize, value, worship, esteem  
Treasure is also a noun.

  • His greatest treasure is his collection of rock records.

    他最珍爱的宝贝是他收藏的摇滚唱片。

treasured

  • These books are still among my most treasured possessions.

    这些书仍然是我最珍爱的宝贝之一。

4 N-COUNT 得力助手;好帮手 If you say that someone is a treasure, you mean that they are very helpful and useful to you. 得力助手;好帮手 [informal]

  • Charlie? Oh, he's a treasure, loves children.

    查理？噢，他是个不可多得的好帮手，非常喜欢孩子。

SYN angel, darling, find, star [informal]
