throw around
PHRASAL VERB 把…挂在嘴边;念叨 If you say that someone throws around a word or name, you disapprove of the fact that they mention it frequently, often in an inappropriate way in order to impress someone. 把…挂在嘴边;念叨 [informal, disapproval] [Also VERB PREPOSITION n (not pron)]

  • Occasionally, he throws fancy words around. [VERB noun PREP.]

    他有时会把华丽的词藻挂在嘴边。

  • The name that I've heard thrown around a lot is Jim Morrison. [VERB-ed PREP.]

    我听到大家时常提起的一个人名是吉姆·莫里森。
