torpedo
tɔːʳpiːdoʊ tɔrˈpidoʊ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense torpedoes, present participle torpedoing, past tense , past participle torpedoed
1 N-COUNT 鱼雷；水雷 A torpedo is bomb that is shaped like a tube and that travels under water. 鱼雷；水雷
2 VERB [usually passive] 用鱼雷袭击(或击中、击沉) If a ship is torpedoed, it is hit, and usually sunk, by a torpedo or torpedoes. 用鱼雷袭击(或击中、击沉)

  • More than a thousand people died when the Lusitania was torpedoed. [be VERB-ed]

    卢西塔尼亚号被鱼雷击沉，导致 1,000 多人死亡。

3 VERB 故意破坏;使彻底完蛋 If someone torpedoes negotiations or plans, they deliberately prevent them from being completed or from being successful. 故意破坏;使彻底完蛋 [informal]

  • These attacks are seen as an effort to torpedo the talks. [VERB noun]

    人们认为这些袭击是为了故意破坏会谈。
