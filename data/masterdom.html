master
mɑːstəʳ mæs- ˈmæstər ˈmɑstər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense masters, present participle mastering, past tense , past participle mastered
1 N-COUNT 主人;雇主;老板 A servant's master is the man that he or she works for. 主人;雇主;老板

  • My master ordered me not to deliver the message except in private.

    我的主人命令我务必私下里传递消息。

  • In 1777 several northern states encouraged white masters to free their slaves for military service.

    1777年，北方的几个州鼓励白人奴隶主解放奴隶，让他们参军。

SYN lord, ruler, commander, chief  
2 N-COUNT [usually poss NOUN] (狗的)拥有者，主人 A dog's master is the man or boy who owns it. (狗的)拥有者，主人

  • The dog yelped excitedly when his master opened a desk drawer and produced his leash.

    主人打开抽屉拿出牵狗用的皮带时，狗兴奋地叫了起来。

SYN owner, keeper  
3 N-COUNT 专家;能手;高手 If you say that someone is a master of a particular activity, you mean that they are extremely skilled at it. 专家;能手;高手

  • She was a master of the English language.

    她是英文高手。

  • He is a master at blocking progress.

    他特别擅长充当拦路虎。

  • They appear masters in the art of making regulations work their way.

    他们似乎十分擅长利用规章制度为自己服务。

  • ...a master craftsman.

    手艺高超的工匠

  • ...a master criminal.

    手段高明的罪犯

SYN expert, maestro, pro [informal] , ace [informal]  
4See also:past master 
5 N-VAR （局势的）控制者，主宰者 If you are master of a situation, you have complete control over it. （局势的）控制者，主宰者

  • Jackson remained calm and always master of his passions. [+ of]

    杰克逊镇定自若，始终克制着情绪。

  • He was under no illusions as to who was master in his house.

    对于谁当家作主，他不抱任何幻想。

6 VERB 掌握;精通 If you master something, you learn how to do it properly or you succeed in understanding it completely. 掌握;精通

  • Duff soon mastered the skills of radio production. [VERB noun]

    达夫很快掌握了广播节目制作的技能。

  • Students are expected to master a second language. [VERB noun]

    学生应该掌握一门第二语言。

SYN learn, understand, pick up, acquire  
7 VERB 控制;掌控 If you master a difficult situation, you succeed in controlling it. 控制;掌控

  • When you have mastered one situation you have to go on to the next. [VERB noun]

    一旦控制住了一个局面，又得接着应付下一个。

  • His genius alone has mastered every crisis. [VERB noun]

    仅凭天分，他就控制了所有的危机。

SYN control, manage, direct, dominate  
8 N-COUNT [usually n NOUN] 男教师 A master is a male teacher. 男教师 [British]

  • Mr Palmer was a retired maths master.

    帕尔默先生是一名退休的数学教师。

9See also:headmaster  housemaster 
10 N-COUNT (已故)著名画家，艺术大师 A famous male painter of the past is often called a master . (已故)著名画家，艺术大师

  • ...a portrait by the Dutch master, Vincent Van Gogh.

    一幅由荷兰著名画家文森特·凡·高所作的画像

11See also:old master 
12 ADJ [ADJ n] （电影、录音等）原始拷贝的，母盘的，母带的 A master copy of something such as a film or a tape recording is an original copy that can be used to produce other copies. （电影、录音等）原始拷贝的，母盘的，母带的

  • Keep one as a master copy for your own reference and circulate the others.

    一盘留作母带自用，其余的都分发出去。

SYN original, primary, archetypal, prototypical  
13 N-COUNT [usually with poss] (商船的)船长 The master of a ship that carries passengers or goods is its captain. (商船的)船长

  • ...the Royal Pacific's master.

    “皇家太平洋”号的船长

SYN captain, commander, skipper  
14 N-SING 硕士学位 A master's degree can be referred to as a master's . 硕士学位

  • I've a master's in economics. [+ in]

    我获得了经济学硕士学位。

15 N-COUNT [oft the N; N-VOC] 长老(信徒对宗教法师或领袖的称呼) Master is sometimes used by the followers of a male religious teacher or leader as a way of referring to him or addressing him. 长老(信徒对宗教法师或领袖的称呼)

  • She believed that she had been selected by the Master to reveal forgotten wisdom.

    她认为自己是被长老选中来揭示被人们遗忘的智慧的。

16 N-TITLE (旧时的)少爷，小主人;君(现在用于信件中对男孩的称谓) In the past, Master was used before a boy's name as a polite way of referring to him or addressing him. Nowadays, Master can be written before a boy's name when addressing a letter to him. (旧时的)少爷，小主人;君(现在用于信件中对男孩的称谓) [old-fashioned]

  • Nice to see you, Master Simon.

    很高兴见到您，西蒙少爷。

17 PHRASE [VERB and NOUN inflect] 自己作主;独立自主 If you say that you are your own master, you mean that your decisions are not controlled by other people and you are free to do what you want. 自己作主;独立自主

  • It was no place for a man who liked to be his own master.

    这绝非喜爱独立自主的人能呆下去的地方。
