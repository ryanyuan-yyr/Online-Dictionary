phoenix
fiːnɪks ˈfinɪks
Word forms: plural phoenixes
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 凤凰；不死鸟 A phoenix is an imaginary bird which, according to ancient stories, burns itself to ashes every five hundred years and is then born again. 凤凰；不死鸟
2 N-SING 起死回生的人（或物） If you describe someone or something as a phoenix, you mean that they return again after seeming to disappear or be destroyed. 起死回生的人（或物） [literary]

  • Out of the ashes of the economic shambles, a phoenix of recovery can arise.

    在经济废墟中可能孕育着经济复苏的苗头。
