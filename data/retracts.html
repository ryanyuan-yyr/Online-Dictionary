retract
rɪtrækt rɪˈtrækt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense retracts, present participle retracting, past tense , past participle retracted
1 VERB 收回，撤回(所说的话或写的东西) If you retract something that you have said or written, you say that you did not mean it. 收回，撤回(所说的话或写的东西) [formal]

  • Mr Smith hurriedly sought to retract the statement, but it had just been broadcast on national radio. [VERB noun]

    史密斯先生想赶快收回这项声明，但它刚刚已经在国家电台播出了。

  • He's hoping that if he makes me feel guilty, I'll retract. [VERB]

    他指望着如果他能让我感到内疚，我就会收回所说的话。

SYN withdraw, take back, revoke, disown  
retraction

  • Miss Pearce said she expected an unqualified retraction of his comments within twenty four hours.

    皮尔斯小姐说她希望他在24小时内无条件收回他的评论。

2 V-ERG （使）缩回;（使）缩入;（使）收起 When a part of a machine or a part of a person's body retracts or is retracted, it moves inwards or becomes shorter. （使）缩回;（使）缩入;（使）收起 [formal] [Also VERB n]

  • Torn muscles retract, and lose strength, structure, and tightness. [VERB]

    撕裂的肌肉会收缩，丧失原来的力量、结构和紧实度。

  • ...when the aircraft's wheels were retracted. [be VERB-ed]

    当飞机的轮子收起来的时候
