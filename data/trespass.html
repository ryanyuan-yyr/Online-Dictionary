trespass
trespəs ˈtrɛspəs ˈtrɛsˌpæs
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense trespasses, present participle trespassing, past tense , past participle trespassed
1 VERB 擅自进入，非法侵入(他人土地) If someone trespasses, they go onto someone else's land without their permission. 擅自进入，非法侵入(他人土地)

  • They were trespassing on private property. [VERB prep.]

    他们擅自闯入私人房屋。

  • You're trespassing! [VERB]

    你这是非法侵入！

SYN intrude, infringe, encroach, enter without permission  
Trespass is the act of trespassing.擅自进入(或非法侵入)行为

  • You could be prosecuted for trespass.

    你可能会因为擅自闯入而受到起诉。

  • …trespasses and demonstrations on privately-owned land.

    擅闯私人领地，进行示威

trespasser

  • Trespassers will be prosecuted.

    非法侵入者将会受到起诉。

2 VERB 干涉;妨碍；插手 If you say that someone is trespassing on something, you mean that they are involving themselves in something that is not their concern. 干涉;妨碍；插手 [Also VERB]

  • They were acting to prevent the state from trespassing on family matters such as sex education. [VERB prep.]

    他们正采取行动以阻止国家干涉诸如性教育之类的家庭问题。
