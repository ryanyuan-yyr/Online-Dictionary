sniff out
1 PHRASAL VERB 发现;找到 If you sniff out something, you discover it after some searching. 发现;找到 [informal] [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • ...journalists who are trained to sniff out sensation or scandal. [VERB PREP. noun]

    训练有素、能够敏锐地嗅出爆炸性新闻或丑闻的记者

  • ...those who like sniffing out bargains. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    喜欢淘便宜货的人

SYN find, discover, spot, recognize  
2 PHRASAL VERB (狗)嗅找(炸药、毒品等) When a dog used by a group such as the police sniffs out hidden explosives or drugs, it finds them using its sense of smell. (狗)嗅找(炸药、毒品等) [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • A police dog, trained to sniff out explosives, found evidence of a bomb in the apartment. [VERB PREP. noun]

    一只训练有素、专门嗅找炸药的警犬在公寓里发现了炸弹的痕迹。

SYN discover, find, reveal, spot
