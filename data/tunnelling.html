tunnel
tʌnəl ˈtʌnəl ˈtʌnəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense tunnels, present participle tunnelling, past tense , past participle tunnelled
in AM, use 美国英语用 tunneling, tunneled
1 N-COUNT 隧道;地道 A tunnel is a long passage which has been made under the ground, usually through a hill or under the sea. 隧道;地道

  • ...two new railway tunnels through the Alps. [+ through]

    穿过阿尔卑斯山的两条新的铁路隧道

  • …the motorway tunnels under the Hudson river.

    哈得孙河下的高速公路隧道

SYN passage, underpass, passageway, subway  
2 VERB 挖隧道;挖地道 To tunnel somewhere means to make a tunnel there. 挖隧道;挖地道 [Also VERB]

  • The rebels tunnelled out of a maximum security jail. [VERB prep./adv.]

    叛乱者挖地道逃离了戒备最为森严的监狱。

  • The caterpillars tunnel into the fruit to grow and mature. [VERB prep./adv.]

    毛虫钻入果实，并在其中生长为成虫。

SYN dig, dig your way, burrow, mine  
3 See also: wind tunnel  light at the end of the tunnel→see: light
