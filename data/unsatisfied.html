unsatisfied
ʌnsætɪsfaɪd ʌnˈsætɪsˌfaɪd
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 失望的；不满意的 If you are unsatisfied with something, you are disappointed because you have not got what you hoped to get. 失望的；不满意的

  • The game ended a few hours too early, leaving players and spectators unsatisfied.

    那场比赛提早几个小时结束了，球员和观众都很失望。

  • The centre helps people who are unsatisfied with the solicitors they are given. [+ with]

    该中心为那些对给他们安排的律师不满意的人提供帮助。

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 未被满足的 If a need or demand is unsatisfied, it is not dealt with. 未被满足的

  • The poll suggests that the strongest unsatisfied appetite for home computers isn't among the richest consumers.

    民意调查显示，家用电脑的最大需求者不是那些最有钱的人。
