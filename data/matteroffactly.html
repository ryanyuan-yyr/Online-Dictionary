matter-of-factlyˌmætərəvˈfæktlɪ
ADJ-GRADED 不动感情的;平静的 If you describe a person as matter-of-fact, you mean that they show no emotions such as enthusiasm, anger, or surprise, especially in a situation where you would expect them to be emotional. 不动感情的;平静的

  • John was doing his best to give Francis the news in a matter-of-fact way.

    约翰尽量不露声色地把消息告诉弗朗西斯。

  • He sounded matter-of-fact and unemotional.

    他语气平静，毫不动情。
