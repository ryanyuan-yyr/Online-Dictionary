bold
boʊld boʊld
Word forms: comparative bolder, superlative boldest
1 ADJ-GRADED 大胆的；勇敢的；无畏的 Someone who is bold is not afraid to do things which involve risk or danger. 大胆的；勇敢的；无畏的

  • Amrita becomes a bold, daring rebel.

    阿姆里塔成了一个勇敢无畏的叛逆者。

  • In 1960 this was a bold move.

    在1960年，这是一个大胆的举动。

  • Poland was already making bold economic reforms.

    波兰已在大刀阔斧地进行经济改革。

SYN fearless, enterprising, brave, daring  
boldly

  • You can and must act boldly and confidently.

    你能够而且必须表现得勇敢和自信。

boldness

  • Don't forget the boldness of his economic programme.

    不要忘记他的经济方案非常大胆。

2 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] （在众人面前）大胆的，放肆的，莽撞的 Someone who is bold is not shy or embarrassed in the company of other people. （在众人面前）大胆的，放肆的，莽撞的

  • I don't feel I'm being bold, because it's always been natural for me to just speak out about whatever disturbs me.

    我不觉得我有什么唐突，因为我素来心直口快，有什么烦心的都会说出来。

boldly

  • 'You should do it,' the girl said, boldly.

    “你应该做这件事，”女孩直言道。

3 ADJ-GRADED （色彩或图案）明亮的，醒目的 A bold colour or pattern is very bright and noticeable. （色彩或图案）明亮的，醒目的

  • ...bold flowers in various shades of red, blue or white.

    色彩鲜艳、浓淡不一的红、蓝、白色花朵

  • ...bold, dramatic colours.

    醒目生动的色彩

SYN bright, conspicuous, strong, striking  
boldly

  • The design is pretty startling and very boldly coloured.

    该设计颇出人意表，用色十分大胆。

4 ADJ-GRADED (线条）粗重的；（图案）清晰的 Bold lines or designs are drawn in a clear, strong way. (线条）粗重的；（图案）清晰的

  • Each picture is shown in colour on one page and as a bold outline on the opposite page.

    每幅图片的彩样都单独印在一页上，而在该页的背面会映出清晰的轮廓。

5 N-UNCOUNT [usually NOUN n] （印刷字体）粗体字，黑体字 Bold is print which is thicker and looks blacker than ordinary printed letters. （印刷字体）粗体字，黑体字 [technical]
