besiege
bɪsiːdʒ biˈsidʒ bɪˈsidʒ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense besieges, present participle besieging, past tense , past participle besieged
1 VERB [usually passive] 烦扰；纠缠 If you are besieged by people, many people want something from you and continually bother you. 烦扰；纠缠

  • She was besieged by the press and the public. [be VERB-ed]

    她被媒体和公众纠缠不休。

SYN harass, worry, trouble, harry  
2 VERB 包围；围攻 If soldiers besiege a place, they surround it and wait for the people in it to stop fighting or resisting. 包围；围攻

  • The main part of the army moved to Sevastopol to besiege the town. [VERB noun]

    军队主力转移到了塞瓦斯托波尔，对该城进行围攻。

  • The Afghan air force was using helicopters to supply the besieged town. [VERB-ed]

    阿富汗空军正用直升机向被围城镇提供补给。

SYN surround, confine, enclose, blockade
