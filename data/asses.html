ass
æs æs
Word forms: plural asses
1 N-COUNT 驴 An ass is an animal which is related to a horse but which is smaller and has long ears. 驴
2 N-COUNT 傻瓜；笨蛋 If you describe someone as an ass, you think that they are silly or do silly things. 傻瓜；笨蛋 [informal, disapproval]

  • He was generally disliked and regarded as a pompous ass.

    他常不招人待见，而且被看作是个自负的大傻帽儿。

SYN fool, dope [informal] , jerk [slang , mainly US , Canadian] , idiot  
3 N-COUNT 屁股 Your ass is your bottom. 屁股 [US, informal, rude]
in BRIT, use 英国英语用 arse bum
4 PHRASE [VERB inflects] （通过语言或暴力）泄愤，发泄不满情绪 To kick ass or to kick someone's ass means to show them that you are angry with them, either by telling them or by using physical force. （通过语言或暴力）泄愤，发泄不满情绪 [US, informal, rude]

  • They've really been kicking ass lately — busting places up, harassing everybody.

    他们最近真是让人气不打一处来，闹得天翻地覆，搅得每个人都不得安宁。

  • He damn well better not try it now or he will damn well get his ass kicked.

    他最好不要现在就试，否则铁定会被气个半死。

5 PHRASE （粗鲁地表示愤怒或异议）去你的，去死 Saying that someone can kiss your ass is a very rude way of expressing anger or disagreement. （粗鲁地表示愤怒或异议）去你的，去死 [US, informal, rude, feelings]
6 PHRASE [VERB and NOUN inflect] 出洋相;闹笑话 If you say that someone makes an ass of themselves, you mean they behave in a way that you think is very silly. 出洋相;闹笑话 [informal, disapproval]

  • I find no pleasure in seeing people make asses of themselves.

    我看见人们出洋相并不幸灾乐祸。
