cash in
1 PHRASAL VERB 利用;从中牟利;从…中捞好处 If you say that someone cashes in on a situation, you are criticizing them for using it to gain an advantage, often in an unfair or dishonest way. 利用;从中牟利;从…中捞好处 [disapproval]

  • Residents said local gang leaders had cashed in on the violence to seize valuable land. [VERB PREP. + on]

    居民们说，当地的帮派头子用暴力夺取宝贵的土地。

  • He said that public servants should use government to serve and not to cash in. [VERB PREP.]

    他说，公务员应该运用政府权力为民服务，而不是从中捞取好处。

2 PHRASAL VERB 将(保险单等)兑现 If you cash in something such as an insurance policy, you exchange it for money. 将(保险单等)兑现 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Avoid cashing in a policy early as you could lose out heavily. [VERB PREP. noun]

    不要提早兑现保单，那样你可能会损失惨重。

  • He did not cash in his shares. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他没有把股票兑换成现款。
