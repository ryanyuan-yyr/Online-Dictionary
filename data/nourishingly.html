nourish
nʌrɪʃ nɜːrɪʃ ˈnɜrɪʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense nourishes, present participle nourishing, past tense , past participle nourished
1 VERB 给…提供营养;滋养 To nourish a person, animal, or plant means to provide them with the food that is necessary for life, growth, and good health. 给…提供营养;滋养

  • The food she eats nourishes both her and the baby. [VERB noun]

    她吃的食物给她和婴儿提供了营养。

  • ...microbes in the soil which nourish the plant. [VERB noun]

    土壤中为植物提供营养的微生物

SYN feed, supply, sustain, nurture  
nourishing

  • Most of these nourishing substances are in the yolk of the egg.

    这些营养物质中的大部分在蛋黄里。

  • ...sensible, nourishing food.

    合理的、有营养的食物

2 VERB 滋长，怀有(感情、信念等) To nourish something such as a feeling or belief means to allow or encourage it to grow. 滋长，怀有(感情、信念等)

  • Journalists on the whole don't create public opinion. They can help to nourish it. [VERB noun]

    大体说来记者不制造舆论。他们会助长舆论。

  • ...a current of thought which has been nourished by historical tradition. [VERB noun]

    在历史传统中汲取养分的思潮

SYN encourage, support, maintain, promote
