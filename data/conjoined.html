conjoin
kəndʒɔɪn kənˈdʒɔɪn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense conjoins, present participle conjoining, past tense , past participle conjoined
V-RECIP-ERG (使)结合;(使)联合;(使)连接 If two or more things conjoin or if you conjoin them, they are united and joined together. (使)结合;(使)联合;(使)连接 [formal]

  • The wisdom of the retired generals and backbench MPs conjoins. [VERB]

    退役将军和后座议员的智慧结合在一起。

  • America's rise in rates was conjoined with higher rates elsewhere. [be VERB-ed + with]

    美国利率上涨，其他国家或地区的利率也高了起来。

  • ...if we conjoin the two responses. [VERB noun]

    如果我们把这两种反应结合起来…
