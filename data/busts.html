bust
bʌst bʌst
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense busts, present participle busting, past tense , past participle busted
The form bust is used as the present tense of the verb, and can also be used as the past tense and past participle. bust的过去式和过去分词可与原形相同。
1 VERB 打碎；破坏 If you bust something, you break it or damage it so badly that it cannot be used. 打碎；破坏 [informal]

  • They will have to bust the door to get him out. [VERB noun]

    他们将不得不撞破门把他弄出来。

SYN break, smash, split, crack  
2 VERB [usually passive] 逮捕；拘留 If someone is busted, the police arrest them. 逮捕；拘留 [informal]

  • They were busted for possession of cannabis. [be VERB-ed]

    他们因藏有大麻被逮捕。

SYN arrest, catch, lift [slang] , raid  
3 VERB （警察）搜查 If police bust a place, they go to it in order to arrest people who are doing something illegal. （警察）搜查 [informal]

  • ...police success in busting UK-based drug factories.

    警察对英国的毒品加工厂成功实施的突击搜查

Bust is also a noun.

  • Six tons of cocaine were seized last week in Panama's biggest drug bust.

    上周在巴拿马规模最大的毒品搜查行动中查获了6吨可卡因。

4 ADJ 破产的；倒闭的 A company or fund that is bust has no money left and has been forced to close down. 破产的；倒闭的 [informal, business]

  • It is taxpayers who will pay most of the bill for bailing out bust banks.

    是纳税人将支付大部分的账单以解救面临倒闭的银行。

5 PHRASE 破产；倒闭 If a company goes bust, it loses so much money that it is forced to close down. 破产；倒闭 [informal, business]

  • ...a Swiss company which went bust last May.

    一家去年5月倒闭的瑞士公司

SYN go bankrupt, fail, go under, break  
6 N-COUNT 半身像；胸像 A bust is a statue of the head and shoulders of a person. 半身像；胸像

  • ...a bronze bust of the Queen. [+ of]

    女王的半身铜像

SYN sculpture, carving, head  
7 N-COUNT （女人的）胸部 You can use bust to refer to a woman's breasts, especially when you are describing their size. （女人的）胸部

  • Good posture also helps your bust look bigger.

    优美的姿势也能让你的胸部看起来更丰满。

SYN bosom, breasts, chest, front
