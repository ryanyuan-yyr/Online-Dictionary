convey
kənveɪ kənˈveɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense conveys, present participle conveying, past tense , past participle conveyed
1 VERB 传达;表达;传递 To convey information or feelings means to cause them to be known or understood by someone. 传达;表达;传递

  • When I returned home, I tried to convey the wonder of this machine to my husband. [VERB noun]

    回到家后，我竭力地想把这台机器的精妙之处描述给丈夫听。

  • In every one of her pictures she conveys a sense of immediacy. [VERB noun]

    她拍的每一张照片中都传递出一种临场感，使人有如亲临现场。

  • He also conveyed his views and the views of the bureaucracy. [VERB noun]

    他也表达了自己的看法和官方的看法。

SYN communicate, impart, reveal, relate  
2 VERB 运输;运送;输送 To convey someone or something to a place means to carry or transport them there. 运输;运送;输送 [formal]

  • The railway company extended a branch line to Brightlingsea to convey fish direct to Billingsgate. [VERB noun]

    铁路公司修建了一条通往布赖特灵西的支线，以便直接将鱼运送到比林斯门。
