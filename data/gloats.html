gloat
gloʊt gloʊt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense gloats, present participle gloating, past tense , past participle gloated
VERB 洋洋得意；幸灾乐祸 If someone is gloating, they are showing pleasure at their own success or at other people's failure in an arrogant and unpleasant way. 洋洋得意；幸灾乐祸 [disapproval] [Also VERB]

  • Anti-abortionists are gloating over the court's decision. [VERB + over]

    法庭的判决让反堕胎主义者非常得意。

  • This is nothing to gloat about. [VERB + about]

    这没什么好幸灾乐祸的。

SYN relish, triumph, glory, crow
