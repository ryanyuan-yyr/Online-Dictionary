bright
braɪt braɪt
Word forms: plural brights, comparative brighter, superlative brightest
1 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] （颜色）鲜艳的，明亮的 A bright colour is strong and noticeable, and not dark. （颜色）鲜艳的，明亮的

  • ...a bright red dress.

    鲜红的连衣裙

  • ...the bright uniforms of the guards parading at Buckingham Palace.

    参加白金汉宫阅兵式的卫兵们色彩鲜艳的制服

SYN vivid, rich, brilliant, intense  
brightly

  • ...a display of brightly coloured flowers.

    色彩艳丽的花卉展示

brightness

  • You'll be impressed with the brightness and the beauty of the colors.

    色彩的明艳美丽会让你大饱眼福。

2 ADJ-GRADED （光线）充足的，明亮的 A bright light, object, or place is shining strongly or is full of light. （光线）充足的，明亮的

  • ...a bright October day.

    10月的一天，天气晴朗

  • She leaned forward, her eyes bright with excitement.

    她身体前倾，眼里闪动着兴奋的光芒。

brightly

  • ...a warm, brightly lit room.

    温暖、灯火通明的房间

  • The sun shone brightly.

    阳光灿烂。

brightness

  • An astronomer can determine the brightness of each star.

    天文学家能够测定每颗恒星的亮度。

3 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] 聪明的；伶俐的 If you describe someone as bright, you mean that they are quick at learning things. 聪明的；伶俐的

  • I was convinced that he was brighter than average.

    我确信他比一般人聪明。

SYN intelligent, smart, clever, knowing  
4 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] （主意）聪明的，巧妙的 A bright idea is clever and original. （主意）聪明的，巧妙的

  • There are lots of books crammed with bright ideas.

    许多书里都有很多绝妙的想法。

  • Ford had the bright idea of paying workers enough to buy cars.

    福特公司想出了付给工人足够的薪水去购买汽车这一绝妙的主意。

SYN clever, brilliant, smart, sensible  
5 ADJ-GRADED 快乐的；活泼的；开朗的 If someone looks or sounds bright, they look or sound cheerful and lively. 快乐的；活泼的；开朗的

  • The boy was so bright and animated.

    这个男孩活泼开朗，充满活力。

  • 'May I help you?' said a bright American voice over the telephone.

    “请问您需要什么帮助？”电话里传出一个充满活力的美国人的声音。

SYN cheerful, happy, glad [archaic] , lively  
brightly

  • He smiled brightly as Ben approached.

    本走过来时，他脸上露出灿烂的笑容。

6 ADJ-GRADED （未来）光明的，有希望的 If the future is bright, it is likely to be pleasant or successful. （未来）光明的，有希望的

  • Both had successful careers and the future looked bright.

    两个人都事业有成，前程似锦。

  • There are much brighter prospects for a comprehensive settlement than before.

    综合性的社区具有比以前更加光明的前景。

SYN promising, good, encouraging, excellent  
7 N-COUNT [usually plural, usually the NOUN] （汽车或其他车辆）打开的前灯 The brights on a car or other vehicle are the headlights when they are switched on fully. （汽车或其他车辆）打开的前灯 [mainly US]
8 PHRASE 看光明的一面；抱乐观态度 If you look on the bright side, you try to be cheerful about a bad situation by thinking of some advantages that could result from it, or thinking that it is not as bad as it could have been. 看光明的一面；抱乐观态度
