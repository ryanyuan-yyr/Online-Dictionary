tremble
trembəl ˈtrɛmbəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense trembles, present participle trembling, past tense , past participle trembled
1 VERB 颤抖;战栗;哆嗦 If you tremble, you shake slightly because you are frightened or cold. 颤抖;战栗;哆嗦

  • His mouth became dry, his eyes widened, and he began to tremble all over. [VERB]

    他嘴唇发干，眼睛圆睁，全身开始颤抖起来。

  • Gil was white and trembling with anger. [VERB + with]

    吉尔脸色发白，气得直发抖。

  • With trembling fingers, he removed the camera from his pocket. [VERB-ing]

    他手指哆嗦着从口袋里取出相机。

Tremble is also a noun.

  • I will never forget the look on the patient's face, the tremble in his hand.

    我永远都不会忘记那个病人脸上的表情，还有他颤抖的手。

2 VERB 颤动;摇动;摇晃 If something trembles, it shakes slightly. 颤动;摇动;摇晃 [literary]

  • He felt the earth tremble under him. [VERB]

    他感到大地在脚下颤动。

  • The leaves trembled in the trees. [VERB]

    树上的叶子瑟瑟抖动。

SYN vibrate, rock, shake, quake  
3 VERB (通常因难过或紧张而声音)发颤，颤抖 If your voice trembles, it sounds unsteady and uncertain, usually because you are upset or nervous. (通常因难过或紧张而声音)发颤，颤抖 [literary] [Also + of]

  • His voice trembled, on the verge of tears. [VERB]

    他声音颤抖，泪水在眼眶里打转。

Tremble is also a noun.

  • 'Please understand this,' she began, a tremble in her voice.

    “请理解这一点，”她开始说道，声音有些发颤。
