subsist
səbsɪst səbˈsɪst
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense subsists, present participle subsisting, past tense , past participle subsisted
VERB 勉强糊口;勉强度日 If people subsist, they are just able to obtain the food or money that they need in order to stay alive. 勉强糊口;勉强度日 [formal]

  • The prisoners subsisted on one mug of the worst quality porridge three times a day. [VERB + on]

    犯人们每日三餐靠一碗稀粥度日。

  • Almost every employee must moonlight in second jobs simply to subsist. [VERB]

    只是为了维持生计，几乎每个职员都要偷偷地做兼职。

SYN stay alive, survive, keep going, make ends meet
