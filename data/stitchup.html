stitch up
1 PHRASAL VERB 陷害;诬陷;算计 To stitch someone up means to trick them so that they are put in a difficult or unpleasant situation, especially one where they are blamed for something they have not done. 陷害;诬陷;算计 [British, informal] [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • He claimed that a police officer had threatened to stitch him up and send him to prison. [VERB noun PREP.]

    他声称，一名警官威胁要陷害他并把他关进监狱。

SYN deceive, trick, cheat, betray  
2 PHRASAL VERB 办妥，促成(协议) To stitch up an agreement, especially a complicated agreement between several people, means to arrange it. 办妥，促成(协议) [mainly British, informal]

  • Shiraz has stitched up major deals all over the world to boost sales. [VERB PREP. noun]

    希拉兹已经在全球促成了几笔大宗协议的签订，以此来提高销量。

SYN clinch, settle, secure, seal
