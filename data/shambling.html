shamble
ʃæmbəl ˈʃæmbəl ˈʃæmbəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense shambles, present participle shambling, past tense , past participle shambled
VERB 拖着脚走;蹒跚而行 If you shamble somewhere, you walk clumsily, pulling your feet along the ground. 拖着脚走;蹒跚而行 [literary]

  • The conductor shambled to the next carriage. [VERB prep./adv.]

    售票员慵懒地拖着脚走向下一节车厢。

  • ...his tall, shambling figure. [VERB-ing]

    他那高大而且步履蹒跚的背影

SYN shuffle, stumble, lurch, limp
