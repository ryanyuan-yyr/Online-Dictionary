detach
dɪtætʃ diˈtætʃ dɪˈtætʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense detaches, present participle detaching, past tense , past participle detached
1 V-ERG 拆卸;(使)分开;(使)分离 If you detach one thing from another that it is fixed to, you remove it. If one thing detaches from another, it becomes separated from it. 拆卸;(使)分开;(使)分离 [formal] [Also VERB]

  • Detach the white part of the application form and keep it. [VERB noun]

    把申请表的白色部分撕下存底。

  • It is easy to detach the currants from the stems. [VERB noun + from]

    很容易就能把醋栗从梗上摘下来。

  • There was an accident when the towrope detached from the car. [VERB + from]

    当拖绳从车子上脱落时发生了事故。

SYN separate, free, remove, divide  
2 VERB 使脱离;使摆脱 If you detach yourself from something, you become less involved in it or less concerned about it than you used to be. 使脱离;使摆脱

  • It helps them detach themselves from their problems and become more objective. [V pron-refl + from]

    这有助于他们从所面临的问题中跳出来，变得更客观一些。

3 VERB 使离开;使脱身 If you detach yourself from a person or place, you leave them. 使离开;使脱身 [formal]

  • Alexis saw his father detach himself from the group and walk away down the hill by himself. [V pron-refl from n]

    亚历克西斯看到父亲离开那一群人，独自走下山去了。
