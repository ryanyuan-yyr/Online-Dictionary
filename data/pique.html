pique
piːk pik
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense piques, present participle piquing, past tense , past participle piqued
1 N-UNCOUNT （因遭人怠慢而感到的）恼怒，愠怒 Pique is the feeling of annoyance you have when you think someone has not treated you properly. （因遭人怠慢而感到的）恼怒，愠怒

  • Mimi had gotten over her pique at Susan's refusal to accept the job. [+ at]

    米米已不再为苏姗拒绝接受这份工作的事生气了。

SYN resentment, offence, irritation, annoyance  
2 VERB 激起，引起（兴趣或好奇心） If something piques your interest or curiosity, it makes you interested or curious. 激起，引起（兴趣或好奇心）

  • This phenomenon piqued Dr Morris' interest. [VERB noun]

    这一现象激起了莫里斯医生的兴趣。

  • Their curiosity piqued, they stopped writing. [VERB-ed]

    他们的好奇心被挑起，停下了手中的笔。

SYN arouse, excite, stir, spur  
3 PHRASE 一怒之下；愤然 If someone does something in a fit of pique, they do it suddenly because they are annoyed at being not treated properly. 一怒之下；愤然

  • Lawrence, in a fit of pique, left the Army and took up a career in the City.

    劳伦斯一气之下离开了陆军，在伦敦金融城开始了工作。
