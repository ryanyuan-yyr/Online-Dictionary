tribute
trɪbjuːt ˈtrɪbjut
Word forms: plural tributes
1 N-VAR 致敬;颂词;献礼 A tribute is something that you say, do, or make to show your admiration and respect for someone. 致敬;颂词;献礼

  • The song is a tribute to Roy Orbison. [+ to]

    那首歌是对罗伊·奥比森的致敬。

  • He paid tribute to the organising committee. [+ to]

    他赞扬了组委会。

  • Over nine-thousand ex-servicemen and women marched past in tribute to their fallen comrades.

    9,000多名退伍的男女战士列队行进而过，以悼念他们倒下的同志。

SYN accolade, testimonial, eulogy, recognition  
2 N-SING (良好结果的)体现，证明 If one thing is a tribute to another, the first thing is the result of the second and shows how good it is. (良好结果的)体现，证明

  • His success has been a tribute to hard work, to professionalism. [+ to]

    他的成功充分体现了他的勤劳苦干和敬业精神。

  • It is a tribute to Mr Chandler's skill that he has fashioned a fascinating book out of such unpromising material.

    钱德勒先生竟然以如此不被看好的素材写出了一本引人入胜的书，这充分证明了他的写作技巧。

SYN testimony to, evidence of, indication of, proof of
