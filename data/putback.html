put back
PHRASAL VERB 拖延；推迟 To put something back means to delay it or arrange for it to happen later than you previously planned. 拖延；推迟 [mainly British] [Also VERB PREPOSITION n]

  • There are always new projects which seem to put the reunion back further. [VERB noun PREP.]

    总是有新的项目使重聚一推再推。

  • News conferences due to be held by both men have been put back. [be VERB-ed PREP.]

    原定两个人共同举行的新闻发布会被推迟。
