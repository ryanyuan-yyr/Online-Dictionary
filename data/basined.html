basin
beɪsən ˈbeɪsən ˈbeɪsɪn
Word forms: plural basins
1 N-COUNT 盆；大碗 A basin is a large or deep bowl that you use for holding liquids, or for mixing or storing food. 盆；大碗

  • Place the eggs and sugar in a large basin.

    把鸡蛋和糖放在一个大碗里。

  • ...a pudding basin.

    布丁碗

SYN bowl, dish, vessel, container  
A basin of something such as water is an amount of it that is contained in a basin.一大碗之量；一盆之量

  • We were given a basin of water to wash our hands in.

    有人给了我们一盆洗手的水。

2 N-COUNT 同 washbasin A basin is the same as a washbasin . 同 washbasin

  • ...a cast-iron bath with a matching basin and wc.

    铸铁浴缸及配套的洗涤槽和马桶

SYN washbasin, sink  
3 N-COUNT [oft in names] 流域 The basin of a large river is the area of land around it from which streams run down into it. 流域

  • ...the Amazon basin.

    亚马孙河流域

SYN valley, hollow, gorge, ravine  
4 N-COUNT [oft in names] 盆地；洼地 In geography, a basin is a particular region of the world where the earth's surface is lower than in other places. 盆地；洼地 [technical]

  • ...countries around the Pacific Basin.

    太平洋盆地的周边国家

SYN bed, depression, trough, concavity  
5 N-COUNT [usually n NOUN] 泊船处 A basin is a partially enclosed area of deep water where boats or ships are kept. 泊船处
