The form -es is also used. The suffix -s is pronounced /-s/ after the consonant sounds /p, t, k, f/ or /θ/. After other sounds -s is pronounced /-z/. The suffix -es is pronounced /-z/ after vowel sounds, and /-ɪz/ after consonant sounds. 亦使用 -es。后缀 -s 在辅音 /p, t, k, f/ 或 /θ/ 后发音为 /-s/。在其他音后发音为 /-z/。后缀 -es 在元音后发音为 /-z/，在辅音后发音为 /-ɪz/。
1 (用于名词后构成复数形式) -s or -es is added to a noun to form a plural. (用于名词后构成复数形式)

  • ...her two beloved cats.

    她心爱的两只猫

  • ...a few problems.

    几个问题

  • ...new houses and flats.

    新的别墅和公寓

  • Most bosses are traditional.

    大多数老板都很传统。

2 (用于动词后构成第三人称单数现在时形式) -s or -es is added to a verb to form the third person singular, present tense. (用于动词后构成第三人称单数现在时形式)

  • He never thinks about it.

    他从来没想过这个问题。

  • She likes her job.

    她喜欢自己的工作。

  • No-one wishes to see that.

    没有人希望看见那一幕。

2 is的口语形式;-'s is the usual spoken form of "is." It is added to the end of the pronoun or noun which is the subject of the verb. For example, "he is" and "she is" can be shortened to "he's" and "she's." is的口语形式;
