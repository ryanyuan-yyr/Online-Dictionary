recompense
rekəmpens ˈrɛkəmˌpɛns ˈrɛkəmˌpɛns
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense recompenses, present participle recompensing, past tense , past participle recompensed
1 N-UNCOUNT [in NOUN] 酬金;酬劳;奖赏;赔偿 If you are given something, usually money, in recompense, you are given it as a reward or because you have suffered. 酬金;酬劳;奖赏;赔偿 [formal]

  • He demands no financial recompense for his troubles. [+ for]

    他没有对自己遭遇的麻烦要求经济赔偿。

  • Substantial damages were paid in recompense.

    巨额损失得到了赔偿。

SYN compensation, pay, payment, satisfaction  
2 VERB （常以金钱形式）偿付，赔偿，报答 If you recompense someone for their efforts or their loss, you give them something, usually money, as a payment or reward. （常以金钱形式）偿付，赔偿，报答 [formal]

  • The fees offered by the NHS do not recompense dental surgeons for their professional time. [VERB noun + for]

    国民医疗保健系统所支付的费用中不涵盖牙科诊疗费用。

  • If they succeed in court, they will be fully recompensed for their loss. [VERB noun for noun]

    如果他们能在法庭上胜诉，他们的损失将会得到全额赔偿。

SYN compensate, reimburse, redress, repay
