protracted
prətræktɪd proʊ-
ADJ-GRADED 延长的；拖延的；持久的 Something, usually something unpleasant, that is protracted lasts a long time, especially longer than usual or longer than you hoped. 延长的；拖延的；持久的 [formal]

  • After protracted negotiations, Ogden got the deal he wanted.

    经过长时间的谈判，奥格登得到了他想要的那笔生意。

  • ...a protracted civil war.

    持久的内战

  • The struggle would be bitter and protracted.

    斗争将会艰苦而漫长。

SYN extended, long, prolonged, lengthy
