mother
mʌðəʳ ˈmʌðər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense mothers, present participle mothering, past tense , past participle mothered
1 N-FAMILY 母亲;妈妈 Your mother is the woman who gave birth to you. You can also call someone your mother if she brings you up as if she was this woman. 母亲;妈妈

  • She sat on the edge of her mother's bed.

    她坐在母亲床边。

  • She's an English teacher and a mother of two children. [+ of]

    她是英语教师，还是两个孩子的母亲。

  • Mother and child form a close attachment.

    母子情深。

  • I'm here, Mother.

    我在这儿呢，妈妈。

2 VERB (通常指母亲)照看，抚养，养育 If a woman mothers a child, she looks after it and brings it up, usually because she is its mother. (通常指母亲)照看，抚养，养育

  • Colleen had dreamed of mothering a large family. [VERB noun]

    科琳曾梦想抚养一群孩子。

SYN give birth to, produce, bear, bring forth  
mothering

  • The reality of mothering is frequently very different from the romantic ideal.

    养育孩子的现实经常与浪漫的理想大相径庭。

3 VERB 爱护;溺爱 If you mother someone, you treat them with great care and affection, as if they were a small child. 爱护;溺爱

  • She felt a great need to mother him. [VERB noun]

    她迫切地感到要细心照料他。

  • Stop mothering me. [VERB noun]

    别再宠我了。

SYN nurture, raise, protect, tend
