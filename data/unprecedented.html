unprecedented
ʌnpresɪdentɪd ʌnˈprɛsɪˌdɛntɪd ʌnˈprɛsəˌdɛntɪd
1 ADJ 前所未有的；空前的；没有先例的 If something is unprecedented, it has never happened before. 前所未有的；空前的；没有先例的

  • Such a move is rare, but not unprecedented.

    这种做法很罕见，但也不是没有先例的。

  • In 1987 the Socialists took the unprecedented step of appointing a civilian to command the force.

    1987年，社会党人史无前例地任命了一位非军队出身的人来指挥军队。

SYN unparalleled, unheard-of, exceptional, new  
2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] （质量、数量、规模）很高的，很大的 If you describe something as unprecedented, you are emphasizing that it is very great in quality, amount, or scale. （质量、数量、规模）很高的，很大的 [emphasis]

  • Each home boasts an unprecedented level of quality throughout.

    每家的质量都堪称一流。

  • The scheme has been hailed as an unprecedented success.

    这个计划广受好评，被认为是一个巨大的成功。

SYN extraordinary, amazing, remarkable, outstanding
