fuddy-duddy fʌdi dʌdi ˈfʌdiˌdʌdi
Word forms: plural fuddy-duddies
N-COUNT 老古板;老古董 If you describe someone as a fuddy-duddy, you are criticizing or making fun of them because they are old-fashioned in their appearance or attitudes. 老古板;老古董 [old-fashioned, disapproval]

  • He didn't want all those old fuddy-duddies around.

    他不想身边有那么多老古董。

SYN conservative, square [informal] , dinosaur, fossil  
Fuddy-duddy is also an adjective.

  • Perhaps we did acquire a somewhat fuddy-duddy image in the later years.

    可能在后来的几年里我们真的让人感到有些古板。
