gallop
gæləp ˈgæləp
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense gallops, present participle galloping, past tense , past participle galloped
1 V-ERG （使）（马）飞奔；（使）（马）疾驰 When a horse gallops, it runs very fast so that all four legs are off the ground at the same time. If you gallop a horse, you make it gallop. （使）（马）飞奔；（使）（马）疾驰

  • The horses galloped away. [VERB adv./prep.]

    群马飞奔而去。

  • Staff officers galloped fine horses down the road. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    参谋官们骑着骏马一路疾驰而去。

SYN run, race, shoot, career  
2 VERB 骑马奔驰；策马飞奔 If you gallop, you ride a horse that is galloping. 骑马奔驰；策马飞奔

  • Major Winston galloped into the distance. [VERB prep./adv.]

    温斯顿少校策马疾驰而去。

3 N-SING 骑马奔驰 A gallop is a ride on a horse that is galloping. 骑马奔驰

  • I was forced to attempt a gallop.

    我被迫试着骑马飞奔。

4 VERB 飞速发展；高速增长 If something such as a process gallops, it develops very quickly and is often difficult to control. 飞速发展；高速增长

  • In spite of the recession, profits have galloped ahead. [VERB adv.]

    尽管还处于萧条期，利润却迅猛增长。

  • ...galloping inflation. [VERB-ing]

    急剧增长的通货膨胀

5 VERB 迅速奔跑；飞奔 If you gallop, you run somewhere very quickly. 迅速奔跑；飞奔 [Also VERB n]

  • They are galloping around the garden playing football. [VERB prep.]

    他们在园子里飞跑着踢足球。

SYN dash, run, race, shoot  
6 PHRASE 迅速地；飞快地 If you do something at a gallop, you do it very quickly. 迅速地；飞快地

  • I read the book at a gallop.

    我飞快地读完了这本书。

SYN swiftly, fast, quickly, rapidly
