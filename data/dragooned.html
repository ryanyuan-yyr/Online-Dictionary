dragoon
drəguːn drəˈgun
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , 3rd person singular present tense dragoons, present participle dragooning, past tense , past participle dragooned
1 N-COUNT (旧时欧洲的)骑兵 A dragoon was a soldier in old European armies. Dragoons usually fought on horseback. (旧时欧洲的)骑兵
2 VERB 迫使;强使…做 If someone dragoons you into doing something that you do not want to do, they persuade you to do it even though you try hard not to agree. 迫使;强使…做

  • ...the history professor who had dragooned me into taking the exam. [VERB noun + into]

    迫使我参加考试的历史教授

  • Her husband had also been dragooned into the excursion. [V n into -ing/n]

    她的丈夫也被拉来参加远足了。

SYN force, drive, compel, bully
