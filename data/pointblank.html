point-blank ˈpɔɪntˈblæŋk
1 ADV [ADVERB after verb] 直截了当地；断然地 If you say something point-blank, you say it very directly or rudely, without explaining or apologizing. 直截了当地；断然地

  • The army apparently refused point blank to do what was required of them.

    军队貌似断然拒绝做他们分内的事情。

  • Mr Patterson was asked point blank if he would resign.

    帕特森先生被直接问到他是否会辞职。

SYN directly, openly, straight, frankly  
Point-blank is also an adjective.

  • ...a point-blank refusal.

    断然拒绝

2 ADV [ADVERB after verb] 近距离地；抵近地 If someone or something is shot point-blank, they are shot when the gun is touching them or extremely close to them. 近距离地；抵近地

  • He fired point-blank at Bernadette.

    他近距离朝贝尔纳黛特开了枪。

Point-blank is also an adjective.

  • He had been shot at point-blank range in the back of the head.

    他被人近距离开枪击中后脑。
