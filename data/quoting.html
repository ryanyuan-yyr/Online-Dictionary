quote
kwoʊt kwoʊt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense quotes, present participle quoting, past tense , past participle quoted
1 VERB 引述;引用 If you quote someone as saying something, you repeat what they have written or said. 引述;引用

  • He quoted Mr Polay as saying that peace negotiations were already underway. [VERB noun + as]

    他引用波利先生的话，说和谈已在进行之中。

  • She was quoted in the Express by an unnamed source as saying: 'I won't bail out those two silly girls.'. [V n as -ing]

    一位匿名的作者在《快报》中引用了她的话说：“我不会保释那两个傻丫头的”。

  • She quoted a great line from a book by Romain Gary. [VERB noun]

    她引用了罗曼·加里的一本书中的名句。

  • Yesterday the Belgian newspaper Le Soir quoted a professor who said he witnessed the killings. [VERB noun]

    昨天比利时《晚报》援引了一名自称目击了谋杀经过的教授的话。

  • I gave the letter to our local press and they quoted from it. [VERB + from]

    我把信交给了我们当地的媒体，他们引用了其中的一部分内容。

2 N-COUNT 引文；引语；语录 A quote from a book, poem, play, or speech is a passage or phrase from it. 引文；引语；语录

  • The article starts with a quote from an unnamed member of the Cabinet. [+ from]

    文章的开头引用了一位未透露姓名的内阁成员说过的话。

3 VERB 援引，引用(法律条款、事实等) If you quote something such as a law or a fact, you state it because it supports what you are saying. 援引，引用(法律条款、事实等)

  • Mr Meacher quoted statistics saying that the standard of living of the poorest people had fallen. [VERB noun]

    米彻先生援引统计数据来说明最贫困人口的生活水平下降了。

4 VERB 报(价);开(价) If someone quotes a price for doing something, they say how much money they would charge you for a service they are offering or for a job that you want them to do. 报(价);开(价)

  • A travel agent quoted her £160 for a flight from Bristol to Palma. [VERB noun noun]

    一位旅行社职员给她开出的从布里斯托尔至帕尔马的机票报价是160英镑。

  • He quoted a price for the repairs. [VERB noun]

    他给出了维修费用的报价。

SYN estimate, state, tender, set  
5 N-COUNT 报价;开价 A quote for a piece of work is the price that someone says they will charge you to do the work. 报价;开价

  • Always get a written quote for any repairs needed. [+ for]

    每次进行修理都索要一份书面的报价单。

6 V-PASSIVE 报…的牌价;为…报价 If a company's shares, a substance, or a currency is quoted at a particular price, that is its current market price. 报…的牌价;为…报价 [business]

  • In early trading in Hong Kong yesterday, gold was quoted at $368.20 an ounce. [be V-ed + at]

    在香港昨天早些时候的交易中，黄金的牌价为每盎司368.20美元。

  • Heron is a private company and is not quoted on the Stock Market. [be V-ed + on]

    赫伦公司是一家私营公司，没有在股票交易市场挂牌。

7 N-PLURAL 同 quotation mark Quotes are the same as quotation marks . 同 quotation mark [informal]

  • The word 'remembered' is in quotes.

    remembered 一词用引号括了起来。

8 CONVENTION 原话是;这是…的原话 You can say 'quote' to show that you are about to quote someone's words. 原话是;这是…的原话 [spoken]

  • He predicts they will have, quote, 'an awful lot of explaining to do'.

    他预计他们需要“大费周章地解释一番”，这是他的原话。
