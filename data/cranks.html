crank
kræŋk kræŋk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense cranks, present participle cranking, past tense , past participle cranked
1 N-COUNT 想法(或行为)古怪的人 If you call someone a crank, you think their ideas or behaviour are strange. 想法(或行为)古怪的人 [informal, disapproval]

  • The Prime Minister called Councillor Marshall 'a crank'.

    首相称马歇尔议员为“怪人”。

  • He looked like a crank.

    他看上去像个怪人。

2 N-COUNT 曲柄;曲轴 A crank is a device that you turn in order to make something move. 曲柄;曲轴
3 VERB (尤指用手摇曲柄)转动(或启动) If you crank an engine or machine, you make it move or function, especially by turning a handle. (尤指用手摇曲柄)转动(或启动)

  • The chauffeur got out to crank the motor. [VERB noun]

    司机下车用曲柄去发动汽车。

crank out crank up
