brawl
brɔːl brɔl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense brawls, present participle brawling, past tense , past participle brawled
1 N-COUNT 打架；斗殴 A brawl is a rough or violent fight. 打架；斗殴

  • He had been in a drunken street brawl.

    他参与了一场酒后街头斗殴。

SYN fight, battle, row [informal] , clash  
2 V-RECIP 打架；斗殴 If someone brawls, they fight in a very rough or violent way. 打架；斗殴

  • He was suspended for a year from University after brawling with police over a speeding ticket. [VERB + with]

    他为了一张超速罚单与警察厮打，被大学勒令停学1年。

  • Two gangs of youths brawled on the dance floor of the ferry. [VERB]

    两伙青年在渡轮的舞池里打起来了。

SYN fight, battle, scrap [informal] , wrestle
