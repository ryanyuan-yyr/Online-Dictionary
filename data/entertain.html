entertain
entəʳteɪn ˌɛntərˈteɪn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense entertains, present participle entertaining, past tense , past participle entertained
1 VERB 使快乐；给…以娱乐；使有兴趣 If a performer, performance, or activity entertains you, it amuses you, interests you, or gives you pleasure. 使快乐；给…以娱乐；使有兴趣

  • ...games and ideas to entertain children. [VERB noun]

    带给孩子们快乐的游戏和想法

  • They were entertained by top singers, dancers and celebrities. [be VERB-ed]

    顶级歌手、舞蹈演员和名人们给他们带来娱乐。

  • Children's television not only entertains but also teaches. [VERB]

    儿童电视节目不仅有娱乐性而且还有教育意义。

entertaining

  • To generate new money the sport needs to be more entertaining.

    要想创造更多的收入，这项体育活动需要变得更加有趣味性。

  • This is a surprisingly entertaining film.

    这部电影的娱乐性十足，让人意想不到。

  • Miro is the most inventive and entertaining of surrealist painters.

    米罗是最具创造力和娱乐性的超现实主义画家。

2 VERB 招待;款待 If you entertain people, you provide food and drink for them, for example when you have invited them to your house. 招待;款待

  • I don't like to entertain guests anymore. [VERB noun]

    我再也不想招待客人了。

  • You weren't allowed to entertain men in your rooms even with a chaperone. [VERB noun]

    即使你有监护人陪同，也不允许在房间里招待男性。

  • The Monroes continued to entertain extravagantly. [VERB]

    门罗一家继续大宴宾客。

SYN show hospitality to, receive, accommodate, treat  
entertaining

  • ...a cosy area for entertaining and relaxing.

    招待和休息的舒适场所

3 VERB 考虑，心存，怀有（主意、建议等） If you entertain an idea or suggestion, you allow yourself to consider it as possible or as worth thinking about seriously. 考虑，心存，怀有（主意、建议等） [formal]

  • I feel how foolish I am to entertain doubts. [VERB noun]

    我竟然心存怀疑，真是太愚蠢了。

  • I wouldn't entertain the idea of such an unsociable job. [VERB noun]

    我不会考虑从事这种不跟人打交道的工作。

SYN consider, support, maintain, imagine
