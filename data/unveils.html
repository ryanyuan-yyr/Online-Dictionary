unveil
ʌnveɪl ʌnˈveɪl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense unveils, present participle unveiling, past tense , past participle unveiled
1 VERB 为…揭幕 If someone formally unveils something such as a new statue or painting, they draw back the curtain which is covering it. 为…揭幕

  • ...a ceremony to unveil a monument to the victims. [VERB noun]

    死难者纪念碑揭幕仪式

unveiling

  • ...the unveiling of a monument to one of the Croatian heroes of the past.

    为一座纪念碑举行的揭幕仪式，它的建成是为了纪念克罗地亚一位昔日的英雄

2 VERB 公布；推出；出台 If you unveil a plan, new product, or some other thing that has been kept secret, you introduce it to the public. 公布；推出；出台

  • Mr Werner unveiled his new strategy this week. [VERB noun]

    沃纳先生本周公布了其新策略。

  • Companies from across Europe are here to unveil their latest models. [VERB noun]

    全欧洲的公司都来到这里展示其最新款产品。

SYN reveal, publish, launch, introduce
