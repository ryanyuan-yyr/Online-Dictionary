stand aside
1 PHRASAL VERB 不干预;不采取行动 If you stand aside from something, you allow it to happen without interfering in it or doing anything to prevent it. 不干预;不采取行动

  • Ireland stood aside from this conflict. [V P from n]

    爱尔兰没有介入这次冲突。

  • The key question was whether they would stand aside or would disrupt the elections. [VERB PREP.]

    关键问题是他们会袖手旁观还是会扰乱选举。

SYN not participate, stand by, sit tight, sit on the fence  
2 PHRASAL VERB (从重要职位上)退下，下台(常为了让位给别人) If someone stands aside, they resign from an important job or position, often in order to let someone else take their place. (从重要职位上)退下，下台(常为了让位给别人) [British]

  • The President said he was willing to stand aside if that would stop the killing. [VERB PREP.]

    总统说，如果他下台能让杀戮停止的话，他愿意这样做。

in AM, use 美国英语用 stand down
