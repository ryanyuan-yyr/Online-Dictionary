dependent
dɪpendənt diˈpɛndənt dɪˈpɛndənt
1 ADJ-GRADED 依靠的;依赖的 To be dependent on something or someone means to need them in order to succeed or be able to survive. 依靠的;依赖的

  • The local economy is overwhelmingly dependent on oil and gas extraction. [+ on/upon]

    当地经济过于依赖石油和天然气的开采。

  • Up to two million people there are dependent on food aid.

    那里有多达 200 万人口依靠食品援助。

  • Britain became increasingly dependent upon American technology.

    英国变得越来越依赖美国的技术了。

  • In his own way, he was dependent on her.

    他以自己的方式依赖着她。

  • Just 26 per cent of households are married couples with dependent children.

    只有26%的家庭是有孩子要扶养的已婚夫妇。

2 ADJ 取决（于…）的;有赖（于…）的 If one thing is dependent on another, the first thing will be affected or determined by the second. 取决（于…）的;有赖（于…）的

  • The results you get from weight training are largely dependent upon how you use those weights and what type of exercise programme you follow.

    举重训练的效果在很大程度上取决于如何使用那些举重器械以及遵照何种锻炼方案。

  • The treatment of infertility is largely dependent on the ability of couples to pay. [+ on/upon]

    对不孕不育的治疗很大程度上视乎夫妇的经济能力。
