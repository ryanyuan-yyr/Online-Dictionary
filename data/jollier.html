jolly
dʒɒli ˈdʒɑli
Word forms: comparative jollier, superlative jolliest
1 ADJ-GRADED 兴高采烈的；快乐的；高兴的 Someone who is jolly is happy and cheerful in their appearance or behaviour. 兴高采烈的；快乐的；高兴的

  • She was a jolly, kindhearted woman.

    她是个心地善良、整天乐呵呵的女人。

SYN happy, bright, funny, lively  
2 ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 令人愉快的；欢快的；惬意的 A jolly event is lively and enjoyable. 令人愉快的；欢快的；惬意的

  • I was looking forward to a jolly party.

    我期待着有一个令人愉快的聚会。

  • She had a very jolly time in Korea.

    她在韩国玩得很惬意。

3 ADV [ADVERB adjective/adverb] （用于强调）很，非常 Jolly is sometimes used to emphasize an adjective or adverb. （用于强调）很，非常 [British, informal, old-fashioned, emphasis]

  • She was jolly good at jigsaws.

    她拼图玩得很棒。

  • It was jolly hard work, but I loved it.

    这可是件很苦的差事，不过我喜欢。

4 PHRASE [PHRASE before verb, PHR adj] （用于强调看法或意图，表示恼火或生气）当然，无疑，真是 Jolly well is sometimes used to emphasize an opinion or intention, and to express annoyance or anger. （用于强调看法或意图，表示恼火或生气）当然，无疑，真是 [British, informal, old-fashioned, emphasis]

  • We can hardly just tell him what we jolly well think of him can we?.

    我们不能就这么直接地告诉他我们到底是怎么看他的，是吧？

  • She was jolly well not going to let them get away with it.

    她肯定不会在这件事上就这么放过他们。
