peel
piːl pil
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense peels, present participle peeling, past tense , past participle peeled
1 N-UNCOUNT （水果）皮，外皮 The peel of a fruit such as a lemon or an apple is its skin. （水果）皮，外皮 [US]

  • ...grated lemon peel.

    擦碎的柠檬皮

SYN rind, skin, peeling, epicarp  
You can also refer to a peel.（一块）果皮

  • ...a banana peel.

    香蕉皮

2 VERB 剥皮；去皮；削皮 When you peel fruit or vegetables, you remove their skins. 剥皮；去皮；削皮

  • She sat down in the kitchen and began peeling potatoes. [VERB noun]

    她坐在厨房里，开始削土豆皮。

SYN skin, scale, strip, pare  
3 V-ERG 剥掉；剥落 If you peel off something that has been sticking to a surface or if it peels off, it comes away from the surface. 剥掉；剥落

  • One of the kids was peeling plaster off the wall. [V n + off/from]

    其中一个孩子在剥墙上的灰泥。

  • It took me two days to peel off the labels. [V n with off/away]

    我花了两天时间才揭下这些标签。

  • Paint was peeling off the walls. [V + off/from]

    墙上的油漆开始剥落。

  • The wallpaper was peeling away close to the ceiling. [V off/away]

    天花板附近的壁纸在剥落。

  • ...an unrenovated bungalow with slightly peeling blue paint. [VERB-ing]

    蓝漆略微剥落的失修平房

4 VERB [usually cont] 掉漆 If a surface is peeling, the paint on it is coming away. 掉漆

  • Its once-elegant white pillars are peeling. [VERB]

    曾经典雅的白色立柱正在掉漆。

5 VERB [usually cont] （通常因为晒伤而）脱皮，起皮 If you are peeling or if your skin is peeling, small pieces of skin are coming off, usually because you have been burned by the sun. （通常因为晒伤而）脱皮，起皮

  • His face, at the moment, was peeling from sunburn. [VERB]

    那时他的脸因为晒伤而脱皮。
