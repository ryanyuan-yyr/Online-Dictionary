furrow
fʌroʊ fɜːr- ˈfɜroʊ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense furrows, present participle furrowing, past tense , past participle furrowed
1 N-COUNT 犁沟 A furrow is a long, thin line in the earth which a farmer makes in order to plant seeds or to allow water to flow along. 犁沟
2 N-COUNT 沟槽;车辙 A furrow is a deep, fairly wide line in the surface of something. 沟槽;车辙

  • Dirt bike trails crisscrossed the grassy furrows.

    越野摩托车的轮迹纵横交错地布满条条草沟。

SYN groove, line, channel, hollow  
3 N-COUNT (脸上的)皱纹 A furrow is a deep fold or line in the skin of someone's face. (脸上的)皱纹

  • ...the deep furrows that marked the corners of his mouth.

    他嘴角边刻着的深深的皱纹

SYN wrinkle, line, crease, crinkle  
4 V-ERG (使)(眉)皱起;(使)(额)蹙起 If someone furrows their brow or forehead or if it furrows, deep folds appear in it because the person is annoyed, unhappy, or confused. (使)(眉)皱起;(使)(额)蹙起 [written]

  • My bank manager furrowed his brow, fingered his calculator and finally pronounced 'Aha!'. [VERB noun]

    我的银行经理眉头紧锁，拨弄着计算器，最后终于“啊哈!”了一声。

  • Midge's forehead furrowed as she saw that several were drinking. [VERB]

    米奇看到几个人正在喝酒时皱起了眉头。

  • Fatigue and stress quickly result in a dull complexion and a furrowed brow. [VERB-ed]

    疲劳和压力会很快导致肤色暗淡无光、额头出现皱纹。

SYN wrinkle, knit, draw together, crease  
5 PHRASE 走自己…的道路;与众不同;独树一帜 If you say that someone ploughs a particular furrow or ploughs their own furrow, you mean that their activities or interests are different or isolated from those of other people. 走自己…的道路;与众不同;独树一帜 [British]

  • Cale has ploughed a more esoteric furrow as a recording artist.

    作为一名录音艺人，凯尔走了一条较为冷僻的道路。

  • The government is more than adept at ploughing its own diplomatic furrow.

    政府在制定自己独树一帜的外交政策时无比娴熟。
