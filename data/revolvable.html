revolve
rɪvɒlv rɪˈvɑlv rɪˈvɔlv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense revolves, present participle revolving, past tense , past participle revolved
1 VERB 围绕;以…为中心;以…为主要特征 If you say that one thing revolves around another thing, you mean that the second thing is the main feature or focus of the first thing. 围绕;以…为中心;以…为主要特征

  • Since childhood, her life has revolved around tennis. [V + around]

    从孩童时期开始，她的生活就以网球为中心。

  • This plot revolves around a youngster who is shown various stages of his life. [V around n]

    这个故事情节围绕着一个年轻人展开，描述了他人生的各个阶段。

SYN be concerned with, focus on, concentrate on, hang on  
2 VERB (讨论或对话)以…为主题，围绕 If a discussion or conversation revolves around a particular topic, it is mainly about that topic. (讨论或对话)以…为主题，围绕

  • The debate revolves around specific accounting techniques. [V + around]

    这场争论的焦点是具体的会计技术。

  • The conversation revolved around the terrible condition of the road. [V around n]

    这次对话主要讨论这条路的糟糕路况。

3 VERB 围绕…旋转 If one object revolves around another object, the first object turns in a circle around the second object. 围绕…旋转

  • The satellite revolves around the Earth once every hundred minutes. [V + around]

    这颗人造卫星每100分钟围绕地球旋转一周。

SYN go round, circle, orbit, gyrate  
4 V-ERG （使）旋转;（使）转动 When something revolves or when you revolve it, it moves or turns in a circle around a central point or line. （使）旋转;（使）转动

  • Overhead, the fan revolved slowly. [VERB]

    风扇在头顶上缓缓地转动着。

  • Monica picked up her Biro and revolved it between her teeth. [VERB noun]

    莫妮卡拿起她的伯罗圆珠笔，放在牙齿间转动着。
