invitation
ɪnvɪteɪʃən ˌɪnvəˈteɪʃən
Word forms: plural invitations
1 N-COUNT [oft NOUN to-infinitive] 邀请 An invitation is a written or spoken request to come to an event such as a party, a meal, or a meeting. 邀请

  • ...an invitation to lunch. [+ to]

    共进午餐的邀请

  • The Syrians have not yet accepted an invitation to attend.

    叙利亚人还没有接到出席邀请。

  • He's understood to be there at the personal invitation of President Daniel Arap Moi. [+ of]

    据悉他是应总统丹尼尔·阿拉普·莫伊亲自邀请而到场的。

SYN request, call, invite [informal] , bidding  
2 N-COUNT 请帖;请柬 An invitation is the card or paper on which an invitation is written or printed. 请帖;请柬

  • Hundreds of invitations are being sent out this week.

    本周将寄出数百份请柬。

  • ...gold embossed invitation cards.

    烫金的请柬

3 N-SING 诱因;招致…的行为 If you believe that someone's action is likely to have a particular result, especially a bad one, you can refer to the action as an invitation to that result. 诱因;招致…的行为

  • ...a war that most liberal Democrats regarded as an invitation to disaster.

    一场大部分自由民主党人认为会招致灾难的战争

  • Don't leave your shopping on the back seat of your car — it's an open invitation to a thief. [+ to]

    别把买的东西放在汽车后座上—那会招来小偷的。

SYN inducement, come-on [informal] , temptation, challenge
