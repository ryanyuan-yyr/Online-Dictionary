slump
slʌmp slʌmp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense slumps, present participle slumping, past tense , past participle slumped
1 VERB 急剧下降;暴跌 If something such as the value of something slumps, it falls suddenly and by a large amount. 急剧下降;暴跌 [Also VERB]

  • Net profits slumped by 41%. [VERB prep.]

    净利润暴跌了 41%。

  • Government popularity in Scotland has slumped to its lowest level since the 1970s. [VERB prep.]

    苏格兰政府的支持率骤跌至 20 世纪 70 年代以来的最低水平。 Slump is also a noun.

  • ...a slump in property prices.

    房地产价格的暴跌

SYN fall, decline, sink, plunge  
2 N-COUNT 不景气;萧条;衰退 A slump is a time when many people in a country are unemployed and poor. 不景气;萧条;衰退

  • ...the slump of the early 1980s. [+ of]

    20 世纪 80 年代初的大萧条

SYN recession, depression, stagnation, inactivity  
3 VERB (因疲劳、生病等而重重)倒下，跌倒 If you slump somewhere, you fall or sit down there heavily, for example because you are very tired or you feel ill. (因疲劳、生病等而重重)倒下，跌倒

  • She slumped into a chair. [VERB prep./adv.]

    她轰然倒在了椅子上。

  • He saw the driver slumped over the wheel. [VERB-ed]

    他看见司机一头栽在了方向盘上。
