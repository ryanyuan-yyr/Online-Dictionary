bounty
baʊnti ˈbaʊnti
Word forms: plural bounties
1 N-VAR 大量提供的东西；慷慨的赠予 You can refer to something that is provided in large amounts as bounty . 大量提供的东西；慷慨的赠予 [literary]

  • ...autumn's bounty of fruits, seeds and berries.

    秋天丰富的水果、种子和浆果

SYN abundance, plenty, exuberance, profusion  
2 N-COUNT （尤指为找到或杀死某人而悬赏的）赏金，奖金 A bounty is money that is offered as a reward for doing something, especially for finding or killing a particular person. （尤指为找到或杀死某人而悬赏的）赏金，奖金

  • A bounty of $50,000 was put on Dr. Alvarez's head.

    有人悬赏50,000美元买阿尔瓦雷斯博士的人头。

  • They paid bounties for people to give up their weapons.

    他们向放下武器的人发放赏金。

SYN reward, present, grant, prize
