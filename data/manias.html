mania
meɪniə ˈmeɪniə ˈmeɪnjə
Word forms: plural manias
1 N-COUNT [usually singular, n NOUN] 狂热;癖好 If you say that a person or group has a mania for something, you mean that they enjoy it very much or spend a lot of time on it. 狂热;癖好

  • It seemed to some observers that the English had a mania for travelling. [+ for

    在某些观察家看来，英国人对旅行似乎抱有一种狂热。

  • The media have a mania about rugby union.

    媒体格外青睐英式橄榄球联盟。

  • ...Mozart mania.

    莫扎特热

2 N-UNCOUNT 躁狂症 Mania is a mental illness which causes the sufferer to become very worried or concerned about something. 躁狂症

  • ...the treatment of mania.

    对躁狂症的治疗

  • He almost wouldn't eat at all and had the oddest manias.

    他几乎颗粒未进，走火入魔。

SYN madness, disorder, frenzy, insanity
