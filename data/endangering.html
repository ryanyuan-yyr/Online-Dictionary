endanger
ɪndeɪndʒəʳ ɛnˈdeɪndʒər ɪnˈdeɪndʒər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense endangers, present participle endangering, past tense , past participle endangered
VERB 使遭遇危险；危及;危害 To endanger something or someone means to put them in a situation where they might be harmed or destroyed completely. 使遭遇危险；危及;危害

  • The debate could endanger the proposed peace talks. [VERB noun]

    这一争论可能会危及提议进行的和平谈判。

  • ...endangered species such as lynx and wolf. [VERB-ed]

    猞猁、狼等濒危物种

SYN put at risk, risk, threaten, compromise
