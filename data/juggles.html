juggle
dʒʌgəl ˈdʒʌgəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense juggles, present participle juggling, past tense , past participle juggled
1 VERB 尽力同时应付；尽量兼顾 If you juggle lots of different things, for example your work and your family, you try to give enough time or attention to all of them. 尽力同时应付；尽量兼顾 [Also V + with]

  • The management team meets several times a week to juggle budgets and resources. [VERB noun]

    管理团队一周碰头几次，以同时兼顾预算和资源。

  • Mike juggled the demands of a family of 11 with a career as a TV reporter. [VERB noun + with]

    身为电视记者的迈克同时尽力照料着一个11口之家。

2 VERB 玩杂耍（指连续向空中抛接多个物体） If you juggle, you entertain people by throwing things into the air, catching each one and throwing it up again so that there are several of them in the air at the same time. 玩杂耍（指连续向空中抛接多个物体）

  • Soon she was juggling five eggs. [VERB noun]

    没多会儿她就开始抛接5个鸡蛋了。

  • I can't juggle. [VERB]

    我不会玩杂耍。
