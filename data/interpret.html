interpret
ɪntɜːʳprɪt ɪnˈtɜrprət
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense interprets, present participle interpreting, past tense , past participle interpreted
1 VERB 解释;说明;阐释 If you interpret something in a particular way, you decide that this is its meaning or significance. 解释;说明;阐释

  • The whole speech might well be interpreted as a coded message to the Americans. [be VERB-ed + as]

    整个演讲完全可以看成是在向美国人传递编码信息。

  • The judge quite rightly says that he has to interpret the law as it's been passed. [VERB noun]

    法官说他必须按照该法的立法初衷来阐释它，这完全没错。

  • Both approaches agree on what is depicted in the poem, but not on how it should be interpreted. [be VERB-ed]

    两种方法对诗中所描绘的是何种事物的看法是一致的,但是对于应该如何阐释这首诗有所分歧。

SYN explain, define, clarify, spell out  
2 VERB 口译 If you interpret what someone is saying, you translate it immediately into another language. 口译

  • The chambermaid spoke little English, so her husband came with her to interpret. [VERB]

    女服务员几乎不会说英语,因此她丈夫跟着来给她做翻译。

  • Interpreters found they could not interpret half of what he said. [VERB noun]

    口译员们发现他说的话他们连一半都翻译不出来。

SYN translate, convert, paraphrase, adapt
