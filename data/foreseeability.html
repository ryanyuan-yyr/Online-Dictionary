foreseeable
fɔːʳsiːəbəl
1 ADJ-GRADED 可预知的;可预见的 If a future event is foreseeable, you know that it will happen or that it can happen, because it is a natural or obvious consequence of something else that you know. 可预知的;可预见的

  • It seems to me that this crime was foreseeable and this death preventable.

    在我看来这起罪行是可以预见的，死亡也是本可避免的。

  • One of the principles of our legal system is that people are accountable for the foreseeable consequences of their actions.

    我们的法律体系的原则之一是人们要对他们可以预见的行为后果负责。

2 PHRASE 长期地;长久地 If you say that something will happen for the foreseeable future, you think that it will continue to happen for a long time. 长期地;长久地

  • Profit and dividend growth looks like being above average for the foreseeable future.

    利润和红利的增长看上去会在较长时间内维持在平均水平之上。

3 PHRASE 在可预见的未来;不久;很快 If you say that something will happen in the foreseeable future you mean that you think it will happen fairly soon. 在可预见的未来;不久;很快

  • So, might they finally have free elections in the foreseeable future?

    那么，他们最终会在不久的将来举行自由选举吗？
