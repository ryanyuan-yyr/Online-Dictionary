landlady
lændleɪdi ˈlændˌleɪdi
Word forms: plural landladies
1 N-COUNT 女房东 Someone's landlady is the woman who allows them to live or work in a building which she owns, in return for rent. 女房东

  • We had been made homeless by our landlady.

    我们被女房东弄得无家可归。

SYN owner, landowner, proprietor, freeholder  
2 N-COUNT （酒吧的）女店主，老板娘 The landlady of a pub is the woman who owns or runs it, or the wife of the man who owns or runs it. （酒吧的）女店主，老板娘 [British]

  • ...Bet, the landlady of the Rovers Return. [+ of]

    “浪子归乡”酒吧老板娘贝特

SYN inkeeper, host, hostess, hotelier  
3 N-COUNT （家庭旅馆或客栈的）女店主 A landlady is the woman who owns or runs a boarding house or inn. （家庭旅馆或客栈的）女店主 [US, also British, old-fashioned]
