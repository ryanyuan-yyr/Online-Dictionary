snitch
snɪtʃ snɪtʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense snitches, present participle snitching, past tense , past participle snitched
1 VERB (向当权者)告发，告密 To snitch on a person means to tell someone in authority that the person has done something bad or wrong. (向当权者)告发，告密 [informal] [Also VERB]

  • She felt like a fifth-grader who had snitched on a classmate. [VERB + on]

    她觉得自己就像一个告发了同班同学的五年级学生。

2 N-COUNT 告密者;告发者 A snitch is a person who snitches on other people. 告密者;告发者 [informal]
3 VERB 偷窃 If you snitch something, you steal it quickly and quietly. 偷窃 [informal]

  • Before I'd finished reading it, she snitched my copy of To Kill a Mockingbird. [VERB noun]

    我还没有读完我那本《杀死一只知更鸟》，她就把书给偷走了。
