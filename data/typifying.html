typify
tɪpɪfaɪ ˈtɪpɪˌfaɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense typifies, present participle typifying, past tense , past participle typified
VERB 是…的典型;代表 If something or someone typifies a situation or type of thing or person, they have all the usual characteristics of it and are a typical example of it. 是…的典型;代表

  • These two buildings typify the rich extremes of Irish architecture. [VERB noun]

    这两座建筑物体现了爱尔兰建筑风格的极致多变。

  • …a reaction against the achievements of science, as typified by the development of nuclear weapons. [VERB noun]

    对以核武器发展为代表的科学成就的反对

  • …a more intelligent and articulate breed of disc jockeys, typified by university graduate Simon Mayo. [VERB noun]

    更机智、更有口才的一类电台音乐主持人，以大学毕业生西蒙·梅奥为代表

SYN represent, illustrate, sum up, characterize
