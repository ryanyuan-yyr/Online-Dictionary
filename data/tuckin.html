tuck in
1 PHRASAL VERB 把…折入;把…塞进;把…掖好 If you tuck in a piece of material, you keep it in position by placing one edge or end of it behind or under something else. For example, if you tuck in your shirt, you place the bottom part of it inside your trousers or skirt. 把…折入;把…塞进;把…掖好

  • 'Probably,' I said, tucking in my shirt. [VERB PREP. noun]

    “大概吧，”我边说边把衬衫掖了进去。

  • Tuck the sheets in firmly. [VERB noun PREP.]

    掖紧被子。

2 PHRASAL VERB 给（孩子）掖好被子；给…盖好被子 If you tuck a child in bed or tuck them in, you make them comfortable by straightening the sheets and blankets and pushing the loose ends under the mattress. 给（孩子）掖好被子；给…盖好被子

  • I read Lili a story and tucked her in her own bed. [VERB noun PREP. noun]

    我为莉莉读了个故事，然后给她盖好被子。

  • My mother would tuck me in, turn out the lights and tiptoe out. [VERB noun PREP.]

    母亲会给我掖好被子，关上灯，然后蹑手蹑脚走出去。

SYN make snug, wrap up, put to bed, bed down
