add on
1 PHRASAL VERB [usually passive] 附加;增设 If one thing is added on to another, it is attached to the other thing, or is made a part of it. 附加;增设

  • Holiday-makers can also add on a week in Majorca before or after the cruise. [VERB PREP. noun]

    度假者们可以在乘游轮游览之前或之后再在马略卡岛呆上一星期。

  • To the rear is a large dining room — added on early this century. [VERB-ed PREP.]

    后边是一个大餐厅—本世纪早期增建的。

2 PHRASAL VERB 附加；加上 If you add on an extra amount or item to a list or total, you include it. 附加；加上 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Many loan application forms automatically add on insurance. [VERB PREP. noun]

    许多贷款申请表自动附加保险。

add-on
ˈædˌɑn
Word forms: plural add-ons
N-COUNT [oft NOUN n] （尤指计算机的）附加装置，外挂，插件 An add-on is an extra piece of equipment, especially computer equipment, that can be added to a larger one which you already own in order to improve its performance or its usefulness. （尤指计算机的）附加装置，外挂，插件

  • Speakers are sold as add-ons for personal stereos. [+ for]

    扬声器作为随身听的附加装置出售。

  • Nintendo hopes to price the add-on modem at less than $200.

    任天堂希望将该外挂调制解调器的价格定在200美元以下。
