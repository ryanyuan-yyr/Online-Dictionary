neutralize
njuːtrəlaɪz nuːt- ˈnutrəˌlaɪz ˈnjutrəˌlaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense neutralizes, present participle neutralizing, past tense , past participle neutralized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 neutralise
1 VERB 使无效;使无法正常工作 To neutralize something means to prevent it from having any effect or from working properly. 使无效;使无法正常工作

  • The US is trying to neutralize the resolution in the UN Security Council.

    美国正试图在联合国安理会使该决议无效。

  • The intruder smashed a window to get in and then neutralized the alarm system. [VERB noun]

    闯入者打碎了一扇窗户进来，然后破坏了警报系统。

neutralization

  • ...the sale or neutralization of the suspected nuclear site.

    可疑核基地的出售或去功能化

2 VERB (用化学品)中和，使成为中性 When a chemical substance neutralizes an acid, it makes it less acid. (用化学品)中和，使成为中性

  • Antacids are alkaline and they relieve pain by neutralizing acid in the contents of the stomach. [VERB noun]

    抗酸剂是碱性的，通过中和胃容物中的酸来减轻疼痛。
