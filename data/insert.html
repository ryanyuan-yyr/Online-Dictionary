insert
ɪnsɜːʳt ɪnsɜːʳt ɪnˈsɜrt ˈɪnˌsɜrt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense inserts, present participle inserting, past tense , past participle inserted
The verb is pronounced /ɪn'sɜːt/. The noun is pronounced /'ɪnsɜːt/. 动词读作 /ɪn'sɜːt/。名词读作 /'ɪnsɜːt/。
1 VERB 插入;嵌入 If you insert an object into something, you put the object inside it. 插入;嵌入

  • He took a small key from his pocket and slowly inserted it into the lock. [VERB noun + into]

    他从口袋里掏出一把小钥匙,慢慢地插到锁眼里。

  • Wait for a couple of minutes with your mouth closed before inserting the thermometer. [VERB noun]

    先合上嘴巴等几分钟再放进体温计。

SYN put, place, set, position  
insertion

  • ...the first experiment involving the insertion of a new gene into a human being.

    将新基因植入人体的首次实验

2 VERB (在文章或讲话中)插入，添加 If you insert a comment into a piece of writing or a speech, you include it. (在文章或讲话中)插入，添加 [Also V n into/in n]

  • They joined with the monarchists to insert a clause calling for a popular vote on the issue. [VERB noun]

    他们与君主主义者联手添加了一个条款,呼吁对该议题进行全民投票。

SYN enter, include, introduce, interject  
insertion

  • He recorded an item for insertion in the programme.

    他录了一条消息插播到节目中。

3 N-COUNT 插入物;(尤指夹在书刊中的)活页广告 An insert is something that is inserted somewhere, especially an advertisement on a piece of paper that is placed between the pages of a book or magazine. 插入物;(尤指夹在书刊中的)活页广告
SYN insertion, addition, inclusion, supplement
