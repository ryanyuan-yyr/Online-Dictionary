exude
ɪgzjuːd ɪɡˈzjuːd -zuːd ɛgˈzjud ɛgˈzud ɪgˈzjud ɪgˈzud
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense exudes, present participle exuding, past tense , past participle exuded
1 V-ERG 充分显露 If someone exudes a quality or feeling, or if it exudes, they show that they have it to a great extent. 充分显露 [formal]

  • The guerrillas exude confidence. Every town, they say, is under their control. [VERB noun]

    游击队员显得信心十足，他们说每座城镇都在他们的控制之下。

  • She exudes an air of relaxed calm. [VERB noun]

    她表现出一副气定神闲的样子。

  • A dogged air of confidence exuded. [VERB]

    坚定不移的信心显露无遗。

SYN radiate, show, display, exhibit  
2 V-ERG （使）渗出;（使）逸出 If something exudes a liquid or smell or if a liquid or smell exudes from it, the liquid or smell comes out of it slowly and steadily. （使）渗出;（使）逸出 [formal]

  • Nearby was a factory which exuded a pungent smell. [VERB noun]

    旁边是一家散发出刺鼻气味的工厂。

  • ...the fluid that exudes from the cane toad's back. [VERB + from]

    海蟾蜍背部分泌的液体

SYN emit, leak, discharge, ooze
