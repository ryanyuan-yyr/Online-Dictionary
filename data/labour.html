labour
leɪbəʳ ˈleɪbər ˈleɪbə
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense labours, present participle labouring, past tense , past participle laboured
in AM, use 美国英语用 labor
1 N-UNCOUNT （辛苦的）劳动，劳作；（通常指）体力劳动 Labour is very hard work, usually physical work. （辛苦的）劳动，劳作；（通常指）体力劳动

  • ...the labour of seeding, planting and harvesting. [+ of]

    播种、种植和收割的辛勤劳作

  • The chef at the barbecue looked up from his labours; he was sweating.

    正在烧烤台上忙活的厨师抬起头来；他的脸上直冒汗。

  • Writing this book has been a great pleasure, a true labour of love.

    写这本书是一大乐事，辛苦绝对是心甘情愿。

2See also:hard labour 
3 VERB （用手）劳动 Someone who labours works hard using their hands. （用手）劳动

  • ...peasants labouring in the fields. [VERB]

    在地里劳作的农民

  • Her husband laboured at the plant for 17 years. [VERB]

    她丈夫在厂里辛勤工作了17年。

SYN work, toil, strive, work hard  
4 VERB 艰苦地做；吃力地做 If you labour to do something, you do it with difficulty. 艰苦地做；吃力地做

  • For twenty-five years now he has laboured to build a religious community. [VERB to-infinitive]

    他呕心沥血地建立一个宗教社区，至今已有25个年头。

  • ...a young man who's labouring under all kinds of other difficulties. [V + under]

    在其他各种困难下艰苦工作的小伙子

SYN struggle, work, strain, work hard  
5 N-UNCOUNT （总称）劳工，工人 Labour is used to refer to the workers of a country or industry, considered as a group. （总称）劳工，工人

  • Latin America lacked skilled labour.

    拉丁美洲缺乏熟练工人。

  • ...the struggle between capital and labour.

    劳资斗争

  • They were cheap labour.

    他们是廉价劳工。

6 N-UNCOUNT [oft poss NOUN] 劳动成果 The work done by a group of workers or by a particular worker is referred to as their labour . 劳动成果

  • Every man should receive a fair price for the product of his labour.

    每个人的劳动成果都应该获得合理的回报。

  • The unemployed cannot withdraw their labour — they have no power.

    失业者没法罢工—他们没有这种实力。

SYN work, effort, employment, toil  
7 N-PROPER-COLL [with singular or plural verb] （英国）工党 In Britain, people use Labour to refer to the Labour Party. （英国）工党

  • He believes that, historically, Labour has been most successful as the party that helped people get on in life.

    他相信一直以来工党在帮助人民改善生活方面表现最为出色。

  • They all vote Labour.

    他们都投工党的票。

8 ADJ 工党的；支持工党的 A Labour politician or voter is a member of a Labour Party or votes for a Labour Party. 工党的；支持工党的

  • ...a Labour MP.

    工党下院议员

  • Millions of Labour voters went unrepresented.

    数以百万计的工党支持者没有议员代表。

9 VERB 误以为；为…所蒙蔽 If you labour under a delusion or misapprehension, you continue to believe something which is not true. 误以为；为…所蒙蔽

  • She laboured under the illusion that I knew what I was doing. [V + under]

    她有一个错觉，以为我知道自己在做什么。

  • You seem to be labouring under considerable misapprehensions. [V + under]

    你似乎存在相当大的误解。

SYN be disadvantaged by, suffer from, be a victim of, be burdened by  
10 VERB 一再重申；反复说明 If you labour a point or an argument, you keep making the same point or saying the same thing, although it is unnecessary. 一再重申；反复说明

  • I don't want to labour the point but there it is. [VERB noun]

    我不想反复重申，但还是不得不说。

SYN overemphasize, stress, elaborate, exaggerate  
11 N-UNCOUNT 分娩；临盆 Labour is the last stage of pregnancy, in which the baby is gradually pushed out of the womb by the mother. 分娩；临盆

  • I thought the pains meant I was going into labour.

    我还以为这些疼痛意味着我要生了呢。

  • Some women prefer to move about during labour.

    有些女人喜欢在分娩期间下床走动。
