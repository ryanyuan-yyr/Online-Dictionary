exacerbate
ɪgzæsəʳbeɪt ɛgˈzæsərˌbeɪt ɪgˈzæsərˌbeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense exacerbates, present participle exacerbating, past tense , past participle exacerbated
VERB 使恶化;使加剧 If something exacerbates a problem or bad situation, it makes it worse. 使恶化;使加剧 [formal]

  • Mr Powell-Taylor says that depopulation exacerbates the problem. [VERB noun]

    鲍威尔-泰勒先生说人口的急剧减少使得该问题更加严重。

  • Longstanding poverty has been exacerbated by racial divisions. [be VERB-ed]

    种族分化已经加剧了长期的贫困问题。

SYN irritate, excite, provoke, infuriate
