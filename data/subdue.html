subdue
səbdjuː -duː səbˈdu səbˈdju
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense subdues, present participle subduing, past tense , past participle subdued
1 VERB 制服;征服;控制 If soldiers or the police subdue a group of people, they defeat them or bring them under control by using force. 制服;征服;控制

  • Senior government officials admit they have not been able to subdue the rebels. [VERB noun]

    政府高级官员承认他们还没能制服反叛者。

SYN overcome, defeat, master, break  
2 VERB 抑制，压制，克制(感情) To subdue feelings means to make them less strong. 抑制，压制，克制(感情)

  • He forced himself to subdue and overcome his fears. [VERB noun]

    他强迫自己克制并战胜恐惧心理。

SYN moderate, control, check, suppress
