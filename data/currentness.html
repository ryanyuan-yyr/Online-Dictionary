current
kʌrənt kɜːr- ˈkɜrənt
Word forms: plural currents
1 N-COUNT (河、湖或海的)水流，潮流 A current is a steady and continuous flowing movement of some of the water in a river, lake, or sea. (河、湖或海的)水流，潮流

  • Under normal conditions, the ocean currents of the tropical Pacific travel from east to west. [+ of]

    在正常情况下，太平洋的热带洋流自东向西流。

  • The couple were swept away by the strong current.

    这对夫妻被巨大的水流卷走了。

SYN flow, course, undertow, jet  
2 N-COUNT 气流 A current is a steady flowing movement of air. 气流

  • I felt a current of cool air blowing in my face. [+ of]

    我觉得脸上有一阵冷风吹过。

SYN draught, flow, breeze, puff  
3 N-COUNT 电流 An electric current is a flow of electricity through a wire or circuit. 电流

  • A powerful electric current is passed through a piece of graphite.

    给一块石墨通入强电流。

4 N-COUNT 思潮;潮流;趋向 A particular current is a particular feeling, idea, or quality that exists within a group of people. 思潮;潮流;趋向

  • Each party represents a distinct current of thought. [+ of]

    每个党派代表一种不同的思潮。

  • A strong current of nationalism runs through ideology and politics in the Arab world.

    一股强烈的民族主义思潮涌入阿拉伯世界的思想体系和政治体系。

SYN mood, feeling, spirit, atmosphere  
5 ADJ [usually ADJ n] 现时的;当前的;进行中的 Current means happening, being used, or being done at the present time. 现时的;当前的;进行中的

  • The current situation is very different to that in 1990.

    当前的形势与1990年截然不同。

  • He plans to repeal a number of current policies.

    他计划废除一些当前的政策。

  • When asked for your views about your current job, on no account must you be negative.

    当你被问及对目前工作的看法时，绝不要表现得消极。

currently

  • Twelve potential vaccines are currently being tested on human volunteers.

    目前正在人类志愿者身上试验12种试用疫苗。

  • He currently has no strong rivals for power.

    目前没有可以和他争夺权势的有力对手。

6 ADJ 通用的;流通的;流行的 Ideas and customs that are current are generally accepted and used by most people. 通用的;流通的;流行的

  • Current thinking suggests that toxins only have a small part to play in the build up of cellulite.

    目前普遍认为，毒素对皮下脂肪团堆积影响不大。

  • This custom was still current in the late 1960s.

    这种习俗在20世纪60年代末仍然很普遍。
