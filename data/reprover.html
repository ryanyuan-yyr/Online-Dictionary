reprove
rɪpruːv rɪˈpruv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense reproves, present participle reproving, past tense , past participle reproved
VERB 谴责;责备;斥责 If you reprove someone, you speak angrily or seriously to them because they have behaved in a wrong or foolish way. 谴责;责备;斥责 [formal]

  • 'There's no call for talk like that,' Mrs Evans reproved him. [VERB with quote]

    “没有必要说那种话，”埃文斯夫人斥责他道。

  • Women were reproved if they did not wear hats in court. [be VERB-ed]

    女性如果在法庭不戴帽子，就会受到谴责。

SYN rebuke, censure, reprimand, scold
