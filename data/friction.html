friction
frɪkʃən ˈfrɪkʃən
Word forms: plural frictions
1 N-UNCOUNT 矛盾;不和;争执 If there is friction between people, there is disagreement and argument between them. 矛盾;不和;争执

  • Sara sensed that there had been friction between her children.

    萨拉感觉到她的孩子之间有过争执。

  • The plan is likely only to aggravate ethnic frictions.

    这一方案很有可能只会加剧种族冲突。

2 N-UNCOUNT 摩擦力 Friction is the force that makes it difficult for things to move freely when they are touching each other. 摩擦力

  • The pistons are graphite-coated to reduce friction.

    活塞上涂了一层石墨以减小摩擦力。

SYN resistance, rubbing, scraping, grating  
3 N-UNCOUNT 摩擦 Friction is the rubbing of one object against another. 摩擦

  • ...the friction of his leg against hers.

    他和她的腿之间的擦蹭
