sicken
sɪkən ˈsɪkən
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense sickens, present participle sickening, past tense , past participle sickened
1 VERB 使厌恶;使作呕 If something sickens you, it makes you feel disgusted. 使厌恶;使作呕

  • The notion that art should be controlled by intellectuals sickened him. [VERB noun]

    他讨厌艺术应由知识分子所掌控的观点。

  • What he saw there sickened him, despite all his years of police work. [VERB noun]

    尽管他干了多年的警察工作，但在那里所见的一切还是让他感到恶心。

SYN disgust, revolt, nauseate, repel  
2 VERB 患病;得病;生病 If you sicken, you become ill. 患病;得病;生病 [old-fashioned]

  • Many of them sickened and died. [VERB]

    他们中的很多人都患病身亡。

SYN fall ill, take sick, ail [literary] , go down with something
