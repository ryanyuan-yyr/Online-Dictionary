dwell
dwel dwɛl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dwells, present participle dwelling, past tense , past participle dwelt, past tense , past participle dwelled
1 VERB 沉湎于,唠叨,赘述(尤指令人不快的事) If you dwell on something, especially something unpleasant, you think, speak, or write about it a lot or for quite a long time. 沉湎于,唠叨,赘述(尤指令人不快的事)

  • I'd rather not dwell on the past. [VERB + on/upon]

    我不想再沉湎于过去了。

2 VERB 居住;栖身 If you dwell somewhere, you live there. 居住;栖身 [formal]

  • They are concerned for the fate of the forest and the Indians who dwell in it. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他们为这片森林及居住于其中的印第安人的命运而担心。

  • Shiva is a dark god; he dwells in the mountains and deserts. [VERB prep./adv.]

    湿婆神匿影藏形于深山和沙漠之中。
