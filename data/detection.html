detection
dɪtekʃən diˈtɛkʃən dɪˈtɛkʃən
1 N-UNCOUNT 觉察;发觉 Detection is the act of noticing or sensing something. 觉察;发觉

  • ...the early detection of breast cancer. [+ of]

    乳腺癌的早期发觉

SYN diagnosis, spotting, recognition, observation  
2 N-UNCOUNT 发现;查出 Detection is the discovery of something which is supposed to be hidden. 发现;查出 [Also + of]

  • They are cheating but are sophisticated enough to avoid detection.

    他们在作弊，但因为手法老练没有被发现。

SYN discovery, arrest, capture, exposure  
3 N-UNCOUNT 侦查;侦破 Detection is the work of investigating a crime in order to find out what has happened and who committed it. 侦查;侦破

  • The detection rate for motor vehicle theft that year was just 11.7 per cent.

    那一年机动车盗窃案的侦破率仅为11.7%。

  • The most important deterrent for most criminals is the likelihood of detection and arrest.

    对大多数罪犯来说，最大的威慑力量是警方可能会破案并将他们逮捕。
