grey
greɪ greɪ ɡreɪ
Word forms: comparative greyer, superlative greyest
in AM, use 美国英语用 gray
1 COLOUR 灰色 Grey is the colour of ashes or of clouds on a rainy day. 灰色

  • ...a grey suit.

    灰色套装

2 ADJ-GRADED （头发）灰白的 You use grey to describe the colour of people's hair when it changes from its original colour, usually as they get old. （头发）灰白的

  • ...my grey hair.

    我灰白的头发

  • Eddie was going grey.

    埃迪的头发开始变白了。

3 ADJ-GRADED （天气）昏暗的，阴沉的 If the weather is grey, there are many clouds in the sky and the light is dull. （天气）昏暗的，阴沉的

  • It was a grey, wet April Sunday.

    这是4月一个阴雨绵绵的星期天。

SYN dull, dark, dim, gloomy  
greyness

  • ...winter's greyness.

    冬日的阴沉

4 ADJ-GRADED 沉闷的；阴郁的；暗淡的 If you describe a situation as grey, you mean that it is dull, unpleasant, or difficult. 沉闷的；阴郁的；暗淡的

  • Brazilians look gloomily forward to a New Year that even the president admits will be grey and cheerless.

    展望新年，巴西人心情沮丧，甚至连总统都承认明年困难重重，不容乐观。

SYN bleak, depressing, grim, discouraging  
greyness

  • In this new world of greyness there is an attempt to remove all risks.

    身处这个前景暗淡的新世界，人们力图排除所有的风险。

5 ADJ-GRADED 单调乏味的；无特色的；不吸引人的 If you describe someone or something as grey, you think that they are boring and unattractive, and very similar to other things or other people. 单调乏味的；无特色的；不吸引人的 [disapproval]

  • ...little grey men in suits.

    身着西服、缺乏个性的小个子男人们

SYN boring, dull, anonymous, faceless  
greyness

  • Journalists are frustrated by his apparent greyness.

    他毫无个人魅力可言，这让记者们很失望。

6 ADJ 老年人的（新闻用语） Journalists sometimes use grey to describe things concerning old people. 老年人的（新闻用语）

  • There was further evidence of grey consumer power last week, when Ford revealed a car designed with elderly people in mind.

    福特汽车公司为老年人设计的一款汽车于上周亮相，这进一步体现了老年消费者的购买力。
