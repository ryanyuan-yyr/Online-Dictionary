run up
1 PHRASAL VERB 积欠(账款、债务等) If someone runs up bills or debts, they acquire them by buying a lot of things or borrowing money. 积欠(账款、债务等)

  • He ran up a £1,400 bill at the Britannia Adelphi Hotel. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他在大不列颠阿德尔菲酒店积欠了1,400英镑的账单

  • Many ran up huge debts as they spent millions to buy foreign players. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    很多俱乐部因为斥巨资购买外援而积欠了巨额债务。

run-up
ˈrʌnʌp ˈrʌnˌʌp
Word forms: plural run-ups
1 N-SING 前奏;预备期 The run-up to an event is the period of time just before it. 前奏;预备期 [mainly British]

  • The issue of the monarchy is complicating politics in the run-up to the elections.

    就在选举拉开序幕前，君主政体的问题使政局变得复杂起来。

  • The company believes the products will sell well in the run-up to Christmas. [+ to]

    公司相信在圣诞节前这些产品的销路会不错。

SYN time leading up to, approach, build-up, preliminaries  
2 N-COUNT (投掷、跳高、跳远等之前的)助跑 In sport, a run-up is the run made by a player or athlete, for example before throwing a ball or a javelin, or before jumping. (投掷、跳高、跳远等之前的)助跑

  • When I began to compete again, I was struggling with my run-up.

    当我重新参赛后，我要解决助跑这一项。
