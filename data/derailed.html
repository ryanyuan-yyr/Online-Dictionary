derail
diːreɪl diˈreɪl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense derails, present participle derailing, past tense , past participle derailed
1 VERB 阻挠;使离开正常进程 To derail something such as a plan or a series of negotiations means to prevent it from continuing as planned. 阻挠;使离开正常进程 [journalism]

  • The present wave of political killings is the work of people trying to derail peace talks. [VERB noun]

    当前的这一系列政治谋杀乃是那些想阻挠和平谈判的人所为。

SYN prevent, stop, block, check  
2 V-ERG (使)(火车)出轨;（使）脱轨 If a train is derailed or if it derails, it comes off the track on which it is running. (使)(火车)出轨;（使）脱轨 [Also VERB n]

  • Several people were injured today when a train was derailed. [be VERB-ed]

    今天有数人在一列火车出轨时受了伤。

  • No-one knows why the train derailed. [VERB]

    没人知道火车为什么会脱轨。
