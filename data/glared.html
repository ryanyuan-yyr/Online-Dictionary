glare
gleəʳ ɡlɛə glɛr
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense glares, present participle glaring, past tense , past participle glared
1 VERB 怒目而视；瞪眼 If you glare at someone, you look at them with an angry expression on your face. 怒目而视；瞪眼

  • The old woman glared at him. [VERB + at]

    那个老妇人狠狠地瞪着他。

  • Jacob glared and muttered something. [VERB]

    雅各布瞪着眼睛，嘴里咕哝着什么。

  • ...glaring eyes. [VERB-ing]

    愤怒的眼神

SYN scowl, frown, glower, look daggers  
2 N-COUNT 怒视；瞪眼 A glare is an angry, hard, and unfriendly look. 怒视；瞪眼

  • His glasses magnified his irritable glare.

    他那怒不可遏的眼神在眼镜片下显得愈发逼人。

SYN scowl, frown, glower, dirty look  
3 VERB 发出强光；发出刺眼的光芒 If the sun or a light glares, it shines with a very bright light which is difficult to look at. 发出强光；发出刺眼的光芒

  • The sunlight glared. [VERB]

    阳光耀眼。

  • ...glaring searchlight beams. [VERB-ing]

    刺眼的探照灯光柱

4 N-UNCOUNT 强光；刺眼的光 Glare is very bright light that is difficult to look at. 强光；刺眼的光

  • ...the glare of a car's headlights.

    汽车头灯的强光

  • Special-purpose glasses reduce glare.

    专用眼镜可使强光减弱。

SYN dazzle, glow, blaze, flare  
5 N-SING 密切关注 If someone is in the glare of publicity or public attention, they are constantly being watched and talked about by a lot of people. 密切关注

  • Norma is said to dislike the glare of publicity.

    据说诺尔玛不喜欢被人过多关注。

  • She attacked police in the full glare of TV cameras.

    她在电视摄像机的镜头聚焦下公然袭击了警察。
