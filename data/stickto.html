stick to
1 PHRASAL VERB 紧随;紧跟;呆在…近旁 If you stick to something or someone when you are travelling, you stay close to them. 紧随;紧跟;呆在…近旁

  • There are interesting hikes inland, but most ramblers stick to the clifftops. [VERB PREP. noun]

    内陆地区有一些很有意思的徒步远足路线，但多数徒步者都去爬悬崖陡壁。

  • Stick to well-lit roads. [VERB PREP. noun]

    要沿着照明好的路走。

SYN keep to, persevere in, cleave to  
2 PHRASAL VERB 坚持不变 If you stick to something, you continue doing, using, saying, or talking about it, rather than changing to something else. 坚持不变

  • Perhaps he should have stuck to writing. [VERB PREP. noun]

    也许他还是一心放在写作上来得保险。

  • Lionel, you just tell the cops what you saw; stick to your story. [VERB PREP. noun]

    莱昂内尔，你就把自己看到的告诉警察，实话实说。

3 PHRASAL VERB 遵守，坚持(诺言、协议、决定或原则等) If you stick to a promise, agreement, decision, or principle, you do what you said you would do, or do not change your mind. to stick to your guns→see: gun  遵守，坚持(诺言、协议、决定或原则等)

  • Immigrant support groups are waiting to see if he sticks to his word. [VERB PREP. noun]

    移民支持团体正等着看他是否说话算话。

  • She made it clear the government would stick to its policies despite union militancy. [VERB PREP. noun]

    她明确表示，尽管工会很强悍，但政府将坚持自己的政策。

  • But one problem is that few people can stick to a diet for long. [VERB PREP. noun]

    但有一个问题是很少有人能长期坚持节食。

4 PHRASAL VERB 遵守，遵循(规则) If you stick to rules, you do what they say you must do. 遵守，遵循(规则)

  • Obviously we are disappointed but the committee could do nothing less than stick to the rules. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我们显然很失望，但委员会只能照章办事，别无他法。

  • Police must stick to the highest standards if they are to win back public confidence. [VERB PREP. noun]

    要想重新赢得公众的信任，警方必须紧遵最高标准。
