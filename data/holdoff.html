hold off
1 PHRASAL VERB 推迟;拖延 If you hold off doing something, you delay doing it or delay making a decision about it. 推迟;拖延

  • The hospital staff held off taking Rosenbaum in for an X-ray. [VERB PREP. verb-ing]

    医院工作人员推迟了罗森鲍姆的X光检查。

  • They have threatened military action but held off until now. [VERB PREP.]

    他们扬言要采取军事行动，但一直拖到现在。

SYN put off, delay, postpone, defer  
2 PHRASAL VERB （在比赛或竞赛中）抵挡住（对手的挑战） If you hold off a challenge in a race or competition, you do not allow someone to pass you. （在比赛或竞赛中）抵挡住（对手的挑战） [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Between 1987 and 1990, Steffi Graf largely held off Navratilova's challenge for the crown. [VERB PREP. noun]

    1987至1990年间，施特菲·格拉芙基本上抵挡住了纳芙拉蒂洛娃对其冠军地位的挑战。
