jammy
dʒæmi
Word forms: comparative jammier, superlative jammiest
ADJ-GRADED [usually ADJ n] 走运的；运气好的 If you describe someone as jammy, you mean that they are very lucky because something good has happened to them, without their making much effort or deserving such luck. 走运的；运气好的 [British, informal]

  • You'd think that at least he'd have the good grace to admit his blinding, jammy luck.

SYN lucky, favoured, charmed, fortunate
