spin
spɪn spɪn
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense spins, present participle spinning, past tense , past participle spun
1 V-ERG (使)旋转 If something spins or if you spin it, it turns quickly around a central point. (使)旋转

  • The latest discs, used for small portable computers, spin 3600 times a minute. [VERB]

    用在小型便携式计算机上的最新光盘转速为每分钟 3,600 转。

  • The Earth spins on its own axis. [VERB]

    地球绕着地轴自转。

  • He spun the wheel sharply and made a U turn in the middle of the road. [VERB noun]

    他猛打方向盘，在路中间掉头。

  • He spun his car round and went after them. [V n round/around]

    他掉转车头，去追他们。

SYN revolve, turn, rotate, wheel  
Spin is also a noun.

  • This driving mode allows you to move off in third gear to reduce wheel-spin in icy conditions.

    在这个驾驶模式下你可以挂 3 挡起步，以减少轮子在结冰路面上打滑的现象。

2 VERB (用甩干机或洗衣机)甩干（衣服），给…脱水 When you spin washing, it is turned round and round quickly in a spin drier or a washing machine to get the water out. (用甩干机或洗衣机)甩干（衣服），给…脱水

  • Just spin the washing and it's nearly dry. [VERB noun]

    只需要将洗好的衣物脱水，它就差不多干了。

Spin is also a noun.

  • Set on a cool wash and finish with a short spin.

    设定用凉水洗衣，最后短时脱水。

3 VERB (因醉酒、生病或兴奋而)头晕 If your head is spinning, you feel unsteady or confused, for example because you are drunk, ill, or excited. (因醉酒、生病或兴奋而)头晕

  • My head was spinning from the wine. [VERB]

    我喝了葡萄酒后晕乎乎的。

  • All those figures make my poor head spin. [VERB]

    那么多数字把我搞得头昏脑胀。

SYN reel, swim, whirl, be giddy  
4 N-SING 带倾向性的解释(或说法) If someone puts a certain spin on an event or situation, they interpret it and try to present it in a particular way. 带倾向性的解释(或说法) [informal]

  • He interpreted the vote as support for the constitution and that is the spin his supporters are putting on the results today.

    他把这次投票解释为对宪法的支持，这也是他的支持者对今天的投票结果所作的解释。

  • ...the wholly improper political spin given to the report, particularly by The New York Times.

    报道中带有的根本不恰当的政治倾向，特别是《纽约时报》的报道

SYN bias, prejudice, slant, turn  
5See also:spin doctor 
6 N-UNCOUNT (政治上的)倾向性报道 In politics, spin is the way in which political parties try to present everything they do in a positive way to the public and the media. (政治上的)倾向性报道

  • The public is sick of spin and tired of promises. It's time for politicians to act.

    公众厌烦了那些有倾向性的报道和种种许诺。该是用政绩说话的时候了。

7 N-SING (乘车)兜风 If you go for a spin or take a car for a spin, you make a short journey in a car just to enjoy yourself. (乘车)兜风
8 VERB 杜撰;虚构;编造 If someone spins a story, they give you an account of something that is untrue or only partly true. 杜撰;虚构;编造 [Also VERB n n]

  • He was surprised, and annoyed that she had spun a story which was too good to be condemned as a simple lie. [VERB noun]

    他很惊讶，也很生气，因为她编的故事太真实了，甚至不能被斥为一个简单的谎言。

SYN tell, relate, recount, develop  
9 VERB 纺纱;纺线 When people spin, they make thread by twisting together pieces of a fibre such as wool or cotton using a device or machine. 纺纱;纺线 [Also VERB]

  • Michelle will also spin a customer's wool fleece to specification at a cost of $2.25 an ounce. [VERB noun]

    米歇尔也会按顾客的要求为其纺羊毛呢，每盎司收费 2.25 美元。

spinning

  • They do their own cooking, spinning, and woodworking.

    他们自己做饭，纺线，做木工活。

10 N-SING [a NOUN] (飞机的)螺旋式急遽下坠，向下旋冲 If a plane goes into a spin, it falls very rapidly towards the ground in a spiral movement. (飞机的)螺旋式急遽下坠，向下旋冲
11 N-UNCOUNT (网球或板球运动中球的）快速旋转，旋转运动 In a game such as tennis or cricket, if you put spin on a ball, you deliberately make it spin rapidly when you hit it or throw it. (网球或板球运动中球的）快速旋转，旋转运动
12 PHRASE [verb-link PHRASE] 感到惊慌失措 If you say that someone is in a spin or in a flat spin, you mean that they are confused and unable to act sensibly because of something that has happened. 感到惊慌失措 [British, informal]

  • Poor Jane was in rather a spin about the party.

    可怜的简对这次聚会真有些不知所措。
