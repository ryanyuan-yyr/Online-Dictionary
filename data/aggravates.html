aggravate
ægrəveɪt ˈægrəˌveɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense aggravates, present participle aggravating, past tense , past participle aggravated
1 VERB 加重；使恶化；加剧 If someone or something aggravates a situation, they make it worse. 加重；使恶化；加剧

  • Stress and lack of sleep can aggravate the situation. [VERB noun]

    压力和睡眠不足会使情况恶化。

  • He would only aggravate the injury by rubbing it. [VERB noun]

    他揉擦伤口只会使伤势加重。

SYN make worse, exaggerate, intensify, worsen  
2 VERB 激怒；使恼火 If someone or something aggravates you, they make you annoyed. 激怒；使恼火 [informal]

  • What aggravates you most about this country? [VERB noun]

    该国的哪一点让你最恼火？

SYN annoy, bother, provoke, needle [informal]  
aggravating

  • You don't realise how aggravating you can be.

    你没有意识到自己有多么气人。

aggravation
