bury
beri ˈbɛri
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense buries, present participle burying, past tense , past participle buried
1 VERB 掩埋；埋藏 To bury something means to put it into a hole in the ground and cover it up with earth. 掩埋；埋藏

  • They make the charcoal by burying wood in the ground and then slowly burning it. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他们通过把木材埋到地下缓慢燃烧来获取木炭。

  • ...squirrels who bury nuts and seeds. [VERB noun]

    掩埋坚果和种子的松鼠

  • ...buried treasure. [VERB-ed]

    被埋藏起来的财宝

2 VERB 埋葬；安葬 To bury a dead person means to put their body into a grave and cover it with earth. 埋葬；安葬

  • ...soldiers who helped to bury the dead in large communal graves. [VERB noun]

    帮忙把死者安葬在大型公共墓地的士兵们

  • I was horrified that people would think I was dead and bury me alive. [VERB noun adj.ive]

    想到人们会认为我死了并把我活埋，我觉得恐惧万分。

  • More than 9,000 men lie buried here. [VERB-ed]

    这里埋葬了9,000多人。

SYN inter, lay to rest, entomb, sepulchre  
3 VERB 失去（亲人） If someone says they have buried one of their relatives, they mean that one of their relatives has died. 失去（亲人）

  • He had buried his wife some two years before he retired. [VERB noun]

    大概在他退休前两年，他妻子去世了。

4 VERB 掩藏；埋藏 If you bury something under a large quantity of things, you put it there, often in order to hide it. 掩藏；埋藏

  • She buried it under some leaves. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    她把它藏在一堆树叶下面。

  • I was looking for my handbag, which was buried under a pile of old newspapers. [be VERB-ed prep./adv.]

    我正在找我那被埋在一堆旧报纸下面的手提包。

SYN hide, cover, conceal, stash [informal]  
5 VERB 覆盖；掩埋；把…埋在下面 If something buries a place or person, it falls on top of them so that it completely covers them and often harms them in some way. 覆盖；掩埋；把…埋在下面

  • Latest reports say that mud slides buried entire villages. [VERB noun]

    最新报道说泥石流掩埋了整个整个的村庄。

  • Their house was buried by a landslide. [VERB noun]

    他们的房子在一次塌方中被掩埋。

  • He was buried under the debris for several hours. [VERB-ed]

    他被埋在废墟下面达好几个小时。

6 VERB （因不高兴而）把（头或脸）贴（或靠）在… If you bury your head or face in something, you press your head or face against it, often because you are unhappy. （因不高兴而）把（头或脸）贴（或靠）在…

  • She buried her face in the pillows . [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    她把脸埋进枕头里。

  • He held her closely, burying his head against her shoulder. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他紧紧地抱住她，把头靠在她的肩上。

SYN sink, embed, immerse, enfold  
7 VERB 深藏；深埋 If something buries itself somewhere, or if you bury it there, it is pushed very deeply in there. 深藏；深埋 [Also VERB n preposition/adverb]

  • The missile buried itself deep in the grassy hillside. [VERB pronoun-reflexive prep./adv.]

    导弹深深地埋在杂草丛生的山坡上。

  • He stood on the sidewalk with his hands buried in the pockets of his dark overcoat. [VERB-ed]

    他站在人行道上，双手插在黑色外套的口袋里。

SYN embed, sink, implant, drive in  
8 VERB 掩藏（感情）；抛开（记忆） If you bury a feeling, you try not to show it. If you bury a memory, you try to forget it. 掩藏（感情）；抛开（记忆） [written]

  • When we feel anger, we bury the emotion and feel guilty instead. [VERB noun]

    当我们感到愤怒时，压抑这种情绪反而会觉得内疚。

  • It is time to bury our past misunderstandings. [VERB noun]

    是我们捐弃前嫌的时候了。

  • ...deeply-buried memories. [VERB-ed]

    深藏于心的记忆

SYN forget, break with, draw a veil over, think no more of  
9 VERB 藏身于；埋头于；专心于 If you bury yourself in a place or in an activity such as your work, you spend all your time in that place or doing that activity, usually because you want to forget about things. 藏身于；埋头于；专心于

  • His reaction was to withdraw, to bury himself in work. [V pron-refl in n]

    他的反应是抽身而退，埋头于工作。

  • ...the popular image of writers burying themselves in the country in order to write. [V pron-refl in n]

    常见的退隐乡间写作的作家形象

SYN engross, involve, occupy, interest  
10 VERB 沉浸于；专心致志于 If you bury your head in something such as a book or newspaper, or bury yourself in it, you look at it closely and concentrate very hard on it. 沉浸于；专心致志于

  • My father buried his head in his newspaper. [V n in n]

    我父亲专心致志地看他的报纸。

  • He buried himself in his detective story again. [V pron-refl in n]

    他又沉浸在他的侦探故事里了。
