frustrate
frʌstreɪt frʌˈstreɪt frʌstreɪt ˈfrʌsˌtreɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense frustrates, present participle frustrating, past tense , past participle frustrated
1 VERB 使灰心;使沮丧;使愤怒 If something frustrates you, it upsets or angers you because you are unable to do anything about the problems it creates. 使灰心;使沮丧;使愤怒

  • These questions frustrated me. [VERB noun]

    这些问题让我沮丧。

  • Doesn't it frustrate you that audiences in the theatre are so restricted? [VERB noun]

    观众在剧场里要受到如此多的限制，这难道不令人恼火吗？

SYN discourage, anger, depress, annoy  
frustrated

  • Roberta felt frustrated and angry.

    罗伯塔既灰心又生气。

  • ...voters who are frustrated with the council.

    对市政会感到失望的选民

frustration

  • The results show the level of frustration among hospital doctors.

    这些结果显示了医院医生的不满程度。

  • ...a man fed up with the frustrations of everyday life.

    受够了日常生活中各种失意挫折的男子

2 VERB 挫败;阻挠…的成功 If someone or something frustrates a plan or attempt to do something, they prevent it from succeeding. 挫败;阻挠…的成功

  • The government has deliberately frustrated his efforts to gain work permits for his foreign staff. [VERB noun]

    政府故意阻挠他为自己的外籍员工申请工作许可证的努力。

  • ...her frustrated attempt to become governor. [VERB-ed]

    她竞选州长的受挫之举

SYN thwart, stop, check, block
