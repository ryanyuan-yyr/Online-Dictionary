unsurprising
ʌnsəʳpraɪzɪŋ ˌʌnsəˈpraɪzɪŋ
ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ, oft ADJ that] 不出所料的；不出奇的；意料中的 If something is unsurprising, you are not surprised by it because you would expect it to happen or be like it is. 不出所料的；不出奇的；意料中的

  • It is unsurprising that he remains so hated.

    他还是那么乞人憎这一事实并不令人觉得奇怪。

  • His choice was unsurprising.

    他作出这样的选择并不出人意料。

SYN predictable, expected, understandable, predicted  
unsurprisingly

  • Unsurprisingly, not everyone agrees that things are better.

    不出所料，并非人人都认同情况有所好转。

  • The proposals were swiftly and unsurprisingly rejected by Western ministers.

    一如所料，这些提议当即遭到西方各部长的反对。
