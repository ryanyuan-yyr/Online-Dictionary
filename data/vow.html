vow
vaʊ vaʊ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense vows, present participle vowing, past tense , past participle vowed
1 VERB 发誓;起誓;立誓 If you vow to do something, you make a serious promise or decision that you will do it. 发誓;起誓;立誓

  • While many models vow to go back to college, few do. [VERB to-infinitive]

    很多模特发誓要重回大学校园，但很少有人做到。

  • I solemnly vowed that someday I would return to live in Europe. [VERB that]

    我郑重发誓，总有一天我将回到欧洲生活。

  • 'I'll kill him,' she vowed. [VERB with quote]

    “我要宰了他，”她发誓说。

  • They have vowed a quick and decisive response. [VERB noun]

    他们立誓要给出一个快速果断的回应。

SYN promise, pledge, swear, commit  
2 N-COUNT [oft NOUN to-infinitive, NOUN that] 誓言;诺言 A vow is a serious promise or decision to do a particular thing. 誓言;诺言

  • I made a silent vow to be more careful in the future.

    我暗暗发誓，日后要倍加小心。

  • I had to admire David's vow that he would leave the programme.

    戴维发誓要离开这个项目，我不得不对他表示钦佩。

3 N-COUNT [usually plural] 誓词;誓约;宣言 Vows are a particular set of serious promises, such as the promises two people make when they are getting married. 誓词;誓约;宣言

  • I took my marriage vows and kept them.

    我结婚时许下誓言，并一直信守。

  • ...a nun who had taken final vows.

    已立誓出家的尼姑

  • He had broken his vow of poverty. [+ of]

    他违背了他安贫乐道的誓言。
