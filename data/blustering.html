bluster
blʌstəʳ ˈblʌstər
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense blusters, present participle blustering, past tense , past participle blustered
VERB 咆哮；叫嚷 If you say that someone is blustering, you mean that they are speaking aggressively but without authority, often because they are angry or offended. 咆哮；叫嚷

  • 'That's lunacy,' he blustered. [VERB with quote]

    “那真是疯了，”他吼道。

  • He was still blustering, but there was panic in his eyes. [VERB]

    他嘴上还在叫嚷着，但眼里却流露出恐慌。

SYN boast, swagger, talk big [slang]  
Bluster is also a noun.

  • ...the bluster of the Conservatives' campaign.

    保守党来势汹汹的宣传运动
