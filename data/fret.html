fret
fret frɛt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense frets, present participle fretting, past tense , past participle fretted
1 VERB 苦恼;烦恼;发愁 If you fret about something, you worry about it. 苦恼;烦恼;发愁

  • I was working all hours and constantly fretting about everyone else's problems. [V + about/over]

    我一刻不闲地工作，总是为别人的事操心。

  • But congressional staffers fret that the project will eventually cost billions more. [VERB that]

    但是国会工作人员担心这项计划最终会多耗资数十亿元。

  • Don't fret, Mary. This is all some crazy mistake. [VERB]

    不要发愁，玛丽。这只是个有些荒唐的错误。

SYN worry, anguish, brood, agonize  
2 N-COUNT (吉他等弦乐器琴颈处的)品 The frets on a musical instrument such as a guitar are the raised lines across its neck. (吉他等弦乐器琴颈处的)品
