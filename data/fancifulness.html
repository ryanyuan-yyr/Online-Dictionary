fanciful
fænsɪfʊl ˈfænsəfəl
1 ADJ-GRADED 空想的;异想天开的 If you describe an idea as fanciful, you disapprove of it because you think it comes from someone's imagination, and is therefore unrealistic or unlikely to be true. 空想的;异想天开的 [disapproval]

  • ...fanciful ideas about Martian life.

    有关火星生命的奇思异想

  • Designing silicon chips to mimic human organs sounds fanciful.

    设计硅芯片模仿人的器官听上去是异想天开。

SYN unreal, wild, ideal, romantic  
2 ADJ-GRADED 奇异的;古怪的;花哨的 If you describe the appearance of something as fanciful, you mean that it is unusual and elaborate rather than plain and simple. 奇异的;古怪的;花哨的

  • The economic gloom of the early 1980s was relieved by fanciful architecture.

    20 世纪 80 年代初期的经济萧条气氛因各种奇异古怪建筑的出现而有所缓解。
