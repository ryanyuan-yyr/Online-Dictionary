unseat
ʌnsiːt ʌnˈsiːt ʌnˈsit
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense unseats, present participle unseating, past tense , past participle unseated
1 VERB 把…革职；罢免 When people try to unseat a person who is in an important job or position, they try to remove him or her from that job or position. 把…革职；罢免

  • It is still not clear who was behind Sunday's attempt to unseat the President. [VERB noun]

    目前还不清楚星期天发动的企图推翻总统的行动的幕后指使人是谁。

  • Only two US representatives were unseated.

    只有两位美国代表被免职。

SYN depose, overthrow, oust, remove  
2 VERB 使从马上摔下 If a horse unseats its rider, it causes him or her to fall off. 使从马上摔下

  • Crystal Spirit unseated his rider in the Berkshire Hurdle at Newbury. [VERB noun]

    在纽伯里举行的伯克郡障碍赛中“水玉精灵”把骑师掀了下来。

  • She was unseated on her first ride. [VERB noun]

    她第一次骑马就从马上摔了下来。

SYN throw, unsaddle, unhorse
