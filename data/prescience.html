prescient
presiənt preʃ-
ADJ-GRADED 预知的；预见性的；能预见未来的 If you say that someone or something was prescient, you mean that they were able to know or predict what was going to happen in the future. 预知的；预见性的；能预见未来的 [formal]

  • ...'Bob Roberts', an eerily prescient comedy about a populist multimillionaire political candidate.

    《天生赢家》，一部关于一位拥有数千万家财的平民党政治候选人的怪诞预言性喜剧

SYN foresighted, psychic, prophetic, divining  
prescience

  • Over the years he's demonstrated a certain prescience in foreign affairs.

    这些年来，他已经在外交事务方面表现出一定的预见性。
