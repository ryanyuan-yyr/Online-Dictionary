dilate
daɪleɪt ˈdaɪˌleɪt daɪˈleɪt dəˈleɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dilates, present participle dilating, past tense , past participle dilated
V-ERG (使)(血管)扩张;(使)(瞳孔)放大 When things such as blood vessels or the pupils of your eyes dilate or when something dilates them, they become wider or bigger. (使)(血管)扩张;(使)(瞳孔)放大

  • At night, the pupils dilate to allow in more light. [VERB]

    到了晚上，瞳孔就会扩大以接收更多光线。

  • Exercise dilates blood vessels on the surface of the brain. [VERB noun]

    运动会使大脑表层的血管扩张。

SYN enlarge, extend, stretch, expand  
dilated

  • His eyes seemed slightly dilated.

    他的瞳孔似乎略微放大。
