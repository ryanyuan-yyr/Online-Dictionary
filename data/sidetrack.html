sidetrack
saɪdtræk ˈsaɪdˌtræk
(also side-track)Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense sidetracks, present participle sidetracking, past tense , past participle sidetracked
VERB 使分散思路;使转变话题;使转移目标 If you are sidetracked by something, it makes you forget what you intended to do or say, and start instead doing or talking about a different thing. 使分散思路;使转变话题;使转移目标

  • He'd managed to avoid being sidetracked by Schneider's problems. [be VERB-ed]

    他最终总算没有因施奈德的问题而分心。

  • The leadership moved to sidetrack the proposal. [VERB noun]

    领导层采取行动来转移对该提议的关注。

  • They have a tendency to try to sidetrack you from your task. [VERB noun + from]

    他们总试图让你从你的任务中分心。

SYN distract, divert, lead off the subject, deflect
