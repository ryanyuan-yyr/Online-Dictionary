intelligence
ɪntelɪdʒəns ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns
1 N-UNCOUNT 聪颖;智慧;灵性 Intelligence is the quality of being intelligent or clever. 聪颖;智慧;灵性

  • She's a woman of exceptional intelligence.

    她是个有着非凡智慧的女子。

SYN intellect, understanding, brains [informal] , mind  
2 N-UNCOUNT 智力;智能 Intelligence is the ability to think, reason, and understand instead of doing things automatically or by instinct. 智力;智能

  • Nerve cells, after all, do not have intelligence of their own.

    毕竟,神经细胞自身并没有智力。

3 N-UNCOUNT [oft NOUN n] 情报;谍报 Intelligence is information that is gathered by the government or the army about their country's enemies and their activities. 情报;谍报

  • She first moved into the intelligence services 22 years ago.

    22年前她初次进入情报部门。

  • The purpose of intelligence is to provide information on how the enemy can be beaten.

    谍报的目的是提供如何打败敌人的信息。

  • Why was military intelligence so lacking?

    为什么军事情报如此匮乏？

SYN espionage, spying, surveillance, information gathering
