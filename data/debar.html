debar
dɪbɑːʳ diː- diˈbɑr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense debars, present participle debarring, past tense , past participle debarred
VERB [usually passive] (法律或法规）禁止 If you are debarred from doing something, you are prevented from doing it by a law or regulation. (法律或法规）禁止 [formal] [Also be V-ed from -ing]

  • If found guilty, she could be debarred from politics for seven years. [be VERB-ed + from]

    如果罪名成立，她可能在 7 年内不得从政。

  • Convictions for criminal offences might lead to the persons concerned being debarred from entering the teaching profession. [be V-ed from n/-ing]

    因刑事罪名被定罪的罪犯将可能被禁止从事教师职业。

SYN bar, exclude, prohibit, black
