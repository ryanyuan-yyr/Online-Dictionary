ascribe
əskraɪb əˈskraɪb
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense ascribes, present participle ascribing, past tense , past participle ascribed
1 VERB 把…归因于；把…归咎于 If you ascribe an event or condition to a particular cause, you say or consider that it was caused by that thing. 把…归因于；把…归咎于 [formal]

  • An autopsy eventually ascribed the baby's death to sudden infant death syndrome. [VERB noun + to]

    尸检最终认定孩子死于婴儿猝死综合征。

SYN attribute, credit, refer, charge  
2 VERB 认为是…的特点;认为…具有 If you ascribe a quality to someone, you consider that they possess it. 认为是…的特点;认为…具有 [formal]

  • We do not ascribe a superior wisdom to government or the state. [VERB noun + to]

    我们并不认为政府或是国家有超凡的智慧。

3 VERB 认为…是…所说（或所为） If you ascribe something such as a quotation or a work of art to someone, you say that they said it or created it. 认为…是…所说（或所为） [formal]

  • He mistakenly ascribes the expression 'survival of the fittest' to Charles Darwin. [VERB noun + to]

    他错误地认为“适者生存”是查尔斯·达尔文说的。
