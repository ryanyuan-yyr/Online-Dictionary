foreboding
fɔːʳboʊdɪŋ fɔrˈboʊdɪŋ ˈfɔrˌboʊdɪŋ
Word forms: plural forebodings
1 N-VAR (不祥的)预兆，预感 Foreboding is a strong feeling that something terrible is going to happen. (不祥的)预兆，预感

  • His triumph was overshadowed by an uneasy sense of foreboding.

    他的胜利因为一种令人不安的不祥预感蒙上了阴影。

2 ADJ-GRADED 给人不祥预感的 If you describe something as foreboding, you mean that it makes you feel that something terrible is going to happen. 给人不祥预感的

  • Prisons like Strangeways, built more than 100 years ago, were intended to look grim and foreboding places.

    诸如斯特兰奇韦斯监狱之类建于 100 多年前的监狱故意营造出一种阴森、不祥的气氛。
