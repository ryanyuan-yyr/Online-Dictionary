gawk
gɔːk gɔk
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense gawks, present participle gawking, past tense , past participle gawked
VERB 呆呆地看；无礼地瞪视 To gawk at someone or something means to stare at them in a rude, stupid, or unthinking way. 呆呆地看；无礼地瞪视 [informal]

  • The youth continued to gawk at her and did not answer. [VERB + at]

    那个年轻人依然傻乎乎地望着她，没有答话。

  • Tens of thousands came to gawk. [VERB]

    成千上万的人赶来看热闹。

SYN stare, gape, goggle, ogle
