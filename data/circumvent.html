circumvent
sɜːʳkəmvent ˌsɜrkəmˈvɛnt ˈsɜrkəmˌvɛnt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense circumvents, present participle circumventing, past tense , past participle circumvented
1 VERB 规避，逃避(规则或限制) If someone circumvents a rule or restriction, they avoid having to obey the rule or restriction, in a clever and perhaps dishonest way. 规避，逃避(规则或限制) [formal]

  • Military planners tried to circumvent the treaty. [VERB noun]

    军事策略家们企图绕开这一条约。

SYN evade, bypass, elude, steer clear of  
circumvention

  • America won't countenance any such circumvention of the sanctions.

    美国不会赞成任何规避制裁的行为。

2 VERB 躲避，回避(尤指意欲伤害自己的人) If you circumvent someone, you cleverly prevent them from achieving something, especially when they are trying to harm you. 躲避，回避(尤指意欲伤害自己的人) [formal]

  • Roosevelt occasionally attempted to circumvent him. [VERB noun]

    罗斯福偶尔会试图避开他。
