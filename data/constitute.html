constitute
kɒnstɪtjuːt -tuːt ˈkɑnstəˌtut ˈkɑnstəˌtjut
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense constitutes, present participle constituting, past tense , past participle constituted
1 V-LINK [no cont] 被视为;可算作 If something constitutes a particular thing, it can be regarded as being that thing. 被视为;可算作

  • Testing patients without their consent would constitute a professional and legal offence. [VERB noun]

    未经患者同意而对其进行检查被视为违反职业操守并触犯法律。

  • The vote hardly constitutes a victory. [VERB noun]

    这次投票很难被视为一次胜利。

  • What constitutes abuse? [VERB noun]

    怎样算是虐待？

SYN represent, be, consist of, embody  
2 V-LINK [no cont] 构成;组成 If a number of things or people constitute something, they are the parts or members that form it. 构成;组成

  • Volunteers constitute more than 95% of The Center's work force. [VERB noun]

    该中心95%以上的工作人员是志愿者。

  • ...the four companies constituting the Aramco partnership. [VERB noun]

    组成阿美合伙公司的4家公司

3 VERB [usually passive] 设立，成立(委员会、政府等) When something such as a committee or government is constituted, it is formally established and given authority to operate. 设立，成立(委员会、政府等) [formal]

  • On 6 July a People's Revolutionary Government was constituted. [be VERB-ed]

    7月6日，人民革命政府正式成立。

  • The accused will appear before a specially constituted military tribunal. [VERB-ed]

    被告将会面临特别军事法庭的审判。

SYN set up, found, name, create
