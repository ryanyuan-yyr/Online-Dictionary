fatigue
fətiːg fəˈtig
Word forms: plural fatigues
1 N-UNCOUNT 疲惫;疲劳;疲倦 Fatigue is a feeling of extreme physical or mental tiredness. 疲惫;疲劳;疲倦

  • She continued to have severe stomach cramps, aches, fatigue, and depression.

    她仍然患有严重的胃痉挛，感觉疼痛、疲乏、抑郁。

  • Clarke says his team could have lasted another 15 days before fatigue would have begun to take a toll.

    克拉克说，他的团队本来还可以再坚持 15 天，只是疲劳可能会造成严重后果，只好作罢。

2 N-UNCOUNT [usually n NOUN] 疲劳;厌倦 You can say that people are suffering from a particular kind of fatigue when they have been doing something for a long time and feel they can no longer continue to do it. 疲劳;厌倦

  • ...compassion fatigue caused by endless TV and celebrity appeals.

    没完没了的电视节目和名人呼吁而导致的同情心疲劳

  • ...the result of four months of battle fatigue.

    4 个月的战斗疲劳带来的后果

3 N-PLURAL (士兵的)作训服，工作服 Fatigues are clothes that soldiers wear when they are fighting or when they are doing routine jobs. (士兵的)作训服，工作服

  • He never expected to return home wearing US combat fatigues.

    他从没想到会穿着美国军服回家。

4 N-UNCOUNT [usually n NOUN] (金属或木材的)疲劳 Fatigue in metal or wood is a weakness in it that is caused by repeated stress. Fatigue can cause the metal or wood to break. (金属或木材的)疲劳

  • The problem turned out to be metal fatigue in the fuselage.

    问题原来是飞机机身出现金属疲劳。
