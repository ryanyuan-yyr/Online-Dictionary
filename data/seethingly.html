seethe
siːð sið
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense seethes, present participle seething, past tense , past participle seethed
1 VERB 强压怒火;生闷气 When you are seething, you are very angry about something but do not express your feelings about it. 强压怒火;生闷气

  • She took it calmly at first but under the surface was seething. [VERB]

    她起初平静地接受了，但内心却气呼呼的。

  • She grinned derisively while I seethed with rage. [VERB + with]

    我怒火中烧，她却嘲弄地咧着嘴笑。

  • He is seething at all the bad press he is getting. [V at]

    他正为所有那些关于自己的负面报道窝着一肚子火。

  • ...a seething anger fueled by decades of political oppression.

    数十年的政治压迫燃起的更加炽烈的怒火

2 VERB 充满，遍布，到处都是(人或物) If you say that a place is seething with people or things, you are emphasizing that it is very full of them and that they are all moving about. 充满，遍布，到处都是(人或物) [emphasis] [Also VERB]

  • The forest below him seethed and teemed with life. [VERB + with]

    他下面的森林里处处生机勃勃。

  • Madrigueras station was a seething mass of soldiers. [VERB-ing]

    马德里古埃拉斯车站到处都是士兵。

SYN teem, be full of, abound, swarm
