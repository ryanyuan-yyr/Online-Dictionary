underline
ʌndəʳlaɪn ˈʌndərˌlaɪn ˌ ʌndərˈlaɪn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense underlines, present participle underlining, past tense , past participle underlined
1 VERB 强调；凸显 If one thing, for example an action or an event, underlines another, it draws attention to it and emphasizes its importance. 强调；凸显 [Also VERB that]

  • The report underlined his concern that standards were at risk. [VERB noun]

    报告强调了他对道德水准可能会受影响所表示的关注。

  • The decision to keep him in hospital for a second night underlines the seriousness of his injury. [VERB noun]

    让他在医院再住一晚的决定表明了其伤势的严重性。

  • But the incident underlines how easily things can go wrong. [VERB wh]

    但这一事件也突出表明出差错是多么容易。

SYN emphasize, stress, highlight, bring home  
2 VERB 在…下画线 If you underline something such as a word or a sentence, you draw a line underneath it in order to make people notice it or to give it extra importance. 在…下画线

  • Underline the following that apply to you. [VERB noun]

    在符合自身情况的项下画线。

  • Take two coloured pens and underline the positive and negative words. [VERB noun]

    用两支彩笔分别在表示肯定和否定意义的词下面画线。

SYN underscore, mark, italicize, rule a line under
