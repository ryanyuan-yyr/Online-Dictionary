homogenize
həmɒdʒənaɪz həˈmɑdʒəˌnaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense homogenizes, present participle homogenizing, past tense , past participle homogenized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 homogenise
1 VERB （尤指不适宜地）使统一，使单一，使类同 If something is homogenized, it is changed so that all its parts are similar or the same, especially in a way that is undesirable. （尤指不适宜地）使统一，使单一，使类同 [disapproval]

  • Even Brussels bureaucrats can't homogenize national cultures and tastes. [VERB noun]

    哪怕是布鲁塞尔的官僚也无法使民族文化和品位单一化。

SYN unite, make uniform, combine, blend  
homogenized

  • ...an increasingly homogenised and bland America.

    变得越来越一元化和乏味的美国

homogenization

  • They no longer worry about the homogenization of culture.

    他们不再担心文化的同质化。
