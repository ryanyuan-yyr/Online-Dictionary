bring down
1 PHRASAL VERB 使（政府或当权者）垮台 When people or events bring down a government or ruler, they cause the government or ruler to lose power. 使（政府或当权者）垮台

  • They were threatening to bring down the government by withdrawing from the ruling coalition. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他们威胁要退出执政联盟，让政府垮台。

  • His challenge to Mrs Thatcher brought her down. [VERB noun PREP.]

    他的挑战导致了撒切尔夫人的下台。

SYN overturn, reduce, undermine, overthrow  
2 PHRASAL VERB 射落；击落；击倒 If someone or something brings down a person or aeroplane, they cause them to fall, usually by shooting them. 射落；击落；击倒 [Also VERB n PREPOSITION]

  • Military historians may never know what brought down the jet. [VERB PREP. noun]

    军事史学家们可能永远也不会知道是什么击落了这架喷气式飞机。
