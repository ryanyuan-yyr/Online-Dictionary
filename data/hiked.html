hike
haɪk haɪk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense hikes, present participle hiking, past tense , past participle hiked
1 N-COUNT 远足;（尤指）徒步旅行 A hike is a long walk in the country, especially one that you go on for pleasure. 远足;（尤指）徒步旅行
2 VERB 远足;徒步旅行 If you hike, you go for a long walk in the country. 远足;徒步旅行 [Also VERB]

  • You could hike through the Fish River Canyon. [VERB prep./adv.]

    你可以徒步穿越鱼河峡谷。

  • We plan to hike the Samaria Gorge. [VERB noun]

    我们计划去撒马利亚峡谷远足。

SYN walk, march, trek, ramble  
hiking

  • ...some harder, more strenuous hiking on cliff pathways.

    沿着悬崖峭壁上的羊肠小路前行的更艰难费力的徒步旅行

  • ...heavy hiking boots.

    沉重的登山靴

3 N-COUNT （价格、比率、税率或数量的）突然上涨,大幅上升 A hike is a sudden or large increase in prices, rates, taxes, or quantities. （价格、比率、税率或数量的）突然上涨,大幅上升 [informal]

  • ...a sudden 1.75 per cent hike in Italian interest rates.

    意大利利率1.75%的突然上浮

  • His economic plan, with its tax hikes and spending cuts, will slow the economy.

    他通过采取上调税率和削减支出等经济计划,将会使经济发展放慢速度。

4 VERB 使（价格、比率、税率或数量）突然上升，大幅提高 To hike prices, rates, taxes, or quantities means to increase them suddenly or by a large amount. 使（价格、比率、税率或数量）突然上升，大幅提高 [informal]

  • It has now been forced to hike its rates by 5.25 per cent. [VERB noun]

    它目前已经被迫将利率提高了5.25%。

  • TSB's monthly credit card rate is now hiked from 1.95% to 2.05%. [VERB noun]

    TSB银行的信用卡月利率现在从1.95%提高到了2.05%。

  • The federal government hiked the tax on hard liquor. [VERB noun]

    联邦政府提高了烈性酒的税率。

SYN increase, raise, inflate, bump up [informal]  
Hike up means the same as hike .hike up 同 hike

  • The insurers have started hiking up premiums by huge amounts.

    保险公司已经开始大幅度提高保费。

  • Big banks were hiking their rates up.

    各大银行正在提高利率。
