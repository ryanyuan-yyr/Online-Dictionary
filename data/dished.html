dish
dɪʃ dɪʃ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense dishes, present participle dishing, past tense , past participle dished
1 N-COUNT 盘;碟;碗 A dish is a shallow container with a wide uncovered top. You eat and serve food from dishes and cook food in them. 盘;碟;碗

  • ...plastic bowls and dishes.

    塑料碗碟

  • Pile potatoes into a warm serving dish.

    把土豆倒在一个保温的盛菜用的盘子里。

SYN bowl, plate, platter, salver  
2 N-COUNT 一盘（或一碟）的量 The contents of a dish can be referred to as a dish of something. 一盘（或一碟）的量

  • Nicholas ate a dish of spaghetti. [+ of]

    尼古拉斯吃了一盘意大利面条。

3 N-COUNT 一盘菜;菜肴;食品 Food that is prepared in a particular style or combination can be referred to as a dish . 一盘菜;菜肴;食品

  • This dish is best served cold.

    这菜最好凉着吃。

  • There are plenty of vegetarian dishes to choose from.

    有多道素菜可供选择。

  • ...a delicious fish dish.

    一道美味的鱼

SYN food, meal, fare, course  
4 N-PLURAL (就餐时用过的)所有餐具 All the objects that have been used to cook, serve, and eat a meal can be referred to as the dishes . (就餐时用过的)所有餐具

  • There were dirty dishes in the sink.

    水槽里放着脏碗。

  • He'd cooked dinner and washed the dishes.

    他做了饭，还洗了碗。

5 N-COUNT 盘状物;碟状物 You can use dish to refer to anything that is round and hollow in shape with a wide uncovered top. 盘状物;碟状物

  • ...a dish used to receive satellite broadcasts.

    用来接收卫星广播的碟形卫星天线

6 See also: satellite dish  side dish 
7 PHRASE 洗餐具 If you do the dishes, you wash the dishes. 洗餐具

  • I hate doing the dishes.

    我讨厌洗碗。

8 to dish the dirt→see: dirt
