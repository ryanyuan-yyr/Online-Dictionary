safeguard
seɪfgɑːʳd seɪfˌgɑrd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense safeguards, present participle safeguarding, past tense , past participle safeguarded
1 VERB 保护;保卫;维护;捍卫 To safeguard something or someone means to protect them from being harmed, lost, or badly treated. 保护;保卫;维护;捍卫 [formal]

  • They will press for international action to safeguard the ozone layer. [VERB noun]

    他们将督促国际社会采取行动保护臭氧层。

  • The interests of minorities will have to be safeguarded under a new constitution. [VERB noun]

    新宪法必须能保护少数群体的利益。

  • They are taking precautionary measures to safeguard their forces from the effects of chemical weapons. [VERB noun + from]

    他们正采取预防措施保护其军队免受化学武器的伤害。

SYN protect, guard, defend, save  
2 N-COUNT 预防性条款(或法规);保障措施 A safeguard is a law, rule, or measure intended to prevent someone or something from being harmed. 预防性条款(或法规);保障措施

  • Many people took second jobs as a safeguard against unemployment. [+ against]

    许多人为防失业都干两份工作。

  • A system like ours lacks adequate safeguards for civil liberties.

    我们这样的制度缺乏有效保护公民自由的条款。

SYN protection, security, defence, guard
