clumsy
klʌmzi ˈklʌmzi
Word forms: comparative clumsier, superlative clumsiest
1 ADJ-GRADED 笨拙的;不灵活的 A clumsy person moves or handles things in a careless, awkward way, often so that things are knocked over or broken. 笨拙的;不灵活的

  • I'd never seen a clumsier, less coordinated boxer.

    我从没见过更笨拙、动作更不协调的拳击手。

  • Unfortunately, I was still very clumsy behind the wheel of the jeep.

    令人遗憾的是我开起吉普来还是笨手笨脚。

SYN awkward, blundering, bungling, lumbering  
clumsily

  • The rooks flew clumsily towards their nests.

    那些秃鼻乌鸦笨拙地飞向它们的巢。

clumsiness

  • Ben's biggest problem is clumsiness.

    本最大的毛病就是笨手笨脚。

2 ADJ-GRADED (行为或措词)不得体的，拙劣的 A clumsy action or statement is not skilful or is likely to upset people. (行为或措词)不得体的，拙劣的

  • The action seemed a clumsy attempt to topple the Janata Dal government.

    这一试图推翻人民党政府的行动似乎缺乏策略。

  • He denied the announcement was clumsy and insensitive.

    他否认声明不得体、不近人情。

clumsily

  • If the matter were handled clumsily, it could cost Miriam her life.

    要是事情处理不当，米丽娅姆可能会送命。

clumsiness

  • I was ashamed at my clumsiness and insensitivity.

    我为自己的笨拙和麻木不仁感到羞愧。

3 ADJ-GRADED (设计或外表)粗糙的，难用的;设计欠佳的 An object that is clumsy is not neat in design or appearance, and is often awkward to use. (设计或外表)粗糙的，难用的;设计欠佳的

  • The keyboard is a large and clumsy instrument as far as portable computers are concerned.

    就手提电脑而言，键盘就显得大而笨重了。

  • It was a clumsy looking aeroplane.

    那架飞机样子很难看。

SYN unwieldy, ill-shaped, unhandy, clunky [informal]
