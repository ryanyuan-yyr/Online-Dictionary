conceive
kənsiːv kənˈsiv
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense conceives, present participle conceiving, past tense , past participle conceived
1 VERB 想象;相信 If you cannot conceive of something, you cannot imagine it or believe it. 想象;相信 [Also VERB that]

  • I just can't even conceive of that quantity of money. [VERB + of]

    那么多钱，我根本都无法想象。

  • He was immensely ambitious but unable to conceive of winning power for himself. [VERB + of]

    他雄心勃勃，但是想不出自己能如何赢得权力。

2 VERB 认为 If you conceive something as a particular thing, you consider it to be that thing. 认为

  • The ancients conceived the earth as afloat in water. [VERB noun + as]

    古人认为地球飘浮在水里。

  • We conceive of the family as being in a constant state of change. [VERB + of]

    我们认为家庭处于不断变化之中。

  • Elvis conceived of himself as a ballad singer. [VERB + of]

    埃尔维斯认为自己是民谣歌手。

3 VERB 构思;设想;想出 If you conceive a plan or idea, you think of it and work out how it can be done. 构思;设想;想出

  • She had conceived the idea of a series of novels. [VERB noun]

    她萌生出撰写系列小说的想法。

  • He conceived of the first truly portable computer in 1968. [VERB + of]

    1968年，他构想出第一台真正的便携式计算机。

4 VERB 怀孕 When a woman conceives, she becomes pregnant. 怀孕

  • Women, he says, should give up alcohol before they plan to conceive. [VERB]

    他说女性在计划怀孕之前应该戒酒。

  • About one in six couples has difficulty conceiving. [VERB]

    大约1/6的夫妻有受孕困难。

  • A mother who already has non-identical twins is more likely to conceive another set of twins. [VERB noun]

    生育过非同卵双胞胎的母亲再怀上双胞胎的可能性更大。

SYN become pregnant, get pregnant, become impregnated
