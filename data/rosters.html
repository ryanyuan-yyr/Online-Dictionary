roster
rɒstəʳ ˈrɑstər
Word forms: plural rosters
1 N-COUNT 值勤表;勤务簿 A roster is a list which gives details of the order in which different people have to do a particular job. 值勤表;勤务簿

  • The next day he put himself first on the new roster for domestic chores.

    第二天，他把自己排在了新的家务值日表的第一位。

  • He was in his office, preparing his duty roster for the coming month.

    他正在自己的办公室里制定下一个月的执勤表。

SYN rota, listing, list, table  
2 N-COUNT 名单;花名册;登记表;运动员名册（尤用于美国英语中） A roster is a list, especially of the people who work for a particular organization or are available to do a particular job. It can also be a list of the sports players who are available for a particular team, especially in American English. 名单;花名册;登记表;运动员名册（尤用于美国英语中）

  • The Amateur Softball Association's roster of umpires has declined to 57,000. [+ of]

    业余垒球协会的裁判员名单上的人数已经减少到了57,000名。

  • From the studio's roster of leading men, Robert Walker was an obvious choice to play the part.

    从该电影公司的男主角名单来看，罗伯特·沃克显然是出演这一角色的不二人选。
