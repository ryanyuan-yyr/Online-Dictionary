top up
1 PHRASAL VERB 重新加满;添足 If you top something up, you make it full again when part of it has been used. 重新加满;添足 [mainly British]

  • We topped up the water tanks. [VERB PREP. noun]

    我们重新把水箱加满。

  • He topped her glass up after complaining she was a slow drinker. [VERB noun PREP.]

    他抱怨她喝得太慢，随即又把她的杯子续满。

SYN fill (up), refresh, recharge, refill  
2See also:top-up 
top-up ˈtɒpʌp
Word forms: plural top-ups
1 N-COUNT 再斟满的一杯 A top-up is another serving of a drink in the same glass that you have just used. 再斟满的一杯 [British]

  • Anyone ready for a top-up?

    谁还要再满上？

2 ADJ [ADJ n] (贷款或支付款项)补足的，追加的 A top-up loan or payment is added to an amount of money in order to bring it up to a required level. (贷款或支付款项)补足的，追加的 [British]

  • Student grants will be frozen at existing levels and top-up loans made available.

    学生助学金将冻结在现有水平，但可以申请补足贷款。
