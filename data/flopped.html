flop
flɒp flɑp
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense flops, present participle flopping, past tense , past participle flopped
1 VERB 猛然坐下;重重地坐下 If you flop into a chair, for example, you sit down suddenly and heavily because you are so tired. 猛然坐下;重重地坐下

  • Bunbury flopped down upon the bed and rested his tired feet. [VERB prep./adv.]

    邦伯里重重地倒在了床上，让他疲惫的双脚休息一下。

  • She flopped, exhausted, on to a sofa. [VERB prep./adv.]

    她精疲力竭，一屁股坐在沙发上。

SYN slump, fall, drop, collapse  
2 VERB 凌乱地垂下;猛落下 If something flops onto something else, it falls there heavily or untidily. 凌乱地垂下;猛落下

  • The briefcase flopped onto the desk. [VERB prep./adv.]

    公文包重重地落在桌上。

  • His hair flopped over his left eye. [VERB prep./adv.]

    他的头发凌乱地垂在左眼前。

SYN hang down, hang, dangle, sag  
3 N-COUNT [oft adjective NOUN] 彻底失败;惨败 If something is a flop, it is completely unsuccessful. 彻底失败;惨败 [informal] [informal]

  • It is the public who decide whether a film is a hit or a flop.

    决定一部电影会走红还是惨败的是大众。

SYN failure, disaster, loser, fiasco  
4 VERB 彻底失败;惨败 If something flops, it is completely unsuccessful. 彻底失败;惨败 [informal]

  • The film flopped badly at the box office. [VERB]

    这部电影票房惨淡。

SYN fail, close, bomb [US , Canadian , slang] , fold [informal]
