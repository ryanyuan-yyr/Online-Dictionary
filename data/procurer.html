procure
prəkjʊəʳ proʊˈkjʊr prəˈkjʊr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense procures, present participle procuring, past tense , past participle procured
1 VERB 努力获得；设法取得 If you procure something, especially something that is difficult to get, you obtain it. 努力获得；设法取得 [formal]

  • It remained very difficult to procure food, fuel and other daily necessities. [VERB noun]

    食物、燃料和其他日用必需品仍然很难得到。

SYN obtain, get, find, buy  
2 VERB 给…拉皮条；为…介绍妓女 If someone procures a prostitute, they introduce the prostitute to a client. 给…拉皮条；为…介绍妓女

  • He procured girls of 16 and 17 to be mistresses for his influential friends. [VERB noun]

    他介绍了些十六七岁的女孩给他那些有权势的朋友当情人。
