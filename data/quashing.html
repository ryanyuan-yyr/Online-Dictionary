quash
kwɒʃ kwɒʃ kwɑʃ kwɔʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense quashes, present participle quashing, past tense , past participle quashed
1 VERB 撤销，废止（决定或判决）;宣布无效 If a court or someone in authority quashes a decision or judgment, they officially reject it. 撤销，废止（决定或判决）;宣布无效

  • The Appeal Court has quashed the convictions of all eleven people. [VERB noun]

    上诉法庭已经撤销对所有11人的判决。

SYN annul, overturn, reverse, cancel  
2 VERB 平息，制止，澄清(谣言) If someone quashes rumours, they say or do something to demonstrate that the rumours are not true. 平息，制止，澄清(谣言)

  • Graham attempted to quash rumours of growing discontent. [VERB noun]

    格雷厄姆试图平息有关不满情绪日益强烈的流言。

3 VERB 镇压;压制;平定 To quash a rebellion or protest means to stop it, often in a violent way. 镇压;压制;平定

  • Troops were displaying an obvious reluctance to get involved in quashing demonstrations. [VERB noun]

    部队显然并不愿意参与镇压示威游行。
