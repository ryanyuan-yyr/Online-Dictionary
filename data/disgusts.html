disgust
dɪsgʌst dɪsˈgʌst
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense disgusts, present participle disgusting, past tense , past participle disgusted
1 N-UNCOUNT 厌恶;憎恶 Disgust is a feeling of very strong dislike or disapproval. 厌恶;憎恶

  • He spoke of his disgust at the incident.

    他谈到了他对这件事的厌恶。

  • A look of disgust came over his face.

    他脸上显出厌恶的表情。

  • I threw the book aside in disgust.

    我厌恶地把书扔到一边。

SYN loathing, revulsion, hatred, dislike  
2 VERB 使厌恶;使憎恶 To disgust someone means to make them feel a strong sense of dislike and disapproval. 使厌恶;使憎恶

  • He disgusted many with his boorish behaviour. [VERB noun]

    他的粗野行为让很多人都讨厌他。

SYN sicken, outrage, offend, revolt
