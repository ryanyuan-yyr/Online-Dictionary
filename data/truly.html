truly
truːli ˈtruli
1 ADV [ADVERB before verb] 真正地;完全地 You use truly to emphasize that something has all the features or qualities of a particular thing, or is the case to the fullest possible extent. 真正地;完全地 [emphasis]

  • ...a truly democratic system.

    真正民主的制度

  • Not all doctors truly understand the reproductive cycle.

    并非所有的医生都真正了解生殖周期。

  • Spain was truly a European nation.

    西班牙是个典型的欧洲国家。

SYN genuinely, really, correctly, truthfully  
2 ADV [ADVERB adjective] 非常;很 You can use truly in order to emphasize your description of something. 非常;很 [emphasis]

  • ...a truly splendid man.

    非常优秀的人

  • They were truly appalling.

    他们非常令人震惊。

SYN really, very, greatly, indeed  
3 ADV [ADVERB adjective, ADVERB before verb] 由衷地;真诚地;真挚地 You use truly to emphasize that feelings are genuine and sincere. 由衷地;真诚地;真挚地 [emphasis]

  • Believe me, Susan, I am truly sorry.

    相信我，苏珊，我实在感到抱歉。

  • He truly loved his children.

    他由衷地爱他的孩子们。

SYN faithfully, firmly, constantly, steadily  
4 ADV [ADVERB with cl] 的确;真的 You can use truly in order to emphasize that what you are saying is true. 的确;真的 [emphasis]

  • I truly never minded caring for Rusty…

    我真的从不介意照顾拉斯蒂。

  • I do not expect a war between my country and yours. Truly I do not.

    我不希望你我两国之间爆发战争。真的不希望。

SYN sincerely, really, genuinely, honestly  
5 well and truly→see: well 
6 CONVENTION (用于正式信件末尾)你忠实的 You write Yours truly at the end of a formal letter to someone you do not know very well. You write your signature after the words 'Yours truly'. (用于正式信件末尾)你忠实的

  • Yours truly, Phil Turner.

    菲尔·特纳谨启

7 PHRASE 我，鄙人 You can say yours truly as a way of referring to yourself. 我，鄙人

  • Yours truly was awoken by a shout: 'Ahoy there!'

    鄙人被一声大喊惊醒：“喂！”
