initiate
ɪnɪʃieɪt ɪnɪʃieɪt ɪnɪʃiət ɪˈnɪʃiˌeɪt ɪˈnɪʃiɪt ɪˈnɪʃiˌeɪt ɪˈnɪʃɪt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , 3rd person singular present tense initiates, present participle initiating, past tense , past participle initiated
The verb is pronounced /ɪ'nɪʃieɪt/. The noun is pronounced /ɪ'nɪʃiət/. 动词读作 /ɪ'nɪʃieɪt/。名词读作 /ɪ'nɪʃiət/。
1 VERB 开始;创始;发起 If you initiate something, you start it or cause it to happen. 开始;创始;发起

  • They wanted to initiate a discussion on economics. [VERB noun]

    他们想启动一次经济学讨论。

  • The trip was initiated by the manager of the community centre. [VERB noun]

    这次旅行由社区活动中心的经理发起。

SYN begin, start, open, launch  
2 VERB 使初步了解;向…传授基本技巧(或基础知识) If you initiate someone into something, you introduce them to a particular skill or type of knowledge and teach them about it. 使初步了解;向…传授基本技巧(或基础知识) [Also VERB n]

  • He initiated her into the study of other cultures. [VERB noun + into]

    他将她领进了研究其他文化的大门。

3 VERB (通过仪式)接纳，使加入 If someone is initiated into something such as a religion, secret society, or social group, they become a member of it by taking part in ceremonies at which they learn its special knowledge or customs. (通过仪式)接纳，使加入 [Also VERB n]

  • In many societies, young people are formally initiated into their adult roles. [be VERB-ed + into]

    在很多社会里,年轻人经过正式仪式才算成年。

  • ...the ceremony that initiated members into the Order. [VERB noun + into]

    吸收成员加入会社的仪式

4 N-COUNT [oft NOUN of n] 有初步知识的成员;新入会的人 An initiate is a person who has been accepted as a member by a particular group or club and been taught its secrets and skills. 有初步知识的成员;新入会的人

  • Chen was an initiate of a Chinese spiritual discipline.

    陈是某个中国神学领域的新入门者。

SYN novice, member, pupil, convert
