jitters
dʒɪtəʳz ˈdʒɪtəz
N-PLURAL 紧张不安；心神不定 If you have the jitters, you feel extremely nervous, for example because you have to do something important or because you are expecting important news. 紧张不安；心神不定 [informal]

  • I had a case of the jitters during my first two speeches.

    我在最初两次讲演时感到非常紧张。

  • Officials feared that any public announcements would only increase market jitters.

    政府官员担心任何公开声明只会加重市场恐慌。
