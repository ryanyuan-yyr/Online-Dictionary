funny
fʌni ˈfʌni
Word forms: comparative funnier, superlative funniest, plural funnies
1 ADJ-GRADED 有趣的;好笑的;滑稽的 Someone or something that is funny is amusing and likely to make you smile or laugh. 有趣的;好笑的;滑稽的

  • Wade was smart and not bad-looking, and he could be funny when he wanted to.

    韦德脑子聪明，相貌也不难看，而且如果他愿意的话还很风趣。

  • I'll tell you a funny story.

    我来给你讲一个有趣的故事。

SYN humorous, amusing, comical, entertaining  
2 ADJ-GRADED 古怪的;奇怪的;令人困惑的 If you describe something as funny, you think it is strange, surprising, or puzzling. 古怪的;奇怪的;令人困惑的

  • Children get some very funny ideas sometimes!.

    小孩子的想法有时候真是稀奇古怪！

  • There's something funny about him.

    他有点儿古怪。

  • It's funny how love can come and go.

    爱情来了又走真让人困惑。

SYN peculiar, odd, strange, unusual  
3 ADJ-GRADED 略感不适的 If you feel funny, you feel slightly ill. 略感不适的 [informal]

  • My head had begun to ache and my stomach felt funny.

    我的头开始疼起来，胃也有些不舒服。

SYN ill, poorly [informal] , queasy, sick  
4 N-PLURAL (报刊上的)幽默漫画,滑稽连环漫画 The funnies are humorous drawings or a series of humorous drawings in a newspaper or magazine. (报刊上的)幽默漫画,滑稽连环漫画 [US, informal]
SYN joke [informal] , crack [slang] , quip, pun  
5 PHRASE 不端行为;见不得人的勾当 Funny business is dishonest or unacceptable behaviour. 不端行为;见不得人的勾当 [informal]

  • ...an inquiry into funny business in Ireland's biggest export industry.

    对爱尔兰最大的出口行业违规行为的调查
