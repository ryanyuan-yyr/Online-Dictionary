interlock
ɪntəʳlɒk ˌɪntərˈlɑk ˈɪntərˌlɑk
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense interlocks, present participle interlocking, past tense , past participle interlocked
1 V-RECIP-ERG (使)连锁;(使)相互扣住 Things that interlock or are interlocked go between or through each other so that they are linked. (使)连锁;(使)相互扣住 [Also V with n]

  • The parts interlock. [VERB]

    各部分相互扣住。

  • Interlock your fingers behind your back. [VERB noun]

    把手十指交错放到背后。

  • Joyce sits with her fingers interlocked under her chin. [VERB-ed]

    乔伊丝坐在那里,十指相扣撑着下巴。

  • ...a series of interlocking rings. [VERB-ing]

    一串连锁环

SYN interweave, intertwine, interlace, cross  
2 V-RECIP-ERG (使)密切联系 If systems, situations, or plans are interlocked or interlock, they are very closely connected. (使)密切联系 [Also VERB n]

  • The problems of Israel, Lebanon, and the Gulf were tightly interlocked. [be VERB-ed]

    以色列、黎巴嫩与海湾的问题一环扣一环。

  • The tragedies begin to interlock. [VERB]

    悲剧开始相互交叠。

  • Your girlfriend's fear seems to interlock with your fear. [VERB + with]

    你女朋友的恐惧似乎与你的恐惧有密切关联。

SYN connect, unite, link, join
