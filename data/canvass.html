canvass
kænvəs ˈkænvəs
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense canvasses, present participle canvassing, past tense , past participle canvassed
1 VERB 游说;拉选票 If you canvass for a particular person or political party, you go around an area trying to persuade people to vote for that person or party. 游说;拉选票

  • I'm canvassing for the Conservative Party. [VERB + for]

    我在为保守党拉选票。

SYN campaign, solicit votes, electioneer  
canvasser

  • ...a Conservative canvasser.

    保守党说客

2 VERB 征询,征求(公众意见) If you canvass public opinion, you find out how people feel about a particular subject. 征询,征求(公众意见)

  • Members of Parliament are spending the weekend canvassing opinion in their constituencies. [VERB noun]

    国会议员这个周末要向所在选区的选民征求意见。

  • The poll canvassed the views of almost eighty economists. [VERB noun]

    民意调查征询了将近80位经济学家的意见。

SYN poll, study, examine, investigate
