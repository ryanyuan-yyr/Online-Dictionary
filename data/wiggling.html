wiggle
wɪgəl ˈwɪɡəl ˈwɪgəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense wiggles, present participle wiggling, past tense , past participle wiggled
V-ERG （使）扭动;（使）摆动 If you wiggle something or if it wiggles, it moves up and down or from side to side in small quick movements. （使）扭动;（使）摆动

  • She wiggled her finger. [VERB noun]

    她扭了扭手指。

  • His ears wiggled if you scratched his chin. [VERB]

    如果你挠他的下巴，他的耳朵就会摆动。

  • Your baby will try to shuffle or wiggle along the floor. [VERB prep./adv.]

    你的小宝宝会拖着脚或一扭一扭地学步。

Wiggle is also a noun.

  • ...a wiggle of the hips.

    屁股的扭摆
