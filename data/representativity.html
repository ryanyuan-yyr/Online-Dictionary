representative
reprɪzentətɪv ˌrɛprəˈzɛntətɪv
Word forms: plural representatives
1 N-COUNT 代表;代理人 A representative is a person who has been chosen to act or make decisions on behalf of another person or a group of people. 代表;代理人

  • ...trade union representatives.

    工会代表

  • Employees from each department elect a representative.

    每个部门的员工选举一名代表。

SYN delegate, member, agent, deputy  
2 N-COUNT 销售代表;销售代理 A representative is a person whose job is to sell a company's products or services, especially by travelling round and visiting other companies. 销售代表;销售代理 [formal]

  • She had a stressful job as a sales representative.

    作为一名销售代表，她的工作压力很大。

SYN agent, salesman, rep, traveller  
3 ADJ [ADJ n] 由代表组成的;代议制的 A representative group consists of a small number of people who have been chosen to make decisions on behalf of a larger group. 由代表组成的;代议制的

  • The new head of state should be chosen by an 87 member representative council.

    新的国家元首应该由包括87名成员的代表委员会选出。

  • ...the institutions of a representative government.

    代议制政府的各个机构

4 ADJ-GRADED 典型的;有代表性的 Someone who is typical of the group to which they belong can be described as representative . 典型的;有代表性的

  • He was in no way representative of dog-trainers in general. [+ of]

    他远不是一般意义上的驯狗师。

  • ...fairly representative groups of adults.

    相当典型的成年人群体

SYN typical, characteristic, archetypal, exemplary  
representativeness

  • ...a process designed to ensure the representativeness of the sample interviewed.

    为保证抽样的对象具有代表性而设计的过程

5 N-COUNT (美国)众议员 In the United States, a Representative is a member of the House of Representatives, the less powerful of the two parts of Congress. (美国)众议员
