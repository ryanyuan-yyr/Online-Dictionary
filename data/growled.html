growl
graʊl graʊl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense growls, present participle growling, past tense , past participle growled
1 VERB （狗等）低声吼叫 When a dog or other animal growls, it makes a low noise in its throat, usually because it is angry. （狗等）低声吼叫

  • The dog was biting, growling and wagging its tail. [VERB]

    那条狗在一边撕咬一边低声吼叫，尾巴也跟着摇摆。

SYN snarl, show its teethof an animal  
Growl is also a noun.

  • The bear exposed its teeth in a muffled growl.

    那只熊龇出牙齿，发出一声低沉的吼叫。

2 VERB （人）发出低沉的怒吼，咆哮 If someone growls something, they say something in a low, rough, and angry voice. （人）发出低沉的怒吼，咆哮 [written]

  • His fury was so great he could hardly speak. He growled some unintelligible words at Pete. [VERB noun]

    他气得几乎说不出话来，冲着皮特不知咆哮些什么。

  • 'I should have killed him,' Sharpe growled. [VERB with quote]

    “我本该宰了他的，”夏普吼道。

Growl is also a noun.

  • ...with an angry growl of contempt for her own weakness.

    对她自己的懦弱发出的一声轻蔑的怒吼

3 VERB 隆隆作响；轰鸣 If you say that something growls, you mean that it makes a deep low noise. 隆隆作响；轰鸣

  • My stomach growled. [VERB]

    我的肚子咕咕叫。

  • The car growls along rutted streets. [VERB prep.]

    汽车在车辙纵横的街上一路轰鸣。

Growl is also a noun.

  • Acceleration is accompanied by a resonating growl from the gearbox.

    变速箱随着加速传来一阵轰鸣。
