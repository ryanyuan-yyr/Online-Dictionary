coincide
koʊɪnsaɪd ˌkoʊɪnˈsaɪd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense coincides, present participle coinciding, past tense , past participle coincided
1 V-RECIP 巧合;同时发生 If one event coincides with another, they happen at the same time. 巧合;同时发生

  • The exhibition coincides with the 50th anniversary of his death. [VERB + with]

    展览恰好在他逝世50周年之际举行。

  • Although his mental illness had coincided with his war service it had not been caused by it. [VERB with noun]

    虽然他的精神病刚好在他参战期间发作，但并不是由此引起的。

  • The beginning of the solar and lunar years coincided every 13 years. [VERB]

    太阳年和太阴年的起始时间每13年重合一次。

SYN occur simultaneously, coexist, synchronize, be concurrent  
2 V-RECIP (想法、利益等)一致，相符 If the ideas or interests of two or more people coincide, they are the same. (想法、利益等)一致，相符

  • The kids' views on life don't always coincide, but they're not afraid of voicing their opinions. [VERB]

    孩子们的人生观并不总是一致，但他们敢于表达自己的观点。

  • ...a case in which public and private interests coincide. [plural-noun VERB]

    一个公众利益和私人利益一致的例子

  • He gave great encouragement to his students, especially if their passions happened to coincide with his own. [VERB + with]

    他给予学生莫大的鼓励，尤其是当他们与自己志趣相投时。

SYN agree, match, accord, square
