lean on
(also lean upon)
1 PHRASAL VERB 依赖；依靠 If you lean on someone or lean upon them, you depend on them for support and encouragement. 依赖；依靠

  • She leaned on him to help her to solve her problems. [VERB PREP. noun]

    她靠他帮忙解决自身问题。

SYN depend on, trust, rely on, cling to  
2 PHRASAL VERB 对…施压；恫吓；威胁 If you lean on someone, you try to influence them, especially by threatening them. 对…施压；恫吓；威胁 [informal]

  • He told us to get stuffed so we leaned on his kid. [VERB PREP. noun]

    他叫我们滚蛋，于是我们威胁他的孩子。

  • Colin was being leaned on by his bankers. [VERB PREP. noun]

    科林正承受来自银行的压力。

SYN pressurize, intimidate, coerce, breathe down someone's neck
