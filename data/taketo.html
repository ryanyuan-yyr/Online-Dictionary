take to
1 PHRASAL VERB (尤指短时间接触后)喜欢上，喜爱 If you take to someone or something, you like them, especially after knowing them or thinking about them for only a short time. (尤指短时间接触后)喜欢上，喜爱

  • Did the children take to him?. [VERB PREP. noun]

    孩子们喜欢上他了么？

  • The first series was really bad. But for some reason the public took to it. [VERB PREP. noun]

    第一部系列剧糟透了，可是不知为何，观众开始喜欢上了它。

SYN like, get on with, warm to, be taken with  
2 PHRASAL VERB 养成(做…)的习惯;开始定时(做…) If you take to doing something, you begin to do it as a regular habit. 养成(做…)的习惯;开始定时(做…)

  • They had taken to wandering through the streets arm-in-arm. [VERB PREP. verb-ing]

    他们养成了挽着胳膊逛街的习惯。

SYN start, resort to, make a habit of, have recourse to
