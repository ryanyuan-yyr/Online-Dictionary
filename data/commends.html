commend
kəmend kəˈmɛnd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense commends, present participle commending, past tense , past participle commended
1 VERB 赞扬;赞许 If you commend someone or something, you praise them formally. 赞扬;赞许 [formal]

  • I commended her for that action. [V n + for/on]

    我赞扬了她的那一举动。

  • I commend Ms. Orth on writing such an informative article. [V n + for/on]

    我对奥思太太写出内容如此翔实的文章表示赞许。

  • The book was widely commended for its candour. [be VERB-ed + for]

    这本书以其直言不讳而广受好评。

  • The reports commend her bravery. [VERB noun]

    这些报道赞扬了她的勇敢。

  • His actions were commended by the Jury. [be VERB-ed + by]

    他的所作所为受到了陪审团的赞许。

commendation

  • The Company received a commendation from the Royal Society of Arts.

    公司受到了皇家艺术协会的赞扬。

2 VERB 推荐;举荐 If someone commends a person or thing to you, they tell you that you will find them good or useful. 推荐;举荐 [formal]

  • I can commend it to him as a realistic course of action. [VERB noun + to]

    我可以把这作为一个切实可行的处理方法推荐给他。

SYN recommend, suggest, approve, advocate  
3 VERB 被接受;得到认可 If something commends itself to you, you approve of it. 被接受;得到认可 [formal]

  • The Rousseau model commended itself to a lot of early socialists. [V pron-refl to n]

    卢梭提出的模式被很多早期的社会主义者接受。

4 PHRASE [usu v PHR pron] 令人称道/乏善可陈 If you say that something has much to commend it, you approve of it. If you say that something has little to commend it, you disapprove of it. 令人称道/乏善可陈 [formal]

  • The Prime Minister's initiative has much to commend it.

    首相的新方案有不少令人赞许之处。
