peek
piːk piːk pik
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense peeks, present participle peeking, past tense , past participle peeked
VERB 瞥；偷看；窥视 If you peek at something or someone, you have a quick look at them, often secretly. 瞥；偷看；窥视

  • On two occasions she had peeked at him through a crack in the wall. [VERB + at]

    她曾两次透过墙缝窥视他。

SYN glance, look, peer, spy  
Peek is also a noun.

  • American firms have been paying outrageous fees for a peek at the technical data.

    美国公司为了一窥这些技术数据一直在支付巨额费用。
