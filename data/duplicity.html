duplicity
djuːplɪsɪti duː- duˈplɪsəti djuˈplɪsəti
N-UNCOUNT 奸诈;欺骗;口是心非;两面派 If you accuse someone of duplicity, you mean that they are deceitful. 奸诈;欺骗;口是心非;两面派 [formal]

  • Malcolm believed he was guilty of duplicity in his private dealings.

    马尔科姆认为他在为其私底下两面派的行为而心生愧疚。

SYN deceit, fraud, deception, hypocrisy
