masquerade
mæskəreɪd ˌmæskəˈreɪd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense masquerades, present participle masquerading, past tense , past participle masqueraded
1 VERB 化装;伪装;冒充 To masquerade as someone or something means to pretend to be that person or thing, particularly in order to deceive other people. 化装;伪装;冒充

  • He masqueraded as a doctor and fooled everyone. [VERB + as]

    他冒充医生，骗过了所有人。

  • ...vices masquerading as virtues. [V as n]

    披着美德外衣的丑恶

SYN pose, pretend to be, impersonate, profess to be  
2 N-COUNT 虚假的行为;掩饰;欺骗 A masquerade is an attempt to deceive people about the true nature or identity of something. 虚假的行为;掩饰;欺骗

  • He told a news conference that the elections would be a masquerade.

    他在新闻发布会上说选举将是一场骗人的把戏。

SYN pretence, disguise, deception, front [informal]  
3 N-COUNT 化装晚会 A masquerade is an event such as a party or dance where people dress up in disguise and wear masks. 化装晚会

  • ...a masquerade ball.

    化装舞会
