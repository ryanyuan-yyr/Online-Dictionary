pacify
pæsɪfaɪ ˈpæsəˌfaɪ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense pacifies, present participle pacifying, past tense , past participle pacified
1 VERB 安抚；抚慰；使平静 If you pacify someone who is angry, upset, or not pleased, you succeed in making them calm or pleased. 安抚；抚慰；使平静

  • Is this a serious step, or is this just something to pacify the critics?. [VERB noun]

    这一措施是来真的？还是仅仅为了安抚批评人士？

  • She shrieked again, refusing to be pacified. [VERB noun]

    她又尖叫了，无法使她平静下来。

SYN calm (down), appease, placate, still  
2 VERB 平定；平息；镇压 If the army or the police pacify a group of people, they use force to overcome their resistance or protests. 平定；平息；镇压

  • Government forces have found it difficult to pacify the rebels. [VERB noun]

    政府军发现难以平定叛军。

  • They were eventually pacified by officers of the local police. [VERB noun]

    当地警察最终把他们镇压了下去。

SYN quell, silence, crush, put down
