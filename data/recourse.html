recourse
rɪkɔːʳs ˈriˌkɔrs rɪˈkɔrs
N-UNCOUNT 依靠;求援;诉诸 If you achieve something without recourse to a particular course of action, you succeed without carrying out that action. To have recourse to a particular course of action means to have to do that action in order to achieve something. 依靠;求援;诉诸 [formal]

  • It enabled its members to settle their differences without recourse to war. [+ to]

    这样，其成员就可以不必诉诸战争来解决分歧。

  • The public believes its only recourse is to take to the streets.

    公众认为唯一的办法就是上街游行。

SYN option, choice, alternative, resort
