trivialize
trɪviəlaɪz ˈtrɪviəˌlaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense trivializes, present participle trivializing, past tense , past participle trivialized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 trivialise
VERB 贬低;轻视;使显得琐碎(或简单) If you say that someone trivializes something important, you disapprove of them because they make it seem less important, serious, and complex than it is. 贬低;轻视;使显得琐碎(或简单) [disapproval]

  • It never ceases to amaze me how the business world continues to trivialize the world's environmental problems. [VERB noun]

    商界一直不重视世界环境问题，总让我惊讶不已。

SYN undervalue, underestimate, play down, minimize
