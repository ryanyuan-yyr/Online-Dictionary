kite
kaɪt kəɪt kaɪt
Word forms: plural kites
1 N-COUNT 风筝 A kite is an object, usually used as a toy, which is flown in the air. It consists of a light frame covered with paper or cloth and has a long string attached which you hold while the kite is flying. 风筝
2 N-COUNT 鸢（一种猛禽） A kite is a bird of prey which hunts and kills small animals for food. 鸢（一种猛禽）
3 PHRASE [VERB and NOUN inflect] 试探舆论（或反应） If you say that someone is flying a kite, you are critical of them for putting forward new ideas just to see how people react, rather than with the intention of putting those ideas into practice. 试探舆论（或反应） [mainly British, disapproval]

  • The Government flies these kites of disinformation, then people feel grateful when the changes don't happen.

    政府故意放出这些虚假消息来试探舆论，而当其中提到的改变并未出现时，老百姓便会心怀感激。

4 PHRASE 极度兴奋的；被酒精（或毒品）麻醉的 If you say that someone is as high as a kite, you mean that they are very excited or that they are greatly affected by alcohol or drugs. 极度兴奋的；被酒精（或毒品）麻醉的

  • I felt so strange on the steroid injections. I was as high as a kite some of the time.

    注射类固醇后我感觉很奇特。我有时会极度兴奋。
