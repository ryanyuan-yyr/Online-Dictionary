wheeze
hwiːz hwiz wiz
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense , 3rd person singular present tense wheezes, present participle wheezing, past tense , past participle wheezed
1 VERB 喘息;气喘吁吁 If someone wheezes, they breathe with difficulty and make a whistling sound. 喘息;气喘吁吁

  • He had quite serious problems with his chest and wheezed and coughed all the time. [VERB]

    他的胸部问题很严重，总是气喘，还不停地咳嗽。

  • 'Boy,' wheezed old Pop Ryan. [VERB with quote]

    “孩子”，老波普·瑞安气喘吁吁地说。

SYN gasp, whistle, cough, hiss  
2 N-COUNT [usually with supplement, oft adjective NOUN] 妙招；小窍门；玩笑；小伎俩 A wheeze is a clever idea, joke, or trick. 妙招；小窍门；玩笑；小伎俩 [informal, old-fashioned]

  • He came up with what seemed like a clever wheeze.

    他提出了一个看起来还不错的主意。

  • ...the temptation to boost profits through accounting wheezes.

    通过在账目上做手脚以提升利润的诱惑。

SYN trick, plan, idea, scheme
