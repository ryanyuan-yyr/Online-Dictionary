equilibrium
iːkwɪlɪbriəm ˌikwɪˈlɪbriəm ˌ ɛkwɪˈlɪbriəm
Word forms: plural equilibria
1 N-VAR 平衡;均衡;均势 Equilibrium is a balance between several different influences or aspects of a situation. 平衡;均衡;均势 [formal]

  • Stocks seesawed ever lower until prices found some new level of equilibrium.

    股票在震荡中进一步下跌，直到股价达到某个新的平衡点。

  • For the economy to be in equilibrium, income must equal expenditure.

    经济要达到平衡，收支必须均衡。

SYN stability, balance, symmetry, steadiness  
2 N-UNCOUNT [oft poss NOUN] （内心的）平静，安宁 Someone's equilibrium is their normal calm state of mind. （内心的）平静，安宁

  • I paused in the hall to take three deep breaths to restore my equilibrium.

    我在大厅里停了一下，深吸了三口气以恢复平静。

  • He had recovered his equilibrium and even his good humour, somehow.

    不知怎的，他已经恢复了平静，连心情也变好了。

SYN composure, calm, stability, poise
