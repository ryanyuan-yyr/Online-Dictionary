kitty
kɪti ˈkɪti
Word forms: plural kitties
1 N-COUNT [usually singular] 共同凑集的一笔钱 A kitty is an amount of money gathered from several people, which is meant to be spent on things that these people will share or use together. 共同凑集的一笔钱

  • You haven't put any money in the kitty for three weeks.

    你有3个星期没往里凑钱了。

2 N-COUNT [usually singular] 全部赌注 A kitty is the total amount of money which is bet in a gambling game, and which is taken by the winner or winners. 全部赌注

  • Each month the total prize kitty is £13.5 million.

    每个月的彩池奖金总额达1,350万英镑。

3 N-COUNT （有时用作昵称）猫咪，小猫 Kitty is sometimes used as an affectionate way of referring to a cat or kitten. （有时用作昵称）猫咪，小猫 [informal]
