dribble
drɪbəl ˈdrɪbəl ˈdrɪbəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense dribbles, present participle dribbling, past tense , past participle dribbled
1 V-ERG (使)滴下;(使)一点一滴地落下 If a liquid dribbles somewhere, or if you dribble it, it drops down slowly or flows in a thin stream. (使)滴下;(使)一点一滴地落下

  • Sweat dribbled down Hart's face. [VERB prep./adv.]

    汗从哈特脸上滴下。

  • Dribble the hot mixture slowly into the blender. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    将热的混合物慢慢滴入食物搅拌器。

SYN run, drip, trickle, drop  
2 N-COUNT [NOUN of n] 点滴;少量 A dribble of a liquid is a very small amount of it. 点滴;少量

  • Apply a dribble of baby shampoo.

    抹一点儿婴儿洗发香波。

  • ...lettuce with dribbles of vinaigrette.

    滴了少量色拉调味汁的莴苣

SYN trickle, drop, drizzle, dash  
3 VERB 运球;带球;盘球 When players dribble the ball in a game such as football or basketball, they keep kicking or tapping it quickly in order to keep it moving. 运球;带球;盘球

  • He dribbled the ball towards Ferris. [VERB noun]

    他把球带向费里斯。

  • He dribbled past four defenders. [VERB]

    他带球过了4名后卫。

  • Her dribbling skills look second to none. [VERB-ing]

    她的带球技巧无人能及。

4 VERB 流口水;垂涎 If a person dribbles, saliva drops slowly from their mouth. 流口水;垂涎

  • ...to protect cot sheets when the baby dribbles. [VERB]

    以防宝宝流口水时将婴儿床的床单弄湿

  • She's dribbling on her collar. [VERB]

    她的口水流到了领子上。

SYN drool, drivel, slaver, slobber  
5 N-UNCOUNT 口水 Dribble is saliva that drops slowly from someone's mouth. 口水

  • His clothes are soaked in dribble.

    他的衣服被口水浸湿了。

6 VERB (人或物)三三两两地慢慢移动 If people or things dribble somewhere, they move there slowly and in small numbers. (人或物)三三两两地慢慢移动

  • ...as the workers dribbled away from the city square. [VERB prep./adv.]

    当工人们三三两两地离开市政广场时
