appellate court
əpelɪt kɔːʳt əˈpɛlɪt kɔːt
Word forms: plural appellate courts
N-COUNT 上诉法院 In the United States, an appellate court is a special court where people who have been convicted of a crime can appeal against their conviction. 上诉法院 [US]

  • A racially mixed jury in Miami convicted Lozano of manslaughter, but an appellate court overturned the convictions.
