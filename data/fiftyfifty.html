fifty-fifty
ˈfɪftiˈfɪfti
1 ADV [ADVERB after verb] 对半;各半;均(分)地 If something such as money or property is divided or shared fifty-fifty between two people, each person gets half of it. 对半;各半;均(分)地 [informal]

  • The proceeds of the sale are split fifty-fifty.

    出售的收益双方平分。

Fifty-fifty is also an adjective.

  • The new firm was owned on a fifty-fifty basis by the two parent companies.

    这个新公司由两家母公司各持一半股份。

2 ADJ [usually ADJ n] (发生概率)一半一半的，百分之五十的 If there is a fifty-fifty chance of something happening, it is equally likely to happen as it is not to happen. (发生概率)一半一半的，百分之五十的 [informal]

  • You've got a fifty-fifty chance of being right.

    你有 50％的可能性是对的。
