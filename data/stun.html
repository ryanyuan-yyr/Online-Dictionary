stun
stʌn stʌn
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense stuns, present participle stunning, past tense , past participle stunned
1 VERB [usually passive] 使震惊;使目瞪口呆;使惊愕 If you are stunned by something, you are extremely shocked or surprised by it and are therefore unable to speak or do anything. 使震惊;使目瞪口呆;使惊愕

  • Many cinema-goers were stunned by the film's violent and tragic end. [be VERB-ed]

    许多来看电影的人都被这部影片暴力而悲惨的结局所震惊。

SYN overcome, shock, amaze, confuse  
stunned

  • When they told me she had gone missing I was totally stunned.

    他们告诉我她不见了时，我当时完全惊呆了。

  • His announcement did not produce any immediate cheers, only a stunned silence while the words sank in.

    他的声明并没有当即引起什么欢呼，人们在领会了他的话后，只有一片令人意想不到的沉默。

2 VERB 使昏迷;使眩晕;打昏 If something such as a blow on the head stuns you, it makes you unconscious or confused and unsteady. 使昏迷;使眩晕;打昏

  • Sam stood his ground and got a blow that stunned him. [VERB noun]

    萨姆站在原地，被一下打昏了。

SYN daze, knock out, stupefy, numb
