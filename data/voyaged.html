voyage
vɔɪɪdʒ ˈvɔɪɪdʒ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense voyages, present participle voyaging, past tense , past participle voyaged
1 N-COUNT 旅行;航行;航海;航天 A voyage is a long journey on a ship or in a spacecraft. →see usage note at: journey 旅行;航行;航海;航天

  • He aims to follow Columbus's voyage to the West Indies.

    他打算重走哥伦布到达西印度群岛的航行路线。

  • ...the first space shuttle voyage to be devoted entirely to astronomy.

    首次完全以天文研究为目的的航天飞行

2 VERB 旅行;（尤指）航海旅行 To voyage to a place means to travel there, especially by sea. 旅行;（尤指）航海旅行 [formal]

  • The Greenpeace flagship is voyaging through the Arctic cold of the Barents Sea. [VERB prep./adv.]

    绿色和平组织的旗舰正在穿越巴伦支海的北极寒冷地带。

SYN travel, journey, tour, cruise  
voyager

  • ...fifteenth-century voyagers to the lands now called America and the Caribbean.

    在15世纪到达今天被称为美洲和加勒比地区的航海家们

voyaging

  • Our boat would not have been appropriate for ocean voyaging.

    我们的船只本不适合海上航行。
