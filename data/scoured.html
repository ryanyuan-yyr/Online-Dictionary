scour
skaʊəʳ skaʊə skaʊr
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense scours, present participle scouring, past tense , past participle scoured
1 VERB 四处搜索;细查 If you scour something such as a place or a book, you make a thorough search of it to try to find what you are looking for. 四处搜索;细查

  • Rescue crews had scoured an area of 30 square miles. [VERB noun]

    救援人员已经搜遍了30平方英里的范围。

  • We scoured the telephone directory for clues. [VERB noun + for]

    我们仔细查阅电话簿以寻找线索。

SYN search, hunt, comb, ransack  
2 VERB 擦净;擦亮 If you scour something such as a sink, floor, or pan, you clean its surface by rubbing it hard with something rough. 擦净;擦亮

  • He decided to scour the sink. [VERB noun]

    他决定把水池擦干净。

SYN scrub, clean, polish, rub
