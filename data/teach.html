teach
tiːtʃ titʃ
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense teaches, present participle teaching, past tense , past participle taught
1 VERB 教;教会 If you teach someone something, you give them instructions so that they know about it or how to do it. 教;教会

  • The trainers have a programme to teach them vocational skills. [VERB noun noun]

    培训师计划教他们一些职业技能。

  • George had taught him how to ride a horse. [VERB noun wh]

    乔治已经教会了他骑马。

  • She taught Julie to read. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    她教朱莉识字。

  • The computer has simplified the difficult task of teaching reading to the deaf. [VERB noun + to]

    电脑简化了教聋人阅读这一艰巨的工作。

SYN show, train, demonstrate  
2 VERB 教育;教导;使学会 To teach someone something means to make them think, feel, or act in a new or different way. 教育;教导;使学会 [Also V n + about]

  • Their daughter's death had taught him humility. [VERB noun noun]

    他们女儿的死使他学会了谦逊。

  • He taught his followers that they could all be members of the kingdom of God. [VERB noun that]

    他教导信徒们说，他们都可以升入天国。

  • Teach them to voice their feelings. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    训练他们敢于表达自己的感受。

3 VERB 教授;讲授;教书 If you teach or teach a subject, you help students to learn about it by explaining it or showing them how to do it, usually as a job at a school, college, or university. 教授;讲授;教书

  • Ingrid is currently teaching Mathematics at Shimla Public School. [VERB noun]

    英格丽德目前在希姆拉公立学校教数学。

  • The topic is not taught in degree courses. [VERB noun]

    学位课程不讲授这个课题。

  • She taught English to Japanese business people. [VERB noun + to]

    她教日本商人学英语。

  • She has taught for 34 years. [VERB]

    她已从教 34 年。

  • She taught children French. [VERB noun noun]

    她教孩子们学法语。

  • ...this twelve month taught course. [VERB-ed]

    这门为期 12 个月的课程

SYN give lessons in, lecture in, give instruction in, tutor
