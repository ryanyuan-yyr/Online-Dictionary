paralyse
pærəlaɪz ˈpærəˌlaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense paralyses, present participle paralysing, past tense , past participle paralysed
in AM, use 美国英语用 paralyze
1 VERB 使麻痹；使瘫痪 If someone is paralysed by an accident or an illness, they have no feeling in their body, or in part of their body, and are unable to move. 使麻痹；使瘫痪

  • Her married sister had been paralysed in a road accident. [be VERB-ed]

    她已婚的妹妹在一起交通事故中瘫痪了。

  • ...a virus which paralysed his legs. [VERB noun]

    使他双腿瘫痪的病毒

SYN disable, cripple, lame, debilitate  
paralysed

  • The disease left him with a paralysed right arm.

    那场病造成他右臂瘫痪。

  • ...sports for people paralysed by illness or injury.

    因病或因伤致瘫者从事的体育活动

2 VERB 使失去活动能力；使无法正常运转；使瘫痪 If a person, place, or organization is paralysed by something, they become unable to act or function properly. 使失去活动能力；使无法正常运转；使瘫痪

  • For weeks now the government has been paralysed by indecision. [be VERB-ed]

    到现在，政府因为无力决断已经陷入瘫痪达数周。

  • He was suddenly paralysed by fear. [be VERB-ed]

    他突然被吓得动弹不得。

  • The strike has virtually paralysed the island. [VERB noun]

    那场罢工实际上使该岛陷入瘫痪。

SYN immobilize, freeze, halt, disable  
paralysed

  • He was absolutely paralysed with shock.

    他惊得呆若木鸡。

  • ...an indefinite period of chaos, with disrupted air services and a paralysed civil service.

    无限期的混乱，航空服务中断，行政部门瘫痪

paralysing

  • ...paralysing shyness.

    令人气馁的害羞

  • ...a wave of paralysing strikes.

    造成瘫痪的罢工潮
