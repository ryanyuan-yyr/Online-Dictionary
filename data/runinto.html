run into
1 PHRASAL VERB 遇到(问题、困难等);陷入(困境) If you run into problems or difficulties, you unexpectedly begin to experience them. 遇到(问题、困难等);陷入(困境)

  • They agreed to sell last year after they ran into financial problems. [VERB PREP. noun]

    陷入财政困境以后，他们于去年同意出售。

  • But the government's plans have run into strong opposition from civil rights campaigners. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    但是政府的计划意外遭到了民权运动人士的强烈反对。

SYN be beset by, encounter, meet with, come across or upon  
2 PHRASAL VERB 意外碰到 If you run into someone, you meet them unexpectedly. 意外碰到

  • He ran into Krettner in the corridor a few minutes later. [VERB PREP. noun]

    几分钟之后他在走廊里意外碰到了克雷特纳。

SYN meet, encounter, bump into, run across  
3 PHRASAL VERB (车辆意外地)撞上，撞倒 If a vehicle runs into something, it accidentally hits it. (车辆意外地)撞上，撞倒

  • The driver failed to negotiate a bend and ran into a tree. [VERB PREP. noun]

    司机没能顺利拐弯，撞到了树上。

SYN collide with, hit, strike, ram  
4 PHRASAL VERB (费用或数量)高达，达到 You use run into when indicating that the cost or amount of something is very great. (费用或数量)高达，达到

  • He said companies should face punitive civil penalties running into millions of pounds. [V P amount]

    他说公司应该面临高达几百万英镑的惩罚性民事罚款。
