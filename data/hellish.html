hellish
helɪʃ ˈhɛlɪʃ
1 ADJ-GRADED 地狱般的;讨厌的;可憎的 You describe something as hellish to emphasize that it is extremely unpleasant. 地狱般的;讨厌的;可憎的 [informal, emphasis]

  • The atmosphere in Washington is hellish.

    华盛顿的气氛令人憎恶。

  • I'm sure you will remember from the hellish years of your boyhood.

    我相信你一定记得自己梦魇般的童年时代的事情。

2 ADV [ADVERB adjective] …得要命;…得够呛 You use hellish to emphasize an unpleasant quality or its great extent. …得要命;…得够呛 [informal, old-fashioned, emphasis]

  • It's hellish cold up here in winter.

    这里的冬天冷得要命。

SYN very, extremely, terribly, seriously [informal]
