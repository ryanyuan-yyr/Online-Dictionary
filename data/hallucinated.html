hallucinate
həluːsɪneɪt həˈlusɪˌneɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense hallucinates, present participle hallucinating, past tense , past participle hallucinated
VERB （因生病或吸毒而）产生幻觉 If you hallucinate, you see things that are not really there, either because you are ill or because you have taken a drug. （因生病或吸毒而）产生幻觉 [Also VERB that]

  • Hunger made him hallucinate. [VERB]

    饥饿使他产生了幻觉。

  • If you stared long enough and hard, you could even begin to hallucinate the appearance of small islands. [VERB noun]

    如果长时间目不转睛地盯着看，你甚至会产生幻觉，看到一些小岛的出现。

SYN imagine, trip [informal] , envision, daydream
