segregate
segrɪgeɪt ˈsɛgrəˌgeɪt ˈsɛgrəgɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense segregates, present participle segregating, past tense , past participle segregated
VERB 使隔离;使分开;使分离 To segregate two groups of people or things means to keep them physically apart from each other. 使隔离;使分开;使分离

  • Police segregated the two rival camps of protesters. [VERB noun]

    警察把两个敌对阵营的抗议人群隔离开来。

  • They segregate you from the rest of the community. [VERB noun prep.]

    他们把你与社团的其他成员隔离开。

SYN set apart, divide, separate, isolate
