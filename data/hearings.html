hearing
hɪərɪŋ ˈhɪrɪŋ
Word forms: plural hearings
1 N-UNCOUNT [oft poss NOUN] 听觉;听力 A person's or animal's hearing is the sense which makes it possible for them to be aware of sounds. 听觉;听力

  • His mind still seemed clear and his hearing was excellent.

    他的头脑似乎还清楚，听觉也很好。

SYN sense of hearing, auditory perception, ear, aural faculty  
2 N-COUNT 听证会;听审;聆讯 A hearing is an official meeting which is held in order to collect facts about an incident or problem. 听证会;听审;聆讯

  • The judge adjourned the hearing until next Tuesday.

    法官宣布休庭至下周二再审。

SYN inquiry, trial, investigation, industrial tribunal  
3 See also: hard of hearing 
4 PHRASE 发表意见的机会 If someone gives you a fair hearing or a hearing, they listen to you when you give your opinion about something. 发表意见的机会

  • Weber gave a fair hearing to anyone who held a different opinion.

    韦伯让持不同意见的人都能有机会发表意见。

5 PHRASE 在…听力所及的范围内 If someone says something in your hearing or within your hearing, you can hear what they say because they are with you or near you. 在…听力所及的范围内

  • No one spoke disparagingly of her father in her hearing.

    没有人在她跟前说过她父亲的坏话。
