sneaky
sniːki ˈsniki
Word forms: comparative sneakier, superlative sneakiest
ADJ-GRADED 秘密的;鬼祟的;偷偷摸摸的 If you describe someone as sneaky, you disapprove of them because they do things secretly rather than openly. 秘密的;鬼祟的;偷偷摸摸的 [informal, disapproval]

  • It is a sneaky and underhand way of doing business.

    这种做生意的方式偷偷摸摸，遮遮掩掩。

  • One kid can generally tell when another kid is sneaky.

    一个小孩鬼鬼祟祟地在干什么事情的时候，通常另一个小孩可以判断出来。

SYN sly, dishonest, devious, mean
