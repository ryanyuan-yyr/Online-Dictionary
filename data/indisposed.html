indisposed
ɪndɪspoʊzd ˌɪndɪˈspəʊzd ˌɪndɪˈspoʊzd
ADJ [usually verb-link ADJ] 不舒服的;有病的 If you say that someone is indisposed, you mean that they are not available because they are ill, or for a reason that you do not want to reveal. 不舒服的;有病的 [formal]

  • The speaker was regrettably indisposed.

    很遗憾,演讲人身体不适。

SYN ill, poorly [informal] , sick, crook [Australian , New Zealand , informal]
