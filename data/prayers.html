prayer
preəʳ ˈpreɪə prɛə ˈpreɪər prɛr
Word forms: plural prayers
1 N-UNCOUNT 祈祷；祷告 Prayer is the activity of speaking to God. 祈祷；祷告

  • They had joined a religious order and dedicated their lives to prayer and good works.

    他们已加入一个宗教团体，终生都将祈祷和行善。

  • The night was spent in prayer.

    整个晚上都在祷告。

SYN supplication, devotion, communion  
2 N-COUNT 祈祷文；祷词 A prayer is the words a person says when they speak to God. 祈祷文；祷词

  • They should take a little time and say a prayer for the people on both sides.

    他们应该花点时间，为双方的人祈祷。

  • ...prayers of thanksgiving.

    感恩节祈祷文

SYN orison, litany, invocation, intercession  
3 N-COUNT 祈求的事；祈望 You can refer to a strong hope that you have as your prayer . 祈求的事；祈望

  • This drug could be the answer to our prayers.

    这种药或许可以满足我们的祈望。

4 N-PLURAL 祈祷仪式 A short religious service at which people gather to pray can be referred to as prayers . 祈祷仪式

  • He promised that the boy would be back at school in time for evening prayers.

    他保证男孩会及时回到学校参加晚祈祷。

  • ...Muslims attending prayers in the main mosque.

    在大清真寺参加祈祷的穆斯林

5 PHRASE [VERB inflects, oft PHR of -ing] 不可能成功 If you say that someone hasn't got a prayer, you mean that it is impossible for them to succeed in what they are trying to do. 不可能成功 [informal]

  • She'd been caught from behind and hadn't a prayer of freeing herself.

    有人从后面抓住她，她不可能脱身。
