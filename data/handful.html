handful
hændfʊl ˈhændˌfʊl
Word forms: plural handfuls
1 N-SING 几个;少数 A handful of people or things is a small number of them. 几个;少数

  • He surveyed the handful of customers at the bar. [+ of]

    他打量着吧台那边零星的几个顾客。

  • One spring morning a handful of potential investors assembled in Quincy.

    春天的一个早上，几位有望投资者聚集在昆西。

2 N-COUNT 一把 A handful of something is the amount of it that you can hold in your hand. 一把

  • She scooped up a handful of sand and let it trickle through her fingers. [+ of]

    她抓起一把沙子，让它从指缝里一点点漏下来。

3 N-SING 难管的人（尤指孩子） If you say that someone, especially a child, is a handful, you mean that they are difficult to control. 难管的人（尤指孩子） [informal]

  • Zara can be a handful sometimes.

    扎拉有时很难管教。

SYN nuisance, bother, pest, pain in the neck [informal]
