stare out
PHRASAL VERB 把…盯得不敢对视下去 If you stare someone out, you look steadily into their eyes for such a long time that they feel that they have to turn their eyes away from you. 把…盯得不敢对视下去

  • He glared at Nikitin but the General Secretary stared him out with hard, pebble-like eyes. [VERB noun PREP.]

    他怒视着尼基廷，但总书记圆睁双眼，用严厉的目光瞪得他转移了视线。
