scandalize
skændəlaɪz ˈskændəˌlaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense scandalizes, present participle scandalizing, past tense , past participle scandalized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 scandalise
VERB 冒犯;使震惊;使反感;使愤慨 If something scandalizes people, they are shocked or offended by it. 冒犯;使震惊;使反感;使愤慨

  • She scandalised her family by falling in love with a married man. [VERB noun]

    她爱上了一个已婚男子，这使得她的家人丢尽了脸。

SYN shock, outrage, appal, disgust
