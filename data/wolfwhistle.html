wolf-whistle
ˈwʊlfwɪsəl
(also wolf whistle)Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense wolf-whistles, present participle wolf-whistling, past tense , past participle wolf-whistled
VERB (挑逗地)吹口哨 If someone wolf-whistles, they make a whistling sound with a short rising note and a longer falling note. Some men wolf-whistle at a woman to show that they think she is attractive, and some women find this offensive. (挑逗地)吹口哨 [Also VERB]

  • They wolf-whistled at me, and I was so embarrassed I tripped up. [VERB + at]

    他们吹口哨挑逗我，我窘迫之下绊了一跤。

Wolf whistle is also a noun.

  • Her dancing brought loud cheers, wolf whistles and applause.

    她的舞蹈赢来震耳的欢呼声、口哨声和掌声。
