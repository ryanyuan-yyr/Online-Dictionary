kibosh
kaɪbɒʃ ˈkaɪˌbɒʃ ˈkaɪˌbɑʃ kɪˈbɑʃ
PHRASE 使失败; 阻止继续If someone or something puts the kibosh on your plans or activities, they cause them to fail or prevent them from continuing. 使失败; 阻止继续 [mainly US, informal]

  • Rattray, however, personally showed up at the meeting to try and put the kibosh on their plans.

    然而，拉特雷亲自出现在会议上，试图挫败他们的计划。

  • ...software that puts the kibosh on pop-up ads if a user doesn't want them.

    ...如果一位用户不想要自动弹出的广告出现就阻止它们的软件。
