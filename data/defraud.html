defraud
dɪfrɔːd diˈfrɔd dɪˈfrɔd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense defrauds, present participle defrauding, past tense , past participle defrauded
VERB 诈骗，欺骗（以获得) If someone defrauds you, they take something away from you or stop you from getting what belongs to you by means of tricks and lies. 诈骗，欺骗（以获得)

  • He pleaded guilty to charges of conspiracy to defraud the government. [VERB noun]

    他承认了阴谋诈骗政府的罪名。

  • ...allegations that he defrauded taxpayers of thousands of dollars. [V n + of/out of]

    声称他从纳税人手中骗取了数千美元的指控

SYN cheat, rob, con [informal] , do [slang]
