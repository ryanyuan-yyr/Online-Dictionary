zero
zɪəroʊ əʊ ˈzɪroʊ ˈziroʊ
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense zeros, plural , 3rd person singular present tense zeroes, present participle zeroing, past tense , past participle zeroed
1 NUM （数字）0 Zero is the number 0. （数字）0

  • Visibility at the city's airport came down to zero, bringing air traffic to a standstill.

    该市机场的能见度降为零，空中交通陷入了停顿。

  • ...a scale ranging from zero to seven.

    从0到7的范围

2 N-UNCOUNT （气温的）零度 Zero is a temperature of 0˚. It is freezing point on the Centigrade and Celsius scales, and 32˚ below freezing point on the Fahrenheit scale. （气温的）零度

  • It's a sunny late winter day, just a few degrees above zero.

    那是晚冬阳光明媚的一天，温度只有零上几度。

  • That night the mercury fell to thirty degrees below zero.

    那天晚上温度计显示气温降到了零下30度。

3 ADJ 毫无;没有 You can use zero to say that there is none at all of the thing mentioned. 毫无;没有

  • This new ministry was being created with zero assets and zero liabilities.

    这个新部委正在筹建，既无资产也无负债。

  • ...zero inflation.

    零通货膨胀率

  • His chances are zero.

    他毫无机会。

Usage Note :

  • As a number, zero is used mainly in scientific contexts, or when you want to be precise. In spoken British English, nought is much more common. ...from nought to 60 in a fraction of one second. However, when you are stating a telephone number, you say o (/əʊ/). In some sports contexts, especially in football scores, nil is used. England beat Poland two-nil at Wembley. In tennis, love is the usual word. ...a two-games-to-love lead.

    zero作为数字，主要用于科学语境中，或表示精确。在英国口语中更常使用nought：from nought to 60 in a fraction of one second（瞬间从0到60）。但是在说到电话号码时用o(/əʊ/)。在某些谈及体育的语境，尤其是谈及足球比赛的得分时用nil：England beat Poland two-nil at Wembley （英格兰队在温布利以2比0战胜波兰队）。网球比赛中通常使用love：a two-games-to-love lead （先胜两局处于领先）。
