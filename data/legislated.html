legislate
ledʒɪsleɪt ˈlɛdʒɪsˌleɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense legislates, present participle legislating, past tense , past participle legislated
VERB 立法；制定（或通过）法律 When a government or state legislates, it passes a new law. 立法；制定（或通过）法律 [formal] [Also V + for/on]

  • Most member countries have already legislated against excessive overtime. [VERB + against]

    大多数成员国均已立法禁止超时加班。

  • You cannot legislate to change attitudes. [VERB to-infinitive]

    态度是不能用立法来改变的。

  • ...attempts to legislate a national energy strategy. [VERB noun]

    为国家能源战略立法的努力

SYN make laws, establish laws, prescribe, enact laws
