officer
ɒfɪsəʳ ɔːf- ˈɔfɪsər ˈɑfɪsər
Word forms: plural officers
1 N-COUNT 军官 In the armed forces, an officer is a person in a position of authority. 军官

  • ...a retired British army officer.

    退役的英国陆军军官

  • Her husband served during the Civil War as an officer in the White Army.

    她的丈夫在内战时期是一名白军的军官。

2 N-COUNT （机构的）高级职员；（尤指政府机构的）官员，公务员 An officer is a person who has a responsible position in an organization, especially a government organization. （机构的）高级职员；（尤指政府机构的）官员，公务员

  • ...a local authority education officer.

    当地政府的教育官员

SYN official, executive, agent, representative  
3 N-COUNT 警察；警官 Members of the police force can be referred to as officers . 警察；警官

  • ...senior officers in the West Midlands police force.

    西米德兰兹郡警队的高级警官

  • Thank you, Officer.

    谢谢你，警官。

SYN police officer, detective, PC, police constable
