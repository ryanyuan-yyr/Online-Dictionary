buck
bʌk bʌk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense bucks, present participle bucking, past tense , past participle bucked
1 N-COUNT 美元；澳元 A buck is a US or Australian dollar. 美元；澳元 [informal]

  • That would probably cost you about fifty bucks.

    那大概要花掉你50美元左右。

  • Why can't you spend a few bucks on a coat?. [+ on]

    你为什么不能花点儿钱买件外套呢？

  • This means big bucks for someone.

    对某些人来说这是一大笔钱。

SYN profit, earnings, return, proceeds  
2 N-COUNT （雄鹿、公羚羊、公兔、雄袋鼠等）雄性动物 A buck is the male of various animals, including the deer, antelope, rabbit and kangaroo. （雄鹿、公羚羊、公兔、雄袋鼠等）雄性动物
3 N-COUNT （美洲印第安裔或非洲裔）小伙子 A buck is a young Native American or African American man. （美洲印第安裔或非洲裔）小伙子 [US, informal, offensive]

  • He'd been a real hell-raiser as a young buck.

    他年轻时是个十足的捣蛋鬼。

4 ADJ [ADJ n] 龅牙的 If someone has buck teeth, their upper front teeth stick forward out of their mouth. 龅牙的
5 VERB （马）猛地弓背跃起，尥起后蹄跳跃 If a horse bucks, it kicks both of its back legs wildly into the air, or jumps into the air wildly with all four feet off the ground. （马）猛地弓背跃起，尥起后蹄跳跃

  • The stallion bucked as he fought against the reins holding him tightly in. [VERB]

    牡马四蹄腾空跳起，想挣脱紧紧缚住它的缰绳。

  • ...cowboys riding bucking broncos. [VERB-ing]

    骑在弓背腾跃的野马上的牛仔

6 VERB 抵抗；抵制；反抗 If you buck the trend, you obtain different results from others in the same area. If you buck the system, you get what you want by breaking or ignoring the rules. 抵抗；抵制；反抗

  • While other newspapers are losing circulation, we are bucking the trend. [VERB noun]

    当其他报纸的发行量越来越少时，我们却逆势而进。

  • He wants to be the tough rebel who bucks the system. [VERB noun]

    他想成为这种体制的坚定反抗者。

  • The company believes it is bucking the recession and says orders continue to be satisfactory. [VERB noun]

    公司认为他们正在摆脱经济萧条的影响，声称订单仍然让人满意。

SYN resist, oppose, defy, thwart  
7 PHRASE 优厚的回报 If you get more bang for the buck, you spend your money wisely and get more for your money than if you were to spend it in a different way. 优厚的回报 [mainly US, informal]

  • I think it's very important for those governments to do whatever they can to get a bigger bang for the buck.

    我认为，重要的是那些政府要采取一切措施来获取更大的资金回报。

8 PHRASE 轻易赚来的钱；（常指）不义之财 When someone makes a fast buck or makes a quick buck, they earn a lot of money quickly and easily, often by doing something which is considered to be dishonest. 轻易赚来的钱；（常指）不义之财 [informal]

  • His life isn't ruled by looking for a fast buck.

    他的生活没有被一味想发大财的心态左右。

  • They were just in it to make a quick buck.

    他们做这事就是为了迅速赚大钱。

9 PHRASE 赚钱；挣钱 If you are trying to make a buck, you are trying to earn some money. 赚钱；挣钱 [informal]

  • The owners don't want to overlook any opportunity to make a buck.

    老板们不想错过任何可以赚钱的机会。

10 PHRASE 一丝不挂 Someone who is buck naked is not wearing any clothes at all. 一丝不挂 [US, informal]
11 PHRASE 推卸责任 If you pass the buck, you refuse to accept responsibility for something, and say that someone else is responsible. 推卸责任 [informal]

  • David says the responsibility is Mr Smith's and it's no good trying to pass the buck.

    戴维说责任在史密斯先生，企图推脱责任是没有用的。

12 PHRASE 责任止于此/应由…承担责任 If you say 'The buck stops here' or 'The buck stops with me', you mean that you have to take responsibility for something and will not try to pass the responsibility on to someone else. 责任止于此/应由…承担责任 [informal]

  • The buck stops with him. He is ultimately responsible for every aspect of the broadcast.

    责任在于他。他是该广播节目各方面的总负责人。
