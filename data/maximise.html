maximize
mæksɪmaɪz ˈmæksəˌmaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense maximizes, present participle maximizing, past tense , past participle maximized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 maximise
1 VERB 使增加至最大限度;充分利用(或重视) If you maximize something, you make it as great in amount or importance as you can. 使增加至最大限度;充分利用(或重视) [Also + of]

  • In order to maximize profit the firm would seek to maximize output. [VERB noun]

    为了获得最大利润，这家公司会把产量增至最大。

  • They were looking for suitable ways of maximising their electoral support. [VERB noun]

    他们正在寻找合适的途径来最大限度地获得选民的支持。

SYN optimize  
maximization

  • ...a pricing policy that was aimed at profit maximisation.

    着眼于实现利益最大化的定价政策

2 VERB 使(计算机屏幕上的窗口)最大化 If you maximize a window on a computer screen, you make it as large as possible. 使(计算机屏幕上的窗口)最大化

  • Click on the square icon to maximize the window. [VERB noun]

    点击方框，把窗口最大化。
