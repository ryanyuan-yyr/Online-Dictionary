compensation
kɒmpənseɪʃən ˌkɒmpɛnˈseɪʃən ˌkɑmpənˈseɪʃən
Word forms: plural compensations
1 N-UNCOUNT 补偿(或赔偿)金 Compensation is money that someone who has experienced loss or suffering claims from the person or organization responsible, or from the state. 补偿(或赔偿)金

  • He received one year's salary as compensation for loss of office. [+ for]

    他得到一年的工资作为失业补偿金。

  • There should be compensation for British farmers hit by the slump in demand.

    应该给受到需求锐减影响的英国农民提供补偿金。

  • The Court ordered Dr Williams to pay £300 compensation and £100 costs after admitting assault.

    在承认侵犯他人身体罪后，法庭判令威廉斯医生支付300英镑的赔偿金和100英镑的诉讼费。

SYN reparation, damages, payment, recompense  
2 N-VAR (对不利事情的)补偿 If something is some compensation for something bad that has happened, it makes you feel better. (对不利事情的)补偿

  • Helen gained some compensation for her earlier defeat by winning the final open class. [+ for]

    海伦在公开组决赛中取胜，这对她早先的失败算是一种补偿。

  • Despite a reduction in earnings there are compensations in moving to the north-east where the quality of life is excellent.

    尽管钱会挣得少一点，搬到生活质量高的东北部去还是有很多好处的。

  • The toy glider she left him as a Christmas present was no compensation for her absence.

    她留给他作为圣诞礼物的玩具滑翔机并不能替代她的存在。

SYN recompense, amends, reparation, restitution
