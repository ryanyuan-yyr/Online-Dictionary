hate
heɪt heɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense hates, present participle hating, past tense , past participle hated
1 VERB 仇恨;憎恨;憎恶 If you hate someone or something, you have an extremely strong feeling of dislike for them. 仇恨;憎恨;憎恶

  • Most people hate him, but they don't dare to say so, because he still rules the country. [VERB noun]

    人们大多憎恨他，但不敢说，因为他仍在统治这个国家。

  • I hated myself for writing that letter. [VERB noun]

    我恨自己写了那封信。

SYN detest, loathe, despise, dislike  
Hate is also a noun.

  • I was 17 and filled with a lot of hate.

    我那时17岁，怀着满腔仇恨。

  • It is difficult to bear the agony of our loved ones' anger and hate.

    为自己所爱的人所怨恨，这种滋味不好受。

  • ...eyes that held a look of chronic hate.

    总是充满仇恨的双眼

hated

  • He's probably the most hated man in this county.

    他可能是这个县里最遭人憎恨的人。

2 VERB [no cont] 讨厌;厌恶;不喜欢 If you say that you hate something such as a particular activity, you mean that you find it very unpleasant. 讨厌;厌恶;不喜欢

  • Ted hated parties, even gatherings of people he liked individually. [VERB noun]

    特德讨厌各种派对，即便是他自己喜欢的人的聚会也不例外。

  • She hated hospitals and didn't like the idea of having an operation. [VERB noun]

    她讨厌医院，也不想做手术。

  • He hates to be interrupted during training. [VERB to-infinitive]

    他不喜欢在训练过程中被人打扰。

  • He hated coming home to the empty house. [VERB verb-ing]

    他不喜欢回到空荡荡的家里。

  • I hate it when people accuse us of that. [VERB noun wh]

    我讨厌别人就那件事指责我们。

  • I would hate him to think I'm trying to trap him. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    我不愿让他以为我是在给他设圈套。

  • She hates me having any fun and is quite jealous and spoiled. [VERB noun verb-ing]

    她见不得我开心，嫉妒心强，被惯坏了。

SYN dislike, detest, shrink from, recoil from  
3 VERB [no cont] 我真不愿麻烦你/我真不想打扰你 You can use hate in expressions such as 'I hate to trouble you' or 'I hate to bother you' when you are apologizing to someone for interrupting them or asking them to do something. 我真不愿麻烦你/我真不想打扰你 [politeness]

  • I hate to rush you but I have another appointment later on. [VERB to-infinitive]

    我真不想催你，但我稍后还有一个约会。

4 VERB [no cont] 我很抱歉这么说/我很遗憾地通知你 You can use hate in expressions such as 'I hate to say it' or 'I hate to tell you' when you want to express regret about what you are about to say, because you think it is unpleasant or should not be the case. 我很抱歉这么说/我很遗憾地通知你 [feelings]

  • I hate to tell you this, but tomorrow's your last day. [VERB to-infinitive]

    我很抱歉地通知你，明天是你的最后一天了。

  • I hate to admit it, but you were right. [VERB to-infinitive]

    我不得不承认，你是对的。

SYN be unwilling, regret, be reluctant, hesitate  
5 to hate someone's guts→see: gut 
6 VERB [no cont] 我很不愿意看到/我很不愿意想 You can use hate in expressions such as 'I hate to see' or 'I hate to think' when you are emphasizing that you find a situation or an idea unpleasant. 我很不愿意看到/我很不愿意想 [emphasis]

  • I just hate to see you doing this to yourself. [VERB to-infinitive]

    我真不愿意看到你这样对待自己。

7 VERB [no cont] 真希望…不是真的（或不会发生） You can use hate in expressions such as 'I'd hate to think' when you hope that something is not true or that something will not happen. 真希望…不是真的（或不会发生）

  • I'd hate to think my job would not be secure if I left it temporarily. [VERB to-infinitive]

    希望我的工作不会因为我暂时休假而不保。
