displease
dɪspliːz dɪsˈpliz ˈdɪspliz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense displeases, present participle displeasing, past tense , past participle displeased
VERB 使不愉快;使生气;得罪;惹怒 If something or someone displeases you, they make you annoyed or rather angry. 使不愉快;使生气;得罪;惹怒

  • Not wishing to displease her, he avoided answering the question. [VERB noun]

    为了不惹她生气，他对这个问题避而不答。

SYN annoy, upset, anger, provoke
