lead up to liːd
1 PHRASAL VERB （一系列事件）导致，致使，引致 The events that led up to a particular event happened one after the other until that event occurred. （一系列事件）导致，致使，引致

  • Alan Tomlinson has reconstructed the events that led up to the deaths. [VERB PREP. PREP. noun]

    艾伦·汤姆林森再现了导致多人死亡的一系列事件。

  • They had a series of arguments, leading up to a decision to separate. [VERB PREP. PREP. noun]

    他们经过连番争吵后决定分居。

2See also:lead-up 
3 PHRASAL VERB [usually cont] （时间）临近，紧挨在…之前 The period of time leading up to an event is the period of time immediately before it happens. （时间）临近，紧挨在…之前

  • ...the weeks leading up to Christmas. [VERB PREP. PREP. noun]

    圣诞节前的几个星期

4 PHRASAL VERB （把谈话）引向（某个话题） If someone leads up to a particular subject, they gradually guide a conversation to a point where they can introduce it. （把谈话）引向（某个话题）

  • I'm leading up to something quite important. [VERB PREP. PREP. noun]

    我正要谈到相当重要的部分。

SYN introduce, approach, prepare for, intimate
