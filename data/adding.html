add
æd æd
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense adds, present participle adding, past tense , past participle added
1 VERB 添加；增加；掺加 If you add one thing to another, you put it in or on the other thing, to increase, complete, or improve it. 添加；增加；掺加 [Also VERB n]

  • Add the grated cheese to the sauce. [VERB noun + to]

    把磨碎的干酪加到调味汁里。

  • Since 1908, chlorine has been added to drinking water. [be VERB-ed + to]

    自1908年以来氯就被添加进饮用水当中。

  • He wants to add a huge sports complex to Binfield Manor. [VERB noun + to]

    他想给宾菲尔德庄园加盖一个大型综合体育场。

SYN include, attach, supplement, increase by  
2 VERB 加 If you add numbers or amounts together, you calculate their total. 加

  • Banks add all the interest and other charges together. [VERB noun with together]

    银行合计所有利息和其他费用。

  • Two and three added together are five. [V-ed together]

    2加3等于5。

SYN count up, total, reckon, sum up  
Add up means the same as add .add up同add

  • More than a quarter of seven year-olds cannot add up properly.

    7岁的孩子中1/4以上都不会正确地做加法运算。

  • We just added all the numbers up and divided one by the other.

    我们只是将所有的数字相加并用一个除以另一个。

  • He said the numbers simply did not add up.

    他说这些数字根本对不上。

3 VERB 增添；增加 If one thing adds to another, it makes the other thing greater in degree or amount. 增添；增加

  • This latest incident will add to the pressure on the government. [VERB + to]

    最近的这次事件将会给政府增加压力。

  • Smiles, nods, and cheerful faces added to the general gaiety. [VERB to noun]

    微笑、点头和喜洋洋的脸庞使喜庆的气氛变得更浓了。

4 VERB 增加，增添（特色） To add a particular quality to something means to cause it to have that quality. 增加，增添（特色）

  • The generous amount of garlic adds flavour. [VERB noun]

    蒜多了能提味。

  • Pictures add interest to plain painted walls. [VERB noun + to]

    相片给素色的墙面增添了一抹情趣。

5 VERB 补充说;进一步说 If you add something when you are speaking, you say something more. 补充说;进一步说

  • 'You can tell that he is extremely embarrassed,' Mr Brigden added. [VERB with quote]

    “你能看出他尴尬极了，”布里格登先生补充道。

  • The President agreed, adding that he hoped for a peaceful solution. [VERB that]

    总统表示赞同并补充说他希望和平解决。

  • Hunt added his congratulations, saying 'Nigel has made a cracking job of it'. [VERB noun]

    亨特在致完贺词后又补了一句：“奈杰尔干得棒极了。”

SYN continue to speak, go on, carry on, persist  
6 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 另外；再者 You use added to this or added to that to introduce a fact that supports or widens what you are saying. 另外；再者

  • More than 750 commercial airliners were involved in fatal accidents last year. Added to that were the 1,550 smaller aircraft.

    去年750多家商业航空公司遭遇过人员死亡的事故。另外还有1,550架小型飞机也出过同样的事故。

7 to add insult to injury→see: insult
