result
rɪzʌlt rɪˈzʌlt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense results, present participle resulting, past tense , past participle resulted
1 N-COUNT [oft as a N] 结果;后果;成果;效果 A result is something that happens or exists because of something else that has happened. 结果;后果;成果;效果

  • Compensation is available for people who have developed asthma as a direct result of their work.

    直接因工作原因患上哮喘的人会获得赔偿。

  • A real pizza oven gives better results than an ordinary home oven.

    专门的比萨烤箱比普通家用烤箱效果更好。

2 VERB 导致;引起;造成 If something results in a particular situation or event, it causes that situation or event to happen. 导致;引起;造成

  • Fifty per cent of road accidents result in head injuries. [VERB + in]

    50％的道路交通事故都会导致头部损伤。

  • Regular trips back to her adopted motherland have resulted in her first book, Tiger Balm. [VERB in noun]

    经常返回她移居的国家让她写出了她的第一本书《万金油》。

3 VERB 由…产生;由…引起 If something results from a particular event or action, it is caused by that event or action. 由…产生;由…引起

  • Many hair problems result from what you eat. [VERB + from]

    很多头发问题都是由饮食引起的。

  • Ignore the early warnings and illness could result. [VERB]

    忽略早期征兆可能会导致疾病。

SYN arise, follow, issue, happen  
4 N-COUNT (比赛的)结果，结局 A result is the situation that exists at the end of a contest. (比赛的)结果，结局

  • 'What was the result?' — 'One-nil to Leeds.'.

    “比赛结果怎样？”—“利兹队以1比0获胜。”

  • The final election results will be announced on Friday.

    选举的最终结果将在周五宣布。

  • ...the football results.

    足球赛结果

5 N-COUNT (计算的)结果;得数 A result is the number that you get when you do a calculation. (计算的)结果;得数

  • They found their computers producing different results from exactly the same calculation.

    他们发现他们的计算机进行完全相同的计算会得出不同的结果。

6 N-COUNT [usually plural] (考试的)成绩，分数 Your results are the marks or grades that you get for examinations you have taken. (考试的)成绩，分数 [mainly British]

  • Kate's exam results were excellent.

    凯特的考试成绩非常出色。
