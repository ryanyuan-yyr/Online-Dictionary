see
siː si
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense sees, present participle seeing, past tense saw, past participle seen
1 VERB [no cont] 看见;看到 When you see something, you notice it using your eyes. 看见;看到

  • You can't see colours at night. [VERB noun]

    晚上看不见颜色。

  • I saw a man making his way towards me. [VERB noun verb-ing]

    我看到一名男子向我走来。

  • She can see, hear, touch, smell, and taste. [VERB]

    她有视觉、听觉、触觉、嗅觉和味觉。

  • As he neared the farm, he saw that a police car was parked outside it. [VERB that]

    他走近农场时，看到一辆警车停在外面。

  • Did you see what happened? [VERB wh]

    你看到发生什么事了吗？

SYN perceive, note, spot, notice  
Usage Note :

  • You use see to talk about things that you are aware of because a visual impression reaches your eyes. You often use can in this case. I can see the fax here on the desk. If you want to say that someone is paying attention to something they can see, you say that they are looking at it or watching it. In general, you look at something that is not moving, while you watch something that is moving or changing. I asked him to look at the picture above his bed... He watched Blake run down the stairs.

    see表示因眼睛获得视觉图像而感觉到某物的存在，常与 can 连用，例如，I can see the fax here on the desk (我能看见放在桌子上的那份传真)。如果想表示某人正在注意看眼睛所看见的某件东西，则用 look at 或 watch。一般而言，look at 用于表示看静止的物体，watch 用于表示看移动或变化的物体:I asked him to look at the picture above his bed (我让他看他床头上方的那幅画), He watched Blake run down the stairs (他看着布莱克跑下楼梯)。

2 VERB 与…见面;看望;会见 If you see someone, you visit them or meet them. 与…见面;看望;会见

  • I saw him yesterday. [VERB noun]

    我昨天见到他了。

  • Mick wants to see you in his office right away. [VERB noun]

    米克要你马上去他的办公室见他。

  • You need to see a doctor. [VERB noun]

    你需要去看医生。

3 VERB [no cont] 观看，看(娱乐节目等) If you see an entertainment such as a play, film, concert, or sports game, you watch it. 观看，看(娱乐节目等)

  • He had been to see a Semi-Final of the FA Cup. [VERB noun]

    他看过一场足总杯半决赛。

  • It was one of the most amazing films I've ever seen. [VERB noun]

    这是我看过的最精彩的电影之一。

SYN watch, view, look at, observe  
4 VERB [no cont] 看出;意识到 If you see that something is true or exists, you realize by observing it that it is true or exists. 看出;意识到

  • I could see she was lonely. [VERB that]

    我能看出她很寂寞。

  • ...a lot of people saw what was happening but did nothing about it. [VERB wh]

    很多人都看出发生了什么事却袖手旁观。

  • You see young people going to school inadequately dressed for the weather. [VERB noun verb-ing]

    你看到年轻人在这种天气里衣衫单薄地去上学。

  • My taste has changed a bit over the years as you can see. [VERB]

    如你所见，这些年我的口味有点改变。

  • You've just been cleaning it, I see. [VERB]

    我看得出你刚才一直在打扫。

  • The army must be seen to be taking firm action. [be VERB-ed to-infinitive]

    军队必须采取坚决行动。

5 VERB [no cont, no passive] 明白;领会;理解 If you see what someone means or see why something happened, you understand what they mean or understand why it happened. 明白;领会;理解

  • Oh, I see what you're saying. [VERB wh]

    哦，我明白你的意思了。

  • I don't see why you're complaining. [VERB wh]

    我不明白你有什么可抱怨的。

  • I really don't see any reason for changing it. [VERB noun]

    我真的不明白为什么要改变它。

  • Now I see that I was wrong. [VERB that]

    现在我知道我错了。

SYN understand, get, follow, realize  
6 VERB 认为;把…看作 If you see someone or something as a certain thing, you have the opinion that they are that thing. 认为;把…看作

  • She saw him as a visionary, but her father saw him as a man who couldn't make a living. [VERB noun + as]

    她认为他是一个有眼光的人，但是她父亲却认为他没有谋生的能力。

  • They have a normal body weight but see themselves as being fat. [V n as n/-ing]

    他们体重正常，但自认为偏胖。

  • Others saw it as a betrayal. [VERB noun + as]

    别人认为这是一种背叛。

  • I don't see it as my duty to take sides. [VERB noun + as]

    我认为我没必要非得向着一方。

  • As I see it, Llewelyn has three choices open to him. [VERB it]

    据我看，卢埃林有 3 个选择。

  • Women are sometimes seen to be less effective as managers. [be VERB-ed to-infinitive]

    女性有时被认为不善于管理。

7 VERB [no cont, no passive] 看出;看上，欣赏(某种特质) If you see a particular quality in someone, you believe they have that quality. If you ask what someone sees in a particular person or thing, you want to know what they find attractive about that person or thing. 看出;看上，欣赏(某种特质)

  • Frankly, I don't know what Paul sees in her. [VERB noun + in]

    坦白地说，我不知道保罗看上她什么了。

  • Young and old saw in him an implacable opponent of apartheid. [VERB + in]

    无论老少都看出他是种族隔离制度的坚决反对者。

8 VERB [no cont] 想象;预测 If you see something happening in the future, you imagine it, or predict that it will happen. 想象;预测

  • A good idea, but can you see Taylor trying it?. [VERB noun verb-ing]

    主意不错，不过你认为泰勒会尝试它吗？

  • We can see a day where all people live side by side. [VERB noun]

    我们可以想象有那么一天所有人都和睦地生活在一起。

SYN foresee, picture, imagine, anticipate  
9 VERB [no passive] 目睹;经历;见证 If a period of time or a person sees a particular change or event, it takes place during that period of time or while that person is alive. 目睹;经历;见证

  • Yesterday saw the resignation of the acting Interior Minister. [VERB noun]

    昨天代理内政部长辞职了。

  • He had worked with the General for three years and was sorry to see him go. [VERB noun infinitive]

    他与将军共事了 3 年，看他离去觉得很难过。

  • Mr Frank has seen the economy of his town slashed by the uprising. [VERB noun -ed]

    弗兰克先生亲历了自己城镇的经济因暴动而大幅下滑的过程。

SYN witness, watch, observe, view  
10 VERB 察看;弄清;了解 You can use see in expressions to do with finding out information. For example, if you say 'I'll see what's happening', you mean that you intend to find out what is happening. 察看;弄清;了解

  • Let me just see what the next song is. [VERB wh]

    我来看看下一首歌是什么。

  • Every time we asked our mother, she said, 'Well, see what your father says.'. [VERB wh]

    每次我们问母亲，她总是说：“噢，看你爸爸怎么说。”

  • Shake him gently to see if he responds. [V if]

    轻轻摇晃他看他是否有反应。

11 VERB 想办法，尽力(帮忙) You can use see to promise to try and help someone. For example, if you say 'I'll see if I can do it', you mean that you will try to do the thing concerned. 想办法，尽力(帮忙)

  • I'll see if I can call her for you. [V if]

    我会尽力帮你把她叫来。

  • We'll see what we can do, miss. [VERB wh]

    我们会尽量想办法，小姐。

SYN consider, decide, think about, judge  
12 VERB [no cont] 确保;保证 If you see that something is done or if you see to it that it is done, you make sure that it is done. 确保;保证

  • See that you take care of him. [VERB that]

    一定要照顾好他。

  • Catherine saw to it that the information went directly to Walter. [V to it that]

    凯瑟琳确保这条消息直接传达给了沃尔特。

SYN make sure, mind, ensure, guarantee  
13 VERB 护送;陪伴 If you see someone to a particular place, you accompany them to make sure that they get there safely, or to show politeness. 护送;陪伴

  • He didn't offer to see her to her car. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他没有提出要陪她去取车。

  • 'Goodnight.' — 'I'll see you out.' [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    “晚安。”—“我送你出去。”

SYN accompany, show, escort, lead  
14 VERB 遇到;拜访 If you see a lot of someone, you often meet each other or visit each other. 遇到;拜访

  • We used to see quite a lot of his wife, Carolyn. [V amount + of]

    我们以前经常见到他的妻子卡罗琳。

  • We didn't see much of each other after that because he was touring. [V amount of n]

    从那之后我们没怎么见过面，因为他去旅行了。

15 VERB 与…交往;与…谈恋爱 If you are seeing someone, you spend time with them socially, and are having a romantic or sexual relationship. 与…交往;与…谈恋爱

  • My husband was still seeing her and he was having an affair with her. [VERB noun]

    我的丈夫仍在和她来往，而且两人还搞婚外情。

SYN go out with, court, date [informal , mainly US] , have an affair with  
16 VERB (我们)知道，了解 Some writers use see in expressions such as we saw and as we have seen to refer to something that has already been explained or described. (我们)知道，了解

  • We saw in Chapter 16 how annual cash budgets are produced. [VERB wh]

    在第 16 章我们了解了年度现金预算是怎样制订的。

  • Using the figures given above, it can be seen that machine A pays back the initial investment in two years. [VERB that]

    从以上的数字我们可以看出，机器A在两年后就收回了最初的投资。

  • As we have seen in previous chapters, visualization methods are varied. [VERB that]

    我们在前几章已经了解到，可视化方法是多种多样的。

17 VERB (书中)见，参见 See is used in books to indicate to readers that they should look at another part of the book, or at another book, because more information is given there. (书中)见，参见

  • Surveys consistently find that men report feeling safe on the street after dark. See, for example, Hindelang and Garofalo (1978). [VERB noun]

    多项调查一致显示，男性认为天黑后在大街上行走是安全的。比如，参见欣德朗和加罗法洛(1978)。

  • See Chapter 7 below for further comments on the textile industry. [VERB noun]

    有关纺织业的更多评论请参看下面第 7 章。

18 PHRASE 既然;因为;鉴于 You can use seeing that or seeing as to introduce a reason for what you are saying. 既然;因为;鉴于 [mainly British, informal, spoken]

  • He is in the marriage bureau business, which is mildly ironic seeing that his dearest wish is to get married himself.

    他从事婚姻介绍工作，这多少有点讽刺意味，因为他最大的愿望就是自己能结婚。

  • Seeing as Mr Moreton is a doctor, I would assume he has a modicum of intelligence.

    既然莫尔顿先生是位医生，我想他应该有点聪明才智。

19 CONVENTION 我明白;原来如此 You can say 'I see' to indicate that you understand what someone is telling you. 我明白;原来如此 [spoken, formulae]

  • 'He came home in my car.' — 'I see.'

    “他坐我的车回家来的。”—“哦，是这样。”

SYN I understand, right, uh huh, mmm  
20 CONVENTION (表示暂不作决定)看看吧，再说吧 People say 'I'll see' or 'We'll see' to indicate that they do not intend to make a decision immediately, and will decide later. (表示暂不作决定)看看吧，再说吧

  • We'll see. It's a possibility.

    再说吧，可能会的。

21 CONVENTION (表示试图记起某事或找到某物)让我想一想，让我看看 People say 'let me see' or 'let's see' when they are trying to remember something, or are trying to find something. (表示试图记起某事或找到某物)让我想一想，让我看看

  • Let's see, they're six — no, make that five hours ahead of us.

    让我想一想，他们比我们早 6 个—不，就算是早 5 个小时完成。

  • Now let me see, who's the man we want?

    现在让我看一看，谁是我们要找的人？

22 PHRASE 意识到错误;明白事理 If you try to make someone see sense or see reason, you try to make them realize that they are wrong or are being stupid. 意识到错误;明白事理

  • He was hopeful that by sitting together they could both see sense and live as good neighbours.

    他希望他们坐到一起，彼此能清醒理智，和睦为邻。

  • He tried again to get her to see reason.

    他再次试图让她明白自己错了。

23 CONVENTION (用于向某人解释某事物并鼓励其倾听和理解)你瞧，你知道，看吧 You can say 'you see' when you are explaining something to someone, to encourage them to listen and understand. (用于向某人解释某事物并鼓励其倾听和理解)你瞧，你知道，看吧 [spoken]

  • Well, you see, you shouldn't really feel that way about it.

    好了，知道吗，对那件事你真的不应该那么想。

  • She was a prime target for blackmail, don't you see?

    她是敲诈的主要目标。你难道看不出来吗？

24 CONVENTION 再见;回头见;呆会儿见 'See you', 'be seeing you', and 'see you later' are ways of saying goodbye to someone when you expect to meet them again soon. 再见;回头见;呆会儿见 [informal, spoken, formulae]

  • 'Talk to you later.' — 'All right. See you love.'

    “ 回头再跟你谈。”—“好的。再见，亲爱的。”

  • 'No time for chattering now.' — 'Be seeing you, then.'

    “现在没空闲聊。”—“那么回见。”

25 CONVENTION 到时候你就知道了;你等着瞧吧 You can say 'You'll see' to someone if they do not agree with you about what you think will happen in the future, and you believe that you will be proved right. 到时候你就知道了;你等着瞧吧

  • The thrill wears off after a few years of marriage. You'll see.

    婚后几年这种兴奋感就会逐渐消逝。你等着瞧吧。

Usagelook , see , and watch
If you look at something, you purposely direct your eyes at it: Daniel kept turning around to look at the big-screen TV-he had never seen one before. If you see something, it is visible to you: Maria couldn't see the TV because Hector was standing in front of her and watching it. If you watch something, you pay attention to it and keep it in sight: Everyone was watching TV instead of looking at the photo album.
