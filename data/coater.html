coat
koʊt koʊt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense coats, present participle coating, past tense , past participle coated
1 N-COUNT 上衣;外衣;外套 A coat is a piece of clothing with long sleeves which you wear over your other clothes when you go outside. 上衣;外衣;外套

  • He turned off the television, put on his coat and walked out.

    他关掉电视，穿上外套，出门了。

SYN jacket, overcoat  
2 N-COUNT [usually with poss] (动物的)皮毛 An animal's coat is the fur or hair on its body. (动物的)皮毛

  • Vitamin B6 is great for improving the condition of dogs' and horses' coats.

    维生素B6对于改善狗和马的皮毛有显著的作用。

SYN fur, hair, skin, hide  
3 VERB 给…涂上(或盖上、裹上) If you coat something with a substance or in a substance, you cover it with a thin layer of the substance. 给…涂上(或盖上、裹上)

  • Coat the fish with seasoned flour. [V n + with/in]

    用拌有佐料的面粉将鱼裹上。

SYN cover, spread, plaster, smear  
coated

  • TV pictures showed a dying bird coated with oil.

    电视画面上出现了一只身上沾满油污、奄奄一息的小鸟。

  • Dip the pieces so they are completely coated.

    把切片浸一浸，使其完全被包裹。

4 N-COUNT (油漆、清漆等的)一层 A coat of paint or varnish is a thin layer of it on a surface. (油漆、清漆等的)一层

  • The front door needs a new coat of paint. [+ of]

    前门需要刷一层新漆了。

  • You will need to apply three coats of varnish.

    需要刷三层清漆。

SYN layer, covering, coating, overlay
