vaccinate
væksɪneɪt ˈvæksəˌneɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense vaccinates, present participle vaccinating, past tense , past participle vaccinated
VERB [usually passive] 给…接种疫苗 If a person or animal is vaccinated, they are given a vaccine, usually by injection, to prevent them from getting a disease. 给…接种疫苗

  • Dogs must be vaccinated against distemper. [be V-ed + against]

    狗必须接种犬瘟热疫苗。

  • Have you had your child vaccinated against whooping cough?. [have n V-ed against n]

    你给你的孩子打过百日咳疫苗了吗？

  • Measles, mumps and whooping cough are spreading again because children are not being vaccinated. [be/get V-ed]

    因为孩子们没接种疫苗，麻疹、腮腺炎和百日咳又蔓延开来。

SYN inoculate, inject, protect, immunize  
vaccination
