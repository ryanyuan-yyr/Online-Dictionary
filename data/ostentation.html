ostentation
ɒstenteɪʃən ˌɑstənˈteɪʃən
N-UNCOUNT 炫耀；夸示；卖弄 If you describe someone's behaviour as ostentation, you are criticizing them for doing or buying things in order to impress people. 炫耀；夸示；卖弄 [formal, disapproval]

  • Consumers are abandoning the excess and ostentation of the 1980s.

    消费者摒弃了20世纪80年代铺张摆阔的陋习。

  • On the whole she had lived modestly, with a notable lack of ostentation.

    大体上说，她生活俭朴，不事张扬。

SYN display, show, parade, boasting
