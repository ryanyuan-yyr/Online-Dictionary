swindle
swɪndəl ˈswɪndəl ˈswɪndəl
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense swindles, present participle swindling, past tense , past participle swindled
VERB 诈骗,骗取，欺诈(尤指钱财) If someone swindles a person or an organization, they deceive them in order to get something valuable from them, especially money. 诈骗,骗取，欺诈(尤指钱财)

  • A City businessman swindled investors out of millions of pounds. [V n + out of]

    一名来自伦敦商业区的商人骗取了投资者们数百万英镑。

  • An oil executive swindled £50,000 out of his firm. [V n out of n]

    一名石油公司主管从其公司骗取了 5 万英镑。

SYN cheat, do [slang] , con, skin [slang]  
Swindle is also a noun.

  • He fled to Switzerland rather than face trial for a tax swindle.

    他没有等着因涉嫌税收诈骗去接受审讯，而是逃到了瑞士。

swindler
