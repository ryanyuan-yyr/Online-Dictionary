truck
trʌk trʌk
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense trucks, present participle trucking, past tense , past participle trucked
1 N-COUNT 卡车 A truck is a large vehicle that is used to transport goods by road. 卡车 [mainly US]

  • Now and then they heard the roar of a heavy truck.

    他们不时地听到大卡车的轰鸣声。

SYN lorry, juggernaut, HGV [British] , heavy goods vehicle  
in BRIT, usually use 英国英语通常用 lorry
2 N-COUNT (铁路)运货车皮，敞篷货运火车 A truck is an open vehicle used for carrying goods on a railway. (铁路)运货车皮，敞篷货运火车 [British]

  • They were loaded on the railway trucks to go to Liverpool.

    它们被装上开往利物浦的货运火车。

in AM, use 美国英语用 freight car
3 VERB [usually passive] 用卡车装运;用货车运送 When something or someone is trucked somewhere, they are driven there in a lorry. 用卡车装运;用货车运送 [mainly US]

  • The liquor was sold legally and trucked out of the state. [be VERB-ed prep./adv.]

    该酒合法出售，用卡车运出了该州。

SYN buy and sell, deal, trade, exchange  
4 PHRASE 不愿参与;不与…来往;不掺和 If you say that you will have no truck with someone or something, you are refusing to be involved with them in any way. 不愿参与;不与…来往;不掺和

  • He would have no truck with deceit.

    他不愿去骗人。

  • As an American, she had no truck with the painful formality of English life.

    作为一名美国人，她不愿拘泥于英国人生活中令人非常不舒服的繁文缛节。
