dissipate
dɪsɪpeɪt ˈdɪsəˌpeɪt
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense dissipates, present participle dissipating, past tense , past participle dissipated
1 V-ERG (使)消失;消除;驱散 When something dissipates or when you dissipate it, it becomes less or becomes less strong until it disappears or goes away completely. (使)消失;消除;驱散 [formal]

  • The tension in the room had dissipated. [VERB]

    房间里的紧张气氛消失了。

  • He wound down the windows to dissipate the heat. [VERB noun]

    他摇下窗子来散热。

SYN disappear, fade, vanish, dissolve  
dissipation

  • ...heat dissipation.

    散热

2 VERB 浪费(金钱、时间或精力);挥霍 When someone dissipates money, time, or effort, they waste it in a foolish way. 浪费(金钱、时间或精力);挥霍 [formal]

  • He is dissipating his time and energy on too many different things. [VERB noun]

    他在太多不同的事情上浪费时间和精力。

  • Her father had dissipated her inheritance. [VERB noun]

    她父亲已经挥霍掉了她继承的遗产。

SYN squander, spend, waste, consume  
dissipation

  • ...the dissipation of my wealth.

    对我财富的挥霍
