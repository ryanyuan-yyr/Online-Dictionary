federal
fedərəl ˈfɛdərəl
Word forms: plural federals
1 ADJ-GRADED [ADJ n] 联邦的;联邦制的 A federal country or system of government is one in which the different states or provinces of the country have important powers to make their own laws and decisions. 联邦的;联邦制的

  • Five of the six provinces are to become autonomous regions in a new federal system of government. [+ of]

    在新的联邦政府体制中，6 省中的 5 个将成为自治区。

2 ADJ-GRADED [ADJ n] 中央集权式的;集权式联邦制的 Some people use federal to describe a system of government which they disapprove of, in which the different states or provinces are controlled by a strong central government. 中央集权式的;集权式联邦制的 [disapproval]

  • He does not believe in a federal Europe with centralising powers.

    他认为一个集权式联邦制在欧洲是不可行的。

3 ADJ [ADJ n] 归属联邦政府的;与联邦政府有关的 Federal also means belonging or relating to the national government of a federal country rather than to one of the states within it. 归属联邦政府的;与联邦政府有关的

  • The federal government controls just 6% of the education budget.

    联邦政府仅掌控 6％的教育预算。

  • ...a federal judge.

    联邦法官

federally

  • ...residents of public housing and federally subsidized apartments.

    公共住房住户和享受联邦政府津贴的公寓住户

4 N-COUNT 同fed Federals are the same as feds . 同fed
