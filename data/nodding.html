nod
nɒd nɑd
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense nods, present participle nodding, past tense , past participle nodded
1 VERB [no passive] 点头;点头赞同 If you nod, you move your head downwards and upwards to show that you are answering 'yes' to a question, or to show agreement, understanding, or approval. 点头;点头赞同

  • 'Are you okay?' I asked. She nodded and smiled. [VERB]

    “你没事吧?”我问她。她点点头，笑了。

  • David said nothing, but simply nodded, as if understanding perfectly. [VERB]

    戴维什么都没说，只是点点头，好像完全理解了。

  • Jacques tasted one and nodded his approval. [VERB noun]

    雅克尝了一个，点头称好。

  • 'Oh, yes,' she nodded. 'I understand you very well.' [VERB with quote]

    “噢，是的，”她点点头说，“我非常理解你。”

SYN agree, concur, assent, show agreement  
Nod is also a noun.

  • She gave a nod and said, 'I see'.

    她点了点头说：“我明白了。”

  • 'Probably,' agreed Hunter, with a slow nod of his head.

    亨特慢慢点了点头，表示赞同地说：“很有可能。”

  • He gave Sabrina a quick nod of acknowledgement.

    他朝萨布里娜快速点点头，示意看到了她。

2 VERB [no passive] (朝…方向)扬头，扬头示意 If you nod in a particular direction, you bend your head once in that direction in order to indicate something or to give someone a signal. (朝…方向)扬头，扬头示意

  • 'Does it work?' he asked, nodding at the piano. [VERB prep.]

    “它能用吗？”他边问边朝钢琴方向扬了下头。

  • She nodded towards the drawing room. 'He's in there.'. [VERB prep.]

    她朝客厅方向扬了扬头说：“他在那里面。”

  • He lifted the end of the canoe, nodding to me to take up mine. [VERB + to]

    他一边抬起独木舟的一端，一边扬头示意我抬起我这端。

SYN signal, indicate, motion, gesture  
3 VERB [no passive] (用于打招呼或告别)点头，点头招呼 If you nod, you bend your head once, as a way of saying hello or goodbye. (用于打招呼或告别)点头，点头招呼

  • All the girls nodded and said 'Hi'. [VERB]

    所有的女孩都点头招呼道：“嗨!”

  • Tom nodded a greeting but didn't say anything. [VERB noun]

    汤姆点头致意，但什么都没说。

  • Both of them smiled and nodded at friends. [V + at/to]

    他们俩都笑着跟朋友们点头打招呼。

  • They nodded goodnight to the security man. [VERB noun + to]

    他们跟保安点头道晚安。

SYN salute, acknowledge, nutate [rare]  
4 VERB (足球中)顶(球) In football, if a player nods the ball in a particular direction, they hit the ball there with their head. (足球中)顶(球) [British, informal]

  • Taylor leapt up to nod the ball home. [VERB noun adv./prep.]

    泰勒跳起来头球破门。

  • Brian McClair nodded in his twenty-third goal of the season. [VERB adv.]

    布赖恩·麦克莱尔头球攻入了自己本赛季第23粒进球。

5 PHRASE [VERB inflects] 点头同意;点头允许 If you give someone the nod, you give them permission to do something. 点头同意;点头允许 [informal]

  • 'Keep him outside till I give you the nod.'

    “没我同意，别让他进来。”

6 PHRASE [PHRASE after verb] (建议)一致接受地，毫无异议地 If a proposal is accepted on the nod, it is accepted without being questioned or argued about. (建议)一致接受地，毫无异议地 [British, informal]

  • He has always argued that the party cannot be seen to let the treaty through on the nod.

    他一直争辩，不能眼看着该党让此条约毫无异议地通过。

  • Big issues are going through on the nod.

    重大议题将会一致通过。
