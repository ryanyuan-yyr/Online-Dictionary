badly
bædli ˈbædli
Word forms: comparative worse, superlative worst
1 ADV-GRADED [ADVERB with verb] 坏；差；拙劣地；不成功地 If something is done badly or goes badly, it is not very successful or effective. 坏；差；拙劣地；不成功地

  • I was angry because I played so badly.

    我很生气，因为自己的表现实在是太差了。

  • The whole project was badly managed.

    整个项目管理不善。

  • The coalition did worse than expected, getting just 11.6 per cent of the vote.

    联盟只获得了11.6%的选票，比预想的还糟。

SYN poorly, incorrectly, carelessly, inadequately  
2 ADV-GRADED [ADVERB with verb, ADVERB adjective] 严重地；厉害地；剧烈地 If someone or something is badly hurt or badly affected, they are severely hurt or affected. 严重地；厉害地；剧烈地

  • The bomb destroyed a police station and badly damaged a church.

    炸弹摧毁了一处警察局，并使一座教堂严重受损。

  • One man was killed and another badly injured.

    一人死亡，一人重伤。

  • It was a gamble that went badly wrong.

    这一冒险代价惨重。

SYN severely, greatly, deeply, seriously  
3 ADV-GRADED [ADVERB with verb] 非常；极度 If you want or need something badly, you want or need it very much. 非常；极度

  • Why do you want to go so badly?.

    为什么你这么想走？

  • Planes landed at Bagram airport today carrying badly needed food and medicine.

    运载急需食物和药品的飞机今天在巴格拉姆机场降落。

SYN much, seriously, desperately  
4 ADV-GRADED [ADVERB with verb] 不友好地；令人不快地；恶劣地 If someone behaves badly or treats other people badly, they act in an unkind, unpleasant, or unacceptable way. 不友好地；令人不快地；恶劣地

  • They have both behaved very badly and I am very hurt.

    他们俩都很不友善，让我非常难过。

  • I would like to know why we pensioners are being so badly treated.

    我想知道为什么我们这些领养老金的人受到如此恶劣的对待。

5 ADV-GRADED [ADVERB after verb] 不利地；负面地；有损名声地 If something reflects badly on someone or makes others think badly of them, it harms their reputation. 不利地；负面地；有损名声地

  • Teachers know that low exam results will reflect badly on them.

    老师知道考试成绩不好将对他们不利。

  • The male sex comes out of the film very badly.

    这部影片中男性的形象非常负面。

  • Despite his illegal act, few people think badly of him.

    尽管他干了违法的事情，但很少有人认为他不好。

6 ADV-GRADED [usually ADVERB -ed, oft ADVERB after verb] （报酬）低；（钱）少 If a person or their job is badly paid, they are not paid very much for what they do. （报酬）低；（钱）少

  • You may have to work part-time, in a badly paid job with unsociable hours.

    你或许只能做个薪水低、非正常工作时间上班的兼职。

  • This is the most dangerous professional sport there is, and the worst paid.

    这是最危险的职业体育运动，但报酬却是最低的。

SYN poorly, meanly, inadequately, insufficiently  
7 See also: worse  worst
