midway
mɪdweɪ ˈmɪdˌweɪ ˈmɪdˌweɪ ˈmɪdˈweɪ
(also mid-way)
1 ADV 中途;半路;中间 If something is midway between two places, it is between them and the same distance from each of them. 中途;半路;中间

  • The studio is midway between his aunt's old home and his cottage.

    工作室就在从他姑姑的老宅到他的小屋的中途。

SYN halfway, in the middle of, part-way, equidistant  
Midway is also an adjective.

  • ...the midway point between Gloucester, Hereford and Worcester.

    格洛斯特、赫里福德和伍斯特三地之间的中间点

2 ADV [ADVERB after verb] (时间)中间地，一半地 If something happens midway through a period of time, it happens during the middle part of it. (时间)中间地，一半地

  • He crashed midway through the race. [+ through]

    他在比赛中途撞车了。

  • He returned midway through the afternoon.

    他下午三四点时返回了。

Midway is also an adjective.

  • They were denied an obvious penalty before the midway point of the first half.

    在上半场前半段，他们明显应得到一个点球，裁判却没有判。
