umbrage
ʌmbrɪdʒ ˈʌmbrɪdʒ
1 PHRASE 愤懑；不快；生气 If you say that a person takes umbrage, you mean that they are upset or offended by something that someone says or does to them, often without much reason. 愤懑；不快；生气 [formal]

  • He takes umbrage against anyone who criticises him.

    不管谁批评他，他都心生愤懑。

SYN take offence, be hurt, be angry, be offended
