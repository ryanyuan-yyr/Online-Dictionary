go ahead
1 PHRASAL VERB 着手；开始做 If someone goes ahead with something, they begin to do it or make it, especially after planning, promising, or asking permission to do it. 着手；开始做 [Also VERB PREPOSITION]

  • The district board will vote today on whether to go ahead with the plan. [VERB PREP. + with]

    地区理事会将于今天投票决定是否开始实施这一计划。

SYN continue, begin, go on, advance  
2 PHRASAL VERB 进行；开展 If a process or an organized event goes ahead, it takes place or is carried out. 进行；开展

  • The event will go ahead as planned in Sheffield next summer. [VERB PREP.]

    这项活动将按计划于明年夏天在谢菲尔德举行。

go-ahead ˈɡəʊəˌhɛd ˈgoʊəˌhɛd
1 N-SING 许可；批准 If you give someone or something the go-ahead, you give them permission to start doing something. 许可；批准

  • The Greek government today gave the go-ahead for five major road schemes.

    希腊政府今天批准了5项重大的道路修建方案。

  • Don't do any major repair work until you get the go-ahead from your insurers.

    除非经保险公司同意，否则不要进行任何大修。

SYN permission, consent, green light, assent  
2 ADJ-GRADED [ADJ n] 有进取心的；具有开拓精神的 A go-ahead person or organization tries hard to succeed, often by using new methods. 有进取心的；具有开拓精神的

  • Fairview Estate is one of the oldest and the most go-ahead wine producers in South Africa.

    “丽景庄园”是南非历史最悠久、最具创新精神的酿酒商之一。

SYN enterprising, pioneering, ambitious, progressive
