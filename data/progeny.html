progeny
prɒdʒəni ˈprɑdʒəni
1 N-PLURAL [usually with poss] （人的）后裔，子女；（动物的）幼崽 You can refer to a person's children or to an animal's young as their progeny. （人的）后裔，子女；（动物的）幼崽 [formal]

  • Davis was never loquacious on the subject of his progeny.

    戴维斯说到他的子女时总是寡言少语。

2 N-PLURAL [usually with poss] （某物的）新一代，下一代 The progeny of a particular thing are the things that develop from it. （某物的）新一代，下一代

  • Among its many progeny, the 1944 Education Act gave birth to the modern youth service.

    在1944年通过的《教育法案》的诸多衍生条款中，就包括了现代青年教育服务。
