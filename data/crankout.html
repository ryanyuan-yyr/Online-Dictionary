crank out
PHRASAL VERB 大量地快速制造;粗制滥造 If you say that a company or person cranks out a quantity of similar things, you mean they produce them quickly, in the same way, and are usually implying that the things are not original or are of poor quality. 大量地快速制造;粗制滥造 [informal, disapproval]

  • In 1933 the studio cranked out fifty-five feature films. [VERB PREP. noun (not pronoun)]

    1993年，该电影公司粗制滥造出了55部故事片。

  • The writer must have cranked it out in his lunch-hour. [VERB noun PREP.]

    作者一定是在午饭时间把它赶出来的。
