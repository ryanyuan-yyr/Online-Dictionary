frigid
frɪdʒɪd ˈfrɪdʒɪd
1 ADJ-GRADED 寒冷的;极冷的 Frigid means extremely cold. 寒冷的;极冷的 [formal]

  • A snowstorm hit the West today, bringing with it frigid temperatures.

    暴风雪今天袭击了西部地区，导致气温骤降。

  • The water was too frigid to allow him to remain submerged for long.

    水冰冷彻骨，他在下面呆不了太长时间。

SYN freezing, cold, frozen, icy  
2 ADJ-GRADED [usually verb-link ADJ] (女子)性冷淡的 If a woman is frigid, she finds it difficult to become sexually aroused. You can often use frigid to show disapproval. (女子)性冷淡的

  • My husband says I am frigid.

    丈夫说我性冷淡。

SYN sexually unresponsive, cold, distant, unfeeling  
frigidity

  • ...an inability to experience orgasm (often called frigidity).

    无法体验到性高潮(常称为性冷淡)

3 ADJ-GRADED 拘谨的;冷淡的;不友好的 If you describe the atmosphere in a place or someone's behaviour as frigid, you mean that it is very formal and unfriendly. 拘谨的;冷淡的;不友好的 [disapproval]

  • He presided at all councils of ministers, where the atmosphere could be frigid on occasions.

    所有的内阁会议都由他主持，有时会上的气氛会很拘谨。

  • 'Well, dear,' her hostess would reply with a frigid smile.

    “噢，亲爱的，”她的女主人会笑容僵硬地回答。
