nobble
nɒbəl ˈnɑbəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense nobbles, present participle nobbling, past tense , past participle nobbled
1 VERB 收买，贿赂，恐吓(委员会等) If someone nobbles an important group of people such as a committee, they offer them money or threaten them in order to make them do something. 收买，贿赂，恐吓(委员会等) [British, informal]

  • The trial was stopped before Christmas after allegations of attempts to nobble the jury. [VERB noun]

    企图收买陪审团的指控传出后，审判在圣诞节前中止了。

  • Sir Gerald had been nobbled. [VERB noun]

    杰拉尔德爵士已经被收买了。

SYN influence, square, win over, pay off [informal]  
2 VERB (赛马前用药物等)害(马)使其不能取胜 If someone nobbles a racehorse, they deliberately harm it, often using drugs, in order to prevent it from winning a race. (赛马前用药物等)害(马)使其不能取胜 [British, informal]

  • ...the drug used to nobble two horses at Doncaster. [VERB noun]

    在唐克斯特用来阻止两匹马取胜的药物

SYN disable, handicap, weaken, incapacitate  
3 VERB 阻止，阻挠(实现愿望) If someone nobbles your plans or chances of succeeding, they prevent you from achieving what you want. 阻止，阻挠(实现愿望) [British, informal]

  • His opportunity to re-establish himself had been nobbled by the manager's tactics. [be VERB-ed]

    他重新树立自己地位的机会因教练的战术而流产了。
