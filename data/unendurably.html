unendurable
ʌnɪndjʊərəbəl -dʊr-
ADJ 不能忍受的；难以容忍的 If you describe a bad situation as unendurable, you mean that it is so extremely unpleasant that you have to end it. 不能忍受的；难以容忍的 [formal]

  • Isaac had found the work unendurable and walked out of the job.

    艾萨克觉得这份工作实在令人难以忍受，就辞职走人了。

  • He had not expected the pain to be unendurable.

    他没料到会如此疼痛难忍。

  • It has placed an almost unendurable strain on their marriage.

    这给他们的婚姻带来了几乎难以承受的压力。

SYN unbearable, intolerable, insufferable, too much [informal]
