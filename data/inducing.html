induce
ɪndjuːs -duːs- ɪnˈdus ɪnˈdjus
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense induces, present participle inducing, past tense , past participle induced
1 VERB 引起;导致 To induce a state or condition means to cause it. 引起;导致

  • Doctors said surgery could induce a heart attack. [VERB noun]

    医生们说手术有可能引起心脏病发作。

  • ...an economic crisis induced by high oil prices. [VERB-ed]

    由高油价引发的经济危机

SYN cause, produce, create, begin  
2 VERB 引诱;劝说 If you induce someone to do something, you persuade or influence them to do it. 引诱;劝说

  • I would do anything to induce them to stay. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    我会想尽一切办法劝他们留下来。

  • More than 4,000 teachers were induced to take early retirement. [be VERB-ed to-infinitive]

    4000 多名教师被说服提前退休。

SYN persuade, encourage, influence, get  
3 VERB 用人工方法引(产) If a doctor or nurse induces labour or birth, they cause a pregnant woman to start giving birth by using drugs or other medical means. 用人工方法引(产) [medicine]

  • He might decide that it is best to induce labour. [VERB noun]

    他也许决定最好引产。

induction

  • ...if there are obvious medical reasons for induction.

    如果有明显的医学原因需要进行引产
