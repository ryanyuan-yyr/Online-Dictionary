haunt
hɔːnt hɔnt hɑnt hænt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense haunts, present participle haunting, past tense , past participle haunted
1 VERB （不愉快的事）萦绕在心头，缠绕 If something unpleasant haunts you, you keep thinking or worrying about it over a long period of time. （不愉快的事）萦绕在心头，缠绕

  • The decision to leave her children now haunts her. [VERB noun]

    抛下孩子们的决定现在总让她不安。

  • He would always be haunted by that scene in Well Park. [VERB noun]

    他永远无法摆脱威尔公园里的那一幕。

SYN plague, trouble, obsess, torment  
2 VERB 长期困扰 Something that haunts a person or organization regularly causes them problems over a long period of time. 长期困扰

  • The stigma of being a bankrupt is likely to haunt him for the rest of his life. [VERB noun]

    破产的耻辱可能会让他余生不得安宁。

3 N-COUNT 常去的地方 A place that is the haunt of a particular person is one which they often visit because they enjoy going there. 常去的地方

  • The Channel Islands are a favourite summer haunt for UK and French yachtsmen alike.

    海峡群岛是英国和法国的帆船运动爱好者夏日最爱去的地方。

SYN meeting place, resort, hangout [informal] , den  
4 VERB （鬼魂等）常出没于 A ghost or spirit that haunts a place or a person regularly appears in the place, or is seen by the person and frightens them. （鬼魂等）常出没于

  • His ghost is said to haunt some of the rooms, banging a toy drum. [VERB noun]

    据说他的鬼魂经常敲着玩具鼓出没在其中的一些房间里。

SYN appear in, materialize in
