aptitude
æptɪtjuːd -tuːd ˈæptəˌtud ˈæptəˌtjud
Word forms: plural aptitudes
N-VAR 天资;天赋;才能 Someone's aptitude for a particular kind of work or activity is their ability to learn it quickly and to do it well. 天资;天赋;才能

  • An aptitude for computing is beneficial for students taking this degree. [+ for]

    计算才能对于攻读这个学位的学生有益处。

  • Some students have more aptitude for academic work than others.

    一些学生比其他学生更有学术才能。

SYN gift, ability, talent, capacity
