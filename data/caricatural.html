caricature
kærɪkətʃʊəʳ -tʃər ˈkærɪkətʃər ˈkærɪkəˌtʃʊr
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense caricatures, present participle caricaturing, past tense , past participle caricatured
1 N-COUNT 漫画;讽刺画;夸张性（或讽刺性）描述 A caricature of someone is a drawing or description of them that exaggerates their appearance or behaviour in a humorous or critical way. 漫画;讽刺画;夸张性（或讽刺性）描述

  • The poster showed a caricature of Hitler with a devil's horns and tail. [+ of]

    海报漫画中的希特勒长着魔鬼的犄角和尾巴。

  • The Spanish Steps were crowded with the sellers of grotesque caricatures.

    西班牙阶梯上挤满了出售荒诞漫画的人。

SYN parody, cartoon, distortion, satire  
2 VERB 把…画成漫画;用讽刺性手法描述;夸大 If you caricature someone, you draw or describe them in an exaggerated way in order to be humorous or critical. 把…画成漫画;用讽刺性手法描述;夸大 [Also V n as n]

  • Her political career has been caricatured in headlines. [be VERB-ed]

    她的政治生涯在头条新闻里遭到夸张嘲弄。

  • He was caricatured as a turnip. [be VERB-ed + as]

    在漫画里他被画成了一棵大头菜。

SYN parody, take off [informal] , mock, distort  
3 N-COUNT 夸大;夸张 If you describe something as a caricature of an event or situation, you mean that it is a very exaggerated account of it. 夸大;夸张 [disapproval]

  • Hall is angry at what he sees as a caricature of the training offered to modern-day social workers. [+ of]

    在霍尔看来,当代社会工作者所受的培训被大肆夸大,他感到很气愤。
