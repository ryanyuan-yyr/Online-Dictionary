confidence
kɒnfɪdəns ˈkɑnfədəns
Word forms: plural confidences
1 N-UNCOUNT 信任;信赖 If you have confidence in someone, you feel that you can trust them. 信任;信赖

  • I have every confidence in you. [+ in]

    我完全相信你。

  • This has contributed to the lack of confidence in the police. [+ in]

    这导致了大家对警方缺乏信任。

  • His record on ceasefires inspires no confidence.

    他以往在停火方面的表现让人对他毫无信心。

SYN trust, belief, faith, dependence  
2 N-UNCOUNT 自信;信心 If you have confidence, you feel sure about your abilities, qualities, or ideas. 自信;信心

  • The band is on excellent form and brimming with confidence.

    这支乐队状态极佳，自信满满。

  • I always thought the worst of myself and had no confidence whatsoever.

    我老是妄自菲薄，没有一点儿信心。

SYN self-assurance, courage, assurance, aplomb  
3 N-UNCOUNT [usu with N] 肯定;把握 If you can say something with confidence, you feel certain it is correct. 肯定;把握

  • I can say with confidence that such rumors were totally groundless.

    我可以肯定地说这样的谣言完全没有根据。

4 N-UNCOUNT [usually in NOUN] 私下;秘密 If you tell someone something in confidence, you tell them a secret. 私下;秘密

  • We told you all these things in confidence.

    我们告诉你的这些事都需要保密。

  • Even telling Lois seemed a betrayal of confidence.

    即使是告诉洛伊丝都好像是在泄密。

If you take someone into your confidence, you tell them a secret.向…吐露秘密

  • If your daughter takes you into her confidence, don't rush off to tell your husband.

    如果女儿告诉你她的秘密，不要急着说给丈夫听。

5 N-COUNT 秘密;知心话 A confidence is a secret that you tell someone. 秘密;知心话

  • Gregory shared confidences with Carmen.

    格雷戈里和卡门说了知心话。
