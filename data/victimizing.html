victimize
vɪktɪmaɪz ˈvɪktəˌmaɪz
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense victimizes, present participle victimizing, past tense , past participle victimized
in BRIT, also use 英国英语亦用 victimise
VERB 迫害;使受害;使受苦 If someone is victimized, they are deliberately treated unfairly. 迫害;使受害;使受苦 [Also VERB n]

  • He felt the students had been victimized because they'd voiced opposition to the government. [be VERB-ed]

    他觉得学生们受到迫害是因为他们表达了对政府的不满。

SYN persecute, bully, pick on, abuse
