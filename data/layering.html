layer
leɪəʳ ˈleɪər
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense layers, present participle layering, past tense , past participle layered
1 N-COUNT 层 A layer of a material or substance is a quantity or piece of it that covers a surface or that is between two other things. 层

  • A fresh layer of snow covered the street. [+ of]

    街上覆盖了一层新雪。

  • ...the depletion of the ozone layer.

    臭氧层的损耗

  • Arrange all the vegetables except the potatoes in layers.

    把除土豆外的所有蔬菜分层放置。

SYN covering, film, cover, sheet  
2 N-COUNT 层次；阶层；等级 If something such as a system or an idea has many layers, it has many different levels or parts. 层次；阶层；等级

  • ...an astounding ten layers of staff between the factory worker and the chief executive. [+ of]

    工厂工人和首席执行官之间10个等级的巨大差距

  • Critics and the public puzzle out the layers of meaning in his photos. [+ of]

    评论界和公众揭开了他照片中的重重深意。

SYN tier, level, seam, stratum  
3 VERB 分层放置 If you layer something, you arrange it in layers. 分层放置

  • Layer the potatoes, asparagus and salmon in the tin. [VERB noun]

    把土豆、芦笋和大麻哈鱼分层装入罐头内。

  • By lifting and layering her hair, Michael created a lighter frame for her face. [VERB noun]

    通过把头发往上梳，增加层次感，迈克尔让她的脸型显得更为柔和。
