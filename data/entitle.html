entitle
ɪntaɪtəl ɛnˈtaɪtəl ɪnˈtaɪtəl
Word forms: 3rd person singular present tense entitles, present participle entitling, past tense , past participle entitled
1 VERB 给予…权利;给予…资格 If you are entitled to something, you have the right to have it or do it. 给予…权利;给予…资格

  • If the warranty is limited, the terms may entitle you to a replacement or refund. [VERB noun + to]

    如果保修有限制，根据条款你也许可以要求退换或者退款。

  • They are entitled to first class travel. [VERB noun to noun]

    他们可以坐头等舱旅行。

  • There are 23 Clubs throughout the U.S., and your membership entitles you to enjoy all of them. [VERB noun to-infinitive]

    全美共有23家俱乐部，您的会员资格可让您享受所有俱乐部的服务。

2 VERB [usually passive] 给（书、电影、绘画等）命名；给…题名 If the title of something such as a book, film, or painting is, for example, 'Sunrise', you can say that it is entitled 'Sunrise'. 给（书、电影、绘画等）命名；给…题名

  • Chomsky's review is entitled 'Psychology and Ideology'.

    乔姆斯基的这篇评论题为《心理学与意识形态》。

  • ...a performance entitled 'United States'. [V-ed quote]

    名为《美国》的一场演出
