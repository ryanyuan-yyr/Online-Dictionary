verse
vɜːʳs vɜrs
Word forms: plural verses
1 N-UNCOUNT 诗;诗体;韵文 Verse is writing arranged in lines which have rhythm and which often rhyme at the end. 诗;诗体;韵文

  • ...a slim volume of verse.

    一本薄薄的诗集

  • I have been moved to write a few lines of verse.

    我因感动而写下了几句诗。

SYN poetry, poems, lyrics, rhyme  
2See also:blank verse 
3 N-COUNT 诗节;诗行；（歌曲的）段落;（《圣经》、《古兰经》等中的）节 A verse is one of the parts into which a poem, a song, or a chapter of the Bible or the Koran is divided. 诗节;诗行；（歌曲的）段落;（《圣经》、《古兰经》等中的）节

  • This verse describes three signs of spring.

    这节诗描绘了春天来临的三个征兆。

  • The choir has sung only two verses of the last hymn.

    合唱团只唱了最后一首赞美诗的两个段落。

SYN stanza, section, stave, canto  
4 chapter and verse→see: chapter
