transport
trænspɔːʳt trænspɔːʳt trænsˈpɔrt ˈtrænsˌpɔrt
Word forms: plural , 3rd person singular present tense transports, present participle transporting, past tense , past participle transported
The noun is pronounced /'trænspɔːt/. The verb is pronounced /træns'pɔːt/. 名词读作/'trænspɔːt/。动词读作 /træns'pɔːt/。
1 N-UNCOUNT 交通工具;运输工具 Transport refers to any vehicle that you can travel in or carry goods in. 交通工具;运输工具 [mainly British]

  • Have you got your own transport?.

    你有自己的交通工具吗？

  • Which type of transport do you prefer?

    你更喜欢哪种类型的代步工具？

SYN vehicle, wheels [informal] , transportation, conveyance [old-fashioned]  
in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 transportation
2 N-UNCOUNT 交通运输系统;公共交通(网) Transport is a system for taking people or goods from one place to another, for example using buses or trains. 交通运输系统;公共交通(网) [mainly British]

  • The extra money could be spent on improving public transport.

    多余的资金可以用于改善公共交通系统。

  • The sudden onset of winter caused havoc with rail and air transport…

    冬天的突然降临让铁路和航空运输系统陷入混乱。

  • An efficient transport system is critical to the long-term future of London.

    高效的公共交通系统对伦敦长远的未来至关重要。

in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 transportation
3 N-UNCOUNT 运输;运送 Transport is the activity of taking goods or people from one place to another in a vehicle. 运输;运送 [mainly British]

  • Local production virtually eliminates transport costs.

    本地生产实际上消除了运输成本。

in AM, usually use 美国英语通常用 transportation
4 VERB 运输;运送 To transport people or goods somewhere is to take them from one place to another in a vehicle. 运输;运送

  • There's no petrol, so it's very difficult to transport goods. [VERB noun]

    没有汽油，因此很难运输货物。

  • They use tankers to transport the oil to Los Angeles. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    他们用油轮把石油输送到洛杉矶。

  • The troops were transported to Moscow. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    部队被运往莫斯科。

SYN convey, take, run, move  
5 VERB 使身处他境 If you say that you are transported to another place or time, you mean that something causes you to feel that you are living in the other place or at the other time. 使身处他境 [mainly literary]

  • Dr Drummond felt that he had been transported into a world that rivalled the Arabian Nights. [be VERB-ed prep./adv.]

    德拉蒙德博士觉得自己仿佛被带入了一个可与《天方夜谭》相媲美的世界。

  • In a dream you can be transported back in time… [be VERB-ed prep./adv.]

    在梦里你可以回到过去。

  • This delightful musical comedy transports the audience to the innocent days of 1950s America. [VERB noun prep./adv.]

    这部令人开怀的音乐喜剧把观众带到了 20 世纪 50 年代美国的纯真时期。

6 N-COUNT [usually supplement NOUN] (军用)运输工具;(尤指)运输机 A military or troop transport is a military vehicle, especially a plane, that is used to carry soldiers or equipment. (军用)运输工具;(尤指)运输机
